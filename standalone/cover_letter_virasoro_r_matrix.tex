\begin{letter}{The Editors\\
\emph{Letters in Mathematical Physics}}

\opening{Dear Editors,}

I am writing to submit the manuscript ``The Virasoro Spectral
$R$-matrix: Closed Form and Non-Formality'' for publication in
\emph{Letters in Mathematical Physics}.

The paper derives a closed-form expression for the spectral
$R$-matrix of the Virasoro algebra on a primary state of conformal
weight~$h$:
\[
R(z) = z^{2h}\exp\!\left(-\frac{c}{4z^2}\right).
\]
The derivation proceeds from the collision residue
$r^{\mathrm{coll}}(z) = (c/2)/z^3 + 2T/z$ of the Virasoro OPE via
$d\log$ absorption (reducing pole orders by one) and path-ordered
exponentiation on the commutative primary sector.

The result has three consequences.  First, only even powers of
$1/z$ appear in the $R$-matrix, reflecting bosonic parity of the
desuspended Virasoro generator.  Second, the non-terminating
character of the exponential series provides a finite algebraic
proof that the Virasoro algebra belongs to shadow class~$M$
(infinite shadow depth), distinguishing it from the Heisenberg
(polynomial $R$-matrix, class~$G$) and affine Kac--Moody
(terminating, class~$L$) families.  Third, the first correction
$R_2 = -c/4$ is the genus-zero $A_\infty$-formality obstruction:
it witnesses the failure of Swiss-cheese formality and generates
the infinite primitive-cumulant series that characterizes
class~$M$.

The cubic shadow coefficient $S_3(\mathrm{Vir}_c) = 2$ is proved
to be independent of the central charge~$c$, providing an
alternative finite proof of class~$M$ membership.

The result is self-contained and connects vertex operator algebra
theory with integrable systems and operadic deformation theory,
making it well suited to \emph{Letters in Mathematical Physics}.

This manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under
consideration by any other journal.

\closing{Sincerely,}
