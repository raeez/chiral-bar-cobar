\begin{letter}{The Editors\\
\emph{Journal of Algebra}}

\opening{Dear Editors,}

I am writing to submit the manuscript ``Chiral Koszul Duality for
Affine Kac--Moody Algebras: A Chiral Extension of Garland--Lepowsky''
for publication in the \emph{Journal of Algebra}.

The classical theorem of Garland and Lepowsky computes the
homology of the negative part
$\mathfrak{g} \otimes t^{-1}\mathbb{C}[t^{-1}]$ of an affine Lie
algebra. This paper extends the Garland--Lepowsky framework to
the chiral bar complex of the affine Kac--Moody vertex algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k$ on algebraic curves.

The main results are: (1)~$\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k$ is chirally
Koszul at all non-critical levels $k \neq -h^\vee$, with the PBW
spectral sequence collapsing at~$E_2$;
(2)~the Verdier intertwining theorem identifies the chiral Koszul
dual with the chiral envelope at the Feigin--Frenkel dual level
$-k - 2h^\vee$; (3)~bar cohomology dimensions are
level-independent outside a finite exceptional set, with
$\dim H^n(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2) = 2n{+}1$ recovering the
Garland--Lepowsky concentration; (4)~the shadow obstruction tower
terminates at degree~$3$ (class~$L$), with the Jacobi identity
killing the cubic shadow; (5)~the modular characteristic
$\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k) =
\dim(\mathfrak{g})(k{+}h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$ is additive and
satisfies $\kappa(k) + \kappa(-k{-}2h^\vee) = 0$.

The paper provides a complete worked verification of all five main
theorems of modular Koszul duality for the affine family. The
\emph{Journal of Algebra} is a natural venue given the algebraic
content: bar constructions, Koszul duality, and Lie algebra
cohomology.

This manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under
consideration by any other journal.

\closing{Sincerely,}
