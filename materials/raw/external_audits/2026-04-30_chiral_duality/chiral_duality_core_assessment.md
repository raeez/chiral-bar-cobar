- **Claim-status drift.** Every theorem-like statement needs visible status: proved here, cited, conditional, conjectural, heuristic, programme, or open.
- **Forward-reference proof risk.** Volume II/III or future open/closed material must not function as proof input for Volume I claims.
- **Ambient category drift.** D-modules, factorization algebras, chain complexes, dg categories, ind-Hilbert completions, and physical BV objects need separation.
- **Hochschild conflation hazard.** Chiral, topological, and categorical Hochschild theories must be firewall-separated everywhere.
- **Duality conflation hazard.** Bar-cobar inversion, Verdier duality, and strict Koszul duality need permanent notation and theorem-status firewalls.
- **Conditional MK3 layer.** Higher-genus inversion/complementarity/Hochschild claims must carry MK1/MK3/PBW propagation hypotheses wherever used.

- **Example computations uneven.** Some examples need explicit OPE tables, low-degree bar differentials, and consistency tests.

- **External literature burden.** Imported results need exact statement, hypothesis, and citation matching.
- **Formula normalization.** κ, central charge shifts, dual-level formulas, W-constants, sign conventions, and hbar/q normalizations need a ledger.
- **Reader audit gap.** The dependency graph, status labels, and theorem/proposition numbering are not yet navigable enough.
- **Physics language can overclaim.** Holography, Chern-Simons, strings, entropy, and arithmetic should be theorem/heuristic/programme separated.
