## What the treatise discovers

Classical Koszul duality is an algebraic operation on graded algebras over a point. Chiral Koszul duality transplants it to algebraic curves, where it acquires genus, curvature, and a modular structure. The transplant produces a single mathematical object — the modular Maurer-Cartan element Θ — that simultaneously:

- classifies twisting morphisms (the universal coupling space)
- determines the algebra on the Koszul locus (faithfulness)

- has universal scalar projection κ·λ_g (the gravitational anomaly coefficient)
- has polynomial coefficient ring (finiteness of the deformation space)

These are the five theorems. They are five facets of one object.

The object Θ lives in a modular convolution algebra whose coefficient cooperad is the chain-level moduli of stable curves. The geometric arena (Fulton-MacPherson compactification, Arnold relations, Hodge bundle) is the input; Θ is the universal section; and every derived invariant of the genus expansion is a natural transformation applied to Θ. This is the Universal Modular Deformation Theorem.
