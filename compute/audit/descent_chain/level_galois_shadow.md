# Galois-Theoretic Structure of the Shadow Tower

## 1. The Quadratic Extension

### Setup

The shadow generating function satisfies the algebraic relation
H(t)^2 = t^4 Q_L(t) (thm:riccati-algebraicity), where the shadow
metric is the quadratic polynomial

    Q_L(t) = 4*kappa^2 + 12*kappa*alpha*t + (9*alpha^2 + 16*kappa*S_4)*t^2

in t over the field F = Q(c)(t).  Setting F(t) := H(t)/t^2, we have
F(t)^2 = Q_L(t), so F(t) = sqrt(Q_L(t)) generates a quadratic
extension

    K_L = F(sqrt(Q_L))  over  F = Q(c)(t).

The Galois group is Gal(K_L/F) = Z/2, generated by the involution
sigma: sqrt(Q_L) -> -sqrt(Q_L).  This is the **Koszul sign**: the
shadow connection (thm:shadow-connection) has monodromy -1 around
each branch point, and sigma is the monodromy operator.

For **Virasoro** at central charge c, the shadow data is:

    kappa = c/2,  alpha = 2,  S_4 = 10/[c(5c+22)],  Delta = 40/(5c+22).

The Gaussian decomposition (cor:gaussian-decomposition) gives:

    Q_L(t) = (c + 6t)^2 + 80t^2/(5c+22).


## 2. Discriminant, Ramification, and Conductor

### The Discriminant

The polynomial discriminant of Q_L as a quadratic in t is
(prop:hankel-extraction(iii), eq:shadow-metric-discriminant):

    disc(Q_L) = q1^2 - 4*q0*q2 = -32*kappa^2 * Delta.

For Virasoro:

    disc(Q_L) = -32*(c/2)^2 * 40/(5c+22) = -320*c^2/(5c+22).

This is the **discriminant of the extension K_L/F** (up to squares
in F).  Its factorization:

    disc = -2^6 * 5 * c^2 * (5c+22)^{-1}.

Removing the perfect square c^2 and 2^6, the **squarefree kernel** is

    -5 * (5c+22)^{-1}   (equivalently, -5*(5c+22) up to squares).

### The Ramified Primes (in the c-direction)

Over the arithmetic base Spec Q[c], the discriminant
-320*c^2/(5c+22) vanishes or has poles at exactly two loci:

| Locus | Behavior | Shadow meaning |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| c = 0 | disc has a zero of order 2 (from c^2) | kappa = 0; bar complex uncurved; S_4 has pole |
| c = -22/5 | disc has a pole of order 1 (from (5c+22)^{-1}) | Lee-Yang central charge; S_4 has pole |

These are the **only** places where the shadow tower has poles
(prop:pole-purity): for every r >= 4,

    S_r = epsilon_r * P_r(c) / [c^{r-3} * (5c+22)^{floor((r-2)/2)}]

with P_r in Z[c] and no new irreducible factors in the denominator.

**Verification**: the pole purity theorem is verified computationally
through arity 15.  The denominator of S_r always factors as a power
of c times a power of (5c+22), matching the ramification locus of
the extension K_L/F exactly.

### The Ring of Integers

Over F = Q(c)(t), the ring of integers O_{K_L} is generated by
sqrt(Q_L).  However, the shadow coefficients a_n = (n+2)*S_{n+2}
(the Taylor coefficients of sqrt(Q_L)) are elements of Q(c), and
their denominators grow with n.  Specifically:

    a_n has (5c+22)^{floor(n/2)} in the denominator.

When c is specialized to a rational value c_0 with 5c_0 + 22 = p^a/q
for some prime p, the p-adic valuation of a_n tends to -infinity.
For example, at c = 1/2 (Ising model), 5c+22 = 49/2, and:

    v_7(a_n) ~ -n   (the shadow coefficients are NOT 7-integral).

The **conductor** of the shadow order (the order generated by the
a_n over Z[1/(c*(5c+22))]) is divisible by all primes dividing the
numerator of 5c+22 when c is specialized.  At generic c, the
conductor ideal in Q[c] is (c) * (5c+22) -- the product of the two
ramified prime ideals.


## 3. Specialized Number Fields at Minimal Models

At a Virasoro minimal model c = c_{p,q} = 1 - 6(p-q)^2/(pq) with
gcd(p,q) = 1 and 2 <= p < q, the discriminant disc(Q_L) specializes
to a rational number

    d(c_{p,q}) = -320*c_{p,q}^2 / (5*c_{p,q} + 22)  in  Q.

Its squarefree part determines a quadratic number field Q(sqrt(d)).

### Census of Shadow Number Fields

|-------|-------|---|-----------|----------|------------|------|
| Ising | (3,4) | 1/2 | -160/49 | -10 | -40 | 2 |
| (3,5) | (3,5) | -3/5 | -576/95 | -95 | -95 | 8 |
| Tricrit. Ising | (4,5) | 7/10 | -1568/255 | -510 | -2040 | 16 |
| 3-state Potts | (5,6) | 4/5 | -512/65 | -130 | -520 | 4 |
| (5,8) | (5,8) | -7/20 | -784/405 | -5 | -20 | 2 |
| (5,7) | (5,7) | 11/35 | -704/525 | -231 | -231 | 12 |
| (4,7) | (4,7) | -13/14 | -27040/1701 | -210 | -840 | 8 |

| (7,8) | (7,8) | 25/28 | -50000/5187 | -25935 | -25935 | 112 |

The class numbers grow rapidly with the complexity of the minimal
model, ranging from h = 2 (Ising, simplest non-trivial) to h = 112
((7,8) model).  The Lee-Yang model (2,5) with c = -22/5 is excluded:
it sits at the pole of S_4, and the shadow extension degenerates.

**Observation**: Models (3,8) and (3,10) yield **positive** squarefree
parts (d = 85 and d = 110 respectively), giving real quadratic fields.
This occurs when c < -22/5 (Lee-Yang region), where 5c+22 < 0, so
disc(Q_L) = -320c^2/(5c+22) > 0.  In this regime, Q_L has two real
roots (the branch points of sqrt(Q_L) are real), and the shadow tower
has sign-definite coefficients rather than oscillatory ones.

### Hilbert Class Field: Ising Model

The Ising model gives Q(sqrt(-10)) with fundamental discriminant -40
and class number h(-40) = 2.  The class group is Z/2, generated by
the non-principal form 2x^2 + 5y^2.

The Hilbert class field H_{-40} is the maximal unramified abelian
extension of Q(sqrt(-10)).  Since h = 2, it is a degree-2 extension:

    H_{-40} = Q(sqrt(-10), sqrt(5)) = Q(sqrt(-10), sqrt(-2)).

The Hilbert class field has degree 4 over Q and is generated by
sqrt(-10) and sqrt(5) (equivalently sqrt(-2)).  Its Galois group
over Q is the Klein four-group V_4 = Z/2 x Z/2.

**Shadow interpretation**: The class number h = 2 means there are
exactly two classes of ideals in O_{Q(sqrt(-10))}.  In the shadow
tower context, this bipartition corresponds to the two distinct
behaviors of primes: those representable as x^2 + 10y^2 (principal
class) and those representable as 2x^2 + 5y^2 (non-principal class).
The primes in the principal class factor completely in the Hilbert
class field; the non-principal primes remain inert in the first
layer but split in the Hilbert class field.

This has a concrete manifestation in the shadow tower: the mod-p
reduction of the shadow generating function sqrt(Q_L(t)) has
**qualitatively different behavior** depending on the ideal class
of p.  Both types split in Q(sqrt(-10)) (they are not inert),
but their splitting patterns in the ray class field extensions
differ.

### Hilbert Class Field: 3-State Potts Model

The 3-state Potts model gives Q(sqrt(-130)) with fundamental
discriminant -520 and class number h(-520) = 4.  The class group
is Cl(K) = Z/2 x Z/2 (elementary abelian 2-group).

The four reduced forms of discriminant -520 are:
x^2 + 130y^2, 2x^2 + 65y^2, 5x^2 + 26y^2, 10x^2 + 13y^2.

The discriminant factors as D = -520 = (-8)(5)(13), a product of
three prime discriminants.  Genus theory gives 2^{t-1} = 4 genera,
and since h = 4 = number of genera, each genus contains exactly
one class.  The class group coincides with the genus group, so the
Hilbert class field equals the genus field:

    H_{-520} = Q(sqrt(-130), sqrt(5), sqrt(13))
             = Q(sqrt(-2), sqrt(5), sqrt(13)).

This is an abelian extension of Q of degree 8, with Galois group
(Z/2)^3 over Q and (Z/2)^2 over K = Q(sqrt(-130)).

**Shadow interpretation**: The four ideal classes correspond to
four distinct splitting patterns of primes in the Hilbert class
field.  A prime p (coprime to 2*5*13) splits completely in H_{-520}
if and only if it is representable as x^2 + 130y^2 (the principal
form).  The other three representations (2x^2 + 65y^2, 5x^2 + 26y^2,
10x^2 + 13y^2) correspond to the three non-trivial ideal classes
and to three intermediate splitting patterns.


## 4. The Koszul Involution on Number Fields

### The Involution c -> 26-c

Koszul duality for Virasoro sends c to 26-c (Vir_c^! = Vir_{26-c}).
Under this involution:

    disc(c) = -320*c^2/(5c+22)
    disc(26-c) = -320*(26-c)^2/(152-5c)

The ratio:

    disc(c)/disc(26-c) = c^2*(152-5c) / [(26-c)^2*(5c+22)]
                       = [c/(26-c)]^2 * (152-5c)/(5c+22).

Since [c/(26-c)]^2 is already a perfect square, the two discriminants
define the **same** quadratic field if and only if

    (5c+22)(152-5c)  is a perfect square in Q.

Equivalently: (5c+22)(152-5c) = -25c^2 + 650c + 3344 must be a
perfect square.

### Computation at Minimal Models

| c | (5c+22)(152-5c) | sqfree part | Same field? |
|---|-----------------|-------------|-------------|
| 1/2 | 14651/4 | 299 | NO |
| 7/10 | 15147/4 | 187 | NO |
| 4/5 | 3848 | 962 | NO |
| 11/35 | 173745/49 | 2145 | NO |
| 6/7 | 190256/49 | 11891 | NO |
| 25/28 | 3061071/784 | 4199 | NO |
| c=13 | 87^2 = 7569 | **1** | **YES** |

**Result**: For every minimal model c_{p,q} in the table, Q(sqrt(d(c)))
and Q(sqrt(d(26-c))) are **distinct** quadratic fields.

The **unique** rational point where the two fields coincide is the
self-dual point c = 13, where:

    (5*13+22)(152-5*13) = 87 * 87 = 87^2,

a perfect square.  At c = 13: disc(13) = disc(13) (trivially, since
26 - 13 = 13), and the shadow metric is self-dual
(thm:shadow-connection(v)).

**Proposition**: The quadratic fields Q(sqrt(d(c))) and Q(sqrt(d(26-c)))
coincide if and only if -25c^2 + 650c + 3344 is a perfect square.
For rational c, this is a Pell-type equation.  The discriminant of
the quadratic -25c^2 + 650c + 3344 is 650^2 + 4*25*3344 = 756100
= 870^2, so the roots are c = (650 +/- 870)/50, giving c = 152/5 and
c = -22/5 (the dual Lee-Yang points!).  The quadratic -25c^2 + 650c
+ 3344 = -25(c + 22/5)(c - 152/5) factors completely.  It is positive
for -22/5 < c < 152/5 and perfect-square only at c = 13 (the midpoint)
among rational values that are not on the boundary.

More precisely: at c = 13, the value is 87^2 = 7569.  For any other
rational c in (-22/5, 152/5), the value (5c+22)(152-5c) has
squarefree part > 1, so the Koszul dual fields are distinct.

**Interpretation**: Koszul duality at the level of quadratic number
fields is maximally asymmetric: the shadow extensions of A and A!
live in different number fields for every minimal model.  The
self-dual point c = 13 is the unique meeting point, consistent with
the general principle that c = 13 is the unique fixed point of the
Koszul involution on the Virasoro family.


## 5. Frobenius Elements and Shadow-Theoretic Splitting

### The Splitting Dictionary

For a prime p not dividing disc(Q_L), the Frobenius element
Frob_p in Gal(K_L/F) = Z/2 is determined by the Legendre/Kronecker
symbol (d/p) where d is the squarefree part of disc(Q_L):

| (d/p) | Frobenius | Q_L mod p | Shadow behavior mod p |
|-------|-----------|-----------|----------------------|
| +1 | trivial (split) | factors as (a+bt)(a'+b't) | sqrt(Q_L) in F_p[t]; shadow LINEAR mod p |
| -1 | generator (inert) | irreducible quadratic | sqrt(Q_L) in F_{p^2}[t]; shadow QUADRATIC mod p |

### Shadow-Theoretic Interpretation

**When p splits**: Q_L(t) = (a+bt)(a'+b't) mod p.  Then sqrt(Q_L)
= a+bt mod p is a linear polynomial.  The Taylor coefficients
a_n = [t^n] sqrt(Q_L) vanish for n >= 2, so:

    a_n = 0 mod p  for all n >= 2,

and consequently the shadow coefficients (n+2)*S_{n+2} = a_n vanish
mod p for n >= 2, i.e., r*S_r = 0 mod p for all r >= 4 (provided
p does not divide r, which could re-introduce p through the 1/r
factor).  The shadow generating function H(t) = t^2*(a+bt) mod p is
a polynomial of degree 3.

The shadow tower **terminates mod p at depth 3** (up to the 1/r
factor) -- the same depth as an affine Kac-Moody algebra (class L).
The infinite-depth structure of the Virasoro shadow tower is
invisible at a split prime.

**When p is inert**: Q_L(t) is irreducible mod p, and sqrt(Q_L) lives
in F_{p^2}[t].  The shadow coefficients S_r mod p follow the full
quadratic recursion and are generically nonzero for all r.  The
infinite depth of the tower is visible mod p.

**The splitting principle**: "The prime p sees the shadow tower as
finite (class L) if and only if p splits in the shadow extension."

### Ising Model: Split and Inert Primes

For the Ising model (d = -10, fundamental discriminant -40):

**Split primes** ((-10/p) = 1): 7, 11, 13, 19, 23, 37, 41, 47, 53, 59, 89, ...
These are primes representable as x^2 + 10y^2 or 2x^2 + 5y^2
(the two reduced forms of discriminant -40, reflecting h = 2).

**Inert primes** ((-10/p) = -1): 3, 17, 29, 31, 43, 61, 67, 71, 79, 83, ...

**Ramified primes** (p | disc): 2 and 5.

**Caveat on integrality**: The shadow coefficients at c = 1/2 have
denominators involving 7 (from 5c+22 = 49/2 = 7^2/2).  Specifically:

    v_7(a_n) ~ -n

so the a_n are not 7-integral.  The splitting dictionary for p = 7
applies at the level of the number field Q(sqrt(-10)), but NOT
directly to the shadow coefficients at c = 1/2.  The correct
formulation: the splitting behavior governs the **algebraic** structure
of Q_L, while the integrality of the shadow coefficients is controlled
by the **conductor** of the shadow order, which includes primes
dividing the numerator of 5c+22.

For primes p coprime to c*(5c+22) (e.g., p = 11, 13 at c = 1/2),
the shadow coefficients are p-integral and the splitting dictionary
applies directly.

### 3-State Potts: Split and Inert Primes

For the Potts model (d = -130, fundamental discriminant -520):

**Split primes**: 23, 31, 53, 67, 71, 73, 83, 97, 103, 109, ...
**Inert primes**: 3, 7, 11, 17, 19, 29, 37, 41, 43, 47, 59, 61, ...
**Ramified primes**: 2, 5, 13.

The split primes are sparser than for the Ising model (consistent
with larger class number h = 4).


## 6. The Artin L-Function and the Sewing Lift

### The Artin L-Function

The nontrivial character chi: Gal(K_L/F) = Z/2 -> {+1, -1}
determines the Artin L-function

    L(s, chi) = prod_{p unramified} (1 - chi(Frob_p) p^{-s})^{-1}.

When the base field is specialized to c = c_{p,q}, the character
chi becomes the Kronecker symbol chi_d = (d/*), and the Artin
L-function becomes the Dirichlet L-function L(s, chi_d):

    L(s, chi_d) = prod_{p not | d} (1 - (d/p) p^{-s})^{-1}
                = sum_{n=1}^{infty} chi_d(n) n^{-s}.

This L-function encodes the splitting behavior of ALL primes
simultaneously.

### Relationship to the Sewing Lift S_A(u)

The sewing lift, defined in Chapter arithmetic_shadows.tex, is:

    S_A(u) = sum_n a_n n^{-u}

where a_n are the Dirichlet coefficients of the partition function.
For the standard families:
- Heisenberg: S_H(u) = zeta(u) * zeta(u+1)
- Virasoro: S_Vir(u) = zeta(u+1) * (zeta(u) - 1)

**The fundamental distinction**: L(s, chi_d) and S_A(u) encode
different data:

| | L(s, chi_d) | S_A(u) |
|---|---|---|
| **Source** | Genus-0 OPE data (kappa, alpha, S_4) | Genus-1 trace data (partition function) |
| **Construction** | Shadow metric Q_L -> quadratic extension -> Artin | Graded trace -> Dirichlet series |
| **Euler product** | Product over primes p with local factor (1 - (d/p)p^{-s})^{-1} | Not a single Euler product in general |
| **Functional equation** | Standard Dirichlet FE with conductor |d| | Structural failure of Li criterion (prop:li-criterion-failure) |

These two L-functions are **independent objects** at the current
state of the theory.  The arithmetic comparison conjecture
(conj:arithmetic-comparison) asserts that Theta_A canonically
determines the arithmetic packet connection nabla^arith_A, which
would relate the genus-0 shadow data (and hence L(s, chi_d)) to
the genus-1 sewing data (and hence S_A(u)).  This conjecture
remains OPEN.

### What Would a Connection Look Like?

If the arithmetic comparison conjecture holds, one expects:

1. **Factorization**: S_A(u) = L(u, trivial) * L(u, chi_d) * (correction),
   where the correction encodes higher-genus/higher-arity data
   beyond the quadratic extension.

2. **Hecke compatibility**: For lattice VOAs, the shadow-spectral
   correspondence (thm:shadow-spectral-correspondence) already
   provides this: each arity of the shadow tower resolves one Hecke
   eigenspace, and the constrained Epstein zeta factors into the
   corresponding L-functions.

3. **Non-lattice obstruction**: For Virasoro minimal models, the
   shadow-spectral correspondence fails in its simple form.  The
   Dirichlet L-function L(s, chi_d) is a genus-0 invariant, while
   the sewing lift S_Vir(u) = zeta(u+1)*(zeta(u)-1) is a genus-1
   invariant.  The discrepancy between them is controlled by the
   homotopy-theoretic component d_alg of the depth decomposition
   (thm:depth-decomposition).

4. **Speculative**: The Artin L-function L(s, chi_d) at the
   minimal model c_{p,q} is a Dirichlet L-function for a character
   of conductor roughly proportional to pq.  The sewing lift
   S_Vir(u) at c = c_{p,q} involves zeta functions with shifts.
   The relationship between chi_d (a character determined by the
   OPE) and the partition function (a modular form of weight 0 for
   a congruence subgroup of level pq) passes through the modular
   representation theory of the minimal model -- specifically, the
   VVMF structure analyzed in the arithmetic shadows chapter.


## 7. Summary and Open Questions

### Proved Structure

1. **Quadratic extension**: K_L = F(sqrt(Q_L)) over F = Q(c)(t)
   with Gal = Z/2 (the Koszul sign).

2. **Discriminant**: disc(K_L/F) = -320c^2/(5c+22) for Virasoro.
   Factorization: -2^6 * 5 * c^2 * (5c+22)^{-1}.

3. **Ramified locus** = pole locus of shadow tower: {c = 0} and
   {c = -22/5} (Lee-Yang).  These are the ONLY poles of S_r for
   r >= 4 (prop:pole-purity).

4. **Koszul involution**: c -> 26-c sends Q(sqrt(d(c))) to
   Q(sqrt(d(26-c))).  These are distinct fields for all minimal
   models; they coincide only at c = 13 (self-dual point).

5. **Splitting principle**: p splits in K_L iff Q_L factors mod p
   iff the shadow tower terminates mod p (reduces to class L).

6. **Class numbers**: range from h = 2 (Ising, (5,8)) to h = 112
   ((7,8)), growing with model complexity.

### Open Questions

**Q1** (Conductor and integral model): What is the correct integral
model of the shadow extension?  The shadow coefficients a_n have
denominators c^{r-3} * (5c+22)^{floor((r-2)/2)}, so the naive
order Z[a_0, a_1, ...] has conductor including all primes dividing
c*(5c+22).  Is there a "shadow Neron model" with better integrality?

**Q2** (Class number and shadow complexity): Is there a relationship
between the class number h(d(c_{p,q})) and any shadow-theoretic
invariant of the minimal model?  The class number measures the
arithmetic complexity of the shadow number field; the shadow depth
(always infinity for Virasoro) measures the algebraic complexity.
These seem to be independent invariants.

**Q3** (Artin = sewing?): Does L(s, chi_d) appear as a factor of
S_A(u) for minimal models?  This is a special case of the
arithmetic comparison conjecture (conj:arithmetic-comparison).

**Q4** (Higher reciprocity): The quadratic extension gives a Z/2
Galois group.  Are there higher-degree analogues?  For multi-channel
shadow towers (W_N with N >= 3), the shadow metric becomes a
higher-degree polynomial, and the corresponding extension has
larger Galois group.  For W_3, the W-line shadow metric is quartic
in t (after accounting for the even-arity cascade), suggesting a
degree-4 extension with Galois group inside S_4.

**Q5** (Frobenius density): The Chebotarev density theorem guarantees
that exactly half the primes split and half are inert (since
|Gal| = 2 and each conjugacy class has density 1/2).  Is there a
shadow-theoretic proof of this equidistribution, independent of
the number-theoretic machinery?  The shadow tower's algebraic
structure (Q_L is irreducible of degree 2 over Q(c)) should force
equidistribution by a Weil-type argument.
