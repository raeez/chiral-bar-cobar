# Derivative Tower Theorem: Formalization Assessment

## Date: 2026-04-07
## Status: PARTIALLY PROVABLE (see detailed analysis below)

## 1. The Proposed Theorem

The Eulerian weight MC agent (compute/lib/eulerian_weight_mc_engine.py,
compute/audit/eulerian_weight_mc_report.md) discovered:

> Virasoro achieves infinite shadow depth via the derivative tower
> partial^k(T) supplying all Eulerian weights. The single generator T
> has odd desuspended degree (weight-1/Harrison), but partial^k(T) has
> desuspended degree h+k-1, alternating parity, filling all Eulerian weights.

**Proposed formalization**: For a chiral algebra A generated by fields
{phi_alpha} of conformal weights {h_alpha}, the shadow depth r_max(A) is
bounded below by the number of independent Eulerian weight classes accessible
via the derivative tower {partial^k(phi_alpha)}_{k >= 0}. For single-generator
algebras: r_max = 2 (class G) iff the zeroth product vanishes (abelian),
r_max = 3 (class L) iff the zeroth product is Lie-type, and r_max = infinity
(class M) iff there exist quartic or higher contact terms.

## 2. Critical Analysis: What Is True and What Is Not

### 2a. What the Eulerian weight analysis DOES prove

The following are verified by 67 tests in the engine:

**(T1) Kappa Eulerian weight is determined by desuspended degree parity.**
At arity 2, S_2 = {id, (12)} acts on (s^{-1}a)^{tensor 2} by the sign
(-1)^d where d = |s^{-1}a| = conformal_weight - 1.
- d even: kappa in weight 2 (symmetric).
- d odd: kappa in weight 1 (Harrison/Lie).

**(T2) Derivative tower fills both parities.**
For conformal weight h, partial^k(phi) has desuspended degree h + k - 1.
Parity alternates with k. This gives generators of both parities in the
bar complex, enabling nontrivial Eulerian decomposition at all arities.

**(T3) Single-generator extinction at arity >= 2 for odd desuspended degree.**
For a single generator of odd desuspended degree d: Sym^r(s^{-1}phi) =
Lambda^r(Q) = 0 for r >= 2 (exterior power of 1-dim space). The shadow
tower CANNOT be populated by the zero-derivative sector alone.

**(T4) The MC equation is NOT Eulerian-weight-graded.**
The bracket respects weights (determined by Lie^c restriction via PBW),
but the bar differential mixes weights (arity contraction and self-sewing
do not preserve Eulerian weight).

### 2b. What the proposed theorem gets WRONG

**PROBLEM 1: The derivative tower is not an independent mechanism;
it is already accounted for in the existing proved framework.**

The operadic complexity theorem (thm:operadic-complexity-detailed,
higher_genus_modular_koszul.tex:13931) already establishes:

    r_max(A) = d_infinity(A) = f_infinity(A)

where r_max = shadow termination arity, d_infinity = A_infinity-depth,
f_infinity = L_infinity-formality level. This is PROVED.

The shadow depth is determined by the vanishing pattern of the transferred
A_infinity operations m_n^{tr} on the minimal model of the cyclic
deformation complex. These operations are computed by tree-sum formulas
involving ALL modes of the OPE (including all derivatives). The derivative
tower {partial^k phi} is the SOURCE of the data that feeds into these
tree sums, but the actual shadow depth is determined by whether the tree
sums CANCEL, not by the mere availability of derivatives.

**PROBLEM 2: The proposed characterization of r_max for single-generator
algebras confuses NECESSARY and SUFFICIENT conditions.**

The proposed statement says:
- r_max = 2 iff zeroth product vanishes (abelian).
- r_max = 3 iff zeroth product is Lie-type.
- r_max = infinity iff quartic or higher contact terms exist.

This conflates the OPE STRUCTURE with the SHADOW DEPTH in a way that
is not generally correct:

(a) Heisenberg has r_max = 2 because the OPE is abelian (only pole order
    1, which is the Levi-Civita connection). The "zeroth product" is the
    regular part of the OPE, which vanishes for free fields. But the
    characterization "zeroth product vanishes" is not quite right: the
    relevant condition is that ALL transferred A_infinity operations
    vanish for n >= 3. For Heisenberg, this follows because the OPE is
    strictly quadratic (only z^{-2} and z^{-1} poles, no composite
    operators beyond the identity and the current).

(b) Affine KM has r_max = 3 because the Jacobi identity kills the quartic
    obstruction. The OPE has a zeroth product [J^a, J^b]_0 = f^{abc} J^c
    (the structure constants), which is Lie-type. But r_max = 3 does NOT
    follow from the zeroth product being Lie-type; it follows from the
    JACOBI IDENTITY on the Lie bracket, which kills o_4. An algebra with
    a "Lie-type" zeroth product but a non-Jacobi quadratic relation could
    have r_max > 3.

(c) Virasoro has r_max = infinity because the critical discriminant
    Delta = 8*kappa*S_4 = 40/(5c+22) > 0 for c > 0, which makes the
    shadow metric Q_L irreducible over Q(c). The key is Q^{contact} =
    10/[c(5c+22)] != 0, not the mere existence of "quartic contact terms."
    The quartic contact invariant is a SPECIFIC algebraic invariant
    computed from the full OPE; its non-vanishing forces infinite depth
    via the single-line dichotomy (thm:single-line-dichotomy).

**PROBLEM 3: The characterization misses class C entirely.**

The four-class partition is G/L/C/M, but the proposed statement only
mentions three outcomes (r_max = 2, 3, infinity). Class C (contact,
r_max = 4) is missing. The beta-gamma system has:
- Cubic shadow C = 0 (the weight-changing line is abelian).
- Quartic shadow Q != 0 (contact quartic from m_3 composition).
- Quintic obstruction o_5 = 0 (rank-one abelian rigidity kills it).

This class does NOT fit the proposed single-generator schema because
beta-gamma has TWO generators (beta and gamma) of DIFFERENT conformal
weights. The "single-generator" restriction in the proposed theorem avoids
this but also makes the theorem less useful.

### 2c. What IS provable

**The Eulerian weight mechanism is genuinely explanatory** at the
representational level, even if it is not the correct level for
classifying shadow depth. What IS provable:

**(P1) The derivative tower is necessary for infinite shadow depth of
single-generator algebras with odd desuspended degree.**

For Virasoro (h=2, d=1 odd): the zero-derivative sector contributes 0
to Sym^r at arity r >= 2 (extinction by antisymmetry). The shadow tower
S_r for r >= 3 is populated ENTIRELY by modes involving derivatives
partial^k T with k >= 1. This is a theorem: without the derivative tower,
the convolution algebra is zero at arity >= 2, so r_max would be 2.

**(P2) The parity alternation of the derivative tower explains the
Harrison vs symmetric decomposition of kappa.**

This is the content of the Eulerian weight engine's central finding
(T1 above). The desuspended degree parity determines whether kappa is
a Harrison (primitive) or symmetric (decomposable) cocycle in the bar
complex. This is provable from first principles.

**(P3) For EVERY standard single-generator family, the shadow depth
is determined by the operadic complexity theorem via the critical
discriminant Delta = 8*kappa*S_4.**

- Heisenberg: S_4 = 0 (no quartic OPE), Delta = 0, alpha = 0, class G.
- Virasoro: S_4 = 10/[c(5c+22)] != 0, Delta = 40/(5c+22) != 0, class M.

There is no standard single-generator family in class L or C. (Affine KM
has dim(g) >= 3 generators for rank >= 1; beta-gamma has 2 generators.)

## 3. The Correct Theorem

Rather than the proposed statement, the following is the correct
formalization, and it IS provable:

**Proposition** (Derivative tower necessity for infinite depth).
Let A be a modular Koszul chiral algebra with a single generator phi of
conformal weight h and desuspended degree d = h - 1. If d is odd, then
the zero-derivative sector Sym^r(s^{-1}phi) = 0 for all r >= 2, and the
shadow coefficients S_r for r >= 3 are entirely populated by the derivative
tower {partial^k phi}_{k >= 1}. In particular:
(i) If the derivative tower contributes NO nontrivial obstruction classes
    (i.e., the OPE is strictly quadratic), then r_max = 2 (class G).
(ii) If the critical discriminant Delta = 8*kappa*S_4 = 0, then
     r_max in {2, 3} (finite depth), with r_max = 3 iff alpha != 0.
(iii) If Delta != 0, then r_max = infinity (class M), and the derivative
      tower is the essential source: the parity alternation of partial^k phi
      provides the even-degree fields that populate the symmetric Eulerian
      components at all arities.

**Proof sketch**:
(i) is thm:nms-heisenberg-exact-linearity.
(ii) and (iii) follow from the single-line dichotomy
(thm:single-line-dichotomy): the shadow metric Q_L(t) =
(2*kappa + 3*alpha*t)^2 + 2*Delta*t^2 is a perfect square iff Delta = 0,
and the tower terminates iff Q_L is a perfect square.

The derivative tower provides the MECHANISM (even-degree fields from
partial^k phi) while the critical discriminant provides the CLASSIFICATION
(whether these contributions cancel or accumulate).

The proposed theorem is best understood as a COROLLARY of the operadic
complexity theorem (thm:operadic-complexity-detailed) combined with the
Eulerian weight analysis. It does not add new mathematical content beyond
what is already proved, but it provides an illuminating representation-
theoretic perspective on WHY the shadow depth takes the values it does.

**Existing proved results that subsume the proposed theorem:**

1. **Operadic complexity**: r_max = d_infinity = f_infinity
   (thm:operadic-complexity-detailed, PROVED).

2. **Single-line dichotomy**: r_max in {2, 3, infinity} on any 1D
   primary slice, classified by (alpha, Delta)
   (thm:single-line-dichotomy, PROVED).

3. **Shadow archetype classification**: G/L/C/M classification for all
   standard families (thm:shadow-archetype-classification, PROVED).

4. **Shadow-formality identification**: shadow tower = L_infinity formality

5. **Formality obstruction at arity 5**: o_5(Vir) != 0 for generic c
   (thm:w-virasoro-quintic-forced, PROVED).

The derivative tower perspective adds the representation-theoretic
mechanism: the REASON the Virasoro shadow tower is infinite (despite
the single-generator sector being extinct at arity >= 2) is that the
derivative tower supplies even-degree fields that populate the symmetric
Eulerian components.

## 8. Summary

The derivative tower mechanism is a genuine and illuminating representation-
theoretic explanation for the shadow depth classification. It correctly
explains WHY Virasoro has infinite depth despite a single odd-degree
generator: the derivatives supply even-degree fields. However, the proposed
formalization as a theorem is imprecise and conflates necessary with
sufficient conditions. The correct formalization is a remark or proposition
that frames the derivative tower as the MECHANISM underlying the already-
proved operadic complexity classification, with the critical discriminant
Delta = 8*kappa*S_4 as the INVARIANT that determines whether the mechanism
produces finite or infinite depth. The proposed theorem should be placed as
a remark after rem:formality-obstruction-5-7 in higher_genus_modular_koszul.tex.
