# Categorical E_1 Primacy: Assessment

## The Question

Can thm:e1-primacy (dg Lie level) be lifted to categories?  The natural
conjecture: C_line = A!-mod (E_1 module category) is the primitive
datum; modular categories are derived via the Drinfeld center Z(C).

## What is proved

thm:e1-primacy (introduction.tex lines 378-413) establishes:
1. **Surjectivity**: av: g^{mod,E_1}_A ->> g^mod_A is surjective.
2. **MC projection**: av(Theta^{E_1}) = Theta_A.
3. **Bracket preservation**: av preserves the dg Lie bracket.
4. **Non-splitting**: ker(av) contains the Drinfeld associator
   Phi_KZ(A) at arity 3; the SES does not split as dg Lie algebras.

The algebraic line-operator model (def:line-operator-category-yb,
yangians_drinfeld_kohno.tex line 6152) is:

    C_T := A_T^!-mod

with a spectral z-dependent comultiplication Delta_z when an
R-matrix r_T(z) is supplied.  This is the E_1 module category.


## The categorical architecture: three levels

### Level 1: dg Lie (PROVED)

The short exact sequence

    0 -> ker(av) -> g^{E_1}_A -> g^mod_A -> 0

is a SES of dg Lie algebras.  The kernel contains the Drinfeld
associator (arity 3) and higher Yangian coherences (arity >= 4).
The non-splitting is PROVED (thm:e1-primacy(iv)):

- At arity 2: ker(av) = 0 for abelian (Heisenberg), nonzero for
  nonabelian.  The Casimir is S_2-symmetric: av(Omega) = Omega.
- At arity 3: [O_12, O_23] lies PURELY in the sign representation
  of S_3.  av annihilates it completely.
- Cross-arity leakage: D maps ker(av_3) partly into im(av_2),
  preventing any dg Lie section.

(compute/lib/e1_nonsplitting_obstruction_engine.py, 47 tests)


### Level 2: categories of modules (PARTIALLY PROVED)

The categorical CG theorem (thm:categorical-cg-all-types) proves MC3
for all simple types on the evaluation-generated core:

    Rep_fd(Y(g)) ~ C_eval(A) (braided monoidal equivalence)

where C_eval(A) is the full subcategory of A!-modules generated by
evaluation modules.  This is an equivalence of BRAIDED MONOIDAL
categories (E_1 module categories with R-matrix structure).

The DK bridge (constr:dk-shadow-projections) organizes this as:
- DK-0/1 (PROVED): chain-level Koszul duality + eval-locus comparison
- DK-2/3 (PROVED on eval core): generated-core comparison (CYBE, KZ)
- DK-4 (structural framework proved, target identification open)
- DK-5 (CONJECTURAL): full categorical equivalence


### Level 3: from braided to modular (OPEN)

The natural categorical statement of E_1 primacy would be:

    "C_line = A!-mod is the primitive braided monoidal category.
     The modular category M is derived from C_line via Z(C_line)."

Here Z(C) is the Drinfeld center (also called the Muger center or
double): for a braided monoidal category C, Z(C) is the modular
tensor category obtained by "closing" C under double-braiding.

**What this requires and what is known:**

(a) **Drinfeld center produces modular categories.**  This is a
    classical fact in quantum group theory: for C = Rep_q(g) at
    generic q, Z(C) = Rep_q(Lg) (the representation category of
    the quantum loop group), which is modular.  At roots of unity,
    the picture is more subtle (quotients, semisimplification).

(b) **The av functor as categorical center.**  At the dg Lie level,
    av: g^{E_1} -> g^mod is Sigma_n-coinvariant projection.  The
    categorical analogue would be:

        av^{cat}: C_line = A!-mod (braided) -> M = Z(A!-mod) (modular)

    where the "modular category" M packages the closed-sector
    (E_infinity) data.  This is NOT proved.  The obstruction is that
    the Drinfeld center Z(C) requires C to be sufficiently well-behaved
    (rigid, finite, etc.) -- conditions that may fail for the full
    dg module category A!-mod.

(c) **The relationship to factorization homology.**  There is a
    competing route to "modular from braided" via factorization
    homology:

        integral_{Sigma_g} C_line  (factorization homology of C over Sigma_g)

    This produces a vector space (the space of conformal blocks at
    genus g) from the braided monoidal category C_line.  When C is
    the representation category of a quantum group, this recovers
    the Witten-Reshetikhin-Turaev invariants.  This is the route
    that the manuscript's factorization framework naturally supports.


## Assessment: what "categorical E_1 primacy" would look like

### The correct statement (conjectural)

The categorical lift of thm:e1-primacy would be:

**Conjecture (Categorical E_1 primacy).**  Let A be a modular Koszul
algebra.  The following data are equivalent:

(i) The E_1 module category C_line = A!-mod with its braided
    monoidal structure (R-matrix, associator, higher coherences).

(ii) The modular factorization data: for each genus g, the space of
     conformal blocks V(Sigma_g, A) as a representation of the
     mapping class group Mod(Sigma_g).

(iii) The universal MC element Theta^{E_1}_A in MC(g^{E_1}_A).

The passage (i) -> (ii) is factorization homology:
V(Sigma_g, A) = integral_{Sigma_g} C_line.

The passage (i) -> (iii) is the E_1 bar-cobar construction.

The passage (iii) -> (ii) is the genus expansion of the modular
MC element.

### What is proved toward this

- (i) <-> (iii) at genus 0, arity 2: the collision-residue extraction
  r(z) = Res^{coll}_{0,2}(Theta^{E_1}) is PROVED.

- (iii) -> (ii): the modular genus expansion F_g(A) = kappa * lambda_g
  is PROVED for uniform-weight algebras.

- (i) -> (ii) for specific families: the Kazhdan-Lusztig equivalence
  Rep_q(g) ~ Rep(KZ-monodromy) is classical.

- The full (i) <-> (ii) <-> (iii) equivalence at all genera is DK-5
  (Conjecture conj:dk5-restricted), which is OPEN.

### The missing ingredient

The categorical center construction Z(C_line) -> modular category
is NOT explicitly discussed in the manuscript.  The manuscript's
route is different: it uses factorization homology
(integral_{Sigma_g} C_line) rather than the Drinfeld center.

The two routes are compatible:
- Factorization homology integral_{Sigma_g} C produces the
  conformal-block spaces at each genus.
- The Drinfeld center Z(C) produces the modular tensor category
  whose conformal blocks ARE those spaces.

So "av^{cat} = Z" is a restatement of the equivalence between
factorization homology and the Drinfeld center for braided
monoidal categories.  This is essentially the content of the
cobordism hypothesis applied to the specific case of 2d TFT
from 1d (E_1) data.


## Falsification checks

1. **Does av^{cat} always produce modular categories?**  NO.
   The Drinfeld center Z(C) is modular only when C is
   non-degenerate (Muger center trivial).  For degenerate C,
   Z(C) is only braided, not modular.  The manuscript does not
   claim this universally.

2. **Is C_line always well-defined?**  The algebraic model
   C_T = A_T^!-mod is defined but may not be rigid or finite.
   The evaluation-generated core is well-behaved (MC3 proved),
   but the full category requires MC4 completion.

3. **Does the non-splitting obstruct categorical lifting?**
   The non-splitting at the dg Lie level (ker(av) nontrivial)
   means the categorical lift cannot be trivial: the braided
   category C_line contains strictly more data than its
   center Z(C_line).  This is expected and correct: the braided
   structure IS the additional data (R-matrix, associator).

4. **AP93 check**: the genus-dependent corrections delta_F_g^cross
   for multi-weight algebras live in the CLOSED sector, not the
   mixed sector.  The categorical E_1 primacy conjecture is not
   affected by multi-weight corrections, since it concerns the
   relationship between braided and modular categories, not the
   scalar genus expansion.


## Conclusion

"Categorical E_1 primacy" is a well-motivated conjecture whose
dg Lie shadow is PROVED (thm:e1-primacy) and whose genus-0
categorical shadow is PROVED on the evaluation core (MC3, DK-2/3).

The full categorical statement would assert that the braided monoidal
category C_line = A!-mod is the primitive datum from which modular
data (genus-g conformal blocks, modular tensor category) is derived
via factorization homology / Drinfeld center.

**Proved components:**
- dg Lie level: thm:e1-primacy (surjectivity, MC projection,
  non-splitting)
- genus-0 categorical: MC3 on evaluation core (all simple types)
- E_1 MC element exists: thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic

**Open components:**
- DK-5: full categorical equivalence (conj:dk5-restricted)
- Explicit construction of av^{cat} = Z or factorization homology
- Extension beyond evaluation-generated core (DK-4/5)
- Modular tensor structure from the Drinfeld center of C_line

The natural next step would be to state this as a formal conjecture
in the manuscript, with the proved components clearly delineated,
and the relationship to the cobordism hypothesis / factorization
homology made explicit.
