# Motivic Sheaves on M_g and the Shadow Spectral Data

**Investigation date**: 2026-04-01
**Question**: Can motivic sheaves on M_g capture the spectral data of the shadow obstruction tower?
**Verdict**: The existing manuscript material (prop:shadow-periods, rem:motivic-decomposition, rem:kummer-motive, rem:mc-motivic-identity, rem:motivic-weight-ek) already provides the correct framework. The proposed "motive M_A" is partially realized. Below is a precise analysis of what works, what fails, and what remains genuinely open.

## 1. Shadow coefficients as periods (PROVED)

**Manuscript status**: Proposition `prop:shadow-periods` (arithmetic_shadows.tex, line 1676).

The shadow coefficient at arity r is:

    S_r(c) = sum_Gamma (1/|Aut(Gamma)|) integral_{C-bar_n(X)} omega_Gamma(c)

where C-bar_n(X) is the Fulton-MacPherson compactification, Gamma ranges over connected stable graphs of weight r, and omega_Gamma(c) is algebraic in c.

**Key fact** (stated in the proof, line 1702): The FM space C-bar_n(P^1) is mixed-Tate (cohomology in even degrees, generated by boundary classes).

**Assessment**: This is CORRECT and well-established in the literature. The FM compactification C-bar_n(P^1) has cohomology that is pure Tate (concentrated in even degrees, spanned by boundary divisors). The graph integrals are therefore mixed-Tate periods in the sense of Kontsevich-Zagier. This is the same class of periods that appears in Kontsevich's formality theorem and the Drinfeld associator. The proof is complete.

**Beilinson check**: The FM space C-bar_n(P^1) being mixed-Tate is standard (De Concini-Procesi wonderful compactification theory). The claim that shadow coefficients are periods of this variety is immediate from the graph-sum formula. No gap here.

## 2. The constrained Epstein zeta at integer values

**Claim from the prompt**: epsilon^c_n = sum d(h)(2h)^{-n} is a rational number for n > 0.

**Analysis**: This requires care. The constrained Epstein zeta is:

    epsilon^c_s(A) = sum_{Delta in S} (2*Delta)^{-s}

where S is the set of scalar primary conformal dimensions. For a FINITE primary spectrum (e.g., minimal models), this is a finite sum of powers of algebraic numbers, hence algebraic at every s in Z. For INFINITE primary spectrum (e.g., generic Virasoro with c > 1), the sum is:

    epsilon^c_s = sum_{n >= 1} a(n) * (2n)^{-s}

where a(n) counts scalar primaries of dimension n. This is a Dirichlet series. At positive integer s, it converges absolutely (for s sufficiently large, by comparison with zeta(s)), and the values are real transcendental numbers in general -- NOT rational.

**Is epsilon^c_n a Kontsevich-Zagier period?** For lattice VOAs: YES. The Hecke factorization (Theorem thm:shadow-spectral-correspondence, eq:epstein-factorization) gives:

    epsilon^r_s(V_Lambda) = 2^{-s} * (c_E * C_E(s) * zeta(s) * zeta(s - r/2 + 1) + sum_j c_j * C_j(s) * L(s, f_j))

At integer values, zeta(n) for n >= 2 even is pi^n * rational (a period), and L(n, f_j) at critical integers is a period of the motive h^1(f_j) (by Deligne's theorem on critical L-values). So epsilon^r_n is a period.

For NON-lattice VOAs: the Dirichlet series does not factor into standard L-functions in any known way. Whether the values are periods is OPEN.

**Correction to prompt claim**: epsilon^c_n is NOT in general a rational number. It is algebraic only for finite primary spectra. For infinite spectra it is transcendental in general. But for lattice VOAs, it IS a period in the Kontsevich-Zagier sense.

## 3. L(1, chi_d) and CM motives

**Claim from the prompt**: L(1, chi_d) = pi*h/(w*sqrt(|d|)) is a period of the CM elliptic curve E_d with CM by Q(sqrt(d)).

**Analysis**: This is CORRECT. By the Chowla-Selberg formula, L(1, chi_d) is expressible in terms of the period Omega of the CM elliptic curve. More precisely: L(1, chi_d) is the period integral of a differential form on E_d against a cycle, and is therefore a period of the motive h^1(E_d). This is a theorem (Shimura, Gross, Colmez).

The manuscript uses this at one specific point: the Z^2 Epstein zeta (Proposition prop:z2-epstein, line 389):

    E_{Z^2}(s) = 4 * zeta(s) * L(s, chi_{-4}) = 4 * zeta_{Q(i)}(s)

At s = 1: L(1, chi_{-4}) = pi/4 (Leibniz formula). This is indeed a period of the CM elliptic curve with j = 1728.

**No new content needed**: The manuscript's treatment is correct. The CM-motive interpretation is standard.

## 4. The shadow obstruction tower coefficients: algebraic vs transcendental

**Claim from the prompt**: The shadow obstruction tower S_r = (1/r)[t^{r-2}] sqrt(Q_L). The function sqrt(Q_L) is algebraic of degree 2. Its Taylor coefficients are ALGEBRAIC numbers (elements of Q(kappa, alpha, S_4)). So {S_r} are algebraic, not transcendental.

**Analysis**: This is CORRECT but requires important qualifications.

For the **Virasoro algebra** at central charge c (viewed as a formal parameter or a specific rational/algebraic value): The shadow generating function H^2 = t^4 * Q_L(t) where Q_L(t) = c^2 + 12ct + alpha(c)*t^2 with alpha(c) = 4(45c+218)/(5c+22) (Theorem thm:spectral-curve, line 1819). The Taylor coefficients S_r are RATIONAL FUNCTIONS of c with coefficients in Q. Specifically, S_r in Q(c). At any algebraic value of c, S_r is algebraic. At any transcendental value of c, S_r is transcendental.

For **lattice VOAs**: The shadow coefficients involve configuration-space integrals weighted by representation numbers r_Lambda(n), which are integers. The integrals themselves, however, are over C-bar_n(P^1) and are mixed-Tate periods. At genus 0, these are rational multiples of powers of pi (from the FM geometry). At genus 1, the integrals involve the Eisenstein series E_2*, elliptic polylogarithms, and potentially L-function values -- these are TRANSCENDENTAL in general.

**The transcendence asymmetry** (rem:transcendence-asymmetry, line 1754) captures this beautifully:
- Leech lattice: depth 4, but periods include L(6, Delta_12) in pi^12 * Q-bar -- deeply transcendental.
- Virasoro: depth infinity, but all periods are rational functions of c -- transcendentally trivial.

This asymmetry is a genuine mathematical phenomenon, not an artifact.

## 5. The central question: Is there a motive M_A?

**Claim from the prompt**: Is there a motive M_A attached to a chirally Koszul algebra A such that H^0(M_A) = shadow obstruction tower, H^1(M_A) = primary spectrum, L(s, M_A) = epsilon^c_s?

### 5a. What already exists (the Kummer motive for Virasoro)

**rem:kummer-motive** (line 1737) identifies the Virasoro shadow generating function G(t) = -log(1 + 6t/c) as the period of a single Kummer motive K(6/c). The iterated extensions S_r = (-1)^{r+1}(6/c)^r / r are the weight-graded pieces. This is correct: the Kummer motive K(a) = [Z -> G_m, 1 |-> a] has period log(a), and the weight filtration gives exactly these Taylor coefficients.

**The MC equation as motivic identity** (rem:mc-motivic-identity, line 1771): The MC equation D*Theta + (1/2)[Theta, Theta] = 0 is a relation in the convolution algebra over configuration spaces. Configuration-space integrals are mixed-Tate periods. The claim is that Theta_A is a "motivic MC element" whose arity filtration is the weight filtration of a mixed motive.

### 5b. What partially works (the lattice case)

For lattice VOAs, the proposed structure partially exists:

- **H^0(M_A)**: The shadow obstruction tower {S_r} ARE periods (prop:shadow-periods). For Virasoro, they are periods of the Kummer motive K(6/c). CHECK.
- **L(s, M_A) = epsilon^c_s**: For lattice VOAs, epsilon^r_s factors as a sum of L(s, f_j) for Hecke eigenforms (Theorem thm:shadow-spectral-correspondence). Each L(s, f_j) is the L-function of the motive h^1(f_j) attached to the eigenform by Deligne (for weight >= 2). So L(s, M_A) = sum_j c_j * L(s, h^1(f_j)). The motive is M_A = sum_j c_j * h^1(f_j) (a direct sum of Grothendieck motives). CHECK.
- **H^1(M_A) = primary spectrum**: This is the WEAKEST link. The primary spectrum {Delta_i} is a discrete set of real numbers. To identify it with H^1 of a motive, one needs a geometric space X_A whose first cohomology encodes these conformal dimensions. For lattice VOAs, the lattice Lambda provides the geometric object: the theta function Theta_Lambda in M_{r/2}(Gamma) is a section of a line bundle on the modular curve, and the conformal dimensions are encoded in the Fourier coefficients. The motive h^1 of the modular curve (at the appropriate level) carries this information. So H^1(M_A) is better described as the Hecke-decomposed cohomology of the modular curve, not the "primary spectrum" directly.

### 5c. What FAILS (the non-lattice case)

For non-lattice VOAs (Virasoro, W-algebras, beta-gamma), the proposed motive M_A faces three obstructions:

**Obstruction 1: No theta function to decompose.** The Hecke machinery (Theorem thm:shadow-spectral-correspondence) depends on the partition function being a classical modular form of known weight and level (Remark rem:lattice-specificity, line 130). For Virasoro, the character chi_0(q) = q^{-c/24} * prod(1-q^n)^{-1} is NOT a classical modular form of any finite weight. It is an automorphic form in a different sense (a Maass form or a vector-valued modular form), but the Hecke decomposition of classical holomorphic modular forms does not apply.

**Obstruction 2: The Galois action question.** The prompt asks whether the motivic Galois group Gal_mot(MT(Q)) acts on the shadow periods and whether this action "factors through the Hecke algebra." This is the content of Route B in the manuscript (line 6323). The manuscript honestly labels this "very hard" and identifies the precise input needed: "the de Rham realization of the shadow motive must be compatible with the Hecke action on the Betti realization, a period comparison theorem for elliptic graph integrals." This is an OPEN problem, and the difficulty is genuine.

**Obstruction 3: The structural separation theorem.** The manuscript proves (Theorem thm:structural-separation, referenced at line 300-325 in rem:structural-obstruction) that the genus-1 Rankin-Selberg integral is holomorphic at the scattering poles s = rho/2. This means the algebraic constraints from the MC equation on the REAL spectral axis cannot reach the scattering poles (which live at COMPLEX spectral parameters). The map from the shadow spectral measure rho to the automorphic scattering matrix is NOT surjective. In motivic language: even if M_A exists, the motive does NOT determine the zeta zeros. The spectral data is CONTROLLED by the motive but not DETERMINED by it.

### 5d. The honest assessment

The question "can motivic sheaves on M_g capture the spectral data?" breaks into three sub-questions with different answers:

**(i) Can the shadow obstruction tower be given motivic meaning?** YES, unconditionally. The shadow coefficients are mixed-Tate periods (proved). The MC equation is a motivic relation. The arity filtration is compatible with the weight filtration. This is prop:shadow-periods + rem:motivic-decomposition + rem:mc-motivic-identity.

**(ii) Does the motivic Galois group act on the shadow data through the Hecke algebra?** OPEN (Route B in the manuscript). The key missing input is a period comparison theorem for elliptic graph integrals at genus 1. The technology of mixed elliptic motives (Brown-Levin) exists but has not been applied to chiral algebra graph sums. The manuscript (line 6329-6336) correctly identifies this as the bottleneck.

**(iii) Does a single motive M_A encode all spectral data (shadow obstruction tower + primary spectrum + Epstein zeta)?** PARTIALLY for lattice VOAs, NO in the proposed form for non-lattice VOAs.

For lattice VOAs, the motive is the sum of Hecke motives h^1(f_j) from the decomposition of Theta_Lambda. The shadow obstruction tower = arity filtration on the periods. The L-function = Epstein zeta. The primary spectrum = Fourier coefficients of the theta function.

For Virasoro, the shadow obstruction tower is controlled by a single Kummer motive K(6/c). The "L-function" L(s, M_A) = L(s, K(6/c)) is NOT the Epstein zeta -- it is the polylogarithm Li_s(-6/c), which is a completely different function from the constrained Epstein zeta epsilon^c_s. The Epstein zeta depends on the FULL primary spectrum (all conformal dimensions), which is NOT determined by the shadow obstruction tower alone (the shadow obstruction tower encodes the A_infinity structure on bar cohomology, not the primary dimensions). The proposed identification L(s, M_A) = epsilon^c_s fails.

## 6. What the manuscript already does RIGHT

The manuscript's existing treatment is more careful and more correct than the proposed investigation in several ways:

1. **Depth decomposition (Theorem thm:depth-decomposition)**: d = 1 + d_arith + d_alg correctly separates the arithmetic (Hecke eigenforms, L-functions) from the homotopy-theoretic (A_infinity non-formality, Massey products) content.

2. **Motivic decomposition (rem:motivic-decomposition)**: d_arith = number of non-Tate summands h^1(f_j), d_alg = depth of the Tate part. This IS the motivic decomposition -- it just isn't packaged as "a single motive M_A."

3. **Transcendence asymmetry (rem:transcendence-asymmetry)**: The counterintuitive phenomenon that finite depth can encode deeply transcendental periods (Leech) while infinite depth encodes algebraically trivial periods (Virasoro) is correctly identified.

4. **Kummer motive (rem:kummer-motive)**: The Virasoro shadow obstruction tower as iterated self-extensions of a single Kummer motive is the correct motivic identification.

5. **MC as motivic identity (rem:mc-motivic-identity)**: The observation that the MC equation connects Tate periods (from C-bar_n) to non-Tate periods (from L(s, f_j)) is the correct bridge.

6. **Structural obstruction (rem:structural-obstruction)**: The proof that the shadow obstruction tower CANNOT determine the zeta zeros (the scattering poles live at complex spectral parameters unreachable from the real spectral axis) is a genuine theorem that blocks the strongest form of the proposed programme.

## 7. Precise mathematical status of each claim from the prompt

### Claim 1: "S_r are periods of the mixed-Tate variety C-bar_n(P^1)"
**STATUS**: PROVED (prop:shadow-periods). The S_r are periods of the FM compactification, which is mixed-Tate.

### Claim 2: "The motivic Galois group Gal_mot(MT(Q)) acts on these periods. If the Galois action factors through the Hecke algebra, then shadow coefficients inherit a prime decomposition."
**STATUS**: OPEN. This is Route B. The action of Gal_mot(MT(Q)) on mixed-Tate periods is standard (Brown's theorem on MT(Z) periods). Whether it factors through the Hecke algebra for chiral algebra graph integrals is unknown. The manuscript identifies this as "very hard" (line 6323).

### Claim 3: "epsilon^c_n at integer s is a period in the Kontsevich-Zagier sense"
**STATUS**: TRUE for lattice VOAs (via Hecke factorization into L-function values at critical integers). UNKNOWN for non-lattice VOAs. The values are NOT rational in general (correction to the prompt).

### Claim 4: "L(1, chi_d) is the period of a CM motive"
**STATUS**: TRUE (Shimura, Gross). Standard, used implicitly in the Z^2 computation.

### Claim 5: "sqrt(Q_L) has algebraic Taylor coefficients, so S_r are algebraic"
**STATUS**: TRUE for Virasoro when c is algebraic. The S_r are in Q(c). For lattice VOAs, the S_r involve transcendental periods (L-function values). The algebraicity claim is FAMILY-DEPENDENT.

### Claim 6: "There exists a motive M_A with H^0 = shadow obstruction tower, H^1 = primary spectrum, L(s, M_A) = epsilon^c_s"
**STATUS**: FALSE as stated.
- H^0 = shadow obstruction tower: correct motivically (periods of Kummer / FM configuration spaces).
- H^1 = primary spectrum: wrong formulation. The primary spectrum is not the H^1 of a motive in any natural sense. The correct statement is: the Hecke eigenforms in the decomposition of Theta_Lambda are sections of line bundles on the modular curve, and THEIR cohomology classes are motives h^1(f_j).
- L(s, M_A) = epsilon^c_s: true for lattice VOAs (by Hecke factorization). False for Virasoro (the Kummer L-function Li_s(-6/c) is not the Epstein zeta).
- "Motivic Galois group controls the spectral data": partially true in the weak sense that the periods are constrained by motivic relations (the MC equation). False in the strong sense that the spectral data DETERMINES the L-function zeros (blocked by the structural separation theorem).

## 8. What would genuinely advance the programme

Three concrete mathematical problems, in order of feasibility:

### Problem A (feasible): Classify the motivic weight of the graph integrals at each arity
At arity r, the graph integral is a sum over graphs Gamma with |V(Gamma)| = r. Each integral is a period of C-bar_r(P^1). The cohomology of C-bar_r(P^1) is concentrated in degrees 0, 2, 4, ..., 2(r-2), all pure Tate. Therefore the shadow coefficient S_r lives in the weight-r piece of the mixed-Tate category MT(Q(c)). The weight filtration on the shadow obstruction tower IS the arity filtration. This is stated in rem:motivic-decomposition (line 1717) but could be promoted to a proposition with a complete proof.

### Problem B (hard): Period comparison for genus-1 graph integrals
At genus 1, the graph integrals involve the genus-1 propagator (the Weierstrass zeta function / E_2* series) and elliptic polylogarithms. The question: do these integrals lie in the category of mixed elliptic motives (Brown-Levin)? If yes, the motivic Galois group of MEM(Q) acts on them, and the Hecke compatibility would follow from the period comparison theorem for elliptic motives. This is Route B, correctly identified as "very hard."

### Problem C (very hard): Motivic interpretation of the arithmetic comparison conjecture
Conjecture conj:arithmetic-comparison (line 8594) asserts that Theta_A canonically determines the arithmetic packet connection nabla^arith_A. In motivic language: the MC element (living in MT(Q(c))) canonically determines a flat connection whose singularities are L-function zeros (living in the motives h^1(f_j)). This is a statement about a BRIDGE between mixed-Tate and non-Tate motives, mediated by the MC equation. Proving this would require a new type of motivic identity: a relation between the mixed-Tate configuration-space integrals (graph sums) and the non-Tate cusp-form periods (L-values).

## 9. Connection to Voevodsky/Ayoub motivic cohomology

The prompt mentions "the motivic world (Voevodsky, Ayoub)." The manuscript does NOT use Voevodsky's motivic cohomology H^*_mot(X, Z(n)) or Ayoub's formalism. The motivic language in the manuscript is informal (rem:motivic-decomposition explicitly says "we work informally with periods in the sense of Kontsevich-Zagier; a precise formulation would use Nori or Voevodsky motives," line 1715-1717).

To make the motivic framework precise, one would need:
1. Nori motives or Voevodsky's DM(k) for the configuration spaces C-bar_n(P^1).
2. The graph integrals as motivic periods (via the motivic fundamental groupoid of C-bar_n(P^1) minus its boundary strata).
3. The MC equation as a relation in the Ext algebra of the motivic category.

This is a well-defined mathematical programme but it is LARGE (comparable to Brown's work on multiple zeta values and periods of M_{0,n}). The manuscript correctly avoids committing to a specific motivic formalism and works at the level of periods.

## 10. The Grothendieck period conjecture

The prompt mentions: "The Grothendieck period conjecture says that all algebraic relations between periods come from algebraic geometry."

Applied to the shadow obstruction tower: the MC equation D*Theta + (1/2)[Theta, Theta] = 0 is an algebraic relation between periods (the S_r). By the Grothendieck period conjecture, this relation should come from algebraic geometry -- specifically, from algebraic relations in the cohomology of the configuration spaces C-bar_n(P^1). This is EXACTLY what happens: the MC equation comes from the codimension-2 boundary relations in the FM compactification (d^2 = 0 on the modular operad). The Grothendieck period conjecture is thus TRIVIALLY satisfied for the MC equation, because the algebraic relation IS the geometric relation (the boundary square is zero).

This observation is already implicit in the manuscript (rem:mc-motivic-identity) but could be stated more explicitly.
