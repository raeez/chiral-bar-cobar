# Assessment: conj:full-derived-dk (DK-3) vs conj:full-dk-bridge (DK-5)

## Location

- **conj:full-derived-dk**: yangians_drinfeld_kohno.tex line 535
- **conj:full-dk-bridge**: yangians_drinfeld_kohno.tex line 2278

## Are they the same conjecture?

No. They are DISTINCT conjectures at different levels of the DK ladder.
The manuscript is explicit about their relationship (rem:dk-proved-vs-remains,
line 662, and the Status paragraph at line 2302).

### conj:full-derived-dk (DK-3)

The E1-factorization categorical equivalence between Yangian and quantum
group factorization categories:

    Fact_{E1}(Y(g)) ~ Fact_{E1}(U_q(g))^op

This is a TWO-TERM equivalence: Yangian factorization = quantum group
factorization. It extends the evaluation-locus theorem
(thm:factorization-dk-eval, proved) to the full E1-factorization category.

### conj:full-dk-bridge (DK-5)

The full derived triple bridge:

    Fact_{E1}(X; A) ~ Mod^comp(Y^dg_A) ~ Rep^spec(QG^spec(R_A))^op

This is a THREE-TERM equivalence adding the dg-shifted Yangian Y^dg_A
as a middle term. It requires four hypotheses: (i) E1-Koszulness,
(ii) pro-nilpotent completion/convergence, (iii) spectral R-matrix data,
(iv) extension of evaluation-locus comparison to full category O.

### Precise relationship

conj:full-derived-dk is the OUTER equivalence (first ~ third term).
conj:full-dk-bridge is the FULL triple equivalence including the
dg-shifted Yangian middle term. From the Status paragraph at line 2302:

> "Conjecture conj:full-derived-dk is the DK-3 outer factorization
> equivalence; Theorem thm:factorization-dk-eval proves DK-1 on the
> evaluation locus. The Yangian middle term and the triple formulation
> are the DK-4/DK-5 completion."

So DK-3 (conj:full-derived-dk) is STRICTLY WEAKER than DK-5
(conj:full-dk-bridge). DK-5 subsumes DK-3 but adds the algebraic
identification with the dg-shifted Yangian.

## What is proved toward DK-3

Prop:yangian-dk3-generated-core (line 558) reduces DK-3 to two inputs:

1. Construction of exact braided monoidal transport functors
   KZ^gen_fact and LZ^gen_fact on the evaluation-generated cores.
2. Determining whether evaluation-generated cores exhaust the full
   factorization categories.

For input (2), thick generation by evaluation modules is proved for all
simple types (cor:dk2-thick-generation-all-types via multiplicity-free
ell-weights). But this is at the module category level, not yet at the
factorization category level.

The yangians_computations.tex file (line 1981) further isolates the
remaining problem: "whether the DK-relevant subcategory of
O_{Y_hbar(sl_N)} is thickly generated by evaluation modules."

## Interaction with broader programme

DK-3 is downstream of MC3 (proved for all simple types). The DK-5 status
assessment (dk5_status_assessment.md) decomposes the remaining gap into
four sub-targets B1-B4, with the bridge criterion theorem showing
B1 + B2 + B4 implies the full bridge.

The E1 primacy work clarifies that DK lives in the E1 (ordered) world,
while Theorems A-D live in the E_infty (symmetric) world. The averaging
map av: g^{mod,E1}_A -> g^mod_A is surjective but non-split, with
ker(av) containing the Drinfeld associator. This non-splitting is a
genuine structural obstruction (47 tests verify it).

## Assessment

**Tractability**: Moderate-to-hard. The evaluation-core technology is
strong (MC3 all types, thick generation all types), but the gap between
evaluation-generated core and full factorization category is a genuine
categorical problem. No known counterexample to DK-3.

**Priority**: High. DK-3 is the gateway to DK-5, which is the ultimate
representation-theoretic target of the monograph.
