## PHASE 1 — Attack (five strike surfaces)

### Strike (i): asymptotic prefundamentals — type A

The Hernandez-Jimbo asymptotic limit L^±_{ω_i,a} = lim_{k→∞} W^{(i)}_{k,a} exists only as a PRO-OBJECT in the completed category Ô. The Frenkel-Hernandez product factorisation of χ_q(L^±_{i,a}) is an identity of formal characters, not of characters of a genuine Y_ℏ(sl_N)-module in the uncompleted O.

Attack: does the MC3 thick-generation statement hold on each finite-dim fundamental evaluation module V_{ω_i}(a) via THIS mechanism, or only on the pro-completed limit?

Finding: the asymptotic-prefundamental mechanism proves thickness in Ô (the pro-completion); inside the uncompleted O the relevant thick-generation statement is carried by the multiplicity-free ℓ-weight mechanism (Proposition~\ref{prop:mc3-uniform-chari-moura-platonic}), not the asymptotic route. The two coexist and are complementary: the asymptotic route is global in spectral parameter on the pro-limit; the multiplicity-free route is pointwise on finite-dim evaluation modules. This is acknowledged in `rem:chari-moura-vs-hernandez-jimbo` (:344-358) but the scope (finite-dim O vs pro-completed Ô) is NOT explicit in the proposition header.

Verdict: no mathematical error, but a latent AP258 (cohomological-vs-chain drift applied to completed-vs-uncompleted categories) that requires explicit scope disambiguation in the proposition.

### Strike (ii): reflection-equation Shapovalov — twisted B/C/D

ACDFR 2003 (arXiv:math/0304188) establishes non-degeneracy of the reflection-equation Shapovalov pairing on Y^tw(so_n) / Y^tw(sp_n) at generic twist. Two attack vectors:

(ii.a) q=1 classical limit: the reflection equation degenerates to a classical r-matrix relation; Shapovalov becomes the Kirillov-Reshetikhin pairing on the classical universal enveloping algebra U(g^σ) (σ the involution). Non-degeneracy survives the degeneration for g^σ = so_n or sp_n (classical Shapovalov is non-degenerate for semisimple g).

(ii.b) q = primitive root of unity: the reflection-equation Shapovalov DEGENERATES on a Weyl-denominator-like locus. This is not a scoping failure: the proposition explicitly restricts to "generic spectral parameters", which on the Zariski-topology side is the complement of a finite union of codim-1 loci in the (q, spectral) parameter space, including the root-of-unity locus. The generation statement at roots of unity is explicitly out of scope per Corollary~\ref{cor:five-family-union-coverage}(v).

Verdict: no mathematical error. The Zariski-generic qualifier correctly scopes (ii.a) and (ii.b). One clarification useful: state explicitly that "generic" means Zariski-open in (q, spectral) joint parameter space, not just spectral.

### Strike (iii): Chari-Moura non-simply-laced exceptional G_2, F_4

AP250 flags G_2, F_4 as per-case verification. The current inscription (`prop:mc3-uniform-chari-moura-nonsimplylaced-platonic`, :304-316) states that per-case verification at the 2 fundamentals of G_2 and the 4 fundamentals of F_4 is carried out in Hernandez 2006 (arXiv:math/0606381) and Hernandez 2010 (KR conjecture).

Attack: are the F_4 fundamentals at dimensions {26, 52, 273, 1274} all covered? What about dimensions {4096, 8645}?

Finding: F_4 has rank 4, hence 4 fundamental weights. In Bourbaki labelling the fundamental dimensions are:
- ω_1 (adjoint): dim 52
- ω_2: dim 1274
- ω_3: dim 273
- ω_4 (26-dim irrep): dim 26

The dimensions {4096, 8645} in the mission brief are NOT fundamentals of F_4. They are higher irreducibles (4096 is dim V(ω_1+ω_4); 8645 is dim V(2ω_4)). Non-fundamental irreducibles are generated by fusion from the 4 fundamentals via the Kirillov-Reshetikhin T-system; their multiplicity-freeness follows from the four fundamentals' multiplicity-freeness and the KR T-system fusion rules (Hernandez 2010 Theorem 6.1).

Verdict: no mathematical error; the 4 F_4 fundamentals are per-case covered. The two G_2 fundamentals (dim 7 and dim 14) are covered by Hernandez 2006 §5.

### Strike (iv): theta-divisor complement — elliptic

The complement of a theta divisor Θ ⊂ E_ρ^{N-1} is a Zariski-open subset of a quasi-projective variety. It is a complex manifold (Zariski-open in a smooth variety is smooth and analytic-open), but NOT a compact complex manifold. The MC3 statement holds on this open complement as a dense-open condition.

Attack: is MC3 a Zariski-dense statement or a compact-manifold statement?

Finding: the proposition reads "thickly generated by evaluation modules at generic parameters on the complement of the theta divisor" — generic on a dense Zariski-open is the correct formulation. The complement is not required to be compact. A weaker statement ("MC3 fails on a codimension-1 divisor") is what is actually being asserted; the positive direction is thickness on the Zariski-open complement. This matches the rational type-A and super cases, where the failure loci are codim-1 hyperplanes and MC3 is asserted on their complements.

Verdict: no mathematical error. The "complement of a divisor" geometry is correctly the Zariski-open complement, matching the other four families.

### Strike (v): parity-balance super-Yangian — Y(gl(m|n)) vs Y(osp(m|2n))

Proposition~\ref{prop:mc3-super-parity-balance-platonic} (:396-413) covers Y_ℏ(gl(m|n)) only. The Nazarov quantum Berezinian factorisation Q(u) = Q^+(u)⊗Q^-(u) is constructed for Y(gl(m|n)) via the Z/2-graded RTT presentation (Zhang 1996; Nazarov 1991). It does NOT extend to Y(osp(m|2n)), which uses reflection-equation machinery (ACDFR 2003) distinct from the RTT-Z/2-graded mechanism.

Cross-volume context: Vol III F19 heal (`rem:gl-to-osp-correction` at `chapters/examples/k3_yangian_chapter.tex:1857-2025`) renamed the K3 super-Yangian target from Y(gl(4|20)) to Y_osp(4|20) on the grounds that Mukai signature (4, 20) is symmetric indefinite orthogonal, hence an OSP super-Yangian. Vol I MC3 five-family theorem does NOT cover the osp super-Yangian series via parity-balance.

Attack: does the Vol I five-family theorem correctly cover the Y(osp(m|2n)) family? Is the K3 super-Yangian Y_osp(4|20) inside the MC3-covered landscape?

Finding: Y(osp(m|2n)) is covered via the REFLECTION-EQUATION route (Proposition~\ref{prop:mc3-type-BCD-reflection-shapovalov-platonic}) in its generalised super form, NOT via the parity-balance route. The Gow-Molev 2010 super-Shapovalov determinant formula as cited in test_mc3_five_family.py covers Y(gl(m|n)) via Nazarov; the ACDFR 2003 reflection-equation super-Yangian construction covers Y(osp(m|2n)) via a reflection-equation Shapovalov analogue with the Sklyanin-Berezinian determinant (sBer K(u)) as witness.

This is a GENUINE GAP in the current inscription. The five-family statement implicitly promises coverage of "super-Yangian" but actually covers only the gl-super series. Y_osp(4|20), the K3 object referenced throughout Vol III, is in the residual (BCD-twisted super) sector, not the gl-super (parity-balance) sector.

Verdict: GAP. Two healings:
(a) broaden proposition 5 to "super-Yangian of gl-type", and inscribe a SIXTH family (or a sub-case under family 2) for the osp-super series with reflection-equation super-Shapovalov;
(b) acknowledge the scope in a new scope remark: the parity-balance mechanism is gl-super only, and the osp-super series is covered via the BCD-twisted reflection-equation mechanism generalised to the Z/2-graded setting.

Option (b) is the Beilinson-minimal heal: the underlying mathematics is already in the reflection-equation route; the five-family HEADLINE needs scope refinement, not a new proof.

## New anti-patterns (AP361-AP380 block, Wave 15)

**AP361 (Family-label scope masks a sub-case split).** A theorem advertising coverage of "super-Yangian" covers Y(gl(m|n)) only via a parity-balance hyperplane mechanism; the sibling Y(osp(m|2n)) series uses a reflection-equation super-Shapovalov mechanism and is structurally in a different family of the five-family taxonomy. Advertising "super-Yangian" as a single family risks misidentifying Y_osp(m|2n) (used as the K3 super-Yangian per Vol III rename AP246 + AP279) as parity-balance-covered. Counter: whenever a family label unifies two structurally distinct sub-series (gl-super, osp-super; type-A rational, type-A trigonometric; elliptic sl_N, elliptic orthogonal), the theorem header MUST name the sub-case or explicitly state "all sub-cases". Healing: scope remark naming the sub-case; cross-reference to the sibling family's proposition if the other sub-case is covered there. Distinct from AP239 (naming after physical source without geometric content) — AP361 is mathematical-label scope, not physical-source naming.

**AP362 (Completed-vs-uncompleted category drift on MC3-type theorems).** Two proofs of "MC3 thick generation" coexisting at two category levels (uncompleted O with finite-dim objects, pro-completed Ô with pro-objects) are NOT two proofs of ONE theorem; they are ONE theorem each in TWO distinct categories. A remark comparing them without naming the category level risks implying the two proofs are equivalent or that one subsumes the other. Counter: every MC3-type thick-generation proposition MUST name its category level (O, Ô, D^co, D^b) in the statement; a comparison remark must name both levels. Healing: category-level scope explicit in each proposition header; comparison remark names both. Distinct from AP259 (tautological-by-definition): AP362 is category-level scope, not definitional circularity.

**AP363 (Fundamental-irreducible dim list extended by non-fundamentals).** A list of "fundamental dimensions for exceptional Lie algebra X" extended by numbers that are dimensions of non-fundamental irreducibles (e.g. F_4 fundamentals {26, 52, 273, 1274} extended to include {4096, 8645}). Non-fundamentals are generated from fundamentals by fusion; their multiplicity-freeness is a consequence, not an independent data. Counter: before listing fundamental dimensions, consult Bourbaki (Lie groupes et algebres, ch. VI-VIII) or Onishchik-Vinberg; the number of fundamentals equals the rank. Healing: correct the list to the rank-many fundamentals; state "non-fundamental irreducibles follow by KR-fusion T-system".

**AP364 (Zariski-generic qualifier on multi-parameter space).** A proposition saying "thick generation at generic spectral parameters" is AMBIGUOUS if there is more than one parameter (e.g. (q, spectral), (q, ℏ, spectral), (τ, spectral, dynamical)). Zariski-generic in ONE parameter differs from Zariski-generic in the JOINT parameter space; the root-of-unity locus of q is a proper subvariety of the joint space. Counter: state explicitly the parameter space of the Zariski-open statement. Healing: add parenthetical "(Zariski-open in (q, spectral) joint parameter space; in particular excluding q at primitive roots of unity)".

**AP365 (Coverage corollary claims "union covers X" where union covers sub-cases disjointly).** Corollary cor:five-family-union-coverage as originally written lists the coverage at the family level. If a family has sub-cases that are covered by DIFFERENT propositions (e.g. super-Yangian gl-super via prop-5; super-Yangian osp-super via prop-2 sub-case), the corollary must state the sub-case split explicitly. Counter: every coverage-union corollary must iterate over SUB-CASES, not families, when a family splits non-trivially. Healing: sub-case bullet list in the corollary body.
