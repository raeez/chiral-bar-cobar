## (i) Location and scope of the conjecture

Canonical inscription: `chapters/examples/yangians_computations.tex:3936-3945`.

```
\begin{conjecture}[DK on compacts and completion extension; \ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:dk-compacts-completion}
...
The bar-cobar Koszul duality equivalence
$\Phi$~\eqref{eq:dk-eval-core} extends from the finite-dimensional
evaluation core to the full completed shifted-prefundamental core
$\mathcal{G}_{\mathrm{shift}}$, and then by pro-nilpotent completion
in the sense of Francis--Gaitsgory to the completed Yangian category.
\end{conjecture>
```

Framing remark at `:3947-3969` (rem:dk-compacts-completion-typeA): the conjecture is stated in type~$A$ and is the "final unclosed packet" there. The proved type~$A$ inputs are:

- (i) evaluation-core DK equivalence (`thm:eval-core-identification`);
- (ii) shifted-prefundamental generation inside the separated completion (`thm:shifted-prefundamental-generation`);
- (iii) pro-Weyl recovery of standards as derived inverse limits (`thm:pro-weyl-recovery`).

What is NOT proved: the compact-core extension of the bar-cobar functor $\Phi$ beyond the evaluation core, together with a Francis-Gaitsgory pro-nilpotent comparison between the chosen completion and the compactly-generated enhancement.

Programme-level status tabulation: `chapters/theory/mc3_five_family_platonic.tex:742-792` enumerates five MC3 status rows, of which:

- full $\mathrm{DK}_\mathfrak{g}$ type~$A$ is listed "conditional (compact-completion)" against `conj:dk-compacts-completion`;
- full $\mathrm{DK}_\mathfrak{g}$ non-type-$A$ is "conjectural" against `conj:mc3-automatic-generalization`.

## (ii) Precise gap

Close read of the proof skeleton at `prop:completion-extension-weight-bounded` (`:4487` onward) and of `rem:francis-gaitsgory-mc3` (`:4444-4452`) shows the gap decomposes into two items, NOT one:

- (G1) Compact-core extension of $\Phi$: the bar-cobar equivalence is proved only on the finite-dimensional evaluation core. Its extension to the compact shifted-prefundamental core $\mathcal{G}_{\mathrm{shift}}$ requires two structural inputs that are packaged as hypotheses (i)-(iii) of `prop:completion-extension-weight-bounded`:
  - (a) weight-bounded t-structure compatibility (hypothesis `it:weight-compatible`);
  - (b) colimit generation of the completion by $\iota(D^b(\mathcal{A}))$ (hypothesis `it:colimit-generation`);
  - (c) compactness preservation of $\iota(D^b(\mathcal{A}))$ inside the completion (hypothesis `it:compactness-preservation`).

  These three hypotheses are exactly where the "modulo conjecture" qualifier lands: the proposition says they SUFFICE for localizing DK extension, but VERIFICATION of each in the chosen completed ambient is not supplied.

- (G2) Completion comparison: even with (G1), the resulting compactly-generated enhancement $\mathcal{D}^{\mathrm{comp}}$ must be identified with the pro-nilpotent / Francis-Gaitsgory completion that the MC3 programme actually targets (the source of "full $\mathrm{DK}_g$" in the preface gloss). This is a SEPARATE identification theorem, not a consequence of (G1).

Two-gap decomposition is consistent with the internal structure of `conj:dk-compacts-completion`, which has two explicit clauses ("extends from the finite-dimensional evaluation core to the full completed shifted-prefundamental core... AND THEN by pro-nilpotent completion... to the completed Yangian category"). The conjunction structure was not visible in the CLAUDE.md single-sentence gloss.

CORRECT FORMULATION (preferred, not "compactly generated by evaluation modules" — the evaluation core is strict-thick, not compact-generating; the prefundamental seed is what generates):

> On the separated completion of the anti-dominant shifted envelope $\widehat{\mathcal{O}}^{\mathrm{sh}}_{\leq 0}(Y(\mathfrak{g}))$, (a) the bar-cobar functor $\Phi$ of `thm:eval-core-identification` extends to a localizing equivalence on the compact shifted-prefundamental core generated by the Baxter-locus seed $\mathcal{G}_{\mathrm{Bax}} = \{V_{\omega_i}(a)\} \cup \{L^-_{i,a-1/2}\}$, AND (b) the chosen completion is the Francis-Gaitsgory pro-nilpotent completion of the ordinary $D^b(\mathcal{O}^{\mathrm{sh}}_{\leq 0})$ in the sense of GR17 Vol II Ch.~4.

"Every compact object in $\mathrm{DK}_g$ is in the thick closure of evaluation-type modules" is a DIFFERENT (stronger) claim — it is the compact-object identification statement from Neeman / Bondal-Van den Bergh, which is supplied unconditionally by Step~1 of `prop:completion-extension-weight-bounded` as a formal consequence of hypotheses (i)-(iii). The conjecture does not include the compact-object identification; it includes the hypothesis VERIFICATION plus the completion comparison.

## (iii) Type~$A$ circularity check

CLAUDE.md phrasing "unconditional only in type A modulo `conj:dk-compacts-completion`" IS circular: "unconditional modulo a conjecture" = conditional.

The accurate honest formulation is: in type~$A$, MC3 on the EVALUATION-GENERATED CORE is unconditional (five-family mechanism + categorical CG Theorem); MC3 on the FULL shifted-prefundamental core inside a chosen separated completion is CONDITIONAL on `conj:dk-compacts-completion`. Both statements are already correctly inscribed in the manuscript (see `thm:mc3-type-a-resolution` plus clause (iv) at `:4196-4204`). The CLAUDE.md gloss compressed two statements into one and introduced the circularity.

VERDICT: manuscript is honest; CLAUDE.md gloss needs rewriting.

## (iv) Non-type-$A$ gap structure

For simple types beyond type~$A$ (types $B, C, D, E_6, E_7, E_8, F_4, G_2$):

- MC3a (categorical CG closure on the evaluation-generated core): PROVED uniformly via multiplicity-free $\ell$-weights (`thm:categorical-cg-all-types`), using Nakajima quiver varieties (simply-laced), Chari-Moura explicit formulas (classical), and the Frenkel-Mukhin algorithm (all types). The AP250 caveat on Frenkel-Mukhin per-type verification for $G_2, F_4$ is handled separately via `prop:mc3-uniform-chari-moura-nonsimplylaced-platonic` and Hernandez 2006/2010.
- MC3b (DK-2/3 on evaluation-generated core): unconditional (`cor:dk23-all-types`).
- MC3c (shifted-envelope generation inside separated completion): CONDITIONAL on `conj:rank-independence-step2` (Lemma~L, lift-and-lower), which is an INDEPENDENT conjecture NOT IDENTICAL TO `conj:dk-compacts-completion`.
- Pro-Weyl and completion packets: CONJECTURAL as `conj:mc3-automatic-generalization`.

So the non-type-$A$ gap has THREE conditional packets, NOT one:
1. Lemma~L (`conj:rank-independence-step2`) for shifted-envelope generation;
2. `conj:mc3-automatic-generalization` (type-independence of remaining mechanisms);
3. `conj:dk-compacts-completion` (compact-completion comparison — same as type~$A$).

The CLAUDE.md gloss "CONJECTURAL elsewhere" is correct as a coarse label but hides the three-packet structure.

Chari-Moura uniformity proves MC3a for classical + simply-laced exceptional; compact completion (packet 3) is SEPARATELY conjectural and does NOT follow from multiplicity-freeness. The three packets are independent.

### HEAL 3: Name the two internal clauses of conj:dk-compacts-completion

The conjecture body at `yangians_computations.tex:3936-3945` reads as a single statement with a conjunctive structure. To make the two-gap decomposition visible to downstream audits, insert a scope remark RIGHT AFTER the conjecture. This is the AP266 sharpened-obstruction pattern (exhibit the gap structure explicitly rather than a single monolithic conjecture).

INSERT after `:3945` (before `rem:dk-compacts-completion-typeA` at `:3947`):

```
\begin{remark}[Two-clause decomposition of Conjecture~\ref{conj:dk-compacts-completion}]
\label{rem:dk-compacts-completion-two-clause}
\index{MC3!compact-completion!two-clause decomposition}
The conjecture statement is a conjunction of two structurally distinct items:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(C\arabic*)},nosep]
\item \emph{Compact-core extension of $\Phi$.} The bar-cobar functor of Theorem~\ref{thm:eval-core-identification} extends from the finite-dimensional evaluation core to the compact shifted-prefundamental core $\mathcal{G}_{\mathrm{shift}}$ inside the separated completion, with the three weight-bounded/colimit/compactness hypotheses of Proposition~\ref{prop:completion-extension-weight-bounded} verified in the chosen completed ambient.
\item \emph{Francis--Gaitsgory pro-nilpotent comparison.} The resulting compactly-generated enhancement agrees with the Francis--Gaitsgory pro-nilpotent completion of $D^b(\mathcal{O}^{\mathrm{sh}}_{\leq 0}(Y(\mathfrak{g})))$ in the sense of~\cite{GR17} Vol~II Ch.~4, so that the extended equivalence is a statement about the same target category that MC3 names in the preface.
\end{enumerate}
Clause~(C1) alone does not close the MC3 gap: one also needs~(C2) to identify the compactly-generated ambient with the factorization-theoretic target. A proof of~(C1) on its own would yield an equivalence on some compactly-generated enhancement, leaving the identification with the Francis--Gaitsgory completion open.
\end{remark}
```

## AP registrations (sparing, AP314)

- **AP1181 (Circular "unconditional modulo" gloss).** A status-row gloss writes "PROVED/UNCONDITIONAL only in [scope] modulo [Conjecture]"; this is identically equivalent to "CONDITIONAL on [Conjecture] in [scope]". The word "unconditional" is used incorrectly — a theorem conditional on a conjecture is, by definition, conditional. Canonical violation: CLAUDE.md MC1-4 row at line 583, "Extension to full DK_g unconditional only in type A modulo `conj:dk-compacts-completion`; CONJECTURAL elsewhere". Counter: use "CONDITIONAL on [Conjecture]" or "UNCONDITIONAL in [subscope] where [Conjecture] is not needed"; reserve "UNCONDITIONAL" for claims whose proof chain has zero open conjectures. Healing: rewrite the gloss to separate UNCONDITIONAL subclaims (MC3 on evaluation-generated core) from CONDITIONAL extensions (full $\mathrm{DK}_g$), with the conditioning conjectures named. Related: AP271 (reverse drift, CLAUDE.md lags manuscript — AP1181 is the specific phrasing-level defect that lets AP271 persist); AP305 (pessimistic drift — AP1181 is the opposite, optimistic gloss).

- **AP1182 (Monolithic conjecture hiding multi-clause gap structure).** A single `\begin{conjecture}` body carries a conjunctive statement ("the bar-cobar equivalence extends... AND THEN by pro-nilpotent completion... to the completed category"); downstream audits treat it as a single obstruction. If the two clauses are structurally distinct (different machinery, different external inputs, different falsification tests), hiding the conjunction inside one label produces an AP280-style headline compression. Canonical witness: `conj:dk-compacts-completion`'s two clauses (C1 compact-core $\Phi$-extension, C2 Francis-Gaitsgory comparison) — mechanically independent. Counter: for any conjecture body containing "AND THEN", "moreover", "and further", or an implicit conjunction across distinct machinery, immediately append a two-clause decomposition remark naming each clause and noting that a proof of one clause alone does not close the conjecture. Related: AP266 (sharpened obstruction — AP1182 is the positive-side complement, sharpening the GAP rather than the obstruction coefficient); AP280 (three-step epistemic inflation, in reverse — here the headline is honest but the internal structure is compressed).
