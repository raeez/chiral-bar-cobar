## Phase 1. Attack: five surviving findings

### F1. CRITICAL — Adjoint-Serre relations confabulated; chiral Steenrod dim `2^{rk g}` is WRONG

**Location.** `Lemma lem:screening-adjoint-squares` and `Def def:chiral-steenrod` lines 89–117 and 468–490.

**Claim.** `Q_i^{adm} Q_j^{adm} + Q_j^{adm} Q_i^{adm} = 0` for adjacent simple roots (`a_{ij} = -1`), giving a finite-dimensional graded algebra of dimension `2^{rk g}` (ordinary exterior algebra).

**Counterattack.** The Feigin–Frenkel screenings at admissible level `k + h^\vee = p/q` satisfy the full **quantum Serre relations** at `q_{qg} = e^{\pi i q/p}`:

  `(ad_{q} Q_i)^{1 - a_{ij}} Q_j = 0`,

which at `a_{ij} = -1` is a **cubic** relation `[Q_i,[Q_i,Q_j]_q]_q = 0`, NOT an anticommutator. The Shapovalov adjoints inherit the opposite quantum-group relations (`U_q^-`-style), which are again cubic, not quadratic. An ordinary exterior algebra is obtained ONLY in the classical limit `q_{qg} \to 1` (i.e. `p \to \infty`), which is outside the admissible regime. Consequently:

- The algebra generated by `{Q_i^{adm}}` is the negative nilpotent part `u_q^-(g)` of the small quantum group, not `\Lambda^\bullet(\mathbb{C}^{rk g})`.
- For `sl_3` (`rk = 2`, `p = 3`): dim `u_q^-(sl_3)` has `p^3 = 27` not `2^2 = 4`.
- The test `compute/tests/test_periodic_cdg_admissible.py::test_chiral_steenrod_rank_matches_uq_and_ckl` hard-codes `2^{rk}` on BOTH sides of the equality, which is pattern-matching across occurrences (AP10/AP128: engine and test shipped the same wrong model).

**Verdict.** The "exterior algebra on anticommuting generators" claim is a confabulation in the classical-limit mental model. `rk g = 1` (only `sl_2`) is the sole case where cubic and anticommutation collapse.

**Heal.** Downgrade the chiral Steenrod algebra to `u_q^-(g)` at `q_{qg} = e^{\pi i q/p}`, dimension `p^{N}` with `N = |\Phi^+|`. Restrict Theorem 4.3 to `sl_2` (where exterior coincidence holds at `p = 2`) or rewrite with the correct Nichols-algebra description. Scope "ordinary exterior" to `sl_2, p = 2`.

### F2. CRITICAL — Step 4 Finkelberg transport relies on a non-statement

**Location.** `thm:periodic-cdg-is-koszul-compatible` Step 4 (lines 290–308).

**Claim.** The bar complex of `ĝ_k` restricted to admissible weights factors through the bar complex of `u_q(g)`, whose bar cohomology is `2p`-periodic in the **Tate sense**, yielding the integer-weight inclusion after transporting along the Finkelberg equivalence.

**Counterattack.** Finkelberg's theorem is that `Tilt(U_q(g))/Neg` is (braided) equivalent to `\mathcal{O}_k^{int}`; the quotient by negligible tilting modules **makes the target semisimple**. In a semisimple abelian category, every `Ext^{\geq 1}` vanishes — so Tate-cohomological periodicity in `Tilt(U_q)/Neg` is vacuous (`0 \to 0` is trivially `2p`-periodic). The non-trivial `2p`-Tate periodicity lives on `u_q(g)`-mod (the finite-dim small quantum group, NON-semisimple), NOT on the semisimplified Finkelberg target. Transport from `u_q(g)`-mod to `O_k^{int}` requires an additional comparison (typically through `Rep(U_q^{big})/Neg` or the DGG/Arkhipov–Gaitsgory long exact triangle), which is the content of the Kazhdan–Lusztig–Lusztig lifting programme (open beyond `sl_2` in full chain-level generality).

**Verdict.** Step 4 as written conflates `u_q`-mod (where periodicity lives) with the semisimple KL target (where periodicity is trivial). The bar-cobar image landing in `O_k^{int}` is not established by the given argument.

**Heal.** State Step 4 as a bridge to `u_q(g)`-mod (not `O_k^{int}`) and mark the passage from `u_q(g)`-mod to the semisimplified KL category as a **separate conjectural step**: this is `conj:kl-bar-cobar-dl` in `derived_langlands.tex`, which remains ClaimStatusConjectured after the present chapter. The programme-level frontier "FM251 closed" must be softened: the **bar-side periodic-CDG structure** is proved on filtration quotients; the **semisimplification descent** remains conjectural.

### F3. MODERATE — "`\sigma_{2p}` is a quasi-isomorphism on each `gr^n F`" is a statement about a finite-dimensional complex, not the bar complex

**Location.** `thm:periodic-cdg-is-koszul-compatible`(i) and Step 2 proof.

**Claim.** Cup product with `Θ_{2p}` is a qi on `gr^n F`, giving `2p`-periodicity on bar cohomology.

**Counterattack.** Step 2 proves `Θ_{2p}` is a top-form generator of a Poincaré-duality pairing on each **finite-dimensional** filtration quotient (Positselski Prop. 3.11). This is a graded-quotient statement, WEAKER than the original `conj:periodic-cdg` assertion that `B^{n+2p}(ĝ_k) \cong B^n(ĝ_k)` as CDG-coalgebras. The periodicity proved here lives on the **associated graded** of the periodic-CDG filtration; the full bar complex is only `2p`-periodic up to a convergent spectral sequence (Step 3 of Thm 3.1), and chain-level isomorphism of graded pieces does not descend to chain-level isomorphism of the total complex without additional splitting data (which is exactly the Massey-vanishing hypothesis — unproved for `sl_3` and higher at admissible levels).

**Verdict.** The theorem proves `2p`-periodicity on filtration quotients and, after spectral-sequence convergence, on **cohomology**. Chain-level `2p`-periodic CDG structure on the full bar complex is weaker than claimed.

**Heal.** Replace "`σ_{2p}` is a quasi-isomorphism on each `gr^n F`" with "`σ_{2p}` induces an isomorphism on `H^\bullet(gr^n F)` and on `H^\bullet(\barB^{ch,\mathrm{adm}}(ĝ_k))`". Mark full chain-level `2p`-periodicity of `\barB^{ch}` as ClaimStatusConjectured (Massey-vanishing hypothesis).

### F4. MODERATE — The `(p-1)(q-1)/2` count for admissible `sl_2` vs. Virasoro minimal model needs scope

**Location.** `cor:class-M-admissible-minimal-model` (line 630) and `test_minimal_model_simple_object_count_matches_kac_table`.

**Claim.** `KL^{adm}_{Vir}(c_{p,q})` has `(p-1)(q-1)/2` simple objects.

**Counterattack.** BPZ 1984 gives the **distinct Virasoro primaries** count `(p-1)(q-1)/2`, using the Kac-table symmetry `(r, s) \sim (p-r, q-s)`. But the admissible `\widehat{sl}_2`-module count at `k = -2 + p/q` is `|Adm_k| = (p-1) \cdot q` (or similar; see Kac–Wakimoto 1989 Lemma 4.1), reduced by Weyl symmetry. The Drinfeld–Sokolov functor collapses admissible `\widehat{sl}_2`-modules onto minimal-model Virasoro primaries `2`-to-`1` (upper vs lower BPZ symmetry fibres). The corollary correctly states the **Virasoro** simple count, but the proof conflates it with the **admissible `\widehat{sl}_2`** count; these differ by the factor `q` (or `2`) at the DS image. Moreover, the test case `(3, 2, 1)` is the `c = 0` trivial minimal model — whether this counts as "1 simple" (identity) or "degenerate" is convention-dependent.

**Verdict.** The count is correct **after DS reduction**, but the proof text elides the reduction count.

**Heal.** Add a sentence to the proof specifying that DS is `2`-to-`1` on admissible `\widehat{sl}_2`-modules onto Virasoro primaries and that the `(p-1)(q-1)/2` count is post-DS; flag `(p, q) = (3, 2)` as the `c = 0` trivial case (one primary, the identity, ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere).

### F5. MINOR — "Closes FM251 + FM256 + sole remaining programme frontier" is an overclaim

**Location.** File header comment (lines 5–7), `cor:open-frontier-resolved-1`, `cor:open-frontier-resolved-2`, `rem:master-conjecture-update-periodic-cdg`.

**Counterattack.** FM251 is `conj:periodic-cdg` as stated in `derived_langlands.tex`: `B^{n+2s}(ĝ_k) \cong B^n(ĝ_k)` with `s = p`. The present chapter proves a **filtration-graded-quotient** version, supplemented by a spectral-sequence argument for the adjunction. This is a genuine advance, but:

- F1 forces a downgrade of Thm 4.3 (`adams-analog-construction`) outside `sl_2`, so FM256 (Adams-analogy) is **NOT fully closed**.
- F2 leaves the `u_q \to O_k^{int}` semisimplification descent conjectural.
- F3 leaves chain-level `2p`-periodicity of `\barB^{ch}` itself conjectural (Massey vanishing).
- `thm:periodic-cdg-is-koszul-compatible`(iii) "image lies in `O_k^{int}` up to periodic shift" inherits the F2 gap.

The honest statement: the chapter reduces FM251 to (i) the Massey-vanishing hypothesis for the full CDG coalgebra, (ii) the `u_q`-to-`Tilt/Neg` descent for bar-cobar. The **sole remaining programme frontier** framing is aspirational.

**Heal.** Rewrite headlines to: "FM251 reduced to Massey-vanishing + small-QG semisimplification descent; FM256 proved for `sl_2`, conjectural for higher rank; the reduction constitutes substantial progress but not closure."
