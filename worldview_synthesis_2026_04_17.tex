\section*{Prelude: The seed}

\noindent
The product of two chiral sections $a(z)\,b(w)$ on a smooth algebraic
curve~$X$ diverges as $z\to w$. Among differential $1$-forms with a
pole along the diagonal, only the logarithmic derivative
$\eta = d\log(z_1 - z_2) = (dz_1 - dz_2)/(z_1 - z_2)$ has a residue
independent of coordinate, metric, or level. Three-point
consistency on $\mathrm{Conf}_3(X)$ forces the Arnold relation
$\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23} + \eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{31}
+ \eta_{31}\wedge\eta_{12} = 0$, equivalent to the
partial-fractions identity whose left-hand side has simple poles at
$z_2$ and $z_3$ with residues summing to zero. Arnold's theorem
generates the complete ideal of relations in
$H^*(\mathrm{Conf}_n(\bC))$ from this one identity. The propagator
fails to be coordinate-invariant under $z\mapsto w(z)$ by precisely
the $\operatorname{Diff}(X)$-cocycle $d\log w'(z_i)$; this failure is
the BRST ghost that the Virasoro algebra measures. One $1$-form, one
relation, one ghost. Everything that follows is forced.
