\begin{abstract}
\itshape

\noindent
The local seed is the logarithmic form
\[
\eta_{12}=d\log(z_1-z_2)
\]
on the configuration space of two points of a smooth algebraic
curve~$X$. On three points it obeys Arnold's relation
\[
\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23}+\eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{31}
+\eta_{31}\wedge\eta_{12}=0,
\]
the nilpotence mechanism of the ordered chiral bar complex
\[
\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}_X(\cA)=T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA),
\qquad |s^{-1}v|=|v|-1,
\]
for a chiral algebra~$\cA$ on~$X$. Its differential is the pullback
of Arnold's flat connection,
\[
d_{\barB}=\mathrm{KZ}^{*}(\nabla_{\mathrm{Arnold}}),
\]
and its all-genus completion is encoded by the Maurer--Cartan
equation
\[
D\Theta_\cA+\tfrac12[\Theta_\cA,\Theta_\cA]=0
\]
in the modular convolution $L_\infty$-algebra. Configuration-space
geometry selects the Borcherds identity; the Borcherds identity makes
the bar differential square to zero; the modular compactification
records the higher-genus curvature.

The logarithmic boundary refines this curvature before it is averaged.
For a simple-normal-crossing pair $(X,D)$, Mok's logarithmic
Fulton--MacPherson spaces label boundary strata by planted forests.
For the relative universal family the corresponding boundary type has
refined degree
\[
\mu=(g,r;\,b,s,\ell,c,p),
\]
where $b$ counts bar/Hamiltonian contractions, $s$ separating nodes,
$\ell$ non-separating nodes, $c$ relative Mok crossings, and $p$ pure
planted-forest depth. The full logarithmic codimension is
$s+\ell+c+p$; the coarse computational tridegree records only
$(g,r,c+p)$. Mok supplies the geometric strata,
codimensions, and degeneration formula. The residue-pushforward,
orientation-line, and coassociativity package that turns those strata
into a chain cooperad is a conditional structure. Under this
filtration the modular bar--cobar pair is filtered and, on associated
gradeds,
\[
\operatorname{gr}_F\Omega\barB^{\mathrm{mod},\log}(\cA)
\;\cong\;
\Omega\bigl(\operatorname{gr}_F\barB^{\mathrm{mod},\log}(\cA)\bigr).
\]

The ordered bar is the primitive object. Averaging ordered
configurations is the unique information-loss operation: passing to
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariants gives the symmetric bar
$\barB^\Sigma_X(\cA)$, the modular shadow of the averaging map
\[
\mathrm{av}\colon\mathfrak g^{\Eone}\longrightarrow
\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}},
\qquad
\mathrm{av}_n=\frac1{n!}\sum_{\sigma\in S_n}\sigma^*.
\]
The degree-two ordered residue is the classical $r$-matrix
$r_\cA(z)$; after averaging it becomes the scalar characteristic
$\kappa(\cA)$. At genus $g\ge1$ the ordered fibre differential has
curvature $(d^{\mathrm{ord}}_{\mathrm{fib}})^2
=r_\cA(z)\cdot\omega_g$. Its scalar-diagonal, uniform-weight shadow is
the Hodge class $\kappa(\cA)\lambda_g$ on $\overline{\cM}_g$; the full
all-weight free energy retains the cross-channel correction measured
by Theorem~D.

Five theorem packages are the invariant content that survives this
ordered-to-symmetric passage. Theorem~A is the chiral bar--cobar
adjunction in its chain-level and factorisation-$\infty$ lanes.
Theorem~B is bar--cobar inversion on the Koszul locus together with
the weight-completed coderived Positselski form off the strict lane.
Theorem~C is derived-centre complementarity. On the Vol~I classical
witnesses the scalar ceiling is
\[
\kappa(\cA)+\kappa(\cA^!)\in
\{0,13,250/3,98/3\}.
\]
The canonical cross-volume bucket adjoins the Vol~III $\mathsf B$-row
Mukai witness $K^\kappa=2c_+(\mathrm{Mukai}(K3))=8$, giving
\[
\{0,8,13,250/3,98/3\}.
\]
Theorem~D is the genus tower
\[
F_g(\cA)=\kappa(\cA)\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}
+\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA),
\]
with $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}=0$ on the uniform-weight lane.
Theorem~H is the curve-level chiral Hochschild theorem:
concentration in degrees $\{0,1,2\}$ on the proved Koszul/generic
surface. Holography, fingerprint completeness, the seven faces of
$r(z)$, and the Calabi--Yau-to-chiral comparison are reconstruction
and comparison surfaces built on this five-theorem spine.

The standard landscape is read by the same residue. The averaging
shadow stratifies the principal families into
\[
\mathsf G/\mathsf L/\mathsf C/\mathsf M/
\mathsf M^{\mathrm{ext}}/\mathsf B
\]
with shadow depths
$r_{\max}\in\{2,3,4,\infty,\infty,\infty\}$: Heisenberg, affine
Kac--Moody, $\beta\gamma$, Virasoro/principal $\cW_N$,
subregular or hook Drinfeld--Sokolov reductions such as
Bershadsky--Polyakov, and the K3 Borcherds--Kac--Moody crown
$\mathbf H_{\Delta_5}$. The critical affine stratum where Sugawara
degenerates is a companion boundary, not a new archetype.

The Virasoro class-\(\mathsf M\) tower carries a normalization
split that is part of the theorem. The weighted
Riccati-metric expansion
\(H_{\mathrm{wt}}^2=t^4Q_c\) computes
\(\widehat S_6^{\mathrm{wt}}=80(45c+193)/[3c^3(5c+22)^2]\);
the manuscript shadow coefficient is instead forced by
\(2S_2S_6+2S_3S_5+S_4^2=0\) and equals
\[
S_6(\mathrm{Vir}_c)=\frac{4(240c+1031)}{c^3(5c+22)^2}.
\]
The same correction fixes the Borel radius
\(|\omega|^2(c)=c^2(5c+22)/[4(45c+218)]\), the Stokes pole
\(c_S=-218/45\), and the interpretation of \(c=-83/20\) as a
secondary Borel-radius phase locus rather than a double root of
\(Q_c\).

On the checked comparison surfaces, the same object has three
readings. Volume~I is the operadic reading: ordered bar, averaging,
the five theorem packages, and the standard landscape on curves.
Volume~II is the holographic reading: the Swiss-cheese
chiral-topological operad
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ governs the derived-centre pair
$(C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\cA)$ and the topologised
holomorphic-topological bulk. Its nonlinear finite-jet
Khan--Zeng/PVA lane has a precise boundary: the shifted-cotangent BV
action and the equivalence between the classical master equation and
the PVA Hamiltonian condition are constructed, while the all-loop QME,
reflected weight identity, boundary vertex algebra, and $E_3$ lift
require the analytic SDR, image-choice Stokes, and self-image
renormalisation package. Volume~III is the geometric reading:
\[
\Phi_d=\mathrm{Sp}^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\Sigma_{d-1},C}
\circ \Phi^{\mathrm{FA}}_d
\]
produces a family of chiral shadows after choosing
$(\Sigma_{d-1},C)$; in the $d\ge3$ lane these are
$\Eone$-chiral shadows on the reference curve. On the K3 lane the
$\mathsf B$-row is pinned by
\[
\hbar^2K=-1,\qquad K=2c_+(L),
\]
with $(K,\hbar^2)=(8,-1/8)$ for the Mukai lattice witness. The
remaining boundary is explicit: critical level, logarithmic
admissible families, super-Yangian shifts, and higher-dimensional
Calabi--Yau shadows beyond the proved loci.
\end{abstract}

\noindent
Vol I of the four-quadrant programme treats the Open quadrant: a
factorisation dg-category $\cC^{\op}$ on a tangential log curve
$(X, D, \tau)$, a chosen vacuum $b \in \cC^{\op}$, and the chart
algebra $A_b = \End_\cC(b)$. The five-level open Beilinson tower
\[
 \cC^{\op} \;\rightleftarrows\; A_b
 \;\rightleftarrows\; B(A_b)
 \;\xrightarrow{\;}\; Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A_b)
 \;\xrightarrow{\;}\; \mathsf{Line}(A_b)
 \;\xrightarrow{\;}\; (\kappa,\,Z_g,\,\Phi_N)
\]
organises the manuscript: Part~I builds levels~$0$ and~$1$; Part~II
builds the bar coalgebra at level~$2$ via Theorem~A; Part~III builds
the derived chiral centre at level~$3$ via Theorems~H and~B;
Part~IV stratifies the chart algebra into five archetypes; Part~V
builds the modular tower at levels~$4$ and~$5$ via Theorem~D;
Part~VI inscribes the seven-face dictionary, the Master
Reconstruction Theorem, the cross-volume vertical equivalences, and
the open frontier.

The reader entering this part has already met the tangential log
curve, the factorisation dg-category, and the augmented chart algebra
in the Heisenberg overture
(Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}). Part~I supplies the
foundations needed to develop those constructions for every chiral
algebra in the standard landscape: classical Koszul duality recalled,
the three depth invariants $p_{\max} \to k_{\max} \to r_{\max}$,
the configuration-space geometry on which the bar differential will
later be defined, and the Koszul-pair structure
$(A_b, A_b^!)$ entering Theorem~A.

Place sections $a_1, \ldots, a_n$ of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ at
distinct points $z_1, \ldots, z_n$ on a smooth curve~$X$. Wedge the
tensor $a_1(z_1) \otimes \cdots \otimes a_n(z_n)$ against the
logarithmic $n$-form $\eta = \bigwedge_{i<j} d\log(z_i - z_j)$ on
the Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\overline{C}_n(X)$, and
extract residues at each collision divisor. The result is a
differential. Arnold's three-term identity
\[
\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23}
\;+\; \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31}
\;+\; \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12}
\;=\; 0
\]
forces $d^2 = 0$. This is the bar complex~$\barB_X(\cA)$: the
categorical logarithm of~$\cA$.

A logarithm has four properties. Each is a theorem.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{(\Alph*)},leftmargin=2.2em]
\item \textbf{Existence.}
 The bar-cobar adjunction $(\barB, \Omega)$, intertwined with
 Verdier duality on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$. The logarithm exists
 and is natural.
\item \textbf{Invertibility.}
 On the Koszul locus,
 $\Omega(\barB(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$: the cobar inverts
 the bar, with the spectral sequence collapsing at~$E_2$. The
 logarithm has an exponential.
\item \textbf{Branch structure.}
 At genus $g \geq 1$, $Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!)$ sit as
 complementary Lagrangians inside
 $R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathcal{Z}(\cA))$.
 The logarithm has monodromy; its sheets are Lagrangian.
\item \textbf{Leading coefficient.}
 The modular characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$ is universal, additive,
 and duality-constrained; its generating function is the $\hat{A}$-genus.
 The leading Taylor coefficient of the logarithm is a topological
 index.
\end{enumerate}
\noindent
\textbf{(H) The coefficient ring.}
$\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is polynomial on the Koszul locus and dual to
$\ChirHoch^*(\cA^!)$. The full Taylor series of the logarithm lives
in a polynomial ring.

\smallskip\noindent
But $\kappa(\cA)$ is only the \emph{linear} leading term.
Part~\ref{part:characteristic-datum} develops the full nonlinear structure: the
\emph{shadow obstruction tower}
$\kappa \to \Delta \to \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{Q} \to \cdots$,
a hierarchy of finite-order invariants (quadratic, cubic, quartic,
and beyond), each proved constructively with obstruction classes
$o_{r+1}(\cA)$ in the cyclic deformation complex.
The all-degree limit of this tower is the universal
class~$\Theta_\cA$, proved by the bar-intrinsic construction
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic});
the constructive content is the finite-order engine.

\label{part:bar-cobar-engine}%

\noindent
Part~\ref{part:foundations-beilinson-tower} fixes the level-$1$ chart
algebra $A_b = \End_\cC(b)$ at a chosen vacuum $b\in\cC^{\op}$. The
question Part~\ref{part:bar-cobar-engine} answers is the natural one:
\emph{Does $A_b$ admit a coalgebra-level dual that recovers $A_b$ on
inversion, intertwined with Verdier duality on the Ran prestack?}

Theorem~A is the affirmative answer in parametric strength. The bar
functor
$\Bbarch_X\colon \ChirAlg^{\aug}_X \to \ChirCoAlg^{\conil}_X$
admits a left adjoint $\Omegach_X$ (the cobar functor); the counit
$\varepsilon\colon \Omegach_X\Bbarch_X(A_b) \xrightarrow{\sim} A_b$
is a chain-level quasi-isomorphism on the Koszul locus. Off the Koszul
locus, the strongest possible reading
\textup{(}$K^2 \simeq \mathrm{id}$ \emph{everywhere}\textup{)} fails; it
is replaced by Theorem~H \textup{(}Hochschild concentration in
$\{0,1,2\}$, Part~\ref{part:bulk}\textup{)} on the completed ambient.
The categorical logarithm therefore exists in two flavours: a strict
chain-level adjunction on $\Kosz(X)$, and a coderived/contraderived
Positselski equivalence off the strict lane.

Within Theorem~A, the universal Loday--Vallette twisting-morphism
contraction $h_{\mathrm{LV}}$ specialises along the convolution
$L_\infty$-algebra $\fg^{\Eone}_{A_b}=\Hom^{\fact}_X(\Bbarch_X(A_b),A_b)$
to a chain-homotopy norm
$\mathcal N(A_b) = \kappa(A_b) + \kappa^!_{\mathrm{LV}}(A_b)$
\textup{(}the Verdier-Ran-on-bar reading at level~$2$,
Convention~\ref{conv:A-two-kappa-shriek}\textup{);} this is distinct
from the master-concordance algebra-level scalar Verdier sum
$K^\kappa(A_b)$ \textup{(}level-$1$ reading\textup{).}
The two scalars coincide only on class~$\mathsf B$ \textup{(}Mukai
conductor $8$\textup{);} on the other archetypes,
$\mathcal N$ records the chain-level pairing the Priddy contraction
inverts, while $K^\kappa$ records the family-stratum complementarity
ceiling that enters Theorem~C.

\part{The Bulk}
\label{part:bulk}

\noindent
Part~\ref{part:bar-cobar-engine} produced the bar coalgebra
$\Bbarch_X(A_b)$ at level~$2$. The reader will reasonably ask: where
is the bulk algebra of the chart $A_b$? It is tempting to identify
the bar coalgebra with the bulk, since the bar mediates between
$A_b$ and its Verdier dual; this identification is wrong
\textup{(}Master Pattern MA-4\textup{).} The bulk is the
\emph{derived chiral centre} $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A_b) =
\ChirHoch^\bullet(A_b, A_b)$ at level~$3$ of the Beilinson tower; the
bar coalgebra at level~$2$ is the twisting datum that resolves the
diagonal bimodule $A_b$ over $A_b\otimes A_b^{\op}$ in the Hochschild
calculus, but it is not the centre.

Theorem~H sharpens the bulk: $\ChirHoch^\bullet(A_b)$ on the Koszul
locus is concentrated in degrees $\{0,1,2\}$; on
$V_k(\mathfrak g)$ at non-critical level
$\ChirHoch^1 \cong \mathfrak g$ and $\ChirHoch^0,\ChirHoch^2$ are
both one-dimensional, so the Hochschild polynomial is
$P_{V_k(\mathfrak g)}(t) = 1 + \dim(\mathfrak g)\,t + t^2$
\textup{(}verified for $\mathfrak{sl}_N$, $N \in \{2,3,4,5,6,10\}$ in
\texttt{compute/lib/chiral\_hochschild\_engine.py}\textup{).}
Theorem~B inscribes the strict bar--cobar inversion
$\Omegach_X(\Bbarch_X(C))\xrightarrow{\sim} C$ on the Koszul locus and
the weight-completed Positselski lane off it. The five-object
firewall keeps the chart algebra, the bar coalgebra, the
cohomology coalgebra, the Verdier dual, and the derived chiral
centre distinct: bar $\neq$ bulk
\textup{(}MA-4\textup{)}; $\Omega B(A_b) = A_b$ is bar--cobar
inversion, not Koszul duality \textup{(}MA-4 corollary\textup{)}.

\part{The Five-Archetype Landscape}
\label{part:standard-landscape}

\noindent
Parts~\ref{part:bar-cobar-engine}--\ref{part:bulk} produced, for every
augmented chart algebra $A_b$, the bar coalgebra $\Bbarch_X(A_b)$, its
derived chiral centre $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A_b)$, and the
Hochschild polynomial $P_{A_b}(t)$. The reader will reasonably ask:
\emph{across the standard landscape of vertex algebras, how does the
chart $A_b$ stratify, and what coordinates classify the strata?}

The answer is the $5{\times}5$ $\kappa$-stratification matrix on the
five archetypes $\mathsf G/\mathsf L/\mathsf C/\mathsf M/\mathsf B$
with shadow depth $r_{\max} \in \{2, 3, 4, \infty, 5\}$. Each chart
algebra admits five distinct $\kappa$-measurements
\textup{(}categorical, chiral Hodge supertrace, Heisenberg-Mukai
chiral shadow, Borcherds-Kac-Moody weight, fibre Euler
characteristic\textup{);} the collapse pattern across columns is the
classification axis. K3$\times E$ anchors row~$\mathsf B$ at
$(0, 0, 3, 5, 24)$ and is the unique row with all five entries
non-zero. The other rows collapse: $\mathsf G,\mathsf L,\mathsf C,
\mathsf M$ have at most one non-zero entry
$\kappa^{\mathrm{Heis}}_{\mathrm{ch}}$ recording the rank of the
canonical Heisenberg subVOA \textup{(}Cartan
$\mathfrak h \subset \fg$ for class $\mathsf L$;
trivial for class $\mathsf M$ at generic $c$; rank-$1$ for class
$\mathsf G$\textup{).} The modular characteristic $\kappa(A_b)$
\textup{(}e.g.\ $c/2$ for $\mathrm{Vir}_c$,
$\dim(\fg)(k+h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$ for $V_k(\fg)$\textup{)} is a
\emph{separate} invariant, recorded in
Table~\ref{tab:master-invariants}; it is not one of the five
stratification coordinates.

\include{chapters/frame/part_iii_platonic_introduction}

\noindent
In Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}, we computed the bar complex, Koszul
dual, curvature tower, and $\hat{A}$-genus for the Heisenberg algebra.
Part~\ref{part:standard-landscape} applies the same programme to every major family of chiral
algebras. These chapters should be read as complete portraits rather
than as subordinate examples: each family reveals a structural feature
that is invisible in the Heisenberg frame alone.
The constructions of Parts~\ref{part:bar-complex}--\ref{part:characteristic-datum} (the bar complex, the genus tower, the Koszul
pair structure, the shadow obstruction tower, and the ordered refinement) are defined for any augmented chiral algebra satisfying mild finiteness
conditions. The question driving Part~\ref{part:standard-landscape} is: what do these constructions
\emph{produce} when applied to the algebras that arise in practice?

The answer is unexpectedly structured. The bar cohomology dimensions
of structurally distinct algebras (the Kac--Moody algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$, the Virasoro algebra, the
$\beta\gamma$-system) share a common discriminant
$\Delta(x) = (1-3x)(1+x)$. The genus expansion of every Koszul
algebra is controlled by a single obstruction invariant~$\kappa$,
and $\kappa$ transforms predictably under Drinfeld--Sokolov
reduction. The complementarity sums $c + c'$ are root-datum
invariants. These are not coincidences; they are the fingerprints
of the general theory made visible in specific examples. Each family
of chiral algebras reveals a distinct face of the modular Koszul
structure: the Feigin--Frenkel involution
$k \mapsto -k - 2h^\vee$ is Koszul duality for Kac--Moody
algebras; Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction is a Koszul functor;
the $\hat{A}$-genus in the Heisenberg genus expansion witnesses a
family index theorem; the Yangian forces a departure from
$E_\infty$-chiral to $E_1$-chiral Koszul duality.

The examples are organized along the axis from the
$\Einf$-commutative atom (Heisenberg) to the $\Eone$-associative
atom (Yangian). Kac--Moody algebras, Virasoro, and
$\mathcal{W}$-algebras are $\Einf$-chiral with increasingly
complex OPE pole structures. Lattice vertex algebras carry an
$\Eone$ sublattice structure visible through screening operators.
The Yangian is purely $\Eone$-chiral: its bar complex uses ordered
configuration spaces and Verdier duality acts by $R$-matrix
inversion, not type exchange.

What governs the genus expansion of the partition function

The answer is Theorem~D and the modular tower at levels~$4$ and~$5$

non-separating boundary divisors) produce the partition function
$Z_g(A_b)$, the modular characteristic $\kappa(A_b)$, and the
obstruction class
$\mathrm{obs}_g(A_b) \in H^*(\overline{\cM}_{g,n})$.

Theorem~D states $\mathrm{obs}_g(A_b) = \kappa(A_b)\,\lambda_g$ on
the uniform-weight scalar lane. The Borel-Riccati spectral analysis
of the shadow-tower coefficients $S_r$ closes a quadrichotomy
across the open archetypes
$\mathsf G/\mathsf L/\mathsf C/\mathsf M$ with shadow depth
$r_{\max} \in \{2,3,4,\infty\}$, while $\mathsf B$ at depth $5$
sits on its own Mukai-K3 row.

physics-bridge witnesses (Feynman diagrams, BV-BRST, Kontsevich

concrete computations.

\label{part:seven-faces}

have built the open Beilinson tower from level~$0$
\textup{(}factorisation dg-cat $\cC^{\op}$\textup{)} to level~$5$
\textup{(}modular trace + clutching\textup{)}, and have established
the proved core: Theorems~A, B, C, D, H. The reader will reasonably
ask three closing questions:

incarnations across the bar coalgebra, the line operators, the
Yangian $r$-matrix, the Gaudin Hamiltonian, and the Sklyanin
Poisson tensor are all forced to agree?} The seven-face dictionary
on the comparison locus

affirmative: the genus-zero binary collision residue
$r_\cA(z) = \mathrm{Res}^{\mathrm{coll}}_{0,2}(\Theta_\cA)$ is the
single object whose images under seven typed comparison morphisms
populate the seven faces.

\textit{(ii)} \emph{Does the open Beilinson tower admit a vertical
holographic equivalence to the CY-quadrant programme of Vol~III at
every level?} The cross-volume vertical equivalences at levels~$0$
\textup{(}factorisation dg-cat $\leftrightarrow$ 6d hCS\textup{),}
$2$ \textup{(}bar coalgebra $\leftrightarrow$
$\mathrm{Sp}^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\Sigma_{d-1}, C}$ specialisation\textup{),}
and $4$ \textup{(}line operators $\leftrightarrow$ Drinfeld double
$D(Y^+(X))$\textup{)} answer this in the affirmative on the witnessed
loci, with explicit hypothesis packages and named obstruction
classes.

single structural meta-theorem?} The Master Reconstruction Theorem
at the close of this part answers this in the affirmative: the
five-level Beilinson tower has reconstruction theorems with named
hypothesis packages at every step, KSDual is the
$\mathbb{Z}/2$-fixed sublocus where every step degenerates from
adjunction to strict equivalence, and Theorems~A--H are corollaries
restratified by Beilinson-tower level.

The collision residue is the geometric core of all three answers; the
master concordance closes the volume by recording the seven-face
dictionary, the cross-volume vertical equivalences, and the open
frontiers F1--F12 in a single trilingual index.
