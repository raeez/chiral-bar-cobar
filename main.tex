\begin{abstract}
\itshape

\noindent
We lift classical Koszul duality---the bar-cobar adjunction for
associative and Lie algebras---to chiral algebras on algebraic curves
via configuration space integrals.  Given a chiral algebra~$\cA$ on a
smooth curve~$X$, we construct a bar complex
$\barB_X(\cA)$ by wedging sections of~$\cA^{\boxtimes n}$ against the
logarithmic form $\eta = \bigwedge d\log(z_i - z_j)$ on the
Fulton--MacPherson compactification~$\overline{C}_n(X)$ and extracting
residues at collision divisors.  The Arnold relation forces $d^2 = 0$.
Verdier duality on~$\overline{C}_n(X)$
interchanges $\barB_X(\cA)$ and $\barB_X(\cA^!)$ for a second chiral
algebra~$\cA^!$, the chiral Koszul dual.  When $X$ is a point the
construction reduces to the bar complex of Loday--Vallette; for the
Heisenberg algebra on an elliptic curve it recovers the
Fourier--Mukai kernel on the Jacobian.

\medskip
\textsc{Theorem~A} (Geometric bar-cobar adjunction).
Verdier duality on FM compactifications mediates a factorization
bar-cobar adjunction; on the Koszul locus the unit and counit are
quasi-isomorphisms.

\textsc{Theorem~B} (Bar-cobar inversion).
On the Koszul locus $\Omega_X(\barB_X(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$,
with the spectral sequence collapsing at~$E_2$.  For affine
Kac--Moody this realizes the Feigin--Frenkel involution
$\widehat{\fg}_k \leftrightarrow \widehat{\fg}_{-k-2h^\vee}$
as Verdier duality on configuration spaces.

\textsc{Theorem~C} (Deformation-obstruction complementarity).
At genus $g \geq 1$ the fiberwise bar differential acquires curvature
$\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$
from prime-form monodromy.
Period integrals restore nilpotence, and Verdier duality splits the
center local system into complementary Lagrangians:
$R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathcal{Z}_\cA)
\simeq
\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \oplus \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)$.
A cyclic $L_\infty$-structure on the deformation complex
supports a shadow Postnikov tower
$\Theta_\cA^{\leq 2}, \Theta_\cA^{\leq 3}, \Theta_\cA^{\leq 4}, \ldots$
of finite-order Maurer--Cartan data, whose conjectural
all-arity limit is a universal element~$\Theta_\cA$.

\textsc{Theorem~D} (Modular characteristic).
A single scalar $\kappa(\cA)$ controls the genus tower; the generating
function of genus free energies recovers the $\hat{A}$-genus.  Under
Koszul duality $\kappa(\cA) + \kappa(\cA^!) = 0$; anomaly cancellation
in string theory is $\kappa_{\mathrm{tot}} = 0$.

\textsc{Theorem~H} (Chiral Hochschild cohomology).
$\ChirHoch^*(\mathcal{W}^k(\fg))$ is polynomial in $r$~generators
of degrees $m_i + 1$, where $m_1, \ldots, m_r$ are the exponents
of~$\fg$.  The Koszul duality functor acts on Hochschild cohomology
via the Connes periodicity operator.

\medskip
Two entry atoms encode the full theory: the Heisenberg
algebra ($\Einf$-chiral, commutative) and the Yangian ($\Eone$-chiral,
braided).  Explicit computations, occupying half this volume, cover
Heisenberg, free fermions, $bc$-$\beta\gamma$, affine Kac--Moody at
Feigin--Frenkel dual levels, Virasoro, principal $\mathcal{W}$-algebras,
lattice algebras, and Yangians with Mittag-Leffler stabilization of the
RTT tower.  All results are checked against a suite of six thousand tests.
\end{abstract}

\part{The Algebraic Engine}

\noindent
Place sections $a_1, \ldots, a_n$ of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ at
distinct points $z_1, \ldots, z_n$ on a smooth curve~$X$.  Wedge the
tensor $a_1(z_1) \otimes \cdots \otimes a_n(z_n)$ against the
logarithmic $n$-form $\eta = \bigwedge_{i<j} d\log(z_i - z_j)$ on
the Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\overline{C}_n(X)$, and
extract residues at each collision divisor.  The result is a
differential.  Arnold's three-term identity
\[
\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23}
\;+\; \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31}
\;+\; \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12}
\;=\; 0
\]
forces $d^2 = 0$.  This is the bar complex~$\barB_X(\cA)$: the
categorical logarithm of~$\cA$.

A logarithm has four properties.  Each is a theorem.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{(\Alph*)},leftmargin=2.2em]
\item \textbf{Existence.}
  The bar-cobar adjunction $(\barB, \Omega)$, intertwined with
  Verdier duality on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$.  The logarithm exists
  and is natural.
\item \textbf{Invertibility.}
  On the Koszul locus,
  $\Omega(\barB(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$: the cobar inverts
  the bar, with the spectral sequence collapsing at~$E_2$.  The
  logarithm has an exponential.
\item \textbf{Branch structure.}
  At genus $g \geq 1$, $Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!)$ sit as
  complementary Lagrangians inside
  $R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathcal{Z}(\cA))$.
  The logarithm has monodromy; its sheets are Lagrangian.
\item \textbf{Leading coefficient.}
  The modular characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$ is universal, additive,
  anti-symmetric; its generating function is the $\hat{A}$-genus.
  The leading Taylor coefficient of the logarithm is a topological
  index.
\end{enumerate}
\noindent
\textbf{(H) The coefficient ring.}
$\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is polynomial on the Koszul locus and dual to
$\ChirHoch^*(\cA^!)$.  The full Taylor series of the logarithm lives
in a polynomial ring.

\smallskip\noindent
But $\kappa(\cA)$ is only the \emph{linear} leading term.
The primary mathematical object of Part~I beyond the linear regime is
the \emph{shadow Postnikov tower}
$\kappa \to \Delta \to \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{Q} \to \cdots$,
a hierarchy of finite-order invariants---quadratic, cubic, quartic,
and beyond---each proved constructively with obstruction classes
$o_{r+1}(\cA)$ in the cyclic deformation complex.
The conjectural all-arity limit of this tower is a universal
class~$\Theta_\cA$;
the proved content is the finite-order engine.
The shadow tower is developed in the final chapter of Part~I and runs,
as a leitmotif, through every subsequent family computation.

\part{The Standard Landscape}

\noindent
In Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}, we computed the bar complex, Koszul
dual, curvature tower, and $\hat{A}$-genus for the Heisenberg algebra.
Part~II applies the same programme to every major family of chiral
algebras.  These chapters should be read as complete portraits rather
than as subordinate examples: each family reveals a structural feature
that is invisible in the Heisenberg frame alone.
The constructions of Part~I---the bar complex, the genus tower, the Koszul
pair structure---are defined for any augmented chiral algebra satisfying mild finiteness
conditions.  The question driving Part~II is: what do these constructions
\emph{produce} when applied to the algebras that arise in practice?

The answer is unexpectedly structured.  The bar cohomology dimensions
of apparently unrelated algebras---the Kac--Moody algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$, the Virasoro algebra, the
$\beta\gamma$-system---share a common discriminant
$\Delta(x) = (1-3x)(1+x)$.  The genus expansion of every Koszul
algebra is controlled by a single obstruction invariant~$\kappa$,
and $\kappa$ transforms predictably under Drinfeld--Sokolov
reduction.  The complementarity sums $c + c'$ are root-datum
invariants.  These are not coincidences; they are the fingerprints
of the general theory made visible in specific examples.  Each family
of chiral algebras reveals a distinct face of the modular Koszul
structure: the Feigin--Frenkel involution
$k \mapsto -k - 2h^\vee$ is Koszul duality for Kac--Moody
algebras; Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction is a Koszul functor;
the $\hat{A}$-genus in the Heisenberg genus expansion witnesses a
family index theorem; the Yangian forces a departure from
$E_\infty$-chiral to $E_1$-chiral Koszul duality.

The examples are organized along the axis from the
$\Einf$-commutative atom (Heisenberg) to the $\Eone$-associative
atom (Yangian).  Kac--Moody algebras, Virasoro, and
$\mathcal{W}$-algebras are $\Einf$-chiral with increasingly
complex OPE pole structures.  Lattice vertex algebras carry an
$\Eone$ sublattice structure visible through screening operators.
The Yangian is purely $\Eone$-chiral: its bar complex uses ordered
configuration spaces and Verdier duality acts by $R$-matrix
inversion, not type exchange.

$\bar\partial$-operator, and the Kontsevich configuration-space
integrand.  The Arnold relation $\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23}
+ \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31} + \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12} = 0$ is

incarnations in every field that uses propagators on configuration

perturbation series; its graph expansion produces Kontsevich weights
and Vassiliev invariants; at critical level $k = -h^\vee$ it
recovers the algebra of opers and enters the geometric Langlands
programme.  The $\En$ hierarchy
lifts the entire mechanism from curves to higher-dimensional
manifolds.

One kernel.  One relation.  Many incarnations.
