\begin{abstract}
\itshape

\noindent
We lift classical Koszul duality---the bar-cobar adjunction for
associative and Lie algebras---to chiral algebras on algebraic curves
via configuration space integrals.  Given a chiral algebra~$\cA$ on a
smooth curve~$X$, we construct a bar complex
$\barB_X(\cA)$ by wedging sections of~$\cA^{\boxtimes n}$ against the
logarithmic form $\eta = \bigwedge d\log(z_i - z_j)$ on the
Fulton--MacPherson compactification~$\overline{C}_n(X)$ and extracting
residues at collision divisors.  The Arnold relation forces $d^2 = 0$.
Verdier duality on~$\overline{C}_n(X)$
interchanges $\barB_X(\cA)$ and $\barB_X(\cA^!)$ for a second chiral
algebra~$\cA^!$, the chiral Koszul dual.  When $X$ is a point the
construction reduces to the bar complex of Loday--Vallette; for the
Heisenberg algebra on an elliptic curve it recovers the
Fourier--Mukai kernel on the Jacobian.

\medskip
\noindent\textsc{The organizing principle.}
Three inputs---Malikov--Schechtman homotopy chiral algebras
(the $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-endomorphism operad of~$A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty$),
Mok's logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson compactifications
$\operatorname{FM}_n(X\mathbin{|}D)$ (the chain cooperad
$\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}}$), and Robert-Nicoud--Wierstra
convolution functoriality (the one-slot $\infty$-morphism convention)---assemble
into a single curvature machine.  The modular convolution algebra
$\gAmod :=
\Convinf\!\bigl(\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}},\;
\operatorname{End}_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty)\bigr)$
is a complete filtered cyclic $L_\infty$-algebra; its strict chart
$\Convstr(\cC,\cP)$ is the dg~Lie model.
For each stable graph~$\Gamma \in \mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}$
with boundary stratum
$D^{\log}_\Gamma \subset \operatorname{FM}^{\log}_n(X\mathbin{|}D)$
and normalisation
$\widetilde{D}^{\log}_\Gamma \to
\prod_{v} \operatorname{FM}_{I_v}(Y_v\mathbin{|}D_v)$,
the graphwise cocomposition
\[
\Delta^{\log}_\Gamma
\;:=\;
(\nu_\Gamma)_* \circ \operatorname{Res}_{D^{\log}_\Gamma}
\]
makes the cooperad structure explicit, and the Taylor coefficients
of the modular $L_\infty$-brackets are the graph sums
\[
\ell_k^{(g)}
\;=\;
\sum_{\substack{
\Gamma\in \mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}},\;
|V(\Gamma)|=k\\
b_1(\Gamma)+\sum_v g(v)=g}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}\,
\ell_\Gamma,
\qquad
\ell_\Gamma(f_1,\ldots,f_k)
= \mu_\Gamma \circ
(\alpha^{\mathrm{mod}}_\Gamma \otimes f_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes f_k)
\circ \Delta^{\log}_\Gamma.
\]
The bar complex~$\barB_X(\cA)$ is an algebra over the Feynman
transform of the commutative modular operad;
$\partial^2 = 0$ at all genera is a formal consequence.
The universal Maurer--Cartan element
\[
\Theta_\cA
\;:=\; D_\cA - d_0
\;=\;
\sum_{\Gamma \in \mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\;W_\Gamma^{\log\mathrm{FM}}
\otimes
\Phi_\Gamma^\cA
\;\in\; \mathrm{MC}(\gAmod)
\]
satisfies~$D_\cA\,\Theta_\cA + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta_\cA,\Theta_\cA] = 0$
because~$D_\cA^{\,2} = 0$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}).
The Maurer--Cartan moduli space of~$\gAmod$ is identified with the
space of modular twisting morphisms:
$\operatorname{MC}_\bullet(\Definfmod_{\log}(\cA))
\simeq
\operatorname{Tw}_{\alpha^{\mathrm{mod}}}
(\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}},\,
\operatorname{End}_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty))$,
and~$\Theta_\cA$ is the universal modular twisting morphism
(Corollary~\ref{cor:vol1-theta-log-fm-twisting-data}).

The total differential of the modular bar functor decomposes as
$D^{\log}_{\mathrm{mod}} =
d_{\check{C}} + d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty} + d_{\mathrm{coll}}
+ d_{\mathrm{sew}} + d_{\mathrm{pf}} + \hbar\,\Delta$;
the three positive-genus pieces are residue correspondences
along boundary strata of the log-FM compactification:
$d_{\mathrm{sew}}$ sums residue-pushforward over separating edges,
$d_{\mathrm{pf}} = \sum_\rho \epsilon_\rho\,
(\kappa_\rho)_*\,\mathrm{pr}_\rho^{\,*}\otimes \mu_\rho$
over rigid planted-forest types---literally Mok's degeneration
formula transported into the convolution algebra---and
$\hbar\Delta$ traces the cyclic pairing over non-separating edges.
The identity~$D^2 = 0$ is the cancellation of codimension-two faces
in the normal-crossings boundary.

Mok's stratification refines~$\Theta_\cA$ to a tridegree
$(g,n,d) = (\text{loop genus},\;\text{arity},\;
\text{log boundary depth})$.
The internal-edge weight filtration
$\Theta_\cA = \sum_{w\geq 0} \Theta_\cA^{[w]}$
stratifies the MC equation by weight:
at weight~$1$, the genus-zero component
$\Theta_\cA^{[1]}|_{(0,4)}
= [\delta_s]\otimes\Phi_s + [\delta_t]\otimes\Phi_t
+ [\delta_u]\otimes\Phi_u$ is the
Malikov--Schechtman Jacobiator homotopy~$F_3$ on the three boundary
classes of~$\overline{\cM}_{0,4}\cong\mathbb{P}^1$,
while~$\Theta_\cA^{[1]}|_{(1,1)} = [\delta_{\mathrm{irr}}]\otimes\Delta$
is the BV loop operator from~$\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$.
Genuine logarithmic modularity begins at weight~$2$, where
rigid planted-forest strata of~$\operatorname{FM}_n(X\mathbin{|}D)$
contribute chain-level corrections with no counterpart in the
classical moduli compactification.

The genus filtration $F^{\leq g}\gAmod$ induces a spectral sequence
$E_1^{p,q} = H^{p+q}(\mathrm{gr}^p_g\,\gAmod) \Rightarrow
H^{p+q}(\gAmod)$ whose differentials are successive genus
obstruction maps~$\mathrm{Ob}_g$; this is distinct from
the PBW spectral sequence and detects modular obstructions
invisible at genus~$0$.
The arity filtration yields
finite-order projections
$\Theta_\cA^{\leq 2}, \Theta_\cA^{\leq 3},
\Theta_\cA^{\leq 4}, \ldots$---the
modular characteristic~$\kappa$ at arity~$2$, the cubic shadow
at arity~$3$, the quartic resonance class at arity~$4$---with
all-arity convergence~$\Theta_\cA =
\varprojlim \Theta_\cA^{\leq r}$
proved unconditionally (Theorem~\ref{thm:recursive-existence}).
The $L_\infty$-twisted modular tangent complex
$T^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\Theta_\cA}
:= (\gAmod,\; d + [\Theta_\cA, -])$
extracts successive Chern--Weil invariants of the universal curvature:
$\kappa(\cA) = \operatorname{tr}(\Theta_\cA)_{2,0}$,\;
$\Delta_\cA(t) = \operatorname{sdet}(1 - t\,H_{d_{\Theta}})$,\;
$\mathfrak{R}^{\mathrm{mod}}_{4,g,n}(\cA) =
\operatorname{pr}_{4,g,n}(\Theta_\cA)$.

\textsc{Theorem~A} (Geometric bar-cobar adjunction).
The bar functor~$\barB_X$ sends a chiral algebra~$\cA$ to a
conilpotent factorization coalgebra on~$\Ran(X)$; the cobar
functor~$\Omega_X$ sends a conilpotent factorization coalgebra~$\cC$
to the free chiral algebra on the desuspended
cogenerators~$s\,\overline{\cC}$ with differential from the coproduct.
The counit
\[
\varepsilon \colon
\Omega_X\bigl(\barB_X(\cA)\bigr) \longrightarrow \cA
\]
contracts bar elements against the universal OPE kernel;
the unit
$\eta \colon \cC \to \barB_X(\Omega_X(\cC))$
embeds cogenerators into bar tensors.
The adjunction $\barB_X \dashv \Omega_X$ is a Quillen equivalence
on the Koszul locus
(Theorem~\ref{thm:quillen-equivalence-chiral}).

\textsc{Theorem~B} (Bar-cobar inversion).
On the Koszul locus the counit~$\varepsilon$ is a quasi-isomorphism,
with the bar-to-cobar spectral sequence collapsing at~$E_2$.
For affine Kac--Moody at level~$k$:
\[
\Omega_X\bigl(\barB_X(\widehat{\fg}_k)\bigr)
\;\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}\;
\widehat{\fg}_k,
\qquad
\barB_X(\widehat{\fg}_k)^{\mathbb{D}}
\;\simeq\;
\barB_X(\widehat{\fg}_{-k-2h^\vee}),
\]
realizing the Feigin--Frenkel involution as Verdier duality
on configuration spaces.

\textsc{Theorem~B${}'$} (Completed bar-cobar).
The strong filtration axiom
$\mu_r(F^{i_1}, \ldots, F^{i_r}) \subset F^{i_1 + \cdots + i_r}$
yields an automatic arity cutoff; the completion closure
$\CompCl(\cF_{\mathrm{ft}})$ carries a quasi-inverse bar-cobar
homotopy equivalence stable under Maurer--Cartan twisting
(Theorem~\ref{thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong}).
In type~$A$, thick generation of the quantum group category
$\cO_q(\mathfrak{sl}_N)$ follows from
\[
V_n \otimes L^-
\;\simeq\;
\bigoplus_{j=0}^{n} L^-(n - 2j)
\qquad
\text{(prefundamental Clebsch--Gordan, all $n$)}
\]
via chromatic filtration and Efimov's categorical completion
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mc3-type-a-resolution}).

\textsc{Theorem~C} (Complementarity).
At genus~$g \geq 1$ the fiberwise bar differential acquires curvature
$\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$
from prime-form monodromy.
Period integrals restore nilpotence; Verdier duality splits the
center local system into complementary Lagrangians:
\[
R\Gamma\bigl(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g,\, \mathcal{Z}_\cA\bigr)
\;\simeq\;
\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \;\oplus\; \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!).
\]

\textsc{Theorem~C${}'$} (Shadow tower).
The all-arity master equation
$\nabla_H(\mathrm{Sh}_r) + o^{(r)} = 0$
on~$\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$ yields a recursive tower of
finite-order Maurer--Cartan data.
When $H^1(F^3 \gAmod / F^4 \gAmod,\, d_2) = 0$ the cubic term is
gauge-trivial (Theorem~\ref{thm:cubic-gauge-triviality})
and the quartic class

is canonical.

\part{The Algebraic Engine}

\noindent
Place sections $a_1, \ldots, a_n$ of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ at
distinct points $z_1, \ldots, z_n$ on a smooth curve~$X$.  Wedge the
tensor $a_1(z_1) \otimes \cdots \otimes a_n(z_n)$ against the
logarithmic $n$-form $\eta = \bigwedge_{i<j} d\log(z_i - z_j)$ on
the Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\overline{C}_n(X)$, and
extract residues at each collision divisor.  The result is a
differential.  Arnold's three-term identity
\[
\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23}
\;+\; \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31}
\;+\; \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12}
\;=\; 0
\]
forces $d^2 = 0$.  This is the bar complex~$\barB_X(\cA)$: the
categorical logarithm of~$\cA$.

A logarithm has four properties.  Each is a theorem.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{(\Alph*)},leftmargin=2.2em]
\item \textbf{Existence.}
  The bar-cobar adjunction $(\barB, \Omega)$, intertwined with
  Verdier duality on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$.  The logarithm exists
  and is natural.
\item \textbf{Invertibility.}
  On the Koszul locus,
  $\Omega(\barB(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$: the cobar inverts
  the bar, with the spectral sequence collapsing at~$E_2$.  The
  logarithm has an exponential.
\item \textbf{Branch structure.}
  At genus $g \geq 1$, $Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!)$ sit as
  complementary Lagrangians inside
  $R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathcal{Z}(\cA))$.
  The logarithm has monodromy; its sheets are Lagrangian.
\item \textbf{Leading coefficient.}
  The modular characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$ is universal, additive,
  anti-symmetric; its generating function is the $\hat{A}$-genus.
  The leading Taylor coefficient of the logarithm is a topological
  index.
\end{enumerate}
\noindent
\textbf{(H) The coefficient ring.}
$\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is polynomial on the Koszul locus and dual to
$\ChirHoch^*(\cA^!)$.  The full Taylor series of the logarithm lives
in a polynomial ring.

\smallskip\noindent
But $\kappa(\cA)$ is only the \emph{linear} leading term.
The primary mathematical object of Part~I beyond the linear regime is
the \emph{shadow Postnikov tower}
$\kappa \to \Delta \to \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{Q} \to \cdots$,
a hierarchy of finite-order invariants---quadratic, cubic, quartic,
and beyond---each proved constructively with obstruction classes
$o_{r+1}(\cA)$ in the cyclic deformation complex.
The all-arity limit of this tower is the universal
class~$\Theta_\cA$, proved by the bar-intrinsic construction
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic});
the constructive content is the finite-order engine.
The shadow tower is developed in the final chapter of Part~I and runs,
as a leitmotif, through every subsequent family computation.

\part{The Standard Landscape}

\noindent
In Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}, we computed the bar complex, Koszul
dual, curvature tower, and $\hat{A}$-genus for the Heisenberg algebra.
Part~II applies the same programme to every major family of chiral
algebras.  These chapters should be read as complete portraits rather
than as subordinate examples: each family reveals a structural feature
that is invisible in the Heisenberg frame alone.
The constructions of Part~I---the bar complex, the genus tower, the Koszul
pair structure---are defined for any augmented chiral algebra satisfying mild finiteness
conditions.  The question driving Part~II is: what do these constructions
\emph{produce} when applied to the algebras that arise in practice?

The answer is unexpectedly structured.  The bar cohomology dimensions
of apparently unrelated algebras---the Kac--Moody algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$, the Virasoro algebra, the
$\beta\gamma$-system---share a common discriminant
$\Delta(x) = (1-3x)(1+x)$.  The genus expansion of every Koszul
algebra is controlled by a single obstruction invariant~$\kappa$,
and $\kappa$ transforms predictably under Drinfeld--Sokolov
reduction.  The complementarity sums $c + c'$ are root-datum
invariants.  These are not coincidences; they are the fingerprints
of the general theory made visible in specific examples.  Each family
of chiral algebras reveals a distinct face of the modular Koszul
structure: the Feigin--Frenkel involution
$k \mapsto -k - 2h^\vee$ is Koszul duality for Kac--Moody
algebras; Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction is a Koszul functor;
the $\hat{A}$-genus in the Heisenberg genus expansion witnesses a
family index theorem; the Yangian forces a departure from
$E_\infty$-chiral to $E_1$-chiral Koszul duality.

The examples are organized along the axis from the
$\Einf$-commutative atom (Heisenberg) to the $\Eone$-associative
atom (Yangian).  Kac--Moody algebras, Virasoro, and
$\mathcal{W}$-algebras are $\Einf$-chiral with increasingly
complex OPE pole structures.  Lattice vertex algebras carry an
$\Eone$ sublattice structure visible through screening operators.
The Yangian is purely $\Eone$-chiral: its bar complex uses ordered
configuration spaces and Verdier duality acts by $R$-matrix
inversion, not type exchange.

programme.  The $\En$ hierarchy
