\begin{abstract}
\itshape

\noindent
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on a smooth algebraic curve~$X$.  Place
sections $a_1, \ldots, a_n \in \cA$ at distinct points
$z_1, \ldots, z_n \in X$, wedge the tensor
$a_1(z_1) \otimes \cdots \otimes a_n(z_n)$ against the logarithmic
$n$-form
$\eta = \bigwedge_{i < j} d\log(z_i - z_j)$
on the Fulton--MacPherson compactification~$\overline{C}_n(X)$, and
extract residues at each collision divisor~$D_{ij}$ where $z_i = z_j$.
The result is a differential~$d$ on
$\barB_X(\cA) := \bigoplus_{n \ge 0}
\Gamma(\overline{C}_n(X),\, \cA^{\boxtimes n} \otimes
\Omega^*_{\log})$.
The Arnold relation
$\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23} + \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31}
+ \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12} = 0$,
which holds because
$(z_3{-}z_1)+(z_1{-}z_2)+(z_2{-}z_3)=0$,
forces $d^2 = 0$: three boundary strata meet at every triple-collision
locus of $\overline{C}_n(X)$, and their residue contributions cancel
cyclically.

\medskip
When $X = \operatorname{Spec} k$ is a point, configuration spaces
collapse and the construction reduces to the bar complex
$T^c(s^{-1}\bar{\cA})$ of Loday--Vallette.  When $\cA$ is the
Heisenberg algebra $\mathcal{H}_1$ on an elliptic curve~$E_\tau$,
the transform recovers the Poincar\'e line bundle on
$\operatorname{Jac}(E_\tau) \times
\widehat{\operatorname{Jac}}(E_\tau)$: the bar complex specializes to the Fourier--Mukai kernel on the Jacobian.  The
classical Fourier transform on~$\mathbb{R}$, the Fourier--Mukai
transform on an abelian variety, and the chiral bar transform on a
curve are three incarnations of the same logarithmic integral
$\int \exp(xy)\,dy$,
$\int \mathcal{P}\,d\mu$,
$\int \cA^{\boxtimes n} \wedge \eta$,
at dimension $0$, $1$ (abelian), and $1$ (non-abelian).

\medskip
The Verdier dualizing functor
$\mathbb{D}_{\Ran}\colon
\mathcal{D}^b(\overline{C}_n(X)) \to
\mathcal{D}^b(\overline{C}_n(X))$
exchanges the bar complex of $\cA$ with the bar complex of a second
chiral algebra~$\cA^!$:
\[
\mathbb{D}_{\Ran}\,\barB_X(\cA)
\;\simeq\; \barB_X(\cA^!).
\]
Both sides are factorization coalgebras on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$;
$\cA^!$ is the \emph{chiral Koszul dual}.
Concretely, $\mathbb{D}_{\Ran}$ pairs the logarithmic $n$-form
$\eta = \bigwedge d\log(z_i - z_j)$ against the distributional
$n$-current $\delta_\Delta$ supported on the collision diagonal:
$\langle \eta,\, \delta_\Delta \rangle = 1$.
For affine Kac--Moody, $\widehat{\fg}_k^! =
\mathrm{CE}^{\mathrm{ch}}(\widehat{\fg}_{-k-2h^\vee})$.  On
the Koszul locus --- where $\barB_X(\cA)$ has cohomology concentrated
in a single degree --- the cobar inverts the bar:
\[
\Omega_X(\barB_X(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\;\sim\;} \cA,
\]
with the bar-cobar spectral sequence collapsing at~$E_2$.  For
$\widehat{\fg}_k$ this produces
$\widehat{\fg}_{-k-2h^\vee}$: the Feigin--Frenkel involution is the
shadow of Verdier duality on configuration spaces.  At critical level
$k = -h^\vee$, the bar complex is uncurved, and
$H^0(\barB(\widehat{\fg}_{-h^\vee}))$ recovers the Feigin--Frenkel
center --- the algebra of functions on the space of
$\fg^\vee$-opers on the formal disc.  The full derived identification
$H^*(\barB(\widehat{\fg}_{-h^\vee})) \cong \Omega^*(\Op_{\fg^\vee})$
gives a vertex-algebraic construction of a central object in the
geometric Langlands programme.

\medskip
At genus $g \geq 1$ the logarithmic propagator $d\log(z_i - z_j)$ is
replaced by $d\log E(z_i, z_j)$, where $E$ is the prime form.  The
prime form has monodromy around $B$-cycles of~$\Sigma_g$, and this
monodromy enters the bar differential as a curvature: the fiberwise
differential satisfies
\[
\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g
\]
where $\kappa(\cA) \in \mathbb{C}$ is determined by the leading OPE
singularity and $\omega_g \in H^{2g}(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g)$ is a
tautological class on moduli.  Genus-$0$ nilpotence $d^2 = 0$ fails by
an amount proportional to one number.  Period integrals along $A$-cycles
of the fiber curves supply the correction terms that restore strict
nilpotence:
$\Dg{g}^{\,2} = 0$.

The scalar $\kappa(\cA)$ controls the entire genus tower.
For affine Kac--Moody:
$\kappa(\widehat{\fg}_k) = \dim(\fg)(k + h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$.
The genus free energies assemble as
\[
\sum_{g=1}^{\infty} F_g(\cA)\, x^{2g}
\;=\; \kappa(\cA) \cdot
\biggl(\frac{x/2}{\sin(x/2)} - 1\biggr),
\]
recovering the $\hat{A}$-genus: it appears because it is the
multiplicative genus of the additive formal group law, and the genus
expansion is additive in~$\kappa$.  Under Koszul duality,
$\kappa(\cA) + \kappa(\cA^!) = 0$
for Kac--Moody, and more generally the complementarity sum
$\kappa + \kappa'$ is a root-datum invariant.  Anomaly cancellation in
string theory is $\kappa_{\mathrm{tot}} = 0$: the condition under
which the curvature vanishes and the bar complex at every genus is an
honest cochain complex.

\medskip
The relative bar complex over the universal curve
$\pi\colon \overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,1} \to \overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$
has fiber cohomology concentrated in the center~$Z(\cA)$: the
Koszul property forces all higher fiber cohomology to vanish, and
the degree-zero identification $H^0 = Z(\cA)$ holds because a
bar-degree-$0$ cocycle is an element whose OPE with every field is
regular --- the defining property of a central element.  The center
forms a local system~$\mathcal{Z}_\cA$ on~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$.
Verdier self-duality of the center equips
$R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathcal{Z}_\cA)$ with a
$(-1)$-shifted symplectic structure in the sense of
Pantev--To\"en--Vaqui\'e--Vezzosi, and Verdier duality
$\cA \leftrightarrow \cA^!$ splits the ambient complex into
complementary Lagrangian halves:
\[
R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathcal{Z}_\cA)
\;\simeq\;
\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \;\oplus\; \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!),
\]
the deformation complex of~$\cA$ and the obstruction complex of its
dual.  The Lagrangian condition
$\langle \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA),\, \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!) \rangle = 0$
means that every first-order deformation of $\cA$ at genus~$g$ is
annihilated by the obstruction map of~$\cA^!$.

Clutching along the boundary divisors of
$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ --- the stable-graph strata where the
curve degenerates --- organizes the genus tower into a single modular
deformation.  A cyclic $L_\infty$-algebra structure on the deformation
complex~$\Defcyc(\cA)$, constructed from the Fay trisecant identity
on FM compactifications, supports a universal Maurer--Cartan element
\[
\Theta_\cA \;\in\;
\operatorname{MC}\bigl(
\Defcyc(\cA) \,\widehat{\otimes}\,
R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,\bullet}, \mathbb{Q})\bigr)
\]
whose scalar trace recovers $\kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g$, whose
clutching compatibility encodes the sewing axiom, and whose Verdier
duality gives $\Theta_\cA \mapsto \Theta_{\cA^!}$.

\medskip
The chiral Hochschild cohomology
$\ChirHoch^*(\mathcal{W}^k(\fg))$ of a principal $\mathcal{W}$-algebra
at generic level is polynomial:
$\mathbb{C}[\Theta_1, \ldots, \Theta_r]$
with $\deg \Theta_i = m_i + 1$ where $m_1, \ldots, m_r$ are the
exponents of~$\fg$.  At critical level~$k = -h^\vee$, the exterior
generators of Chevalley--Eilenberg cohomology reappear:
$\Lambda^*(P_1, \ldots, P_r) \otimes \mathbb{C}[\Theta_1, \ldots,
\Theta_r]$.  The Koszul duality functor acts on Hochschild cohomology
via the Connes periodicity operator~$S$.

\medskip
The construction has two genuinely different specializations because
the logarithmic propagator on a curve interacts with two distinct
algebraic structures.

On the commutative ($\Einf$-chiral) side, the Heisenberg
algebra~$\mathcal{H}_k$ is the first nontrivial example.  The current
$J(z)$ with OPE $J(z)J(w) = k/(z{-}w)^2$ gives a bar element
$J \otimes J \otimes \eta_{12} \in \barB^1$; the bar differential
produces $\Res_{\epsilon=0}[\epsilon^{-3}\,d\epsilon] = 0$ (triple
pole, no residue).  The level~$k$ is invisible at genus~$0$ and appears
at genus~$1$ as curvature $\kappa = k$.  Every feature of the general
theory --- Arnold nilpotence, Verdier duality, complementarity,
the $\hat{A}$-genus, the Hodge bundle correction --- is already
present and computable.

On the braided ($\Eone$-chiral) side, the Yangian $Y(\fg)$ is the
first nontrivial example.  The RTT presentation
$R_{12}(u{-}v)\,T_1(u)\,T_2(v) = T_2(v)\,T_1(u)\,R_{12}(u{-}v)$
replaces commutativity with the Yang--Baxter equation, and the bar
differential acts on ordered configuration spaces.  Koszul duality
inverts the $R$-matrix: $Y(\fg)^! \cong Y_{R^{-1}}(\fg)$.  The
derived Drinfeld--Kohno theorem --- that $\Eone$-factorization
categories of Yangians and quantum groups are equivalent on the
evaluation locus --- is proved for the evaluation-generated core at
all simple Lie types.  For lattice vertex algebras $V_\Lambda$,
sectorwise finiteness of the $\Lambda$-graded bar complex bypasses
thick generation entirely, yielding unconditional factorization
equivalences for all even positive-definite lattices.

\medskip
Explicit computations, occupying half this volume, cover the
Heisenberg algebra (the $\Einf$ atom), free fermions and the
$bc$-$\beta\gamma$ Koszul pair (Com-Lie duality incarnate), affine
Kac--Moody at Feigin--Frenkel dual levels with the oper space emerging
at critical level, the Virasoro algebra with its
$c \leftrightarrow 26 - c$ complementarity, principal
$\mathcal{W}$-algebras through Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction with
complete stage-$4$ OPE structure constants for~$W_4$, ADE lattice
algebras with their quantum deformations, and Yangians with
Mittag-Leffler stabilization of the RTT bar-cohomology tower.
Bar cohomology dimensions, genus free energies through $g = 10$,
Koszul dual Hilbert series, and modular characteristics are computed
for every family and checked against a suite of six thousand tests.
\end{abstract}

\part{Core Theory}

\noindent
In Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}, we saw the bar complex,
Koszul duality, genus-$1$ curvature, and the $\hat{A}$-genus tower
for the Heisenberg algebra.

Part~I is the proved modular Koszul core:
it establishes these results in full generality while marking the exact
boundary beyond which the full modular homotopy theory remains
programmatic.

Its governing claim is that on a curve the bar-cobar transform is
already modular: genus enters through the same configuration-space
logarithm, Verdier duality, and clutching formalism that define
genus-$0$ duality, so the genus tower is internal to the transform
rather than an external correction.

The argument proceeds through four stages: classical Koszul duality is
the point-level shadow; configuration spaces provide the geometric
kernel; bar-cobar duality and Verdier intertwining give the genus-$0$
equivalence; higher genus internalizes the same construction through
Hodge-theoretic curvature and modular-operadic clutching.  The proved
spine consists of Theorem~A (bar-cobar duality intertwined with
Verdier duality on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$), Theorem~B (higher-genus
inversion on the Koszul locus, with coderived persistence off it),
Theorem~C (Lagrangian complementarity of deformation and obstruction),
Theorem~D$_{\mathrm{scal}}$ and Theorem~D$_\Delta$ (the scalar and
spectral characteristic layers), the higher-genus PBW entry theorem
for affine Kac--Moody, Virasoro, and principal finite-type
$\mathcal{W}$-algebras, and the universal Maurer--Cartan class
$\Theta_{\cA}$ constructed by Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-full-resolution}.

\part{Complete Portraits}

\noindent
In Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}, we computed the bar complex, Koszul
dual, curvature tower, and $\hat{A}$-genus for the Heisenberg algebra.
Part~II applies the same programme to every major family of chiral
algebras.  These chapters should be read as complete portraits rather
than as subordinate examples: each family reveals a structural feature
that is invisible in the Heisenberg frame alone.
The constructions of Part~I---the bar complex, the genus tower, the Koszul
pair structure---are defined for any augmented chiral algebra satisfying mild finiteness
conditions.  The question driving Part~II is: what do these constructions
\emph{produce} when applied to the algebras that arise in practice?

The answer is unexpectedly structured.  The bar cohomology dimensions
of apparently unrelated algebras---the Kac--Moody algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$, the Virasoro algebra, the
$\beta\gamma$-system---share a common discriminant
$\Delta(x) = (1-3x)(1+x)$.  The genus expansion of every Koszul
algebra is controlled by a single obstruction invariant~$\kappa$,
and $\kappa$ transforms predictably under Drinfeld--Sokolov
reduction.  The complementarity sums $c + c'$ are root-datum
invariants.  These are not coincidences; they are the fingerprints
of the general theory made visible in specific examples.  Each family
of chiral algebras reveals a distinct face of the modular Koszul
structure: the Feigin--Frenkel involution
$k \mapsto -k - 2h^\vee$ is Koszul duality for Kac--Moody
algebras; Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction is a Koszul functor;
the $\hat{A}$-genus in the Heisenberg genus expansion witnesses a
family index theorem; the Yangian forces a departure from
$E_\infty$-chiral to $E_1$-chiral Koszul duality.

The examples are organized along the axis from the
$\Einf$-commutative atom (Heisenberg) to the $\Eone$-associative
atom (Yangian).  Kac--Moody algebras, Virasoro, and
$\mathcal{W}$-algebras are $\Einf$-chiral with increasingly
complex OPE pole structures.  Lattice vertex algebras carry an
$\Eone$ sublattice structure visible through screening operators.
The Yangian is purely $\Eone$-chiral: its bar complex uses ordered
configuration spaces and Verdier duality acts by $R$-matrix
inversion, not type exchange.

\part{Synthesis and Programmes}

\noindent
The Heisenberg frame chapter (Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}) demonstrated
that a single chiral algebra encodes bar cohomology, Koszul duality, genus
corrections, and an index-theoretic $\hat{A}$-genus simultaneously.

The four theorems of Part~I and the computations of Part~II establish
chiral Koszul duality as a self-contained mathematical framework.  But the
framework is not isolated: its constructions appear, in different guises,
throughout modern mathematics and mathematical physics.  The genus-$0$
bar/BRST comparison is proved, and the all-genera BV/BRST bridge is
formulated as a downstream conjectural comparison in
Chapter~\ref{ch:bv-brst}.  The Kontsevich integral of knot theory is
treated in \S\ref{subsec:vassiliev} as a topological shadow whose
comparison with the holomorphic propagator remains conjectural.  The
Costello--Li construction of chiral algebras from four-dimensional
gauge theories provides the factorization-algebra target against which
Chapter~\ref{ch:ht-boundary} formulates boundary comparisons.  Part~III
is the synthesis layer of the book: it traces these theorem-level and
programme-level bridges and states the larger subject they are
converging toward.

The chapters here are more concise than those of Parts~I--II,
indicating directions of active investigation rather than definitive
treatments.  Part~III is synthesis rather than a loose
appendix of applications.  Several results are labeled
\emph{Conjectured} with scope
remarks identifying the additional input---typically from gauge theory or
string theory---needed for a complete proof.  The final chapter
(\S\ref{sec:modular-koszul-programme}) articulates the programme toward which
the entire monograph points: modular homotopy theory for factorization
algebras on curves.
