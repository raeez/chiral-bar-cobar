\begin{abstract}
\itshape

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Two-dimensional conformally invariant quantum field theory} on Riemann surfaces admits operator product expansions with structure constants emerging as residues of meromorphic forms.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{The Prism Principle.} This fact leads to configuration spaces acting as diffracting prisms decomposing chiral algebras across their operadic spectrum. Logarithmic differential forms $d\log(z_i - z_j)$ on the Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\mathrm{Conf}_n[X]$ separate global algebraic structure into local operator product channels at collision divisors $D_{ij} \subset \partial\mathrm{Conf}_n[X]$. Residue maps $\mathrm{Res}_{D_{ij}}$ extract structure constants $C_{ij}^k$ from chiral products, while the Arnold--Orlik--Solomon relations among logarithmic forms encode associativity through $d^2 = 0$. This geometric spectroscopy transforms abstract chiral algebra operations into explicit computations on stratified spaces, providing both conceptual clarity and computational power.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Main Results.}
We construct a geometric realization of the bar construction $\bar{B}_{\text{geom}}: \mathsf{ChirAlg}_X \to \mathsf{dgCoalg}_X$ for chiral algebras on an algebraic curve $X$. This construction extends the genus-zero framework of Beilinson--Drinfeld to incorporate: (i) one-loop quantum corrections via elliptic chiral homology on the formal torus $\hat{E}_\tau$, (ii) higher-genus contributions through the universal chiral homology over the moduli stack $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,n}$, and (iii) quantum deformation parameters $t_k \in H^1(\Sigma_g, \mathbb{C})$ (period integrals over $A$-cycles) controlling genus-$g$ amplitudes. The bar construction is realized through residue calculus on the Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\mathrm{Conf}_n[X]$ of configuration spaces, with the differential $d_{\text{geom}} = \sum_{D \in \partial \mathrm{Conf}_n[X]} (-1)^{|D|} \mathrm{Res}_D$ summing residues over boundary divisors. The nilpotence $d_{\text{geom}}^2 = 0$ follows from the Arnold--Orlik--Solomon relations in $H^*(\mathrm{Conf}_n[X])$, providing a geometric incarnation of the associativity of chiral operations.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Quantum Corrections and Higher Genus.} At genus zero, the construction recovers classical bar-cobar duality. At genus $g \geq 1$, quantum corrections enter through period integrals of logarithmic forms over cycles of the curve~$\Sigma_g$. These corrections encode central extensions, anomalies, and the full tower of deformations---directly linking integrability obstructions with chiral operator algebra structure. On a fixed $\Sigma_g$, the fiberwise collision differential is curved: $\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\mathcal{A})\cdot\omega_g$, where $\kappa(\mathcal{A})$ is the leading OPE anomaly.  Incorporating period corrections from $A$-cycles restores strict nilpotence: the total corrected differential $\Dg{g} = \dzero + \sum_k t_k\, d_k$ satisfies $\Dg{g}^{\,2} = 0$, with $t_k = \oint_{A_k}\omega_k \in H^1(\Sigma_g, \mathbb{C})$.  The construction naturally encodes canonical $A_\infty$ and $L_\infty$ structures determined by configuration space stratifications, enabling systematic treatment of non-quadratic chiral algebras.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Koszul Duality and Quantum Complementarity.}
For chiral Koszul pairs $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{A}^!)$,
we establish deformation-obstruction complementarity:
\[
Q_g(\mathcal{A}) \oplus Q_g(\mathcal{A}^!)
\simeq H^*(\mathcal{M}_g, Z(\mathcal{A})),
\]
where $Q_g(\mathcal{A})$ denotes genus-$g$ loop corrections and $Z(\mathcal{A})$
is the center.
This implies that what one algebra sees as deformation, its dual sees as obstruction.
Chiral Hochschild cohomology also satisfies
\[
HH^n_{\mathrm{chiral}}(\mathcal{A})
\simeq HH^{2-n}_{\mathrm{chiral}}(\mathcal{A}^!)^\vee,
\]
manifesting modular, quantum, and geometric periodicities whose interplay is
essential at critical level.

\part{The Heisenberg algebra}

\noindent
The Heisenberg chiral algebra $\mathcal{H}_k$ --- a single current
with a double-pole OPE --- is the simplest object carrying the full
structure of chiral Koszul duality.  In this opening chapter we build
the bar complex by hand, watch $d^2 = 0$ emerge from the Arnold
relation, read off the Koszul dual $\mathrm{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*)$,
pass to genus~$1$ where curvature appears, and recognize the
$\hat{A}$-genus in the genus tower.  Every theorem proved in
Parts~I--III is previewed here in concrete form.

\part{Theory}

\noindent
In Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}, we saw the bar complex,
Koszul duality, genus-$1$ curvature, and the $\hat{A}$-genus tower
for the Heisenberg algebra.  Part~I establishes these results in
full generality.  The argument proceeds through four stages: classical
Koszul duality (the algebraic template), configuration spaces (the
geometric substrate), the bar-cobar construction (the synthesis),
and the genus tower (the modular completion).  Four theorems are
proved: bar-cobar equivalence for Koszul pairs, intertwined with
Verdier duality on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$ (Theorem~A); higher-genus
inversion on the Koszul locus, with a curved replacement in the
coderived category off this locus (Theorem~B); the Lagrangian
complementarity of deformation and obstruction spaces under a
shifted-symplectic pairing from Verdier duality on the center local
system (Theorem~C); and the modular characteristic theorem, which
shows that a single scalar $\kappa(\mathcal{A})$ determines the
scalar genus tower (Theorem~D$_{\mathrm{scal}}$); the full modular
characteristic package $(\Delta_{\cA}, \Pi_{\cA}, \Theta_{\cA})$
requires additional spectral data beyond~$\kappa$.

\part{Examples}

\noindent
In Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}, we computed the bar complex, Koszul
dual, curvature tower, and $\hat{A}$-genus for the Heisenberg algebra.
Part~II applies the same programme to every major family of chiral algebras.
The constructions of Part~I---the bar complex, the genus tower, the Koszul
pair structure---are defined for any chiral algebra satisfying mild finiteness
conditions.  The question driving Part~II is: what do these constructions
\emph{produce} when applied to the algebras that arise in practice?

The answer is unexpectedly structured.  The bar cohomology dimensions
of apparently unrelated algebras---the Kac--Moody algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$, the Virasoro algebra, the
$\beta\gamma$-system---share a common discriminant
$\Delta(x) = (1-3x)(1+x)$.  The genus expansion of every Koszul
algebra is controlled by a single obstruction invariant~$\kappa$,
and $\kappa$ transforms predictably under Drinfeld--Sokolov
reduction.  The complementarity sums $c + c'$ are root-datum
invariants.  These are not coincidences; they are the fingerprints
of the general theory made visible in specific examples.  Each family
of chiral algebras reveals a distinct face of the modular Koszul
structure: the Feigin--Frenkel involution
$k \mapsto -k - 2h^\vee$ is Koszul duality for Kac--Moody
algebras; Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction is a Koszul functor;
the $\hat{A}$-genus in the Heisenberg genus expansion witnesses a
family index theorem; the Yangian forces a departure from
$E_\infty$-chiral to $E_1$-chiral Koszul duality.

\part{Connections and Applications}

\noindent
The Heisenberg frame chapter (Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}) demonstrated
that a single chiral algebra encodes bar cohomology, Koszul duality, genus
corrections, and an index-theoretic $\hat{A}$-genus simultaneously.
The four theorems of Part~I and the computations of Part~II establish
chiral Koszul duality as a self-contained mathematical framework.  But the
framework is not isolated: its constructions appear, in different guises,
throughout modern mathematics and mathematical physics.  The BV-BRST complex
of perturbative quantum field theory \emph{is} the bar complex, with the
ghost fields incarnating the desuspended generators and the BRST differential
incarnating the bar differential
(Chapter~\ref{ch:bv-brst}).  The Kontsevich integral of knot theory \emph{is}
the restriction of the configuration space propagator from complex curves to
embedded circles (\S\ref{subsec:vassiliev}).  The Costello--Li construction
of chiral algebras from four-dimensional gauge theories \emph{is} the
factorization algebra incarnation of the bar-cobar adjunction applied to
the four-dimensional factorization algebra
(Chapter~\ref{ch:ht-boundary}).  Part~III traces these identifications.

The chapters here are more concise than those of Parts~I--II,
indicating directions of active investigation rather than definitive
treatments.  Several results are labeled \emph{Conjectured} with scope
remarks identifying the additional input---typically from gauge theory or
string theory---needed for a complete proof.  The final chapter
(\S\ref{sec:modular-koszul-programme}) articulates the programme toward which
the entire monograph points: modular Koszul duality for factorization algebras.
