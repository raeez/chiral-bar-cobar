\begin{abstract}
\itshape

\noindent
This monograph studies one phenomenon: the chiral Koszul
reflection $K\colon\mathrm{Kosz}(X)\to\mathrm{Kosz}(X)^{\mathrm{op}}$
on chirally Koszul algebras over a smooth algebraic curve~$X$,
together with its conductor and its modular shadow. Two
equations carry the entire content. The first identifies the
bar differential as the pullback of Arnold's universal flat
connection on configuration spaces:
\[
d_{\barB}\;=\;\mathrm{KZ}^{\ast}\!\bigl(\nabla_{\mathrm{Arnold}}\bigr).
\]
The second identifies the universal Koszul conductor with the
ghost charge of the BRST resolution:
\[
K(\cA)\;=\;-c_{\mathrm{ghost}}\bigl(\mathrm{BRST}(\cA)\bigr).
\]
Reading these as one structure, the Koszul reflection is
involutive: $K^{2}\simeq\id$ on $\mathrm{Kosz}(X)$, and
$\Omega_X B^{\mathrm{ord}}_X$ is a symmetric-monoidal adjoint
equivalence on the Koszul locus.

The primitive object is the ordered bar coalgebra
\[
\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}_X(\cA)\;=\;T^{c}\bigl(s^{-1}\bar\cA\bigr),
\]
a cofree $\Eone$-coalgebra with deconcatenation coproduct
encoding the full OPE data of~$\cA$ through the logarithmic
kernel $\eta=d\log(z_1-z_2)$ on Fulton--MacPherson
compactifications and Mok logarithmic spaces. Its degree-$2$
component is the classical $r$-matrix $r_\cA(z)$; at
genus~$g\ge1$ the fibered differential acquires
matrix-valued curvature $(d^{\mathrm{ord}}_{\mathrm{fib}})^{2}
= r_\cA(z)\cdot\omega_g$ against the Hodge class~$\omega_g$.
The universal Maurer--Cartan element
$\Theta_\cA\in\MC(\gAmod)$, $D\Theta_\cA
+\tfrac12[\Theta_\cA,\Theta_\cA]=0$, packages every genus.
The symmetric bar $\barB^{\Sigma}_X(\cA)$ on $\Ran(X)$ is
the $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant shadow: a formality bridge gives
$\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}\simeq\barB^{\Sigma}$ for $\Einf$-chiral
input; the bridge fails for genuinely $\Eone$-chiral input,
with $\GRTone(\bQ)$-dependent kernel from the Drinfeld
associator inaccessible to any $\Sigma_n$-invariant. At
degree~$2$, $\mathrm{av}(r_\cA(z))=\kappa_{\mathrm{dp}}(\cA)$,
and the modular characteristic is
$\kappa(\cA)=\mathrm{av}(r_\cA(z))+\dim(\fg)/2$ for
non-abelian affine Kac--Moody, $\kappa(\cA)=\mathrm{av}(r_\cA(z))$
for abelian and scalar families.

Seven theorems are corollaries of the two equations.
Theorem~A is the Koszul reflection: a bar--cobar
$(\infty,2)$-adjunction of properads with $R$-twisted
$\Sigma_n$-descent and Verdier intertwining
$\bD_{\Ran}\barB(\cA)\simeq\barB(\cA^{!}_\infty)$.
Theorem~B is bar--cobar inversion
$\Omega_X(\barB_X(\cA))\xrightarrow{\sim}\cA$ on the
Koszul locus at all genera, via Positselski's
nonhomogeneous curved-dg coalgebra theorem in chiral form.
Theorem~C is the canonical $-(3g{-}3)$-shifted symplectic
structure on the derived chiral centre
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)=
R\Hom_{\cA\otimes\cA^{\mathrm{op}}}(\cA,\cA)$ over
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$, with Lagrangian section from
$\cA\otimes\cA^{!}_\infty$ and family-specific scalar
complementarity
\[
\kappa(\cA)+\kappa(\cA^{!})\;=\;\varrho_\cA\,K(\cA),
\]
with $K(\cA)=c(\cA)+c(\cA^{!})$ the Trinity conductor of the
Ghost Identity and $\varrho_\cA$ the family-specific anomaly
ratio (principal $\cW_N$: $\varrho_N=H_N-1$; KM/free:
$\varrho_\cA=0$; BP: $\varrho_{\mathrm{BP}}=1/6$):
$\kappa+\kappa^{!}=0$ for affine Kac--Moody and free fields
($K=2\dim\fg$), $13$ for Virasoro at self-dual $c=13$ ($K=26$),
$250/3$ for $\cW_3$ ($K=100$), $98/3$ for
Bershadsky--Polyakov ($K=196$). The Trinity Theorem records that
$K_E=K_c=K_g$: Euler characteristic of the bar tower equals
sum of central charges equals ghost charge of the BRST
resolution. Theorem~D extracts the Arakelov tensor curvature
$\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA)=\kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_g$
\textup{(UNIFORM-WEIGHT)} at all genera, with cross-channel
correction $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$ at $g\ge 2$ off the
uniform-weight lane (for $\cW_3$ at $g=2$,
$\delta F_2^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cW_3)=(c+204)/(16c)$),
and generating function
$\sum_{g\ge 1}F_g\,x^{2g}=\kappa(\cA)\,(\hat{A}(ix)-1)$.
Theorem~H concentrates the chiral Hochschild cohomology
$\ChirHoch^{\bullet}(\cA)$ in degrees $\{0,1,2\}$ with sharp
Hilbert series
\[
P(t,q)\;=\;\mathrm{HS}_{Z(\cA)}(q)
+\mathrm{HS}_{\ChirHoch^{1}(\cA)}(q)\cdot t
+\mathrm{HS}_{Z(\cA^{!})}(q)\cdot t^{2}
\]
and universal $\Ethree$-algebra structure. Theorem~F is the
universal holography functor
$\Phi_{\mathrm{hol}}\colon\ChirAlg^{\omega,\mathrm{BL}}_X
\to\HTQFT_{X\times\bR}$ sending a conilpotent
boundary-Lagrangian chiral algebra to the bulk--boundary
pair $(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)
\rightsquigarrow(\cA,\cA^{!}_\infty))$. Theorem~G is the
completeness of the five-slot fingerprint
$\phi(\cA)=(p_{\max},r_{\max},\chi_{\mathrm{VOA}},
n_{\mathrm{strong}},\mathrm{coset})$ on the standard
landscape, classifying $\barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ and
$\cA^{!}$ up to isomorphism, with critical-level companion
stratum~$FF$.

The shadow obstruction tower of $\Theta_\cA$ obeys the
Riccati identity $H(t)^{2}=t^{4}Q_c(t)$ on each primary
deformation line, with discriminant $\Delta=8\kappa S_4$.
This Quadrichotomy partitions the standard landscape into
five depth classes: $\mathbf{G}$ (Heisenberg, free fermion,
lattice; $r_{\max}=2$, $\Delta=0$); $\mathbf{L}$ (affine
Kac--Moody at non-critical level; $r_{\max}=3$);
$\mathbf{C}$ ($\beta\gamma$, $bc$ ghosts; $r_{\max}=4$,
nilpotent $R$-matrix); $\mathbf{M}$ (Virasoro, $\cW_N$,
Bershadsky--Polyakov; $r_{\max}=\infty$); and the
Feigin--Frenkel companion $\mathbf{FF}$ at $V_{-h^\vee}(\fg)$
where Sugawara is undefined and $\ChirHoch^{\bullet}$ is
unbounded. The algebraic depth $d_{\mathrm{alg}}\in
\{0,1,2,\infty\}$ admits no intermediate finite value;
Swiss-cheese formality holds if and only if $\cA\in\mathbf{G}$.
On non-logarithmic class~$\mathbf{M}$ the shadow-exponential
base is universal: $C_\cA=6$ on the $T$-line, $C_{\cW_3}=12$
on the $W$-line, with closed-form Sigma-projector
$\Sigma_{\mathrm{Vir}}(z)=4z^{3}/9-z^{2}/9+z/27
-\log(1+6z)/162$.

On the Koszul locus three structures on
$\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ determine one another: the
classical $r$-matrix $r_\cA(z)$, the curved chiral
$A_\infty$-structure $\{m_k\}_{k\ge 0}$, and the chiral
coproduct $\Delta^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA$: the chiral
quantum-group equivalence. Five archetypes span the
classification. Heisenberg $\cH_k$ has $r(z)=k/z$,
$\kappa=k$. Affine $V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ has rational KZ
connection, $\kappa=\dim(\fg)(k+h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$. The
$\beta\gamma$ system has nilpotent $R$-matrix and cubic
observable algebra. Virasoro $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ has irregular
KZ with four Stokes rays, self-dual at $c=13$, $\kappa=c/2$.
The Yangian $Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ is genuinely $\Eone$:
its ordered chiral homology is strictly richer than its
symmetric quotient, with $\GRTone$-equivariance manifest.
At genus~$1$ the bar propagator specialises to the
logarithmic derivative $d\log E(z,w)=(\theta_1'/\theta_1)\,dz$
of the prime form (distinct from the Weierstrass
$\zeta$-function, which differs by the non-holomorphic
$2\eta_1 z$ term and would break the CYBE), the shadow
connection restricts to the
Knizhnik--Zamolodchikov--Bernard connection, and the
Bismut--Gillet--Soul\'e fiberwise Chern-curvature formula
supplies $\lambda_g=c_1(\mathbb{E})$ on $\overline{\cM}_g$.

The derived chiral centre sits on an $\En$ ladder closing
into a holographic circle: $\Eone$-chiral input gives
$\Etwo$, $\Einf$-chiral input gives $\Ethree$ via the
Higher Deligne Conjecture, and the Drinfeld centre supplies
the only nontrivial source of braiding when $\Eone$ lifts
to~$\Etwo$. Topologization (Sugawara at non-critical level)
promotes $\SCchtop$ to $E_3^{\mathrm{top}}$, proved for
affine Kac--Moody at $k\neq -h^\vee$, weight-completed for
class~$\mathbf{M}$, conjectural chain-level on the original
complex for class~$\mathbf{M}$. For simple $\fg$ at
non-critical $k$, the $\Ethree$-deformation space of
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))$ is
$\hbar H^{3}(\fg)[[\hbar]]$, recovering the
Costello--Francis--Gwilliam perturbative Chern--Simons
$\Ethree$-algebra at the formal disk.

On the non-degenerate locus $\{\kappa\ne 0,\
\text{non-critical level},\ \text{Koszul locus}\}$ each
of the seven theorems admits at least two genuinely
independent verification paths registered in the
programme's independent-verification infrastructure:
the bell theorem.
Off the locus (critical stratum~$FF$, the
Creutzig--Kanade--Linshaw logarithmic admissible lane,
the super-Yangian cluster, the Calabi--Yau functor at
complex dimension $d\ge 3$) the bell rings and the
residual frontier is explicit and scoped.

The programme comprises four volumes read through three
lenses (operadic in Volume~I, holographic in Volume~II,
geometric in Volume~III) on the same chiral-categorical
fact. Volume~I (this volume) establishes the two-equation
climax and proves the seven theorems. Volume~II lifts to
$\bC\times\bR$: $\SCchtop$ governs the derived centre pair,
$E_3^{\mathrm{top}}$ governs the topologised bulk, the
iterated Sugawara tower escalates $W_N\to E_{N+1}^{\mathrm{top}}$
and culminates in 3d quantum gravity at the $\cW_\infty$
endpoint via $\Phi_{\mathrm{hol}}$. Volume~III constructs
the geometric source through the Calabi--Yau-to-chiral
functor $\Phi_{\mathrm{CY}}$, native level dependent on
complex dimension $d$ (an $\Etwo$-chiral output for $d\le 2$,
an $\Eone$-chiral output for $d\ge 3$), producing the K3
Yangian, the Borcherds--Kac--Moody conductor
$\kBKM(X)=c_N(0)/2$ universally for K3-fibered Class~A,
and the class-$\mathbf{M}$ $\Ethree$ bar of dimension $6^{g}$.
The universal trace identity bridges the volumes:
\[
K(\cA)\;=\;-c_{\mathrm{ghost}}(\mathrm{BRST}(\cA))
\quad\text{and}\quad
\kBKM(X)\;=\;\frac{c_{N}(0)}{2}
\]
are evaluations of one principle through $\Phi_{\mathrm{CY}}$
on K3-fibered Class~A; the identity is conjectural at
programme level and verified per-family. Volume~IV inscribes
the HEAL and UPGRADE theorems explicitly, brings the
independent-verification decorators to complete coverage,
and aligns the computational infrastructure into a single
registry.
\end{abstract}

\part{The Bar Complex}
\label{part:bar-complex}

\noindent
Place sections $a_1, \ldots, a_n$ of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ at
distinct points $z_1, \ldots, z_n$ on a smooth curve~$X$. Wedge the
tensor $a_1(z_1) \otimes \cdots \otimes a_n(z_n)$ against the
logarithmic $n$-form $\eta = \bigwedge_{i<j} d\log(z_i - z_j)$ on
the Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\overline{C}_n(X)$, and
extract residues at each collision divisor. The result is a
differential. Arnold's three-term identity
\[
\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23}
\;+\; \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31}
\;+\; \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12}
\;=\; 0
\]
forces $d^2 = 0$. This is the bar complex~$\barB_X(\cA)$: the
categorical logarithm of~$\cA$.

A logarithm has four properties. Each is a theorem.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{(\Alph*)},leftmargin=2.2em]
\item \textbf{Existence.}
 The bar-cobar adjunction $(\barB, \Omega)$, intertwined with
 Verdier duality on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$. The logarithm exists
 and is natural.
\item \textbf{Invertibility.}
 On the Koszul locus,
 $\Omega(\barB(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$: the cobar inverts
 the bar, with the spectral sequence collapsing at~$E_2$. The
 logarithm has an exponential.
\item \textbf{Branch structure.}
 At genus $g \geq 1$, $Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!)$ sit as
 complementary Lagrangians inside
 $R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathcal{Z}(\cA))$.
 The logarithm has monodromy; its sheets are Lagrangian.
\item \textbf{Leading coefficient.}
 The modular characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$ is universal, additive,
 and duality-constrained; its generating function is the $\hat{A}$-genus.
 The leading Taylor coefficient of the logarithm is a topological
 index.
\end{enumerate}
\noindent
\textbf{(H) The coefficient ring.}
$\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is polynomial on the Koszul locus and dual to
$\ChirHoch^*(\cA^!)$. The full Taylor series of the logarithm lives
in a polynomial ring.

\smallskip\noindent
But $\kappa(\cA)$ is only the \emph{linear} leading term.
Part~\ref{part:characteristic-datum} develops the full nonlinear structure: the
\emph{shadow obstruction tower}
$\kappa \to \Delta \to \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{Q} \to \cdots$,
a hierarchy of finite-order invariants (quadratic, cubic, quartic,
and beyond), each proved constructively with obstruction classes
$o_{r+1}(\cA)$ in the cyclic deformation complex.
The all-degree limit of this tower is the universal
class~$\Theta_\cA$, proved by the bar-intrinsic construction
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic});
the constructive content is the finite-order engine.

The constructions of Parts~\ref{part:bar-complex}--\ref{part:characteristic-datum} (the bar complex, the genus tower, the Koszul

conditions. The question driving Part~\ref{part:standard-landscape} is: what do these constructions
\emph{produce} when applied to the algebras that arise in practice?

The answer is unexpectedly structured. The bar cohomology dimensions
of structurally distinct algebras (the Kac--Moody algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$, the Virasoro algebra, the

$\Delta(x) = (1-3x)(1+x)$. The genus expansion of every Koszul
algebra is controlled by a single obstruction invariant~$\kappa$,
and $\kappa$ transforms predictably under Drinfeld--Sokolov
reduction. The complementarity sums $c + c'$ are root-datum
invariants. These are not coincidences; they are the fingerprints
of the general theory made visible in specific examples. Each family
of chiral algebras reveals a distinct face of the modular Koszul
structure: the Feigin--Frenkel involution
$k \mapsto -k - 2h^\vee$ is Koszul duality for Kac--Moody
algebras; Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction is a Koszul functor;
the $\hat{A}$-genus in the Heisenberg genus expansion witnesses a
family index theorem; the Yangian forces a departure from
$E_\infty$-chiral to $E_1$-chiral Koszul duality.

The examples are organized along the axis from the
$\Einf$-commutative atom (Heisenberg) to the $\Eone$-associative
atom (Yangian). Kac--Moody algebras, Virasoro, and
$\mathcal{W}$-algebras are $\Einf$-chiral with increasingly
complex OPE pole structures. Lattice vertex algebras carry an
$\Eone$ sublattice structure visible through screening operators.
The Yangian is purely $\Eone$-chiral: its bar complex uses ordered
configuration spaces and Verdier duality acts by $R$-matrix
inversion, not type exchange.
