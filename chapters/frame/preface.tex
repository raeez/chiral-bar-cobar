\section*{I.\quad The logarithmic seed}

\noindent
Consider two distinct points $z_1, z_2$ on a smooth algebraic curve~$X$, and the $1$-form
\[
\eta_{12} \;=\; d\log(z_1 - z_2)
\;=\; \frac{dz_1 - dz_2}{z_1 - z_2}
\]
on the configuration space $C_2(X) = \{(z_1,z_2) \in X^2 : z_1 \neq z_2\}$.
It has a simple pole along the diagonal with residue~$1$---not residue~$k$,
not residue depending on the curve or the algebra.  Just~$1$.
It is conformally invariant.

For three distinct points, the \emph{Arnold relation}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-arnold}
\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23}
\;+\; \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31}
\;+\; \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12}
\;=\; 0
\end{equation}
holds because $(z_1 - z_2)(z_2 - z_3) + (z_2 - z_3)(z_3 - z_1) + (z_3 - z_1)(z_1 - z_2) = 0$, which holds because $a + b + c = 0$ implies $ab + bc + ca = 0$.  The commutativity of addition implies the vanishing of a symmetric function of differences.  It is the only relation in $H^*(\mathrm{Conf}_n(\C), \C)$, replicated at all higher degrees by the same identity.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The bar complex.}\enspace
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on~$X$ (Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite{BD04}).
Place elements $a_1, \ldots, a_n$ at $n$ distinct points on~$X$, dress them in logarithmic forms, and define a differential~$d$ by extracting OPE residues along the collision divisors of the Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\overline{C}_n(X)$:
\[
d_{\barB}[s^{-1}a \,|\, s^{-1}b]
\;=\;
\operatorname{Res}_{z_1 = z_2}\!\bigl[a(z_1)\,b(z_2) \cdot \eta_{12}\bigr].
\]
The Arnold relation kills every triple residue: $d^2 = 0$ at genus~$0$.  The resulting cochain complex $\barB_X(\cA) = (T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA), d_{\barB})$ is the \emph{chiral bar complex}, where $T^c$ is the cofree conilpotent coalgebra (desuspended, coinvariants under $\Sigma_n$; cohomological grading $|d| = +1$).

Under a coordinate change $z \mapsto w(z)$, the propagator transforms as $\eta_{ij} \mapsto \eta_{ij} + d\log\frac{w(z_i) - w(z_j)}{z_i - z_j}$.  Near the diagonal this becomes $d\log w'(z_i)$: the standard cocycle for $\mathrm{Diff}(X)$.  The propagator is a BRST ghost for diffeomorphisms.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The two-pole obstruction.}\enspace
When the OPE has both a simple pole (the bracket) and a double pole (the metric), the bracket component of the bar differential does not square to zero.  The identity that holds is
\[
d_{\mathrm{br}}^2 \;=\; -\{d_{\mathrm{br}},\, d_{\mathrm{lev}}\},
\]
not $d_{\mathrm{br}}^2 = 0$.  This was verified across all $2048$ sign conventions arising from eleven independent binary choices.  The total $d$ squares to zero by the Borcherds identity, which governs all poles simultaneously---the algebraic shadow of Stokes' theorem on $\overline{C}_n(X)$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Three differentials.}\enspace
At genus~$g \ge 1$ the configuration space fibres over $\overline{\cM}_g$ and the fibrewise differential acquires curvature:
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$, a single scalar $\kappa(\cA)$ times a tautological class.  The period corrections from the degeneration divisors of~$\overline{\cM}_g$ restore nilpotence: the total corrected differential $D_g$ satisfies $D_g^2 = 0$.

Three differentials:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),nosep]
\item $d_0$: genus-$0$ collision differential.  $d_0^2 = 0$ by Arnold.
\item $d_{\mathrm{fib}}$: fiberwise on $\Sigma_g$.  $d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g \neq 0$.
\item $D_g$: total corrected.  $D_g^2 = 0$.
\end{enumerate}
The correction that restores nilpotence is a Lagrangian splitting: the $A$-cycles form a Lagrangian subspace of $H^1(\Sigma_g, \C)$ for the intersection pairing; the curvature lies in the symplectic complement; the $A$-period corrections project it to zero.

In the curved $A_\infty$ framework: $m_0 = \kappa \cdot \omega_g$, and $m_1^2(a) = [m_0, a]$ (the commutator, with its sign).  The condition $\kappa = 0$ is simultaneously: $d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = 0$; anomaly cancellation for the bosonic string; $c = 26$; the bar complex being an honest derived object.


\section*{II.\quad Five theorems through the Heisenberg}

The Heisenberg algebra $\cH_k$ has OPE $\alpha(z)\,\alpha(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$: a double pole, no simple pole.  The chiral bracket is abelian.  Every transferred operation beyond arity~$2$ vanishes.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~A} (bar-cobar adjunction and Verdier intertwining).
The bar differential extracts the single mode product $\alpha_{(1)}\alpha = k$:
$d_{\barB}[s^{-1}\alpha \,|\, s^{-1}\alpha] = k$.
Bar cohomology at weight~$n$ counts partitions of $n-2$: $1, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, \ldots$---the generating function $\prod_{n \ge 2}(1-q^n)^{-1}$.  A modular form, at the simplest example.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~B} (bar-cobar inversion).
The cobar of the bar recovers $\cH_k$: $\Omega(\barB(\cH_k)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cH_k$.  This is the Koszul property.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~C} (complementarity).
The Koszul dual $\cH_k^! = \mathrm{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*)$ is the chiral symmetric algebra---not $\cH_{-k}$.  Heisenberg non-self-duality is the first critical pitfall.  At genus~$g \ge 1$:
\[
Q_g(\cH_k) \;\oplus\; Q_g(\cH_k^!) \;\cong\; H^*(\overline{\cM}_g,\, \cZ_{\cH_k}),
\]
the complementarity decomposition of the centre local system.  Central charges satisfy $c(\cH_k) + c(\cH_k^!) = 2$, independent of~$k$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~D} (modular characteristic).
The single scalar $\kappa(\cH_k) = k$ determines the entire genus tower:
\[
\sum_{g \ge 1} F_g(\cH_k)\,\hbar^{2g-2}
\;=\;
k\Bigl(\frac{\hbar/2}{\sin(\hbar/2)} - 1\Bigr)
\;=\;
\frac{k}{24}\hbar^2 + \frac{7k}{5760}\hbar^4 + \frac{31k}{967680}\hbar^6 + \cdots
\]
The $\hat{A}$-genus, from index theory.  The Bernoulli numbers encode the failure of Arnold's relation at higher genus.  At genus~$1$: $F_1 = k/24$, the tadpole amplitude $\kappa \cdot E_2(\tau)$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~H} (chiral Hochschild).
$\mathrm{ChirHoch}^*(\cH_k) = (\C, \C, \C)$ in degrees $\{0, 1, 2\}$: polynomial, Koszul-functorial.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The general theorems.}\enspace
For a chiral algebra $\cA$ on~$X$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*),nosep]
\item \emph{Bar-cobar adjunction:} $\barB_X$ and $\Omega_X$ form an adjunction on the Ran space $\mathrm{Ran}(X)$, intertwined by Verdier duality: $\mathbb{D}_{\mathrm{Ran}} \circ \barB \simeq \barB \circ (-)^!$.
\item \emph{Inversion:} $\Omega(\barB(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$ on the Koszul locus; the comparison map is a quasi-isomorphism when the PBW spectral sequence degenerates at~$E_2$.
\item \emph{Complementarity:} $Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!) \cong H^*(\overline{\cM}_g, \cZ_\cA)$, upgraded to a shifted-symplectic Lagrangian geometry.
\item \emph{Modular characteristic:} $\kappa(\cA)$ is universal (depends only on the leading OPE), additive ($\kappa(\cA \otimes \cB) = \kappa(\cA) + \kappa(\cB)$), and duality-constrained ($\kappa(\cA) + \kappa(\cA^!) = 0$ for Kac--Moody and free fields; $\kappa + \kappa' = \rho \cdot K$ for $\cW$-algebras).
\item \emph{Hochschild:} $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^*(\cA)$ is polynomial on the Koszul locus, Koszul-functorial, and governs deformations and obstructions.
\end{enumerate}

Central charge complementarity for Kac--Moody:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-cc-complementarity}
c(\widehat{\fg}_k) + c(\widehat{\fg}_{-k-2h^\vee}) \;=\; 2\dim(\fg),
\end{equation}
independent of~$k$.  For $\fsl_2$: $c + c' = 6$.  This is the Freudenthal--de~Vries formula.

At the critical level $k = -h^\vee$: the Sugawara tensor is undefined (not ``$c$ diverges''), the bar complex is uncurved ($\kappa = 0$), and $H^0(\barB(\widehat{\fg}_{-h^\vee})) \cong \operatorname{Fun}(\operatorname{Op}_{\fg^\vee}(D))$: functions on the space of Langlands-dual opers.

The Feigin--Frenkel involution $k \mapsto -k - 2h^\vee$ is Verdier duality.


\section*{III.\quad The master object}

\subsection*{The graph-sum formula}

For each stable graph $\Gamma \in \mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}$, let $\Phi_v^\cA$ denote the transferred chiral operation at vertex~$v$, obtained by homological perturbation transfer from a strong deformation retract $(\cA, H^*(\cA), \pi, \iota, h)$: the tree-level sum over planar binary trees with $m_2$ at vertices and $h$ on internal edges.  Write $W_\Gamma \in C_\bullet(\overline{\cM}_{g,n})$ for the fundamental chain of the boundary stratum $\overline{\cM}_\Gamma = \prod_v \overline{\cM}_{g(v),\mathrm{val}(v)}$.

The \emph{modular bar construction}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-modular-bar}
\barB^{(g)}_X(\cA)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\;W_\Gamma\;\otimes\;
\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}\Phi_v^\cA\,.
\end{equation}
The \emph{universal Maurer--Cartan element}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-theta}
\Theta_\cA
\;=\;
\sum_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\;W_\Gamma\;\otimes\;\Phi_\Gamma^\cA
\;\in\;
\mathrm{MC}\bigl(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA\bigr)
\end{equation}
satisfies $D\Theta_\cA + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta_\cA, \Theta_\cA] = 0$ because $\partial^2 = 0$ on $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.

\subsection*{Bar-intrinsic construction}

The genus-completed bar differential is $D_\cA = d_0 + \sum_{g \ge 1} \hbar^g d_\cA^{(g)}$.  Define
\[
\Theta_\cA \;:=\; D_\cA - d_0.
\]
Since $d_0^2 = 0$ and $D_\cA^2 = 0$:
\[
0 = (d_0 + \Theta_\cA)^2 = [d_0, \Theta_\cA] + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta_\cA, \Theta_\cA].
\]
The Maurer--Cartan equation.  $\Theta_\cA$ is MC because $D_\cA^2 = 0$---no construction is needed beyond the existence of the genus-completed bar differential.

$D_\cA^2 = 0$ at the convolution level follows from $\partial^2 = 0$ on $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.  At the ambient level---with planted-forest corrections from the log FM compactification---$D_\cA^2 = 0$ is proved via Mok's normal-crossings theorem on $\FM_n(X|D)$ for snc pairs~\cite{Mok25}.  The planted forests are $\mathrm{Trop}(\FM_n(C|D))$: tropicalisation of the collision geometry.  The all-arity inverse limit $\Theta_\cA = \varprojlim \Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ converges (Theorem~\ref{thm:recursive-existence}).

Every theorem of this monograph is a projection of $\Theta_\cA$.

\subsection*{Scalar saturation}

For every simple~$\fg$, $H^2_{\mathrm{cyc}}(\fg, \fg) = H^3(\fg) = \C$: one-dimensional.  The cubic Casimir of $\fsl_3$ lives in $H^5(\fg)$---degree~$5$ in cyclic cohomology, while Maurer--Cartan lives at degree~$2$.  The degrees do not meet.  Higher Casimirs cannot contribute, at any rank.

Consequence: $\Theta_\cA^{\min} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \eta \otimes \Lambda$.  One number, one kernel, one class.  Scalar saturation is proved for all algebraic families with rational OPE coefficients (Theorem~\ref{thm:algebraic-family-rigidity}), covering the entire standard Lie-theoretic landscape at all non-critical levels.

\section*{IV.\quad The convolution architecture}

Every algebraic structure in this monograph is a Maurer--Cartan element in a convolution dg Lie algebra.  This is the Chriss--Ginzburg organising principle: the bar complex plays the role of the Steinberg variety, and the MC element $\Theta_\cA$ plays the role of the fundamental class whose projections recover all convolution products.

\subsection*{The convolution ladder}

At three levels of generality, the bar-type construction is a coefficient of a convolution dg Lie algebra:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-convolution-ladder}
\begin{array}{r@{\;:\quad}c@{\qquad}l}
\text{algebra} &
  \Hom\bigl(B(\mathsf{Ass}),\,\End_A\bigr)
  & A_\infty\text{-structures on }A \\[4pt]
\text{operad} &
  \Hom_\Sigma\bigl(B(\cP),\,\End_V\bigr)
  & \cP_\infty\text{-structures on }V \\[4pt]

  \Hom_\Sigma\bigl(F\cO,\,\End_{\barB(\cA)}\bigr)

\end{array}
\end{equation}
The first line is the Hochschild cochain complex; the second is the deformation complex (Ginzburg--Kapranov~\cite{GK94}); the third, with $\cO = \mathsf{Com}$, governs this book.

\subsection*{The modular operad and Feynman transform}

A \emph{modular operad}~$\cO$ (Getzler--Kapranov~\cite{GeK98}) has operations indexed by connected stable graphs~$\Gamma$ of arbitrary genus, with structure maps from separating and non-separating gluing of boundary divisors of $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.  Trees are the genus-$0$ case.

The \emph{Feynman transform} replaces trees by stable graphs:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-feynman-sum}
F\cO(g,n)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}}
\frac{\hbar^{g(\Gamma)}}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\;\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}\cO\bigl(g(v),\mathrm{In}(v)\bigr)
\;\otimes\;\det\bigl(E(\Gamma)\bigr).
\end{equation}
The differential contracts one edge; $d^2 = 0$ is the codimension-$2$ boundary cancellation on the moduli space.  At genus~$0$: the operadic bar construction.

When $\cO = \mathsf{Com}$ (the commutative modular operad), each vertex factor is one-dimensional, and the Feynman transform becomes the stable graph complex $F\!\mathsf{Com}$---a chain complex spanned by stable graphs with edge-contraction differential.  The bar complex $\barB_X(\cA)$ is an $F\!\mathsf{Com}$-algebra, and the modular convolution dg Lie algebra
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-gmod}
\mathfrak{g}^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA
\;:=\;
\Hom_\Sigma\bigl(F\!\mathsf{Com},\;\End_{\barB(\cA)}\bigr)
\end{equation}
is the ambient home of $\Theta_\cA$.

Replacing $\mathsf{Com}$ by $\mathsf{Ass}$ equips each vertex with a cyclic ordering, producing the ribbon graph complex~$F\!\mathsf{Ass}$.  This is the algebraic form of the 't~Hooft $1/N$ expansion: planar diagrams dominate at large~$N$, and the shadow Postnikov tower is the truncation to $r$-point 't~Hooft interactions.

\subsection*{Three formative preprints}

Three pillars force upgrades to the foundational architecture:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*),nosep]
\item \emph{Malikov--Schechtman~\cite{MS24}}: the primitive local object is a \emph{homotopy chiral algebra} ($\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$), not strict.  The strict chiral algebra appears after rectification.

\item \emph{Mok~\cite{Mok25}}: the global collision geometry is the log FM compactification $\FM_n(X|D)$ on snc pairs, with tropicalisation $\mathrm{Trop}(\FM_n(C|D))$ recovering planted forests as combinatorial shadows.

Nine of the eleven identification theorems connecting these pillars to the bar construction are proved; the remaining two are structural.

\subsection*{Completion}

The strong completion-tower theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong}) shows the finite-stage bar-cobar passes to inverse limits automatically: the strong filtration axiom $\mu_r(F^{i_1}, \ldots, F^{i_r}) \subset F^{i_1 + \cdots + i_r}$ forces an arity cutoff (Lemma~\ref{lem:arity-cutoff}) that makes continuity and Mittag--Leffler automatic.
