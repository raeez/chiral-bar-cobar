\section*{1.\quad The categorical logarithm}
% ====================================================================

Let $X$ be a smooth algebraic curve over~$\mathbb{C}$ and
$\cA$ a chiral algebra on~$X$.  Place $n$ sections
$a_1,\dots,a_n\in \cA$ at distinct points $z_1,\dots,z_n\in X$.
Form the tensor
\[
a_1(z_1)\otimes\cdots\otimes a_n(z_n)
\otimes
\bigwedge_{(i,j)\in E(T)}\eta_{ij},
\qquad
\eta_{ij}:=d\log(z_i-z_j)=\frac{dz_i-dz_j}{z_i-z_j},
\]
summed over edges of a planar tree~$T$.  Extract residues along the
collision divisors of the Fulton--MacPherson compactification
$\overline C_n(X)$.  The result is the \emph{bar complex}
\[
\barB_X(\cA)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{n\ge 1}
(s^{-1}\bar\cA)^{\otimes n}_{\Sigma_n}
\otimes
H^{n-1}(\overline C_n(X),\,\Omega^{n-1}_{\log}),
\]
a graded coalgebra with differential $d_{\barB}$ induced by the
operator product expansion.  The Arnold relation
\[
\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23}
+\eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{31}
+\eta_{31}\wedge\eta_{12}
\;=\;0
\]
kills triple residues at codimension-two strata.  The Borcherds
identity---the defining axiom of a vertex algebra, coupling all OPE
pole orders in a single statement---kills the algebraic factor.
Together: $d_{\barB}^2=0$.  The bar construction accepts exactly
chiral algebras.

The bar construction is a categorical logarithm: it maps the
multiplicative OPE structure to a nilpotent differential.  The
logarithmic form~$\eta_{ij}$ is its kernel.  The cobar construction
$\Omega(\barB(\cA))$ is the exponential.  Verdier duality on
$\operatorname{Ran}(X)$ interchanges the bar of~$\cA$ with the bar
of a second chiral algebra~$\cA^!$, the \emph{chiral Koszul dual}:
\[
\mathbb D_{\operatorname{Ran}}\,\barB_X(\cA)
\;\simeq\;
\barB_X(\cA^!).
\]
When~$\cA$ is \emph{chirally Koszul}, the cobar inverts the bar:
$\Omega(\barB(\cA))\xrightarrow{\sim}\cA$.  The spectral sequence
collapses at~$E_2$, the bar coalgebra has cohomology concentrated in
degree~$1$, and the Koszul dual algebra~$\cA^!$ governs a linear
resolution of~$\cA$.  Classical Koszul duality over a point
(Priddy, Beilinson--Ginzburg--Soergel, Loday--Vallette) is the
special case $X=\operatorname{Spec}\mathbb C$.

\section*{2.\quad Curvature and the genus tower}
% ====================================================================

At genus~$0$, the logarithm $d\log(z_1-z_2)$ is single-valued and
$d^2_{\barB}=0$ follows from the Arnold relation alone.  At
genus~$g\ge 1$ the function $z_1-z_2$ has no global meaning.  The
replacement is
\[
\eta^{(g)}_{12}
\;=\;
d\log E(z_1,z_2)+(\text{period corrections}),
\]
where $E(z_1,z_2)$ is the prime form---a section of
$K^{-1/2}\boxtimes K^{-1/2}$ (not~$K^{+1/2}$)---and the period
corrections involve the Hodge bundle
$\mathbb E\to\overline{\cM}_g$ via $(\operatorname{Im}\Omega)^{-1}$.

The prime form has monodromy around $B$-cycles.  The Arnold relation
acquires a defect:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pref-curvature}
d_{\mathrm{fib}}^{\,2}\;=\;\kappa(\cA)\cdot\omega_g,
\end{equation}
where $\omega_g$ is the Arakelov $(1,1)$-form and $\kappa(\cA)$ is
a scalar determined by the leading OPE singularity.  Period integrals
along $A$-cycles restore nilpotence: $D_g^{\,2}=0$.

\emph{Nilpotence is the logarithm of periodicity.}  The nilpotent
datum~$\kappa$ determines the periodic datum---modular
transformations, the monodromy representation---via exponentiation.
The bar complex traces a family of curved cochain complexes over
$\overline{\cM}_g$, and the obstruction class
\[
\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA)
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_g
\;\in\;
H^*(\overline{\cM}_g),
\qquad
\lambda_g=c_g(\mathbb E),
\]
is a characteristic class of this family.  The generating function is
\[
\sum_{g\ge 1}\mathrm{obs}_g\cdot\hbar^{2g-2}
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA)\cdot\bigl(\hat A(i\hbar)-1\bigr):
\]
the $\hat A$-genus emerges from Grothendieck--Riemann--Roch applied
to the universal curve over~$\overline{\cM}_g$.

Verdier duality splits the center local system into complementary
Lagrangians:
\[
R\Gamma(\overline{\cM}_g,\,\cZ_\cA)
\;\simeq\;
\mathbf Q_g(\cA)\oplus\mathbf Q_g(\cA^!).
\]
What one algebra sees as deformation, its dual sees as obstruction.
The scalar characteristics satisfy $\kappa(\cA)+\kappa(\cA^!)=0$:
anomaly cancellation in string theory is the vanishing of the total
modular characteristic.

\section*{3.\quad The modular convolution $L_\infty$-algebra}
% ====================================================================

The constructions of sections 1--2 are shadows of a single
chain-level object.  Three recent structural inputs produce it.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Input A} (Malikov--Schechtman).  The primitive
local object is a \emph{homotopy chiral algebra}
$(\mathrm{Ch}_\infty)$, not a strict one.  For an affine
cover~$\mathfrak U$ of~$X$, the \v Cech totalisation
\[
A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty
\;:=\;
\check C^\bullet(\mathfrak U;\cA)
\]
carries a $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$ structure: higher operations~$F_n$
encode derived OPE data at all pole orders.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Input B} (Mok).  For a logarithmic boundary pair
$(X,D)$ or a semistable degeneration $W\to B$, the chain complexes
\[
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{X|D}(n)
\;:=\;
C_\bullet\!\bigl(\operatorname{FM}_n(X|D)\bigr)
\]
carry cooperadic structure: the boundary stratification of Mok's
logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson compactification produces graphwise
cocomposition maps and the degeneration formula rewrites each rigid
boundary type as a push--pull correspondence.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Input C} (Robert-Nicoud--Wierstra, Vallette).
The filtered convolution $sL_\infty$-algebra
$\operatorname{hom}_\alpha(C,A)$ of a twisting morphism
$\alpha\colon C\to A$ carries a canonical $L_\infty$ structure.
It extends to $\infty$-morphisms in either slot separately, but
not both simultaneously---a structural constraint, not a technical
annoyance.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Assembly.}  The three inputs produce the
\emph{modular convolution $L_\infty$-algebra}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pref-convolution}
\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA
\;:=\;
\Convinf\!\Bigl(
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}},\;
\operatorname{End}_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty)
\Bigr).
\end{equation}
Its strict chart is the coderivation dg Lie algebra
$\Convstr(\cC,\cP)$; the one-slot convention fixes the geometric
cooperad and lets the chiral input vary by $\infty$-morphisms.

The modular bar construction in these coordinates is the
stable-graph sum
\[
\mathrm B^{\log}_{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)(g,n)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}}
\left(
\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}
\bar B^{\mathrm{ch}}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty;\mathrm{In}(v))
\otimes
C_\bullet(\operatorname{FM}^{\log}_\Gamma)
\otimes
\orline{E_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)}
\right)_{\!\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)}.
\]
Each vertex carries a transferred $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-bar
contribution; each edge carries the complementarity propagator; the
chain complex $C_\bullet(\operatorname{FM}^{\log}_\Gamma)$ is the
log-FM stratum indexed by~$\Gamma$; the orientation
line~$\orline{E_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)}$ controls signs.

Its total differential has five geometrically distinct pieces:
\[
D^{\log}_{\mathrm{mod}}
\;=\;
\underbrace{d_{\check C}}_{\text{\v Cech}}
+
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}}_{\text{transferred chiral}}
+
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{coll}}}_{\text{collision}}
+
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{sew}}}_{\text{clutching}}
+
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{loop}}}_{\text{genus-raising}}.
\]
On a pure tensor $a\otimes\eta$ in the summand indexed by~$\Gamma$,
the positive-genus pieces act by residue and pushforward along
boundary strata:
\begin{align*}
d_{\mathrm{sew}}(a\otimes\eta)
&=
\sum_{e\in E_{\mathrm{sep}}(\Gamma)}
\pm\,\mu_e(a)\otimes(\xi_e)_*\operatorname{Res}_{D_e^{\log}}(\eta),
\\
d_{\mathrm{pf}}(a\otimes\eta)
&=
\sum_{F\in\mathsf{PF}^{\mathrm{rig}}(\Gamma)}
\pm\,\mu_F(a)\otimes(\xi_F)_*\operatorname{Res}_{D_F^{\log}}(\eta),
\\
\hbar\Delta(a\otimes\eta)
&=
\hbar\sum_{e\in E_{\mathrm{ns}}(\Gamma)}
\pm\,\operatorname{Tr}_e(a)\otimes(\xi_e)_*\operatorname{Res}_{D_e^{\log}}(\eta).
\end{align*}
Codimension-one separating strata produce~$d_{\mathrm{sew}}$;
codimension-one non-separating strata produce~$\Delta$;
rigid codimension-two planted-forest strata produce~$d_{\mathrm{pf}}$.
The identity $D^2=0$ is the cancellation of codimension-two faces in
Mok's normal-crossings compactification.

\section*{4.\quad The universal Maurer--Cartan element}
% ====================================================================

For each rigid stable graph
$\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}$, the \emph{graphwise
cocomposition}
\[
\Delta^{\log}_\Gamma
\;:=\;
(\nu_\Gamma)_*\circ\operatorname{Res}_{D^{\log}_\Gamma}
\;\colon\;
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}}(g,n)
\;\longrightarrow\;
\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}}(g(v),I_v\sqcup H(v))
[-|E_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)|]
\]
decomposes the cooperadic structure over Mok's birational
correspondence~$\nu_\Gamma$ on the normalised boundary stratum.
The \emph{$\Gamma$-amplitude} of
$f_1,\dots,f_k\in\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA$ is
\[
\ell_\Gamma(f_1,\dots,f_k)
\;=\;
\mu_\Gamma\circ
(\alpha^{\mathrm{mod}}_\Gamma\otimes f_1\otimes\cdots\otimes f_k)
\circ\Delta^{\log}_\Gamma,
\]
and the Taylor coefficients of the modular $L_\infty$-structure are
\[
\ell_k^{(g)}
\;=\;
\sum_{\substack{
\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}},\;|V(\Gamma)|=k\\
b_1(\Gamma)+\sum_v g(v)=g}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}\;\ell_\Gamma.
\]

The \emph{universal Maurer--Cartan element} is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pref-theta}
\Theta_\cA
\;:=\;
D_\cA-d_0
\;=\;
\sum_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}}
\frac{W^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_\Gamma}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\otimes\Phi^\cA_\Gamma
\;\in\;
\operatorname{MC}(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA).
\end{equation}
The vertex labels $\Phi^\cA_\Gamma$ are transferred
$\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-operations; the edge weights
$W^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_\Gamma$ are chain propagators on log-FM strata.
The Maurer--Cartan equation
$D_\cA\,\Theta_\cA+\tfrac12[\Theta_\cA,\Theta_\cA]=0$ holds
because $D_\cA^{\,2}=0$.  No restriction to simple Lie symmetry
is needed.

The Maurer--Cartan space of the log-FM chart is canonically
equivalent to the simplicial set of modular twisting data:
\[
\operatorname{MC}_\bullet\!\bigl(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA\bigr)
\;\simeq\;
\operatorname{Tw}_{\alpha^{\mathrm{mod}}}\!\Bigl(
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}},\,
\operatorname{End}_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty)
\Bigr).
\]
Under this identification, $\Theta_\cA$ is the universal modular
twisting morphism determined by the total differential~$D$.

Each coefficient of~$\Theta_\cA$ acquires a \emph{tridegree}
$(g,n,d)=(\text{loop genus},\;\text{arity},\;\text{logarithmic
boundary depth})$ from Mok's planted-forest stratification.  The
all-arity inverse limit
$\Theta_\cA=\varprojlim_r\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ converges
unconditionally.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The weight filtration.}
Decompose by internal-edge count:
$\Theta_\cA=\sum_{r\ge 1}\Theta_\cA^{[r]}$.  The Maurer--Cartan
equation stratifies:
\[
[D_{\mathrm{loc}},\Theta^{[1]}_\cA]=0,
\qquad
[D_{\mathrm{loc}},\Theta^{[2]}_\cA]
+\tfrac12[\Theta^{[1]}_\cA,\Theta^{[1]}_\cA]=0,
\qquad\dots
\]
Weight~$1$ is completely explicit:
\[
\Theta^{[1]}_\cA\big|_{(0,4)}
=
[\delta_s]\otimes\Phi_s
+[\delta_t]\otimes\Phi_t
+[\delta_u]\otimes\Phi_u,
\qquad
\Theta^{[1]}_\cA\big|_{(1,1)}
=
[\delta_{\mathrm{irr}}]\otimes\Delta.
\]
The combination $\Phi_s+\Phi_t+\Phi_u$ is the Malikov--Schechtman
secondary Borcherds Jacobiator homotopy~$F_3$.  Every genuinely
logarithmic contribution---every contribution that uses Mok's
planted-forest geometry rather than the classical moduli
compactification---first appears at weight~$2$.

The $L_\infty$-twisted differential
\[
d_{\Theta_\cA}(x)
\;:=\;
\sum_{n\ge 0}\sum_{g\ge 0}
\frac{\hbar^g}{n!}\,

$T^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\Theta_\cA}
:=(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA,\,d_{\Theta_\cA})$,
governing infinitesimal deformations of the Maurer--Cartan
element.  In the strict chart, $d_{\Theta_\cA}$
reduces to~$d+[\Theta_\cA,-]$.  Its characteristic projections
are:
\begin{align*}
\kappa(\cA)
&=\operatorname{tr}(\Theta_\cA)_{2,0}
&&\text{(first Chern class)},\\[3pt]
\Delta_\cA(t)
&=\operatorname{sdet}(1-t\,H(d_{\Theta_\cA}|_{\mathrm{red}}))
&&\text{(Chern character)},\\[3pt]
\mathfrak R^{\mathrm{mod}}_{4,g,n}(\cA)
&=\operatorname{pr}_{4,g,n}(\Theta_\cA)
&&\text{(first nonlinear shadow)}.

The scalar $\kappa$ is the first Chern number of the modular
connection; $\Delta_\cA$ is the determinant class of the linearised

\section*{6.\quad The shadow Postnikov tower}
% ====================================================================

The finite-order truncations
\[
\Theta_\cA^{\le 2},\;
\Theta_\cA^{\le 3},\;
\Theta_\cA^{\le 4},\;\dots
\]
constitute the \emph{shadow Postnikov tower}: the modular
characteristic~$\kappa$ at arity~$2$, the cubic
shadow~$\mathfrak C(\cA)$ at arity~$3$, the quartic resonance
class~$\mathfrak Q(\cA)$ at arity~$4$.  At each level, the
obstruction class
\[
o_{r+1}(\cA)
\;:=\;
\bigl(D_\cA\Theta^{\le r}_\cA
+\tfrac12[\Theta^{\le r}_\cA,\Theta^{\le r}_\cA]\bigr)_{r+1}
\;\in\;
H^2(\cA^{\mathrm{sh}}_{r+1,0})
\]
measures the failure to extend.

When $H^1(F^3\mathfrak g/F^4\mathfrak g,d_2)=0$, the cubic term
is gauge-trivial and the quartic class
$[\mathfrak Q(\cA)]\in H^2(F^4/F^5,d_2)$ is canonical---the first
genuinely nonlinear invariant.  Its clutching law under
stable-curve degeneration follows from the log Fulton--MacPherson
compactification on snc pairs.

The shadow tower stratifies chiral algebras into four depth classes:
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{lccl}
\textbf{Class} & $r_{\max}$ & $\ell_3^{(0)}$,\;$\ell_4^{(0)}$
  & \textbf{Examples}\\
\hline
Gaussian & $2$ & $0,\;0$ & Heisenberg, free fermion\\
Lie/tree & $3$ & $\neq 0,\;0$ & Affine $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$\\
Contact/quartic & $4$ & $0,\;\neq 0$ & $\beta\gamma$ system\\
Mixed modular & $\infty$ & $\neq 0,\;\neq 0$
  & Virasoro, $\mathcal W_N$
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Shadow depth classifies the \emph{complexity} of a Koszul algebra,
not Koszulness itself: both finite-depth and infinite-depth algebras
are chirally Koszul.

At genus~$2$, the correction decomposes into three primitive shells:
\[
\Theta^{(2)}_\cA
\;=\;
\underbrace{\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}\circ\mathrm{loop}}}_{%
\text{iterated loop}}
+
\underbrace{\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}}_{%

Heisenberg sees only the first; affine $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$
sees the first two; Virasoro and $\mathcal W_N$ see all three.
Genus~$2$ is the first point where loops, clutching, and planted
forests interact genuinely rather than sequentially.

\section*{7.\quad The standard landscape}
% ====================================================================

\noindent\textbf{Heisenberg.}
The MC element collapses to a scalar:
$\Theta_{\mathcal H_k}=k\cdot\eta\otimes\Lambda$.  Every
projection reduces to~$\kappa=k$ times a universal form.  The
five-component differential collapses: $d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}=0$,
$d_{\mathrm{coll}}=0$, $d_{\mathrm{pf}}=0$.  Only
$d_{\check C}+d_{\mathrm{loop}}$ survive.  Shadow depth~$2$
(Gaussian).  The Koszul dual is the curved symmetric chiral algebra
$\mathcal H_k^!=(\operatorname{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*),\,m_0=-k\omega)$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Affine Kac--Moody.}
Both simple and double poles are present:
$J^a(z)J^b(w)\sim f^{ab}{}_c J^c(w)/(z-w)+k\kappa^{ab}/(z-w)^2$.
The bar differential acquires a bracket component from the simple
pole; the Borcherds identity replaces Jacobi.  Shadow depth~$3$
(Lie/tree): the cubic shadow is the Lie bracket, the quartic
obstruction vanishes by the Jacobi identity.  The Feigin--Frenkel
involution $k\leftrightarrow -k-2h^\vee$ is Koszul duality on
configuration spaces.  PBW concentration at all genera is proved:
$E_\infty^{p,q}(g)=0$ for $q\neq 0$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Virasoro.}
One generator~$T$ of conformal weight~$2$, with $T_{(1)}T=2T$
producing the cubic and the quartic contact shadow
$Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}=10/[c(5c+22)]$.  The quintic
obstruction
$o^{(5)}_{\mathrm{Vir}}=\{C_{\mathrm{Vir}},Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}\}_H\neq 0$
forces the shadow tower to be infinite.  Shadow depth~$\infty$
(mixed modular).  $\operatorname{Vir}_c^!=\operatorname{Vir}_{26-c}$.
The genus-$1$ Hessian correction
$\delta H^{(1)}_{\mathrm{Vir}}=120/[c^2(5c+22)]\cdot x^2$ and the
loop ratio $\rho^{(1)}_{\mathrm{Vir}}=240/[c^3(5c+22)]$ carry
information beyond~$\kappa=c/2$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{$\boldsymbol{\mathcal W_3}$.}
Two generators: stress tensor~$T$ (weight~$2$) and primary~$W$
(weight~$3$).  The $WW$~OPE contains the quasi-primary composite
\[
\Lambda\;=\;{:}TT{:}-\tfrac{3}{10}\partial^2 T
\]
at order $(z-w)^{-2}$ with coefficient $16/(22+5c)$.  The
quasi-primary~$\Lambda$ is the unique weight-$4$ composite orthogonal
to derivatives; the coefficient~$-3/10$ is fixed by requiring
$T(z)\Lambda(w)$ to have no third-order pole.  At $c=-22/5$,
$\Lambda$ becomes null and $\mathcal W_3$ is not finitely generated.
The quartic contact channel through~$\Lambda$ is the first
planted-forest correction: the bar-side avatar of the nonlinear
$WW$-bracket.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{$\boldsymbol{\beta\gamma}$.}
Weight-changing OPE:
$\beta(z)\gamma(w)\sim 1/(z-w)$.  Contact/quartic archetype:
$\ell_3^{(0)}=0$ on the weight line, $\ell_4^{(0)}\neq 0$.
The quartic contact invariant $\mu_{\beta\gamma}=0$ by rank-one
abelian rigidity.  Shadow depth~$4$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Lattice vertex algebras.}
$\kappa(V_\Lambda^{N,q})=\operatorname{rank}(\Lambda)$, independent
of cocycle.  Curvature and braiding are orthogonal.  Shadow
depth~$2$ (Gaussian/formal).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Yangians.}
The $E_1$-chiral atom: ordered configurations, $R$-matrices, the
Drinfeld--Kohno ladder.  Thick generation of the quantum group
category is proved in type~$A$ at all ranks via chromatic filtration
on prefundamental representations, Clebsch--Gordan closure, and
Efimov's categorical completion.  The Mittag-Leffler stabilisation
of the RTT tower yields completed bar-cobar at the H-level.  The
dg-shifted Yangian $Y_T^{\mathrm{mod}}$ is a pro-Maurer--Cartan
object: $R_T^{\mathrm{mod}}(z;\hbar)$ is an MC element in the
pronilpotent completion of the convolution dg Lie algebra.

\section*{8.\quad Scalar saturation and the Koszulness programme}
% ====================================================================

For all algebraic families with rational OPE coefficients---covering
the entire standard Lie-theoretic landscape at all non-critical
levels, including admissible ones---\emph{scalar saturation
universality} holds: the full MC element reduces to~$\kappa$ times a
universal tensor.  The proof uses Whitehead reduction and algebraic
semicontinuity.

Twelve conditions on a chiral algebra are conjecturally equivalent to
chiral Koszulness; four are proved:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item $A_\infty$-formality of bar cohomology implies Koszulness.
\item The shadow tower equals the $L_\infty$-formality obstruction
  tower at arities $2$, $3$, $4$.
\item $E_2$-formality of $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^*(\cA)$ for Koszul
  algebras.
\item Genus-$0$ Koszulness is independent of the curve~$X$.
\end{enumerate}
The PBW criterion is unconditional: every universal affine vertex
algebra $V_k(\mathfrak g)$ is chirally Koszul at every level.

\section*{9.\quad The Swiss-cheese world (Volume II)}
% ====================================================================

The bar differential lives in~$\mathbb C$: it extracts OPE residues
along collision divisors of $\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)$.  The
coproduct lives in~$\mathbb R$: it splits an ordered sequence of
tensor factors at a point in the topological variable.  A bar element
of degree~$k$ is parametrised by
$\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)\times\operatorname{Conf}_k(\mathbb R)$.
The differential sees the first factor.  The coproduct sees the
second.  This product is the operation space of the
\emph{holomorphic-topological Swiss-cheese operad}
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$, and the bar complex, equipped
with both structures, is an algebra over it.

$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ is homotopy-Koszul: Kontsevich
formality gives a quasi-isomorphism to the classical Swiss-cheese
operad, which is Koszul (Livernet, Voronov), and bar-cobar transfer
yields $\Omega\mathbf B(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})
\xrightarrow{\sim}\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$.  The bar-cobar
adjunction on $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebras is a Quillen
equivalence.

On $H^\bullet(A,Q)$, the regular part of~$m_2$ is a commutative
product, the singular part is a $\lambda$-bracket, and $m_{k\ge 3}$
vanishes by topological contractibility of~$\mathbb R$: the pair
$(\mu,\{-_\lambda-\})$ is a $(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex algebra.
Jacobi from Arnold on $\operatorname{FM}_3(\mathbb C)$; Leibniz from
three-face Stokes on $\partial\operatorname{FM}_3(\mathbb C)$.

The Koszul dual~$\cA^!$ governs line operators: $\cA^!$-mod is the
category of lines, and the spectral braiding
