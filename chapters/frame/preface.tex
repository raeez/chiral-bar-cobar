\section*{I.\quad The construction of $\Theta_\cA$}

\subsection*{From algebras to operads}

\noindent
The bar construction of an augmented associative algebra~$A$ is a
sum over \emph{sequences}: the differential graded coalgebra
$B(A)=(T^c(s^{-1}\bar A),\,d_B)$ whose differential contracts
adjacent pairs through the multiplication of~$A$.  The identity
$d_B^2=0$ is associativity: the two ways of contracting three
consecutive elements agree.  The cobar construction
$\Omega(C)=(T(s\bar C),\,d_\Omega)$ inverts the bar on the
\emph{Koszul locus}---the algebras for which
$\varepsilon\colon\Omega(B(A))\xrightarrow{\sim}A$.
This is Koszul duality over a point
(Priddy, Beilinson--Ginzburg--Soergel, Loday--Vallette).

An operad~$\cP$ has composition operations indexed not by sequences
but by \emph{trees}: connected genus-$0$ graphs.  The operadic bar
construction sums over all trees with $n$ leaves, the differential
contracts edges, and $d^2=0$ because every codimension-two boundary
of the associahedron is reached by exactly two edge contractions
with opposite signs.  The operadic Koszul duality
$\mathrm{Com}^!=\mathrm{Lie}$ implies that the
Chevalley--Eilenberg complex is the commutative-operad bar
construction (Ginzburg--Kapranov).

\subsection*{From operads to modular operads}

A \emph{modular operad} is an operad whose operations are indexed
not by trees but by \emph{connected stable graphs}---graphs of
arbitrary genus (Getzler--Kapranov).  A \emph{connected stable
graph}~$\Gamma$ has a genus label $g(v)\in\mathbb Z_{\ge 0}$ at
each vertex satisfying $2g(v)-2+\mathrm{val}(v)>0$, and total
genus $g(\Gamma)=b_1(\Gamma)+\sum_v g(v)$.  Trees are the genus-$0$
case.  A graph with one loop has genus~$1$.  Two loops, genus~$2$.

The passage from trees to graphs is the passage from genus~$0$ to
all genera.  A tree records the pattern of a sequence of binary
compositions.  A stable graph records a degeneration of a smooth
algebraic curve into a nodal curve.  The
vertices of~$\Gamma$ are the irreducible components, the edges are
the nodes, the genus labels are the genera of the components, and
the half-edges are the marked points.  The set of stable graphs
$\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}$ parametrises the boundary
stratification of the Deligne--Mumford moduli space
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.  Every codimension-one boundary divisor is
a one-edge degeneration; every codimension-two boundary is reached
by exactly two one-edge degenerations with cancelling orientations.

The \emph{Feynman transform} $F\cM$ of a modular operad~$\cM$ is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-feynman-sum}
F\cM(g,n)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}}
\frac{\hbar^{g(\Gamma)}}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\;\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}\cM\bigl(g(v),\mathrm{In}(v)\bigr)
\;\otimes\;\det\bigl(E(\Gamma)\bigr).
\end{equation}
Its differential contracts one internal edge; $d^2=0$
is the codimension-two cancellation on~$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.
At genus~$0$ (trees, $\hbar^0=1$) it reduces to the operadic bar
construction.

The Feynman transform is not a definition in isolation: it is the
coefficient object of a convolution algebra.
Given a chiral algebra~$\cA$ with bar complex~$\barB(\cA)$,
the \emph{modular convolution dg Lie algebra}
\[
\gAmod
\;:=\;
\Hom_\Sigma\bigl(F\Com,\;\End_{\barB(\cA)}\bigr)
\]
carries a dg Lie bracket from operadic composition, and its
Maurer--Cartan elements
$\Theta \in \operatorname{MC}(\gAmod)$
are in bijection with consistent genus towers.
The bar complex is an $F\Com$-algebra; the universal MC element
$\Theta_\cA := D_\cA - d_0$ is bar-intrinsic ($D_\cA^2 = 0$
implies $\Theta_\cA \in \operatorname{MC}$), and every theorem
in this monograph is a projection of~$\Theta_\cA$.

The associative variant replaces stable graphs by
\emph{ribbon graphs} (fatgraphs):
$\gAmod^{E_1} := \Hom_\Sigma(F\!\Ass,\,
\End_{\barB^{E_1}(\cA)})$.
This is the algebraic form of the 't~Hooft $1/N$ expansion.
Setting $\hbar = 1/N^2$, the MC element
$\Theta^{E_1}_\cA \in \operatorname{MC}(\gAmod^{E_1})$
is the genus expansion
$\sum_g N^{2-2g}F_g(\lambda)$ summed over ribbon graphs weighted
by the genus of their embedding surface.
Planar diagrams ($g=0$) dominate at large~$N$; the shadow
Postnikov tower $\Theta^{E_1,\le r}$ is the truncation to
$r$-point 't~Hooft interactions.
The coinvariant map $F\!\Ass \to F\Com$ projects
$\Theta^{E_1} \mapsto \Theta$:
colour-ordered amplitudes to full amplitudes.

\subsection*{From a point to a curve}

Over a point, the bar construction depends only on the algebra.
Over a smooth curve~$X$, it depends on the geometry of~$X$
through configuration spaces.  Place $n$ points on~$X$ and
compactify by the Fulton--MacPherson space
$\overline{C}_n(X)$---the iterated real oriented blowup along
diagonals.  The logarithmic $1$-forms
$\eta_{ij}:=d\log(z_i-z_j)$ on configuration space satisfy the
\emph{Arnold relation}
\[
\eta_{ij}\wedge\eta_{jk}
+\eta_{jk}\wedge\eta_{ki}
+\eta_{ki}\wedge\eta_{ij}
\;=\;0.
\]
The bar differential on~$X$ extracts residues of the chiral OPE
along the boundary divisors of~$\overline{C}_n(X)$; the Arnold
relation forces $d^2=0$ at genus~$0$---it is the
form-theoretic incarnation of the Borcherds identity.

At genus~$g\ge 1$ the configuration space fibres over
$\overline{\cM}_g$ and the fibrewise bar differential acquires
curvature from the period structure of the curve:
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\cA)\cdot\omega_g$, a scalar
$\kappa(\cA)$ times a tautological class~$\omega_g$.
The period corrections that restore nilpotence come from the
degeneration divisors of~$\overline{\cM}_g$---the
codimension-one boundary strata indexed by one-edge stable
graphs.  The genus tower is the tower of these corrections.

\subsection*{The graph-sum formula}

Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra
on~$X$.  For each stable graph
$\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}$, decorate every
vertex with the transferred chiral operation~$\Phi_v^\cA$, weight
the graph by a chain $W_\Gamma\in C_\bullet(\overline{\cM}_{g,n})$,
and contract internal edges by the chiral pairing.  The
\emph{modular bar construction} is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-modular-bar}
\barB^{(g)}_X(\cA)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\;W_\Gamma\;\otimes\;
\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}\Phi_v^\cA\,,
\end{equation}
and the \emph{universal Maurer--Cartan element}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-theta}
\Theta_\cA
\;=\;
\sum_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\;W_\Gamma\;\otimes\;\Phi_\Gamma^\cA
\;\in\;
\mathrm{MC}\bigl(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA\bigr)
\end{equation}
satisfies $D\Theta_\cA+\tfrac12[\Theta_\cA,\Theta_\cA]=0$
because $\partial^2=0$ on~$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.

\section*{II.\quad First consequences}

\subsection*{The Heisenberg algebra}

The Heisenberg algebra $\cH_k$ has OPE
$\alpha(z)\,\alpha(w)\sim k/(z-w)^2$---a double pole with no
simple pole.  The chiral bracket is abelian:
$\alpha_{(0)}\alpha=0$.  Every transferred operation beyond
arity~$2$ vanishes, so the only stable graphs that contribute
to~\eqref{eq:preface-theta} have all vertices of valence~$2$.
Stability forces $g(v)\ge 1$ at each bivalent vertex.  The
unique genus-$g$ graph with no legs is a necklace of $g$ loops,
each vertex carrying genus~$1$, each edge contracting the metric
$\langle\alpha,\alpha\rangle=k$.  The graph sum collapses:
\[
\Theta_{\cH_k}\;=\;k\cdot\eta\otimes\Lambda.
\]
A single scalar $\kappa(\cH_k)=k$ determines every projection:
$F_g(\cH_k)=k\cdot B_{2g}/(2g(2g-2))$,
and the generating function
$\sum_{g\ge 1}F_g\,\hbar^{2g-2}=k(\hat A(i\hbar)-1)$.

\subsection*{The Koszul dual and complementarity}

Bar cohomology $\cA^{\mathrm i}:=H^*(\barB(\cA))$ is a graded
coalgebra.  Its graded linear dual
$\cA^!:=(\cA^{\mathrm i})^\vee$ is the \emph{chiral Koszul
dual}---not the cobar, but a genuinely different algebra obtained
by Verdier duality:
$\mathbb D_{\mathrm{Ran}}\,\barB_X(\cA)\simeq\barB_X(\cA^!)$.
At genus~$g\ge 1$ the center local system splits:
\[
R\Gamma(\overline{\cM}_g,\,\cZ_\cA)
\;\simeq\;
Q_g(\cA)\;\oplus\;Q_g(\cA^!),
\qquad
\kappa(\cA)+\kappa(\cA^!)=0.
\]
For $\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k$: the dual is
$\widehat{\mathfrak g}_{-k-2h^\vee}$ (Feigin--Frenkel),
$\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)
=(k+h^\vee)\dim\mathfrak g/(2h^\vee)$,
and complementarity is $\kappa_k+\kappa_{-k-2h^\vee}=0$.

\subsection*{Curvature at genus $\boldsymbol{g \ge 1}$}

On a curve of genus~$g$, the prime-form kernel
$\varpi_{ij}=d_id_j\log E(z_i,z_j)$ replaces $\eta_{ij}$ and
has periods
\[
\oint_{A_\mu}\varpi_{ij}=0,
\qquad
\oint_{B_\mu}\varpi_{ij}=2\pi i\;\omega_\mu(z_j),
\]
where $\omega_1,\dots,\omega_g$ are normalised abelian
differentials.  The fibrewise bar differential built from these
forms satisfies
\[
d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA)\cdot\omega_g,
\qquad
\omega_g=c_1(\mathbb E)\in H^2(\overline{\cM}_g),
\]
with $\mathbb E=R^1\pi_*\cO_{\cC_g}$ the Hodge bundle.
Period corrections from the degeneration divisors
of~$\overline{\cM}_g$ restore $D_g^2=0$ on the total space.
Computed values:
\[
\kappa(\cH_k)=k,
\quad
\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2{}_k)=\frac{3(k+2)}{4},
\quad
\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c)=\frac{c}{2},
\quad
\kappa(\beta\gamma_\lambda)=6\lambda^2-6\lambda+1.
\]

\subsection*{The Virasoro shadow tower}

The Virasoro algebra $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ has OPE
$T(z)\,T(w)\sim\frac{c/2}{(z-w)^4}
+\frac{2T(w)}{(z-w)^2}+\frac{\partial T(w)}{z-w}$.
The cubic bracket $\ell_3^{(0)}$ is nonzero (the Jacobiator
homotopy $F_3$ does not vanish---the Lie bracket $T_{(0)}$ acts
nontrivially on itself).  The quartic contact invariant
\[
\mathfrak Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\frac{10}{c(5c+22)}
\]
is the first nonlinear shadow: it measures the obstruction to
extending the cubic truncation $\Theta_\cA^{\le 3}$ to arity~$4$,
and it arises from the quartic OPE contact stratum on
$\overline{C}_4(X)$.  At $c=0$ and $c=-22/5$ (the
$(2,5)$-minimal model) the invariant diverges---these are the
resonance points where the shadow tower reorganises.

At genus~$2$, the MC element decomposes into three shells:
\[
\Theta_{\mathrm{Vir}}^{(2)}
\;=\;
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}\circ\mathrm{loop}}
\;+\;
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}
\;+\;
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{pf}}.
\]
Iterated BV, mixed separating/loop clutching, and
planted-forest correction from codimension-$2$ log-FM
strata, respectively.
The quintic obstruction
$o^{(5)}_{\mathrm{Vir}}\neq 0$ forces the tower to continue:
Virasoro has infinite shadow depth.

The Koszul dual of $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ is $\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$:
$\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c)+\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c})
=c/2+(26-c)/2=13$, the unique standard family where
complementarity gives a nonzero constant.
Self-duality occurs at $c=13$.

\section*{III.\quad The modular homotopy type}

\subsection*{The modular convolution algebra}

The $L_\infty$-algebra
$\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA$ has brackets
\[
\ell_k^{(g)}
\;=\;
\sum_{\substack{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}\\
|V|=k,\;g(\Gamma)=g}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}\,\ell_\Gamma,
\]
where $\ell_\Gamma$ decorates vertices with $\Phi_v^\cA$,
contracts internal edges by the chiral pairing, and integrates
against $W_\Gamma$.  Trees give genus-$0$ brackets; one-loop
graphs give $\hbar\Delta$; the $L_\infty$ identity
$\sum\ell\circ\ell=0$ is the codimension-two cancellation
on $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.

\subsection*{Homotopy types: a construction in three stages}

\noindent\textsc{Stage 1: over a point.}\enspace
Take $A = \Bbbk[x]/(x^2)$, the exterior algebra on one
generator.  Its bar complex is $B(A) = T^c(sx)$: one
cogenerator~$sx$ in degree~$-1$, with
$d_B(sx|sx) = s(x \cdot x) = 0$ since $x^2 = 0$.  The bar
cohomology $H^{-1}(B(A)) = \Bbbk\cdot sx$ is one-dimensional.
Now take $A' = \Bbbk[x]/(x^3)$.  Its bar complex has the same
cogenerator~$sx$, but now
$d_B(sx|sx) = s(x^2) \neq 0$, so $H^{-1}(B(A'))$ receives a
contribution from $x^2$.  The two algebras $A$ and $A'$ have the
same underlying vector space $\Bbbk\{1, x\}$ at low degree, but
their bar complexes---and hence their deformation theories---differ.

Transfer the $A_\infty$-structure from $B(A')$ to its cohomology
via the homological perturbation lemma.  The result is a minimal
$A_\infty$-algebra $(H^*(B(A')), m_2, m_3, \ldots)$ where $m_2$
is the induced product on cohomology and $m_3$ is the first Massey
product: $m_3(\xi,\xi,\xi) = [x^2 \cdot x]$ in cohomology,
encoding the triple relation $x \cdot x \cdot x = 0$.  For $A$,
the transferred structure has $m_3 = 0$---formality.

The \emph{homotopy type} of~$A$ is the quasi-isomorphism class of
$(B(A), d_B)$ as a dg coalgebra, or equivalently the
$A_\infty$-isomorphism class of the minimal model.  Two algebras of
the same homotopy type have equivalent Maurer--Cartan moduli:
$\mathrm{MC}(\mathrm{Hoch}^*(A) \otimes R)/{\sim}\; \simeq\;
\mathrm{MC}(\mathrm{Hoch}^*(A') \otimes R)/{\sim}$ for every local
Artin~$R$.  An algebra is \emph{formal} when $m_n = 0$ for all
$n \ge 3$: the homotopy type is determined by the cohomology
algebra alone.  Koszul algebras are formal in this sense.

\smallskip
\noindent\textsc{Stage 2: over a manifold.}\enspace
Sullivan replaces algebra with topology.  For a simply connected
space~$X$, the de~Rham algebra $\Omega^*(X)$ is a commutative dga.
Its minimal model is a free commutative dga
$(\Lambda V, d)$ with $V$ graded, $d$ decomposable, and a
quasi-isomorphism $(\Lambda V, d) \xrightarrow{\sim} \Omega^*(X)$.
The generators of~$V$ in degree~$n$ are the dual rational homotopy
groups $\mathrm{Hom}(\pi_n(X),\mathbb{Q})$; the differential~$d$
encodes Whitehead products at quadratic order and Massey products at
higher orders.  Two spaces have the same rational homotopy type iff
their minimal models are isomorphic.

K\"ahler manifolds are formal: $d = 0$ on the minimal model, and
the rational homotopy type is determined by the cohomology ring
$H^*(X;\mathbb{Q})$ (Deligne--Griffiths--Morgan--Sullivan).  The
Heisenberg algebra is formal in the same sense: its shadow tower
terminates at arity~$2$, all higher obstructions vanish, and the
modular homotopy type is determined by the scalar~$\kappa$ alone.

\smallskip
\noindent\textsc{Stage 3: over a curve.}\enspace
For a chiral algebra~$\cA$ on a curve~$X$, the controlling
$L_\infty$-algebra is no longer Hochschild or de~Rham but the
modular convolution algebra $\gAmod$ constructed above.  The
passage from Stage~1 to Stage~3 enlarges the indexing graphs from
\emph{sequences} (the bar complex of an associative algebra sums
over sequences of elements) through \emph{trees} (the operadic bar
construction sums over planar trees) to \emph{connected stable
graphs of arbitrary genus} (the modular bar construction sums over
all stable graphs on~$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$).  The differential
changes accordingly: from contraction of adjacent pairs
(associativity) through contraction of tree edges (operadic
composition) to contraction of graph edges (clutching of stable
curves).  At each stage, $d^2 = 0$ is a codimension-two
cancellation---first in the associahedron, then in the moduli
space.

The $A_\infty$-products $m_3, m_4, \ldots$ of Stage~1 become the
modular $L_\infty$-brackets $\ell_3^{(g)}, \ell_4^{(g)}, \ldots$
of Stage~3, indexed by genus.  Formality ($m_n = 0$ for $n \ge 3$)
becomes the Gaussian archetype ($\ell_n^{(g)} = 0$ for $n \ge 3$
at all~$g$).  The shadow Postnikov tower is the modular Massey
product tower: its depth---the first arity at which
$\ell_n \ne 0$---classifies the complexity of the modular
homotopy type.

\subsection*{The modular homotopy type}

Consider a first-order deformation $\alpha \in \gAmod \otimes
\varepsilon\Bbbk$ at genus~$0$.  The Maurer--Cartan equation is
$D_0\alpha + \tfrac{1}{2}[\alpha,\alpha]_0 = 0$, where the
subscript~$0$ restricts to tree-level graphs.  For the Heisenberg
algebra, $[\alpha,\alpha]_0 = 0$ (the bracket is abelian at
genus~$0$), and $D_0\alpha = 0$ is automatic: the deformation is
unobstructed.

Now ask whether this deformation extends to genus~$1$.  The
genus-$1$ MC equation acquires a new term from the single self-loop
graph $\Gamma_{\mathrm{loop}}$ (one vertex of genus~$0$ with one
self-edge):
\[
D_0\alpha_1 + [\alpha_0, \alpha_1]_0
+ \hbar\,\Delta\alpha_0 = 0,
\]
where $\Delta = \ell_{\Gamma_{\mathrm{loop}}}$ is the BV operator
tracing the cyclic pairing over the self-edge.  The obstruction is
$[\hbar\,\Delta\alpha_0] \in H^1(\mathfrak{g}_{\cA}^{\mathrm{mod},\,g=1})$: if it is nonzero,
no genus-$1$ extension exists.  For the Heisenberg algebra,
$\Delta\alpha_0$ is a scalar multiple of the Hodge
class~$\omega_1$, and the extension $\alpha_1 = \kappa \cdot
\eta \otimes \Lambda$ solves the equation.  The deformation
survives.

For a non-abelian chiral algebra $\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k$,
the bracket term $[\alpha_0, \alpha_1]_0 \ne 0$ contributes at
genus~$1$: trees with one loop glued on interact with the
tree-level Lie bracket.  Both the BV term and the bracket term must
cancel simultaneously.  At genus~$2$, three graph types contribute
(iterated loop, separating-plus-loop, planted forest), and the MC
equation at genus~$2$ demands coherence of all three.  The
\emph{modular homotopy type} is the formal moduli problem that
encodes these coherence conditions at all genera simultaneously:
not just "does a deformation exist?" but "does it persist through
every stable-curve degeneration?"

The genus-$0$ homotopy type---the MC moduli of tree-level
$L_\infty$-brackets alone---sees only operator compositions.  It
cannot detect the curvature $\kappa(\cA)$ (a genus-$1$ invariant),
the cubic shadow $\mathfrak{C}(\cA)$ (which involves one-loop
graphs), or the quartic resonance class $\mathfrak{Q}(\cA)$
(which involves planted-forest corrections from the log-FM
compactification).  The modular homotopy type sees all of these:
its brackets range over all stable graphs, and its
$L_\infty$-identities are the codimension-two cancellations on
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.

The \emph{modular homotopy type} of~$\cA$ is the resulting formal
moduli problem:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:preface-modular-homotopy-type}
\mathcal M^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA(R)
\;:=\;
\mathrm{MC}\bigl(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA
\,\widehat\otimes_{\mathfrak m}\,R\bigr)\big/\!\sim
\end{equation}
for local Artin dg algebras~$R$.
This is the derived deformation theory of~$\cA$ \emph{as a
modular-operad algebra}: it classifies infinitesimal deformations
of~$\cA$ that persist coherently across all genera and all numbers
of insertions.  The Maurer--Cartan equation
$D\alpha + \tfrac{1}{2}[\alpha,\alpha] = 0$ in
$\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA\,\widehat\otimes\,R$ says that the
deformation is compatible with every stable-curve degeneration
simultaneously; the gauge equivalence~$\sim$ identifies deformations
connected by a path through the modular operad's gluing maps.

Two chiral algebras have the same modular homotopy type if and only
if their modular bar constructions are connected by a zigzag of
quasi-isomorphisms of modular-operad coalgebras.  The modular
homotopy type is therefore the finest invariant that the bar
construction can see.

The universal MC element
$\Theta_\cA\in\mathrm{MC}(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA)$ of
equation~\eqref{eq:preface-theta} is the \emph{basepoint} of this
moduli problem: it is the canonical deformation of the genus-$0$ bar
differential to all genera, and every named invariant of this
monograph is a shadow of~$\Theta_\cA$ under a projection:

The Heisenberg algebra has the simplest possible modular
homotopy type: $\Theta_{\cH_k}=k\cdot\eta\otimes\Lambda$,
a single formal line through~$k$ with no higher corrections.
Affine Kac--Moody has a cubic correction from the Lie bracket;
$\beta\gamma$ has a quartic correction from~$m_3$;
Virasoro has corrections at all arities.
The modular homotopy type distinguishes these:
their scalar invariants may coincide
($\kappa(\cH_1) = \kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_2) = 1$),
but their shadow towers differ
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-separation}).

\subsection*{Completion}

The Heisenberg algebra has one generator.  Affine Kac--Moody
$\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k$ has $\dim\mathfrak{g}$ generators.
The principal $\mathcal{W}_N$-algebra has $N-1$.  The inverse
limit $\mathcal{W}_\infty = \varprojlim_N \mathcal{W}_N$ has
infinitely many---one in each conformal weight $2, 3, 4, \ldots$

The bar-cobar quasi-isomorphism $\Omega(\barB(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$
is proved for each finite-type stage $\mathcal{W}_N$.  The passage
to the inverse limit requires completed bar-cobar: a filtration
$F^0\cA \supset F^1\cA \supset \cdots$ that makes the bar
differential continuous and the Mittag--Leffler condition
automatic.  The strong filtration axiom
$\mu_r(F^{i_1},\dots,F^{i_r}) \subset F^{i_1+\cdots+i_r}$
achieves this in one stroke: on each quotient $\cA_{\le N}$ only
arities $r \le N$ survive, so the completed differential is
quotientwise finite and the quasi-isomorphism passes to the limit
(Theorem~\ref{thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong}).  The completion
closure $\CompCl(\cF_{\mathrm{ft}})$---the class of all such
inverse limits---carries a quasi-inverse bar-cobar equivalence
stable under Maurer--Cartan twisting.

The completed bar complex decomposes into \emph{finite windows}.
Define the \emph{reduced weight} $\rho(a) := \mathrm{wt}(a) - 1$
for a field of conformal weight $\mathrm{wt}(a)$, and extend
additively to bar words: $\rho(sa_1|\cdots|sa_\ell) = \sum_i
\rho(a_i)$.  The \emph{$q$-window} $K_q(\cA)$ is the span of
bar words of reduced weight $\le q$.

For Virasoro at $q = 2$: the window $K_2(\mathrm{Vir})$ has
basis $\{sT,\; sT|sT\}$---one letter of reduced weight~$1$ and
one two-letter word of total reduced weight~$2$.  The bar
differential on this two-dimensional complex is
$d(sT|sT) = s(T \cdot T)$.  The product $T \cdot T$ decomposes
into the quasi-primary composite $\Lambda = :(TT): -
\tfrac{3}{10}\partial^2 T$, derivatives $\partial^2 T$, and the
central term $\tfrac{c}{2}\cdot\mathbf{1}$.  But $\Lambda$ has
conformal weight~$4$ (reduced weight~$3 > 2$) and $\partial^2 T$
has reduced weight~$3 > 2$, so both project to zero in $K_2$.
Only the central term survives:
\[
d(sT|sT)\big|_{K_2} \;=\; \frac{c}{2}\cdot s\mathbf{1}.
\]
This is $\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c) = c/2$ emerging from a
$2 \times 1$ matrix computation.  The curvature is not an abstract
scalar extracted from a theorem; it is the sole surviving
coefficient of the bar differential on the smallest nontrivial
window.

Each window $K_q(\cA)$ depends only on fields of conformal
weight $\le q+1$: a letter in the window has $\mathrm{wt}(a) \le
q+1$, and the bar differential cannot create higher-weight letters
because the OPE estimate

\subsection*{Bar and cobar over a point}

Let $A$ be an augmented associative algebra over a field~$\Bbbk$,
with augmentation $\varepsilon\colon A\to\Bbbk$ and augmentation
ideal~$\bar A=\ker\varepsilon$.  The \emph{bar construction} is the
differential graded coalgebra
\[
B(A)
\;=\;
\bigl(T^c(s^{-1}\bar A),\;d_B\bigr),
\]
where $T^c(V)=\bigoplus_{n\ge 0}V^{\otimes n}$ is the cofree
conilpotent coalgebra and $s^{-1}$ is the desuspension:
$|s^{-1}a|=|a|-1$.  The coproduct is deconcatenation,
\[
\Delta(s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n)
\;=\;
\sum_{i=0}^{n}
(s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_i)
\otimes
(s^{-1}a_{i+1}|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n),
\]
and the differential acts on cogenerators by contracting adjacent
pairs through the multiplication of~$A$:
\[
d_B(s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n)
\;=\;
\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}
(-1)^{|a_1|+\cdots+|a_i|+i}\,
s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}(a_i\cdot a_{i+1})|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n.
\]
Cohomological convention throughout: $|d|=+1$.

Dually, for a coaugmented conilpotent coalgebra~$C$ with
coaugmentation coideal~$\bar C$, the \emph{cobar construction} is
the free algebra on the suspension:
\[
\Omega(C)
\;=\;
\bigl(T(s\bar C),\;d_\Omega\bigr),
\qquad
d_\Omega(sc_1|\cdots|sc_n)
\;=\;
-\sum_{i=1}^{n}
(-1)^{\epsilon_i}\,
sc_1|\cdots|sc'_i|sc''_i|\cdots|sc_n,
\]
where $\bar\Delta(c_i)=\sum c'_i\otimes c''_i$ is the reduced
coproduct and $\epsilon_i=|c_1|+\cdots+|c_{i-1}|+i-1$.  The sign
arises from permuting~$s$ past the preceding suspended elements.

The adjunction $B\dashv\Omega$ is the bar-cobar adjunction.  The
\emph{comparison map} $\varepsilon\colon\Omega(B(A))\to A$ is the
counit: it sends $s(s^{-1}a)\mapsto a$ on tensor-degree~$1$ and
vanishes in higher degrees.  The algebra~$A$ is \emph{Koszul} when
$\varepsilon$ is a quasi-isomorphism---the cobar of the bar recovers
$A$ up to homotopy.

\subsection*{Quadratic duality}

A \emph{quadratic algebra} is $A=T(V)/(R)$ for a vector space~$V$ and
a subspace $R\subseteq V\otimes V$ of quadratic relations.  Its
\emph{quadratic dual} is
\[
A^!\;=\;T(V^*)/(R^\perp),
\qquad
R^\perp\;=\;\bigl\{\xi\in V^*\otimes V^*
\;\big|\;\xi(r)=0\;\text{for all}\;r\in R\bigr\}.
\]
When~$A$ is Koszul, $A^!$ is the algebra governing the minimal
resolution: $B(A)$ has cohomology concentrated in the diagonal
degrees, and $H^*(B(A))\cong (A^!)^\vee$ as a graded coalgebra.

The operadic instances: $\mathrm{Com}^!=\mathrm{Lie}$ and
$\mathrm{Sym}^!=\Lambda$.  Koszulness of the commutative operad
implies that the Chevalley--Eilenberg complex of a Lie algebra is a
commutative dga---the bar construction of the enveloping algebra~$U\mathfrak g$,
restricted along $\mathrm{Com}\to\mathrm{Assoc}$, recovers the
Lie algebra cochain complex.  When Koszulness fails, the full
cobar-bar resolution $\Omega(B(A))\xrightarrow{\sim}A$ is the
universal, infinite, replacement.

\subsection*{Chiral algebras on a curve}

Let $X$ be a smooth algebraic curve over~$\mathbb C$.  A
\emph{chiral algebra}~$\cA$ on~$X$ is a $\mathcal D_X$-module
equipped with a \emph{chiral bracket}
\[
\mu^{\mathrm{ch}}
\;\colon\;
j_*j^*(\cA\boxtimes\cA)
\;\longrightarrow\;
\Delta_!(\cA),
\]
where $j\colon X\times X\setminus\Delta\hookrightarrow X\times X$
is the inclusion of the complement of the diagonal and
$\Delta_!\cA$ is the $!$-pushforward along the diagonal embedding
$\Delta\colon X\hookrightarrow X\times X$.  The source
$j_*j^*(\cA\boxtimes\cA)$ captures sections of the external
product with poles of arbitrary order along~$\Delta$; the target
$\Delta_!\cA$ extracts the residue---the $\delta$-function
component supported on~$\Delta$.

In local coordinates, the chiral bracket encodes the \emph{operator
product expansion}: for sections~$a,b\in\cA$,
\[
a(z)\,b(w)
\;\sim\;
\sum_{n\ge 0}\frac{a_{(n)}b(w)}{(z-w)^{n+1}}\,.
\]
The $n$-th products $a_{(n)}b\in\cA$ are the \emph{Fourier modes}
of the bracket.  The zeroth product $a_{(0)}b$ is the Lie-type
bracket (first-order pole); the first product $a_{(1)}b$
contributes the second-order pole, carrying the symmetric/metric
data.  All higher products $(n\ge 2)$ are determined by the
$\mathcal D_X$-module structure, i.e.\ by translation covariance.

The \emph{Borcherds identity}, coupling all pole orders in a single
algebraic constraint:
\[
\boxed{
\sum_{j\ge 0}\binom{m}{j}(a_{(n+j)}b)_{(m+k-j)}c
\;=\;
\sum_{j\ge 0}(-1)^j\binom{n}{j}
\Bigl(
a_{(m+n-j)}(b_{(k+j)}c)
-(-1)^n b_{(n+k-j)}(a_{(m+j)}c)
\Bigr).
}
\]
At $n=0$: the Jacobi identity for the Lie bracket~$a_{(0)}$.
At $m=0$: the commutator formula.  At $n=1$, $m=k=0$:
the derivation property of $a_{(0)}$ with respect to $a_{(1)}b$.
The identity is simultaneously the locality axiom, the Jacobi
identity, and the associativity constraint.  A chiral algebra is
\emph{exactly} a $\mathcal D_X$-module whose $n$-th products
satisfy the Borcherds identity.

\subsection*{The bar complex on~$X$}

Place sections $a_1,\dots,a_n\in\cA$ at distinct points
$z_1,\dots,z_n\in X$.  Assign to each ordered pair $(i,j)$ the
logarithmic $1$-form
\[
\eta_{ij}
\;:=\;
d\log(z_i-z_j)
\;=\;
\frac{dz_i-dz_j}{z_i-z_j}\,.
\]
For a planar tree~$T$ on $\{1,\dots,n\}$ with edge set~$E(T)$,
form the decorated tensor
\[
\omega_T(a_1,\dots,a_n)
\;:=\;
a_1(z_1)\otimes\cdots\otimes a_n(z_n)
\;\otimes\;
\bigwedge_{(i,j)\in E(T)}\eta_{ij}\,.
\]
The form $\bigwedge_{(i,j)\in E(T)}\eta_{ij}$ is a section of
$\Omega^{n-1}_{\log}$ on the configuration space
$\mathrm{Conf}_n(X)$---its degree is~$|E(T)|=n-1$ since~$T$ is a
tree.

The Fulton--MacPherson compactification
$\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_n(X)$ resolves $\mathrm{Conf}_n(X)$ by
iterated real oriented blowup along all diagonal strata.  This is a
\emph{blowup}, not the complement of the diagonal: points of
$\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_n(X)\setminus\mathrm{Conf}_n(X)$ record
the limiting direction, rate, and relative scales of approach as
points collide.  The boundary is a manifold with corners; the
codimension-one faces are indexed by subsets $S\subseteq\{1,\dots,n\}$
with $|S|\ge 2$, recording which points have collided.

The \emph{chiral bar complex} on~$X$:
\[
\barB_X(\cA)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{n\ge 1}
\bigl(s^{-1}\bar\cA\bigr)^{\otimes n}_{\Sigma_n}
\;\otimes\;
H^{n-1}\bigl(\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_n(X),\;
\Omega^{n-1}_{\log}\bigr).
\]
This is a graded coalgebra---the coproduct is the dual of the
operad composition on trees---equipped with a differential
$d_{\barB}$ that decomposes into three pieces:
\[
d_{\barB}
\;=\;
d_{\mathrm{int}}+d_{\mathrm{res}}+d_{\mathrm{dR}}\,.
\]
The three components:
\begin{itemize}
\item $d_{\mathrm{int}}$: the internal differential of~$\cA$
(the translation operator, acting on each tensor factor).
\item $d_{\mathrm{res}}$: the \emph{residue differential}. For
a codimension-one boundary face $D_S$ of
$\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_n(X)$ corresponding to the collision
$\{z_i\}_{i\in S}\to z_0$, the residue along~$D_S$ extracts
the OPE coefficient: the iterated contraction
$a_{i_1(n_1)}(a_{i_2(n_2)}(\cdots))$ at the pole orders
dictated by the form $\bigwedge\eta_{ij}$.  In formulas: for
$S=\{i,j\}$,
\[
d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(ij)}
\bigl(
a_1\otimes\cdots\otimes a_n\otimes\eta_{ij}\wedge\omega'
\bigr)
\;=\;
\pm\,
a_1\otimes\cdots\otimes
\widehat{a_i}\otimes\cdots\otimes
\widehat{a_j}\otimes\cdots\otimes
a_{i(p)}a_j
\;\otimes\;
\omega',
\]
where $p$ is the pole order read from the form-theoretic
coefficient, and hats denote omission.
\item $d_{\mathrm{dR}}$: the de~Rham differential on the
logarithmic forms on~$\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_n(X)$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{The proof that $d_{\barB}^2=0$}

Expand $(d_{\mathrm{int}}+d_{\mathrm{res}}+d_{\mathrm{dR}})^2$
into nine terms.  The diagonal terms: $d_{\mathrm{int}}^2=0$
(since~$\cA$ is a cochain complex), $d_{\mathrm{dR}}^2=0$
(de~Rham), and $d_{\mathrm{res}}^2$ must be controlled.  The
cross-terms $d_{\mathrm{int}}\,d_{\mathrm{dR}}+
d_{\mathrm{dR}}\,d_{\mathrm{int}}=0$ by
the Leibniz rule.  The remaining six terms pair into three
cancellation mechanisms, governed by the combinatorics of index
overlap:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item \emph{Disjoint supports.}\; When $\{i,j\}\cap\{k,l\}
=\emptyset$, the boundary faces~$D_{\{i,j\}}$
and~$D_{\{k,l\}}$ are transverse in
$\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_n(X)$.  The corresponding residue
operators commute, so the cross-terms cancel:
$d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(ij)}\,d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(kl)}
+d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(kl)}\,d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(ij)}=0$.
This is the operadic associativity of disjoint contractions.
\item \emph{Shared index.}\; When
$\{i,j\}\cap\{k,l\}=\{j=k\}$, the triple collision
$z_i=z_j=z_l$ lies in a codimension-two stratum.  The
form-theoretic contribution involves
$\eta_{ij}\wedge\eta_{jl}$.  The \emph{Arnold relation}
\[
\eta_{ij}\wedge\eta_{jl}
+\eta_{jl}\wedge\eta_{li}
+\eta_{li}\wedge\eta_{ij}
\;=\;0
\]
identifies this with a cyclic sum over the three ordered
approaches to the triple-collision point.  On the algebraic
side, the three terms produce
$(a_{i(m)}a_j)_{(n)}a_l$, $(a_{j(m)}a_l)_{(n)}a_i$,
$(a_{l(m)}a_i)_{(n)}a_j$ (with appropriate pole orders),
whose sum vanishes by the Borcherds identity.  The Arnold
relation kills the form; the Borcherds identity kills the
algebra.  Neither alone suffices---both are needed, and both
are \emph{exactly} consumed.
\item \emph{Same pair.}\;
$d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(ij)}\,d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(ij)}=0$ by the
sign rule on desuspensions: extracting a residue twice at the
same diagonal is zero for degree reasons.
\end{enumerate}

The result: $d_{\barB}^2=0$ if and only if the $n$-th products
satisfy the Borcherds identity.  The bar construction accepts
exactly chiral algebras.

\subsection*{The categorical logarithm}

The bar construction is a categorical logarithm.  The fundamental
property of $\log$---that $\log(ab)=\log a+\log b$---becomes:
\[
B(\cA\otimes\cA')
\;\simeq\;
B(\cA)\oplus B(\cA').
\]
The bar functor sends tensor products to direct sums.  The
logarithmic form $\eta_{ij}=d\log(z_i-z_j)$ is its integral
kernel.  The Arnold relation
\[
\eta_{ij}\wedge\eta_{jk}
+\eta_{jk}\wedge\eta_{ki}
+\eta_{ki}\wedge\eta_{ij}
\;=\;0
\]
is the cocycle condition for $\log$: the three-term identity states
that $d\log$ takes values in a commutative algebra ($1$-forms on
configuration space satisfy the Arnold algebra relations), so that
the logarithm is well-defined on products.

The cobar functor $\Omega(\barB(\cA))$ is the exponential.  The
domain on which the exponential inverts the logarithm---the
\emph{radius of convergence}---is the Koszul locus: the full
subcategory of chiral algebras for which
\[
\varepsilon\colon\Omega(\barB(\cA))
\;\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}\;
\cA.
\]
Outside the Koszul locus, the cobar-bar resolution still exists as
a free resolution---but it is infinite, carrying higher homotopies
that measure the failure of the exponential to converge.

\subsection*{Verdier duality and the Koszul dual}

Verdier duality on $\mathrm{Ran}(X)$ interchanges the bar
complex of~$\cA$ with the bar complex of a second chiral
algebra~$\cA^!$, the \emph{chiral Koszul dual}:
\[
\mathbb D_{\mathrm{Ran}}\,\barB_X(\cA)
\;\simeq\;
\barB_X(\cA^!).
\]
The dual~$\cA^!$ is obtained by a two-step procedure.  First, take
the bar cohomology:
\[
\cA^{\mathrm i}
\;:=\;
H^*\bigl(B(\cA)\bigr),
\]
a graded coalgebra (the coproduct of $B(\cA)$ descends to
cohomology).  Second, apply graded linear duality:
\[
\cA^!
\;:=\;
\bigl(\cA^{\mathrm i}\bigr)^\vee.
\]
This is the Koszul dual algebra---not the cobar.  The distinction is
essential: $\Omega(B(\cA))\simeq\cA$ is bar-cobar \emph{inversion},
recovering the original algebra; $\cA^!$ is obtained by
\emph{Verdier/linear duality}, producing a genuinely different algebra
except in the self-dual case.

When~$\cA$ is chirally Koszul, $B(\cA)$ has cohomology concentrated
in a single row, $\cA^{\mathrm i}$ is a coalgebra with a unique
grading, and $\cA^!$ governs a linear (quadratic) resolution
of~$\cA$.  The bar-to-cobar spectral sequence collapses at~$E_2$.
Classical Koszul duality
(Priddy, Beilinson--Ginzburg--Soergel, Loday--Vallette) is
recovered on $X=\mathbb A^1$: the affine line is contractible,
so $\overline{C}_n(\mathbb A^1)$ deformation-retracts onto
a point, the FM chain cooperad becomes quasi-isomorphic to the
associative cooperad, and the bar construction reduces to the
classical bar complex of the underlying Lie algebra.

\subsection*{The Heisenberg atom}

The Heisenberg chiral algebra $\mathcal H_k$ is generated by a
single field~$\alpha$ of conformal weight~$1$ with OPE
\[
\alpha(z)\,\alpha(w)
\;\sim\;
\frac{k}{(z-w)^2}\,.
\]
Only the first product is nonzero: $\alpha_{(1)}\alpha=k\cdot\mathbf 1$
and $\alpha_{(n)}\alpha=0$ for $n\neq 1$.  No first-order pole:
$\alpha_{(0)}\alpha=0$.  The Borcherds identity is satisfied
trivially: all triple products vanish since the only nontrivial
product is $\alpha_{(1)}\alpha\in\Bbbk\cdot\mathbf 1$, and
$\mathbf 1_{(n)}=0$ for $n\ge 0$.

The bar complex at tensor degree~$n$:
\[
\barB^n_X(\mathcal H_k)
\;=\;
(s^{-1}\alpha)^{\otimes n}_{\Sigma_n}
\;\otimes\;
H^{n-1}\bigl(\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_n(X),\;
\Omega^{n-1}_{\log}\bigr).
\]
\emph{Tensor degree~$1$}: the space is $s^{-1}\alpha$, one-dimensional,
with $d_{\barB}=d_{\mathrm{int}}=0$.

\emph{Tensor degree~$2$}: the bar component is
$(s^{-1}\alpha)^{\otimes 2}\otimes\langle\eta_{12}\rangle$.
The residue differential acts:
\[
d_{\mathrm{res}}(s^{-1}\alpha\otimes s^{-1}\alpha
\otimes\eta_{12})
\;=\;
k\cdot s^{-1}\mathbf 1.
\]
The form~$\eta_{12}$ has a simple pole along the diagonal;
extracting the residue produces the OPE coefficient~$k$.

\emph{Tensor degree~$n\ge 3$}: every residue at a codimension-one
collision stratum $D_{\{i,j\}}$ extracts $\alpha_{(1)}\alpha
=k\cdot\mathbf 1$ on the pair $(i,j)$.  The remaining $n-2$
factors are~$\alpha$; since $\mathbf 1_{(p)}\alpha=0$ for
$p\ge 0$, no further contractions occur.  All structure is
captured at tensor degree~$2$: the bar complex is
\emph{quadratic}.  The Heisenberg algebra is chirally Koszul.

The modular characteristic:
\[
\kappa(\mathcal H_k)\;=\;k.
\]
The Koszul dual is the \emph{curved} symmetric chiral algebra
\[
\mathcal H_k^!
\;=\;
\bigl(\mathrm{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*),\;m_0=-k\omega\bigr),
\]
where $V^*$ is the $\Bbbk$-linear dual of the generating space and
$m_0=-k\omega$ is the curvature: a degree-$2$ element satisfying
the curved $A_\infty$ relation
\[
m_1^{\,2}(a)\;=\;[m_0,a]
\]
(commutator, with the minus sign).  The differential $m_1$ does not
square to zero; its failure is controlled by the curvature.  The
curvature~$m_0$ is the bar-dual of the OPE coefficient~$k$:
the algebra side has a scalar OPE; the dual side has a scalar
curvature.

\subsection*{The Kac--Moody OPE}

The affine Kac--Moody algebra $\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k$ at level~$k$
is generated by currents $J^a$, $a=1,\dots,\dim\mathfrak g$, with
OPE
\[
J^a(z)\,J^b(w)
\;\sim\;
\frac{f^{ab}{}_{c}\,J^c(w)}{z-w}
\;+\;
\frac{k\,\kappa^{ab}}{(z-w)^2}\,,
\]
where $f^{ab}{}_{c}$ are the structure constants of the simple Lie
algebra~$\mathfrak g$ and $\kappa^{ab}$ is the Killing form
normalised so that long roots have squared length~$2$.  Both poles
are present: the first-order pole carries the Lie bracket
$J^a_{(0)}J^b=f^{ab}{}_{c}J^c$; the second-order pole carries
the invariant inner product
$J^a_{(1)}J^b=k\,\kappa^{ab}\cdot\mathbf 1$.  The bar complex
of $\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k$ therefore has nontrivial contributions
at all tensor degrees: the first-order pole generates structure
constants at tensor degree~$n$ from tree-level OPE contractions,
and the second-order pole contributes the metric/curvature data.

The bracket and curvature decomposition:
\[
\mu^{\mathrm{ch}}
\;=\;
\underbrace{f^{ab}{}_{c}\,J^c\cdot\eta_{12}}_{\text{Lie bracket
(first-order pole)}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{k\,\kappa^{ab}\cdot\mathbf 1\cdot
d\eta_{12}}_{\text{inner product (second-order pole)}}.
\]
The Lie bracket contributes the tree-level bar differential
(combinatorics of the Chevalley--Eilenberg complex); the inner
product contributes the curvature~$\kappa$.  The interplay between
these two poles---qualitatively different from the pure
second-order-pole Heisenberg case---is what makes
$\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k$ the first nontrivial example of the
theory.

\subsection*{The genus-$g$ propagator}

At genus~$0$, the difference $z_1-z_2$ is a globally defined
function on $\mathbb P^1\times\mathbb P^1$ (away from $\infty$),
and $\eta_{12}=d\log(z_1-z_2)$ is single-valued.  At genus
$g\ge 1$, $z_1-z_2$ has no global meaning: the curve~$X$ is
compact, and any meromorphic function with a single simple zero
must also have a simple pole.

The replacement is the \emph{prime form} $E(z_1,z_2)$: a section
of $K^{-1/2}\boxtimes K^{-1/2}$ on $X\times X$, vanishing to
first order along the diagonal $\Delta$, with no other zeros.  The
bundle is $K^{-1/2}\boxtimes K^{-1/2}$, \emph{not}
$K^{+1/2}\boxtimes K^{+1/2}$: the prime form is a
$(-\frac12,-\frac12)$-differential.  In a local coordinate where
the diagonal is $z_1=z_2$,
\[
E(z_1,z_2)
\;=\;
(z_1-z_2)\cdot\bigl(1+O(z_1-z_2)\bigr)
\cdot(dz_1)^{-1/2}(dz_2)^{-1/2}.
\]

The prime form has nontrivial monodromy.  Analytic continuation
around the $B$-cycle $B_j$ in the variable~$z_1$:
\[
E(z_1+B_j,z_2)
\;=\;
-\exp\bigl(
-\pi i\Omega_{jj}-2\pi i\textstyle\int_{z_2}^{z_1}\omega_j
\bigr)
\cdot E(z_1,z_2),
\]
where $\Omega=(\Omega_{jk})$ is the period matrix of~$X$
($\Omega_{jk}=\oint_{B_k}\omega_j$) and
$\omega_1,\dots,\omega_g$ are the holomorphic differentials
normalised by $\oint_{A_j}\omega_k=\delta_{jk}$.  The
$A$-cycle monodromy is trivial: $E(z_1+A_j,z_2)=E(z_1,z_2)$.

The logarithmic derivative
\[
\omega(z_1,z_2)
\;:=\;
d_{z_1}\log E(z_1,z_2)
\]
is the fundamental meromorphic differential of the second kind:
symmetric, a double pole on~$\Delta$, vanishing $A$-periods.  The
\emph{genus-$g$ propagator}:
\[
\eta^{(g)}_{12}
\;=\;
d\log E(z_1,z_2)
\;+\;
\pi\sum_{j,k=1}^{g}
(\mathrm{Im}\,\Omega)^{-1}_{jk}\,
\mathrm{Im}\!\Bigl(\int_{z_2}^{z_1}\omega_j\Bigr)\,
\omega_k(z_1).
\]
The period correction $(\mathrm{Im}\,\Omega)^{-1}_{jk}$
compensates the $B$-cycle monodromy, rendering $\eta^{(g)}_{12}$
single-valued on~$X$.  The price: $\eta^{(g)}_{12}$ is no longer
holomorphic---the correction term is a $(1,0)$-form in~$z_1$
multiplied by a real-analytic function of both variables.  The
propagator couples to the Hodge bundle
$\mathbb E\to\overline{\mathcal M}_g$ through the period
matrix~$\Omega$.

\subsection*{Curvature}

The Arnold relation at genus~$g$ acquires a defect.  At genus~$0$,
\[
\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23}
+\eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{31}
+\eta_{31}\wedge\eta_{12}
\;=\;0
\]
holds identically.  At genus $g\ge 1$, the period corrections
produce a residual $(1,1)$-form: the three-term sum does not vanish.
The fibrewise bar differential satisfies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pref-curvature}
d_{\mathrm{fib}}^{\,2}\;=\;\kappa(\cA)\cdot\omega_g,
\end{equation}
where $\omega_g$ is the Arakelov $(1,1)$-form on the fibre of the
universal curve $\pi\colon\mathcal C_g\to\overline{\mathcal M}_g$,
and $\kappa(\cA)\in\Bbbk$ is the scalar \emph{modular
characteristic}, determined entirely by the leading OPE singularity.

For the Heisenberg algebra: the only OPE datum entering the bar
differential is $\alpha_{(1)}\alpha=k$.  Every residue at a
codimension-one collision stratum extracts this coefficient.  The
defect of the Arnold relation at genus~$g$ is
\[
d_{\mathrm{fib}}^{\,2}\big|_{\mathcal H_k}
\;=\;
k\cdot\omega_g,
\qquad\text{so}\quad
\kappa(\mathcal H_k)=k.
\]
For the Kac--Moody algebra: the bracket
$J^a_{(0)}J^b=f^{ab}{}_{c}J^c$ and the inner product
$J^a_{(1)}J^b=k\kappa^{ab}$ both contribute.  The curvature
receives the trace over the adjoint representation:
\[
\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)
\;=\;
\frac{(k+h^\vee)\dim\mathfrak g}{2h^\vee}\,.
\]
The numerator $k+h^\vee$ is the \emph{shifted level}; $h^\vee$ is
the dual Coxeter number.  At $k=-h^\vee$ (the critical level),
$\kappa=0$ and the bar complex is \emph{uncurved}---the Sugawara
construction is undefined (the central charge
$c=k\dim\mathfrak g/(k+h^\vee)$ has a pole), but the curvature
parameter vanishes.

\subsection*{Period integrals restore nilpotence}

The curvature~\eqref{eq:pref-curvature} obstructs
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2=0$, but not the total $D_g^2=0$.
Integration against $A$-cycle periods defines a connection on the
family of bar complexes parameterised by~$\overline{\mathcal M}_g$.
The corrected total differential
\[
D_g\;=\;d_{\mathrm{fib}}+\nabla^{\mathrm{GM}}
\]
incorporates the Gauss--Manin connection on the Hodge bundle, and
$D_g^{\,2}=0$: the $(1,1)$-curvature of the fibrewise differential
is absorbed by the $(0,1)$-part of the base connection.

The mechanism: the fibrewise curvature $\kappa\cdot\omega_g$ is a
class in $H^{1,1}(\mathcal C_g)$; the period map
$\overline{\mathcal M}_g\to\mathcal A_g$ (to the moduli of abelian
varieties) identifies this class with the $(0,1)$-curvature of the
Gauss--Manin connection on $R^1\pi_*\mathbb C$.  The two
curvatures cancel:
\[
D_g^{\,2}
\;=\;
d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2
+[d_{\mathrm{fib}},\nabla^{\mathrm{GM}}]
+(\nabla^{\mathrm{GM}})^2
\;=\;
\kappa\cdot\omega_g-\kappa\cdot\omega_g+0
\;=\;0.
\]

\subsection*{The genus tower}

The bar complex traces a family of curved cochain complexes over
$\overline{\mathcal M}_g$.  The obstruction to flatness at
genus~$g$ is the characteristic class
\[
\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA)
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_g
\;\in\;
H^*(\overline{\mathcal M}_g),
\qquad
\lambda_g\;=\;c_g(\mathbb E),
\]
where $\mathbb E$ is the Hodge bundle.  Grothendieck--Riemann--Roch
applied to the universal curve
$\pi\colon\mathcal C_g\to\overline{\mathcal M}_g$ computes the
total obstruction: the Chern character of $R\pi_*\omega_\pi$
involves the Todd class of the relative tangent bundle~$T_\pi$,
which is the $\hat A$-genus.  The generating function:
\[
\sum_{g\ge 1}\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA)\cdot\hbar^{2g-2}
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA)\cdot\bigl(\hat A(i\hbar)-1\bigr).
\]
The first terms, from the Bernoulli-number expansion
$\hat A(x)=1-\frac{1}{24}x^2+\frac{7}{5760}x^4-\cdots$:
\begin{align*}
F_1(\cA)
&\;=\;
-\frac{\kappa(\cA)}{24}\,,\\[3pt]
F_2(\cA)
&\;=\;
\frac{7\,\kappa(\cA)}{5760}\,,\\[3pt]
F_3(\cA)
&\;=\;
-\frac{31\,\kappa(\cA)}{967680}\,.
\end{align*}
The pattern: the genus-$g$ term is
$\kappa(\cA)\cdot(-1)^g B_{2g}/(2g)!$ up to normalisation, where
$B_{2g}$ are the Bernoulli numbers.  The emergence of the
$\hat A$-genus from the bar complex is the bridge between chiral
algebra and index theory.

\subsection*{Complementarity}

Verdier duality splits the center local system into complementary
summands:
\[
R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal M}_g,\;\mathcal Z_\cA)
\;\simeq\;
\mathbf Q_g(\cA)\;\oplus\;\mathbf Q_g(\cA^!).
\]
What~$\cA$ sees as deformation, $\cA^!$ sees as obstruction.  The
splitting carries a shifted-symplectic structure: the natural
pairing
\[
\mathbf Q_g(\cA)\otimes\mathbf Q_g(\cA^!)
\;\longrightarrow\;
R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal M}_g,\;\omega_{\overline{\mathcal M}_g})
\;\simeq\;\Bbbk[-(3g-3)]
\]
is a $(-(3g-3))$-shifted symplectic form, and the two summands are
\emph{Lagrangian}: each is isotropic and of half the total
dimension.  At the scalar level, complementarity reduces to
\[
\kappa(\cA)+\kappa(\cA^!)\;=\;0.
\]

\subsection*{Feigin--Frenkel duality}

The Sugawara construction produces the Virasoro field
\[
T(z)
\;=\;
\frac{1}{2(k+h^\vee)}\sum_{a=1}^{\dim\mathfrak g}
{:}J^a(z)J^a(z){:}\,,
\]
with central charge $c=k\dim\mathfrak g/(k+h^\vee)$.  At the
critical level $k=-h^\vee$, the denominator vanishes: $T(z)$ is
\emph{undefined}---not ``$c$ diverges.''  The chiral algebra
acquires a large center, the Feigin--Frenkel center
$\mathfrak z(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_{-h^\vee})$, isomorphic to the
algebra of functions on the space of ${}^L\mathfrak g$-opers.

The Feigin--Frenkel involution
\[
k\;\longleftrightarrow\;-k-2h^\vee
\]
is Koszul duality on configuration spaces: it interchanges~$\cA$
and~$\cA^!$ while preserving the bar-cobar adjunction.  At the
level of modular characteristics,
$\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)+
\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_{-k-2h^\vee})=0$:
complementarity is a direct consequence.  At the critical
level, the Koszul dual is the Langlands-dual critical-level
algebra~$\widehat{{}^L\mathfrak g}_{-{}^Lh^\vee}$---not a
renormalised copy of the original.

\subsection*{The total space}

The universal configuration fibration
\[
\pi_n\colon
\overline{\mathcal C}_n(\mathcal C_g)
\;\longrightarrow\;
\overline{\mathcal M}_g
\]
packages the bar complex into a single global object.  Its boundary
divisors carry two distinct geometric roles:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Collision divisors} (vertical): the fibrewise boundary
of $\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_n(X_s)$ over a point
$s\in\overline{\mathcal M}_g$.  These produce the bar
differential~$d_{\mathrm{res}}$---the OPE residues.
\item \emph{Degeneration divisors} (horizontal): the boundary of
$\overline{\mathcal M}_g$ itself (nodal curves, reducible
fibres).  These produce the period corrections---the
Gauss--Manin connection terms in~$D_g$.
\end{itemize}
The collision divisors are the ``algebraic'' boundary (controlled by
the Borcherds identity); the degeneration divisors are the
``geometric'' boundary (controlled by the period map and the
clutching morphisms of stable curves).  The interaction between them
is the curvature~$\kappa$: the fibrewise bar differential fails to
be nilpotent precisely because the vertical and horizontal
boundaries do not commute.

\section*{3.\quad Modular homotopy theory}
% ====================================================================

The genus tower of section~2 assembles the bar complexes
$\barB^{(g)}(\cA)$ over all genera into a single inverse system.
The \emph{modular homotopy type} of~$\cA$ is the formal moduli
problem that controls this system:
\[
\mathcal M^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA(R)
\;:=\;
\mathrm{MC}\!\bigl(
\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA \,\widehat\otimes_{\mathfrak m}\, R
\bigr)\big/{\sim}
\]
for local Artin rings~$R$, where
$\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA$ is a complete filtered cyclic
$L_\infty$-algebra---the \emph{modular convolution algebra}---whose
construction requires three inputs: a homotopy chiral algebra on
the curve (the algebraic input), a cooperadic chain complex on
logarithmic configuration spaces (the geometric input), and a
convolution $L_\infty$-structure mediating between them (the
homotopical input).  The bar complex, the Koszul dual, the genus
tower, the complementarity splitting, the shadow Postnikov tower,
and the holographic correspondence of Volume~II are all controlled
by $\mathcal M^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA$: they are representations of
the formal group it defines.

The convolution algebra $\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA$ has a
canonical Maurer--Cartan element
$\Theta_\cA\in\mathrm{MC}(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA)$
(section~4), a tangent complex governing its deformations
(section~5), and a Postnikov tower of finite-order
projections (section~6).  What follows constructs each piece.

\subsection*{3.1.\enspace Homotopy chiral algebras}

Fix an affine cover $\mathfrak U=\{U_\alpha\}_{\alpha\in I}$ of the
curve~$X$.  The \v{C}ech totalisation
\[
A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty
\;:=\;
\check C^\bullet(\mathfrak U;\cA)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{p\ge 0}
\prod_{\alpha_0<\cdots<\alpha_p}
\cA(U_{\alpha_0}\cap\cdots\cap U_{\alpha_p})
\]
carries a $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebra structure
(Malikov--Schechtman): the chiral bracket of~$\cA$, restricted to
overlaps of affine opens, does not satisfy the Jacobi identity on
the nose---it satisfies it up to a coherent system of higher
homotopies, the \emph{secondary Borcherds operations}
\[
F_n\colon\mathrm{Lie}(n)\otimes\cY(n)
\;\longrightarrow\;
P_n(\{A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty\},\,A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty),
\qquad n\ge 2,
\]
encoding derived OPE data at all pole orders.  The operation~$F_2$
is the chiral bracket itself.

The Jacobiator homotopy~$F_3$ arises from the moduli space
$\overline{\mathcal M}_{0,4}\cong\mathbb P^1$: its boundary
$\partial\overline{\mathcal M}_{0,4}$ consists of three points,
corresponding to three OPE compositions
$(a_{(m)}b)_{(n)}c$, $(b_{(m)}c)_{(n)}a$,
$(c_{(m)}a)_{(n)}b$.  The chain
$C_1(\overline{\mathcal M}_{0,4})\cong\Bbbk$ provides a
$1$-chain whose boundary is the alternating sum of the three
vertices; $F_3$ is the chain homotopy witnessing their equality
up to~$d$.

Higher operations $F_n$ are determined inductively by
$C_*(\overline{\mathcal M}_{0,n+1})$ through the homotopy transfer
theorem applied to the inclusion
$H_*(\overline{\mathcal M}_{0,n+1})
\hookrightarrow
C_*(\overline{\mathcal M}_{0,n+1})$.
The cellular chain complex of $\overline{\mathcal M}_{0,n+1}$ is
a free resolution of the $(n-2)$-associahedron; the transferred
operations $F_n$ are the $A_\infty$-corrections at arity~$n$.

Different affine covers yield quasi-isomorphic
$\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebras: the \v{C}ech-to-derived spectral
sequence degenerates for $\mathcal D$-modules on smooth curves.
The strict chiral algebra~$\cA$ is the $H^0$ of this structure---the
primitive local object is the homotopy chiral algebra; the strict
algebra appears after rectification.  The rectification theorem
(Vallette): the homotopy category of $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebras
is equivalent to the homotopy category of strict chiral algebras,
via the bar-cobar adjunction at the operadic level.

\subsection*{3.2.\enspace Logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson spaces}

For a simple-normal-crossing pair $(X,D)$ with $D$ a reduced divisor,
Mok constructs the \emph{logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson
compactification} $\mathrm{FM}_n(X\mathbin{|}D)$: a smooth
variety with corners, compactifying the open configuration space
$\mathrm{Conf}_n(X\setminus D)$, whose boundary stratification is
indexed by \emph{rigid planted-forest types}.

A planted-forest type~$\rho$ consists of:
\begin{itemize}
\item a partition of $\{1,\dots,n\}$ into blocks (the roots of the
forest);
\item for each block, a rooted tree (the planted tree) recording the
hierarchy of collisions;
\item a grid-depth assignment $w_v\in\mathbb Z_{\ge 0}$ to each
vertex, recording the order of tangency with~$D$.
\end{itemize}
The ordinary Fulton--MacPherson compactification is the case
$D=\emptyset$: all grid depths are zero, and the planted trees are
the collision trees of the classical theory.

The \emph{degeneration formula}: for a semistable family $W\to B$
with singular fibre $W_0=\bigcup Y_v$ (components indexed by
vertices of the dual graph), and a rigid type~$\rho$,
\[
\mathrm{FM}_n(W/B)(\rho)
\;\longrightarrow\;
\prod_{v\in V(S_\rho)}
\mathrm{FM}_{I_v}(Y_v\mathbin{|}D_v)
\]
is a proper birational correspondence factoring the global
FM geometry into a fibre product of local FM pieces.

The tropicalisation: planted forests are dual graphs of tropical
curves.  Precisely,
\[
\mathrm{Trop}\bigl(\mathrm{FM}_n(\mathbb C\mathbin{|}D)\bigr)
\;=\;
G_{\mathrm{pf}},
\]
the planted-forest poset with grafting as partial order.  Grafting of
planted forests corresponds to degeneration of the underlying curve;
the poset structure on~$G_{\mathrm{pf}}$ is the face poset of the
boundary stratification.

The \emph{Mok codimension formula}: for a planted-forest type with
grid depths $(w_1,\dots,w_r)$ and tree vertex counts
$(|V(T_j)|)_{j=1,\dots,s}$,
\[
\mathrm{codim}(W_\rho)
\;=\;
\textstyle\sum_{i=1}^r w_i\;+\;\sum_{j=1}^s(|V(T_j)|-1).
\]
The two summands: grid-depth contributions (tangency with~$D$)
and tree-depth contributions (collision complexity).

For $\mathrm{FM}_3$: seven boundary types.  Codimension~$1$:
$\{12\}$, $\{23\}$, $\{13\}$, $\{123\}$ (four strata, recording
pairwise and triple collisions).  Codimension~$2$:
$\{12{<}123\}$, $\{23{<}123\}$, $\{13{<}123\}$ (three strata,
recording sequential collisions---first a pair, then the third
point joins).

The chain complexes
$\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{X|D}(n)
:=C_\bullet(\mathrm{FM}_n(X\mathbin{|}D))$ carry cooperadic
structure: the inclusion of boundary strata defines cocomposition
maps.  The \emph{graphwise cocomposition} for a rigid stable
graph~$\Gamma$:
\[
\Delta^{\log}_\Gamma
\;:=\;
(\nu_\Gamma)_*\circ\mathrm{Res}_{D^{\log}_\Gamma}
\;\colon\;
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}}(g,n)
\;\longrightarrow\;
\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}}\bigl(g(v),\,
I_v\sqcup H(v)\bigr)[-|E_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)|],
\]
where $\nu_\Gamma$ is the Mok birational correspondence on the
normalised boundary stratum indexed by~$\Gamma$, and
$\mathrm{Res}_{D^{\log}_\Gamma}$ is the residue along the
logarithmic boundary divisor.  The degree shift
$|E_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)|$ accounts for the codimension of the
stratum (one for each internal edge).

\subsection*{3.3.\enspace The convolution $L_\infty$-algebra}

A twisting morphism $\alpha\colon\cC\to\cP$ between a cooperad and
an operad determines a convolution $sL_\infty$-algebra
$\mathrm{hom}_\alpha(\cC,\cP)$ (Robert-Nicoud--Wierstra, after
Vallette).  The underlying graded vector space:
\[
\mathrm{hom}_\alpha(\cC,\cP)
\;=\;
\prod_{n\ge 1}
\mathrm{Hom}_{\Sigma_n}\bigl(\cC(n),\,\cP(n)\bigr).
\]
The $L_\infty$ brackets:
\[
\ell_k(f_1,\dots,f_k)(x)
\;=\;
\sum
\gamma_{\cP}\!\bigl(
(\alpha\circ 1)(f_1\otimes\cdots\otimes f_k)\,
\Delta^{(k)}_{\cC}(x)
\bigr),
\]
where $\Delta^{(k)}_{\cC}$ is the iterated decomposition of the
cooperad and $\gamma_{\cP}$ is the operadic composition.  The
bracket $\ell_1$ is the convolution differential (pre- and
post-composition with the differentials of~$\cC$ and~$\cP$).  The
bracket $\ell_2$ is the Lie bracket of coderivations.  Higher
brackets $\ell_k$, $k\ge 3$, arise from the higher cooperadic
decompositions and are nontrivial whenever $\cC$ is not
concentrated in a single arity.

The $sL_\infty$-algebra $\mathrm{hom}_\alpha(\cC,\cP)$ extends to
$\infty$-morphisms in \emph{either slot separately}: given a
quasi-isomorphism $\cC\xrightarrow{\sim}\cC'$ of cooperads (or
$\cP\xrightarrow{\sim}\cP'$ of operads), there is an induced
$\infty$-quasi-isomorphism of convolution algebras.  But
\emph{not both simultaneously}: the convolution is functorial in
each slot, but does not assemble into a bifunctor on the
$\infty$-categorical level.  This is a structural constraint:
the MC3 categorical lift of bar-cobar duality must proceed one
slot at a time.

The bar-cobar adjunction $B_\kappa\dashv\Omega_\kappa$ is a
\emph{Quillen equivalence} (Vallette): it induces an equivalence
on homotopy categories.  Different $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$
presentations yield quasi-isomorphic deformation theories.

\subsection*{3.4.\enspace Assembly}

The \emph{modular convolution $L_\infty$-algebra}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pref-convolution}
\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA
\;:=\;
\Convinf\!\Bigl(
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}},\;
\mathrm{End}_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty)
\Bigr).
\end{equation}
The cooperadic input is the log-FM chain cooperad
$\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}}$: the modular cooperad of
chain complexes on logarithmic FM spaces, with cocomposition given
by the graphwise maps~$\Delta^{\log}_\Gamma$.  The operadic input
is the $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-endomorphism operad of the homotopy
chiral algebra~$A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty$: the operad of multilinear
maps respecting the transferred homotopy-chiral structure.

One-slot convention: fix the log-FM cooperad (the
``geometric'' slot), vary the chiral algebra input (the
``algebraic'' slot) by $\infty$-morphisms.

The \emph{strict chart}: let $\mathfrak U$ be an affine cover
of~$X$.  The coderivation dg Lie algebra
\[
\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod},\log}_\cA(\mathfrak U)
\;:=\;
\Convstr\!\bigl(
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}},\;
\mathrm{End}_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty)
\bigr)
\]
is the \emph{strict model}: classical Lie bracket (commutator of
coderivations), no higher $L_\infty$ brackets, but the \emph{same}
Maurer--Cartan moduli.  MC elements in the strict chart are in
natural bijection with MC elements in the full convolution
$L_\infty$-algebra.  The strict chart suffices for constructing the
universal MC element and computing shadows; the full $L_\infty$
structure is needed for transfer, formality, and gauge equivalence.

\subsection*{3.5.\enspace The modular bar functor}

In log-FM coordinates, the modular bar functor at type $(g,n)$:
\[
\mathrm B^{\log}_{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)(g,n)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}}
\left(
\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}
\bar B^{\mathrm{ch}}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty;\mathrm{In}(v))
\;\otimes\;
C_\bullet(\mathrm{FM}^{\log}_\Gamma)
\;\otimes\;
\orline{E_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)}
\right)_{\!\mathrm{Aut}(\Gamma)}.
\]
The sum is over stable graphs~$\Gamma$ of type $(g,n)$: graphs with
first Betti number $b_1(\Gamma)+\sum_v g(v)=g$ and $n$ external
legs.  The ingredients:
\begin{itemize}
\item For each vertex~$v\in V(\Gamma)$: the transferred
$\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-bar contribution
$\bar B^{\mathrm{ch}}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty;\mathrm{In}(v))$
---the chiral bar complex on the incoming half-edges of~$v$,
computed in the homotopy-chiral framework.
\item For each internal edge: the propagator
$P_\cA=H_\cA^{-1}$, contracting the two half-edges.
\item The geometric factor: the chain complex
$C_\bullet(\mathrm{FM}^{\log}_\Gamma)$ on the log-FM stratum
indexed by~$\Gamma$.
\item The orientation line
$\orline{E_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)}
=\det(\Bbbk\,E(\Gamma))
\otimes\det(H_1(\Gamma;\Bbbk))^{-1}$:
signs from edge orientations and loop orderings.
\item Automorphism quotient: coinvariants under
$\mathrm{Aut}(\Gamma)$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{3.6.\enspace The six-component differential}

The total differential on the modular bar functor:
\[
D^{\log}_{\mathrm{mod}}
\;=\;
\underbrace{d_{\check C}}_{\text{\v Cech}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}}_{\text{transferred chiral}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{coll}}}_{\text{collision}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{sew}}}_{\text{separating}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{pf}}}_{\text{planted-forest}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{\hbar\,\Delta}_{\text{genus-raising}}.
\]
The six components, their geometric sources, and their action on a
pure tensor $a\otimes\eta$ indexed by a stable graph~$\Gamma$:

$d_{\check C}$: the \v{C}ech differential of the cover
$\mathfrak U$.  Acts on the \v{C}ech indices of the vertex labels.
Source: the nerve of the cover.

$d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}$: the transferred chiral differential
on each vertex contribution.  Acts on the
$\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-bar complex at each vertex.  Source: the
secondary Borcherds operations~$F_n$.

$d_{\mathrm{coll}}$: the collision (residue) differential.
Contracts an internal edge of~$\Gamma$ by extracting the OPE
residue at the corresponding codimension-one collision stratum.
Source: codimension-one faces of $\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_n(X)$.

$d_{\mathrm{sew}}$, $d_{\mathrm{pf}}$, and $\hbar\,\Delta$: the three
positive-genus boundary operators.  On a pure tensor $a\otimes\eta$
indexed by~$\Gamma$:
\begin{align*}
d_{\mathrm{sew}}(a\otimes\eta)
&\;=\;
\sum_{e\in E_{\mathrm{sep}}(\Gamma)}
\pm\,\mu_e(a)\otimes(\xi_e)_*\,
\mathrm{Res}_{D_e^{\log}}(\eta),
\\[4pt]
d_{\mathrm{pf}}(a\otimes\eta)
&\;=\;
\sum_{F\in\mathsf{PF}^{\mathrm{rig}}(\Gamma)}
\pm\,\mu_F(a)\otimes(\xi_F)_*\,
\mathrm{Res}_{D_F^{\log}}(\eta),
\\[4pt]
\hbar\,\Delta(a\otimes\eta)
&\;=\;
\hbar\sum_{e\in E_{\mathrm{ns}}(\Gamma)}
\pm\,\mathrm{Tr}_e(a)\otimes(\xi_e)_*\,
\mathrm{Res}_{D_e^{\log}}(\eta).
\end{align*}
Separating edges $E_{\mathrm{sep}}(\Gamma)$ (cutting the graph
into two connected components)
$\to d_{\mathrm{sew}}$: the clutching morphism, gluing two
lower-genus curves along a node.
Non-separating edges $E_{\mathrm{ns}}(\Gamma)$ (cutting increases
$b_1$ by one) $\to\hbar\Delta$: the genus-raising operator,
self-gluing a curve at two marked points.  Planted-forest strata
$\mathsf{PF}^{\mathrm{rig}}(\Gamma)$ $\to d_{\mathrm{pf}}$:
the log-FM degeneration corrections.

The maps: $\mu_e$ contracts the algebra labels at the two half-edges
of~$e$ using the chiral bracket; $\mathrm{Tr}_e$ contracts using the
bilinear pairing (supertrace over the two half-edges of~$e$);
$\mu_F$ applies the tree-level multilinear operation dictated by the
planted forest~$F$.  The maps $(\xi_e)_*$ and $(\xi_F)_*$ are the
pushforward along the normalisation of the boundary stratum.

The planted-forest differential as push--pull:
\[
d_{\mathrm{pf}}
\;=\;
\sum_{\rho\in\mathsf{PF}^{\mathrm{rig}}}
\epsilon_\rho\,
(\kappa_\rho)_*\,\mathrm{pr}_\rho^*\otimes\mu_\rho\,,
\]
where $\mathrm{pr}_\rho^*$ pulls back chains to the planted-forest
stratum and $(\kappa_\rho)_*$ pushes forward along the Mok
birational correspondence.  This is Mok's degeneration formula,
internalised in the convolution algebra: the push--pull
correspondence on log-FM strata becomes a summand of the total
differential.

\subsection*{3.7.\enspace $D^2=0$}

The nilpotence $(D^{\log}_{\mathrm{mod}})^2=0$ holds by three
cancellation mechanisms:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \emph{Vertex labels}: $d_{\check C}^{\,2}=0$,
$d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}^{\,2}=0$, and their anticommutator
$\{d_{\check C},d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}\}=0$ by the
$\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebra axioms on
$A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty$.
\item \emph{One-edge expansions}: the anticommutator of a
vertex differential ($d_{\check C}$ or
$d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}$) with an edge-contraction differential
($d_{\mathrm{coll}}$, $d_{\mathrm{sew}}$, $d_{\mathrm{pf}}$,
or $\hbar\Delta$) vanishes by the Leibniz rule for coderivations.
Each edge contraction is a coderivation of the coalgebra
structure, and the vertex differentials are derivations of the
algebra structure.
\item \emph{Codimension-two cancellation}: the sum of all
products of two distinct edge-contraction operators vanishes.
The codimension-two boundary of
Mok's log-FM compactification is a normal-crossings divisor
(Mok, Theorem~3.3.1), and each codimension-two face appears as
the intersection of exactly two codimension-one faces.  The
two boundary contributions cancel because the face maps compose
with opposite orientations.  This is $\partial^2=0$ for the
boundary of a manifold with corners, lifted through the
residue correspondence.
\end{enumerate}

The third mechanism is the decisive one.  The normal-crossings
property of Mok's compactification is essential: it guarantees
that every codimension-two intersection is transverse and
contributes to exactly one cancelling pair.  Without normal crossings,
the codimension-two faces could have excess intersection, and the
cancellation would fail.

\subsection*{4.1.\enspace $\Gamma$-amplitudes and Taylor coefficients}

For a stable graph $\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}$ with
$|V(\Gamma)|=k$ vertices and elements $f_1,\dots,f_k\in\gAmod$
assigned to those vertices, the \emph{$\Gamma$-amplitude} assembles
the chiral operations, the propagator, and the modular twisting
morphism into a single multilinear operation.  Let
$\mu_\Gamma\colon\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}A^{\otimes n(v)}\to A$
denote the graph composition: at each vertex~$v$ of genus~$g(v)$ and
valence~$n(v)$, place the transferred chiral operation
$m^{(h)}_{g(v),n(v)}$; along each internal edge, contract with the
propagator~$P_\cA=\iota\circ\pi$ (homotopy retraction of the
augmentation ideal to its cohomology).  Write
$\alpha^{\mathrm{mod}}_\Gamma$ for the component of the modular
twisting morphism on~$\Gamma$, and
$\Delta^{\log}_\Gamma\colon\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}}
\to\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{g(v),n(v)}$
for the cooperadic cocomposition.  The $\Gamma$-amplitude is:
\[
\ell_\Gamma(f_1,\dots,f_k)
\;=\;
\mu_\Gamma\circ
(\alpha^{\mathrm{mod}}_\Gamma\otimes f_1\otimes\cdots\otimes f_k)
\circ\Delta^{\log}_\Gamma.
\]
This is a $k$-linear map
$\ell_\Gamma\colon(\gAmod)^{\otimes k}\to\gAmod$ of cohomological
degree $2-2g(\Gamma)-k$, where $g(\Gamma)=b_1(\Gamma)+\sum_v g(v)$
is the total genus.

The modular $L_\infty$ structure on~$\gAmod$ has Taylor coefficients
obtained by summing over all stable graphs of fixed genus and vertex
count:
\[
\ell_k^{(g)}
\;=\;
\sum_{\substack{
\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}},\;|V(\Gamma)|=k\\
b_1(\Gamma)+\sum_v g(v)=g}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}\;\ell_\Gamma.
\]
Here $\ell_k^{(g)}$ is a symmetric $k$-linear bracket of genus~$g$.
The full modular $L_\infty$ structure is the formal sum
$\ell=\sum_{g\ge 0}\sum_{k\ge 1}\hbar^g\,\ell_k^{(g)}/k!$, and the
homotopy Jacobi identity is encoded by $D^2=0$ on the total
convolution algebra.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Explicit low-order Taylor coefficients.}\enspace
The first several brackets admit direct geometric description.  At
tree level ($g=0$), the binary bracket is the Lie bracket on
cohomology transferred through the homotopy retraction data
$(\pi,\iota,h)$:
\[
\ell_2^{(0)}(\alpha,\beta)
\;=\;
\pi\bigl[m_2\bigl(\iota(\alpha),\iota(\beta)\bigr)\bigr].
\]
The ternary bracket is a sum over binary trees with three leaves:
\begin{align*}
\ell_3^{(0)}(\alpha,\beta,\gamma)
&=\sum_{T\in\mathsf{Trees}_3}
|\operatorname{Aut}(T)|^{-1}\,
(\pi\circ m_T^{(h)}\circ\iota^{\otimes 3})(\alpha,\beta,\gamma)\\[3pt]
&=\pi\Bigl(
m_2\bigl(h\,m_2(\iota\alpha,\iota\beta),\,\iota\gamma\bigr)
+m_2\bigl(\iota\alpha,\,h\,m_2(\iota\beta,\iota\gamma)\bigr)\\
&\qquad\quad
+m_2\bigl(h\,m_2(\iota\alpha,\iota\gamma),\,\iota\beta\bigr)
\Bigr).
\end{align*}
There are three binary trees in $\mathsf{Trees}_3$ (one for each
channel $s$, $t$, $u$), each with trivial automorphism group.  The
homotopy~$h$ propagates the intermediate state through~$P_\cA$
before re-composing.  This is the classical homological perturbation
lemma in its $L_\infty$ incarnation.

The quaternary bracket sums over the fifteen labeled trees in
$\mathsf{Trees}_4$---five trivalent topologies, each with three
planar labelings:
\begin{align*}
\ell_4^{(0)}(\alpha,\beta,\gamma,\delta)
&=\sum_{T\in\mathsf{Trees}_4}
\pm\,|\operatorname{Aut}(T)|^{-1}\,
(\pi\circ m_T^{(h)}\circ\iota^{\otimes 4})
(\alpha,\beta,\gamma,\delta).
\end{align*}
Each tree~$T$ has two internal edges, hence two propagator
insertions.  The signs are Koszul signs from the symmetric monoidal
structure.  The five trivalent topologies correspond to the five
faces of the associahedron~$K_4$.

The first genus-raised bracket is unary:
\[
\ell_1^{(1)}(\alpha)
\;=\;
\hbar\,\operatorname{tr}(P_\cA\circ\alpha\circ
\delta^{\mathrm{ns}})
\;=\;
\hbar\sum_{i}
\langle e^i,\,\alpha(e_i)\rangle_{\mathrm{Sh}},
\]
where $\{e_i\}$, $\{e^i\}$ is a dual basis for the cyclic pairing
on~$H^*(\bar\cA)$ and $\delta^{\mathrm{ns}}$ is the non-separating
node operator.  Geometrically, this is the BV operator: the unique
genus-one graph with one vertex and one self-loop.  The trace is
over the propagator contracting the two half-edges at the
non-separating node of~$\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$.

The genus-one binary bracket accounts for the separating node
of~$\overline{\cM}_{1,2}$:
\[
\ell_2^{(1)}(\alpha,\beta)
\;=\;
\hbar\,\operatorname{tr}\bigl(
P_\cA\circ m_2(\iota\alpha,-)
\circ\delta^{\mathrm{ns}}\circ\iota\beta
\bigr)
\;+\;
(\alpha\leftrightarrow\beta).
\]
These low-order brackets are the only data needed for all shadow
computations through arity~$4$.

\subsection*{4.2.\enspace The bar-intrinsic construction}

The key construction of the monograph: $\Theta_\cA$ is not
\emph{computed} by solving an equation---it is \emph{extracted}
from a differential that already squares to zero.

The total bar differential
$D_\cA\colon\mathrm{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)\to
\mathrm{B}^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$ acts on the modular bar complex.
Decompose $D_\cA=d_0+\Theta_\cA$, where
$d_0=d_{\mathrm{int}}+[\tau,-]$ is the tree-level local part: the
internal differential of~$\cA$ plus the commutator with the tautological
twisting morphism~$\tau$.  The \emph{universal Maurer--Cartan element}
is the remainder:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pref-theta}
\Theta_\cA
\;:=\;
D_\cA-d_0
\;=\;
\sum_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}}
\frac{W^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_\Gamma}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\otimes\Phi^\cA_\Gamma
\;\in\;
\operatorname{MC}(\gAmod).
\end{equation}
Here $W^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_\Gamma\in
H^*(\overline{\cM}^{\log}_{g(\Gamma),n(\Gamma)})$ is the log-FM
weight (the fundamental class of the boundary stratum indexed
by~$\Gamma$), and
$\Phi^\cA_\Gamma\in\operatorname{Hom}(\bar\cA^{\otimes
n(\Gamma)},\Bbbk)$ is the chiral amplitude on~$\Gamma$ (propagators
on internal edges, operations at vertices, cyclic trace at the output).

The MC property is immediate: $D_\cA^{\,2}=0$ by the geometric fact
that $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}^{\log}$ has normal-crossings boundary;
$d_0^2=0$ by construction; expanding $(d_0+\Theta_\cA)^2=0$ gives
\[
d_0\Theta_\cA+\Theta_\cA\,d_0
+\Theta_\cA^2=0
\qquad\Longleftrightarrow\qquad
[d_0,\Theta_\cA]+\tfrac12[\Theta_\cA,\Theta_\cA]=0.
\]
No simple-Lie restriction.  No convergence hypothesis.  No choice of
basis.  No solution of an equation.  The MC property is a formal
consequence of $\partial^2=0$ on the moduli space.

\subsection*{4.3.\enspace $\Theta_\cA$ as universal modular twisting morphism}

The Maurer--Cartan space of the modular convolution $L_\infty$
algebra is identified with the space of modular twisting morphisms:
\[
\operatorname{MC}_\bullet\!\bigl(\gAmod\bigr)
\;\simeq\;
\operatorname{Tw}_{\alpha^{\mathrm{mod}}}\!\Bigl(
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}},\,
\operatorname{End}_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty)
\Bigr).
\]
Under this identification, $\Theta_\cA$ is the \emph{universal
modular twisting morphism}---the operadic analogue of the canonical
flat connection on a principal bundle.  A twisting
morphism $\alpha\colon\cC\to\cP$ between a cooperad and an operad
determines a twisted composition product $\cC\circ_\alpha\cP$ whose
differential squares to zero if and only if~$\alpha$ satisfies the
MC equation.  $\Theta_\cA$ is the unique twisting morphism that
simultaneously twists the modular bar complex and intertwines with
the log-FM boundary structure.

The analogy with principal bundles is precise.  The modular bar
bundle $\mathrm{B}^{\log}_{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)\to
\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$ is the principal bundle; $\Theta_\cA$ is the
connection; the MC equation is the flatness condition; the shadow
tower (Section~6) is the Chern--Weil theory.  The structure group is
the pro-unipotent MC gauge group
$\exp(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod},0}_\cA)$.

\subsection*{4.4.\enspace Tridegree, depth, and convergence}

Each coefficient of~$\Theta_\cA$ carries a natural tridegree
\[
(g,n,d)
\;=\;
(\text{loop genus},\;\text{external arity},\;\text{log boundary depth}),
\]
where $d(\rho)=\#E_{\mathrm{sep}}(\Gamma_\rho)+
\#E_{\mathrm{ns}}(\Gamma_\rho)+\operatorname{depth}_{\mathrm{pf}}
(\Gamma_\rho)$ is the codimension of the log-FM stratum indexed by
the degeneration pattern~$\rho$.  Here $E_{\mathrm{sep}}$ counts
separating edges, $E_{\mathrm{ns}}$ counts non-separating edges, and
$\operatorname{depth}_{\mathrm{pf}}$ counts planted-forest depth
levels.  The total depth equals the codimension of the stratum
$D^{\log}_\rho$ in the log-FM compactification.

The depth filtration
$F^d\gAmod=\bigoplus_{d'\ge d}(\gAmod)_{*,*,d'}$ makes
the convolution algebra complete:
\[
\gAmod\;=\;\varprojlim_{d}\;\gAmod/F^d\gAmod.
\]
The inverse limit
$\Theta_\cA=\varprojlim_r\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ converges
unconditionally: no analytic input, no growth estimate, no
restriction on the algebra~$\cA$.  The convergence is purely
algebraic, guaranteed by the completeness of the depth filtration.

The tridegree decomposition organizes computation.  At fixed
$(g,n)$, only finitely many stable graph types~$\Gamma$ contribute
(by stability: $2g(\Gamma)-2+n(\Gamma)>0$ forces a finite graph
set).  The depth filtration refines this further: at depth~$d$,
the stratum has codimension~$d$ in $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}^{\log}$,
and the amplitude $\Phi^\cA_\Gamma$ has internal-edge count
(hence propagator insertions) bounded by~$d$.

\subsection*{4.5.\enspace The weight filtration}

The weight filtration is the primary organizational tool for the MC
equation.  Decompose by internal-edge count (equivalently, by total
propagator insertions):
\[
\Theta_\cA\;=\;\sum_{r\ge 1}\Theta^{[r]}_\cA,
\qquad
\Theta^{[r]}_\cA\;=\;
\sum_{\substack{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}\\
|E_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)|=r}}
\frac{W^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_\Gamma}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\otimes\Phi^\cA_\Gamma.
\]
The MC equation decomposes by weight into an inductive tower:
\begin{alignat*}{2}
&\text{Weight }1{:}\quad
&&[D_{\mathrm{loc}},\Theta^{[1]}_\cA]=0,\\[4pt]
&\text{Weight }2{:}\quad
&&[D_{\mathrm{loc}},\Theta^{[2]}_\cA]
+\tfrac12[\Theta^{[1]}_\cA,\Theta^{[1]}_\cA]=0,\\[4pt]
&\text{Weight }3{:}\quad
&&[D_{\mathrm{loc}},\Theta^{[3]}_\cA]
+[\Theta^{[1]}_\cA,\Theta^{[2]}_\cA]=0,\\[4pt]
&\text{Weight }N{:}\quad
&&[D_{\mathrm{loc}},\Theta^{[N]}_\cA]
+\tfrac12\sum_{\substack{i+j=N\\i,j\ge 1}}
[\Theta^{[i]}_\cA,\Theta^{[j]}_\cA]=0.
\end{alignat*}
At each stage, $[D_{\mathrm{loc}},\Theta^{[N]}]$ is determined by
the quadratic error from previous stages.  The obstruction to
extending from weight~$N-1$ to weight~$N$ lies in
$H^2(\gAmod/F^{N+1},D_{\mathrm{loc}})$.  The bar-intrinsic
construction solves all stages simultaneously.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Weight~$1$: boundary divisors.}\enspace
The weight-one component $\Theta^{[1]}_\cA$ lives on boundary
divisors of moduli spaces.  In degree $(0,4)$: the three boundary
points of $\overline{\cM}_{0,4}\cong\mathbb P^1$, corresponding to
the three channels $(12|34)$, $(13|24)$, $(14|23)$:
\[
\Theta^{[1]}_\cA\big|_{(0,4)}
=[\delta_s]\otimes\Phi_s
+[\delta_t]\otimes\Phi_t
+[\delta_u]\otimes\Phi_u.
\]
Here $[\delta_s]$, $[\delta_t]$, $[\delta_u]$ are the fundamental
classes of the three boundary points $\{0,1,\infty\}\subset\mathbb
P^1$, and $\Phi_s$, $\Phi_t$, $\Phi_u$ are the corresponding chiral
four-point amplitudes (binary composition in channel $s$, $t$, $u$
respectively).  The weight-one MC equation
$[D_{\mathrm{loc}},\Theta^{[1]}]=0$ at $(0,4)$ reads:
\[
\Phi_s+\Phi_t+\Phi_u=F_3,
\]
where $F_3$ is the Malikov--Schechtman Jacobiator homotopy---the
failure of the chiral bracket to satisfy the Jacobi identity on the
nose.  This is the first Borcherds identity: the Jacobiator is a
boundary in the bar complex.

In degree $(1,1)$: the single boundary divisor
$\delta_{\mathrm{irr}}\subset\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$ (the irreducible
nodal cubic):
\[
\Theta^{[1]}_\cA\big|_{(1,1)}
=[\delta_{\mathrm{irr}}]\otimes\Delta,
\]
where $\Delta=\sum_i e_i\otimes e^i$ is the Casimir element of the
cyclic pairing---the non-separating sewing propagator.  The weight-one
MC equation at $(1,1)$ encodes the consistency of the one-loop trace
with the modular structure.

In degree $(0,5)$: the weight-one component restricts to the ten
boundary divisors of $\overline{\cM}_{0,5}$ (the Petersen graph), and
the MC equation recovers the pentagon identity for the $A_\infty$
structure.  The entire $A_\infty$ hierarchy is the tree-level,
genus-zero projection of a single MC element.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Weight~$2$: the first planted-forest layer.}\enspace
At weight~$2$, the codimension-two strata of the log-FM
compactification enter.  These strata have two types: classical
(products of two boundary divisors in $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$) and
genuinely logarithmic (planted-forest strata of
$\operatorname{FM}_n(X\mathbin{|}D)$ with no counterpart in the
classical Deligne--Mumford compactification).  Mok's rigid
planted-forest types appear here for the first time.

The weight-two MC equation is:
\[
[D_{\mathrm{loc}},\Theta^{[2]}_\cA]
\;=\;
-\tfrac12[\Theta^{[1]}_\cA,\Theta^{[1]}_\cA].
\]
The right-hand side is a quadratic expression in the weight-one data:
the square of the boundary-divisor class.  The planted-forest
correction $\Theta^{[2]}_{\mathrm{pf}}$ is the genuinely new
contribution: it records how collision limits in the planted-forest
sector of $\operatorname{FM}_n(X\mathbin{|}D)$ correct the naive
square.

For the $\mathcal W_3$ central-parameter line, the self-coupling of
the unique genus-one tangent direction at weight one is $d_0$-exact
(the quadratic self-interaction is removable by gauge), so any
nontrivial genus-two obstruction comes from this planted-forest sector.
Cubic gauge triviality (Section~6.3): the weight-$2$ obstruction
vanishes by gauge; the first genuine invariant is at weight~$3$.

\subsection*{4.6.\enspace The all-genus recursion}

Project the MC equation to fixed genus~$g$ and arity~$n$:
\[
d\Theta^{(g,n)}_\cA
+\tfrac12
\!\!\!
\sum_{\substack{g_1+g_2=g\\n_1+n_2=n+2}}
\!\!\!
\ell_2^{(0)}(\Theta^{(g_1,n_1)}_\cA,\Theta^{(g_2,n_2)}_\cA)
+\Delta_{\mathrm{ns}}\,\Theta^{(g-1,n+2)}_\cA
+\sum_{k\ge 3}\frac{1}{k!}
\ell_k^{(\mathrm{tree})}(\Theta_\cA,\dots,\Theta_\cA)^{(g,n)}
=0.
\]
The four terms have direct geometric meaning.  \emph{Internal
differential}: the action of~$d$ on the bar labels at fixed
$(g,n)$.  \emph{Separating clutching}: the sum over splittings
$g_1+g_2=g$, $n_1+n_2=n+2$, identifying the separating node as a
binary bracket $\ell_2^{(0)}$ applied to lower-genus and
lower-arity MC data.  \emph{Non-separating BV operator}: the
contraction $\Delta_{\mathrm{ns}}$ raises arity by~$2$ and lowers
genus by~$1$, applied to $\Theta^{(g-1,n+2)}$.
\emph{Planted-forest corrections}: the higher brackets
$\ell_k^{(\mathrm{tree})}$ for $k\ge 3$ encode the genuinely
logarithmic corrections from Mok's planted-forest strata.

This recursion determines $\Theta^{(g,n)}_\cA$ inductively from
lower $(g',n')$ with $2g'-2+n'<2g-2+n$.  The base cases are
$\Theta^{(0,3)}_\cA$ (the chiral bracket) and
$\Theta^{(0,4)}_\cA$ (the Jacobiator data from weight~$1$).
The recursion terminates at each $(g,n)$: only finitely many stable
graphs contribute.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{The clutching identity.}\enspace
On each rigid boundary stratum~$\rho$ of the log-FM
compactification, the restriction of~$\Theta_\cA$ factors as a
tensor product over vertices of the dual graph:
\[
\Theta_\cA\big|_\rho
\;=\;
(\kappa_\rho)_*\!\left(
\bigotimes_{v\in V(S_\rho)}\Theta_{\cA,v}
\right).
\]
Here $S_\rho$ is the stable dual graph of the stratum~$\rho$,
$\Theta_{\cA,v}$ is the MC element restricted to the irreducible
component at vertex~$v$, and $\kappa_\rho$ is the clutching
morphism: the amplitude on the degenerate curve equals the product
of amplitudes on the irreducible components, sewn by propagators
along the nodes.  It is
the MC-level incarnation of the sewing axiom for conformal field
theory, and the starting point for the clutching law of the quartic
shadow (Section~6.3).

\bigskip
\noindent
\emph{Summary.}\enspace
The universal MC element $\Theta_\cA$ is the organising datum of the
theory.  It is not computed by solving an equation; it is extracted
from the total bar differential by subtracting the tree-level part.
It exists unconditionally, carries a tridegree from Mok's geometry,
converges as an inverse limit, factors over rigid strata by the
clutching law, and determines---through its projections---every
invariant constructed in sections~1 and~2: the bar complex, the
Koszul dual, the genus tower, the complementarity splitting, and the
$\hat A$-genus.  The next two sections extract finer invariants
from~$\Theta_\cA$ that go beyond the scalar characteristic~$\kappa$.

\subsection*{5.1.\enspace The $L_\infty$-twisted differential}

The MC element $\Theta_\cA$ determines the $L_\infty$-twisted
differential on the convolution algebra.  For $x\in\gAmod$:
\[
d_{\Theta_\cA}(x)
\;:=\;
\sum_{n\ge 0}\sum_{g\ge 0}
\frac{\hbar^g}{n!}\,
\ell_{n+1}^{(g)}(\Theta_\cA^{\otimes n},x).
\]
Expanding:
\[
d_{\Theta_\cA}(x)
\;=\;
d(x)
\;+\;
[\Theta_\cA,x]
\;+\;
\tfrac{1}{2!}\ell_3^{(0)}(\Theta_\cA,\Theta_\cA,x)
\;+\;
\hbar\,\ell_1^{(1)}(x)
\;+\;
\hbar\,\ell_2^{(1)}(\Theta_\cA,x)
\;+\;\cdots
\]
In the strict chart (the dg Lie model $\Convstr$), this collapses to:
\[
d_{\Theta_\cA}(x)\;=\;d(x)+[\Theta_\cA,x].
\]
The identity $d_{\Theta_\cA}^2=0$ is equivalent to the MC equation
for~$\Theta_\cA$.

\subsection*{5.2.\enspace The modular tangent complex}

The \emph{modular tangent complex at~$\Theta_\cA$} is the twisted
cochain complex:
\[
T^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\Theta_\cA}
\;:=\;
(\gAmod,\;d_{\Theta_\cA}).
\]
Its cohomology computes the deformation theory of the MC
element.  In classical terms:
\begin{align*}
H^0(T^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\Theta_\cA})
&=\text{infinitesimal automorphisms of~$\Theta_\cA$}
  &&\text{(stabiliser)},\\[3pt]
H^1(T^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\Theta_\cA})
&=\text{first-order deformations of~$\Theta_\cA$}
  &&\text{(tangent space)},\\[3pt]
H^2(T^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\Theta_\cA})
&=\text{obstructions to extending deformations}
  &&\text{(obstruction space)}.
\end{align*}
When $H^2=0$, the MC moduli is smooth at~$\Theta_\cA$.
When $H^1=0$, the MC element is rigid: every nearby element is
gauge-equivalent.

For a one-parameter family $\cA_t$ varying continuously in
level~$k(t)$ or central charge~$c(t)$, the tangent complex
$T^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\Theta_{\cA_t}}$ forms a local system over the
parameter space, and the obstruction class traces a path
in~$H^2$.  The vanishing locus of this obstruction path is the
moduli of liftable deformations.

\subsection*{5.3.\enspace Three characteristic projections}

Three projections of~$\Theta_\cA$:

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{The scalar curvature $\kappa(\cA)$:}\enspace
the arity-two, genus-zero trace:
\[
\kappa(\cA)
\;=\;
\operatorname{tr}(\Theta_\cA)_{2,0}
\;=\;
\operatorname{tr}(\pi\circ m_2\circ\iota^{\otimes 2}
\circ\Delta_{\mathrm{sep}})
\;\in\;\Bbbk.
\]
This is the first Chern class of the modular bar bundle.  It
controls the genus expansion: $\operatorname{obs}_g(\cA)=
\kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_g$, where $\lambda_g$ is the Hodge
class on~$\overline{\cM}_g$.  The Mumford relation
$\lambda_g^{g+1}=0$ implies that genus-$g$ obstructions at the
scalar level live in a vector space of dimension at most~$g$.

Explicit values across the standard landscape:
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
\begin{tabular}{lccl}
\textbf{Algebra $\cA$}
  & $\kappa(\cA)$
  & $\kappa(\cA^!)$
  & \textbf{Sum rule}\\
\hline
Heisenberg $\cH_k$ & $k$ & $-k$ & $0$\\[2pt]
Affine $\widehat{\fg}_k$
  & $\dfrac{(k+h^\vee)\dim\fg}{2h^\vee}$
  & $-\dfrac{(k+h^\vee)\dim\fg}{2h^\vee}$ & $0$\\[6pt]
Virasoro $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ & $c/2$ & $(26-c)/2$ & $13$\\[2pt]
$\beta\gamma$ system & $1$ & $-1$ & $0$\\[2pt]
Lattice $V_\Lambda$ & $\operatorname{rank}(\Lambda)$
  & $-\operatorname{rank}(\Lambda)$ & $0$\\[2pt]
$\mathcal W_N(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_N)$
  & $\dfrac{(N-1)(N^2k+N^2-1)}{N+k}$
  & $-\dfrac{(N-1)(N^2k+N^2-1)}{N+k}$ & $0$\\[6pt]

The complementarity sum rule $\kappa(\cA)+\kappa(\cA^!)=0$ holds for
all self-contragredient algebras (type~I in the Huang--Lepowsky
sense).  The Virasoro exception $\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c)+
\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c^!)=13$ reflects the non-vanishing of the
central charge of the dual $\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$; equivalently,
$\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c)+\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c})=13$ and
self-duality occurs at $c=13$.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{The spectral discriminant $\Delta_\cA(x)$
and the branch transport operator.}

The second characteristic invariant is finer than a number: it is the
characteristic polynomial of a finite-rank operator.  To construct
it, start from the Koszul dual Hilbert series
\[
h_{\cA^!}(q)
\;=\;
\sum_{n\ge 0}\dim H^n(\barB(\cA))\,q^n,
\]
which is a formal power series in~$q$ recording the dimensions of bar
cohomology.  For every standard family, $h_{\cA^!}(q)$ is a
\emph{rational} function: it satisfies a holonomic recurrence relation
of finite order~$d$, and the transfer matrix of this recurrence is
a $d\times d$ matrix $T_{\mathrm{rec}}$.

Define the \emph{spectral branch object} to be a finite-rank perfect
complex $V^{\mathrm{br}}_\cA$ together with a \emph{branch transport
operator} $T_{\mathrm{br},\cA}\colon
V^{\mathrm{br}}_\cA\to V^{\mathrm{br}}_\cA$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pref-delta-def}
\Delta_\cA(x)
\;:=\;
\det\!\bigl(1-x\,T_{\mathrm{br},\cA}\bigr).
\end{equation}
The rank of $V^{\mathrm{br}}_\cA$ equals the degree of algebraicity
of~$h_{\cA^!}(q)$: the order of the minimal recurrence.  The
eigenvalues of $T_{\mathrm{br},\cA}$ are the reciprocal branch
points of the analytic continuation of~$h_{\cA^!}$.

Three structural properties:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item $\Delta_{\cA^!}(x)=\Delta_\cA(x)$---the spectral
  discriminant is \emph{self-dual}, unlike~$\kappa$ which is
  anti-symmetric.
\item Exact factorization functors preserving quadratic OPE data
  preserve~$T_{\mathrm{br},\cA}$.
\item The spectral radius
  $\rho(T_{\mathrm{br},\cA})=\max_i|\lambda_i|$ equals
  $\dim\mathfrak g$ for affine Kac--Moody at generic level.
\end{enumerate}

\emph{Example: $\mathfrak{sl}_3$.}  The Koszul dual Hilbert series
of $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_3$ satisfies a degree-$3$ recurrence.
The transfer matrix is $3\times 3$ with eigenvalues $8$,
$(3+\sqrt{13})/2$, $(3-\sqrt{13})/2$.  The spectral discriminant is
\[
\Delta_{\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_3}(x)
\;=\;
(1-8x)(1-3x-x^2).
\]
The dominant eigenvalue $8=\dim(\mathfrak{sl}_3)$ controls the
exponential growth of bar cohomology dimensions.  The remaining
eigenvalues $(3\pm\sqrt{13})/2$ are the roots of the DS
discriminant factor, shared with the $\mathcal W_3$ generating
function.

\emph{Example: Heisenberg.}  The Hilbert series
$h_{\mathcal H_k^!}(q)=1+q$ is a polynomial (the recurrence has
order~$0$ after the first term), so
$\Delta_{\mathcal H_k}(x)=1$: there is no non-scalar
structure.  This is the Gaussian archetype: $\kappa$ alone
determines everything.

\emph{Example: $\beta\gamma$.}  The Hilbert series is
$1/(1-q)^2$; the discriminant is
$\Delta_{\beta\gamma}(x)=(1-x)^2$.  The double root at $x=1$
reflects the two-dimensional weight space.

In the Chern--Weil dictionary, $\Delta_\cA$ plays the role of the
Chern character: the logarithmic derivative
\[
\operatorname{ch}_r(\cA)
\;=\;
\frac{(-1)^{r-1}}{(r-1)!}\,
\frac{d^r}{dt^r}\Big|_{t=0}\log\Delta_\cA(t)
\;=\;
\frac{1}{r}\operatorname{tr}(T_{\mathrm{br},\cA}^r)
\]
recovers the power-sum symmetric functions of the eigenvalues.  The
scalar $\kappa$ is the first Chern class (a number); $\Delta_\cA$
is the Chern character (a polynomial encoding a finite spectrum);
$\mathfrak R^{\mathrm{mod}}_4$ is the first class that sees the
nonlinear structure beyond this spectrum.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{The quartic shadow
$\mathfrak R^{\mathrm{mod}}_{4,g,n}(\cA)$:}
\[
\mathfrak R^{\mathrm{mod}}_{4,g,n}(\cA)
\;=\;
\operatorname{pr}_{4,g,n}(\Theta_\cA)
\;\in\;
H^*(\overline{\cM}_{g,n})\otimes
(\bar\cA^{\vee})^{\otimes n}_{\mathrm{cyc}}.
\]
This is the first \emph{nonlinear} characteristic class: the first
invariant that cannot be recovered from~$\kappa$ and~$\Delta_\cA$.
Its genus-zero, four-point specialisation is the quartic contact
invariant $Q^{\mathrm{contact}}(\cA)$.  Explicit:
\[
Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\frac{10}{c(5c+22)},
\qquad
Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\cH}=0,
\qquad
Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\beta\gamma}
\;\neq\; 0.
\]
For Heisenberg, the quartic vanishes because the OPE has no cubic
pole.  For $\beta\gamma$, the quartic is the first nonvanishing
shadow (the cubic also vanishes).  For Virasoro, both cubic and
quartic are nonvanishing, and the quartic has the explicit rational
form $10/[c(5c+22)]$.

\subsection*{5.5.\enspace The genus spectral sequence}

The genus filtration $F^g L_{\mathrm{mod}}:=\bigoplus_{g'\ge g}
(\gAmod)_{g',*,*}$ yields a convergent spectral sequence controlling
the lift of MC data across genera.  Write
$D_0=D_{\mathrm{int}}+D_{\mathrm{sep}}$ for the genus-preserving
part of the differential and $D_1=D_{\mathrm{nsep}}$ for the
genus-raising part.  These satisfy $D_0^2=0$,
$D_0D_1+D_1D_0=0$, $D_1^2=0$ (a bicomplex structure).

The spectral sequence:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item $E_0^{p,q}=\operatorname{gr}_F^p L_{\mathrm{mod}}^{p+q}$
  with differential $d_0=[D_0,-]$.
\item $E_1^{p,*}=H^*(\operatorname{gr}_F^p L_{\mathrm{mod}},d_0)$:
  the associated graded cohomology, genus by genus.  At $p=0$:
  tree-level (the ordinary, unlooped homotopy transfer data).  At
  $p=1$: one-loop (the BV trace and its deformations).  At $p=2$:
  genus-two shells (loops, clutching, and planted forests interact
  for the first time).
\item $d_1\colon E_1^{0,*}\to E_1^{1,*-1}$ is the genus-one
  obstruction $\operatorname{Ob}_1$: the obstruction to lifting a
  tree-level MC element to one-loop.  More generally,
  $d_r\colon E_r^{p,*}\to E_r^{p+r,*-r+1}$ encodes
  $\operatorname{Ob}_r$.
\item $E_\infty^{p,q}\cong\operatorname{gr}_F^p H^{p+q}
  (L_{\mathrm{mod}})$: the associated graded of the full modular
  cohomology.
\end{enumerate}
Convergence follows from completeness of the depth filtration and
pronilpotence of the weight filtration: the spectral sequence is
conditionally convergent in the Boardman sense, and the
$\varprojlim^1$ term vanishes by the Mittag-Leffler condition
(finite-dimensionality in each conformal weight).

This spectral sequence is \emph{distinct} from the PBW spectral
sequence (which decomposes by conformal weight within a fixed genus).
The PBW spectral sequence computes \emph{what} bar cohomology is;
the genus spectral sequence determines \emph{whether} the MC element
extends across genera.  For Heisenberg, the genus spectral sequence
degenerates at $E_1$ (every obstruction vanishes).  For Virasoro,
$d_1\neq 0$ and the spectral sequence has nontrivial
differentials at every page.

\subsection*{5.6.\enspace Homotopy invariance and functoriality}

The shadow algebra
$\cA^{\mathrm{sh}}=H_\bullet(\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA))$ is a
homotopy invariant of the chiral algebra.  For any
$\infty_\alpha$-quasi-isomorphism
$f\colon\cA\xrightarrow{\sim}\cA'$, the induced map on MC
$\infty$-groupoids
\[
f_*\colon
\operatorname{MC}_\bullet(\gAmod)
\;\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}
\operatorname{MC}_\bullet(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\cA'})
\]
is a weak homotopy equivalence (a quasi-isomorphism on tangent
complexes at corresponding MC elements).  In particular:
\begin{itemize}
\item The scalar $\kappa(\cA)$ is a quasi-isomorphism invariant.
\item The spectral determinant $\Delta_\cA(t)$ is a
  quasi-isomorphism invariant.
\item The full shadow tower
  $(\kappa,\Delta,\mathfrak C,\mathfrak Q,\mathrm{Sh}_5,
  \mathrm{Sh}_6,\dots)$ consists of quasi-isomorphism invariants.
\item The MC element $\Theta_\cA$ is a quasi-isomorphism invariant
  up to gauge: $f_*(\Theta_\cA)$ and $\Theta_{\cA'}$ are
  gauge-equivalent in $\mathrm{MC}_\bullet(\mathfrak
  g^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\cA'})$.
\end{itemize}

The assignment $\cA\mapsto\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$ is
functorial under $\infty_\alpha$-quasi-isomorphisms in the source.
The full assignment
$\cA\mapsto(\gAmod,\Theta_\cA)$ is a functor from the
$\infty$-category of chiral algebras (with $\infty_\alpha$-quasi-isos
inverted) to the $\infty$-category of MC moduli problems.  This
functoriality is the mathematical content of the statement that
modular Koszul duality is a derived invariant.

One important subtlety: the convolution $L_\infty$ algebra
$\Convinf(\cC,\cP)$ is functorial in each slot separately, but
\emph{not} simultaneously as a bifunctor (the no-bifunctor
obstruction of \S3).  Functoriality of
$\cA\mapsto\gAmod$ proceeds through the source slot,
with the target (the endomorphism operad) held fixed.

\subsection*{6.1.\enspace The extension tower}

Define the \emph{total weight} $w(g,r,d):=2g-2+r+d$, where $g$ is
loop genus, $r$ is arity, and $d$ is log boundary depth.  The total
weight filtration $F^N\gAmod:=\bigoplus_{w\ge N}(\gAmod)_{g,r,d}$
is an exhaustive, separated, complete filtration.  The
\emph{modular extension tower}:
\[
\cE_\cA(N)
:=\operatorname{MC}\bigl(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{amb}}_\cA/F^{N+1}\bigr),
\qquad N\ge 1.
\]
There are natural restriction maps
$\cE_\cA(N+1)\to\cE_\cA(N)$ (truncate the top weight).  A
\emph{full lift} is a compatible point of the inverse system:
\[
\Theta_\cA\;\in\;
\varprojlim_{N}\,\cE_\cA(N).
\]
The bar-intrinsic construction provides such a point
unconditionally.  The shadow Postnikov tower is the study of the
extension tower $\cE_\cA(1)\leftarrow
\cE_\cA(2)\leftarrow\cE_\cA(3)\leftarrow\cdots$ and the
obstructions that arise at each stage.

\subsection*{6.2.\enspace Obstruction classes and the all-arity master equation}

Define the truncated MC elements $\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ by projecting
$\Theta_\cA$ to total weight $\le r$.  The \emph{shadow Postnikov
tower} is the sequence
\[
\Theta_\cA^{\le 2},\quad
\Theta_\cA^{\le 3},\quad
\Theta_\cA^{\le 4},\quad
\Theta_\cA^{\le 5},\quad\dots
\]
At each truncation, the MC equation fails by a remainder:
\[
D\Theta^{\le r}_\cA
+\tfrac12[\Theta^{\le r}_\cA,\Theta^{\le r}_\cA]
\;=\;
-o_{r+1}(\cA),
\]
where the \emph{obstruction class} $o_{r+1}(\cA)$ lives in the
$(r+1)$-graded piece:
\[
o_{r+1}(\cA)
\;\in\;
Z^2\!\left(\frac{F^{r+1}\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{amb}}_\cA}
{F^{r+2}\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{amb}}_\cA},\;d_{\le r}\right).
\]
The obstruction is a cocycle for the differential twisted by the
already-constructed truncation.  The truncation extends to
$\Theta^{\le r+1}$ if and only if $[o_{r+1}(\cA)]=0$ in
$H^2(F^{r+1}/F^{r+2},d_{\le r})$.  When the class is nonzero,
$\Theta^{\le r}$ \emph{cannot} be extended---but the bar-intrinsic
construction guarantees a priori that extension exists.  The
obstruction class is therefore always exact, and the shadow tower
records the gauge choices made at each stage.

The all-arity master equation at arity~$r$ is:
\[
\nabla_H(\mathrm{Sh}_r(\cA))+o^{(r)}(\cA)=0,
\qquad r\ge 3,
\]
where $\nabla_H$ is the Hodge covariant derivative (the linearised
MC operator twisted by the previous stages) and $\mathrm{Sh}_r(\cA)$
is the arity-$r$ shadow (the named invariant extracted from
$\Theta^{\le r}$).  This equation determines $\mathrm{Sh}_r$
uniquely modulo gauge if $H^1(F^r/F^{r+1},d_{\le r-1})=0$, and
up to the image of a gauge map otherwise.

\subsection*{6.3.\enspace Cubic gauge triviality and the canonical quartic}

The first nontrivial obstruction analysis occurs at the cubic level.

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Cubic gauge triviality.}\enspace
When $H^1(F^3\mathfrak g/F^4\mathfrak g,\,d_2)=0$, the cubic MC
term $\Theta^{[3]}_\cA$ is gauge-trivial: there exists a gauge
transformation $\exp(\xi_3)$ with $\xi_3\in F^3$ such that
$\exp(\xi_3)\cdot\Theta^{\le 3}_\cA$ has vanishing weight-$3$
component.
\end{quote}

After this gauge transformation, the quartic obstruction class
$[\mathfrak Q(\cA)]\in H^2(F^4\mathfrak g/F^5\mathfrak g,\,d_2)$
is \emph{canonical}: it is independent of all choices (of homotopy
retraction data, of gauge at the cubic level, of representative
within the MC gauge orbit).  This is the first genuinely nonlinear
invariant of the chiral algebra---the first characteristic class
that is not a polynomial in~$\kappa$.

The abstract mechanism: at the cubic level, the obstruction class
$o_3(\cA)\in H^2(F^3/F^4,d_2)$ is always exact (it must be, since
$\Theta_\cA$ exists).  But more: the \emph{cochain} $\Theta^{[3]}$
lifting this obstruction is itself unique up to coboundary when
$H^1(F^3/F^4,d_2)=0$.  After removal, the quartic error is a
well-defined cohomology class.  This is the standard Postnikov
mechanism: trivial $H^1$ at level~$n$ makes the $H^2$ class at
level~$n+1$ canonical.

The clutching law for the quartic class is a consequence of Mok's log
FM degeneration formula.  For a one-parameter degeneration
$C\leadsto C_0=C_1\cup_p C_2$:
\[
\mathfrak Q(\cA)\big|_{C_0}
\;=\;
\sum_{a+b=4}\mathfrak Q^{(a)}(\cA)\big|_{C_1}
\cdot\mathfrak Q^{(b)}(\cA)\big|_{C_2}
\;+\;
\text{planted-forest correction}.
\]
The planted-forest correction is the genuinely logarithmic
contribution from Mok's log FM compactification; it has no
counterpart in the classical Deligne--Mumford setting.

The quartic contact invariant has the explicit value:
\[
Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\frac{10}{c(5c+22)}
\;\in\;
H^2(F^4/F^5,d_2).
\]
The denominator $c(5c+22)$ has physical meaning: $c=0$ is the
logarithmic threshold; $c=-22/5$ is the minimal model accumulation
point $\mathcal M(2,5)$.  The numerator~$10=\binom{5}{2}$ counts
the arity-four Catalan channels.

\subsection*{6.4.\enspace The four depth classes}

The \emph{shadow depth} $r_{\max}(\cA)$ is the smallest integer~$r$
such that $o_{r+1}(\cA)=0$ for all subsequent levels, or
$r_{\max}=\infty$ if the tower never terminates.  Equivalently,
$r_{\max}$ is the largest arity at which the shadow tower carries a
nontrivial invariant.

The standard landscape partitions into exactly four depth classes:

\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
\begin{tabular}{lcccll}
\textbf{Class} & \textbf{Symbol} & $r_{\max}$
  & $\ell_3^{(0)},\;\ell_4^{(0)}$
  & \textbf{Potential type} & \textbf{Examples}\\
\hline
Gaussian & G & $2$ & $0,\;0$
  & quadratic
  & Heisenberg $\cH_k$, free fermion $\psi$\\
Lie/tree & L & $3$ & $\neq 0,\;0$
  & cubic
  & Affine $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_N$, current algebras\\
Contact & C & $4$ & $0,\;\neq 0$
  & quartic
  & $\beta\gamma$ system, $bc$ ghosts\\
Mixed & M & $\infty$ & $\neq 0,\;\neq 0$
  & non-polynomial
  & Virasoro, $\mathcal W_N$, $\mathcal W_\infty$
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent
The depth class is determined by the OPE structure:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Gaussian} ($r_{\max}=2$): the OPE has no simple pole
  (purely singular of order~$\ge 2$).  The bracket
  $\ell_2^{(0)}=\pi m_2\iota^{\otimes 2}$ on bar cohomology is
  central, and all higher brackets vanish.  The shadow tower
  terminates at~$\kappa$.  The potential on the MC moduli is
  quadratic.
\item \textbf{Lie/tree} ($r_{\max}=3$): the OPE has a simple pole
  generating a Lie bracket, but the transferred quaternary bracket
  $\ell_4^{(0)}$ vanishes on bar cohomology.  The ternary bracket
  is the last nontrivial shadow.  The potential is cubic.
\item \textbf{Contact} ($r_{\max}=4$): the simple-pole bracket
  vanishes on bar cohomology (as in Heisenberg), but the quaternary
  bracket survives.  The first nontrivial shadow is
  $\mathfrak Q(\cA)$, and the tower terminates there.
  The potential is quartic.
\item \textbf{Mixed} ($r_{\max}=\infty$): both cubic and quartic
  shadows are nontrivial, and their interaction (the Poisson bracket
  $\{\mathfrak C,\mathfrak Q\}_H$) generates a nonvanishing quintic
  obstruction, which in turn generates a sextic, and so on.  The
  tower never terminates.  The potential is transcendental.
\end{itemize}

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Shadow depth is not Koszulness.}\enspace
All four depth classes contain chirally Koszul algebras.  Heisenberg
(depth~$2$), affine at generic level (depth~$3$), $\beta\gamma$
(depth~$4$), and Virasoro (depth~$\infty$) are all chirally Koszul.
Shadow depth classifies the \emph{complexity} of Koszul algebras,
not Koszulness itself.

The \emph{operadic complexity conjecture}: the shadow depth equals
the $A_\infty$-depth equals the $L_\infty$-formality level:
\[
r_{\max}(\cA)
\;=\;
A_\infty\text{-depth}(\cA)
\;=\;
L_\infty\text{-formality level}(\cA).
\]
Here $A_\infty$-depth is the largest~$r$ such that $m_r\neq 0$ in
the minimal $A_\infty$ model of the bar complex, and $L_\infty$-formality
level is the largest~$r$ such that the $L_\infty$ structure on bar
cohomology has a nontrivial $r$-ary bracket (not removable by
$L_\infty$-isomorphism).  The identification of the shadow tower with
the $L_\infty$ formality obstruction tower is proved at
arities~$2$, $3$, $4$; the general statement is conjectural.

\subsection*{6.5.\enspace Genus-two shells}

Genus~$2$ is the first level where all three types of boundary
stratum interact simultaneously.  The genus-two MC component
decomposes as:
\[
\Theta^{(2)}_\cA
\;=\;
\underbrace{\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}\circ\mathrm{loop}}}_{%
\text{iterated BV: two self-loops}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}}_{%
\text{clutching $\times$ BV: one separating + one self-loop}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{pf}}}_{%
\text{planted-forest: Mok's log-FM strata}}.
\]

The iterated-BV term $\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}^2}$ involves the
graph with one vertex of genus~$0$ and two self-loops (the
``figure-eight'').  Its amplitude is the iterated trace:
\[
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}^2}
\;=\;
\tfrac{1}{2}\operatorname{tr}_1\operatorname{tr}_2
\bigl(P_\cA^{\otimes 2}\circ m^{(h)}_{0,4}\circ\Delta^{\otimes 2}\bigr).
\]

The separating-times-loop term
$\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}$ is the graph with
two vertices connected by a separating edge, one vertex carrying a
self-loop.  Its amplitude is:
\[
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}
\;=\;
\operatorname{tr}_{\mathrm{ns}}\bigl(
P_\cA\circ\ell_2^{(0)}(\Theta^{(1,\bullet)},\Theta^{(1,\bullet)})
\bigr).
\]

The planted-forest term is the genuinely logarithmic contribution,
present only when the algebra has nontrivial cubic or quartic
shadows.  It arises from depth-$2$ strata of
$\operatorname{FM}_n(X\mathbin{|}D)$ that have no classical
counterpart.

\medskip

\noindent
The family-by-family structure:

\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
\begin{tabular}{lcccc}
& $\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}^2}$
& $\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}$
& $\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{pf}}$
& \textbf{Depth class}\\
\hline
\textbf{Heisenberg} $\cH_k$
  & $\checkmark$ & --- & --- & G\\
\textbf{Affine} $\widehat{\fg}_k$
  & $\checkmark$ & $\checkmark$ & --- & L\\
\textbf{$\beta\gamma$ system}
  & $\checkmark$ & --- & $\checkmark$ & C\\
\textbf{Virasoro} $\mathrm{Vir}_c$
  & $\checkmark$ & $\checkmark$ & $\checkmark$ & M\\
$\boldsymbol{\mathcal W_N}$
  & $\checkmark$ & $\checkmark$ & $\checkmark$ & M
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

For Heisenberg: only the iterated BV contributes (two independent
loop contractions on a free field).  No separating clutching (the Lie
bracket on bar cohomology is central) and no planted forests (no
higher interactions).  For affine: the separating-times-loop term
appears (the Lie bracket creates a nontrivial genus-one correction
after one clutching), but planted forests are absent (the cubic
shadow terminates the tower).  For Virasoro and $\mathcal W_N$: all
three terms contribute, and the planted-forest term carries the
quartic resonance data.  For $\beta\gamma$: the separating term
vanishes (the Lie bracket is trivial on bar cohomology) but the
planted-forest term is nontrivial (the quartic contact invariant
generates genus-two corrections through the planted-forest sector).

\subsection*{6.6.\enspace The genus loop operator}

The \emph{genus loop operator}
$\Lambda_P\colon\operatorname{Sym}^r(\bar\cA^\vee)_{\mathrm{cyc}}
\to\operatorname{Sym}^{r-2}(\bar\cA^\vee)_{\mathrm{cyc}}$ contracts
two legs of an arity-$r$ cyclic cochain with the propagator:
\[
\Lambda_P(\phi)(a_1,\dots,a_{r-2})
\;=\;
\sum_i\phi(a_1,\dots,a_{r-2},e_i,e^i),
\]
where $\{e_i\}$, $\{e^i\}$ is a dual basis for the cyclic pairing.
This operator raises genus by~$1$ and lowers arity by~$2$,
converting an arity-$r$ shadow at genus~$g$ into an arity-$(r-2)$
shadow at genus~$g+1$.  It is the shadow-level incarnation of the
BV operator $\Delta_{\mathrm{ns}}$.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Explicit Virasoro computation.}\enspace
On the Virasoro primary line, the conformal-weight-$2$ bar
cohomology is one-dimensional, spanned by the class~$x$ dual to the
stress tensor~$T$.  The Killing form is $H=\frac{c}{2}x^2$ and the
propagator is $P=\frac{2}{c}$.  The quartic contact invariant is
$Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}\cdot x^4$ with
$Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}=10/[c(5c+22)]$.  The genus loop
operator applied to this quartic:
\[
\Lambda_P\!\left(\frac{10}{c(5c+22)}\cdot x^4\right)
\;=\;
\binom{4}{2}\cdot\frac{2}{c}\cdot
\frac{10}{c(5c+22)}\cdot x^2
\;=\;
\frac{120}{c^2(5c+22)}\,x^2.
\]
The binomial coefficient $\binom{4}{2}=6$ counts the ways to select
two legs from four for the propagator contraction.  The factor
$2/c$ is the propagator.  The result is the \emph{genus-one Hessian
correction} $\delta_{H,\mathrm{Vir}}^{(1)}=120/[c^2(5c+22)]\cdot x^2$:
the genus-one correction to the quartic shadow, expressed as a
quadratic form on the same one-dimensional bar cohomology.

The \emph{loop ratio}:
\[
\rho^{(1)}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\frac{\Lambda_P(Q\cdot x^4)}{\kappa\cdot x^2}
\;=\;
\frac{120/[c^2(5c+22)]}{c/2}
\;=\;
\frac{240}{c^3(5c+22)}.
\]
This ratio carries information strictly beyond $\kappa=c/2$: it
detects the quartic structure of the Virasoro algebra through its
genus-one projection.  The asymptotic behaviour
$\rho^{(1)}\sim 48/c^4$ for $c\to\infty$ reflects the weakening of
quartic corrections in the semiclassical limit.

\subsection*{6.7.\enspace Quintic forcing and infinite towers}

The quintic obstruction class determines whether the shadow tower
terminates at arity~$4$.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Virasoro.}\enspace
The cubic shadow $\mathfrak C_{\mathrm{Vir}}$ and the quartic contact
invariant $Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}$ are both nonzero.
Their Poisson bracket under the Hodge pairing gives the quintic
obstruction:
\[
o^{(5)}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\{\mathfrak C_{\mathrm{Vir}},\,
Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}\}_H
\;=\;
\frac{480}{c^2(5c+22)}\,x^5
\;\neq\;0.
\]
Since $o^{(5)}\neq 0$ for all $c\neq 0,-22/5$, the Virasoro shadow
tower does not terminate at any finite arity.  The tower is infinite:
$r_{\max}(\mathrm{Vir}_c)=\infty$.  The same holds for every
$\mathcal W_N$ algebra ($N\ge 2$): the cubic and quartic shadows
coexist and their bracket forces an infinite sequence of higher
obstructions.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Heisenberg.}\enspace
$\mathfrak C_{\cH}=0$ and $Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\cH}=0$.  The
quintic obstruction $o^{(5)}=\{0,0\}_H=0$.  Every higher obstruction
also vanishes.  The tower terminates at $r_{\max}=2$.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Affine.}\enspace
$\mathfrak C_{\widehat\fg}\neq 0$ but
$Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\widehat\fg}=0$ (the quaternary bracket
vanishes on bar cohomology by the PBW theorem for Kac--Moody
algebras).  The quintic obstruction
$o^{(5)}=\{\mathfrak C,0\}_H=0$.  The tower terminates at
$r_{\max}=3$.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{$\beta\gamma$.}\enspace
$\mathfrak C_{\beta\gamma}=0$ (the Lie bracket is trivial on bar
cohomology) but $Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\beta\gamma}\neq 0$.
The quintic obstruction $o^{(5)}=\{0,Q\}_H=0$.  The tower terminates
at $r_{\max}=4$.

\medskip

\noindent
The pattern:
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{lcccc}
& $\mathfrak C$ & $\mathfrak Q$
  & $o^{(5)}=\{\mathfrak C,\mathfrak Q\}_H$ & $r_{\max}$\\
\hline
Heisenberg & $0$ & $0$ & $0$ & $2$\\
Affine & $\neq 0$ & $0$ & $0$ & $3$\\
$\beta\gamma$ & $0$ & $\neq 0$ & $0$ & $4$\\
Virasoro & $\neq 0$ & $\neq 0$ & $\neq 0$ & $\infty$
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Finite termination requires at least one of $\mathfrak C=0$ or
$\mathfrak Q=0$.  When both are nonzero, their bracket generates
the quintic, the quintic and quartic generate the sextic, and
the induction continues without bound.  The shadow tower
becomes a genuine non-polynomial object: a transcendental potential
on the MC moduli.

\subsection*{6.8.\enspace Independent sum factorisation}

For a direct sum $L=L_1\oplus L_2$ of Lie conformal algebras with
vanishing mixed OPE ($[L_1{}_\lambda L_2]=0$), all shadow invariants
separate:
\begin{align*}
\kappa(L_1\oplus L_2)
&=\kappa(L_1)+\kappa(L_2)
&&\text{(additive)},\\[3pt]
T^{\mathrm{br}}(L_1\oplus L_2)
&=T^{\mathrm{br}}(L_1)\oplus T^{\mathrm{br}}(L_2)
&&\text{(direct sum)},\\[3pt]
\Delta_{L_1\oplus L_2}(t)
&=\Delta_{L_1}(t)\cdot\Delta_{L_2}(t)
&&\text{(multiplicative)},\\[3pt]
R_4^{\mathrm{mod}}(L_1\oplus L_2)
&=R_4^{\mathrm{mod}}(L_1)+R_4^{\mathrm{mod}}(L_2)
&&\text{(additive)}.
\end{align*}
More generally, the full MC element factorises:
\[
\Theta_{L_1\oplus L_2}
\;=\;
\Theta_{L_1}\hat\otimes 1+1\hat\otimes\Theta_{L_2}
\;\in\;
\gAmod[L_1]\;\hat\otimes\;\gAmod[L_2],
\]
where $\hat\otimes$ is the completed tensor product with respect to
the depth filtration; at the level of partition functions,
$Z_{L_1\oplus L_2}=Z_{L_1}\cdot Z_{L_2}$.

The factorisation principle reduces computation for composite
algebras to their primitive summands.  Every algebra in the standard
landscape is either primitive (Heisenberg, Virasoro, $\beta\gamma$)
or built from primitive pieces by direct sum plus extension
(affine = current algebra over simple Lie, lattice = exponential
extension of Heisenberg, $\mathcal W_N$ = quantum Drinfeld--Sokolov
reduction of affine).  The full shadow tower of any composite
algebra is determined by its primitive constituents.

\section*{7.\quad The standard landscape}
% ====================================================================

The machine of sections~1--6 accepts any chiral algebra as input and
produces a universal MC element, a shadow tower, a genus expansion,
and a hierarchy of characteristic classes.  The seven families
below exhaust the standard Lie-theoretic landscape: each exhibits a
different bar-complex structure, a different shadow archetype, a
different genus-two shell profile, and a different completion
behaviour.  No two families share all four invariants.  Taken
together, they realise every depth class (G/L/C/M), every curvature
sign, and every possible pattern of genus-two shell activation.

The format is uniform: for each family we display the complete OPE,
the bar complex with explicit differential, the Koszul dual, the
shadow tower with depth classification, the modular characteristic
with complementarity, the genus-two shell profile, and any structural
feature unique to that family.


% ----- 7.1 Heisenberg -----
\subsection*{7.1.\quad Heisenberg}

A single bosonic field $\alpha$ of conformal weight~$1$.  The OPE is
\[
\alpha(z)\,\alpha(w)
\;\sim\;
\frac{k}{(z-w)^2}.
\]
No simple pole: the singularity is purely of order~$2$.  The mode
algebra is the Heisenberg Lie algebra
$[\alpha_m,\alpha_n]=km\,\delta_{m+n,0}$, and the vertex algebra
$\mathcal H_k$ is the vacuum module at level~$k\neq 0$.  Central
charge $c=1$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Bar complex.}
The augmentation ideal
$\bar{\mathcal H}_k=\mathcal H_k/\mathbb C\cdot\mathbf{1}$ is
spanned by normally ordered monomials in~$\alpha$ and its
derivatives.  The bar construction is the tensor coalgebra on the
desuspension:
\[
\barB(\mathcal H_k)
\;=\;
T^c\!\bigl(s^{-1}\bar{\mathcal H}_k\bigr),
\qquad
d_{\barB}\bigl(s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n\bigr)
\;=\;
\sum_{i<j}\pm\,
s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}\operatorname{Res}_{z_i=z_j}
\bigl(a_i(z_i)\,a_j(z_j)\,\eta_{ij}\bigr)|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n.
\]
Because the only singularity is a double pole (producing a scalar),
$d_{\barB}$ acts by contraction: it pairs two tensor factors and
replaces them by a scalar.  Explicitly:
\begin{align*}
d_{\barB}(s^{-1}\alpha) &= 0,\\
d_{\barB}(s^{-1}\alpha|s^{-1}\alpha) &=
  k\cdot s^{-1}\mathbf{1} = 0 \quad
  \text{(in the reduced complex)},\\
d_{\barB}(s^{-1}\partial^m\alpha|s^{-1}\partial^n\alpha) &=
  k\binom{m+n+1}{m}\cdot s^{-1}\mathbf{1} = 0 \quad (m+n\ge 1).
\end{align*}
Bar cohomology concentrates:
\[
H^1\bigl(\barB(\mathcal H_k)\bigr) = \mathbb C\cdot\alpha^*,
\qquad
H^n\bigl(\barB(\mathcal H_k)\bigr) = 0 \;\;\text{for}\;\; n\ge 2.
\]
PBW concentration: bar cohomology is one-dimensional in degree~$1$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Koszul dual.}
Verdier duality on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$ sends
$\barB(\mathcal H_k)$ to a curved chiral coalgebra.  The linear dual
is
\[
\mathcal H_k^!
\;=\;
\bigl(\operatorname{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*),\;m_0=-k\omega\bigr),
\]
where $V^*=\mathbb C\cdot\alpha^*$ is the dual generator space and
$m_0=-k\omega$ is the curvature: a curving element, not a
differential.  The curved $A_\infty$-relation reads
$m_1^2(a)=[m_0,a]$ (commutator, with sign).  The Koszul dual of
Heisenberg is \emph{not} Heisenberg: $\mathcal H_k^!$ is a
\emph{curved} symmetric algebra, while $\mathcal H_k$ is a
\emph{flat} vertex algebra.  This asymmetry persists at all levels;
it is the simplest instance of the general phenomenon that chiral
Koszul duality is not an involution but a Verdier-type reflection.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Shadow tower.}
The universal MC element collapses to a scalar:
\[
\Theta_{\mathcal H_k}
\;=\;
k\cdot\eta\otimes\Lambda.
\]
Here $\eta=d\log(z_1-z_2)$ is the propagator on
$\operatorname{FM}_2(X)$ and $\Lambda$ is the universal two-point
form on the modular operad.  Every shadow is a scalar multiple of a
universal geometric datum: the five-component differential collapses
completely.  In detail:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item $d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}=0$: no higher transferred operations
  (the OPE has no simple pole),
\item $d_{\mathrm{coll}}=0$: collisions produce scalars only,
\item $d_{\mathrm{pf}}=0$: no planted-forest contributions (no
  arity-$3$ operations to compose),
\item only $d_{\check C}+d_{\mathrm{loop}}$ survive.
\end{itemize}
Cubic shadow: $\mathfrak{C}(\mathcal H_k)=0$.  Quartic contact
shadow: $Q^{\mathrm{contact}}(\mathcal H_k)=0$.  All higher
obstructions vanish.  Shadow depth~$2$.  Gaussian archetype.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Modular characteristic and complementarity.}
\[
\kappa(\mathcal H_k) = k,
\qquad
\kappa(\mathcal H_k^!) = -k,
\qquad
\kappa(\mathcal H_k)+\kappa(\mathcal H_k^!) = 0.
\]
The genus tower is captured by the $\hat A$-genus:
\[
\sum_{g\ge 0}Q_g(\mathcal H_k)\cdot\hbar^{2g-2}
\;=\;
k\,\hat A(i\hbar).
\]
At genus~$g$, $Q_g(\mathcal H_k)$ is a polynomial of degree~$2g$
in~$k$, computed entirely from iterated loop contractions of the
single propagator~$\eta$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Genus-two shell profile.}
Only the loop$\circ$loop shell contributes:
\[
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}\circ\mathrm{loop}}\neq 0,
\qquad
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}= 0,
\qquad
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{pf}}= 0.
\]
The Heisenberg bar complex is the unique family where every genus
correction reduces to iterated loop contractions of a single
propagator.  It is the Gaussian measure of chiral algebra theory:
the reference point against which all nonlinearities are measured.


% ----- 7.2 Kac-Moody -----
\subsection*{7.2.\quad Affine Kac--Moody}

Let $\mathfrak g$ be a simple Lie algebra of rank~$\ell$,
dimension~$d=\dim\mathfrak g$, dual Coxeter number~$h^\vee$,
with structure constants $f^{ab}{}_c$ and normalised Killing
form~$\kappa^{ab}$.  The affine vertex algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k$ has generators $J^a$,
$a=1,\dots,d$, of conformal weight~$1$, with OPE
\[
J^a(z)\,J^b(w)
\;\sim\;
\frac{f^{ab}{}_c\,J^c(w)}{z-w}
\;+\;
\frac{k\,\kappa^{ab}}{(z-w)^2}.
\]
Both pole orders are present.  The simple pole carries the Lie
bracket; the double pole carries the invariant inner product at
level~$k$.  The mode algebra is the affine Lie algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak g}$:
\[
[J^a_m,J^b_n]
\;=\;
f^{ab}{}_c\,J^c_{m+n}
\;+\;
km\,\kappa^{ab}\,\delta_{m+n,0}.
\]

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Bar complex.}
The simple-pole residue contributes a bracket component
to~$d_{\barB}$:
\[
d_{\barB}(s^{-1}J^a|s^{-1}J^b)
\;=\;
f^{ab}{}_c\,s^{-1}J^c
\;+\;
k\,\kappa^{ab}\cdot(\text{curvature term}).
\]
The bracket component is the Chevalley--Eilenberg differential of the
loop algebra $L\mathfrak g$; the curvature component is the central
extension by~$k$.  The identity $d_{\barB}^2=0$ requires the
\emph{Borcherds identity}, not the Jacobi identity.  The Borcherds
identity encodes the $n$-fold residue relations from all pole orders
simultaneously; it is the vertex-algebraic statement that replaces
the classical quadratic relation $[d,[d,-]]=0$.  The $n$th component
of the bar differential extracts the $(n-1)$-fold iterated residue of
the $n$-point correlation function, including all mixed-order poles.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{PBW spectral sequence.}
Filter $\barB(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)$ by conformal weight.  The
spectral sequence $E_1^{p,q}(g)$ at genus~$g$ has $E_1$ page given
by the bar cohomology of the associated graded---a free-field
computation in the Heisenberg algebra
$\mathcal H_k^{\otimes d}$.  PBW concentration at all genera is
proved: $E_\infty^{p,q}(g)=0$ for $q\neq 0$.  The enriched
genus-$g$ $E_1$ page carries the structure of a free resolution of the
diagonal $\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k$-bimodule, with the Hodge
bundle~$\mathbb E$ supplying the curvature twist at each genus.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Sugawara construction.}
At non-critical level $k\neq -h^\vee$, the stress tensor is
\[
T(z)
\;=\;
\frac{1}{2(k+h^\vee)}
\sum_{a=1}^{d}{:}J^a(z)J^a(z){:}\,,
\qquad
c
\;=\;
\frac{k\,\dim\mathfrak g}{k+h^\vee}.
\]
The Sugawara field is a composite: it lies in the normally-ordered
algebra generated by the currents, not in the generating set.  At
the critical level $k=-h^\vee$, the Sugawara construction is
\emph{undefined}---not ``$c$ diverges'' but $c$ does not exist.  The
Virasoro field is not a composite of currents.  The Feigin--Frenkel
center $\mathfrak z(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_{-h^\vee})$ is a
commutative vertex algebra isomorphic to the ring of functions on the
space of ${}^L\mathfrak g$-opers on the formal disk.  In the bar
complex, the critical-level specialisation replaces the Sugawara
curvature by a flat but noncommutative coalgebra structure.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Shadow tower.}
The cubic shadow is the Lie bracket itself:
\[
\mathfrak C(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)
\;=\;
\bigl[f_{abc}\,J^a\otimes J^b\otimes J^c\bigr]
\;\in\;
H^1(F^3\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}/F^4\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}},\,d_2).
\]
The quartic obstruction $o^{(4)}(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)=0$: the
Jacobi identity on~$\mathfrak g$ kills the only candidate quartic
cocycle via $f^{ab}{}_e f^{ec}{}_d+\text{cyclic}=0$.  The quintic
obstruction vanishes by the same mechanism.  Shadow depth~$3$.
Lie/tree archetype.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Modular characteristic and complementarity.}
\[
\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)
\;=\;
\frac{(k+h^\vee)\dim\mathfrak g}{2h^\vee},
\qquad
\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k^!)
\;=\;
-\frac{(k+h^\vee)\dim\mathfrak g}{2h^\vee}.
\]
Complementarity: $\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)+
\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k^!)=0$ for all $k\neq -h^\vee$.
The Feigin--Frenkel involution
\[
k\;\longleftrightarrow\; -k-2h^\vee
\]
is Koszul duality in the modular sense: the level of the dual algebra
is the Langlands dual level.  The symmetry $c(k)=c(-k-2h^\vee)$
becomes the complementarity relation evaluated on this family.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Genus-two shell profile.}
At genus~$2$, the loop$\circ$loop and sep$\circ$loop shells both
contribute:
\[
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}\circ\mathrm{loop}}\neq 0,
\qquad
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}\neq 0,
\qquad
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{pf}}= 0.
\]
The planted-forest shell vanishes because the quartic shadow is zero
and planted forests require arity~$\ge 4$.  Separating degeneration
activates through the cubic shadow (the Lie bracket) composed with
the loop contraction: the genus-two correction factors through
$\mathfrak g \otimes \mathfrak g \xrightarrow{[-,-]}
\mathfrak g \xrightarrow{\mathrm{loop}} \mathbb C$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Special feature: Feigin--Frenkel at critical level.}
At $k=-h^\vee$, the vertex algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak g}_{-h^\vee}$ has an enormous center.
The center $\mathfrak z(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_{-h^\vee})
\cong \operatorname{Fun}(\operatorname{Op}_{{}^L\mathfrak g}(D))$
is the ring of opers on the formal disk for the Langlands dual.  The
bar complex at critical level is \emph{flat} ($m_0^{(1)}=0$): the
modular characteristic $\kappa(-h^\vee)=0$.  Critical-level chiral
Koszul duality is the geometric Langlands correspondence at the
level of the bar construction.


% ----- 7.3 Virasoro -----
\subsection*{7.3.\quad Virasoro}

One generator~$T$ of conformal weight~$2$.  The OPE is
\[
T(z)\,T(w)
\;\sim\;
\frac{c/2}{(z-w)^4}
\;+\;
\frac{2\,T(w)}{(z-w)^2}
\;+\;
\frac{\partial T(w)}{z-w}.
\]
Three pole orders.  The fourth-order pole carries the central
charge~$c$; the second-order pole carries the conformal weight
of~$T$ itself ($T_{(1)}T=2T$); the simple pole carries the
translation~$\partial T$.  Unlike Kac--Moody, the field~$T$ appears
in its own OPE at second order---a self-coupling that is the source of
all nonlinear complexity.  The mode algebra is the Virasoro algebra:
\[
[L_m,L_n]
\;=\;
(m-n)L_{m+n}
\;+\;
\frac{c}{12}(m^3-m)\,\delta_{m+n,0}.
\]

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Bar complex.}
The bar differential on the Virasoro vacuum module has three components:
\begin{align*}
d_{\barB}(s^{-1}T|s^{-1}T)
&=
2\,s^{-1}T + \frac{c}{2}\cdot(\text{curvature})
\quad\text{(from $(z-w)^{-2}$ and $(z-w)^{-4}$ poles)},\\
d_{\barB}(s^{-1}T|s^{-1}\partial T)
&=
s^{-1}\partial T + 3\,s^{-1}T
\quad\text{(from $(z-w)^{-1}$ and $(z-w)^{-2}$ poles)}.
\end{align*}
At higher tensor degree, the bar differential on
$s^{-1}T|s^{-1}T|s^{-1}T$ produces terms involving the
self-coupling $T_{(1)}T=2T$ composed with itself---the first
instance of a bar differential that is genuinely quadratic in the
structure constants.  PBW concentration still holds: at every
genus~$g$, the spectral sequence collapses to $E_\infty^{p,0}$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Koszul dual.}
\[
\operatorname{Vir}_c^!
\;=\;
\operatorname{Vir}_{26-c}.
\]
The duality is an involution on the one-parameter Virasoro family.
The fixed point is $c=13$, \emph{not}~$c=26$.  Self-duality:
$\operatorname{Vir}_c \cong \operatorname{Vir}_c^!$ iff $c=13$.
The central charge~$26$ plays no special role in Koszul duality; its
appearance in string theory is the vanishing of
$\kappa(\operatorname{Vir}_{26})=13=
\kappa(\operatorname{Vir}_{26}^!)$: the
\emph{self-complementarity} condition, not the self-duality condition.

The same-family shadow $\operatorname{Vir}_{26-c}$ is the
depth-zero resonance shadow: the image of the finite-dimensional
resonance truncation $R_{\mathrm{Vir}}$, not the final Koszul dual in
the completed sense.  The genuine $\mathcal W_\infty$-type dual
requires the full resonance-filtered completion.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Shadow tower.}
The cubic shadow is determined by the self-coupling $T_{(1)}T=2T$:
\[
\mathfrak{C}_{\mathrm{Vir}} \;\neq\; 0.
\]
The quartic contact shadow, extracted from the four-point collision
residue on $\overline{C}_4(\mathbb P^1)$, is
\[
Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\frac{10}{c(5c+22)}.
\]
The poles at $c=0$ and $c=-22/5$ reflect the degeneration of the
Virasoro vacuum module: at $c=0$ the identity representation
degenerates, and at $c=-22/5$ the $(2,5)$ minimal model forces the
vacuum Verma module to acquire a singular vector at weight~$4$.

The quintic obstruction is the Poisson bracket in the shadow algebra:
\[
o^{(5)}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\bigl\{C_{\mathrm{Vir}},\,
Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}\bigr\}_H
\;=\;
\frac{480}{c^2(5c+22)}\,x^5
\;\neq\; 0.
\]
The nonvanishing of~$o^{(5)}$ forces the shadow tower to be
\emph{infinite}: no finite truncation of the MC element captures the
Virasoro algebra.  Shadow depth~$\infty$.  Mixed modular archetype.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Modular characteristic and complementarity.}
\[
\kappa(\operatorname{Vir}_c) = \frac{c}{2},
\qquad
\kappa(\operatorname{Vir}_{26-c}) = \frac{26-c}{2} = 13 - \frac{c}{2},
\qquad
\kappa(\operatorname{Vir}_c)+\kappa(\operatorname{Vir}_c^!)
= 13.
\]
This is the unique standard family where complementarity gives a
\emph{nonzero} constant: $\kappa+\kappa^!=13$ rather than~$0$.  The
constant~$13$ is half the critical dimension~$26$, and its
nonvanishing reflects the fact that $\operatorname{Vir}$ is a
one-generator algebra whose bar complex has nontrivial
self-coupling---the curvature of the dual cannot cancel the curvature
of the algebra when they live in the same one-dimensional family.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Higher-genus corrections.}
At genus~$1$, the shadow tower produces corrections beyond the
linear curvature.  The genus-$1$ Hessian correction:
\[
\delta H^{(1)}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\frac{120}{c^2(5c+22)}\cdot x^2.
\]
The loop ratio:
\[
\rho^{(1)}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\frac{240}{c^3(5c+22)}.
\]
The Hessian correction $\delta H^{(1)}$ is the genus-$1$ quadratic
deviation from the linearised curvature; the loop
ratio~$\rho^{(1)}$ is the ratio of the quadratic correction
to~$\kappa^2$.  Both are determined by the quartic contact shadow
and carry information strictly beyond~$\kappa=c/2$: they are the
first nonlinear genus-$1$ invariants, visible only because the shadow
tower has depth~$\ge 4$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Genus-two shell profile.}
All three primitive shells contribute:
\[
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}\circ\mathrm{loop}}\neq 0,
\qquad
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}\neq 0,
\qquad
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{pf}}\neq 0.
\]
The loop$\circ$loop shell comes from iterated curvature; the
sep$\circ$loop shell from mixed clutching; and the planted-forest
shell from the quartic contact channel~$Q^{\mathrm{contact}}$.  The
Virasoro algebra is the simplest family in which the planted-forest
shell is nonzero---the first family that sees the log-FM boundary
strata of $\overline{\mathcal M}_{2,n}$.


% ----- 7.4 W_3 -----
\subsection*{7.4.\quad $\boldsymbol{\mathcal W_3}$}

Two generators: the stress tensor~$T$ (weight~$2$) and a primary
field~$W$ (weight~$3$).  The $TT$~OPE is Virasoro.  The $TW$~OPE
encodes the primary property:
\[
T(z)\,W(w)
\;\sim\;
\frac{3\,W(w)}{(z-w)^2}
\;+\;
\frac{\partial W(w)}{z-w}.
\]
The $WW$~OPE is the first genuinely nonlinear datum in the standard
landscape:
\begin{align*}
W(z)\,W(w)
\;\sim\;\;
&\frac{c/3}{(z-w)^6}
\;+\;
\frac{2\,T(w)}{(z-w)^4}
\;+\;
\frac{\partial T(w)}{(z-w)^3}\\[4pt]
&+\;
\frac{1}{(z-w)^2}
\biggl[
\frac{16}{22+5c}\,\Lambda(w)
\;+\;
\frac{3}{10}\,\partial^2 T(w)
\biggr]
\;+\;\cdots\,,
\end{align*}
where the composite field
\[
\Lambda
\;=\;
{:}TT{:}\;-\;\tfrac{3}{10}\,\partial^2 T
\]
is the unique quasi-primary of weight~$4$ in the vacuum module.  The
coefficient $-3/10$ is fixed by requiring $T(z)\Lambda(w)$ to have
no third-order pole: quasi-primary projection orthogonal to the
descendant~$\partial^2 T$.  The structure constant
$16/(22+5c)$ governs the $WW\Lambda$ coupling.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The $c=-22/5$ degeneration.}
At $c=-22/5$, the denominator vanishes and $\Lambda$ becomes a null
field.  The vacuum Verma module has a singular vector at weight~$4$:
the Kac determinant vanishes there, and $\mathcal W_3$ is not
finitely generated by $T$ and~$W$ alone.  Infinitely many new
generators are required.  This is the first $\mathcal W$-algebra
where the structure constants have poles in the central charge.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Shadow tower.}
The cubic shadow inherits the Virasoro self-coupling $T_{(1)}T=2T$
and acquires a contribution from $T_{(1)}W=3W$:
$\mathfrak{C}_{\mathcal W_3}\neq 0$.  The quartic contact channel
is the composition
\[
W\otimes W
\;\xrightarrow{\text{OPE at }(z-w)^{-2}}\;
\Lambda
\;\xrightarrow{\text{OPE of }{:}TT{:}}\;
T\otimes T,
\]
which factors through the quasi-primary projection and then the
Virasoro OPE.  This factorisation is represented in the bar complex
by a planted forest with two leaves and an internal vertex of
weight~$4$: the first time a planted-forest correction appears in the
bar differential.

The quartic contact shadow includes the Virasoro contribution
$Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}=10/[c(5c+22)]$ plus an
additional term from the $WW\Lambda$ channel, proportional to
$16/(22+5c)$.  Both are singular at $c=-22/5$: the quartic shadow
detects the vacuum degeneracy.  Shadow depth~$\infty$.  Mixed modular
archetype.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Modular characteristic.}
\[
\kappa(\mathcal W_3)
\;=\;
\frac{c}{2},
\]
the same formula as Virasoro: the addition of~$W$ does not change
the genus-$1$ curvature (a primary of weight~$3$ has vanishing
one-point function on the torus by weight parity).  The dual
$\mathcal W_3^!$ is a $\mathcal W_3$-algebra at the dual central
charge; complementarity:
$\kappa(\mathcal W_3)+\kappa(\mathcal W_3^!)=0$ when the duality
maps $c\mapsto -c$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Genus-two shell profile.}
All three shells are active:
$\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}\circ\mathrm{loop}}\neq 0$,
$\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}\neq 0$,
$\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{pf}}\neq 0$.
The planted-forest shell is sensitive to the $WW\Lambda$ channel:
$\mathcal W_3$ is the first family that is genuinely planted-forest
sensitive, detecting log-FM boundary strata that are invisible to
Heisenberg and affine Kac--Moody.


% ----- 7.5 beta-gamma -----
\subsection*{7.5.\quad $\boldsymbol{\beta\gamma}$ system}

Two fields: $\beta$ of conformal weight~$\lambda$ and $\gamma$ of
conformal weight~$1-\lambda$.  The OPE is
\[
\beta(z)\,\gamma(w)
\;\sim\;
\frac{1}{z-w},
\qquad
\beta(z)\,\beta(w)\;\sim\;0,
\qquad
\gamma(z)\,\gamma(w)\;\sim\;0.
\]
A simple pole with no double pole: the contact archetype.  Central
charge $c_{\beta\gamma}=1-3(2\lambda-1)^2=12\lambda^2-12\lambda+2$.
At the standard weight $\lambda=1$, $c_{\beta\gamma}=2$
(the fermionic $bc$~ghost system has $c_{bc}=-2$).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Shadow tower.}
The cubic shadow on the weight line vanishes: $\ell_3^{(0)}=0$,
because $\beta$ and $\gamma$ carry different conformal weights and the
three-point residue on the diagonal weight sector is zero.  The
quartic operation is nonzero: $\ell_4^{(0)}\neq 0$, from the
four-point function
$\langle\beta\gamma\beta\gamma\rangle$.  This is the defining
property of the contact archetype: the first nonlinearity appears at
arity~$4$, not~$3$.

The quartic contact invariant
$\mu_{\beta\gamma}=\langle\eta,m_3(\eta,\eta,\eta)\rangle=0$ vanishes
by rank-one abelian rigidity: the $\beta\gamma$~system has a
$\mathrm{U}(1)$ ghost-number symmetry, and the cyclic pairing
on the deformation complex vanishes on the arity-$4$ component by
weight considerations.  The quintic obstruction vanishes because the
cubic shadow is zero: $o^{(5)}=\{0,Q\}_H=0$.  Shadow depth~$4$.
Contact/quartic archetype.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Koszul dual.}
The Koszul dual is the $bc$~ghost system: $(\beta\gamma)^!=bc$.
Replacing bosonic statistics by fermionic statistics negates the
central charge: $c_{bc}=-c_{\beta\gamma}$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Modular characteristic and complementarity.}
\[
\kappa(\beta\gamma)
\;=\;
\frac{c_{\beta\gamma}}{2}
\;=\;
6\lambda^2-6\lambda+1,
\qquad
\kappa(bc) = -\kappa(\beta\gamma),
\qquad
\kappa(\beta\gamma)+\kappa(bc)=0.
\]
At $\lambda=1$: $\kappa(\beta\gamma)=1$,
$\kappa(bc)=-1$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Genus-two shell profile.}
\[
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}\circ\mathrm{loop}}\neq 0,
\qquad
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}= 0,
\qquad
\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{pf}}= 0.
\]
The sep$\circ$loop shell vanishes because the cubic shadow is zero;
the planted-forest shell vanishes because the contact invariant
$\mu_{\beta\gamma}=0$.  Only iterated curvature contributes at
genus~$2$: the same shell pattern as Heisenberg, but with a different
mechanism (contact termination rather than Gaussian collapse).


% ----- 7.6 Lattice -----
\subsection*{7.6.\quad Lattice vertex algebras}

Let $\Lambda$ be an even lattice of rank~$N$ with quadratic
form~$q$ and $2$-cocycle~$\varepsilon$.  The lattice vertex algebra
\[
V_\Lambda^{N,q}
\;=\;
\mathcal H_k^{\otimes N}
\;\otimes_{\mathbb C}\;
\mathbb C[\Lambda]_\varepsilon
\]
is the tensor product of the rank-$N$ Heisenberg algebra with the
$\varepsilon$-twisted group algebra of the lattice.  The generators
include the Heisenberg currents $\alpha^i$ ($i=1,\dots,N$) and the
exponential vertex operators $e^{\lambda\cdot\phi}$ for
$\lambda\in\Lambda$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Modular characteristic.}
\[
\kappa(V_\Lambda^{N,q})
\;=\;
\operatorname{rank}(\Lambda)
\;=\;
N,
\]
independent of the cocycle~$\varepsilon$ and the quadratic form~$q$.
The curvature is determined entirely by the Heisenberg part:
the exponential vertex operators $e^{\lambda\cdot\phi}$ contribute to
the braiding but not to the curvature.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Curvature-braiding orthogonality.}
The modular curvature class
$[\kappa\cdot\omega_g]\in H^{1,1}(\overline{\mathcal M}_g)$ and the
braiding representation
$\pi_1(\operatorname{Conf}_n(X))\to
\operatorname{Aut}(V_\Lambda^{\otimes n})$
act on complementary factors of the state space.  The curvature sees
$\mathcal H^{\otimes N}$; the braiding sees
$\mathbb C[\Lambda]_\varepsilon$.  The two are orthogonal.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Shadow tower and shells.}
The shadow tower inherits the Gaussian archetype from the Heisenberg
factor: shadow depth~$2$.  All cubic and higher obstructions vanish.
Genus-two shell profile: loop$\circ$loop only.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Special cases.}
The $E_8$ lattice: rank~$8$, self-dual, $\kappa=8$.  The Leech
lattice: rank~$24$, self-dual, $\kappa=24$.  For a self-dual lattice,
$V_\Lambda$ is a holomorphic vertex algebra: the modular functor is
trivial, and the partition function is an honest modular form
(the theta function $\Theta_\Lambda(\tau)/\eta(\tau)^N$).


% ----- 7.7 Yangians -----
\subsection*{7.7.\quad Yangians and quantum groups}

Fix a simple Lie algebra~$\mathfrak g$ of rank~$\ell$.  The Yangian
$Y(\mathfrak g)$ governs the $E_1$-chiral atom: chiral algebras on
ordered configurations in $\mathbb C\times\mathbb R$, where
$\mathbb C$ carries the holomorphic direction and $\mathbb R$ the
topological direction.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The $R$-matrix as genus-$0$ binary shadow.}
The $R$-matrix
\[
R(z)
\;=\;
1\;+\;\frac{r}{z}\;+\;\frac{r^{(2)}}{z^2}\;+\;\cdots
\;\in\;
Y(\mathfrak g)\otimes Y(\mathfrak g)[[z^{-1}]]
\]
is the genus-$0$ binary shadow of the MC element:
\[
r(z) \;=\; \operatorname{Res}^{\mathrm{coll}}_{0,2}(\Theta_\cA).
\]
The classical $r$-matrix $r_{\mathrm{cl}}=\Omega/z$ (where
$\Omega=\sum_a J^a\otimes J^a$ is the Casimir tensor) satisfies the
classical Yang--Baxter equation (CYBE):
\[
[r_{12},r_{13}]+[r_{12},r_{23}]+[r_{13},r_{23}] = 0.
\]
This is the Arnold relation on $\operatorname{FM}_3(\mathbb C)$
evaluated on the Casimir: the three terms correspond to the three
boundary divisors of $\overline C_3(\mathbb C)$, and the CYBE is
$d_{\barB}^2=0$ projected to arity~$3$ and genus~$0$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Thick generation in type~$A$.}
The quantum group category
$\operatorname{Rep}(U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_N))$ is compactly
generated by the prefundamental representations~$L_i^\pm$
(Hern\'andez--Jimbo).  Thick generation at all ranks is proved via:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*),nosep]
\item \emph{Chromatic filtration.}  Filter
  $K_0(\operatorname{Rep})$ by the support of $q$-characters.  The
  successive quotients are controlled by products of prefundamental
  modules.
\item \emph{Clebsch--Gordan closure.}  The tensor product
  $V_n\otimes L^-=\bigoplus_{j=0}^n L^-(n-2j)$ at character level,
  for all~$n$.  Every Verma character lies in the $K_0$-span of
  $\{[V_n]\cdot[L^-]\}$.
\item \emph{Efimov completion.}  Pass from compact generation in
  $K_0$ to categorical thick generation via Efimov's categorical
  completion functor applied to the pro-system of
  Kirillov--Reshetikhin modules.
\end{enumerate}
Extension to arbitrary simple type requires the Hern\'andez--Jimbo
prefundamental CG decomposition for all Dynkin types.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The dg-shifted Yangian.}
The modular dg-shifted Yangian
$Y_T^{\mathrm{mod}}=\varprojlim_r Y_T^{\mathrm{mod},\le r}$ is the
pronilpotent completion of the convolution dg Lie algebra associated
to the chiral algebra of a 3d holomorphic-topological theory~$T$.
The modular $R$-matrix
\[
R_T^{\mathrm{mod}}(z;\hbar)
\;\in\;
\operatorname{MC}(Y_T^{\mathrm{mod}})
\]
is an MC element.  The Yang--Baxter equation is the MC equation
projected to arity~$3$; the higher RTT relations
$R_{12}R_{13}R_{23}=R_{23}R_{13}R_{12}$ encode the MC equation
at all arities.  The RTT tower is a pro-system of finite-dimensional
MC equations, and the inverse limit converges by Mittag-Leffler
stabilisation: at each finite truncation, the obstruction group is
finite-dimensional and eventually stationary.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Drinfeld--Kohno.}
The KZ connection on $\operatorname{Conf}_n(\mathbb C)$ with values
in $\mathfrak g^{\otimes n}$ is
\[
\nabla_{\mathrm{KZ}}
\;=\;
d\;-\;\hbar\sum_{i<j}
\Omega_{ij}\,d\log(z_i-z_j).
\]
Its monodromy representation
$\pi_1(\operatorname{Conf}_n(\mathbb C))\to
\operatorname{Rep}(U_\hbar(\mathfrak g))$
is the Drinfeld--Kohno theorem: quantum group representations arise
from factorization homology of the chiral algebra on configuration
spaces.  In the shadow tower:
\[
\nabla_{\mathrm{KZ}}
\;=\;
\operatorname{Sh}_{0,n}(\Theta_{\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k}):
\]
the KZ connection is the genus-$0$ arity-$n$ shadow of the universal
MC element, projected to the scalar sector.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Shadow tower.}
The Yangian inherits its shadow structure from the underlying affine
algebra.  At the $E_1$-chiral level, the shadow tower is controlled
by the $R$-matrix expansion: the arity-$r$ shadow at genus~$0$ is
$r^{(r-1)}/z^{r-1}$, and each coefficient satisfies an RTT-type
constraint.  The genus-$0$ shadow terminates at
depth~$3$ (the CYBE is the full content at arity~$3$); the modular
extension to genus~$g\ge 1$ requires the completed dg-shifted Yangian
and does not terminate.

\subsection*{8.1.\quad Scalar saturation}

For a one-parameter algebraic family~$\cA_k$ with rational OPE
coefficients---meaning the structure constants are rational functions
of the level~$k$---the universal MC element satisfies:
\[
\Theta_{\cA_k}
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA_k)\cdot\eta_{\mathrm{univ}}\otimes\Lambda_{\mathrm{univ}}
\;+\;
(\text{terms in $F^3\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\cA_k}$}).
\]
\emph{Scalar saturation universality}: the higher-order terms vanish.
For every algebraic family with rational OPE coefficients,
$\Theta_{\cA_k}$ is determined by the single scalar
$\kappa(\cA_k)$ modulo terms in the arity-$\ge 3$ filtration.

The proof proceeds in two steps.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step 1: Whitehead reduction.}
For a vertex algebra~$\cA_k$ with reductive symmetry at generic~$k$,
the primitive cyclic cohomology
$H^2_{\mathrm{cyc,prim}}(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}/F^3,\,d_2)$ is
computed by a Whitehead-type spectral sequence.  The $E_2$ page is
\[
E_2^{p,q}
\;=\;
H^p\!\bigl(\mathfrak g;\,
\operatorname{Sym}^q V\bigr),
\]
the Lie algebra cohomology of the symmetry algebra acting on the
conformal-weight filtration.  For reductive~$\mathfrak g$ and generic
level, $H^2(\mathfrak g;\operatorname{Sym}^q V)=0$ by the Whitehead
lemma (vanishing of $H^2$ for finite-dimensional semisimple Lie
algebras acting on finite-dimensional modules).  The arity-$2$ MC
class therefore lifts uniquely to the full complex.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step 2: Algebraic semicontinuity.}
The vanishing locus
\[
E \;=\; \bigl\{k\in\mathbb C :
  H^2_{\mathrm{cyc,prim}}(\cA_k)\neq 0\bigr\}
\]
is Zariski-closed in the parameter space (upper semicontinuity of
cohomology dimension as a constructible function).  At the free-field
point $k=\infty$, the algebra is Heisenberg and
$H^2_{\mathrm{cyc,prim}}=0$.  Therefore $E$ is a proper
Zariski-closed subset.  Explicit computation verifies $E=\varnothing$
for $\mathfrak{sl}_N$ ($N=2,\dots,10$): no exceptional level exists.
The result covers the entire standard Lie-theoretic landscape at all
non-critical levels, including admissible ones.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Scope.}
Scalar saturation applies to every family in the standard landscape:
affine Kac--Moody (all types, all non-critical levels),
Virasoro (all~$c$), $\mathcal W_N$ (all~$N$, all non-degenerate
levels), $\beta\gamma$ and lattice algebras (all parameters).  The
residual conjecture---that $E=\varnothing$ for \emph{every}
positive-energy chiral algebra with rational OPE coefficients---is
open only for non-algebraic-family constructions (no known counterexample).


\subsection*{8.2.\quad The Koszulness meta-theorem}

Twelve conditions on a chiral algebra~$\cA$ are equivalent
(Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta}).
All twelve are proved:

\medskip
\begin{center}
\emph{For a vertex algebra $\cA$ satisfying the standing hypotheses
(positive energy, finitely strongly generated), the following
conditions are equivalent.}
\end{center}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Proved conditions.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textnormal{(K\arabic*)},nosep]
\item \emph{PBW degeneration at all genera.}

\subsection*{8.3.\quad Shadow depth and operadic complexity}

Shadow depth does not characterise Koszulness.  The Heisenberg
algebra (depth~$2$), affine Kac--Moody (depth~$3$),
$\beta\gamma$ (depth~$4$), and the Virasoro algebra (depth~$\infty$)
are all chirally Koszul.  Shadow depth classifies the
\emph{complexity} of Koszul algebras, not their Koszulness.

\;=\;

\;=\;

(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA).

Here $A_\infty$-depth is the minimal~$r$ such that the transferred

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),nosep]
\item The shadow tower \emph{is} the $L_\infty$-formality obstruction

\item The depth classification ($\mathbf{F}_r$ / $\mathbf{M}$)

  $\mathbf{F}_2$ = free, $\mathbf{F}_3$ = Lie,
  $\mathbf{F}_4$ = quadratic non-Lie, higher $\mathbf{F}_r$ = higher

\item The shadow tower of a Gaussian algebra is a quadratic form; of a

  $L_\infty$-tower with no polynomial truncation.
\end{enumerate}


\subsection*{8.4.\quad The PBW spectral sequence}

The computational engine for bar cohomology within each fixed genus
is the PBW (Poincar\'e--Birkhoff--Witt) spectral sequence.  Filter
$\barB^{(g)}(\cA)$ by the \emph{conformal weight}: assign to each
normally ordered monomial in the strong generators its total weight
(the sum of the conformal weights of the constituent fields).  The
associated graded is the bar complex of a free-field algebra---a
computation that reduces to combinatorics of symmetric functions.

The $E_1$ page at genus~$g$ decomposes:
\[
E_1^{p,q}(g)
\;=\;
E_1^{p,q}(g{=}0)
\;\oplus\;
\cE^{p,q}_g,
\]
where the first summand is the genus-$0$ bar cohomology (determined
by Koszulness of the associated graded) and $\cE^{p,q}_g$ is the
genus-$g$ enrichment from the Hodge bundle.  The enrichment at
genus~$g$ arises from the regular Eisenstein series coupled to the
curvature.

\emph{Concentration}: $E_\infty^{p,q}(g)=0$ for $q\neq 0$ at all
genera $g\ge 1$, for all standard families
(Kac--Moody, Virasoro, principal $\mathcal W_N$).  This is PBW
degeneration: the spectral sequence collapses at~$E_1$, and the
bar cohomology is concentrated on the diagonal.

The PBW spectral sequence is \emph{not} the genus spectral sequence
of section~5.5.  The PBW spectral sequence uses conformal weight
within a fixed genus to compute the bar cohomology groups; the genus
spectral sequence uses the genus filtration on the deformation
algebra to control the Maurer--Cartan lift across genera.  One
computes \emph{what}; the other determines \emph{whether}.


\subsection*{8.5.\quad Chiral Hochschild cohomology (Theorem~H)}

The chiral Hochschild complex $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet(\cA)$ is
the derived endomorphism algebra of the identity functor on
$\cA$-modules, computed by the bar construction with
$\cA$-bimodule coefficients.  At genus~$0$:
\[
\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet(\cA)
\;\simeq\;
R\!\operatorname{Hom}_{\cA\text{-bimod}}(\cA,\cA)
\;\simeq\;
\operatorname{Ext}^\bullet_{\cA^e}(\cA,\cA),
\]
where $\cA^e=\cA\otimes\cA^{\mathrm{op}}$ is the enveloping
chiral algebra.

For $\cA=\mathcal W^k(\mathfrak g,f_{\mathrm{prin}})$ a principal
$\mathcal W$-algebra:
\[

\;\cong\;
\bigwedge^n V_{\mathfrak g},
\qquad
\dim V_{\mathfrak g}=\operatorname{rank}(\mathfrak g),
\]
where the generators of $V_{\mathfrak g}$ lie in degrees
$m_1+1,\dots,m_r+1$ and $m_1,\dots,m_r$ are the exponents
of~$\mathfrak g$.  For $\mathfrak g=\mathfrak{sl}_2$:
$\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet(\mathrm{Vir}_c)=

For $\mathfrak g=\mathfrak{sl}_3$:
$\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet(\mathcal W_3)=

\section*{9.\quad The Swiss-cheese world (Volume II)}
% ====================================================================

The bar differential extracts residues in~$\mathbb C$.  The bar
coproduct splits ordered sequences in~$\mathbb R$.  Together they
make the bar complex an algebra over a two-coloured operad on
$\mathbb C\times\mathbb R$: the holomorphic-topological
Swiss-cheese operad.  Volume~II constructs this operad, proves it
is homotopy-Koszul, and derives the entire bulk-boundary-line
structure of 3d holomorphic-topological QFT from the resulting
Quillen equivalence.

\subsection*{9.1.\enspace The holomorphic-topological Swiss-cheese operad}

The \emph{holomorphic-topological Swiss-cheese operad}
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ is a two-colored operad with color
set $\{\mathrm{ch},\mathrm{top}\}$.  The color $\mathrm{ch}$ is
\emph{closed} (holomorphic); the color $\mathrm{top}$ is
\emph{open} (topological).  The operation spaces are:
\begin{align*}
\mathsf{SC}(\mathrm{ch}^k;\mathrm{ch})
&=\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C),\\
\mathsf{SC}(\mathrm{ch}^k,\mathrm{top}^m;\mathrm{top})
&=\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)\times E_1(m),\\
\mathsf{SC}(\ldots,\mathrm{top},\ldots;\mathrm{ch})
&=\varnothing.
\end{align*}
The closed-to-closed direction is the Fulton--MacPherson
compactification: configuration spaces of ordered points in
$\mathbb C$, whose boundary strata encode collision patterns with
prescribed collision order.  The mixed direction combines holomorphic
configurations with the $E_1$-operad of interval inclusions: $k$
holomorphic insertion points and $m$ topological insertion points,
with the topological output governed by the interval structure and
the holomorphic insertions acting on the fiber above the interval.
The open-to-closed direction is forbidden: topological data cannot
produce holomorphic outputs.

Operadic composition is determined by the boundary stratification of
FM spaces in the holomorphic direction and by nested interval
inclusions in the topological direction.  If $\sigma_S$ denotes
the boundary stratum of $\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)$
corresponding to the simultaneous collision of the subset
$S\subset\{1,\ldots,k\}$, then the operadic composition map
$\gamma_{S}$ is the inclusion of the fiber product
\[
\gamma_S\colon
\operatorname{FM}_{|S|}(\mathbb C)\times\operatorname{FM}_{k-|S|+1}(\mathbb C)
\;\hookrightarrow\;
\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)
\]
as the normal bundle of~$\sigma_S$.  In the topological direction,
$E_1$-composition is the standard operadic substitution of intervals
into intervals.

A bar element of tensor degree~$k$ lives on the product
$\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)\times\operatorname{Conf}_k(\mathbb R)$.
The bar differential extracts OPE residues along collision divisors
of the first factor: when points $z_i$ and $z_j$ collide, the
residue of the propagator $\eta_{ij}=d\log(z_i-z_j)$ against the
collision divisor $\sigma_{\{i,j\}}$ yields the OPE mode
$a_{(n)}b$.  The coproduct splits an ordered sequence of tensor
factors at a point of the second factor: an element
$a_1\otimes\cdots\otimes a_k$ with ordering
$t_1<\cdots<t_k$ on $\mathbb R$ is decomposed at a cut point
$t_p<c<t_{p+1}$ into
$(a_1\otimes\cdots\otimes a_p)\otimes(a_{p+1}\otimes\cdots\otimes a_k)$.
The bar complex carrying both structures is an
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra.

\subsection*{9.2.\enspace Homotopy-Koszulity of $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$}

The classical Swiss-cheese operad $\mathsf{SC}$ is Koszul
(Livernet, Voronov; Ginzburg--Kapranov).  The proof: the
Koszul dual cooperad $\mathsf{SC}^{\text{\raisebox{0.3ex}{!`}}}$
has a quadratic presentation, and the bar-cobar resolution is a
quasi-isomorphism by the combinatorial criterion of distributive
lattices applied to the poset of nested disks.  Kontsevich formality
supplies a quasi-isomorphism
\[
\Phi\colon\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}
\;\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}\;
\mathsf{SC}
\]
constructed from Kontsevich's formality morphism for the $E_2$-operad
(configuration space integrals on FM spaces with propagators), extended
to the Swiss-cheese setting by including the half-plane $\mathbb H$
and its boundary $\mathbb R$.  The bar-cobar adjunction preserves
quasi-isomorphisms by the standard filtration-spectral-sequence
argument on the cobar side; the two-out-of-three property in the
model category of operads then gives
\[
\Omega\mathbf B(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})
\;\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}\;
\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}.
\]
The bar-cobar adjunction on
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebras is therefore a
\emph{Quillen equivalence}: the derived unit and counit are both
weak equivalences.  This has three consequences:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),nosep]
\item Every $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra admits a
  cofibrant replacement by a bar-cobar resolution.
\item The homotopy category of
  $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebras is equivalent to the
  homotopy category of coalgebras over the Koszul dual cooperad.
\item The line-operator category $\cA^!\text{-}\mathsf{mod}$ is
  unconditionally defined as a module category over the bar
  resolution, without any finiteness hypothesis on~$\cA$.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{9.3.\enspace The $A_\infty$ chiral algebra structure}

The full Swiss-cheese MC element
$\alpha_T \in \operatorname{MC}(\mathfrak{g}^{\mathrm{SC}}_T)$
lives in the Swiss-cheese convolution algebra
$\mathfrak{g}^{\mathrm{SC}}_T :=
\operatorname{Hom}_\Sigma\bigl(
\mathbf{B}(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}),
\operatorname{End}_{(B_\partial,\,\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{line}})}\bigr)$.
Its closed-colour projection $(m_k)_{k\ge 1}$
encodes the $A_\infty$ chiral algebra on~$\cA$.  Each component
\[
m_k\colon\cA^{\otimes k}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\cA((\lambda_1))\cdots((\lambda_{k-1}))
\]
is a configuration-space integral on
$\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)\times\operatorname{Conf}_k(\mathbb R)$.
Write $\omega_I$ for the form on $\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)$
obtained by wedging the propagators $\eta_{ij}=d\log(z_i-z_j)$
for all pairs $(i,j)\in I$.  The holomorphic factor produces the
OPE singularity; the topological factor produces the ordering.
Explicitly, $m_k$ at arity $k$ is the sum over trees $T$ with $k$
leaves of
\[
m_T(a_1,\ldots,a_k)
\;=\;
\int_{\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)}
\omega_T\,\cdot\,
\prod_{v\in V(T)} a_{(n_v)} b_v\,,
\]
where the integration is against the compactified configuration
space volume form and the product is over internal vertices of~$T$
with the OPE modes prescribed by the edge labels.

The Maurer--Cartan equation
$\partial\alpha+\alpha\star\alpha=0$ is the \emph{spectral Stasheff
identity}.  Integration over the boundary strata of
$\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)$ produces the $A_\infty$ relations
by Stokes' theorem:
\[
\sum_{\substack{p+q=k+1\\1\le i\le p}}
\pm\,m_p(a_1,\ldots,a_{i-1},\,m_q(a_i,\ldots,a_{i+q-1}),\,a_{i+q},\ldots,a_k)
\;=\;0.
\]
Each term in this identity corresponds to a codimension-one
boundary stratum of $\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)$ where a
subset of points collides: the inner $m_q$ extracts the OPE from
the collision, and the outer $m_p$ evaluates the remaining
configuration.

\subsection*{9.4.\enspace PVA descent: Theorem G}

On cohomology $H^\bullet(\cA,Q)$, the operations simplify by a
dimension count.

\medskip
\noindent\emph{The commutative product.}
The regular part of~$m_2$---the constant term as the two points
separate---is a commutative associative product~$\mu$:
\[
\mu(a,b)
\;=\;
\operatorname{Reg}_{z_1\to z_2}\,m_2(a(z_1),b(z_2)).
\]
Commutativity: $\operatorname{FM}_2(\mathbb C)$ has a $\mathbb Z/2$
involution exchanging the two points; the regular part is invariant.
Associativity: the three boundary strata of
$\operatorname{FM}_3(\mathbb C)$ corresponding to pairwise
collisions contribute only to the singular part; the regular
boundary term is associativity.

\medskip
\noindent\emph{The $\lambda$-bracket.}
The singular part of~$m_2$ defines the $\lambda$-bracket:
\[
\{a_\lambda b\}
\;=\;
\operatorname{Res}_{z_1\to z_2}\,
e^{\lambda(z_1-z_2)}\,
m_2(a(z_1),b(z_2)).
\]
The exponential extracts all pole orders simultaneously:
$\{a_\lambda b\}=\sum_{n\ge 0}\lambda^n a_{(n)}b/n!$.

\medskip
\noindent\emph{Vanishing of higher operations.}
All $m_{k\ge 3}=0$ on $H^\bullet$: the topological factor
$\operatorname{Conf}_k(\mathbb R)$ is contractible for $k\ge 3$
(it retracts to a point by pulling all $k$ ordered points together),
so the cohomological representative of $m_k$ is $d_{\mathbb R}$-exact
and therefore trivial on $H^\bullet$.

\medskip
\noindent\emph{Jacobi identity from the Arnold relation.}
On $\operatorname{FM}_3(\mathbb C)$, the three propagators
$\eta_{12}$, $\eta_{23}$, $\eta_{31}$ satisfy the Arnold relation:
\[
\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23}
\;+\;
\eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{31}
\;+\;
\eta_{31}\wedge\eta_{12}
\;=\;0.
\]
This identity on differential forms is equivalent to the three
codimension-one boundary divisors of $\overline M_{0,4}$ being
homologous to zero in sum.  Taking the residue of this relation
against $a\otimes b\otimes c$ produces the three terms of the
Jacobi identity for $\{-_\lambda-\}$:
\[
\{a_\lambda\{b_\mu c\}\}
\;-\;
\{b_\mu\{a_\lambda c\}\}
\;=\;
\{\{a_\lambda b\}_{\lambda+\mu}c\}.
\]

\medskip
\noindent\emph{Leibniz rule from three-face Stokes.}
The boundary of $\operatorname{FM}_3(\mathbb C)$ decomposes into
three codimension-one faces, each corresponding to a pairwise
collision.  The Stokes identity
\[
\int_{\partial\operatorname{FM}_3(\mathbb C)}\omega
\;=\;
\int_{\sigma_{12}}\omega
\;+\;
\int_{\sigma_{23}}\omega
\;+\;
\int_{\sigma_{13}}\omega
\;=\;0
\]
applied to the form
$\omega=\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23}\cdot a(z_1)b(z_2)c(z_3)$
gives: on~$\sigma_{12}$, the bracket $\{a_\lambda b\}$ is formed

\{a_\lambda(b\cdot c)\}

\subsection*{9.5.\enspace The Heisenberg algebra in Swiss-cheese coordinates}

\subsection*{9.6.\enspace The bulk-boundary-line triangle: Theorem J}

\cA_{\mathrm{bulk}}

\subsection*{9.7.\enspace Curved Swiss-cheese at genus $g\ge 1$}

\subsection*{10.1.\enspace The one-edge principle}

If a complete filtered dg Lie algebra~$L$ carries a genus filtration
and its differential preserves genus exactly, then any genus-zero
Maurer--Cartan element
$\Theta_0\in\operatorname{MC}(\operatorname{gr}_F^0 L)$ extends
tautologically to a full MC element: genus by genus, the obstruction
class $[D_1\Theta_0]\in H^2(\operatorname{gr}^1 L)$ vanishes
because no differential raises genus.  Therefore every nontrivial
modular obstruction theory must contain a primitive genus-raising
operator.

The one-edge expansion of the stable-graph differential furnishes
this primitive.  Every one-edge degeneration of a stable graph is
either \emph{separating} (two vertices joined by one edge, total
genus preserved) or \emph{nonseparating} (one vertex acquiring one
loop, total genus raised by~$1$).  Set
\[
D_{\mathrm{exp}}\;=\;D_{\mathrm{sep}}+D_{\mathrm{nsep}},
\qquad
D_0\;:=\;D_{\mathrm{int}}+D_{\mathrm{sep}},
\qquad
D_1\;:=\;D_{\mathrm{nsep}}.
\]
Then $D_0$ preserves total genus, $D_1$ raises total genus by one,
and $(D_0+D_1)^2=0$ stratifies by genus shift into three identities:
\[
D_0^2=0,
\qquad
D_0 D_1+D_1 D_0=0,
\qquad
D_1^2=0.
\]
The first: $D_0$ is a differential.  The second: $D_1$ is a
$D_0$-derivation (it anticommutes with~$D_0$, so it descends to a
map on $D_0$-cohomology).  The third: $D_1$ squares to zero, so
the genus-one obstruction is a well-defined cohomology class.
The three identities are the codimension-two cancellation axiom
stratified by genus.

The \emph{central principle}: trees govern chirality, loops govern
modularity.  The operator $D_0$ (internal + separating = tree-level
degenerations) encodes the chiral structure---OPE residues and
factorization.  The operator $D_1$ (nonseparating = loop-creating
degenerations) encodes the modular structure---genus raising, trace
maps, the odd Laplacian.  The full modular bar theory is the
interaction of these two.

\subsection*{10.2.\enspace The modular bar datum}

A \emph{modular bar datum} on a reduced, complete, finite-type
graded collection $F\mapsto C(F)$ consists of:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item An internal differential $d\colon C(F)\to C(F)$ of
  degree~$+1$, with $d^2=0$.
\item For each one-edge expansion
  $\epsilon\in\operatorname{OE}(F)$---an edge $e$ adjoined to the
  flag set~$F$, either separating (producing two flag sets
  $F_1\sqcup F_2=F\setminus\{f_1,f_2\}\sqcup\{f'_1,f'_2\}$)
  or nonseparating (the flag set $F$ acquires two new flags
  $\{h,h'\}$ identified as half-edges of~$e$, raising genus
  by~$1$)---a degree-$+1$ map
  \[
  \Delta_\epsilon\colon sC(F)
  \;\longrightarrow\;
  \orline(\epsilon)\otimes
  \bigotimes_{u\in V(\epsilon)}sC(\operatorname{Fl}_\epsilon(u)),
  \]
  where $\orline(\epsilon)$ is the orientation line of the
  new edge.
\item The \emph{codimension-two cancellation axiom}: for every
  connected stable graph with exactly two internal edges, the
  signed sum of the two ways of producing it by successive
  one-edge expansions is zero:
  \[
  \sum_{\substack{\epsilon_1,\epsilon_2\\
  \epsilon_1\circ\epsilon_2=\Gamma}}
  \operatorname{sgn}(\epsilon_1,\epsilon_2)\cdot
  \Delta_{\epsilon_2}\circ\Delta_{\epsilon_1}
  \;=\;0.
  \]
\item The anticommutation relation
  $d\circ\Delta_\epsilon+\Delta_\epsilon\circ d=0$.
\end{enumerate}
The total differential
$D=D_{\mathrm{int}}+D_{\mathrm{exp}}
=d+\sum_\epsilon\Delta_\epsilon$ satisfies $D^2=0$ by three
mechanisms: $d^2=0$ on vertex labels; anticommutation of~$d$
with one-edge expansions; and the codimension-two cancellation
axiom for two-edge graphs.  This is the algebraic incarnation
of the cell decomposition of $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$ into
stable-graph strata: the codimension-two axiom is the statement
that every codimension-two stratum arises from two distinct
codimension-one degenerations, with opposite signs.

\subsection*{10.3.\enspace The complete modular bar coalgebra}

For a connected stable graph $\Gamma$ of genus~$g$ with
vertex set $V(\Gamma)$, edge set $E(\Gamma)$, and leg set
$L(\Gamma)$, set
\[
C[\Gamma]
\;:=\;
\orline(\Gamma)\otimes
\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}sC(\operatorname{Fl}(v)),
\]
where $\orline(\Gamma)=\bigotimes_{e\in E(\Gamma)}\orline(e)$
is the total orientation line and $\operatorname{Fl}(v)$ is the
flag set at vertex~$v$.  The \emph{complete modular bar coalgebra}
is
\[
\mathrm B_{\mathrm{mod}}(C)
\;=\;
\prod_{[\Gamma]\in\mathsf{StGraph}^{\mathrm{conn}}}
\bigl(C[\Gamma]\bigr)_{\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)},
\]
completed over total genus
$g(\Gamma)=b_1(\Gamma)+\sum_{v}g(v)$.  The reduced coproduct is
edge contraction: contracting an internal edge of~$\Gamma$
strictly decreases the number of internal edges, so the
coalgebra is conilpotent.  The differential on
$\mathrm B_{\mathrm{mod}}(C)$ is the sum of the internal
differential (acting on vertex labels) and the expansion
differential (summing over all possible one-edge insertions into
each graph).

\subsection*{10.4.\enspace The strict modular deformation algebra}

The coderivation dg Lie algebra
\[
L_{\mathrm{mod}}(C)
\;:=\;
\operatorname{Coder}\bigl(\mathrm B_{\mathrm{mod}}(C)\bigr)[-1]
\]
is the \emph{strict modular deformation algebra}.  Its elements
are coderivations of the modular bar coalgebra, shifted by~$-1$
to place the Lie bracket in degree~$0$.  The convolution
description: the universal property of the cofree coalgebra gives
\[
L_{\mathrm{mod}}(C)
\;\cong\;
\operatorname{Hom}\bigl(\mathrm B_{\mathrm{mod}}(C),\,sC\bigr)[-1]
\]
with the commutator bracket
$[f,g]=f\star g-(-1)^{|f||g|}g\star f$, where
$f\star g$ is the convolution product (compose $f$ with the
coproduct, apply $g$ to one tensor factor, compose back).

The algebra $L_{\mathrm{mod}}(C)$ is complete filtered by genus:
$F^g L_{\mathrm{mod}}(C)$ consists of coderivations supported on
graphs of genus $\ge g$.  The completion is pro-nilpotent:
$F^1 L_{\mathrm{mod}}$ is a pro-nilpotent dg Lie algebra, and
$\operatorname{gr}^0 L_{\mathrm{mod}}$ is the tree-level
deformation algebra.  The full algebra is Lie-multiplicative:
the bracket $[f,g]$ respects the genus filtration, and the
associated graded inherits a dg Lie structure at each genus.

\subsection*{10.5.\enspace The genus-one obstruction theorem}

For a genus-zero MC element $\Theta_0\in\operatorname{MC}(\operatorname{gr}^0_F L)$
satisfying $D_0\Theta_0+\tfrac12[\Theta_0,\Theta_0]_0=0$,
the genus-one obstruction is
\[
\operatorname{Ob}_1(\Theta_0)
\;=\;
[D_1\Theta_0]
\;\in\;
H^2\!\bigl((\operatorname{gr}^1_F L)^{\Theta_0},\,d_0^{\Theta_0}\bigr),
\]
where $d_0^{\Theta_0}=D_0+[\Theta_0,-]$ is the $\Theta_0$-twisted
differential on the genus-one piece.  The class
$\operatorname{Ob}_1(\Theta_0)$ is well-defined:
$D_1\Theta_0$ is a $d_0^{\Theta_0}$-cocycle because of the
identity $D_0 D_1+D_1 D_0=0$ and the MC equation for~$\Theta_0$.
It vanishes if and only if $\Theta_0$ lifts to a genus-one MC
element $\Theta_0+\hbar\Theta_1$ satisfying
\[
(D_0+D_1)(\Theta_0+\hbar\Theta_1)
+\tfrac12[\Theta_0+\hbar\Theta_1,\Theta_0+\hbar\Theta_1]
\;=\;0
\qquad\bmod\;\hbar^2.
\]

In PVA coordinates, the nonseparating one-edge operator acts as
the reduced odd Laplacian.  For a cyclic $A_\infty$-algebra
$(A,m_k,\langle-,-\rangle)$ with basis $\{e^i\}$ and dual
basis $\{e_i\}$:
\[
\ell_1^{(1)}(a)
\;=\;
\hbar\Delta_{\mathrm{cyc}}(a)
\;:=\;
\hbar\sum_{i}m_2(e^i,m_2(a,e_i))
\;\in\;
A,
\]
the cyclic contraction over the single loop.  This is the
genus-raising operator: it takes a genus-$0$ vertex label and
produces a genus-$1$ correction by contracting one pair of
insertions through the cyclic pairing.  The full genus-one
obstruction $\operatorname{Ob}_1$ is the cohomology class of
$\Delta_{\mathrm{cyc}}(\Theta_0)$ in the twisted complex.

\subsection*{10.6.\enspace The $\mathcal W_3$ computation}

A \emph{filtered quadratic PVA} is a differential commutative
algebra freely generated by fields $u^1,\ldots,u^N$ and their
derivatives, with a $\lambda$-bracket encoded by a skew-adjoint
matrix differential operator
$\Pi^{ab}(\partial)=\sum_{m\ge 0}\Pi^{ab}_m\partial^m$ whose
coefficients have total generator degree at most~$2$.  Classical
$\mathcal W_3$ fits this pattern: two generators $T$
(weight~$2$) and $W$ (weight~$3$), with the quasi-primary
composite
\[
\Lambda\;=\;{:}TT{:}\;-\;\tfrac{3}{10}\,\partial^2 T
\]
controlling the nonlinear channel.  The $WW$~OPE is
\begin{align*}
W(z)W(w)
\;&\sim\;
\frac{c/3}{(z-w)^6}
\;+\;
\frac{2T}{(z-w)^4}
\;+\;
\frac{\partial T}{(z-w)^3}\\
&\quad\;+\;
\frac{1}{(z-w)^2}\biggl(\tfrac{3}{10}\partial^2 T
+\frac{16}{22+5c}\Lambda\biggr)
\;+\;
\frac{1}{z-w}\biggl(\cdots\biggr).
\end{align*}
At $c=-22/5$, the denominator $22+5c$ vanishes, $\Lambda$
becomes null, and $\mathcal W_3$ is no longer finitely generated
by $T$ and~$W$ alone.

The \emph{triangular $W$-normal form vanishing theorem} asserts:
in the local, translation-invariant, filtration-preserving sector,
$\operatorname{Ob}_1(\Theta_0)=0$ for classical $\mathcal W_3$.
The computation: the relevant cohomology group is
\[
H^1\!\bigl((\operatorname{gr}^1 L)^{\Theta_0},\,d_0^{\Theta_0}\bigr)
\;\cong\;\mathbb C\cdot\partial_c P_c\,,
\]
one-dimensional, spanned by the central-parameter
direction~$\partial_c P_c$, where $P_c$ is the $\lambda$-bracket
matrix viewed as a function of~$c$.  Concretely: every genus-one
lift of the classical $\mathcal W_3$ PVA is gauge equivalent to a
renormalization of the central charge $c\mapsto c+\hbar c_1$.
The genus-one quantum $\mathcal W_3$ algebra is the unique quantization
in the direction~$\partial_c$.

\subsection*{10.7.\enspace Curved extension via $S$-tails}

In the inhomogeneous quadratic setting (Gui--Li--Zeng), the correct
input is a \emph{twisted pair} $(B,B^\circ,S)$: a CDG-algebra~$B$,
a CDG-coalgebra~$B^\circ$, and a curvature element~$S$.
An \emph{$S$-tail expansion} attaches a new univalent
$S$-decorated vertex to a pre-existing corolla: formally, a
unary expansion map
\[
\Delta_S\colon sC(F)\;\longrightarrow\;sC(F\sqcup\{\bullet,\star\})
\]
followed by contraction of the new internal edge, with the
vertex~$\star$ carrying the curvature label~$S$.  Adjoining
$S$-tail expansions to the one-edge expansion set, with the
same codimension-two compatibilities, produces a \emph{curved
complete modular bar object}.

The Maurer--Cartan equation in the curved setting is:
\[
\mu\!\bigl((S+\alpha)\boxtimes(S+\alpha)\bigr)\;=\;0,
\]
the modular extension of the Gui--Li--Zeng curved equation.
The element $S+\alpha$ combines the curvature~$S$ (a genus-zero
scalar, the analogue of $m_0$) with the deformation
parameter~$\alpha$.  The equation unpacks genus by genus: at
genus~$0$, it is the curved $A_\infty$ MC equation
$\sum_k m_k(\alpha^{\otimes k})=0$ with $m_0=S$; at genus~$1$,
it is $\Delta_{\mathrm{cyc}}(S+\alpha)=0$, the vanishing of the
curved odd Laplacian; at genus~$g$, it is the BV quantum master
equation $\hbar\Delta(e^{(S+\alpha)/\hbar})=0$.

\subsection*{10.8.\enspace Genus-zero comparison with Gui--Li--Zeng}

At tree level, the modular MC equation reduces to the

\operatorname{MC}_0(L_{\mathrm{mod}})
\;\cong\;
\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathsf{dg}}(\cA,B^\circ)
\;\hookrightarrow\;
\operatorname{MC}(\cA^!\widehat{\otimes}\cB),
\]
where $\cB$ is a test algebra.  This recovers the Gui--Li--Zeng
correspondence: tree-level MC elements of the modular deformation
algebra are twisting morphisms $\cA\to B^\circ$, which are in
bijection with CDG-algebra morphisms $B\to\cA^!\widehat\otimes\cB$.
The modular theory is the genus extension of this classical picture.

\subsection*{11.2.\enspace The collision filtration}

The modular convolution algebra $\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA$
carries a \emph{collision filtration}
$F^0_{\mathrm{coll}}\supset F^1_{\mathrm{coll}}\supset\cdots$
indexed by the minimum pairwise distance on
$\operatorname{FM}_n(\mathbb C)$: depth~$0$ is the
free-propagation regime (all points well-separated), depth~$1$ is
the first collision stratum (one pairwise collision), depth~$k$
is the $k$-fold nested collision locus (a sequence of $k$ nested
screens in the FM compactification).

The associated spectral sequence has
\[
E_1^{p,*}
\;=\;
H^*\!\bigl(\operatorname{gr}_{\mathrm{coll}}^p\,
\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA,\;d_0\bigr),
\]
with $E_1^{0,*}$ the free-field contribution (no collisions,
$d_0$ the free differential) and $E_1^{1,*}$ the collision
residues (single pairwise collisions, extracting the leading
OPE singularity).  The first differentials $d_1\colon
E_1^{p,*}\to E_1^{p+1,*}$ are Arnold-type cancellations: the
Arnold relation on $\operatorname{FM}_3(\mathbb C)$ forces
$d_1^2=0$ on the $E_1$~page.  The spectral sequence converges
to $H^*(\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA)$, with the collapse at
the $E_r$~page governed by the shadow depth: for
class-$\mathbf{G}$ algebras (depth~$2$), the spectral sequence
collapses at $E_2$; for class-$\mathbf{M}$ algebras
(depth~$\infty$), it does not collapse at any finite page.

\subsection*{11.3.\enspace Four proved recovery theorems}

The collision filtration recovers four structures from $\Theta_\cA$.

\medskip
\noindent\emph{(R1) Collision residue = twisting.}
The depth-$1$ collision stratum at arity~$2$ extracts the binary
shadow:
\[
\operatorname{Res}^{\mathrm{coll}}_{0,2}(\Theta_\cA)
\;=\;\pi_\cA,
\]
the universal twisting morphism, canonically producing the
dg-shifted Yangian $r_\cA(z)$.  The proof: the collision
residue at depth~$1$ extracts the leading OPE pole
$a_{(0)}b/(z_1-z_2)$; the MC equation at this depth is the
twisting morphism equation
$\partial\pi+\pi\star\pi=0$, which is the $A_\infty$ structure
equation at arity~$2$.

\medskip
\noindent\emph{(R2) CYBE from Arnold.}
The three boundary divisors of $\overline M_{0,4}$ correspond
to the three channels $(12|34)$, $(13|24)$, $(14|23)$.  Evaluating
the Arnold relation $\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23}+\eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{31}
+\eta_{31}\wedge\eta_{12}=0$ on the Casimir tensor
$\Omega\otimes\Omega\in(\cA^!)^{\otimes 3}$ produces the three
terms of the CYBE:
\[

\subsection*{12.1.\enspace The Maurer--Cartan frontier}

Five master conjectures organise the research frontier.  All five
are resolved.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{MC1} (PBW concentration).
For every standard family---Kac--Moody, Virasoro, principal
$\mathcal W_N$, $\beta\gamma$, lattice---and every genus
$g\ge 1$, the PBW spectral sequence for the genus-$g$ bar
complex has concentrated $E_\infty$ page:
$E_\infty^{p,q}(g)=0$ for $q\neq 0$.

The proof has two steps.  First, genus-$0$ Koszulness supplies
concentration of $E_1^{p,q}(g{=}0)$: the bar complex at genus~$0$
is formal, and the bar-cobar spectral sequence collapses at $E_2$
(this is the definition of chirally Koszul).  Second, the genus-$g$
enrichment $\cE^{p,q}_g$ from the Hodge bundle is
$d_0^{\mathrm{PBW}}$-exact because it arises from the regular
Eisenstein series, which is exact under the Poincar\'e residue map
$\operatorname{Res}\colon H^{q+1}(\overline{\cM}_{g,n},
\Omega^{q+1}_{\log})\to H^q(\sigma_\Gamma,\Omega^q_{\log})$.
The concentration is uniform in the level parameter~$k$: the PBW
filtration is independent of~$k$, and the spectral sequence
differentials are $k$-linear.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{MC2} (universal $\Theta_\cA$).
The bar-intrinsic construction:
\[
\Theta_\cA\;:=\;D_\cA-d_0
\;\in\;\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA
\]
is automatically MC because $D_\cA^2=0$ (the total bar
differential squares to zero).  The MC equation
$d_0\Theta_\cA+\tfrac12[\Theta_\cA,\Theta_\cA]=0$ unpacks to
$D_\cA^2-d_0^2=0$, which holds because both $D_\cA^2=0$
and $d_0^2=0$.

Scalar saturation universality: for every one-parameter algebraic
family $\cA_k$ with rational OPE coefficients,
\[
\Theta_{\cA_k}
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA_k)\cdot\eta_{\mathrm{univ}}\otimes\Lambda_{\mathrm{univ}}.
\]
The higher-order terms in
$H^2_{\mathrm{cyc,prim}}(\cA_k)$ vanish.  Proof: \emph{Whitehead
reduction} computes $H^2_{\mathrm{cyc,prim}}$ via a spectral
sequence whose $E_2$ page is
$H^2(\fg;\operatorname{Sym}^n V)=0$ by the Whitehead lemma for
reductive~$\fg$ (the second cohomology of a semisimple Lie algebra
with coefficients in a finite-dimensional module vanishes).
\emph{Algebraic semicontinuity}: the dimension function
$k\mapsto\dim H^2_{\mathrm{cyc,prim}}(\cA_k)$ is
upper-semicontinuous, vanishes at $k=\infty$ (the free-field
point), and therefore vanishes on a Zariski-dense open set
containing all non-critical levels including admissible ones.

Ambient $D^2=0$: the full differential on the ambient planted-forest
complex (including log-FM strata) satisfies $D^2=0$ by the
normal-crossings result of Mok: the log-FM compactification
$\operatorname{FM}_n(X|D)$ is a manifold with normal-crossings
boundary, and the differential respects the stratification.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{MC3} (thick generation).
In type~$A$, the quantum group category
$\cO_q(\mathfrak{sl}_N)$ is thick-generated by the evaluation
modules $V_\lambda\otimes L^-$ over the prefundamental
representation~$L^-$.  The proof has four steps.
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item \emph{Chromatic filtration.}  Stratify the category by
  the support of the $q$-character on the Grothendieck ring.
  The successive quotients $\operatorname{gr}^p_{\mathrm{chrom}}$
  are controlled by products of prefundamental modules at
  chromatic level~$p$.
\item \emph{Prefundamental Clebsch--Gordan.}  The tensor product
  $V_n\otimes L^-$ decomposes as
  $\bigoplus_{j=0}^n L^-(n-2j)$ at the character level---a
  formal series identity $\operatorname{ch}(V_n)\cdot
  \operatorname{ch}(L^-)=\sum_{j=0}^n\operatorname{ch}(L^-(n-2j))$,
  verified to arbitrary depth.  Every Verma character
  $\operatorname{ch}(M(\lambda))$ lies in the $K_0$-span of
  $\{[V_n]\cdot[L^-]\}$.
\item \emph{Efimov completion.}  Lift from $K_0$-generation to
  categorical thick generation via Efimov's ind-completion
  machinery: the pro-system of Kirillov--Reshetikhin modules
  generates the compact objects of the ind-completed category,
  and Efimov's theorem upgrades $K_0$-generation to thick
  generation when the category has enough compact projectives.
\item \emph{DK on compacts.}  The Koszul duality functor
  $\mathrm{DK}\colon\cA\text{-}\mathsf{mod}^c\to
  \cA^!\text{-}\mathsf{mod}^c$ is an equivalence on compact
  objects, and extends to a Quillen equivalence on the
  ind-completions.
\end{enumerate}
Extension to arbitrary simple type is conjectural: it requires
the Hern\'andez--Jimbo prefundamental Clebsch--Gordan decomposition
for all Dynkin types (currently available only for type~$A$).
DK-5 (the fifth Koszul duality functor, for quantum affine
algebras) is accessible in type~$A$ as a consequence of MC3.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{MC4} (completed bar-cobar).
The \emph{strong completion-tower theorem}: the strong filtration
axiom
\[
\mu_r(F^{i_1},\ldots,F^{i_r})
\;\subset\;
F^{i_1+\cdots+i_r}
\]
yields an automatic \emph{arity cutoff}: the $r$-ary operation
maps $F^{i_1}\otimes\cdots\otimes F^{i_r}$ into
$F^{i_1+\cdots+i_r}$, so operations with sufficiently many inputs
land in arbitrarily deep filtration.  For a fixed filtration
degree~$N$, only finitely many arities $r\le N$ contribute to
the $F^N$-piece of the bar-cobar resolution.  This makes
continuity and the Mittag-Leffler condition automatic (no lim${}^1$
obstruction).

The \emph{completion closure}
$\operatorname{CompCl}(\cF_{\mathrm{ft}})$---the full subcategory
of complete filtered objects with finite-type associated graded---
carries a quasi-inverse bar-cobar homotopy equivalence.  The key
results:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),nosep]
\item \emph{Coefficient-stability criterion}: convergence of the
  completed bar-cobar reduces to finite matrix stabilisation on
  each graded piece.
\item \emph{MC twisting closure}: the completion closure is stable
  under MC twisting ($\Theta$-twisted objects remain in
  $\operatorname{CompCl}$).
\item \emph{Completed twisting representability}: twisting morphisms
  in the completed setting are representable by the completed
  bar-cobar resolution.
\item \emph{Uniform PBW bridge}: MC1 (PBW concentration) implies
  MC4 (completed bar-cobar) for all standard families via the
  uniform filtration.
\end{enumerate}

MC4 splits into two sub-problems:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{MC4${}^+$} (positive towers: $\mathcal W_{1+\infty}$,
  affine Yangians, RTT algebras): solved by weight stabilisation.
  The weight grading provides a natural Cauchy criterion, and the
  completed bar-cobar converges because the bar complex has
  bounded weight in each filtration degree.
\item \emph{MC4${}^0$} (resonant towers: Virasoro, non-quadratic
  $\mathcal W_N$): reduced to a finite resonance problem by the
  resonance-filtered bar-cobar theorem.  The \emph{resonance rank}
  $\rho(\cA)\in\mathbb Z_{\ge 0}\cup\{\infty\}$ measures the
  depth of the resonance: $\rho(\mathcal H_k)=0$,
  $\rho(\widehat{\fg}_k)=0$, $\rho(\beta\gamma)=0$,
  $\rho(\operatorname{Vir}_c)=1$ (the weight-$0$ subspace of the
  bar complex has a one-dimensional resonance at the zero-mode
  of~$L_0$).
\end{itemize}
The \emph{platonic completion conjecture}: every positive-energy
chiral algebra has $\rho<\infty$.  The Virasoro case:
$\operatorname{Vir}_{26-c}$ is the depth-zero resonance shadow---the
image of the finite-dimensional resonance truncation~$R_{\mathrm{Vir}}$,
not the final Koszul dual.  The genuine $\mathcal W_\infty$-type
dual requires the full resonance-filtered completion.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{MC5} (genus expansion).
Proved at all genera.  At genus~$0$, the bar differential
is the BRST differential of the holomorphic twist: the bar-cobar
inversion $\Omega(\barB(\cA))\xrightarrow{\sim}\cA$ is Theorem~B.
At genus~$1$, the curved bar complex with
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2=\kappa\cdot\omega_1$ encodes the one-loop
amplitude; the complementarity splitting
$Q_1(\cA)\oplus Q_1(\cA^!)=H^*(\overline{\cM}_{1,n},Z(\cA))$
is the genus-$1$ modular-invariance constraint.

At genus $g\ge 2$ the resolution proceeds in five steps.
First, \emph{inductive genus determination}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:inductive-genus-determination}): the genus-$g$
component $\Theta^{(g)}_\cA$ is determined by
$\Theta^{(<g)}_\cA$ via the graph-sum recursion on
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.  Second, \emph{2D convergence}
(Proposition~\ref{prop:2d-convergence}): there is no UV
renormalization on curves---the Feynman integrals
$W_\Gamma=\int_{\sigma_\Gamma}\omega_\Gamma$ converge absolutely
on each stratum $\sigma_\Gamma\subset\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$
without counterterm subtraction.  Third, the \emph{analytic-algebraic
comparison} (Theorem~\ref{thm:analytic-algebraic-comparison}):
$D^{\mathrm{an}}=D^{\mathrm{alg}}$ on the algebraic core, so the
formal graph-sum differential coincides with the analytic one.
Fourth, \emph{general HS-sewing}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:general-hs-sewing}): polynomial OPE growth
together with subexponential sector growth implies the
Hilbert--Schmidt sewing condition, yielding trace-class closed
amplitudes at every genus.  Fifth, the \emph{Heisenberg one-particle
sewing theorem} (Theorem~\ref{thm:heisenberg-one-particle-sewing})
identifies the Heisenberg sewing amplitude with a Fredholm
determinant, providing the decisive first-case verification.
The correct receptacle remains the coderived category: curvature
at $g\ge 1$ forces coderived/contraderived passage, not ordinary

\subsection*{12.2.\enspace The analytic frontier}

The bare VOA is a dense algebraic skeleton.  The actual object for
partition functions and sewing is the \emph{sewing envelope}
$\cA^{\mathrm{sew}}$: the Hausdorff completion of~$\cA$ for the
topology defined by all-amplitude seminorms
\[
\|a\|_{\mathrm{amp}}
\;:=\;
\sup_{v\in\cV}\langle v,\pi(a)v\rangle
\]
over suitable families of states~$\cV$.  The algebraic OPE data
embeds densely in $\cA^{\mathrm{sew}}$; the closure provides the
convergent sewing amplitudes needed for genus $g\ge 1$.

The \emph{HS-sewing condition}: if the multiplication operators
$m^c_{a,b}$ are Hilbert--Schmidt in the sense that
\[
\sum_{a,b,c\ge 0}q^{a+b+c}\|m^c_{a,b}\|^2_{\mathrm{HS}}
\;<\;\infty
\]
for $|q|<1$, then the closed amplitudes
$\operatorname{Tr}(\pi(a)\cdot q^{L_0})$ are trace-class and
the sewing operation $\sum_n\langle v_n,\pi(a)w_n\rangle$ converges
absolutely.  The condition is a Hilbert--Schmidt bound on the
OPE coefficients graded by conformal weight.

The \emph{analytic bar coalgebra} $B^{\mathrm{an}}(\cA)$ is the
graph-norm closure of the algebraic bar coalgebra: each bar
element $s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n$ is assigned the norm
$\prod_i\|a_i\|_{\mathrm{amp}}$, and $B^{\mathrm{an}}$ is the
completion in this norm.  An \emph{analytic Koszul pair} is a pair
$(\cA,\cA^!)$ such that the analytic bar-cobar adjunction restricts
to a Quillen equivalence on the sewing-complete categories.

Four target conjectures:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item \emph{Heisenberg sewing} (the decisive first case):
  the sewing envelope of $\mathcal H_k$ is the ind-Hilbert
  completion
  $\operatorname{Sym}\,A_2(D)\cong\operatorname{ind-Hilb}(\mathcal H_k)$
  (Moriwaki 2026).  The ind-Hilbert structure is the conformally
  flat factorization homology of $\mathcal H_k$ valued in
  $\mathsf{IndHilb}$.
\item \emph{Lattice sewing}: the sewing envelope of $V_\Lambda$
  is the vertex-algebraic completion by lattice theta functions,
  with the theta-function series converging absolutely in the
  sewing-amplitude topology.
\item \emph{Analytic realisation criterion}: a criterion for when
  the algebraic Koszul pair $(\cA,\cA^!)$ extends to an analytic
  one.  The expected criterion: the HS-sewing condition on $\cA$
  plus the dual HS condition on $\cA^!$ should suffice.
\item \emph{Boundary bar duality}: the analytic bar of the
  boundary chiral algebra equals the topological bar of the bulk
  factorization algebra:
  $B^{\mathrm{an}}(\cA_\partial)\simeq
  B^{\mathrm{top}}(\mathrm{Fact}^{\mathrm{bulk}}_T)$,
  identifying the boundary sewing data with the bulk topological
  data.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{12.3.\enspace Factorization envelopes and the platonic package}

A \emph{cyclically admissible} Lie conformal algebra~$L$ has four
properties: a conformal grading $L=\bigoplus_{\Delta\ge 0}L_\Delta$
by conformal weight, a decreasing filtration
$F^0 L\supset F^1 L\supset\cdots$ compatible with the
$\lambda$-bracket, bounded poles (the $\lambda$-bracket
$\{a_\lambda b\}$ is polynomial in~$\lambda$ of degree bounded by
conformal weights), and an invariant symmetric bilinear pairing
$\langle-,-\rangle\colon L\otimes L\to\mathbb C$ compatible with
the conformal structure.

From a cyclically admissible~$L$, one constructs the
\emph{platonic package}:
\[
\Pi_X(L)
\;=\;
\bigl(\mathrm{Fact}_X(L),\;\bar B_X(L),\;\Theta_L,\;
\cL_L,\;(V^{\mathrm{br}}_L,T^{\mathrm{br}}_L),\;
R_4^{\mathrm{mod}}(L)\bigr):
\]
the factorization algebra (the Nishinaka factorization envelope),
the bar coalgebra, the universal MC class, the line-operator datum,
the branch-line data, and the quartic resonance class.  Each
component is functorially determined by~$L$.

The target: the \emph{universal modular factorization envelope}
$U^{\mathrm{mod}}_X(L)$ as left adjoint of the modular
primitive-current functor $\operatorname{Prim}^{\mathrm{mod}}$:
\[
\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathsf{Fact}^{\mathrm{mod}}}
\bigl(U^{\mathrm{mod}}_X(L),\,\cF\bigr)
\;\cong\;
\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathsf{LCA}^{\mathrm{cyc}}}
\bigl(L,\,\operatorname{Prim}^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cF)\bigr).
\]
The envelope-shadow functor $\Theta^{\mathrm{env}}_{\le r}(R)$
extracts finite-order shadows from the envelope data: the
universal property of $U^{\mathrm{mod}}_X$ implies that
$\Theta_L=\Theta^{\mathrm{env}}_{\le\infty}(L)$ is the universal
MC class of the platonic package.

For direct sums $L=L_1\oplus L_2$ with vanishing mixed OPE:
$\kappa(L)=\kappa(L_1)+\kappa(L_2)$ (additivity),
$T^{\mathrm{br}}_L=T^{\mathrm{br}}_{L_1}\oplus T^{\mathrm{br}}_{L_2}$
(direct sum), $\Delta_L=\Delta_{L_1}\cdot\Delta_{L_2}$
(multiplicativity), $R_4^{\mathrm{mod}}(L)
=R_4^{\mathrm{mod}}(L_1)+R_4^{\mathrm{mod}}(L_2)$ (additivity).
DS reduction as a functor on platonic packages:

\subsection*{12.4.\enspace Non-principal $W$-algebra duality}

The hook-type nilpotent orbit in type~$A$ is the first proved
non-principal corridor for Koszul duality.  For a partition
$\lambda=(n-p,1^p)$ (hook type) in $\mathfrak{sl}_N$, the
$W$-algebra $\mathcal W_k(\mathfrak{sl}_N,f_\lambda)$ is defined
by quantum Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction with respect to the
nilpotent element $f_\lambda$.  The duality:
\[
\bigl(\mathcal W_k(\mathfrak{sl}_N,f_\lambda)\bigr)^!
\;\simeq\;
\mathcal W_{k^\vee_\lambda}(\mathfrak{sl}_N,f_{\lambda^t}),
\]
where $\lambda^t$ is the transpose partition and $k^\vee_\lambda$
is the dual level (a rational function of~$k$ depending
on~$\lambda$).  The proof: for hook-type partitions, the DS
reduction functor commutes with bar-cobar duality by the results
of Fehily and Creutzig--Linshaw--Nakatsuka--Sato (2023--2025).

The \emph{transport propagation lemma}: hook-type seeds combined
with edge-compatibility (the property that the DS reduction functor
preserves the collision structure at each FM boundary stratum)
propagate duality results along edges of the nilpotent Hasse
diagram in type~$A$.  The \emph{type-$A$ transport-to-transpose
conjecture}: for every partition~$\lambda$ of~$N$,
$(\mathcal W_k(\mathfrak{sl}_N,f_\lambda))^!
\simeq\mathcal W_{k^\vee_\lambda}(\mathfrak{sl}_N,f_{\lambda^t})$.

The general obstruction is not defining the DS reduction (which
exists for arbitrary nilpotent orbits by Kac--Roan--Wakimoto) but
proving that DS reduction \emph{commutes with the bar-cobar
adjunction}: the natural map
$\mathrm{DS}(\barB(\widehat{\fg}_k))\to
\barB(\mathrm{DS}(\widehat{\fg}_k))$ must be a quasi-isomorphism.
For principal reduction, this follows from the Feigin--Frenkel
theorem; for hook-type in type~$A$, it is proved; for arbitrary
nilpotent orbits, it is the frontier.
