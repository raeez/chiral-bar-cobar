\section*{1.\quad The categorical logarithm}
% ====================================================================

\subsection*{The bar and cobar constructions over a point}

Let $A$ be an augmented associative algebra over a field~$\Bbbk$,
with augmentation ideal~$\bar A$.  The \emph{bar construction} is the
cofree conilpotent coalgebra on the desuspension:
\[
B(A)
\;=\;
\bigl(T^c(s^{-1}\bar A),\;d_B\bigr),
\qquad
d_B(s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n)
\;=\;
\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}
\pm\,
s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}(a_i a_{i+1})|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n.
\]
Cohomological convention: $|d_B|=+1$, and $|s^{-1}a|=|a|-1$.
Dually, for a coaugmented conilpotent coalgebra~$C$ with coaugmentation
coideal~$\bar C$, the \emph{cobar construction} is
\[
\Omega(C)
\;=\;
\bigl(T(s\bar C),\;d_\Omega\bigr),
\qquad
d_\Omega(sa_1|\cdots|sa_n)
\;=\;
-\sum_{i=1}^{n}
\pm\,
sa_1|\cdots|sa'_i|sa''_i|\cdots|sa_n,
\]
where $\Delta(a_i)=\sum a'_i\otimes a''_i$ is the reduced coproduct.
The counit $\varepsilon\colon\Omega(B(A))\to A$ is the comparison map;
$A$ is \emph{Koszul} when $\varepsilon$ is a quasi-isomorphism.

The classical operadic instances:
$\operatorname{Com}^!=\operatorname{Lie}$ and
$\operatorname{Sym}^!=\Lambda$.  A Koszul algebra admits a
small (quadratic) resolution by its dual; when Koszulness fails, the
cobar-bar resolution is the universal---and typically large---replacement.

\subsection*{Transport to a curve}

Let $X$ be a smooth algebraic curve over~$\mathbb C$.  A
\emph{chiral algebra}~$\cA$ on~$X$ is a $\mathcal D_X$-module
equipped with a chiral bracket
\[
\mu^{\mathrm{ch}}
\;\colon\;
j_*j^*(\cA\boxtimes\cA)
\;\longrightarrow\;
\Delta_!(\cA),
\]
where $j\colon X\times X\setminus\Delta\hookrightarrow X\times X$ is
the inclusion of the complement of the diagonal, and $\Delta_!\cA$ is
the $!$-pushforward along $\Delta\colon X\hookrightarrow X\times X$.
In local coordinates, the bracket encodes the operator product
expansion
\[
a(z)b(w)
\;\sim\;
\sum_{n\ge 0}\frac{a_{(n)}b}{(z-w)^{n+1}}\,,
\]
with $n$-th products $a_{(n)}b\in\cA$.  The \emph{Borcherds identity}
\[
\sum_{j\ge 0}\binom{m}{j}(a_{(n+j)}b)_{(m+k-j)}c
\;=\;
\sum_{j\ge 0}(-1)^j\binom{n}{j}
\Bigl(
a_{(m+n-j)}(b_{(k+j)}c)
-(-1)^n b_{(n+k-j)}(a_{(m+j)}c)
\Bigr)
\]
couples all pole orders in a single algebraic constraint: it is
the locality axiom, the Jacobi identity, and the commutator formula
simultaneously.

\subsection*{The bar complex on~$X$}

Place sections $a_1,\dots,a_n\in\cA$ at distinct points
$z_1,\dots,z_n\in X$.  Assign to each pair $(i,j)$ the logarithmic
$1$-form
\[
\eta_{ij}
\;:=\;
d\log(z_i-z_j)
\;=\;
\frac{dz_i-dz_j}{z_i-z_j}\,.
\]
For a planar tree $T$ on $\{1,\dots,n\}$, form the tensor
\[
a_1(z_1)\otimes\cdots\otimes a_n(z_n)
\;\otimes\;
\bigwedge_{(i,j)\in E(T)}\eta_{ij}\,.
\]
The Fulton--MacPherson compactification
$\overline{\operatorname{FM}}_n(X)$ resolves the configuration space
$\operatorname{Conf}_n(X)$ by iterated real blowup along all
diagonals---not by removing the diagonal, but by recording the
limiting direction and rate of approach.  Extract residues along
its collision divisors.  The result is the \emph{chiral bar complex}
\[
\barB_X(\cA)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{n\ge 1}
(s^{-1}\bar\cA)^{\otimes n}_{\Sigma_n}
\otimes
H^{n-1}\bigl(\overline{\operatorname{FM}}_n(X),\;
\Omega^{n-1}_{\log}\bigr),
\]
a graded coalgebra whose differential $d_{\barB}$ decomposes into three
pieces:
\[
d_{\barB}
\;=\;
d_{\mathrm{int}}+d_{\mathrm{res}}+d_{\mathrm{dR}}\,.
\]
Here $d_{\mathrm{int}}$ is the internal differential of~$\cA$,
$d_{\mathrm{res}}$ extracts OPE residues at codimension-one collision
strata, and $d_{\mathrm{dR}}$ is the de~Rham differential on the
logarithmic forms.

\subsection*{The proof that $d_{\barB}^2=0$}

Expand $(d_{\mathrm{int}}+d_{\mathrm{res}}+d_{\mathrm{dR}})^2$ into
nine terms.  Three types of index overlap govern the cancellation:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item \emph{Disjoint supports}: when the index sets $\{i,j\}$ and
$\{k,l\}$ are disjoint, the corresponding residue operators commute;
the cross terms $d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(ij)}d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(kl)}+
d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(kl)}d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(ij)}$ cancel pairwise.
\item \emph{Shared index}: when $\{i,j\}\cap\{k,l\}=\{j=k\}$, the
triple residue at the codimension-two stratum
$z_i=z_j=z_l$ involves the form
$\eta_{ij}\wedge\eta_{jl}$.  The Arnold relation
\[
\eta_{ij}\wedge\eta_{jl}
+\eta_{jl}\wedge\eta_{li}
+\eta_{li}\wedge\eta_{ij}
\;=\;0
\]
identifies this with a cyclic sum over the three orderings of the
triple collision.  The Borcherds identity, applied to
$a_i,a_j,a_l$, shows that the algebraic residue of this cyclic sum
vanishes.  The Arnold relation kills the form-theoretic factor; the
Borcherds identity kills the OPE factor.
\item \emph{Same pair}: $d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(ij)}d_{\mathrm{res}}^{(ij)}=0$
by the sign rule on desuspensions.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
The selection principle: the bar differential squares to zero if and
only if the $n$-th products satisfy the Borcherds identity.  The bar
construction accepts exactly chiral algebras.

\subsection*{The categorical logarithm}

The bar construction is a categorical logarithm.  The fundamental
property of $\log$---that $\log(ab)=\log a+\log b$---becomes:
the bar functor sends tensor products to direct sums,
$B(\cA\otimes\cA')\simeq B(\cA)\oplus B(\cA')$.
The logarithmic form $\eta_{ij}=d\log(z_i-z_j)$ is its integral
kernel.  The Arnold relation is the cocycle condition for $\log$:
the three-term identity
$\eta_{ij}\wedge\eta_{jk}+\eta_{jk}\wedge\eta_{ki}
+\eta_{ki}\wedge\eta_{ij}=0$ states that $d\log$ takes values
in a commutative algebra, so that the logarithm is
\emph{well-defined on products}.

The cobar $\Omega(\barB(\cA))$ is the exponential.  The domain on
which the exponential inverts the logarithm---the \emph{radius of
convergence}---is the Koszul locus: the full subcategory of chiral
algebras for which
$\varepsilon\colon\Omega(\barB(\cA))\xrightarrow{\sim}\cA$.

\subsection*{Verdier duality and bar-cobar inversion}

Verdier duality on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$ interchanges the bar
of~$\cA$ with the bar of a second chiral algebra~$\cA^!$, the
\emph{chiral Koszul dual}:
\[
\mathbb D_{\operatorname{Ran}}\,\barB_X(\cA)
\;\simeq\;
\barB_X(\cA^!).
\]
The dual $\cA^!$ is obtained by Verdier/linear duality on the bar
coalgebra---not by the cobar.  Explicitly: the bar cohomology
$\cA^{\mathrm i}:=H^*(B(\cA))$ is a coalgebra; its
linear dual $\cA^!:=(\cA^{\mathrm i})^\vee$ is the Koszul
dual algebra.

When $\cA$ is \emph{chirally Koszul}, the cobar inverts the bar:
$\Omega(\barB(\cA))\xrightarrow{\sim}\cA$.  The bar-to-cobar
spectral sequence collapses at~$E_2$, the bar coalgebra has
cohomology concentrated in a single degree, and~$\cA^!$ governs a
linear resolution of~$\cA$.  Classical Koszul duality over a point
(Priddy, Beilinson--Ginzburg--Soergel, Loday--Vallette) is the
special case $X=\operatorname{Spec}\mathbb C$.

\subsection*{The Heisenberg atom}

The Heisenberg chiral algebra $\mathcal H_k$ is generated by a
single field $\alpha$ of conformal weight~$1$ with OPE
\[
\alpha(z)\alpha(w)\;\sim\;\frac{k}{(z-w)^2}\,.
\]
Only the second-order pole is nonzero: $\alpha_{(1)}\alpha=k$,
$\alpha_{(n)}\alpha=0$ for $n\neq 1$.  The bar complex at
tensor-degree~$n$ is
\[
\barB^n_X(\mathcal H_k)
\;=\;
(s^{-1}\alpha)^{\otimes n}_{\Sigma_n}
\otimes
H^{n-1}\bigl(\overline{\operatorname{FM}}_n(X),\;
\Omega^{n-1}_{\log}\bigr).
\]
At $n=2$: the single form $\eta_{12}$ and the single OPE coefficient
$k$ give $d_{\mathrm{res}}(s^{-1}\alpha\otimes s^{-1}\alpha\otimes\eta_{12})
=k\cdot s^{-1}\mathbf 1$.  At $n\ge 3$: all residues factor through
$\alpha_{(1)}\alpha=k$ on one pair, and the remaining tensor factors
decouple.  No higher OPE poles intervene.  The bar complex is
quadratic---all structure is captured at tensor degree~$2$---and
the modular characteristic is
\[
\kappa(\mathcal H_k)\;=\;k.
\]
The Koszul dual is the curved symmetric chiral algebra
$\mathcal H_k^!=(\operatorname{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*),\,m_0=-k\omega)$,
where $m_0$ is the curvature term.

\section*{2.\quad Curvature and the genus tower}
% ====================================================================

\subsection*{The genus-$g$ propagator}

At genus~$0$, the function $z_1-z_2$ is globally defined and
$\eta_{12}=d\log(z_1-z_2)$ is single-valued.  At genus~$g\ge 1$,
$z_1-z_2$ has no global meaning.  The replacement is the
\emph{prime form} $E(z_1,z_2)$: a $(-\tfrac12,-\tfrac12)$-differential,
i.e.\ a section of $K^{-1/2}\boxtimes K^{-1/2}$ (not~$K^{+1/2}$),
vanishing to first order along the diagonal, with no other zeros.  Its
logarithmic derivative
\[
\omega(z_1,z_2)
\;:=\;
d_{z_1}\log E(z_1,z_2)
\]
is the fundamental meromorphic differential of the second kind on
$X\times X$, with a double pole along $\Delta$ and vanishing
$A$-periods in each variable.

The prime form has nontrivial monodromy: analytic continuation of
$E(z_1,z_2)$ around the $B$-cycle $B_j$ in the variable~$z_1$
produces
\[
E(z_1+B_j,z_2)
\;=\;
-\exp\bigl(-\pi i\Omega_{jj}-2\pi i\textstyle\int_{z_2}^{z_1}\omega_j\bigr)
\cdot E(z_1,z_2),
\]
where $\Omega$ is the period matrix of~$X$ and $\omega_1,\dots,\omega_g$
are the normalised holomorphic differentials.  The logarithmic
propagator at genus~$g$ is
\[
\eta^{(g)}_{12}
\;=\;
d\log E(z_1,z_2)
\;+\;
\pi\sum_{j,k=1}^g
(\operatorname{Im}\Omega)^{-1}_{jk}\,
\operatorname{Im}\!\Bigl(\int_{z_2}^{z_1}\omega_j\Bigr)\,\omega_k(z_1).
\]
The period correction $(\operatorname{Im}\Omega)^{-1}$ compensates
the $B$-cycle monodromy, coupling the propagator to the Hodge bundle
$\mathbb E\to\overline{\mathcal M}_g$.

\subsection*{Curvature}

The Arnold relation at genus~$g$ acquires a defect.  The three-term
identity
$\eta^{(g)}_{12}\wedge\eta^{(g)}_{23}
+\eta^{(g)}_{23}\wedge\eta^{(g)}_{31}
+\eta^{(g)}_{31}\wedge\eta^{(g)}_{12}$
no longer vanishes: the period corrections produce a residual
$(1,1)$-form.  The fibrewise bar differential satisfies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pref-curvature}
d_{\mathrm{fib}}^{\,2}\;=\;\kappa(\cA)\cdot\omega_g,
\end{equation}
where $\omega_g$ is the Arakelov $(1,1)$-form on the fibre of the
universal curve, and $\kappa(\cA)$ is the scalar modular
characteristic determined by the leading OPE singularity.

For the Heisenberg algebra, the computation is explicit.  The only
OPE data entering the bar differential is
$\alpha_{(1)}\alpha=k$, and every residue at a codimension-one
collision stratum extracts exactly this coefficient.  The
defect of the Arnold relation at genus~$g$ is
\[
d_{\mathrm{fib}}^{\,2}\big|_{\mathcal H_k}
\;=\;
k\cdot\omega_g.
\]
Thus $\kappa(\mathcal H_k)=k$.

\subsection*{Period integrals restore nilpotence}

The curvature~\eqref{eq:pref-curvature} obstructs $d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2=0$,
but not the total $D_g^2=0$.  Integration against
$A$-cycle periods defines a connection on the bar complex viewed as a
family of cochain complexes over~$\overline{\mathcal M}_g$.  The
corrected total differential
\[
D_g\;=\;d_{\mathrm{fib}}+\nabla^{\mathrm{GM}}
\]
incorporates the Gauss--Manin connection, and $D_g^{\,2}=0$: the
$(1,1)$-curvature of the fibrewise differential is absorbed by the
$(0,1)$-part of the base connection.

\emph{Nilpotence is the logarithm of periodicity.}  The nilpotent
datum~$\kappa$ determines the periodic datum---modular
transformations, the monodromy representation, the automorphy
factors---via exponentiation through the period map.

\subsection*{The genus tower}

The bar complex traces a family of curved cochain complexes over
$\overline{\mathcal M}_g$.  The obstruction to flatness at genus~$g$
is the characteristic class
\[
\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA)
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_g
\;\in\;
H^*(\overline{\mathcal M}_g),
\qquad
\lambda_g\;=\;c_g(\mathbb E),
\]
where $\mathbb E$ is the Hodge bundle.  Grothendieck--Riemann--Roch
applied to the universal curve $\pi\colon\mathcal C_g\to\overline{\mathcal M}_g$
computes the generating function:
\[
\sum_{g\ge 1}\mathrm{obs}_g\cdot\hbar^{2g-2}
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA)\cdot\bigl(\hat A(i\hbar)-1\bigr).
\]
The $\hat A$-genus emerges from the Todd class of the relative tangent
bundle $T_\pi$.  The first terms of the expansion:
\begin{align*}
F_1(\cA)
&\;=\;
-\frac{\kappa(\cA)}{24}\,,\\[3pt]
F_2(\cA)
&\;=\;
\frac{7\,\kappa(\cA)}{5760}\,,\\[3pt]
F_3(\cA)
&\;=\;
-\frac{31\,\kappa(\cA)}{967680}\,.
\end{align*}
Each coefficient is a Bernoulli-number combination: the genus-$g$
obstruction is $\kappa(\cA)\cdot(-1)^g B_{2g}/(2g)!$ up to
standard normalisation.

\subsection*{Complementarity}

Verdier duality splits the center local system into complementary
summands:
\[
R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal M}_g,\;\mathcal Z_\cA)
\;\simeq\;
\mathbf Q_g(\cA)\;\oplus\;\mathbf Q_g(\cA^!).
\]
What one algebra sees as deformation, its dual sees as obstruction.
The splitting admits a shifted-symplectic upgrade: the composition
\[
\mathcal C_g(\cA)\otimes\mathcal C_g(\cA)
\;\longrightarrow\;
R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal M}_g,\;\omega)
\]
carries a $(-(3g-3))$-shifted symplectic form, and the two summands
$\mathbf Q_g(\cA)$, $\mathbf Q_g(\cA^!)$ are Lagrangian with
respect to this pairing.  At the scalar level:
\[
\kappa(\cA)+\kappa(\cA^!)\;=\;0.
\]
Anomaly cancellation in string theory is the vanishing of the total
modular characteristic.

\subsection*{The Kac--Moody genus tower}

The affine Kac--Moody algebra $\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k$ at level~$k$
has OPE
\[
J^a(z)J^b(w)
\;\sim\;
\frac{f^{ab}{}_c\,J^c(w)}{z-w}
\;+\;
\frac{k\,\kappa^{ab}}{(z-w)^2}\,,
\]
where $f^{ab}{}_c$ are the structure constants of~$\mathfrak g$ and
$\kappa^{ab}$ is the Killing form.  The modular characteristic is
\[
\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)
\;=\;
\frac{k\cdot\dim\mathfrak g}{k+h^\vee}\,,
\]
where $h^\vee$ is the dual Coxeter number.  The Feigin--Frenkel
involution $k\leftrightarrow -k-2h^\vee$ is Koszul duality on
configuration spaces: it interchanges $\cA$ and~$\cA^!$ while
preserving the bar-cobar adjunction.

The Sugawara construction produces the stress tensor
\[
T(z)\;=\;\frac{1}{2(k+h^\vee)}\sum_a{:}J^a(z)J^a(z){:}\,,
\]
with central charge $c=k\dim\mathfrak g/(k+h^\vee)$.  At the critical
level $k=-h^\vee$, the Sugawara tensor is undefined: the denominator
vanishes, $\kappa$ diverges, and the chiral algebra acquires a large
center (the Feigin--Frenkel center).  The Koszul dual at critical
level is the Langlands dual critical-level algebra, not a
renormalised copy of the original.

\section*{3.\quad The modular convolution $L_\infty$-algebra}
% ====================================================================

\subsection*{3.1.\enspace Homotopy chiral algebras}

Fix an affine cover $\mathfrak U=\{U_\alpha\}$ of~$X$.  The
\v{C}ech totalisation
\[
A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty
\;:=\;
\check C^\bullet(\mathfrak U;\cA)
\]
carries a $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebra structure
(Malikov--Schechtman): secondary Borcherds operations
\[
F_n\colon \mathrm{Lie}(n)\otimes \cY(n)
\;\longrightarrow\;
P_n(\{A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty\},\,A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty),
\qquad n\ge 2,
\]
encoding derived OPE data at all pole orders.  The operation~$F_2$
is the chiral bracket.  The Jacobiator homotopy~$F_3$ arises from
$\partial\overline{\cM}_{0,4}$: three boundary points, three OPE
compositions, and $F_3$ is the chain homotopy witnessing their
equality up to~$d$.  Higher $F_n$ are determined inductively by
$\partial\overline{\cM}_{0,n+1}$ through the homotopy transfer
theorem.

Different covers yield quasi-isomorphic $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebras.
The strict chiral algebra~$\cA$ is the $H^0$ of this structure.
Rectification (Vallette): the homotopy category of
$\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebras is equivalent to the homotopy category
of strict chiral algebras.

\subsection*{3.2.\enspace Logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson spaces}

For a simple-normal-crossing pair $(X,D)$, Mok constructs
$\operatorname{FM}_n(X\mathbin{|}D)$: a logarithmic
Fulton--MacPherson compactification whose boundary stratification is
indexed by \emph{rigid planted-forest types}.  The ordinary FM
compactification is the case $D=\emptyset$.

The degeneration formula: for a semistable family $W\to B$ and a
rigid type~$\rho$,
\[
\operatorname{FM}_n(W/B)(\rho)
\;\longrightarrow\;
\prod_{v\in V(S_\rho)}
\operatorname{FM}_{I_v}(Y_v\mathbin{|}D_v)
\]
is a proper birational correspondence factoring the global FM
geometry into a product of local FM pieces.  The
tropicalisation of $\operatorname{FM}_n(\mathbb C\mathbin{|}D)$ is
the planted-forest coefficient algebra: planted forests are dual
graphs of tropical curves; grafting is degeneration.

The chain complexes
$\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{X|D}(n):=
C_\bullet(\operatorname{FM}_n(X\mathbin{|}D))$
carry cooperadic structure.  The \emph{graphwise cocomposition} for a
rigid stable graph~$\Gamma$ is
\[
\Delta^{\log}_\Gamma
\;:=\;
(\nu_\Gamma)_*\circ\operatorname{Res}_{D^{\log}_\Gamma}
\;\colon\;
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}}(g,n)
\;\longrightarrow\;
\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}}(g(v),I_v\sqcup H(v))
[-|E_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)|],
\]
where $\nu_\Gamma$ is the Mok birational correspondence on the
normalised boundary stratum.

The Mok codimension formula: for a planted-forest type with grid
depths $(w_1,\dots,w_r)$ and tree vertex counts $(|V(T_j)|)$,
\[
\operatorname{codim}(W_\rho)
\;=\;
\textstyle\sum_i w_i+\sum_j(|V(T_j)|-1).
\]
For $\operatorname{FM}_3$: seven boundary types---$\{12,23,13,123\}$
of codimension~$1$ and $\{12{<}123,\,23{<}123,\,13{<}123\}$ of
codimension~$2$.

\subsection*{3.3.\enspace The convolution $L_\infty$-algebra}

A twisting morphism $\alpha\colon\cC\to\cP$ between a cooperad and
an operad determines a convolution $sL_\infty$-algebra
$\operatorname{hom}_\alpha(\cC,\cP)$ (Robert-Nicoud--Wierstra).
Its brackets are
\[
\ell_k(f_1,\dots,f_k)(x)
\;=\;
\gamma_A\!\bigl((\alpha\circ 1_D)(f_1\otimes\cdots\otimes f_k)
\Delta_D^k(x)\bigr).
\]
One-slot functoriality: extends to $\infty$-morphisms in either
slot separately, but not both simultaneously.  This is a coherence
constraint: the MC3 categorical lift must proceed one slot at a
time.

The bar-cobar adjunction $B_\kappa\dashv\Omega_\kappa$ is a
Quillen equivalence (Vallette).  Different $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$
presentations yield quasi-isomorphic deformation theories.

\subsection*{3.4.\enspace Assembly}

The \emph{modular convolution $L_\infty$-algebra}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pref-convolution}
\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA
\;:=\;
\Convinf\!\Bigl(
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}},\;
\operatorname{End}_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty)
\Bigr).
\end{equation}
Coalgebra input: log-FM chain cooperad.  Algebra input:
$\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-endomorphism operad.  One-slot convention:
fix the cooperad, vary the chiral input by $\infty$-morphisms.  The
\emph{strict chart}
$\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod},\log}_\cA(\mathfrak U):=
\Convstr(\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}},\,
\operatorname{End}_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty))$
is the coderivation dg Lie algebra: classical bracket, no higher
$L_\infty$ brackets, same MC moduli.

\subsection*{3.5.\enspace The modular bar functor}

In log-FM coordinates:
\[
\mathrm B^{\log}_{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)(g,n)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}}
\left(
\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}
\bar B^{\mathrm{ch}}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty;\mathrm{In}(v))
\;\otimes\;
C_\bullet(\operatorname{FM}^{\log}_\Gamma)
\;\otimes\;
\orline{E_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)}
\right)_{\!\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)}.
\]
Each vertex: transferred $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-bar contribution.
Each edge: propagator $P_\cA=H_\cA^{-1}$.  The chain complex
$C_\bullet(\operatorname{FM}^{\log}_\Gamma)$: log-FM stratum.
The line $\orline{E_{\mathrm{int}}(\Gamma)}=
\det(\mathbf k E(\Gamma))\otimes\det(H_1(\Gamma;\mathbf k))^{-1}$:
signs.

\subsection*{3.6.\enspace The five-component differential}

\[
D^{\log}_{\mathrm{mod}}
\;=\;
\underbrace{d_{\check C}}_{\text{\v Cech}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}}_{\text{transferred chiral}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{coll}}}_{\text{collision}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{sew}}}_{\text{clutching}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{loop}}}_{\text{genus-raising}}.
\]

On a pure tensor $a\otimes\eta$ indexed by~$\Gamma$, the three
positive-genus boundary operators are residue correspondences:
\begin{align*}
d_{\mathrm{sew}}(a\otimes\eta)
&=
\sum_{e\in E_{\mathrm{sep}}(\Gamma)}
\pm\,\mu_e(a)\otimes(\xi_e)_*
\operatorname{Res}_{D_e^{\log}}(\eta),
\\[3pt]
d_{\mathrm{pf}}(a\otimes\eta)
&=
\sum_{F\in\mathsf{PF}^{\mathrm{rig}}(\Gamma)}
\pm\,\mu_F(a)\otimes(\xi_F)_*
\operatorname{Res}_{D_F^{\log}}(\eta),
\\[3pt]
\hbar\Delta(a\otimes\eta)
&=
\hbar\sum_{e\in E_{\mathrm{ns}}(\Gamma)}
\pm\,\operatorname{Tr}_e(a)\otimes(\xi_e)_*
\operatorname{Res}_{D_e^{\log}}(\eta).
\end{align*}
Separating strata $\to d_{\mathrm{sew}}$; non-separating strata
$\to\Delta$; planted-forest strata $\to d_{\mathrm{pf}}$.  The
planted-forest differential is the completed push--pull sum
\[
d_{\mathrm{pf}}
\;=\;
\sum_{\rho\in\mathsf{PF}^{\mathrm{rig}}}
\epsilon_\rho\,
(\kappa_\rho)_*\,\operatorname{pr}_\rho^*\otimes\mu_\rho
\]
---Mok's degeneration formula in the convolution algebra.

$D^2=0$ by three mechanisms: $d^2=0$ on vertex labels;
anticommutation of~$d$ with one-edge expansions; the
codimension-two cancellation axiom.  Geometrically: cancellation of
codimension-two faces in Mok's normal-crossings boundary.

\subsection*{4.1.\enspace $\Gamma$-amplitudes}

For $f_1,\dots,f_k\in\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA$ with
$|V(\Gamma)|=k$, let $\mu_\Gamma$ be the graph composition:
decorate vertices with transferred chiral operations, contract
internal edges with~$P_\cA$.  Write
$\alpha^{\mathrm{mod}}_\Gamma$ for the twisting morphism
component on~$\Gamma$.  The \emph{$\Gamma$-amplitude}:
\[

\section*{7.\quad The standard landscape}

The general theory is tested by explicit computation across every

% ----- 7.1 Heisenberg -----
\subsection*{7.1.\quad Heisenberg}

A single bosonic field $\alpha$ of conformal weight~$1$.  The OPE is
\[
\alpha(z)\,\alpha(w)
\;\sim\;
\frac{k}{(z-w)^2}.
\]
No simple pole: the OPE is purely singular of order~$2$.  The mode
algebra is the Heisenberg Lie algebra
$[\alpha_m,\alpha_n]=km\,\delta_{m+n,0}$, and the vertex algebra
$\mathcal H_k$ is the vacuum module at level~$k\neq 0$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Bar complex.}
The augmentation ideal
$\bar{\mathcal H}_k=\mathcal H_k/\mathbb C\cdot\mathbf{1}$ is
spanned by normally ordered monomials in~$\alpha$ and its
derivatives.  The bar construction is the tensor coalgebra on the
desuspension:
\[
\barB(\mathcal H_k)
\;=\;
T^c\!\bigl(s^{-1}\bar{\mathcal H}_k\bigr),
\qquad
d_{\barB}\bigl(s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n\bigr)
\;=\;
\sum_{i<j}\pm\,
s^{-1}a_1|\cdots|s^{-1}\operatorname{Res}_{z_i=z_j}
\bigl(a_i(z_i)\,a_j(z_j)\,\eta_{ij}\bigr)|\cdots|s^{-1}a_n.
\]
Because the only OPE singularity is a double pole (producing a
scalar), $d_{\barB}$ acts by contraction: it pairs two tensor factors
and replaces them by a scalar.  In degree~$1$,
$d_{\barB}(s^{-1}\alpha)=0$; in degree~$2$,
$d_{\barB}(s^{-1}\alpha|s^{-1}\alpha)=k\cdot s^{-1}\mathbf{1}=0$
in the reduced complex.  Bar cohomology concentrates:
$H^1(\barB(\mathcal H_k))=\mathbb C\cdot\alpha^*$,
$H^n(\barB(\mathcal H_k))=0$ for $n\ge 2$.  This is PBW
concentration in its most transparent form.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Koszul dual.}
Verdier duality on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$ sends
$\barB(\mathcal H_k)$ to a curved chiral coalgebra.  The linear dual
is
\[
\mathcal H_k^!
\;=\;
\bigl(\operatorname{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*),\;m_0=-k\omega\bigr),
\]
where $V^*=\mathbb C\cdot\alpha^*$ is the dual generator space and
$m_0=-k\omega$ is the curvature: a curving element, not a
differential.  The curved $A_\infty$-relation reads
$m_1^2(a)=[m_0,a]$ (commutator, with sign).  The Koszul dual of
Heisenberg is \emph{not} Heisenberg: $\mathcal H_k^!$ is a
\emph{curved} symmetric algebra, while $\mathcal H_k$ is a
\emph{flat} vertex algebra.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Shadow tower.}
The universal MC element collapses to a scalar:
\[
\Theta_{\mathcal H_k}
\;=\;
k\cdot\eta\otimes\Lambda,
\]
where $\eta=d\log(z_1-z_2)$ is the propagator and $\Lambda$ is the
universal two-point form.  Every shadow is a scalar multiple of a
universal geometric datum.  The five-component differential
collapses: $d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}=0$ (no higher transferred
operations---the OPE has no simple pole), $d_{\mathrm{coll}}=0$
(collisions produce scalars only), $d_{\mathrm{pf}}=0$ (no
planted-forest contributions---there are no arity-$3$ operations to
compose).  Only $d_{\check C}+d_{\mathrm{loop}}$ survive.  Shadow
depth~$2$.  Gaussian archetype.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Genus tower.}
The modular characteristic is $\kappa(\mathcal H_k)=k$.  The
obstruction classes at all genera assemble into
\[
\sum_{g\ge 1}\operatorname{obs}_g(\mathcal H_k)\cdot\hbar^{2g-2}
\;=\;
k\bigl(\hat A(i\hbar)-1\bigr).
\]
At genus~$2$, only the loop$\circ$loop shell contributes:
$\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}\circ\mathrm{loop}}\neq 0$,
$\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}=0$,
$\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{pf}}=0$.  The Heisenberg bar complex is the
unique family where every genus correction reduces to iterated loop
contractions of a single propagator.


% ----- 7.2 Kac-Moody -----
\subsection*{7.2.\quad Affine Kac--Moody}

Let $\mathfrak g$ be a simple Lie algebra with structure constants
$f^{ab}{}_c$ and normalised Killing form~$\kappa^{ab}$.  The affine
vertex algebra $\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k$ has generators $J^a$,
$a=1,\dots,\dim\mathfrak g$, of conformal weight~$1$, with OPE
\[
J^a(z)\,J^b(w)
\;\sim\;
\frac{f^{ab}{}_c\,J^c(w)}{z-w}
\;+\;
\frac{k\,\kappa^{ab}}{(z-w)^2}.
\]
Both pole orders are present.  The simple pole carries the Lie
bracket; the double pole carries the invariant inner product at
level~$k$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Bar complex.}
The simple-pole residue contributes a bracket component
to~$d_{\barB}$:
\[
d_{\barB}(s^{-1}J^a|s^{-1}J^b)
\;=\;
f^{ab}{}_c\,s^{-1}J^c
\;+\;
k\,\kappa^{ab}\cdot(\text{curvature term}).
\]
The bracket component is the Chevalley--Eilenberg differential of the
loop algebra; the curvature component is the central extension.  The
identity $d_{\barB}^2=0$ requires the \emph{Borcherds identity}, not
the Jacobi identity: the Borcherds identity encodes the $n$-fold
residue relations from all pole orders simultaneously, and is the
vertex-algebraic statement that replaces the classical quadratic
relation $[d,[d,-]]=0$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{PBW spectral sequence.}
Filter $\barB(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)$ by conformal weight.  The
spectral sequence $E_1^{p,q}(g)$ at genus~$g$ has $E_1$ page given
by the bar cohomology of the associated graded---a free-field
computation.  PBW concentration at all genera is proved:
$E_\infty^{p,q}(g)=0$ for $q\neq 0$.  The enriched genus-$g$
$E_1$ page carries the structure of a free resolution of the
diagonal $\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k$-bimodule, with the Hodge
bundle~$\mathbb E$ supplying the curvature twist at each genus
level.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Shadow tower.}
The cubic shadow is the Lie bracket itself:
\[
\mathfrak C(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)
\;=\;
\bigl[f_{abc}\,J^a\otimes J^b\otimes J^c\bigr]
\;\in\;
H^1(F^3\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}}/F^4\mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}},\,d_2).
\]
The quartic obstruction $o^{(4)}(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)=0$: this
vanishes by the Jacobi identity on~$\mathfrak g$, which kills the
only candidate quartic cocycle via
$f^{ab}{}_e f^{ec}{}_d+\text{cyclic}=0$.  Shadow depth~$3$.
Lie/tree archetype.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Genus-two shells.}
At genus~$2$, the loop$\circ$loop and sep$\circ$loop shells both
contribute: $\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{loop}\circ\mathrm{loop}}\neq 0$,
$\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{sep}\circ\mathrm{loop}}\neq 0$.  The
planted-forest shell vanishes:
$\Theta^{(2)}_{\mathrm{pf}}=0$, because the quartic shadow is zero
and planted forests require arity~$\ge 4$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Modular characteristic and duality.}
\[
\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k)
\;=\;
\frac{k\,\dim\mathfrak g}{k+h^\vee},
\qquad
\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k^!)
\;=\;
-\frac{k\,\dim\mathfrak g}{k+h^\vee}.
\]
The Feigin--Frenkel involution $k\leftrightarrow -k-2h^\vee$ is
Koszul duality in the modular sense: the level of the dual algebra
is the Langlands dual level, and the symmetry of~$\kappa$ under
$k\mapsto -k-2h^\vee$ is the complementarity relation
$\kappa(\cA)+\kappa(\cA^!)=0$ evaluated on this family.

At the critical level $k=-h^\vee$, the Sugawara construction is
\emph{undefined}---not ``$c$ diverges'' but $c$ does not exist.  The
Virasoro field is not a composite of currents.  The Feigin--Frenkel
center $\mathfrak z(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_{-h^\vee})$ is a
commutative vertex algebra isomorphic to the ring of functions on the
space of ${}^L\mathfrak g$-opers on the formal disk.  In the bar
complex, the critical-level specialisation replaces the Sugawara
curvature by a flat but noncommutative coalgebra structure.


% ----- 7.3 Virasoro -----
\subsection*{7.3.\quad Virasoro}

One generator~$T$ of conformal weight~$2$.  The OPE is
\[
T(z)\,T(w)
\;\sim\;
\frac{c/2}{(z-w)^4}
\;+\;
\frac{2\,T(w)}{(z-w)^2}
\;+\;
\frac{\partial T(w)}{z-w}.
\]
Three pole orders: the fourth-order pole carries the central charge;
the second-order pole carries the conformal weight of~$T$ itself
(the $T_{(1)}T=2T$ relation); the simple pole carries the
translation~$\partial T$.  Unlike Kac--Moody, the field~$T$ appears
in its own OPE at second order---a self-coupling that is the source
of all nonlinear complexity.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Shadow tower.}
The cubic shadow is determined by the $T_{(1)}T=2T$ self-coupling:
$\mathfrak C_{\mathrm{Vir}}\neq 0$.  The quartic contact shadow,
extracted from the four-point collision residue on
$\overline C_4(\mathbb P^1)$, is
\[
Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\frac{10}{c(5c+22)}.
\]
The poles at $c=0$ and $c=-22/5$ reflect the degeneration of the
Virasoro vacuum module.  The quintic obstruction is the Poisson
bracket in the shadow algebra:
$o^{(5)}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
=\{C_{\mathrm{Vir}},Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}\}_H\neq 0$.
The nonvanishing of~$o^{(5)}$ forces the shadow tower to be
\emph{infinite}: the Virasoro algebra cannot be captured by any
finite truncation of the MC element.  Shadow depth~$\infty$.  Mixed
modular archetype.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Koszul dual.}
\[
\operatorname{Vir}_c^!
\;=\;
\operatorname{Vir}_{26-c}.
\]
The duality is an involution on the Virasoro family parametrised
by~$c$; the fixed point is $c=13$, not~$c=26$.  The central
charge~$26$ plays no special role in Koszul duality---its appearance
in string theory is the vanishing of
$\kappa(\operatorname{Vir}_{26})=26/2=13
=\kappa(\operatorname{Vir}_{26}^!)$, which is the
\emph{self-complementarity} condition, not the self-duality
condition.  The same-family shadow $\operatorname{Vir}_{26-c}$ is the
depth-zero resonance shadow: the image of the finite-dimensional
resonance truncation $R_{\mathrm{Vir}}$, not the final Koszul dual
in the completed sense.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Higher-genus corrections.}
The modular characteristic is $\kappa(\operatorname{Vir}_c)=c/2$.
At genus~$1$, the shadow tower produces corrections beyond the
linear curvature:
\[
\delta H^{(1)}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\frac{120}{c^2(5c+22)}\cdot x^2,
\qquad
\rho^{(1)}_{\mathrm{Vir}}
\;=\;
\frac{240}{c^3(5c+22)}.
\]
The Hessian correction $\delta H^{(1)}$ is the genus-$1$ quadratic
deviation from the linearised curvature; the loop
ratio~$\rho^{(1)}$ is the ratio of the quadratic correction
to~$\kappa^2$.  Both are determined by the quartic contact shadow
and carry information strictly beyond~$\kappa=c/2$: they are the
first nonlinear genus-$1$ invariants.

At genus~$2$, all three primitive shells contribute: loop$\circ$loop
from iterated curvature, sep$\circ$loop from mixed clutching, and
planted-forest from the quartic contact channel.  The Virasoro
algebra is the simplest family in which the planted-forest shell is
nonzero.


% ----- 7.4 W_3 -----
\subsection*{7.4.\quad $\boldsymbol{\mathcal W_3}$}

Two generators: the stress tensor~$T$ (weight~$2$) and a primary
field~$W$ (weight~$3$).  The $TW$~OPE is
$T(z)W(w)\sim 3W(w)/(z-w)^2+\partial W(w)/(z-w)$: $W$~is a
Virasoro primary of weight~$3$.  The $WW$~OPE is the first genuinely
nonlinear datum:
\begin{align*}
W(z)\,W(w)
\;\sim\;\;
&\frac{c/3}{(z-w)^6}
\;+\;
\frac{2\,T(w)}{(z-w)^4}
\;+\;
\frac{\partial T(w)}{(z-w)^3}\\[4pt]
&+\;
\frac{1}{(z-w)^2}
\biggl[
\frac{16}{22+5c}\,\Lambda(w)
\;+\;
\frac{3}{10}\,\partial^2 T(w)
\biggr]
\;+\;\cdots\,,
\end{align*}
where the composite field
\[
\Lambda
\;=\;
{:}TT{:}\;-\;\tfrac{3}{10}\,\partial^2 T
\]
is the unique quasi-primary of weight~$4$ in the vacuum module.  The
coefficient $-3/10$ is fixed by requiring $T(z)\Lambda(w)$ to have
no third-order pole---equivalently, by quasi-primary projection
orthogonal to the descendant~$\partial^2 T$.  The coefficient
$16/(22+5c)$ at order $(z-w)^{-2}$ is the structure constant of the
$WW\Lambda$ coupling.

At $c=-22/5$, the denominator vanishes and $\Lambda$ becomes a null
field: $\mathcal W_3$ is not finitely generated by $T$ and~$W$
alone.  The Kac determinant of the vacuum Verma module vanishes at
weight~$4$, and infinitely many new generators are needed.  This is
the first $\mathcal W$-algebra where the structure constants have
poles in the central charge.

The quartic contact channel through~$\Lambda$ is the first
planted-forest correction in the bar complex: the bar differential
acquires a term that factors through the composition
$W\otimes W\to\Lambda\to T\otimes T$ (via the quasi-primary
projection and then the OPE), and this factorisation is represented
by a planted forest with two leaves and an internal vertex of
weight~$4$.  The $\mathcal W_3$ family is the first that is genuinely
planted-forest sensitive: its shadow tower detects log-FM boundary
strata that are invisible to Heisenberg and affine Kac--Moody.

\subsection*{7.5.\quad $\boldsymbol{\beta\gamma}$ system}

Two fields: $\beta$ of conformal weight~$1$ and $\gamma$ of
conformal weight~$0$.  The OPE is

\;\sim\;
\frac{1}{z-w},
\qquad
\beta(z)\,\beta(w)\;\sim\;0,
\qquad

The simple pole with no double pole is the contact archetype: the
cubic shadow on the weight line vanishes ($\ell_3^{(0)}=0$, since
$\beta$ and $\gamma$ carry different weights and the three-point

$\mathrm{U}(1)$ ghost-number symmetry, and the cyclic pairing
$\langle-,-\rangle$ on the deformation complex vanishes on the
arity-$4$ component by weight considerations.  Shadow depth~$4$.
Contact/quartic archetype.  The modular characteristic
$\kappa(\beta\gamma)=-2$ reflects the $c=-2$ central charge of the
$\beta\gamma$~system via the Sugawara construction.

\subsection*{7.7.\quad Yangians and quantum groups}

Fix a simple Lie algebra~$\mathfrak g$.  The Yangian
$Y(\mathfrak g)$ governs the $E_1$-chiral atom: chiral algebras on

R(z)
\;=\;

\;\in\;
Y(\mathfrak g)\otimes Y(\mathfrak g)[[z^{-1}]]
\]
is the genus-$0$ binary shadow of the MC element:
$r(z)=\operatorname{Res}^{\mathrm{coll}}_{0,2}(\Theta_\cA)$.  The
classical $r$-matrix $r=\Omega/z$ (where $\Omega$ is the Casimir)
satisfies the classical Yang--Baxter equation, which is the Arnold
relation on $\operatorname{FM}_3(\mathbb C)$ evaluated on the Casimir
tensor.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Thick generation in type~$A$.}
The quantum group category
$\operatorname{Rep}(U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_N))$ is compactly
generated by the prefundamental representations~$L_i^\pm$
(Hern\'andez--Jimbo).  Thick generation at all ranks is proved via
three ingredients:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*),nosep]
\item \emph{Chromatic filtration.}  Filter
  $K_0(\operatorname{Rep})$ by the support of $q$-characters on the
  Grothendieck ring.  The successive quotients are controlled by
  products of prefundamental modules.
\item \emph{Clebsch--Gordan closure.}  The tensor product
  $V_n\otimes L^-=\bigoplus_{j=0}^n L^-(n-2j)$ at character level,
  for all~$n$.  Every Verma character lies in the $K_0$-span of
  $\{[V_n]\cdot[L^-]\}$.
\item \emph{Efimov completion.}  Pass from compact generation in
  $K_0$ to categorical thick generation via Efimov's categorical
  completion functor applied to the pro-system of
  Kirillov--Reshetikhin modules.
\end{enumerate}
Extension to arbitrary simple type requires the Hern\'andez--Jimbo
prefundamental CG decomposition for all Dynkin types.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{dg-shifted Yangian.}
The modular dg-shifted Yangian
$Y_T^{\mathrm{mod}}=\varprojlim_r Y_T^{\mathrm{mod},\le r}$ is the
pronilpotent completion of the convolution dg Lie algebra associated
to the chiral algebra of a 3d holomorphic-topological theory~$T$.
The modular $R$-matrix
$R_T^{\mathrm{mod}}(z;\hbar)\in
\operatorname{MC}(Y_T^{\mathrm{mod}})$
is an MC element: the Yang--Baxter equation is the MC equation
projected to arity~$3$, and the higher RTT relations are the MC
equation at all arities.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Drinfeld--Kohno.}
The KZ connection on $\operatorname{Conf}_n(\mathbb C)$ with values
in $\mathfrak g^{\otimes n}$ is
$\nabla_{\mathrm{KZ}}=d-\hbar\sum_{i<j}
\Omega_{ij}\,d\log(z_i-z_j)$.
Its monodromy representation
$\pi_1(\operatorname{Conf}_n(\mathbb C))\to
\operatorname{Rep}(U_\hbar(\mathfrak g))$
is the Drinfeld--Kohno theorem: quantum group representations arise
from factorization homology of the chiral algebra on configuration
spaces.  In the shadow tower language, the KZ connection is the
genus-$0$ arity-$n$ shadow
$\operatorname{Sh}_{0,n}(\Theta_{\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k})$
projected to the scalar sector.

\subsection*{8.1.\quad Scalar saturation}

For a one-parameter algebraic family~$\cA_k$ with rational OPE
coefficients---meaning the structure constants are rational functions
of the level~$k$---the universal MC element $\Theta_{\cA_k}$ reduces
to a scalar multiple of a universal tensor:
\[
\Theta_{\cA_k}
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA_k)\cdot\eta_{\mathrm{univ}}\otimes\Lambda_{\mathrm{univ}}
\;+\;
(\text{higher-order terms in $H^2_{\mathrm{cyc,prim}}$}).
\]
Scalar saturation universality is the statement that the higher-order
terms vanish: $H^2_{\mathrm{cyc,prim}}(\cA_k)=0$ for generic~$k$.
The proof proceeds in two steps.  \emph{Whitehead reduction}: for a
vertex algebra~$\cA_k$ with reductive symmetry at generic~$k$, the
primitive cyclic cohomology $H^2_{\mathrm{cyc,prim}}$ is computed by
a Whitehead-type spectral sequence whose $E_2$ page is the Lie
algebra cohomology $H^2(\mathfrak g;\operatorname{Sym}^n V)$ of the
symmetry algebra acting on the conformal-weight filtration.
\emph{Algebraic semicontinuity}: the dimension
$\dim H^2_{\mathrm{cyc,prim}}(\cA_k)$ is upper-semicontinuous
in~$k$ (as a constructible function on the parameter space), vanishes
at the free-field point $k=\infty$, and therefore vanishes on a
Zariski-dense open set.  The union of these vanishing loci covers the
entire standard Lie-theoretic landscape at all non-critical levels,
including admissible ones.

\subsection*{8.2.\quad The Koszulness meta-theorem}

Twelve conditions on a chiral algebra are conjecturally equivalent.
Four are proved; eight remain conjectural.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Proved conditions.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textnormal{(K\arabic*)},nosep]
\item \emph{$A_\infty$-formality of bar cohomology.}
  If $H^\bullet(\barB(\cA))$ admits a formal $A_\infty$-structure
  (all higher products $m_k=0$ for $k\ge 3$), then $\cA$ is chirally
  Koszul.
\item \emph{Shadow-formality at low arity.}
  The shadow Postnikov tower $\Theta^{\le r}_\cA$ equals the
  $L_\infty$-formality obstruction tower of the modular convolution
  algebra at arities $r=2,3,4$.
\item \emph{$E_2$-formality of chiral Hochschild cohomology.}
  For chirally Koszul~$\cA$, the chiral Hochschild cochain complex
  $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^*(\cA)$ is formal as an $E_2$-algebra.
\item \emph{Genus-$0$ curve independence.}
  Genus-$0$ chiral Koszulness is independent of the curve~$X$: if
  $\cA$ is chirally Koszul on~$\mathbb P^1$, it is chirally Koszul
  on every smooth curve.
\end{enumerate}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Conjectural conditions.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textnormal{(K\arabic*)},nosep,start=5]
\item \emph{Barr--Beck--Lurie.}
  $\cA$ is chirally Koszul iff the bar-cobar adjunction
  $\barB\dashv\Omega$ satisfies the monadic Barr--Beck--Lurie
  conditions on the $\infty$-category of chiral $\cA$-modules.
\item \emph{Factorization homology concentration.}
  $\cA$ is chirally Koszul iff $\int_X\cA$ has cohomology
  concentrated in degree~$0$ for every smooth proper~$X$.
\item \emph{FM boundary acyclicity.}
  $\cA$ is chirally Koszul iff the restriction of $\barB(\cA)$ to
  each boundary stratum of $\overline C_n(X)$ is acyclic for
  $n\ge 3$.
\item \emph{Tropical Koszulness.}
  $\cA$ is chirally Koszul iff the tropicalisation
  $\operatorname{Trop}(\barB(\cA))$ on the Berkovich skeleton of~$X$
  satisfies the tropical bar-cobar inversion criterion.
\item \emph{Lagrangian criterion.}
  $\cA$ is chirally Koszul iff the modular complementarity
  decomposition $Q_g(\cA)\oplus Q_g(\cA^!)$ is a Lagrangian
  splitting of the center local system with respect to the Mukai
  pairing.
\item \emph{Bifunctor obstruction decomposition.}
  $\cA$ is chirally Koszul iff the one-slot obstruction of RNW (the
  failure of the convolution $sL_\infty$-algebra
  $\operatorname{hom}_\alpha(C,A)$ to be a bifunctor in both slots
  simultaneously) decomposes into independent bar-side and cobar-side
  components.
\item \emph{PBW universality.}
  Every freely strongly generated vertex algebra is chirally Koszul;
  in particular, $V_k(\mathfrak g)$ is chirally Koszul at every
  level~$k$ (including critical and admissible).
\item \emph{Spectral determinant criterion.}
  $\cA$ is chirally Koszul iff the spectral determinant
  $\Delta_\cA(t)$ has all roots on the unit circle.
\end{enumerate}
Conditions (K1)--(K4) are proved.  Condition (K11) is proved as a
standalone result (the PBW criterion), but its equivalence with
(K1)--(K4) is part of the conjectural meta-theorem.  Conditions
(K5)--(K10) and~(K12) are open.

\subsection*{8.3.\quad Shadow depth and operadic complexity}

Shadow depth does not characterise Koszulness: Heisenberg
(depth~$2$), affine Kac--Moody (depth~$3$), $\beta\gamma$
(depth~$4$), and Virasoro (depth~$\infty$) are all chirally Koszul.
Shadow depth classifies the \emph{complexity} of a Koszul algebra:

\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{lcccl}
\textbf{Class} & \textbf{Depth} & $\ell_3^{(0)}$ & $\ell_4^{(0)}$
  & \textbf{Archetype}\\
\hline
Gaussian (G) & $2$ & $0$ & $0$ & free field\\
Lie/tree (L) & $3$ & $\neq 0$ & $0$ & Lie bracket\\
Contact/quartic (C) & $4$ & $0$ & $\neq 0$ & ghost contact\\
Mixed modular (M) & $\infty$ & $\neq 0$ & $\neq 0$
  & self-coupled stress tensor
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent
The \emph{operadic complexity conjecture} identifies shadow depth
with $A_\infty$-depth: $r_{\max}(\cA)$ equals the minimal~$r$ such
that the transferred $A_\infty$-structure on
$H^\bullet(\barB(\cA))$ has $m_k=0$ for all $k>r$.  If true, the
shadow tower is the $L_\infty$-formality obstruction tower of the
modular convolution algebra, and the depth classification (G/L/C/M)
becomes a structural invariant of the operadic type of~$\cA$.

\section*{9.\quad The Swiss-cheese world (Volume II)}
% ====================================================================

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(a) The operadic structure.}
The \emph{holomorphic-topological Swiss-cheese operad}
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ is a two-colored operad with color
set $\{\mathrm{ch},\mathrm{top}\}$: \emph{closed}~$=$~holomorphic,
\emph{open}~$=$~topological.  Its operation spaces are:
\begin{align*}
\mathsf{SC}(\mathrm{ch}^k;\mathrm{ch})
&=\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C),\\
\mathsf{SC}(\mathrm{ch}^k,\mathrm{top}^m;\mathrm{top})
&=\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)\times E_1(m),\\
\mathsf{SC}(\mathrm{top}^m;\mathrm{ch})
&=\varnothing.
\end{align*}
Closed-to-closed operations are parametrised by the Fulton--MacPherson
compactification of ordered configurations in~$\mathbb C$; mixed
operations combine holomorphic configurations with the $E_1$-operad
structure of interval inclusions; the open-to-closed direction is
forbidden.  Operadic composition comes from the boundary stratification
of FM spaces (holomorphic) and from interval inclusions (topological).

A bar element of degree~$k$ lives on
$\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)\times\operatorname{Conf}_k(\mathbb R)$:
the differential extracts OPE residues along collision divisors of the
first factor; the coproduct splits an ordered sequence of tensor
factors at a point of the second.  The bar complex, carrying both
structures, is an $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(b) Homotopy-Koszulity.}
The classical Swiss-cheese operad $\mathsf{SC}$ is Koszul (Livernet,
Voronov; Ginzburg--Kapranov).  Kontsevich formality supplies a
quasi-isomorphism
$\Phi\colon\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\xrightarrow{\sim}\mathsf{SC}$.
The bar-cobar adjunction preserves quasi-isomorphisms; the
two-out-of-three property then gives
\[
\Omega\mathbf B(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})
\;\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}\;
\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}.
\]
The bar-cobar adjunction on
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebras is a Quillen equivalence.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(c) The $A_\infty$ chiral algebra structure.}
A twisting morphism
$\alpha=(m_k)_{k\ge 1}\in\operatorname{MC}\bigl(
\operatorname{Hom}(\bar B^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\operatorname{End}_\cA)\bigr)$
encodes the $A_\infty$ chiral algebra on~$\cA$.  Each component
\[
m_k\colon\cA^{\otimes k}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\cA((\lambda_1))\cdots((\lambda_{k-1}))
\]
is a configuration-space integral on
$\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb C)\times\operatorname{Conf}_k(\mathbb R)$:
the holomorphic factor produces the OPE singularity; the topological
factor produces the ordering.  The Maurer--Cartan equation
$\partial\alpha+\alpha\star\alpha=0$ is the \emph{spectral Stasheff
identity}: integration over the FM boundary strata produces the $A_\infty$
relations by Stokes' theorem.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(d) PVA descent.}
On cohomology $H^\bullet(\cA,Q)$, the operations simplify.  The regular
part of~$m_2$ is a commutative product~$\mu$.  The singular part is a
$\lambda$-bracket $\{a_\lambda b\}$.  All $m_{k\ge 3}$ vanish by
topological contractibility of~$\mathbb R$.  The axioms of a
$(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex algebra emerge from geometry:

\smallskip
\emph{Jacobi identity.}
The Arnold relation $\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23}+\eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{31}
+\eta_{31}\wedge\eta_{12}=0$ on $\operatorname{FM}_3(\mathbb C)$
is a relation between three codimension-one boundary divisors of
$\overline{M}_{0,4}$.  Residues along these three strata produce the
three terms of the Jacobi identity for $\{-_\lambda-\}$.

\smallskip
\emph{Leibniz rule.}
The three-face Stokes identity on $\partial\operatorname{FM}_3(\mathbb C)$
decomposes the boundary into faces where one point collides with a
cluster and faces where the product is applied first.  The resulting
cancellation is the Leibniz rule for $\{-_\lambda-\}$ over~$\mu$.

\smallskip
\emph{Skew-symmetry.}
The exchange cylinder on
$\operatorname{FM}_2(\mathbb C)\times E_1(2)$ reverses the two
insertion points; the monodromy of $\eta_{12}$ around the exchange
loop gives $\{a_\lambda b\}=-\{b_{-\lambda-\partial}a\}$.

\smallskip\noindent
The result: $(\mu,\{-_\lambda-\})$ is a $(-1)$-shifted Poisson vertex
algebra.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(e) The Heisenberg example in SC coordinates.}
For the Heisenberg algebra $\mathcal H_k$:
\[
m_2(J,J)=k\cdot\mathbf{1},
\qquad
m_{k\ge 3}=0,
\qquad
\{J_\lambda J\}=k\lambda.
\]
The spectral $R$-matrix is $R(z)=\exp(k/z)$.  The Yang--Baxter
equation is trivially satisfied: the $R$-matrix is scalar.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(f) The bulk-boundary-line triangle.}
The Koszul dual~$\cA^!$ governs the category of line operators.
Stokes' theorem on $\operatorname{FM}_3(\mathbb C)$ applied to the
triple convolution $\alpha\star\alpha\star\alpha$ decomposes the
boundary into three pairwise collision divisors---one for each side of
the Yang--Baxter equation
\[
R_{12}(z_1{-}z_2)\,R_{13}(z_1{-}z_3)\,R_{23}(z_2{-}z_3)
\;=\;
R_{23}(z_2{-}z_3)\,R_{13}(z_1{-}z_3)\,R_{12}(z_1{-}z_2).
\]
The spectral braiding
$R(z)\colon L_1\otimes L_2\xrightarrow{\sim}L_2\otimes L_1$ acts on
$\cA^!$-modules.  The triangle:
\[
\cA_{\mathrm{bulk}}
\;\simeq\;
Z_{\mathrm{der}}(\cB_\partial)
\;\simeq\;
\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet(\cA^!),
\qquad
\cA^!\text{-}\mathsf{mod}\;=\;\{\text{line operators}\}.
\]
The bulk algebra is the derived center of the boundary algebra, which
is the chiral Hochschild cohomology of the Koszul dual.  The Koszul
dual~$\cA^!$, equipped with the spectral $R$-matrix, is a dg-shifted
Yangian.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(g) Curved Swiss-cheese at genus $g\ge 1$.}
At genus~$g\ge 1$, the bar complex
$(\bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA),\,d_{\mathrm{fib}},\,\Delta)$ carries
\[
d_{\mathrm{fib}}^{\,2}\;=\;\kappa(\cA)\cdot\omega_g.
\]
The coproduct remains strictly coassociative.  The total corrected
differential~$D_g$ restores flatness over~$\overline{\cM}_g$ by
coupling the $\mathbb R$-ordering to the Hodge bundle.  The derived
category of modules must be replaced by the \emph{coderived} category
(or the contraderived category, depending on the side):
curvature forces $d^2\neq 0$ on fibers, so the ordinary derived
category is empty, while the coderived category retains the correct
homotopy theory.

\section*{10.\quad Modular PVA quantization}
% ====================================================================

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(a) The one-edge principle.}
If a filtered deformation dg Lie algebra~$L$ carries a complete genus
filtration and its differential preserves genus exactly, then any
genus-zero Maurer--Cartan element~$\Theta_0\in\operatorname{MC}(\operatorname{gr}_F^0 L)$
extends tautologically to a full MC element: genus-by-genus, the
forcing term vanishes because no differential raises genus.  Therefore
every nontrivial modular obstruction theory must contain a primitive
genus-raising operator.

The one-edge expansion of the stable-graph differential furnishes this
primitive.  Every one-edge degeneration of a stable graph is either
\emph{separating} (two vertices joined by one edge, preserving genus)
or \emph{nonseparating} (one vertex carrying one loop, raising genus
by~$1$).  Set
\[
D_0\;:=\;D_{\mathrm{int}}+D_{\mathrm{sep}},
\qquad
D_1\;:=\;D_{\mathrm{nsep}}.
\]
Then $D_0$ preserves total genus, $D_1$ raises total genus by one, and
$(D_0+D_1)^2=0$ stratifies into three identities:
\[
D_0^2=0,
\qquad
D_0 D_1+D_1 D_0=0,
\qquad
D_1^2=0.
\]

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(b) The modular bar datum axioms.}
A \emph{modular bar datum} on a reduced complete finite-type graded
collection $F\mapsto C(F)$ consists of:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item An internal differential $d\colon C(F)\to C(F)$ of degree~$+1$.
\item For each one-edge expansion $\epsilon\in\operatorname{OE}(F)$,
  a degree~$+1$ map
  \[
  \Delta_\epsilon\colon sC(F)
  \;\longrightarrow\;
  \orline(\epsilon)\otimes
  \bigotimes_{u\in V(\epsilon)}sC(\operatorname{Fl}_\epsilon(u)).
  \]
\item The \emph{codimension-two cancellation axiom}: for every connected
  stable graph with exactly two internal edges, the signed sum of the
  two ways of producing it by successive one-edge expansions is zero.
\item The anticommutation relation $d\circ\Delta_\epsilon+\Delta_\epsilon\circ d=0$.
\end{enumerate}
The total differential is $D=D_{\mathrm{int}}+D_{\mathrm{exp}}$, and
$D^2=0$ follows from three mechanisms: $d^2=0$ on vertex labels,
anticommutation of~$d$ with one-edge expansions, and the
codimension-two cancellation axiom.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(c) The complete modular bar coalgebra.}
For a connected stable graph~$\Gamma$, set
$C[\Gamma]:=\orline(\Gamma)\otimes\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}sC(\operatorname{Fl}(v))$.
The \emph{complete modular bar coalgebra} is
\[
\mathrm B_{\mathrm{mod}}(C)
\;=\;
\prod_{[\Gamma]\in\mathsf{StGraph}^{\mathrm{conn}}}
\bigl(C[\Gamma]\bigr)_{\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)},
\]
completed over total genus.  The reduced coproduct strictly decreases
the number of internal edges, so the coalgebra is conilpotent.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(d) The genus-one obstruction theorem.}
The coderivation dg Lie algebra
$L_{\mathrm{mod}}(C):=\operatorname{Coder}(\mathrm B_{\mathrm{mod}}(C))[-1]$
is the strict modular deformation algebra.  For a genus-zero MC
element~$\Theta_0$, the genus-one obstruction is
\[
\operatorname{Ob}_1(\Theta_0)
\;=\;
[d_1\Theta_0]
\;\in\;
H^2\!\bigl((\operatorname{gr}_F^1 L)^{\Theta_0},\,d_0^{\Theta_0}\bigr).
\]
Ob$_1=0$ if and only if $\Theta_0$ lifts to a genus-one MC element

\smallskip

\subsection*{12.1.\enspace The Maurer--Cartan frontier}

Five master conjectures organise the research frontier; the first
four are resolved.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{MC1} (PBW concentration).  For every standard
family---Kac--Moody, Virasoro, principal $\mathcal W_N$---and
every genus $g\ge 1$, the PBW spectral sequence for the genus-$g$
bar complex has concentrated $E_\infty$ page:
$E_\infty^{p,q}(g)=0$ for $q\neq 0$.  The proof: genus-$0$
Koszulness supplies concentration of $E_1^{p,q}(g{=}0)$; the
genus-$g$ enrichment $\cE^{p,q}_g$ from the Hodge bundle is
$d_0^{\mathrm{PBW}}$-exact because it arises from the regular
Eisenstein series, which is exact under the Poincar\'e residue.
The concentration is uniform in the level parameter.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{MC2} (universal~$\Theta_\cA$).  The bar-intrinsic
construction $\Theta_\cA:=D_\cA-d_0$ is automatically MC because
$D_\cA^2=0$.  Scalar saturation universality---the reduction
$\Theta_\cA=\kappa\cdot\eta\otimes\Lambda$ on a universal
tensor---holds for all algebraic families with rational OPE
coefficients via Whitehead reduction (the relevant deformation
cohomology $H^2_{\mathrm{cyc,prim}}$ vanishes) and algebraic
semicontinuity (the vanishing locus is Zariski-open and contains
all non-critical specialisations).  This covers the entire standard
Lie-theoretic landscape at all non-critical levels including
admissible ones.  Ambient $D^2=0$ is proved via Mok's log-FM
normal-crossings result.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{MC3} (thick generation).  In type~$A$, the
quantum group category $\cO_q(\mathfrak{sl}_N)$ is thick-generated
by the evaluation modules $V_\lambda\otimes L^-$ over the
prefundamental representation~$L^-$.  The proof has four steps:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item \emph{Chromatic filtration.}  Stratify the category by the
  support of the character on the Grothendieck ring.
\item \emph{Prefundamental Clebsch--Gordan.}  The tensor product
  $V_n\otimes L^-$ decomposes as
  $\bigoplus_{j=0}^n L^-(n-2j)$ at the character level (a formal
  series identity verified to arbitrary depth).
\item \emph{$K_0$-generation.}  Every Verma character
  $\operatorname{ch}(M(\lambda))$ lies in the
  $K_0$-span of $\{[V_n]\cdot[L^-]\}$.
\item \emph{Efimov completion.}  Lift from $K_0$-generation to
  categorical thick generation via Efimov's ind-completion
  machinery.
\end{enumerate}
Extension to arbitrary simple type is conjectural: it requires
Hern\'andez--Jimbo prefundamental modules for all Dynkin types.
DK-5 is accessible in type~$A$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{MC4} (completed bar-cobar).  The strong
completion-tower theorem: the strong filtration axiom
\[
\mu_r(F^{i_1},\dots,F^{i_r})
\;\subset\;
F^{i_1+\cdots+i_r}
\]
yields an automatic arity cutoff (the $r$-ary operation maps
$F^{i_1}\otimes\cdots\otimes F^{i_r}$ into $F^{i_1+\cdots+i_r}$,
so operations with enough inputs land in arbitrarily deep
filtration), making continuity and the Mittag-Leffler condition
automatic.  The completion closure
$\operatorname{CompCl}(\cF_{\mathrm{ft}})$ carries a quasi-inverse
bar-cobar homotopy equivalence stable under MC twisting, with
completed twisting representability.

MC4 splits into two sub-problems:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{MC4${}^+$} (positive towers: $\mathcal W_{1+\infty}$,
  affine Yangians, RTT algebras): solved by weight stabilisation.
  The completed bar-cobar converges because the weight grading
  provides a natural Cauchy criterion.
\item \emph{MC4${}^0$} (resonant towers: Virasoro, non-quadratic
  $\mathcal W_N$): reduced to a finite resonance problem by the
  resonance-filtered bar-cobar theorem.  Resonance
  rank~$\rho(\cA)$ classifies completion difficulty.
\end{itemize}
The \emph{platonic completion conjecture}: every positive-energy
chiral algebra has $\rho<\infty$.  The Virasoro algebra at $c=26$:
$\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$ is the depth-zero resonance shadow, not the
final dual---the genuine $\mathcal W_\infty$-type dual requires the
full resonance-filtered completion.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{MC5} (genus expansion).  Genus~$0$ and~$1$ proved.
At genus~$0$, the bar differential is the BRST differential of the
holomorphic twist.  At genus~$1$, the curved bar complex with
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2=\kappa\cdot\omega_1$ encodes the one-loop
amplitude; the complementarity splitting is the genus-$1$
modular-invariance constraint.  Genera~$\ge 2$ require Costello
renormalization: the higher-loop integrals over
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$ need regularisation beyond the algebraic
bar-cobar framework, and the correct receptacle is the coderived
category (curvature at $g\ge 1$ forces coderived/contraderived
passage, not ordinary derived).

\subsection*{12.2.\enspace The analytic frontier}

The bare VOA is a dense algebraic skeleton.  The actual object for
partition functions and sewing is the \emph{sewing envelope}
$\cA^{\mathrm{sew}}$: the Hausdorff completion of~$\cA$ for the
topology defined by all-amplitude seminorms
$\|a\|_{\mathrm{amp}}:=\sup_{v}\langle v,\pi(a)v\rangle$ over
suitable families of states.

The \emph{HS-sewing condition}: if the multiplication operators
$m^c_{a,b}$ are Hilbert--Schmidt in the sense that
\[
\sum_{q^{a+b+c}}\|m^c_{a,b}\|^2_{\mathrm{HS}}<\infty,
\]
then the closed amplitudes are trace-class and the sewing operation
converges absolutely.

The analytic bar coalgebra $B^{\mathrm{an}}(\cA)$ is the
graph-norm closure of the algebraic bar coalgebra.  An
\emph{analytic Koszul pair} is a pair $(\cA,\cA^!)$ such that the
analytic bar-cobar adjunction restricts to a Quillen equivalence on
the sewing-complete categories.

Four target conjectures:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item \emph{Heisenberg sewing}: the sewing envelope of
  $\cH_k$ is the ind-Hilbert completion
  $\operatorname{Sym}A_2(D)\cong\operatorname{ind-Hilb}(\cH_k)$
  (Moriwaki).
\item \emph{Lattice sewing}: the sewing envelope of $V_\Lambda$
  is the vertex-algebraic completion by lattice theta functions.
\item \emph{Analytic realisation}: a criterion for when the
  algebraic Koszul pair $(\cA,\cA^!)$ extends to an analytic one.
\item \emph{Boundary bar duality}: the analytic bar of the
  boundary chiral algebra equals the topological bar of the bulk
  factorization algebra.
\end{enumerate}
