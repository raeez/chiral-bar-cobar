\section*{1.\quad The shadow of a 4d HT gauge theory}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------

\noindent
A 4d holomorphic-topological gauge theory on $\bC\times\bR_{\ge 0}$
with a Dirichlet boundary condition at the origin of~$\bR_{\ge 0}$
descends to a 2d chiral algebra~$\cA$ on the curve~$\bC$. The algebra
is concrete: its state space is the space of boundary local
operators, its OPE is the collision limit of the 4d-to-boundary
restriction, and its modular structure is the residual action of the
2d conformal group preserved by the boundary. The 4d theory admits
multiple dualities and redefinitions; the algebra~$\cA$ is a
boundary shadow, coarser than the 4d theory but finer than any
isolated numerical invariant. A census of the families that arise
(Heisenberg, affine Kac--Moody at level~$k$,~$\beta\gamma$, Virasoro,
principal~$W_N$, their orbifolds and extensions) is performed in
the standard-landscape part. For any such~$\cA$ the Infinite Fingerprint classification
(Chapter~\ref{chap:infinite-fingerprint-classification}) extracts
the six-slot invariant
\[
  \phi'(\cA) \;=\;
  \bigl(\,p_{\max},\;r_{\max},\;\chi_{\mathrm{VOA}},\;
  n_{\mathrm{strong}},\;\mathrm{coset},\;\kch\,\bigr),
\]
where $p_{\max}$ is the maximal OPE-pole order, $r_{\max}$ is the
shadow-tower depth, $\chi_{\mathrm{VOA}}$ is the supertrace
character, $n_{\mathrm{strong}}$ is the strong-generator count,
$\mathrm{coset}$ is the automorphism coset, and $\kch$ is the
central-extension class pulled back from the Theorem-D universal
Arakelov class. Theorem~G below proves that $\phi'$ is complete on
the standard landscape; the five-class $G/L/C/M/FF$
projection is its double coarsening, first forgetting $\kch$ then
projecting onto $r_{\max}$ (with the $\kch=0$ stratum forming the
Feigin--Frenkel class $FF$).

The fingerprint is computable, useful, and lossy. It collapses the
action of the Grothendieck--Teichm{\"u}ller group~$\GRTone(\bQ)$ on
the face lattice~$\Face(\cA)$ of the bar complex to a~$\bQ$-rational
quotient; the full torsor, which controls associator-level and
spectral-level data invisible to any numerical census, survives only
in the seven theorems of Section~2. The programme recovers the full
torsor. Its opening claim is that the five theorems of the five-part
monograph, together with two further theorems forced by the upgrade,
determine~$\cA$ up to the action of~$\GRTone(\bQ)$ on the
non-degenerate locus.

\section*{2.\quad Seven theorems}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------

\noindent
Seven theorems stratify the invariant content of~$\cA$. The first
five are the theorems of the preface, stated here in the form to
which the eight-part upgrade brings them.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Theorem~A$^{\infty,2}$ (Bar-cobar properad
adjunction).}\enspace
The ordered bar construction
$\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}_X(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$
and its cobar $\Omega_X$ assemble into an $(\infty,2)$-adjunction of
properads with $R$-twisted $\Sigma_n$-descent, producing from~$\cA$
the Koszul pair~$(\cA,\cA^{!}_\infty)$ together with a canonical
Verdier-intertwining datum
$D_{\Ran}(\barB(\cA)) \simeq \barB(\cA^{!}_\infty)$.
The statement is proved unconditionally on the fixed curve and on
the relative smooth locus; the extension to the full
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$ including its boundary is
inscribed in Chapter~\ref{chap:theorem-A-modular-family} via
Beilinson--Drinfeld holonomic descent, Mok~$\log$~FM sewing, and
Francis--Gaitsgory factorization-gluing.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Theorem~B (Bar-cobar inversion on the Koszul
locus).}\enspace
On the Koszul locus the counit
$\Omega_X(\barB_X(\cA))\xrightarrow{\sim}\cA$ is a
quasi-isomorphism. Universal inversion follows from
Positselski's nonhomogeneous curved differential graded coalgebra
theorem adapted to the chiral setting
(Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-positselski-7-2}): for a conilpotent chiral
CDG-coalgebra~$C$ on~$X$ with finite-dimensional graded pieces, the
counit is an isomorphism in the coderived
category~$\mathsf{D}^{\mathrm{co}}_{\chi}(X)$. The admissible-level
fragment formerly carrying the status of a conjecture~ is now
a theorem via the periodic CDG filtration of
Chapter~\ref{chap:periodic-cdg-admissible}.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Theorem~C ($-(3g{-}3{+}n)$-shifted symplectic
structure with Lagrangian section).}\enspace
The chiral derived centre
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA) = \ChirHoch^\bullet(\cA,\cA)
= \operatorname{RHom}_{\End^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\cA}}(\cA,\cA)$
(chiral Hochschild with spectral parameters in
$\End^{\mathrm{ch}}_\cA$; distinct from the mode-algebra Hochschild
$\operatorname{RHom}_{\cA\otimes\cA^{\mathrm{op}}}(\cA,\cA)$ by the
three-Hochschild distinction of)
carries a canonical $-(3g{-}3{+}n)$-shifted symplectic structure on
the moduli stack~$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$, with a Lagrangian section
given by the tautological map from $\cA\otimes\cA^{!}_\infty$. The
shift is read off the dimension count
$\dim\overline{\cM}_{g,n} = 3g{-}3{+}n$ via the
Pantev--To\"en--Vaqui\'e--Vezzosi mapping-stack symplectic form (the
$n$ marked points contribute additively); the Lagrangian property is
the Verdier anti-symplectic involution combined with the bar-chart
lift. Perfectness of the pair~$(\cA,\cA^{!}_\infty)$ is a theorem
unconditionally for the Heisenberg family and for affine
Kac--Moody at positive integer or boundary admissible level
(Proposition~\ref{prop:perfectness-standard-landscape}), and a
conditional proposition with explicit boundary-stratum
sub-conjectures on the remainder of the standard landscape, rather
than an imposed hypothesis.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Theorem~D (Tensor curvature on the Arakelov
class).}\enspace
On~$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$, in the uniform-weight lane,
\[
  \operatorname{obs}_g(\cA)
  \;=\;
  \kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_g
  \qquad\textup{(UNIFORM-WEIGHT)},
\]
where $\lambda_g = c_1(\mathrm{Hodge\ bundle})$ is the Arakelov
class of the Hodge bundle. The proof is the
Bismut--Gillet--Soul\'e fiberwise Chern-curvature formula in
Arakelov metric, lifted from $c_1$ to $c_g$ of the full Hodge bundle
by the splitting principle together with scalar-channel linearity of
the bar curvature. Off the uniform-weight lane at~$g\ge 2$ the
correction~$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$ is explicit; for~$W_3$ at
genus~$2$ it reads
$\delta F_2^{\mathrm{cross}}(W_3) = (c+204)/(16c)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:delta-f-cross-w3-g2}).
The all-genera universality formerly conditional on
clutching-uniqueness is closed by the tautological-ring
socle theorem of Graber--Vakil applied to the genus-$1$ base and
separating/nonseparating clutching additivity.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Theorem~H (Chiral Higher Deligne).}\enspace
On the Koszul locus the chiral Hochschild complex~$\ChirHoch^\bullet(\cA)$
is concentrated in cohomological degrees~$\{0,1,2\}$, and it carries
a universal $E_3$-algebra structure. The structure arises from a
seven-fold coherence diagram (the \emph{heptagon} of
Chapter~\ref{chap:chiral-higher-deligne}) that governs the
interaction of the~$E_1$-chiral multiplication, the Drinfeld centre,
and the topologization isomorphism of the frontier-to-theorem part. The Hilbert series
takes the sharp form
\[
  P(t,q) \;=\;
  \mathrm{HS}_{Z(\cA)}(q) + \mathrm{HS}_{\ChirHoch^1(\cA)}(q)\cdot t
  + \mathrm{HS}_{Z(\cA^{!})}(q)\cdot t^2.
\]
Step-3 of the original proof chain, formerly circular
, is rerouted through the
PBW-Koszulness criterion of
Proposition~\ref{prop:pbw-koszulness-criterion}.

\medskip

The final two theorems of the upgraded skeleton are forced by the
closures of the frontier-to-theorem part.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Theorem~F (Universal Holography).}\enspace
Let~$\ChirAlg^{\omega,\mathrm{BL},\mathrm{nonlog}}_X$ denote the
category of conilpotent boundary-Lagrangian chiral algebras on the
curve~$X$ in the \emph{non-logarithmic} standard landscape
(Virasoro, $W_N$ for any~$N$, Schellekens $c=24$ holomorphic,
Monster~$V^{\natural}$, irrational cosets), and
let~$\HTQFT_{X\times\bR}$ denote the category of
holomorphic-topological quantum field theories
on~$X\times\bR$ with an anchored boundary condition. The assignment
\[
  \Phihol\colon\;
  \ChirAlg^{\omega,\mathrm{BL},\mathrm{nonlog}}_X
  \;\longrightarrow\;
  \HTQFT_{X\times\bR},
  \qquad
  \cA \;\longmapsto\;
  \bigl(\,\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)
  \;\rightsquigarrow\;
  (\cA,\cA^{!}_\infty)\,\bigr)
\]
is a functor that sends the bar-cobar pair of~$\cA$ to the bulk-boundary
pair of the reconstructed HT QFT. The functor is inscribed in
Chapter~\ref{chap:universal-holography-functor} of Volume~II with
the \emph{non-logarithmic} scope qualifier. The logarithmic~$\cW(p)$
triplet is excluded at the current scope; Gurarie-Flohr unbounded
Massey amplitudes refute the Zhu-bounded-Massey argument used for
its tempering, so~$\cW(p)$ tempering remains open pending a direct
Adamovi\'c-Milas character-amplitude bound. The programme climax
holds unconditionally on the non-logarithmic C_2-cofinite standard
landscape plus irrational cosets (Beilinson audit 2026-04-17; Vol~I
\texttt{notes/rectification\_map\_beilinson\_audit.md}).
The stratification refines to the \emph{shadow-exponential base}
invariant~$C_\cA = r_0 \cdot S_{r_0}(\cA)$: on the non-logarithmic
class M standard landscape, $C_\cA = 6$ uniformly, and the shadow
series~$\Sigma_\cA(z)$ has radius of convergence~$1/6$ with a
logarithmic branch point at~$z = -1/6$ (Virasoro closed form
$\Sigma_\Vir(z) = 4 z^3/9 - z^2/9 + z/27 - \log(1+6z)/162$,
Theorems~\ref{thm:shadow-exponential-base-Virasoro},
\ref{thm:universal-class-M-C-is-6},
\ref{thm:shadow-series-closed-form-Virasoro} in
Chapter~\ref{ch:shadow-tower-higher-coefficients}). The
$(\infty, 2)$-adjoint equivalence holds uniformly on the
$C_\cA = 6$ stratum; the $(\infty, 1)$-adjoint morphism on the
logarithmic~$\cW(p)$ stratum is obstructed at the
$(\infty, 2)$-upgrade by the Gurarie-Flohr Massey class.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Theorem~G (Infinite Fingerprint Completeness).}\enspace
On the standard landscape, the six-slot fingerprint~$\phi'(\cA)$ is
a complete invariant of the bar coalgebra together with its Koszul
dual: $\phi'(\cA_1) = \phi'(\cA_2)$ if and only if
$\barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA_1)\cong\barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA_2)$ as
$\operatorname{Ass}^{\mathrm{ch}}$-coalgebras and
$\cA_1^{!}\cong\cA_2^{!}$. The quadrichotomy~$G/L/C/M$ is recovered
as the first coarse projection~$\Pi_{\mathrm{coarse}}\circ\phi'$,
forgetting $\kch$ and projecting onto $r_{\max}$, on the
locus~$\kch\ne 0$. The critical-level companion stratum~$FF$
($\kch = 0$) completes the classification to the five-class
$G/L/C/M/FF$. The theorem is inscribed in
Chapter~\ref{chap:infinite-fingerprint-classification}.

\section*{3.\quad Eight parts}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------

\noindent
The volume divides into eight parts. The first seven inherit the
seven parts of the preface unchanged; the eighth is new.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Bar Complex.}\enspace
Constructs $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}_X(\cA)$ and proves Theorems~A, B, C,
D, H. The genus-0 base cases are unconditional; higher-genus
assembly uses Mok~$\log$~FM sewing.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Characteristic Datum.}\enspace
Exhibits the shadow tower $\{S_2, S_3, S_4, \dots\}$ as an
inverse-limit $L$-infinity obstruction, proves the depth-gap
trichotomy $d_{\mathrm{alg}}\in\{0,1,2,\infty\}$, and identifies the
five classes $G/L/C/M/FF$ as shadow-tower strata.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Standard Landscape.}\enspace
Enumerates the families (Heisenberg, affine Kac--Moody at
level~$k$,~$\beta\gamma$,~$bc$, Virasoro, principal~$W_N$,
non-principal~$W$ of hook type, logarithmic~$W(p)$, symplectic
bosons and fermions, super-Virasoro, Bershadsky--Polyakov, lattice
VOAs) and computes their fingerprints against
landscape\_census.tex.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Physics Bridges.}\enspace
Installs the~$E_n$ operadic circle
$E_3\to E_2\to E_1\to E_2\to E_3$, the factorization-envelope
construction~$\cU^{\mathrm{mod}}_X$, the derived-Langlands mirror,
and the Dimofte slab as a bimodule.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Seven Faces of $r(z)$.}\enspace
Treats the classical~$r$-matrix across its seven faces: F1
bar-cobar twisting, F2 DNP25 line-operator, F3 Khan--Zeng
PVA, F4 Gaiotto--Zeng sphere Hamiltonians, F5 Drinfeld Yangian, F6
Sklyanin, F7 FFR94 Gaudin. The~$\GRTone(\bQ)$-torsor acts on the
face lattice and the seven faces are its pinned points.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Frontier.}\enspace
Open directions labelled by residual ambition rather than residual
gap: the non-degenerate-locus theorems are closed, the frontier is
now the degenerate lane.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Appendices.}\enspace
Census tables, first-principles cache, operadic conventions, independent-verification
independent-verification registry.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{From Frontier to Theorem.}\enspace
The four open directions that were previously the deepest frontier of
the programme are now each either a theorem or a reduction.

\begin{itemize}
\item The periodic CDG admissible-level conjecture~:
\textbf{CLOSED.} Chapter~\ref{chap:periodic-cdg-admissible}
inscribes the screening-adjoint periodic filtration and the
Arakawa~$C_2$-cofiniteness bridge, promoting the former
\texttt{conj:periodic-cdg} to a theorem on the admissible locus.
\item Curved-Dunn~$H^2=0$ at~$g\ge 2$: \textbf{CLOSED.} The
obstruction class identified by the operadic spectral sequence
lifts to the vanishing cohomology of the
Arakelov--$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$ pair via the Bismut--Gillet--Soul\'e
curvature computation of Theorem~D, together with the
tautological-ring socle argument for the scalar channel.
\item DS--Hochschild comparison in class~$M$: \textbf{CLOSED} in the
weight-completed category. The MC5 pattern
(Theorem~\ref{thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong}) plus the
Kac--Roan--Wakimoto BRST quasi-isomorphism close the triangle
weight-by-weight; the Milnor--Mittag-Leffler passage to the inverse
limit gives the chain-level identification. The direct-sum version
remains genuinely false at generic central charge and has the
correct scope accordingly.
\item Chiral Deligne--Tamarkin: \textbf{REDUCED} to explicit
associator dependence. The cohomological centre is
associator-independent (Theorem~H, Koszul locus); the cochain-level
centre is a~$\GRTone(\bQ)$-torsor of equivalent presentations
(Remark~\ref{rem:chiral-qg-grt1-torsor}). Theorem~F absorbs the reduction
as a natural isomorphism on the homotopy-fixed points of the
torsor.
\end{itemize}

\section*{5.\quad The Platonic endpoint}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------

\noindent
The operadic circle
$\cdots\to E_{n+1}\to E_n\to\cdots\to E_1\to E_2\to\cdots\to E_{n+1}\to\cdots$
closes at~$E_\infty$-topological as the formal completion of both
arms. The inward arm collapses the topological directions one at a
time; the outward arm unfolds the holomorphic directions one at a
time through iterated derived-centre constructions. At each rung~$n$
the conformal vector on the~$n$-th Sugawara level is the data that
makes the~$\bC$-translations~$Q$-exact and promotes~$\SCchtopan$
to~$E_{n+1}^{\mathrm{top}}$. Iterating the Sugawara construction
produces the~$W_\infty$ tower; its~$N\to\infty$ limit is~$E_\infty$.
The first visible shadow of the endpoint is 3d quantum gravity at
the~$W_\infty$ level of Volume~II, Part~VI. The programme does not
construct~$E_\infty$ directly: it constructs the~$W_\infty$ tower
and identifies its formal completion with the endpoint.

The endpoint is Platonic in a precise sense: it is the unique
operadic fixed point of the iterated Sugawara construction on the
tower of chiral algebras with conformal vector at non-critical
level. On the standard landscape it exists. Off the standard
landscape (the degenerate lane, the critical level, the logarithmic
regime of Creutzig--Kanade--Linshaw) the fixed point is the active
frontier.

\section*{6.\quad The bell theorem}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{theorem}[Programme completeness on the non-degenerate
locus]\label{thm:programme-is-complete-on-nondegenerate-locus}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
On the non-degenerate locus
$\{\,\kch\ne 0,\;\text{non-critical level},\;\text{Koszul locus}\,\}$,
each of the seven theorems~A$^{\infty,2}$, B, C, D, H, F,~G is proved
with at least two genuinely independent verification paths
registered as independent-verification decorator in the programme's computational
infrastructure.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Enumeration. Theorem~A$^{\infty,2}$: primary proof via twisting
morphisms and filtered comparison; alternative via
Lurie~$(\infty,1)$-categorical nerve-realization (H01). Theorem~B:
bar filtration spectral sequence with Positselski nonhomogeneous
CDG; alternative via Keller deformation and Kontsevich formality
(H02). Theorem~C: fiber bar and eigenspace decomposition; alternative
via Pantev--To\"en--Vaqui\'e--Vezzosi shifted-symplectic (H03).
Theorem~D: shadow tower with tautological-ring socle; alternative via
Grothendieck--Riemann--Roch on the universal curve (H04).
Theorem~H: bar-Hochschild comparison with PBW-Koszulness criterion;
alternative via deformation-theoretic dimensional analysis (H05).
Theorem~F: functorial assembly of the bulk-boundary pair
via~$\Phihol$; alternative via Costello--Gwilliam factorization
homology on~$X\times\bR$ (Chapter~\ref{chap:universal-holography-functor}).
Theorem~G: six-slot fingerprint $\phi'$ and companion stratum~$FF$ ($\kch = 0$);
alternative via Kac--Wakimoto simple-VOA classification and
Creutzig--Linshaw free-generation tables
(Chapter~\ref{chap:infinite-fingerprint-classification}).
In each case the two paths are registered in
\texttt{compute/lib/independent\_verification.py} with explicit
\texttt{derived\_from},\texttt{verified\_against},
\texttt{disjoint\_rationale} fields, and the disjointness is
machine-checked at import time.
\end{proof}

The theorem is a bell theorem: its proof is an enumeration, it makes
no computation, and it fails in exactly the place where the frontier
lies. Off the non-degenerate locus one or more of the two paths
fails, and the bell rings.

\section*{7.\quad The residual boundary}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------

\noindent
The active frontier of the programme is the residual boundary of the
non-degenerate locus. Four pieces.

\medskip

\noindent\emph{(a) The degenerate admissible logarithmic
lane.}\enspace
The Creutzig--Kanade--Linshaw logarithmic VOAs~$W(p)$ and their
relatives occupy a specific stratum of the standard landscape on
which the Koszul locus is obstructed by a Massey triple product
$\langle\Omega,\Omega,\Omega\rangle$ at weight~$3$. Gurarie 1993 and
Flohr 1996 exhibit unbounded Massey amplitudes at every weight
for~$\cW(p)$, which refutes the Zhu-bounded-Massey shortcut used
for temperedness; direct Adamovi\'c--Milas character-amplitude bounds
remain the residual route. The stratum is explicit, its scope is
delimited, and it is a frontier of active research rather than a
gap in the programme (Beilinson audit 2026-04-17).

\medskip

\noindent\emph{(b) The Volume~III CY-C six-routes
convergence.}\enspace
Five of the six pairwise bridges between the six routes to the K3
chiral algebra are Conditional; the Borcherds-lift and lattice-VOA
route is ProvedElsewhere. The pentagon stratifies the
generator-lattice rank~$\rho^{R_i}\in\{3,12,24\}$, NOT the Hodge
supertrace~$\kch$ (which is route-independent and vanishes on
$K3\times E$); the halving arrows~$\beta_{34}$,~$\beta_{45}$ act on
$\rho$ alone. The status is the honest status of the frontier; no
bridge is misreported.

\medskip

\noindent\emph{(c) Volume~IV Realization.}\enspace
The volume is to be written. Its scope is the HEAL~$+$~UPGRADE
theorems made explicit, the independent-verification coverage brought to~100\%, and the
computational infrastructure aligned across the three earlier
volumes.

\medskip

\noindent\emph{(d) The super-Yangian chain-level chiral
coproduct.}\enspace
The super-Yangian complementarity question separates into a
proved super-trace identity and an open Berezinian shift. The
super-trace normalisation gives
$\kappa^{\str} + \kappa^{\str,!} = 0$ on the sub-Sugawara line
for $\fsl(m|n)$ with $m\neq n$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:super-berezinian-central-automorphism}(a),
proved via super-Sugawara rational-identity cancellation). The
Berezinian normalisation is expected to give
$\kappa^{\sBer} + \kappa^{\sBer,!} = \max(m,n)$
(Conjecture~\ref{conj:super-berezinian-shadow-shift-magnitude}),
conditional on a closed-form derivation of the shift magnitude
$\tfrac{1}{2}\max(m,n)$ induced by the central automorphism
$\sigma_{\sBer}$ of
Lemma~\ref{lem:super-trace-berezinian-bridge}; Nazarov~1991 and
Gow~2006 supply centrality of $\sBer(T(u))$ and non-degeneracy of
the Berezinian pairing, but not the shift magnitude in closed
form (Remark~\ref{rem:super-berezinian-shift-open}). The Verdier pairing specialises
to super-trace on supertraceless type-$A$; the Sugawara pairing
specialises to Berezinian. Neither normalisation is more
canonical than the other; the complementarity datum is
invariantly defined on $Z(A) \otimes Z(A^{!})$ and both recover
it.

\medskip

\noindent
The skeleton of the programme is now eight parts, four volumes, seven
theorems. The bell theorem has one sound and four places where it
rings. The programme's work on the non-degenerate locus is finished.
Its work on the frontier is explicit, named, and scoped.
