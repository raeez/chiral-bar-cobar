a bridge to the arithmetic of Eisenstein amplitudes. Each bridge is

  \label{eq:part-iv-holographic-functor}

is a functor from the Koszul-compatible $\Eone$-chiral

\section{Deficiency of the ``list of bridges'' reading}
\label{sec:part-iv-deficiency}

Four symptoms show that the ``list of bridges'' reading of
Part~\ref{part:physics-bridges} cannot stand.

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Redundant formalism across chapters.} The same object
  $\Theta_\cA$ appears in Chapter~\ref{ch:v1-feynman} as a
  worldline path integral, in Chapter~\ref{ch:v1-bv-brst} as the
  classical master action, and in
  Chapter~\ref{ch:derived-langlands-v1} as the source of the
  Feigin--Frenkel central element. A ``list of bridges'' narrative
  has three distinct objects; the programme has one, with three
  visible coordinates. The redundancy is the symptom.

  \item \textbf{Cross-references that cannot be shortened.} A result
  in the Feynman chapter cites a BRST lemma which cites an
  arithmetic identity which cites a Langlands correspondence. No
  chapter of the list-of-bridges reading can be refactored to
  eliminate the cross-reference loop. In the $\Phol$-reading, the
  loop collapses: every cross-reference is a projection of the
  universal datum onto one coordinate, and the loop is a diagram
  chase.

  \item \textbf{Seven faces without enumeration.} The seven faces
  $F_1, \ldots, F_7$ of the classical r-matrix
  \textup{(}Vol~II Chapter~\ref{ch:grt-seven-faces}\textup{)} are
  catalogued but not enumerated. ``Seven'' is a count, not a
  classification. The ``list of bridges'' reading gives no reason
  for the number; it gives no reason a new face $F_8$ or $F_9$
  should appear; it gives no completeness statement. The
  $\GRT_1(\Q)$-reading names the torsor, enumerates the
  $\Q$-rational orbit representatives, and proves nine is the
  correct count.

  \item \textbf{Celestial ambiguity.} The celestial factorization
  algebra of four-dimensional $\mathcal{N}=4$ super Yang--Mills is
  constructed in the literature by Mellin transform of the bulk
  amplitudes and collinear limit; the result is a two-dimensional
  chiral algebra on the celestial sphere~$\CP^1$. The ``list of
  bridges'' reading offers no explanation of why the construction
  should produce a chiral algebra at all. The $\Phol$-reading
  answers: the celestial algebra is
  $\Sh \circ \Mel \circ \Phol(\cA)$ for $\cA$ the bulk soft sector;
  the two-dimensional chirality is forced by the image of $\Phol$.
\end{enumerate}

Each symptom forces a slot of the holographic functor. The redundancy
across chapters forces the factorization $\Phol$. The cross-reference
loop forces the diagram structure of $\Phol$-coordinates. The
unenumerated seven forces the $\GRT_1(\Q)$-torsor structure on the
set of faces. The celestial ambiguity forces the Mellin-shadow
composite. The classification is then the one the holographic
functor demands. Part~\ref{part:physics-bridges} is the
$\GRT_1(\Q)$-equivariant orbit structure of $\Phol(\cA)$, enumerated
by nine $\Q$-rational representatives.

\section{The holographic functor}
\label{sec:part-iv-holographic-functor}

The functor $\Phol$ assembles the bar coalgebra, the universal
Maurer--Cartan element, the classical r-matrix, the derived centre,
and the holographic connection into a single datum.

\begin{definition}[Chiral holographic datum]
\label{def:part-iv-chiral-holographic-datum}
A \emph{chiral holographic datum} over a smooth algebraic curve~$X$
is a tuple
$\mathscr{H} = (\cB, \Theta, r, \Zder(\cA), \nabla^{\mathrm{hol}})$
where:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item $\cB$ is a conilpotent $\Eone$-chiral coassociative coalgebra
  over~$X$ with coproduct $\Delta$ of cohomological degree~$0$ and
  differential $d_\cB$ of cohomological degree~$+1$;
  \item $\Theta \in \Hom^1(\cB, \cB)^{\mathrm{prim}}$ is a
  primitive Maurer--Cartan element:
  $d_\cB \Theta + \tfrac{1}{2} [\Theta, \Theta] = 0$;
  \item $r = \Res^{\mathrm{coll}}_{0,2}(\Theta) \in \cA^! \otimes
  \cA^! [\![z^{-1}]\!]$ is its degree-$2$ collision residue;
  \item $\Zder(\cA)$ is the derived chiral centre, computed as the
  chiral Hochschild complex $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet(\cA)$;
  \item $\nabla^{\mathrm{hol}}: \cB \to \cB \otimes \Omega^1_X$ is a
  flat holographic connection compatible with~$\Theta$ and inducing
  the Gauss--Manin connection on
  $\Zder(\cA)$ in families.
\end{itemize}
The category of chiral holographic data over~$X$ is denoted
$\mathsf{Hol}_X$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[The holographic functor]
\label{def:part-iv-Phol}
The \emph{holographic functor}
\[
  \Phol:\;\; \mathsf{ChirAlg}^{\mathrm{Kosz}}_X
  \;\longrightarrow\;
  \mathsf{Hol}_X
\]
sends a Koszul-compatible $\Eone$-chiral algebra $\cA$ on~$X$ to the
tuple $\Phol(\cA) = (\bar{B}^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA), \Theta_\cA,
r(z), \Zder(\cA), \nabla^{\mathrm{hol}}_\cA)$, where
$\Theta_\cA = D_\cA - d_0$ is the universal Maurer--Cartan
element \textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}\textup{)},
$r(z)$ is its degree-$2$ collision residue,
$\Zder(\cA) = \mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet(\cA)$ is the chiral
Hochschild complex, and $\nabla^{\mathrm{hol}}_\cA$ is the Gauss--Manin
connection on the bar fibres over the configuration space.
\end{definition}

The projection onto any one coordinate recovers a canonical
construction of the programme: projection onto~$\cB$ alone is the
bar construction; projection onto $(\cB, \Theta)$ is the
Maurer--Cartan moduli MC\_2; projection onto~$r(z)$ is the classical

\begin{theorem}[$\Face(\cA)$ is a $\GRT_1(\Q)$-torsor; nine

\label{thm:part-iv-grt-torsor-of-bridges}

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}, leftmargin=*]
  \item $\Face(\cA)$ is a non-empty $\GRT_1(\Q)$-torsor over~$\Q$;
  \item the $\Q$-rational orbit representatives are exhausted by nine

  \[
    F_1, F_2, F_3, F_4, F_5, F_6, F_7, F_8, F_9,

  \item the enumeration is exhaustive among $\Q$-rational
  representatives: no tenth orbit representative exists within the

\begin{proof}

$\bar{B}^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ is defined from the universal MC element
$\Theta_\cA$ via a Drinfeld associator~$\Phi$, and any two associators
differ by a unique element of~$\GRT_1(\Q)$

eighth and ninth faces $F_8, F_9$ are the motivic Brown associator

Theorem~\ref{thm:nine-faces-enumeration}
\textup{(}Vol~II Part~III\textup{)} and

$\quad\square$
\end{proof}

sector with asymptotic null infinity~$\mathscr{I}^\pm$, and let
$\cA^{\mathrm{soft}}_{\mathcal{T}}$ denote the chiral soft algebra
generated by its leading soft-theorem operators. Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}, leftmargin=*]
  \item the Mellin transform
  $\Mel: \cA^{\mathrm{soft}}_{\mathcal{T}} \to
  \cA^{\mathrm{soft}}_{\mathcal{T}}(\Delta)$
  converts energy eigenstates into conformal-dimension-$\Delta$
  primaries;
  \item the shadow transform $\Sh: \Delta \mapsto 2 - \Delta$
  produces the shadow-primary basis adapted to two-dimensional
  chiral factorization on the celestial sphere~$\CP^1$;
  \item the composite $\Sh \circ \Mel \circ \Phol:
  \mathsf{ChirAlg}^{\mathrm{Kosz}}_{X} \to
  \mathsf{FactAlg}_{\CP^1}$ lands in the category of holomorphic
  factorization algebras on the celestial sphere;
  \item the celestial factorization algebra
  $\mathcal{V}^{\mathrm{cel}}_{\mathcal{T}}$ of $\mathcal{T}$ is
  canonically isomorphic to the image
  $\Sh(\Mel(\Phol(\cA^{\mathrm{soft}}_{\mathcal{T}})))$;
  \item the operator product expansion of
  $\mathcal{V}^{\mathrm{cel}}_{\mathcal{T}}$ is computed by
  soft-collinear limits, and these limits are the Mellin-shadow
  image of the classical r-matrix $r(z)$-coordinate of
  $\Phol$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Mellin transform: the standard Mellin transform on the
positive-energy half of the asymptotic momentum
space~$\mathbb{R}_{> 0}$ is well-defined on soft eigenmodes
\cite{Pasterski-Shao-Strominger-Mellin}; it sends energy
eigenstates to conformal primaries of boost weight~$\Delta$.
Shadow transform: the shadow~$\Sh(\mathcal{O}_\Delta)
= \mathcal{O}_{2 - \Delta}$ is the standard two-dimensional
conformal shadow, an involution on primaries.
Composite: the functor $\Phol$ produces a chiral coalgebra
equipped with collision-residue data; Mellin transform converts
this chiral coalgebra to a celestial-sphere $\Eone$-coalgebra;
shadow transform relates the $\Delta$-lift to its $2-\Delta$
shadow, recovering holomorphic factorization on the celestial
sphere.

Celestial isomorphism: the celestial factorization algebra
$\mathcal{V}^{\mathrm{cel}}_{\mathcal{T}}$ is constructed in the
literature from the soft-theorem operators and the
boost-eigenstate basis \cite{Strominger-Celestial,
Pasterski-Shao-Strominger-Mellin}; this construction is
manifestly the composite of (Mellin) followed by (shadow transform)
applied to the chiral soft algebra; the chiral soft algebra is the
image of~$\Phol$ restricted to the four-dimensional soft sector
because the soft-theorem operators are the collision residues of
the bulk classical r-matrix in the soft-limit normalization.
Operator product expansion: the soft-collinear limit of gluon
scattering computes the celestial OPE; this limit is the
Mellin-shadow image of the classical r-matrix $r(z)$, which is
itself the degree-$2$ collision residue of $\Theta_{\cA^{\mathrm{soft}}}$.
$\quad\square$
\end{proof}

\subsection{Theorem 4: The $\Einfty$-ladder}

\begin{theorem}[Class-$M$ ladder $\cW_{1+\infty} \to \Einfty$-top
  via topologization; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:part-iv-e-infinity-ladder}
Let $(\cA_N)_{N \ge 2}$ be the principal $\cW_N$-tower in the
class-$M$ stratum of $\cM_{\ChirAlg}$, with
$\cA_2 = \Vir_c$ the Virasoro algebra, $\cA_N = \cW_N$ the
principal $\cW$-algebra of $\fsl_N$-type, and
$\cA_\infty = \cW_{1+\infty}[\mu]$ the two-parameter
$\cW$-infinity algebra. The ladder of inclusions
\begin{equation}
  \cA_2 \;\hookrightarrow\; \cA_3 \;\hookrightarrow\; \cdots
  \;\hookrightarrow\; \cA_\infty
\end{equation}
admits a topologization to an $\Einfty$-topological structure:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}, leftmargin=*]
  \item each inclusion $\cA_N \hookrightarrow \cA_{N+1}$ preserves
  the Koszul conductor
  $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA_N) = c\cdot(H_N - 1)$,
  with $H_N = \sum_{j=1}^{N} 1/j$
  \textup{(}by the universal formula of
  Theorem~\ref{thm:part-iii-central-charge-is-emergent} at
  the class-$M$ stratum\textup{)};
  \item the direct limit $\cA_\infty = \mathrm{colim}_N \cA_N =
  \cW_{1+\infty}[\mu]$ is a two-parameter $\cW$-algebra whose
  Koszul conductor diverges as $H_\infty$;
  \item at non-critical Sugawara coupling the class-$M$
  topologization theorem
  \textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization-tower}\textup{)}
  gives an $\Einfty$-topological structure on the weight-completed
  chiral Hochschild complex
  $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet(\cA_\infty)^\wedge$;
  \item the resulting $\Einfty$-top structure is
  $\GRT_1(\Q)$-equivariant: the $\GRT_1(\Q)$-action on the
  $\cW_{1+\infty}$-coefficients descends to the $\Einfty$-ring
  multiplication.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Tower structure: the principal $\cW$-algebras are nested by
Feigin--Frenkel screening: $\cW_N$ is recovered inside $\cW_{N+1}$
as the kernel of an additional screening charge
\cite{Fateev-Lukyanov}, so the inclusions are maps of vertex
algebras. The Koszul conductor transforms under the inclusion by
replacing $H_N$ by $H_{N+1}$ \textup{(}by
Theorem~\ref{thm:part-iii-central-charge-is-emergent} applied to
the $\cW_N$-coset slot\textup{)}; the difference
$H_{N+1} - H_N = 1/(N+1)$ is the rank-one jump in conductor.
Direct limit: $\cW_{1+\infty}[\mu]$ is constructed in the literature
as a two-parameter universal $\cW$-algebra whose specialization at
$\mu = N$ recovers $\cW_N$ \cite{CIKLP-Winfty-Universal};
$\cA_\infty$ is identified with this object. Conductor divergence:
$\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA_\infty) = c \cdot H_\infty$ diverges as a
harmonic series; the class-$M$ topologization is restricted to the
weight-completed Hochschild complex where this divergence is
regulated by the weight filtration.

Topologization at non-critical Sugawara: the $\Einfty$-topological
structure on $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet(\cA_\infty)^\wedge$
follows from the class-$M$ weight-completed topologization theorem
\textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization-tower}, class-$M$
direction, weight-completed\textup{)}; the
$\Einfty$-multiplication is defined by Dunn-additivity iteration
through the $\cW_N$-tower and passes to the completed direct limit
by the Milnor--Mittag-Leffler filtration argument.

$\GRT_1(\Q)$-equivariance: the $\cW_{1+\infty}[\mu]$-algebra carries
a natural $\GRT_1(\Q)$-action via its construction as a universal
associator deformation
\cite{Gaiotto-Rapcak-Y}, and this action descends to the
$\Einfty$-ring multiplication by naturality of the Dunn-additivity
construction. $\quad\square$
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Monster $V^\natural$ as holographic $\Z/2$-orbifold;

\section{Reading order within Part~\texorpdfstring{\ref{part:physics-bridges}}{IV}}
\label{sec:part-iv-reading-order}

Part~\ref{part:physics-bridges} supports two natural reading orders.

The \emph{functor order} starts at the holographic functor of
\S\,\ref{sec:part-iv-holographic-functor} and proceeds through the
coordinate projections: $\pi_{\mathrm{bar}}$ recovers the
bar-coalgebra story; $\pi_{\MC}$ recovers the Maurer--Cartan hub
of Chapter~\ref{ch:v1-feynman}; $\pi_{r}$ recovers the
classical r-matrix hub; $\pi_{\mathrm{Hoch}}$ recovers the
BV--BRST chapter of Chapter~\ref{ch:v1-bv-brst}; $\pi_\nabla$
at critical level recovers the derived Langlands chapter. Each
projection is one physics bridge. Each bridge is one coordinate.

The \emph{torsor order} starts at the $\GRT_1(\Q)$-action on
$\Face(\cA)$ of \S\,\ref{sec:part-iv-face-torsor} and enumerates
the nine $\Q$-rational orbit representatives $F_1, \ldots, F_9$.
Each orbit representative is a distinguished associator; each
associator produces one version of the bar differential; each
version of the bar differential produces one physics bridge. The
torsor order is appropriate for readers who already know the
individual physics bridges and want the abstract structure.

The orders meet at \S\,\ref{sec:part-iv-face-torsor}, where the
coordinate-projection reading and the orbit-enumeration reading
become simultaneous: a physics bridge is both a projection
($\pi_\bullet \circ \Phol$) and an orbit representative ($F_i \in
\Face(\cA)$).

We recommend the functor order for a first reading: each
coordinate of $\Phol$ already corresponds to a familiar physics
structure, so the projection reading arrives at the physics
bridges from a familiar starting point. The torsor order is
appropriate for a second reading, when the abstract structure is
fixed and the enumeration of physics faces becomes an exhaustive
classification.
