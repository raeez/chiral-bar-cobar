\subsection*{The ordered-bar ledger}
\label{subsec:guide-ordered-bar-ledger}

The local seed of the volume is the logarithmic collision form
$\eta_{ij}=d\log(z_i-z_j)$ on ordered configuration spaces. Arnold's
three-term relation supplies the configuration-space part of
nilpotence for the ordered bar coalgebra
\[
  \barB^{\mathrm{ord}}_X(\cA)=T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA),
  \qquad |s^{-1}v|=|v|-1,
\]
and the bar differential is the pullback
$d_{\barB}=\mathrm{KZ}^{*}(\nabla_{\mathrm{Arnold}})$. The universal
Maurer--Cartan element
\[
  \Theta_\cA\in \MC(\mathfrak g_{\cA}^{\mathrm{mod}}),
  \qquad
  D\Theta_\cA+\tfrac12[\Theta_\cA,\Theta_\cA]=0,
\]
packages the genus tower. Its degree-two component is the classical
collision residue $r_\cA(z)$; at genus $g\ge 1$ the ordered fibre
differential acquires matrix-valued curvature
\[
  (d^{\mathrm{ord}}_{\mathrm{fib}})^2
  =r_\cA(z)\cdot\omega_g,
  \qquad
  \omega_g=c_g(\mathbb{E}),
\]
against the Hodge bundle $\mathbb{E}=\pi_*\omega_\pi$ on the universal
curve.

The symmetric bar is the derived quotient of this ordered datum under
averaging:
\[
  \mathrm{av}\colon
  \mathfrak g^{\Eone}\longrightarrow \mathfrak g^{\mathrm{mod}},
  \qquad
  \mathrm{av}_n=\frac1{n!}\sum_{\sigma\in S_n}\sigma^*.
\]
For $\Einf$-chiral input the symmetric and ordered bars agree through
formality. For genuinely $\Eone$-chiral input the
$\mathrm{GRT}_1(\mathbb Q)$-dependent ordered kernel is erased by
$\mathrm{av}$. The standard landscape therefore appears as the
averaging shadow
\[
  \mathsf G/\mathsf L/\mathsf C/\mathsf M/
  \mathsf M^{\mathrm{ext}}/\mathsf B,
  \qquad
  r_{\max}\in\{2,3,4,\infty,\infty,\infty\}.
\]
The witnesses are Heisenberg, affine Kac--Moody at non-critical level,
$\beta\gamma$, Virasoro with principal $\mathcal W_N$ extensions,
subregular or hook Drinfeld--Sokolov reductions such as
Bershadsky--Polyakov, and the K3 Borcherds--Kac--Moody crown
$\mathbf H_{\Delta_5}$. The critical affine stratum is a companion
boundary where Sugawara degenerates, not another row.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Five derived evaluations.}\enspace
Theorem~A reads the chiral bar--cobar adjunction
$\Omega_X^{\mathrm{ch}}\dashv \barB_X^{\mathrm{ch}}$ in two lanes.
On the chain lane the counit has an explicit homotopy
$h$ with $[d,h]=\id-p$, where $h$ inserts the desuspended generator at
the cofree position and $p$ projects to the generator subcomplex. On
the factorization-$\infty$ lane the same adjunction lives on
$\operatorname{Ran}(X)$, with Verdier duality identifying
$\mathbb D_{\operatorname{Ran}}\barB_X^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ with the
Koszul-dual factorization algebra $\cA^!_\infty$.

Theorem~B is the inversion surface. On the Koszul locus the counit
$\Omega_X(\barB_X(\cA))\to\cA$ is a quasi-isomorphism. For
$\mathbf H_{\Delta_5}$ over $\overline{\mathcal A}_2$, the strict
locus is the admissible-Humbert complement
\[
  U^{\mathrm{adm}}
  =\overline{\mathcal A}_2
  \setminus\bigcup_{n\ \mathrm{admissible}}H_n,
\]
where admissibility is the Bruinier singular-theta obstruction
condition of Remark~\ref{rem:tbsp-why-admissibility}. Off the strict
lane the weight-completed chiral bar coalgebra carries the
coderived/contraderived Positselski equivalence. The chain-level
octachotomy has the eight strata recorded in
Corollary~\ref{thm:codim4-admissible-heegner-emptiness}: Koszul locus,
codimension-one single-monodromy, codimension-two bi-unipotent
Deligne--Malcev, admissible-Heegner Malcev ladder, codimension-three
tri-unipotent extension, Baily--Borel zero-cusp, weight-completed
coderived, and the $A_\infty$-corrected neighbourhood of $H_3$.
The Eichler--Zagier congruence $n\equiv3,5\bmod8$ belongs to the
separate pentagon/Humbert obstruction filter. The first non-empty
admissible Heegner triple is $H_3\cap H_4\cap H_7$, realised by
$[E_{\sqrt{-3}}\times E_{\sqrt{-7}}]$ on $\overline{\mathcal A}_2$;
the corresponding quadruple intersections are empty.

Theorem~C is derived-centre complementarity. C0 identifies the fibre
centre with
\[
  \mathcal Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)
  =\operatorname{RHom}_{\cA\otimes\cA^{\mathrm{op}}}(\cA,\cA).
\]
The identification is unconditional in the coderived category and
ordinary-derived on the flat perfect locus. C1 gives the Verdier
eigenspace decomposition for all genera, with duality and Lagrangian
polarization for $g\ge1$ and genus~$0$ treated separately. C2 is the
shifted-symplectic/BV realization on the BV-perfect,
Verdier-nondegenerate, uniform-weight-perfect bar-chart surface. On the
canonical landmark witnesses
\[
  \kappa(\cA)+\kappa(\cA^!)
  \in\{0,8,13,250/3,98/3\},
\]
with $8$ the Vol~III $\mathsf B$-row Mukai witness; this is a landmark
bucket, not a universal bound.

Theorem~D is the all-weight genus tower
\[
  F_g(\cA)=\kappa(\cA)\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}
  +\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA).
\]
On the uniform-weight lane the correction vanishes and
$\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA)=\kappa(\cA)\lambda_g$ with
$\lambda_g=c_g(\mathbb{E})$. Universally
$\delta F_1^{\mathrm{cross}}=0$. For $\mathcal W_3$ at genus~$2$ the
cross-channel correction is
\[
  \delta F_2^{\mathrm{cross}}(\mathcal W_3)=\frac{c+204}{16c}.
\]

Theorem~H is the curve-level chiral Hochschild theorem on the proved
Koszul/generic surface with $E_\infty$-chiral PBW completion:
\[
  P(t,q)=\mathrm{HS}_{Z(\cA)}(q)
  +\mathrm{HS}_{\ChirHoch^1(\cA)}(q)t
  +\mathrm{HS}_{Z(\cA^!)}(q)t^2.
\]
The Calabi--Yau higher-degree shadows, including the degree-three
K3$\times E$ class
\[
  \chi_3={:}T\partial T{:}-\tfrac14\partial^3T
  +\hbar\,\mathrm{qt}(J^{(3)}),
\]
belong to the Vol~III companion lane, where the pairing with the
K3$\times E$ generator gives
\[
  \langle[\chi_3],[e_3^{\,\mathrm{K3}\times E}]\rangle
  =2\,\mathrm{Vol}(E)(2\pi i)^3
  =\chi(\mathcal O_{\mathrm{K3}})
  \Res_{s=1/2}[E_5^{(2)}(Z,s)]\big|_{K(1)}.
\]
Those classes refine the cross-volume picture; they are not the
curve-level statement of Theorem~H.

Positselski inversion is the closure step. Once Theorem~B recovers the
object in the correct ambient, Theorems~C, D, and H act on the
recovered chiral coalgebra. Without B the derived centre has no
canonical object, the Hodge-weighted fibre has no anchor, and bounded
$\mathcal D_C$-support has no ambient.

\noindent\textbf{The collision residue.}\enspace
The classical residue $r_\cA(z)$ has seven faces organised in three
tiers. Tier~I records intrinsic OPE data and the
shifted-symplectic/BV datum on its perfect locus:
\[
  r^{\mathrm{Heis}}_k(z)=\frac{k}{z},
  \qquad
  r^{\mathrm{KM},\fg}_k(z)=\frac{k\,\Omega_{\mathrm{tr}}}{z},
  \qquad
  r^{\mathrm{Vir}}_c(z)=\frac{c/2}{z^3}+\frac{2T}{z}.

Tier~II is the $E_d$-holomorphic factorisation-algebra braiding,
pinned up to the $\mathrm{GRT}_1(\mathbb Q)$ torsor by
Willwacher's $H^0(\mathsf{GC}_2)=\mathfrak{grt}_1$. Tier~III is the
Stage-2 chiral quantum-group output: Maulik--Okounkov spectral
$R$-matrix on $\mathrm{Hilb}^{[n]}(\mathrm{K3})$, Drinfeld RTT
current via the associative bar multiplication, and Belavin elliptic
non-dynamical $R$-matrix. Cross-tier maps specialise from Tier~I to
Tier~II to Tier~III; intra-tier maps are
$\mathrm{GRT}_1(\mathbb Q)$-gauge equivalences.

\subsection*{The MC frontier}

MC1 through MC4 are proved. MC5 is proved analytically/coderived and
on the strict Mittag--Leffler pro-object / $J$-adic / filtered
weight-completed chain surface. The analytic HS-sewing package is established at all genera
(Theorem~\ref{thm:general-hs-sewing}); at genus~$0$ the algebraic
BRST/bar comparison is proved
(Theorem~\ref{thm:algebraic-string-dictionary}); the genuswise
BV/BRST/bar comparison on these completed/pro ambients is proved
\textup{(}Theorems~\ref{thm:mc5-class-m-chain-level-pro-ambient},
\ref{thm:mc5-class-m-topological-chain-level-j-adic};
Proposition~\ref{prop:ambient-equivalence}\textup{)}; the bounded
direct-sum ambient $\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$ fails in class~$M$
already at weight~$4$; and the separate tree-level amplitude pairing is
conditional on Corollary~\ref{cor:string-amplitude-genus0}. MC3 is proved for all
simple types on the evaluation-generated core; the residual DK-4/5
(extension beyond evaluation modules) is downstream.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{MC\arabic*},leftmargin=3em]
\item \textbf{PBW concentration}: proved for all standard families
 (Proposition~\ref{prop:pbw-universality}).
\item \textbf{Bar-intrinsic MC element}: $\Theta_\cA := D_\cA - \dzero$
 is automatically MC because $D_\cA^2 = 0$
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}).
\item \textbf{All-types MC3 reduction}: categorical prefundamental
 Clebsch--Gordan closure and the DK comparison on the
 evaluation-generated core are proved for all simple Lie types
 (Corollary~\ref{cor:mc3-all-types}).
\item \textbf{Completion towers}: strong completion-tower theorem
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong}).
 MC4 splits into MC4$^+$ (positive towers, solved) and
 MC4$^0$ (resonant towers, reduced to finite resonance).
\item \textbf{Sewing at all genera}: general HS-sewing criterion
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:general-hs-sewing}).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{The shadow obstruction tower}

The universal MC element $\Theta_\cA$ has finite-order
projections $\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$, each a graph sum through
degree~$r$ with obstruction class
$\mathfrak{o}_{r+1}(\cA) \in \mathrm{Def}_{\mathrm{cyc}}^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$.
The named shadows are:
\[
\underbrace{\kappa}_{\text{degree 2}}
\;\to\;
\underbrace{C}_{\text{degree 3}}
\;\to\;
\underbrace{Q}_{\text{degree 4}}
\;\to\; \cdots
\]
Shadow depth classifies complexity within the Koszul world:
class~G ($r_{\max} = 2$, Heisenberg), class~L ($r_{\max} = 3$,
affine), class~C ($r_{\max} = 4$, $\beta\gamma$), class~M
($r_{\max} = \infty$, Virasoro/$\cW_N$). The full tower
$\Theta_\cA = \varprojlim \Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ converges
(Theorem~\ref{thm:recursive-existence}).

\subsection*{Dependency structure}

Logical dependencies:
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=small, row sep=small]

 & \text{D} \ar[r] & \text{C} \\

\end{center}
Theorem~A (the adjunction) is logically first. Theorem~B
(inversion) requires~A. Theorem~D (modular characteristic)
requires~A at genus~$0$ and extends inductively. Theorem~C
(complementarity) requires~D. Theorem~H (chiral Hochschild) is
independent. The MC element $\Theta_\cA$ depends on~A and
implies~D as a projection.

\subsection*{Three-pillar foundational architecture}
\label{sec:guide-three-pillars}
\index{three-pillar architecture|textbf}

Three recent papers force upgrades at three levels of the foundations.
The three pillars form a triangle whose center is the master MC equation
$D\cdot\Theta_\cA + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta_\cA,\Theta_\cA] = 0$.
Each pillar provides one face of~$\Theta_\cA$: Pillar~A gives its
\emph{local algebraic nature} (a homotopy chiral algebra on the
curve), Pillar~B gives its \emph{deformation-theoretic home} (the
convolution $sL_\infty$-algebra that controls all twisting
morphisms), and Pillar~C gives its \emph{global geometric skeleton}
(the log-FM compactification that provides the collision geometry).

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\Alph*)}]
\item \textbf{Local algebraic object} (Pillar~A). The primitive local
 object is a \emph{homotopy chiral algebra}
 ($\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebra) in the sense of
 Malikov--Schechtman~\cite{MS24}, not a strict chiral algebra. A strict
 chiral algebra appears only after rectification or on cohomology.

\item \textbf{Deformation-control object} (Pillar~B). The universal
 deformation machine is a \emph{filtered convolution $sL_\infty$-algebra}
 $\operatorname{hom}_\alpha(\cC, \cA)$ relative to a twisting morphism
 $\alpha$, in the sense of Robert-Nicoud--Wierstra~\cite{RNW19} and
 Vallette~\cite{Val16}. The dg~Lie algebra $\gAmod$ of
 Definition~\ref{def:modular-convolution-dg-lie} is the strict model.
 \emph{Design constraint}:
 $\operatorname{hom}_\alpha$ extends to $\infty$-morphisms in either
 slot separately but not both simultaneously~\cite[Section~6]{RNW19}.

\item \textbf{Global collision geometry} (Pillar~C). The natural
 compactification for punctured curves and nodal degenerations
 is \emph{logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson}
 $\overline{\operatorname{FM}}_n(X|D)$ on simple normal crossings pairs
