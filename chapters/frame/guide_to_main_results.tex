\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Guide to main results}

\noindent
This guide lists the principal results proved in the monograph,
with labels, locations, and logical dependencies.  All five
theorems hold for augmented chiral algebras~$\cA$ on a smooth
projective curve~$X$ satisfying the standing finiteness
conditions (\S\ref{sec:standing-assumptions}).

\subsection*{The MC frontier}

The five MC conjectures, which together constitute the
programme of extending bar--cobar duality to the full modular
envelope, are addressed: MC1, MC2, MC4, MC5 are proved; MC3 is
partially resolved (CG all types, post-CG open beyond
type~$A$).  The full BV/BRST/bar identification at higher
genus remains conjectural
(Chapter~\ref{chap:editorial-constitution}).
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{MC\arabic*},leftmargin=3em]
\item \textbf{PBW concentration}: proved for all standard families
  (Proposition~\ref{prop:pbw-universality}).
\item \textbf{Bar-intrinsic MC element}: $\Theta_\cA := D_\cA - \dzero$
  is automatically MC because $D_\cA^2 = 0$
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}).
\item \textbf{All-types MC3 reduction}: categorical prefundamental
  Clebsch--Gordan closure and the DK comparison on the
  evaluation-generated core are proved for all simple Lie types
  (Corollary~\ref{cor:mc3-all-types}).
\item \textbf{Completion towers}: strong completion-tower theorem
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong}).
  MC4 splits into MC4$^+$ (positive towers, solved) and
  MC4$^0$ (resonant towers, reduced to finite resonance).
\item \textbf{Sewing at all genera}: general HS-sewing criterion
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:general-hs-sewing}).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{The shadow Postnikov tower}

The universal MC element $\Theta_\cA$ has finite-order
projections $\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$, each a graph sum through
arity~$r$ with obstruction class
$\mathfrak{o}_{r+1}(\cA) \in \mathrm{Def}_{\mathrm{cyc}}^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$.
The named shadows are:
\[
\underbrace{\kappa}_{\text{arity 2}}
\;\to\;
\underbrace{C}_{\text{arity 3}}
\;\to\;
\underbrace{Q}_{\text{arity 4}}
\;\to\; \cdots
\]
Shadow depth classifies complexity within the Koszul world:
class~G ($r_{\max} = 2$, Heisenberg), class~L ($r_{\max} = 3$,
affine), class~C ($r_{\max} = 4$, $\beta\gamma$), class~M
($r_{\max} = \infty$, Virasoro/$\cW_N$).  The full tower
$\Theta_\cA = \varprojlim \Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ converges
(Theorem~\ref{thm:recursive-existence}).

\subsection*{Dependency structure}

The logical dependencies among the main theorems are:
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=small, row sep=small]
  & \text{A} \ar[r] \ar[d] & \text{B} \\
  & \text{D} \ar[r] & \text{C} \\
  \text{H} & & \Theta_\cA \ar[u] \ar[ul]
\end{tikzcd}
\end{center}
Theorem~A (the adjunction) is logically first.  Theorem~B
(inversion) requires~A.  Theorem~D (modular characteristic)
requires~A at genus~$0$ and extends inductively.  Theorem~C
(complementarity) requires~D.  Theorem~H (Hochschild) is
independent.  The MC element $\Theta_\cA$ depends on~A and
implies~D as a projection.
