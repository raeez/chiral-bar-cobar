\subsection*{The MC frontier}

MC1 through MC4 are proved. MC5 is partially proved: the analytic
HS-sewing package is established at all genera
(Theorem~\ref{thm:general-hs-sewing}); at genus~$0$ the algebraic
BRST/bar comparison is proved
(Theorem~\ref{thm:algebraic-string-dictionary}) and the tree-level
amplitude pairing is conditional on
Corollary~\ref{cor:string-amplitude-genus0}; the full genuswise
BV/BRST/bar identification at higher genus remains conjectural
(Chapter~\ref{chap:editorial-constitution}). MC3 is proved for all
simple types on the evaluation-generated core; the residual DK-4/5
(extension beyond evaluation modules) is downstream.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{MC\arabic*},leftmargin=3em]
\item \textbf{PBW concentration}: proved for all standard families
 (Proposition~\ref{prop:pbw-universality}).
\item \textbf{Bar-intrinsic MC element}: $\Theta_\cA := D_\cA - \dzero$
 is automatically MC because $D_\cA^2 = 0$
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}).
\item \textbf{All-types MC3 reduction}: categorical prefundamental
 Clebsch--Gordan closure and the DK comparison on the
 evaluation-generated core are proved for all simple Lie types
 (Corollary~\ref{cor:mc3-all-types}).
\item \textbf{Completion towers}: strong completion-tower theorem
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong}).
 MC4 splits into MC4$^+$ (positive towers, solved) and
 MC4$^0$ (resonant towers, reduced to finite resonance).
\item \textbf{Sewing at all genera}: general HS-sewing criterion
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:general-hs-sewing}).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{The shadow obstruction tower}

The universal MC element $\Theta_\cA$ has finite-order
projections $\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$, each a graph sum through
degree~$r$ with obstruction class
$\mathfrak{o}_{r+1}(\cA) \in \mathrm{Def}_{\mathrm{cyc}}^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$.
The named shadows are:
\[
\underbrace{\kappa}_{\text{degree 2}}
\;\to\;
\underbrace{C}_{\text{degree 3}}
\;\to\;
\underbrace{Q}_{\text{degree 4}}
\;\to\; \cdots
\]
Shadow depth classifies complexity within the Koszul world:
class~G ($r_{\max} = 2$, Heisenberg), class~L ($r_{\max} = 3$,
affine), class~C ($r_{\max} = 4$, $\beta\gamma$), class~M
($r_{\max} = \infty$, Virasoro/$\cW_N$). The full tower
$\Theta_\cA = \varprojlim \Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ converges
(Theorem~\ref{thm:recursive-existence}).

\subsection*{Dependency structure}

Logical dependencies:
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=small, row sep=small]
 & \text{A} \ar[r] \ar[d] & \text{B} \\
 & \text{D} \ar[r] & \text{C} \\
 \text{H} & & \Theta_\cA \ar[u] \ar[ul]
\end{tikzcd}
\end{center}
Theorem~A (the adjunction) is logically first. Theorem~B
(inversion) requires~A. Theorem~D (modular characteristic)
requires~A at genus~$0$ and extends inductively. Theorem~C
(complementarity) requires~D. Theorem~H (chiral Hochschild) is
independent. The MC element $\Theta_\cA$ depends on~A and
implies~D as a projection.

\subsection*{Three-pillar foundational architecture}
\label{sec:guide-three-pillars}
\index{three-pillar architecture|textbf}

Three recent papers force upgrades at three levels of the foundations.
The three pillars form a triangle whose center is the master MC equation
$D\cdot\Theta_\cA + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta_\cA,\Theta_\cA] = 0$.
Each pillar provides one face of~$\Theta_\cA$: Pillar~A gives its
\emph{local algebraic nature} (a homotopy chiral algebra on the
curve), Pillar~B gives its \emph{deformation-theoretic home} (the
convolution $sL_\infty$-algebra that controls all twisting
morphisms), and Pillar~C gives its \emph{global geometric skeleton}
(the log-FM compactification that provides the collision geometry).

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\Alph*)}]
\item \textbf{Local algebraic object} (Pillar~A). The primitive local
 object is a \emph{homotopy chiral algebra}
 ($\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebra) in the sense of
 Malikov--Schechtman~\cite{MS24}, not a strict chiral algebra. A strict
 chiral algebra appears only after rectification or on cohomology.

\item \textbf{Deformation-control object} (Pillar~B). The universal
 deformation machine is a \emph{filtered convolution $sL_\infty$-algebra}
 $\operatorname{hom}_\alpha(\cC, \cA)$ relative to a twisting morphism
 $\alpha$, in the sense of Robert-Nicoud--Wierstra~\cite{RNW19} and
 Vallette~\cite{Val16}. The dg~Lie algebra $\gAmod$ of
 Definition~\ref{def:modular-convolution-dg-lie} is the strict model.
 \emph{Design constraint}:
 $\operatorname{hom}_\alpha$ extends to $\infty$-morphisms in either
 slot separately but not both simultaneously~\cite[Section~6]{RNW19}.

\item \textbf{Global collision geometry} (Pillar~C). The natural
 compactification for punctured curves and nodal degenerations
 is \emph{logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson}
 $\overline{\operatorname{FM}}_n(X|D)$ on simple normal crossings pairs
 $(X, D)$, in the sense of Mok~\cite{Mok25}. Ordinary FM
 compactification is the special case $D = \emptyset$.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Family index theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Family index theorem for genus expansions;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]\label{thm:guide-family-index}
\index{index theorem!modular deformation|textbf}
\index{Grothendieck--Riemann--Roch!genus expansion|textbf}
Let $\cA$ be a Koszul chiral algebra with obstruction coefficient
$\kappa(\cA)$, let $\pi \colon \overline{\mathcal{C}}_g \to
\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ be the universal curve with relative
dualizing sheaf~$\omega_\pi$, and let
$\mathbb{E} = R^0\pi_*\omega_\pi$ be the Hodge bundle.
The following holds for uniform-weight modular Koszul algebras
at all genera; the genus-$1$ statements are unconditional for
all families.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{GRR formula.}
For uniform-weight algebras, the genus-$g$ free energy is the
GRR pushforward:
\[
F_g(\cA)
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA) \cdot
\int_{\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,1}} \psi^{2g-2}\, c_g(\mathbb{E}).
\]

\item \emph{$\hat{A}$-genus identification.}
The total generating function is
\[
\sum_{g \geq 1} F_g(\cA)\, x^{2g}
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA) \cdot \bigl(\hat{A}(ix) - 1\bigr)
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA) \cdot
\left(\frac{x/2}{\sin(x/2)} - 1\right).
\]

\item \emph{Verdier compatibility.}
Under Koszul duality $\cA \leftrightarrow \cA^!$,
$\kappa(\cA) + \kappa(\cA^!)$ is a root-datum invariant
\textup{(}$0$ for KM/free fields, $\varrho(\fg) \cdot K_N$ for
$\cW_N$-algebras; e.g.\ $13$ for Virasoro, $250/3$
for~$\cW_3$\textup{)}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\subsection*{Structural principles}

\begin{principle}[Gravitational primitivity]
\label{princ:guide-gravitational-primitivity}
\index{coproduct!gravitational primitivity|textbf}
For any principal Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction
$W_k(\fg) = H^0_{\mathrm{BRST}}(\widehat{\fg}_k, f_{\mathrm{prin}})$,
the HPL-transferred coproduct is strictly primitive at all degrees:
$\Delta_{z,n}^W = 0$ for all $n \ge 2$.
All nontrivial structure of the transferred dg-shifted Yangian
resides in the $\Ainf$ products $\{m_n^W\}_{n \ge 3}$ and in
the collision residue $r(z)$. The mechanism is a ghost-number
obstruction intrinsic to DS reduction.
\end{principle}

\begin{remark}[The four-test interface]
\label{rem:guide-four-test-interface}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
\index{four-test interface|textbf}
The modular Koszul machine has a complete interface with
$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,n}$, consisting of four
independent proved tests:
\textup{(1)}~$D_\cA^2 = 0$ at all genera and degrees
\textup{(}Theorems~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-modular-operad},
\ref{thm:quantum-diff-squares-zero}}\textup{)};
\textup{(2)}~$\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_g$
\textup{(}UNIFORM-WEIGHT\textup{})
for uniform-weight algebras at all genera,
unconditionally at genus~$1$
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}}\textup{)};
\textup{(3)}~$Q_g(\cA)+Q_g(\cA^!)=H^*(\overline{\mathcal M}_g,\cZ_\cA)$:
complementarity assembles the Koszul pair into a Lagrangian
decomposition
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}}\textup{)};
\textup{(4)}~sewing: genus-$g$ amplitudes converge from lower-genus data
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:general-hs-sewing}}\textup{)}.
\end{remark}

\subsection*{The four-test boundary of control}
\label{subsec:guide-four-test-boundary}
\index{boundary of control|textbf}

The algebraic engine controls everything
\emph{inside} the modular Koszul locus. The boundary
of that locus is determined by four independent tests,
each representing a genuine mathematical barrier.
Crossing any one of the four boundary walls (non-Koszulness,
non-uniform-weight, non-convergence, non-algebraic family)
requires new methods. The engine extends to each wall
but does not cross it.

\subsection*{Factorization-envelope technology}
\label{sec:guide-nishinaka-vicedo}
\index{factorization envelope!Lie conformal|textbf}

The Nishinaka~\cite{Nishinaka25} and
Vicedo~\cite{Vicedo25} constructions provide a functorial
pipeline from Lie conformal algebras to factorization algebras
and vertex algebras. Starting from a Lie conformal algebra~$R$,
the factorization envelope $\operatorname{Fact}(R)$ produces the
enveloping vertex algebra $U^{\mathrm{vert}}(R)$, which carries
the bar complex and the full shadow obstruction tower. The
\emph{envelope-shadow functor} $\Theta^{\mathrm{env}}(R)
:= \Theta(U^{\mathrm{vert}}(R))$ upgrades the shadow tower from
a case-by-case invariant to a functor on Lie conformal input.

\subsection*{Modular cooperad completion}
\label{sec:concordance-modular-cooperad-completion}
\index{modular cooperad!completion|textbf}

The modular completion of the bar coalgebra extends the genus-$0$
factorization coalgebra structure to all genera via the modular
operad. The sewing amplitudes at genus~$g$ are the compositional
structure maps of the completed modular cooperad
$\barB^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$.
