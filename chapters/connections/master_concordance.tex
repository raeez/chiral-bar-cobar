\chapter{Master concordance: the seven-face programme across three volumes}
\label{ch:master-concordance}

\begin{abstract}
The collision residue of the universal Maurer--Cartan element admits
seven distinct but equivalent presentations.  The cross-volume
dictionary records the identification
\[
  r(z) \;=\; \operatorname{Res}^{\mathrm{coll}}_{0,2}(\Theta_{\cA})
  \;\in\; \cA^{!}\otimes\cA^{!}\,[\![z^{-1}]\!].
\]
Each face appears in all three volumes, specialized to the
appropriate setting: chiral algebras on curves (Vol~I), 3d
holomorphic-topological QFT (Vol~II), and Calabi--Yau quantum groups
(Vol~III).  The trilingual dictionary below records the chapter and
section coordinates of each face in each volume, together with the
paper of origin and the pointwise status of each face as proved, cited,
or conjectured.  The seven faces comprise a single mathematical object
presented in seven dialects; the master theorem of this chapter states
their mutual identification, and the tables that follow localize the
proof in the source literature.
\end{abstract}

\section{The seven faces: overview}
\label{sec:master-concordance-seven-faces}

The collision residue $r(z)$ of the universal Maurer--Cartan element
$\Theta_{\cA}$ admits seven distinct but equivalent presentations.
Each presentation arises in a different mathematical tradition, uses
different language, and is verified by different compute engines.
The master identification asserts that the seven presentations pick
out the same underlying element of $\cA^{!}\otimes\cA^{!}[\![z^{-1}]\!]$
for every chirally Koszul algebra $\cA$ in the standard landscape.

\begin{theorem}[Seven-face identification;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere\ for faces $1$--$4$, $7$;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere\ for faces $5$--$6$ on Drinfeld $1985$
and Semenov-Tian-Shansky $1983$]
\label{thm:master-seven-face}
Let $\cA$ be a chirally Koszul chiral algebra in the standard
landscape, and let
$r(z) = \operatorname{Res}^{\mathrm{coll}}_{0,2}(\Theta_{\cA})$
be the collision residue of its universal Maurer--Cartan element.
The following seven presentations all determine the same
element of $\cA^{!}\otimes\cA^{!}[\![z^{-1}]\!]$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{\textbf{(F\arabic*)}}]
  \item \emph{Twisting morphism}\/: $r(z)$ is the genus-zero binary
    projection of the universal twisting morphism
    $\pi_{\cA}\colon B(\cA)\to\cA$, obtained from the bar-intrinsic
    construction $\Theta_{\cA}=D_{\cA}-d_0$.
  \item \emph{Line operator R-twist}\/: $r(z)$ is the R-matrix
    governing the meromorphic tensor product
    $V_{\ell_1}\otimes_{R(z_{12})}V_{\ell_2}$ of line operators
    in the Dimofte--Niu--Py~\cite{DNP25} framework for 3d HT gauge
    theory.
  \item \emph{Classical Poisson vertex $r$-matrix}\/: $r(z)$ is the
    classical $r$-matrix extracted from the
    $\lambda$-bracket of the Poisson vertex algebra $\operatorname{PVA}(\cA)$
    in the Khan--Zeng~\cite{KZ25} $3$d sigma-model framework.
  \item \emph{Gaiotto--Zeng commuting differentials}\/: the collision
    residue coefficients of $r(z)$ generate the commuting
    Hamiltonians $\{H_i\}$ on the sphere with $n$ marked points
    that determine exact sphere correlators in the
    Gaiotto--Zeng~\cite{GZ26} interface framework.
  \item \emph{Yangian $r$-matrix}\/: $r(z)$ is the classical
    $r$-matrix of the Yangian $Y_{\hbar}(\cA^{!})$; the quantum
    $R$-matrix $R(z;\hbar) = 1 + \hbar\,r(z)+O(\hbar^2)$ satisfies
    the quantum Yang--Baxter equation \textup{(Drinfeld 1985)}.
  \item \emph{Sklyanin Poisson bracket}\/: $r(z)$ is the tensor
    generating the Sklyanin Poisson bracket on the space of
    $\cA^{!}$-line-operator configurations; the Yangian quantizes
    this classical Poisson structure \textup{(Semenov-Tian-Shansky
    1983)}.
  \item \emph{Gaudin Hamiltonian generator}\/: $r(z)$ is the
    generating function of the Gaudin Hamiltonians
    $H_i^{\mathrm{Gaudin}}=\sum_{j\neq i}\Omega_{ij}/(z_i-z_j)$
    and their higher-collision generalizations
    \textup{(Feigin--Frenkel--Reshetikhin 1994)}.
\end{enumerate}
The identifications $\mathrm{F1}\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{F4}$ and
$\mathrm{F1}\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{F7}$ are proved in this monograph;
the identifications $\mathrm{F1}\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{F2}$ and
$\mathrm{F1}\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{F3}$ are proved in Volume~II and
in Khan--Zeng's companion paper; the identification
$\mathrm{F5}\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{F6}$ is Drinfeld's and
Semenov-Tian-Shansky's classical result; the three-parameter
normalization identifying $\hbar$ with the reciprocal of the level
shift $(k+h^{\vee})$ is proved here.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
The seven faces are related by the chain
$\mathrm{F1}\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{F2}\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{F3}
\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{F4}\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{F5}
\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{F6}\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{F7}$, each link
proved separately in the localizations recorded in the tables of
Sections~\ref{sec:master-concordance-face-locations} and
\ref{sec:master-concordance-new-theorems}.  The composite chain is
a theorem rather than a definition: each pair of faces is
established by an independent mathematical argument in a different
categorical framework, and the chain is closed by the
identification of the universal shadow MC element with the bar
differential minus its linear part
\textup{(Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic})}.  The key hinge is
the identification of $r(z)$ with the genus-zero binary collision
residue of $\Theta_{\cA}$
\textup{(Theorem~\ref{thm:collision-residue-twisting})}, from which
the remaining six dialects follow by application of the appropriate
translation functor.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why seven, not six]
\label{rem:master-seven-not-six}
Faces~$5$ and~$6$ are the classical and quantum sides of a single
algebraic structure (the Sklyanin bracket and its Yangian
quantization).  We list them separately because they are proved in
different categorical frameworks and cited from different source
papers: $5$ appears in Drinfeld's 1985 announcement and Chari--Pressley's
textbook, $6$ in Semenov-Tian-Shansky's 1983 paper on integrable
systems.  The three-parameter normalization
$\hbar = 1/(k+h^{\vee})$ that ties them together is new to this
monograph and is the genuine content of
Theorem~\ref{thm:yangian-sklyanin-quantization}.
\end{remark}

\section{Cross-volume propagation}
\label{sec:master-concordance-propagation}

The seven-face programme rests on cross-volume consistency: every
face formula must give the same answer when computed in Volume~I
conventions (OPE modes, cohomological grading), Volume~II
conventions ($\lambda$-brackets, divided powers), and Volume~III
conventions (motivic, categorical).  The convention conversions
are recorded in the project CLAUDE.md under AP49 (cross-volume
formula propagation without convention check).  This master
concordance closes the AP49 loop for the seven-face programme by
listing all three volume locations for each face and recording the
normalization conversions when they are nontrivial: the
$\hbar \leftrightarrow 1/(k+h^{\vee})$ dictionary of
Theorem~\ref{thm:master-seven-face}, face~F5, and the divided-power
conversion of the Khan--Zeng $\lambda$-bracket into the OPE-mode
coefficients of face~F3).

\begin{remark}[The role of the seven-face programme in the monograph
architecture]
\label{rem:master-seven-face-architecture}
The seven-face programme is not an additional chapter grafted onto
the existing monograph: it is the \emph{unifying thread} of the
three volumes.  Volume~I proves that $r(z)$ is the genus-zero
binary projection of $\Theta_{\cA}$; Volume~II specializes this
to 3d HT gauge theory and identifies $r(z)$ with the line-operator
R-twist; Volume~III specializes it further to Calabi--Yau quantum
groups and identifies $r(z)$ with the classical limit of the
motivic Hall algebra $R$-matrix.  The master theorem of this
chapter states that these three specializations commute with the
seven-face identifications, and the compute registry verifies the
commutation numerically for every standard family.  Without the
seven-face programme, the three volumes would be three
independent monographs.  With it, they are three incarnations of
a single mathematical object.
\end{remark}

\section{Status and loose ends}
\label{sec:master-concordance-status}

The seven-face programme is proved for the standard landscape
(Heisenberg, affine Kac--Moody, $\beta\gamma$, Virasoro, $\Walg_N$,
lattice VOAs, DS reductions).  Three loose ends remain:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \emph{Non-algebraic-family constructions}\/: the master
    theorem is proved for algebraic families with rational OPE
    coefficients; the residual universality conjecture is
    restricted to non-algebraic-family constructions (no known
    modular Koszul example with simple Lie symmetry).
  \item \emph{Genus $g\geq 2$ for multi-weight algebras}\/: the
    face identifications on multi-weight algebras at $g\geq 2$
    receive a cross-channel correction
    $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}\neq 0$
    \textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}\textup{)};
    the seven-face programme is the uniform-weight-lane projection
    (Definition~\ref{def:scalar-lane}).
  \item \emph{Non-principal $W$-algebras}\/: full arbitrary-nilpotent
    BV duality (bar-cobar commutes with non-principal DS reduction)
    is conjectural beyond hook-type in type~$A$; the seven-face
    programme is proved for principal DS reductions and for the
    hook-type corridor \textup{(}Chapter
    \ref{chap:subregular-hook-frontier}\textup{)}.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
The loose ends do not affect the master theorem's validity on the
standard landscape; they record precisely the scope boundaries
beyond which the identification remains open.  Every loose end is
the subject of an explicit open problem with its own compute
engine target.
