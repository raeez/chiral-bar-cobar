\chapter{Holomorphic-topological boundary conditions and 4d origins}
\label{ch:ht-boundary}

\index{holomorphic-topological theory|textbf}

Where do the algebraic beasts of Parts~I and~II come from physically?
The answer is: from boundary conditions and defects in
holomorphic-topological field theories.  A four-dimensional theory that
is holomorphic in two directions and topological in the remaining two
produces, upon restriction to a boundary or defect locus, a chiral
algebra on the holomorphic plane.  The bar complex of that chiral
algebra then governs the category of line defects ending on the
boundary, the cobar complex reconstructs bulk local operators from
boundary data, and Koszul duality is the passage between the two
perspectives.  Boundaries and defects are therefore not decorations
appended to the algebraic framework---they are structural necessities
that explain why bar-cobar duality has physical content.

The development of this programme in full requires the Swiss-cheese
operad (factorization on $\mathbb{C} \times \mathbb{R}$), the descent
calculus for mixed holomorphic-topological directions, and the
recognition theorem identifying the bar coproduct with the
$\mathbb{R}$-direction factorization.  These tools are assembled in
Volume~II; what remains in the present chapter is the bridge: the
algebraic input from Volume~I that feeds the physical construction, and
the celestial and boundary transfer results that illustrate the
mechanism in simplified settings.

This chapter has been moved to Volume~II, where it is developed in
the context of the Swiss-cheese operad and
holomorphic--topological factorization on $\mathbb{C} \times \mathbb{R}$.
The bar complex of this volume, equipped with its coassociative
coproduct (Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-swiss-cheese}), provides the
algebraic input; Volume~II identifies the physical theories that
produce it.

\subsection{Celestial holography and boundary transfer}
\label{subsec:celestial-boundary-transfer}

The Kaluza--Klein reduction of six-dimensional holomorphic theories to
three-dimensional holomorphic-topological theories produces transferred
$A_\infty$- and $L_\infty$-structures on the tangential Cauchy--Riemann
cohomology $H_b^{0,\bullet}(S^3)$.  These transferred structures are
the algebraic skeleton of celestial holography in the twisted setting.

The \emph{wedge $A_\infty$-algebra} construction quotients by
composite operators and shows that the single-particle celestial
algebra depends only on the linearized dg algebra; all genuinely
nonlinear content is isolated in the composite sector, measured by a
tower of obstruction classes $\operatorname{Ob}_r$ in the bar-filtered
coderivation complex.

For holomorphic BF, the transferred $A_\infty$-algebra admits closed
highest-weight ladder formulas in every arity, governed by simplex
integrals and a simplex summation law.  For Poisson BF, the
odd highest-weight ladder of the transferred $L_\infty$-deformation of
$T^*[1]\operatorname{Ham}(\mathbb{C}^2)$ is canonically realized by
first-order differential operators
$D_m = -\partial_t \circ M_{t^{2m+2}}$ on $\mathbb{C}[t]$---the
odd positive Witt modes acting on the density line (the
\emph{Airy--Witt normal form}).

For $W$-type Poisson vertex algebras, the first nonlinear
obstruction class $\operatorname{Ob}_1$ depends only on the linearized
Lie conformal structure and the lowest nonlinear homogeneous part of
the generator $\lambda$-brackets.  For the classical $W_3$ PVA this
means the first celestial higher-spin correction is controlled entirely
by the quadratic terms $\frac{32}{5c}T^2$ in the
$\{W_\lambda W\}$ bracket---a precise connection to the $W_3$
composite-field structure computed in
\S\ref{sec:w3-composite-complete}.

The full development of the celestial and boundary transfer programme,
including the wedge construction, the obstruction tower, the exact
ladder theorems, and the Airy--Witt realization, is carried out in
Volume~II.
