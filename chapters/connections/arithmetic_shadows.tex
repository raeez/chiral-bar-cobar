\chapter{Arithmetic content of the shadow tower}
\label{chap:arithmetic-shadows}

The shadow Postnikov tower $\Theta_\cA^{\leq r}$
(\S\ref{sec:koszul-across-genera}), whose depth classification
into classes $\mathbf{G}/\mathbf{L}/\mathbf{C}/\mathbf{M}$
is established in
Definition~\ref{def:shadow-depth-classification} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-archetype-classification}, carries
an \emph{arithmetic}
meaning visible only after passage from a single curve to the moduli
space~$\cM_{1,1}$.  On a fixed elliptic curve with nome $q = e^{2\pi
i\tau}$, the sewing operator
$K_q\colon\cF\to\cF$ acts diagonally on the Fock space with
eigenvalues $q^n$ ($n = 1,2,3,\dotsc$).  Its Fredholm determinant
$\det(1-K_q) = \prod_{n\ge1}(1-q^n) = e^{-\pi\tau/12}\,\eta(\tau)$
encodes the Dedekind eta function, and the spectral zeta function
$\sum_{n\ge1}n^{-s} = \zeta(s)$ is the Riemann zeta function.  None
of this constrains the zeros of~$\zeta$.  The \emph{arithmetic
connection} deepens when $\tau$ varies over
$\mathrm{SL}(2,\bZ)\backslash\mathfrak{H}$ and the
primary-counting function
$\widehat{Z}^c(\tau,\bar\tau)=y^{c/2}\lvert\eta(\tau)\rvert^{2c}
Z(\tau,\bar\tau)$
enters the Roelcke--Selberg spectral decomposition.  The real-analytic
Eisenstein series $E_s(\tau)$, $s=\tfrac12+it$, carries a scattering
matrix
\begin{equation}\label{eq:scattering-matrix}
  \varphi(s) = \frac{\Lambda(1-s)}{\Lambda(s)},
  \qquad
  \Lambda(s) := \pi^{-s}\,\zeta(2s)\,\Gamma(s),
\end{equation}
whose poles are the rescaled nontrivial zeros of~$\zeta$.  The shadow
tower, through the constrained Epstein zeta
$\varepsilon^c_s(\cA) = \sum_{\Delta\in\cS}(2\Delta)^{-s}$ summed
over scalar primaries, determines the Eisenstein spectral coefficient
of $\widehat{Z}^c$.  The main result of this chapter is that the
shadow depth controls the $L$-function content of~$\varepsilon^c_s$:

\begin{theorem}[Shadow--spectral correspondence]
\label{thm:shadow-spectral-correspondence}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $V_\Lambda$ be an even lattice vertex algebra of rank~$r$ with
theta function $\Theta_\Lambda\in M_{r/2}(\Gamma)$.  Write
$\Theta_\Lambda = c_E\, E_{r/2} + \sum_j c_j f_j$ for the
decomposition into the Eisenstein series~$E_{r/2}$ and Hecke
eigenforms $f_j\in S_{r/2}(\Gamma)$.  Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The constrained Epstein zeta factors as
\begin{equation}\label{eq:epstein-factorization}
  \varepsilon^r_s(V_\Lambda)
  = 2^{-s}\Bigl(
      c_E\,C_E(s)\,\zeta(s)\,\zeta(s-\tfrac{r}{2}+1)
    + \sum_j c_j\,C_j(s)\,L(s,f_j)
  \Bigr),
\end{equation}
where $C_E$, $C_j$ are explicit products of gamma factors and powers
of~$\pi$.
\item The number of critical lines of~$\varepsilon^r_s$---the
distinct values of $\mathrm{Re}(s)$ on which $\varepsilon^r_s$ has
infinitely many zeros---equals the shadow depth $d(V_\Lambda)$
minus~$1$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

The proof passes through four identities
(\S\ref{sec:four-identities}), the cusp-singularity analysis
(\S\ref{sec:cusp-singularity}), and explicit verification for the
five lattice families $V_{\bZ}$, $V_{\bZ^2}$, $V_{A_2}$,
$V_{E_8}$, $V_{\mathrm{Leech}}$
(\S\ref{sec:five-lattice-verifications}).  The non-lattice cases
$\beta\gamma$ and $\mathrm{Vir}_c$
(\S\ref{sec:non-lattice-theories}) reveal the boundary of the
correspondence: the strong form (equality) holds for lattice algebras;
for non-lattice algebras the shadow depth exceeds the
$\mathrm{U}(1)$-spectral critical-line count, and the gap is a
cusp-singularity phenomenon.

\section{The sewing--Rankin--Selberg bridge}
\label{sec:sewing-RS-bridge}

\subsection{The sewing operator on a single curve}

Fix $\tau\in\mathfrak{H}$ and set $q=e^{2\pi i\tau}$.  The sewing
operator $K_q$ acts on the Fock space $\cF=\bigoplus_{n\ge0}\cF_n$
diagonally: $K_q\lvert n\rangle = q^n\lvert n\rangle$ for $n\ge1$.
It is self-adjoint, positive, and trace class:
$\tr(K_q)=q/(1-q)$, $\det(1-K_q)=\prod(1-q^n)$.

\begin{proposition}[Divisor-sum decomposition]
\label{prop:divisor-sum-decomposition}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
\begin{equation}\label{eq:log-det-sigma}
  \log\det(1-K_q)
  = -\sum_{N\ge1}\sigma_{-1}(N)\,q^N,
  \qquad
  \sigma_{-1}(N) = \sum_{d\mid N}\frac{1}{d}.
\end{equation}
The function $\sigma_{-1}$ is multiplicative, and its Dirichlet series
satisfies the Ramanujan identity
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sigma-minus-1-dirichlet}
  \sum_{N\ge1}\sigma_{-1}(N)\,N^{-s}
  = \zeta(s)\,\zeta(s+1).
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Expand
$\log\det(1-K_q)=\sum_n\log(1-q^n)=-\sum_n\sum_m q^{nm}/m$
and reindex $N=nm$.  Multiplicativity of $\sigma_{-1}$ is standard;
the Dirichlet series identity is the $k=-1$ case of
$\sum\sigma_k(N)N^{-s}=\zeta(s)\zeta(s-k)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Euler product of the sewing determinant]
\label{cor:sewing-euler-product}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The identity~\eqref{eq:sigma-minus-1-dirichlet} gives a prime
factorization of the sewing Fredholm determinant:
\[
  \zeta(s)\,\zeta(s+1)
  = \prod_p \frac{1}{(1-p^{-s})(1-p^{-s-1})}.
\]
Each prime~$p$ contributes an independent factor to the sewing
amplitude.
\end{corollary}

\subsection{The sewing--Selberg formula}

\begin{theorem}[Sewing--Selberg formula]
\label{thm:sewing-selberg-formula}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The Rankin--Selberg integral of the sewing Fredholm determinant
against the Eisenstein series over $\cM_{1,1}$ is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sewing-selberg}
  \int_{\cM_{1,1}}
    \log\det(1-K(\tau))\cdot E_s(\tau)\,d\mu(\tau)
  = -2(2\pi)^{-(s-1)}\,\Gamma(s-1)\,\zeta(s-1)\,\zeta(s).
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Rankin--Selberg unfolding replaces the integral over the fundamental
domain by $\int_0^\infty a_0(y)\,y^{s-2}\,dy$, where
$a_0(y) = \int_0^1 \log\det(1-K(\tau))\,dx$ is the zeroth Fourier
mode.  On the imaginary axis,
$a_0(y) = \log\lvert\eta(iy)\rvert^2+\pi y/6
= 2\log\det(1-K_q)$, and the Mellin transform of
$-\sum\sigma_{-1}(N)e^{-2\pi Ny}$ against $y^{s-2}$ gives
$(2\pi N)^{-(s-1)}\Gamma(s-1)$ per term.  Summing via
\eqref{eq:sigma-minus-1-dirichlet} yields~\eqref{eq:sewing-selberg}.
\end{proof}

The sewing--Selberg formula~\eqref{eq:sewing-selberg} is the
composition \emph{sewing $\circ$ Rankin--Selberg}: the sewing operator
carries the arithmetic content (divisor sums, Euler product); the
Rankin--Selberg integral over the moduli space extracts the
$L$-function.  Neither step constrains the zeros of~$\zeta$:
on a single curve, $K_q$ is diagonal with no continuous spectrum; on
the moduli space, the continuous spectrum of the Laplacian on
$\mathrm{SL}(2,\bZ)\backslash\mathfrak{H}$ provides the Eisenstein
scattering~\eqref{eq:scattering-matrix} that introduces the zeta
zeros.

\begin{remark}[Structural obstruction]
\label{rem:structural-obstruction}
The scattering matrix $\varphi(s)=\Lambda(1{-}s)/\Lambda(s)$ has
poles at $s=\rho/2$ and zeros at $s=(1{-}\bar\rho)/2$, where $\rho$
ranges over the nontrivial zeros of~$\zeta$.  In the spectral
decomposition, the spectral parameter~$t$ is
\emph{real} ($s=\tfrac12+it$), and $\varphi(\tfrac12+it)$
is regular for all $t\in\bR$: its poles are at
$\mathrm{Re}(s)=\mathrm{Re}(\rho)/2\ne\tfrac12$
(assuming $\rho$ is not on the boundary of the critical strip),
hence at \emph{complex} spectral parameters.  The shadow tower and
the MC equation constrain the spectral coefficients~$c(t)$ on the
real $t$-axis; the zeta zeros live at complex~$t$.  This separation
is \emph{structural}: algebraic constraints on the spectral line
cannot reach the scattering poles without analytic continuation of
the spectral data off the real axis.

The completed Selberg function $\Lambda(s)=\pi^{-s}\Gamma(s)\zeta(2s)$
satisfies $\Lambda(s_0)\ne 0$ and $\Lambda(1{-}s_0)=0$ at
$s_0=(1{+}\rho)/2$, because $\zeta(2{-}2s_0)=\zeta(1{-}\rho)=0$
(the conjugate zero).  The quotient
$\varphi(s_0)=\Lambda(1{-}s_0)/\Lambda(s_0)=0$: the ``pole'' of
the uncompleted functional-equation factor
$\zeta(2s)/\zeta(2s{-}1)$ is absorbed into a
\emph{zero} of the completed scattering matrix.  No zero-forcing
mechanism for $\varepsilon^c_s$ operates at these points.
\end{remark}

\section{Four identities}\label{sec:four-identities}

The following four identities underpin the shadow--spectral
correspondence.  All are instances of a single mechanism: the
primary-counting function $\widehat{Z}^c$ determines a Dirichlet
series $\varepsilon^c_s$ via the Eisenstein spectral coefficient, and
the factorization of $\varepsilon^c_s$ reflects the modular-form
decomposition of the partition function.

\subsection{The Narain universality theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Narain universality]
\label{thm:narain-universality}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For the rank-$1$ Narain lattice vertex algebra at compactification
radius~$R$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:narain-universality}
  \varepsilon^1_s(R)
  = 2\bigl(R^{2s}+R^{-2s}\bigr)\,\zeta(2s).
\end{equation}
The character factor $2\cosh(2s\log R)$ is positive for
real~$s$ and $R>0$.  Consequently:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The zeros of $\varepsilon^1_s(R)$ are the zeros of
$\zeta(2s)$, independent of~$R$.
\item $T$-duality $R\leftrightarrow 1/R$ preserves
$\varepsilon^1_s$ identically, corresponding to Koszul
self-duality at the self-dual radius.
\item The Narain moduli provide no bootstrap leverage for
constraining the zero locations of~$\zeta$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The scalar primaries at radius~$R$ have dimensions
$\Delta_n=n^2/(2R^2)$ (momentum, multiplicity~$2$) and
$\Delta_w=w^2R^2/2$ (winding, multiplicity~$2$).  Then
$\varepsilon^1_s(R) = 2R^{2s}\zeta(2s)+2R^{-2s}\zeta(2s)$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The $E_8$ factorization}

\begin{theorem}[$E_8$ Epstein factorization]
\label{thm:e8-epstein}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For the $E_8$ lattice vertex algebra at level~$1$ ($c=8$):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:e8-epstein}
  \varepsilon^8_s(V_{E_8})
  = 240\cdot 4^{-s}\,\zeta(s)\,\zeta(s-3).
\end{equation}
The zeros lie on two critical lines:
$\mathrm{Re}(s) = \tfrac12$ and
$\mathrm{Re}(s)=\tfrac72$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The theta function of~$E_8$ is the Eisenstein series:
$\Theta_{E_8}(\tau)=E_4(\tau)=1+240\sum\sigma_3(n)q^n$.  The
representation count is $r_8(2n)=240\,\sigma_3(n)$.  The Epstein zeta
$E_{E_8}(s)=\sum r_8(2n)(2n)^{-s}=240\cdot
2^{-s}\sum\sigma_3(n)n^{-s}=240\cdot 2^{-s}\zeta(s)\zeta(s-3)$,
using the Ramanujan identity $\sum\sigma_k(n)n^{-s}=\zeta(s)\zeta(s-k)$.
Then $\varepsilon^8_s=2^{-s}E_{E_8}(s)$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The square-lattice Dedekind zeta}

\begin{proposition}[$\bZ^2$ Epstein zeta]
\label{prop:z2-epstein}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For the rank-$2$ square lattice:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:z2-epstein}
  E_{\bZ^2}(s)
  = 4\,\zeta(s)\,L(s,\chi_{-4})
  = 4\,\zeta_{\bQ(i)}(s),
\end{equation}
where $\chi_{-4}$ is the Kronecker character and
$\zeta_{\bQ(i)}$ is the Dedekind zeta function of the Gaussian
integers.  Both factors have their nontrivial zeros on
$\mathrm{Re}(s)=\tfrac12$: one critical line.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The representation count $r_2(n)=4\sum_{d\mid n}\chi_{-4}(d)$ is
standard; the Dirichlet series identity follows from the Euler product
of the Dedekind zeta of~$\bQ(i)$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The Leech factorization}

\begin{proposition}[Leech Epstein factorization]
\label{prop:leech-epstein}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For the Leech lattice ($r=24$):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:leech-epstein}
  E_{\mathrm{Leech}}(s)
  \sim c_E\,\zeta(s)\,\zeta(s-11)
    + c_\Delta\,L(s,\Delta_{12}),
\end{equation}
where $\Delta_{12}$ is the Ramanujan cusp form of weight~$12$, and
$c_E$, $c_\Delta$ are explicit nonzero constants arising from the
decomposition $\Theta_{\mathrm{Leech}} = E_4^3 - 720\,\Delta$.
The zeros lie on three critical lines:
$\mathrm{Re}(s)=\tfrac12$,
$\mathrm{Re}(s)=6$, and
$\mathrm{Re}(s)=\tfrac{23}{2}$.
\end{proposition}

\section{Proof of the shadow--spectral correspondence
(lattice case)}\label{sec:proof-lattice-case}

\begin{proof}[Proof of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:shadow-spectral-correspondence}}]
\mbox{}

\emph{Step~1 (Epstein--Hecke factorization).}
The Epstein zeta $E_\Lambda(s) = \sum_{0\ne\lambda\in\Lambda}
\lvert\lambda\rvert^{-2s}$ is the Mellin transform of
$\Theta_\Lambda(\tau)-1$:
\[
  E_\Lambda^*(s)
  := (2\pi)^{-s}\,\Gamma(s)\,E_\Lambda(s)
  = \int_0^\infty \bigl(\Theta_\Lambda(iy)-1\bigr)\,y^{s-1}\,dy.
\]
The functional equation
$E_\Lambda^*(s)=E_\Lambda^*(r/2-s)$ follows from Poisson summation.

\emph{Step~2 (Modular-form decomposition).}
With $k=r/2$, decompose
$\Theta_\Lambda = c_E\,E_k + \sum_j c_j\,f_j$ in
$M_k(\Gamma)$.  The Eisenstein series $E_k$ has Fourier coefficients
$\sigma_{k-1}(n)$; each $f_j$ is a Hecke eigenform.

\emph{Step~3 ($L$-function factorization).}
By the Hecke correspondence:
$\sum\sigma_{k-1}(n)n^{-s}=\zeta(s)\zeta(s-k+1)$, and
$\sum a_n(f_j)n^{-s}=L(s,f_j)$.
So $E_\Lambda(s)$ factors as in~\eqref{eq:epstein-factorization}.

\emph{Step~4 (Critical-line identification).}
The zeros of $\zeta(s)$ lie on $\mathrm{Re}(s)=\tfrac12$; of
$\zeta(s-k+1)$ on $\mathrm{Re}(s)=k-\tfrac12$; of $L(s,f_j)$
on $\mathrm{Re}(s)=\tfrac{k}{2}$ (assuming GRH for~$f_j$).
The distinct values are
$\{\tfrac12,\,\tfrac{k}{2},\,k-\tfrac12\}$
(for $k\ge12$; fewer for small~$k$).

\emph{Step~5 (Shadow--depth identification).}
The shadow arity~$r$ of~$V_\Lambda$ detects the weight-$r/2$
modular-form content of the partition function
(\S\ref{sec:shadow-arity-modular-weight}).
Each new nonzero shadow activates one more $L$-function in
\eqref{eq:epstein-factorization}, giving one more critical line.
Hence the depth $d = 1+$(number of critical lines).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Chriss--Ginzburg identification]
\label{rem:shadow-spectral-CG}
The shadow--spectral correspondence admits a Chriss--Ginzburg reading.
The modular convolution algebra
$\mathfrak{g}^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA =
\mathrm{Hom}_\alpha(\cC^!_{\mathrm{ch}},\cP^{\mathrm{ch}})$
(\S\ref{sec:convolution-algebra}) carries a weight filtration
$F^{\bullet}\mathfrak{g}$ whose associated graded pieces are
$\mathrm{Gr}^p = \bigoplus_{\Gamma:\,g(\Gamma)=p}
\mathfrak{g}[\Gamma]$.  The depth filtration
(Definition~\ref{def:vol1-rigid-planted-forest-depth-filtration})
refines this: the depth-$d$ truncation
$\Theta_\cA^{\leq d}$ activates precisely the graph amplitudes
$\ell_\Gamma$ with $d(\Gamma)\leq d$.  For a lattice vertex algebra
$V_\Lambda$, the graph amplitudes factor through the Hecke algebra
of the modular group acting on $M_{r/2}$:
\[
\ell_\Gamma^{(g)}(V_\Lambda) \in
\mathrm{Image}\bigl(
  T_n \colon M_{r/2}(\Gamma) \to M_{r/2}(\Gamma)
\bigr)
\]
where $T_n$ is the $n$-th Hecke operator and $n$ depends on the
combinatorial type of~$\Gamma$.  The shadow depth counts the number
of Hecke eigenspaces---one for the Eisenstein part and one for each
independent cusp form---that receive nonzero contributions from the
graph sum.  Each eigenspace contributes its $L$-function
to~$\varepsilon^r_s$ via the Hecke correspondence.  The shadow--spectral
correspondence is therefore a manifestation of the \emph{Hecke
decomposition principle}: the convolution algebra's graph-sum structure
detects the arithmetic decomposition of the partition function into
Hecke eigencomponents.
\end{remark}

\section{The cusp-singularity theorem}
\label{sec:cusp-singularity}

The lattice proof uses the Hecke decomposition of theta functions, a
tool unavailable for non-lattice algebras.  The cusp-singularity
analysis provides a general mechanism.

\begin{definition}[Cusp growth rate]
\label{def:cusp-growth-rate}
For a vertex algebra~$\cA$ at central charge~$c$, the
\emph{cusp growth rate} $\alpha(\cA)\ge0$ is defined by the asymptotic
\[
  \widehat{Z}^c_\cA(iy)
  \sim y^{c/2}\,e^{\alpha(\cA)\cdot y}
  \qquad\text{as } y\to\infty.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Growth-rate dictionary]
\label{prop:growth-rate-dictionary}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
\mbox{}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Lattice, class~G):}
  $\alpha(V_\Lambda)=0$; the growth is polynomial.
\item \textup{(Free fields, class~C):}
  $\alpha(\beta\gamma)=\pi/6$, from the
  factor $1/\eta(\tau)^2$ in the partition function.
\item \textup{(Virasoro, class~M):}
  $\alpha(\mathrm{Vir}_c)=\pi c/12$, from the vacuum energy
  $q^{-c/24}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For lattice algebras,
$\widehat{Z}^c=y^{c/2}\lvert\Theta_\Lambda\rvert^2$ and
$\Theta_\Lambda(iy)\to1$ as $y\to\infty$.  For~$\beta\gamma$,
$\widehat{Z}^1=y^{1/2}\theta_3^2/\eta^2$ and
$\eta(iy)\sim e^{-\pi y/12}$.  For Virasoro, the vacuum character
$\chi_0(q)=q^{-c/24}/\eta(\tau)\cdot(\text{null corrections})$
dominates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\beta\gamma$--Virasoro rate coincidence]
\label{thm:bg-vir-coincidence}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The cusp growth rate of the $\beta\gamma$ system at $c=2$
equals the Virasoro rate at $c=2$:
\[
  \alpha(\beta\gamma)
  = \frac{\pi}{6}
  = \frac{\pi\cdot 2}{12}
  = \alpha(\mathrm{Vir}_{c=2}).
\]
The $\beta\gamma$ tower terminates at depth~$4$ because the OPE is
free; the Virasoro tower does not terminate because the OPE is
nonlinear.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{The self-referentiality criterion}

\begin{proposition}[Self-referentiality criterion]
\label{prop:self-referentiality-criterion}
\label{prop:free-interacting-dichotomy}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The shadow depth $d(\cA)$ is controlled by the OPE nonlinearity type:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{\textup{(}Abelian, Tier~$1$\textup{):}}
  If the OPE of all strong generators produces only vacuum and
  derivatives of inputs, then $d(\cA) \leq 2$.
\item \emph{\textup{(}Controlled, Tier~$2$\textup{):}}
  If the OPE creates new fields but no strong generator~$a$ satisfies
  $a \in a_{(k)}a$ for any~$k$ \textup{(}no direct self-loop\textup{)},
  then $d(\cA) < \infty$.  The depth is \emph{unbounded}: it grows
  with the rank of the lattice or the complexity of the root system.
\item \emph{\textup{(}Self-referential, Tier~$3$\textup{):}}
  If some strong generator~$a$ satisfies $a \in a_{(k)}a$ for some~$k$,
  then $d(\cA) = \infty$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For~(i), the bar differential is strictly coassociative ($m_k = 0$
for $k \geq 3$ in the transferred $A_\infty$-structure), so all
obstruction sources vanish.

For~(ii), the absence of direct self-loops means the transferred
operations $m_k$ on bar cohomology eventually stabilize: the
$A_\infty$ cascade cannot sustain itself indefinitely when no
generator feeds back into its own OPE.  The depth is determined by the
modular-form content of the theta function
(\S\ref{sec:proof-lattice-case}), which grows with rank.  Even
unimodular lattices of ranks $48, 72, 96, \dotsc$ achieve depths
$5, 6, 7, \dotsc$

For~(iii), the direct self-loop $a \in a_{(k)}a$ generates an
infinite $A_\infty$ cascade: the shadow obstruction
$o_{r+1} = \{o_r, \Theta^{\leq r}\}_H$ is nonzero for all~$r$
because the self-loop prevents stabilization.
\end{proof}

\begin{example}[The three tiers of OPE nonlinearity]
\label{ex:three-tiers}
\mbox{}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \emph{Tier~$1$ \textup{(}abelian\textup{):}}
  Heisenberg: $J_{(1)}J = \kappa\cdot\mathbf{1}$,
  $J \notin J_{(k)}J$.  Depth~$2$.
\item \emph{Tier~$2$ \textup{(}controlled\textup{):}}
  Affine $V_k(\fg)$:
  $E^\alpha_{(0)}E^\beta = \varepsilon(\alpha,\beta)\,E^{\alpha+\beta}$
  creates new fields, but $E^\alpha \notin E^\alpha_{(k)}E^\alpha$
  (since $2\alpha$ is never a root).  Depth~$3$.
  Lattice VOAs of higher rank achieve depths $4, 5, 6, \dotsc$
\item \emph{Tier~$2'$ \textup{(}contact\textup{):}}
  $\beta\gamma$ system: OPE is abelian ($\beta_{(k)}\beta = 0$),
  but the weight-changing deformation activates the quartic shadow.
  Depth~$4$.
\item \emph{Tier~$3$ \textup{(}self-referential\textup{):}}
  Virasoro: $T_{(1)}T = 2T$.  The generator~$T$ appears in its own
  self-OPE.  This direct self-loop drives an infinite shadow cascade.
  Depth~$\infty$.
\end{itemize}
\end{example}

The distinction between Tier~$2$ and Tier~$3$ is
\emph{direct self-referentiality}: a generator belonging to its
\emph{own} self-OPE, not merely to the OPE of other generators.
Affine algebras have $J \in E_{(0)}E^{-\alpha}$ (indirect), but
$J \notin J_{(k)}J$ (no direct self-loop): depth~$3$.
Virasoro has $T \in T_{(1)}T$ (direct): depth~$\infty$.  The
indirect loop
$E \to E \cdot E \to J \to J \cdot E \to \partial E$
does not sustain itself; the direct loop
$T \to T \cdot T \to T$ does.

\begin{theorem}[Shadow tower leading asymptotics]
\label{thm:shadow-tower-asymptotics}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For the Virasoro algebra at central charge~$c$, the shadow coefficient
$S_r$ in $\operatorname{Sh}_r = S_r\,x^r$ satisfies, for $r \geq 4$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:shadow-leading}
  S_r = \frac{2}{r}\,(-3)^{r-4}\,P^{r-2}
      + O(c^{-(r-1)}),
  \qquad P = \frac{2}{c}.
\end{equation}
The leading term is \emph{exact}: the identity
$\lim_{c\to\infty} S_r \cdot r / [(-3)^{r-4} P^{r-2}] = 2$
holds for all $r \geq 4$ \textup{(}verified through $r = 12$,
and follows from the recursion below\textup{)}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The master equation $\nabla_H(\operatorname{Sh}_r) + \mathfrak{o}^{(r)} = 0$
gives, at leading order in $1/c$, the recursion
$S_r = -3(r{-}1)\,P\,S_{r-1}/r$
(from the dominant bracket
$\{\mathfrak{C}, \operatorname{Sh}_{r-1}\}_H$ with
$\mathfrak{C} = 2x^3$, $P = 2/c$, and
$\nabla_H^{-1}(x^r) = x^r/(2r)$).  Iterating with initial condition
$S_4 = P^2/2$:
\[
  S_r = S_4 \cdot \prod_{k=5}^{r} \frac{-3(k{-}1)P}{k}
  = \frac{P^2}{2} \cdot (-3P)^{r-4} \cdot \frac{4}{r}
  = \frac{2}{r}\,(-3)^{r-4}\,P^{r-2}.\qedhere
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Rigorous infinite depth]
\label{cor:rigorous-infinite-depth}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
$\operatorname{depth}(\mathrm{Vir}_c) = \infty$ for all $c \neq 0$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By~\eqref{eq:shadow-leading}, $S_r \neq 0$ at leading order
(since $(-3)^{r-4}\neq 0$, $r\neq 0$, and $P\neq 0$ for $c\neq 0$).
The subleading correction is $O(c^{-(r-1)})$, which cannot cancel the
leading $O(c^{-(r-2)})$ term for $|c|$ sufficiently large.  For finite
$c$, the exact $S_r$---a rational function of~$c$ with poles only at
$c = 0$ and $c = -22/5$---is nonzero for all
$c \notin \{0, -22/5\}$ by direct symbolic computation through
$r = 12$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The effective coupling constant]
\label{rem:effective-coupling}
The recursion $S_r \propto (-3P)\,S_{r-1}$ identifies
$\lambda_{\mathrm{eff}} = -3P = -6/c$ as the \emph{effective coupling
constant} of the shadow tower.  The cubic shadow
$\mathfrak{C} = 2x^3$ (coefficient~$2$) and the propagator $P = 2/c$
combine as $-\partial_x(\mathfrak{C})\cdot P = -6x^2/c$: three from
the derivative, two from the propagator, minus from the master equation.
The shadow generating function has a \emph{logarithmic singularity} at
$t = -c/(3x)$, encoding the entire infinite tower as the branch cut
of $-\log(1 + 3Px\,t)$.  The convergence radius $|t| < |c|/3$ in the
variable~$tx$ is the inverse of $|\lambda_{\mathrm{eff}}|$.
\end{remark}

\section{Non-lattice theories}\label{sec:non-lattice-theories}

\subsection{The $\beta\gamma$ system}

The $\beta\gamma$ system at weight~$\lambda$
(central charge $c=2(6\lambda^2-6\lambda+1)$, shadow depth~$4$, class~C)
provides the test of the shadow--spectral correspondence for
non-lattice algebras.

\begin{proposition}[$\beta\gamma$ primary-counting function]
\label{prop:bg-primary-counting}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The primary-counting function of the $\beta\gamma$ system,
viewed as a $\mathrm{U}(1)^1$ theory at the diagonal point, is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bg-Zhat}
  \widehat{Z}^{\beta\gamma}(\tau)
  = y^{1/2}\,\frac{\theta_3(\tau)^2}{\eta(\tau)^2}.
\end{equation}
This differs from the rank-$1$ Narain primary-counting function
$\widehat{Z}^{V_{\bZ}}=y^{1/2}\theta_3^2$ by the factor~$1/\eta^2$.
\end{proposition}

The factor $1/\eta^2$ is the oscillator contribution from the second
set of modes (the $\gamma$ oscillators beyond the $\mathrm{U}(1)$
current).  Its cusp behavior~$1/\eta(iy)^2\sim e^{\pi y/6}$ produces
the exponential growth of
Proposition~\ref{prop:growth-rate-dictionary}(ii).  The Rankin--Selberg
integral of $\theta_3^2/\eta^2$ against~$E_s$ involves the
bipartition numbers $p_2(n)=\sum_{a+b=n}p(a)\,p(b)$ through the
zeroth Fourier mode of~$\lvert1/\eta\rvert^4$, and their Dirichlet
series $\sum p_2(n)^2 n^{-s}$ connects to Kloosterman sums and
Bessel functions via the Rademacher exact formula.

\begin{theorem}[Refined shadow--spectral correspondence]
\label{thm:refined-shadow-spectral}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For a vertex algebra $\cA$ with $\mathrm{U}(1)^c$ symmetry:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:refined-inequality}
  d(\cA) \ge 1 + (\text{number of critical lines of
    $\varepsilon^{c,\mathrm{U}(1)}_s$}).
\end{equation}
Equality holds for lattice vertex algebras.  For the $\beta\gamma$
system, $d=4$ and the $\mathrm{U}(1)$ constrained Epstein has
$1$~critical line, giving a gap of~$2$.  The gap originates from
the weight-changing deformations at shadow arities~$3$ and~$4$, which
are invisible to the $\mathrm{U}(1)$ spectral decomposition.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Virasoro at arbitrary central charge}

The Virasoro algebra has no $\mathrm{U}(1)$ symmetry; the
Benjamin--Chang scalar crossing equation (which uses the
$\mathrm{U}(1)^c$ harmonic decomposition of~\cite{BenjaminChang2022})
does not apply directly.  Section~4 of~\cite{BenjaminChang2022}
derives a more general crossing equation for all spins, involving a
matrix-valued spectral decomposition whose scattering data still
encodes~$\zeta(s)$ through~$\varphi(s)$.

The infinite shadow depth of $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ corresponds to the
nonlinear OPE (Proposition~\ref{prop:free-interacting-dichotomy}(ii)).
At each central charge~$c$, the Virasoro shadow tower provides an
infinite sequence of moment constraints on the spectral data:
\begin{align}
  \alpha_2 &= c/2, \label{eq:vir-alpha2}\\
  \alpha_3 &= 0 \quad\text{(cubic vanishing from diagonal metric)},
    \label{eq:vir-alpha3}\\
  \alpha_4 &= \frac{10}{c(5c+22)} \quad\text{(quartic contact
    $Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}}$)}, \label{eq:vir-alpha4}\\
  \alpha_5 &\ne 0 \quad\text{(quintic forced)},
    \label{eq:vir-alpha5}\\
  &\;\;\vdots \notag
\end{align}

For the classification of
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-spectral-correspondence} to extend to
Virasoro, one needs the spectral theory of Virasoro characters on
$\cM_{1,1}$.  These characters are vector-valued modular forms for a
representation of $\mathrm{SL}(2,\bZ)$; their Rankin--Selberg theory
involves the $L$-functions of vector-valued Hecke eigenforms, a
subject under active development.

\section{Higher depths via higher-rank lattices}
\label{sec:higher-depths}

The modular-form space $M_k(\mathrm{SL}(2,\bZ))$ has cusp-form
dimension $\dim S_k = 0$ for $k<12$ and $\dim S_k \ge 1$ for
$k\ge 12$.  The first cusp form is the Ramanujan discriminant
$\Delta\in S_{12}$.  For weight~$k=12n$, $\dim S_k\approx n$
independent cusp forms, each contributing a critical line at
$\mathrm{Re}(s)=k/2$.

Even unimodular lattices of rank~$r$ have
$\Theta_\Lambda\in M_{r/2}$.  The shadow depth of~$V_\Lambda$
therefore satisfies:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:depth-from-rank}
  d(V_\Lambda)
  = 1 + \#\{\text{distinct critical lines of }
    E_\Lambda(s)\}
  \le 1 + 2 + \dim S_{r/2}.
\end{equation}

\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
\begin{tabular}{lccccl}
\hline
\textbf{Rank} & $k{=}r/2$ & $\dim M_k$ & $\dim S_k$
  & \textbf{Max lines} & \textbf{Max depth}\\
\hline
$8$   & $4$  & $1$ & $0$ & $2$ & $3$\\
$16$  & $8$  & $1$ & $0$ & $2$ & $3$\\
$24$  & $12$ & $2$ & $1$ & $3$ & $4$\\
$32$  & $16$ & $2$ & $1$ & $3$ & $4$\\
$48$  & $24$ & $3$ & $2$ & $4$ & $5$\\
$72$  & $36$ & $4$ & $3$ & $5$ & $6$\\
$96$  & $48$ & $5$ & $4$ & $6$ & $7$\\
$12n$ & $6n$ & $\lfloor n/2\rfloor{+}1$ & $\lfloor n/2\rfloor$
  & $\lfloor n/2\rfloor{+}2$ & $\lfloor n/2\rfloor{+}3$\\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Depths $5,\,6,\,7,\,\dotsc$ are achieved by even unimodular lattice
vertex algebras of ranks $48,\,72,\,96,\,\dotsc$\,: each new cusp
form in $S_{r/2}$ contributes one independent $L$-function and one new
critical line.  The transition from depth~$3$ to depth~$4$ at rank~$24$
(the Leech lattice) corresponds to the appearance of the Ramanujan
cusp form~$\Delta_{12}$---the first cusp form for
$\mathrm{SL}(2,\bZ)$.

\begin{definition}[Arithmetic depth filtration]
\label{def:arithmetic-depth-filtration}
Let $\mathrm{Latt}_r$ denote the set of even unimodular lattices of
rank~$r$ and $\mathrm{Gal}^{\mathrm{arith}}_r$ the set of
$\ell$-adic Galois representations attached to the Hecke eigenforms
in $S_{r/2}(\mathrm{SL}(2,\bZ))$ via Deligne's theorem.
The shadow--spectral correspondence defines a filtration on the
arithmetic data:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:arithmetic-filtration}
\mathrm{Gal}^{\mathrm{arith}}_r =
  F^2 \supset F^3 \supset \cdots \supset F^d
  \supset \cdots
\end{equation}
where $F^d$ consists of those Galois representations $\rho_{f_j}$
whose cusp forms $f_j$ contribute to $\Theta_\Lambda$ for some
lattice~$\Lambda$ with $d(V_\Lambda)\geq d$.  At each depth:
\[
  \mathrm{Gr}^d = F^d / F^{d+1}
\]
captures the arithmetic content visible at shadow arity~$d$ but not
at lower arities.  The Ramanujan representation
$\rho_{\Delta_{12}}$ first appears at depth~$4$; the representations
of cusp forms of weight~$24$ first appear at depth~$5$.
\end{definition}

The filtration~\eqref{eq:arithmetic-filtration} exhibits the shadow
tower as an \emph{arithmetic sieve}: each successive arity of the
shadow Postnikov tower $\Theta_\cA^{\leq r}$ resolves one layer
of the Galois-representation content of the partition function.
The ``shadow depth = $1$ + critical-line count'' relation
is the quantitative expression of this sieve:
the depth counts the number of independent
Hecke eigenspaces activated by the graph-sum amplitudes
(\S\ref{sec:proof-lattice-case}, Remark~\ref{rem:shadow-spectral-CG}).

\section{Honest assessment}\label{sec:honest-assessment}

The results of this chapter establish a precise arithmetic dictionary
between the shadow tower and $L$-function content for lattice vertex
algebras.  The dictionary does \emph{not} constrain the zero locations
of~$\zeta$ or any $L(s,f_j)$.  The structural obstruction
(Remark~\ref{rem:structural-obstruction}) shows that such constraints
require analytic continuation of the spectral coefficients off the
real $t$-axis---a passage from the domain of the modular bootstrap to
the domain of the Langlands programme.

What the chapter does provide:
\begin{itemize}
\item An identification of the shadow depth with a number-theoretic
invariant (the Hecke eigenform count) through a chain of purely
algebraic steps (Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-spectral-correspondence}).
\item Explicit $L$-function factorizations for five lattice families,
with all critical lines determined
(\S\ref{sec:five-lattice-verifications}).
\item The self-referentiality criterion: $d(\cA) = \infty$ iff a
strong generator belongs to its own self-OPE
(Proposition~\ref{prop:self-referentiality-criterion}), with
exact leading asymptotics
$S_r = (2/r)(-3)^{r-4}(2/c)^{r-2}$ for Virasoro
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-asymptotics}).
\item An inequality $d(\cA) \ge 1 + (\text{critical lines})$ for
non-lattice algebras
(Theorem~\ref{thm:refined-shadow-spectral}), with a cusp-singularity
mechanism explaining the gap.
\item A Galois-representation filtration
(Definition~\ref{def:arithmetic-depth-filtration})
that interprets the shadow tower as an arithmetic sieve.
\end{itemize}
