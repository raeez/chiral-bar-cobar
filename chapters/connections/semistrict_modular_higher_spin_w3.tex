\section{The semistrict \texorpdfstring{$W_3$}{W3} sector}
\label{sec:why-semistrict-modular-W3}

Protected holographic sectors are useful only when the algebraic
object being compared is fixed. Here the object is the formal
three-dimensional holomorphic-topological Poisson sigma model at the
vacuum attached to the classical $W_3$ Poisson vertex algebra, together
with its central Hodge-shadow extension. It is not the physical boundary
chiral algebra and it is not the chiral derived centre.

The boundary PVA controls the Taylor degree of this formal moduli
problem. For the classical $W_3$ PVA the only nonlinear coefficients in
the $\lambda$-brackets are quadratic, so the associated cyclic
$L_\infty$ algebra has no brackets beyond $\ell_3$. The modular problem
over a fixed scalar Hodge shadow is therefore a filtered cubic recursion
around a central Maurer--Cartan base point.

The structural statement is:
\begin{center}
\emph{the specialization of the universal shadow obstruction tower
$\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ to the classical $W_3$ family has a semistrict
three-dimensional HT formal envelope, with brackets only through
\(\ell_3\); the modular shadow tower still has a degree-$4$ quartic
resonance class \(\mathfrak Q\), a boundary obstruction created by
contact and sewing terms rather than by an $\ell_4$ vertex.}
\end{center}
Every construction below is an MC element in a specified completed
convolution or cyclic $L_\infty$ algebra, and every finite-degree
shadow truncation is a projection of the bar-intrinsic $\Theta_\cA$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}).

\begin{convention}[Scope and semantic level for this chapter]
\label{conv:scope-semistrict-W3}
The H/M/S distinctions of Chapter~\ref{chap:concordance} remain in force.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
 \item Statements about universal Maurer--Cartan classes, formal moduli problems, or derived centers are \textbf{H}-level when they are model-independent.
 \item Explicit BV differentials, filtered completions, obstruction classes, and graph identities are \textbf{M}-level.
 \item Scalar characteristic packages, such as the Hodge shadow $\kappa(\mathcal A)\lambda_g$, are \textbf{S}-level.
 \item Throughout this chapter, ``modular'' refers to the open-category trace $\mathrm{Tr}_{\mathcal C}$ and clutching morphisms on the open factorisation category $\mathcal C^{\mathrm{op}}$ on $(X,D,\tau)$, not to a closed-algebra property of $W_3^{\mathrm{cl}}$ or $\HS(W_3^{\mathrm{cl}})$. Modular reconstruction is the level-$5\to 4$ functor of master-concordance Convention~\textup{MA-3}; closed shadows have modular consequences only when traced through that open category.
\end{enumerate}
The proved modular Koszul core of
Chapters~\ref{chap:bar-cobar} and~\ref{chap:higher-genus} is not
altered here. The universal class $\Theta_{\mathcal A}$ is proved in
the completed coinvariant modular convolution algebra
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}). Its $W_3$ specialization has two
separate projections. The local three-dimensional HT formal moduli
problem has the semistrict cyclic $L_\infty$ envelope
\(\HS(W_3^{\mathrm{cl}})\), with no brackets $\ell_n$ for $n \ge 4$.
The modular shadow tower has degree projections of $\Theta_{W_3}$:
the cubic shadow~$\mathfrak{C}$ is the degree-$3$ projection, while the
quartic resonance class~$\mathfrak{Q}$ is the degree-$4$ contact/sewing
obstruction in the shadow tower. The second statement does not assert
an $\ell_4$ operation in the semistrict HT envelope.
\end{convention}

\begin{remark}[The smallest nonlinear higher-spin model]
\label{rem:why-W3-first}
The classical $W_3$ algebra is the first nontrivial point where three constraints meet at once.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
 \item The three-dimensional HT theory attached to the $W_3$ PVA is written in closed form in~\cite{KhanZeng25}.
 \item Boundary chiral algebras, higher operations, and homotopy transfer are available in the surrounding HT literature~\cite{CDG20,Zeng23,GKW24}.
 \item The Virasoro field and the spin-$3$ current give a genuine higher-spin sector, while the quadratic nonlinearity keeps the formal moduli problem semistrict.
\end{enumerate}
\end{remark}

\section{From boundary \texorpdfstring{$\lambda$}{lambda}-brackets to bulk semistrictity}
\label{sec:boundary-to-bulk-semistrictity}

The order of boundary nonlinearity controls the degree of the bulk deformation algebra. At this interface the boundary PVA, the BV description of the bulk, and cyclic $L_\infty$-algebras meet.

\begin{definition}[Nonlinear generator-degree]
\label{def:nonlinear-generator-degree-chapter}
Let $V$ be a freely generated Poisson vertex algebra on generators $u_i$. We say that $V$ has \emph{nonlinear generator-degree at most $d$} if every coefficient appearing in every $\lambda$-bracket
\[
\{u_i{}_{\lambda}u_j\}
\]
is a differential polynomial in the generators and their $z$-derivatives whose monomials involve at most $d$ generator factors.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Higher-spin envelope]
\label{def:higher-spin-envelope-chapter}
Let $V$ be a freely generated Poisson vertex algebra for which the
Khan--Zeng three-dimensional HT Poisson sigma model is defined as a
formal BV theory at the vacuum. Let $\mathfrak M_V$ be that formal
moduli problem, and let
\[
Q_{\mathrm{BV}}=\sum_{n\ge 1}Q_n
\]
be the Taylor expansion of the BV homological vector field into homogeneous polynomial vector fields of degree~$n$. The \emph{higher-spin envelope} of~$V$ is the completed cyclic $L_\infty$-algebra
\[
\HS(V)
\]
whose $n$-ary bracket $\ell_n$ is the shifted multilinear operation corresponding to~$Q_n$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-degree theorem for polynomial PVAs \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:finite-degree-polynomial-pva-chapter}
\textup{[}Type signature:
$(\mathrm{quadrant}=\mathrm{Open}\cap\mathrm{Chain},
\mathrm{presentation}=\mathrm{chain\text{-}level\ cyclic\ }L_\infty,
\mathrm{level}=2\to 3,
\mathrm{hypothesis\ package}=\mathrm{freely\ generated\ PVA};
\mathrm{nonlinear\ generator\text{-}degree\ }d;
\mathrm{Khan\text{--}Zeng\ formal\ BV\ HT\ sigma\ model\ exists\ at\ vacuum})$\textup{]}
The statement is at the local formal envelope of the HT bulk, not at
the modular-shadow level: a finite-degree generator OPE controls the
local cyclic $L_\infty$ envelope, but the modular shadow tower may
admit higher-degree projections from contact and sewing strata
\textup{(}Convention~\textup{\ref{conv:scope-semistrict-W3}}\textup{)}.
Let $V$ be a freely generated Poisson vertex algebra of nonlinear
generator-degree at most $d$, and assume its three-dimensional HT
Poisson sigma model exists in the formal BV sense used in
Definition~\ref{def:higher-spin-envelope-chapter}. Then $\HS(V)$ is a
completed cyclic $L_\infty$-algebra satisfying
\[
\ell_n=0
\qquad
\text{for all } n>d+1.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
In the formal neighbourhood of the vacuum, the BV differential of the
three-dimensional Poisson sigma model associated to~$V$ has the form
\[
Q_{\mathrm{BV}}\Phi^i
=
 d_{\mathrm{HT}}\Phi^i + \Pi^{ij}(\partial,\Phi)\,\eta_j,
\]
\[
Q_{\mathrm{BV}}\eta_i
=
 d_{\mathrm{HT}}\eta_i
+
\frac12\sum_{n\ge 0}(-\partial)^n\eta_j
\frac{\partial \Pi^{jk}(\partial,\Phi)}{\partial(\partial^n\Phi^i)}\eta_k,
\]
where $\Pi$ records the $\lambda$-bracket coefficients of~$V$; compare~\cite{KhanZeng25}. If each coefficient of~$\Pi$ has nonlinear generator-degree at most~$d$, then the first formula is polynomial of total degree at most $d+1$: a degree-$\le d$ coefficient multiplies one ghost $\eta_j$. In the second formula, differentiation with respect to a field or jet variable reduces the field degree by one, while multiplication by two ghosts raises the total degree by two; hence this expression also has degree at most $d+1$.

Therefore the Taylor expansion of $Q_{\mathrm{BV}}$ contains no homogeneous terms of degree $>d+1$. Equivalently,
\[
Q_n=0 \qquad (n>d+1),
\]
and hence the corresponding brackets satisfy
\[
\ell_n=0 \qquad (n>d+1).
\]
Since $Q_{\mathrm{BV}}^2=0$, the collection $\{\ell_n\}$ obeys the $L_\infty$ identities. Since $Q_{\mathrm{BV}}$ is Hamiltonian for the BV pairing, the resulting $L_\infty$-algebra is cyclic.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Semistrictity of the classical $W_3$ bulk \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:semistrictity-classical-W3-chapter}
\textup{[}Type signature:
$(\mathrm{Open}\cap\mathrm{Chain},\mathrm{chain\text{-}level},
\mathrm{level}=2,
\mathrm{hypothesis\ package}=\mathrm{classical\ }W_3\mathrm{\ PVA};
\mathrm{Khan\text{--}Zeng\ formal\ BV\ envelope};
\mathrm{quadratic\ nonlinearity\ in\ }\lambda\mathrm{\text{-}brackets})$\textup{]}
The corollary asserts semistrictity of the formal HT envelope $\HS(W_3^{\mathrm{cl}})$, not of the
modular shadow tower of the quantum $\Walg_3$: the quartic resonance
class $\mathfrak Q$ of the shadow tower remains nonzero
\textup{(}Convention~\textup{\ref{conv:scope-semistrict-W3}}\textup{)}.
Quadratic PVA $\lambda$-bracket nonlinearity does \emph{not} entail
all-loop quantum HT theory; passage to the quantum $\cW_3$ shadow
requires the Yamada finite presentation and the KZ analytic SDR.
Let $W_3^{\mathrm{cl}}$ denote the classical $W_3$ Poisson vertex algebra. Then
\[
\HS(W_3^{\mathrm{cl}})
\]
is semistrict and cyclic:
\[
\ell_n=0
\qquad
(n\ge 4).
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The only genuinely nonlinear monomials in the classical $W_3$ $\lambda$-brackets are quadratic, namely the $T^2$ and $T\partial T$ terms in the $\{W{}_{\lambda}W\}$ bracket; see~\cite[\S\,4]{KhanZeng25}. Thus the nonlinear generator-degree is $d=2$, and Theorem~\ref{thm:finite-degree-polynomial-pva-chapter} implies $\ell_n=0$ for $n\ge 4$.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Tree identity for semistrict cyclic theories \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:tree-identity-semistrict-chapter}
Let $L$ be a semistrict cyclic $L_\infty$-algebra. Consider a connected tree-level Feynman graph with $n$ external legs, $v_3$ trivalent interaction vertices, and $v_4$ quadrivalent interaction vertices. Then
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:tree-identity-semistrict-chapter}
v_3+2v_4=n-2.
\end{equation}
In particular, only finitely many interaction trees occur at fixed~$n$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let $e$ be the number of internal edges. For a connected tree,
\[
e=v_3+v_4-1.
\]
Counting half-edges gives
\[
3v_3+4v_4=2e+n.
\]
Substituting the tree relation into the half-edge count yields
\[
3v_3+4v_4=2(v_3+v_4-1)+n,
\]
which simplifies to~\eqref{eq:tree-identity-semistrict-chapter}. Finiteness follows immediately because only finitely many integer pairs $(v_3,v_4)$ satisfy the relation for fixed~$n$.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Reduction of the gravitational problem]
\label{rem:first-reduction-gravitational-problem}
Theorem~\ref{thm:finite-degree-polynomial-pva-chapter} and
Corollary~\ref{cor:semistrictity-classical-W3-chapter} identify the
classical $W_3$ formal HT sector as a semistrict higher-spin
deformation problem. Its tree-level graphs use only trivalent and
quadrivalent vertices, with external valence constrained by the
diophantine identity~\eqref{eq:tree-identity-semistrict-chapter}.
\end{remark}

\section{Central Hodge-shadow geometry}
\label{sec:central-hodge-shadow-geometry}

The scalar Hodge-shadow package proved earlier lives at S-level
(Chapter~\ref{chap:concordance}). Its lift to a Maurer--Cartan element
in a canonical central extension solves the central sector of the
universal problem, not the noncentral semistrict sector.

\begin{definition}[Hodge-shadow coefficient algebra]
\label{def:hodge-shadow-coefficient-algebra-chapter}
Define the completed commutative Hopf algebra
\[
\mathcal H_{\lambda}:=\widehat{\Sym}\!\Big(\bigoplus_{g\ge 1}\Bbbk\,\sigma_g\Big),
\]
where each $\sigma_g$ has cohomological degree~$0$ and Hodge weight
\[
\wt(\sigma_g):=2g.
\]
The coproduct is determined by primitivity:
\[
\Delta(\sigma_g)=\sigma_g\otimes 1 + 1\otimes \sigma_g.
\]
There is a realization morphism
\[
\rho:\mathcal H_{\lambda}\longrightarrow \prod_{g\ge 1} H^{2g}(\mathcal M_g,\mathbb Q),
\qquad
\rho(\sigma_g)=\lambda_g.
\]
This is the completed polynomial algebra on the Hodge-shadow
coefficients only. It does not assert that the full tautological ring is
freely generated by the classes \(\lambda_g\), and it does not include
the noncentral \(W_3\) contact or sewing sectors.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Central Hodge-shadow extension]
\label{def:central-hodge-shadow-extension-chapter}
Let $L$ be a complete $L_\infty$-algebra with brackets $\ell_n$. Define
its one-dimensional central extension by
\[
\widetilde L:=L\oplus \Bbbk\varepsilon,
\qquad
|\varepsilon|=1,
\]
where \(\widetilde\ell_1(\varepsilon)=0\),
\(\widetilde\ell_n(x_1,\ldots,x_n)=\ell_n(x_1,\ldots,x_n)\) for
\(x_i\in L\), and every higher bracket with at least one
\(\varepsilon\)-input is zero.
The associated modular extension is
\[
L^{\modu}(L):=\widetilde L\,\widehat{\otimes}\,\mathcal H_{\lambda},
\]
and its noncentral summand is
\[
L^{\nc}(L):=L\,\widehat{\otimes}\,\mathcal H_{\lambda}
\subset L^{\modu}(L).
\]
Write
\[
\operatorname{ctr}:L^{\modu}(L)\to \mathcal H_{\lambda}
\]
for projection to the coefficient of~$\varepsilon$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Canonical central Hodge-shadow lift \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:canonical-central-hodge-shadow-lift-chapter}
For every complete $L_\infty$-algebra $L$ and every scalar
$\kappa\in\Bbbk$, the element
\[
\Theta^{\sh}_{\kappa}:=\kappa\,\varepsilon\otimes \sum_{g\ge 1}\sigma_g
\in L^{\modu}(L)^1
\]
is a Maurer--Cartan element. It is the unique Maurer--Cartan element in
the central summand
\(\Bbbk\varepsilon\widehat\otimes\mathcal H_\lambda\) whose central
trace equals
\[
\operatorname{ctr}(\Theta^{\sh}_{\kappa})=\kappa\sum_{g\ge 1}\sigma_g.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Because $\varepsilon$ is closed and all brackets with an
$\varepsilon$-input vanish,
\[
\widetilde\ell_1(\Theta^{\sh}_{\kappa})=0,
\qquad
\widetilde\ell_n(\Theta^{\sh}_{\kappa},\ldots,\Theta^{\sh}_{\kappa})=0
\quad(n\ge2),
\]
so the Maurer--Cartan equation holds identically.

Now let $\Theta$ be a Maurer--Cartan element in
\(\Bbbk\varepsilon\widehat\otimes\mathcal H_\lambda\) with the same
central trace. Then
\[
\Theta=\varepsilon\otimes h
\]
for some $h\in \mathcal H_{\lambda}$. The trace condition forces
\[
h=\kappa\sum_{g\ge 1}\sigma_g,
\]
and therefore $\Theta=\Theta^{\sh}_{\kappa}$. The infinite sum converges in the Hodge-weight completion because $\wt(\sigma_g)=2g\to\infty$.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Clutching additivity and duality symmetry \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:clutching-duality-shadow-lift-chapter}
The canonical central Hodge-shadow lift satisfies the following functorial properties.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
 \item Under the Hopf coproduct on $\mathcal H_{\lambda}$,
 \[
 \Delta\bigl(\Theta^{\sh}_{\kappa}\bigr)
 =\Theta^{\sh}_{\kappa}\otimes 1 + 1\otimes \Theta^{\sh}_{\kappa}.
 \]
 \item If an involution $\mathbb D$ acts by $\mathbb D(\varepsilon)=-\varepsilon$ and the scalar shadow satisfies $\kappa(A^!)=-\kappa(A)$ \textup{(}this holds for affine Kac--Moody and free-field families, but not in general; for Virasoro, $\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c) + \kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}) = 13$, as recorded in Chapter~\ref{chap:concordance}\textup{)}, then
 \[
 \mathbb D\bigl(\Theta_A^{\sh}\bigr)=\Theta_{A^!}^{\sh}.
 \]
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Part \textup{(i)} follows from the primitivity of the generators $\sigma_g$:
\[
\Delta\Big(\sum_{g\ge1}\sigma_g\Big)
=
\sum_{g\ge1}(\sigma_g\otimes 1+1\otimes\sigma_g)
=
\Big(\sum_{g\ge1}\sigma_g\Big)\otimes 1 + 1\otimes\Big(\sum_{g\ge1}\sigma_g\Big).
\]
Multiplying by the central factor $\kappa\varepsilon$ gives the claim.

For part \textup{(ii)},
\[
\mathbb D\bigl(\Theta_A^{\sh}\bigr)
=
\kappa(A)\,\mathbb D(\varepsilon)\otimes\sum_{g\ge1}\sigma_g
=
-\kappa(A)\,\varepsilon\otimes\sum_{g\ge1}\sigma_g
=
\kappa(A^!)\,\varepsilon\otimes\sum_{g\ge1}\sigma_g
=
\Theta_{A^!}^{\sh}.
\]
This is exactly the stated symmetry.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Fiber decomposition over the shadow base point \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:fiber-decomposition-shadow-base-point-chapter}
For any complete $L_\infty$-algebra $L$ and scalar $\kappa$, translation
by the shadow base point induces a canonical bijection
\[
\MC\bigl(L^{\nc}(L)\bigr)
\xrightarrow{\ \sim\ }
\Bigl\{\Theta\in\MC\bigl(L^{\modu}(L)\bigr)
\,\Big|\,
\operatorname{ctr}(\Theta)=\kappa\sum_{g\ge1}\sigma_g\Bigr\},
\qquad
\alpha\longmapsto \Theta^{\sh}_{\kappa}+\alpha.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Every degree-one element of $L^{\modu}(L)$ with central trace
$\kappa\sum_{g\ge1}\sigma_g$ has a unique decomposition
\[
\Theta=\Theta^{\sh}_{\kappa}+\alpha,
\qquad
\alpha\in L^{\nc}(L)^1.
\]
Because $\Theta^{\sh}_{\kappa}$ is closed and all mixed brackets with it
vanish, the Maurer--Cartan equation for $\Theta$ reduces to
\[
\sum_{n\ge1}\frac1{n!}\ell_n(\alpha,\ldots,\alpha)=0,
\]
which is exactly the Maurer--Cartan equation in $L^{\nc}(L)$. This
proves the claimed bijection.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Shadow-centered reduction \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:shadow-centered-reduction-chapter}
Inside the central-extension model of
Definition~\ref{def:central-hodge-shadow-extension-chapter}, the
modular lift problem above a fixed scalar shadow reduces canonically to
the noncentral sector. Every noncentral semistrict refinement of the
classical $W_3$ formal problem is measured relative to the single
distinguished point $\Theta^{\sh}_{W_3}$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:fiber-decomposition-shadow-base-point-chapter} to
the complete semistrict $L_\infty$ algebra $\HS(W_3^{\mathrm{cl}})$ and
to the scalar $\kappa(W_3)$.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\section{From quadratic-cubic relations to cubic Maurer--Cartan theory}
\label{sec:quadratic-cubic-to-cubic-MC}

The $W_3$ deformation problem is an MC equation in a specified
convolution algebra. On the finite quadratic-cubic presentation used
below, the dual cooperadic datum is $3$-truncated; hence the associated
convolution $L_\infty$-algebra has $\ell_n = 0$ for $n \ge 4$. The MC
equation is cubic in this presentation. This is the algebraic analogue
of the semistrict HT envelope, not an assertion that every shadow
projection of $\Theta_{W_3}$ terminates at degree~$3$.

\begin{definition}[Truncated cooperad]
\label{def:truncated-cooperad-chapter}
Let $C$ be a coaugmented conilpotent dg cooperad, and let $\bar C$ denote the coaugmentation coideal. For each $n\ge 2$, write
\[
\bar\Delta_n:\bar C\longrightarrow \bar C^{\otimes n}
\]
for the reduced $n$-ary decomposition map. We say that $C$ is \emph{$N$-truncated} if
\[
\bar\Delta_n=0
\qquad
(n>N).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-degree convolution theorem \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:finite-degree-convolution-chapter}
Let $C$ be an $N$-truncated conilpotent dg cooperad and $P$ a dg operad. Then the convolution $L_\infty$-algebra
\[
\mathfrak{Conv}(C,P):=\Hom_{\mathbb S}(\bar C,P)
\]
satisfies
\[
\ell_n=0
\qquad
(n>N).
\]
Equivalently, its Maurer--Cartan equation truncates at degree~$N$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The standard convolution construction defines the $n$-ary bracket by composing the $n$-ary reduced decomposition of $C$ with $n$ maps into~$P$, followed by operadic composition in~$P$. Up to the standard Koszul signs,
\[
\ell_n(f_1,\dots,f_n)
=
\gamma_P\circ(f_1\otimes\cdots\otimes f_n)\circ\bar\Delta_n.
\]
If $C$ is $N$-truncated, then $\bar\Delta_n=0$ for all $n>N$. Hence $\ell_n=0$ for all $n>N$. The Maurer--Cartan equation therefore contains only terms of degree at most~$N$.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Quadratic-cubic chiral datum]
\label{def:quadratic-cubic-chiral-datum-chapter}
Let
\[
A=\mathrm{Free}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)/(R_2\oplus R_3)
\]
be a finite-type chiral algebra generated by a finite-dimensional graded space~$V$, with quadratic relation space $R_2\subset V^{\otimes 2}$ and cubic relation space $R_3\subset V^{\otimes 3}$. Define its \emph{quadratic-cubic dual cooperadic datum}
\[
A^{\vee}_{\qc}
\]
to be the cofree conilpotent cooperad on $sV$ equipped with a codifferential
\[
d=d_2+d_3
\]
whose binary and ternary Taylor coefficients are dual to the inclusions of $R_2$ and~$R_3$, and whose higher Taylor coefficients vanish.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Truncated Maurer--Cartan set]
\label{def:truncated-MC-set-chapter}
If $L$ is an $L_\infty$-algebra with $\ell_n=0$ for $n>N$, define
\[
\MC_{\le N}(L)
:=
\Bigl\{\alpha\in L^1\;\Big|\; \sum_{n=1}^{N}\frac{1}{n!}\ell_n(\alpha,\dots,\alpha)=0\Bigr\}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Quadratic-cubic twisting theorem \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:quadratic-cubic-twisting-theorem-chapter}
\textup{[}Type signature:
$(\mathrm{Open}\cap\mathrm{Chain},\mathrm{chain\text{-}level},
\mathrm{level}=1\to 2,
\mathrm{hypothesis\ package}=
\mathrm{finite\text{-}type\ quadratic\text{-}cubic\ chiral\ algebra};
A=\mathrm{Free}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)/(R_2\oplus R_3);
B\mathrm{\ complete\ dg\ chiral})$\textup{]}
Let $A=\mathrm{Free}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)/(R_2\oplus R_3)$ be a finite-type quadratic-cubic chiral algebra, and let $B$ be a complete dg chiral algebra. Then there is a natural bijection
\[
\Hom_{\mathrm{ch}}(A,B)
\cong
\MC_{\le 3}\!\bigl(\Hom_{\mathbb S}(A^{\vee}_{\qc},\End_B)\bigr).
\]
Equivalently, maps from $A$ into $B$ are controlled by the cubic Maurer--Cartan equation
\[
\partial\alpha+\frac12\ell_2(\alpha,\alpha)+\frac16\ell_3(\alpha,\alpha,\alpha)=0.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
A chiral algebra map $A\to B$ is the same as a map
\[
\phi:\mathrm{Free}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)\to B
\]
that vanishes on $R_2$ and~$R_3$. By freeness, such a map is determined by a linear map
\[
f:V\to B.
\]
Via suspension and the standard free/cofree adjunction, $f$ corresponds to a degree-one element
\[
\alpha_f\in \Hom_{\mathbb S}(A^{\vee}_{\qc},\End_B).
\]
The binary component of the codifferential on $A^{\vee}_{\qc}$ records the quadratic relation space, so the quadratic part of the Maurer--Cartan expression for $\alpha_f$ vanishes exactly when $\phi(R_2)=0$. Likewise, the ternary component records the cubic relation space, so the cubic part vanishes exactly when $\phi(R_3)=0$.

By construction, $A^{\vee}_{\qc}$ has no higher Taylor coefficients. Hence it is $3$-truncated, and Theorem~\ref{thm:finite-degree-convolution-chapter} implies that the convolution algebra has no brackets $\ell_n$ for $n\ge 4$. Therefore the full Maurer--Cartan equation is precisely the cubic equation displayed above. Solving it is equivalent to requiring that the free map $\phi$ kill both relation spaces $R_2$ and $R_3$, which is equivalent to the factorization of $\phi$ through the quotient $A$. This establishes the desired bijection.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The first nonquadratic step]
\label{rem:first-nonquadratic-step}
The usual quadratic Maurer--Cartan correspondence is the zeroth approximation. Theorem~\ref{thm:quadratic-cubic-twisting-theorem-chapter} is the first nonlinear upgrade needed for $W$-algebras: the Maurer--Cartan problem is still finite, but cubic rather than quadratic, the algebraic expression of semistrictity.
\end{remark}

\section{The weighted modular semistrict envelope of \texorpdfstring{$W_3$}{W3}}
\label{sec:weighted-modular-semistrict-envelope}

The preceding ingredients assemble the central-shadow-fixed semistrict model
of the $W_3$ modular problem. The shadow obstruction
tower of Part~\ref{part:bar-complex} gives the ambient framework:
$\Theta_{W_3}^{\le r}$
is the projection of the bar-intrinsic MC element to degree~$\le r$,
and each level is a finite graph sum with obstruction class
$o_{r+1}(W_3)$ in the cyclic deformation complex. For $W_3$,

\begin{proposition}[Admissibility and finite-dimensional weight slices \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:admissibility-finite-slices-chapter}
The filtration $F^{\bullet}L_{W_3}^{\modu}$ is complete, separated, and admissible:
\[
\ell_1(F^N)\subseteq F^N,
\qquad
\ell_2(F^i,F^j)\subseteq F^{i+j},
\qquad
\ell_3(F^i,F^j,F^k)\subseteq F^{i+j+k}.
\]
Each quotient
\[
F^N L_{W_3}^{\modu}\big/ F^{N+1}L_{W_3}^{\modu}
\]
is finite-dimensional.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Completeness and separatedness are built into the weight-adic completion. The differential $\ell_1$ is induced by the linearized BV differential and preserves total weight. The operations $\ell_2$ and $\ell_3$ are built from multiplication, derivatives, and the explicit polynomial coefficients appearing in the $W_3$ brackets; these operations can only add weights, never decrease them. Hence the stated filtration inclusions hold.

For finite-dimensionality, first note that there are only finitely many jet monomials in $T$ and~$W$ of a fixed total weight, because such a monomial is determined by a partition of an integer into parts of the form $m+2$ and $m+3$. Likewise, there are only finitely many monomials in the Hodge-shadow variables $\sigma_g$ of any given total Hodge weight because $\wt(\sigma_g)=2g$ and integer partitions are finite in number. Combining the two observations yields finite-dimensionality of each weight slice.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cubic weight recursion around the shadow base point \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:cubic-weight-recursion-chapter}
\textup{[}Type signature:
$(\mathrm{Open}\cap\mathrm{Chain},\mathrm{chain\text{-}level},
\mathrm{level}=2\to 3,
\mathrm{hypothesis\ package}=
\mathrm{semistrict\ }L_{W_3}^{\modu};
\mathrm{Hodge\text{-}weight\ admissible\ filtration};
\mathrm{finite\text{-}dimensional\ slices})$\textup{]}
Write a putative modular lift in the form
\[
\Theta
=
\Theta_{W_3}^{\sh}+\alpha,
\qquad
\alpha=\sum_{N\ge1}\alpha_N,
\qquad
\alpha_N\in F^N/F^{N+1}.
\]
Then $\Theta\in \MC(L_{W_3}^{\modu})$ if and only if, for every $N\ge1$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:cubic-weight-recursion-chapter}
\ell_1(\alpha_N)
=
-\frac12\sum_{i+j=N}\pi_N\ell_2(\alpha_i,\alpha_j)
-\frac16\sum_{i+j+k=N}\pi_N\ell_3(\alpha_i,\alpha_j,\alpha_k),
\end{equation}
where $\pi_N:F^N\to F^N/F^{N+1}$ is the quotient map. In particular, each stage of the modular lift problem is a finite linear equation in the unknown coefficient~$\alpha_N$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Proposition~\ref{prop:canonical-central-hodge-shadow-lift-chapter}, the base point $\Theta_{W_3}^{\sh}$ is closed and central. By Corollary~\ref{cor:semistrictity-classical-W3-chapter}, the only nonzero brackets in the noncentral sector are $\ell_1$, $\ell_2$, and~$\ell_3$. Therefore the Maurer--Cartan equation for $\Theta$ is exactly
\[
\ell_1(\alpha)+\frac12\ell_2(\alpha,\alpha)+\frac16\ell_3(\alpha,\alpha,\alpha)=0.
\]
Expanding $\alpha=\sum_{N\ge1}\alpha_N$ and collecting the part of weight~$N$ yields~\eqref{eq:cubic-weight-recursion-chapter}, thanks to the admissibility of the filtration proved in Proposition~\ref{prop:admissibility-finite-slices-chapter}. Finiteness of each stage follows from finite-dimensionality of the quotient $F^N/F^{N+1}$.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Cubic obstruction classes \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:cubic-obstruction-classes-chapter}
At weight $N$, the obstruction to solving~\eqref{eq:cubic-weight-recursion-chapter} lies in
\[
H^2\bigl(F^N/F^{N+1},\ell_1\bigr),
\]
and depends polynomially of degree at most three on the lower-weight coefficients $\alpha_1,\dots,\alpha_{N-1}$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The right-hand side of~\eqref{eq:cubic-weight-recursion-chapter} is $\ell_1$-closed by the semistrict $L_\infty$ identities. Hence it defines a cohomology class in $H^2(F^N/F^{N+1},\ell_1)$, which vanishes exactly when the weight-$N$ coefficient can be chosen. Because only $\ell_2$ and $\ell_3$ occur, the dependence on lower-weight data is at most cubic.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Stable-graph identity for semistrict modular theories \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:stable-graph-identity-chapter}
Let $\Gamma$ be a connected stable graph contributing to a modular semistrict cyclic $L_\infty$-theory. Suppose $\Gamma$ has total genus $g$, $n$ external legs, vertex genera $g(v)$, $v_3$ trivalent interaction vertices, and $v_4$ quadrivalent interaction vertices. Then
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:stable-graph-identity-chapter}
v_3+2v_4+2\sum_{v\in V(\Gamma)}g(v)=n+2g-2.
\end{equation}
In particular,
\[
v_3+2v_4\le n+2g-2,
\]
so only finitely many stable graphs contribute for fixed $(g,n)$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let $E(\Gamma)$ and $V(\Gamma)$ denote the edge and vertex sets, and let $k_v$ be the valence of a vertex~$v$. Since each internal edge contributes two half-edges,
\[
\sum_{v\in V(\Gamma)}k_v=2|E(\Gamma)|+n.
\]
Hence
\[
\sum_{v\in V(\Gamma)}(k_v-2)
=n+2\bigl(|E(\Gamma)|-|V(\Gamma)|+1\bigr)-2.
\]
Now
\[
b_1(\Gamma)=|E(\Gamma)|-|V(\Gamma)|+1,
\qquad
g=b_1(\Gamma)+\sum_{v\in V(\Gamma)}g(v),
\]
so
\[
\sum_{v\in V(\Gamma)}(k_v-2)
=
 n+2g-2-2\sum_{v\in V(\Gamma)}g(v).
\]
If every interaction vertex has valence $3$ or $4$, then the left-hand side is $v_3+2v_4$. This gives~\eqref{eq:stable-graph-identity-chapter}, and finiteness follows immediately.\qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Completed boundary model]
\label{def:completed-boundary-model-chapter}
Let $L$ be a positively weighted complete semistrict $L_\infty$-algebra whose weight slices are finite-dimensional. Define its \emph{completed boundary model} by
\[
\Bdy(L):=\widehat{\CE}^{\bullet}(\tau_{>0}L)
:=
\prod_{m\ge 0}\Sym^m\!\bigl((\tau_{>0}L[1])^{\vee}\bigr),
\]
with continuous differential
\[
d_{\CE}=d_1+d_2+d_3
\]
dual to the coderivation determined by $\ell_1$, $\ell_2$, and~$\ell_3$.
This is a Chevalley--Eilenberg model attached to the positive-weight
part of the formal bulk envelope. It is not, by definition, the physical
boundary chiral algebra, and its Hochschild centre is not identified
with the classical $W_3$ PVA without an additional open/closed
comparison map.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Well-definedness of the completed boundary model \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:well-definedness-completed-boundary-model-chapter}

\[
\Bdy(W_3):=\Bdy\bigl(\HS(W_3^{\mathrm{cl}})\bigr)
\]
is well-defined.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The positive-weight truncation $\tau_{>0}L$ has finite-dimensional weight slices, so its graded dual is defined weightwise and the completed symmetric algebra above makes sense. The maps dual to $\ell_1$, $\ell_2$, and~$\ell_3$ are continuous because these brackets preserve positive weight and raise filtration as in Proposition~\ref{prop:admissibility-finite-slices-chapter}. Their sum therefore defines a continuous derivation $d_{\CE}$ of the completed symmetric algebra.

The square $d_{\CE}^2$ is dual to the square of the coderivation on the completed symmetric coalgebra $\widehat{S}^c(\tau_{>0}L[1])$ induced by the brackets $\ell_1$, $\ell_2$, and~$\ell_3$. Because these brackets satisfy the semistrict $L_\infty$ identities, that coderivation squares to zero. Therefore $d_{\CE}^2=0$, and $\Bdy(L)$ is a complete dg algebra.\qedhere
\end{proof}
