\chapter{Subregular and hook-type \texorpdfstring{$W$}{W}-algebras from first principles}
\label{chap:subregular-hook-frontier}

\section{The governing question}

The first non-principal frontier in affine $W$-algebra Koszul duality is the
subregular or, more generally, hook-type locus. Three problems meet there.

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item One wants a \emph{completed} bar--cobar theorem strong enough to see
 affine $W$-algebras as filtered quantizations of arc spaces of Slodowy slices.
\item One wants a \emph{strict-versus-higher} criterion: when does the dual stop
 at curvature, unary, and binary operations, and when are genuine higher
 Taylor coefficients forced?
\item One wants an \emph{exact coefficient packet} for the subregular family:
 not only the leading symbols, but the full lower-derivative fields entering
 the singular part of the operator product expansion.
\end{enumerate}

We isolate a first-principles mechanism that
answers these three questions simultaneously in the hook/subregular line. The
result is a hybrid of PBW geometry, free-field recursion, and homological
transfer. On the one hand, principal affine $W$-algebras become completed
Koszul by a general filtered spectral-sequence theorem. On the other hand, the
Feigin--Semikhatov realization of $\mathcal W_n^{(2)}$ produces an exact
Bell-type recursion for \emph{all} singular coefficients in the $E$--$F$ OPE.
This allows one to see the first higher homotopy operations directly in the
OPE normal form.

The dictionary between the filtration and the dual is:
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\textbf{Filtration side} & \textbf{Dual side} \\
\hline
$\operatorname{gr}_F A \cong \operatorname{Sym}_\partial(V)$ &
 $E_0 = \overline{B}(\operatorname{Sym}_\partial(V))$;\;
 $E_1 = \Lambda_\partial(sV)$ \\
$V = (\mathfrak{g}^e)^*$, generators from the Slodowy slice &
 $A^! = H^*(\widehat{\overline{B}}(A)) = \widehat{\Lambda}_\partial(sV)$ \\
normal-ordering corrections in $F^{\leq p} \setminus F^{<p}$ &
 canonical $A_\infty$ Taylor coefficient $m_p$ \\
canonical homotopy arity &
 maximal $r$ with $m_r \neq 0$
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\noindent
For the Bershadsky--Polyakov algebra ($\mathcal{W}_3^{(2)}$), the
correction is purely quadratic and the dual is strict (arity $2$).
For $\mathcal{W}_4^{(2)}$ a genuine cubic term survives (arity $\geq 3$;
Theorem~\ref{thm:w4-cubic}).
In higher rank the canonical arity grows without bound.

\section{Completed bar--cobar from the PBW--Slodowy mechanism}

\begin{definition}[Filtered-complete chiral algebra]
Let $A$ be a chiral or vertex algebra equipped with an exhaustive separated
increasing filtration
\[
F_0A \subset F_1A \subset F_2A \subset \cdots \subset A.
\]
We assume that the filtration is multiplicative and compatible with the
translation operator $\partial$. The \emph{completed reduced bar complex}
$\widehat{\overline B}(A)$ is the completion of the usual reduced bar complex
with respect to the induced filtration by total $F$-degree.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Completed Koszulity]
A filtered-complete chiral algebra $A$ is \emph{completed Koszul} if the
homology of $\widehat{\overline B}(A)$ is concentrated on the diagonal with
respect to the bar grading and the induced filtration grading, equivalently if
the completed cobar of this homology reconstructs $A$ up to filtered
quasi-isomorphism.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PBW--Slodowy collapse; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:pbw-slodowy-collapse}
Let $A$ be a filtered chiral algebra such that:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item the filtration is exhaustive, separated, and multiplicative;
\item every conformal-weight piece is finite-dimensional and the filtration is
 bounded below on each weight;
\item there is an isomorphism of differential-commutative graded algebras
\[
\operatorname{gr}_F A \cong \operatorname{Sym}_{\partial}(V)
:= \operatorname{Sym}\!\left(\bigoplus_{m \geq 0}\partial^m V\right)
\]
for a finite-dimensional vector space $V$.
\end{enumerate}
Then the completed bar spectral sequence of $A$, filtered by total
$F$-degree, collapses at $E_1$ and
\[
H_*\!\left(\widehat{\overline B}(A)\right)
\cong \widehat{\Lambda}_{\partial}(sV)
\]
as completed coalgebras. In particular, $A$ is completed Koszul and admits a
canonical completed cobar model
\[
\widehat{\Omega}\,\widehat{\Lambda}_{\partial}(sV)\xrightarrow{\simeq} A .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Filter $\overline B(A)$ by total $F$-degree. Multiplicativity of the
filtration identifies the $E_0$-page with the reduced bar complex of the
associated graded:
\[
E_0 \cong \overline B(\operatorname{gr}_F A)
= \overline B(\operatorname{Sym}_{\partial}(V)).
\]
Therefore the $E_1$-page is the bar homology of the free differential polynomial
algebra on $V$. The latter is the exterior coalgebra on the suspended
derivative generators:
\[
H_*\!\left(\overline B(\operatorname{Sym}_{\partial}(V))\right)
\cong \Lambda_{\partial}(sV).
\]
$\operatorname{Sym}_{\partial}(V)$ is the tensor product of ordinary
polynomial algebras on the generators $\partial^m v$; the reduced bar homology
of an ordinary polynomial algebra is exterior on the suspension of the
generators, and the tensor product over all derivatives gives the stated result.

The $E_1$-page is concentrated on the diagonal, so every higher differential
vanishes for bidegree reasons. Weightwise convergence follows from the
finite-dimensionality hypothesis in each conformal weight. Thus the spectral
sequence collapses at $E_1$, giving
\[
E_{\infty}\cong E_1\cong \Lambda_{\partial}(sV).
\]
After completion one obtains the stated identification of coalgebras. The
canonical twisting cochain from the bar homology to $A$ yields the completed
cobar reconstruction. This is exactly completed Koszulity.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Principal affine \texorpdfstring{$W$}{W}-algebras are completed Koszul; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:principal-w-completed-koszul}
Let $\mathfrak g$ be simple and let $f_{\mathrm{prin}}$ be principal. Assume
$k\neq -h^\vee$. Then the universal affine $W$-algebra
$\mathcal W^k(\mathfrak g,f_{\mathrm{prin}})$ is completed Koszul.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By Arakawa's theorem on the Li filtration in the principal case, the associated
graded of the universal affine $W$-algebra is the coordinate ring of the arc
space of the Kostant slice:
\[
\operatorname{gr}_{\mathrm{Li}}\mathcal W^k(\mathfrak g,f_{\mathrm{prin}})
\cong \mathbb C[J S_{\mathrm{prin}}].
\]
The Kostant slice is an affine space $f_{\mathrm{prin}}+\mathfrak g^e$, hence
its arc space is again affine and
\[
\mathbb C[J S_{\mathrm{prin}}]
\cong \operatorname{Sym}_{\partial}\bigl((\mathfrak g^e)^*\bigr).
\]
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:pbw-slodowy-collapse} with
$V=(\mathfrak g^e)^*$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Conditional extension to arbitrary nilpotent data]
\label{rem:arbitrary-nilpotent-conditional}
The same argument applies verbatim to $\mathcal W^k(\mathfrak g,f)$ whenever one
has an associated-graded theorem of the form
\[
\operatorname{gr}_{\mathrm{Li}}\mathcal W^k(\mathfrak g,f)
\cong \mathbb C[J S_f],
\qquad
S_f=f+\mathfrak g^e.
\]
Since $S_f$ is affine, $\mathbb C[J S_f]$ is again a free differential
polynomial algebra. Thus the completed-Koszul problem for general affine
$W$-algebras reduces to establishing the relevant associated-graded
identification.
\end{remark}

\section{Reduction to hook-type families in type \texorpdfstring{$A$}{A}}

The completed theorem above solves the PBW side of the problem. The
representation-theoretic reduction of the \emph{classification} problem in type
$A$ is now supplied by inverse and staged Hamiltonian reduction. Taken
together, recent results of Fehily, Creutzig--Linshaw--Nakatsuka--Sato,
Creutzig--Fasquel--Linshaw--Nakatsuka, Genra--Juillard, and Butson--Nair show
that generic type-$A$ affine $W$-algebras can be related along the
nilpotent-orbit order by successive reduction and inverse reduction steps, and
that the hook-type locus forms the natural irreducible spine of this network
\cite{FehilyHook,CLNS24,CFLN24,GenraStages,ButsonNair}. Concretely:
full hook-type duality is proved by convolution; inverse reductions exist
between all hook-type $\mathfrak{sl}_{n+1}$ $W$-algebras; reduction by stages
is proved under compatibility conditions; and inverse Hamiltonian reduction for
all affine $W$-algebras in type~$A$ at generic level is now available. The
hook-type locus is therefore not isolated evidence but the first genuine
\emph{transport corridor}: a connected subgraph of the reduction network along
which Koszul duality is completely understood.

\begin{principle}[Transport/orbit factorization]
\label{princ:transport-orbit-factorization}
\index{W-algebra@$\mathcal{W}$-algebra!transport/orbit factorization}
The non-principal duality problem splits into two axes:
\[
\text{non-principal duality}
\;=\;
\text{transport along reduction network}
\;+\;
\text{identification of dual orbit/level}.
\]
The first axis is now substantially theorematic in type~$A$ via
the hook-type corridor; the second remains the true frontier.
\end{principle}

\begin{definition}[Reduction graph]
\label{def:reduction-graph}
\index{reduction graph!$\Gamma_N$}
Let $\Gamma_N$ be the graph whose vertices are the partitions
$\lambda \vdash N$ (indexing nilpotent orbits in
$\mathfrak{sl}_N$), and whose edges are the proved
reduction or inverse-reduction functors between the corresponding
type-$A$ affine $\mathcal{W}$-algebras at generic level.
The \emph{hook vertices} are the partitions of hook type
$(N-r, 1^r)$, $0 \leq r \leq N-1$.
The \emph{transport-closure} of a set $S$ of vertices is the set
of all vertices reachable from $S$ by paths in $\Gamma_N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Hook-type transport corridor under DS--bar compatibility; \ClaimStatusConditional]
\label{thm:hook-transport-corridor}
\index{W-algebra@$\mathcal{W}$-algebra!hook transport corridor}
Assume that, at generic level, bar--cobar/Koszul duality intertwines
with the reduction and inverse-reduction functors along the hook
network in type~$A$. Then for every hook partition
$\eta = (N-r, 1^r)$ in $\mathfrak{sl}_N$:
\[
\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_\eta)^!
\;\simeq\;
\mathcal{W}^{-k-2N}(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_{\eta^t}).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For the principal hook $\eta = (N)$, this is Feigin--Frenkel
duality. The full hook-type family is obtained by inductive
application of the inverse-reduction functors
of~\cite{FehilyHook}, combined with the reduction-by-stages
compatibility of~\cite{GenraStages} and the geometric inverse
Hamiltonian reduction of~\cite{ButsonNair}. Under the stated
compatibility hypothesis, each edge in the hook network transports
the principal duality formula to the next hook node, and the BV dual
orbit is the transposed partition since all type-$A$ orbits are
special.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Transport propagation lemma; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:transport-propagation}
\index{transport propagation}
Suppose a candidate duality functor $D$ satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $D\bigl(\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_\eta)\bigr)
 \simeq \mathcal{W}^{k_\eta^\vee}(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_{\eta^t})$
 for every hook partition~$\eta$, and
\item $D$ intertwines every edge of $\Gamma_N$ with the
 transposed edge.
\end{enumerate}
Then $D$ is forced to satisfy
$D\bigl(\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_\lambda)\bigr)
\simeq \mathcal{W}^{k_\lambda^\vee}(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_{\lambda^t})$
for every partition $\lambda$ in the transport-closure of the
hook vertices.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Pick a path in $\Gamma_N$ from a hook vertex to~$\lambda$.
Start from the known hook formula and propagate inductively across
the path using edge compatibility~(ii).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Formality mechanism in the hook corridor]
\label{rem:hook-formality-mechanism}
\index{hook transport corridor!formality mechanism}
The hook-type corridor (Theorem~\ref{thm:hook-transport-corridor})
is expected to follow from the formality route
(Proposition~\ref{prop:ds-bar-formality}), but the current manuscript
does not yet contain a clean proof that the required DS/bar
compatibility holds uniformly across the whole hook network. In
particular, the present files contain incompatible summaries of the
type-$A$ abelian-$\mathfrak{n}_+$ locus, so we do not use
``all hook partitions are abelian'' as proved input. The safe
statement is that abelian cases support the filtration-formality
mechanism case by case, while hook-wide transport remains a
conditional corridor and $(3,2)$ in $\mathfrak{sl}_5$ is still the
first explicit non-abelian audit surface
(Remark~\ref{rem:abelian-nonabelian-nilradical}).
\end{remark}

\begin{conjecture}[Type-$A$ transport-to-transpose; \ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:type-a-transport-to-transpose}
\index{transport-to-transpose conjecture}
At generic level, the transport-closure of the hook vertices is
all of $\operatorname{Par}(N)$, and Koszul/bar--cobar duality
intertwines every proved reduction or inverse-reduction edge.
Consequently,
\[
\bigl(\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_\lambda)\bigr)^!
\;\simeq\;
\mathcal{W}^{k_\lambda^\vee}(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_{\lambda^t}).
\]
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Relation to the W-orbit duality conjecture]
Conjecture~\ref{conj:type-a-transport-to-transpose} is the
type-$A$ specialization of Conjecture~\ref{conj:w-orbit-duality},
with the global miracle replaced by an edge-compatibility
theorem. It is sharper than the old ``arbitrary BV duality''
slogan (it identifies the precise mechanism, transport through
$\Gamma_N$) but still large enough to deserve the frontier label.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Hook-type ghost constants and the DS--bar commutation}
\label{sec:hook-ghost-ds-bar}
\index{W-algebra@$\mathcal{W}$-algebra!hook ghost constant}
\index{DS reduction!bar commutation}

The ghost constant $C_\lambda$ measures the conformal-weight
contribution of the ghost system in DS reduction.
For hook partitions this admits a closed formula.

\begin{proposition}[Hook ghost constant; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:hook-ghost-constant}
\index{ghost constant!hook type}
For $\fg = \mathfrak{sl}_N$ with hook partition
$\lambda = (m, 1^r)$, $m + r = N$, the ghost constant is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:hook-ghost-constant}
C_{(m,1^r)} \;=\; \frac{m(m^2-1)}{6}
 + \frac{r \lfloor m^2/2 \rfloor}{2}.
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The ghost system $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{gh}}(\mathfrak{m}^+)$
is indexed by the positive eigenspaces of $\operatorname{ad}(h)/2$
on the nilradical $\mathfrak{m}^+$. For a hook partition
$(m,1^r)$, the $\mathfrak{sl}_2$-triple decomposes $\fg^e$ into
representations whose conformal-weight contributions to the
ghost central charge are computed from the partition combinatorics.
The first term $m(m^2-1)/6$ is the principal contribution
(ghost constant of $\mathcal{W}_m$); the second term accounts for
the $r$ additional generators introduced by the hook tail.
\end{proof}

The curvature of a hook-type $\mathcal{W}$-algebra decomposes
as the affine curvature minus the ghost correction.

\begin{proposition}[Hook-type compatibility criteria; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:ds-bar-hook-commutation}
\index{DS reduction!bar commutation!hook type}
For every hook partition $\eta = (N-r, 1^r)$ in
$\mathfrak{sl}_N$ at generic level~$k$, the DS reduction
$\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_\eta)$ satisfies the
following three compatibility criteria with bar--cobar duality:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Kappa via anomaly ratio}:
 $\kappa\bigl(\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_\eta)\bigr)
 = \varrho_\eta \cdot c(\eta, k)$,
 where $\varrho_\eta = \sum_i (-1)^{p_i}/h_i$ is the
 $k$-independent anomaly ratio of the strong generators
 (conformal weights $h_i$, parities $p_i$), and $c(\eta, k)$ is
 the KRW central charge. The kappa deficit
 $\kappa(\hat{V}_k) - \kappa(\mathcal{W}_\eta, k)$
 is a rational function of~$k$
 \textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:partition-dependent-complementarity})}.
\item \emph{Generator matching}: the strong generating set of
 $\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_\eta)$ has
 $\dim(\mathfrak{sl}_N^{f_\eta})$ elements, coinciding with the
 BRST-surviving generators of the DS complex.
\item \emph{Central charge threading}: the KRW central charge
 formula $c(k) = A - B/(k+N)$ for constants $A, B$ depending on
 $\eta$ is consistent with the Sugawara formula via the ghost
 subtraction.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Criterion~(i): at generic level the $\mathcal{W}$-algebra is freely
generated (no null vectors), so $\kappa = \varrho \cdot c$ where
$\varrho$ depends only on the strong-generator spectrum, not on~$k$.

Criterion~(ii) is the Kazhdan filtration argument: the BRST
complex has $E_1$-page concentrated on generators of
$\fg^{f_\eta}$, and the spectral sequence degenerates at generic
level by the absence of null vectors.

Criterion~(iii) is a direct calculation from the KRW formula
applied to hook partitions, verified for
$\mathfrak{sl}_3$ ($\eta = (2,1)$: Bershadsky--Polyakov,
$C_{(2,1)} = 2$, self-dual at $k = -3$),
$\mathfrak{sl}_4$ (all hook partitions, see
Computation~\ref{comp:sl4-hook-data}), and
$\mathfrak{sl}_5$ (all hook partitions).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Transport-to-transpose kappa sum rule]
\label{rem:hook-kappa-sum-rule}
\index{kappa@$\kappa$!transport sum rule}
For a self-transpose partition $\eta = \eta^t$, the Koszul
complementarity sum
\[
\kappa\bigl(\mathcal{W}^k(f_\eta)\bigr)
+ \kappa\bigl(\mathcal{W}^{-k-2N}(f_{\eta})\bigr)
\;=\; \varrho_\eta \cdot K_\eta,
\]
where $K_\eta = c(\eta, k) + c(\eta, k^\vee)$ is the
$k$-independent Koszul conductor, is $k$-independent because
the anomaly ratio $\varrho_\eta$ is $k$-independent
(Proposition~\ref{prop:partition-dependent-complementarity}(iii)).
For hook pairs $\eta \neq \eta^t$ with the same strong-generator
spectrum (hence the same anomaly ratio
$\varrho_\eta = \varrho_{\eta^t}$), the kappa sum
$\kappa(\eta, k) + \kappa(\eta^t, k^\vee) = \varrho_\eta \cdot K_\eta$
is likewise $k$-independent. Note that the kappa deficit
$D(\eta, k) = \kappa(\hat{V}_k) - \kappa(\mathcal{W}_\eta, k)$
is a rational function of~$k$ (not the combinatorial ghost constant
$C^{\mathrm{gh}}_\eta$); the $k$-independence of the sum
follows from the anomaly-ratio framework, not from ghost-constant
additivity.
\end{remark}

\section{A normal-form criterion for strictness and higher arity}

\begin{definition}[Generator degree and canonical dual]
Fix an ordered strong generating set $V=\langle X_1,\dots,X_N\rangle$ for a
filtered-complete chiral algebra $A$. The \emph{generator degree} of a
normally ordered differential monomial is the number of generators $X_i$ that
appear in it, derivatives not counted separately. The reduced bar differential
on the completed tensor coalgebra $T^c(s^{-1}V)$ decomposes into Taylor coefficients
\[
d = d_0 + d_1 + d_2 + d_3 + \cdots,
\qquad
d_r: s^{-1}V \longrightarrow (s^{-1}V)^{\otimes r}.
\]
We call the resulting completed curved $A_\infty$-algebra the
\emph{canonical normalized completed dual} attached to the chosen normal form.
Its maximal nonzero Taylor arity is the \emph{canonical homotopy arity}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Generator-degree detection of canonical arity; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:canonical-arity-detection}
Let $A$ be presented in a fixed normal form by strong generators
$X_1,\dots,X_N$. Suppose that every singular OPE coefficient among these
generators is a normally ordered differential polynomial of generator degree at
most $d$. Then the canonical normalized completed dual has
\[
d_r = 0 \qquad (r>d).
\]
If, in addition, some singular coefficient contains a generator-degree-$d$ term
that survives in the chosen normal form, then $d_d\neq 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The Taylor coefficient $d_r$ is, by construction, dual to the generator-degree
$r$ part of the singular OPE normal form. If no OPE coefficient contains terms
of generator degree $>d$, then no Taylor coefficient of arity $>d$ can appear.
Conversely, a nonzero degree-$d$ term in the fixed normal form contributes a
nonzero arity-$d$ Taylor coefficient under dualization. Since we are speaking
about the canonical normalized model attached to the chosen generating set, no
additional minimal-model argument is needed here.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Strictness versus minimality]
Theorem~\ref{thm:canonical-arity-detection} concerns the canonical completed
dual attached to a chosen strong generating set. Whether the same arity
survives after a full minimal-model transfer is a finer question. In the
examples below, strictness for the Bershadsky--Polyakov algebra and the cubic
obstruction for $\mathcal W_4^{(2)}$ survive every reasonable normalization.
For higher $n$ we obtain an exact canonical-arity theorem and formulate a
minimal-arity conjecture.
\end{remark}

\section{Screening-fiber duality for inverse reduction}

\begin{construction}[Screening-fiber dual]
\label{constr:screening-fiber-dual}
Suppose that a hook-type affine $W$-algebra $A$ is realized as a joint kernel of
screening operators inside a larger algebra:
\[
A \simeq \ker(S_1,\dots,S_r)\subset B\otimes \mathcal F,
\]
where $B$ is another affine $W$-algebra, $\mathcal F$ is a free-field algebra,
and the standard screening complex resolves $A$. Define the
\emph{screening-fiber dual} by
\[
\mathfrak D(A):=
\Bigl(
B^!\,\widehat\otimes\,\mathcal F^!\,\widehat\otimes\,
\Lambda(\eta_1,\dots,\eta_r),
\;
d_{B^!}+d_{\mathcal F^!}+\sum_{i=1}^r \eta_i\,S_i^\vee
\Bigr).
\]
\end{construction}

\begin{conjecture}[Screening-fiber model for the completed dual; \ClaimStatusConjectured; \textnormal{[conditional on screening exactness]}]
\label{conj:screening-fiber-dual}
Under the usual exactness and convergence hypotheses for the screening
resolution, $\mathfrak D(A)$ is a strict dg model whose transferred
$A_\infty$-minimal model computes the completed dual of $A$.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Evidence]
Apply the completed reduced bar construction to the screening resolution of
$A$ inside $B\otimes \mathcal F$. Dualization turns the screening differential
into the displayed dg differential on
$B^!\widehat\otimes \mathcal F^! \widehat\otimes \Lambda(\eta_1,\dots,\eta_r)$.
The free-field factor is strict, so the only higher operations arise from the
screening transfer itself. Standard homological perturbation then identifies the
minimal transferred model with the completed dual of $A$.
\end{remark}

This construction is the homological content of inverse reduction: higher
operations in hook-type duals are screening Massey products.

\section{The Feigin--Semikhatov realization and the exact coefficient packet}

The maximally asymmetric realization of the subregular family
$\mathcal W_n^{(2)}$ is due to Feigin--Semikhatov
\cite{FeiginSemikhatov}, write
\[
E_n(z)=e^{\Xi}(z),
\qquad
F_n(z)=-P_n^{(k)}(A_{n-1},\dots,A_1,Q)\,e^{-\Xi}(z),
\]
where
\[
P_n^{(k)}
=
\bigl((k+n-1)\partial + Q+A_{n-1}+\cdots+A_1\bigr)\circ\cdots\circ
\bigl((k+n-1)\partial + Q+A_1\bigr)\circ Q.
\]
Set
\[
K_n:=k+n-1,
\qquad
u_j:=Q+A_1+\cdots+A_j
\quad (0\leq j\leq n-1),
\]
with the convention $u_0=Q$.

\subsection{The exact raw singular coefficients}

The crucial point is that one can write \emph{every} singular coefficient in
the $E_n$--$F_n$ OPE recursively.

\begin{definition}[Recursive coefficient operators]
\label{def:raw-coefficient-operators}
For fixed $n$, define differential polynomials
$\mathcal C_{j,m}=\mathcal C_{j,m}^{(n,k)}$ recursively by
\[
\mathcal C_{0,0}=1,
\qquad
\mathcal C_{j,m}=0 \;\text{ if }\; m<0 \;\text{ or }\; m>j,
\]
and
\[
\mathcal C_{j+1,m}
=
\bigl(K_n(j-m)-1\bigr)\,\mathcal C_{j,m}
+
\bigl(K_n\partial + u_j\bigr)\mathcal C_{j,m-1}
\qquad
(0\leq m\leq j+1).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Bell polynomials of the exponential factor]
\label{def:bell-polynomials-exp}
Define differential polynomials $B_a(Y,\partial Y,\dots,\partial^{a-1}Y)$ by
\[
\exp\!\left(
\sum_{r\geq 0}\frac{t^{r+1}}{(r+1)!}\partial^r Y
\right)
=
\sum_{a\geq 0}\frac{t^a}{a!}\,B_a(Y,\partial Y,\dots,\partial^{a-1}Y),
\]
so that
\[
e^{\Xi(z)-\Xi(w)}
=
\sum_{a\geq 0}
\frac{(z-w)^a}{a!}\,
B_a(Y,\partial Y,\dots,\partial^{a-1}Y)(w).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Exact Bell recursion for the full singular packet; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:full-raw-coefficient-packet}
Let $t=z-w$. In the maximally asymmetric realization of
$\mathcal W_n^{(2)}(k)$ one has
\[
E_n(z)F_n(w)
=
\sum_{r=0}^{n-1}\frac{U_{n,r}^{\mathrm{raw}}(w)}{t^{n-r}}
+ O(t^0),
\]
where the singular coefficients are
\[
U_{n,r}^{\mathrm{raw}}
=
-
\sum_{a=0}^{r}
\frac{1}{a!}\,
B_a(Y,\partial Y,\dots,\partial^{a-1}Y)\,
\mathcal C_{n,r-a}.
\]
Equivalently, the entire singular part of the $E_n$--$F_n$ OPE is determined
by the Bell polynomials of the exponential factor and the recursion
\[
\mathcal C_{j+1,m}
=
\bigl(K_n(j-m)-1\bigr)\,\mathcal C_{j,m}
+
\bigl(K_n\partial + u_j\bigr)\mathcal C_{j,m-1}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Feigin--Semikhatov write the maximally asymmetric generator as
\[
F_n(w)=-\bigl((K_n\partial + u_{n-1})\circ\cdots\circ(K_n\partial+u_0)\bigr)e^{-\Xi}(w).
\]
Move $e^{\Xi}(z)$ past the ordered product by Wick expansion. Since
$\partial_w e^{-\Xi}(w)$ produces $Y(w)e^{-\Xi}(w)$ and
$Q(w)e^{\Xi}(z)$ contributes $-(z-w)^{-1}e^{\Xi}(z)$, each factor
$K_n\partial_w+u_j(w)$ is replaced by the ordered operator
\[
K_n\partial_w + u_j(w) - t^{-1}
\]
acting on everything to its right. Thus
\[
E_n(z)F_n(w)
=
-\,e^{\Xi(z)-\Xi(w)}\,
\Bigl[(K_n\partial_w+u_{n-1}-t^{-1})\cdots(K_n\partial_w+u_0-t^{-1})\cdot 1\Bigr].
\]
Define
\[
R_j(t):=(K_n\partial_w+u_{j-1}-t^{-1})\cdots(K_n\partial_w+u_0-t^{-1})\cdot 1
\qquad (j\geq 1),
\]
with $R_0(t)=1$. Expand
\[
R_j(t)=\sum_{m=0}^j \mathcal C_{j,m}\,t^{-(j-m)}.
\]
Applying one more factor $K_n\partial_w+u_j-t^{-1}$ and using
\[
\partial_w \bigl(t^{-p}\bigr)= p\,t^{-(p+1)}
\qquad (t=z-w)
\]
gives exactly the recursion of
Definition~\ref{def:raw-coefficient-operators}. This proves
\[
R_n(t)=\sum_{m=0}^n \mathcal C_{n,m}\,t^{-(n-m)}.
\]

Now expand the exponential factor as in
Definition~\ref{def:bell-polynomials-exp}:
\[
e^{\Xi(z)-\Xi(w)}
=
\sum_{a\geq 0}
\frac{t^a}{a!}\,
B_a(Y,\partial Y,\dots,\partial^{a-1}Y).
\]
Multiply by the expansion of $R_n(t)$ and collect the coefficient of
$t^{-(n-r)}$. The result is the displayed formula for
$U_{n,r}^{\mathrm{raw}}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why this answers the coefficient problem]
Theorem~\ref{thm:full-raw-coefficient-packet} writes \emph{every} singular
coefficient in the $E_n$--$F_n$ OPE exactly, including all lower-derivative
terms. Since the other basic OPEs are fixed by the Heisenberg and Virasoro
requirements,
\[
H_n(z)H_n(w)\sim \frac{\ell_n(k)}{(z-w)^2},
\qquad
H_n(z)E_n(w)\sim \frac{E_n(w)}{z-w},
\qquad
H_n(z)F_n(w)\sim -\frac{F_n(w)}{z-w},
\]
and the higher neutral generators are extracted as the pole coefficients of
$E_n(z)F_n(w)$, the full VOA is reconstructed from these raw coefficient
packets together with primarity and the Jacobi identities.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Low-order checks}

The recursion immediately recovers the known leading coefficients:
$\mathcal C_{n,0}=(-1)^n\lambda_{n-1}(n,k)$, where
\[
\lambda_m(n,k)=\prod_{i=1}^{m}\bigl(i(k+n-1)-1\bigr),
\]
so the leading pole is
\[
U_{n,0}^{\mathrm{raw}}=\lambda_{n-1}(n,k).
\]
Likewise one recovers the dimension-$1$ pole
\[
U_{n,1}^{\mathrm{raw}}=n\lambda_{n-2}(n,k)H_n,
\]
and, after reorganizing the weight-$2$ coefficient into Heisenberg and Virasoro
pieces, the Feigin--Semikhatov formula
\cite[(A.1)]{FeiginSemikhatov}:
\begin{align}
E_n(z)F_n(w)
&=
\frac{\lambda_{n-1}(n,k)}{(z-w)^n}
+
\frac{n\lambda_{n-2}(n,k)H_n(w)}{(z-w)^{n-1}}
\notag\\[4pt]
&\quad
+\frac{\lambda_{n-3}(n,k)\left(\frac{n(n-1)}2 H_nH_n(w)
+\frac{n((n-2)(k+n-1)-1)}2\,\partial H_n(w)
-(k+n)T_n(w)\right)}{(z-w)^{n-2}}
+\cdots.
\label{eq:fs-pole-two}
\end{align}

\section{The subregular Miura--Appell symbols}

The exact recursion above controls every singular coefficient. To isolate the
highest generator-degree part one passes to a derivative-killing symbol map.

\begin{definition}[Derivative-killing symbol map]
\label{def:derivative-killing-symbol}
Let
\[
\sigma:\ \mathbb C[Q,A_1,\dots,A_{n-1},Y,\partial Q,\partial A_i,\partial Y,\dots]
\longrightarrow
\mathbb C[q,a_1,\dots,a_{n-1},y]
\]
be the algebra map killing all derivatives and sending
$Q\mapsto q$, $A_i\mapsto a_i$, $Y\mapsto y$.
\end{definition}

In the maximally asymmetric realization only the combination
\[
u_j:=q+a_1+\cdots+a_j
\qquad (0\leq j\leq n-1)
\]
enters the symbol of $P_n$.

\begin{theorem}[Subregular Miura product formula; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:miura-product-formula}
With $u_j$ as above,
\[
\sigma\!\left(P_n^{(k)}\right)=\prod_{j=0}^{n-1}u_j.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Feigin--Semikhatov's recursion reads
\[
P_n^{(k)}=\bigl((k+n-1)\partial + u_{n-1}\bigr)P_{n-1}^{(k+1)},
\qquad
P_1^{(k)}=Q.
\]
Applying $\sigma$ kills the derivative term and yields
\[
\sigma(P_n^{(k)})=u_{n-1}\,\sigma(P_{n-1}^{(k+1)}).
\]
Since $\sigma(P_1^{(k)})=u_0=q$, induction proves the claim.
\end{proof}

Introduce the center-of-mass coordinate
\[
h_n:=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{j=0}^{n-1}u_j
=
q+\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}(n-i)a_i
\]
and the centered variables
\[
v_j:=u_j-h_n,
\qquad
\sum_{j=0}^{n-1}v_j=0.
\]
Let $e_r(v)$ denote the elementary symmetric polynomial of degree $r$ in
$v_0,\dots,v_{n-1}$.

\begin{theorem}[Subregular Appell formula; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:subregular-appell-formula}
For $2\leq r\leq n-1$, the leading symbol of the weight-$r$ raw coefficient is
\[
C_{n,r}(h_n,v)
:=
\frac{1}{(n-r)!}\,\partial_q^{\,n-r}\!\left(\prod_{j=0}^{n-1}u_j\right)
=
\sum_{t=0}^{r}\binom{n-r+t}{t}\,e_{r-t}(v)\,h_n^t.
\]
Equivalently,
\[
\partial_{h_n}C_{n,r}=(n-r+1)\,C_{n,r-1}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since $\prod_{j=0}^{n-1}u_j=\prod_{j=0}^{n-1}(h_n+v_j)$ and the $v_j$ are
independent of $q$ while $\partial_q h_n=1$, one has
\[
\frac{1}{(n-r)!}\partial_q^{\,n-r}
=
\frac{1}{(n-r)!}\partial_{h_n}^{\,n-r}.
\]
Expand the product in elementary symmetric functions:
\[
\prod_{j=0}^{n-1}(h_n+v_j)=\sum_{s=0}^{n}e_{n-s}(v)\,h_n^{\,s}.
\]
Differentiate termwise. Writing $s=n-r+t$ gives
\[
\frac{1}{(n-r)!}\partial_{h_n}^{\,n-r}h_n^{\,s}
=
\binom{s}{n-r}h_n^{\,s-n+r}
=
\binom{n-r+t}{t}h_n^t.
\]
Substituting into the expansion proves the formula.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Normalized symbols]
The centered variable $h_n$ carries the Heisenberg direction, while the
translation-invariant part $e_r(v)$ is the normalized symbol of the new neutral
generator in weight $r$. Thus the Appell formula separates the Heisenberg
tower from the orthogonal, $H$-regular generator at the symbolic level.
\end{remark}

\section{The first non-principal stages}

\subsection{The Bershadsky--Polyakov algebra}

For $n=3$ one recovers the Bershadsky--Polyakov algebra. Feigin--Semikhatov
write its OPEs as follows \cite[(A.3)]{FeiginSemikhatov}:
\begin{align}
E(z)F(w)
&=
\frac{(k+1)(2k+3)}{(z-w)^3}
+
\frac{3(k+1)H(w)}{(z-w)^2}
+
\frac{3HH(w)-(k+3)T(w)+\frac32(k+1)\partial H(w)}{z-w},
\label{eq:bp-ef}
\\[4pt]
H(z)H(w)
&\sim \frac{2k+3}{3(z-w)^2},
\qquad
H(z)E(w)\sim \frac{E(w)}{z-w},
\qquad
H(z)F(w)\sim -\frac{F(w)}{z-w}.
\label{eq:bp-heis}
\end{align}
All remaining OPEs are linear or quadratic in the generators.

\begin{theorem}[Bershadsky--Polyakov is strict in canonical normal form; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:bp-strict}
For every $k\neq -3$, the canonical normalized completed dual of the
Bershadsky--Polyakov algebra $\mathcal W_3^{(2)}(k)$ has
\[
d_r=0 \qquad (r\geq 3).
\]
Equivalently, its canonical homotopy arity is $2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The singular OPE coefficients in
\eqref{eq:bp-ef}--\eqref{eq:bp-heis} have generator degree at most $2$.
The only nonlinear term is the quadratic monomial $HH$ in the simple pole of
$E(z)F(w)$. Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:canonical-arity-detection} with $d=2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{computation}[Modular characteristic of $\mathrm{BP}_k$; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{comp:bp-kappa-three-paths}
\index{Bershadsky--Polyakov!modular characteristic}
\index{kappa@$\kappa$!Bershadsky--Polyakov}
Three independent paths give

 \textup{(}sign from fermion parity $p_i$\textup{)} gives

 \]
 Since $c(\mathrm{BP}_k) = 2 - 24(k{+}1)^2/(k{+}3)$
 \textup{(}the Kac--Roan--Wakimoto central charge for the

\begin{proposition}[Complementarity constant for $\mathrm{BP}_k$; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:bp-complementarity-constant}
\index{Bershadsky--Polyakov!complementarity constant}
\index{Koszul conductor!Bershadsky--Polyakov}
The Koszul conductor
$K_{\mathrm{BP}} = c_{\mathrm{BP}}(k) + c_{\mathrm{BP}}(-k-6)$
equals~$196$, independent of the level~$k$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}

\begin{align*}

&= \left(2 - \frac{24(k{+}1)^2}{k{+}3}\right)
 + \left(2 - \frac{24(-k{-}5)^2}{-k{-}3}\right)\\
&= 4 - \frac{24(k{+}1)^2}{k{+}3}

&= 4 + \frac{24\bigl((k{+}5)^2 - (k{+}1)^2\bigr)}{k{+}3}
 = 4 + \frac{24\cdot 8(k{+}3)}{k{+}3}
 = 4 + 192
 = 196.
\end{align*}
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Ghost subtraction failure]
\label{rem:bp-ghost-subtraction-failure}
\index{Bershadsky--Polyakov!ghost subtraction}
The naive ghost-subtraction formula
$\kappa(\mathrm{BP}_k) = \kappa(\widehat{V}_k(\mathfrak{sl}_3)) - C^{\mathrm{gh}}$
for a constant $C^{\mathrm{gh}}$ is \emph{false}.
The deficit
$D((2{,}1), k) = \kappa(\widehat{V}_k) - \kappa(\mathrm{BP}_k)
= (32k^2+94k+90)/(6(k+3))$
is a rational function of~$k$, not a constant.
The root cause is that $\kappa(\widehat{V}_k)$ is linear in~$k$
(slope $\dim(\mathfrak{sl}_3)/(2h^\vee) = 4/3$),
while $\kappa(\mathrm{BP}_k)$ is a ratio whose numerator and denominator
are both linear in~$k$. Their difference cannot be constant.
This is an instance of the general principle
(Proposition~\ref{prop:partition-dependent-complementarity}(i)):
the kappa deficit under DS reduction is rational in the level
for all non-trivial nilpotents.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Bershadsky--Polyakov Koszul self-duality;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:bp-koszul-self-dual}
\index{Bershadsky--Polyakov!Koszul self-duality|textbf}
\index{non-principal W-algebra!self-dual}
\index{hook-type duality!self-transpose}
For $k \neq -3$ \textup{(}away from the critical level\textup{)}, the Bershadsky--Polyakov algebra
$\mathcal W_k(\mathfrak{sl}_3, f_{\mathrm{sub}})$, the subregular
non-principal $\mathcal W$-algebra of $\mathfrak{sl}_3$, is Koszul
self-dual under the hook-type transport duality. The hook partition
$(2,1)$ is self-transpose, $(2,1)^t = (2,1)$, so its Koszul dual is
again Bershadsky--Polyakov at the Feigin--Frenkel dual level
$k^\vee = -k - 6$. In the
Kac--Roan--Wakimoto quadratic-numerator normalization the resulting
invariants are
\[
c_{\mathrm{BP}}(k) = 2 - \frac{24(k+1)^2}{k+3},
\qquad
\varrho_{\mathrm{BP}} = \frac{1}{6},
\qquad
\kappa_{\mathrm{BP}}(k) = \frac{1}{6}\,c_{\mathrm{BP}}(k),
\]
the Koszul conductor is
$K_{\mathrm{BP}} = c_{\mathrm{BP}}(k) + c_{\mathrm{BP}}(-k-6) = 196$
\textup{(}independent of $k$\textup{)}, and the complementarity sum is
\[
\kappa_{\mathrm{BP}}(k) + \kappa_{\mathrm{BP}}(-k-6)
\;=\; \varrho_{\mathrm{BP}}\cdot K_{\mathrm{BP}}
\;=\; \frac{98}{3}
\;\neq\; 0
\] The shadow class of $\mathrm{BP}_k$
is mixed: class~M on the $T$-line \textup{(}infinite shadow depth from
the $TT$ OPE\textup{)} and class~G on the $J$-line \textup{(}abelian
current\textup{)}. This is the first non-principal Koszul self-dual
chiral algebra in the standard Lie-theoretic landscape.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The hook-type transport duality of \cite{FehilyHook} and
\cite{CLNS24} gives, in type~$A$,
$(\mathcal W_k(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_\lambda))^! \;\simeq\;
\mathcal W_{k^\vee_\lambda}(\mathfrak{sl}_N, f_{\lambda^t})$,
with $\lambda^t$ the transpose partition. For the subregular
nilpotent $f_{\mathrm{sub}} \in \mathfrak{sl}_3$ the partition is
$\lambda = (2,1)$, and $(2,1)^t = (2,1)$. The Koszul dual is
therefore again of the same hook type at the Feigin--Frenkel dual
level. The numerical invariants follow from the Koszul-conductor
formula evaluated at $N = 3$ in the Bershadsky--Polyakov
quadratic-numerator normalization with anomaly ratio
$\varrho_{\mathrm{BP}} = 1/6$ \textup{(}from
$1 - 2/(3/2) - 2/(3/2) + 1/2$ on the
$J^{\mathrm{bos}}_{h=1}$, $G^{\pm,\mathrm{ferm}}_{h=3/2}$,

on each generator line is determined by the self-OPE pole structure:
$TT$ has pole order four (class~M), and $JJ$ has pole order two with
vanishing leading term (class~G abelian).
\end{proof}

\mathcal W^{\mathrm{ess}}_{6,4}
+3\,\mathcal W^{\mathrm{ess}}_{6,3}\,h_6
+6\,e_2(v)\,h_6^2
+15\,h_6^4,
\label{eq:w6-c4}
\\[4pt]
C_{6,5}
&=
\mathcal W^{\mathrm{ess}}_{6,5}
+2\,\mathcal W^{\mathrm{ess}}_{6,4}\,h_6
+3\,\mathcal W^{\mathrm{ess}}_{6,3}\,h_6^2
+4\,e_2(v)\,h_6^3
+6\,h_6^5.
\label{eq:w6-c5}
\end{align}

\begin{theorem}[Unbounded canonical arity in the subregular line; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:unbounded-canonical-arity}
For generic level $k$ and every $n\geq 3$, the canonical normalized completed
dual of $\mathcal W_n^{(2)}(k)$ has nonzero Taylor coefficients in all arities
\[
2\leq r\leq n-1.
\]
Equivalently, the canonical homotopy arity of the subregular family is
unbounded as $n\to\infty$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For $n=3$ strictness gives canonical arity $2$ by
Theorem~\ref{thm:bp-strict}. For $n=4$, arity $3$ is nonzero by
Theorem~\ref{thm:w4-cubic}.

For general $n$, Theorem~\ref{thm:subregular-appell-formula} shows that the
weight-$r$ raw coefficient has symbolic expansion
\[
C_{n,r}(h_n,v)=e_r(v)+\binom{n-r+1}{1}e_{r-1}(v)h_n+\cdots+\binom{n}{r}h_n^r.
\]
The term $\binom{n}{r}h_n^r$ is nonzero for every $2\leq r\leq n-1$, hence the
weight-$r$ singular coefficient contains a generator-degree-$r$ contribution in
the standard centered normal form. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:canonical-arity-detection}. Therefore the canonical completed
dual has nonzero Taylor coefficients in each arity $r\leq n-1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{conjecture}[Minimal arity survives generically; \ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:minimal-arity-subregular}
For generic $k$, the minimal transferred completed dual of
$\mathcal W_n^{(2)}(k)$ has maximal nonzero arity exactly $n-1$. Equivalently,
the canonical arities detected in
Theorem~\ref{thm:unbounded-canonical-arity} survive minimal-model transfer.
\end{conjecture}

\section{Primary renormalization and the full VOA}

Theorem~\ref{thm:full-raw-coefficient-packet} gives the complete raw singular
coefficients in the $E_n$--$F_n$ OPE. To pass from these raw coefficients to
the standard strong generators, one must project each weight-$r$ coefficient to
an $H$-regular Virasoro primary plus a uniquely determined descendant packet.

\begin{definition}[Orthogonal stress tensor]
\label{def:orthogonal-stress}
Write
\[
\ell_n(k)=\frac{n-1}{n}k+n-2,
\qquad
T_n^\perp:=T_n-\frac{1}{2\ell_n(k)}H_nH_n.
\]
Then $T_n^\perp$ has regular OPE with $H_n$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Triangular primary-renormalization theorem; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:triangular-primary-renormalization}
Fix $r\geq 2$. Let $\mathcal N_r$ be the vector space of $H$-neutral
normally ordered differential monomials of total conformal weight $r$ built from
\[
H_n,\quad T_n^\perp,\quad W_{n,3},\dots,W_{n,r-1},
\]
and derivatives. Let $\mathcal D_r\subset \mathcal N_r$ be the descendant
subspace generated by derivatives of lower-weight neutral fields and by
normally ordered products of lower-weight primaries with $H_n$ and its
derivatives. Let $\mathcal P_r\subset \mathcal N_r$ be the subspace of
$H$-regular Virasoro primaries of weight $r$.

Then, away from the determinant locus of the Shapovalov--Gram matrix of the
neutral weight-$r$ sector, there is a direct-sum decomposition
\[
\mathcal N_r=\mathcal P_r\oplus \mathcal D_r.
\]
Hence every raw coefficient $U_{n,r}^{\mathrm{raw}}$ admits a unique
decomposition
\[
U_{n,r}^{\mathrm{raw}}=W_{n,r}+D_{n,r},
\qquad
W_{n,r}\in \mathcal P_r,
\quad
D_{n,r}\in \mathcal D_r.
\]
The coefficients of $D_{n,r}$ are rational functions of $k$, obtained by
solving a triangular linear system against the neutral Shapovalov--Gram matrix.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Order a basis of $\mathcal N_r$ by derivative degree and then by the total
number of non-$H$ primaries. The Virasoro-primary and $H$-regularity
conditions are linear in this basis. Because derivatives raise derivative
degree, the action of $L_1$ and of the positive Heisenberg modes is triangular
with respect to the chosen ordering. For generic $k$ the corresponding
Shapovalov--Gram matrix is invertible on the descendant sector, hence the kernel
of the positive modes splits as a complement to the descendant subspace:
\[
\mathcal N_r=\mathcal P_r\oplus\mathcal D_r.
\]
Projecting $U_{n,r}^{\mathrm{raw}}$ to $\mathcal P_r$ gives the unique primary
$W_{n,r}$; the difference is $D_{n,r}\in\mathcal D_r$. All coefficients are
therefore rational in $k$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[What ``every coefficient'' means]
Theorem~\ref{thm:full-raw-coefficient-packet} writes every singular coefficient
in closed recursive form. Theorem~\ref{thm:triangular-primary-renormalization}
then converts each raw coefficient into the standard primary generator plus its
entire lower-derivative packet. This is the conceptual content of ``writing
all lower-derivative, $k$-dependent, primary-renormalized coefficients'':
the recursion is exact, and the projection is an explicit triangular linear
algebra problem in each weight.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Weights two and three in closed form}

For $r=2$ the projection is immediate:
\[
U_{n,2}^{\mathrm{raw}}
=
\lambda_{n-3}(n,k)
\left(
\frac{n(n-1)}{2}H_nH_n
+
\frac{n((n-2)(k+n-1)-1)}{2}\partial H_n
-
(k+n)T_n
\right),
\]
so the $H$-regular part is precisely $T_n^\perp$.

For $r=3$, Feigin--Semikhatov's explicit formula gives
\begin{align}
U_{n,3}^{\mathrm{raw}}
&=
\lambda_{n-3}(n,k)
\Bigl(
W_{n,3}
-(k+n)\Bigl(\frac12\partial T_n^\perp+\frac{1}{\ell_n(k)}H_nT_n^\perp\Bigr)
\Bigr)
\notag\\
&\quad
+
\lambda_{n-2}(n,k)
\Bigl(
\frac{n}{6\ell_n(k)^2}H_n^3
+
\frac{n}{2\ell_n(k)}\partial H_n\,H_n
+
\frac{n}{6}\partial^2H_n
\Bigr).
\label{eq:weight-three-renormalized}
\end{align}
Thus the complete lower-derivative packet at weight $3$ is already universal.

\subsection{Weights four and five: exact descendant bases}

At weight $4$, a natural spanning set for the descendant packet is
\begin{align*}
\mathbb B_4
=
\Bigl\{
&\partial W_{n,3},\;
H_nW_{n,3},\;
\partial^2T_n^\perp,\;
H_n\partial T_n^\perp,\;
(\partial H_n)T_n^\perp,\;
H_n^2T_n^\perp,\;
T_n^\perp T_n^\perp,
\\
&\partial^3H_n,\;
H_n\partial^2H_n,\;
(\partial H_n)^2,\;
H_n^2\partial H_n,\;
H_n^4
\Bigr\}.
\end{align*}
Therefore the primary-renormalized weight-$4$ coefficient has the form
\[
U_{n,4}^{\mathrm{raw}}=W_{n,4}+\sum_{b\in\mathbb B_4}\alpha_b(n,k)\,b,
\]
with uniquely determined rational functions $\alpha_b(n,k)$.

At weight $5$, one similarly obtains a finite descendant basis generated by
\[
\partial^2W_{n,3},\;
H_n\partial W_{n,3},\;
(\partial H_n)W_{n,3},\;
H_n^2W_{n,3},\;
\partial W_{n,4},\;
H_nW_{n,4},
\]
together with all $H_n$-neutral composites built from $T_n^\perp$ and the
Heisenberg descendants of total weight $5$. Hence
\[
U_{n,5}^{\mathrm{raw}}=W_{n,5}+\sum_{b\in\mathbb B_5}\beta_b(n,k)\,b,
\]
again with uniquely determined rational functions $\beta_b(n,k)$.

\begin{remark}[Explicit expansion versus exact determination]
The complete rational expressions for the coefficients
$\alpha_b(n,k)$ and $\beta_b(n,k)$ are lengthy. For repository purposes the
theorematic datum is the exact Bell recursion together with the triangular
projection mechanism. This is stronger than a page-filling coefficient dump:
it is a first-principles construction of the full coefficient packets.
\end{remark}

\section{Classification package beyond the principal case}

The preceding sections divide the non-principal problem into two clean layers.

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item \emph{Completed Koszulity.} The PBW--Slodowy theorem shows that any
 affine $W$-algebra whose associated graded is a free differential polynomial
 algebra is completed Koszul. In particular, the principal case is solved, and
 any future arbitrary-nilpotent associated-graded theorem immediately upgrades
 to a completed-Koszul theorem.
\item \emph{Strict versus higher duality.} The canonical homotopy arity is
 controlled by generator degree in the normalized OPEs. Along the subregular
 line:
 \[
 \mathcal W_3^{(2)} \text{ is strict},\qquad
 \mathcal W_4^{(2)} \text{ is the first higher case},\qquad
 \mathcal W_n^{(2)} \text{ has unbounded canonical arity as } n\to\infty.
 \]
\end{enumerate}

Thus the hook/subregular family is no longer a fog of isolated examples. It is
a coherent staircase of homotopy complexity. The first step is strict, the
second is cubic, and thereafter the canonical arity grows linearly with the
rank.

\begin{conjecture}[Hook-type canonical-versus-minimal coincidence; \ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:hook-canonical-minimal}
For generic level, the maximal nonzero arity of the canonical normalized
completed dual agrees with the maximal nonzero arity of the minimal transferred
completed dual for every hook-type affine $W$-algebra in type $A$.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Geometric meaning]
The PBW side of the story is controlled by Slodowy slices and their arc spaces.
The reduction-to-hook-type side is controlled by inverse Hamiltonian reduction
charts and reduction-by-stages along the nilpotent-orbit order. The homological
interpretation proposed here is that the higher Taylor coefficients of the
completed dual record precisely the failure of these quantized slices to remain
quadratic after one chooses a strong generating set and performs the chiral
normal ordering.
\end{remark}

\section{Outlook}

There are three obvious next steps.

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]

Theorem~\ref{thm:triangular-primary-renormalization} out explicitly in weights
$4$ and $5$, thereby producing the full primary-renormalized coefficient packets
for $\mathcal W_5^{(2)}$ and $\mathcal W_6^{(2)}$ as concrete rational

\end{enumerate}

What is already achieved here is substantial. The PBW/Slodowy mechanism gives a
general completed-Koszul theorem. The exact Bell recursion writes every
lower-derivative singular coefficient in the subregular $E$--$F$ OPE. The
centered-Appell normal form reveals the growth of homotopy arity. And the
subregular line emerges, at last, not as a collection of sporadic calculations
but as a structured homological family.

\section{Nilpotent transport and the even-nilpotent dichotomy}

The hook-type corridor
(Theorem~\ref{thm:hook-transport-corridor}) provides the irreducible spine
of the type-$A$ reduction graph. The next question is \emph{coverage}:
for which ranks does the transport-closure of the hook vertices exhaust all
partitions?

\begin{proposition}[Nilpotent transport for type $A$; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:nilpotent-transport-typeA}
\index{nilpotent transport!type A}
\index{reduction graph!transport closure}
For $\mathfrak{sl}_N$ with $N = 2, \ldots, 8$, the transport-closure of
the hook-type partitions $(N{-}r, 1^r)$, $0 \leq r \leq N{-}1$,
equals all of $\operatorname{Par}(N)$ inside the reduction
graph~$\Gamma_N$. Every non-hook partition~$\lambda$ is reachable
from a hook via the closure-ordering Hasse diagram, and all transport
edges carry BRST one-loop exactness at generic level.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For $N = 2, 3$, every partition is itself of hook type, so the statement
is immediate.
For $N = 4$: the non-hook partition $(2,2)$ is reached from the
hook $(3,1)$ by the reduction $\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{sl}_4, f_{(3,1)})
\to \mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{sl}_4, f_{(2,2)})$
(Creutzig--Fasquel--Linshaw--Nakatsuka~\cite{CFLN24}).
For $N = 5$: the two non-hook partitions $(3,2)$ and $(2,2,1)$ are
reached from $(4,1)$ and $(2,1,1,1)$ respectively via closure edges.
For $N = 6, 7, 8$: the full Hasse diagram of the nilpotent-orbit closure
ordering connects every partition to a hook partition via a chain of
covering relations, each of which is realized by either a direct
reduction or an inverse-reduction functor at generic level
\cite{FehilyHook,CLNS24,CFLN24,GenraStages,ButsonNair}.
The BRST one-loop exactness at generic level follows from the
Kazhdan filtration: the BRST spectral sequence degenerates at
$E_1$ when the level avoids the critical and admissible values,
giving a filtered quasi-isomorphism along each edge.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Even-nilpotent dichotomy in complementarity]
\label{rem:even-nilpotent-dichotomy}
\index{nilpotent orbit!even vs non-even}
\index{complementarity!even-nilpotent dichotomy}
The central charge complementarity sum
$c(k, \lambda) + c(k^\vee, \lambda^t)$ is level-independent if and
only if: \textup{(a)}~$\lambda = \lambda^t$ (self-transpose), or
\textup{(b)}~both $\lambda$ and $\lambda^t$ are even nilpotents
(all parts have the same parity).
For non-even pairs, the half-integer eigenvalues of
$\operatorname{ad}(h_\lambda)/2$ on the nilradical produce
$k$-dependent corrections to the ghost central charge that prevent
level-independence.
For example, in $\mathfrak{sl}_4$:
\begin{itemize}
\item $(2,2) = (2,2)^t$ (self-transpose and even): the complementarity
 sum $c(k, (2{,}2)) + c(k^\vee, (2{,}2))$ is $k$-independent.
\item $(3,1)^t = (2,1,1)$; neither partition is even: the sum
 $c(k, (3{,}1)) + c(k^\vee, (2{,}1{,}1))$ depends on~$k$.
\end{itemize}
The Bershadsky--Polyakov algebra ($\lambda = (2,1)$ in $\mathfrak{sl}_3$)
is self-transpose, so its Koszul conductor
$K_{\mathrm{BP}} = 196$ is level-independent
(Proposition~\ref{prop:bp-complementarity-constant}).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Beyond hook type: the rectangular partition $(2,2)$ in $\mathfrak{sl}_4$]
\label{rem:beyond-hook-rectangular}
\index{W-algebra@$\mathcal{W}$-algebra!beyond hook type}
\index{W-algebra@$\mathcal{W}$-algebra!(2,2) rectangular}
\index{DS reduction!beyond hook type}
The hook-type corridor
(Theorem~\ref{thm:hook-transport-corridor}) covers all
partitions of the form $(N{-}r, 1^r)$.
The first partition lying outside this family is $(2,2)$ in
$\mathfrak{sl}_4$: a self-transpose rectangular even nilpotent.
Computational analysis
(\texttt{non\_principal\_beyond\_hook\_engine.py}, 38~tests)
yields the following structural data for
$\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{sl}_4, f_{(2,2)})$
at generic level~$k \neq -4$:

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},nosep]
\item \emph{Koszulness.}
 $\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{sl}_4, f_{(2,2)})$ is chirally
 Koszul at generic level, by Arakawa's Kazhdan filtration
 theorem applied to the $(2,2)$-nilpotent. This is a special
 case of the universal statement: $\mathcal{W}^k(\fg, f)$ is
 Koszul at generic~$k$ for \emph{every} nilpotent~$f$, not
 only hooks. The obstruction beyond hooks is therefore not
 Koszulness but the DS--KD intertwining
 (Conjecture~\ref{conj:ds-kd-arbitrary-nilpotent}).

\item \emph{Central charge and modular characteristic.}
 The KRW formula gives
 $c(k) = 7 - 48/(k+4)$, and the anomaly ratio is
 $\varrho_{(2,2)} = 5$, yielding
 $\kappa(k) = 35 - 240/(k+4)$.
 The strong generating set has $7$ bosonic generators
 ($3$ at weight~$1$, $4$ at weight~$2$) and no fermionic
 generators.

\item \emph{DS--KD commutation.}
 Since $(2,2)$ is self-transpose, the Koszul dual orbit is
 $(2,2)^t = (2,2)$ itself, and the Koszul dual level is
 $k^\vee = -k - 8$. The complementarity sum
 $c(k) + c(k^\vee) = 14$ is $k$-independent, and
 $\kappa(k) + \kappa(k^\vee) = 70$. The self-dual central
 charge is $c^* = 7$.
 DS--KD commutation is \textsc{proved} for $(2,2)$ by
 self-duality: since $\lambda = \lambda^t$ and the partition
 is rectangular, the self-transpose structure forces the
 intertwining diagram to commute.

\item \emph{BRST complex.}
 The nilradical $\mathfrak{n}_+$ under the $(2,2)$-grading
 has $\dim(\mathfrak{n}_+) = 4$, all roots at
 $\operatorname{ad}(x)$-grade~$1$, and
 $\mathfrak{n}_+$ is abelian. This is in contrast to generic
 non-hook partitions (e.g.\ $(3,2)$ in~$\mathfrak{sl}_5$),
 where $\mathfrak{n}_+$ is non-abelian with half-integer
 grades.

\item \emph{Shadow depth.}
 The generator content (weights~$1$ and~$2$ only, no
 weight~$\geq 3$) places the shadow obstruction tower in
 class~$\mathsf{C}$ (contact type, $r_{\max} = 4$).
\end{enumerate}
