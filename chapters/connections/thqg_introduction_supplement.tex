\section{The holographic triangle}
\label{sec:holographic-triangle}
\index{holographic triangle|textbf}
\index{bulk-boundary-line triangle|textbf}

Three algebraic objects attach to a chiral algebra~$\cA$ on a smooth
projective curve~$X$.  They form a triangle whose vertices are the
\emph{bulk}, the \emph{boundary}, and the \emph{lines}.  Each vertex
is defined by a single algebraic construction; the edges are
functorially determined.  The triangle is not a metaphor but a
commutative diagram of categories with exact functors between them.

\subsection{Vertex 1: the boundary algebra}
\label{subsec:vertex-boundary}
\index{boundary algebra|textbf}

Let $(\cA, \varepsilon \colon \cA \to \omega_X)$ be an augmented
chiral algebra on~$X$.  The \emph{boundary algebra} is $\cA$ itself:
an augmented $\mathcal{D}_X$-module equipped with a chiral bracket
\begin{equation}\label{eq:supp-boundary-chiral-bracket}
\mu^{\mathrm{ch}} \colon j_* j^*(\cA \boxtimes \cA)
\longrightarrow \Delta_!(\cA),
\end{equation}
where $j \colon X^2 \setminus \Delta \hookrightarrow X^2$ is the
complement of the diagonal.  The boundary algebra carries the full
OPE data: for sections $a, b \in \cA$ and local coordinates $z, w$,
\[
a(z)\, b(w) \;\sim\;
\sum_{n \geq 0} \frac{(a_{(n)} b)(w)}{(z - w)^{n+1}}.
\]
The maximal pole order $N(a,b) = \max\{n : a_{(n)}b \neq 0\}$ and
the conformal grading $\Delta \colon \cA \to \cA$ (eigenvalue
decomposition of $L_0$) are part of the input data.
The boundary algebra is the starting point; the other two vertices are
constructed from it.

\subsection{Vertex 2: the bulk algebra (derived center)}
\label{subsec:vertex-bulk}
\index{bulk algebra|textbf}
\index{derived center|textbf}

The \emph{bulk algebra} is the derived center of~$\cA$.  Define the
center local system on the universal curve
$\pi \colon \mathcal{C}_g \to \overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ by
\begin{equation}\label{eq:supp-center-local-system-def}
\mathcal{Z}(\cA) \;:=\;
R\mathcal{H}om_{\cA\text{-bimod}}(\cA,\, \cA),
\end{equation}
where the bimodule structure uses left and right chiral actions.
On a fixed smooth curve~$\Sigma_g$ of genus~$g$, the fiber of the
center local system is
\[
Z(\cA|_{\Sigma_g}) \;=\;
\operatorname{End}_{\cA\text{-bimod}}(\cA|_{\Sigma_g})
\;\cong\;
H^0(\Sigma_g,\, \cA / [\cA, \cA]_{\mathrm{ch}}),
\]
the space of chiral-central sections: those $\phi \in \cA$ satisfying
$\phi_{(n)} a = 0$ for all $a \in \cA$ and all $n \geq 0$.  For
conformal vertex algebras, the center is the zeroth Zhu algebra, and
includes the Virasoro vector when $\cA$ has a conformal structure.

The \emph{ambient complex} at genus~$g$ is the derived global sections
of the center local system:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:supp-ambient-complex-def}
\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) \;:=\;
R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g,\, \mathcal{Z}(\cA)).
\end{equation}
This is a perfect complex carrying a canonical shifted-symplectic
structure from Verdier duality on the center
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shifted-symplectic-complementarity}).  The Verdier
involution $\sigma = \mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}$ acts on
$\mathbf{C}_g(\cA)$ and decomposes it into eigenspaces:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:supp-complementarity-eigenspaces}
\mathbf{C}_g(\cA)
\;\simeq\;
\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \oplus \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!),
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) = \operatorname{fib}(\sigma - \mathrm{id})$
and $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!) = \operatorname{fib}(\sigma + \mathrm{id})$
are the $+1$ and $-1$ eigenspaces respectively.  Each eigenspace is
Lagrangian for the shifted-symplectic pairing
(Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}).  The
complementarity decomposition~\eqref{eq:supp-complementarity-eigenspaces}
is Theorem~C of the algebraic engine.

The bulk algebra in the holographic sense is the factorization algebra
on~$X$ whose genus-$g$ factorization homology is $\mathbf{C}_g(\cA)$.
It is reconstructed from the boundary algebra~$\cA$ by the protected
holographic transform
$\mathbb{H}_X(\cA) = \Omega_X \circ \mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}
\circ \barB_X(\cA)$
(Definition~\ref{def:frontier-protected-holographic-transform}).
On the Koszul locus, $\mathbb{H}_X(\cA) \simeq \cA^!$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:frontier-protected-bulk-antiinvolution}).

\subsection{Vertex 3: the line-operator category}
\label{subsec:vertex-lines}
\index{line-operator category|textbf}
\index{Koszul dual!module category}

The \emph{line-operator category} is the derived module category
of the Koszul dual algebra:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:supp-line-category-def}
\mathcal{C}(\cA) \;:=\; \cA^!\text{-}\mathsf{mod}.
\end{equation}
Here $\cA^! = (H^*(\barB_X(\cA)))^\vee$ is the Koszul dual chiral
algebra, constructed by first taking bar cohomology, then Verdier/linear
duality.  The module category $\cA^!\text{-}\mathsf{mod}$ is a
$\mathbb{C}$-linear dg category whose objects are $\cA^!$-modules
(factorization modules on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$) and whose morphisms
are chain maps.

The category $\mathcal{C}(\cA)$ carries a meromorphic tensor structure:
for $M, N \in \cA^!\text{-}\mathsf{mod}$, the tensor product
$M \otimes_{r(z)} N$ is defined by the fusion product along the
spectral parameter~$z$, with the binary OPE controlled by the
$r$-matrix
$r(z) \in \cA^! \,\widehat{\otimes}\, \cA^![\![z^{-1}, z]\!]$.
The tensor structure is \emph{meromorphic}: the poles of $r(z)$ in
the spectral parameter produce non-trivial monodromy, and the
category is not genuinely braided but carries an $\Eone$-monoidal
structure (ordered configurations on $\mathbb{R} \subset \mathbb{C}$).

For affine Kac--Moody $\cA = \widehat{\fg}_k$ at non-critical level
$k \neq -h^\vee$, the Koszul dual is
$\cA^! \cong \widehat{\fg}_{k'}$ with $k' = -k - 2h^\vee$, and the
line-operator category identifies with the Kazhdan--Lusztig category:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:supp-line-kl-identification}
\mathcal{C}(\widehat{\fg}_k)
\;\simeq\;
\mathcal{O}_{k'}(\widehat{\fg})
\;\simeq\;
\mathcal{C}(U_q(\fg)),
\end{equation}
where $q = e^{\pi i/(k + h^\vee)}$ and the last equivalence is the
Kazhdan--Lusztig theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:kazhdan-lusztig-equivalence}).

\subsection{The triangle diagram}
\label{subsec:triangle-diagram}

The three vertices assemble into a commutative triangle of categories
and functors:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:supp-holographic-triangle-diagram}
\begin{tikzcd}[row sep=huge, column sep=huge]
& \text{Bulk: } \mathbf{C}_g(\cA)
  \arrow[dl, "\text{restrict}"']
  \arrow[dr, "\text{cofiber}"]
& \\
\text{Boundary: } \cA
  \arrow[rr, "\text{Koszul dual}"']
  \arrow[ur, "\barB_X"]
& & \text{Lines: } \cA^!\text{-}\mathsf{mod}
\end{tikzcd}
\end{equation}
The left edge is the bar construction $\barB_X$, which maps the
boundary algebra to the bar coalgebra (a factorization coalgebra
contributing to the ambient complex).  The right edge is the
holographic cofiber: $\mathbf{H}_g^{\mathrm{hol}}(\cA) =
\operatorname{cofib}(\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \to \mathbf{C}_g(\cA))
\simeq \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:frontier-protected-bulk-antiinvolution}(iii)).
The bottom edge is Koszul duality: $\cA \mapsto \cA^!$ by the
bar-then-dualize construction, with the module category
$\cA^!\text{-}\mathsf{mod}$ as the categorical output.

The content of the holographic programme is that all three vertices,
and the functors between them, are controlled by a single
Maurer--Cartan element~$\Theta_\cA$.

\subsection{Holographic datum: the six-fold package}
\label{subsec:holographic-datum-sixfold}
\index{holographic modular Koszul datum|textbf}

The three vertices of the triangle, together with three connecting
data, assemble into the \emph{holographic modular Koszul datum}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:supp-holographic-datum-intro}
\mathcal{H}(\cA)
\;=\;
\bigl(\cA,\;\cA^!,\;\mathcal{C},\;r(z),\;\Theta_\cA,\;
\nabla^{\mathrm{hol}}\bigr).
\end{equation}
The six entries are:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The boundary algebra $\cA$: augmented chiral algebra on~$X$.
\item The universal defect algebra $\cA^!$: Koszul dual of~$\cA$.
\item The line-operator category
  $\mathcal{C} \simeq \cA^!\text{-}\mathsf{mod}$: meromorphic tensor
  category.
\item The spectral $r$-matrix $r(z)$: binary genus-zero collision
  residue of~$\Theta_\cA$ (extracted in \S\ref{subsec:collision-filtration-recovery}).
\item The modular MC element
  $\Theta_\cA \in \MC(\gAmod)$: bar-intrinsic universal
  Maurer--Cartan element
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}).
\item The modular shadow connection
  $\nabla^{\mathrm{hol}}_{g,n} = d - \operatorname{Sh}_{g,n}(\Theta_\cA)$:
  flat connection on derived conformal blocks
  (constructed in \S\ref{sec:shadow-connection-construction}).
\end{enumerate}
Each entry is a determined projection of the single element
$\Theta_\cA$.  The entire datum is subordinated to the MC equation
\begin{equation}\label{eq:supp-mc-equation-holographic-intro}
D_\cA\,\Theta_\cA + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta_\cA, \Theta_\cA] \;=\; 0
\end{equation}
in the modular convolution dg~Lie algebra~$\gAmod$, and the single
identity $D_\cA^2 = 0$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:convolution-d-squared-zero}).
