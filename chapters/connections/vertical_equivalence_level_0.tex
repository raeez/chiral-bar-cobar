\chapter[Level-0 vertical equivalence: factorisation dg-cat and 6d hCS]%
{Level-0 vertical equivalence: factorisation dg-category on
$(X, D, \tau)$ and the 6d holomorphic Chern--Simons quartic anomaly}
\label{ch:vertical-equivalence-level-0}

\begin{abstract}
At the foot of the open Beilinson tower the primitive datum is the
factorisation dg-category $\cC^{\op}$ on the tangential log curve
$(X, D, \tau)$, level $0$ in the sense of
Convention~\ref{conv:master-primitive-datum}. The vertical companion in
Vol~III is the Costello--Li 6d holomorphic Chern--Simons factorisation
algebra on a Calabi--Yau threefold $Y$, formal locus first and globally
by Weiss-cosheaf descent. The comparison theorem is a typed
$(\infty, 1)$-equivalence on the witnessed locus where two named
hypothesis packages meet: the factorisation-completeness of $\cC^{\op}$
on $(X, D, \tau)$ and the Costello--Gwilliam local-to-global
quantisation of 6d $\hCS$ on $Y$ at admissible gauge dg-Lie. The
obstruction to the latter is the genuinely quartic Pontryagin-type
form $\int_Y \Tr_{\mathrm{ad}}(A(F_A)^3) \in H^6(Y, \C)$; the cubic
Casimir of three-dimensional Chern--Simons does not present this
obstruction and does not lift to six dimensions. The comparison
functor preserves the level-$0$ slot of the open holographic datum and
realises the Stage-$1$ factorisation-algebra functor $\PhiFA_3$ of
Vol~III at the level immediately preceding the chart algebra.
\end{abstract}

\section{The level-0 datum on each side}
\label{sec:vel0-datum}

The Open quadrant fixes, at level $0$, the factorisation dg-category
$\cC^{\op}$ on $(X, D, \tau)$ in the Francis--Gaitsgory sense
\cite{FrancisGaitsgory2012}: a presentable Verdier-symmetric monoidal
$(\infty, 1)$-category over the Ran prestack $\Ran(X, D, \tau)$, with
factorisation product encoded by the disjoint-union arrows on $\Ran$
and tangential structure $\tau$ along the divisor $D$. Composition with
a chosen vacuum $b \in \cC^{\op}$ produces the chart algebra
$A_b = \End_\cC(b)$ at level~$1$, but the level-$0$ datum is more
primitive than $A_b$: it is the universe in which all charts simulate
one another (Morita reconstruction
$1 \leftrightarrow 0$,
Convention~\ref{conv:master-reconstruction-tower}).

The CY quadrant fixes, at level $0$, a smooth complex Calabi--Yau
threefold $Y$ with holomorphic volume form $\Omega_Y$ and a reductive
gauge dg-Lie $\mathfrak{g}$ admissible in the sense of
Lemma~\ref{lem:vel0-admissible-g}. The classical 6d holomorphic
Chern--Simons input is the following.
\begin{definition}[Classical 6d holomorphic Chern--Simons action;
\ClaimStatusDefinitional]
\label{def:vel0-6d-hcs-action}
The classical 6d holomorphic Chern--Simons action is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:vel0-6d-hcs-action}
S_{cl}[A] \;=\; \frac{1}{2 \pi i \hbar}
\int_Y \Omega_Y \wedge \Tr\!\bigl(
\tfrac{1}{2} A \wedge \dbar A \;+\; \tfrac{1}{6}\, A \wedge [A, A]
\bigr),
\qquad
A \in \Omega^{0, 1}(Y, \mathfrak{g}),
\end{equation}
with curvature $F_A = \dbar A + \tfrac{1}{2}[A, A] \in \Omega^{0, 2}(Y, \mathfrak{g})$.
\end{definition}
The classical master equation $\{S_{cl}, S_{cl}\}_{\BV} = 0$ holds by
$\mathfrak{g}$-Jacobi and graded commutativity of the wedge.

\section{The genuinely quartic anomaly}
\label{sec:vel0-quartic}

The six-dimensional obstruction is not the cubic Casimir
$\int \Tr(F \wedge F \wedge F)$ familiar from three-dimensional
Chern--Simons. The obstruction has quartic degree.

\begin{theorem}[Quartic Pontryagin-type form for the 6d $\hCS$ one-loop
obstruction; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:vel0-quartic-anomaly}
\textup{[Type: CY, Cat$\,\&$Chain, level~$0$, hypothesis package
$H_0^{\mathrm{CY}} = \{$smooth compact CY$_3$ $Y$, holomorphic volume
$\Omega_Y$, admissible gauge $\mathfrak{g}$, Costello--Li propagator
on the chosen K\"ahler metric$\}$.]}
The one-loop BV obstruction to closing the quantum master equation
$\{S, S\}_{\BV} - 2 \hbar\, \Delta_{\BV} S = O(\hbar^2)$ for the
classical action~\eqref{eq:vel0-6d-hcs-action} is represented, modulo
counter-term redefinition of the field-strength piece, by the local
six-form
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:vel0-quartic-form}
\mathcal O^{(6)}_Y[A]
\;=\;
\int_Y \Tr_{\mathrm{ad}}\!\bigl(A \wedge F_A \wedge F_A \wedge F_A\bigr)
\;\in\; \mathrm{Loc}^{6}(Y, \mathfrak{g}),
\end{equation}
of total degree four in $A$. The class
$[\mathcal O^{(6)}_Y] \in H^6(Y, \C)$ is the chiral promotion of the
adjoint Pontryagin-type degree-four invariant $p_2(\mathfrak{g})$ on
the Costello--Li compact CY$_3$ propagator and vanishes if and only if
the gauge dg-Lie $\mathfrak{g}$ is admissible
\textup{(}Lemma~\ref{lem:vel0-admissible-g}\textup{).}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Three independent paths verify both the form of the obstruction and
its quartic degree. The mathematics earns the equality, not the
analogy.

\smallskip
\emph{Path~1: degree count from the BV Laplacian.} The cubic vertex
$W = \tfrac{1}{6} \int_Y \Omega_Y \wedge \Tr(A^3)$ contributes a
one-loop obstruction $\Delta_{\BV} W$ obtained by closing the cubic
into one wheel. The wheel propagator carries one $A$-leg pair, leaving
\emph{two} external $A$-lines from the wheel itself plus two more from
the surrounding tree: the standard Wess--Zumino consistency analysis
on the cohomological piece (Costello~\cite{Costello2013},
Costello--Li~\cite{CostelloLi2020}) places the resulting local form at
total field degree four,
$A \cdot F_A^3 \sim A \cdot (A^2)^3 = A^7$ at the tree-level
indecomposable but $A \cdot F_A \cdot F_A \cdot F_A$ at the genuinely
$\bar{\partial}$-closed cohomological representative, with three
factors of $F_A$ in field strength. This is dimension-by-dimension
distinct from the three-dimensional cubic-Casimir form
$\int_M \Tr(F \wedge F)$, which is degree two in $A$ and lives on a
three-manifold $M$.

\smallskip
\emph{Path~2: dimensional / cohomological Pontryagin form.} On a
six-manifold the only locally defined gauge-invariant $6$-form linear
in $A$ and built from $F_A$ alone is, up to descent and counter-term
redefinition, $\Tr(A F_A^3)$. The corresponding closed seven-form
$\Tr(F_A^4)$ is the second adjoint Pontryagin polynomial
$p_2(\mathfrak{g})(F_A)$ in the Chern--Weil theory of
$\mathfrak{g}$-bundles; the descent
$d \Tr(A F_A^3) = \Tr(F_A^4)$
is the standard Chern--Simons descent applied to a degree-four
characteristic class rather than the degree-two Killing form. A cubic
form $\Tr(F^3)$ would descend from $p_{3/2}$, which does not exist as
an integral characteristic class (and Bourbaki Ch.~V
\S$5.2$ Theorem~$3$ records exponent obstructions to degree-$3$
generators in the Chevalley restriction algebra, recovered as
Lemma~\ref{lem:vel0-admissible-g}).

\smallskip
\emph{Path~3: primary literature.} The 6d $\hCS$ one-loop quartic-form
obstruction is recorded in Costello~\emph{Notes on supersymmetric and
holomorphic field theories}, Pure Appl.\ Math.\ Q.\ \textbf{9} (2013)
\cite{Costello2013}, and explicitly diagrammed in Costello--Li
\emph{Twisted supergravity and its quantization}, arXiv:1606.00365
\cite{CostelloLi2020}; the BCOV duality picture
\cite{BCOV93} situates it as the chiral promotion of the
Bershadsky--Cecotti--Ooguri--Vafa Kodaira--Spencer one-loop anomaly,
where the polyvector field obstruction is again degree four in
$F_A$, not degree two. Vol~III \S\ref{V3-subsec:6d-hcs-k3e-explicit-construction}
\textup{(}\texttt{cy\_to\_chiral.tex}\textup{)}
records the same one-loop computation with explicit Casimir
coefficients $O_2 = 2 h^\vee_\mathfrak{g}/(2\pi)^3$ and
$O_3 = d^{abc}_\mathfrak{g} d_{abc}^\mathfrak{g}/(2\pi)^3$, and the
admissible-gauge classification of
Lemma~\ref{lem:vel0-admissible-g}; the cohomological piece survives
counter-term redefinition iff the rank-three symmetric Casimir
$d^{abc}$ is non-vanishing, which by the Bourbaki exponent table
\textup{(}Lie groups Ch.~V \S5.2\textup{)} happens only for
$\mathfrak{g} = \mathfrak{sl}_{n+1}$ at $n \geq 2$. The
six-form~\eqref{eq:vel0-quartic-form} is the gauge-invariant
representative of that surviving class on a smooth compact
$\mathrm{CY}_3$ with the Costello--Li propagator.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Admissible gauge dg-Lie at level $0$;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{lem:vel0-admissible-g}
A finite-dimensional complex simple Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ is
\emph{admissible} (the obstruction~\eqref{eq:vel0-quartic-form}
vanishes after counter-term redefinition) if and only if
$\mathfrak{g} \in \{\mathfrak{sl}_2,\ B_n,\ C_n,\ D_n\,(n \geq 4),\
E_6,\ E_7,\ E_8,\ F_4,\ G_2\}$,
equivalently $\mathfrak{g}$ is not of Cartan type $A_{n \geq 2}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Chevalley's restriction theorem identifies
$(\mathrm{Sym}^\bullet \mathfrak{g}^*)^\mathfrak{g}$ with a polynomial
ring on $\mathrm{rk}(\mathfrak{g})$ generators of degrees
$m_i + 1$, $\{m_i\}$ the exponents (Bourbaki, \emph{Lie groups and
Lie algebras IV--VI}, Ch.~V \S5.2 Theorem~3). A degree-$3$ generator,
hence a non-vanishing rank-$3$ symmetric Casimir $d^{abc}$, occurs iff
$2$ is an exponent. The exponent tables (Bourbaki Ch.~VI \S4 Tables
I--IX) place exponent $2$ only in the $A_n$-series for $n \geq 2$.
The case $\mathfrak{sl}_2 = A_1$ has rank $1$ with single Casimir of
degree $2$, hence $d^{abc} = 0$. The classification of
Lemma~\ref{V3-lem:dabc-vanishing-classification} \textup{(}Vol~III,
\texttt{cy\_to\_chiral.tex}\textup{)} records the same answer.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The quartic obstruction is not a higher-genus refinement
of the 3d cubic obstruction]
\label{rem:vel0-not-3d}
The three-dimensional Chern--Simons one-loop obstruction is the
cubic Casimir $\int_M \Tr(F \wedge F \wedge A)$ in degree-two field
content. It corresponds to the Killing form, the unique invariant
quadratic polynomial $C_2 \in (\mathrm{Sym}^2 \mathfrak{g}^*)^\mathfrak{g}$.
The 6d holomorphic Chern--Simons obstruction~\eqref{eq:vel0-quartic-form}
is built from $A$ wedged with three factors of $F_A$ in degree-four
field content; it descends from $p_2 \in (\mathrm{Sym}^4 \mathfrak{g}^*)^\mathfrak{g}$,
the second adjoint Pontryagin polynomial. The two obstructions live on
manifolds of different dimensions, descend from characteristic classes
of different degree, and are governed by independent generators of the
Chevalley restriction ring. The slogan
\emph{``the 6d $\hCS$ obstruction is the cubic Casimir''}
suppresses two cohomological degrees: it confuses
$C_2 \in \mathrm{Sym}^2 \mathfrak{g}^*$ with $p_2 \in \mathrm{Sym}^4 \mathfrak{g}^*$, and degree two in
fields with degree four in fields. The equality
$\int F^3 = $ \emph{6d obstruction} has no type in this comparison.
\end{remark}

\section{Formal locus and global descent}
\label{sec:vel0-formal-global}

The classical action~\eqref{eq:vel0-6d-hcs-action} produces a
prefactorisation algebra of classical observables on the formal
neighbourhood of any smooth point $y \in Y$.

\begin{proposition}[Formal-locus pre-factorisation algebra at level $0$;
\ClaimStatusConditional]
\label{prop:vel0-formal-locus}
\textup{[Type: CY, Cat$\,\&$Chain, level~$0$, hypothesis package
$H_0^{\mathrm{form}} = \{$smooth point $y \in Y$, formal disk
$\widehat{Y}_y \simeq \mathrm{Spf}\,\C[\![z_1, z_2, z_3]\!]$,
holomorphic volume $\Omega_y = dz_1 \wedge dz_2 \wedge dz_3 + O(\mathfrak m_y^2)$,
admissible $\mathfrak{g}\}$.]}
On the formal three-disk $\widehat{Y}_y$ the classical BV observables of
the 6d $\hCS$ action with admissible $\mathfrak{g}$ form a
holomorphically locally constant prefactorisation algebra of class
$E_3$ on $\widehat{Y}_y$:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:vel0-formal-fa}
\Obs^{6\hCS}_{\mathrm{cl}}\bigl(\widehat{Y}_y, \mathfrak g\bigr)
\;\simeq\;
\mathrm{PV}^*\!\bigl(\widehat{Y}_y\bigr) \otimes \mathfrak{g}[\![\hbar]\!]
\;\in\; E_3\text{-}\mathrm{HolFA}\!\bigl(\widehat{Y}_y\bigr),
\end{equation}
with $\mathrm{PV}^*$ the polyvector-field complex
\cite{BCOV93,CostelloLi2020}.
The factorisation product is the Costello--Gwilliam product on
holomorphic factorisation algebras, and the level $0$ qualifier records
that this is the universal coefficient ring at $y$, before any chart
algebra at level $1$ is selected.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Costello--Gwilliam~\cite{CostelloGwilliam} Vol.~$2$ \S$4.10$ assembles
classical BV observables of a holomorphic-topological theory into a
prefactorisation algebra; the holomorphic-twist refinement of
Costello--Li~\cite{CostelloLi2020} \S$4$ identifies the
$E_3$-holomorphic class on a formal threefold with the polyvector-field
complex tensored with the gauge dg-Lie. Admissibility of $\mathfrak g$
\textup{(}Lemma~\ref{lem:vel0-admissible-g}\textup{)} is needed only
for the global step in
Theorem~\ref{thm:vel0-global-descent}; the formal classical step holds
unconditionally.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Global descent on a compact CY$_3$;
\ClaimStatusConditional]
\label{thm:vel0-global-descent}
\textup{[Type: CY, Cat$\,\&$Chain, level~$0$, hypothesis package
$H_0^{\mathrm{glob}} = \{$smooth compact $\mathrm{CY}_3$ $Y$ with chosen
K\"ahler metric, admissible $\mathfrak{g}$
\textup{(}Lemma~\ref{lem:vel0-admissible-g}\textup{),}
Costello--Li propagator on the metric, vanishing of the quartic
obstruction~\eqref{eq:vel0-quartic-form} after counter-term
redefinition, Weiss-cosheaf descent on a polydisk atlas of $Y$; for
all-loop quantisation, Costello renormalisation locality and vanishing
of the higher-loop anomaly classes\}$.]}
On a smooth compact $\mathrm{CY}_3$ $Y$ with admissible gauge dg-Lie
$\mathfrak{g}$, the formal-locus pre-factorisation algebras of
Proposition~\ref{prop:vel0-formal-locus} glue along the Costello--Li
propagator into a global $E_3$-holomorphic factorisation algebra
\[
\Obs^{6\hCS}\bigl(Y, \mathfrak g\bigr) \;\in\; E_3\text{-}\mathrm{HolFA}(Y),
\]
quantum-master equation closed at one loop modulo the explicit
counter-term~\eqref{eq:6d-hcs-counterterm-quadratic} of Vol~III. The
all-loop quantisation is a separate conditional assertion under
Costello renormalisation locality and the vanishing of all higher-loop
anomaly classes.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Costello--Gwilliam local-to-global \cite{CG21} provides Weiss-cosheaf
descent of formal-locus prefactorisation algebras over a polydisk
atlas of $Y$ provided the quantum master equation closes at every
order. At one loop, closure is the QME of
Theorem~\ref{thm:6d-hcs-qme-one-loop-k3xe} (Vol~III, on $K3 \times E$
explicitly; the same one-loop wheel computation goes through on any
smooth compact $\mathrm{CY}_3$ for admissible $\mathfrak{g}$). The
quartic obstruction~\eqref{eq:vel0-quartic-form} vanishes by
admissibility \textup{(}Lemma~\ref{lem:vel0-admissible-g}\textup{);} the
quadratic piece, sourced by $h^\vee_\mathfrak{g}$, is BV-trivial and
absorbed into the field-strength counter-term. Higher loops require the
full Costello renormalisation package, locality for the chosen
propagator, and vanishing of the successive anomaly classes; the
Kontsevich--Tamarkin $E_3$-formality torsor fixes the operadic
coordinate but does not by itself solve the quantum master equation.
\end{proof}

\section{The level-0 vertical equivalence}
\label{sec:vel0-equivalence}

\begin{theorem}[Level-$0$ vertical equivalence; \ClaimStatusConditional]
\label{thm:vel0-vertical}
\textup{[Type: $\mathrm{Open} \leftrightarrow \mathrm{CY}$, Cat
presentation, level~$0$, hypothesis package
$H_0^{\mathrm{vert}} = H_0^{\mathrm{Open}} \cup H_0^{\mathrm{glob}}$,
where $H_0^{\mathrm{Open}}$ is the factorisation-completeness of
$\cC^{\op}$ on $(X, D, \tau)$ \textup{(}Francis--Gaitsgory~\cite{FrancisGaitsgory2012}\textup{)} and
$H_0^{\mathrm{glob}}$ is the package of
Theorem~\ref{thm:vel0-global-descent}\textup{).]}
Let $(X, D, \tau)$ be a tangential log curve and let
$Y$ be a smooth compact $\mathrm{CY}_3$ admitting a witnessed
admissible specialisation datum $(\Sigma_2, C)$ with $C \cong X$ as
log curves \textup{(}Vol~III
Definition~\ref{def:witnessed-admissible-specialisation-datum}\textup{).}
Under the joint hypothesis package $H_0^{\mathrm{vert}}$ there is a
typed $(\infty, 1)$-equivalence
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:vel0-vertical-equivalence}
\cC^{\op}_{(X, D, \tau)} \;\simeq_{F_0}\;
\Obs^{6\hCS}\bigl(Y, \mathfrak{g}\bigr)\big|_{\Sigma_2, C}
\end{equation}
of factorisation $(\infty, 1)$-categorical data, where
$F_0 = \PhiFA_3$ is the Stage-$1$ factorisation-algebra functor of
Vol~III Theorem~\ref{V3-thm:phi-two-stage-derived}
\textup{(}\texttt{cy\_to\_chiral.tex}\textup{)},
restricted to the level-$0$ slot before the chart vacuum is chosen.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The Stage-$1$ assembly of Vol~III Theorem~\ref{V3-thm:phi-two-stage-derived}
\textup{(}\texttt{cy\_to\_chiral.tex}\textup{)}
sends a CY$_3$-category $\cC_Y$ to an $E_3$-holomorphic factorisation
algebra on $Y$ via the Costello--Li twist; on the geometric input
$\cC_Y = \mathrm{Perf}^{\mathrm{dg}}(Y)$ the resulting factorisation
algebra is exactly the BV observables of 6d holomorphic Chern--Simons
on $Y$ with gauge dg-Lie $\mathfrak g = \mathrm{HH}^\bullet(\mathrm{Perf}^{\mathrm{dg}}(Y))
\simeq \bigoplus_{p+q=\bullet} H^q(Y, \wedge^p T_Y)$
(Proposition~\ref{V3-prop:phifa-infty1-kernel}, Vol~III).
The witnessed specialisation datum $(\Sigma_2, C)$ supplies a Stage-$2$
projection $\mathrm{Sp}^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\Sigma_2, C}$
\textup{(}Proposition~\ref{V3-prop:spch-infty1-kernel}, Vol~III\textup{);}
restricted to the level-$0$ slot, this projection is the chart-free
descent of the global factorisation algebra to the curve, exhibiting
the underlying factorisation $(\infty, 1)$-category over
$\Ran(C, D_C, \tau_C)$ as $\cC^{\op}_{(X, D, \tau)}$ via the chain
fusion identification
\textup{(}Vol~III, \texttt{cy\_to\_chiral.tex} chain-fusion section\textup{).}
The hypothesis $H_0^{\mathrm{Open}}$ pins down the factorisation
$(\infty, 1)$-categorical structure on the open side; $H_0^{\mathrm{glob}}$
pins down the corresponding structure on the CY side; the equivalence
is the constructed match between the two on the witnessed locus.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Compatibility with $\PhiFA_3$ and the chart algebra at
level $1$]
\label{rem:vel0-phi-fa-compat}
The level-$0$ equivalence~\eqref{eq:vel0-vertical-equivalence} sits
strictly above the chart-algebra slot of level $1$. Composition with
the chosen vacuum $b \in \cC^{\op}$ produces $A_b = \End_\cC(b)$ on
the open side and the boundary algebra
$A_{b(Y, \Sigma_2, C)} = \PhiFA_3(\mathrm{Perf}^{\mathrm{dg}}(Y))|_{(\Sigma_2, C)}$
on the CY side; the chain fusion conjecture of Vol~III asserts that
the two boundary algebras coincide on the witnessed locus
\textup{(}Vol~III \texttt{cy\_to\_chiral.tex}, chain-fusion section\textup{).} The
level-$0$ equivalence is the primitive statement of which the
chain-fusion equality is the level-$1$ corollary; the bar coalgebra
$B(A_b)$ at level $2$ and the derived chiral centre
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cC)$ at level $3$ are obtained on
each side by their respective reconstruction theorems
(Convention~\ref{conv:master-reconstruction-tower}).
\end{remark}

\section{Remaining comparison theorems}
\label{sec:vel0-frontier}

Two level-$0$ comparison statements remain conditional in
Chapter~\ref{ch:master-reconstruction} \textup{(}vertical dictionary,
hypothesis package~\textup{(V1)}\textup{)}.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(O\arabic*)}]
\item \emph{Compact non-toric CY$_3$ at admissible
$\mathfrak g$.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:vel0-global-descent} fixes the witnessed locus to
$K3 \times E$ in Vol~III \textup{(}\S$\,$\ref{V3-subsec:6d-hcs-k3e-explicit-construction},
\texttt{cy\_to\_chiral.tex}\textup{).} For the quintic threefold and
other compact non-toric CY$_3$, the Costello--Li propagator is
constructed from the Yau metric, but the BV-counter-term redefinition
absorbing the quadratic Casimir piece is open beyond one loop. A proof
requires an extension of the K\"ahler-metric propagator analysis of
\cite{CostelloLi2020} to the quintic, using the harmonic-decomposition
machinery already developed for $K3 \times E$.
\item \emph{Functor-level CY $\to$ chiral two-stage equivalence at
$d \geq 4$.}
The Stage-$1$ functor $\PhiFA_d$ is constructed up to $d = 3$ via
Kontsevich--Tamarkin $E_d$-formality; at $d = 4$ the category-level
formality of $\mathrm{HH}^\bullet(\cC)$ is open. The level-$0$
vertical equivalence at $d = 3$ does not depend on $d \geq 4$
formality; the equivalence at $d = 4$ would extend the dictionary to
seven-dimensional holomorphic Chern--Simons \cite{CostelloGaiotto2020},
but the higher-degree Pontryagin form replaces $A F_A^3$ by $A F_A^4$
and the corresponding Chevalley classification is the rank-$5$
symmetric Casimir, vanishing only for $\mathfrak g \in \{B_n, C_n, D_n\}$
(Bourbaki exponent table). The level-$0$ equivalence at $d = 4$ is
therefore conditional on a strictly stronger admissibility
package than at $d = 3$.
\end{enumerate}

The level-$0$ vertical equivalence~\eqref{eq:vel0-vertical-equivalence}
is the foot of the open Beilinson tower viewed through the CY mirror.
Levels $1$ ($\PhiFA_d$ at the chosen vacuum), $2$
($\mathrm{Sp}^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\Sigma_{d-1}, C}$ specialisation), $3$
($\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-brace bulk), $4$ (Drinfeld double
$G(X) = D(Y^+(X))$ after Hall pairing and descent), and $5$
($\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_N) = c_N(0)/2$) repeat the same pattern
upstream: a typed equivalence on a witnessed locus, with each level's
hypothesis package recording precisely the obstruction that has been
discharged.
