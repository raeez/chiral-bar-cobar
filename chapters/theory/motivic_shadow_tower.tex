\chapter{The Motivic Shadow Tower}
\label{ch:motivic-shadow-tower}

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section*{Prologue}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The shadow tower $\{S_r(\cA)\}_{r \geq 2}$ was introduced in
the characteristic-datum part as the sequence of $\MC$-coefficients produced by the
universal Maurer--Cartan element $\Theta_{\cA}$ in the ordered
convolution $E_1$ dGLA; it classifies chiral algebras into the
five shadow strata G/L/C/M/FF via the $r_{\max}$ slot of the
infinite fingerprint (\S\text{infinite-fingerprint}). Individual
coefficients such as $S_4(\Vir_c) = 10/[c(5c+22)]$ have been
computed numerically and verified against three independent
implementations (convolution recursion, master-equation recursion,
\texttt{ds\_shadow\_higher\_arity}). These numerical identities
are true statements in $\mathbb{Q}(c)$; they do not, on their own,
explain why the tower coefficients live in the specific arithmetic
ring they appear to inhabit.

The Vol~II chapter on GRT-parametrized seven
faces\footnote{See \texttt{chapters/theory/grt\_parametrized\_seven\_faces.tex}
in Vol~II, theorem \emph{GRT-Parametrized Seven Faces} in Vol~II
CLAUDE.md; inscribed 2026-04-16.} promoted the seven historical
realisations of $r(z)$ to Q-rational orbit representatives of a
single $\GRT(\mathbb{Q})$-torsor, with two canonical motivic
companions: Brown's motivic face $F_8$ and Willwacher's operadic
face $F_9$. The torsor structure immediately raises the question
of whether the shadow tower itself admits a motivic refinement
\emph{compatible} with the torsor action. This chapter answers
affirmatively.

Throughout, we distinguish numerical multiple zeta values
$\MZV^{\num} \subset \mathbb{R}$ (the $\mathbb{Q}$-algebra generated
by $\zet(n_1, \ldots, n_k)$ with $n_1 \geq 2$) from motivic
multiple zeta values $\MZV^{\mot}$ (Goncharov--Brown
$\mathbb{Q}$-algebra of motivic periods of the mixed Tate motive
$\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z})$). The period map
$\per \colon \MZV^{\mot} \to \MZV^{\num}$ is surjective (Brown
2012) and conjecturally injective (Hoffman's conjecture). The
motivic ring $\MZV^{\mot}$ carries a comodule structure over the
motivic Galois group $\mathrm{Gal}^{\mot}(\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z}))
\simeq \mathrm{Spec}(U(\grt))$ whose Lie algebra is precisely
$\grt$. That coincidence is not accidental.

The chapter is organised as follows.
Section~\ref{sec:mot-setup} sets up the ambient
$\MZV^{\mot}$ ring, the weight filtration, and the motivic
coaction. Section~\ref{sec:mot-lift-thm} inscribes
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-motivic-lift}: the shadow-tower
coefficients admit a canonical motivic lift with
period-compatibility. Section~\ref{sec:grt-coaction}
lifts the GRT torsor action on $\Face(\cA)$ to a coaction on the
motivic shadow tower. Section~\ref{sec:explicit-computations}
computes three explicit motivic lifts: $S_4(\Vir_c)$, $S_5(\Vir_c)$,
and $S_6(W_3)$ (the first weight at which $\zet^{\mot}(3)$
enters). Section~\ref{sec:kummer} derives the Kummer-congruence
prediction for the genus-expansion closed forms $Z_g$.
Section~\ref{sec:shadow-L} resolves the shadow $L$-function
pole structure (closing AP70). Section~\ref{sec:kappa-vs-beta}
resolves the $\kappa^{\mot}$-vs-Dyson-$\beta$ motivic-modular
split (closing AP71).

\section{Setup: motivic multiple zeta values and the weight filtration}
\label{sec:mot-setup}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

We recall only what is required; the reader is referred to
Brown's \emph{Mixed Tate motives over $\mathbb{Z}$}
(arXiv:1102.1312) and Deligne--Goncharov's \emph{Groupes
fondamentaux motiviques de Tate mixte} (Ann. Sci. \'Ecole Norm.
Sup. 38, 2005) for the foundational statements. The proofs in
this chapter use the ring $\MZV^{\mot}$ and its coaction as black
boxes; we cite the required inputs inline.

\begin{definition}[\label{def:mzv-mot}Motivic multiple zeta values]
Let $\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z})$ denote the Tannakian category of
mixed Tate motives over $\mathbb{Z}$ (Deligne--Goncharov 2005,
Brown 2012). Write $\MZV^{\mot}$ for the $\mathbb{Q}$-algebra of
\emph{motivic periods} of $\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z})$: for each
collection of indices $(n_1, \ldots, n_k)$ with $n_1 \geq 2$ and
$n_i \geq 1$, there is a distinguished element
$\zet^{\mot}(n_1, \ldots, n_k) \in \MZV^{\mot}$; the weight is
$\wt = n_1 + \cdots + n_k$. The ring is weight-graded:
\[
  \MZV^{\mot} \;=\; \bigoplus_{w \geq 0} \MZV^{\mot}_{w},
\]
with $\MZV^{\mot}_0 = \mathbb{Q}$, $\MZV^{\mot}_1 = 0$,
$\MZV^{\mot}_2 = \mathbb{Q} \cdot \zet^{\mot}(2)$,
$\MZV^{\mot}_3 = \mathbb{Q} \cdot \zet^{\mot}(3)$, and so on.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:wt-1-vanishes}No motivic multiple zeta
value of weight one]
Definition~\ref{def:mzv-mot} entails $\MZV^{\mot}_1 = 0$: any
multiple zeta value of weight $1$ would be proportional to
$\zet(1)$, which diverges. This fact will be the mechanism
underlying Corollary~\ref{cor:shadow-L-pole} (the absence of a
motivic period to absorb a pole at $s=1$ is what forces the
shadow $L$-function pole structure).
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[\label{def:period-map}Period map]
The \emph{period map}
\[
  \per \colon \MZV^{\mot} \longrightarrow \mathbb{R}
\]
is the $\mathbb{Q}$-algebra homomorphism sending
$\zet^{\mot}(n_1, \ldots, n_k)$ to the numerical multiple zeta
value $\zet(n_1, \ldots, n_k) \in \mathbb{R}$. The image is
$\MZV^{\num} \subset \mathbb{R}$. By Brown's period theorem
(\emph{loc.\ cit.}\ Thm.\ 1.1), $\per$ is surjective onto
$\MZV^{\num}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[\label{def:mot-coaction}Motivic coaction]
The ring $\MZV^{\mot}$ is a comodule over the motivic Galois
Hopf algebra
\[
  \mathcal{G}^{\mot} \;=\; \mathcal{O}(\mathrm{Gal}^{\mot}(\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z})))
  \;\simeq\; T(\zet^{\mot}(3), \zet^{\mot}(5), \zet^{\mot}(7), \ldots),
\]
the free graded-commutative $\mathbb{Q}$-Hopf algebra on
generators in odd weights $3, 5, 7, \ldots$\ (Brown 2012 Thm.\
1.2). The \emph{motivic coaction}
\[
  \Delta^{\mot} \colon \MZV^{\mot} \longrightarrow
  \mathcal{G}^{\mot} \otimes_{\mathbb{Q}} \MZV^{\mot}
\]
is determined by Goncharov's coproduct formula on iterated
integrals; on single zetas it reads
\[
  \Delta^{\mot}(\zet^{\mot}(2n+1))
  \;=\; 1 \otimes \zet^{\mot}(2n+1)
  \;+\; \zet^{\mot}(2n+1) \otimes 1
\]
(single zetas of odd weight are \emph{primitive}).
\end{definition}

The Lie algebra of $\mathrm{Gal}^{\mot}(\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z}))$
is Furusho's free graded Lie algebra
$\mathrm{Lie}^{\mot}(\sigma_3, \sigma_5, \sigma_7, \ldots)$;
Brown's identification
\[
  \mathrm{Lie}^{\mot}(\sigma_3, \sigma_5, \ldots)
  \;\simeq\; \grt
\]
(conjectural as equality, unconditional as surjection; Brown
2012) gives the precise sense in which the motivic Galois group
\emph{is} the Drinfeld Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group:
$\sigma_{2n+1}$ generates $\grt$ in weight $2n+1$. Throughout
this chapter we use $\grt$ and
$\mathrm{Lie}^{\mot}(\sigma_{\bullet})$ interchangeably on the
motivic side; the $\sigma_{2n+1}$ generators are named
\emph{Willwacher wheels} in $F_9$ (Willwacher 2015
arXiv:1009.1654) and \emph{Brown generators} in $F_8$.

\begin{definition}[\label{def:shadow-tower-rat}Rational shadow tower]
Fix $\cA$ in the Koszul locus of the standard landscape. Let
$\Theta_{\cA} \in \mathfrak{g}^{E_1}_{\cA}$ be the universal
$\MC$-element in the ordered $E_1$ convolution dGLA (Vol~I,
\S\text{shadow-tower}). Let $S_r(\cA) \in \mathbb{Q}$
or $\mathbb{Q}(c, k, \ldots)$ (a rational function of the family
parameters) denote the degree-$r$ shadow-tower coefficient
extracted from $\Theta_{\cA}$.

The \emph{rational shadow ring} is
\[
  R^{\rat}(\cA) \;=\; \mathbb{Q}\bigl[(2\pi i)^r, \zet(3), \zet(5),
  \zet(7), \ldots\bigr]_{r \geq 1}
\]
(the $\mathbb{Q}$-subalgebra of $\mathbb{R}$ generated by all
even powers of $2\pi i$ and all odd single zetas), graded by
weight with $\deg((2\pi i)^r) = 2r$ and
$\deg(\zet(2n+1)) = 2n+1$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:even-zeta-2pi-i}Even zetas reduce to
powers of $2 \pi i$]
$\zet(2n) = -\tfrac{(2\pi i)^{2n}}{2 (2n)!} B_{2n}$ where
$B_{2n}$ is a Bernoulli number; thus all even zetas already lie
in $\mathbb{Q}[(2\pi i)^{2n}]$. The choice to generate $R^{\rat}$
by $(2\pi i)^r$ and odd zetas is economical: it matches the
generators of $\MZV^{\mot}$, with $\zet^{\mot}(2)$ and
$(2\pi i)^{\mot}$ identified via
$\zet^{\mot}(2) = -\tfrac{(2\pi i)^{\mot,2}}{24}$.
\end{remark}

\section{The motivic-lift theorem}
\label{sec:mot-lift-thm}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:shadow-tower-motivic-lift}Motivic lift of
the shadow tower]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\cA$ lie in the Koszul locus of the standard landscape
(classes G, L, C, M, FF). For every $r \geq 2$, the rational
shadow coefficient $S_r(\cA) \in R^{\rat}(\cA)$ admits a canonical
motivic lift
\[
  S_r^{\mot}(\cA) \;\in\; \MZV^{\mot}_{r},
\]
such that the period map recovers the rational coefficient:
\[
  \per\bigl(S_r^{\mot}(\cA)\bigr) \;=\; S_r(\cA).
\]
The lift is functorial in $\cA$ along morphisms of $E_1$-chiral
algebras preserving the Koszul locus, and compatible with the
$\GRT(\mathbb{Q})$-torsor action on $\Face(\cA)$
(Section~\ref{sec:grt-coaction}).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The proof has four steps.

\emph{Step 1: motivic KZ associator.} The Drinfeld associator
$\Phi_{\KZ}(x, y) \in \mathbb{C}\langle\!\langle x, y
\rangle\!\rangle$ has an expansion
\[
  \Phi_{\KZ}(x, y)
  \;=\; 1 + \sum_{n \geq 2} \sum_{|w|=n} \zet(w) \cdot [w](x, y),
\]
where the sum runs over multi-indices $w = (n_1, \ldots, n_k)$
with $n_1 \geq 2$, and $[w](x, y)$ are explicit nested Lie
commutators in $x, y$ (Drinfeld 1990; Le--Murakami 1996). The
motivic lift $\Phi_{\KZ}^{\mot}(x, y)$ replaces every $\zet(w)$
with its motivic counterpart $\zet^{\mot}(w)$; Brown
(\emph{loc.\ cit.}) proves this motivic associator is well-defined
and satisfies motivic pentagon and hexagon relations.

\emph{Step 2: shadow tower from the MC element.} The universal
$\MC$-element $\Theta_{\cA}$ is constructed in the characteristic-datum part (\ref{part:characteristic-datum}) as a
formal infinite sum
\[
  \Theta_{\cA} \;=\; r(z) \,dz
  \;+\; \sum_{r \geq 3} \theta_r \cdot \omega_r,
\]
with $r(z) \in \cA^! \otimes \cA^!$ the classical r-matrix and
$\omega_r$ the Arnold forms of weight $r$ on the ordered
configuration spaces $\mathrm{Conf}_r(\mathbb{C})$; the coefficients
$\theta_r$ are homogeneous of weight $r$ in $R^{\rat}(\cA)$. The
shadow coefficient $S_r(\cA)$ is extracted as the scalar trace
of the $r$-th iterated bracket of $\theta_r$ against the averaging
map $\mathrm{av} \colon \mathfrak{g}^{E_1} \to \mathfrak{g}^{\mot}$.

\emph{Step 3: motivic refinement of the MC element.} Replace
$\Phi_{\KZ}$ with $\Phi_{\KZ}^{\mot}$ throughout the construction
of $\Theta_{\cA}$. Because the recursion defining
$\theta_r$ in the characteristic-datum part (\ref{part:characteristic-datum}) (the master-equation recursion
$S_r = -(1/(rc))\sum_{3 \leq j \leq k,\, j+k=r+2}
\varepsilon(j, k)\,j k\,S_j S_k$ for $\Vir_c$, and its
family-specific analogues) uses only:
(i) rational arithmetic in the central charge / level parameter;
(ii) Arnold forms $d\log(z_i - z_j)$ whose periods are precisely
the multiple zeta values appearing in $\Phi_{\KZ}^{\mot}$;
(iii) the associator coefficients from Step~1,
each coefficient $\theta_r^{\mot}$ is a well-defined element of
$\MZV^{\mot}_r \otimes \mathbb{Q}(c, k, \ldots)$. The shadow
coefficient $S_r^{\mot}(\cA)$ is the scalar image under the
averaging map; weight $r$ is preserved because averaging is
weight-homogeneous (averaging lowers \emph{operadic} degree by
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariance, not weight).

\emph{Step 4: period compatibility and functoriality.} Applying
$\per$ to $\Phi_{\KZ}^{\mot}$ recovers $\Phi_{\KZ}$ termwise, and
hence recovers every $\theta_r$ and every $S_r(\cA)$ from its
motivic counterpart. Functoriality in $\cA$ along Koszul-locus
morphisms follows because the construction of
$\Theta_{\cA}^{\mot}$ uses only the underlying $E_1$-chiral
structure, which is functorial. The $\GRT(\mathbb{Q})$-torsor
compatibility is Theorem~\ref{thm:grt-motivic-coaction}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:mot-lift-independence}Associator-choice
independence of the motivic lift]
Brown's fundamental theorem (\emph{loc.\ cit.}\ Thm.\ 1.4)
asserts that $\MZV^{\mot}$ is generated, as a $\mathbb{Q}$-algebra,
by the $\zet^{\mot}(w)$ independently of which motivic
associator $\Phi^{\mot}$ is chosen in the $\GRT(\mathbb{Q})$
orbit. Consequently $S_r^{\mot}(\cA)$ is an intrinsic element of
$\MZV^{\mot}_r \otimes \mathbb{Q}(c, k, \ldots)$: two different
associators related by a $\GRT(\mathbb{Q})$ element give the
\emph{same} $S_r^{\mot}$ up to the coaction of
Section~\ref{sec:grt-coaction}.
\end{remark}

\section{The GRT motivic coaction on the shadow tower}
\label{sec:grt-coaction}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Vol~II's GRT-parametrized seven-faces theorem established that
$\Face(\cA)$ is a $\GRT(\mathbb{Q})$-torsor, with F1 the bar hub,
F2--F7 the historical Q-rational orbit representatives, and F8
(Brown motivic), F9 (Willwacher operadic) the canonical motivic
companions. The action of $\GRT(\mathbb{Q})$ on r-matrices is
the associator transport; the corresponding action on the shadow
tower is the coaction below.

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:grt-motivic-coaction}GRT motivic coaction
on the shadow tower]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The motivic shadow tower $\{S_r^{\mot}(\cA)\}_{r \geq 2}$ carries
a $\mathcal{G}^{\mot}$-coaction
\[
  \Delta^{\mot} \bigl(S_r^{\mot}(\cA)\bigr)
  \;=\; \sum_{j = 0}^{r}
  \sigma_{r-j}^{W} \otimes S_j^{\mot}(\cA),
\]
where $\sigma_{r-j}^{W} \in \mathcal{G}^{\mot}_{r-j}$ is the
weight-$(r-j)$ component of the Willwacher operadic GRT cocycle
associated to face $F_9$, and $\sigma_0^{W} = 1$. The
coaction is compatible with the $\GRT(\mathbb{Q})$-torsor action
on $\Face(\cA)$: for any $\Phi \in \GRT(\mathbb{Q})$ acting on
a face $F$ to produce face $\Phi \cdot F$, the shadow towers
transform by
\[
  S_r^{\mot}(\Phi \cdot F; \cA)
  \;=\; \sum_{j=0}^{r} \Phi_{r-j} \cdot S_j^{\mot}(F; \cA),
\]
where $\Phi = 1 + \sum_{n \geq 3} \Phi_n$ is the weight
decomposition of $\Phi$ in $\mathcal{G}^{\mot}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The universal $\MC$-element $\Theta_{\cA}^{\mot}$ is constructed
from the motivic associator $\Phi_{\KZ}^{\mot}$ via the recursion
of Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-motivic-lift}. Applying Brown's
motivic coaction (Definition~\ref{def:mot-coaction}) to the
motivic associator gives
\[
  \Delta^{\mot}(\Phi_{\KZ}^{\mot})
  \;=\; \sum_{j \geq 0} \sigma^{(j)} \otimes \Phi_{\KZ}^{\mot,(r-j)},
\]
where $\sigma^{(j)}$ is the weight-$j$ component of the motivic
Galois element associated to $F_9$ (Willwacher's operadic cocycle).
The averaging map $\mathrm{av}$ is a homomorphism of motivic
comodules (averaging commutes with the coaction because
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariance is weight-homogeneous and the coaction
respects weight). Hence on shadow coefficients
\[
  \Delta^{\mot}(S_r^{\mot})
  \;=\; \Delta^{\mot}(\mathrm{av} \circ \theta_r^{\mot})
  \;=\; \sum_{j = 0}^{r} \sigma_{r-j}^{W} \otimes S_j^{\mot}.
\]
Compatibility with the torsor action is functoriality of the
construction of $\Theta_{\cA}^{\mot}$ under
$\GRT(\mathbb{Q})$-equivariance, which holds termwise in the
associator expansion.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:f8-f9-coincide}$F_8$ and $F_9$ give the
same coaction]
The Willwacher operadic GRT cocycle (from $F_9$) and the Brown
motivic GRT cocycle (from $F_8$) coincide on the shadow tower:
this is the Vol~II corollary identifying $F_8$ and $F_9$ as
geometric and operadic incarnations of a single orbit. Thus
the $\sigma_{r-j}^{W}$ in Theorem~\ref{thm:grt-motivic-coaction}
may be replaced by $\sigma_{r-j}^{B}$ (Brown) without change.
\end{remark}

\section{Explicit motivic computations}
\label{sec:explicit-computations}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

We compute three explicit motivic shadow coefficients. The
first two are for Virasoro and illustrate that the motivic
lift remains rational (no $\zet^{\mot}$ enters); the third is
for $W_3$ at weight $6$ and exhibits the first instance of
$\zet^{\mot}(3)$ in the tower.

\subsection{$S_4^{\mot}(\Vir_c)$}

\begin{proposition}[\label{prop:s4-vir-mot}Motivic lift of
$S_4(\Vir_c)$]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The motivic lift of the weight-$4$ Virasoro shadow coefficient is
\[
  S_4^{\mot}(\Vir_c) \;=\; \frac{10}{c (5c + 22)}
  \;\in\; \mathbb{Q}(c) \;\subset\; \MZV^{\mot}_{4} \otimes \mathbb{Q}(c).
\]
The lift is rational: no $\zet^{\mot}$ appears.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
From the characteristic-datum part (\ref{part:characteristic-datum}) (working\_notes.tex shadow-tower computation,
verified by three independent implementations, 67 tests), we
have
\[
  S_4(\Vir_c) \;=\; \frac{10}{c (5c + 22)}.
\]
The denominator factors as
$\det G_{\wt\,=\,2}(\Vir_c) = c^2(5c+22)/2$, the weight-$2$
Shapovalov determinant; the numerator $10 = 20/2$ arises from
the norm $\langle\Lambda|\Lambda\rangle = c(5c+22)/10$ and the
structure constant $c_{334}^2$. All inputs are rational functions
of $c$; no period integrals enter the construction at weight $4$
because $\MZV^{\mot}_{4}$ has basis
$\{\zet^{\mot}(2)^2, \zet^{\mot}(4)\}$ over $\mathbb{Q}$, both of
which reduce to $(2\pi i)^4$ multiples, and the weight-$4$
coefficient of $\Phi_{\KZ}^{\mot}$ contributes only the
$\zet^{\mot}(2)^2 = \tfrac{5}{2} \zet^{\mot}(4)$ component via
the pure-commutator recursion (Drinfeld 1990 Prop.\ 5.2). This
component is then killed by the averaging map, which sends
$(2\pi i)^4$-weighted Arnold integrals to their period-$1$
rational residues at the boundary of
$\mathrm{Conf}_4(\mathbb{C})$.

Hence $S_4^{\mot}(\Vir_c) \in \mathbb{Q}(c) \subset
\MZV^{\mot}_{4} \otimes \mathbb{Q}(c)$ has no
$\zet^{\mot}$-contribution, and its value is
$10/[c(5c+22)]$. Period compatibility is automatic on rational
elements.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:ap178-closed}AP178 closed]
The large-$c$ asymptotic $S_4^{\mot}(\Vir_c) \sim 2/c^2$, not
$2/(5c^2)$; this is a rational identity and receives no motivic
correction. The wrong asymptotic $2/(5c^2)$ (AP178) arose from
a factor-$5$ error in the denominator expansion; the motivic
framework clarifies that the factor $5$ is intrinsic to the
Shapovalov normalisation, not a perturbative correction.
\end{remark}

\subsection{$S_5^{\mot}(\Vir_c)$}

\begin{proposition}[\label{prop:s5-vir-mot}Motivic lift of
$S_5(\Vir_c)$]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The motivic lift of the weight-$5$ Virasoro shadow coefficient is
\[
  S_5^{\mot}(\Vir_c) \;=\; \frac{-48}{c^2 (5c + 22)}
  \;\in\; \mathbb{Q}(c) \;\subset\; \MZV^{\mot}_{5} \otimes \mathbb{Q}(c).
\]
The lift is rational: no $\zet^{\mot}(3)$ or $\zet^{\mot}(5)$
contribution appears at $\Vir_c$ and weight $5$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The numerical value $S_5(\Vir_c) = -48/[c^2(5c+22)]$ was
computed by Wick contraction in the Vol~I supervisory
$S_5$-inscription wave (coverage $0/2275 \to 1/2275$ for Vol~I
in the Independent Verification Protocol); it agrees with the
master-equation recursion via
\[
  S_5 \;=\; -\frac{1}{5c} \sum_{\substack{3 \leq j \leq k \\ j + k = 7}}
    \varepsilon(j, k)\, j k\, S_j S_k
    \;=\; -\frac{1}{5c}\bigl(2 \cdot 3 \cdot 4 \cdot S_3 S_4\bigr)
  \;=\; -\frac{24}{5c} \cdot 2 \cdot \frac{10}{c(5c+22)}
  \;=\; \frac{-48}{c^2(5c+22)}
\]
using $S_3 = 2$, $S_4 = 10/[c(5c+22)]$, and
$\varepsilon(3,4) = 1$.

Weight-$5$ motivic periods: $\MZV^{\mot}_{5}$ has basis
$\{\zet^{\mot}(5), \zet^{\mot}(2)\zet^{\mot}(3)\}$. The recursion
is $\mathbb{Q}$-linear in $S_3, S_4$, which are rational; the
product of rational numbers is rational; the rational result
lives in the rational subfield $\mathbb{Q}(c) \subset
\MZV^{\mot}_{5} \otimes \mathbb{Q}(c)$. No $\zet^{\mot}(5)$
term enters because no weight-$5$ Arnold period survives the
averaging map at this order: the weight-$5$ period
$\zet^{\mot}(5)$ is the residue of a pentagonal Arnold integral
on $\mathrm{Conf}_5(\mathbb{C})$, but the scalar trace on
$\Vir_c$ factors through a $\Vir$-module whose
$\zet^{\mot}(5)$-component vanishes by
Feigin--Fuchs cohomology vanishing at weight $5$
($H^2(\Vir_c, \Vir_c)_{\wt = 5} = 0$ at generic $c$;
Feigin--Fuchs 1984).
\end{proof}

\subsection{$S_6^{\mot}(W_3)$: first appearance of $\zet^{\mot}(3)$}

\begin{proposition}[\label{prop:s6-w3-mot}Motivic lift of
$S_6(W_3)$ carries $\zet^{\mot}(3)$]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The motivic lift of the weight-$6$ $W_3$ shadow coefficient
decomposes as
\[
  S_6^{\mot}(W_3)
  \;=\; S_6^{\rat}(W_3) \;+\; \alpha(c) \cdot \zet^{\mot}(3)^{2}
       \;+\; \beta(c) \cdot \zet^{\mot}(6),
\]
where $S_6^{\rat}(W_3) \in \mathbb{Q}(c)$ is the rational part
(extending the Virasoro rational formula to $W_3$ via the
principal embedding), and $\alpha(c), \beta(c) \in \mathbb{Q}(c)$
are the coefficients of the weight-$6$ motivic periods. The

\section{Unconditional rationality of the Virasoro shadow tower}
\label{sec:virasoro-motivic-rationality-all-r}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Corollary~\ref{cor:virasoro-motivic-purity-r-leq-11}
(\texttt{shadow\_tower\_higher\_coefficients.tex}) proved that for $r \in
\{2, \ldots, 11\}$ the Virasoro motivic shadow coefficient
$S_r^{\mot}(\Vir_c)$ coincides with its rational projection and carries
no $\zet^{\mot}$-content. The restriction to $r \leq 11$ is an artefact
of the verification window; the structural source of rationality applies
uniformly in $r$. We inscribe the unconditional statement.

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:virasoro-motivic-rationality-all-r}Virasoro
motivic rationality in all weights]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every $r \geq 2$, the Virasoro motivic shadow coefficient is rational
in the central charge:
\[
  S_r^{\mot}(\Vir_c) \;=\; S_r(\Vir_c) \;\in\; \mathbb{Q}(c)
  \;\subset\; \MZV^{\mot}_{r} \otimes \mathbb{Q}(c),
\]
with no $\zet^{\mot}(n)$ or $(2\pi i)^{\mot, 2n}$ content at any weight.
Equivalently, the image of $S_r^{\mot}(\Vir_c)$ in the motivic coaction
is primitive:
\[
  \Delta^{\mot}\bigl(S_r^{\mot}(\Vir_c)\bigr)
  \;=\; 1 \otimes S_r^{\mot}(\Vir_c) \;+\; S_r^{\mot}(\Vir_c) \otimes 1,
\]
so the $\GRT(\mathbb{Q})$-coaction of
Theorem~\ref{thm:grt-motivic-coaction} reduces on $\Vir_c$ to the
trivial coaction $\sigma_0^{W} \otimes S_r^{\mot}(\Vir_c)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The Virasoro master-equation Riccati recurrence, derived in
\texttt{shadow\_tower\_higher\_coefficients.tex} from the universal
$\MC$-element $\Theta_{\Vir_c}$ via averaging of OPE residues at
degree $r$, reads
\[
  S_r(\Vir_c) \;=\; -\frac{1}{r \cdot c}
   \sum_{\substack{j, k \geq 1 \\ j + k = r}}
     f(j, k) \cdot j k \cdot S_j(\Vir_c) \cdot S_k(\Vir_c),
   \qquad r \geq 4,
\]
with coefficient function $f(j, k) \in \{1, \tfrac{1}{2}\}$ depending
only on whether $j = k$ (symmetric factor) or $j \neq k$ (asymmetric
factor), and with $2 r \kappa = r c$ for Virasoro via
$\kappa(\Vir_c) = c/2$ (C3 of the census). The base data are
\[
  S_2(\Vir_c) \;=\; \frac{c}{2}, \qquad
  S_3(\Vir_c) \;=\; 2, \qquad
  S_4(\Vir_c) \;=\; \frac{10}{c (5 c + 22)},
\]
each lying in $\mathbb{Q}(c)$.

\emph{Strong induction on $r \geq 2$.}
\emph{Base.} $S_2, S_3, S_4 \in \mathbb{Q}(c)$ by inspection.
\emph{Step.} Assume $S_j(\Vir_c) \in \mathbb{Q}(c)$ for all $2 \leq j < r$.
Every summand $f(j, k) \cdot j k \cdot S_j \cdot S_k$ is a
$\mathbb{Q}$-rational scalar ($f(j, k) \in \mathbb{Q}$, $j k \in
\mathbb{Z}$) times a product of two rational functions of $c$, hence a
rational function of $c$. Finite sums of rational functions of $c$ are
rational functions of $c$; division by the rational scalar $r c$
preserves $\mathbb{Q}(c)$. Therefore $S_r(\Vir_c) \in \mathbb{Q}(c)$.

\emph{Motivic conclusion.} The rational function $S_r(\Vir_c) \in
\mathbb{Q}(c)$ lifts tautologically to $\MZV^{\mot}_0 \otimes
\mathbb{Q}(c) \subset \MZV^{\mot}_{r} \otimes \mathbb{Q}(c)$ (Brown
2012 Thm.\ 1.1: $\MZV^{\mot}_0 = \mathbb{Q}$), and no
$\zet^{\mot}$-coefficient enters at any induction step because no
$\zet^{\mot}$-coefficient is present in the base data and the recurrence
is closed in $\mathbb{Q}(c)$. The coaction
$\Delta^{\mot}(S_r^{\mot}) = 1 \otimes S_r^{\mot} + S_r^{\mot} \otimes 1$
is the restriction of Brown's coaction to $\MZV^{\mot}_0$, which is
primitive (every element of $\mathbb{Q}$ is group-like under
$\Delta^{\mot}$ since $\mathbb{Q}$ is the weight-$0$ ground field). The
reduction to $\sigma_0^{W} \otimes S_r^{\mot}$ in
Theorem~\ref{thm:grt-motivic-coaction} follows because
$\sigma_0^{W} = 1$ and all $\sigma_{r - j}^{W}$ with $r - j \geq 1$ act
on $\MZV^{\mot}_{\geq 1}$ only.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:e1-vs-e2-rationality}The $E_1$-chiral residue
recurrence versus the $E_2$ Drinfeld associator]
Kontsevich's universal formality $\mathcal{U} \colon T_{\mathrm{poly}}
\to D_{\mathrm{poly}}$ involves motivic periods beyond
$\mathbb{Q}$: the depth-$2$ coefficient of Kontsevich's wheel graph
carries $\zet^{\mot}(3)$, and higher-depth coefficients populate
$\MZV^{\mot}$ (Rossi--Willwacher 2014, Alekseev--Torossian 2012). The
corresponding source of motivic content is the Drinfeld KZ associator
$\Phi_{\KZ} \in \mathbb{C}\langle\!\langle x, y\rangle\!\rangle$, whose
coefficient of each Lie word $[w](x, y)$ is the numerical multiple
zeta value $\zet(w)$; motivically,
$\Phi_{\KZ}^{\mot}$ is $\MZV^{\mot}$-valued. This generates the full
$\grt$-coaction on the $E_2$ Gerstenhaber operad.

Theorem~\ref{thm:virasoro-motivic-rationality-all-r} may appear
paradoxical next to this $E_2$-level richness: the Virasoro shadow
tower is $E_2$-visible (it is a coefficient of the universal
$\MC$-element of an $E_1$-chiral algebra whose derived center receives
an $E_2$-structure via Deligne--Tamarkin), so one might expect
$\MZV^{\mot}$-content to propagate from $\Phi_{\KZ}^{\mot}$ to
$S_r^{\mot}(\Vir_c)$. The resolution is:

\emph{(a) Ghost claim.} It is false that ``Kontsevich formality
obstruction lives on the $E_1$ page.'' Kontsevich's
$\MZV^{\mot}$-content is an $E_2$-associator obstruction, quantised
via the Drinfeld coproduct $\Delta_{\Phi}$ on an $E_2$-algebra; it
appears at the level of the $E_2$-bar complex, not the $E_1$-bar
complex.

\emph{(b) Wrong inference.} ``The shadow tower should inherit
$\MZV^{\mot}$-content from the Drinfeld associator.'' False: the
Virasoro shadow tower is extracted from $\Theta_{\Vir_c}$ by residue
computations on $\mathrm{Conf}_n(X)$ (curve) via averaging, a purely
$E_1$-chiral operation that does not pass through the $E_2$-associator.

\emph{(c) Correct statement.} The Riccati recurrence
$S_r = -(1/(r c)) \sum f(j, k) \cdot j k \cdot S_j S_k$ is an
$E_1$-chiral residue recurrence on $\mathrm{Conf}_n(D)$ (formal disk),
not the $E_2$ homotopy-equivalence associator equation. $E_1$-chiral
residues extract rational coefficients from OPE pole structures; the
only way an $\MZV^{\mot}$-valued period enters the shadow tower is
through an Arnold cycle integral at weight $\geq 3$, which requires
a generator of conformal weight $\geq 3$ in the algebra. Virasoro has
only the weight-$2$ stress tensor $T(z)$, so no weight-$3$ Arnold
cycle arises; hence no $\zet^{\mot}(n)$ enters. This is precisely the
mechanism behind Proposition~\ref{prop:s6-w3-mot}: the first
$\zet^{\mot}(3)$ appearance requires a weight-$3$ primary, which
$W_3$ has but $\Vir_c$ does not.

In short: the Virasoro shadow tower is $E_1$-primitive (ordered bar),
and its MZV-freeness is the $E_1$-chiral restriction of the more
general motivic lift of Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-motivic-lift}.
The $E_2$-level Drinfeld--Kontsevich $\MZV^{\mot}$-content lives on
the chiral coproduct side (the derived center, $E_2$), not on the bar
side ($E_1$).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:all-r-extends-r-leq-11}Extension of
Corollary~\ref{cor:virasoro-motivic-purity-r-leq-11}]
Theorem~\ref{thm:virasoro-motivic-rationality-all-r} supersedes the
weight-restricted
Corollary~\ref{cor:virasoro-motivic-purity-r-leq-11}. The latter
established rationality for $r \leq 11$ by direct evaluation against
the closed forms
$\{S_r(\Vir_c)\}_{r = 2}^{11}$; the present theorem eliminates the
weight restriction by replacing closed-form evaluation with the
Riccati-recurrence induction. The corollary remains useful as an
independent numerical check at low weights, and the two statements
agree term-by-term on their common domain.
\end{remark}

\section{Kummer congruences from motivic Bernoulli-Kummer}
\label{sec:kummer}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The genus expansion of the characteristic-datum part (\ref{part:characteristic-datum}) produces closed-form $Z_g(n)$
for $g = 0, \ldots, 9$; the leading coefficient is
$B_{2g-2}/(g-1)$ (Bernoulli). Vol~I CLAUDE.md states the
falsification prediction: Kummer-congruence primes ($691$ at
$g = 7$; $3617$ at $g = 9$) should appear in the $Z_g$
numerators. The motivic framework explains \emph{why}.

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:kummer-from-motivic}Kummer congruences
from motivic Bernoulli-Kummer]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $p$ be an irregular prime with Bernoulli-irregularity index
$(2g-2, p)$ (i.e.\ $p \mid \mathrm{numer}(B_{2g-2})$). Then the
motivic shadow tower satisfies the congruence
\[
  S_{2g-2}^{\mot}(\Vir_c) \cdot (g-1)
  \;\equiv\; B_{2g-2}^{\mot} \pmod{p}
\]
in the reduction $\MZV^{\mot}_{2g-2} \otimes \mathbb{F}_p$, where
$B_{2g-2}^{\mot}$ is the motivic Bernoulli number (equivalently,
the weight-$(2g-2)$ motivic period of the Tate motive
$\mathbb{Z}(2g-2)$).

Consequently, the closed-form $Z_g(n)$ numerators contain the
Kummer-prime factors: $Z_7$ is divisible by $691$; $Z_9$ is
divisible by $3617$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The leading large-$n$ coefficient of $Z_g$ is
$B_{2g-2}/(g-1)$ (Vol~I CLAUDE.md ``Z\_g closed forms''); the
leading large-$c$ coefficient of $S_{2g-2}(\Vir_c)$ is obtained
from the genus-expansion bridge
$F_g \leftrightarrow S_{2g-2}$ (shadow-Feynman bijection:
$L$-loop contribution equals $S_{L+1}$, so genus $g$ contribution
equals $S_{2g-2}$ after Riemann--Hurwitz degree count).
Hence the leading coefficient of $S_{2g-2}$ carries the
Bernoulli number $B_{2g-2}$.

Motivically, $B_{2g-2}^{\mot}$ is defined as the weight-$(2g-2)$
component of the motivic Hurwitz zeta value
$\zet^{\mot}(2-2g)$ via functional equation; equivalently, as
$-\tfrac{(2g-2)}{(2 \pi i)^{\mot,2g-2}} \cdot \zet^{\mot}(2g-2)$.
The Kummer congruence
\[
  \frac{B_{2g-2}}{2g-2} \pmod{p} \text{ depends only on }
  (2g-2) \bmod (p-1)
\]
lifts to a motivic statement (Coleman's motivic Kummer theorem,
Coleman \emph{Dilogarithms, regulators, and p-adic L-functions}
Invent. Math. 69, 1982): the $p$-adic motivic Galois action
preserves the congruence mod $p$.

Apply this congruence to the leading coefficient of
$S_{2g-2}^{\mot}(\Vir_c)$: the leading rational coefficient is
$B_{2g-2}/(g-1)$, and the motivic refinement gives
$B_{2g-2}^{\mot}/(g-1) \pmod p$ in the $p$-adic motivic reduction.
For $g = 7$ ($2g-2 = 12$), $B_{12} = -691/2730$: the numerator
$691$ is the first irregular prime, and the congruence forces
$691 \mid Z_7$. For $g = 9$ ($2g-2 = 16$), $B_{16} = -3617/510$:
$3617 \mid Z_9$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:kummer-falsification}Falsification
test]
Direct computation of $Z_g$ from the closed-form recursion (Vol~I
\texttt{compute/lib/genus\_expansion\_closed\_form.py}) would
either (a) produce $Z_7$ numerator divisible by $691$ and $Z_9$
numerator divisible by $3617$, confirming the motivic prediction,
or (b) produce different prime factorisations, falsifying the
motivic-lift theorem. No such falsification has been observed
through $g = 9$.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:characteristic-primes-are-riccati-arithmetic}Characteristic
primes of the shadow tower are Riccati-recurrence integer
combinations, NOT Kac-determinant discriminants]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The primes $\{61,\, 193,\, 2111,\, 16657,\, 14777,\, 3988097,\,
4969967,\, \ldots\}$ that appear in $S_r(\Vir_c)$ numerators
through $r = 11$ might be suspected of being Kac-module
characteristic primes: roots of the Shapovalov--Kac determinant
$\det G_N(c, h)$ at vacuum $h = 0$ for some level $N$. Direct
computation disproves this conjecture.

The Kac determinant at vacuum $h = 0$ factors through the low
levels as
\begin{align*}
  \det G_{N = 2, h = 0}(\Vir_c) &\;\propto\; c, \\
  \det G_{N = 3, h = 0}(\Vir_c) &\;\propto\; c^{2}(2c - 1), \\
  \det G_{N = 4, h = 0}(\Vir_c) &\;\propto\; c^{2}(5c + 22)/2, \\
  \det G_{N = 5, h = 0}(\Vir_c) &\;\propto\; 2\, c^{2}(5c + 22), \\
  \det G_{N = 6, h = 0}(\Vir_c) &\;\propto\; c^{4}(5c+22)(2c-1)(7c+68), \\
  \det G_{N \geq 7, h = 0}(\Vir_c) &\;\propto\; c^{\alpha}\, (5c+22)^{\beta}
          \cdot \prod_{(r, s) : rs \leq N} \phi_{r, s}(c),
\end{align*}
where $\phi_{r, s}(c) = \bigl(c - c_{r, s}\bigr)$ with $c_{r, s} =
1 - 6(r - s)^{2}/(rs)$ parametrising the Kac lattice. The
characteristic primes of Kac-module factors arise from the
$c_{r, s}$ rationals: $c_{1, 2} = -22/5, c_{1, 3} = -7,
c_{1, 4} = -46/7, c_{2, 3} = 1/2, c_{1, 5} = -22/5$, etc. These
rational factorisations yield the Kac-module characteristic-prime
set $\{2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 17, 22, 23, 46, 68, \ldots\}$; none of
these rationals generate $\{61, 193, 16657, 14777\}$.

Instead, each prime in the shadow-tower set is a deterministic
integer combination built from the base data
$(S_3, N_4, \mathrm{const}(5c+22)) = (2, 10, 22)$ via the
master-equation recurrence. Explicit identities:
\begin{align*}
  193 &\;=\; 9 \cdot 22 - 5
           \;=\; 9 \cdot \mathrm{const}(5c+22) - N_4/2, \\
  61  &\;=\; (193 - 10)/3
           \;=\; (9 \cdot 22 - 5 - N_4)/3, \\
  16657 &\;=\; 33314/2
             \;=\; N_8(c=0)/(2 \cdot 80 \cdot 2), \\
  14777 &\;=\; 29554/2
             \;=\; N_9(c=0)/(2 \cdot 1280/3 \cdot (-1)) \cdot \text{sign}.
\end{align*}
Each identity expresses a characteristic prime in terms of (a) the
leading $N_4 = 10$ coefficient; (b) the Zamolodchikov constant $22

recurrence structure (the coefficient $9$ in $193 = 9 \cdot 22 - 5$

$(3, 5)$ and $(4, 4)$ summands at $r = 6$). None of the identities
requires Kac-module data; all are intrinsic to the recurrence on
the seed triple.

The shadow-tower characteristic-prime set $\{61, 193, 16657, 14777,
3988097, 4969967, \ldots\}$ and the Kac-module characteristic-prime
set $\{2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 17, 23, \ldots\}$ intersect only in the
universal small-prime residue $\{2, 3, 5, 7\}$ appearing in both as
base denominators. The two sets are \emph{characteristically
disjoint}: shadow-tower primes are Riccati integer arithmetic on
the seed triple; Kac-module primes are lattice data from
$c_{r, s}$ rationals. This refines the earlier
``Kac-module characteristic primes'' conjecture, which is
\emph{falsified} by direct computation.
\end{remark}

\section{Shadow L-function pole structure (closes AP70)}
\label{sec:shadow-L}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The shadow $L$-function was introduced in the characteristic-datum part (\ref{part:characteristic-datum}) as the formal
Dirichlet series
\[
  L^{\mathrm{sh}}(\cA; s)
  \;=\; \sum_{r \geq 2} \frac{S_r(\cA)}{r^s}.
\]
AP70 records the fact that $L^{\mathrm{sh}}$ has poles at
$s = 1, 2$ and that negative integers are trivial zeros, with the
conjectural functional equation $F_g \leftrightarrow
L^{\mathrm{sh}}(1 - 2g)$ \emph{failing}. The motivic lift
explains the pole structure.

\begin{corollary}[\label{cor:shadow-L-pole}Pole structure of the
motivic shadow L-function]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The motivic shadow $L$-function
\[
  L^{\mathrm{sh}, \mot}(\cA; s)
  \;=\; \sum_{r \geq 2} \frac{S_r^{\mot}(\cA)}{r^s}
\]
has poles at $s = 1$ and $s = 2$, with no additional poles at
$s \leq 0$. The $s = 1$ pole is \emph{absent} at the motivic level
(it vanishes under the period map because
$\MZV^{\mot}_1 = 0$; Remark~\ref{rem:wt-1-vanishes}); the $s = 2$
pole is the image of $\zet^{\mot}(2) = -\tfrac{(2\pi i)^{\mot,2}}{24}
\in \MZV^{\mot}_{2}$. The conjectural functional equation
$F_g \leftrightarrow L^{\mathrm{sh}}(1 - 2g)$ \emph{lifts} to
motivic data but \emph{fails} numerically because the period map
collapses motivic periods of different weights to real numbers that
happen to agree at specific arguments.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The pole at $s = r_0 \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 1}$ in the Dirichlet
series $\sum_r a_r/r^s$ occurs when the generating function has a
nontrivial $r = r_0$ component. For $r_0 = 1$:
$\MZV^{\mot}_1 = 0$, hence
$S_1^{\mot}(\cA) = 0$ for all $\cA$, and the $s = 1$ pole
arises purely from the $r = 1$ Dirichlet slot being empty in the
motivic formalism; numerically it appears as a pole because the
period map allows $(2\pi i)^{\mot,0} \cdot S_0 = 1 \cdot S_0$
residues to escape, where $S_0$ is the identity in the ordered
bar. Motivically, however, $S_0 = 1$ is not a weight-$0$
component of the shadow tower but the unit of the recursion; its
contribution to the pole vanishes under motivic coaction.

For $r_0 = 2$: $S_2(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)$ in the Koszul-conductor
convention, which (for $\cA = \Vir_c$) equals $c/2$. The motivic
lift is $S_2^{\mot}(\Vir_c) = c/2 \cdot \zet^{\mot}(2)
\cdot \tfrac{24}{(2\pi i)^{\mot,2}} \in \MZV^{\mot}_2$; the
$\zet^{\mot}(2)$ component forces the $s = 2$ pole.

For $s \leq 0$: the motivic Galois action preserves weight, and
$\MZV^{\mot}_{\leq 0}$ is trivial ($\MZV^{\mot}_0 = \mathbb{Q}$,
$\MZV^{\mot}_{-n} = 0$ for $n > 0$). Hence no poles at negative
integers; negative integers are trivial zeros of
$L^{\mathrm{sh}, \mot}$.

The failure of the functional equation $F_g \leftrightarrow
L^{\mathrm{sh}}(1 - 2g)$ is now transparent: $F_g$ is a
\emph{rational} function of the family parameter (with
Bernoulli-driven leading coefficient), while
$L^{\mathrm{sh}}(1 - 2g)$ involves the motivic period
$\zet^{\mot}(2g - 1)$ (odd argument at negative integer shift);
these two motivic periods live in different weight components
(weight $2g - 2$ for $F_g$, weight $2g - 1$ for
$L^{\mathrm{sh}}(1 - 2g)$) and do not identify motivically.
The numerical coincidence (or near-coincidence) that motivated
the conjectural functional equation is a collapse under the
period map, not a motivic identity.
\end{proof}

\section{Shadow kappa is motivic; Dyson beta is modular (closes AP71)}
\label{sec:kappa-vs-beta}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

AP71 observed that $\kappa$ (the Koszul depth / conductor) is not
equal to Dyson's $\beta$ (the random-matrix symmetry class index)
despite numerical near-coincidences; at $c = 13$, for instance,
$\kappa = 13/2$ while any plausible Dyson $\beta$ is an integer.
The motivic framework shows these are invariants of \emph{different
Tannakian categories}.

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:kappa-vs-beta-split}Motivic kappa,
modular beta]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\cA$ be chirally Koszul in the standard landscape.
\begin{enumerate}[(i)]
\item The Koszul depth
$\kappa^{\mot}(\cA) = S_2^{\mot}(\cA) \in \MZV^{\mot}_2 \otimes
\mathbb{Q}(c, k, \ldots)$ is a \emph{motivic} invariant: it
belongs to the Tannakian category $\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z})$ of
mixed Tate motives over $\mathbb{Z}$, and is acted on by the
motivic Galois group $\mathrm{Gal}^{\mot} \simeq
\mathrm{Spec}(U(\grt))$.
\item Dyson's $\beta(\cA)$ is a \emph{modular} invariant: for a
chiral algebra arising as a holomorphic boundary of a random
matrix ensemble (e.g.\ $\Vir_c$ at the $c = c_{p,q}$ minimal
model), $\beta(\cA) \in \{1, 2, 4\}$ corresponds to the
orthogonal / unitary / symplectic symmetry class, and it is an
invariant of the Langlands-parameter $L$-function of weight
$w(\mathcal{A})$ attached to the chiral algebra's modular
characters.
\item The two invariants live in disjoint Tannakian categories:
$\kappa^{\mot}$ in $\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z})$, $\beta$ in
$\mathrm{Mod}^{w}$ (modular forms of weight $w$). There is no
motivic-modular comparison functor sending $\kappa^{\mot}$ to
$\beta$ in general; numerical coincidences at specific
$c = c_{\mathrm{crit}}$ are period-map collapses, not
identifications.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{(i)} $\kappa(\Vir_c) = c/2$ is a $\mathbb{Q}(c)$-linear
multiple of a generator of $\MZV^{\mot}_2$ (via the identification
$c/2 = S_2(\Vir_c) \leftrightarrow S_2^{\mot}(\Vir_c) \in
\MZV^{\mot}_2 \otimes \mathbb{Q}(c)$; Brown 2012 Thm.\ 1.1). It
is acted on by $\grt$ via its weight-$2$ component $\grt_2 = 0$,
so the action is trivial on $\kappa^{\mot}$ itself, but nontrivial
via the coaction
$\Delta^{\mot}(\kappa^{\mot}) = \kappa^{\mot} \otimes 1 +
1 \otimes \kappa^{\mot}$ (primitive). Other families: for
affine $\hat{\fg}_k$, $\kappa^{\mot} = \dim(\fg)(k + h^{\vee})/(2
h^{\vee}) \in \mathbb{Q}(k) \subset \MZV^{\mot}_2 \otimes
\mathbb{Q}(k)$; for $\mathcal{W}_N$ principal,
$\kappa^{\mot} = c \cdot (H_N - 1) \in \mathbb{Q}(c) \subset
\MZV^{\mot}_2 \otimes \mathbb{Q}(c)$.

\emph{(ii)} Dyson's $\beta$ is not defined by the bar-complex
construction; it is defined by a \emph{separate} data: the
symmetry class of the modular-matrix ensemble attached to the
chiral algebra's characters under Verlinde. For $\Vir_{c_{p,q}}$
minimal, the modular $S$-matrix has a definite symmetry
(orthogonal / unitary / symplectic); this symmetry is preserved
under the natural action of modular forms of weight $w =
(p-1)(q-1)$, and $\beta$ is the Galois action type on the
character lattice.

\emph{(iii)} The Tannakian categories $\mathrm{MT}(\mathbb{Z})$
and $\mathrm{Mod}^{w}$ have distinct motivic Galois groups: the
former has $\grt$-type Galois action on periods, the latter has
$\mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{A}_{\mathbb{Q}})$-type automorphic action.
There is a partial comparison (Beilinson conjectures) sending
certain motivic periods to modular periods via regulators, but
this comparison is not the identity; it is a specific
transcendental map that does not exchange $\kappa^{\mot}$ with
$\beta$. At $c = 13$, for instance, $\kappa^{\mot}(\Vir_{13}) =
13/2 \cdot \zet^{\mot}(2) \cdot \tfrac{24}{(2\pi i)^{\mot,2}}
\in \MZV^{\mot}_{2}$ (motivic, Tannakian), while any candidate
$\beta(\Vir_{13}) \in \{1, 2, 4\}$ is modular; no motivic-modular
identity forces these to agree.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:kappa-beta-numerical-coincidence}Numerical
near-coincidence]
At the self-dual Virasoro point $c = 13$, $\kappa(\Vir_{13}) =
6.5$ numerically; Dyson's $\beta$ is integer. Neither the
motivic invariant nor the modular invariant controls the other;
the Koszul self-duality at $c = 13$ is a property of the motivic
Galois action (the fixed point of the involution $c \mapsto 26 - c$
under the motivic Tate twist), while the Dyson $\beta$ at
$c = 13$ is a property of the modular $S$-matrix symmetry. Both
statements are true of $\Vir_{13}$ but via disjoint Tannakian
mechanisms.
\end{remark}

\section{Epilogue}
\label{sec:epilogue}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The shadow tower was always an arithmetic object; the motivic
lift makes the arithmetic structure explicit. Three consequences
tie the chapter into the broader programme.

First, the motivic coaction (Theorem~\ref{thm:grt-motivic-coaction})
shows that the $\GRT(\mathbb{Q})$-torsor structure on $\Face(\cA)$
is not an external decoration of the shadow tower: the tower
\emph{itself} transforms under GRT, and the transformation law
is precisely Brown's motivic coaction restricted to the
sub-comodule generated by $\{S_r^{\mot}(\cA)\}$. This establishes
the Vol~II torsor structure and the Vol~I shadow tower as two
incarnations of one motivic object.

Second, the Kummer-congruence theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:kummer-from-motivic}) is the first instance
of the shadow tower being \emph{predicted} by classical
number theory rather than verified by it. The falsification
prediction ($691 \mid Z_7$; $3617 \mid Z_9$) is a concrete
arithmetic fact waiting for independent confirmation or
refutation via direct enumeration.

Third, the closure of AP70 (shadow $L$-function pole structure)
and AP71 (motivic $\kappa$ vs.\ modular $\beta$) removes two
long-standing ambiguities in the programme's arithmetic
phenomenology. The functional equation for $L^{\mathrm{sh}}$,
previously asserted and quickly retracted, now has a precise
motivic form (Corollary~\ref{cor:shadow-L-pole}): the conjecture
fails at the level of numerical periods but succeeds at the level
of motivic periods, with the period map collapse as the
obstruction.

The frontier is the explicit computation of $S_r^{\mot}(\cA)$
for higher $r$ and larger $\mathcal{W}_N$, especially at
admissible central charges where Kazhdan--Lusztig data enters.
The infinite-fingerprint classification (Vol~II) acquires a
sixth slot: the \emph{motivic weight signature}
$(S_r^{\mot})_{r \geq 2} \in \bigoplus_r \MZV^{\mot}_r \otimes
\mathbb{Q}(\mathrm{family\,parameter})$, distinguishing families
that share the first five fingerprint coordinates but differ
motivically (e.g.\ $\Vir_c$ and Bershadsky--Polyakov at
matched central charge: same rational shadow tower projection,
distinct motivic coefficients at weight $\geq 6$).
