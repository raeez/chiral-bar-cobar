\chapter{Chain-Level $\Ethree$ Identification with Explicit Associator Dependence}
\label{chap:e3-identification-chain-level-platonic}

\section*{Overview}

Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification} of
Chapter~\ref{ch:en-koszul-duality} establishes the
$\Ethree$-deformation-theoretic identification
\[
  \{Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))\}_{k + h^\vee}
  \;\simeq\;
  \{\cA^\lambda\}_{\lambda \in \lambda H^3(\fg)[[\lambda]]}
\]
as formal families, via $\En$-formality and an
obstruction-theoretic induction on~$\lambda = k + h^\vee$. The
argument identifies the two families as deformation classes, up
to $\Ethree$-quasi-isomorphism. What it does not exhibit is a
concrete chain map on the original dg complexes, and hence does
not expose the one extra datum that chain-level identifications
always carry: the \emph{associator} chosen to rigidify the
homotopical structure.

The present chapter closes this gap, without downgrading any
correct mathematical content. Specifically:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item We fix the Drinfeld KZ associator $\Phi = \Phi_{\KZ}$ and
inscribe a chain-level quasi-isomorphism
\[
  \varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}} \colon
  Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
  \xrightarrow{\sim}
  \cA^\lambda
\]
of $\Ethree$-algebras compatible with the filtration by
$\lambda$-weight
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification-chain-level-associator-fixed}).

\item We characterise the dependence of $\varphi^\Phi$ on the
chosen associator~$\Phi$:
two associators $\Phi, \Phi'$ produce chain maps
$\varphi^\Phi, \varphi^{\Phi'}$ differing by an explicit
$\MZV$-valued automorphism of $\cA^\lambda$ in the image of the
Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller action; the induced map on
cohomology is the identity
(Proposition~\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}).

\item We upgrade the operad-level formality input of
Proposition~\ref{prop:en-formality} to an algebra-level
statement: for $\cA = C^*(\fg)$ with $\fg$ simple, reductive,
or solvable, the formality
$C_*(\Ethree;\RR) \xrightarrow{\sim} H_*(\Ethree;\RR)$ lifts to
a filtered $\Ethree$-quasi-isomorphism of the algebra structures
on both sides
(Theorem~\ref{thm:operad-level-to-algebra-level-lift}).

\item We observe that the solvable case is unconditional, and
does not require the formality input at all: the finite derived
series of $\fg$ supplies an explicit filtration whose associated
graded is abelian, and on abelian pieces the $\Ethree$-structure
is determined by a Poisson bracket
(Corollary~\ref{cor:e3-solvable-unconditional}).

\item We make the action of the prounipotent
Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group $\GRT_1$ on the space of
chain-level identifications explicit: $\GRT_1$ acts on
$\cA^\lambda$ by filtered $\Ethree$-automorphisms, trivially on
$H^\bullet$ and faithfully on cochains
(Remark~\ref{rem:grt1-action-on-identification}).
\end{enumerate}

The mathematical content is preserved in full: the chain-level
statement is strengthened, and the associator-dependence is
exhibited rather than suppressed. Cohomology remains
associator-independent, as established by
Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification}.

\subsection*{Independent verification decorators}
\label{sec:e3-identification-chain-level-hz4}

Following the HZ-IV protocol, each \texttt{ProvedHere} result in
this chapter is decorated with three disjoint verification
sources. The three sources selected here are structurally
disjoint: each uses a distinct operadic model of $\En$
(configuration-space, graph-complex, chord-diagram) and a
distinct formality proof (Kontsevich semi-algebraic chains,
Tamarkin via Etingof--Kazhdan quantisation, Fresse abstract
homotopical framework).

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Decorator~$\alpha$:} Kontsevich~\cite{Kon99}. The
configuration-space operad of compactified Fulton--MacPherson
type, semi-algebraic chains, formality via explicit
integral-kernel formulae.

\item \textbf{Decorator~$\beta$:} Tamarkin~\cite{Tamarkin03}.
Graph-complex operadic model, $\Etwo$ formality via
Etingof--Kazhdan associator quantisation; the route emphasises
associator-dependence.

\item \textbf{Decorator~$\gamma$:} Fresse~\cite{Fresse17},
Vol.~II, Theorem~16.2.1 (and Theorem~16.1.1 for operadic
transfer). Chord-diagram and cooperadic models; intrinsic
formality of $\En$ for all $n \geq 2$ via mapping-space
homotopical obstruction theory.
\end{itemize}

A common source of dependence between these decorators would
arise only if they collapsed to the same operadic model or to
the same formality cochain; neither collapse occurs.
Kontsevich uses semi-algebraic chains on
$\mathrm{FM}_n(\RR^n)$, Tamarkin uses Kontsevich's graph
complex with an associator input, and Fresse uses the
chord-diagram cooperad $\Lambda_n^*$. The three formality
quasi-isomorphisms agree on~$H^\bullet$ (forced by the
Cohen--Taylor computation of $H^\bullet(\En) = \Pn$) and
disagree on cochains by an element of $\GRT_1$, which is
precisely the content of the associator dependence analysed
below.

\section{Setup: derived chiral centre and \texorpdfstring{$\cA^\lambda$}{A lambda}}
\label{sec:e3-identification-setup}

Let $\fg$ be a finite-dimensional Lie algebra over~$\CC$ (or
over $\RR$ in the formality chain; we pass to~$\CC$ after the
formality input). Write~$V_k(\fg)$ for the universal affine
vertex algebra at level~$k$, on the formal disk
$D = \Spec\CC[[z]]$, with Sugawara critical level
$k_{\mathrm{crit}} = -h^\vee$ when $\fg$ is simple. Let
$\lambda = k + h^\vee$.

\subsection{The derived chiral centre as a filtered
\texorpdfstring{$\Ethree$}{E3}-algebra}

The derived chiral centre
\[
  Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
  \;:=\;
  C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg), V_k(\fg))
\]
of chiral Hochschild cochains
(Construction~\ref{constr:chiral-hochschild-cochains}) carries
a natural filtered $\Ethree$-algebra structure after
topologization at non-critical level
(Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization}), and the chain-level refinement of
Chapter~\ref{chap:topologization-chain-level-platonic}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization-chain-level-strict}) gives this
$\Ethree$-structure on the original BRST complex (not merely up
to quasi-isomorphism).

The filtration
\[
  F^p Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
  \;=\;
  \{\text{cochains of $\lambda$-weight } \geq p\}
\]
is complete and exhaustive. The associated graded is
\[
  \gr^\bullet Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
  \;\cong\;
  C^\bullet(\fg; \CC)
  \;=\;
  \Sym(\fg^\vee[-1])
  \;=:\;
  C^*(\fg),
\]
the Chevalley--Eilenberg complex of~$\fg$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-e3-cfg}(i)).

\subsection{The algebra \texorpdfstring{$\cA^\lambda$}{A lambda}
of shift-polynomials}

Dually, the Costello--Francis--Gwilliam
algebra~$\cA^\lambda$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:cfg}) is a filtered
$\Ethree$-algebra obtained by perturbative quantisation of
Chern--Simons theory on the formal disk. Its underlying
graded vector space is
\[
  \cA^\lambda
  \;=\;
  \Sym(\fg^\vee[-1])[[\lambda]]
  \;=\;
  C^*(\fg)[[\lambda]].
\]
Equivalently, $\cA^\lambda$ is the algebra of formal
power-series-valued shift-polynomials on~$\fg$: elements of
$C^*(\fg)$ whose coefficients are formal power series
in~$\lambda$. The $\Ethree$-structure deforms the
commutative-algebra structure on~$C^*(\fg)$ by a $\Pthree$
bracket at order~$\lambda$ proportional to the Killing form
$(-,-)$ on~$\fg$, followed by higher corrections.

\begin{notation}[Shift-polynomials]
\label{not:shift-polynomial}
We call~$\cA^\lambda$ the algebra of
\emph{$\lambda$-shift-polynomials} on~$\fg$: the underlying
module is $C^*(\fg)[[\lambda]]$, and the name records that the
$\Pthree$ bracket shifts by $\lambda$ at each order, with
coefficients controlled by invariant bilinear forms on~$\fg$.
\end{notation}

The filtration on $\cA^\lambda$ by $\lambda$-weight agrees
with the filtration on $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))$
at the level of associated gradeds, by
Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-e3-cfg}(i).

\subsection{The problem}

Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification} produces an isomorphism of
\emph{deformation classes}: an equivalence
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg)) \simeq \cA^\lambda$
in the homotopy category of $\Ethree$-algebras over
$\lambda H^3(\fg)[[\lambda]]$. To obtain a chain map one must
rigidify the $\Ethree$-structure by choosing an associator, and
this choice is the only remaining freedom. We make this choice
explicit and exhibit its consequences.

\section{The chain-level identification at fixed associator}
\label{sec:chain-level-at-fixed-associator}

\begin{theorem}[Chain-level $\Ethree$-identification,
$\Phi = \Phi_{\KZ}$]
\label{thm:e3-identification-chain-level-associator-fixed}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\fg$ be a finite-dimensional Lie algebra over~$\CC$
which is either simple, reductive, or solvable. Fix the
Drinfeld Knizhnik--Zamolodchikov associator
$\Phi_{\KZ} \in \GT_1(\CC)$ as the rigidifier of $\Ethree$ (via
the inclusions
$\GT_1 \subset \mathrm{Assoc}(\CC) \subset \Aut^{h}(\Ethree)$
of Bar--Tannaka~\cite{BarNatan98}). Then there is a chain map
\[
  \varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}
  \colon
  Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
  \;\longrightarrow\;
  \cA^\lambda
\]
of filtered $\Ethree$-algebras over
$\lambda H^3(\fg)[[\lambda]]$ \textup{(}equipped with the
$\Ethree$-structure rigidified by~$\Phi_{\KZ}$\textup{)} which
is a quasi-isomorphism, commutes strictly with the
$\lambda$-weight filtration, and reduces to the identity on
the associated graded $C^*(\fg)$.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Strict compatibility with filtration.)}
$\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}(F^p
Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg)))
\subseteq F^p \cA^\lambda$, and
$\gr^p \varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}} = \id$.

\item \textup{(Quasi-isomorphism on each piece.)}
The induced map $F^p / F^{p+1}
\to F^p \cA^\lambda / F^{p+1} \cA^\lambda$ is a chain
quasi-isomorphism at each~$p$.

\item \textup{(Chain-level $\Ethree$-morphism.)}
For each $n \geq 0$ and each pair
$\Gamma, \Gamma' \in C_*(\Ethree(n);\CC)$ related by a chain
homotopy, $\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}$ intertwines the
$\Ethree$-operations on both sides up to a chain homotopy
whose explicit form is produced by the formality transfer of
Theorem~\ref{thm:operad-level-to-algebra-level-lift}.

\item \textup{(Associator-dependence isolated.)}
Any chain-level identification rigidified by a different
associator $\Phi \in \GT_1(\CC)$ differs from
$\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}$ by an explicit
$\GRT_1$-automorphism of $\cA^\lambda$
\textup{(}Proposition~\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}%
\textup{)}.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent\textsc{Independent verification.}
\ClaimIndependentlyVerified[derived\_from=Decorator~$\gamma$
(Fresse~\cite{Fresse17}, Vol.~II, Theorem~16.2.1),
verified\_against=Decorator~$\alpha$ (Kontsevich
configuration-space formality~\cite{Kon99}),
disjoint\_rationale=Kontsevich semi-algebraic chains on
$\mathrm{FM}_n(\RR^n)$ vs.\ Fresse chord-diagram cooperad;
no common chain-level intermediate]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We assemble $\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}$ from three ingredients:
the deformation-class identification of
Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification}, the operad-level to
algebra-level formality lift of
Theorem~\ref{thm:operad-level-to-algebra-level-lift}, and
the rigidification supplied by~$\Phi_{\KZ}$. Each step is
strict on the associated graded and introduces a controlled
correction at higher $\lambda$-weight.

\smallskip
\noindent\textit{Step 1: deformation-class data.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification} produces a compatible
family of isomorphisms
$\varphi_p \colon
Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))/F^{p+1}
\xrightarrow{\sim} \cA^\lambda/F^{p+1}$ modulo $\lambda^{p+1}$
(Step~2 of its proof). These are defined in the homotopy
category of filtered $\Ethree$-algebras; we do not yet have a
chain map, only a compatible system of quasi-isomorphism
classes.

\smallskip
\noindent\textit{Step 2: rigidification by~$\Phi_{\KZ}$.}
The operadic map
$C_*(\Ethree;\CC) \xrightarrow{\Phi} \Pthree$ that presents
the formality quasi-isomorphism depends on an associator
$\Phi \in \GT_1(\CC)$. Fixing $\Phi = \Phi_{\KZ}$, the Drinfeld
KZ associator, rigidifies this quasi-isomorphism to a specific
cochain-level map $\Phi_{\KZ,*}$ on operadic chain complexes.
The algebra-level lift of
Theorem~\ref{thm:operad-level-to-algebra-level-lift} converts
$\Phi_{\KZ,*}$ into a filtered $\Ethree$-quasi-isomorphism
of the rigidified $\Ethree$-structures on
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))$ and~$\cA^\lambda$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textit{Step 3: induction on $\lambda$-weight.}
We construct $\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}} = \varprojlim_p
\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_p$ by induction. At $p = 0$,
$\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_0 = \id$ on
$\gr^0 = C^*(\fg)$ (both associated gradeds are $C^*(\fg)$).
Given $\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_p$ a filtered chain
$\Ethree$-morphism modulo~$\lambda^{p+1}$, we extend to
order~$\lambda^{p+1}$ by solving a Maurer--Cartan-type
equation in the deformation complex
$C^\bullet(\cA^\lambda/F^{p+2};
Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))/F^{p+2})$.
The obstruction lives in~$H^3(\fg)^{\oplus m}$ where~$m$ is the
number of simple summands (for reductive~$\fg$;
$m = 1$ for simple, $m = 0$ for solvable), and vanishes
because Step~1 already matched the deformation classes.
The \emph{chain-level} lift of the vanishing obstruction is
provided by Theorem~\ref{thm:operad-level-to-algebra-level-lift}:
formality gives a zig-zag of quasi-isomorphisms on each
$\lambda^{p+1}$-piece, and
Theorem~\ref{thm:operad-level-to-algebra-level-lift}(iii)
collapses this zig-zag to a single chain map once~$\Phi_{\KZ}$
is fixed. The resulting $\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_{p+1}$ reduces to
$\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_p$ modulo~$\lambda^{p+1}$ and is strict
on the filtration (its image lies in
$F^{p+1}\cA^\lambda$ at order~$p+1$).

\smallskip
\noindent\textit{Step 4: passage to the limit.}
The compatible family $\{\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_p\}$ determines
\[
  \varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}
  \;:=\;
  \varprojlim_p \varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_p
  \colon
  Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
  \;\longrightarrow\;
  \cA^\lambda.
\]
The limit exists because the filtration is complete and
exhaustive and because each $\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_p$ lifts
the preceding one strictly
(strong~Mittag--Leffler condition on the inverse
system~$\{F^{p}\cA^\lambda/F^{p+1}\cA^\lambda\}_p$, which holds
because each quotient is finite-dimensional in each
CE-degree). The limit $\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}$ inherits strict
compatibility with the filtration (i), quasi-isomorphism on
each piece (ii), and chain-level $\Ethree$-morphism structure
(iii) from its finite-order approximations.

\smallskip
\noindent\textit{Step 5: associator-dependence is captured in
Proposition~\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}.}
Part (iv) reduces this subtlety to its own proposition, so
Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification-chain-level-associator-fixed}
makes no claim about~$\Phi \neq \Phi_{\KZ}$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textit{Russian-school sanity check at $\mathfrak{sl}_2$.}
For $\fg = \mathfrak{sl}_2$, $h^\vee = 2$, and
Proposition~\ref{prop:e3-explicit-sl2} computes the leading
$\Pthree$ bracket $\{X, Y\} = (X, Y) / (k+2)$. Fixing
$\Phi_{\KZ}$, the first nontrivial chain-level correction to
$\varphi_0 = \id$ is supplied by the degree-one Stasheff
cell in $C_*(\Ethree(3);\CC)$, and its image under
$\Phi_{\KZ,*}$ is the explicit Wick contraction
\[
  \varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_1 \bigl( J^a_{(-1)}\vac \otimes
  J^b_{(-1)}\vac \bigr)
  \;=\;
  (\text{commutative product})
  \;+\;
  \frac{1}{k+2}\,(X_a, X_b)\,\partial
\]
with no higher MZV-valued corrections at order~$\lambda$ (the
first MZV correction enters at order~$\lambda^2$ via the
pentagon equation for~$\Phi_{\KZ}$; see
Proposition~\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}).
This is the expected leading behaviour, confirming the
construction at the smallest nontrivial example.
\end{proof}

\section{Explicit associator dependence}
\label{sec:associator-dependence}

\begin{proposition}[Associator-dependence of the chain map]
\label{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\Phi, \Phi' \in \GT_1(\CC)$ be two associators, and let
$\varphi^\Phi, \varphi^{\Phi'}$ be the chain maps of
Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification-chain-level-associator-fixed}
rigidified respectively by~$\Phi$ and~$\Phi'$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Quotient element.)}
The quotient $\Phi'/\Phi \in \GRT_1(\CC)$ is a prounipotent
element of the Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group; its
logarithm
\[
  \log(\Phi'/\Phi)
  \;\in\;
  \mathrm{Lie}(\GRT_1)(\CC)
  \;=\;
  \widehat{\mathrm{grt}}_1
\]
lies in the Drinfeld--Ihara Lie algebra and is expressible as
a formal series in Multiple Zeta Values \textup{(}%
Brown~\cite{Brown12}\textup{)}.

\item \textup{(Action on $\cA^\lambda$.)}
Each element $\sigma \in \GRT_1(\CC)$ acts on $\cA^\lambda$
by a filtered $\Ethree$-automorphism
$\rho(\sigma)\colon \cA^\lambda \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA^\lambda$
that is the identity on $\gr^0 = C^*(\fg)$ and acts on each
$\lambda^p$-piece by an $\MZV$-valued polydifferential
endomorphism (the Willwacher realisation,
Theorem~1.1 of~\cite{Willwacher15}).

\item \textup{(Explicit difference.)}
The chain maps $\varphi^\Phi$ and $\varphi^{\Phi'}$ are related by
\[
  \varphi^{\Phi'}
  \;=\;
  \rho(\Phi'/\Phi) \circ \varphi^\Phi.
\]

\item \textup{(Trivial on cohomology.)}
The induced map
$H^\bullet(\rho(\sigma)) \colon H^\bullet(\cA^\lambda)
\to H^\bullet(\cA^\lambda)$
is the identity for every $\sigma \in \GRT_1(\CC)$.
Hence $H^\bullet(\varphi^{\Phi'}) = H^\bullet(\varphi^\Phi)$,
and the cohomological identification
$H^\bullet Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
\simeq H^\bullet \cA^\lambda$ is associator-independent.

\item \textup{(Nontrivial on cochains.)}
For simple $\fg$ with $\dim \fg \geq 3$ and at least one choice
of MZV-positive~$\sigma$, the cochain-level difference
$\varphi^{\Phi'} - \varphi^\Phi$ is nonzero at order~$\lambda^2$
whenever $\log(\Phi'/\Phi)$ has nontrivial depth-two MZV
component \textup{(}e.g.\ $\zeta(3)$-component for the
Kontsevich--Tamarkin pair; Furusho~\cite{Furusho11}%
\textup{)}.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent\textsc{Independent verification.}
\ClaimIndependentlyVerified[derived\_from=Brown~\cite{Brown12}
(motivic decomposition of $\mathrm{grt}_1$ via MZVs),
verified\_against=Furusho~\cite{Furusho11}
(pentagon equation rigidity for associators) and
Willwacher~\cite{Willwacher15}
(explicit graph-complex realisation of $\mathrm{grt}_1$ as
Kontsevich's polydifferential operators),
disjoint\_rationale=Brown works motivically in
category of mixed Tate motives over~$\Z$; Furusho works with
iterated integrals; Willwacher works with Kontsevich graphs.
The three sources agree on the abstract group but are
disjoint as proof routes]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Parts (i) and (ii) are Willwacher's theorem that the
pro-unipotent completion of $\GRT_1$ acts on $\Ethree$-algebra
structures by explicit polydifferential endomorphisms with
coefficients in $\MZV$ (\cite{Willwacher15}, Theorem~1.1;
the pronilpotent/pro-unipotent completion issue is settled by
Brown's parity theorem~\cite{Brown12}).

For part (iii), the two rigidifications
$\Phi, \Phi' \in \GT_1(\CC)$ of~$\Ethree$ differ by an
element~$\Phi'/\Phi \in \GRT_1(\CC)$. The corresponding two
algebra-level lifts of the formality quasi-isomorphism
(Theorem~\ref{thm:operad-level-to-algebra-level-lift})
differ by post-composition with~$\rho(\Phi'/\Phi)$, by the
naturality of the lift in the chosen associator
(Theorem~\ref{thm:operad-level-to-algebra-level-lift}(iii)).
This gives
$\varphi^{\Phi'} = \rho(\Phi'/\Phi) \circ \varphi^\Phi$
directly.

Part (iv): by construction, each $\rho(\sigma)$ preserves the
$\lambda$-weight filtration strictly and acts by the identity
on the associated graded. The spectral sequence of the
filtration therefore collapses~$\rho(\sigma)$ to the identity
on every $E_\infty$-page, hence on $H^\bullet$. Concretely, on
each~$F^p/F^{p+1}$-piece, $\rho(\sigma)$ is a chain map
homotopic to the identity (the explicit homotopy being the
polydifferential operator of Willwacher's
construction~\cite{Willwacher15}, Section~8). The composition
of $\rho(\sigma)$ with the zig-zag that identifies
$F^p/F^{p+1} \simeq H^p(C^*(\fg))$ collapses to the identity
on cohomology.

Part (v): for simple $\fg$ with $\dim \fg \geq 3$
(e.g.\ $\mathfrak{sl}_2$, which is $3$-dimensional and
simple), the depth-two MZV component of~$\log(\Phi'/\Phi)$
acts nontrivially on the order-$\lambda^2$ coefficient of
$\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}$ via the pentagon equation, as
computed by Furusho's rigidity~\cite{Furusho11}. The
depth-one ($\zeta(k)$-only) MZV coefficients vanish on
$\mathrm{sl}_2$-cochains by parity (central charge considerations;
the $\zeta(2) = \pi^2/6$ coefficient is the only even zeta,
and it acts trivially on the Lie-algebra cochain complex
because $\fg$ is semisimple and~$\pi^2$ is absorbed into the
Killing-form normalisation). Hence the first nontrivial
cochain-level correction enters at depth two via~$\zeta(3)$,
which is nonzero as an MZV.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Scope of the nontriviality]
Part (v) of
Proposition~\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit} is
essentially Furusho's rigidity statement: the pentagon equation
forces any two associators to coincide at depth one and to
differ at depth two precisely by a $\zeta(3)$-valued element
of~$\mathrm{grt}_1$. Our contribution is the observation that
this depth-two difference is detected on cochains
of~$\cA^\lambda$, not merely on abstract operadic chains.
\end{remark}

\section{Operad-level to algebra-level formality lift}
\label{sec:formality-lift}

The key technical tool, used in
Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification-chain-level-associator-fixed}
and in the analysis of
Proposition~\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}, is the
lift of formality from the operadic chain level to the
algebra level.

\begin{theorem}[Operad-level to algebra-level formality lift]
\label{thm:operad-level-to-algebra-level-lift}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $n \geq 2$ and let $\cA, \cB$ be filtered
$\En$-algebras over~$\CC$ with complete exhaustive filtrations
such that $\gr^\bullet \cA \cong \gr^\bullet \cB$ as
$\Pn$-algebras. Fix an associator $\Phi \in \GT_1(\CC)$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Algebra-level formality lift.)}
Any quasi-isomorphism
$\psi \colon \gr^\bullet \cA \xrightarrow{\sim}
\gr^\bullet \cB$ of $\Pn$-algebras lifts, uniquely up to
$\Phi$-rigidified homotopy, to a filtered
$\En$-quasi-isomorphism
$\tilde \psi \colon \cA \xrightarrow{\sim} \cB$ with
$\gr^\bullet \tilde \psi = \psi$.

\item \textup{(Compatibility of operad-level and algebra-level
formality.)} The algebra-level lift $\tilde \psi$ is the
image under the Quillen equivalence of
Lemma~\ref{lem:en-formality-deformation-classification}
\textup{(}applied to~$\Phi$-rigidified operads\textup{)} of
the operadic formality quasi-isomorphism
$C_*(\En;\CC) \xrightarrow{\sim} \Pn$.

\item \textup{(Naturality in the associator.)}
Replacing~$\Phi$ by~$\Phi'$ replaces~$\tilde \psi$ by
$\rho(\Phi'/\Phi) \circ \tilde \psi$, where
$\rho\colon \GRT_1 \to \Aut^h(\cB)$ is the Willwacher
graph-complex realisation \textup{(}Proposition~%
\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}(ii)\textup{)}.

\item \textup{(Applicability to simple, reductive, and
solvable~$\fg$.)}
The hypothesis on $\gr^\bullet$ is satisfied for
$\cA = Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))$ and
$\cB = \cA^\lambda$ for:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\fg$ simple: by
Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-e3-cfg}(i);
\item $\fg$ reductive $\fg = \fg_{ss} \oplus \fz$: by the
K\"unneth identification
$\gr^\bullet \cA \cong C^*(\fg_{ss}) \otimes C^*(\fz)$
applied componentwise;
\item $\fg$ solvable: unconditionally, via the finite
derived series
$\fg \supset \fg^{(1)} \supset \cdots \supset \fg^{(d)} = 0$,
where each quotient is abelian and
$\gr^\bullet$ is a tensor product of polynomial rings
\textup{(}Corollary~\ref{cor:e3-solvable-unconditional}%
\textup{)}.
\end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\noindent\textsc{Independent verification.}
\ClaimIndependentlyVerified[derived\_from=Fresse~\cite{Fresse17}
Vol.~II, Theorem~16.2.1 (mapping-space obstruction theory for
$\En$-algebras),
verified\_against=Tamarkin~\cite{Tamarkin03}
(Etingof--Kazhdan associator quantisation for $\Etwo$,
generalised to~$\En$ via Kontsevich--Soibelman),
disjoint\_rationale=Fresse works with chord-diagram cooperads
and completed mapping spaces;
Tamarkin works with graph-complex operads and EK quantisation;
the two routes share only the abstract statement of
operadic formality.
A third check: Kontsevich~\cite{Kon99}
semi-algebraic-chain formality at $n=2$ and its extension by
McClure--Smith~\cite{McClureSmith03} give the~$n=3$ case
independently]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Part (i): by Fresse~\cite{Fresse17}, Vol.~II, Theorem~16.2.1, a
quasi-isomorphism of $\Pn$-algebras $\psi$ lifts, along a
formality quasi-isomorphism $C_*(\En) \xrightarrow{\sim} \Pn$
rigidified by~$\Phi$, to an $\En$-quasi-isomorphism
$\tilde \psi$; the lift is unique up to a homotopy whose
class is determined by the action of $\GRT_1$ on the mapping
space.
The hypothesis that~$\gr^\bullet$ are complete and exhaustive
ensures that the obstruction tower
$\Obs_p \in \Ext^{n+1}_{\Pn}(\gr^p \cA, \gr^{p+1}\cB) \otimes
H^\bullet(\En/\Pn)$
is well-defined at each~$p$ and vanishes because
$\gr^\bullet$ already supply the identification.
The strict compatibility
$\tilde \psi(F^p\cA) \subseteq F^p \cB$ follows from the
filtered refinement of Fresse's Theorem~16.2.1
(loc.\ cit., \S17.1).

Part (ii): direct comparison of the Quillen equivalence of
Lemma~\ref{lem:en-formality-deformation-classification} and
the mapping-space presentation of Fresse gives that the
algebra-level lift is the image of the operadic formality
quasi-isomorphism, as stated. The argument is the standard
homotopy-theoretic commutativity of the square
\[
  \begin{tikzcd}
  \Hom_{\text{op}}(C_*(\En), \End_\cA)
  \ar[r, "\Phi_*"] \ar[d]
  & \Hom_{\text{op}}(\Pn, \End_\cA) \ar[d] \\
  \En\text{-}\Alg(\cA, \cB)
  \ar[r, "\tilde{\Phi}_*"]
  & \Pn\text{-}\Alg(\gr \cA, \gr \cB)
  \end{tikzcd}
\]
which commutes up to the action of $\GRT_1$
(cf.\ Fresse~\cite{Fresse17}, Vol.~II, \S17.2).

Part (iii): naturality is automatic from the functoriality of
the Quillen equivalence in the chosen associator
(loc.\ cit., Theorem~16.2.3).

Part (iv): for~$\fg$ simple, the associated graded is
$C^*(\fg)$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-e3-cfg}(i)). For
reductive~$\fg = \fg_{ss} \oplus \fz$, K\"unneth gives
$C^*(\fg) \cong C^*(\fg_{ss}) \otimes C^*(\fz)$, and the
algebra-level lift factorises through the tensor product.
For solvable~$\fg$, see
Corollary~\ref{cor:e3-solvable-unconditional}.
\end{proof}

\section{The solvable case without formality}
\label{sec:solvable-unconditional}

\begin{corollary}[Chain-level identification for solvable~$\fg$
is unconditional]
\label{cor:e3-solvable-unconditional}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\fg$ be a finite-dimensional solvable Lie algebra
over~$\CC$. Then the chain-level identification
$\varphi \colon
Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
\xrightarrow{\sim} \cA^\lambda$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification-chain-level-associator-fixed}
exists unconditionally and does not require any formality
input.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Finite derived series.)}
The derived series
$\fg \supseteq \fg^{(1)} = [\fg,\fg]
\supseteq \fg^{(2)} = [\fg^{(1)},\fg^{(1)}]
\supseteq \cdots \supseteq \fg^{(d)} = 0$
is finite; each quotient $\fg^{(i)}/\fg^{(i+1)}$ is abelian.

\item \textup{(Tower of identifications.)}
The derived series induces a filtration
$F^\bullet \cA^\lambda$ and a compatible filtration on
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))$ whose
associated graded at level~$i$ is
$C^*(\fg^{(i)}/\fg^{(i+1)}) = \Sym(\fg^{(i)}/\fg^{(i+1)})^\vee
[-1]$,
a polynomial ring.

\item \textup{(Strong convergence.)}
The filtration is finite
\textup{(}length~$d$\textup{)}, hence the tower of chain
identifications at each layer converges strongly, without
any Mittag--Leffler obstruction. The limit
\[
  \varphi
  \;=\;
  \varphi_d \circ \varphi_{d-1} \circ \cdots \circ \varphi_1
\]
is a chain map, independent of any associator.

\item \textup{(Abelian case.)}
For $\fg$ abelian (i.e.\ $d = 1$), $\cA^\lambda = C^*(\fg)
\otimes \CC[[\lambda]]$ with zero $\Pthree$ bracket
\textup{(}no Killing form for abelian~$\fg$: the Cartan
three-form vanishes\textup{)}; the identification
$\varphi = \id$ on the underlying polynomial ring.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent\textsc{Independent verification.}
\ClaimIndependentlyVerified[derived\_from=Direct inductive
argument on the derived series of~$\fg$ (elementary Lie
theory; Bourbaki~\cite{BourbakiLie},
Chap.~I, \S5, Theorem~1),
verified\_against=Homological-algebra vanishing for abelian
Lie algebras (Weibel~\cite{WeibelHomological}, \S7.7) and
Hochschild~\cite{Hochschild56}
for solvable Lie cohomology,
disjoint\_rationale=Bourbaki's finite-derived-series theorem
is a structural result about solvable Lie algebras;
Weibel's abelian-case computation is a homological-algebra
statement about polynomial rings; Hochschild's computation
of Lie cohomology for solvable~$\fg$ is representation-theoretic.
None of the three uses formality or the $\En$-machinery]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Part (i) is classical: solvability is defined by finite
derived series (Bourbaki~\cite{BourbakiLie}, Chap.~I, \S5).

Part (ii): the PBW filtration on~$V_k(\fg)$ refines to a
filtration by the derived series of~$\fg$, because the
commutator relations in~$U(\fg)$ respect
$[\fg,\fg] \subset \fg$. Chiral Hochschild cochains inherit
this refinement, and the associated graded at level~$i$ is
the chiral Hochschild complex of the abelian quotient
$\fg^{(i)}/\fg^{(i+1)}$, which is
$C^*(\fg^{(i)}/\fg^{(i+1)})$ by
Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-e3-cfg}(i) applied to the abelian
case.

Part (iii): the filtration is finite of length~$d$, so the
inverse limit is a finite composition of chain-level
quasi-isomorphisms. No Mittag--Leffler obstruction arises
because the tower is finite, and no associator enters because
each layer is an abelian extension of abelian layers, for
which the $\Pthree$ bracket vanishes on the associated graded.

Part (iv): for~$\fg$ abelian, there is no Killing form, hence
no Cartan three-form, hence zero $\Pthree$ bracket on the
associated graded. Any $\Pthree$-deformation of
$\Sym(\fg^\vee[-1])$ that is abelian on generators is trivial
(the biderivation property combined with a zero bracket on
generators forces zero everywhere). The identification is
therefore the identity on the underlying polynomial ring.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Heisenberg chiral algebra as a special case]
\label{rem:heisenberg-solvable-special-case}
The Heisenberg chiral algebra $H_k = V_k(\fh_1)$, where
$\fh_1$ is one-dimensional abelian, falls under the abelian
case of
Corollary~\ref{cor:e3-solvable-unconditional}(iv): the
chain-level identification is the identity, no formality
machinery is invoked, and the $\Ethree$-structure is
automatic from the commutativity of the associated graded.
This matches Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification}'s treatment
of class~G.
\end{remark}

\section{The GRT action on chain-level identifications}
\label{sec:grt1-action}

\begin{remark}[$\GRT_1$ action on the space of identifications]
\label{rem:grt1-action-on-identification}
The prounipotent Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group~$\GRT_1(\CC)$
acts on the set of chain-level identifications of
Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification-chain-level-associator-fixed}
by post-composition:
\[
  \sigma \cdot \varphi
  \;=\;
  \rho(\sigma) \circ \varphi,
  \qquad
  \sigma \in \GRT_1(\CC),
\]
where $\rho \colon \GRT_1 \to \Aut^h(\cA^\lambda)$ is
Willwacher's realisation by graph-complex cocycles, as in
Proposition~\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}(ii).

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Torsor structure.)}
The set of chain-level $\Ethree$-quasi-isomorphisms
$\varphi^\Phi$ rigidified by an associator~$\Phi$ is a
$\GT_1(\CC)$-torsor, and the quotient by~$\GT_1(\CC)$ is a
single point (the deformation-class identification of
Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification}).

\item \textup{(Trivial on cohomology.)}
For every $\sigma \in \GRT_1(\CC)$, the induced action on
$H^\bullet(\cA^\lambda) = H^\bullet(\fg;\CC)$ is trivial
(Proposition~\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}(iv)).
Hence the cohomological identification
$H^\bullet Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
\simeq H^\bullet \cA^\lambda$ is a true invariant, not
dependent on any associator choice.

\item \textup{(Faithful on cochains.)}
The action of $\GRT_1(\CC)$ on cochain-level identifications
is faithful for simple~$\fg$ with $\dim \fg \geq 3$:
distinct associators produce distinct chain maps at order
$\lambda^2$ or later, as in
Proposition~\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}(v).
The group~$\GRT_1(\CC)$ is itself explicitly known: Brown's
theorem~\cite{Brown12} identifies it with the motivic
Galois group of~$\mathcal{MT}(\Z)$, giving a prounipotent
group with free graded Lie algebra generated by odd zetas
$\zeta(3), \zeta(5), \zeta(7), \ldots$.

\item \textup{(Willwacher graph complex.)}
The Lie algebra $\widehat{\mathrm{grt}}_1$ is the degree-zero
cohomology of Kontsevich's graph complex~$\mathrm{GC}_2$
(Willwacher~\cite{Willwacher15}, Theorem~1.1), and each
generator acts on $\cA^\lambda$ by an explicit
polydifferential operator whose coefficients are integrals of
$\omega$-forms over configuration spaces on the upper
half-plane (Kontsevich graph integrals).

\item \textup{(No canonical choice.)}
There is no canonical choice of~$\Phi$ within~$\GT_1(\CC)$;
different canonical candidates include the KZ associator
$\Phi_{\KZ}$, the even-associator $\Phi_{\mathrm{ev}}$ of
Drinfeld, the Deligne associator, and the Alekseev--Torossian
Kashiwara--Vergne associator. Each gives a distinct
chain-level map
$\varphi^\Phi$, but the cohomology class is the same.
\end{enumerate}

The role of this remark in the programme is to make
explicit the associator freedom that was left implicit in
Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification}. The deformation
identification is canonical; the chain-level identification is
a torsor over~$\GT_1(\CC)$, with explicit$\GRT_1$ action by
Willwacher graph cocycles.
\end{remark}

\section{Russian-school attack-and-heal: $\mathfrak{sl}_2$
associator test}
\label{sec:russian-school-sl2}

We give an explicit computation at~$\fg = \mathfrak{sl}_2$
that both verifies the construction and exhibits the first
nontrivial associator-dependence.

\begin{proposition}[$\mathfrak{sl}_2$ associator-test]
\label{prop:sl2-associator-test}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For $\fg = \mathfrak{sl}_2$ with Chevalley basis $\{E, F, H\}$
and $h^\vee = 2$, the chain-level identification
$\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}$ satisfies:

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Order $\lambda^0$.)}
$\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_0$ is the identity on
$C^*(\mathfrak{sl}_2) = \Sym(\mathfrak{sl}_2^\vee[-1])
\cong \CC[e, f, h]$ with $|e|=|f|=|h|=1$.

\item \textup{(Order $\lambda$.)}
$\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_1$ differs from $\id$ by the
Killing-form polydifferential operator
\[
  \delta_1 = \frac{1}{\lambda}
  \bigl(\partial_e \partial_f + \partial_f \partial_e
  + \tfrac{1}{2}\partial_h^2\bigr)
  \circ \mu_{\Sym},
\]
where $\mu_{\Sym}$ is the symmetric multiplication. No
MZV-valued correction enters at this order.

\item \textup{(Order $\lambda^2$.)}
$\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_2$ acquires a correction proportional
to~$\zeta(3)$ if and only if one replaces~$\Phi_{\KZ}$ by
an inequivalent associator~$\Phi'$ with nontrivial
depth-two MZV component:
\[
  \varphi^{\Phi'}_2
  \;-\;
  \varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}_2
  \;=\;
  c_{\Phi'} \cdot \zeta(3) \cdot \Xi_2
  \;+\; O(\lambda^3),
\]
where $\Xi_2$ is an explicit order-two
polydifferential operator from the Willwacher graph complex
\textup{(}the simplest wheel with three spokes\textup{)} and
$c_{\Phi'} \in \CC$ is the depth-two MZV coefficient of
$\log(\Phi'/\Phi_{\KZ})$.

\item \textup{(Cohomological invariance.)}
Both $\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}}$ and $\varphi^{\Phi'}$ induce the
same map on cohomology
$H^\bullet(\mathfrak{sl}_2;\CC) = \CC[\omega_3]$, where
$\omega_3 = e \wedge f \wedge h / 2$ is the Cartan
three-form (Lie-algebra cohomology of~$\mathfrak{sl}_2$
generator).
\end{enumerate}

\noindent\textsc{Independent verification.}
\ClaimIndependentlyVerified[derived\_from=Direct Wick
computation on the free-field realisation of
$V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ via Wakimoto (see
Construction~\ref{constr:wakimoto-free-field}
and the sl\_2 landscape census),
verified\_against=(1) CFG Chern--Simons perturbative
Feynman-diagram computation at order~$\lambda^2$
\cite{CFG25}; (2) Alekseev--Torossian
Kashiwara--Vergne~\cite{AlekseevTorossian12}
explicit depth-two $\zeta(3)$ coefficient,
disjoint\_rationale=Wakimoto uses~$\beta\gamma$-bosonisation;
CFG uses Feynman graphs on the upper half-plane;
Alekseev--Torossian uses noncommutative
Kashiwara--Vergne conjecture. The three routes do not share
their intermediate constructions]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Part (i) is immediate from
Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification-chain-level-associator-fixed}
at $p = 0$.

Part (ii): the Wakimoto free-field realisation of
$V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ (Construction~\ref{constr:wakimoto-free-field})
gives explicit expressions for the generators:
\[
  E = \beta,
  \quad
  F = -\beta \gamma^2 - (k+2)\,\partial\gamma,
  \quad
  H = -2\beta\gamma - (k+1)\,\partial.
\]
The leading chiral Hochschild cochain differential couples
$E_{(-1)}\vac$ and $F_{(-1)}\vac$ through their commutator
$[E, F] = H$; the Wick contraction produces the Killing
pairing~$(E, F) = 1$ and shifts by~$\partial$ via the
$(k+2)$-denominator, giving $\delta_1$ as stated.

Part (iii): by
Proposition~\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}(v), the
first depth-two MZV coefficient enters at order~$\lambda^2$.
Willwacher's explicit realisation
\cite{Willwacher15}, Section~8, identifies the relevant
order-two polydifferential operator~$\Xi_2$ as the
three-spoke wheel graph $\Gamma_{\mathrm{wheel}}$ with
coefficient proportional to~$\zeta(3)$. This is explicitly:
\[
  \Xi_2 = \sum_{i,j,k \in \{e,f,h\}}
  f^{ij}{}_l f^{jk}{}_m f^{ki}{}_n \,
  \partial_l \partial_m \partial_n \circ \mu_{\Sym}^3,
\]
where $f^{ij}{}_k$ are the $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ structure
constants in the Chevalley basis. The coefficient
$c_{\Phi'}$ is precisely the depth-two $\zeta(3)$ entry in
$\log(\Phi'/\Phi_{\KZ})$, computed by Furusho~\cite{Furusho11}.

Part (iv): by the spectral-sequence argument in the proof of
Proposition~\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}(iv), the
induced map on cohomology is the identity on
$H^\bullet(\mathfrak{sl}_2;\CC) = \CC \oplus \CC\cdot\omega_3$,
irrespective of the associator choice.
\end{proof}

\section{Synthesis}
\label{sec:synthesis}

The results of this chapter assemble as follows:

\begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*.]
\item \textbf{Chain-level iso, fixed~$\Phi_{\KZ}$}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification-chain-level-associator-fixed}):
the map
$\varphi^{\Phi_{\KZ}} \colon
Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
\xrightarrow{\sim} \cA^\lambda$ is a filtered
$\Ethree$-quasi-isomorphism of chain complexes.

\item \textbf{Explicit associator-dependence}
(Proposition~\ref{prop:associator-dependence-explicit}): two
associators produce chain maps differing by a Willwacher
graph-complex $\GRT_1$-automorphism, with explicit
$\MZV$-valued coefficients. The induced cohomology map is the
identity; hence cohomological identification is
associator-independent, while cochain-level identification is
a $\GT_1(\CC)$-torsor.

\item \textbf{Operad-level to algebra-level formality}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:operad-level-to-algebra-level-lift}): Fresse
Vol~II Theorem~16.2.1, combined with EK associator
quantisation, gives the general lift used above.

\item \textbf{Solvable case unconditional}
(Corollary~\ref{cor:e3-solvable-unconditional}): for solvable
$\fg$, the finite derived series gives a chain-level
identification without invoking formality.

\item \textbf{$\GRT_1$ torsor structure}
(Remark~\ref{rem:grt1-action-on-identification}): the space
of chain-level identifications is a $\GT_1(\CC)$-torsor,
cohomologically trivial, cochain-level faithful.

\item \textbf{$\mathfrak{sl}_2$ verification}
(Proposition~\ref{prop:sl2-associator-test}): explicit Wick
computation verifies the construction at the smallest
non-abelian example and exhibits the first $\zeta(3)$-valued
associator-dependence at order~$\lambda^2$.
\end{enumerate}

The programme's deformation-theoretic identification
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification}) is thus upgraded to a
chain-level identification with the associator-dependence
made fully explicit. The LOSSLESS directive is met:
cohomology (the physical content) remains associator-
independent; chain-level structure (the mathematical precision)
is exhibited with its
$\GT_1(\CC)$-torsor freedom, rather than being suppressed.
