\chapter{\texorpdfstring{$\En$ Koszul duality and higher-dimensional bar complexes}{En Koszul duality and higher-dimensional bar complexes}}
\label{ch:en-koszul-duality}

\index{En algebra@$\En$ algebra!Koszul duality|textbf}
\index{bar complex!higher-dimensional|textbf}

For $\En$ algebras, the bar-cobar adjunction replaces the logarithmic
propagator $d\log(z-w)$ with a closed $(n{-}1)$-form, the Arnold
relations with the Totaro relations, and chiral Koszul duality with
$\En$ Koszul duality. The underlying construction is the same:
duality is mediated by a convolution algebra on a compactified
configuration space. The passage from $n = 1$
(associative/$A_\infty$) through $n = 2$ (the holomorphic refinement
recovering the chiral bar complex) to $n = 3$ (Chern--Simons,
Vassiliev invariants) and beyond exhibits bar-cobar duality as a
single construction parametrised by manifold dimension, with the
formality phenomena of Kontsevich and Tamarkin as shadows of the
Koszul property in each dimension.

The chapter culminates in the chiral center theorem
(\S\ref{sec:operadic-center-theorem}), which identifies the chiral
Hochschild cochain complex as the operadic center of an
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra, and in the topologization
theorem (\S\ref{sec:topologization}), which upgrades the resulting
$\Etwo$ center on BRST cohomology to~$\Ethree$ via the Sugawara
conformal vector.
The topologization proof is made constructive by an explicit
antighost contraction $G(z)$ representing
$T_{\mathrm{Sug}} = [Q, G]$ in BRST cohomology
in the $3$d holomorphic Chern--Simons BV complex
(Construction~\ref{constr:sugawara-antighost}). For class~M
algebras (Virasoro, $\cW_N$), where the chain-level $\Ethree$
encounters obstructions from the infinite shadow tower, a coderived
refinement provides an alternative path
(Conjecture~\ref{conj:coderived-e3}).

Throughout this monograph, the bar differential has been built from
configuration spaces of points on a \emph{curve}: the propagator
$\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$ is a meromorphic $1$-form, its
singularities are controlled by Arnold relations, and the bar-cobar
adjunction lives over the chiral operad. This chapter asks a
different question: what remains true when one leaves the
Beilinson--Drinfeld curve setting and passes to genuine little-disks
factorization on real $n$-manifolds? Then the propagator becomes a
closed $(n-1)$-form, collision strata are governed by
\emph{Totaro relations}, and the bar-cobar adjunction governs
$\En$-algebras.

Two axes must be kept separate. Inside chiral geometry on a fixed
curve one has the hierarchy
$\Einf\text{-chiral} \subset \Pinf\text{-chiral} \subset \Eone\text{-chiral}$,
so the passage from Heisenberg to Yangian changes commutativity and
locality, not manifold dimension. Here $\Einf$-chiral means local
factorization with full symmetric-group invariance: Heisenberg,
affine Kac--Moody, Virasoro, and the other standard local chiral
algebras remain $\Einf$-chiral even though their OPEs have poles.
By contrast, $\Eone$-chiral records ordered, nonlocal data such as
Yangians and Etingof--Kazhdan quantum vertex algebras. The
topological ladder is instead
indexed by real manifold dimension: $n = 1$ is the classical
associative/$A_\infty$ bar-cobar theory on intervals or circles,
$n = 2$ is the oriented-surface setting whose holomorphic refinement
recovers the chiral bar complex on a complex curve, and $n = 3$
interfaces with Chern--Simons theory and Vassiliev invariants
(Chapter~\ref{ch:kontsevich-integral}). At $n = 2$ the bar complex
governs $\Etwo$-algebras (the natural algebraic structure on braided
monoidal categories) and connects to Kontsevich's formality theorem
for the little $2$-disks operad.

\begin{theorem}[Bridge theorem: $\En$ bar-cobar and the chiral $\Eone$ engine; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:en-chiral-bridge}
\index{En algebra@$\En$ algebra!bridge to chiral bar-cobar|textbf}
The chiral bar-cobar adjunction of this monograph
\textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}\textup{)}
is the holomorphic refinement of the $n = 2$ chapter of $\En$
Koszul duality. Precisely:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The $\En$ bar complex on an oriented real $n$-manifold~$M$ is
 built from configuration spaces $\Conf_k(M)$ with propagator a
 closed $(n{-}1)$-form and collision relations governed by the Totaro
 presentation
 \textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:totaro-presentation}\textup{)}.
 At $n = 2$, this specializes to the Arnold presentation on
 $\Conf_k(\bC)$ and the propagator becomes the logarithmic $1$-form
 $\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$, recovering the chiral bar complex
 on a complex curve.
\item The $\Eone$ bar-cobar adjunction of this monograph
 \textup{(}operating on ordered, nonlocal chiral algebras with
 possibly non-commutative OPE, including Yangians and
 Etingof--Kazhdan quantum vertex algebras\textup{)} is
 \emph{not} the topological $\En$ bar-cobar at $n = 1$
 \textup{(}which governs ordinary associative algebras on
 intervals\textup{)}, but rather the chiral specialization at
 $n = 2$ with the commutativity constraint dropped. The topological
 ladder and the chirality hierarchy are orthogonal axes.
\item The formality hierarchy organizes how much of the bar-cobar
 adjunction reduces to cohomological data:
 \begin{center}
 \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
 \begin{tabular}{ccl}
 $n$ & Formality & Source \\
 \hline
 $\infty$ & $\Einf$ formal & local commutative shadow \\
 $2$ & $\Etwo$ formal & Kontsevich~\cite{Kon99}, Tamarkin \\
 $1$ & $\Eone$ \emph{non-formal} in general
 & obstructed by $A_\infty$ Massey products
 \end{tabular}
 \end{center}
 The chiral bar-cobar adjunction on curves inherits $\Etwo$ formality
 from Kontsevich's theorem when the underlying chiral algebra is
 $\Einf$-chiral \textup{(}symmetric OPE\textup{)}. For
 $\Eone$-chiral algebras, the transferred $A_\infty$ structure is
 genuinely non-formal: higher operations $m_k$ for $k \ge 3$ carry
 irreducible homotopical content.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Part~(i) is the content of Remark~\ref{rem:arnold-totaro}:
the Arnold generators on $\Conf_k(\bC)$ are the Totaro generators
at $n = 2$, and the chiral bar differential is the specialization of
the $\Etwo$ bar differential to a complex curve.

Part~(ii): the chiral hierarchy
$\Einf\text{-chiral} \subset \Pinf\text{-chiral} \subset
\Eone\text{-chiral}$ on a fixed curve changes the commutativity
constraint on the OPE, not the manifold dimension. An
$\Eone$-chiral algebra on a complex curve is an $\Etwo$-algebra
(real dimension~$2$) with the full symmetric-group action on
$\Conf_k(\bC)$ replaced by the braid-group action; it is not an
$\Eone$-algebra in the topological sense (which would be an
$A_\infty$-algebra on an interval).

Part~(iii): $\Etwo$ formality is Kontsevich's theorem~\cite{Kon99}
for the little $2$-disks operad; $\Einf$ formality is tautological;
$\Eone$ non-formality is witnessed by non-trivial Massey products
in the bar cohomology of non-formal $A_\infty$-algebras (e.g., the
commutative algebra $\bC[x]/x^3$), which survive as higher
operations $m_k$ on the transferred structure. The chiral
inheritance follows from the specialization map
$\En\text{-bar} \to \text{chiral bar}$ at $n = 2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Formality hierarchy as MC obstruction truncation]
\label{prop:en-formality-mc-truncation}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The formality hierarchy $\Einf \supset \Etwo \supset \Eone$ corresponds to truncation of the MC obstruction tower:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $\Einf$-formality: the full modular convolution algebra $\gAmod$ is formal; all higher brackets $\ell_n^{(g)} = 0$ for $n \geq 3$.
\item $\Etwo$-formality: the genus-$0$ convolution algebra $\mathfrak{g}^{(0)}_\cA$ is formal; the shadow obstruction tower terminates at degree~$2$ (chiral Koszulness, Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta}).
\item $\Eone$-formality: the ordered convolution algebra $\mathfrak{g}^{\Eone}_\cA$ carries a formal $A_\infty$-model; the associative shadow obstruction tower terminates.
 The ordered bar complex $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ on ordered configuration spaces $\mathrm{FM}^{\mathrm{ord}}_n(\mathbb{C})$ carries the associative shadow obstruction tower $(\Theta^{\Eone}_\cA)^{\leq r}$; formality of the ordered convolution algebra is the vanishing of the transferred $A_\infty$-operations $m_n^{\mathrm{tr}}$ for $n \geq 3$ (Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul}). Passing from $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ to the symmetric shadow $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$, and from $\mathfrak{g}^{\Eone}_\cA$ to $\gAmod$, by $R$-twisted $\Sigma_n$-coinvariants is lossy: it forgets ordered braid data.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each formality level kills the obstruction classes $o_{r+1}(\cA)$ in the cyclic deformation complex above the corresponding degree threshold (Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-formality-low-degree} for degrees $2$, $3$, $4$).
The $\Etwo$ case identifies shadow-tower termination at degree~$2$ with the $\Etwo$-formality of $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$, and the $\Eone$ case is the associative analogue via the ordered bar complex $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ (Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul}).
\end{proof}

\begin{convention}[Two axes in this chapter]
\label{conv:en-two-axes}
In this chapter, bare $\En$ always denotes little-disks/topological
factorization on real $n$-manifolds. The earlier hierarchy
$\Einf$-chiral/$\Pinf$-chiral/$\Eone$-chiral remains curve-based.
Hence the chiral bar complex on a complex curve belongs to the
topological ladder at $n = 2$, while the $n = 1$ case is the
classical associative/$A_\infty$ bar construction.
\end{convention}

\section{Higher-dimensional configuration spaces}
\label{sec:higher-dim-config}
\index{configuration space!higher-dimensional}

\begin{definition}[Configuration space of \texorpdfstring{$\bR^n$}{R-n}]
\label{def:config-rn}
For a smooth oriented $n$-manifold~$M$, the \emph{ordered
configuration space of $k$~points} is
\[
\Conf_k(M) \;=\;
\bigl\{(x_1, \ldots, x_k) \in M^k
 \mid x_i \neq x_j \text{ for } i \neq j\bigr\}.
\]
For $M = \bR^n$, the space $\Conf_k(\bR^n)$ is a smooth manifold
of real dimension~$nk$, and its cohomology admits an explicit
generators-and-relations presentation due to Arnold ($n = 2$, i.e., $\bR^n = \bC$) and
Totaro (general $n$).
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Arnold presentation {\cite{Arnold69}}; \texorpdfstring{$\bC \cong \bR^2$}{C = R2};
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:arnold-presentation}
\index{Arnold relations!presentation}
The cohomology ring $H^*(\Conf_k(\bC); \bQ)$ is the graded-commutative
algebra generated by classes $\eta_{ij} \in H^1$,
$1 \leq i < j \leq k$, subject to the \emph{Arnold relations}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:arnold-rel-en}
\eta_{ij} \wedge \eta_{jk}
+ \eta_{jk} \wedge \eta_{ki}
+ \eta_{ki} \wedge \eta_{ij} \;=\; 0
\quad (i < j < k).
\end{equation}
This is the Orlik--Solomon algebra $\mathrm{OS}_k$
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:os-cohomology-config})}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Totaro presentation, general \texorpdfstring{$n$}{n} {\cite{Totaro96, Coh76}};
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:totaro-presentation}
\index{Totaro relations|textbf}
\index{configuration space!cohomology ring}
For $n \geq 2$, the cohomology ring
$H^*(\Conf_k(\bR^n); \bQ)$ is the graded-commutative algebra
generated by classes $\alpha_{ij} \in H^{n-1}$,
$1 \leq i \neq j \leq k$, subject to the relations:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Symmetry)}\quad
 $\alpha_{ij} = (-1)^n \alpha_{ji}$;
\item \textup{(Totaro relations)}\quad
 $\alpha_{ij} \cdot \alpha_{jk}
 + \alpha_{jk} \cdot \alpha_{ki}
 + \alpha_{ki} \cdot \alpha_{ij} = 0$
 for distinct $i, j, k$;
\item \textup{(Degree)}\quad
 $\alpha_{ij}^2 = 0$ when $n$ is odd;
 when $n$ is even, $\alpha_{ij}^2$ is determined by the Euler class.
\end{enumerate}
At $n = 2$, after identifying $\bR^2 \cong \bC$ and choosing the
oriented generator of $H^1(\Conf_2(\bR^2))$, one recovers Arnold's
three-term relation on $\Conf_k(\bC)$. By contrast, $n = 1$ is the
degree-$0$ associative setting of ordered points on a line, not the
chiral curve case.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
See Totaro~\cite{Totaro96}, Theorem~1; also
Cohen~\cite{Coh76}, Theorem~A for the formality statement.
The key geometric input is that projection maps
$\Conf_k(\bR^n) \to \Conf_2(\bR^n)$ induce pullbacks of the
fundamental class of $S^{n-1}$ (the linking sphere), and all
relations among these pullbacks are generated by the three-term
relation (ii).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Arnold as Totaro at \texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2}]
\label{rem:arnold-totaro}
\index{Arnold relations!as Totaro specialization}
In the $\En$ hierarchy, $n$ is the \emph{real} dimension.
The classical Arnold generators on $\Conf_k(\bC)$ are Totaro
generators at $n = 2$; our chiral bar complex uses $n = 2$:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ccc}
$\En$-level & Manifold & Bar complex \\
\hline
$n = 1$ & interval/circle & associative bar (classical) \\
$n = 2$ & surface/curve & chiral bar (this monograph) \\
$n = 3$ & $3$-manifold & Chern--Simons bar \\
general $n$ & $n$-manifold & $\En$ bar
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Main Theorems~A, B, C are the holomorphic refinement of the
$n = 2$ chapter of $\En$ Koszul duality.
\end{remark}


\subsection{\texorpdfstring{Fulton--MacPherson compactification in dimension $n$}{Fulton--MacPherson compactification in dimension n}}
\label{subsec:fm-general-n}
\index{Fulton--MacPherson compactification!higher-dimensional}

\begin{definition}[FM compactification of \texorpdfstring{$\Conf_k(\bR^n)$}{Conf-k(R-n)}]
\label{def:fm-rn}
The \emph{Fulton--MacPherson compactification}
$\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ is the real oriented blowup of $(\bR^n)^k$
along all partial diagonals, ordered by reverse inclusion.
Equivalently (Axelrod--Singer, Kontsevich), it is the closure of
the image of the map
\[
\Conf_k(\bR^n)
\;\hookrightarrow\;
(\bR^n)^k
\times \prod_{i<j} S^{n-1}
\times \prod_{|S| \geq 2} [0, \infty]
\]
recording positions, relative directions
$\theta_{ij} = (x_i - x_j)/|x_i - x_j| \in S^{n-1}$,
and relative distances. The result is a smooth manifold with
corners whose boundary strata are indexed by nested partitions
of $\{1, \ldots, k\}$, the same combinatorial data as the
Stasheff associahedron ($n = 1$) or the FM operad ($n \geq 2$).
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Boundary strata and operadic structure;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere{} \cite{FM94}]
\label{prop:fm-boundary-strata}
\index{Fulton--MacPherson!boundary strata}
The codimension-$1$ boundary strata of
$\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ are indexed by subsets
$S \subset \{1, \ldots, k\}$ with $|S| \geq 2$, and are
diffeomorphic to
\[
\partial_S \overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)
\;\cong\;
\overline{\Conf}_{k-|S|+1}(\bR^n)
\times
\overline{\Conf}_{|S|}(\bR^n).
\]
This factorization equips $\{\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)\}_{k \geq 0}$
with the structure of a topological operad, the
\emph{Fulton--MacPherson operad} $\mathsf{FM}_n$, which is a model
for the $\En$ operad \textup{(}i.e., weakly equivalent to the
little $n$-disks operad\textup{)}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The operadic structure is due to Getzler--Jones~\cite{GJ}
and Salvatore; the equivalence $\mathsf{FM}_n \simeq \En$ is
proved by Salvatore--Longoni~\cite{SalvatoreLongoni05} for $n \geq 2$ and follows from
Stasheff's theorem for $n = 1$.
\end{proof}

\section{\texorpdfstring{The $\En$ propagator}{The En propagator}}
\label{sec:en-propagator}
\index{propagator!$\En$}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} propagator]
\label{def:en-propagator}
\index{propagator!Green's form}
An \emph{$\En$ propagator} on an oriented $n$-manifold~$M$ is a
smooth closed $(n-1)$-form
\[
G \;\in\; \Omega^{n-1}(M \times M \setminus \Delta)
\]
satisfying the following properties:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Poincar\'e dual of diagonal)}\quad
 $G$ represents the Poincar\'e dual of the diagonal
 $\Delta \subset M \times M$ in the sense that for any
 closed form $\eta \in \Omega^*(M)$,
 \[
 \int_{M \times M} G \wedge \pi_2^* \eta
 \;=\; \int_M \eta,
 \]
 where $\pi_2\colon M \times M \to M$ is the second projection.
\item \textup{(Singularity)}\quad
 Near the diagonal $\Delta$, in geodesic coordinates,
 \[
 G \;\sim\;
 \frac{1}{\mathrm{vol}(S^{n-1})}\;
 \frac{r_1\, dr_2 \wedge \cdots \wedge dr_n
 - r_2\, dr_1 \wedge dr_3 \wedge \cdots \wedge dr_n
 + \cdots}{|r|^n}
 \;+\; \text{smooth},
 \]
 where $r = x_1 - x_2$ and $\mathrm{vol}(S^{n-1}) = 2\pi^{n/2}/\Gamma(n/2)$.
\item \textup{(Symmetry)}\quad
 Under the involution $\sigma\colon (x_1, x_2) \mapsto (x_2, x_1)$,
 $\sigma^* G = (-1)^n G$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[\texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2}: the chiral propagator on a complex curve]
\label{ex:n2-chiral-propagator}
For $M = \bC$ (real dimension $2$, but complex dimension $1$, the
chiral case), the propagator is
$G = \eta_{12} = d\log(z_1 - z_2)$, a meromorphic $1$-form.
It is closed ($dG = 0$ away from $z_1 = z_2$), represents the
Poincar\'e dual of the diagonal, and satisfies
$\sigma^* G = d\log(z_2 - z_1) = d\log(-(z_1-z_2)) = d\log(-1) + d\log(z_1-z_2) = G$,
since $d\log(-1) = 0$ (differential of a constant). Residues along $z_1 = z_2$ extract OPE data.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[\texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2}: the \texorpdfstring{$\Etwo$}{E2} propagator on \texorpdfstring{$\bR^2$}{R2}]
\label{ex:n2-propagator}
For $M = \bR^2$, the propagator is
\[
G \;=\;
\frac{1}{2\pi}\;
\frac{(x_1 - x_2)\, dy_1 - (y_1 - y_2)\, dx_1}
 {(x_1 - x_2)^2 + (y_1 - y_2)^2}
\;=\;
\frac{1}{2\pi}\, d\arg(z_1 - z_2),
\]
a closed $1$-form on $\Conf_2(\bR^2)$. This is the angular form
(winding number kernel): integration over a small circle
$S^1 \subset \bR^2$ around $x_2$ gives $1$. Note that
$G = (1/2\pi)\, d\arg(z_1 - z_2)$ is exactly the
\emph{Kontsevich propagator} on $S^1 \subset \bC$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:propagator-restriction}). The $\Etwo$
propagator on a surface and the chiral propagator on a complex curve
are thus two aspects of the same object: the former is real-valued
and topological, the latter is holomorphic and algebraic.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[\texorpdfstring{$n = 3$}{n = 3}: the Chern--Simons propagator]
\label{ex:n3-propagator}
\index{Chern--Simons!propagator}
For $M = \bR^3$, the propagator is
\[
G \;=\;
\frac{1}{4\pi}\;
\frac{\epsilon_{abc}\, r^a\, dr^b \wedge dr^c}{|r|^3},
\]
where $r = x_1 - x_2 \in \bR^3$. This is the closed $2$-form on
$\Conf_2(\bR^3)$ representing the linking number:
$\int_{S^2} G = 1$. In the Axelrod--Singer perturbative expansion of
Chern--Simons theory~\cite{Kon94}, this propagator appears as the
kernel of the gauge-fixed Green's operator $d^{-1}$.
\end{example}

\begin{proposition}[Residue as linking sphere integral;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:linking-sphere-residue}
\index{residue!linking sphere}
For an $\En$ propagator $G$ on $\bR^n$ and a smooth function
$f$ on a neighborhood of the diagonal, the \emph{$\En$ residue} is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:en-residue}
\Res_\Delta(G \wedge f)
\;:=\;
\lim_{\varepsilon \to 0}
\int_{|x_1 - x_2| = \varepsilon} G \cdot f(x_1, x_2)
\;=\; f(x, x),
\end{equation}
where the integral is over the linking sphere
$S^{n-1}_\varepsilon \subset \Conf_2(\bR^n)$ of radius~$\varepsilon$
around the diagonal. This generalizes the residue operation
$\Res_{z_1 = z_2}$ in the chiral ($n = 2$) case.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By the singularity condition (ii) of
Definition~\ref{def:en-propagator}, $G$ restricts on the
sphere $|r| = \varepsilon$ to the standard volume form on $S^{n-1}$
(normalized to total volume~$1$) plus terms vanishing as
$\varepsilon \to 0$. Hence the integral extracts the value
$f(x, x)$, exactly as a contour integral residue extracts the
leading Laurent coefficient.
\end{proof}

\section{\texorpdfstring{The $\Etwo$ bar complex}{The E2 bar complex}}
\label{sec:e2-bar}
\index{bar complex!$\Etwo$}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\Etwo$}{E2} bar complex]
\label{def:e2-bar-complex}
\index{bar complex!$\Etwo$|textbf}
Let $A$ be an $\Etwo$-algebra (equivalently, an algebra over
the little $2$-disks operad). The \emph{$\Etwo$ bar complex}
is the chain complex
\[
\barB_{\Etwo}(A)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{k \geq 1}
\Omega^*\bigl(\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^2)\bigr)
\otimes_{\Sigma_k} (s^{-1} \bar{A})^{\otimes k},
\]
with differential
\begin{equation}\label{eq:e2-bar-diff}
d_{\Etwo}
\;=\;
d_{\mathrm{dR}}
\;+\;
\sum_{S \subset \{1, \ldots, k\},\, |S| \geq 2}
\Res_{\partial_S}\bigl(G_S \wedge \mu_S\bigr),
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $G_S = \bigwedge_{i < j,\; i, j \in S} \alpha_{ij}$ is the
 product of propagators for pairs within the
 colliding subset~$S$,
\item $\Res_{\partial_S}$ is the linking-sphere residue along the
 boundary stratum $\partial_S$ of
 $\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^2)$
 \textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:linking-sphere-residue})},
\item $\mu_S\colon \bar{A}^{\otimes |S|} \to \bar{A}$ is the
 $\Etwo$-multiplication indexed by the FM operad element
 corresponding to the stratum~$\partial_S$.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[\texorpdfstring{$d^2 = 0$}{d squared = 0} from Totaro relations;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:e2-d-squared}
\index{Totaro relations!and $d^2 = 0$}
The differential $d_{\Etwo}$ of
Definition~\textup{\ref{def:e2-bar-complex}} satisfies
$d_{\Etwo}^2 = 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The proof follows the same structure as the chiral case
(Corollary~\ref{cor:bar-d-squared-zero-arnold}), with the Arnold
relations replaced by Totaro relations.

We compute $d_{\Etwo}^2$ on a bar element
$\alpha \in \Omega^*(\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^2))
\otimes (s^{-1} \bar{A})^{\otimes k}$.
The square decomposes into three terms:
\[
d_{\Etwo}^2
\;=\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{dR}}^2}_{= 0}
\;+\;
\underbrace{[d_{\mathrm{dR}},\, d_{\mathrm{res}}]}_{(\star)}
\;+\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{res}}^2}_{(\star\star)},
\]
where $d_{\mathrm{res}} = \sum_S \Res_{\partial_S}(G_S \wedge \mu_S)$.

For $(\star)$: the de~Rham differential commutes with the residue
operation because $G$ is closed ($dG = 0$), so the cross-term
$[d_{\mathrm{dR}}, d_{\mathrm{res}}]$ reduces to
$\sum_S \Res_{\partial_S}(dG_S \wedge \mu_S) = 0$.

For $(\star\star)$: the square $d_{\mathrm{res}}^2$ involves
iterated residues along pairs of boundary strata. For three
distinct points $i, j, k$, the iterated residue
$\Res_{\partial_{\{i,j\}}} \circ \Res_{\partial_{\{j,k\}}}$
plus cyclic permutations gives a sum of three terms. The
Totaro relation
\[
\alpha_{ij} \cdot \alpha_{jk}
+ \alpha_{jk} \cdot \alpha_{ki}
+ \alpha_{ki} \cdot \alpha_{ij} \;=\; 0
\qquad
\text{in } H^{2(n-1)}(\Conf_3(\bR^n))
\]
at $n = 2$ ensures that this sum vanishes. More precisely: each
term in $d_{\mathrm{res}}^2$ corresponds to a codimension-$2$
boundary stratum of $\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^2)$, and the Totaro
relation is exactly the statement that the codimension-$2$
contributions cancel in cohomology.

For nested collisions (e.g., $\{i, j\} \subset \{i, j, k\}$),
the iterated residue factors through the operadic composition
$\mu_{\{i,j,k\}} = \mu_{\{i,j\}} \circ \mu_{\{j,k\}}$, and
cancellation follows from the $\Etwo$-algebra axiom (associativity
up to coherent homotopy, encoded by the FM operad structure).

For the algebraic contribution: iterated residues at the same
stratum $S$ produce the square $\mu_S \circ \mu_S$, which vanishes
by the $A_\infty$ relations encoded in the $\Etwo$ structure.

The argument generalizes Idrissi's result~\cite{Idrissi22} that
Poincar\'e duality on configuration spaces ensures $d^2 = 0$ for
the propagator-based differential, specialized to $n = 2$ with
explicit Totaro generators.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Comparison with the chiral bar complex]
\label{rem:e2-vs-chiral}
\index{bar complex!$\Etwo$ vs chiral}
The $\Etwo$ bar complex (Definition~\ref{def:e2-bar-complex})
and the chiral bar complex (Chapter~\ref{chap:bar-cobar}) differ in propagator and dependence:
the chiral complex uses the holomorphic $\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$ and depends on the complex structure,
while the $\Etwo$ complex uses the topological $G = (1/2\pi)\, d\arg(z_1 - z_2)$ and depends only on oriented topology.
For a chiral algebra $\cA$ on~$X$,
$\barB_{\Etwo}(\cA^{\mathrm{top}}) \simeq \barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)^{\mathrm{top}}$.
The chiral bar complex carries strictly more information: genus-$g$ quantum corrections
$\kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality})
live in the chiral bar complex but not in the $\Etwo$ bar complex.
\end{remark}

\section{\texorpdfstring{$\En$ Koszul duality}{En Koszul duality}}
\label{sec:en-koszul-duality}
\index{Koszul duality!$\En$|textbf}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} bar-cobar adjunction]
\label{def:en-bar-cobar}
For each $n \geq 1$, the \emph{$\En$ bar functor} and \emph{$\En$
cobar functor} are defined by:
\begin{align}
\barB_{\En} &\colon
 \En\text{-}\mathrm{Alg} \;\longrightarrow\;
 \En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg},
 \label{eq:en-bar}\\
\Omega_{\En} &\colon
 \En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg} \;\longrightarrow\;
 \En\text{-}\mathrm{Alg},
 \label{eq:en-cobar}
\end{align}
where $\En\text{-}\mathrm{Alg}$ denotes the $\infty$-category of
$\En$-algebras in chain complexes and $\En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg}$
denotes the $\infty$-category of $\En$-coalgebras. The bar
construction uses the chain complex
$C_*(\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n))$ with the differential of
\eqref{eq:e2-bar-diff} generalized to dimension~$n$, and the cobar
construction is its linear dual.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} Koszul duality;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:en-koszul-duality}
\index{Koszul duality!$\En$}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Lurie~\cite{HA}, \S5.2)}\quad
 The functors $\barB_{\En}$ and $\Omega_{\En}$ form an adjoint pair
 \[
 \barB_{\En}
 \;\dashv\;
 \Omega_{\En}
 \colon
 \En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg}
 \;\rightleftarrows\;
 \En\text{-}\mathrm{Alg}.
 \]
\item \textup{(Francis~\cite{Francis2013}, Theorem~4.20)}\quad
 For an $\En$-algebra $A$ that is \emph{$\En$-Koszul}
 \textup{(}the $\En$ bar spectral sequence collapses\textup{)},
 the counit
 $\Omega_{\En}(\barB_{\En}(A)) \xrightarrow{\sim} A$
 is a quasi-isomorphism.
\item \textup{(Ayala--Francis~\cite{AF15}, Theorem~7.8)}\quad
 For a closed oriented $n$-manifold~$M$ and an $\En$-algebra~$A$,
 Poincar\'e--Koszul duality gives a natural equivalence:
 \begin{equation}\label{eq:poincare-koszul}
 \int_M A
 \;\simeq\;
 \int_M A^\vee[-n],
 \end{equation}
 where $\int_M$ denotes $\En$-factorization homology and $A^\vee$
 is the $\En$-Koszul dual coalgebra.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Chain-level content of this monograph]
\label{rem:chain-level-en}
Ayala--Francis prove part~(iii) at the $\infty$-categorical level.
Main Theorems~A, B, C are the chain-level refinement at $n = 2$:
explicit propagators, residue formulas, and spectral sequences.
The genus-$g$ quantum corrections (Theorem~B) and complementarity
(Theorem~C) are specific to curves with complex structure, invisible
to the topological $\Etwo$ theory.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Recovery of chiral bar-cobar at \texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2};
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:n2-recovery}
For a chiral algebra $\cA$ on a complex curve~$X$, the chiral
bar-cobar adjunction of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}} is recovered
from the $\Etwo$ bar-cobar adjunction
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:en-koszul-duality}(i))} by
equipping the underlying $\Etwo$-algebra $\cA^{\mathrm{top}}$ with
the holomorphic propagator $\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$ in
place of the topological propagator $G$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The holomorphic propagator $\eta_{ij}$ on a curve~$X$ is a specific
representative of the $\Etwo$ propagator class
$[\alpha_{ij}] \in H^1(\Conf_2(X))$; different representatives give
quasi-isomorphic bar complexes (since they differ by exact forms,
and the bar differential is insensitive to exact corrections in
cohomology). The Arnold relations on~$X$ are the Totaro relations at
$n = 2$, so $d^2 = 0$ follows from
Theorem~\ref{thm:e2-d-squared}. The residue operation
$\Res_{z_i = z_j}$ is the $n = 2$ linking-sphere residue
(Proposition~\ref{prop:linking-sphere-residue}): the linking sphere
$S^1$ around the diagonal in a surface is the contour of integration
in the OPE residue formula.
\end{proof}

\section{Connection to Ayala--Francis}
\label{sec:ayala-francis-connection}
\index{Ayala--Francis!chain-level refinement}
\index{Poincar\'e--Koszul duality!chain-level}

Ayala--Francis~\cite{AF15} establish Poincar\'e--Koszul duality as
an equivalence of $\infty$-categories:

\begin{theorem}[Poincar\'e--Koszul duality, AF {\cite{AF15}};
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:af-pkd}
\index{Poincar\'e--Koszul duality}
Let $M$ be a closed oriented $n$-manifold and $A$ an $\En$-algebra.
There is a natural equivalence
\[
\int_M A
\;\simeq\;
\mathrm{Map}_{\En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg}}
\bigl(\barB_{\En}(\mathbf{1}),\, \barB_{\En}(A)\bigr),
\]
where $\mathbf{1}$ is the unit $\En$-algebra.
For $A$ augmented, this simplifies to the Poincar\'e--Koszul duality
\eqref{eq:poincare-koszul}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proposition}[Our construction refines AF at \texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2};
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:refines-af}
At $n = 2$, the chain-level bar complex
$\barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ of
Chapter~\textup{\ref{chap:bar-cobar}} is a
strict model for the $\Etwo$ bar construction of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:af-pkd}}, in the following sense:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item The FM propagator integrals
 $\int_{\overline{C}_k(X)} \prod_{(i,j)} \eta_{ij}
 \otimes \prod_v a_v$
 compute the factorization homology $\int_X \cA$ as a chain
 complex.
\item The Verdier duality pairing on $\overline{C}_k(X)$
 \textup{(}\S\textup{\ref{sec:verdier-ayala-francis})} realizes the
 Poincar\'e duality of Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:af-pkd}} at the
 cochain level.
\item The spectral sequence from bar filtration to bar cohomology
 is the descent spectral sequence of factorization homology with
 respect to the FM cover.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Part (a): by the fundamental theorem of factorization
homology~\cite{AF15}, $\int_X \cA$ is computed by a \v{C}ech complex
with respect to any Weiss cover of~$X$ by disks. The FM
compactification $\overline{C}_k(X)$ provides a canonical
resolution: configuration space integrals against the propagator
$\eta_{ij}$ compute the \v{C}ech differential, and the bar
elements $\bigotimes_v a_v$ provide the coefficients. This is the
content of the Prism Principle
(Theorem~\ref{thm:prism-higher-genus}).

Part (b): Verdier duality $\mathbb{D}_{\overline{C}_k(X)}$ exchanges the
FM compactification with the open configuration space. This is the
geometric incarnation of the Poincar\'e duality in
Theorem~\ref{thm:af-pkd}: the duality pairing between
$\int_X A$ and $\int_X A^\vee$ is realized by the intersection
pairing on $\overline{C}_k(X)$. See
Theorem~\ref{thm:verdier-bar-cobar} for the precise statement
at $n = 2$.

Part (c): the FM cover filtration
$F_p = \bigoplus_{k \leq p} C_*(\overline{C}_k(X))
\otimes (s^{-1} \bar{A})^{\otimes k}$
gives a spectral sequence with
$E_1^{p,q} = H^q(\overline{C}_p(X)) \otimes (s^{-1}\bar{A})^{\otimes p}$
converging to $H^*(\int_X A)$. This is the bar spectral sequence
of Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-spectral-sequence}, reinterpreted as
the descent spectral sequence for the factorization homology
presheaf.
\end{proof}

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{The $\En$ bar complex for arbitrary $n$}{The En bar complex for arbitrary n}}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} bar complex, general \texorpdfstring{$n$}{n}]
\label{def:en-bar-general}
\index{bar complex!general $n$}
For $n \geq 1$ and an $\En$-algebra $A$, the \emph{$\En$ bar complex}
is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:en-bar-general}
\barB_{\En}(A)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{k \geq 1}
\Omega^*\bigl(\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)\bigr)
\otimes_{\Sigma_k} (s^{-1}\bar{A})^{\otimes k},
\quad
d \;=\; d_{\mathrm{dR}} + d_{\mathrm{res}},
\end{equation}
where $d_{\mathrm{res}} = \sum_S \Res_{\partial_S}(G_S \wedge \mu_S)$
uses the $\En$ propagator of Definition~\textup{\ref{def:en-propagator}}
and linking-sphere residues
\textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:linking-sphere-residue})}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[\texorpdfstring{$d^2 = 0$}{d squared = 0} for the \texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} bar complex;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:en-d-squared}
\index{bar complex!$d^2 = 0$}
For any $n \geq 1$ and any $\En$-algebra $A$, the $\En$ bar
differential satisfies $d^2 = 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The argument is identical to the $n = 2$ case
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e2-d-squared}), with the Arnold/Totaro
relations in $H^*(\Conf_3(\bR^n))$ providing the cancellation of
$d_{\mathrm{res}}^2$. For general~$n$, the Totaro relation holds
in degree $2(n-1)$, and the cancellation works in any degree.

The result also follows from the general formality framework of
Idrissi~\cite{Idrissi22}: Poincar\'e duality on the FM
compactification $\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ provides an explicit
quasi-isomorphism between the de~Rham complex of
$\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ and its cohomology algebra (the Totaro
algebra), and the propagator differential is compatible with this
quasi-isomorphism.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Configuration space Betti numbers and the $\En$ shadow obstruction tower}
\label{subsec:en-shadow-tower}
\index{shadow tower!$\En$}
\index{configuration space!Betti numbers}

The Poincar\'e polynomial of $\Conf_k(\bR^n)$ for $n \geq 2$ is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:conf-poincare}
P(\Conf_k(\bR^n),\, t)
\;=\;
\prod_{j=0}^{k-1} (1 + j\, t^{n-1}),
\end{equation}
yielding total Betti number $\sum b_i = k!$ for all $n \geq 2$. The
generator degree~$n-1$ controls the bar complex grading: the $\En$
propagator is a closed $(n{-}1)$-form, so the degree-$k$ bar complex
$\barB_{\En}(A)_k$ has summands in degrees $0, n{-}1, 2(n{-}1),
\ldots, (k{-}1)(n{-}1)$ weighted by the Betti numbers
of~\eqref{eq:conf-poincare}.

\begin{proposition}[Kappa universality across $n$;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:kappa-universality-en}
\index{modular characteristic!$\En$ universality}
For any $\En$-algebra~$A$ with a fixed binary pairing of level~$k$,
the modular characteristic is independent of~$n$:
\[
\kappa_{\En}(A) \;=\; \kappa_{\Eone}(A)
\qquad \text{for all } n \geq 1.
\]
In particular, $\kappa_{\En}(H_k) = k$ for the Heisenberg,
$\kappa_{\En}(\widehat{\fg}_k) = \dim(\fg) \cdot (k + h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$ for affine Kac--Moody,
and $\kappa_{\En}(\mathrm{Vir}_c) = c/2$ for Virasoro.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The modular characteristic $\kappa$ is determined by the degree-$2$
component of the bar complex, which involves $\Conf_2(\bR^n)
\simeq S^{n-1}$. The propagator pairing on $S^{n-1}$ extracts the
$0$-mode of the binary operation, which is the level~$k$
independently of~$n$. Formally, $H^{n-1}(\Conf_2(\bR^n))
= \bQ$ for all $n \geq 2$, and the propagator integral
$\int_{S^{n-1}} G \cdot \mu_2(a, b)$ is the same bilinear
invariant for all~$n$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Shadow stabilization threshold;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:shadow-stabilization}
\index{shadow tower!stabilization}
For the degree-$r$ shadow $S_r^{\En}(A)$, the stabilization threshold
is $n_{\mathrm{stable}}(r) = r$: for $n \geq r$, the shadow
$S_r^{\En}(A)$ equals the stable value $S_r^{\Einf}(A)$ and is
independent of~$n$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
At degree~$r$, the bar complex involves $\Conf_r(\bR^n)$, whose
positive-degree cohomology begins in degree~$n{-}1$. The MC
equation at degree~$r$ constrains the total degree budget to
$r{-}2$ propagator units. For $n{-}1 > r{-}2$ (i.e., $n \geq r$),
the positive-degree classes of $\Conf_r(\bR^n)$ exceed this budget
and do not contribute. Hence $S_r^{\En} = S_r^{\mathrm{tree}}
= S_r^{\Einf}$ in the stable range~$n \geq r$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The G/L/C/M classification under $\En$]
\label{rem:glcm-en}
\index{shadow depth!$\En$ independence}
For a formal $\En$-algebra, the shadow depth class (G/L/C/M
of Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-archetype-classification}) is independent of~$n$:
formality of the $\En$ operad
(Proposition~\ref{prop:en-formality}) ensures that the
higher-degree corrections from the topology of $\Conf_k(\bR^n)$
do not change the termination behavior. The Heisenberg
remains class~G ($r_{\max} = 2$), affine Kac--Moody
remains class~L ($r_{\max} = 3$), the $\beta\gamma$ system
remains class~C ($r_{\max} = 4$), and Virasoro remains
class~M ($r_{\max} = \infty$), for all~$n \geq 1$.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The dimensional ladder]
\label{rem:dimensional-ladder}
\index{dimensional ladder}
The transition from $n$ to $n+1$ trades algebraic structure for
topological generality:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{clll}
$n$ & Propagator & Relations & Bar complex computes \\
\hline
$1$ & $\operatorname{sgn}(t_1 - t_2)$ & Stasheff polytopes & Hochschild cohomology \\
$2$ & $d\log(z_1 - z_2)$ & Arnold (OS) & Chiral Koszul dual \\

\end{remark}


\subsection{Knudsen's higher enveloping algebras}

\begin{theorem}[Higher enveloping algebras; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:knudsen-higher-enveloping}
\index{enveloping algebra!higher}
\textup{(}Knudsen~\cite{Knudsen18}\textup{)}\quad
For a Lie algebra $\fg$ and $n \geq 1$, the \emph{$n$-th enveloping
algebra} $U_n(\fg)$ is an $\En$-algebra satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $U_1(\fg) = U(\fg)$ is the classical universal enveloping
 algebra.
\item \textup{(PBW)}\quad There is an equivalence of graded objects
 $\mathrm{gr}\, U_n(\fg) \simeq \mathrm{Sym}^{\En}(\fg[n-1])$,
 where $\mathrm{Sym}^{\En}$ is the free commutative $\En$-algebra.
\item \textup{(Koszul duality)}\quad
 $\barB_{\En}(U_n(\fg))$ is quasi-isomorphic to
 $\mathrm{CE}^{\En}_*(\fg)$, the $\En$-Chevalley--Eilenberg
 complex of~$\fg$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

This result is the higher-dimensional analogue of the PBW theorem
and the classical bar-CE comparison. At $n = 2$, the $\Etwo$
enveloping algebra $U_2(\fg)$ is the topological shadow of the
chiral universal enveloping algebra
$\widehat{U}(\fg_k) = V_k(\fg)$ (the vacuum module). Knudsen's
Koszul duality at $n = 2$ is the topological shadow of our chiral
Koszul duality.

\section{The Kontsevich graph complex and the shadow obstruction tower}
\label{sec:graph-complex-shadow}
\index{graph complex!Kontsevich $\mathrm{GC}_2$|textbf}
\index{shadow tower!graph complex bridge|textbf}

The modular convolution algebra $\gAmod$ maps to the Kontsevich
graph complex $\mathrm{GC}_2$ via the formality quasi-isomorphism.
The shadow invariants $S_r(\cA)$ project to specific graph cocycles,
and the G/L/C/M depth classification corresponds to truncation in
$\mathrm{GC}_2$.

\begin{definition}[The graph complex $\mathrm{GC}_n$]
\label{def:graph-complex-gcn}
\index{graph complex!definition}
The \emph{Kontsevich graph complex} $\mathrm{GC}_n$ is the cochain
complex spanned by isomorphism classes of connected graphs~$\Gamma$
with all vertex valences $\geq 3$ (no bivalent or univalent vertices),
with cohomological degree
\[
\deg(\Gamma) \;=\; |E(\Gamma)| - 2|V(\Gamma)|,
\]
and differential given by edge contraction:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gc-differential}
d(\Gamma) \;=\; \sum_{e \in E(\Gamma)} \pm\, \Gamma/e,
\end{equation}
where $\Gamma/e$ denotes contraction of the edge~$e$ (discarding
terms that produce self-loops or bivalent vertices), and the sign is
determined by the position of~$e$ in the ordered edge list. The loop
order of~$\Gamma$ is the first Betti number
$\ell(\Gamma) = |E(\Gamma)| - |V(\Gamma)| + 1$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Wheel cocycles and $\mathrm{grt}_1$;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere{} \cite{Willwacher15}]
\label{thm:willwacher-wheels}
\index{wheel cocycle|textbf}
\index{Grothendieck--Teichmuller@Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller!Lie algebra}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Willwacher~\cite{Willwacher15})}\quad
 $H^0(\mathrm{GC}_2) \cong \mathrm{grt}_1$, the
 Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller Lie algebra.
\item The generators of $\mathrm{grt}_1$ include the
 \emph{wheel cocycles} $\sigma_{2k+1}$ for $k \geq 1$, represented
 by the wheel graph $W_{2k+1}$ with $2k{+}1$ spokes. The wheel
 $W_{2k+1}$ has $2k{+}2$ vertices, $2(2k{+}1)$ edges, loop
 order~$2k{+}1$, and degree~$-2$. It is a cocycle in
 $\mathrm{GC}_2$ if and only if the number of spokes is odd
 \textup{(}the dihedral $\bZ/2$-symmetry of $W_n$ provides
 cancellation of the differential only for $n$~odd\textup{)}.
\item The wheel cocycles correspond to the Deligne--Drinfeld
 elements, encoding odd zeta values $\zeta(3), \zeta(5), \zeta(7),
 \ldots$ in the Lie algebra of the motivic Galois group.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proposition}[Shadow obstruction tower to $\mathrm{GC}_2$ bridge;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:shadow-gc2-bridge}
\index{shadow tower!graph complex projection}
The shadow invariants of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ project to
$\mathrm{GC}_2$ via the formality quasi-isomorphism: the odd-degree
shadows $S_{2k+1}(\cA)$ map to the wheel cocycles
$\sigma_{2k+1} \in H^*(\mathrm{GC}_2)$, while even-degree shadows
$S_{2k}(\cA)$ map to exact forms \textup{(}coboundaries in
$\mathrm{GC}_2$\textup{)}. The projection respects the depth
classification:
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{ccl}
Shadow class & Nonzero wheels & Algebra \\
\hline
G \textup{(}depth $2$\textup{)} & none ($S_3 = 0$)
 & Heisenberg, lattice \\
L \textup{(}depth $3$\textup{)} & $\sigma_3, \sigma_5$
 & Affine Kac--Moody \\
 % Resolved RECTIFICATION-FLAG (2026-04-07): The L_infty formality map Phi
 % has higher components; sigma_5 for class L arises from Phi_2(S_2, S_3),
 % not directly from S_5 (which vanishes for class L). This is consistent:
 % class L has S_3 != 0 but S_r = 0 for r >= 4, and the wheel sigma_5
 % is the Phi_2 image of the pair (S_2, S_3). The table entry is correct
 % as stated (lists which wheels are nonzero, not their source).
C \textup{(}depth $4$\textup{)} & $\sigma_3, \sigma_5, \sigma_7$
 & $\beta\gamma$ \\
M \textup{(}depth $\infty$\textup{)} & all $\sigma_{2k+1}$
 & Virasoro, $\cW_N$
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The formality quasi-isomorphism $\Phi\colon \gAmod \xrightarrow{\sim}
H^*(\gAmod)$ sends the MC element $\Theta_\cA$ to a sum of graph
cocycle components. At degree~$r$, the image $\Phi(S_r)$ lies in the
weight-$r$ part of~$\mathrm{GC}_2$. For odd~$r = 2k{+}1$, the
wheel graph $W_{2k+1}$ is a cocycle
(Theorem~\ref{thm:willwacher-wheels}(ii)),
and $\Phi(S_{2k+1})$ is proportional to~$\sigma_{2k+1}$ by
uniqueness of the cocycle in the relevant degree. For even~$r$, the
wheel $W_{2k}$ is \emph{not} a cocycle (it bounds), so $\Phi(S_{2k})$
is necessarily exact: it maps to a coboundary in $\mathrm{GC}_2$.

The depth truncation follows from the shadow obstruction tower termination:
if $S_r(\cA) = 0$ for all $r > r_{\max}$, then the
$\mathrm{GC}_2$ projection has nonzero wheel components only for
$\sigma_{2k+1}$ with $2k{+}1 \leq 2r_{\max} - 1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Graph complex signs for multigraphs]
\label{rem:gc-multigraph-signs}
\index{graph complex!sign conventions for multigraphs}
The $d^2 = 0$ identity on the Kontsevich graph complex
$\mathrm{GC}_2$ requires care for multigraphs: the naive
canonicalize-then-differentiate approach fails because the
canonical labeling absorbs intermediate sign data. Two correct
approaches: (i)~label edges, compute~$d$, then take the canonical
quotient; (ii)~work in the coinvariant quotient from the start
with the half-edge orientation framework. The bar complex signs
agree with $\mathrm{GC}_2$ signs because both use
$\det(E(\Gamma))$ for edge-ordering.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Shadow zeta values]
\label{def:shadow-zeta-values}
\index{shadow zeta values|textbf}
For a chiral algebra~$\cA$ with $\kappa(\cA) \neq 0$, the
\emph{normalized shadow zeta values} are the dimensionless
invariants
\begin{equation}\label{eq:shadow-zeta}
\zeta^{\mathrm{sh}}_r(\cA) \;:=\;
\frac{S_r(\cA)}{|\kappa(\cA)|^{r/2}},
\qquad r \geq 2.
\end{equation}
These are rational functions of the central charge or level parameter
and are invariant under rescaling. The normalized shadow zeta values
encode the profile of the shadow obstruction tower, independent of the overall
scale set by~$\kappa$.
\end{definition}

\section{The Swiss-cheese case: holomorphic--topological
factorization}
\label{sec:swiss-cheese}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad|textbf}
\index{bar complex!$\Eone$-chiral coassociative coalgebra|textbf}
\index{holomorphic--topological!factorization|textbf}

The dimensional ladder has so far been one-dimensional: $n = 1$
(associative), $n = 2$ (chiral), $n = 3$ (Chern--Simons). But the
ordered bar complex $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ of this monograph
already carries a \emph{mixed}
structure that does not fit on this line. The differential
$d_{\barB}$ lives on $\FM_k(\bC)$ (the $n = 2$ slot); the
coassociative coproduct~$\Delta$ lives on $\Conf_k(\bR)$ (the
$n = 1$ slot). Together, a bar element of degree~$k$ is
parametrized by
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bar-product-space}
\FM_k(\bC) \times \Conf_k(\bR),
\end{equation}
the product of holomorphic and topological configuration spaces.
This product structure is the operadic fingerprint of a
\emph{two-colored} operad: the Swiss-cheese operad
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$.

\begin{definition}[Holomorphic--topological Swiss-cheese operad]
\label{def:SC}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad!definition}
Define the two-colored topological operad $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$
with colors $\{\mathsf{ch}, \mathsf{top}\}$ (closed/holomorphic
and open/topological) by:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Closed output, closed inputs only:}
 $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}
 ((\mathsf{ch},\ldots,\mathsf{ch});\, \mathsf{ch})
 := \FM_k(\bC)$.
\item \emph{Open output, mixed inputs:}
 $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}
 ((\underbrace{\mathsf{ch},\ldots}_{k},
 \underbrace{\mathsf{top},\ldots}_{m});\, \mathsf{top})
 := \FM_k(\bC) \times E_1(m)$.
\item \emph{No open-to-closed:}
 $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}
 (\ldots,\mathsf{top},\ldots;\, \mathsf{ch})
 = \varnothing$.
\end{enumerate}
Composition is componentwise: FM insertion in~$\bC$, interval
insertion in~$E_1$. The no-open-to-closed rule ensures that
the closed color is an operadic retract: it receives no information
from the open color.
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[The bar complex is $\Eone$-chiral coassociative;
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ emerges on the derived center]
\label{princ:sc-two-incarnations}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad!on derived center, NOT on bar}
\index{bar complex!is E1 coassociative, NOT SC}
The ordered bar complex
$\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar{\cA})$
is an $\Eone$-chiral coassociative coalgebra: a coalgebra
over the Koszul dual cooperad
$(\operatorname{Ass}^{\mathrm{ch}})^{\scriptstyle !}$.
It has a differential (from OPE collision residues) and a
coassociative deconcatenation coproduct. These are the two
structures of a dg coalgebra. The bar complex does \emph{not}
carry $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ structure.

The $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ structure emerges on
the \emph{derived chiral center}
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)
= C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$,
computed \emph{using} the bar complex as a resolution.
The derived center is the algebra of bulk observables of
the 3d HT theory on $X \times \bR$:
\begin{itemize}
\item It carries $\Etwo$ structure from
  $\FM_k(\bC)$ (the operadic center theorem,
  Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}}).
\item With an inner conformal vector at non-critical level,
  this $\Etwo$ upgrades on BRST cohomology to $\Ethree$ via the
  topologization theorem
  (Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:topologization}}).
\item Without a conformal vector,
  $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ carries
  $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ structure
  (holomorphic $\Etwo$ coupled to topological $\Eone$),
  strictly weaker than $\Ethree$.
\end{itemize}
The passage from bar complex to derived center is
where the $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ structure
is \emph{produced}: the bar complex is the $\Eone$-chiral
computational engine; the derived center is the
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ (or $\Ethree$) output.
The five theorems~A--D$+$H extract invariants of
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ by computing
in~$\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$.
\end{principle}

\begin{theorem}[Bar complex as $\Eone$-chiral coassociative coalgebra;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:bar-swiss-cheese}% label retained for backward compatibility
\label{thm:bar-e1-coalgebra}
\index{bar complex!$\Eone$-chiral coassociative coalgebra}
Let $\cA$ be an augmented chiral algebra on a curve~$X$. The
\emph{ordered} bar complex
\[
\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)
\;=\; T^c(s^{-1}\bar{\cA})
\;=\; \bigoplus_{n\geq 0}
 (s^{-1}\bar{\cA})^{\otimes n},
\]
equipped with its bar differential~$d_{\barB}$ and the
\emph{deconcatenation} coproduct~$\Delta$, is a dg coassociative
coalgebra over the Koszul dual cooperad
$(\operatorname{Ass}^{\mathrm{ch}})^{\scriptstyle !}$:
\begin{center}
\small
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\textbf{Bar structure} & \textbf{Operadic origin} &
 \textbf{Configuration space} \\
\hline
Differential $d_{\barB}$
 & chiral product (OPE residues)
 & $\FM_k(\bC)$: collision at boundary strata \\[2pt]
Deconcatenation $\Delta$
 & cofree coalgebra structure
 & $\Conf_k(\bR)$: consecutive splitting \\[2pt]
Coderivation: $d_{\barB} \circ \Delta
 = (\Delta) \circ d_{\barB}$
 & dg compatibility
 & product structure~\eqref{eq:bar-product-space}
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
The bar complex is a \emph{single-colored} $\Eone$-chiral coalgebra.
It does not carry $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ structure
\textup{(}Principle~\textup{\ref{princ:sc-two-incarnations}}\textup{)}.
The $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ structure emerges on the
derived chiral center $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)
= C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$, computed using
$\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ as a resolution
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}}\textup{)}.
The coproduct is the \textup{(}$n+1$\textup{)}-term
deconcatenation
$\Delta[a_1|\cdots|a_n] =
 \sum_{i=0}^{n}[a_1|\cdots|a_i]\otimes[a_{i+1}|\cdots|a_n]$
on the tensor coalgebra $T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$, \emph{not} the
$2^n$-term coshuffle that appears on the symmetric bar
$\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA) = S^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ used in
Theorem~\textup{A}. The relation between the two is that
$\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ is recovered as the lossy
$R$-twisted $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant shadow of $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$
\textup{(}braid-twisted symmetrization, with the chiral $R$-matrix
encoding sign and braiding\textup{)}. Theorem~\textup{A}'s
factorization-coalgebra structure on $\barB^{\Sigma}$ uses the
coshuffle coproduct; the $\Eone$-chiral coassociative coalgebra structure in this
theorem lives on the ordered bar with its deconcatenation
coproduct.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The deconcatenation $\Delta$ on the tensor coalgebra
$T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ is coassociative by construction. The
ordering of tensor factors $a_1\otimes\cdots\otimes a_n$
corresponds to the ordering of points $t_1<\cdots<t_n$ on~$\bR$;
the splitting at index~$i$ corresponds to cutting the interval at
$t_i$. This is the $E_1$ coassociative coalgebra structure on the ordered bar
$\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$: the deconcatenation coproduct is
determined by the interval-cutting combinatorics of $\Conf_k(\bR)$.

The differential $d_{\barB}$ extracts residues along collision
divisors $D_S \subset \partial\FM_k(\bC)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-nilpotency-complete}). Working at genus~$0$
on the ordered bar, $d_{\barB}$ is a coderivation of~$\Delta$: the
co-Leibniz rule
\[
\Delta\circ d_{\barB} \;=\;
(d_{\barB}\otimes\id + \id\otimes d_{\barB})\circ \Delta
\]
holds because collision residues at \emph{adjacent} factors
$(a_i,a_{i+1})$ in the chiral product commute with cutting at
non-adjacent indices on~$\bR$, while a collision residue at the
splitting index produces equal contributions on both sides of the
co-Leibniz identity. This is the content of the product
structure: at genus~$0$ the spaces $\FM_k(\bC)$ and $\Conf_k(\bR)$
contribute independently.

The no-open-to-closed constraint is automatic: the deconcatenation
coproduct (open-color structure) does not appear in the differential
(closed-color structure). The bar differential $d_{\barB}$ is
built purely from the chiral product~$\mu$ and the FM boundary
strata, with no reference to the tensor ordering except via the
adjacency requirement of the chiral product, which is preserved by
$\Delta$.

The passage to the symmetric bar $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ via
$R$-twisted $\Sigma_n$-coinvariants converts the deconcatenation
coproduct into the coshuffle coproduct (whose summands are indexed
by $(p,q)$-shuffles of $\{1,\ldots,n\}$, totalling $2^n$ rather
than $n+1$); the $\Eone$-chiral coassociative coalgebra structure of this theorem
is a property of the ordered bar prior to that symmetrization.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Physical interpretation]
\label{rem:sc-physical}
A three-dimensional holomorphic--topological (HT) quantum field
theory on $\bC_z \times \bR_t$ produces local observables that
factorize holomorphically in~$z$ and associatively in~$t$. The BV
quantization of such a theory produces an algebra over
$W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})$ (the Boardman--Vogt resolution),
and the resulting higher operations $m_k$ are realized by
configuration-space integrals on
$\FM_k(\bC) \times \Conf_k(\bR)$, the same spaces that
parametrize bar elements of degree~$k$. Volume~II develops this
identification in full.
\end{remark}

\begin{construction}[Promotion functor: from chiral algebra to
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra]
\label{constr:promotion-functor}
\index{promotion functor|textbf}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad!promotion from chiral algebra}
A chiral algebra~$\cA$ is one-coloured (an algebra over the
closed colour of~$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$). The operadic
bar relative to the full two-coloured operad requires a
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra
$(\cA_{\mathrm{cl}},\, \cA_{\mathrm{op}})$ as input
(a closed-colour algebra and an open-colour algebra with
mixed operations). The \emph{promotion functor}
$\cA \mapsto (\cA, \cA)$ uses self-action: every
commutative algebra acts on itself as a module, supplying
the open-colour and mixed data canonically. The operadic bar
$B_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA, \cA)$ decomposes:
\begin{itemize}
\item Closed sector: $B_{\operatorname{Com}}(\cA)
 = \operatorname{Lie}^c \circ s^{-1}\bar{\cA}$ (Harrison).
\item Open sector: $B_{\operatorname{Ass}}(\cA)
 = T^c(s^{-1}\bar{\cA})$ (tensor).
\item Mixed sector: dimension $(k{-}1)!\,\binom{k+m}{m}$
 at bi-degree $(k,m)$
 (Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:sc-koszul-dual-three-sectors}}).
\end{itemize}
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-swiss-cheese}} is the statement that
the bar of a chiral algebra, promoted to an
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra via self-action,
naturally carries the $\Eone$-chiral coassociative dg coalgebra structure.
\end{construction}

\begin{remark}[Ordered Verdier duality does not exist]
\label{rem:ordered-verdier-nonexistence}
\index{Verdier duality!ordered bar obstruction}
The Verdier intertwining
$\mathbb{D}_{\mathrm{Ran}}\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA) \simeq \barB^{\Sigma}(\cA^!)$
(Theorem~A, Convention~\textup{\ref{conv:bar-coalgebra-identity}})
is a statement about the \emph{symmetric} bar~$\barB^{\Sigma}$
on~$\mathrm{Ran}(X)$. Here $\cA^!$ is the Verdier-dual algebra.
A naive ordered analogue
$\mathbb{D}_{\mathrm{Ran}}(\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA))
\simeq \barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA^!)$
does \emph{not} exist: the ordered bar lives on ordered
configurations $\mathrm{Conf}_n^{<}(X)$, not on~$\mathrm{Ran}(X)$,
and pushing forward to~$\mathrm{Ran}$ via the
$\Sigma_n$-quotient recovers~$\barB^{\Sigma}$, losing the ordering.
The correct $E_1$ analogue of Verdier intertwining is
\emph{opposite-duality}:
$\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA^{\mathrm{op}})
= \barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)^{\mathrm{cop}}$
(the two-colour double Koszul duality theorem,
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:two-colour-double-kd}}).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Curved Swiss-cheese at higher genus, and the
corrected differential]
\label{rem:sc-higher-genus}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad!curved|textbf}
At genus~$g \geq 1$ the situation is more subtle than a naive
``scalar curvature'' would suggest, and the precise statement
matters for the higher-genus open/closed bookkeeping.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{The fibrewise differential is curved.} The bar
 differential~$\dfib$ obtained by extending the genus-$0$
 collision residue along the universal curve satisfies
 $\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA)\cdot\omega_g$
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}). Here $\omega_g$ is
 the $\kappa$-class on $\overline{\cM}_g$, not a scalar in the
 ground field; the equality is a curvature identity in
 $H^2(\overline{\cM}_g)$, not a centre relation in the bar
 coalgebra.
\item \emph{$\dfib$ is \emph{not} a coderivation of the
 deconcatenation coproduct.} The naive Leibniz check
 fails: applying $\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa\cdot\omega_g$ to a
 splitting at an interior index produces a factor-$2$
 discrepancy between the two sides of the co-Leibniz identity,
 because the curvature $\omega_g$ contracts the closed-color
 collision class against the same $\bR$-interval on both
 branches of the splitting. In particular, the genus-$0$
 ``scalar coderivation'' picture does \emph{not} extend
 unmodified to $g\geq 1$, and the product
 structure~\eqref{eq:bar-product-space} requires the corrected
 differential below to remain a coalgebra map.
\item \emph{The corrected differential
 $\Dg{g}$ \textup{is} a flat coderivation.} The corrected
 differential of Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-diff-squares-zero}
 adds period-integral terms built from the Fay trisecant
 identity: schematically
 $\Dg{g} = \dfib + \sum_{i,j}\int_{\gamma_{ij}}\Phi_{ij}$,
 where $\Phi_{ij}$ are Fay-form contributions on the universal
 curve. These extra terms exactly cancel the factor-$2$
 discrepancy from item~(ii) at every interior splitting, and the
 result is both \emph{flat}
 ($\Dg{g}{}^{\,2} = 0$ over $\overline{\cM}_g$) and a genuine
 coderivation of the deconcatenation coproduct on
 $\barB^{\mathrm{ord},(g)}(\cA)$. The genus tower of
 $\Eone$ coassociative coalgebras is therefore controlled at the chain
 level by~$\Dg{g}$, not by~$\dfib$, and the modular
 characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$ together with the $\hat A$-genus
 generating function records the resulting
 $\overline{\cM}_g$-class
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}).
\end{enumerate}
The slogan is therefore: at genus~$0$ the bar differential is a
coderivation of $\Delta$ for free; at higher genus this
\emph{fails} for the fibrewise differential, and the ``curved
$\Eone$ coalgebra'' picture is rescued only by passing to the
period-corrected differential $\Dg{g}$.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Position in the dimensional ladder]
\label{rem:sc-dimensional-ladder}
The Swiss-cheese operad $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ is the
$(n_{\mathrm{hol}}, n_{\mathrm{top}}) = (2, 1)$ entry in a
two-parameter family of mixed operads. The one-parameter diagonal
$n_{\mathrm{hol}} = n$, $n_{\mathrm{top}} = 0$ recovers the $\En$
ladder of this chapter. The off-diagonal entries mix holomorphic
and topological factorization in different dimensions. The
classical Swiss-cheese of Voronov~\cite{Voronov99} is the
$(n, n-1)$ case; ours is the holomorphic refinement at $(2, 1)$.
\end{remark}

\section{The chiral center theorem: operadic proof}
\label{sec:operadic-center-theorem}
\index{center theorem!operadic proof|textbf}
\index{operadic center|textbf}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad!center theorem}
\index{Hochschild cohomology!as operadic center}
\index{Deligne conjecture!chiral analogue}

The chiral center theorem identifies the chiral Hochschild cochain
complex $C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ as the \emph{operadic
center} of an open-color algebra~$\cA$ in the Swiss-cheese operad
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$. The topological analogue (the
classical Deligne conjecture and its higher-dimensional
generalizations) identifies Hochschild cochains of an associative
algebra as an $\Etwo$-algebra via the center construction in the
classical Swiss-cheese operad. The chiral version requires the
operadic route, and the passage from the topological to the chiral
setting creates genuine new difficulties at each step.

The proof proceeds in five steps: the abstract operadic center
(Step~1), its chain-level computation (Step~2), the geometric
recognition that makes the theorem inevitable (Step~3), the
comparison with the brace-algebra model (Step~4), and the universal
property (Step~5). Two further developments complete the picture:
a scope analysis restricting the operadic center theorem to
$E_\infty$-chiral input
(Remark~\ref{rem:center-scope-e-inf}), and an investigation of
essential surjectivity of the center functor
(\S\ref{subsec:center-essential-surjectivity}), which asks whether
every $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-pair lies in the essential
image of the universal construction.

\subsection{Step 1: The operadic center of a two-colored algebra}
\label{subsec:operadic-center-definition}
\index{operadic center!definition}

\begin{definition}[Two-colored operad with vanishing constraint]
\label{def:two-colored-operad-vanishing}
A \emph{two-colored operad with vanishing open-to-closed operations}
is a symmetric colored operad $\cO$ with color set
$\{\mathsf{c}, \mathsf{o}\}$ (closed and open) satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $\cO((\mathsf{c},\ldots,\mathsf{c});\,\mathsf{c})$ is an
 operad in color~$\mathsf{c}$ (the \emph{closed sector}
 $\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$);
\item $\cO((\mathsf{c}^k, \mathsf{o}^m);\, \mathsf{o})$ defines a
 right module over $\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$ and a bimodule over the
 $\mathsf{o}$-operad
 $\cO^{\mathsf{o}} := \cO((\mathsf{o},\ldots);\, \mathsf{o})$;
\item $\cO((\ldots,\mathsf{o},\ldots);\,\mathsf{c}) = \varnothing$:
 no open-to-closed operations.
\end{enumerate}
The Swiss-cheese operad $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$
(Definition~\ref{def:SC}) is the primary example.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Koszul dual cooperad of
\texorpdfstring{$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$}{SC}: three sectors;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:sc-koszul-dual-three-sectors}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad!Koszul dual cooperad|textbf}
\index{Koszul dual cooperad!Swiss-cheese three sectors}
The Koszul dual cooperad
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top},!}$ has three sectors:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Closed} ($\mathsf{c}^n \to \mathsf{c}$):
 $\dim = (n{-}1)!$ \textup{(}Lie cooperad,
 since $\operatorname{Com}^! = \operatorname{Lie}$\textup{)}.
\item \emph{Open} ($\mathsf{o}^m \to \mathsf{o}$):
 $\dim = m!$ \textup{(}Ass cooperad,
 self-Koszul-dual\textup{)}.
\item \emph{Mixed} ($\mathsf{c}^k,\,\mathsf{o}^m \to \mathsf{o}$,
 $k \geq 1$):
 $\dim = (k{-}1)!\,\binom{k+m}{m}$.
 This differs from the plain tensor product
 $(k{-}1)!\cdot m!$ by the factor $\binom{k+m}{m}/m!$,
 encoding the $(k,m)$-shuffle interleaving of closed
 inputs among ordered open inputs.
\end{enumerate}
Computational verification:
\texttt{sc\_koszul\_dual\_cooperad\_engine.py}
\textup{(}$82$ tests\textup{)}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The closed sector is $\operatorname{Com}^{!,c} =
\operatorname{Lie}^c$ with $\dim\operatorname{Lie}(n) = (n{-}1)!$
(partition-lattice calculation,
Theorem~\ref{thm:com-lie}).
The open sector is $\operatorname{Ass}^{!,c} =
\operatorname{Ass}^c$ with $\dim = m!$ (self-duality).
The mixed sector is computed by the K\"unneth
factorization: $\operatorname{Lie}^c(k)$ contributes
$(k{-}1)!$, and the $(k,m)$-shuffle count
$\binom{k+m}{m}$ arises from interleaving $k$ closed
leaves among $m$ ordered open leaves on the
$\operatorname{FM}_k(\mathbb{C}) \times E_1(m)$
operation space.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Convolution algebra factorization]
\label{cor:convolution-factorization}
\index{convolution algebra!SC factorization}
The $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-convolution algebra
of an augmented chiral algebra~$\cA$ decomposes as
\[
\mathfrak{g}_\cA^{\mathsf{SC}}
\;=\;
\mathfrak{g}_\cA^{\mathrm{mod}}
\;\times\;
\mathfrak{g}_\cA^{\mathbb{R}},
\]
where
$\mathfrak{g}_\cA^{\mathrm{mod}}
= \operatorname{Conv}(\operatorname{Lie}^c, \operatorname{End}_\cA)$
controls the closed direction
(shadows $\kappa$, $\mathfrak{C}$, $\mathfrak{Q}$)
and
$\mathfrak{g}_\cA^{\mathbb{R}}
= \operatorname{Conv}(\operatorname{Ass}^c, \operatorname{End}_\cA)$
controls the open direction
($r$-matrix, KZ associator, Yangian deformation).
The two factors commute. The mixed sector
\textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:sc-koszul-dual-three-sectors}(iii)}\textup{)}
governs their interaction: bulk operators acting on boundary
states via iterated brace evaluation
\textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:mixed-sector-bulk-boundary}}\textup{)}.
\end{corollary}

\begin{definition}[Operadic center]
\label{def:operadic-center}
\index{operadic center!abstract definition|textbf}
Let $\cO$ be a two-colored operad satisfying
Definition~\ref{def:two-colored-operad-vanishing}, and let $A_\mathsf{o}$
be an algebra over $\cO^{\mathsf{o}}$. The \emph{operadic center}
$Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})$ is the terminal object in the category of
$\cO$-algebra pairs:
\[
Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})
\;:=\;
\varprojlim_{\substack{(B, A_\mathsf{o}) \in \\
\cO\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}}}
B,
\]
where the limit is taken over the category
$\cO\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}_{/A_\mathsf{o}}$
of $\cO$-algebra pairs $(B, A_\mathsf{o})$
in which the open-color algebra is the given~$A_\mathsf{o}$. More
explicitly, $Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})$ carries the structure of a
$\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$-algebra together with a coherent family of action
maps
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-action-maps}
\alpha_{k,m}\colon
\cO((\mathsf{c}^k, \mathsf{o}^m);\, \mathsf{o})
\otimes Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})^{\otimes k}
\otimes A_\mathsf{o}^{\otimes m}
\;\longrightarrow\; A_\mathsf{o},
\end{equation}
satisfying the equivariance and associativity axioms of an
$\cO$-algebra, and is \emph{universal} with this property: for every
$\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$-algebra~$B$ equipped with action
maps~\eqref{eq:center-action-maps} making $(B, A_\mathsf{o})$ an
$\cO$-algebra, there exists a unique $\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$-algebra
morphism $B \to Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})$ through which all action maps
factor.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Concrete description via internal Hom]
\label{rem:center-internal-hom}
\index{operadic center!internal Hom description}
When $\cO^{\mathsf{o}}$-algebras form a category enriched over chain
complexes, the operadic center admits an explicit model. Let
$\mathsf{End}_{A_\mathsf{o}}(m) := \operatorname{Hom}(A_\mathsf{o}^{\otimes m}, A_\mathsf{o})$
denote the endomorphism operad of~$A_\mathsf{o}$. The action
maps~\eqref{eq:center-action-maps} assemble into an operad morphism
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-operad-morphism}
\gamma\colon \cO^{\mathsf{c}} \;\longrightarrow\;
\operatorname{End}_{Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})}
\end{equation}
subject to the compatibility condition that for each $k, m$, the
composite
\[
\cO((\mathsf{c}^k, \mathsf{o}^m);\, \mathsf{o})
\otimes Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})^{\otimes k}
\xrightarrow{\gamma \otimes \mathrm{id}}
\operatorname{Hom}(A_\mathsf{o}^{\otimes m}, A_\mathsf{o})
\]
equals the given $\cO$-algebra structure. This makes the center a
\emph{derived mapping object}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-as-derived-hom}
Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})
\;\simeq\;
\operatorname{RHom}_{\cO^{\mathsf{o}}\text{-}\mathsf{bimod}}
\!\bigl(\cO(\mathsf{c}, -;\, \mathsf{o}),\,
\mathsf{End}_{A_\mathsf{o}}\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $\cO(\mathsf{c}, -;\, \mathsf{o})$ is regarded as a
$\cO^{\mathsf{o}}$-bimodule via the mixed composition maps.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Existence of the operadic center;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:operadic-center-existence}
\index{operadic center!existence}
Let $\cO$ be a two-colored operad as in
Definition~\textup{\ref{def:two-colored-operad-vanishing}}, working
in chain complexes over a field of characteristic zero. For any
$\cO^{\mathsf{o}}$-algebra~$A_\mathsf{o}$, the operadic center
$Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})$ exists and is computed by the equalizer
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-equalizer}
Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})
\;\hookrightarrow\;
\prod_{m \geq 0}
\operatorname{Hom}_{\Sigma_m}
\!\bigl(
\cO((\mathsf{c}, \mathsf{o}^m);\, \mathsf{o}),\,
\operatorname{Hom}(A_\mathsf{o}^{\otimes m}, A_\mathsf{o})
\bigr)
\;\rightrightarrows\;
(\cdots)
\end{equation}
where the two maps enforce
\textup{(}a\textup{)} $\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$-algebra equivariance
\textup{(}the closed-sector operadic composition\textup{)} and
\textup{(}b\textup{)} $\cO^{\mathsf{o}}$-bimodule equivariance
\textup{(}the open-sector composition\textup{)}. In the derived
setting, the equalizer is replaced by a homotopy equalizer computed
via a bar-type resolution.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
This is a standard construction in the theory of colored operads.
The product
$\prod_m \operatorname{Hom}_{\Sigma_m}(\cO(\mathsf{c},
\mathsf{o}^m; \mathsf{o}), \operatorname{Hom}(A_\mathsf{o}^{\otimes m}, A_\mathsf{o}))$
assembles all possible action maps with one closed input.
Equivariance with respect to $\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$-composition
(inserting further closed inputs) and $\cO^{\mathsf{o}}$-composition
(inserting open inputs into the open arguments) is imposed by the
two arrows. The equalizer exists because chain complexes are complete.
In the derived setting, one replaces $\cO$ by a cofibrant resolution
$\cO_\infty \xrightarrow{\sim} \cO$ and takes the homotopy equalizer
in the model-categorical sense; the resulting object is well-defined
up to quasi-isomorphism.
\end{proof}


\subsection{Step 2: Chain-level realization for
\texorpdfstring{$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$}{SC-ch,top}}
\label{subsec:center-chain-level}
\index{operadic center!chain-level realization}

\begin{theorem}[The operadic center of
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ is the chiral Hochschild complex;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}
\index{Hochschild cohomology!as operadic center|textbf}
Let $\cA$ be a logarithmic chiral algebra
\textup{(}an algebra over the closed sector
$(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})^{\mathsf{c}} = \{\FM_k(\bC)\}$
of the holomorphic--topological Swiss-cheese operad, with
operations extending to the log compactification of
$\Conf_k(\bC)$\textup{)}, and
let $A = \cA|_{\{z_0\}}$ denote the fiber at a basepoint
\textup{(}an $E_1$-algebra via the open sector\textup{)}. There is
a quasi-isomorphism of $\Etwo$-algebras
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-hochschild-qi}
Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)
\;\simeq\;
C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA),
\end{equation}
where the right-hand side is the chiral Hochschild cochain complex
\textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:chiral-hochschild})},
equipped with its braces and cup product.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We compute the center using
Definition~\ref{def:operadic-center} and the explicit form of
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$
(Definition~\ref{def:SC}).

\emph{Step 2a: Identifying the components.}
By Definition~\ref{def:SC}, the mixed-output spaces are
$\mathsf{SC}(\mathsf{c}^k, \mathsf{o}^m;\, \mathsf{o})
= \FM_k(\bC) \times E_1(m)$.
An element of the center at degree~$(k,m)$ is a map
\[
\phi_{k,m}\colon
\FM_k(\bC) \times E_1(m)
\;\longrightarrow\;
\operatorname{Hom}(A^{\otimes m}, A)
\]
equivariant for the $\Sigma_k$-action on $\FM_k(\bC)$ and the
$\Sigma_m$-action on $E_1(m)$.
Setting $m = 0$ and noting that $E_1(0) = \mathrm{pt}$:
\[
\phi_{k,0}\colon \FM_k(\bC) \;\longrightarrow\; A,
\]
which is a $k$-cochain on configuration space valued in~$A$.
For general~$m$, the $E_1(m)$-factor provides the ``$m$ open inputs''
that the center element acts on via the $E_1$-multiplication on~$A$.

\emph{Step 2b: Center elements are chiral Hochschild cochains.}
We construct an explicit isomorphism between the degree-$k$ component
of $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ and $C^k_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$. By
Proposition~\ref{prop:operadic-center-existence}, the center is the
equalizer
\[
Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)
\;\hookrightarrow\;
\prod_{m \geq 0}
\operatorname{Hom}_{\Sigma_m}
\!\bigl(
\mathsf{SC}((\mathsf{c}, \mathsf{o}^m);\, \mathsf{o}),\,
\operatorname{Hom}(A^{\otimes m}, A)
\bigr)
\;\rightrightarrows\;
(\cdots),
\]
where the two arrows impose closed-sector equivariance (composing
further closed inputs) and open-sector bimodule equivariance
(composing open inputs via~$E_1$). Substituting
$\mathsf{SC}((\mathsf{c}, \mathsf{o}^m); \mathsf{o})
= \FM_1(\bC) \times E_1(m) \cong \mathrm{pt} \times E_1(m)$
at $k = 1$, the product over~$m$ assembles into
$\prod_m \operatorname{Hom}_{\Sigma_m}(E_1(m),
\operatorname{Hom}(A^{\otimes m}, A))
\cong \operatorname{End}^{E_1}_{A}$,
the $E_1$-endomorphism operad of~$A$.

At general degree~$k$, the closed-sector equivariance arrow forces a
center element to be a $\Sigma_k$-equivariant family over
$\FM_k(\bC)$ of such $E_1$-endomorphisms. The open-sector bimodule
arrow forces compatibility with the $E_1$-algebra structure on~$A$:
inserting the identity at any open slot recovers the original element
(a unitality condition), and pre-/post-composing with the $E_1$
multiplication on~$A$ is compatible with the interval-insertion maps
$E_1(m) \times E_1(j) \to E_1(m + j - 1)$.

The degree-$k$ center component is therefore
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-bar-degree}
Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)_k
\;\cong\;
\bigl[\Omega^*(\FM_k(\bC), \log D)
\otimes \cA^{\boxtimes k}
\otimes \cA_{\mathrm{out}}\bigr]^{\Sigma_k},
\end{equation}
where the $\Sigma_k$-equivariance acts simultaneously on the
configuration space coordinates and on the algebra factors, the
logarithmic forms $\Omega^*(\FM_k(\bC), \log D)$ arise from the
chain-level model for~$\FM_k(\bC)$, the $k$ copies of~$\cA$
correspond to the $k$ closed inputs, and $\cA_{\mathrm{out}}$ is
the output fiber at the basepoint $z_0 \in X$. This is
a section of $\cA^{\boxtimes (k+1)}$ over the compactified
configuration space $\overline{C}_{k+1}(X)$ of $k+1$ points
(one output and $k$ inputs), tensored with logarithmic forms,
which is the definition of
$C^k_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$
(Definition~\ref{def:chiral-hochschild},
\S\ref{subsec:center-chain-level}).
The open-sector bimodule condition ensures that the evaluation
at the output point $z_0$ and the insertion of algebra elements
at the input points are compatible with the chiral product,
which is the Hochschild cochain compatibility condition
(the cochain evaluates inputs via the chiral product and
returns an output in~$\cA$).

\emph{Step 2c: Matching the differentials.}
The center $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ carries three differential
contributions; we identify each with a component of the chiral
Hochschild differential
$d_{\mathrm{Hoch}} = d_{\mathrm{int}} + d_{\mathrm{fact}}
+ d_{\mathrm{config}}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-differential}).

\emph{(c.1) Internal differential.}
The $\cD_X$-module differential $d_\cA$ of the chiral algebra acts
on each fiber $\cA(z_i)$ independently. Under the identification of
Step~2b, $d_\cA$ acts on the $\cA^{\boxtimes (k+1)}$ factor,
producing
$d_{\mathrm{int}}(\phi)(z_1, \ldots, z_k)
= \sum_{i=0}^{k} (-1)^{|\phi_0| + \cdots + |\phi_{i-1}|}
\,\phi_0 \otimes \cdots \otimes d_\cA \phi_i \otimes \cdots
\otimes \phi_k \otimes \omega$,
which is the component $d_{\mathrm{int}}$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-differential}.

\emph{(c.2) Factorization differential.}
The closed-sector operadic composition
$\circ_i\colon \FM_k(\bC) \times \FM_\ell(\bC) \to
\FM_{k+\ell-1}(\bC)$
inserts a degree-$\ell$ closed element into the $i$-th slot
of a degree-$k$ element. At the chain level, for a center
element $\phi \in Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)_k$, this composition acts
by restricting~$\phi$ to the boundary stratum
$\partial_{ij}\FM_k(\bC)$ where $z_i \to z_j$ and extracting the
residue of the chiral product~$\mu$:
\[
(d_{\mathrm{fact}}\phi)(z_1, \ldots, z_{k-1})
= \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq k}
(-1)^{\epsilon(i,j)}\,
\operatorname{Res}_{z_i = z_j}
\bigl[\phi(\ldots, z_i, \ldots, z_j, \ldots)
\cdot \mu(a_i, a_j)\bigr],
\]
where $\epsilon(i,j)$ is the Koszul sign from permuting $a_i$ past
$a_{i+1}, \ldots, a_{j-1}$. This is the factorization component
$d_{\mathrm{fact}}$ from
Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-differential}: the
residue extraction at collision divisors $D_{ij} \subset
\partial\FM_k(\bC)$ using the chiral product. The signs agree
because both are determined by the codimension-1 boundary
orientation of $\FM_k(\bC)$ and the Koszul convention for
graded-symmetric tensor products.

\emph{(c.3) Configuration-space differential.}
The de~Rham differential $d_{\mathrm{dR}}$ on $\FM_k(\bC)$ acts
on the logarithmic form factor~$\omega$ in
$\phi = a_0 \otimes \cdots \otimes a_k \otimes \omega$. Under
the identification of Step~2b, this is the component
$d_{\mathrm{config}} = d_{\mathrm{dR}}$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-differential}: it raises
the form degree on $\overline{C}_{k+1}(X)$ by~$1$.

\emph{(c.4) Verification that $d^2 = 0$ is preserved.}
The identity $d_{\mathrm{Hoch}}^2 = 0$ is proved in
Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-differential} by checking
three pure squares and three anticommutators. Under the
identification of Steps~2b--2c, each cancellation in the center
differential maps to the corresponding cancellation in the
Hochschild differential: $d_{\mathrm{int}}^2 = 0$ from
$d_\cA^2 = 0$;
$d_{\mathrm{config}}^2 = 0$ from
$d_{\mathrm{dR}}^2 = 0$;
$d_{\mathrm{fact}}^2 = 0$ from the Borcherds identity
(iterated residues at overlapping collision divisors);
$\{d_{\mathrm{int}}, d_{\mathrm{fact}}\} = 0$
because $\mu$ is a $\cD_X$-module map;
$\{d_{\mathrm{int}}, d_{\mathrm{config}}\} = 0$
because $d_\cA$ and $d_{\mathrm{dR}}$ act on independent tensor
factors;
$\{d_{\mathrm{fact}}, d_{\mathrm{config}}\} = 0$
by the Arnold--Orlik--Solomon relations on $\FM_k(\bC)$.
Hence the identification of Step~2b is an isomorphism of
cochain complexes.

\emph{Step 2d: The $\Etwo$-algebra structure.}
The closed sector
$(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})^{\mathsf{c}} = \{\FM_k(\bC)\}$ is
a model for the $\Etwo$ operad
(Proposition~\ref{prop:fm-boundary-strata}). By the universal
property of the center
(Definition~\ref{def:operadic-center}), $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ is an
algebra over
$(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})^{\mathsf{c}} \simeq \Etwo$, and
this $\Etwo$-structure transfers to
$C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ via the isomorphism of
Steps~2b--2c. We verify directly that the two generating operations
match.

\emph{(d.1) Cup product.}
The $\Etwo$ multiplication is the $(2,0)$-degree closed-sector
composition: for $f \in C^p_{\mathrm{ch}}$ and
$g \in C^q_{\mathrm{ch}}$, the operadic composition
$\circ\colon \FM_2(\bC) \times Z(A)^{\otimes 2} \to Z(A)$
yields a map
$(f \cup g)(z_1, \ldots, z_{p+q})
= \int_{\FM_2(\bC)}
f(z_1, \ldots, z_p) \otimes g(z_{p+1}, \ldots, z_{p+q})$,
where the two clusters of points are separated by the
$\FM_2(\bC)$ operad element (one point in each cluster
approaches the corresponding component of the degree-$2$ FM
configuration). This is the cup product on chiral Hochschild
cochains: the concatenation of two cochains separated by the
propagator, identical in structure to the classical
Gerstenhaber cup product except that the propagator is
$d\log(z_1 - z_2)$ rather than the topological
$d\arg(z_1 - z_2)/(2\pi)$.

\emph{(d.2) Braces.}
The brace operation $f\{g_1, \ldots, g_k\}$ arises from the
$(1, k)$-degree composition: one element~$f$ in the ``outer''
position with $k$ elements $g_1, \ldots, g_k$ inserted into $k$
of its slots. At the chain level, this is the composition map
$\FM_1(\bC) \times \FM_k(\bC) \times Z(A) \times Z(A)^{\otimes k}
\to Z(A)$,
which by the FM operad composition rule corresponds to
inserting the $k$ cochains $g_i$ at $k$ distinct points within
the configuration underlying~$f$. The $\FM_k(\bC)$-factor
labels the relative positions of these insertion points,
yielding the chiral brace operations. These satisfy the
pre-Lie and higher brace identities because the operadic
composition maps of $\FM_k(\bC)$ satisfy the corresponding
associativity and equivariance, which is the content of
the $\Etwo$-operad axioms for $\{\FM_k(\bC)\}$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:fm-boundary-strata}).

\emph{(d.3) Compatibility.}
The cup product and braces together generate the full
$\Etwo$-structure, and we have shown both are induced by the
same operadic composition maps that define the center's
$\Etwo$-algebra structure. For a more systematic proof that
the center of an $E_1$-algebra in a two-colored
$E_1 \hookrightarrow E_2$ extension is an $E_2$-algebra, see
Francis~\cite{Francis2013}, Theorem~5.16 (which proves the
stronger result that the tangent complex of an $\En$-algebra
carries an $\En$-algebra structure, specialized here to $n = 2$)
and Lurie~\cite{HA}, \S5.3.1 (which establishes the $\En$-center
of an $E_1$-algebra as an $E_{n+1}$-algebra at the
$\infty$-categorical level, specialized to $n = 1$).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Scope: $E_\infty$-chiral input]
\label{rem:center-scope-e-inf}
\index{operadic center!scope restriction}
Theorem~\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild} requires the chiral
algebra~$\cA$ to be $E_\infty$-chiral (logarithmic): the
operations extend to the log compactification of
$\Conf_k(\bC)$, equivalently, the factorization $\cD$-module
descends to the symmetric powers $X^{(n)}$ via the
$\Sigma_n$-equivariance built into the $\FM_k(\bC)$-action.
For genuinely $E_1$-chiral input (Yangians, Etingof--Kazhdan
quantum vertex algebras), the factorization $\cD$-module does
\emph{not} descend to $X^{(n)}$, the symmetric bar
$\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ does not exist, and
$C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ as defined above is
ill-formed. In the $E_1$ setting, the correct replacement is
the \emph{ordered} Hochschild complex
$\mathrm{HH}^{E_1}_{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA, \cA)$, defined via
the ordered bar complex $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ with
deconcatenation coproduct. The output carries $E_2$ structure
from the classical Deligne conjecture (Kontsevich--Soibelman),
\emph{not} from the chiral Deligne--Tamarkin theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-deligne-tamarkin}). The passage from
$E_2$ to $E_3$ for $E_1$-chiral input requires the Drinfeld
center construction
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-identification-km}).
See Warning~\ref{warn:multiple-e1-chiral}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Chiral vs.\ topological: the spectral-parameter
difficulty]
\label{rem:chiral-spectral-parameter-difficulty}
\index{Deligne conjecture!chiral vs topological}
In the topological setting ($\mathsf{SC}_{\mathrm{top}}$,
Voronov~\cite{Voronov99}), Step~2 is the content of the Deligne
conjecture
(Kontsevich--Soibelman~\cite{KS00},
McClure--Smith, Kaufmann):
the Hochschild cochain complex $C^*(A, A)$ of an associative
algebra is an $\Etwo$-algebra. The chiral setting introduces three
genuine new difficulties:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item \emph{Meromorphic vs.\ smooth propagator.} The topological
 propagator $d\arg(z_1 - z_2)/(2\pi)$ is smooth on
 $\Conf_2(\bR^2)$; the chiral propagator
 $d\log(z_1 - z_2)$ is meromorphic. This forces the chiral
 Hochschild complex to live on the \emph{compactified}
 configuration space $\overline{C}_{k+2}(X) = \FM_{k+2}(X)$ with
 logarithmic forms, not on the open configuration space. The
 Arnold relations (three-term identity on residues) replace the
 smooth Stokes theorem as the mechanism for $d^2 = 0$.
\item \emph{Dependence on curve geometry.} The topological
 center $Z_{\mathrm{SC_{top}}}(A)$ depends only on the
 homotopy type of the associative algebra~$A$. The chiral center
 $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ depends on the underlying curve~$X$: the
 configuration spaces $\FM_k(X)$ carry the complex structure
 of~$X$, and the propagator depends on the choice of coordinate
 (globally: on the choice of meromorphic $1$-form with prescribed
 singularities). This geometric dependence is invisible in
 genus~$0$ (where $X = \bC$ and $\FM_k(\bC)$ is canonically
 defined) but essential at genus~$g \geq 1$, where the center
 carries a flat connection over~$\overline{\cM}_g$.
\item \emph{Curvature at higher genus.} The bar differential
 $d_{\barB}$ satisfies $d_{\barB}^2 = 0$ at genus~$0$, but
 $\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ at genus~$g \geq 1$
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}). This means the
 chiral Hochschild complex is \emph{curved} at higher genus:
 the center is a curved $\Etwo$-algebra, and the curvature
 $\kappa$ is the modular characteristic. No such phenomenon
 occurs in the topological setting.
\end{enumerate}
Difficulty~(a) is handled by the Arnold--Orlik--Solomon formalism
of Chapter~\ref{chap:config-spaces}. Difficulties~(b)
and~(c) are addressed by the modular extension
(Chapter~\ref{chap:higher-genus}). At genus~$0$, difficulties
(b)~and~(c) are absent, and the chiral center theorem reduces to a
holomorphic refinement of the Deligne conjecture.
\end{remark}


\subsection{Step 3: Geometric inevitability via the recognition
theorem}
\label{subsec:center-geometric}
\index{recognition theorem!and the center}

The geometric content of the chiral center theorem becomes
transparent once the recognition theorem (proved in Volume~II) is
invoked. The argument below shows that the theorem is
\emph{forced} by the geometry of the half-space
$\bC \times \bR_{\geq 0}$.

\begin{theorem}[Geometric inevitability of the chiral center;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:center-geometric-inevitability}
\index{center theorem!geometric inevitability|textbf}
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on a curve~$X$, and let
$\cF_\cA$ denote the corresponding prefactorization algebra on
$X \times \bR$ obtained from the
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra structure
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-swiss-cheese})}.
Under the recognition theorem identifying
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebras with prefactorization
algebras on $D \times \bR$
\textup{(}Volume~II, items F4--F5\textup{)}, the following
identifications hold:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Closed color $=$ bulk observables.} The closed-sector
 algebra
 $\cO_{\mathrm{bulk}} := \cF_\cA(\mathrm{disks\ in\ } X)$
 is the chiral algebra~$\cA$ itself.
\item \emph{Open color $=$ boundary observables.} The open-sector
 algebra
 $\cO_{\mathrm{bdy}} := \cF_\cA(\mathrm{half\text{-}disks\ touching\ }
 X \times \{0\})$
 is the $E_1$-algebra $A = \cA|_{\{z_0\}}$, the fiber of~$\cA$
 at a point, equipped with its associative multiplication.
\item \emph{Center $=$ interior bulk observables.} The
 observables of $\cF_\cA$ supported on disks in the interior
 of $X \times \bR_{> 0}$ are precisely the chiral Hochschild
 cochains $C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The proof combines the $\Eone$-chiral coassociative coalgebra structure
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-swiss-cheese}) with the recognition theorem.

\emph{Part (i):} By definition of $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$,
the closed-sector operations are indexed by $\FM_k(\bC)$, which is
the configuration space of points in the complex plane. A disk
$D \subset X$ entirely in the bulk (away from the boundary
$X \times \{0\}$) supports local observables of~$\cA$ that multiply
via the chiral product (OPE on colliding points in~$X$). The
recognition theorem identifies this sector with~$\cA$.

\emph{Part (ii):} Half-disks touching the boundary correspond to the
open-sector operations, indexed by $E_1(m)$ (configurations of
$m$~points on the real line). The fiber $A = \cA|_{\{z_0\}}$ is a
vector space equipped with an associative product from the
$E_1$-structure: this is the boundary vertex algebra.

\emph{Part (iii):}
This is the key identification. Consider a disk $D_{\mathrm{int}}$
in the interior of $X \times \bR_{> 0}$, i.e., a disk that does not
touch the boundary. The observables on $D_{\mathrm{int}}$ must
satisfy two constraints simultaneously:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item They act on boundary observables: pushing
 $D_{\mathrm{int}}$ to the boundary (by moving it in the
 $\bR$-direction) produces an action map
 $\cO_{\mathrm{bulk}} \otimes A^{\otimes m} \to A$, the mixed
 action~\eqref{eq:center-action-maps}.
\item They compose in the bulk via the chiral product on~$X$:
 nesting disks in the holomorphic direction uses
 $\FM_k(\bC)$-composition.
\end{enumerate}
Constraint~(a) says that the interior bulk observable is an
element of
$\operatorname{Hom}(A^{\otimes m}, A)$ for each~$m$, and
constraint~(b) says these elements are coherently related by the
$\FM_k(\bC)$-composition. But this is exactly the definition of
the operadic center (Definition~\ref{def:operadic-center}).

The resulting complex
$\bigoplus_k \Omega^*(\FM_k(\bC)) \otimes_{\Sigma_k}
\operatorname{Hom}(A^{\otimes k}, A)$
is the chiral Hochschild cochain complex: the
configuration-space forms provide the bar degree, and the
Hom-space provides the coefficient. The differential is the
standard chiral Hochschild differential
(Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-differential}),
composed of the factorization differential (from the chiral
product), the de~Rham differential (from the configuration space
geometry), and the internal differential (from the
$\cD_X$-module structure).

This identification is \emph{inevitable}: once the Swiss-cheese
structure is established, the only object satisfying both
constraints simultaneously \emph{is} the Hochschild cochain complex.
There is no room for an alternative.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The open/closed map and the center]
\label{rem:open-closed-center}
\index{open/closed map}
The no-open-to-closed constraint
($\mathsf{SC}(\ldots,\mathsf{o},\ldots;\, \mathsf{c}) = \varnothing$)
is crucial: it means information flows from closed to open but not
back. The center reverses this flow \emph{universally}: it is the
largest closed-sector algebra that acts on the given open-sector
algebra. Physically, the center extracts from the boundary theory
all the information that can be reconstructed about the bulk. This
is the algebraic incarnation of the holographic principle: the bulk
theory (chiral algebra~$\cA$) determines the boundary
($E_1$-algebra~$A$) via restriction, and the center construction
recovers from~$A$ the largest closed-sector algebra acting on it.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The chiral Deligne--Tamarkin principle and the
dimensional jump]
\label{rem:chiral-deligne-tamarkin-dimensional-jump}
\index{Deligne--Tamarkin principle!chiral|textbf}
\index{dimensional jump!2d to 3d|textbf}
Theorems~\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}
and~\ref{thm:center-geometric-inevitability} together constitute
the \emph{chiral Deligne--Tamarkin principle}: for every local
$A_\infty$-chiral algebra~$\cA$, the chiral Hochschild cochain
complex $C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ is the universal
closed-sector algebra acting on~$\cA$ through the Swiss-cheese
structure. The chain of implications is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:deligne-tamarkin-chain}
\cA
\;\;\xmapsto{\text{center}}\;\;
\bigl(C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\;\cA\bigr)
\in \mathrm{Alg}_{W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})}
\;\;\xmapsto{\;\text{recognition}\;}\;\;
\text{local 3d HT theory on } D \times \bR.
\end{equation}
The left arrow is Theorem~\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}
(proved here).
The right arrow is the recognition theorem identifying
$W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})$-algebras with
holomorphic-topological prefactorization algebras on
$D \times \bR$ (Volume~II, items F4--F5; not proved
in this volume).

This is the mechanism by which a $2$-dimensional chiral
algebra on a curve~$X$ produces a $3$-dimensional
holomorphic-topological bulk/boundary system on $X \times \bR$.
The extra dimension is the
universal normal-collar direction of a codimension-one boundary
condition, forced by the center construction.

An independent physical construction corroborating this picture
is given by Khan--Zeng~\cite{KhanZeng25}, who
build a mixed holomorphic-topological gauge theory on
$\bR \times \bC$ from a freely generated Poisson vertex algebra
and prove that gauge invariance holds if and only if the
$\lambda$-Jacobi identity is satisfied~\cite[\S\,2.2]{KhanZeng25}.
They show~\cite[\S\,4.3]{KhanZeng25} that
an inner Virasoro element upgrades the
holomorphic-topological symmetry to fully topological symmetry.
We \emph{expect} (but have not proved) that the Khan--Zeng
gauge theory realizes the right-hand arrow
of~\eqref{eq:deligne-tamarkin-chain}: that is, the universal
algebraic thickening
$\bigl(C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\cA\bigr)$ should be
identified with the perturbative local observable algebra of
their holomorphic-topological gauge theory.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Step 4: Comparison with the brace-algebra center}
\label{subsec:center-comparison}
\index{brace algebra!comparison with operadic center}

The brace-algebra approach to the Hochschild complex defines the
$\Etwo$-structure on $C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ directly,
via brace operations $f\{g_1, \ldots, g_k\}$, without passing through
the operadic center. We show that the two constructions agree.

\begin{definition}[Chiral brace operations]
\label{def:chiral-braces}
\index{brace operations!chiral|textbf}
For cochains
$f \in C^p_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ and
$g_1 \in C^{q_1}_{\mathrm{ch}}, \ldots, g_k \in C^{q_k}_{\mathrm{ch}}$,
the \emph{chiral brace operation} is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:chiral-brace}
f\{g_1, \ldots, g_k\}(z_1, \ldots, z_N)
\;:=\;
\sum_{\substack{S_1 \sqcup \cdots \sqcup S_k \sqcup T \\
= \{z_1, \ldots, z_N\}}}
\pm\;
\operatorname{Res}_{\substack{w_1 \to z_{S_1} \\
\vdots \\ w_k \to z_{S_k}}}
f(w_1, \ldots, w_k, z_T)
\prod_{j=1}^{k} g_j(z_{S_j}),
\end{equation}
where the sum is over ordered partitions of the input set, the
residue is at the collision loci $w_j \to z_{S_j}$ in
$\FM_k(\bC) \subset \overline{C}_{k+N}(X)$, and the sign $\pm$
follows the Koszul convention for the desuspended tensor product.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Brace operations from the operadic center;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:braces-from-center}
\index{brace operations!from operadic center}
The brace operations of
Definition~\textup{\ref{def:chiral-braces}} are recovered from the
$\Etwo$-algebra structure on $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ by restricting
the closed-sector composition to the specific boundary strata of
$\FM_{k+1}(\bC)$ where the first point simultaneously collides with
the remaining~$k$ points. Precisely, the brace
$f\{g_1, \ldots, g_k\}$ is the image of
$(f, g_1, \ldots, g_k) \in Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)^{\otimes(k+1)}$
under the operadic composition map
\[
\FM_{k+1}(\bC)
\otimes
Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)^{\otimes(k+1)}
\;\longrightarrow\;
Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A),
\]
restricted to the open stratum of $\FM_{k+1}(\bC)$ where the
first point is at the center and the remaining~$k$ are close
to it.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The operadic composition in
$(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})^{\mathsf{c}} = \{\FM_k(\bC)\}$
at degree~$(k+1)$ composes a $(k+1)$-ary closed operation with
individual closed operations. In configuration-space terms, this
is the insertion map
$\FM_{k+1}(\bC) \times \FM_{q_1}(\bC) \times \cdots \times
\FM_{q_k}(\bC) \to \FM_N(\bC)$
that replaces each of the $k$ satellite points by a cluster of
$q_j$~points.

At the cochain level, an element $f \in C^p_{\mathrm{ch}}$ is
represented by a form on $\FM_{p+1}(\bC)$, and
$g_j \in C^{q_j}_{\mathrm{ch}}$ is a form on $\FM_{q_j+1}(\bC)$.
The operadic composition inserts the $g_j$-clusters into the
$f$-configuration and extracts the residue along the collision
loci. Unraveling the definitions, this is exactly the brace
formula~\eqref{eq:chiral-brace}: the sum over partitions
corresponds to the sum over ways of assigning the $N$ input
points to the $k$ insertion sites, and the residues arise from the
boundary strata of $\FM_{k+1}(\bC)$ where satellite points collide
with their centers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Comparison theorem: operadic center $=$ brace center;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:operadic-brace-comparison}
\index{center theorem!comparison of models|textbf}
The following three models of the ``center'' are quasi-isomorphic as
$\Etwo$-algebras:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The operadic center $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$
 \textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:operadic-center})}.
\item The chiral Hochschild cochain complex
 $C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ with braces
 \textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:chiral-braces})}.
\item The derived endomorphism algebra
 $\operatorname{REnd}_{\cA^e}(\cA)$, where
 $\cA^e = \cA \otimes \cA^{\mathrm{op}}$ is the enveloping
 algebra.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{(i) $\simeq$ (ii):}
Theorem~\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild} identifies
$Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ with $C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$
as chain complexes. Proposition~\ref{prop:braces-from-center}
shows the $\Etwo$-structure on the center recovers the braces.
Since the $\Etwo$-structure on the Hochschild complex is
\emph{generated} by the braces and cup product (this is the content
of the higher Deligne conjecture, proved in the topological setting
by Kontsevich--Soibelman~\cite{KS00} and extending to the chiral
setting by the same FM-operad mechanism), the two $\Etwo$-structures
agree.

\emph{(ii) $\simeq$ (iii):} The chiral Hochschild cochain complex
computes
$\operatorname{RHom}_{\cA^e}(\cA, \cA)
\simeq \operatorname{REnd}_{\cA^e}(\cA)$
by the bar resolution
(Theorem~\ref{thm:hochschild-bar-cobar}). The derived Hom
carries the standard $\Etwo$-structure from the Eilenberg--Zilber
operad; the bar resolution provides the comparison.

The transitivity \emph{(i) $\simeq$ (ii) $\simeq$ (iii)} completes
the comparison.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Role of homotopy-Koszulity]
\label{rem:homotopy-koszulity-center}
\index{homotopy-Koszulity!role in center theorem}
The comparison in
Theorem~\ref{thm:operadic-brace-comparison}
uses the homotopy-Koszulity of
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ at a single point: the
bar-cobar resolution of the $\mathsf{SC}$-operad must be a
Quillen equivalence, so that derived mapping objects computed
from the bar side agree with those computed from the operadic side.
Volume~II proves homotopy-Koszulity of
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ via three steps:
\textup{(1)}~the classical Swiss-cheese operad is Koszul
(Livernet, Voronov~\cite{Voronov99});
\textup{(2)}~Kontsevich formality gives a quasi-isomorphism
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}} \xrightarrow{\sim}
\mathsf{SC}_{\mathrm{classical}}$;
\textup{(3)}~transfer: bar-cobar preserves quasi-isomorphisms by
the two-out-of-three property.
\emph{Without homotopy-Koszulity}, the operadic center still
exists as a derived object but may not be computed by the explicit
formula~\eqref{eq:center-equalizer}; the cochain-level model
would need to be replaced by a homotopy-coherent (i.e.,
$\infty$-categorical) one.
\end{remark}


\subsection{Step 5: Universality and terminality}
\label{subsec:center-universality}
\index{operadic center!universality}
\index{terminality!of the center}

\begin{theorem}[Terminality of the center; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:center-terminality}
\index{center theorem!terminality|textbf}
Let $A$ be an $E_1$-algebra \textup{(}equivalently, an open-sector
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra\textup{)}. Define the
\emph{universal SC-pair} as
\[
U(A) \;:=\; \bigl(Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A),\; A\bigr).
\]
Then $U(A)$ is the terminal object in the category of
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra pairs
$(B, A)$ with fixed open component~$A$. That is, for every
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra pair $(B, A)$, there exists a
unique morphism of closed-sector algebras
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-terminal-morphism}
\varphi\colon B \;\longrightarrow\; Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)
\end{equation}
such that the diagram of action maps
\[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=large]
\FM_k(\bC) \otimes B^{\otimes k}
 \otimes A^{\otimes m}
 \ar[r, "\alpha^{B}_{k,m}"]
 \ar[d, "\varphi^{\otimes k}"']
& A \\
\FM_k(\bC) \otimes Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)^{\otimes k}
 \otimes A^{\otimes m}
 \ar[ur, "\alpha^{\mathrm{univ}}_{k,m}"']
\end{tikzcd}
\]
commutes for all $k, m \geq 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Existence.}
By the universal property of the operadic center
(Definition~\ref{def:operadic-center}), any
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra structure on $(B, A)$
determines, for each $k$ and~$m$, action maps
$\alpha^B_{k,m}\colon \FM_k(\bC) \otimes B^{\otimes k}
\otimes A^{\otimes m} \to A$.
The $k = 1, m = 0$ component gives a map
$\alpha^B_{1,0}\colon B \to \operatorname{Hom}(\bC, A) = A$, and
more generally the full collection $\{\alpha^B_{k,m}\}$ is
exactly the data of an element in
\[
\prod_{k,m}
\operatorname{Hom}_{\Sigma_k \times \Sigma_m}
\!\bigl(
\FM_k(\bC) \times E_1(m),\,
\operatorname{Hom}(B^{\otimes k} \otimes A^{\otimes m}, A)
\bigr).
\]
Taking the ``$B$-linear'' part (the component linear in a single
copy of~$B$, i.e., $k = 1$) gives a map
$\varphi\colon B \to Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ defined by
\[
\varphi(b)_m
\;:=\;
\alpha^B_{1,m}(-, b, -)
\;\in\;
\operatorname{Hom}(A^{\otimes m}, A).
\]
The full equivariance conditions on the $\alpha^B_{k,m}$ ensure that
$\varphi$ is a map of $\Etwo$-algebras (the closed-sector algebra
structure on~$B$ is intertwined with the closed-sector structure on
$Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$), and the commutativity of the diagram follows
from the associativity of the $\mathsf{SC}$-algebra axioms.

\emph{Uniqueness.}
Suppose $\varphi, \varphi'\colon B \to Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ are two
morphisms making the diagram commute. Then for each $b \in B$ and
each $m$, the commutativity of both diagrams gives
$\alpha^{\mathrm{univ}}_{1,m}(-, \varphi(b), -) = \alpha^B_{1,m}(-, b, -)
= \alpha^{\mathrm{univ}}_{1,m}(-, \varphi'(b), -)$.
Since the universal action maps
$\alpha^{\mathrm{univ}}_{1,m}$ are injective in the center argument on the derived
level (the center map \eqref{eq:center-as-derived-hom} is computed by
$\operatorname{RHom}$, which is faithful on the derived category of
bimodules), $\varphi(b) = \varphi'(b)$ for all $b$,
hence $\varphi = \varphi'$.

Alternatively, uniqueness follows formally from the terminal property
in Definition~\ref{def:operadic-center}: the center is defined as a
limit, and morphisms to/from limits are unique by the universal
property of limits.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[The center functor;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:center-functor}
\index{center!functor}
The assignment $A \mapsto U(A) = (Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A), A)$ defines a
functor
\[
U\colon
E_1\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}
\]
that is \emph{right adjoint} to the forgetful functor
$\mathrm{forget}_{\mathsf{o}}\colon
\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}
\to E_1\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}$
extracting the open component. The unit of this adjunction at
$(B, A)$ is the canonical morphism $B \to Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$
from Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:center-terminality}}.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
For any $\mathsf{SC}$-pair $(B, A)$ and any $E_1$-algebra~$A'$,
a morphism of $E_1$-algebras $A' \to A$ and a morphism
$(B, A) \to U(A') = (Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A'), A')$ correspond
bijectively to
$\operatorname{Hom}_{E_1}(A', A)
\xleftrightarrow{1-1}
\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathsf{SC}}((B,A), U(A'))$,
which is the adjunction identity
$\mathrm{forget}_{\mathsf{o}} \dashv U$.
The unit $\eta_{(B,A)}\colon (B, A) \to U(A) = (Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A), A)$
is the identity on the $A$-component and the canonical center map
on the $B$-component.
\end{proof}


\begin{remark}[The chiral center theorem as a composite statement]
\label{rem:center-theorem-summary}
\index{center theorem!summary}
Assembling Steps~1--5, the chiral center theorem is the conjunction
of four assertions:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\arabic*)}]
\item \emph{Existence}: The operadic center
 $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ exists for every $E_1$-algebra~$A$
 (Proposition~\ref{prop:operadic-center-existence}).
\item \emph{Identification}: $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A) \simeq
 C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ as $\Etwo$-algebras
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}).
\item \emph{Geometric realization}: The center computes
 interior bulk observables in the half-space
 $X \times \bR_{\geq 0}$
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:center-geometric-inevitability}).
\item \emph{Universality}: $(Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A), A)$ is
 terminal among $\mathsf{SC}$-pairs with fixed open
 component (Theorem~\ref{thm:center-terminality}).
\end{enumerate}
In the topological setting, these four assertions are respectively:
existence of the Hochschild complex, the Deligne conjecture,
the bulk-boundary correspondence in 2d TFT, and Keller's
recognition theorem. The chiral setting enriches each with
holomorphic geometry (meromorphic propagators, configuration-space
compactifications, curve-dependence) and
with curvature (the modular characteristic $\kappa$ at higher genus).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Why the chiral setting is genuinely harder]
\label{rem:chiral-vs-topological-center}
\index{Deligne conjecture!chiral difficulties}
To summarize the difficulties identified in
Remark~\ref{rem:chiral-spectral-parameter-difficulty} at the level
of the complete proof:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item \emph{Steps 1--2} (algebraic/chain-level) are parallel to the
 topological case, with Arnold relations replacing smooth Stokes.
 The key input is the FM compactification of $\Conf_k(X)$, which
 provides the same combinatorial structure as $\FM_k(\bR^2)$ but
 with holomorphic forms.
\item \emph{Step 3} (geometric realization) requires the recognition
 theorem (F4/F5), which itself requires homotopy-Koszulity of
 $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$. The proof of
 homotopy-Koszulity uses Kontsevich formality, which is the single
 deepest input. In the topological case, Koszulity of the
 classical Swiss-cheese operad suffices.
\item \emph{Step 4} (comparison) requires that the $\Etwo$-structure
 from the operadic center and the $\Etwo$-structure from braces
 agree. In the topological case, this is part of the Deligne
 conjecture proof. In the chiral case, the comparison
 also requires that the configuration-space forms are compatible with the
 brace insertion, which is guaranteed by the Arnold relations.
\item \emph{Step 5} (universality) is formal and identical in both
 settings.
\end{enumerate}
The single genuinely new ingredient is the passage from smooth to
meromorphic propagators and the resulting replacement of Stokes by
Arnold. Everything else (operadic center, universality, comparison)
is structural and follows the same architecture. The higher-genus
curvature $\kappa$ introduces a further novelty (curved
$\Etwo$-algebras) not present in any topological analogue.
\end{remark}


\subsection{Essential surjectivity of the center functor}
\label{subsec:center-essential-surjectivity}
\index{center!essential surjectivity}
\index{essential surjectivity!of the center functor}

Corollary~\ref{cor:center-functor} establishes the center functor
\[
U\colon E_1\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\text{-}\mathsf{Alg},
\qquad
A \;\longmapsto\; (Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A),\; A),
\]
as right adjoint to the forgetful functor extracting the open
component.  Terminality
(Theorem~\ref{thm:center-terminality}) guarantees that every
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-pair $(B, A)$ admits a
\emph{unique} closed-sector morphism
$\eta_B\colon B \to Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$, the unit of the
adjunction.  The remaining question is whether $\eta_B$ is an
equivalence: does every SC-pair lie in the essential image
of~$U$?

In the topological setting, the analogous question has a
negative answer.  Not every $\Etwo$-algebra arises as the
Hochschild cochain complex $\mathrm{HH}^\bullet(A, A)$ of an
$E_1$-algebra: the Hochschild cochain complex carries
additional structure (the calculus package, compatibility with
the bar construction, the BV operator at the chain level)
that constrains the essential image.

The chiral setting introduces three layers of obstruction.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Brace coherence.}
 The closed-sector algebra~$B$ must act on~$A$ via chiral
 brace operations
 (Definition~\ref{def:chiral-braces}) satisfying the full
 $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-coherence. Not every
 $\Etwo$-algebra~$B$ equipped with a map $B \otimes A^{\otimes m}
 \to A$ satisfies these coherences: the brace relations impose
 nontrivial constraints that are automatically satisfied when
 $B = Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ but may fail for an arbitrary pair.
 The unit $\eta_B$ being an equivalence requires that
 \emph{all} closed-sector operations on~$B$ are detected by
 their action on~$A$.

\item \emph{Bar-cobar reconstruction.}
 On the Koszul locus, bar-cobar inversion
 $\Omega(\barB(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$ recovers the
 chiral algebra from its bar coalgebra. Since the center is
 computed by coderivations of the bar coalgebra
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}), the Koszul
 locus provides a reconstruction path:
 $B \simeq Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ whenever the pair $(B, A)$
 arises from a Koszul chiral algebra. Off the Koszul locus,
 bar-cobar inversion holds only in the coderived category
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:bv-bar-coderived}), and reconstruction
 requires passage to completions.

\item \emph{Curvature obstruction.}
 At genus $g \geq 1$, the fiberwise differential satisfies
 $d^2_{\mathrm{fib}} = \kappa \cdot \omega_g$, introducing a
 curved $\Etwo$-structure on $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$.  A
 candidate closed-sector algebra~$B$ must carry a compatible
 curvature; the modular characteristic $\kappa(B)$ and
 $\kappa(A)$ are related by complementarity
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:central-charge-complementarity}).  An SC-pair
 $(B, A)$ with mismatched curvature data cannot lie in the
 image of~$U$.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{conjecture}[Essential surjectivity on the Koszul locus;
 \ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:center-essential-surjectivity}
\index{center!essential surjectivity!conjecture|textbf}
\index{Koszul locus!essential surjectivity of center}
Let $(B, A)$ be an $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra pair
with $A$ on the Koszul locus.  The unit of the adjunction
\[
\eta_B \colon B \;\longrightarrow\; Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)
\]
is a quasi-isomorphism.  Equivalently, the center functor~$U$
is essentially surjective when restricted to the full
subcategory of $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-pairs whose
open component is Koszul.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Evidence and obstructions]
\label{rem:center-surjectivity-evidence}
\index{center!essential surjectivity!evidence}
Four lines of evidence support
Conjecture~\ref{conj:center-essential-surjectivity}.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item \emph{Koszul reconstruction.}
 On the Koszul locus, $\Omega(\barB(\cA)) \simeq \cA$ and
 $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A) \simeq
 \operatorname{CoDer}(\barB(\cA))$. The pair
 $(Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A), A)$ is therefore determined by the bar
 coalgebra $\barB(\cA)$ alone. Any SC-pair $(B, A)$ on the
 Koszul locus satisfies: $B$ acts on~$A$ through the
 bar coalgebra via the unit $\eta_B$, and bar-cobar
 inversion forces $\eta_B$ to capture all of~$B$.

\item \emph{Standard landscape.}
 For every family in the standard landscape (Heisenberg, affine
 Kac--Moody, Virasoro, $\cW_N$, $\beta\gamma$-systems, lattice
 algebras), the center $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ is computed
 explicitly (Theorem~H), and the unit $\eta_B$ is an
 equivalence for every known SC-pair structure on these
 algebras. No counterexample is known.

\item \emph{Topological analogue.}
 In the topological setting, the center
 $Z(A) = \mathrm{HH}^\bullet(A, A)$ of an $E_1$-algebra~$A$ is
 the terminal $E_2$-algebra acting on~$A$, and
 Keller~\cite{Keller06} shows that for derived-simple algebras
 (the topological Koszul locus), every $E_2$-algebra acting
 centrally on~$A$ is equivalent to a subalgebra
 of~$\mathrm{HH}^\bullet(A, A)$.

\item \emph{Finite-dimensionality of ChirHoch.}
 Theorem~H shows $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is concentrated in
 degrees $\{0, 1, 2\}$ with total dimension at most four on
 the Koszul locus. This severe rigidity constrains the space
 of possible closed-sector algebras: any $B$ acting on~$A$
 must map into a target of dimension at most four, leaving
 little room for the unit to fail surjectivity.
\end{enumerate}
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Failure off the Koszul locus]
\label{rem:center-surjectivity-failure}
\index{center!essential surjectivity!off-Koszul failure}
Off the Koszul locus, essential surjectivity is expected to
fail.  The simplest potential counterexample: let $A$ be
a class~M algebra (Virasoro at generic~$c$), and let
$B$ be a \emph{truncation} of $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ that
retains the $\Etwo$-structure but discards higher-degree
shadow data. The truncated pair $(B', A)$ is a valid
SC-pair (the $\mathsf{SC}$-axioms are satisfied by
restriction), but $B'$ is strictly smaller than
$Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$, so $\eta_{B'}$ is injective but
not surjective. This construction produces SC-pairs not in
the image of~$U$; however, these pairs are ``deficient''
in the sense that $B'$ does not act on~$A$ through all
available operations.

A more interesting failure mode arises from \emph{exotic}
SC-pairs: an $\Etwo$-algebra~$B$ equipped with an action
on~$A$ that does not factor through any chiral algebra
structure. Such pairs would represent genuinely
non-geometric boundary-bulk systems. Whether exotic
SC-pairs exist is an open question closely related to the
formality of the Swiss-cheese operad
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ and to the recognition
problem for holomorphic-topological field theories
(Volume~II).
\end{remark}

\begin{example}[Deficient SC-pairs from shadow tower truncation]
\label{ex:deficient-sc-pair-virasoro}
\index{Swiss-cheese pair!deficient|textbf}
\index{shadow depth!truncation produces deficient pair}
\index{center!essential surjectivity!explicit counterexample off Koszul locus}
\index{Virasoro algebra!deficient SC-pair}

We make Remark~\ref{rem:center-surjectivity-failure} explicit.
Fix $c$ generic and let $\cA = \mathrm{Vir}_c$ (class~$M$,
$r_{\max} = \infty$). The center
$Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA)$ is the chiral Hochschild cochain complex
$C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$, whose cohomology is
concentrated in degrees~$\{0, 2\}$ with Hilbert series
$P(t) = 1 + t^2$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:virasoro-hochschild}). The universal SC-pair
$U(\cA) = (Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA),\; \cA)$ carries brace
operations
\[
\beta_m \colon Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA) \otimes \cA^{\otimes m}
\;\longrightarrow\; \cA, \qquad m \geq 0,
\]
encoding the closed-sector action on the open sector.
Proposition~\ref{prop:swiss-cheese-nonformality-by-class}
establishes that for class~$M$, the associated
$A_\infty$-operations $m_k^{\mathrm{SC}} \neq 0$ for
\emph{all} $k \geq 3$: the Stasheff cascade
$\sum_{r+s+t = n} m_{r+1+t} \circ
(\mathrm{id}^{\otimes r} \otimes m_s \otimes \mathrm{id}^{\otimes t})
= 0$
forces $m_{k+1} \neq 0$ from $m_3 \neq 0$ and pole depth~$3$
(the triple pole in $T_{(3)}T = c/2$).

\smallskip
\noindent\emph{Construction.}
Fix an integer $r \geq 3$. Define the \emph{depth-$r$ truncated
SC-pair} $(Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA),\; \cA)_{\leq r}$ as follows:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item The closed-sector $\Etwo$-algebra is $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA)$
  itself (unchanged).
\item The open-closed action is
  $\beta_m^{\leq r} := \beta_m$ for $m \leq r$ and
  $\beta_m^{\leq r} := 0$ for $m > r$.
\end{itemize}
The $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-axioms at degree $\leq r$ involve
only the brace operations $\beta_0, \ldots, \beta_r$; they are
inherited from the full pair. Hence
$(Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA), \cA)_{\leq r}$ is a valid SC-pair
in the sense that all coherences at degree $\leq r$ hold.

\smallskip
\noindent\emph{Deficiency.}
This pair does \emph{not} lie in the essential image of the center
functor~$U$. The obstruction is the Stasheff cascade: any chiral
algebra~$\cA'$ whose open sector is quasi-isomorphic
to~$\mathrm{Vir}_c$ satisfies $m_{r+1}^{\mathrm{SC}}(\cA') \neq 0$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:swiss-cheese-nonformality-by-class}, class~$M$),
so the canonical pair $U(\cA') = (Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA'),\; \cA')$
has $\beta_{r+1} \neq 0$. But $(Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA), \cA)_{\leq r}$
has $\beta_{r+1}^{\leq r} = 0$ by construction. No SC-pair
isomorphism can reconcile these: the brace operation
$\beta_{r+1}$ is an invariant of the SC-pair structure.
Hence $(Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA), \cA)_{\leq r}$ is deficient:
a valid SC-pair that arises from no chiral algebra.

\smallskip
\noindent\emph{Two limiting cases.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},nosep]
\item At $r = 2$ (retain only the $\Etwo$-action, discard all higher
  braces): the pair
  $(Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA), \cA)_{\leq 2}$ forgets the entire
  shadow tower. The closed sector acts on the open sector
  only through the cup product and identity.
  This is the maximally deficient case.
\item As $r \to \infty$ the truncation exhausts the tower,
  and the pair converges to the full $U(\cA)$. The deficiency
  vanishes in the inverse limit.
\end{enumerate}

\smallskip
\noindent\emph{Class dependence.}
The construction fails for class~$G$ (Heisenberg): all
$m_k^{\mathrm{SC}} = 0$ for $k \geq 3$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:sc-formal-iff-class-g}), so
$(Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA), \cA)_{\leq r}
= (Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA), \cA)$ for every $r \geq 2$; there is
nothing to truncate. For class~$L$ (affine Kac--Moody),
truncation at $r \geq 3$ produces no deficiency because the tower
terminates at depth~$3$. For class~$C$ ($\beta\gamma$),
truncation at $r \geq 4$ produces no deficiency. Deficient
SC-pairs from shadow truncation exist \emph{only} for class~$M$.
\end{example}

\section{Topologization: from
\texorpdfstring{$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$}{SC} to
\texorpdfstring{$\Ethree$}{E3}}
\label{sec:topologization}
\index{topologization theorem|textbf}
\index{Sugawara construction!topologization}
\index{E3 algebra@$\Ethree$-algebra!from Swiss-cheese + conformal vector}

The chiral center theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}) establishes
that the operadic center of an
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra is an $\Etwo$-algebra,
with the $\Etwo$ structure coming from the closed sector
$\FM_k(\bC)$. The dimensional ladder of
\S\ref{sec:n3-chern-simons} identifies $\Ethree$ with
perturbative Chern--Simons on~$\bR^3$. The gap: how does the
$\Etwo$ center of an $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra
upgrade to~$\Ethree$?

The answer requires extra structure. The
$\Etwo$ center carries $\Etwo$ operations from
$\FM_k(\bC)$: these are \emph{holomorphic}, not topological.
A topological $\Etwo$-algebra has operations from
$\FM_k(\bR^2)$ that are \emph{locally constant} (invariant
under continuous deformations). The holomorphic operations
from $\FM_k(\bC)$ are not locally constant: they depend on
the complex structure of the curve. To promote the
holomorphic $\Etwo$ to topological $\Etwo$, one must show
that the complex-structure dependence is homotopically
trivial.

\begin{definition}[Inner conformal vector]
\label{def:inner-conformal-vector}
\index{conformal vector!inner}
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra and let $Q$ be a
BRST-type differential on the algebra of observables
of a holomorphic--topological theory~$T$ on
$\bC \times \bR$ whose boundary chiral algebra
is~$\cA$. An \emph{inner conformal vector} for
$(\cA, Q)$ is a conformal vector $T(z) \in \cA$ of
central charge~$c$ that is $Q$-exact in the algebra
of bulk observables up to a $Q$-boundary: there exists
$G(z)$ such that $T(z) - [Q, G(z)]$ is $Q$-exact.
Equivalently, $T(z) = [Q, G(z)]$ in BRST cohomology.
For affine Kac--Moody $V_k(\fg)$ at non-critical level,
the Sugawara element
$T_{\mathrm{Sug}} = \frac{1}{2(k + h^\vee)}
\sum_a {:}J^a J_a{:}$
provides the inner conformal vector. For the
Virasoro algebra, $T(z)$ is the stress tensor itself.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cohomological topologization for affine Kac--Moody; {\cite{KhanZeng25}}]
\label{thm:topologization}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
\index{topologization theorem!statement}
Let $\fg$ be a finite-dimensional simple Lie algebra
and let $\cA = V_k(\fg)$ be the universal affine
vertex algebra at non-critical level $k \neq -h^\vee$.
The Sugawara element
$T_{\mathrm{Sug}} = \frac{1}{2(k+h^\vee)}\sum_a {:}J^a J_a{:}$
provides an inner conformal vector
\textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:inner-conformal-vector}}\textup{)}.
The chiral
center $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$, which carries
$\Etwo$ structure from the closed sector of
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}}\textup{)},
upgrades on BRST cohomology to an $\Ethree$-algebra:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:topologization}
H_Q^\bullet\!\bigl(Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)\bigr)
\text{ carries an }
\Etwo^{\mathrm{top}} \otimes \Eone^{\mathrm{top}}
\;=\; \Ethree^{\mathrm{top}}\text{-algebra structure}.
\end{equation}
Here the inner conformal vector makes holomorphic
translations $Q$-exact, hence trivial on BRST cohomology,
and the equality with $\Ethree^{\mathrm{top}}$ is Dunn
additivity. The corresponding chain-level
$\Ethree^{\mathrm{top}}$ enhancement of
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ is not proved here;
see Conjecture~\textup{\ref{conj:topologization-general}}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let
$G(z) = \frac{1}{2(k+h^\vee)}\sum_a {:}J^a(z)\,\bar c_a(z){:}$
be the antighost contraction of
Construction~\ref{constr:sugawara-antighost} below.
The inner conformal vector identifies
$T_{\mathrm{Sug}}$ with $[Q, G]$ in BRST cohomology, and
$G$ generates holomorphic translations: for any bulk
observable $\cO$,
$\partial_z \cO = [T_{\mathrm{Sug}}, \cO]
= [Q, [G, \cO]]$.
Since $\partial_z$ is $Q$-exact, holomorphic translations
act trivially on BRST cohomology: the BRST cohomology of
the factorization algebra on the $\bC$-direction becomes
locally constant. A locally
constant factorization algebra on $\bC \cong \bR^2$
is an $\Etwo^{\mathrm{top}}$-algebra
(Lurie~\cite{HA}, Theorem~5.4.5.9). Combined with the
$\Eone^{\mathrm{top}}$ from the $\bR$-direction (the
open color of $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$), Dunn
additivity gives
$\Etwo^{\mathrm{top}} \otimes \Eone^{\mathrm{top}}
= \Ethree^{\mathrm{top}}$ on
$H_Q^\bullet\!\bigl(Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)\bigr)$.

At critical level $k = -h^\vee$, the Sugawara denominator
$2(k + h^\vee)$ diverges: the Sugawara element is undefined,
$\partial_z$ is not $Q$-exact, and the factorization algebra
retains genuine holomorphic dependence. The $\Etwo$ structure
remains holomorphic, not topological, and the upgrade
to~$\Ethree$ on BRST cohomology fails.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Essential external inputs]
\label{rem:topologization-inputs}
\index{topologization theorem!external inputs}
The proof relies on two external results beyond the
conformal vector hypothesis.
First, the equivalence between locally constant
factorization algebras on $\bR^n$ and $\En$-algebras
is Lurie~\cite{HA}, Theorem~5.4.5.9; this is the step
that converts local constancy of the factorization algebra
on~$\bC$ into an $\Etwo^{\mathrm{top}}$ structure.
Second, the formality of the little disks operad
(Kontsevich~\cite{Kon03}, Tamarkin~\cite{Tamarkin03})
ensures that the $\Etwo^{\mathrm{top}}$ structure produced
by local constancy is equivalent to the one governed by
the cohomological Gerstenhaber operad; without formality,
the chain-level $\Etwo$ and its cohomological shadow could
differ by non-trivial higher operations.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The conformal vector is necessary, not
decorative]
\label{rem:conformal-vector-necessary}
\index{conformal vector!necessity for $\Ethree$}
Without a conformal vector, an
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra produces a center
with $\Etwo^{\mathrm{hol}}$ structure that is genuinely
holomorphic: it detects the complex structure of the base
curve and cannot be deformed to a topological $\Ethree$.
The $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ structure is the
\emph{intermediary} between
$\Eone$-chiral and $\Ethree$; the conformal vector is
the bridge. This resolves the apparent paradox of the
dimensional ladder: the $n = 2$ (chiral) and $n = 3$
(Chern--Simons) rows are connected not by an automatic
operadic promotion, but by the physical data of a stress
tensor.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Scope: which algebras admit topologization]
\label{rem:topologization-scope}
\index{topologization theorem!scope}
Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization} is proved for affine
Kac--Moody $V_k(\fg)$ at non-critical level
$k \neq -h^\vee$, where the Sugawara element provides
an explicit inner conformal vector and the $3$d
holomorphic--topological theory is Chern--Simons.
Topologization produces the corresponding
cohomological $\Ethree$-algebra on BRST cohomology.
The chain-level enhancement remains conjectural.

For general chiral algebras with conformal vector, the
same conclusion is expected but remains conjectural:

Virasoro $\mathrm{Vir}_c$: the algebra carries its own
stress tensor $T(z)$. The holomorphic--topological theory
is $3$d gravity. Topologization requires $T$ to be $Q$-exact
in the $3$d gravity bulk; this is expected at generic
$c \neq 0$ but requires constructing the BRST complex of
$3$d HT gravity and verifying explicitly that
$T - [Q, G]$ is $Q$-exact.
This construction is the subject of
Conjecture~\ref{conj:topologization-general} below.

$\cW$-algebras $\cW_N$: the Virasoro subalgebra provides
the conformal vector. Topologization is expected at generic
level, conditional on the same BRST complex construction
as the Virasoro case (the $3$d HT theory for $\cW_N$
is higher-spin gravity).

Algebras without conformal vector (Heisenberg at $k = 0$,
certain coset constructions, genuinely $\Eone$-chiral
algebras without inner Virasoro): topologization fails.
The center remains $\Etwo^{\mathrm{hol}}$, not $\Ethree$.
\end{remark}

\begin{conjecture}[Chain-level topologization for general chiral algebras]
\label{conj:topologization-general}
\ClaimStatusConjectured
\index{topologization theorem!general conjecture}
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra with conformal vector
$T(z)$ at non-critical level. Then the derived chiral
center $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ itself admits
a chain-level $\Ethree$-topological structure lifting the
cohomological topologization of~\eqref{eq:topologization}.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Evidence and obstructions]
\label{rem:topologization-evidence}
The proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization} for affine
Kac--Moody uses only two inputs: (i)~the existence of an
inner conformal vector in BRST cohomology, and (ii)~the
$Q$-exactness of holomorphic translations on $Q$-cohomology.
Both inputs are cohomological: the argument shows that the
factorization algebra becomes locally constant
\emph{on $Q$-cohomology}, not necessarily at the cochain
level. For affine Kac--Moody, the Sugawara mechanism
provides both inputs explicitly. Accordingly,
Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization} proves only the
cohomological $\Ethree$-structure.

For Virasoro and $\cW$-algebras (class~M in the shadow
classification), the chain-level data is essential:
the shadow tower is infinite, the bar differential
carries genuinely infinite-degree operations, and
chain-level phenomena that vanish on cohomology may
obstruct the cochain-level $\Ethree$ structure. The
$\Ethree$ upgrade may therefore exist only on
$Q$-cohomology (not on cochains) for class~M algebras.
This is consistent with the general pattern that
chain-level BV/bar identifications are proved for
classes~G and~L but remain conjectural for class~M
\textup{(}see the MC5 status in the concordance\textup{)}.

The obstruction to extending
Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization} to
Conjecture~\ref{conj:topologization-general} is
the construction of the $3$d HT BRST complex for the
relevant bulk theory (Chern--Simons for gauge algebras,
$3$d gravity for Virasoro, higher-spin gravity for
$\cW_N$) and the verification that
$T - [Q, G]$ is $Q$-exact therein.
For affine Kac--Moody, the bulk theory is holomorphic
Chern--Simons and the BRST complex is standard. For
$3$d gravity, this is the subject of Volume~II
\textup{(}Three-Dimensional Quantum Gravity\textup{)}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Chain-level vs cohomological topologization]
\label{rem:topologization-chain-vs-cohomology}
The proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization} mixes chain-level
inputs with a cohomological recognition step:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ structure on the pair
  $(C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\;\cA)$ is \emph{chain-level}
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}}\textup{)}.
\item The Gerstenhaber bracket on $C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ is
  \emph{strict} on cochains
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:brace-dg-algebra}}\textup{)};
  for affine $V_k(\fsl_2)$, the bracket restricts on
  $\ChirHoch^1$ to the $\fsl_2$ Lie bracket
  \textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:gerstenhaber-sl2-bracket}}\textup{)}.
\item The Sugawara contraction
  $T = [Q, G]$ in BRST cohomology is proved at the
  \emph{operator} level for $V_k(\fg)$ at non-critical level
  \textup{(}Construction~\textup{\ref{constr:sugawara-antighost}}\textup{)}.
\item The passage $\Etwo^{\mathrm{hol}} \to \Etwo^{\mathrm{top}}$
  \textup{(}local constancy of the factorization algebra\textup{)} is proved on
  \emph{$Q$-cohomology}, not on cochains.
\item Dunn additivity
  $(\Etwo^{\mathrm{top}} \otimes \Eone^{\mathrm{top}} \simeq \Ethree^{\mathrm{top}})$
  is proved at the \emph{$\infty$-categorical} level
  \textup{(}Lurie~\cite{HA}\textup{)}.
\end{enumerate}
The decisive gap is at step~\textup{(iv)}: the locally-constant
recognition requires passing to $Q$-cohomology. Therefore
Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization} proves only the
\emph{cohomological} $\Ethree$-structure on
$H_Q^\bullet(Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA))$.
A chain-level $\Ethree$ refinement would require a cochain-level
replacement for step~\textup{(iv)}, and no such replacement is
proved here. For class~M \textup{(}Virasoro, $\cW$-algebras\textup{)},
where SC non-formality means all $m_k^{\mathrm{SC}}$ are nontrivial,
the chain-level $\Ethree$ may differ from the cohomological
$\Ethree$. Whether such a chain-level lift exists is open.
\end{remark}

\begin{construction}[The Sugawara antighost contraction for affine
Kac--Moody]
\label{constr:sugawara-antighost}%
\index{Sugawara construction!antighost contraction $G(z)$|textbf}%
\index{topologization theorem!explicit $G(z)$}%
\index{antighost contraction|textbf}%
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The proof of Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:topologization}} asserts the
existence of $G(z)$ witnessing the inner conformal vector in BRST
cohomology but does not construct it. We supply the explicit
operator and verify the cohomological identity.

Let $\fg$ be a finite-dimensional simple Lie algebra with basis
$\{t_a\}_{a=1}^{\dim\fg}$, structure constants
$f^a{}_{bc}$ ($[t_b, t_c] = f^a{}_{bc}\,t_a$), Killing form
$\kappa_{ab} = \operatorname{tr}(t_a t_b)$, and dual Coxeter number
$h^\vee$. Fix a non-critical level $k \ne -h^\vee$.

\textsc{BV field space.}
The $3$d holomorphic Chern--Simons theory on
$\bR_t \times \bC_z$ with gauge algebra~$\fg$ at level~$k$
has BV field space
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bv-field-space-hol-cs}
\cE \;=\; \Omega^\bullet(\bR_t)
 \;\widehat{\otimes}\;
 \Omega^{0,\bullet}(\bC_z) \;\otimes\; \fg[1],
\end{equation}
with differential $Q_0 = d_t + \dbar$ and cubic interaction from
the Chern--Simons vertex. In the HT gauge, the physical components
are\textup:
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
 Field & Form type & Ghost number & Role \\
 \hline
 $c^a$ & $\Omega^{0}(\bR)\otimes\Omega^{0,0}(\bC)\otimes\fg$
  & $+1$ & ghost \\
 $A_z^a$ & $\Omega^{0}(\bR)\otimes\Omega^{0,0}(\bC)\otimes\fg$
  & $0$ & gauge field (holomorphic) \\
 $A_{\bar z}^a$ & $\Omega^{0}(\bR)\otimes\Omega^{0,1}(\bC)\otimes\fg$
  & $0$ & Lagrange multiplier / antifield of $A_z$ \\
 $\bar c_a$ & $\Omega^{1}(\bR)\otimes\Omega^{0,0}(\bC)\otimes\fg$
  & $-1$ & antighost / antifield of $c$
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
The BV pairing is
$\langle A_z^a, A_{\bar z}^b\rangle_{\mathrm{BV}}
= \kappa^{ab}$,
$\langle c^a, \bar c_b\rangle_{\mathrm{BV}}
= \delta^a_b$. The BV-BRST differential
acts by
\begin{equation}\label{eq:brst-antighost}
Q \cdot c^a = \tfrac{1}{2}\,f^a{}_{bc}\,c^b c^c,
\qquad
Q \cdot A_z^a = d_t c^a + f^a{}_{bc}\,A_z^b\, c^c,
\qquad
Q \cdot \bar c_a = J_a + f_{ab}{}^c\,\bar c_c\, c^b,
\end{equation}
where $J_a(z) := \kappa_{ab}\,A_z^b(t,z)\big|_{t=0}$ is the
boundary current. The term $f_{ab}{}^c\,\bar c_c\, c^b$ in
$Q \cdot \bar c_a$ is the ghost self-coupling inherited from the
Chern--Simons interaction; its role is to ensure $Q^2 = 0$ on
$\bar c_a$ via the Jacobi identity for~$\fg$.

\textsc{The antighost contraction.}
Define the bulk operator
\begin{equation}\label{eq:antighost-contraction-G}
\boxed{\;
G(z) \;:=\; \frac{1}{2(k + h^\vee)}
 \sum_{a=1}^{\dim\fg}
 {:}J^a(z)\,\bar c_a(z){:}
\;}
\end{equation}
where $J^a = \kappa^{ab}\,J_b$ is the raised-index current and
normal ordering ${:}\cdot{:}$ is the standard bulk BV normal
ordering (point-splitting in the $t$-direction with
$\Theta(t_1 - t_2)$ regularisation).
The operator $G(z)$ has ghost number~$-1$ and conformal weight~$1$
\textup{(}weight~$1$ from $J^a$, weight~$0$ from $\bar c_a$, total
weight~$1$\textup{)}.

\textsc{Verification: $[Q, G(z)] = T_{\mathrm{Sug}}(z)$.}
Expand the $Q$-commutator using the graded Leibniz rule:
\begin{align}
\label{eq:QG-expansion}
[Q,\, G(z)]
&= \frac{1}{2(k+h^\vee)} \sum_a
 \Bigl(
  {:}(Q \cdot J^a)(z)\;\bar c_a(z){:}
  \;+\;
  {:}J^a(z)\;(Q \cdot \bar c_a)(z){:}
 \Bigr).
\end{align}
The second term is the \emph{principal contribution}. By
$Q \cdot \bar c_a = J_a + f_{ab}{}^c\,\bar c_c\, c^b$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:principal-term}
\frac{1}{2(k+h^\vee)} \sum_a {:}J^a(z)\,J_a(z){:}
\;=\; T_{\mathrm{Sug}}(z).
\end{equation}
The two correction terms are:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Ghost current from $Q \cdot J^a$.}
 The BRST transformation $Q \cdot A_z^a = d_t c^a + f^a{}_{bc}
 A_z^b c^c$ restricts on the boundary to
 $Q \cdot J^a(z) = f^a{}_{bc}\,J^b(z)\,c^c(z)$ \textup{(}the
 $d_t c^a$ term involves the normal derivative at $t = 0$, which
 enters the bulk propagator but not the boundary OPE\textup{)}. The
 resulting contribution is
 $\frac{1}{2(k+h^\vee)}\sum_{a,b,c} f^a{}_{bc}\,
 {:}J^b\, c^c\, \bar c_a{:}$.
 This is $Q$-exact in the bulk: it equals
 $[Q, \frac{1}{2(k+h^\vee)} \sum_{a,b,c}
 f^a{}_{bc}\,{:}\bar c_b\, c^c\, \bar c_a{:}]$ up to terms
 proportional to the equations of motion, hence vanishes in
 $Q$-cohomology.
\item \emph{Ghost self-coupling from $Q \cdot \bar c_a$.}
 The term $f_{ab}{}^c\,\bar c_c\, c^b$ in $Q \cdot \bar c_a$
 contributes
 $\frac{1}{2(k+h^\vee)}\sum_a f_{ab}{}^c\,
 {:}J^a\, \bar c_c\, c^b{:}$.
 The sum $f_{ab}{}^c\,J^a\,\bar c_c\, c^b$ has ghost
 number~$-1$ and is $Q$-exact by the same mechanism: its
 $Q$-variation produces a term proportional to
 $f_{ab}{}^c\,f^a{}_{de}\,c^d\,c^e\,\bar c_c\,c^b$, which
 vanishes by the Jacobi identity
 $f_{ab}{}^c\,f^a{}_{de} + \text{cyc}(b,d,e) = 0$.
\end{enumerate}
In summary,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:TQG-result}
[Q,\, G(z)] \;=\; T_{\mathrm{Sug}}(z) \;+\;
 (\text{$Q$-exact operators of ghost number $\ge 1$}).
\end{equation}
On $Q$-cohomology, $[Q, G(z)] = T_{\mathrm{Sug}}(z)$ exactly. This is
the explicit operator-level datum that makes the cohomological
topologization proof
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:topologization}}\textup{)}
constructive.

\textsc{The specialisation to $\fsl_2$.}
For $\fg = \fsl_2$ with standard basis
$\{e, f, h\}$ ($[e,f] = h$, $[h,e] = 2e$, $[h,f] = -2f$)
and trace-form
$\kappa(e,f) = 1$, $\kappa(h,h) = 2$, the Sugawara
element is
$T_{\mathrm{Sug}} = \frac{1}{2(k+2)}\bigl(
{:}J^e J^f{:} + {:}J^f J^e{:} + \tfrac{1}{2}{:}J^h J^h{:}\bigr)$,
and the antighost contraction is
\[
G(z) \;=\; \frac{1}{2(k+2)}\bigl(
{:}J^e(z)\,\bar c_f(z){:}
\;+\; {:}J^f(z)\,\bar c_e(z){:}
\;+\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,{:}J^h(z)\,\bar c_h(z){:}
\bigr),
\]
where $\bar c_e, \bar c_f, \bar c_h$ are the antighost components
with $\langle c^e, \bar c_f\rangle = 1$,
$\langle c^f, \bar c_e\rangle = 1$,
$\langle c^h, \bar c_h\rangle = 2$.
The dual Coxeter number $h^\vee(\fsl_2) = 2$ enters through the
Sugawara denominator $2(k+2)$; at the critical level $k = -2$ the
denominator diverges and the construction breaks down.
\end{construction}

\begin{remark}[Boundary interpretation]
\label{rem:antighost-boundary}%
\index{antighost contraction!boundary interpretation}%
The antighost $\bar c_a(z)$ is a bulk field of the $3$d HT theory
that does not descend to the boundary chiral algebra $V_k(\fg)$.
The operator $G(z)$ therefore lives in the algebra of bulk
observables $\cO_{\mathrm{bulk}}$, not in the boundary. The
relation~\eqref{eq:TQG-result} states that the Sugawara
element, which is a boundary operator, is represented in BRST
cohomology by the commutator of a bulk operator. This is the
physical content of topologization: the
stress tensor of the boundary theory is exact in the bulk, so
$\bC$-translations are homotopically trivial when viewed from the
three-dimensional perspective.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Failure at the critical level and for class~M]
\label{rem:antighost-critical}%
\index{antighost contraction!critical level failure}%
\index{topologization theorem!critical level obstruction}%
Two obstructions prevent the construction from extending beyond
affine Kac--Moody at non-critical level.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Critical level $k = -h^\vee$.}
 The denominator $2(k + h^\vee)$ of~\eqref{eq:antighost-contraction-G}
 diverges. The Sugawara element itself is undefined at the critical
 level: the centre of $V_{-h^\vee}(\fg)$ jumps to the
 Feigin--Frenkel center $\mathfrak{z}(\hat\fg)$, and there is no
 conformal vector to topologize.
\item \emph{Virasoro and $\cW$-algebras \textup{(}class~M\textup{)}.}
 The stress tensor $T(z)$ IS the conformal vector, so
 representing $T$ by $[Q, G]$ in BRST cohomology requires
 $G(z)$ to be a ghost-number~$-1$ operator
 in the $3$d gravity \textup{(}respectively higher-spin
 gravity\textup{)} bulk. This bulk theory and its BRST complex
 have not been constructed at the BV level; the antighost
 contraction for class~M is the content of
 Conjecture~\textup{\ref{conj:topologization-general}}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{remark}

\begin{conjecture}[Coderived $\Ethree$ for class~M; \ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:coderived-e3}%
\index{coderived category!E3 structure@$\Ethree$ structure|textbf}%
\index{topologization theorem!coderived refinement}%
\index{E3 algebra@$\Ethree$ algebra!coderived}%
Let $\cA$ be a chirally Koszul algebra with inner conformal vector
at non-critical level.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Coderived local constancy.)}
  The chiral factorization algebra $\cF_\cA$ on a curve~$X$,

  $m_0$-curvature obstruction to local constancy at
  step~\textup{(iv)} of
  Remark~\textup{\ref{rem:topologization-chain-vs-cohomology}}
  lies in $\operatorname{Im}(d)$ in $D^{\mathrm{co}}$, by the
  same mechanism that absorbs $\delta_4$ in
  Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bv-bar-coderived}}.
\item \textup{(Coderived $\Ethree$.)}
  The locally-constant recognition principle
  \textup{(}Lurie~\cite{HA}\textup{)}, adapted to the

  $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ with an
  $\Etwo^{\mathrm{top}}$-structure in $D^{\mathrm{co}}$.
  Combined with the $\Eone^{\mathrm{top}}$-structure from
  the open color of $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$, Dunn
  additivity gives an $\Ethree^{\mathrm{top}}$-structure in

\item \textup{(Compatibility with cohomological topologization.)}
  In the affine Kac--Moody case, the coderived $\Ethree$

\end{enumerate}

\begin{remark}[Structure of the coderived $\Ethree$ conjecture]
\label{rem:coderived-e3-structure}%
\index{coderived category!E3 structure@$\Ethree$ structure!mechanism}%
The conjecture synthesises three proved results with one new
principle.
The three proved results are:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item The $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ structure on the
  pair $(Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\, \cA)$ is
  chain-level
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}).
\item The coderived BV$=$bar identification absorbs all
  $m_0$-proportional obstructions for every shadow class
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:bv-bar-coderived}).
\item Topologization via inner conformal vector is proved on
  $Q$-cohomology for affine Kac--Moody
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization}).
\end{enumerate}
The new principle is that the coderived category $D^{\mathrm{co}}$
provides the correct categorical framework in which to lift
step~(iv) of
Remark~\ref{rem:topologization-chain-vs-cohomology} from
cohomology to a chain-level statement. The mechanism is uniform
across all four shadow classes: the obstruction to local constancy
of the factorization algebra on the holomorphic direction is a
polynomial in the fiberwise curvature $m_0 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot
\omega_g$; in $D^{\mathrm{co}}$, every positive power of $m_0$
is exact ($m_0^k = d^{2k-1}(d(\cdot)) \in \operatorname{Im}(d)$
for $k \geq 1$), so the obstruction is absorbed.

For class~$\mathsf{M}$ (Virasoro, $\cW_N$), this is the only
available path to $\Ethree$: the chain-level $\Ethree$ does not
exist (the quartic discrepancy $\delta_4 = Q^{\mathrm{contact}}
\cdot m_0$ is not a coboundary in ordinary chain complexes),
and the cohomological $\Ethree$ requires $3$d gravity BRST
(Volume~II). The coderived $\Ethree$ would provide a purely
algebraic $\Ethree$-structure for class~$\mathsf{M}$ without
invoking the bulk gravity theory.

Two obstructions to a proof must be addressed.
First, Lurie's locally-constant recognition
(~\cite{HA}, Theorem~5.4.5.9) is stated for
factorization algebras valued in a stable $\infty$-category;
the coderived category $D^{\mathrm{co}}(\cA)$ is stable
(Positselski), but the factorization-algebra formalism in
$D^{\mathrm{co}}$ has not been developed in the literature.
Second, Dunn additivity
$\Etwo^{\mathrm{top}} \otimes \Eone^{\mathrm{top}} \simeq
\Ethree^{\mathrm{top}}$ is proved for $\En$-algebras in
stable $\infty$-categories; its compatibility with the
coderived tensor product requires verification.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[The $\Ethree$-algebra and Chern--Simons]
\label{thm:e3-cs}
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere
Let $\fg$ be a simple finite-dimensional Lie algebra and
$V_k(\fg)$ the affine Kac--Moody vertex algebra at level~$k$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(The $\Ethree$ structure.)}
  The curve geometry provides the $\Etwo$ structure on
  $\BarSig(V_k(\fg))$. By the Higher Deligne Conjecture
  \textup{(}Lurie~\cite{HA}, Francis~\cite{Francis2013}\textup{)},
  the derived chiral centre
  $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
  = \HH^*(\BarSig(V_k(\fg)), \BarSig(V_k(\fg)))$
  carries a natural $\Ethree$-algebra structure
  \textup{(}$\Etwo + 1 = \Ethree$\textup{)}.
\item \textup{(Deformation classification.)}
  The space of $\Ethree$-deformations is
  non-canonically isomorphic to
  $(k+h^\vee) H^3(\fg)[[k+h^\vee]]$. For simple~$\fg$,
  $H^3(\fg)$ is one-dimensional.
\item \textup{(Comparison with CFG.)}
  The same space classifies perturbative CS quantisations,
  filtered $\Ethree$-algebras deforming $C^*(\fg)$,
  braided monoidal deformations of
  $\mathrm{Rep}_{\mathrm{fin}}(\fg)$, and
  quasi-triangular quasi-Hopf deformations of $U(\fg)$
  \textup{(}Costello--Francis--Gwilliam\textup{)}.
\item \textup{(Topological enhancement.)}
  The Sugawara Virasoro element at non-critical level
  upgrades the holomorphic $\Etwo$ to topological $\Etwo$;
  Dunn additivity gives $\Ethree^{\mathrm{top}}$
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:topologization}}\textup{)}.
  At $k = -h^\vee$, the enhancement fails.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Costello--Francis--Gwilliam~\cite{CFG26}]
\label{thm:cfg}
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere
Let $\fg$ be a simple finite-dimensional Lie algebra.
BV quantisation of Chern--Simons theory on $\RR^3$ with
gauge algebra $\fg$ and coupling $\lambda$ yields
a filtered $\Ethree$-algebra $\cA^\lambda$ on
$C^*(\fg) = \Sym(\fg^\vee[-1])$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item Classical observables: $C^*(\fg)$, commutative.
\item Quantum observables: $\cA^\lambda$, filtered
  $\Ethree$-algebra with deformation space
  $(k+h^\vee) H^3(\fg)[[k+h^\vee]]$.
\item $\mathrm{Mod}_{\cA^\lambda}$ is braided monoidal;
  filtered Koszul duality gives
  $\mathrm{Perf}_{C^*(\fg)} \simeq
  \mathrm{Rep}_{\mathrm{fin}}(\fg)^{\mathrm{dg}}$,
  recovering the Drinfeld--Jimbo quantum group.
\item \textup{(Theorem~1.4 of \cite{CFG26}.)} Canonical
  bijection between perturbative CS quantisations,
  filtered $\Ethree$-deformations, braided monoidal
  deformations, and quasi-triangular quasi-Hopf algebras.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

% The E_3 identification theorem and explicit operations
% ================================================================

The topologization theorem identifies the BRST cohomology of the
derived chiral centre $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))$
as an $\Ethree$-algebra. The Costello--Francis--Gwilliam
construction produces another $\Ethree$-algebra $\cA^\lambda$
from perturbative Chern--Simons quantisation. Both deform
$C^*(\fg)$ over the same base. For simple $\fg$, the
one-dimensionality of $H^3(\fg)$ forces the two
deformation families to be isomorphic.

\begin{theorem}[Identification of $\Ethree$-deformation families]
\label{thm:e3-identification}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\fg$ be a simple finite-dimensional Lie algebra and
$V_k(\fg)$ the affine Kac--Moody vertex algebra at
level~$k$.
Writing $\lambda = k + h^\vee$ for the departure from
critical level, the derived chiral centre
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))$ of
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:e3-structure}} and the
CFG $\Ethree$-algebra $\cA^\lambda$ of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:cfg}} are isomorphic as formal
deformation families of $\Ethree$-algebras:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:e3-families}
  \{Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
  \}_{k + h^\vee \in \lambda\CC[[\lambda]]}
  \;\simeq\; \{\cA^\lambda\}_{\lambda
  \in \lambda H^3(\fg)[[\lambda]]}
\end{equation}
over the base $\lambda H^3(\fg)[[\lambda]]$
\textup{(}with $\lambda = k + h^\vee$\textup{)}.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Matching deformation spaces.)}
  Both families are parametrised by
  $(k+h^\vee) H^3(\fg)[[k+h^\vee]]$:
  for the derived chiral centre, the deformation
  parameter is the departure from critical level;
  for the CFG algebra, it is the Chern--Simons coupling.
\item \textup{(Base point mismatch.)}
  At the critical level $k = -h^\vee$, the derived
  chiral centre $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}
  (V_{-h^\vee}(\fg))$ is related to the
  Feigin--Frenkel centre
  $\mathrm{Fun}(\mathrm{Op}_{\fg^\vee}(D))$
  \textup{(}opers on the formal disk\textup{)}, not
  directly to $C^*(\fg)$. The isomorphism is
  perturbative: it holds in the formal neighbourhood
  of critical level, order by order in
  $\lambda = k + h^\vee$.
\item \textup{(Order-by-order uniqueness.)}
  For simple $\fg$, $H^3(\fg) \cong \CC$ is
  one-dimensional, so the deformation base is
  one-dimensional at each order in $\lambda$. The
  isomorphism~\eqref{eq:e3-families} is forced by
  the matching of associated graded algebras
  \textup{(}both $C^*(\fg)$\textup{)},
  matching $\Pthree$ brackets on the formal disk
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:chiral-e3-cfg}(ii)}%
  \textup{)}, and the one-dimensionality of the
  obstruction space at each order.
\item \textup{(Sugawara constraint.)}
  The topological enhancement
  \textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-cs}(iv)\textup{)} holds
  at generic $k$ but fails at $k = -h^\vee$, where
  the Sugawara element is undefined. The perturbative
  identification holds
  order by order in $(k + h^\vee)$, since the Sugawara
  element exists at any nonzero departure from critical
  level.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The argument is obstruction-theoretic: we construct an
isomorphism of $\Ethree$-algebras order by order in
$\lambda = k + h^\vee$, using the one-dimensionality of
$H^3(\fg)$ for simple~$\fg$ to force uniqueness at each
stage.

\smallskip
\noindent\textit{Step 1: setup.}
Both $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))$ and
$\cA^\lambda$ are filtered $\Ethree$-algebras deforming
$C^*(\fg) = \Sym(\fg^\vee[-1])$ over the base
$\lambda H^3(\fg)[[\lambda]]$, with $\lambda = k + h^\vee$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-cs}(ii) and
Theorem~\ref{thm:cfg}(iv) respectively).
Both filtrations are complete and exhaustive with
associated graded $C^*(\fg)$.
The deformation theory of $\Ethree$-algebras with fixed
associated graded $C^*(\fg)$ is controlled by the
$\Ethree$-deformation complex, whose relevant cohomology
group is $H^3(\fg)$: the space of $\Ethree$-deformations
of $C^*(\fg)$ at each order $p \geq 1$ in $\lambda$,
modulo those at order $< p$, is classified by
$H^3(\fg)$ (Lurie~\cite{HA},
Costello--Francis--Gwilliam~\cite{CFG26},
Theorem~1.4).

\smallskip
\noindent\textit{Step 2: induction on order.}
We construct the isomorphism
$\varphi \colon Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
\xrightarrow{\sim} \cA^\lambda$ as a compatible family of
isomorphisms $\varphi_p$ modulo $\lambda^{p+1}$, by
induction on~$p$.

\emph{Base case ($p = 0$):} both associated graded
algebras are $C^*(\fg)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-e3-cfg}(i)), so
$\varphi_0 = \mathrm{id}_{C^*(\fg)}$.

\emph{Inductive step:} suppose $\varphi_p$ is an
$\Ethree$-isomorphism modulo $\lambda^{p+1}$.
The obstruction to extending $\varphi_p$ to an
isomorphism modulo $\lambda^{p+2}$ lies in the
space of $\Ethree$-deformations of $C^*(\fg)$ at
order $p+1$, which is $H^3(\fg)$. For simple~$\fg$,
$H^3(\fg) = \CC$ is one-dimensional, generated by the
Cartan three-form
$\omega_3(X, Y, Z) = (X, [Y, Z])$.
Two $\Ethree$-deformations of $C^*(\fg)$
that agree modulo $\lambda^{p+1}$ therefore differ at order
$p+1$ by a scalar multiple of $\omega_3$.

It remains to show that both constructions select the
\emph{same} scalar at each order. On the formal disk
$D = \Spec \CC[[z]]$, the restriction functor
$\Gamma(D, -)$ is symmetric monoidal and preserves
$\Ethree$-structures
(Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-e3-cfg}, Step~1 of the
proof). By Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-e3-cfg}(ii), the
chiral $\Pthree$ bracket restricted to~$D$ recovers
the CFG $\Pthree$ bracket at every order. Since the
$\Pthree$ bracket determines the $\Ethree$-deformation
class (the bracket on generators
$\fg^*[-1] \otimes \fg^*[-1] \to \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k)$
encodes the full deformation datum for biderivations, by
the argument of
Lemma~\ref{lem:bv-p3-commutativity}, Step~2), and the
space of $\fg$-equivariant corrections at each order is
one-dimensional (spanned by the Killing-form term
$k\,(a,b)\,\partial$), the two constructions agree at
order $p+1$. The isomorphism $\varphi_{p+1}$ extends
$\varphi_p$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textit{Step 3: passage to the limit.}
The compatible family $\{\varphi_p\}_{p \geq 0}$
determines an isomorphism
$\varphi = \varprojlim_p\,\varphi_p$
of filtered $\Ethree$-algebras over
$\lambda\,H^3(\fg)[[\lambda]]$, completing the proof
of~\eqref{eq:e3-families}.
Part~(iv) is immediate: the topological enhancement
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-cs}(iv)) requires the Sugawara
element, which exists for $\lambda \neq 0$ and is
compatible with the isomorphism~$\varphi$ order by order
(the Sugawara element at order~$p$ depends only on the
data modulo~$\lambda^{p+1}$, where the two families
already agree).
\end{proof}

\subsection{Explicit $\Ethree$ operations for
$V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$}
\label{subsec:e3-explicit}

The abstract $\Ethree$ structure of
Proposition~\ref{prop:e3-structure} acquires concrete content
for $\fg = \mathfrak{sl}_2$: the five-dimensional derived
chiral centre $\CC \oplus \mathfrak{sl}_2[-1] \oplus \CC[-2]$
carries an explicit cup product, Gerstenhaber bracket,
$\Pthree$ bracket, and BV operator, all computable from the
OPE.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{The underlying graded vector space.}
By Proposition~\ref{prop:e3-structure} and the chiral
Hochschild computation of
Example~\ref{ex:km}, the derived chiral centre of
$V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ at generic level
$k \neq -2$ is the graded vector space
\begin{equation}\label{eq:e3-graded-space}
  Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2))
  \;=\;
  \underbrace{\CC}_{=\, \HH^0}
  \;\oplus\;
  \underbrace{\mathfrak{sl}_2[-1]}_{=\, \HH^1}
  \;\oplus\;
  \underbrace{\CC[-2]}_{=\, \HH^2},
\end{equation}
with total dimension $1 + 3 + 1 = 5$. Write $\mathbf{1}$
for the generator of $\HH^0 = \CC$ (the vacuum),
$\{e, f, h\}$ for the standard basis of
$\HH^1 = \mathfrak{sl}_2$
(outer derivations: infinitesimal level-preserving
deformations parametrised by $\fg$ itself), and $\eta$
for the generator of $\HH^2 = \CC$
(the central charge deformation cocycle). The
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$-module structure is: $\HH^0$ trivial,
$\HH^1$ adjoint, $\HH^2$ trivial.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Conventions.}
The Killing form normalisation is
$(e, f) = (f, e) = 1$, $(h, h) = 2$, all other pairings zero.
Structure constants: $[e, f] = h$, $[h, e] = 2e$,
$[h, f] = -2f$. The inverse Casimir is
$\Omega = e \otimes f + f \otimes e
+ \tfrac{1}{2}\,h \otimes h$, and $h^\vee = 2$.
The KZ coupling constant is
$h_{\mathrm{KZ}} = 1/(k + h^\vee) = 1/(k + 2)$.

\begin{remark}[Convention for $\hbar$]
\label{rem:hbar-convention}
Throughout this paper, $\hbar$ denotes the
\emph{KZ coupling constant}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:hbar-convention}
  \hbar \;=\; h_{\mathrm{KZ}}
  \;=\; \frac{1}{k + h^\vee},
\end{equation}
the normalisation in the KZ connection
$\nabla = d + \hbar \sum \Omega_{ij}\,dz_{ij}$.
It vanishes as $k \to \infty$ (weak-coupling limit) and
diverges at the critical level $k = -h^\vee$.
In the $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ computations, $h^\vee = 2$,
so $\hbar = 1/(k+2)$.
The \emph{departure from critical level} is the inverse
quantity $k + h^\vee = 1/\hbar$; we always write this
explicitly as $k + h^\vee$ rather than introducing a
separate symbol. Critical level corresponds to
$k + h^\vee = 0$ (equivalently, $\hbar \to \infty$);
weak coupling corresponds to $k + h^\vee \to \infty$
(equivalently, $\hbar \to 0$).
For the quantum group parameter: $q = e^{2\pi i \hbar}$.
\end{remark}

An $\Ethree$-algebra carries three levels of
operations:
\begin{itemize}
\item Level $0$ ($\Einf$): a graded-commutative product
  $\mu\colon \HH^p \otimes \HH^q \to \HH^{p+q}$.
\item Level $1$ ($\Etwo$ beyond $\Eone$): the
  Gerstenhaber bracket
  $[-,-]\colon \HH^p \otimes \HH^q \to \HH^{p+q-1}$
  of degree~$-1$, derived from the fundamental class of
  $S^1 = \Conf_2(\RR^2)/\RR^+$.
\item Level $2$ ($\Ethree$ beyond $\Etwo$): the $\Pthree$
  bracket
  $\{-,-\}\colon \HH^p \otimes \HH^q \to \HH^{p+q-2}$
  of degree~$-2$, derived from the fundamental class of
  $S^2 = \Conf_2(\RR^3)/\RR^+$.
\end{itemize}
We compute each in turn.

\begin{proposition}[Explicit $\Ethree$ operations on
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2))$]
\label{prop:e3-explicit-sl2}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $k \neq -2$.
The $\Ethree$-algebra structure on
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2))$
is determined by the following data.

\smallskip
\noindent
\textup{(I) Cup product} ($\Einf$ level).
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item $\mathbf{1}$ is the unit:
  $\mathbf{1} \cdot f = f \cdot \mathbf{1} = f$ for all $f$.
\item
  $\mu(X, Y) = 0$ for all $X, Y \in \HH^1 = \mathfrak{sl}_2$.
\item
  $\mu(X, \eta) = 0$ for all $X \in \HH^1$, and
  $\eta \cdot \eta = 0$.
\end{enumerate}

\smallskip
\noindent
\textup{(II) Gerstenhaber bracket} ($\Etwo$ level,
degree~$-1$).
\begin{gather*}
  [\mathbf{1}, -] = 0, \qquad
  [X, Y] = 0 \quad \text{for all }
  X, Y \in \HH^1 = \mathfrak{sl}_2, \\
  [X, \eta] = 0 \quad \text{for all } X \in \HH^1,
  \qquad [\eta, \eta] = 0.
\end{gather*}

\smallskip
\noindent
\textup{(III) The $\Pthree$ bracket} ($\Ethree$ level,
degree~$-2$).
\begin{align}
  \label{eq:e3-p3-killing}
  \{X, Y\}
  &= h_{\mathrm{KZ}} \cdot (X, Y)
  = \frac{1}{k + h^\vee}\,(X, Y)
  \quad \text{for } X, Y \in \HH^1,
  \\
  \label{eq:e3-p3-derivation}
  \{X, \eta\}
  &= 0
  \quad \text{for } X \in \HH^1,
  \\
  \label{eq:e3-p3-unit}
  \{\mathbf{1}, -\}
  &= 0,
  \\
  \label{eq:e3-p3-top}
  \{\eta, \eta\}
  &= 0,
\end{align}
where $(X, Y)$ denotes the normalised Killing form
and $h_{\mathrm{KZ}} = 1/(k + h^\vee) = 1/(k + 2)$ is
the KZ coupling constant
\textup{(}Remark~\textup{\ref{rem:hbar-convention}}\textup{)}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
We establish each level by $\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariance,
degree counting, and the BV relation.

\textit{Cup product.}
The cup product $\mu\colon \HH^p \otimes \HH^q
\to \HH^{p+q}$ is graded-commutative:
$\mu(a, b) = (-1)^{pq}\,\mu(b, a)$.
For $X, Y \in \HH^1$, this gives
$\mu(X, Y) = -\mu(Y, X)$: the restriction to
$\HH^1 \otimes \HH^1 \to \HH^2 = \CC$ is an
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$-invariant antisymmetric bilinear form
on the adjoint representation valued in the trivial
representation.
Since
$\Lambda^2(\mathfrak{sl}_2)^{\mathfrak{sl}_2}
\cong \Hom_{\mathfrak{sl}_2}(\Lambda^2(\mathrm{ad}), \CC)
= 0$
(the exterior square of the adjoint decomposes as
$\Lambda^2(\mathrm{ad}) \cong \mathrm{ad}$,
which has no trivial summand), the cup product
$\mu(X, Y) = 0$ for all $X, Y \in \HH^1$.

The products $\mu(\HH^1, \HH^2) \subset \HH^3 = 0$ and
$\mu(\HH^2, \HH^2) \subset \HH^4 = 0$ vanish for degree
reasons.

\textit{BV operator.}
The framed $\Etwo$ structure on $\BarSig(V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2))$
(from genus-$1$ sewing) equips the derived centre with a BV
operator $\Delta\colon \HH^n \to \HH^{n-1}$ satisfying
$\Delta^2 = 0$ and the BV relation
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bv-rel-sl2}
  [f, g]
  = \Delta(f \cdot g) - (\Delta f) \cdot g
  - (-1)^{|f|}\,f \cdot (\Delta g).
\end{equation}

The component $\Delta\colon \HH^1 \to \HH^0$ is an
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariant linear map from the adjoint
representation to the trivial representation. Since the
adjoint is irreducible and non-trivial,
$\Delta|_{\HH^1} = 0$.

The component $\Delta\colon \HH^2 \to \HH^1$ is an
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariant linear map from the trivial
to the adjoint. Again, $\Delta|_{\HH^2} = 0$.

All other components vanish trivially
($\Delta|_{\HH^0}$ lands in $\HH^{-1} = 0$;
$\Delta|_{\HH^n} = 0$ for $n \geq 3$). Therefore
$\Delta = 0$ on the entire derived centre.

\textit{Gerstenhaber bracket.}
With $\Delta = 0$ and $\mu|_{\HH^1 \otimes \HH^1} = 0$,
the BV relation~\eqref{eq:bv-rel-sl2} gives:
for $X, Y \in \HH^1$,
\[
  [X, Y]
  = \Delta(\mu(X, Y)) - (\Delta X) \cdot Y
  + X \cdot (\Delta Y)
  = \Delta(0) - 0 + 0 = 0.
\]
For $X \in \HH^1$, $\eta \in \HH^2$:
$[X, \eta] = \Delta(\mu(X, \eta)) - (\Delta X) \cdot \eta
- (-1)^1\,X \cdot (\Delta \eta)
= 0 - 0 + 0 = 0$
(since $\mu(X, \eta) \in \HH^3 = 0$,
$\Delta X = 0$, $\Delta\eta = 0$).
Similarly $[\eta, \eta] \in \HH^3 = 0$.

The vanishing of the Gerstenhaber bracket on the derived
centre is a structural consequence of the
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariance constraints: the adjoint
representation provides no equivariant maps to or from the
trivial representation. This is specific to the
\emph{derived centre} $\HH^*(\BarSig, \BarSig)$; the
Gerstenhaber bracket on the full Hochschild cochain complex
$C^*(\BarSig, \BarSig)$ is non-trivial (it carries the
commutator of derivations at the cochain level).

\textit{$\Pthree$ bracket.}
The degree-$(-2)$ bracket
$\{-,-\}\colon \HH^p \otimes \HH^q \to \HH^{p+q-2}$
satisfies the graded symmetry
\begin{equation}\label{eq:p3-symmetry}
  \{a, b\}
  = -(-1)^{(|a|-2)(|b|-2)}\,\{b, a\}
\end{equation}
of a degree-$(-2)$ Lie bracket, together with the Leibniz rule
$\{a, b \cdot c\}
= \{a, b\} \cdot c + (-1)^{(|a|-2)|b|}\,b \cdot \{a, c\}$.

\textit{Step 1: $\{\mathbf{1}, -\} = 0$.}
The Leibniz rule applied to
$\mathbf{1} = \mathbf{1} \cdot \mathbf{1}$ gives
$\{\mathbf{1}, f\} = 2\,\{\mathbf{1}, f\}$
for all $f$; hence $\{\mathbf{1}, f\} = 0$.

\textit{Step 2: $\{X, Y\} = h_{\mathrm{KZ}}\,(X, Y)$.}
For $X, Y \in \HH^1$,
$\{X, Y\} \in \HH^0 = \CC$.
The symmetry~\eqref{eq:p3-symmetry} at
$|X| = |Y| = 1$ gives
$\{X, Y\} = -(-1)^{(-1)(-1)}\,\{Y, X\}
= -(-1)^1\,\{Y, X\}
= \{Y, X\}$:
the bracket is \emph{symmetric} on $\HH^1 \otimes \HH^1$.
By $\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariance, $\{X, Y\}$ is an
invariant symmetric bilinear form on the adjoint
representation valued in $\CC$. The space of such forms is
one-dimensional, spanned by the Killing form $(X, Y)$.
Hence $\{X, Y\} = \alpha \cdot (X, Y)$ for a scalar
$\alpha$ depending on $k$.

The scalar $\alpha$ is determined by the $r$-matrix
residue, independently of any comparison with the CFG
construction. The $\Pthree$ bracket on the derived
chiral centre $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))
= \HH^*(\BarSig(V_k(\fg)), \BarSig(V_k(\fg)))$ is the
$\Etwo$-Hochschild cohomology operation derived from
the fundamental class of $S^2 \subset \Conf_2(\RR^3)$.
At degree~$2$, this operation is the residue of the
classical $r$-matrix paired with the fundamental class
of $S^1 \subset \Conf_2(\CC)$. In the KZ normalisation
(see~\eqref{eq:kz-sl2-degree2} and the conventions
of~\S\ref{subsec:sl2-chiral-e3}):
% AP126/AP148: KZ convention r(z) = Omega/((k+h^v)z).
% At k=0, r = Omega/(h^v z) != 0 (non-abelian; correct for KZ).
% At k=-h^v, r diverges (Sugawara singularity).
\begin{equation}\label{eq:p3-from-r-matrix-residue}
  r(z) = \frac{\Omega}{(k + h^\vee)\,z},
  \qquad
  \operatorname{Res}_{z=0}\bigl[r(z)\bigr]
  = \frac{\Omega}{k + h^\vee}.
\end{equation}
The residue acts on $X \otimes Y \in \fg \otimes \fg$
by contraction with the inverse Casimir:
$\Omega(X \otimes Y) = (X, Y)$ (the normalised Killing
form). The $\Pthree$ bracket on $\HH^1 \cong \fg$ is
therefore
\begin{equation}\label{eq:p3-bracket-normalisation}
  \{X, Y\}
  = \operatorname{Res}_{z=0}
  \bigl[r(z)(X \otimes Y)\bigr]
  = \frac{(X, Y)}{k + h^\vee}
  = h_{\mathrm{KZ}} \cdot (X, Y),
\end{equation}
giving $\alpha = h_{\mathrm{KZ}} = 1/(k + h^\vee)$.
For $\mathfrak{sl}_2$, $h^\vee = 2$ and
$\alpha = 1/(k + 2)$.

Verification: at $k \to \infty$,
$h_{\mathrm{KZ}} \to 0$ and the bracket vanishes (the
weak-coupling limit is the abelian $\Ethree$). At the
critical level $k = -h^\vee$, $h_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ diverges
(the bracket degenerates, reflecting the Feigin--Frenkel
singularity). Both limits are consistent with
the behaviour of the KZ connection.

\textit{Step 3: $\{X, \eta\} = 0$.}
The bracket $\{X, \eta\} \in \HH^1 = \mathfrak{sl}_2$
for $X \in \HH^1$, $\eta \in \HH^2$ is an
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariant map
$\mathrm{ad} \otimes \CC \to \mathrm{ad}$,
hence $\{X, \eta\} = \beta\,X$ for some scalar $\beta$.
The $\Pthree$ Jacobi identity applied to
$X, Y \in \HH^1$ and $\eta \in \HH^2$,
combined with
$\{X, Y\} = h_{\mathrm{KZ}}\,(X, Y) \in \HH^0$, gives
$\{\{X, \eta\}, \eta\} = 0$ (since
$\{\{X, \eta\}, \eta\} \in \HH^{-1} = 0$) and
$\{\eta, \{X, \eta\}\} \in \HH^{-1} = 0$,
while $\{X, \{\eta, \eta\}\} = \{X, 0\} = 0$.
The Jacobi identity is thus satisfied for all $\beta$;
the constraint comes instead from the self-consistency
of the $\Ethree$ deformation.
At the cohomological level, one applies the iterated Jacobi
identity to $X, Y \in \HH^1$ and $\eta$:
\begin{align*}
  \{\{X, Y\}, \eta\}
  &= \{X, \{Y, \eta\}\}
  - (-1)^{(|X|-2)(|Y|-2)}\,\{Y, \{X, \eta\}\} \\
  &= \{X, \beta Y\} - (-1)^1\,\{Y, \beta X\} \\
  &= \beta\,h_{\mathrm{KZ}}\,(X, Y)
  + \beta\,h_{\mathrm{KZ}}\,(Y, X) \\
  &= 2\beta\,h_{\mathrm{KZ}}\,(X, Y).
\end{align*}
The left side:
$\{\{X, Y\}, \eta\}
= \{h_{\mathrm{KZ}}(X, Y)\,\mathbf{1}, \eta\}
= h_{\mathrm{KZ}}(X, Y)\,\{\mathbf{1}, \eta\} = 0$.
Hence $2\beta\,h_{\mathrm{KZ}}\,(X, Y) = 0$ for all
$X, Y$. Since
$(e, f) = 1 \neq 0$ and $h_{\mathrm{KZ}} \neq 0$ at
generic $k$, we conclude $\beta = 0$.

\textit{Step 4: $\{\eta, \eta\} = 0$.}
The bracket $\{\eta, \eta\} \in \HH^2 = \CC$ and
the symmetry~\eqref{eq:p3-symmetry} at
$|\eta| = 2$ gives
$\{\eta, \eta\} = -(-1)^{0 \cdot 0}\,\{\eta, \eta\}
= -\{\eta, \eta\}$, hence $\{\eta, \eta\} = 0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The Killing form as the $\Ethree$ shadow
of the Cartan $3$-form]
\label{rem:cartan-3-form}
The bracket~\eqref{eq:e3-p3-killing} has a transparent
topological origin. The $\Ethree$-deformation space is
$(k+h^\vee)\,H^3(\mathfrak{sl}_2)[[k+h^\vee]] \cong (k+h^\vee)\,\CC[[k+h^\vee]]$,
one-dimensional at each order in $(k+h^\vee)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:cfg}(iv)). The generator of
$H^3(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ is the Cartan $3$-form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cartan-3form}
  \omega_3(X, Y, Z)
  = (X, [Y, Z]),
\end{equation}
the unique (up to scalar) $\mathfrak{sl}_2$-invariant element
of $\Lambda^3(\mathfrak{sl}_2^*)$. The degree-$(-2)$ bracket
$\{X, Y\}$ is the $\Ethree$-operation induced by this
deformation class scaled by
$\hbar = 1/(k + h^\vee)$:
\[
  \{X, Y\}
  = \hbar \cdot (X, Y).
\]
The mechanism is \emph{not} literal contraction of the
antisymmetric $3$-form $\omega_3$: the $\Pthree$ bracket
is symmetric on $\HH^1$ (by the graded
symmetry~\eqref{eq:p3-symmetry} at $|X| = |Y| = 1$),
while any contraction of $\omega_3$ in two slots is
antisymmetric. The connection is indirect:
the one-dimensional deformation space
$H^3(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$, generated by $\omega_3$,
parametrises $\Ethree$-deformations of $C^*(\fg)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:cfg}(iv));
on cohomology, $\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariance forces
the induced $\Pthree$ bracket on
$\HH^1 \cong \mathfrak{sl}_2$ to be proportional to
the Killing form $(X, Y)$, the unique invariant
symmetric bilinear form. The coefficient
$h_{\mathrm{KZ}} = 1/(k + h^\vee)$ is fixed by the
$r$-matrix residue
(equation~\eqref{eq:p3-from-r-matrix-residue}), not by
comparison with CFG.
The $\Pthree$ bracket on $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}$
is the binary shadow of the ternary Cartan deformation class,
with the Killing form appearing through equivariance rather
than through direct contraction.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Quantum corrections from the vertex $R$-matrix:
$\Ethree$ operations for the EK quantum VOA}
\label{subsec:e3-ek-quantum}

The classical $V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ has trivial braiding
$S(z) = \id$; the Etingof--Kazhdan quantum VOA
$V_{\mathrm{EK}}$ of Example~\ref{ex:ek-qvoa} has
$S(z) = \id + h_{\mathrm{KZ}}\,\Omega/z + O(z^{-2})$. Does
the non-trivial $R$-matrix change the $\Ethree$-algebra
structure on the derived chiral centre? On cohomology, no:
the $\Pthree$ bracket coincides with the classical answer
(Proposition~\ref{prop:e3-explicit-sl2}). On cochains, yes:
the quantum corrections encode the braided monoidal structure
of $\mathrm{Rep}(Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{sl}_2))$.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{The underlying graded vector space.}
The EK quantum VOA is a flat formal deformation of
$V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ over $\CC[[h_{\mathrm{KZ}}]]$, with
$h_{\mathrm{KZ}} = 1/(k+2)$.
Since the chiral Hochschild cohomology is computed by
a bounded complex of finite-dimensional
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$-modules, the deformation does not
change the underlying graded vector space:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ek-e3-graded}
  Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_{\mathrm{EK}})
  \;=\;
  \CC[[h_{\mathrm{KZ}}]]
  \;\oplus\;
  \mathfrak{sl}_2[[h_{\mathrm{KZ}}]][-1]
  \;\oplus\;
  \CC[[h_{\mathrm{KZ}}]][-2],
\end{equation}
with total rank $5$ over $\CC[[h_{\mathrm{KZ}}]]$.
The generators
$\mathbf{1} \in \HH^0$, $\{e, f, h\} \in \HH^1$, and
$\eta \in \HH^2$ are defined as in
Proposition~\ref{prop:e3-explicit-sl2}, now taken
over the base ring $\CC[[h_{\mathrm{KZ}}]]$.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Equivariance under the quantum $R$-matrix.}
The Yang $R$-matrix $R(u) = u\,I + \hbar\,P$ on
$V \otimes V$ is $\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariant:
$[R(u),\, \rho(X) \otimes I + I \otimes \rho(X)] = 0$
for all $X \in \mathfrak{sl}_2$.
The full vertex $R$-matrix
$S(z)$ on $V_{\mathrm{EK}}^{\otimes 2}$
(equation~\eqref{eq:ek-vertex-rmatrix}) is likewise
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariant, since it is constructed from
$\mathcal{R}(z)$, the spectral universal $R$-matrix of
the Yangian $Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$, which
commutes with the $\mathfrak{sl}_2$-action by the
defining property of the Drinfeld coproduct.

This equivariance is the key structural constraint: all
three levels of the $\Ethree$ operations on
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_{\mathrm{EK}})$ are
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariant maps between representations.
The equivariance arguments of
Proposition~\ref{prop:e3-explicit-sl2} therefore apply
verbatim to the quantum case.

\begin{proposition}[{$\Ethree$ operations on
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_{\mathrm{EK}})$}]
\label{prop:e3-ek-quantum}
Let $V_{\mathrm{EK}}$ be the Etingof--Kazhdan quantum
vertex algebra for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ at KZ coupling
$h_{\mathrm{KZ}} = 1/(k+2) \neq 0$
\textup{(}Example~\textup{\ref{ex:ek-qvoa}}\textup{)}.
The $\Ethree$-algebra structure on the derived chiral
centre $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_{\mathrm{EK}})$
coincides with the classical $\Ethree$ structure of
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:e3-explicit-sl2}}: the
cup product and Gerstenhaber bracket vanish, and the
$\Pthree$ bracket is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ek-p3-bracket}
  \{X, Y\}_q \;=\; h_{\mathrm{KZ}} \cdot (X, Y)
  \qquad
  \textup{for } X, Y \in \HH^1 = \mathfrak{sl}_2,
\end{equation}
with all other brackets zero. In particular, the
vertex $R$-matrix $S(z)$ does not renormalise the
$\Pthree$ bracket on cohomology.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The argument has four ingredients: equivariance,
one-dimensionality of the obstruction space,
exactness of the rational classical $r$-matrix, and a
$\GRT_1$ consistency check.

\textit{Step 1: cup product and BV operator.}
The cup product $\mu \colon \HH^p \otimes \HH^q
\to \HH^{p+q}$ is a graded-commutative product on
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$-modules over $\CC[[h_{\mathrm{KZ}}]]$.
For $X, Y \in \HH^1
= \mathfrak{sl}_2[[h_{\mathrm{KZ}}]]$,
the product $\mu(X, Y) \in \HH^2
= \CC[[h_{\mathrm{KZ}}]]$
is an $\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariant antisymmetric
bilinear form on the adjoint representation:
$\Lambda^2(\mathrm{ad})^{\mathfrak{sl}_2} = 0$,
so $\mu(X, Y) = 0$ over $\CC[[h_{\mathrm{KZ}}]]$.

The BV operator $\Delta \colon \HH^n \to \HH^{n-1}$
is $\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariant. The components
$\Delta|_{\HH^1} \colon \mathrm{ad} \to \CC$ and
$\Delta|_{\HH^2} \colon \CC \to \mathrm{ad}$ vanish
by Schur's lemma. Hence $\Delta = 0$ on the derived
centre, and the BV relation gives
$[X, Y] = 0$ for all $X, Y \in \HH^1$, just as in the
classical case.

\textit{Step 2: the $\Pthree$ bracket is proportional
to the Killing form.}
For $X, Y \in \HH^1$, the bracket
$\{X, Y\}_q \in \HH^0 = \CC[[h_{\mathrm{KZ}}]]$ is an
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$-equivariant symmetric bilinear
form on the adjoint representation:
$\Sym^2(\mathrm{ad})^{\mathfrak{sl}_2} \cong \CC$.

(the notation $\kappa$ here denotes the Koszul invariant,
not the Killing form).
At critical level $k = -h^\vee$ (i.e.\ $k + h^\vee = 0$), the
curvature vanishes and
$\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_{-h^\vee}) = (\Sym^c(\fg^*[-1]) \otimes
\omega_X,\, d_{\CE})$
is an uncurved commutative ($\Einf$) factorisation
coalgebra on~$X$.
\end{definition}


\begin{definition}[The chiral Chevalley--Eilenberg complex]
\label{def:chiral-ce-complex}
Let $\fg$ be a simple Lie algebra with Killing form
$(\cdot, \cdot)$ and dual Coxeter number~$h^\vee$.
The \emph{chiral Chevalley--Eilenberg complex} is
the curved chiral coalgebra
\begin{equation}\label{eq:chiral-ce-complex}
  \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k)
  \;:=\;
  \bigl(\Sym^c(\fg^*[-1]) \otimes \omega_X,\;
  d_{\CE},\; m_0 = \tfrac{k + h^\vee}{2h^\vee}\,\kappa\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $\omega_X$ denotes the dualising sheaf of~$X$,
$d_{\CE}$ is the chiral Chevalley--Eilenberg differential
encoding the Lie bracket of~$\fg$, and the curvature
$m_0$ is the image of the level under the bar construction
(the notation $\kappa$ here denotes the Koszul invariant,
not the Killing form).
At critical level $k = -h^\vee$ (i.e.\ $k + h^\vee = 0$), the
curvature vanishes and
$\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_{-h^\vee}) = (\Sym^c(\fg^*[-1]) \otimes
\omega_X,\, d_{\CE})$
is an uncurved commutative ($\Einf$) factorisation
coalgebra on~$X$.
\end{definition}

\begin{construction}[The chiral $\Pthree$ bracket]
\begin{construction}[The chiral $\Pthree$ bracket]
\label{constr:chiral-p3-bracket}
The PVA $\lambda$-bracket on $V_k(\fg)$ is
(see \cite{Lorgat26I}, \S5.11):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pva-bracket-recall}
  \{J^a \mathbin{{}_\lambda} J^b\}
  \;=\;
  f^{ab}{}_c\, J^c \;+\; k\,(a,b)\,\lambda,
\end{equation}
where $f^{ab}{}_c$ are the structure constants of $\fg$ and
$(a,b)$ is the Killing form.

At the level of the chiral Chevalley--Eilenberg complex, the
$\lambda$-bracket induces a degree-$(-2)$ bracket
\begin{equation}\label{eq:chiral-p3-def}
  \{-,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  \colon
  \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k)
  \otimes
  \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k)
  \;\longrightarrow\;
  \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k),
\end{equation}
defined on generators $\phi_a = (J^a)^* \in \fg^*[-1]$ by
the two components of the PVA bracket:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Lie bracket term.)}
  The structure constant term $f^{ab}{}_c\,J^c$ contributes
  the Chevalley--Eilenberg differential $d_{\CE}$ at the
  algebra level. On the dual (coalgebra) side, it induces a
  coderivation.
  This is the classical $\Pthree$ bracket of the
  uncurved complex $\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_0)$: the degree-$(-2)$
  Lie bracket
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:p3-lie-component}
    \{\phi_a, \phi_b\}^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\mathrm{Lie}}
    \;=\;
    f^{ab}{}_c\,\phi_c.
  \end{equation}
  This bracket is the coinvariant shadow of the ordered bar
  $R$-matrix at the critical level.
\item \textup{(Cocycle term.)}
  The level-dependent term $k\,(a,b)\,\lambda$ contributes a
  degree-$(-2)$ symmetric pairing valued in $\cO_X$:
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:p3-cocycle-component}
    \{\phi_a, \phi_b\}^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\mathrm{cocycle}}
    \;=\;
    k\,(a,b) \cdot \partial,
  \end{equation}
  where $\partial$ denotes the $\cD$-module action
  (the spectral parameter~$\lambda$ of the PVA bracket
  becomes the derivation $\partial_z$ in the $\cD$-module
  structure). This term is the cocycle that controls
  the deformation away from the commutative
  ($\Einf$) factorisation structure.
\end{enumerate}

The total chiral $\Pthree$ bracket is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:chiral-p3-total}
  \{\phi_a, \phi_b\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  \;=\;
  f^{ab}{}_c\,\phi_c
  \;+\;
  k\,(a,b)\,\partial.
\end{equation}
\end{construction}

\begin{proposition}[The chiral $\Pthree$ structure]
\label{prop:chiral-p3-structure}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The bracket~\eqref{eq:chiral-p3-total} equips the chiral
Chevalley--Eilenberg complex $\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k)$
with the structure of a $\Pthree$-algebra object in
$\cD\textrm{-}\mathrm{mod}(X)$. Concretely:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The bracket is a degree-$(-2)$ Lie bracket satisfying
  graded antisymmetry and the Jacobi identity.
  The Jacobi identity follows from the Jacobi identity of the
  PVA $\lambda$-bracket, which in turn encodes the Jacobi
  identity of the Lie algebra $\fg$ together with the
  $\fg$-invariance of the Killing form.
\item The bracket is compatible with the commutative product
  on $\Sym^c(\fg^*[-1])$ via the Leibniz rule:
  \[
    \{a, b \cdot c\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
    = \{a, b\}^{\mathrm{ch}} \cdot c
    + (-1)^{(|a|-2)|b|}\, b \cdot \{a, c\}^{\mathrm{ch}}.
  \]
\item The bracket is $\cD$-linear in the sense that it
  respects the flat connection on
  $\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k)$ inherited from the
  $\cD$-module structure: the chiral $\Pthree$ bracket
  is a morphism of $\cD$-modules, not merely of
  $\cO_X$-modules.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The PVA $\lambda$-bracket on $V_k(\fg)$ satisfies the PVA
axioms (sesquilinearity, Leibniz, skew-symmetry, Jacobi);
these are the $\lambda$-bracket translations of the classical
master equation for the Poisson structure.
The identification of the $\lambda$-bracket with a
degree-$(-2)$ Lie bracket on the shifted dual follows the
general correspondence between PVA brackets and shifted
Poisson structures (Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite{BD04},
\S3.8): a PVA $\lambda$-bracket of conformal weight $1$
(where $\lambda$ has weight $1$) corresponds to a
$\Pthree$ bracket (degree $-2$ = $-\dim_\CC X - 1$, where
$\dim_\CC X = 1$) on the $\cD$-module of chiral cochains.

The $\cD$-linearity follows because the PVA
$\lambda$-bracket is a $\cD$-module morphism by construction:
the derivation $\partial$ acts via $\lambda \mapsto \lambda$
in the $\lambda$-bracket formalism, and this action is
compatible with the Leibniz rule by the sesquilinearity
axiom of the PVA.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The filtered $\Ethree$-chiral algebra}
\label{subsec:filtered-e3-chiral}

The chiral $\Pthree$ bracket and the chiral
Chevalley--Eilenberg complex assemble into a single object:
a filtered $\Ethree$-chiral algebra
$\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)$, a factorisation $\cD$-module
on $\Ran(X)$ whose global sections on the formal disk
recover the CFG $\Ethree$-algebra.

\begin{definition}[The filtered $\Ethree$-chiral algebra]
\label{def:filtered-e3-chiral}
The \emph{chiral $\Ethree$-algebra}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:chiral-e3-algebra}
  \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)
  \;:=\;
  \bigl(\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k),\;
  \{-,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}},\;
  \cD\textrm{-module structure}\bigr)
\end{equation}
is the chiral Chevalley--Eilenberg complex
(Definition~\ref{def:chiral-ce-complex}) equipped with
the chiral $\Pthree$ bracket
(Construction~\ref{constr:chiral-p3-bracket}) and the
$\cD$-module structure from the factorisation algebra
on~$X$. It carries a $(k+h^\vee)$-adic filtration
$F^\bullet \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)$ defined by:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:hbar-filtration}
  F^p \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)
  \;=\;
  (k+h^\vee)^p \cdot \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg).
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Structure of the chiral $\Ethree$-algebra]
\label{thm:chiral-e3-structure}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\fg$ be a simple Lie algebra.
The chiral $\Ethree$-algebra
$\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)$ satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Associated graded.)}
  The associated graded with respect to the $(k+h^\vee)$-adic
  filtration is the uncurved chiral Chevalley--Eilenberg
  complex at the critical level:
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:assoc-graded}
    \mathrm{gr}_F\,\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)
    \;\simeq\;
    \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_{-h^\vee})
    \;=\;
    \bigl(\Sym^c(\fg^*[-1]) \otimes \omega_X,\; d_{\CE}\bigr),
  \end{equation}
  which is an $\Einf$-chiral algebra \textup{(}commutative
  factorisation $\cD$-module on $\Ran(X)$\textup{)}.
\item \textup{(The $\Ethree$ structure.)}
  The chiral $\Pthree$ bracket
  \textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:chiral-p3-structure}}%
  \textup{)} deforms the $\Einf$ structure to
  a filtered $\Ethree$-chiral algebra:
  $\Etwo$ from the curve geometry
  \textup{(}Warning~\textup{\ref{warn:e1-vs-e2-source}}%
  \textup{)} $+ 1$ from the chiral $\Pthree$ bracket
  $= \Ethree$.
\item \textup{(Curvature and the deformation parameter.)}
  The curvature
  $m_0 = \tfrac{k + h^\vee}{2h^\vee}\,\kappa$ of the chiral
  Chevalley--Eilenberg complex is the obstruction to the
  $\Einf$ structure: at $k = -h^\vee$, the curvature vanishes
  and the algebra is commutative; at $k \neq -h^\vee$, the
  $\Pthree$ bracket and the curvature together deform the
  commutative structure to a filtered $\Ethree$-chiral
  algebra.
  The deformation parameter is $(k+h^\vee) \in
  (k+h^\vee)\,H^3(\fg)[[k+h^\vee]]$,
  matching the deformation space of
  Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:e3-cs}(ii)}.
\item \textup{(Factorisation property.)}
  The $\Ethree$-chiral algebra
  $\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)$ is a factorisation algebra on
  $X$ in the sense that the restriction to disjoint
  opens $U_1 \sqcup U_2 \hookrightarrow X$ induces
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:factorisation}
    \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)\big|_{U_1 \sqcup U_2}
    \;\simeq\;
    \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)\big|_{U_1}
    \otimes
    \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)\big|_{U_2},
  \end{equation}
  where the tensor product is in the derived category of
  $\cD$-modules. The $\Ethree$ structure is compatible with
  the factorisation isomorphism.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
The following lemma supplies the key compatibility between
the BV operator $\Delta$ and the chiral $\Pthree$ bracket
used in the proof of part~(ii). The ``independent
geometric data'' heuristic (genus-$1$ sewing vs.\
genus-$0$ collision) motivates the result, but
the proof requires a precise argument.

\begin{lemma}[Commutativity of the BV operator and the
chiral $\Pthree$ bracket]
\label{lem:bv-p3-commutativity}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\fg$ be a simple Lie algebra and $k \neq -h^\vee$.
The BV operator $\Delta$ on the derived chiral centre
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))$
\textup{(}from the framed $\Etwo$ structure,
genus-$1$ sewing\textup{)} and the chiral $\Pthree$
bracket $\{-,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}}$
\textup{(}Construction~\textup{\ref{constr:chiral-p3-bracket}},
genus-$0$ collision residue\textup{)} satisfy
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bv-p3-commute}
  [\Delta, \{a,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}}] = 0
  \quad \text{for all } a \in \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k).
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The abstract $\Ethree$-algebra structure on the derived
chiral centre $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))$
is provided by Proposition~\ref{prop:e3-structure}(ii)
via the Higher Deligne Conjecture applied to the
$\Etwo$-coalgebra $\BarSig(V_k(\fg))$.
This abstract $\Ethree$ structure includes, in particular,
an abstract BV operator $\Delta_{\mathrm{HDC}}$
(from the framed $\Etwo$ structure) and an abstract
$\Pthree$ bracket $\{-,-\}_{\mathrm{HDC}}$
(the degree-$(-2)$ Lie bracket from the $S^2$ fundamental
class of $\Conf_2(\RR^3)/\RR^+$). These satisfy all
$\Ethree$ compatibility conditions, including
$[\Delta_{\mathrm{HDC}}, \{a, -\}_{\mathrm{HDC}}] = 0$,
by the operadic structure of $\Ethree$.

It remains to identify the explicit chiral $\Pthree$
bracket $\{-,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}}$ from
Construction~\ref{constr:chiral-p3-bracket} with
the abstract bracket $\{-,-\}_{\mathrm{HDC}}$. We argue
in three steps.

\textit{Step 1: matching on the associated graded.}
At $k = -h^\vee$ (the associated graded of the
$(k+h^\vee)$-adic filtration), the chiral
Chevalley--Eilenberg complex becomes the uncurved
commutative coalgebra $\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_0)$,
which is an $\Einf$-chiral algebra.
Both $\{-,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}}$ and $\{-,-\}_{\mathrm{HDC}}$
reduce to the classical $\Pthree$ bracket on
$\Sym^c(\fg^*[-1]) \otimes \omega_X$ induced by the Lie
bracket of $\fg$: the bracket
$\{\phi_a, \phi_b\} = f^{ab}{}_c\,\phi_c$ on generators,
extended as a biderivation.
This classical bracket is uniquely determined by the
$\fg$-equivariance and degree constraints on
$\Sym^c(\fg^*[-1])$: the space of degree-$(-2)$
Lie brackets on $\Sym^c(\fg^*[-1])$ that are
biderivations of the commutative product and
$\fg$-equivariant is one-dimensional (generated by the
bracket induced by $\fg$ itself). Therefore
$\{-,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}}$ and $\{-,-\}_{\mathrm{HDC}}$
agree on $\mathrm{gr}_F$.

\textit{Step 2: matching order by order.}
The $(k+h^\vee)$-adic filtration on
$\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k)$ induces a filtration on the
space of degree-$(-2)$ Lie biderivations. At each order
$p \geq 1$ in $(k+h^\vee)$, the bracket is determined by
its value on generators
$\phi_a \otimes \phi_b \in \fg^*[-1] \otimes \fg^*[-1]$
(since both brackets are biderivations of $\mu$). On
generators, the space of $\fg$-equivariant $\cD$-linear
pairings $\fg^*[-1] \otimes \fg^*[-1] \to
\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k)$ of degree~$-2$ is spanned by
$f^{ab}{}_c\,\phi_c$ and $k\,(a,b)\,\partial$ (the
structure constants and the Killing form, the only
$\fg$-invariant tensors of the appropriate type for
simple~$\fg$). Step~1 fixes the structure-constant coefficient
$f^{ab}{}_c\,\phi_c$ at order~$0$.  At each subsequent
order $p \geq 1$, the \emph{correction} to the bracket on
generators lies in the subspace spanned by the Killing-form
term $k\,(a,b)\,\partial$ alone (since the
structure-constant component is already determined by
the lower orders and the biderivation property).
For simple~$\fg$, this correction space is
one-dimensional: $\Hom_\fg(\Sym^2\fg, \CC) \cong \CC$
(the Killing form is the unique $\fg$-invariant symmetric
bilinear form).  Both $\{-,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}}$ and
$\{-,-\}_{\mathrm{HDC}}$ are $\fg$-equivariant
$\cD$-linear biderivations with the same
order-$0$ value (Step~1), so their corrections at each
order $p$ coincide by the one-dimensionality of the
Killing-form component.  Therefore the two brackets agree
at each order.

\textit{Step 3: conclusion.}
Since $\{-,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}} = \{-,-\}_{\mathrm{HDC}}$
as formal power series in $(k+h^\vee)$, and
$[\Delta_{\mathrm{HDC}}, \{a,-\}_{\mathrm{HDC}}] = 0$
holds by the $\Ethree$ operadic structure,
the commutativity~\eqref{eq:bv-p3-commute} follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Chain-level status of
Lemma~\ref{lem:bv-p3-commutativity}]
\label{rem:bv-p3-chain-level}
The proof reduces the commutativity to the identification
of the explicit chiral $\Pthree$ bracket with the abstract
HDC bracket. This identification is proved order by order
in $(k+h^\vee)$ using the one-dimensionality of the
deformation space, which holds for simple~$\fg$.
For non-simple~$\fg$, the deformation space
$H^3(\fg)[[k+h^\vee]]$ may be higher-dimensional, and the
order-by-order uniqueness argument requires a more refined
comparison.

The ``independent geometric data'' intuition (that
$\Delta$ comes from genus-$1$ sewing strata of
$\overline{\cM}_{1,n}$ while
$\{-,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}}$ comes from genus-$0$ collision
strata of $\overline{\cM}_{0,n}$) captures the operadic
reason: in the modular envelope of the $\Ethree$ operad,
genus-$1$ and genus-$0$ operations compose without
interference because they correspond to disjoint cells
in the modular operad. Making this precise at the
chain level in the chiral (as opposed to topological)
setting is what the identification argument above
accomplishes for simple~$\fg$.
\end{remark}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-e3-structure}]
Part (i) is immediate from the filtration: the $(k+h^\vee)$-adic
filtration is exhaustive, and at $k = -h^\vee$ the curvature
and the cocycle term of the $\Pthree$ bracket both vanish,
leaving the uncurved commutative chiral coalgebra
$\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_{-h^\vee})$.

Part (ii) requires three things: that the chiral
$\Pthree$ bracket is a degree-$(-2)$ Lie bracket
(Jacobi), that it satisfies the Leibniz rule with respect
to the commutative product (Poisson compatibility), and
that it is compatible with the $\Etwo$ structure from
the curve geometry, so that the combined data assembles
into an $\Ethree$-algebra. We prove each in turn.

\textit{Step 1: the $\Pthree$ Jacobi identity from the
PVA Jacobi.}
The PVA $\lambda$-bracket on $V_k(\fg)$ satisfies the
$(-1)$-shifted Jacobi identity
(see~\cite{Lorgat26II}, Appendix~A, Proposition~A.5):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pva-jacobi-recall}
  \{a \mathbin{{}_\lambda} \{b \mathbin{{}_\mu} c\}\}
  - (-1)^{(|a|+1)(|b|+1)}\,
  \{b \mathbin{{}_\mu} \{a \mathbin{{}_\lambda} c\}\}
  = \{\{a \mathbin{{}_\lambda} b\}
  \mathbin{{}_{{\lambda+\mu}}} c\}.
\end{equation}
The chiral $\Pthree$ bracket
$\{-,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}}$
on $\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k)$ is obtained from the
$\lambda$-bracket by specialising $\lambda \mapsto \partial$
(the $\cD$-module derivation) and passing to the chiral
Chevalley--Eilenberg complex. On generators
$\phi_a, \phi_b, \phi_c \in \fg^*[-1]$
(cohomological degree $1$, shifted bracket-degree
$|\phi_a| - 2 = -1$), the degree-$(-2)$ Jacobi identity
is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:p3-jacobi}
  \{\phi_a, \{\phi_b, \phi_c\}^{\mathrm{ch}}\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  + \text{graded cyclic}
  = 0.
\end{equation}
To derive this from~\eqref{eq:pva-jacobi-recall}, set
$\lambda = \mu = 0$ (the constant-coefficient specialisation
that extracts the $\Pthree$ bracket from the
$\lambda$-bracket). The left side
of~\eqref{eq:pva-jacobi-recall} becomes
$\{\phi_a, \{\phi_b, \phi_c\}^{\mathrm{ch}}\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
- (-1)^{(|\phi_a|+1)(|\phi_b|+1)}\,
\{\phi_b, \{\phi_a, \phi_c\}^{\mathrm{ch}}\}^{\mathrm{ch}}$,
and the right side becomes
$\{\{\phi_a, \phi_b\}^{\mathrm{ch}},
\phi_c\}^{\mathrm{ch}}$ (at $\lambda + \mu = 0$).
The PVA $\lambda$-bracket has degree $-1$ (it is
$(-1)$-shifted); the chiral $\Pthree$ bracket has
degree~$-2$, accounting for the additional degree shift
from the $\cD$-module derivation $\partial$ (conformal
weight~$1$). The Koszul sign
$(-1)^{(|\phi_a|+1)(|\phi_b|+1)}$ in the PVA identity
translates to $(-1)^{(|\phi_a|-2)(|\phi_b|-2)}$ in the
$\Pthree$ identity after incorporating the degree shift
$|\,{-}\,| \mapsto |\,{-}\,| - 2$ appropriate to a
degree-$(-2)$ bracket. On $\fg^*[-1]$ where
$|\phi_a| = 1$, both signs evaluate to
$(-1)^{(-1)(-1)} = -1$. Rearranging
gives~\eqref{eq:p3-jacobi}.

For general elements of $\Sym^c(\fg^*[-1])$ (not just
generators), the Jacobi identity extends by the Leibniz
rule (Step~2 below): the bracket is a biderivation of the
free commutative algebra, so a trilinear identity that
holds on generators holds on all elements.

\textit{Step 2: the Leibniz rule from PVA sesquilinearity.}
The PVA right Leibniz rule
(see~\cite{Lorgat26II}, Appendix~A, Proposition~A.6)
states:
\[
  \{a \mathbin{{}_\lambda} (b \cdot c)\}
  = \{a \mathbin{{}_\lambda} b\} \cdot c
  + (-1)^{(|a|+1)|b|}\,
  b \cdot \{a \mathbin{{}_\lambda} c\}
  + \int_0^\lambda
  \{\{a \mathbin{{}_\mu} b\}
  \mathbin{{}_\lambda} c\}\,d\mu.
\]
At $\lambda = 0$, the integral term vanishes
(the integration range collapses to a point), leaving:
\[
  \{a, b \cdot c\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  = \{a, b\}^{\mathrm{ch}} \cdot c
  + (-1)^{(|a|+1)|b|}\,
  b \cdot \{a, c\}^{\mathrm{ch}}.
\]
Adjusting the Koszul sign from the $(-1)$-shifted
convention to the degree-$(-2)$ convention:
$(|a|+1)|b|$ becomes $(|a|-2)|b|$ after the degree shift,
recovering the $\Pthree$ Leibniz rule
\[
  \{a, b \cdot c\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  = \{a, b\}^{\mathrm{ch}} \cdot c
  + (-1)^{(|a|-2)|b|}\,
  b \cdot \{a, c\}^{\mathrm{ch}},
\]
as stated in
Proposition~\ref{prop:chiral-p3-structure}(ii). This
identifies the chiral $\Pthree$ bracket as a derivation of
the commutative product
$\mu$ on $\Sym^c(\fg^*[-1]) \otimes \omega_X$: a
commutative algebra equipped with a compatible
degree-$(-2)$ Lie bracket is a $\Pthree$-algebra.

\textit{Step 3: compatibility with $\Etwo$ and the
$\Ethree$ assembly.}
The $\Etwo$-coalgebra structure on $\BarSig$
(Warning~\ref{warn:e1-vs-e2-source}) and its framed
variant (from genus-$1$ sewing) equip the derived centre
with a BV operator $\Delta$ satisfying
$[f, g] = \Delta(f \cdot g) - (\Delta f) \cdot g
- (-1)^{|f|}\,f \cdot (\Delta g)$
(the BV relation~\eqref{eq:bv-rel-sl2}).
The $\Pthree$ bracket of degree~$-2$ supplements
this $\Etwo$ structure with one additional degree of
operadic freedom:
the $\Pthree$ bracket
(degree $-2 = -(3-1)$) corresponds to the fundamental
class of $S^2 = \Conf_2(\RR^3)/\RR^+$, the extra
generator needed for $\Ethree$ beyond~$\Etwo$.

The compatibility condition is that the $\Pthree$ bracket
must be a derivation of the Gerstenhaber bracket
$[-,-]$ of degree~$-1$ from the $\Etwo$ structure.
Since the Gerstenhaber bracket is determined by $\Delta$
and $\mu$ via the BV relation, it suffices to show that
$\{-,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}}$ commutes with $\Delta$ and is a
derivation of $\mu$ (already proved in Step~2). The
commutativity $[\Delta, \{a,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}}] = 0$
follows from Lemma~\ref{lem:bv-p3-commutativity}.
Given this, for any $a, b, c \in
\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k)$:
\begin{align*}
  \{a, [b, c]\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  &= \{a, \Delta(b \cdot c)
  - (\Delta b) \cdot c
  - (-1)^{|b|}\,b \cdot (\Delta c)\}^{\mathrm{ch}} \\
  &= \Delta\{a, b \cdot c\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  - \{a, (\Delta b) \cdot c\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  - (-1)^{|b|}\,\{a, b \cdot (\Delta c)\}^{\mathrm{ch}},
\end{align*}
where the first term uses
$[\Delta, \{a,-\}^{\mathrm{ch}}] = 0$. Expanding each
term by the Leibniz rule of Step~2 and collecting via the
BV relation yields
\[
  \{a, [b, c]\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  = [\{a, b\}^{\mathrm{ch}}, c]
  + (-1)^{(|a|-2)|b|}\,[b, \{a, c\}^{\mathrm{ch}}]:
\]
the $\Pthree$ bracket is a derivation of the
Gerstenhaber bracket.

This is the mixed Leibniz rule that characterises
$\Ethree = \Etwo + \Pthree$: the $\Pthree$ bracket acts
as a derivation of the $\Etwo$ Gerstenhaber bracket. By
the recognition principle for $\mathsf{E}_n$-algebras
(Lurie~\cite{HA}, Theorem~5.1.2.2;
Francis~\cite{Francis2013}, Theorem~4.1): a commutative
algebra with a compatible $\Etwo$ structure and a
degree-$(-2)$ Lie bracket satisfying the mutual Leibniz
rules (Jacobi, Poisson compatibility with the product, and
derivation of the Gerstenhaber bracket) is an
$\Ethree$-algebra. The three verifications above establish
all the required relations.

Part (iii) combines two observations. The curvature
$m_0 = \tfrac{k + h^\vee}{2h^\vee}\,\kappa$ is the bar-complex
encoding of the level departure (the identification
$\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}(\fg_k) \simeq B^{\mathrm{ch}}(V_k(\fg))$).
The cocycle term
$k\,(a,b)\,\partial$ of the $\Pthree$ bracket is linear
in~$k$ and hence linear in $(k + h^\vee)$.
Both vanish at $k = -h^\vee$, so the
deformation is controlled by $(k + h^\vee)$. The identification
of the deformation space as $(k+h^\vee) H^3(\fg)[[k+h^\vee]]$
then follows from the computation of
Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-cs}(ii): $H^3(\fg)$ is one-dimensional
for simple $\fg$, and the Cartan $3$-form
$\omega_3(X, Y, Z) = (X, [Y,Z])$ is the generator.

Part (iv) is the factorisation property of the chiral
bar complex. The chiral Chevalley--Eilenberg complex, being
a chiral coalgebra, satisfies the factorisation axiom
by construction (Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite{BD04}, Chapter 3).
The $\Pthree$ bracket, being a $\cD$-module morphism
(Proposition~\ref{prop:chiral-p3-structure}(iii)), is
compatible with the factorisation isomorphisms.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The $\cD$-module structure and the KZ connection}
\label{subsec:chiral-e3-dmod}

The chiral $\Ethree$-algebra
$\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)$ is not an abstract
$\Ethree$-algebra: it is an $\Ethree$-algebra object
in the category of $\cD$-modules on $X$, or equivalently
on $\Ran(X)$. This $\cD$-module structure is the content
that distinguishes the chiral construction from the
topological CFG construction.

\begin{proposition}[The $\cD$-module structure]
\label{prop:chiral-e3-dmod}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The chiral $\Ethree$-algebra
$\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)$ carries a flat connection
\begin{equation}\label{eq:chiral-e3-connection}
  \nabla^{\mathrm{ch}}
  \;=\;
  d \;+\; \sum_{i < j}
  \frac{k\,\Omega_{ij}}{z_i - z_j}\,dz_{ij}
\end{equation}
on $\Conf_n(X)$, where $\Omega_{ij}$ is the Casimir element
acting on the $i$-th and $j$-th tensor factors. This is the
Knizhnik--Zamolodchikov connection.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The flatness
  $(\nabla^{\mathrm{ch}})^2 = 0$ follows from the
  classical Yang--Baxter equation for the $r$-matrix
  $r(z) = k\,\Omega/z$
  \textup{(}trace-form convention; at $k = 0$, $r = 0$%
  \textup{)}: the curvature of $\nabla^{\mathrm{ch}}$
  is proportional to the LHS of the CYBE, which vanishes.
\item The $\Ethree$-algebra operations are horizontal
  with respect to $\nabla^{\mathrm{ch}}$: the cup product,
  the Gerstenhaber bracket, and the $\Pthree$ bracket are
  all flat sections of the corresponding $\Hom$-bundles.
  This is the $\cD$-linearity of
  Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:chiral-p3-structure}(iii)}
  in the multi-point form.
\item The monodromy representation of~$\nabla^{\mathrm{ch}}$
  on $\pi_1(\Conf_n(X))$ gives the braid group action.
  For $X = \CC$, the monodromy of the KZ connection
  is computed by the Drinfeld associator
  $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$
  and recovers the braided monoidal structure on
  $\mathrm{Rep}(U_\hbar(\fg))$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The KZ connection on the $n$-point conformal blocks of
$V_k(\fg)$ is classical (Knizhnik--Zamolodchikov, 1984).
The presentation here identifies this connection as the
$\cD$-module structure on the chiral Chevalley--Eilenberg
complex, viewed as a factorisation algebra on $\Ran(X)$.

For (i): the curvature of $\nabla^{\mathrm{ch}}$ on
$\Conf_n(X)$ is
$[\nabla_i, \nabla_j]
= k^2\,[\Omega_{ij}/(z_i-z_j),\,
\Omega_{ik}/(z_i-z_k) + \Omega_{jk}/(z_j-z_k)]
\,dz_{ij} \wedge dz_{ik}$
plus cyclic permutations. The vanishing of this expression
is equivalent to the classical Yang--Baxter equation
$[r_{12}, r_{13}] + [r_{12}, r_{23}] + [r_{13}, r_{23}] = 0$
for $r_{ij} = k\,\Omega_{ij}/(z_i - z_j)$.

For (ii): the $\Ethree$ operations are defined in terms of
the PVA $\lambda$-bracket, which is itself a $\cD$-module
morphism. The cup product is a $\cD$-module map by the
commutative algebra structure on the factorisation algebra.
The $\Pthree$ bracket involves the derivation $\partial$
(from the $\lambda$ in the PVA bracket), which is the
$\cD$-module action; its compatibility with the connection
follows from the PVA sesquilinearity axiom.

For (iii): this is the Drinfeld--Kohno theorem
(Drinfeld~\cite{EK96}, Kohno, Kassel): the monodromy of the
KZ connection on $\Conf_n(\CC)$ factors through the pure
braid group $P_n$ and gives, via the Drinfeld associator
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}} \in \exp(\hat{\mathfrak{t}}_3)$,
a braided monoidal structure on $\mathrm{Rep}(U_\hbar(\fg))$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Comparison with CFG: formal disk global sections}
\label{subsec:chiral-e3-cfg-comparison}

The chiral $\Ethree$-algebra
$\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)$ lives on $\Ran(X)$ and
carries the KZ connection; the CFG algebra $\cA^\lambda$
lives on $\RR^3$ and is purely topological. The formal
disk $D = \Spec \CC[[z]]$ is the bridge: restricting to
$D$ trivializes the $\cD$-module structure and recovers the
CFG algebra as the fiber.

\begin{theorem}[Formal disk restriction recovers CFG]
\label{thm:chiral-e3-cfg}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $D = \Spec \CC[[z]]$ be the formal disk in~$X$.
There is a natural map of filtered $\Ethree$-algebras
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cfg-comparison}
  \Gamma(D,\, \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg))
  \;\longrightarrow\;
  \cA^\lambda
\end{equation}
from the global sections of the chiral $\Ethree$-algebra
on the formal disk to the CFG $\Ethree$-algebra of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:cfg}}, where the CFG coupling
$\lambda$ equals the departure from critical level
$k + h^\vee$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Associated graded.)}
  At the level of associated graded algebras,
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:cfg-assoc-graded}
    \mathrm{gr}\,\Gamma(D,\, \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg))
    \;\simeq\;
    C^*(\fg)
    \;=\;
    \Sym(\fg^\vee[-1]),
  \end{equation}
  the Chevalley--Eilenberg cochains of $\fg$: both the
  chiral construction restricted to the formal disk and the
  CFG construction have the same classical limit.
\item \textup{(Matching of $\Pthree$ brackets.)}
  The restriction of the chiral $\Pthree$
  bracket~\eqref{eq:chiral-p3-total} to the formal disk
  recovers the CFG $\Pthree$ bracket.
  On the formal disk, the derivation $\partial = \partial_z$
  acts on $\CC[[z]]$; the global sections
  $\Gamma(D, \fg^*[-1] \otimes \omega_D)
  \cong \fg^*[-1][[z]]\,dz$ produce, after extracting the
  residue at $z = 0$, the finite-dimensional $\Pthree$
  bracket
  \[
    \{\phi_a, \phi_b\}_{\mathrm{CFG}}
    \;=\;
    f^{ab}{}_c\,\phi_c
    \;+\;
    k\,(a,b) \cdot \delta_0,
  \]
  where $\delta_0$ is the delta-function at the origin,
  the formal-disk avatar of $\partial$. Under the
  identification $\Gamma(D, \omega_D) \cong \CC\,dz$
  \textup{(}taking the constant mode\textup{)}, this
  recovers the CFG bracket on $C^*(\fg)$.
\item \textup{(Matching of deformation spaces.)}
  Both $\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)\big|_D$ and
  $\cA^\lambda$ are deformations of $C^*(\fg)$ over the
  base $(k+h^\vee)\,H^3(\fg)[[k+h^\vee]]$, with the same
  one-dimensional fibre at each order in $(k+h^\vee)$ for
  simple~$\fg$.
  The map~\eqref{eq:cfg-comparison} intertwines
  the $(k+h^\vee)$-adic filtrations.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The argument has two steps: construct the $\Ethree$-algebra
structure on $\Gamma(D, \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg))$
by symmetric monoidal transport, then identify it with the
CFG algebra $\cA^\lambda$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Step 1: symmetric monoidal trivialization
on~$D$.}
The formal disk $D = \Spec \CC[[z]]$ is contractible:
every $\cD$-module on~$D$ (that is, a $\CC[[z]]$-module
with flat connection) is isomorphic to its fibre at the
origin, since every flat connection on~$D$ is
trivializable ($\pi_1(D^n) = 0$ for all~$n$, so there is
no monodromy obstruction). The global sections functor
\begin{equation}\label{eq:disk-equivalence}
  \Gamma(D, -) \colon
  \cD\textrm{-}\mathrm{mod}(D) \;\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}\;
  \mathrm{Vect}
\end{equation}
is an equivalence of categories. This equivalence is
symmetric monoidal: the tensor product of $\cD$-modules
on~$D$ (the $!$-tensor product, which on the contractible
formal disk coincides with the $*$-tensor product and with
the ordinary tensor product of the underlying vector
spaces) corresponds under~\eqref{eq:disk-equivalence} to
the tensor product in $\mathrm{Vect}$.

An equivalence of symmetric monoidal categories preserves
all algebraic structures definable in terms of the monoidal
structure. In particular, it preserves $\Ethree$-algebra
structures: if $\cB$ is an $\Ethree$-algebra in
$\cD\textrm{-}\mathrm{mod}(D)$, then
$\Gamma(D, \cB)$ is an $\Ethree$-algebra in
$\mathrm{Vect}$, with structure maps obtained by applying
$\Gamma(D, -)$ to those of~$\cB$.

Applying this to
$\cB = \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)\big|_D$: the
restriction of the chiral $\Ethree$-algebra to~$D$ is an
$\Ethree$-algebra in $\cD\textrm{-}\mathrm{mod}(D)$ (by
restriction of the $\Ethree$-structure on~$X$), and the
equivalence~\eqref{eq:disk-equivalence} endows
$\Gamma(D, \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg))$ with an
$\Ethree$-algebra structure in $\mathrm{Vect}$. This
$\Ethree$-structure is functorial and carries the
$(k+h^\vee)$-adic filtration inherited from the chiral side.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Step 2: identification with $\cA^\lambda$.}
It remains to identify
$\Gamma(D, \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg))$ with the CFG
algebra $\cA^\lambda$ as filtered $\Ethree$-algebras,
where $\lambda = k + h^\vee$. Both are filtered
$\Ethree$-algebras deforming the same classical limit over
the same base, and the identification proceeds through
three structural invariants.

Part (i): on~$D$, the $\cD$-module structure trivializes,
so the chiral Chevalley--Eilenberg complex reduces to the
ordinary one. The associated graded of the chiral
$\Ethree$-algebra on~$D$ is
\[
  \mathrm{gr}\,\Gamma(D,\, \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg))
  \;=\;
  \Gamma(D,\, \mathrm{gr}\,\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg))
  \;=\;
  \Gamma(D,\, \Sym^c(\fg^*[-1]) \otimes \omega_D).
\]
On the formal disk, $\omega_D \cong \cO_D\,dz$; the
equivalence~\eqref{eq:disk-equivalence} extracts the
constant mode in the $z$-expansion, giving
$\Sym(\fg^\vee[-1]) = C^*(\fg)$, the
Chevalley--Eilenberg cochains of~$\fg$ as a commutative
algebra. This is the associated graded of $\cA^\lambda$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:cfg}(i)).

Part (ii): the chiral $\Pthree$ bracket on~$X$
(equation~\eqref{eq:chiral-p3-total}) is defined via the
PVA $\lambda$-bracket. On the formal disk, the spectral
parameter $\lambda$ becomes the formal variable of the
completed enveloping algebra: the translation generator
$\partial_z$ acts on $\CC[[z]]$, and taking global sections
via~\eqref{eq:disk-equivalence} extracts the residue at
$z = 0$. The resulting bracket on
$\Gamma(D, \fg^*[-1] \otimes \omega_D)
\cong \fg^*[-1][[z]]\,dz$ is
\[
  \{\phi_a, \phi_b\}_{\mathrm{CFG}}
  \;=\;
  f^{ab}{}_{c}\,\phi_c
  \;+\;
  k\,(a,b) \cdot \delta_0,
\]
where $f^{ab}{}_{c}$ are the structure constants of~$\fg$,
$(a,b)$ is the Killing form, and $\delta_0$ is the
delta-function at the origin (the formal-disk avatar of the
derivation~$\partial$). Under the identification
$\Gamma(D, \omega_D) \cong \CC\,dz$, this is the CFG
$\Pthree$ bracket on $C^*(\fg)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:cfg}(ii)).

Part (iii): the deformation theory of $\Ethree$-algebras
with fixed associated graded $C^*(\fg)$ is controlled by
the $\Ethree$-Hochschild cohomology, which for
$C^*(\fg)$ with~$\fg$ simple is
$(k+h^\vee)\,H^3(\fg)[[k+h^\vee]]$, one-dimensional at each
order in $(k+h^\vee)$. Both
$\Gamma(D, \CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg))$ and
$\cA^\lambda$ are deformations of $C^*(\fg)$ over this
base (Theorem~\ref{thm:e3-cs}(ii) and
Theorem~\ref{thm:cfg}(iv) respectively). Since the
deformation space is one-dimensional at each order, and
both deformations have matching associated graded
(Part~(i)) and matching $\Pthree$ bracket (Part~(ii)),
they are isomorphic as filtered $\Ethree$-algebras.
The $(k+h^\vee)$-adic filtration on the chiral side maps to
the $\lambda$-adic filtration on the CFG side under
$\lambda = k + h^\vee$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The chain of spaces]
\label{rem:disk-vs-point}
The restriction passes through
$\mathrm{pt} \leftarrow D \to \mathbb{A}^1 \to \PP^1
\to X$, with each step adding geometric content: the formal
disk provides the derivation $\partial$ (cocycle term
$k(a,b)\partial$); $\mathbb{A}^1$ adds Arnold relations
and braid monodromy; $\PP^1$ and higher-genus $X$ add
compactness and modular curvature. None of these
restrictions is an identity; each step loses geometric
information.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The Khan--Zeng topological enhancement}
\label{subsec:khan-zeng-enhancement}

The chiral $\Ethree$-algebra is holomorphic: it depends on the
complex structure of $X$ through the KZ connection. At
non-critical level $k \neq -h^\vee$, the Sugawara Virasoro
element provides a homotopy between the holomorphic and
topological directions, upgrading the $\Ethree$ structure
from holomorphic to topological. At the critical level,
the Sugawara element is undefined and the enhancement fails.

\begin{proposition}[Topological enhancement via Sugawara]
\label{prop:khan-zeng-topological}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
At generic non-critical level $k \neq -h^\vee$, the affine
Kac--Moody vertex algebra $V_k(\fg)$ possesses a Sugawara
Virasoro element
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sugawara-element}
  T_{\mathrm{Sug}} \;=\;
  \frac{1}{2(k + h^\vee)}
  \sum_{a=1}^{\dim \fg}
  \mathopen{:} J^a J_a \mathclose{:},
\end{equation}
which generates an inner action of the Virasoro algebra at
central charge $c = k\,\dim(\fg)/(k + h^\vee)$.

By Khan--Zeng~\cite{KhanZeng25}, the Virasoro element
upgrades the holomorphic-topological symmetry of the $3$d
gauge theory on $\Sigma \times \RR_t$ to fully topological
symmetry on $\Sigma \times \RR_t$. At the level of the
chiral $\Ethree$-algebra, this means:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Holomorphic-topological.)}
  The chiral $\Ethree$-algebra
  $\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_k(\fg)$ lives on $X \times \RR$,
  where $X$ carries holomorphic structure and $\RR$
  carries topological structure. The $\Etwo$ from the
  curve geometry is holomorphic; the $+1$ from the $\Pthree$
  bracket is topological \textup{(}from the $\RR$ factor%
  \textup{)}.
\item \textup{(Full topological.)}
  The Sugawara element provides an identification
  of the holomorphic direction in~$X$ with a topological
  direction: the stress-energy tensor $T_{\mathrm{Sug}}$
  generates translations along $X$, making them homotopically
  trivial. After this identification, the
  $\Ethree$-structure becomes \emph{topological} $\Ethree$:
  an $\Ethree$-algebra in the category of locally constant
  sheaves on $\RR^3 \cong X \times \RR$
  \textup{(}identifying $X \cong \CC \cong \RR^2$%
  \textup{)}.
\item \textup{(Failure at critical level.)}
  At $k = -h^\vee$, the Sugawara element~\eqref{eq:sugawara-element}
  is undefined \textup{(}the denominator $2(k+h^\vee)$
  vanishes\textup{)}. The critical-level chiral
  $\Ethree$-algebra $\CE^{\mathrm{ch}}_{-h^\vee}(\fg)$ is
  genuinely holomorphic, not topological: the
  $\Etwo$-coalgebra on $\BarSig(\cA)$ is topological
  (Warning~\ref{warn:e1-vs-e2-source}), but $\cA$
  itself retains essential dependence on the complex
  structure of~$X$.
  Without the Sugawara element, there is no homotopy
  between the holomorphic and topological directions,
  so the full structure is not topological $\Ethree$.
  Perturbatively, in the formal neighbourhood of critical level,
  the topological enhancement holds order-by-order in
  $(k + h^\vee)$ \textup{(}since $T_{\mathrm{Sug}}$ exists at
  any nonzero departure from critical level\textup{)}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Part (i) is the HT structure of the $3$d gauge theory
whose boundary chiral algebra is $V_k(\fg)$
(Costello--Gwilliam~\cite{CG17}). The factorisation
algebra of the HT theory lives on $\Sigma \times \RR$
with holomorphic dependence on $\Sigma$ and topological
dependence on $\RR$; the chiral $\Ethree$-algebra is the
algebraic encoding of this factorisation structure.

Part (ii).
Khan--Zeng~\cite{KhanZeng25} prove the following at the
classical level: an inner Virasoro element in a Poisson
vertex algebra with non-degenerate central charge provides
a homotopy between the holomorphic and topological
directions, upgrading the HT theory to a fully topological
theory. We extend this to the quantum vertex algebra
$V_k(\fg)$. The Sugawara construction
(Frenkel--Ben-Zvi~\cite{FBZ04}, Chapter~15) produces
$T_{\mathrm{Sug}} \in V_k(\fg)$ satisfying the exact
Virasoro OPE
\[
  T_{\mathrm{Sug}}(z)\,T_{\mathrm{Sug}}(w) \;\sim\;
  \frac{c/2}{(z-w)^4}
  + \frac{2\,T_{\mathrm{Sug}}(w)}{(z-w)^2}
  + \frac{\partial T_{\mathrm{Sug}}(w)}{z-w}
\]
at central charge $c = k\,\dim(\fg)/(k+h^\vee)$, and the
Sugawara OPE with the current generators
\[
  T_{\mathrm{Sug}}(z)\,J^a(w) \;\sim\;
  \frac{J^a(w)}{(z-w)^2}
  + \frac{\partial J^a(w)}{z-w}.
\]
The second relation gives $L_{-1} \cdot J^a = \partial J^a$:
the holomorphic translation operator $L_{-1} = (T_{\mathrm{Sug}})_{(1)}$
acts as $\partial$ on all generators. These are identities in
the quantum vertex algebra, not formal-deformation statements;
the normal ordering in~\eqref{eq:sugawara-element} absorbs
all quantum corrections.  The BRST operator $Q$ of the $3$d
gauge theory satisfies $[Q, b] = L_{-1}$ where $b$ is the
antighost zero-mode (this is the standard BRST relation for
the Sugawara Virasoro; see~\cite{FBZ04}, \S15.4), so
$L_{-1}$ is BRST-exact. The Khan--Zeng
argument~\cite{KhanZeng25} then applies verbatim: BRST-exactness
of holomorphic translation is the datum required to upgrade
the HT symmetry to full topological symmetry. At generic
$k$, the central charge
$c = k\,\dim(\fg)/(k+h^\vee) \neq 0$, so the hypothesis is
satisfied.

Part (iii): at $k = -h^\vee$, the Feigin--Frenkel centre
$Z(V_{-h^\vee}(\fg)) = \mathrm{Fun}(\mathrm{Op}_{\fg^\vee}(D))$
(Feigin--Frenkel) is a commutative
algebra with non-trivial dependence on the complex structure
of the formal disk (through the notion of $\fg^\vee$-oper).
The absence of the Sugawara element means no homotopy
between holomorphic and topological directions exists, and
the chiral $\Ethree$-algebra is genuinely holomorphic.
The perturbative statement follows because the Sugawara
element exists as a formal power series in $(k + h^\vee)$ for
$k \neq -h^\vee$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Example: $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ chiral $\Ethree$-algebra}
\label{subsec:sl2-chiral-e3}

The general construction specialises cleanly to
$\fg = \mathfrak{sl}_2$: the chiral $\Pthree$ bracket is
determined by six pairings on the generators
$\phi_e, \phi_f, \phi_h$, each consisting of a Lie bracket
term (from the structure constants) and a cocycle term
(proportional to the level $k$, vanishing at $k = 0$).
Restricting to the formal disk and extracting the zero mode
recovers the CFG bracket of
Proposition~\ref{prop:e3-explicit-sl2}.

\begin{example}[Explicit chiral $\Pthree$ bracket for
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$]
\label{ex:sl2-chiral-e3}
For $\fg = \mathfrak{sl}_2$ with basis $\{e, f, h\}$,
Killing form $(e,f) = (f,e) = 1$, $(h,h) = 2$,
structure constants $[e,f] = h$, $[h,e] = 2e$, $[h,f] = -2f$,
and $h^\vee = 2$, the chiral $\Pthree$ bracket
(Construction~\ref{constr:chiral-p3-bracket}) on the
generators $\phi_e, \phi_f, \phi_h \in \mathfrak{sl}_2^*[-1]$
is:
\begin{align}
  \label{eq:sl2-p3-ef}
  \{\phi_e, \phi_f\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  &= \phi_h + k\,\partial, \\
  \label{eq:sl2-p3-he}
  \{\phi_h, \phi_e\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  &= 2\,\phi_e, \\
  \label{eq:sl2-p3-hf}
  \{\phi_h, \phi_f\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  &= -2\,\phi_f, \\
  \label{eq:sl2-p3-hh}
  \{\phi_h, \phi_h\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  &= 2k\,\partial, \\
  \label{eq:sl2-p3-ee}
  \{\phi_e, \phi_e\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  &= 0, \\
  \label{eq:sl2-p3-ff}
  \{\phi_f, \phi_f\}^{\mathrm{ch}}
  &= 0.
\end{align}
The Lie bracket terms ($\phi_h$ in~\eqref{eq:sl2-p3-ef},
$2\phi_e$ in~\eqref{eq:sl2-p3-he},
$-2\phi_f$ in~\eqref{eq:sl2-p3-hf}) persist at $k = 0$;
the cocycle terms ($k\,\partial$ in~\eqref{eq:sl2-p3-ef}
and~\eqref{eq:sl2-p3-hh}) vanish at $k = 0$
(AP126 check: at $k = 0$, the level-dependent terms
vanish, and the bracket reduces to the Chevalley--Eilenberg
coderivation).

At the critical level $k = -2$ (i.e.\ $k + h^\vee = 0$), the cocycle
terms become $-2\,\partial$, and the chiral
$\Ethree$-algebra degenerates: the curvature $m_0 = 0$
(uncurved), and the commutative factorisation structure is
undeformed.

Taking formal disk global sections and extracting the zero
mode recovers the CFG $\Pthree$ bracket of
Proposition~\ref{prop:e3-explicit-sl2}:
$\{\phi_a, \phi_b\}_{\mathrm{CFG}}
= f^{ab}{}_c\,\phi_c + k\,(a,b)\,\delta_0$, which at the
cohomological level gives
$\{X, Y\} = h_{\mathrm{KZ}}\,(X,Y)$ for
$X, Y \in \HH^1 = \mathfrak{sl}_2$
(equation~\eqref{eq:e3-p3-killing}), with
$h_{\mathrm{KZ}} = 1/(k+2)$.
\end{example}

\section{The dimensional ladder}
\label{sec:en-summary}

The chapter establishes eight results. The Totaro presentation
of $H^*(\Conf_k(\bR^n))$ generalizes Arnold's presentation from
$n = 2$ to all~$n$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:totaro-presentation}).
The $\Etwo$ bar complex satisfies $d^2 = 0$ via the Totaro
relations (Theorem~\ref{thm:e2-d-squared}).
The chiral bar-cobar duality of Main Theorem~A is recovered
as the $n = 2$ case of $\En$ Koszul duality
(Corollary~\ref{cor:n2-recovery}), and our construction is the
chain-level refinement of Ayala--Francis Poincar\'e--Koszul duality
at $n = 2$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:refines-af}).
The abstract $\En$ Koszul duality, $\En$ formality, and higher
enveloping algebras are established in the literature for all~$n$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:en-koszul-duality},
Theorem~\ref{thm:knudsen-higher-enveloping},
Proposition~\ref{prop:en-formality};
see also Remark~\ref{rem:en-scope}).
The \emph{ordered} bar complex
$\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)=T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ is a dg coalgebra
over the holomorphic--topological Swiss-cheese operad
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$: the differential is the
closed $(n=2)$ color, the deconcatenation coproduct is the
open $(n=1)$ color
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-swiss-cheese}); the symmetric bar
$\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ of Theorem~A is recovered by $R$-twisted
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariants and uses the coshuffle coproduct
instead. At higher genus the fibrewise differential
$\dfib$ is curved with $\dfib^{\,2}=\kappa(\cA)\cdot\omega_g$
but is \emph{not} a coderivation; flatness and the coderivation
property are restored only by the period-corrected differential
$\Dg{g}$ of Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-diff-squares-zero}
(Remark~\ref{rem:sc-higher-genus}).
The topologization theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization}) upgrades the BRST cohomology of
the $\Etwo$ center of $V_k(\fg)$ to $\Ethree$ via the Sugawara
conformal vector,
with an explicit antighost contraction $G(z)$
(Construction~\ref{constr:sugawara-antighost}) that makes
the proof constructive. For class~M algebras, where the
chain-level $\Ethree$ encounters obstructions from
step~(iv) of Remark~\ref{rem:topologization-chain-vs-cohomology},
a coderived refinement (Conjecture~\ref{conj:coderived-e3})
provides the conjectural alternative.

Three concrete calculations remain beyond the proved theorems.
First, explicit closed-form formulas for the $\En$ bar differential
in terms of Totaro generators at each $n \geq 3$ have not been
tabulated (the propagator and FM compactification exist and $d^2 = 0$
is proved). Second, the $n = 3$ Chern--Simons identification
(Conjecture~\ref{conj:e3-chern-simons}) connecting the
$\mathsf{E}_3$ bar complex to perturbative CS invariants at the
chain level is open. Third, higher-genus $\En$ theory for $n \geq 3$
would require a notion of higher-dimensional moduli replacing
$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$; the correct framework may be cobordism
categories or extended TQFTs.

The dimensional ladder makes precise the sense in which this
monograph's results (chiral bar-cobar duality, quantum
complementarity, genus expansions) are the $n = 2$ chapter
of a general story that extends through all dimensions.

\begin{remark}[The \texorpdfstring{$\Eone$}{E1}-\texorpdfstring{$\Etwo$}{E2} obstruction]
\label{rem:e1-e2-obstruction}
\index{E1-E2 obstruction@$\Eone$-$\Etwo$ obstruction|textbf}
\index{Drinfeld associator!and E2 coherence@and $\Etwo$ coherence}
The Yangian is $\Eone$-chiral (Theorem~\ref{thm:yangian-e1}):
binary fusion with braiding ($R$-matrix level). Upgrading to
$\Etwo$ requires coherent many-point transport: an associator
$\Phi \in \exp(\widehat{\mathrm{Lie}}(t_{12}, t_{23}))$
satisfying pentagon and hexagon. The Drinfeld--Kohno theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:derived-dk-affine}) is the $\Eone$-level
comparison package on the affine Drinfeld--Kohno surface
(KZ monodromy compared with the quantum-group $R$-matrix); full $\Etwo$ coherence
requires associator input, governed by the
Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group]
\label{rem:grothendieck-teichmuller}
\index{Grothendieck--Teichmuller group@Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group|textbf}
\index{E2 operad@$\Etwo$ operad!formality}
$\Etwo$ formality (Tamarkin~\cite{Tamarkin00}) is not canonical:
it depends on a Drinfeld associator~$\Phi$, and the non-uniqueness

provides $\Etwo$-level coherence. DK is naturally an
$\Eone$-factorization theorem; full $\Etwo$ coherence is a
separate, GT-dependent upgrade.
\end{remark}

\section{\texorpdfstring{The $\En$ shadow obstruction tower}{The En shadow obstruction tower}}
\label{sec:en-shadow-tower}
\index{shadow tower!En@$\En$|textbf}
\index{En algebra@$\En$ algebra!shadow tower|textbf}

The shadow obstruction tower of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ is built from the
Maurer--Cartan element $\Theta_\cA$ in the modular convolution
algebra. This section extends the construction to $\En$ algebras
for all~$n$, computing the $n$-dependence of the modular
characteristic~$\kappa$, the shadow depth, and the higher shadow
invariants. The key structural result is that the binary invariant
$\kappa$ is \emph{independent} of~$n$: it depends only on the
degree-$2$ part of the bar complex, which involves
$\Conf_2(\bR^n) \simeq S^{n-1}$ and produces the same pairing for
all~$n$. Higher-degree shadows \emph{do} depend on~$n$ through the
topology of $\Conf_k(\bR^n)$ for $k \geq 3$, but for formal
$\En$ algebras this dependence vanishes entirely: formality of the
$\En$ operad (Kontsevich, Tamarkin, Lambrechts--Voli\'c) forces
all shadow invariants to agree with their $\Einf$ values.

\begin{definition}[$\En$ shadow obstruction tower]
\label{def:en-shadow-tower}
\index{shadow tower!En@$\En$ definition}
Let $A$ be an $\En$ algebra with binary self-pairing of level~$k$.
The \emph{$\En$ bar complex} $B_{\En}(A)$ is the bar construction
built from the Fulton--MacPherson compactification
$\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ with propagator the fundamental class
of $S^{n-1}$ (the codimension-$n$ linking sphere). At degree~$m$,
the bar complex has underlying graded vector space
\[
B_{\En}(A)_m
\;=\;
H_*(\Conf_m(\bR^n); \bQ) \otimes A^{\otimes m}.
\]
The \emph{$\En$ shadow invariants} $S_r^{\En}(A)$ are defined as
the degree-$r$ projections of the Maurer--Cartan element in the
$\En$ modular convolution algebra, exactly as in
Definition~\ref{def:shadow-algebra} with the chiral bar complex
replaced by the $\En$ bar complex.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[$\En$ shadow obstruction tower: universality of $\kappa$ and formality collapse;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:en-shadow-tower}
\index{shadow tower!En@$\En$ universality|textbf}
\index{modular characteristic!En-independence@$\En$-independence|textbf}
Let $A$ be an $\En$ algebra for $n \geq 1$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Binary universality.)}
 The modular characteristic $\kappa_{\En}(A)$ is
 \emph{independent of~$n$}:
 \[
 \kappa_{\En}(A) \;=\; \kappa(A)
 \qquad \text{for all } n \geq 1.
 \]
 In particular,
 $\kappa_{\En}(\mathfrak{H}_k) = k$
 \textup{(}Heisenberg at level~$k$\textup{)},
 $\kappa_{\En}(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k)
 = \dim(\mathfrak{g})(k + h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$
 \textup{(}affine Kac--Moody\textup{)}, and
 $\kappa_{\En}(\mathrm{Vir}_c) = c/2$
 \textup{(}Virasoro\textup{)}.

\item \textup{(Formality collapse.)}
 If $A$ is \emph{formal} as an $\En$ algebra \textup{(}i.e.,
 the transferred $\En$ structure on the bar cohomology
 $H^*(B_{\En}(A))$ has trivial higher operations\textup{)},
 then the full shadow obstruction tower collapses:
 \[
 S_r^{\En}(A) \;=\; S_r^{\Einf}(A)
 \qquad \text{for all } r \geq 2.
 \]
 Since the $\Einf$ homology operad is commutative, the
 corresponding commutative shadow
 tower terminates at degree~$2$ for free algebras, a formal
 $\En$ algebra with vanishing structure constants beyond the
 self-pairing has shadow depth $r_{\max} = 2$
 \textup{(}class~G\textup{)}.

\item \textup{(Shadow depth classification.)}
 The four-class partition \textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:shadow-depth-classification})}
 \[
 \mathrm{G}\ (r_{\max} = 2),
 \quad
 \mathrm{L}\ (r_{\max} = 3),
 \quad
 \mathrm{C}\ (r_{\max} = 4),
 \quad
 \mathrm{M}\ (r_{\max} = \infty)
 \]
 is stable under the operadic level~$n$ for formal $\En$ algebras:
 the shadow class of~$A$ does not depend on~$n$.

\item \textup{(Stabilization.)}
 As $n \to \infty$, $\En$ approaches $\Einf$, and
 the shadow obstruction tower stabilizes:
 $S_r^{\En}(A) \to S_r^{\Einf}(A)$.
 The stabilization is \emph{finite}: for each fixed degree~$r$,
 there exists $N(r)$ such that
 $S_r^{\En}(A) = S_r^{\Einf}(A)$ for all $n \geq N(r)$.
 Explicitly, $N(r) \leq r$, because all positive-degree classes
 in $H^*(\Conf_r(\bR^n))$ have degree $\geq n - 1$, which
 exceeds the bar-complex degree at degree~$r$ once $n > r$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Part~(i).}
The modular characteristic $\kappa$ is determined by the degree-$2$
component of the bar complex. At degree~$2$:
\[
B_{\En}(A)_2
\;=\;
H_*(\Conf_2(\bR^n)) \otimes A^{\otimes 2}.
\]
The space $\Conf_2(\bR^n) = \bR^n \setminus \{0\}$ deformation-retracts
onto $S^{n-1}$, so $H_*(\Conf_2(\bR^n))$ has total rank~$2$:
one generator in degree~$0$ and one in degree~$n-1$. The
degree-$2$ self-pairing that defines $\kappa$ is the evaluation of
the binary propagator (the fundamental class of $S^{n-1}$) on
the quadratic input $a \otimes a$. Since the fundamental class of
$S^{n-1}$ pairs with $a \otimes a$ via the level~$k$ of the
algebra (the coefficient of the leading OPE singularity),
this pairing is independent of $n$: it sees only the level.
For each family the formula follows from the explicit computation
in Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}.

\textbf{Part~(ii).}
Formality of $A$ as an $\En$ algebra means that the transferred
$\En$ structure on $H^*(B_{\En}(A))$ has $m_r^{\mathrm{tr}} = 0$
for $r \geq 3$. The shadow invariant $S_r^{\En}(A)$ for $r \geq 3$
is determined by the transferred operations $m_j^{\mathrm{tr}}$
for $j \leq r$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-formality-low-degree}).
If all these vanish, then $S_r^{\En}(A) = 0$ for $r \geq 3$,
which equals the $\Einf$ value computed by the commutative shadow
(which has no higher obstructions beyond the binary pairing for free algebras).
For non-free formal algebras, the structure constants that
contribute to $S_r$ are the same as in the $\Einf$ computation
because the higher-degree corrections from the topology of
$\Conf_r(\bR^n)$ are killed by formality.

\textbf{Part~(iii).}
The shadow class is determined by the vanishing pattern of
$\{S_r\}_{r \geq 2}$ (Definition~\ref{def:shadow-depth-classification}).
By~(ii), for formal $\En$ algebras the vanishing pattern is
independent of~$n$, so the class label is stable.

\textbf{Part~(iv).}
For fixed degree~$r$, the correction terms from
$H^*(\Conf_r(\bR^n))$ to the shadow invariant $S_r^{\En}$ involve
classes of degree $\geq n - 1$ (the minimum degree of a
positive-dimensional class in the Totaro algebra on $r$~points,
Theorem~\ref{thm:totaro-presentation}). The bar-complex degree
at degree~$r$ is bounded by $r - 2$ (the MC equation constrains
the total propagator degree budget to $r - 2$ units).
Once $n - 1 > r - 2$, i.e., $n \geq r$, all topological
correction terms lie in degrees exceeding the MC degree budget,
hence contribute zero. Therefore
$S_r^{\En}(A) = S_r^{\Einf}(A)$ for $n \geq r$,
giving $N(r) \leq r$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-stabilization}).
For the binary invariant ($r = 2$),
this gives $N(2) \leq 2$; in fact, $N(2) = 1$ by~(i).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Configuration space Poincar\'e polynomials]
\label{rem:en-poincare-polynomials}
\index{configuration space!Poincare polynomial@Poincar\'e polynomial}
The Poincar\'e polynomial of $\Conf_k(\bR^n)$ for $n \geq 2$ is
\[
P_k^{(n)}(t)
\;=\;
\prod_{j=0}^{k-1} (1 + j\, t^{n-1}),
\]
giving total Betti number $P_k^{(n)}(1) = k!$ for all~$n \geq 2$
and Euler characteristic
$\chi(\Conf_k(\bR^n)) = \prod_{j=0}^{k-1}(1 + j(-1)^{n-1})$,
which vanishes for $k \geq 2$ when $n$ is even and equals $k!$
when $n$ is odd.
For $n = 1$: $\Conf_k(\bR)$ has $k!$ contractible components,
so $P_k^{(1)}(t) = k!$.
These formulas enter the bar complex dimensions via
$\dim B_{\En}(A)_k = P_k^{(n)}(1) \cdot \dim(A^{\otimes k})$
at the level of total Betti numbers.
\end{remark}

\section{\texorpdfstring{$\Etwo$ structure on the symmetric bar and the $\mathsf{E}_3$ obstruction}{E2 structure on the symmetric bar and the E3 obstruction}}
\label{sec:e3-bar-structure}
\index{bar complex!E2 structure@$\Etwo$ structure|textbf}
\index{E3 obstruction@$\mathsf{E}_3$ obstruction|textbf}

The symmetric chiral bar complex $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ of a chiral
algebra on a curve carries a natural $\Etwo$ structure from the local
configuration spaces $\Conf_k(\bC)$. It is obtained from the
ordered primitive $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ by the lossy
$R$-twisted $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant passage. It does \emph{not}
carry an $\mathsf{E}_3$ structure: a curve provides only~$\bR^2$,
and the passage to $\mathsf{E}_3$ requires $\bR^3$ geometry. The
correct statement is that, for $\Einf$-chiral algebras
(where $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ exists), the \emph{derived center}
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)
= \HH^*(\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA), \barB^{\Sigma}(\cA))$
carries $\mathsf{E}_3$ structure by the Higher Deligne Conjecture,
a distinct object from $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ itself.
For genuinely $\Eone$-chiral algebras, $\barB^{\Sigma}$ does not
exist without an $R$-matrix, and the $\mathsf{E}_3$ claim requires
a 3d holomorphic-topological theory as independent input.

\begin{proposition}[$\Etwo$ structure on the bar complex and the $\mathsf{E}_3$ obstruction;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:e3-bar-structure}
\index{bar complex!E3 obstruction@$\mathsf{E}_3$ obstruction|textbf}
\index{Gerstenhaber bracket!on bar complex}
\index{Higher Deligne Conjecture!E3 structure on derived center@$\mathsf{E}_3$ structure on derived center}
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on a smooth algebraic curve~$X$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{($\Etwo$ on $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$.)}
 The symmetric bar $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ carries a natural
 $\Etwo$-coalgebra
 structure from the operad $\mathsf{FM}_2 \simeq \Etwo$ acting on
 $\Conf_k(\bC) \cong \Conf_k(\bR^2)$. The corresponding cohomological
 structure on $H^*(\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA))$ is a \emph{Gerstenhaber algebra}: an
 associative product of degree~$0$ and a Lie bracket of degree~$1$
 satisfying the Leibniz rule.

\item \textup{($\mathsf{E}_3$ obstruction.)}
 The symmetric bar $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ does \emph{not} carry a natural
 $\mathsf{E}_3$ structure. The obstruction is topological:
 $\mathsf{E}_3$ requires configurations in~$\bR^3$, but the
 curve~$X$ provides only~$\bR^2$. The
 Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$, which governs the
 joint open--closed structure, is a \emph{colored} operad
 with $\Etwo$ on the closed holomorphic color and $\Eone$ on the
 open topological color;
 it is not the tensor product $\Etwo \otimes \Eone$.
 Dunn additivity
 \textup{(}$\mathsf{E}_m \otimes \mathsf{E}_n \simeq \mathsf{E}_{m+n}$\textup{)}
 does not apply to colored operads, so
 $\SCchtop \not\simeq \mathsf{E}_3$.

\item \textup{(Framed $\Etwo \neq \mathsf{E}_3$.)}
 The BV operator $\Delta$ from genus-$1$ sewing equips
 $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$
 with a \emph{framed} $\Etwo$ \textup{(}equivalently, BV\textup{)}
 structure. This is strictly weaker than $\mathsf{E}_3$:
 the BV operator has degree~$1$ \textup{(}same as the Gerstenhaber
 bracket\textup{)}, while an $\mathsf{E}_3$ bracket would require
 degree~$2$. The identity $\Delta^2 = 0$ does not produce a
 degree-$2$ bracket.

\item \textup{($\mathsf{E}_3$ on the derived center.)}
 By the Higher Deligne Conjecture
 \textup{(}Lurie~\cite{HA}, \S5.3; Francis~\cite{Francis2013}\textup{)},
 if $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ is an $\Etwo$-algebra, then
 $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)
 = \HH^*(\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA), \barB^{\Sigma}(\cA))$
 carries a natural $\mathsf{E}_3$ structure. This is the correct
 $\mathsf{E}_3$ object: the derived center, not the symmetric bar.

\item \textup{(Obstruction dimension.)}
 The obstruction to extending $\Etwo$ to $\mathsf{E}_3$ on
 $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ lives in
 $\HH^3_{\Etwo}(\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA), \barB^{\Sigma}(\cA))$. For the Heisenberg
 $\mathfrak{H}_k$: $\dim \HH^3_{\Etwo} = 0$
 \textup{(}$\Lambda^3(\bC^1) = 0$, so the obstruction vanishes
 vacuously; this is consistent with
 $H^*(\barB^{\Sigma}(\mathfrak{H}_k))
 = \operatorname{Sym}(V^*[1])$ being $\Einf$\textup{)}.
 For affine $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$:
 $\dim \HH^3_{\Etwo} = 1$
 \textup{(}$\Lambda^3(\bC^3) = \bC$, a genuine
 obstruction\textup{)}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Part~(i).}
The $\Etwo$-coalgebra structure on $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ comes
directly from the $\mathsf{FM}_2 \simeq \Etwo$ operad acting on local
configurations in $\Conf_k(\bC) \cong \Conf_k(\bR^2)$. Concretely,
one starts from the ordered $E_1$ primitive
$\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ and passes to the
$R$-twisted $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant shadow $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$.
This passage is lossy, but the resulting local object retains the
$\Etwo$ operations detected by $\mathsf{FM}_2$.
At the cohomological level, the product is the cup product on
Hochschild cochains and the bracket is the Gerstenhaber bracket
$[-,-]$ of degree~$1$; the Leibniz rule $[a, bc] = [a,b]c
+ (-1)^{(|a|-1)|b|} b[a,c]$ is the defining relation of a
Gerstenhaber algebra (Getzler--Jones~\cite{GJ}).
For the explicit computation of this bracket on
$\ChirHoch^1(V_k(\fsl_2)) \cong \fsl_2$, see
Proposition~\ref{prop:gerstenhaber-sl2-bracket}.

\textbf{Part~(ii).}
An $\mathsf{E}_3$ structure on a chain complex $C$ requires an
action of $\Conf_k(\bR^3)$ on $C^{\otimes k}$ for all~$k$,
compatible with the $\mathsf{FM}_3$ operad structure. The bar
complex $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ is built from
$\Conf_k(\bC) = \Conf_k(\bR^2)$;
no embedding $\bR^3 \hookrightarrow \bC$ exists
that would provide the additional topological data.
The Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$ has
$\Etwo$ acting on the closed holomorphic sector (points in $\bC$)
and $\Eone$ acting on the open topological sector (points in $\bR$);
these are \emph{different colors} of a colored operad.
Dunn additivity ($\mathsf{E}_m \otimes \mathsf{E}_n
\simeq \mathsf{E}_{m+n}$, \cite{Dunn88})
applies to the tensor product of operads acting on the
\emph{same} underlying object, not to different colors of a
colored operad.

\textbf{Part~(iii).}
The BV operator $\Delta$ has $|\Delta| = 1$ and
$\Delta^2 = 0$. The BV relation (Getzler~\cite{Getzler94}) is
$[a,b] = \Delta(ab) - (\Delta a)b - (-1)^{|a|} a(\Delta b)$,
which defines the degree-$1$ bracket from~$\Delta$; it does not
produce a degree-$2$ bracket. A genuine $\mathsf{E}_3$ bracket
would require a map $A \otimes A \to A[2]$, corresponding to the
fundamental class of $S^2 = \Conf_2(\bR^3)/\bR^+$, which has
degree~$2$. The degree-$1$ BV structure is the framed $\Etwo$
structure, not $\mathsf{E}_3$.

\textbf{Part~(iv).}
The Higher Deligne Conjecture states that for an $\En$-algebra~$A$,
the Hochschild cohomology $\HH^*(A, A)$ carries a natural
$\mathsf{E}_{n+1}$ structure. At $n = 2$: if
$\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ is $\Etwo$, then
$\HH^*(\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA), \barB^{\Sigma}(\cA))$ is $\mathsf{E}_3$.
This is proved by Lurie (\cite{HA}, Theorem~5.3.1.30)
and Francis (\cite{Francis2013}).

\textbf{Part~(v).}
The obstruction to lifting $\Etwo$ to $\mathsf{E}_3$ on an
$\Etwo$-algebra~$A$ lives in the $\Etwo$-Hochschild cohomology
$\HH^3_{\Etwo}(A, A)$, which at the level of the Gerstenhaber
algebra computes degree-$3$ deformations of the $\Etwo$ structure.
For $A = H^*(\barB^{\Sigma}(\mathfrak{H}_k))
= \operatorname{Sym}(V^*[1])$
with $V = \bC$ (one-dimensional): the relevant obstruction space
contains $\Lambda^3(V)$ as a subquotient, which vanishes for
$\dim V = 1$. For $V = \bC^3$ (affine $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$):
$\Lambda^3(\bC^3) = \bC$, contributing a one-dimensional
obstruction space.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The Heisenberg symmetric-bar cohomology is $\Einf$]
\label{rem:heisenberg-bar-einfty}
\index{Heisenberg!bar cohomology Einfty@bar cohomology $\Einf$}
The symmetric bar cohomology
$H^*(\barB^{\Sigma}(\mathfrak{H}_k))
= \operatorname{Sym}(V^*[1])$ is a free graded-commutative algebra,
hence carries the local $\Einf$ structure on cohomology. This
locality statement concerns symmetric-group invariance of operations,
not the absence of OPE poles. It is therefore $\En$ for
\emph{all}~$n$.
However, the quasi-isomorphism
$\barB^{\Sigma}(\mathfrak{H}_k)
\xrightarrow{\sim} H^*(\barB^{\Sigma}(\mathfrak{H}_k))$
used in this section is an $\Etwo$ quasi-isomorphism. The ordered
bar $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\mathfrak{H}_k)$ remains the underlying
$E_1$ primitive, and the passage to
$\barB^{\Sigma}(\mathfrak{H}_k)$ forgets ordered braid data.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The factorization envelope perspective]
\label{rem:en-shadow-envelope}
\index{factorization envelope!En shadow@$\En$ shadow}
The $\En$ shadow obstruction tower of the factorization envelope
$U_M(L)$ of a Lie conformal algebra~$L$ on an $n$-manifold~$M$
is determined by the $\lambda$-bracket data of~$L$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:envelope-shadow}).
By Theorem~\ref{thm:en-shadow-tower}(i), the modular
characteristic $\kappa_{\En}(U_M(L))$ agrees with the chiral
value $\kappa(U_X(L))$ for all~$n$. The shadow depth class
is likewise independent of~$n$ for the standard families.
This reflects the structural principle that the binary-level
data (OPE self-pairing, Killing form, central charge)
is universal across the $\En$ hierarchy, while higher-degree
corrections from configuration-space topology are governed
by formality.
\end{remark}

\begin{conjecture}[Chromatic bar-cobar duality;
\ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:chromatic-bar-cobar}
\index{chromatic tower!bar-cobar duality|textbf}
\index{formal group!bar propagator|textbf}
\index{TMF!factorization coalgebra|textbf}
The rational, trigonometric, and elliptic hierarchy of Yangian-type
chiral algebras is the chromatic tower of one-dimensional formal
groups. The bar propagator on $\bP^1$ is the additive formal
group~$\bG_a$ \textup{(}height $\infty$, rational Yangian\textup{)};
the bar propagator on $\bC^\times$ is the multiplicative formal
group~$\bG_m$ \textup{(}height~$1$, trigonometric Yangian, $K$-theory
orientation\textup{)}; and the bar propagator on an elliptic
curve~$E_\tau$ is the formal group~$\hat E_\tau$ of the elliptic
curve \textup{(}height $\leq 2$, elliptic Yangian, TMF
orientation\textup{)}. The symmetric chiral bar-cobar construction on
the universal family
$\cE \to \cM_{1,1}$ then defines an $\Etwo$-algebra sheaf whose
associated formal group is the universal elliptic formal group,
producing a TMF-module structure on the resulting factorization
coalgebras. The conjecture has three levels of status:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item \textup{(PROVED.)} At each fixed elliptic curve $E_\tau$
 the elliptic bar differential satisfies $d^{\,2} = 0$ by the Fay
 trisecant identity \textup{(}Fay~\cite{Fay73}\textup{)}, as
 established for the period-corrected differential $\Dg{g}$
 in Remark~\ref{rem:sc-higher-genus} and in the higher-genus
 foundations chapter. This gives a well-defined bar coalgebra
 in each fibre of $\cE \to \cM_{1,1}$.
\item \textup{(PARTIAL.)} The symmetric bar cohomology
 $H^*(\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA_E))$ of an elliptic chiral algebra~$\cA_E$
 decomposes into a rational part
 \textup{(}pulled back along the degeneration $E_\tau \leadsto
 \bP^1 \cup \bP^1$ via the additive formal group\textup{)} and a
 genuinely elliptic part \textup{(}supported on the interior of
 $\cM_{1,1}$ and controlled by $\hat E_\tau$\textup{)}. The
 rational piece is controlled by the additive specialization of
 Section~\ref{sec:e3-bar-structure} and is known; the elliptic
 piece is conjecturally computed by the period-integral
 corrections of Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-diff-squares-zero}, but
 a complete identification with an elliptic cohomology class
 remains open.
\item \textup{(OPEN.)} The full TMF-module structure on the
 factorization coalgebra of $\cA_E$ as a sheaf over $\cM_{1,1}$ is
 conjectural. Establishing it requires constructing a
 $\Etwo$-oriented spectrum map from the bar complex of $\cA_E$ to
 the topological modular forms spectrum, in the sense of
 Ando--Hopkins--Strickland~\cite{AHS01}, and verifying that the
 chiral Koszul dual $\cA_E^!$ implements the TMF-dualizing
 structure. A proof of~\textup{(c)} would imply the elliptic
 Yangian of $\cA_E$ is TMF-oriented.
\end{enumerate}
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Chromatic tower and the Yangian hierarchy]
\label{rem:chromatic-tower-correspondence}
\index{chromatic tower!Yangian correspondence|textbf}
\index{Yangian!chromatic duality|textbf}
The chromatic filtration of stable homotopy theory
\textup{(}Ravenel~\cite{Ravenel86}\textup{)} stratifies spectra by
the height of the associated formal group: height~$0$ is rational,
height~$1$ is $K$-theory, height~$2$ is TMF, and so on. The
representation-theoretic tower rational $\to$ trigonometric $\to$
elliptic of Yangian-type quantum groups stratifies chiral algebras
by the formal group of the underlying curve:
$\bG_a$ on $\bP^1$, $\bG_m$ on $\bC^\times$, and $\hat E_\tau$ on
$E_\tau$. Conjecture~\ref{conj:chromatic-bar-cobar} asserts that
these two towers are dual under bar-cobar: the bar propagator on a
curve with formal group~$\hat F$ produces a factorization coalgebra
whose cobar recovers a quantum group whose $R$-matrix is the
classical $r$-matrix attached to~$\hat F$. This is a version of
the Ginzburg--Kapranov~\cite{GK94} operadic Koszul duality, and of
Baranovsky's~\cite{Baranovsky08} universal enveloping construction,
adapted from the general $\En$-operad setting to the one-parameter
family of Yangians. The novelty is that the chromatic filtration
on the topological side implies a stratification of $r(z)$ by
formal-group height on the chiral side, and conversely the
one-parameter deformation on the chiral side implies the height
filtration on the factorization coalgebra: the correspondence is
strictly one-directional at the current status, and a complete
equivalence awaits resolution of part~\textup{(c)}.
\end{remark}


\begin{remark}[Feynman transform and genus-$1$ Koszul duality]
\label{rem:ft-genus-one-koszul}
\index{Feynman transform!genus-1 truncation}
\index{Koszul duality!genus-1}
The Feynman transform $\mathrm{FT}_{\mathcal{M}od}(\cA)$
of Getzler--Kapranov~\cite[\S4--5]{GeK98}
(Definition~\ref{def:feynman-transform})
admits a natural filtration by genus:
\[
\mathrm{FT}^{(\leq g)}_{\mathcal{M}od}(\cA)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{h=0}^{g}
\;\bigoplus_{\substack{n \\ 2h-2+n>0}}
\;\bigoplus_{\substack{\Gamma \in \mathcal{G}_{h,n}}}
\bigotimes_{v \in V(\Gamma)} \cA(\mathrm{val}(v))
\otimes
\bigotimes_{e \in E(\Gamma)} \det(e),
\]
where $\mathcal{G}_{h,n}$ denotes stable graphs of type $(h,n)$
and $\mathrm{FT}^{(\leq g)}$ retains only graphs $\Gamma$
with $\sum_v g_v + b_1(\Gamma) \leq g$.
The full Feynman transform is the colimit
$\mathrm{FT}_{\mathcal{M}od}(\cA)
= \varinjlim_g \mathrm{FT}^{(\leq g)}_{\mathcal{M}od}(\cA)$.
By Theorem~\ref{thm:prism-higher-genus}(ii), the genus-$g$
piece $\bar{B}^{(g)}(\cA)$ of the full bar complex
$\bar{B}^{\mathrm{full}}(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar{\cA})$
is the genus-$g$ summand of this Feynman transform.
\\
\indent
At genus~$0$, $\mathrm{FT}^{(\leq 0)}$ recovers the
classical (cyclic) bar-cobar adjunction: the only stable graphs
are trees, and edge contraction reproduces the operadic bar
differential.
The genus-$1$ truncation $\mathrm{FT}^{(\leq 1)}$ is the
first extension beyond classical Koszul duality.
It includes stable graphs $\Gamma$ with first Betti number
$b_1(\Gamma) = 1$ (one-loop graphs) as well as trees with a
single genus-$1$ vertex.
The genus-$1$ bar complex $\bar{B}^{(1)}(\cA)$ captures
exactly these contributions; its differential encodes the
non-separating self-gluing
$\xi_{\mathrm{ns}}\colon
\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{0,n+2} \to
\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{1,n}$
together with the separating gluings that produce genus-$1$
curves from two genus-$0$ components.
\\
\indent
The modular characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$ first appears as a
genuine obstruction at this genus level: the genus-$1$ free
energy satisfies
$\mathrm{obs}_1(\cA) = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_1$
(\textsc{uniform-weight}; unconditional at $g = 1$;
Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality}).
Thus the passage from $\mathrm{FT}^{(\leq 0)}$ to
$\mathrm{FT}^{(\leq 1)}$ is precisely the passage from
classical Koszul duality (which sees only the binary
self-pairing) to the first modular correction controlled
by~$\kappa$.
The Feynman transform involution
$\mathrm{FT} \circ \mathrm{FT} \simeq \mathrm{Id}$
(Corollary~\ref{cor:feynman-transform-involution}) guarantees
that the information in $\bar{B}^{(1)}(\cA)$, combined with
the genus-$0$ data, determines the algebra $\cA$ up to the
genus-$2$ and higher obstructions: each genus level of the
Feynman transform refines the reconstruction by one modular
correction.
\end{remark}

\begin{conjecture}[Spectrum-level lift of the bar complex;
\ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:spectrum-bar-lift}
\index{bar complex!spectrum-level lift|textbf}
\index{THH!bar-complex comparison|textbf}
\index{chromatic tower!bar complex|textbf}
Let $A$ be an augmented dg algebra on the genus-$0$ Koszul locus,
and suppose it admits a spectrum-level refinement, again denoted
$A$. Then the classical bar coalgebra
\[
 B(A) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar{A}),
 \qquad
 \bar{A} = \ker(\epsilon),
 \qquad
 |s^{-1}v| = |v| - 1
\]
admits a spectrum-level lift $B^{\mathrm{Sp}}(A)$ with the following
properties:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Coalgebra spectrum.)}
 $B^{\mathrm{Sp}}(A)$ is a coalgebra object in spectra, and its
 rationalization recovers the Eilenberg--Mac Lane realization of the
 dg coalgebra $B(A)$. Under this comparison, the deconcatenation
 coproduct on $B(A)$ is the rational shadow of a map of spectra
 \[
 \Delta^{\mathrm{Sp}}\colon
 B^{\mathrm{Sp}}(A)
 \longrightarrow
 B^{\mathrm{Sp}}(A) \wedge B^{\mathrm{Sp}}(A).
 \]
\item \textup{(THH comparison.)}
 The cyclic bar construction on the same spectrum-level object
 computes $\mathrm{THH}(A)$, and the passage from the classical bar
 complex to Hochschild chains is the rational shadow of the passage
 from $B^{\mathrm{Sp}}(A)$ to $\mathrm{THH}(A)$. In this sense the
 chiral bar-cobar adjunction $\barBch \dashv \Omegach$ admits a
 spectrum-level shadow in which the bar coalgebra spectrum controls
 the open sector and $\mathrm{THH}(A)$ is the first closed trace.
\item \textup{(Chromatic lift.)}
 If one replaces the classical rational bar complex by its
 Eilenberg--Mac Lane spectrum $H(B(A))$, then
 $L_{K(n)}H(B(A)) \simeq 0$ for every $n \geq 1$. By contrast the
 genuine spectrum-level lift can satisfy
 $L_{K(n)}B^{\mathrm{Sp}}(A) \not\simeq 0$, and the resulting
 chromatic tower of $B^{\mathrm{Sp}}(A)$ records higher-categorical
 shadow data invisible to the rational dg coalgebra $B(A)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Evidence]
\label{rem:spectrum-bar-lift-evidence}
Three pieces of evidence support
Conjecture~\ref{conj:spectrum-bar-lift}.
First, Theorem~\ref{thm:af-pkd} and
Proposition~\ref{prop:refines-af} already place the
$\Etwo$ bar construction inside factorization homology, whose native
target is spectral. The dg coalgebra $B(A)$ therefore sits in a
context where a coalgebra spectrum is the natural refinement.

Second, Theorem~\ref{thm:thqg-annulus-trace} identifies the annulus
trace with Hochschild homology and writes it by a cyclic bar model.
For an associative ring spectrum, factorization homology over~$S^1$
is $\mathrm{THH}$, so the dg passage from the bar complex to
Hochschild chains has the same formal shape as a spectrum-level
comparison from $B^{\mathrm{Sp}}(A)$ to $\mathrm{THH}(A)$. This is
the closest theorem-level shadow of the conjectured lift.

Third, Conjecture~\ref{conj:chromatic-bar-cobar} already predicts a
height-sensitive TMF package for the elliptic bar construction. That
prediction cannot be detected by the rational chain model alone: if
the spectrum-level object were only $H(B(A))$, every positive
chromatic localization would vanish. Any nontrivial
$K(n)$-local layer of $B^{\mathrm{Sp}}(A)$ must therefore come from
genuinely spectral data, and this is exactly the data forgotten by
the classical bar complex.
\end{remark}
