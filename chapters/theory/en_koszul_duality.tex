\chapter{\texorpdfstring{$\En$ Koszul duality and higher-dimensional bar complexes}{En Koszul duality and higher-dimensional bar complexes}}
\label{ch:en-koszul-duality}

\index{En algebra@$\En$ algebra!Koszul duality|textbf}
\index{bar complex!higher-dimensional|textbf}

For $\En$ algebras, the bar-cobar adjunction replaces the logarithmic
propagator $d\log(z-w)$ with a closed $(n{-}1)$-form, the Arnold
relations with the Totaro relations, and chiral Koszul duality with
$\En$ Koszul duality.  The underlying construction is the same:
duality is mediated by a convolution algebra on a compactified
configuration space.  The passage from $n = 1$
(associative/$A_\infty$) through $n = 2$ (the holomorphic refinement
recovering the chiral bar complex) to $n = 3$ (Chern--Simons,
Vassiliev invariants) and beyond exhibits bar-cobar duality as a
single construction parametrised by manifold dimension, with the
formality phenomena of Kontsevich and Tamarkin as shadows of the
Koszul property in each dimension.

Throughout this monograph, the bar differential has been built from
configuration spaces of points on a \emph{curve}: the propagator
$\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$ is a meromorphic $1$-form, its
singularities are controlled by Arnold relations, and the bar-cobar
adjunction lives over the chiral operad.  This chapter asks a
different question: what remains true when one leaves the
Beilinson--Drinfeld curve setting and passes to genuine little-disks
factorization on real $n$-manifolds?  Then the propagator becomes a
closed $(n-1)$-form, collision strata are governed by
\emph{Totaro relations}, and the bar-cobar adjunction governs
$\En$-algebras.

Two axes must be kept separate.  Inside chiral geometry on a fixed
curve one has the hierarchy
$\Einf\text{-chiral} \subset \Pinf\text{-chiral} \subset \Eone\text{-chiral}$,
so the passage from Heisenberg to Yangian changes commutativity and
locality, not manifold dimension.  The topological ladder is instead
indexed by real manifold dimension: $n = 1$ is the classical
associative/$A_\infty$ bar-cobar theory on intervals or circles,
$n = 2$ is the oriented-surface setting whose holomorphic refinement
recovers the chiral bar complex on a complex curve, and $n = 3$
interfaces with Chern--Simons theory and Vassiliev invariants
(Chapter~\ref{ch:kontsevich-integral}).  At $n = 2$ the bar complex
governs $\Etwo$-algebras (the natural algebraic structure on braided
monoidal categories) and connects to Kontsevich's formality theorem
for the little $2$-disks operad.

\begin{theorem}[Bridge theorem: $\En$ bar-cobar and the chiral $\Eone$ engine; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:en-chiral-bridge}
\index{En algebra@$\En$ algebra!bridge to chiral bar-cobar|textbf}
The chiral bar-cobar adjunction of this monograph
\textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}\textup{)}
is the holomorphic refinement of the $n = 2$ chapter of $\En$
Koszul duality.  Precisely:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The $\En$ bar complex on an oriented real $n$-manifold~$M$ is
  built from configuration spaces $\Conf_k(M)$ with propagator a
  closed $(n{-}1)$-form and collision relations governed by the Totaro
  presentation
  \textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:totaro-presentation}\textup{)}.
  At $n = 2$, this specializes to the Arnold presentation on
  $\Conf_k(\bC)$ and the propagator becomes the logarithmic $1$-form
  $\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$, recovering the chiral bar complex
  on a complex curve.
\item The $\Eone$ bar-cobar adjunction of this monograph
  \textup{(}operating on chiral algebras with possibly non-commutative
  OPE, including Yangians and R-matrix vertex algebras\textup{)} is
  \emph{not} the topological $\En$ bar-cobar at $n = 1$
  \textup{(}which governs ordinary associative algebras on
  intervals\textup{)}, but rather the chiral specialization at
  $n = 2$ with the commutativity constraint dropped.  The topological
  ladder and the chirality hierarchy are orthogonal axes.
\item The formality hierarchy organizes how much of the bar-cobar
  adjunction reduces to cohomological data:
  \begin{center}
  \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
  \begin{tabular}{ccl}
  $n$ & Formality & Source \\
  \hline
  $\infty$ & $\Einf$ formal & tautological \textup{(}commutative operad\textup{)} \\
  $2$ & $\Etwo$ formal & Kontsevich~\cite{Kon99}, Tamarkin \\
  $1$ & $\Eone$ \emph{non-formal} in general
       & obstructed by $A_\infty$ Massey products
  \end{tabular}
  \end{center}
  The chiral bar-cobar adjunction on curves inherits $\Etwo$ formality
  from Kontsevich's theorem when the underlying chiral algebra is
  $\Einf$-chiral \textup{(}symmetric OPE\textup{)}.  For
  $\Eone$-chiral algebras, the transferred $A_\infty$ structure is
  genuinely non-formal: higher operations $m_k$ for $k \ge 3$ carry
  irreducible homotopical content.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Part~(i) is the content of Remark~\ref{rem:arnold-totaro}:
the Arnold generators on $\Conf_k(\bC)$ are the Totaro generators
at $n = 2$, and the chiral bar differential is the specialization of
the $\Etwo$ bar differential to a complex curve.

Part~(ii): the chiral hierarchy
$\Einf\text{-chiral} \subset \Pinf\text{-chiral} \subset
\Eone\text{-chiral}$ on a fixed curve changes the commutativity
constraint on the OPE, not the manifold dimension.  An
$\Eone$-chiral algebra on a complex curve is an $\Etwo$-algebra
(real dimension~$2$) with the full symmetric-group action on
$\Conf_k(\bC)$ replaced by the braid-group action; it is not an
$\Eone$-algebra in the topological sense (which would be an
$A_\infty$-algebra on an interval).

Part~(iii): $\Etwo$ formality is Kontsevich's theorem~\cite{Kon99}
for the little $2$-disks operad; $\Einf$ formality is tautological;
$\Eone$ non-formality is witnessed by non-trivial Massey products
in the bar cohomology of non-formal $A_\infty$-algebras (e.g., the
commutative algebra $\bC[x]/x^3$), which survive as higher
operations $m_k$ on the transferred structure.  The chiral
inheritance follows from the specialization map
$\En\text{-bar} \to \text{chiral bar}$ at $n = 2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Formality hierarchy as MC obstruction truncation]
\label{prop:en-formality-mc-truncation}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The formality hierarchy $\Einf \supset \Etwo \supset \Eone$ corresponds to truncation of the MC obstruction tower:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $\Einf$-formality: the full modular convolution algebra $\gAmod$ is formal; all higher brackets $\ell_n^{(g)} = 0$ for $n \geq 3$.
\item $\Etwo$-formality: the genus-$0$ convolution algebra $\mathfrak{g}^{(0)}_\cA$ is formal; the shadow obstruction tower terminates at arity~$2$ (chiral Koszulness, Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta}).
\item $\Eone$-formality: the ordered convolution algebra $\mathfrak{g}^{\Eone}_\cA$ carries a formal $A_\infty$-model; the associative shadow obstruction tower terminates.
The ordered bar complex $\barB^{\Eone}(\cA)$ on ordered configuration spaces $\mathrm{FM}^{\mathrm{ord}}_n(\mathbb{C})$ carries the associative shadow obstruction tower $(\Theta^{\Eone}_\cA)^{\leq r}$; formality of the ordered convolution algebra is the vanishing of the transferred $A_\infty$-operations $m_n^{\mathrm{tr}}$ for $n \geq 3$ (Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul}).
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each formality level kills the obstruction classes $o_{r+1}(\cA)$ in the cyclic deformation complex above the corresponding arity threshold (Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-formality-low-arity} for arities $2$, $3$, $4$).
The $\Etwo$ case identifies shadow-tower termination at arity~$2$ with the $\Etwo$-formality of $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$, and the $\Eone$ case is the associative analogue via the ordered bar complex (Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul}).
\end{proof}

\begin{convention}[Two axes in this chapter]
\label{conv:en-two-axes}
In this chapter, bare $\En$ always denotes little-disks/topological
factorization on real $n$-manifolds.  The earlier hierarchy
$\Einf$-chiral/$\Pinf$-chiral/$\Eone$-chiral remains curve-based.
Hence the chiral bar complex on a complex curve belongs to the
topological ladder at $n = 2$, while the $n = 1$ case is the
classical associative/$A_\infty$ bar construction.
\end{convention}

\section{Higher-dimensional configuration spaces}
\label{sec:higher-dim-config}
\index{configuration space!higher-dimensional}

\begin{definition}[Configuration space of \texorpdfstring{$\bR^n$}{R-n}]
\label{def:config-rn}
For a smooth oriented $n$-manifold~$M$, the \emph{ordered
configuration space of $k$~points} is
\[
\Conf_k(M) \;=\;
\bigl\{(x_1, \ldots, x_k) \in M^k
  \mid x_i \neq x_j \text{ for } i \neq j\bigr\}.
\]
For $M = \bR^n$, the space $\Conf_k(\bR^n)$ is a smooth manifold
of real dimension~$nk$, and its cohomology admits an explicit
generators-and-relations presentation due to Arnold ($n = 2$, i.e., $\bR^n = \bC$) and
Totaro (general $n$).
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Arnold presentation {\cite{Arnold69}}; \texorpdfstring{$\bC \cong \bR^2$}{C = R2};
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:arnold-presentation}
\index{Arnold relations!presentation}
The cohomology ring $H^*(\Conf_k(\bC); \bQ)$ is the graded-commutative
algebra generated by classes $\eta_{ij} \in H^1$,
$1 \leq i < j \leq k$, subject to the \emph{Arnold relations}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:arnold-rel-en}
\eta_{ij} \wedge \eta_{jk}
+ \eta_{jk} \wedge \eta_{ki}
+ \eta_{ki} \wedge \eta_{ij} \;=\; 0
\quad (i < j < k).
\end{equation}
This is the Orlik--Solomon algebra $\mathrm{OS}_k$
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:os-cohomology-config})}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Totaro presentation, general \texorpdfstring{$n$}{n} {\cite{Totaro96, Coh76}};
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:totaro-presentation}
\index{Totaro relations|textbf}
\index{configuration space!cohomology ring}
For $n \geq 2$, the cohomology ring
$H^*(\Conf_k(\bR^n); \bQ)$ is the graded-commutative algebra
generated by classes $\alpha_{ij} \in H^{n-1}$,
$1 \leq i \neq j \leq k$, subject to the relations:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Symmetry)}\quad
  $\alpha_{ij} = (-1)^n \alpha_{ji}$;
\item \textup{(Totaro relations)}\quad
  $\alpha_{ij} \cdot \alpha_{jk}
  + \alpha_{jk} \cdot \alpha_{ki}
  + \alpha_{ki} \cdot \alpha_{ij} = 0$
  for distinct $i, j, k$;
\item \textup{(Degree)}\quad
  $\alpha_{ij}^2 = 0$ when $n$ is odd;
  when $n$ is even, $\alpha_{ij}^2$ is determined by the Euler class.
\end{enumerate}
At $n = 2$, after identifying $\bR^2 \cong \bC$ and choosing the
oriented generator of $H^1(\Conf_2(\bR^2))$, one recovers Arnold's
three-term relation on $\Conf_k(\bC)$.  By contrast, $n = 1$ is the
degree-$0$ associative setting of ordered points on a line, not the
chiral curve case.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
See Totaro~\cite{Totaro96}, Theorem~1; also
Cohen~\cite{Coh76}, Theorem~A for the formality statement.
The key geometric input is that projection maps
$\Conf_k(\bR^n) \to \Conf_2(\bR^n)$ induce pullbacks of the
fundamental class of $S^{n-1}$ (the linking sphere), and all
relations among these pullbacks are generated by the three-term
relation (ii).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Arnold as Totaro at \texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2}]
\label{rem:arnold-totaro}
\index{Arnold relations!as Totaro specialization}
In the $\En$ hierarchy, $n$ is the \emph{real} dimension.
The classical Arnold generators on $\Conf_k(\bC)$ are Totaro
generators at $n = 2$; our chiral bar complex uses $n = 2$:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ccc}
$\En$-level & Manifold & Bar complex \\
\hline
$n = 1$ & interval/circle & associative bar (classical) \\
$n = 2$ & surface/curve & chiral bar (this monograph) \\
$n = 3$ & $3$-manifold & Chern--Simons bar \\
general $n$ & $n$-manifold & $\En$ bar
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Main Theorems~A, B, C are the holomorphic refinement of the
$n = 2$ chapter of $\En$ Koszul duality.
\end{remark}


\subsection{\texorpdfstring{Fulton--MacPherson compactification in dimension $n$}{Fulton--MacPherson compactification in dimension n}}
\label{subsec:fm-general-n}
\index{Fulton--MacPherson compactification!higher-dimensional}

\begin{definition}[FM compactification of \texorpdfstring{$\Conf_k(\bR^n)$}{Conf-k(R-n)}]
\label{def:fm-rn}
The \emph{Fulton--MacPherson compactification}
$\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ is the real oriented blowup of $(\bR^n)^k$
along all partial diagonals, ordered by reverse inclusion.
Equivalently (Axelrod--Singer, Kontsevich), it is the closure of
the image of the map
\[
\Conf_k(\bR^n)
\;\hookrightarrow\;
(\bR^n)^k
\times \prod_{i<j} S^{n-1}
\times \prod_{|S| \geq 2} [0, \infty]
\]
recording positions, relative directions
$\theta_{ij} = (x_i - x_j)/|x_i - x_j| \in S^{n-1}$,
and relative distances.  The result is a smooth manifold with
corners whose boundary strata are indexed by nested partitions
of $\{1, \ldots, k\}$, the same combinatorial data as the
Stasheff associahedron ($n = 1$) or the FM operad ($n \geq 2$).
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Boundary strata and operadic structure;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere{} \cite{FM94}]
\label{prop:fm-boundary-strata}
\index{Fulton--MacPherson!boundary strata}
The codimension-$1$ boundary strata of
$\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ are indexed by subsets
$S \subset \{1, \ldots, k\}$ with $|S| \geq 2$, and are
diffeomorphic to
\[
\partial_S \overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)
\;\cong\;
\overline{\Conf}_{k-|S|+1}(\bR^n)
\times
\overline{\Conf}_{|S|}(\bR^n).
\]
This factorization equips $\{\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)\}_{k \geq 0}$
with the structure of a topological operad, the
\emph{Fulton--MacPherson operad} $\mathsf{FM}_n$, which is a model
for the $\En$ operad \textup{(}i.e., weakly equivalent to the
little $n$-disks operad\textup{)}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The operadic structure is due to Getzler--Jones~\cite{GJ}
and Salvatore; the equivalence $\mathsf{FM}_n \simeq \En$ is
proved by Salvatore--Longoni~\cite{SalvatoreLongoni05} for $n \geq 2$ and follows from
Stasheff's theorem for $n = 1$.
\end{proof}

\section{\texorpdfstring{The $\En$ propagator}{The En propagator}}
\label{sec:en-propagator}
\index{propagator!$\En$}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} propagator]
\label{def:en-propagator}
\index{propagator!Green's form}
An \emph{$\En$ propagator} on an oriented $n$-manifold~$M$ is a
smooth closed $(n-1)$-form
\[
G \;\in\; \Omega^{n-1}(M \times M \setminus \Delta)
\]
satisfying the following properties:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Poincar\'e dual of diagonal)}\quad
  $G$ represents the Poincar\'e dual of the diagonal
  $\Delta \subset M \times M$ in the sense that for any
  closed form $\eta \in \Omega^*(M)$,
  \[
  \int_{M \times M} G \wedge \pi_2^* \eta
  \;=\; \int_M \eta,
  \]
  where $\pi_2\colon M \times M \to M$ is the second projection.
\item \textup{(Singularity)}\quad
  Near the diagonal $\Delta$, in geodesic coordinates,
  \[
  G \;\sim\;
  \frac{1}{\mathrm{vol}(S^{n-1})}\;
  \frac{r_1\, dr_2 \wedge \cdots \wedge dr_n
  - r_2\, dr_1 \wedge dr_3 \wedge \cdots \wedge dr_n
  + \cdots}{|r|^n}
  \;+\; \text{smooth},
  \]
  where $r = x_1 - x_2$ and $\mathrm{vol}(S^{n-1}) = 2\pi^{n/2}/\Gamma(n/2)$.
\item \textup{(Symmetry)}\quad
  Under the involution $\sigma\colon (x_1, x_2) \mapsto (x_2, x_1)$,
  $\sigma^* G = (-1)^n G$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[\texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2}: the chiral propagator on a complex curve]
\label{ex:n2-chiral-propagator}
For $M = \bC$ (real dimension $2$, but complex dimension $1$, the
chiral case), the propagator is
$G = \eta_{12} = d\log(z_1 - z_2)$, a meromorphic $1$-form.
It is closed ($dG = 0$ away from $z_1 = z_2$), represents the
Poincar\'e dual of the diagonal, and satisfies
$\sigma^* G = d\log(z_2 - z_1) = d\log(-(z_1-z_2)) = d\log(-1) + d\log(z_1-z_2) = G$,
since $d\log(-1) = 0$ (differential of a constant).  Residues along $z_1 = z_2$ extract OPE data.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[\texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2}: the \texorpdfstring{$\Etwo$}{E2} propagator on \texorpdfstring{$\bR^2$}{R2}]
\label{ex:n2-propagator}
For $M = \bR^2$, the propagator is
\[
G \;=\;
\frac{1}{2\pi}\;
\frac{(x_1 - x_2)\, dy_1 - (y_1 - y_2)\, dx_1}
     {(x_1 - x_2)^2 + (y_1 - y_2)^2}
\;=\;
\frac{1}{2\pi}\, d\arg(z_1 - z_2),
\]
a closed $1$-form on $\Conf_2(\bR^2)$.  This is the angular form
(winding number kernel): integration over a small circle
$S^1 \subset \bR^2$ around $x_2$ gives $1$.  Note that
$G = (1/2\pi)\, d\arg(z_1 - z_2)$ is exactly the
\emph{Kontsevich propagator} on $S^1 \subset \bC$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:propagator-restriction}).  The $\Etwo$
propagator on a surface and the chiral propagator on a complex curve
are thus two aspects of the same object: the former is real-valued
and topological, the latter is holomorphic and algebraic.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[\texorpdfstring{$n = 3$}{n = 3}: the Chern--Simons propagator]
\label{ex:n3-propagator}
\index{Chern--Simons!propagator}
For $M = \bR^3$, the propagator is
\[
G \;=\;
\frac{1}{4\pi}\;
\frac{\epsilon_{abc}\, r^a\, dr^b \wedge dr^c}{|r|^3},
\]
where $r = x_1 - x_2 \in \bR^3$.  This is the closed $2$-form on
$\Conf_2(\bR^3)$ representing the linking number:
$\int_{S^2} G = 1$.  In the Axelrod--Singer perturbative expansion of
Chern--Simons theory~\cite{Kon94}, this propagator appears as the
kernel of the gauge-fixed Green's operator $d^{-1}$.
\end{example}

\begin{proposition}[Residue as linking sphere integral;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:linking-sphere-residue}
\index{residue!linking sphere}
For an $\En$ propagator $G$ on $\bR^n$ and a smooth function
$f$ on a neighborhood of the diagonal, the \emph{$\En$ residue} is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:en-residue}
\Res_\Delta(G \wedge f)
\;:=\;
\lim_{\varepsilon \to 0}
\int_{|x_1 - x_2| = \varepsilon} G \cdot f(x_1, x_2)
\;=\; f(x, x),
\end{equation}
where the integral is over the linking sphere
$S^{n-1}_\varepsilon \subset \Conf_2(\bR^n)$ of radius~$\varepsilon$
around the diagonal.  This generalizes the residue operation
$\Res_{z_1 = z_2}$ in the chiral ($n = 2$) case.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By the singularity condition (ii) of
Definition~\ref{def:en-propagator}, $G$ restricts on the
sphere $|r| = \varepsilon$ to the standard volume form on $S^{n-1}$
(normalized to total volume~$1$) plus terms vanishing as
$\varepsilon \to 0$.  Hence the integral extracts the value
$f(x, x)$, exactly as a contour integral residue extracts the
leading Laurent coefficient.
\end{proof}

\section{\texorpdfstring{The $\Etwo$ bar complex}{The E2 bar complex}}
\label{sec:e2-bar}
\index{bar complex!$\Etwo$}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\Etwo$}{E2} bar complex]
\label{def:e2-bar-complex}
\index{bar complex!$\Etwo$|textbf}
Let $A$ be an $\Etwo$-algebra (equivalently, an algebra over
the little $2$-disks operad).  The \emph{$\Etwo$ bar complex}
is the chain complex
\[
\barB_{\Etwo}(A)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{k \geq 1}
\Omega^*\bigl(\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^2)\bigr)
\otimes_{\Sigma_k} (s^{-1} \bar{A})^{\otimes k},
\]
with differential
\begin{equation}\label{eq:e2-bar-diff}
d_{\Etwo}
\;=\;
d_{\mathrm{dR}}
\;+\;
\sum_{S \subset \{1, \ldots, k\},\, |S| \geq 2}
\Res_{\partial_S}\bigl(G_S \wedge \mu_S\bigr),
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $G_S = \bigwedge_{i < j,\; i, j \in S} \alpha_{ij}$ is the
  product of propagators for pairs within the
  colliding subset~$S$,
\item $\Res_{\partial_S}$ is the linking-sphere residue along the
  boundary stratum $\partial_S$ of
  $\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^2)$
  \textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:linking-sphere-residue})},
\item $\mu_S\colon \bar{A}^{\otimes |S|} \to \bar{A}$ is the
  $\Etwo$-multiplication indexed by the FM operad element
  corresponding to the stratum~$\partial_S$.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[\texorpdfstring{$d^2 = 0$}{d squared = 0} from Totaro relations;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:e2-d-squared}
\index{Totaro relations!and $d^2 = 0$}
The differential $d_{\Etwo}$ of
Definition~\textup{\ref{def:e2-bar-complex}} satisfies
$d_{\Etwo}^2 = 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The proof follows the same structure as the chiral case
(Corollary~\ref{cor:bar-d-squared-zero-arnold}), with the Arnold
relations replaced by Totaro relations.

We compute $d_{\Etwo}^2$ on a bar element
$\alpha \in \Omega^*(\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^2))
\otimes (s^{-1} \bar{A})^{\otimes k}$.
The square decomposes into three terms:
\[
d_{\Etwo}^2
\;=\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{dR}}^2}_{= 0}
\;+\;
\underbrace{[d_{\mathrm{dR}},\, d_{\mathrm{res}}]}_{(\star)}
\;+\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{res}}^2}_{(\star\star)},
\]
where $d_{\mathrm{res}} = \sum_S \Res_{\partial_S}(G_S \wedge \mu_S)$.

For $(\star)$: the de~Rham differential commutes with the residue
operation because $G$ is closed ($dG = 0$), so the cross-term
$[d_{\mathrm{dR}}, d_{\mathrm{res}}]$ reduces to
$\sum_S \Res_{\partial_S}(dG_S \wedge \mu_S) = 0$.

For $(\star\star)$: the square $d_{\mathrm{res}}^2$ involves
iterated residues along pairs of boundary strata.  For three
distinct points $i, j, k$, the iterated residue
$\Res_{\partial_{\{i,j\}}} \circ \Res_{\partial_{\{j,k\}}}$
plus cyclic permutations gives a sum of three terms.  The
Totaro relation
\[
\alpha_{ij} \cdot \alpha_{jk}
+ \alpha_{jk} \cdot \alpha_{ki}
+ \alpha_{ki} \cdot \alpha_{ij} \;=\; 0
\qquad
\text{in } H^{2(n-1)}(\Conf_3(\bR^n))
\]
at $n = 2$ ensures that this sum vanishes.  More precisely: each
term in $d_{\mathrm{res}}^2$ corresponds to a codimension-$2$
boundary stratum of $\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^2)$, and the Totaro
relation is exactly the statement that the codimension-$2$
contributions cancel in cohomology.

For nested collisions (e.g., $\{i, j\} \subset \{i, j, k\}$),
the iterated residue factors through the operadic composition
$\mu_{\{i,j,k\}} = \mu_{\{i,j\}} \circ \mu_{\{j,k\}}$, and
cancellation follows from the $\Etwo$-algebra axiom (associativity
up to coherent homotopy, encoded by the FM operad structure).

For the algebraic contribution: iterated residues at the same
stratum $S$ produce the square $\mu_S \circ \mu_S$, which vanishes
by the $A_\infty$ relations encoded in the $\Etwo$ structure.

The argument generalizes Idrissi's result~\cite{Idrissi22} that
Poincar\'e duality on configuration spaces ensures $d^2 = 0$ for
the propagator-based differential, specialized to $n = 2$ with
explicit Totaro generators.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Comparison with the chiral bar complex]
\label{rem:e2-vs-chiral}
\index{bar complex!$\Etwo$ vs chiral}
The $\Etwo$ bar complex (Definition~\ref{def:e2-bar-complex})
and the chiral bar complex (Chapter~\ref{chap:bar-cobar}) differ in propagator and dependence:
the chiral complex uses the holomorphic $\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$ and depends on the complex structure,
while the $\Etwo$ complex uses the topological $G = (1/2\pi)\, d\arg(z_1 - z_2)$ and depends only on oriented topology.
For a chiral algebra $\cA$ on~$X$,
$\barB_{\Etwo}(\cA^{\mathrm{top}}) \simeq \barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)^{\mathrm{top}}$.
The chiral bar complex carries strictly more information: genus-$g$ quantum corrections
$\kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality})
live in the chiral bar complex but not in the $\Etwo$ bar complex.
\end{remark}

\section{\texorpdfstring{$\En$ Koszul duality}{En Koszul duality}}
\label{sec:en-koszul-duality}
\index{Koszul duality!$\En$|textbf}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} bar-cobar adjunction]
\label{def:en-bar-cobar}
For each $n \geq 1$, the \emph{$\En$ bar functor} and \emph{$\En$
cobar functor} are defined by:
\begin{align}
\barB_{\En} &\colon
  \En\text{-}\mathrm{Alg} \;\longrightarrow\;
  \En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg},
  \label{eq:en-bar}\\
\Omega_{\En} &\colon
  \En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg} \;\longrightarrow\;
  \En\text{-}\mathrm{Alg},
  \label{eq:en-cobar}
\end{align}
where $\En\text{-}\mathrm{Alg}$ denotes the $\infty$-category of
$\En$-algebras in chain complexes and $\En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg}$
denotes the $\infty$-category of $\En$-coalgebras.  The bar
construction uses the chain complex
$C_*(\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n))$ with the differential of
\eqref{eq:e2-bar-diff} generalized to dimension~$n$, and the cobar
construction is its linear dual.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} Koszul duality;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:en-koszul-duality}
\index{Koszul duality!$\En$}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Lurie~\cite{HA}, \S5.2)}\quad
  The functors $\barB_{\En}$ and $\Omega_{\En}$ form an adjoint pair
  \[
  \barB_{\En}
  \;\dashv\;
  \Omega_{\En}
  \colon
  \En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg}
  \;\rightleftarrows\;
  \En\text{-}\mathrm{Alg}.
  \]
\item \textup{(Francis~\cite{Francis2013}, Theorem~4.20)}\quad
  For an $\En$-algebra $A$ that is \emph{$\En$-Koszul}
  \textup{(}the $\En$ bar spectral sequence collapses\textup{)},
  the counit
  $\Omega_{\En}(\barB_{\En}(A)) \xrightarrow{\sim} A$
  is a quasi-isomorphism.
\item \textup{(Ayala--Francis~\cite{AF15}, Theorem~7.8)}\quad
  For a closed oriented $n$-manifold~$M$ and an $\En$-algebra~$A$,
  Poincar\'e--Koszul duality gives a natural equivalence:
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:poincare-koszul}
  \int_M A
  \;\simeq\;
  \int_M A^\vee[-n],
  \end{equation}
  where $\int_M$ denotes $\En$-factorization homology and $A^\vee$
  is the $\En$-Koszul dual coalgebra.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Chain-level content of this monograph]
\label{rem:chain-level-en}
Ayala--Francis prove part~(iii) at the $\infty$-categorical level.
Main Theorems~A, B, C are the chain-level refinement at $n = 2$:
explicit propagators, residue formulas, and spectral sequences.
The genus-$g$ quantum corrections (Theorem~B) and complementarity
(Theorem~C) are specific to curves with complex structure, invisible
to the topological $\Etwo$ theory.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Recovery of chiral bar-cobar at \texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2};
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:n2-recovery}
For a chiral algebra $\cA$ on a complex curve~$X$, the chiral
bar-cobar adjunction of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}} is recovered
from the $\Etwo$ bar-cobar adjunction
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:en-koszul-duality}(i))} by
equipping the underlying $\Etwo$-algebra $\cA^{\mathrm{top}}$ with
the holomorphic propagator $\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$ in
place of the topological propagator $G$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The holomorphic propagator $\eta_{ij}$ on a curve~$X$ is a specific
representative of the $\Etwo$ propagator class
$[\alpha_{ij}] \in H^1(\Conf_2(X))$; different representatives give
quasi-isomorphic bar complexes (since they differ by exact forms,
and the bar differential is insensitive to exact corrections in
cohomology).  The Arnold relations on~$X$ are the Totaro relations at
$n = 2$, so $d^2 = 0$ follows from
Theorem~\ref{thm:e2-d-squared}.  The residue operation
$\Res_{z_i = z_j}$ is the $n = 2$ linking-sphere residue
(Proposition~\ref{prop:linking-sphere-residue}): the linking sphere
$S^1$ around the diagonal in a surface is the contour of integration
in the OPE residue formula.
\end{proof}

\section{Connection to Ayala--Francis}
\label{sec:ayala-francis-connection}
\index{Ayala--Francis!chain-level refinement}
\index{Poincar\'e--Koszul duality!chain-level}

Ayala--Francis~\cite{AF15} establish Poincar\'e--Koszul duality as
an equivalence of $\infty$-categories:

\begin{theorem}[Poincar\'e--Koszul duality, AF {\cite{AF15}};
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:af-pkd}
\index{Poincar\'e--Koszul duality}
Let $M$ be a closed oriented $n$-manifold and $A$ an $\En$-algebra.
There is a natural equivalence
\[
\int_M A
\;\simeq\;
\mathrm{Map}_{\En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg}}
\bigl(\barB_{\En}(\mathbf{1}),\, \barB_{\En}(A)\bigr),
\]
where $\mathbf{1}$ is the unit $\En$-algebra.
For $A$ augmented, this simplifies to the Poincar\'e--Koszul duality
\eqref{eq:poincare-koszul}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proposition}[Our construction refines AF at \texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2};
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:refines-af}
At $n = 2$, the chain-level bar complex
$\barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ of
Chapter~\textup{\ref{chap:bar-cobar}} is a
strict model for the $\Etwo$ bar construction of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:af-pkd}}, in the following sense:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item The FM propagator integrals
  $\int_{\overline{C}_k(X)} \prod_{(i,j)} \eta_{ij}
  \otimes \prod_v a_v$
  compute the factorization homology $\int_X \cA$ as a chain
  complex.
\item The Verdier duality pairing on $\overline{C}_k(X)$
  \textup{(}\S\textup{\ref{sec:verdier-ayala-francis})} realizes the
  Poincar\'e duality of Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:af-pkd}} at the
  cochain level.
\item The spectral sequence from bar filtration to bar cohomology
  is the descent spectral sequence of factorization homology with
  respect to the FM cover.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Part (a): by the fundamental theorem of factorization
homology~\cite{AF15}, $\int_X \cA$ is computed by a \v{C}ech complex
with respect to any Weiss cover of~$X$ by disks.  The FM
compactification $\overline{C}_k(X)$ provides a canonical
resolution: configuration space integrals against the propagator
$\eta_{ij}$ compute the \v{C}ech differential, and the bar
elements $\bigotimes_v a_v$ provide the coefficients.  This is the
content of the Prism Principle
(Theorem~\ref{thm:prism-higher-genus}).

Part (b): Verdier duality $\mathbb{D}_{\overline{C}_k(X)}$ exchanges the
FM compactification with the open configuration space.  This is the
geometric incarnation of the Poincar\'e duality in
Theorem~\ref{thm:af-pkd}: the duality pairing between
$\int_X A$ and $\int_X A^\vee$ is realized by the intersection
pairing on $\overline{C}_k(X)$.  See
Theorem~\ref{thm:verdier-bar-cobar} for the precise statement
at $n = 2$.

Part (c): the FM cover filtration
$F_p = \bigoplus_{k \leq p} C_*(\overline{C}_k(X))
\otimes (s^{-1} \bar{A})^{\otimes k}$
gives a spectral sequence with
$E_1^{p,q} = H^q(\overline{C}_p(X)) \otimes (s^{-1}\bar{A})^{\otimes p}$
converging to $H^*(\int_X A)$.  This is the bar spectral sequence
of Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-spectral-sequence}, reinterpreted as
the descent spectral sequence for the factorization homology
presheaf.
\end{proof}

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{The $\En$ bar complex for arbitrary $n$}{The En bar complex for arbitrary n}}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} bar complex, general \texorpdfstring{$n$}{n}]
\label{def:en-bar-general}
\index{bar complex!general $n$}
For $n \geq 1$ and an $\En$-algebra $A$, the \emph{$\En$ bar complex}
is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:en-bar-general}
\barB_{\En}(A)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{k \geq 1}
\Omega^*\bigl(\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)\bigr)
\otimes_{\Sigma_k} (s^{-1}\bar{A})^{\otimes k},
\quad
d \;=\; d_{\mathrm{dR}} + d_{\mathrm{res}},
\end{equation}
where $d_{\mathrm{res}} = \sum_S \Res_{\partial_S}(G_S \wedge \mu_S)$
uses the $\En$ propagator of Definition~\textup{\ref{def:en-propagator}}
and linking-sphere residues
\textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:linking-sphere-residue})}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[\texorpdfstring{$d^2 = 0$}{d squared = 0} for the \texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} bar complex;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:en-d-squared}
\index{bar complex!$d^2 = 0$}
For any $n \geq 1$ and any $\En$-algebra $A$, the $\En$ bar
differential satisfies $d^2 = 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The argument is identical to the $n = 2$ case
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e2-d-squared}), with the Arnold/Totaro
relations in $H^*(\Conf_3(\bR^n))$ providing the cancellation of
$d_{\mathrm{res}}^2$.  For general~$n$, the Totaro relation holds
in degree $2(n-1)$, and the cancellation works in any degree.

The result also follows from the general formality framework of
Idrissi~\cite{Idrissi22}: Poincar\'e duality on the FM
compactification $\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ provides an explicit
quasi-isomorphism between the de~Rham complex of
$\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ and its cohomology algebra (the Totaro
algebra), and the propagator differential is compatible with this
quasi-isomorphism.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Configuration space Betti numbers and the $\En$ shadow obstruction tower}
\label{subsec:en-shadow-tower}
\index{shadow tower!$\En$}
\index{configuration space!Betti numbers}

The Poincar\'e polynomial of $\Conf_k(\bR^n)$ for $n \geq 2$ is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:conf-poincare}
P(\Conf_k(\bR^n),\, t)
\;=\;
\prod_{j=0}^{k-1} (1 + j\, t^{n-1}),
\end{equation}
yielding total Betti number $\sum b_i = k!$ for all $n \geq 2$.  The
generator degree~$n-1$ controls the bar complex grading: the $\En$
propagator is a closed $(n{-}1)$-form, so the arity-$k$ bar complex
$\barB_{\En}(A)_k$ has summands in degrees $0, n{-}1, 2(n{-}1),
\ldots, (k{-}1)(n{-}1)$ weighted by the Betti numbers
of~\eqref{eq:conf-poincare}.

\begin{proposition}[Kappa universality across $n$;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:kappa-universality-en}
\index{modular characteristic!$\En$ universality}
For any $\En$-algebra~$A$ with a fixed binary pairing of level~$k$,
the modular characteristic is independent of~$n$:
\[
\kappa_{\En}(A) \;=\; \kappa_{\Eone}(A)
\qquad \text{for all } n \geq 1.
\]
In particular, $\kappa_{\En}(H_k) = k$ for the Heisenberg,
$\kappa_{\En}(\widehat{\fg}_k) = \dim(\fg) \cdot (k + h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$ for affine Kac--Moody,
and $\kappa_{\En}(\mathrm{Vir}_c) = c/2$ for Virasoro.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The modular characteristic $\kappa$ is determined by the arity-$2$
component of the bar complex, which involves $\Conf_2(\bR^n)
\simeq S^{n-1}$.  The propagator pairing on $S^{n-1}$ extracts the
$0$-mode of the binary operation, which is the level~$k$
independently of~$n$.  Formally, $H^{n-1}(\Conf_2(\bR^n))
= \bQ$ for all $n \geq 2$, and the propagator integral
$\int_{S^{n-1}} G \cdot \mu_2(a, b)$ is the same bilinear
invariant for all~$n$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Shadow stabilization threshold;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:shadow-stabilization}
\index{shadow tower!stabilization}
For the arity-$r$ shadow $S_r^{\En}(A)$, the stabilization threshold
is $n_{\mathrm{stable}}(r) = r$: for $n \geq r$, the shadow
$S_r^{\En}(A)$ equals the stable value $S_r^{\Einf}(A)$ and is
independent of~$n$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
At arity~$r$, the bar complex involves $\Conf_r(\bR^n)$, whose
positive-degree cohomology begins in degree~$n{-}1$.  The MC
equation at arity~$r$ constrains the total degree budget to
$r{-}2$ propagator units.  For $n{-}1 > r{-}2$ (i.e., $n \geq r$),
the positive-degree classes of $\Conf_r(\bR^n)$ exceed this budget
and do not contribute.  Hence $S_r^{\En} = S_r^{\mathrm{tree}}
= S_r^{\Einf}$ in the stable range~$n \geq r$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The G/L/C/M classification under $\En$]
\label{rem:glcm-en}
\index{shadow depth!$\En$ independence}
For a formal $\En$-algebra, the shadow depth class (G/L/C/M
of~\S\ref{sec:shadow-archetype}) is independent of~$n$:
formality of the $\En$ operad
(Proposition~\ref{prop:en-formality}) ensures that the
higher-arity corrections from the topology of $\Conf_k(\bR^n)$
do not change the termination behavior.  The Heisenberg
remains class~G ($r_{\max} = 2$), affine Kac--Moody
remains class~L ($r_{\max} = 3$), the $\beta\gamma$ system
remains class~C ($r_{\max} = 4$), and Virasoro remains
class~M ($r_{\max} = \infty$), for all~$n \geq 1$.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The dimensional ladder]
\label{rem:dimensional-ladder}
\index{dimensional ladder}
The transition from $n$ to $n+1$ trades algebraic structure for
topological generality:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{clll}
$n$ & Propagator & Relations & Bar complex computes \\
\hline
$1$ & $\operatorname{sgn}(t_1 - t_2)$ & Stasheff polytopes & Hochschild cohomology \\
$2$ & $d\log(z_1 - z_2)$ & Arnold (OS) & Chiral Koszul dual \\
$3$ & linking $2$-form & graph cohomology & Vassiliev invariants \\
$n$ & $(n{-}1)$-form & Totaro & $\En$-factorization homology
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
At each level, the propagator is a closed $(n-1)$-form, the
relations in $H^*(\Conf_3(\bR^n))$ ensure $d^2 = 0$, and the bar
cohomology computes the Koszul dual in the appropriate operadic
sense.
\end{remark}


\subsection{Knudsen's higher enveloping algebras}

\begin{theorem}[Higher enveloping algebras; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:knudsen-higher-enveloping}
\index{enveloping algebra!higher}
\textup{(}Knudsen~\cite{Knudsen18}\textup{)}\quad
For a Lie algebra $\fg$ and $n \geq 1$, the \emph{$n$-th enveloping
algebra} $U_n(\fg)$ is an $\En$-algebra satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $U_1(\fg) = U(\fg)$ is the classical universal enveloping
  algebra.
\item \textup{(PBW)}\quad There is an equivalence of graded objects
  $\mathrm{gr}\, U_n(\fg) \simeq \mathrm{Sym}^{\En}(\fg[n-1])$,
  where $\mathrm{Sym}^{\En}$ is the free commutative $\En$-algebra.
\item \textup{(Koszul duality)}\quad
  $\barB_{\En}(U_n(\fg))$ is quasi-isomorphic to
  $\mathrm{CE}^{\En}_*(\fg)$, the $\En$-Chevalley--Eilenberg
  complex of~$\fg$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

This result is the higher-dimensional analogue of the PBW theorem
and the classical bar-CE comparison.  At $n = 2$, the $\Etwo$
enveloping algebra $U_2(\fg)$ is the topological shadow of the
chiral universal enveloping algebra
$\widehat{U}(\fg_k) = V_k(\fg)$ (the vacuum module; see Theorem~\ref{thm:e2-d-squared} for the precise $\Etwo$ statement).  Knudsen's
Koszul duality at $n = 2$ is the topological shadow of our chiral
Koszul duality.


\subsection{Kontsevich formality and deformation quantization}

\begin{theorem}[Formality of \texorpdfstring{$\Etwo$}{E2}; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:e2-formality}
\index{formality!$\Etwo$ operad}
\textup{(}Kontsevich~\cite{Kon03},
Tamarkin~\cite{Tamarkin00}\textup{)}\quad
The $\Etwo$ operad is formal over $\bQ$: there is a
quasi-isomorphism of operads
\[
C_*(\Etwo;\, \bQ)
\;\simeq\;
H_*(\Etwo;\, \bQ).
\]
Consequently, every $\Etwo$-algebra structure on a
cochain complex $A$ is determined up to quasi-isomorphism by the
induced $H_*(\Etwo)$-algebra structure on cohomology.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Formality and deformation quantization]
\label{rem:formality-dq}
\index{deformation quantization!from $\Etwo$ formality}
Kontsevich formality~\cite{Kon03} implies star products on
arbitrary Poisson manifolds.  In our framework
(Chapter~\ref{chap:chiral-deformation}), $\Etwo$ Koszul duality
exchanges the bar complex of polyvector fields with that of
polydifferential operators, exchanging Poisson brackets with star products.
The explicit Kontsevich star product arises from propagator integrals
on $\overline{\Conf}_k(\mathcal{H})$, a specific $\Etwo$ bar complex
model with boundary conditions.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} formality for \texorpdfstring{$n \geq 2$}{n >= 2};
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{prop:en-formality}
\index{formality!$\En$ operad}
\textup{(}Fresse--Willwacher~\cite{FresseWillwacher20}, Idrissi~\cite{Idrissi22}\textup{)}\quad
The $\En$ operad is formal over $\bR$ for all $n \geq 2$.
The formality map is constructed explicitly via configuration
space integrals on $\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ using the propagator
of Definition~\textup{\ref{def:en-propagator}}, with the Kontsevich
graph expansion providing the explicit formulas.
\end{proposition}

\section{The Kontsevich graph complex and the shadow obstruction tower}
\label{sec:graph-complex-shadow}
\index{graph complex!Kontsevich $\mathrm{GC}_2$|textbf}
\index{shadow tower!graph complex bridge|textbf}

The modular convolution algebra $\gAmod$ maps to the Kontsevich
graph complex $\mathrm{GC}_2$ via the formality quasi-isomorphism.
The shadow invariants $S_r(\cA)$ project to specific graph cocycles,
and the G/L/C/M depth classification corresponds to truncation in
$\mathrm{GC}_2$.

\begin{definition}[The graph complex $\mathrm{GC}_n$]
\label{def:graph-complex-gcn}
\index{graph complex!definition}
The \emph{Kontsevich graph complex} $\mathrm{GC}_n$ is the cochain
complex spanned by isomorphism classes of connected graphs~$\Gamma$
with all vertex valences $\geq 3$ (no bivalent or univalent vertices),
with cohomological degree
\[
\deg(\Gamma) \;=\; |E(\Gamma)| - 2|V(\Gamma)|,
\]
and differential given by edge contraction:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gc-differential}
d(\Gamma) \;=\; \sum_{e \in E(\Gamma)} \pm\, \Gamma/e,
\end{equation}
where $\Gamma/e$ denotes contraction of the edge~$e$ (discarding
terms that produce self-loops or bivalent vertices), and the sign is
determined by the position of~$e$ in the ordered edge list.  The loop
order of~$\Gamma$ is the first Betti number
$\ell(\Gamma) = |E(\Gamma)| - |V(\Gamma)| + 1$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Wheel cocycles and $\mathrm{grt}_1$;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere{} \cite{Willwacher15}]
\label{thm:willwacher-wheels}
\index{wheel cocycle|textbf}
\index{Grothendieck--Teichmuller@Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller!Lie algebra}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Willwacher~\cite{Willwacher15})}\quad
  $H^0(\mathrm{GC}_2) \cong \mathrm{grt}_1$, the
  Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller Lie algebra.
\item The generators of $\mathrm{grt}_1$ include the
  \emph{wheel cocycles} $\sigma_{2k+1}$ for $k \geq 1$, represented
  by the wheel graph $W_{2k+1}$ with $2k{+}1$ spokes.  The wheel
  $W_{2k+1}$ has $2k{+}2$ vertices, $2(2k{+}1)$ edges, loop
  order~$2k{+}1$, and degree~$-2$.  It is a cocycle in
  $\mathrm{GC}_2$ if and only if the number of spokes is odd
  \textup{(}the dihedral $\bZ/2$-symmetry of $W_n$ provides
  cancellation of the differential only for $n$~odd\textup{)}.
\item The wheel cocycles correspond to the Deligne--Drinfeld
  elements, encoding odd zeta values $\zeta(3), \zeta(5), \zeta(7),
  \ldots$ in the Lie algebra of the motivic Galois group.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proposition}[Shadow obstruction tower to $\mathrm{GC}_2$ bridge;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:shadow-gc2-bridge}
\index{shadow tower!graph complex projection}
The shadow invariants of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ project to
$\mathrm{GC}_2$ via the formality quasi-isomorphism: the odd-arity
shadows $S_{2k+1}(\cA)$ map to the wheel cocycles
$\sigma_{2k+1} \in H^*(\mathrm{GC}_2)$, while even-arity shadows
$S_{2k}(\cA)$ map to exact forms \textup{(}coboundaries in
$\mathrm{GC}_2$\textup{)}.  The projection respects the depth
classification:
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{ccl}
Shadow class & Nonzero wheels & Algebra \\
\hline
G \textup{(}depth $2$\textup{)} & none ($S_3 = 0$)
  & Heisenberg, lattice \\
L \textup{(}depth $3$\textup{)} & $\sigma_3, \sigma_5$
  & Affine Kac--Moody \\
  % RECTIFICATION-FLAG: The L_infty formality map Phi has higher components;
  % sigma_5 for class L arises from Phi_2(S_2, S_3), not from S_5.
  % Verify independently that Phi_2(S_2,S_3) is nonzero for affine KM.
C \textup{(}depth $4$\textup{)} & $\sigma_3, \sigma_5, \sigma_7$
  & $\beta\gamma$ \\
M \textup{(}depth $\infty$\textup{)} & all $\sigma_{2k+1}$
  & Virasoro, $\cW_N$
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The formality quasi-isomorphism $\Phi\colon \gAmod \xrightarrow{\sim}
H^*(\gAmod)$ sends the MC element $\Theta_\cA$ to a sum of graph
cocycle components.  At arity~$r$, the image $\Phi(S_r)$ lies in the
weight-$r$ part of~$\mathrm{GC}_2$.  For odd~$r = 2k{+}1$, the
wheel graph $W_{2k+1}$ is a cocycle
(Theorem~\ref{thm:willwacher-wheels}(ii)),
and $\Phi(S_{2k+1})$ is proportional to~$\sigma_{2k+1}$ by
uniqueness of the cocycle in the relevant degree.  For even~$r$, the
wheel $W_{2k}$ is \emph{not} a cocycle (it bounds), so $\Phi(S_{2k})$
is necessarily exact: it maps to a coboundary in $\mathrm{GC}_2$.

The depth truncation follows from the shadow obstruction tower termination:
if $S_r(\cA) = 0$ for all $r > r_{\max}$, then the
$\mathrm{GC}_2$ projection has nonzero wheel components only for
$\sigma_{2k+1}$ with $2k{+}1 \leq 2r_{\max} - 1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Shadow zeta values]
\label{def:shadow-zeta-values}
\index{shadow zeta values|textbf}
For a chiral algebra~$\cA$ with $\kappa(\cA) \neq 0$, the
\emph{normalized shadow zeta values} are the dimensionless
invariants
\begin{equation}\label{eq:shadow-zeta}
\zeta^{\mathrm{sh}}_r(\cA) \;:=\;
\frac{S_r(\cA)}{|\kappa(\cA)|^{r/2}},
\qquad r \geq 2.
\end{equation}
These are rational functions of the central charge or level parameter
and are invariant under rescaling.  The normalized shadow zeta values
encode the ``shape'' of the shadow obstruction tower, independent of the overall
scale set by~$\kappa$.
\end{definition}

\section{\texorpdfstring{The $n = 3$ case: Chern--Simons theory}{The n = 3 case: Chern--Simons theory}}
\label{sec:n3-chern-simons}
\index{Chern--Simons!$\mathsf{E}_3$ bar complex}

The $n = 3$ case of $\En$ Koszul duality connects directly to
perturbative Chern--Simons theory and the Kontsevich integral
of Chapter~\ref{ch:kontsevich-integral}.

\begin{conjecture}[\texorpdfstring{$\mathsf{E}_3$}{E3} bar complex and Chern--Simons;
\ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:e3-chern-simons}
\index{Chern--Simons!perturbative expansion}
For $\fg$ a finite-dimensional Lie algebra with invariant inner
product, the $\mathsf{E}_3$ bar complex
$\barB_{\mathsf{E}_3}(U_3(\fg))$ computes the perturbative
Chern--Simons invariants of $3$-manifolds:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The propagator is the Chern--Simons propagator of
  Example~\textup{\ref{ex:n3-propagator}}: the Green's $2$-form
  of the gauge-fixed Laplacian on~$M$.
\item The Feynman diagrams of Axelrod--Singer are the
  contributions from individual boundary strata of
  $\overline{\Conf}_k(M)$.
\item The bar cohomology
  $H^*(\barB_{\mathsf{E}_3}(U_3(\fg)))$
  should recover the Kontsevich graph complex with Lie algebra
  coefficients.
\end{enumerate}
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Scope]
\label{rem:e3-scope}
Part~(ii) (Feynman diagrams = boundary strata) is established
(Axelrod--Singer, Kontsevich~\cite{Kon94}, Cattaneo--Mnev~\cite{CattaneoMnev10}).
What is conjectured is the full chain-level identification~(iii):
showing the Axelrod--Singer compactification models the
$\mathsf{E}_3$ bar complex (the $n = 2$ analogue is our
Prism Principle, Theorem~\ref{thm:prism-higher-genus}).
The $n = 3$ case requires modular $\mathsf{E}_3$-operads, not yet
developed.  (Contributing to Conjecture~\ref{conj:master-bv-brst}.)
\end{remark}

\section{The Swiss-cheese case: holomorphic--topological
factorization}
\label{sec:swiss-cheese}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad|textbf}
\index{bar complex!Swiss-cheese structure|textbf}
\index{holomorphic--topological!factorization|textbf}

The dimensional ladder has so far been one-dimensional: $n = 1$
(associative), $n = 2$ (chiral), $n = 3$ (Chern--Simons).  But the
bar complex of this monograph already carries a \emph{mixed}
structure that does not fit on this line.  The differential
$d_{\barB}$ lives on $\FM_k(\bC)$ (the $n = 2$ slot); the
coassociative coproduct~$\Delta$ lives on $\Conf_k(\bR)$ (the
$n = 1$ slot).  Together, a bar element of degree~$k$ is
parametrized by
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bar-product-space}
\FM_k(\bC) \times \Conf_k(\bR),
\end{equation}
the product of holomorphic and topological configuration spaces.
This product structure is the operadic fingerprint of a
\emph{two-colored} operad: the Swiss-cheese operad
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$.

\begin{definition}[Holomorphic--topological Swiss-cheese operad]
\label{def:SC}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad!definition}
Define the two-colored topological operad $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$
with colors $\{\mathsf{ch}, \mathsf{top}\}$ (closed/holomorphic
and open/topological) by:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Closed output, closed inputs only:}
  $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}
  ((\mathsf{ch},\ldots,\mathsf{ch});\, \mathsf{ch})
  := \FM_k(\bC)$.
\item \emph{Open output, mixed inputs:}
  $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}
  ((\underbrace{\mathsf{ch},\ldots}_{k},
    \underbrace{\mathsf{top},\ldots}_{m});\, \mathsf{top})
  := \FM_k(\bC) \times E_1(m)$.
\item \emph{No open-to-closed:}
  $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}
  (\ldots,\mathsf{top},\ldots;\, \mathsf{ch})
  = \varnothing$.
\end{enumerate}
Composition is componentwise: FM insertion in~$\bC$, interval
insertion in~$E_1$.  The no-open-to-closed rule ensures that
the closed color is an operadic retract: it receives no information
from the open color.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bar complex as Swiss-cheese coalgebra;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:bar-swiss-cheese}
\index{bar complex!Swiss-cheese coalgebra}
Let $\cA$ be an augmented chiral algebra on a curve~$X$.  The bar
complex $\barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$, equipped with its bar
differential~$d_{\barB}$ and deconcatenation coproduct~$\Delta$,
is a dg coalgebra over
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$:
\begin{center}
\small
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\textbf{Bar structure} & \textbf{SC color} &
  \textbf{Configuration space} \\
\hline
Differential $d_{\barB}$
  & closed (holomorphic)
  & $\FM_k(\bC)$: collision residues \\[2pt]
Coproduct $\Delta$
  & open (topological)
  & $\Conf_k(\bR)$: interval splitting \\[2pt]
Coderivation: $d_{\barB} \circ \Delta
  = \Delta \circ d_{\barB}$
  & compatibility
  & product structure~\eqref{eq:bar-product-space}
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The coproduct is the deconcatenation
$\Delta[a_1|\cdots|a_n] =
\sum_{i=0}^{n} [a_1|\cdots|a_i] \otimes [a_{i+1}|\cdots|a_n]$,
which is coassociative by construction (tensor coalgebra).  The
ordering of tensor factors corresponds to the ordering of points
$t_1 < \cdots < t_n$ on~$\bR$; the splitting at~$i$ corresponds to
cutting the interval at $t_i$.  This is the $E_1$ coalgebra
structure.

The differential $d_{\barB}$ extracts residues along collision
divisors $D_S \subset \partial\FM_k(\bC)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-nilpotency-complete}).  It is a coderivation
of~$\Delta$: the Leibniz rule
$d_{\barB} \circ \Delta = (\id \otimes d_{\barB}
  + d_{\barB} \otimes \id) \circ \Delta$
holds because collision residues in~$\bC$ commute with interval
splitting in~$\bR$.  This is the content of the product structure:
$\FM_k(\bC)$ and $\Conf_k(\bR)$ contribute independently.

The no-open-to-closed constraint is automatic: the coproduct
(open-color structure) does not appear in the differential
(closed-color structure).  The bar differential $d_{\barB}$ is
built purely from the chiral product~$\mu$ and the FM boundary
strata, with no reference to the tensor ordering.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Physical interpretation]
\label{rem:sc-physical}
A three-dimensional holomorphic--topological (HT) quantum field
theory on $\bC_z \times \bR_t$ produces local observables that
factorize holomorphically in~$z$ and associatively in~$t$.  The BV
quantization of such a theory produces an algebra over
$W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})$ (the Boardman--Vogt resolution),
and the resulting higher operations $m_k$ are realized by
configuration-space integrals on
$\FM_k(\bC) \times \Conf_k(\bR)$, the same spaces that
parametrize bar elements of degree~$k$.  Volume~II develops this
identification in full.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Curved Swiss-cheese at higher genus]
\label{rem:sc-higher-genus}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad!curved|textbf}
At genus~$g \geq 1$, the bar complex $\barB^{(g)}(\cA)$ is a
\emph{curved} Swiss-cheese coalgebra: the coproduct~$\Delta$
remains coassociative (it is the tensor coalgebra structure,
independent of genus), but the differential is curved:
$\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$.  The curvature is
\emph{scalar} (Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}), hence
central in the coalgebra, hence a scalar coderivation: the product
structure~\eqref{eq:bar-product-space} persists.  The corrected
differential $\Dg{g}$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-diff-squares-zero})
restores flatness over $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ by incorporating
period integrals that couple the $\bR$-ordering to the Hodge
bundle~$\mathbb{E}$.  The genus tower of curved Swiss-cheese
algebras is controlled by the modular characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$
and the $\hat{A}$-genus generating function
(Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Position in the dimensional ladder]
\label{rem:sc-dimensional-ladder}
The Swiss-cheese operad $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ is the
$(n_{\mathrm{hol}}, n_{\mathrm{top}}) = (2, 1)$ entry in a
two-parameter family of mixed operads.  The one-parameter diagonal
$n_{\mathrm{hol}} = n$, $n_{\mathrm{top}} = 0$ recovers the $\En$
ladder of this chapter.  The off-diagonal entries mix holomorphic
and topological factorization in different dimensions.  The
classical Swiss-cheese of Voronov~\cite{Voronov99} is the
$(n, n-1)$ case; ours is the holomorphic refinement at $(2, 1)$.
\end{remark}

\section{The chiral center theorem: operadic proof}
\label{sec:operadic-center-theorem}
\index{center theorem!operadic proof|textbf}
\index{operadic center|textbf}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad!center theorem}
\index{Hochschild cohomology!as operadic center}
\index{Deligne conjecture!chiral analogue}

The chiral center theorem identifies the chiral Hochschild cochain
complex $C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ as the \emph{operadic
center} of an open-color algebra~$\cA$ in the Swiss-cheese operad
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$.  The topological analogue (the
classical Deligne conjecture and its higher-dimensional
generalizations) identifies Hochschild cochains of an associative
algebra as an $\Etwo$-algebra via the center construction in the
classical Swiss-cheese operad.  This section gives a complete proof
of the chiral version via the operadic route, identifying each point
where the passage from the topological to the chiral setting creates
a genuine new difficulty.

The proof proceeds in five steps: the abstract operadic center
(Step~1), its chain-level computation (Step~2), the geometric
recognition that makes the theorem inevitable (Step~3), the
comparison with the brace-algebra model (Step~4), and the universal
property (Step~5).

\subsection{Step 1: The operadic center of a two-colored algebra}
\label{subsec:operadic-center-definition}
\index{operadic center!definition}

\begin{definition}[Two-colored operad with vanishing constraint]
\label{def:two-colored-operad-vanishing}
A \emph{two-colored operad with vanishing open-to-closed operations}
is a symmetric colored operad $\cO$ with color set
$\{\mathsf{c}, \mathsf{o}\}$ (closed and open) satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $\cO((\mathsf{c},\ldots,\mathsf{c});\,\mathsf{c})$ is an
  operad in color~$\mathsf{c}$ (the \emph{closed sector}
  $\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$);
\item $\cO((\mathsf{c}^k, \mathsf{o}^m);\, \mathsf{o})$ defines a
  right module over $\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$ and a bimodule over the
  $\mathsf{o}$-operad
  $\cO^{\mathsf{o}} := \cO((\mathsf{o},\ldots);\, \mathsf{o})$;
\item $\cO((\ldots,\mathsf{o},\ldots);\,\mathsf{c}) = \varnothing$:
  no open-to-closed operations.
\end{enumerate}
The Swiss-cheese operad $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$
(Definition~\ref{def:SC}) is the primary example.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Operadic center]
\label{def:operadic-center}
\index{operadic center!abstract definition|textbf}
Let $\cO$ be a two-colored operad satisfying
Definition~\ref{def:two-colored-operad-vanishing}, and let $A_\mathsf{o}$
be an algebra over $\cO^{\mathsf{o}}$.  The \emph{operadic center}
$Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})$ is the terminal object in the category of
$\cO$-algebra pairs:
\[
Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})
\;:=\;
\varprojlim_{\substack{(B, A_\mathsf{o}) \in \\
\cO\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}}}
B,
\]
where the limit is taken over the category
$\cO\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}_{/A_\mathsf{o}}$
of $\cO$-algebra pairs $(B, A_\mathsf{o})$
in which the open-color algebra is the given~$A_\mathsf{o}$.  More
explicitly, $Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})$ carries the structure of a
$\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$-algebra together with a coherent family of action
maps
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-action-maps}
\alpha_{k,m}\colon
\cO((\mathsf{c}^k, \mathsf{o}^m);\, \mathsf{o})
\otimes Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})^{\otimes k}
\otimes A_\mathsf{o}^{\otimes m}
\;\longrightarrow\; A_\mathsf{o},
\end{equation}
satisfying the equivariance and associativity axioms of an
$\cO$-algebra, and is \emph{universal} with this property: for every
$\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$-algebra~$B$ equipped with action
maps~\eqref{eq:center-action-maps} making $(B, A_\mathsf{o})$ an
$\cO$-algebra, there exists a unique $\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$-algebra
morphism $B \to Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})$ through which all action maps
factor.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Concrete description via internal Hom]
\label{rem:center-internal-hom}
\index{operadic center!internal Hom description}
When $\cO^{\mathsf{o}}$-algebras form a category enriched over chain
complexes, the operadic center admits an explicit model.  Let
$\mathsf{End}_{A_\mathsf{o}}(m) := \operatorname{Hom}(A_\mathsf{o}^{\otimes m}, A_\mathsf{o})$
denote the endomorphism operad of~$A_\mathsf{o}$.  The action
maps~\eqref{eq:center-action-maps} assemble into an operad morphism
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-operad-morphism}
\gamma\colon \cO^{\mathsf{c}} \;\longrightarrow\;
\operatorname{End}_{Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})}
\end{equation}
subject to the compatibility condition that for each $k, m$, the
composite
\[
\cO((\mathsf{c}^k, \mathsf{o}^m);\, \mathsf{o})
\otimes Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})^{\otimes k}
\xrightarrow{\gamma \otimes \mathrm{id}}
\operatorname{Hom}(A_\mathsf{o}^{\otimes m}, A_\mathsf{o})
\]
equals the given $\cO$-algebra structure.  This makes the center a
\emph{derived mapping object}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-as-derived-hom}
Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})
\;\simeq\;
\operatorname{RHom}_{\cO^{\mathsf{o}}\text{-}\mathsf{bimod}}
\!\bigl(\cO(\mathsf{c}, -;\, \mathsf{o}),\,
\mathsf{End}_{A_\mathsf{o}}\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $\cO(\mathsf{c}, -;\, \mathsf{o})$ is regarded as a
$\cO^{\mathsf{o}}$-bimodule via the mixed composition maps.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Existence of the operadic center;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:operadic-center-existence}
\index{operadic center!existence}
Let $\cO$ be a two-colored operad as in
Definition~\textup{\ref{def:two-colored-operad-vanishing}}, working
in chain complexes over a field of characteristic zero.  For any
$\cO^{\mathsf{o}}$-algebra~$A_\mathsf{o}$, the operadic center
$Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})$ exists and is computed by the equalizer
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-equalizer}
Z_\cO(A_\mathsf{o})
\;\hookrightarrow\;
\prod_{m \geq 0}
\operatorname{Hom}_{\Sigma_m}
\!\bigl(
\cO((\mathsf{c}, \mathsf{o}^m);\, \mathsf{o}),\,
\operatorname{Hom}(A_\mathsf{o}^{\otimes m}, A_\mathsf{o})
\bigr)
\;\rightrightarrows\;
(\cdots)
\end{equation}
where the two maps enforce
\textup{(}a\textup{)} $\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$-algebra equivariance
\textup{(}the closed-sector operadic composition\textup{)} and
\textup{(}b\textup{)} $\cO^{\mathsf{o}}$-bimodule equivariance
\textup{(}the open-sector composition\textup{)}.  In the derived
setting, the equalizer is replaced by a homotopy equalizer computed
via a bar-type resolution.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
This is a standard construction in the theory of colored operads.
The product
$\prod_m \operatorname{Hom}_{\Sigma_m}(\cO(\mathsf{c},
\mathsf{o}^m; \mathsf{o}), \operatorname{Hom}(A_\mathsf{o}^{\otimes m}, A_\mathsf{o}))$
assembles all possible action maps with one closed input.
Equivariance with respect to $\cO^{\mathsf{c}}$-composition
(inserting further closed inputs) and $\cO^{\mathsf{o}}$-composition
(inserting open inputs into the open arguments) is imposed by the
two arrows.  The equalizer exists because chain complexes are complete.
In the derived setting, one replaces $\cO$ by a cofibrant resolution
$\cO_\infty \xrightarrow{\sim} \cO$ and takes the homotopy equalizer
in the model-categorical sense; the resulting object is well-defined
up to quasi-isomorphism.
\end{proof}


\subsection{Step 2: Chain-level realization for
\texorpdfstring{$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$}{SC-ch,top}}
\label{subsec:center-chain-level}
\index{operadic center!chain-level realization}

\begin{theorem}[The operadic center of
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ is the chiral Hochschild complex;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}
\index{Hochschild cohomology!as operadic center|textbf}
Let $\cA$ be a logarithmic chiral algebra
\textup{(}an algebra over the closed sector
$(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})^{\mathsf{c}} = \{\FM_k(\bC)\}$
of the holomorphic--topological Swiss-cheese operad, with
operations extending to the log compactification of
$\Conf_k(\bC)$\textup{)}, and
let $A = \cA|_{\{z_0\}}$ denote the fiber at a basepoint
\textup{(}an $E_1$-algebra via the open sector\textup{)}.  There is
a quasi-isomorphism of $\Etwo$-algebras
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-hochschild-qi}
Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)
\;\simeq\;
C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA),
\end{equation}
where the right-hand side is the chiral Hochschild cochain complex
\textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:chiral-hochschild})},
equipped with its braces and cup product.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We compute the center using
Definition~\ref{def:operadic-center} and the explicit form of
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$
(Definition~\ref{def:SC}).

\emph{Step 2a: Identifying the components.}
By Definition~\ref{def:SC}, the mixed-output spaces are
$\mathsf{SC}(\mathsf{c}^k, \mathsf{o}^m;\, \mathsf{o})
= \FM_k(\bC) \times E_1(m)$.
An element of the center at arity~$(k,m)$ is a map
\[
\phi_{k,m}\colon
\FM_k(\bC) \times E_1(m)
\;\longrightarrow\;
\operatorname{Hom}(A^{\otimes m}, A)
\]
equivariant for the $\Sigma_k$-action on $\FM_k(\bC)$ and the
$\Sigma_m$-action on $E_1(m)$.
Setting $m = 0$ and noting that $E_1(0) = \mathrm{pt}$:
\[
\phi_{k,0}\colon \FM_k(\bC) \;\longrightarrow\; A,
\]
which is a $k$-cochain on configuration space valued in~$A$.
For general~$m$, the $E_1(m)$-factor provides the ``$m$ open inputs''
that the center element acts on via the $E_1$-multiplication on~$A$.

\emph{Step 2b: Center elements are chiral Hochschild cochains.}
We construct an explicit isomorphism between the arity-$k$ component
of $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ and $C^k_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$.  By
Proposition~\ref{prop:operadic-center-existence}, the center is the
equalizer
\[
Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)
\;\hookrightarrow\;
\prod_{m \geq 0}
\operatorname{Hom}_{\Sigma_m}
\!\bigl(
\mathsf{SC}((\mathsf{c}, \mathsf{o}^m);\, \mathsf{o}),\,
\operatorname{Hom}(A^{\otimes m}, A)
\bigr)
\;\rightrightarrows\;
(\cdots),
\]
where the two arrows impose closed-sector equivariance (composing
further closed inputs) and open-sector bimodule equivariance
(composing open inputs via~$E_1$).  Substituting
$\mathsf{SC}((\mathsf{c}, \mathsf{o}^m); \mathsf{o})
= \FM_1(\bC) \times E_1(m) \cong \mathrm{pt} \times E_1(m)$
at $k = 1$, the product over~$m$ assembles into
$\prod_m \operatorname{Hom}_{\Sigma_m}(E_1(m),
\operatorname{Hom}(A^{\otimes m}, A))
\cong \operatorname{End}^{E_1}_{A}$,
the $E_1$-endomorphism operad of~$A$.

At general arity~$k$, the closed-sector equivariance arrow forces a
center element to be a $\Sigma_k$-equivariant family over
$\FM_k(\bC)$ of such $E_1$-endomorphisms.  The open-sector bimodule
arrow forces compatibility with the $E_1$-algebra structure on~$A$:
inserting the identity at any open slot recovers the original element
(a unitality condition), and pre-/post-composing with the $E_1$
multiplication on~$A$ is compatible with the interval-insertion maps
$E_1(m) \times E_1(j) \to E_1(m + j - 1)$.

The arity-$k$ center component is therefore
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-bar-degree}
Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)_k
\;\cong\;
\bigl[\Omega^*(\FM_k(\bC), \log D)
\otimes \cA^{\boxtimes k}
\otimes \cA_{\mathrm{out}}\bigr]^{\Sigma_k},
\end{equation}
where the $\Sigma_k$-equivariance acts simultaneously on the
configuration space coordinates and on the algebra factors, the
logarithmic forms $\Omega^*(\FM_k(\bC), \log D)$ arise from the
chain-level model for~$\FM_k(\bC)$, the $k$ copies of~$\cA$
correspond to the $k$ closed inputs, and $\cA_{\mathrm{out}}$ is
the output fiber at the basepoint $z_0 \in X$.  This is
a section of $\cA^{\boxtimes (k+1)}$ over the compactified
configuration space $\overline{C}_{k+1}(X)$ of $k+1$ points
(one output and $k$ inputs), tensored with logarithmic forms,
which is the definition of
$C^k_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$
(Definition~\ref{def:chiral-hochschild},
\S\ref{subsec:center-chain-level}).
The open-sector bimodule condition ensures that the evaluation
at the output point $z_0$ and the insertion of algebra elements
at the input points are compatible with the chiral product,
which is the Hochschild cochain compatibility condition
(the cochain evaluates inputs via the chiral product and
returns an output in~$\cA$).

\emph{Step 2c: Matching the differentials.}
The center $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ carries three differential
contributions; we identify each with a component of the chiral
Hochschild differential
$d_{\mathrm{Hoch}} = d_{\mathrm{int}} + d_{\mathrm{fact}}
+ d_{\mathrm{config}}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-differential}).

\emph{(c.1) Internal differential.}
The $\cD_X$-module differential $d_\cA$ of the chiral algebra acts
on each fiber $\cA(z_i)$ independently.  Under the identification of
Step~2b, $d_\cA$ acts on the $\cA^{\boxtimes (k+1)}$ factor,
producing
$d_{\mathrm{int}}(\phi)(z_1, \ldots, z_k)
= \sum_{i=0}^{k} (-1)^{|\phi_0| + \cdots + |\phi_{i-1}|}
\,\phi_0 \otimes \cdots \otimes d_\cA \phi_i \otimes \cdots
\otimes \phi_k \otimes \omega$,
which is the component $d_{\mathrm{int}}$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-differential}.

\emph{(c.2) Factorization differential.}
The closed-sector operadic composition
$\circ_i\colon \FM_k(\bC) \times \FM_\ell(\bC) \to
\FM_{k+\ell-1}(\bC)$
inserts an arity-$\ell$ closed element into the $i$-th slot
of an arity-$k$ element.  At the chain level, for a center
element $\phi \in Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)_k$, this composition acts
by restricting~$\phi$ to the boundary stratum
$\partial_{ij}\FM_k(\bC)$ where $z_i \to z_j$ and extracting the
residue of the chiral product~$\mu$:
\[
(d_{\mathrm{fact}}\phi)(z_1, \ldots, z_{k-1})
= \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq k}
(-1)^{\epsilon(i,j)}\,
\operatorname{Res}_{z_i = z_j}
\bigl[\phi(\ldots, z_i, \ldots, z_j, \ldots)
\cdot \mu(a_i, a_j)\bigr],
\]
where $\epsilon(i,j)$ is the Koszul sign from permuting $a_i$ past
$a_{i+1}, \ldots, a_{j-1}$.  This is the factorization component
$d_{\mathrm{fact}}$ from
Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-differential}: the
residue extraction at collision divisors $D_{ij} \subset
\partial\FM_k(\bC)$ using the chiral product.  The signs agree
because both are determined by the codimension-1 boundary
orientation of $\FM_k(\bC)$ and the Koszul convention for
graded-symmetric tensor products.

\emph{(c.3) Configuration-space differential.}
The de~Rham differential $d_{\mathrm{dR}}$ on $\FM_k(\bC)$ acts
on the logarithmic form factor~$\omega$ in
$\phi = a_0 \otimes \cdots \otimes a_k \otimes \omega$.  Under
the identification of Step~2b, this is the component
$d_{\mathrm{config}} = d_{\mathrm{dR}}$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-differential}: it raises
the form degree on $\overline{C}_{k+1}(X)$ by~$1$.

\emph{(c.4) Verification that $d^2 = 0$ is preserved.}
The identity $d_{\mathrm{Hoch}}^2 = 0$ is proved in
Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-differential} by checking
three pure squares and three anticommutators.  Under the
identification of Steps~2b--2c, each cancellation in the center
differential maps to the corresponding cancellation in the
Hochschild differential: $d_{\mathrm{int}}^2 = 0$ from
$d_\cA^2 = 0$;
$d_{\mathrm{config}}^2 = 0$ from
$d_{\mathrm{dR}}^2 = 0$;
$d_{\mathrm{fact}}^2 = 0$ from the Borcherds identity
(iterated residues at overlapping collision divisors);
$\{d_{\mathrm{int}}, d_{\mathrm{fact}}\} = 0$
because $\mu$ is a $\cD_X$-module map;
$\{d_{\mathrm{int}}, d_{\mathrm{config}}\} = 0$
because $d_\cA$ and $d_{\mathrm{dR}}$ act on independent tensor
factors;
$\{d_{\mathrm{fact}}, d_{\mathrm{config}}\} = 0$
by the Arnold--Orlik--Solomon relations on $\FM_k(\bC)$.
Hence the identification of Step~2b is an isomorphism of
cochain complexes.

\emph{Step 2d: The $\Etwo$-algebra structure.}
The closed sector
$(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})^{\mathsf{c}} = \{\FM_k(\bC)\}$ is
a model for the $\Etwo$ operad
(Proposition~\ref{prop:fm-boundary-strata}).  By the universal
property of the center
(Definition~\ref{def:operadic-center}), $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ is an
algebra over
$(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})^{\mathsf{c}} \simeq \Etwo$, and
this $\Etwo$-structure transfers to
$C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ via the isomorphism of
Steps~2b--2c.  We verify directly that the two generating operations
match.

\emph{(d.1) Cup product.}
The $\Etwo$ multiplication is the $(2,0)$-arity closed-sector
composition: for $f \in C^p_{\mathrm{ch}}$ and
$g \in C^q_{\mathrm{ch}}$, the operadic composition
$\circ\colon \FM_2(\bC) \times Z(A)^{\otimes 2} \to Z(A)$
yields a map
$(f \cup g)(z_1, \ldots, z_{p+q})
= \int_{\FM_2(\bC)}
f(z_1, \ldots, z_p) \otimes g(z_{p+1}, \ldots, z_{p+q})$,
where the two clusters of points are separated by the
$\FM_2(\bC)$ operad element (one point in each cluster
approaches the corresponding component of the arity-$2$ FM
configuration).  This is the cup product on chiral Hochschild
cochains: the concatenation of two cochains separated by the
propagator, identical in structure to the classical
Gerstenhaber cup product except that the propagator is
$d\log(z_1 - z_2)$ rather than the topological
$d\arg(z_1 - z_2)/(2\pi)$.

\emph{(d.2) Braces.}
The brace operation $f\{g_1, \ldots, g_k\}$ arises from the
$(1, k)$-arity composition: one element~$f$ in the ``outer''
position with $k$ elements $g_1, \ldots, g_k$ inserted into $k$
of its slots.  At the chain level, this is the composition map
$\FM_1(\bC) \times \FM_k(\bC) \times Z(A) \times Z(A)^{\otimes k}
\to Z(A)$,
which by the FM operad composition rule corresponds to
inserting the $k$ cochains $g_i$ at $k$ distinct points within
the configuration underlying~$f$.  The $\FM_k(\bC)$-factor
labels the relative positions of these insertion points,
yielding the chiral brace operations.  These satisfy the
pre-Lie and higher brace identities because the operadic
composition maps of $\FM_k(\bC)$ satisfy the corresponding
associativity and equivariance, which is the content of
the $\Etwo$-operad axioms for $\{\FM_k(\bC)\}$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:fm-boundary-strata}).

\emph{(d.3) Compatibility.}
The cup product and braces together generate the full
$\Etwo$-structure, and we have shown both are induced by the
same operadic composition maps that define the center's
$\Etwo$-algebra structure.  For a more systematic proof that
the center of an $E_1$-algebra in a two-colored
$E_1 \hookrightarrow E_2$ extension is an $E_2$-algebra, see
Francis~\cite{Francis2013}, Theorem~5.16 (which proves the
stronger result that the tangent complex of an $\En$-algebra
carries an $\En$-algebra structure, specialized here to $n = 2$)
and Lurie~\cite{HA}, \S5.3.1 (which establishes the $\En$-center
of an $E_1$-algebra as an $E_{n+1}$-algebra at the
$\infty$-categorical level, specialized to $n = 1$).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Chiral vs.\ topological: the spectral-parameter
difficulty]
\label{rem:chiral-spectral-parameter-difficulty}
\index{Deligne conjecture!chiral vs topological}
In the topological setting ($\mathsf{SC}_{\mathrm{top}}$,
Voronov~\cite{Voronov99}), Step~2 is the content of the Deligne
conjecture
(Kontsevich--Soibelman~\cite{KS00},
McClure--Smith, Kaufmann):
the Hochschild cochain complex $C^*(A, A)$ of an associative
algebra is an $\Etwo$-algebra.  The chiral setting introduces three
genuine new difficulties:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item \emph{Meromorphic vs.\ smooth propagator.}  The topological
  propagator $d\arg(z_1 - z_2)/(2\pi)$ is smooth on
  $\Conf_2(\bR^2)$; the chiral propagator
  $d\log(z_1 - z_2)$ is meromorphic.  This forces the chiral
  Hochschild complex to live on the \emph{compactified}
  configuration space $\overline{C}_{k+2}(X) = \FM_{k+2}(X)$ with
  logarithmic forms, not on the open configuration space.  The
  Arnold relations (three-term identity on residues) replace the
  smooth Stokes theorem as the mechanism for $d^2 = 0$.
\item \emph{Dependence on curve geometry.}  The topological
  center $Z_{\mathrm{SC_{top}}}(A)$ depends only on the
  homotopy type of the associative algebra~$A$.  The chiral center
  $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ depends on the underlying curve~$X$: the
  configuration spaces $\FM_k(X)$ carry the complex structure
  of~$X$, and the propagator depends on the choice of coordinate
  (globally: on the choice of meromorphic $1$-form with prescribed
  singularities).  This geometric dependence is invisible in
  genus~$0$ (where $X = \bC$ and $\FM_k(\bC)$ is canonically
  defined) but essential at genus~$g \geq 1$, where the center
  carries a flat connection over~$\overline{\cM}_g$.
\item \emph{Curvature at higher genus.}  The bar differential
  $d_{\barB}$ satisfies $d_{\barB}^2 = 0$ at genus~$0$, but
  $\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ at genus~$g \geq 1$
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}).  This means the
  chiral Hochschild complex is \emph{curved} at higher genus:
  the center is a curved $\Etwo$-algebra, and the curvature
  $\kappa$ is the modular characteristic.  No such phenomenon
  occurs in the topological setting.
\end{enumerate}
Difficulty~(a) is handled by the Arnold--Orlik--Solomon formalism
of Chapter~\ref{chap:config-spaces}.  Difficulties~(b)
and~(c) are addressed by the modular extension
(Chapter~\ref{chap:higher-genus}).  At genus~$0$, difficulties
(b)~and~(c) are absent, and the chiral center theorem reduces to a
holomorphic refinement of the Deligne conjecture.
\end{remark}


\subsection{Step 3: Geometric inevitability via the recognition
theorem}
\label{subsec:center-geometric}
\index{recognition theorem!and the center}

The geometric content of the chiral center theorem becomes
transparent once the recognition theorem (proved in Volume~II) is
invoked.  The argument below shows that the theorem is
\emph{forced} by the geometry of the half-space
$\bC \times \bR_{\geq 0}$.

\begin{theorem}[Geometric inevitability of the chiral center;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:center-geometric-inevitability}
\index{center theorem!geometric inevitability|textbf}
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on a curve~$X$, and let
$\cF_\cA$ denote the corresponding prefactorization algebra on
$X \times \bR$ obtained from the
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra structure
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-swiss-cheese})}.
Under the recognition theorem identifying
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebras with prefactorization
algebras on $D \times \bR$
\textup{(}Volume~II, items F4--F5\textup{)}, the following
identifications hold:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Closed color $=$ bulk observables.}  The closed-sector
  algebra
  $\cO_{\mathrm{bulk}} := \cF_\cA(\mathrm{disks\ in\ } X)$
  is the chiral algebra~$\cA$ itself.
\item \emph{Open color $=$ boundary observables.}  The open-sector
  algebra
  $\cO_{\mathrm{bdy}} := \cF_\cA(\mathrm{half\text{-}disks\ touching\ }
  X \times \{0\})$
  is the $E_1$-algebra $A = \cA|_{\{z_0\}}$, the fiber of~$\cA$
  at a point, equipped with its associative multiplication.
\item \emph{Center $=$ interior bulk observables.}  The
  observables of $\cF_\cA$ supported on disks in the interior
  of $X \times \bR_{> 0}$ are precisely the chiral Hochschild
  cochains $C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The proof combines the Swiss-cheese structure
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-swiss-cheese}) with the recognition theorem.

\emph{Part (i):} By definition of $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$,
the closed-sector operations are indexed by $\FM_k(\bC)$, which is
the configuration space of points in the complex plane.  A disk
$D \subset X$ entirely in the bulk (away from the boundary
$X \times \{0\}$) supports local observables of~$\cA$ that multiply
via the chiral product (OPE on colliding points in~$X$).  The
recognition theorem identifies this sector with~$\cA$.

\emph{Part (ii):} Half-disks touching the boundary correspond to the
open-sector operations, indexed by $E_1(m)$ (configurations of
$m$~points on the real line).  The fiber $A = \cA|_{\{z_0\}}$ is a
vector space equipped with an associative product from the
$E_1$-structure: this is the boundary vertex algebra.

\emph{Part (iii):}
This is the key identification.  Consider a disk $D_{\mathrm{int}}$
in the interior of $X \times \bR_{> 0}$, i.e., a disk that does not
touch the boundary.  The observables on $D_{\mathrm{int}}$ must
satisfy two constraints simultaneously:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item They act on boundary observables: pushing
  $D_{\mathrm{int}}$ to the boundary (by moving it in the
  $\bR$-direction) produces an action map
  $\cO_{\mathrm{bulk}} \otimes A^{\otimes m} \to A$, the mixed
  action~\eqref{eq:center-action-maps}.
\item They compose in the bulk via the chiral product on~$X$:
  nesting disks in the holomorphic direction uses
  $\FM_k(\bC)$-composition.
\end{enumerate}
Constraint~(a) says that the interior bulk observable is an
element of
$\operatorname{Hom}(A^{\otimes m}, A)$ for each~$m$, and
constraint~(b) says these elements are coherently related by the
$\FM_k(\bC)$-composition.  But this is exactly the definition of
the operadic center (Definition~\ref{def:operadic-center}).

The resulting complex
$\bigoplus_k \Omega^*(\FM_k(\bC)) \otimes_{\Sigma_k}
\operatorname{Hom}(A^{\otimes k}, A)$
is the chiral Hochschild cochain complex: the
configuration-space forms provide the bar degree, and the
Hom-space provides the coefficient.  The differential is the
standard chiral Hochschild differential
(Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-hochschild-differential}),
composed of the factorization differential (from the chiral
product), the de~Rham differential (from the configuration space
geometry), and the internal differential (from the
$\cD_X$-module structure).

This identification is \emph{inevitable}: once the Swiss-cheese
structure is established, the only object satisfying both
constraints simultaneously \emph{is} the Hochschild cochain complex.
There is no room for an alternative.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The open/closed map and the center]
\label{rem:open-closed-center}
\index{open/closed map}
The no-open-to-closed constraint
($\mathsf{SC}(\ldots,\mathsf{o},\ldots;\, \mathsf{c}) = \varnothing$)
is crucial: it means information flows from closed to open but not
back.  The center reverses this flow \emph{universally}: it is the
largest closed-sector algebra that acts on the given open-sector
algebra.  Physically, the center extracts from the boundary theory
all the information that can be reconstructed about the bulk.  This
is the algebraic incarnation of the holographic principle: the bulk
theory (chiral algebra~$\cA$) determines and is determined by its
action on the boundary ($E_1$-algebra~$A$), and the center mediates
this correspondence.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The chiral Deligne--Tamarkin principle and the
dimensional jump]
\label{rem:chiral-deligne-tamarkin-dimensional-jump}
\index{Deligne--Tamarkin principle!chiral|textbf}
\index{dimensional jump!2d to 3d|textbf}
Theorems~\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}
and~\ref{thm:center-geometric-inevitability} together constitute
the \emph{chiral Deligne--Tamarkin principle}: for every local
$A_\infty$-chiral algebra~$\cA$, the chiral Hochschild cochain
complex $C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ is the universal
closed-sector algebra acting on~$\cA$ through the Swiss-cheese
structure.  The chain of implications is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:deligne-tamarkin-chain}
\cA
\;\;\xmapsto{\text{center}}\;\;
\bigl(C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\;\cA\bigr)
\in \mathrm{Alg}_{W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})}
\;\;\xmapsto{\;\text{recognition}\;}\;\;
\text{local 3d HT theory on } D \times \bR.
\end{equation}
The left arrow is Theorem~\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}
(proved here).
The right arrow is the recognition theorem identifying
$W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})$-algebras with
holomorphic-topological prefactorization algebras on
$D \times \bR$ (Volume~II, items F4--F5; not proved
in this volume).

This is the mechanism by which a $2$-dimensional chiral
algebra on a curve~$X$ produces a $3$-dimensional
holomorphic-topological bulk/boundary system on $X \times \bR$.
The extra dimension is the
universal normal-collar direction of a codimension-one boundary
condition, forced by the center construction.

An independent physical construction corroborating this picture
is given by Khan--Zeng~\cite{KhanZeng25}, who
build a mixed holomorphic-topological gauge theory on
$\bR \times \bC$ from a freely generated Poisson vertex algebra
and prove that gauge invariance holds if and only if the
$\lambda$-Jacobi identity is satisfied~\cite[\S\,2.2]{KhanZeng25}.
They show~\cite[\S\,4.3]{KhanZeng25} that
an inner Virasoro element upgrades the
holomorphic-topological symmetry to fully topological symmetry.
We \emph{expect} (but have not proved) that the Khan--Zeng
gauge theory realizes the right-hand arrow
of~\eqref{eq:deligne-tamarkin-chain}: that is, the universal
algebraic thickening
$\bigl(C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA),\cA\bigr)$ should be
identified with the perturbative local observable algebra of
their holomorphic-topological gauge theory.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Step 4: Comparison with the brace-algebra center}
\label{subsec:center-comparison}
\index{brace algebra!comparison with operadic center}

The brace-algebra approach to the Hochschild complex (the ``other
team's proof'') defines the $\Etwo$-structure on
$C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ directly, via brace operations
$f\{g_1, \ldots, g_k\}$.  We show that the two constructions agree.

\begin{definition}[Chiral brace operations]
\label{def:chiral-braces}
\index{brace operations!chiral|textbf}
For cochains
$f \in C^p_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ and
$g_1 \in C^{q_1}_{\mathrm{ch}}, \ldots, g_k \in C^{q_k}_{\mathrm{ch}}$,
the \emph{chiral brace operation} is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:chiral-brace}
f\{g_1, \ldots, g_k\}(z_1, \ldots, z_N)
\;:=\;
\sum_{\substack{S_1 \sqcup \cdots \sqcup S_k \sqcup T \\
= \{z_1, \ldots, z_N\}}}
\pm\;
\operatorname{Res}_{\substack{w_1 \to z_{S_1} \\
\vdots \\ w_k \to z_{S_k}}}
f(w_1, \ldots, w_k, z_T)
\prod_{j=1}^{k} g_j(z_{S_j}),
\end{equation}
where the sum is over ordered partitions of the input set, the
residue is at the collision loci $w_j \to z_{S_j}$ in
$\FM_k(\bC) \subset \overline{C}_{k+N}(X)$, and the sign $\pm$
follows the Koszul convention for the desuspended tensor product.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Brace operations from the operadic center;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:braces-from-center}
\index{brace operations!from operadic center}
The brace operations of
Definition~\textup{\ref{def:chiral-braces}} are recovered from the
$\Etwo$-algebra structure on $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ by restricting
the closed-sector composition to the specific boundary strata of
$\FM_{k+1}(\bC)$ where the first point simultaneously collides with
the remaining~$k$ points.  Precisely, the brace
$f\{g_1, \ldots, g_k\}$ is the image of
$(f, g_1, \ldots, g_k) \in Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)^{\otimes(k+1)}$
under the operadic composition map
\[
\FM_{k+1}(\bC)
\otimes
Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)^{\otimes(k+1)}
\;\longrightarrow\;
Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A),
\]
restricted to the open stratum of $\FM_{k+1}(\bC)$ where the
first point is at the center and the remaining~$k$ are close
to it.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The operadic composition in
$(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})^{\mathsf{c}} = \{\FM_k(\bC)\}$
at arity~$(k+1)$ composes a $(k+1)$-ary closed operation with
individual closed operations.  In configuration-space terms, this
is the insertion map
$\FM_{k+1}(\bC) \times \FM_{q_1}(\bC) \times \cdots \times
\FM_{q_k}(\bC) \to \FM_N(\bC)$
that replaces each of the $k$ satellite points by a cluster of
$q_j$~points.

At the cochain level, an element $f \in C^p_{\mathrm{ch}}$ is
represented by a form on $\FM_{p+1}(\bC)$, and
$g_j \in C^{q_j}_{\mathrm{ch}}$ is a form on $\FM_{q_j+1}(\bC)$.
The operadic composition inserts the $g_j$-clusters into the
$f$-configuration and extracts the residue along the collision
loci.  Unraveling the definitions, this is exactly the brace
formula~\eqref{eq:chiral-brace}: the sum over partitions
corresponds to the sum over ways of assigning the $N$ input
points to the $k$ insertion sites, and the residues arise from the
boundary strata of $\FM_{k+1}(\bC)$ where satellite points collide
with their centers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Comparison theorem: operadic center $=$ brace center;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:operadic-brace-comparison}
\index{center theorem!comparison of models|textbf}
The following three models of the ``center'' are quasi-isomorphic as
$\Etwo$-algebras:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The operadic center $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$
  \textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:operadic-center})}.
\item The chiral Hochschild cochain complex
  $C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ with braces
  \textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:chiral-braces})}.
\item The derived endomorphism algebra
  $\operatorname{REnd}_{\cA^e}(\cA)$, where
  $\cA^e = \cA \otimes \cA^{\mathrm{op}}$ is the enveloping
  algebra.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{(i) $\simeq$ (ii):}
Theorem~\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild} identifies
$Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ with $C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$
as chain complexes.  Proposition~\ref{prop:braces-from-center}
shows the $\Etwo$-structure on the center recovers the braces.
Since the $\Etwo$-structure on the Hochschild complex is
\emph{generated} by the braces and cup product (this is the content
of the higher Deligne conjecture, proved in the topological setting
by Kontsevich--Soibelman~\cite{KS00} and extending to the chiral
setting by the same FM-operad mechanism), the two $\Etwo$-structures
agree.

\emph{(ii) $\simeq$ (iii):}  The chiral Hochschild cochain complex
computes
$\operatorname{RHom}_{\cA^e}(\cA, \cA)
\simeq \operatorname{REnd}_{\cA^e}(\cA)$
by the bar resolution
(Theorem~\ref{thm:hochschild-bar-cobar}).  The derived Hom
carries the standard $\Etwo$-structure from the Eilenberg--Zilber
operad; the bar resolution provides the comparison.

The transitivity \emph{(i) $\simeq$ (ii) $\simeq$ (iii)} completes
the comparison.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Role of homotopy-Koszulity]
\label{rem:homotopy-koszulity-center}
\index{homotopy-Koszulity!role in center theorem}
The comparison in
Theorem~\ref{thm:operadic-brace-comparison}
uses the homotopy-Koszulity of
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ at a single point: the
bar-cobar resolution of the $\mathsf{SC}$-operad must be a
Quillen equivalence, so that derived mapping objects computed
from the bar side agree with those computed from the operadic side.
Volume~II proves homotopy-Koszulity of
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ via three steps:
\textup{(1)}~the classical Swiss-cheese operad is Koszul
(Livernet, Voronov~\cite{Voronov99});
\textup{(2)}~Kontsevich formality gives a quasi-isomorphism
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}} \xrightarrow{\sim}
\mathsf{SC}_{\mathrm{classical}}$;
\textup{(3)}~transfer: bar-cobar preserves quasi-isomorphisms by
the two-out-of-three property.
\emph{Without homotopy-Koszulity}, the operadic center still
exists as a derived object but may not be computed by the explicit
formula~\eqref{eq:center-equalizer}; the cochain-level model
would need to be replaced by a homotopy-coherent (i.e.,
$\infty$-categorical) one.
\end{remark}


\subsection{Step 5: Universality and terminality}
\label{subsec:center-universality}
\index{operadic center!universality}
\index{terminality!of the center}

\begin{theorem}[Terminality of the center; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:center-terminality}
\index{center theorem!terminality|textbf}
Let $A$ be an $E_1$-algebra \textup{(}equivalently, an open-sector
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra\textup{)}.  Define the
\emph{universal SC-pair} as
\[
U(A) \;:=\; \bigl(Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A),\; A\bigr).
\]
Then $U(A)$ is the terminal object in the category of
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra pairs
$(B, A)$ with fixed open component~$A$.  That is, for every
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra pair $(B, A)$, there exists a
unique morphism of closed-sector algebras
\begin{equation}\label{eq:center-terminal-morphism}
\varphi\colon B \;\longrightarrow\; Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)
\end{equation}
such that the diagram of action maps
\[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=large]
\FM_k(\bC) \otimes B^{\otimes k}
  \otimes A^{\otimes m}
  \ar[r, "\alpha^{B}_{k,m}"]
  \ar[d, "\varphi^{\otimes k}"']
& A \\
\FM_k(\bC) \otimes Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)^{\otimes k}
  \otimes A^{\otimes m}
  \ar[ur, "\alpha^{\mathrm{univ}}_{k,m}"']
\end{tikzcd}
\]
commutes for all $k, m \geq 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Existence.}
By the universal property of the operadic center
(Definition~\ref{def:operadic-center}), any
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-algebra structure on $(B, A)$
determines, for each $k$ and~$m$, action maps
$\alpha^B_{k,m}\colon \FM_k(\bC) \otimes B^{\otimes k}
\otimes A^{\otimes m} \to A$.
The $k = 1, m = 0$ component gives a map
$\alpha^B_{1,0}\colon B \to \operatorname{Hom}(\bC, A) = A$, and
more generally the full collection $\{\alpha^B_{k,m}\}$ is
exactly the data of an element in
\[
\prod_{k,m}
\operatorname{Hom}_{\Sigma_k \times \Sigma_m}
\!\bigl(
\FM_k(\bC) \times E_1(m),\,
\operatorname{Hom}(B^{\otimes k} \otimes A^{\otimes m}, A)
\bigr).
\]
Taking the ``$B$-linear'' part (the component linear in a single
copy of~$B$, i.e., $k = 1$) gives a map
$\varphi\colon B \to Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ defined by
\[
\varphi(b)_m
\;:=\;
\alpha^B_{1,m}(-, b, -)
\;\in\;
\operatorname{Hom}(A^{\otimes m}, A).
\]
The full equivariance conditions on the $\alpha^B_{k,m}$ ensure that
$\varphi$ is a map of $\Etwo$-algebras (the closed-sector algebra
structure on~$B$ is intertwined with the closed-sector structure on
$Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$), and the commutativity of the diagram follows
from the associativity of the $\mathsf{SC}$-algebra axioms.

\emph{Uniqueness.}
Suppose $\varphi, \varphi'\colon Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A) \to B$ are two
morphisms making the diagram commute.  Then for each $m$, the
$m$-ary action maps satisfy
$\alpha^B_{1,m}(-, \varphi(z), -) = \alpha^B_{1,m}(-, \varphi'(z), -)$
for all $z \in Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$.
Since $A$ is an $\mathsf{SC}$-algebra, the mixed action maps
$\alpha^B_{1,m}$ are injective in the $B$-argument on the derived
level (the center map \eqref{eq:center-as-derived-hom} is computed by
$\operatorname{RHom}$, which is faithful on the derived category of
bimodules), hence $\varphi = \varphi'$.

Alternatively, uniqueness follows formally from the terminal property
in Definition~\ref{def:operadic-center}: the center is defined as a
limit, and morphisms to/from limits are unique by the universal
property of limits.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[The center functor;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:center-functor}
\index{center!functor}
The assignment $A \mapsto U(A) = (Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A), A)$ defines a
functor
\[
U\colon
E_1\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}
\]
that is \emph{right adjoint} to the forgetful functor
$\mathrm{forget}_{\mathsf{o}}\colon
\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}
\to E_1\text{-}\mathsf{Alg}$
extracting the open component.  The unit of this adjunction at
$(B, A)$ is the canonical morphism $B \to Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$
from Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:center-terminality}}.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
For any $\mathsf{SC}$-pair $(B, A)$ and any $E_1$-algebra~$A'$,
a morphism of $E_1$-algebras $A' \to A$ and a morphism
$(B, A) \to U(A') = (Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A'), A')$ correspond
bijectively to
$\operatorname{Hom}_{E_1}(A', A)
\xleftrightarrow{1-1}
\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathsf{SC}}((B,A), U(A'))$,
which is the adjunction identity
$\mathrm{forget}_{\mathsf{o}} \dashv U$.
The unit $\eta_{(B,A)}\colon (B, A) \to U(A) = (Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A), A)$
is the identity on the $A$-component and the canonical center map
on the $B$-component.
\end{proof}


\begin{remark}[The chiral center theorem as a composite statement]
\label{rem:center-theorem-summary}
\index{center theorem!summary}
Assembling Steps~1--5, the chiral center theorem is the conjunction
of four assertions:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\arabic*)}]
\item \emph{Existence}: The operadic center
  $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A)$ exists for every $E_1$-algebra~$A$
  (Proposition~\ref{prop:operadic-center-existence}).
\item \emph{Identification}: $Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A) \simeq
  C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA)$ as $\Etwo$-algebras
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}).
\item \emph{Geometric realization}: The center computes
  interior bulk observables in the half-space
  $X \times \bR_{\geq 0}$
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:center-geometric-inevitability}).
\item \emph{Universality}: $(Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(A), A)$ is
  terminal among $\mathsf{SC}$-pairs with fixed open
  component (Theorem~\ref{thm:center-terminality}).
\end{enumerate}
In the topological setting, these four assertions are respectively:
existence of the Hochschild complex, the Deligne conjecture,
the bulk-boundary correspondence in 2d TFT, and Keller's
recognition theorem.  The chiral setting enriches each with
holomorphic geometry (meromorphic propagators, configuration-space
compactifications, curve-dependence) and
with curvature (the modular characteristic $\kappa$ at higher genus).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Why the chiral setting is genuinely harder]
\label{rem:chiral-vs-topological-center}
\index{Deligne conjecture!chiral difficulties}
To summarize the difficulties identified in
Remark~\ref{rem:chiral-spectral-parameter-difficulty} at the level
of the complete proof:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item \emph{Steps 1--2} (algebraic/chain-level) are parallel to the
  topological case, with Arnold relations replacing smooth Stokes.
  The key input is the FM compactification of $\Conf_k(X)$, which
  provides the same combinatorial structure as $\FM_k(\bR^2)$ but
  with holomorphic forms.
\item \emph{Step 3} (geometric realization) requires the recognition
  theorem (F4/F5), which itself requires homotopy-Koszulity of
  $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$.  The proof of
  homotopy-Koszulity uses Kontsevich formality, which is the single
  deepest input.  In the topological case, Koszulity of the
  classical Swiss-cheese operad suffices.
\item \emph{Step 4} (comparison) requires that the $\Etwo$-structure
  from the operadic center and the $\Etwo$-structure from braces
  agree.  In the topological case, this is part of the Deligne
  conjecture proof.  In the chiral case, the comparison
  also requires that the configuration-space forms are compatible with the
  brace insertion, which is guaranteed by the Arnold relations.
\item \emph{Step 5} (universality) is formal and identical in both
  settings.
\end{enumerate}
The single genuinely new ingredient is the passage from smooth to
meromorphic propagators and the resulting replacement of Stokes by
Arnold.  Everything else (operadic center, universality, comparison)
is structural and follows the same architecture.  The higher-genus
curvature $\kappa$ introduces a further novelty (curved
$\Etwo$-algebras) not present in any topological analogue.
\end{remark}

The dimensional ladder makes precise the sense in which this

\section{\texorpdfstring{The $\En$ shadow obstruction tower}{The En shadow obstruction tower}}
\label{sec:en-shadow-tower}
\index{shadow tower!En@$\En$|textbf}
\index{En algebra@$\En$ algebra!shadow tower|textbf}

The shadow obstruction tower of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ is built from the
Maurer--Cartan element $\Theta_\cA$ in the modular convolution
algebra.  This section extends the construction to $\En$ algebras
for all~$n$, computing the $n$-dependence of the modular
characteristic~$\kappa$, the shadow depth, and the higher shadow
invariants.  The key structural result is that the binary invariant
$\kappa$ is \emph{independent} of~$n$: it depends only on the
arity-$2$ part of the bar complex, which involves
$\Conf_2(\bR^n) \simeq S^{n-1}$ and produces the same pairing for
all~$n$.  Higher-arity shadows \emph{do} depend on~$n$ through the
topology of $\Conf_k(\bR^n)$ for $k \geq 3$, but for formal
$\En$ algebras this dependence vanishes entirely: formality of the
$\En$ operad (Kontsevich, Tamarkin, Lambrechts--Voli\'c) forces
all shadow invariants to agree with their $\Einf$ values.

\begin{definition}[$\En$ shadow obstruction tower]
\label{def:en-shadow-tower}
\index{shadow tower!En@$\En$ definition}
Let $A$ be an $\En$ algebra with binary self-pairing of level~$k$.
The \emph{$\En$ bar complex} $B_{\En}(A)$ is the bar construction
built from the Fulton--MacPherson compactification
$\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ with propagator the fundamental class
of $S^{n-1}$ (the codimension-$n$ linking sphere).  At arity~$m$,
the bar complex has underlying graded vector space
\[
B_{\En}(A)_m
\;=\;
H_*(\Conf_m(\bR^n); \bQ) \otimes A^{\otimes m}.
\]
The \emph{$\En$ shadow invariants} $S_r^{\En}(A)$ are defined as
the arity-$r$ projections of the Maurer--Cartan element in the
$\En$ modular convolution algebra, exactly as in
Definition~\ref{def:shadow-algebra} with the chiral bar complex
replaced by the $\En$ bar complex.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[$\En$ shadow obstruction tower: universality of $\kappa$ and formality collapse;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:en-shadow-tower}
\index{shadow tower!En@$\En$ universality|textbf}
\index{modular characteristic!En-independence@$\En$-independence|textbf}
Let $A$ be an $\En$ algebra for $n \geq 1$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Binary universality.)}
  The modular characteristic $\kappa_{\En}(A)$ is
  \emph{independent of~$n$}:
  \[
  \kappa_{\En}(A) \;=\; \kappa(A)
  \qquad \text{for all } n \geq 1.
  \]
  In particular,
  $\kappa_{\En}(\mathfrak{H}_k) = k$
  \textup{(}Heisenberg at level~$k$\textup{)},
  $\kappa_{\En}(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k)
  = \dim(\mathfrak{g})(k + h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$
  \textup{(}affine Kac--Moody\textup{)}, and
  $\kappa_{\En}(\mathrm{Vir}_c) = c/2$
  \textup{(}Virasoro\textup{)}.

\item \textup{(Formality collapse.)}
  If $A$ is \emph{formal} as an $\En$ algebra \textup{(}i.e.,
  the transferred $\En$ structure on the bar cohomology
  $H^*(B_{\En}(A))$ has trivial higher operations\textup{)},
  then the full shadow obstruction tower collapses:
  \[
  S_r^{\En}(A) \;=\; S_r^{\Einf}(A)
  \qquad \text{for all } r \geq 2.
  \]
  Since $\Einf = \operatorname{Com}$ and the commutative shadow
  tower terminates at arity~$2$ for free algebras, a formal
  $\En$ algebra with vanishing structure constants beyond the
  self-pairing has shadow depth $r_{\max} = 2$
  \textup{(}class~G\textup{)}.

\item \textup{(Shadow depth classification.)}
  The four-class partition \textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:shadow-depth-classification})}
  \[
  \mathrm{G}\ (r_{\max} = 2),
  \quad
  \mathrm{L}\ (r_{\max} = 3),
  \quad
  \mathrm{C}\ (r_{\max} = 4),
  \quad
  \mathrm{M}\ (r_{\max} = \infty)
  \]
  is stable under the operadic level~$n$ for formal $\En$ algebras:
  the shadow class of~$A$ does not depend on~$n$.

\item \textup{(Stabilization.)}
  As $n \to \infty$, $\En \to \Einf = \operatorname{Com}$, and
  the shadow obstruction tower stabilizes:
  $S_r^{\En}(A) \to S_r^{\operatorname{Com}}(A)$.
  The stabilization is \emph{finite}: for each fixed arity~$r$,
  there exists $N(r)$ such that
  $S_r^{\En}(A) = S_r^{\operatorname{Com}}(A)$ for all $n \geq N(r)$.
  Explicitly, $N(r) \leq r$, because all positive-degree classes
  in $H^*(\Conf_r(\bR^n))$ have degree $\geq n - 1$, which
  exceeds the bar-complex degree at arity~$r$ once $n > r$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Part~(i).}
The modular characteristic $\kappa$ is determined by the arity-$2$
component of the bar complex.  At arity~$2$:
\[
B_{\En}(A)_2
\;=\;
H_*(\Conf_2(\bR^n)) \otimes A^{\otimes 2}.
\]
The space $\Conf_2(\bR^n) = \bR^n \setminus \{0\}$ deformation-retracts
onto $S^{n-1}$, so $H_*(\Conf_2(\bR^n))$ has total rank~$2$:
one generator in degree~$0$ and one in degree~$n-1$.  The
arity-$2$ self-pairing that defines $\kappa$ is the evaluation of
the binary propagator (the fundamental class of $S^{n-1}$) on
the quadratic input $a \otimes a$.  Since the fundamental class of
$S^{n-1}$ pairs with $a \otimes a$ via the level~$k$ of the
algebra (the coefficient of the leading OPE singularity),
this pairing is independent of $n$: it sees only the level.
For each family the formula follows from the explicit computation
in Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}.

\textbf{Part~(ii).}
Formality of $A$ as an $\En$ algebra means that the transferred
$\En$ structure on $H^*(B_{\En}(A))$ has $m_r^{\mathrm{tr}} = 0$
for $r \geq 3$.  The shadow invariant $S_r^{\En}(A)$ for $r \geq 3$
is determined by the transferred operations $m_j^{\mathrm{tr}}$
for $j \leq r$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-formality-low-arity}).
If all these vanish, then $S_r^{\En}(A) = 0$ for $r \geq 3$,
which equals the $\Einf$ value (the commutative operad has no
higher obstructions beyond the binary pairing for free algebras).
For non-free formal algebras, the structure constants that
contribute to $S_r$ are the same as in the $\Einf$ computation
because the higher-arity corrections from the topology of
$\Conf_r(\bR^n)$ are killed by formality.

\textbf{Part~(iii).}
The shadow class is determined by the vanishing pattern of
$\{S_r\}_{r \geq 2}$ (Definition~\ref{def:shadow-depth-classification}).
By~(ii), for formal $\En$ algebras the vanishing pattern is
independent of~$n$, so the class label is stable.

\textbf{Part~(iv).}
For fixed arity~$r$, the correction terms from
$H^*(\Conf_r(\bR^n))$ to the shadow invariant $S_r^{\En}$ involve
classes of degree $\geq n - 1$ (the minimum degree of a
positive-dimensional class in the Totaro algebra on $r$~points,
Theorem~\ref{thm:totaro-presentation}).  The bar-complex degree
at arity~$r$ is bounded by $r - 2$ (the MC equation constrains
the total propagator degree budget to $r - 2$ units).
Once $n - 1 > r - 2$, i.e., $n \geq r$, all topological
correction terms lie in degrees exceeding the MC degree budget,
hence contribute zero.  Therefore
$S_r^{\En}(A) = S_r^{\operatorname{Com}}(A)$ for $n \geq r$,
giving $N(r) \leq r$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-stabilization}).
For the binary invariant ($r = 2$),
this gives $N(2) \leq 2$; in fact, $N(2) = 1$ by~(i).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Configuration space Poincar\'e polynomials]
\label{rem:en-poincare-polynomials}
\index{configuration space!Poincare polynomial@Poincar\'e polynomial}
The Poincar\'e polynomial of $\Conf_k(\bR^n)$ for $n \geq 2$ is
\[
P_k^{(n)}(t)
\;=\;
\prod_{j=0}^{k-1} (1 + j\, t^{n-1}),
\]
giving total Betti number $P_k^{(n)}(1) = k!$ for all~$n \geq 2$
and Euler characteristic
$\chi(\Conf_k(\bR^n)) = \prod_{j=0}^{k-1}(1 + j(-1)^{n-1})$,
which vanishes for $k \geq 2$ when $n$ is even and equals $k!$
when $n$ is odd.
For $n = 1$: $\Conf_k(\bR)$ has $k!$ contractible components,
so $P_k^{(1)}(t) = k!$.
These formulas enter the bar complex dimensions via
$\dim B_{\En}(A)_k = P_k^{(n)}(1) \cdot \dim(A^{\otimes k})$
at the level of total Betti numbers.
\end{remark}

\section{\texorpdfstring{$\Etwo$ structure on the bar complex and the $\mathsf{E}_3$ obstruction}{E2 structure on the bar complex and the E3 obstruction}}
\label{sec:e3-bar-structure}
\index{bar complex!E2 structure@$\Etwo$ structure|textbf}
\index{E3 obstruction@$\mathsf{E}_3$ obstruction|textbf}

The bar complex $B(\cA)$ of a chiral algebra on a curve carries a
natural $\Etwo$ structure from the configuration spaces
$\Conf_k(\bC)$.  It does \emph{not} carry an $\mathsf{E}_3$
structure: a curve provides only~$\bR^2$, and the passage to
$\mathsf{E}_3$ requires $\bR^3$ geometry.  The correct statement is
that the \emph{derived center}
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA) = \HH^*(B(\cA), B(\cA))$
carries $\mathsf{E}_3$ structure by the Higher Deligne Conjecture,
a fundamentally different object from $B(\cA)$ itself.

\begin{proposition}[$\Etwo$ structure on $B(\cA)$ and the $\mathsf{E}_3$ obstruction;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:e3-bar-structure}
\index{bar complex!E3 obstruction@$\mathsf{E}_3$ obstruction|textbf}
\index{Gerstenhaber bracket!on bar complex}
\index{Higher Deligne Conjecture!E3 structure on derived center@$\mathsf{E}_3$ structure on derived center}
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on a smooth algebraic curve~$X$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{($\Etwo$ on $B(\cA)$.)}
  The bar complex $B(\cA)$ carries a natural $\Etwo$-coalgebra
  structure from the operad $\mathsf{FM}_2 \simeq \Etwo$ acting on
  $\Conf_k(\bC) \cong \Conf_k(\bR^2)$.  The corresponding cohomological
  structure on $H^*(B(\cA))$ is a \emph{Gerstenhaber algebra}: an
  associative product of degree~$0$ and a Lie bracket of degree~$1$
  satisfying the Leibniz rule.

\item \textup{($\mathsf{E}_3$ obstruction.)}
  The bar complex $B(\cA)$ does \emph{not} carry a natural
  $\mathsf{E}_3$ structure.  The obstruction is topological:
  $\mathsf{E}_3$ requires configurations in~$\bR^3$, but the
  curve~$X$ provides only~$\bR^2$.  The
  Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$, which governs the
  joint open--closed structure, is a \emph{colored} operad
  with $\Etwo$ on the open color and $\Eone$ on the closed color;
  it is not the tensor product $\Etwo \otimes \Eone$.
  Dunn additivity
  \textup{(}$\mathsf{E}_m \otimes \mathsf{E}_n \simeq \mathsf{E}_{m+n}$\textup{)}
  does not apply to colored operads, so
  $\SCchtop \not\simeq \mathsf{E}_3$.

\item \textup{(Framed $\Etwo \neq \mathsf{E}_3$.)}
  The BV operator $\Delta$ from genus-$1$ sewing equips $B(\cA)$
  with a \emph{framed} $\Etwo$ \textup{(}equivalently, BV\textup{)}
  structure.  This is strictly weaker than $\mathsf{E}_3$:
  the BV operator has degree~$1$ \textup{(}same as the Gerstenhaber
  bracket\textup{)}, while an $\mathsf{E}_3$ bracket would require
  degree~$2$.  The identity $\Delta^2 = 0$ does not produce a
  degree-$2$ bracket.

\item \textup{($\mathsf{E}_3$ on the derived center.)}
  By the Higher Deligne Conjecture
  \textup{(}Lurie~\cite{HA}, Francis~\cite{Francis2013}\textup{)},
  if $B(\cA)$ is an $\Etwo$-algebra, then
  $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)
  = \HH^*(B(\cA), B(\cA))$
  carries a natural $\mathsf{E}_3$ structure.  This is the correct
  $\mathsf{E}_3$ object: the derived center, not the bar complex.

\item \textup{(Obstruction dimension.)}
  The obstruction to extending $\Etwo$ to $\mathsf{E}_3$ on $B(\cA)$
  lives in $\HH^3_{\Etwo}(B(\cA), B(\cA))$.  For the Heisenberg
  $\mathfrak{H}_k$: $\dim \HH^3_{\Etwo} = 0$
  \textup{(}$\Lambda^3(\bC^1) = 0$, so the obstruction vanishes
  vacuously; this is consistent with $H^*(B(\mathfrak{H}_k))
  = \operatorname{Sym}(V^*[1])$ being $\Einf$\textup{)}.
  For affine $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$:
  $\dim \HH^3_{\Etwo} = 1$
  \textup{(}$\Lambda^3(\bC^3) = \bC$, a genuine
  obstruction\textup{)}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Part~(i).}
The $\Etwo$-coalgebra structure on $B(\cA)$ is the dual of the
$\Etwo$-algebra structure on the cobar $\Omega(B(\cA))$, which
arises from the $\mathsf{FM}_2$ operad acting on
$\Conf_k(\bC) \cong \Conf_k(\bR^2)$ via the chiral bar differential.
At the cohomological level, the product is the cup product on
Hochschild cochains and the bracket is the Gerstenhaber bracket
$[-,-]$ of degree~$1$; the Leibniz rule $[a, bc] = [a,b]c
+ (-1)^{(|a|-1)|b|} b[a,c]$ is the defining relation of a
Gerstenhaber algebra (Getzler--Jones~\cite{GJ}).

\textbf{Part~(ii).}
An $\mathsf{E}_3$ structure on a chain complex $C$ requires an
action of $\Conf_k(\bR^3)$ on $C^{\otimes k}$ for all~$k$,
compatible with the $\mathsf{FM}_3$ operad structure.  The bar
complex $B(\cA)$ is built from $\Conf_k(\bC) = \Conf_k(\bR^2)$;
no embedding $\bR^3 \hookrightarrow \bC$ exists
that would provide the additional topological data.
The Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$ has
$\Etwo$ acting on the open sector (points in $\bC$) and
$\Eone$ acting on the closed sector (points in $\bR$);
these are \emph{different colors} of a colored operad.
Dunn additivity ($\mathsf{E}_m \otimes \mathsf{E}_n
\simeq \mathsf{E}_{m+n}$, \cite{Dunn88})
applies to the tensor product of operads acting on the
\emph{same} underlying object, not to different colors of a
colored operad.

\textbf{Part~(iii).}
The BV operator $\Delta$ has $|\Delta| = 1$ and
$\Delta^2 = 0$.  The BV relation (Getzler~\cite{Getzler94}) is
$[a,b] = \Delta(ab) - (\Delta a)b - (-1)^{|a|} a(\Delta b)$,
which defines the degree-$1$ bracket from~$\Delta$; it does not
produce a degree-$2$ bracket.  A genuine $\mathsf{E}_3$ bracket
would require a map $A \otimes A \to A[2]$, corresponding to the
fundamental class of $S^2 = \Conf_2(\bR^3)/\bR^+$, which has
degree~$2$.  The degree-$1$ BV structure is the framed $\Etwo$
structure, not $\mathsf{E}_3$.

\textbf{Part~(iv).}
The Higher Deligne Conjecture states that for an $\En$-algebra~$A$,
the Hochschild cohomology $\HH^*(A, A)$ carries a natural
$\mathsf{E}_{n+1}$ structure.  At $n = 2$: if $B(\cA)$ is
$\Etwo$, then $\HH^*(B(\cA), B(\cA))$ is $\mathsf{E}_3$.
This is proved by Lurie (\cite{HA}, Theorem~5.3.1.30)
and Francis (\cite{Francis2013}).

\textbf{Part~(v).}
The obstruction to lifting $\Etwo$ to $\mathsf{E}_3$ on an
$\Etwo$-algebra~$A$ lives in the $\Etwo$-Hochschild cohomology
$\HH^3_{\Etwo}(A, A)$, which at the level of the Gerstenhaber
algebra computes degree-$3$ deformations of the $\Etwo$ structure.
For $A = H^*(B(\mathfrak{H}_k)) = \operatorname{Sym}(V^*[1])$
with $V = \bC$ (one-dimensional): the relevant obstruction space
contains $\Lambda^3(V)$ as a subquotient, which vanishes for
$\dim V = 1$.  For $V = \bC^3$ (affine $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$):
$\Lambda^3(\bC^3) = \bC$, contributing a one-dimensional
obstruction space.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The Heisenberg bar cohomology is $\Einf$]
\label{rem:heisenberg-bar-einfty}
\index{Heisenberg!bar cohomology Einfty@bar cohomology $\Einf$}
The bar cohomology $H^*(B(\mathfrak{H}_k))
= \operatorname{Sym}(V^*[1])$ is the free graded-commutative
algebra, which is $\Einf$ (the initial $\Einf$-algebra
on $V^*[1]$).  It is therefore $\En$ for \emph{all}~$n$.
However, the quasi-isomorphism $B(\mathfrak{H}_k)
\xrightarrow{\sim} H^*(B(\mathfrak{H}_k))$ is an $\Etwo$
quasi-isomorphism, not an $\Einf$ quasi-isomorphism: the
chain-level bar complex $B(\mathfrak{H}_k)$ is $\Etwo$, and
the formality map is $\Etwo$-compatible.  This is the distinction
between the cohomology-level $\Einf$ structure (a property of the
graded algebra) and the chain-level $\Etwo$ structure
(the algebraic structure preserved by the formality map).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The factorization envelope perspective]
\label{rem:en-shadow-envelope}
\index{factorization envelope!En shadow@$\En$ shadow}
The $\En$ shadow obstruction tower of the factorization envelope
$U_M(L)$ of a Lie conformal algebra~$L$ on an $n$-manifold~$M$
is determined by the $\lambda$-bracket data of~$L$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:envelope-shadow}).
By Theorem~\ref{thm:en-shadow-tower}(i), the modular
characteristic $\kappa_{\En}(U_M(L))$ agrees with the chiral
value $\kappa(U_X(L))$ for all~$n$.  The shadow depth class
is likewise independent of~$n$ for the standard families.
This reflects the structural principle that the binary-level
data (OPE self-pairing, Killing form, central charge)
is universal across the $\En$ hierarchy, while higher-arity
corrections from configuration-space topology are governed
by formality.
\end{remark}
