\chapter{\texorpdfstring{$\En$ Koszul duality and higher-dimensional bar complexes}{En Koszul duality and higher-dimensional bar complexes}}
\label{ch:en-koszul-duality}

\index{En algebra@$\En$ algebra!Koszul duality|textbf}
\index{bar complex!higher-dimensional|textbf}

This chapter widens the horizon beyond curves.  The entire engine of
Parts~I and~II---bar differentials, cobar reconstructions, Koszul
duality, shadow invariants---has been built from configuration spaces
of points on a one-dimensional complex manifold.  Once that logic is
understood in one holomorphic direction, the natural question is: what
survives for higher-dimensional configuration spaces and $\En$
structures?  The propagator changes character (from a meromorphic
$1$-form to a closed $(n{-}1)$-form), the collision relations change
(from Arnold to Totaro), and the bar-cobar adjunction changes (from
chiral to $\En$), but the underlying architectural principle---that
duality is mediated by a convolution algebra on a compactified
configuration space---persists.

The payoff is both structural and practical.  Structurally, the passage
from $n = 1$ (associative/$A_\infty$) through $n = 2$ (the
holomorphic refinement that recovers the chiral bar complex) to
$n = 3$ (Chern--Simons, Vassiliev invariants) and beyond reveals
bar-cobar duality as a single construction parametrized by manifold
dimension, with the formality phenomena of Kontsevich and
Tamarkin as shadows of the Koszul property in each dimension.
Practically, higher-dimensional bar complexes govern the algebraic
structures that arise in topological and partially topological field
theories in dimension $> 2$---the natural setting for much of the
physics discussed in the preceding chapters of this Part.

Throughout this monograph, the bar differential has been built from
configuration spaces of points on a \emph{curve}: the propagator
$\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$ is a meromorphic $1$-form, its
singularities are controlled by Arnold relations, and the bar-cobar
adjunction lives over the chiral operad.  This chapter asks a
different question: what remains true when one leaves the
Beilinson--Drinfeld curve setting and passes to genuine little-disks
factorization on real $n$-manifolds?  Then the propagator becomes a
closed $(n-1)$-form, collision strata are governed by
\emph{Totaro relations}, and the bar-cobar adjunction governs
$\En$-algebras.

Two axes must be kept separate.  Inside chiral geometry on a fixed
curve one has the hierarchy
$\Einf\text{-chiral} \subset \Pinf\text{-chiral} \subset \Eone\text{-chiral}$,
so the passage from Heisenberg to Yangian changes commutativity and
locality, not manifold dimension.  The topological ladder is instead
indexed by real manifold dimension: $n = 1$ is the classical
associative/$A_\infty$ bar-cobar theory on intervals or circles,
$n = 2$ is the oriented-surface setting whose holomorphic refinement
recovers the chiral bar complex on a complex curve, and $n = 3$
interfaces with Chern--Simons theory and Vassiliev invariants
(Chapter~\ref{ch:kontsevich-integral}).  At $n = 2$ the bar complex
governs $\Etwo$-algebras---the natural algebraic structure on braided
monoidal categories---and connects to Kontsevich's formality theorem
for the little $2$-disks operad.

\begin{theorem}[Bridge theorem: $\En$ bar-cobar and the chiral $\Eone$ engine; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:en-chiral-bridge}
\index{En algebra@$\En$ algebra!bridge to chiral bar-cobar|textbf}
The chiral bar-cobar adjunction of this monograph
\textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}\textup{)}
is the holomorphic refinement of the $n = 2$ chapter of $\En$
Koszul duality.  Precisely:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The $\En$ bar complex on an oriented real $n$-manifold~$M$ is
  built from configuration spaces $\Conf_k(M)$ with propagator a
  closed $(n{-}1)$-form and collision relations governed by the Totaro
  presentation
  \textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:totaro-presentation}\textup{)}.
  At $n = 2$, this specializes to the Arnold presentation on
  $\Conf_k(\bC)$ and the propagator becomes the logarithmic $1$-form
  $\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$---recovering the chiral bar complex
  on a complex curve.
\item The $\Eone$ bar-cobar adjunction of this monograph
  \textup{(}operating on chiral algebras with possibly non-commutative
  OPE, including Yangians and R-matrix vertex algebras\textup{)} is
  \emph{not} the topological $\En$ bar-cobar at $n = 1$
  \textup{(}which governs ordinary associative algebras on
  intervals\textup{)}, but rather the chiral specialization at
  $n = 2$ with the commutativity constraint dropped.  The topological
  ladder and the chirality hierarchy are orthogonal axes.
\item The formality hierarchy organizes how much of the bar-cobar
  adjunction reduces to cohomological data:
  \begin{center}
  \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
  \begin{tabular}{ccl}
  $n$ & Formality & Source \\
  \hline
  $\infty$ & $\Einf$ formal & tautological \textup{(}commutative operad\textup{)} \\
  $2$ & $\Etwo$ formal & Kontsevich~\cite{Kon99}, Tamarkin \\
  $1$ & $\Eone$ \emph{non-formal} in general
       & obstructed by $A_\infty$ Massey products
  \end{tabular}
  \end{center}
  The chiral bar-cobar adjunction on curves inherits $\Etwo$ formality
  from Kontsevich's theorem when the underlying chiral algebra is
  $\Einf$-chiral \textup{(}symmetric OPE\textup{)}.  For
  $\Eone$-chiral algebras, the transferred $A_\infty$ structure is
  genuinely non-formal: higher operations $m_k$ for $k \ge 3$ carry
  irreducible homotopical content.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Part~(i) is the content of Remark~\ref{rem:arnold-totaro}:
the Arnold generators on $\Conf_k(\bC)$ are the Totaro generators
at $n = 2$, and the chiral bar differential is the specialization of
the $\Etwo$ bar differential to a complex curve.

Part~(ii): the chiral hierarchy
$\Einf\text{-chiral} \subset \Pinf\text{-chiral} \subset
\Eone\text{-chiral}$ on a fixed curve changes the commutativity
constraint on the OPE, not the manifold dimension.  An
$\Eone$-chiral algebra on a complex curve is an $\Etwo$-algebra
(real dimension~$2$) with the full symmetric-group action on
$\Conf_k(\bC)$ replaced by the braid-group action; it is not an
$\Eone$-algebra in the topological sense (which would be an
$A_\infty$-algebra on an interval).

Part~(iii): $\Etwo$ formality is Kontsevich's theorem~\cite{Kon99}
for the little $2$-disks operad; $\Einf$ formality is tautological;
$\Eone$ non-formality is witnessed by non-trivial Massey products
in the bar cohomology of non-formal $A_\infty$-algebras (e.g., the
commutative algebra $\bC[x]/x^3$), which survive as higher
operations $m_k$ on the transferred structure.  The chiral
inheritance follows from the specialization map
$\En\text{-bar} \to \text{chiral bar}$ at $n = 2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Formality hierarchy as MC obstruction truncation]
\label{prop:en-formality-mc-truncation}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The formality hierarchy $\Einf \supset \Etwo \supset \Eone$ corresponds to truncation of the MC obstruction tower:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $\Einf$-formality: the full modular convolution algebra $\gAmod$ is formal; all higher brackets $\ell_n^{(g)} = 0$ for $n \geq 3$.
\item $\Etwo$-formality: the genus-$0$ convolution algebra $\mathfrak{g}^{(0)}_\cA$ is formal; the shadow tower terminates at arity~$2$ (chiral Koszulness, Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta}).
\item $\Eone$-formality: the ordered convolution algebra $\mathfrak{g}^{\Eone}_\cA$ carries a formal $A_\infty$-model; the associative shadow tower terminates.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each formality level kills the obstruction classes $o_{r+1}(\cA)$ in the cyclic deformation complex above the corresponding arity threshold (Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-formality-low-arity} for arities $2$, $3$, $4$).
The $\Etwo$ case identifies shadow-tower termination at arity~$2$ with the $\Etwo$-formality of $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$, and the $\Eone$ case is the associative analogue via the ordered bar complex (Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul}).
\end{proof}

\begin{convention}[Two axes in this chapter]
\label{conv:en-two-axes}
In this chapter, bare $\En$ always denotes little-disks/topological
factorization on real $n$-manifolds.  The earlier hierarchy
$\Einf$-chiral/$\Pinf$-chiral/$\Eone$-chiral remains curve-based.
Hence the chiral bar complex on a complex curve belongs to the
topological ladder at $n = 2$, while the $n = 1$ case is the
classical associative/$A_\infty$ bar construction.
\end{convention}

\label{rem:en-koszul-hms}%

\section{Higher-dimensional configuration spaces}
\label{sec:higher-dim-config}
\index{configuration space!higher-dimensional}

\begin{definition}[Configuration space of \texorpdfstring{$\bR^n$}{R-n}]
\label{def:config-rn}
For a smooth oriented $n$-manifold~$M$, the \emph{ordered
configuration space of $k$~points} is
\[
\Conf_k(M) \;=\;
\bigl\{(x_1, \ldots, x_k) \in M^k
  \mid x_i \neq x_j \text{ for } i \neq j\bigr\}.
\]
For $M = \bR^n$, the space $\Conf_k(\bR^n)$ is a smooth manifold
of real dimension~$nk$, and its cohomology admits an explicit
generators-and-relations presentation due to Arnold ($n = 2$, i.e., $\bR^n = \bC$) and
Totaro (general $n$).
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Arnold presentation {\cite{Arnold69}}; \texorpdfstring{$\bC \cong \bR^2$}{C = R2};
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:arnold-presentation}
\index{Arnold relations!presentation}
The cohomology ring $H^*(\Conf_k(\bC); \bQ)$ is the graded-commutative
algebra generated by classes $\eta_{ij} \in H^1$,
$1 \leq i < j \leq k$, subject to the \emph{Arnold relations}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:arnold-rel-en}
\eta_{ij} \wedge \eta_{jk}
+ \eta_{jk} \wedge \eta_{ki}
+ \eta_{ki} \wedge \eta_{ij} \;=\; 0
\quad (i < j < k).
\end{equation}
This is the Orlik--Solomon algebra $\mathrm{OS}_k$
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:os-cohomology-config})}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Totaro presentation, general \texorpdfstring{$n$}{n} {\cite{Coh76}};
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:totaro-presentation}
\index{Totaro relations|textbf}
\index{configuration space!cohomology ring}
For $n \geq 2$, the cohomology ring
$H^*(\Conf_k(\bR^n); \bQ)$ is the graded-commutative algebra
generated by classes $\alpha_{ij} \in H^{n-1}$,
$1 \leq i \neq j \leq k$, subject to the relations:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Symmetry)}\quad
  $\alpha_{ij} = (-1)^n \alpha_{ji}$;
\item \textup{(Totaro relations)}\quad
  $\alpha_{ij} \cdot \alpha_{jk}
  + \alpha_{jk} \cdot \alpha_{ki}
  + \alpha_{ki} \cdot \alpha_{ij} = 0$
  for distinct $i, j, k$;
\item \textup{(Degree)}\quad
  $\alpha_{ij}^2 = 0$ when $n$ is odd;
  when $n$ is even, $\alpha_{ij}^2$ is determined by the Euler class.
\end{enumerate}
At $n = 2$, after identifying $\bR^2 \cong \bC$ and choosing the
oriented generator of $H^1(\Conf_2(\bR^2))$, one recovers Arnold's
three-term relation on $\Conf_k(\bC)$.  By contrast, $n = 1$ is the
degree-$0$ associative setting of ordered points on a line, not the
chiral curve case.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
See Totaro~\cite{Totaro96}, Theorem~1; also
Cohen~\cite{Coh76}, Theorem~A for the formality statement.
The key geometric input is that projection maps
$\Conf_k(\bR^n) \to \Conf_2(\bR^n)$ induce pullbacks of the
fundamental class of $S^{n-1}$ (the linking sphere), and all
relations among these pullbacks are generated by the three-term
relation (ii).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Arnold as Totaro at \texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2}]
\label{rem:arnold-totaro}
\index{Arnold relations!as Totaro specialization}
In the $\En$ hierarchy, $n$ is the \emph{real} dimension.
The classical Arnold generators on $\Conf_k(\bC)$ are Totaro
generators at $n = 2$; our chiral bar complex uses $n = 2$:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ccc}
$\En$-level & Manifold & Bar complex \\
\hline
$n = 1$ & interval/circle & associative bar (classical) \\
$n = 2$ & surface/curve & chiral bar (this monograph) \\
$n = 3$ & $3$-manifold & Chern--Simons bar \\
general $n$ & $n$-manifold & $\En$ bar
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Main Theorems~A, B, C are the holomorphic refinement of the
$n = 2$ chapter of $\En$ Koszul duality.
\end{remark}


\subsection{\texorpdfstring{Fulton--MacPherson compactification in dimension $n$}{Fulton--MacPherson compactification in dimension n}}
\label{subsec:fm-general-n}
\index{Fulton--MacPherson compactification!higher-dimensional}

\begin{definition}[FM compactification of \texorpdfstring{$\Conf_k(\bR^n)$}{Conf-k(R-n)}]
\label{def:fm-rn}
The \emph{Fulton--MacPherson compactification}
$\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ is the real oriented blowup of $(\bR^n)^k$
along all partial diagonals, ordered by reverse inclusion.
Equivalently (Axelrod--Singer, Kontsevich), it is the closure of
the image of the map
\[
\Conf_k(\bR^n)
\;\hookrightarrow\;
(\bR^n)^k
\times \prod_{i<j} S^{n-1}
\times \prod_{|S| \geq 2} [0, \infty]
\]
recording positions, relative directions
$\theta_{ij} = (x_i - x_j)/|x_i - x_j| \in S^{n-1}$,
and relative distances.  The result is a smooth manifold with
corners whose boundary strata are indexed by nested partitions
of $\{1, \ldots, k\}$---the same combinatorial data as the
Stasheff associahedron ($n = 1$) or the FM operad ($n \geq 2$).
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Boundary strata and operadic structure;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere{} \cite{FM94}]
\label{prop:fm-boundary-strata}
\index{Fulton--MacPherson!boundary strata}
The codimension-$1$ boundary strata of
$\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ are indexed by subsets
$S \subset \{1, \ldots, k\}$ with $|S| \geq 2$, and are
diffeomorphic to
\[
\partial_S \overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)
\;\cong\;
\overline{\Conf}_{k-|S|+1}(\bR^n)
\times
\overline{\Conf}_{|S|}(\bR^n).
\]
This factorization equips $\{\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)\}_{k \geq 0}$
with the structure of a topological operad---the
\emph{Fulton--MacPherson operad} $\mathsf{FM}_n$---which is a model
for the $\En$ operad \textup{(}i.e., weakly equivalent to the
little $n$-disks operad\textup{)}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The operadic structure is due to Getzler--Jones~\cite{GJ}
and Salvatore; the equivalence $\mathsf{FM}_n \simeq \En$ is
proved by Salvatore--Longoni for $n \geq 2$ and follows from
Stasheff's theorem for $n = 1$.
\end{proof}

\section{\texorpdfstring{The $\En$ propagator}{The En propagator}}
\label{sec:en-propagator}
\index{propagator!$\En$}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} propagator]
\label{def:en-propagator}
\index{propagator!Green's form}
An \emph{$\En$ propagator} on an oriented $n$-manifold~$M$ is a
smooth closed $(n-1)$-form
\[
G \;\in\; \Omega^{n-1}(M \times M \setminus \Delta)
\]
satisfying the following properties:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Poincar\'e dual of diagonal)}\quad
  $G$ represents the Poincar\'e dual of the diagonal
  $\Delta \subset M \times M$ in the sense that for any
  closed form $\eta \in \Omega^*(M)$,
  \[
  \int_{M \times M} G \wedge \pi_2^* \eta
  \;=\; \int_M \eta,
  \]
  where $\pi_2\colon M \times M \to M$ is the second projection.
\item \textup{(Singularity)}\quad
  Near the diagonal $\Delta$, in geodesic coordinates,
  \[
  G \;\sim\;
  \frac{1}{\mathrm{vol}(S^{n-1})}\;
  \frac{r_1\, dr_2 \wedge \cdots \wedge dr_n
  - r_2\, dr_1 \wedge dr_3 \wedge \cdots \wedge dr_n
  + \cdots}{|r|^n}
  \;+\; \text{smooth},
  \]
  where $r = x_1 - x_2$ and $\mathrm{vol}(S^{n-1}) = 2\pi^{n/2}/\Gamma(n/2)$.
\item \textup{(Symmetry)}\quad
  Under the involution $\sigma\colon (x_1, x_2) \mapsto (x_2, x_1)$,
  $\sigma^* G = (-1)^n G$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[\texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2}: the chiral propagator on a complex curve]
\label{ex:n2-chiral-propagator}
For $M = \bC$ (real dimension $2$, but complex dimension $1$---the
chiral case), the propagator is
$G = \eta_{12} = d\log(z_1 - z_2)$, a meromorphic $1$-form.
It is closed ($dG = 0$ away from $z_1 = z_2$), represents the
Poincar\'e dual of the diagonal, and satisfies
$\sigma^* G = d\log(z_2 - z_1) = d\log(-(z_1-z_2)) = d\log(-1) + d\log(z_1-z_2) = G$,
since $d\log(-1) = 0$ (differential of a constant).  Residues along $z_1 = z_2$ extract OPE data.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[\texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2}: the \texorpdfstring{$\Etwo$}{E2} propagator on \texorpdfstring{$\bR^2$}{R2}]
\label{ex:n2-propagator}
For $M = \bR^2$, the propagator is
\[
G \;=\;
\frac{1}{2\pi}\;
\frac{(x_1 - x_2)\, dy_1 - (y_1 - y_2)\, dx_1}
     {(x_1 - x_2)^2 + (y_1 - y_2)^2}
\;=\;
\frac{1}{2\pi}\, d\arg(z_1 - z_2),
\]
a closed $1$-form on $\Conf_2(\bR^2)$.  This is the angular form
(winding number kernel): integration over a small circle
$S^1 \subset \bR^2$ around $x_2$ gives $1$.  Note that
$G = (1/2\pi)\, d\arg(z_1 - z_2)$ is exactly the
\emph{Kontsevich propagator} on $S^1 \subset \bC$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:propagator-restriction}).  The $\Etwo$
propagator on a surface and the chiral propagator on a complex curve
are thus two aspects of the same object: the former is real-valued
and topological, the latter is holomorphic and algebraic.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[\texorpdfstring{$n = 3$}{n = 3}: the Chern--Simons propagator]
\label{ex:n3-propagator}
\index{Chern--Simons!propagator}
For $M = \bR^3$, the propagator is
\[
G \;=\;
\frac{1}{4\pi}\;
\frac{\epsilon_{abc}\, r^a\, dr^b \wedge dr^c}{|r|^3},
\]
where $r = x_1 - x_2 \in \bR^3$.  This is the closed $2$-form on
$\Conf_2(\bR^3)$ representing the linking number:
$\int_{S^2} G = 1$.  In the Axelrod--Singer perturbative expansion of
Chern--Simons theory~\cite{Kon94}, this propagator appears as the
kernel of the gauge-fixed Green's operator $d^{-1}$.
\end{example}

\begin{proposition}[Residue as linking sphere integral;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:linking-sphere-residue}
\index{residue!linking sphere}
For an $\En$ propagator $G$ on $\bR^n$ and a smooth function
$f$ on a neighborhood of the diagonal, the \emph{$\En$ residue} is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:en-residue}
\Res_\Delta(G \wedge f)
\;:=\;
\lim_{\varepsilon \to 0}
\int_{|x_1 - x_2| = \varepsilon} G \cdot f(x_1, x_2)
\;=\; f(x, x),
\end{equation}
where the integral is over the linking sphere
$S^{n-1}_\varepsilon \subset \Conf_2(\bR^n)$ of radius~$\varepsilon$
around the diagonal.  This generalizes the residue operation
$\Res_{z_1 = z_2}$ in the chiral ($n = 2$) case.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By the singularity condition (ii) of
Definition~\ref{def:en-propagator}, $G$ restricts on the
sphere $|r| = \varepsilon$ to the standard volume form on $S^{n-1}$
(normalized to total volume~$1$) plus terms vanishing as
$\varepsilon \to 0$.  Hence the integral extracts the value
$f(x, x)$, exactly as a contour integral residue extracts the
leading Laurent coefficient.
\end{proof}

\section{\texorpdfstring{The $\Etwo$ bar complex}{The E2 bar complex}}
\label{sec:e2-bar}
\index{bar complex!$\Etwo$}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\Etwo$}{E2} bar complex]
\label{def:e2-bar-complex}
\index{bar complex!$\Etwo$|textbf}
Let $A$ be an $\Etwo$-algebra (equivalently, an algebra over
the little $2$-disks operad).  The \emph{$\Etwo$ bar complex}
is the chain complex
\[
\barB_{\Etwo}(A)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{k \geq 1}
\Omega^*\bigl(\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^2)\bigr)
\otimes_{\Sigma_k} (s^{-1} \bar{A})^{\otimes k},
\]
with differential
\begin{equation}\label{eq:e2-bar-diff}
d_{\Etwo}
\;=\;
d_{\mathrm{dR}}
\;+\;
\sum_{S \subset \{1, \ldots, k\},\, |S| \geq 2}
\Res_{\partial_S}\bigl(G_S \wedge \mu_S\bigr),
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $G_S = \bigwedge_{i < j,\; i, j \in S} \alpha_{ij}$ is the
  product of propagators for pairs within the
  colliding subset~$S$,
\item $\Res_{\partial_S}$ is the linking-sphere residue along the
  boundary stratum $\partial_S$ of
  $\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^2)$
  \textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:linking-sphere-residue})},
\item $\mu_S\colon \bar{A}^{\otimes |S|} \to \bar{A}$ is the
  $\Etwo$-multiplication indexed by the FM operad element
  corresponding to the stratum~$\partial_S$.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[\texorpdfstring{$d^2 = 0$}{d squared = 0} from Totaro relations;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:e2-d-squared}
\index{Totaro relations!and $d^2 = 0$}
The differential $d_{\Etwo}$ of
Definition~\textup{\ref{def:e2-bar-complex}} satisfies
$d_{\Etwo}^2 = 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The proof follows the same structure as the chiral case
(Corollary~\ref{cor:bar-d-squared-zero-arnold}), with the Arnold
relations replaced by Totaro relations.

We compute $d_{\Etwo}^2$ on a bar element
$\alpha \in \Omega^*(\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^2))
\otimes (s^{-1} \bar{A})^{\otimes k}$.
The square decomposes into three terms:
\[
d_{\Etwo}^2
\;=\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{dR}}^2}_{= 0}
\;+\;
\underbrace{[d_{\mathrm{dR}},\, d_{\mathrm{res}}]}_{(\star)}
\;+\;
\underbrace{d_{\mathrm{res}}^2}_{(\star\star)},
\]
where $d_{\mathrm{res}} = \sum_S \Res_{\partial_S}(G_S \wedge \mu_S)$.

For $(\star)$: the de~Rham differential commutes with the residue
operation because $G$ is closed ($dG = 0$), so the cross-term
$[d_{\mathrm{dR}}, d_{\mathrm{res}}]$ reduces to
$\sum_S \Res_{\partial_S}(dG_S \wedge \mu_S) = 0$.

For $(\star\star)$: the square $d_{\mathrm{res}}^2$ involves
iterated residues along pairs of boundary strata.  For three
distinct points $i, j, k$, the iterated residue
$\Res_{\partial_{\{i,j\}}} \circ \Res_{\partial_{\{j,k\}}}$
plus cyclic permutations gives a sum of three terms.  The
Totaro relation
\[
\alpha_{ij} \cdot \alpha_{jk}
+ \alpha_{jk} \cdot \alpha_{ki}
+ \alpha_{ki} \cdot \alpha_{ij} \;=\; 0
\qquad
\text{in } H^{2(n-1)}(\Conf_3(\bR^n))
\]
at $n = 2$ ensures that this sum vanishes.  More precisely: each
term in $d_{\mathrm{res}}^2$ corresponds to a codimension-$2$
boundary stratum of $\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^2)$, and the Totaro
relation is exactly the statement that the codimension-$2$
contributions cancel in cohomology.

For nested collisions (e.g., $\{i, j\} \subset \{i, j, k\}$),
the iterated residue factors through the operadic composition
$\mu_{\{i,j,k\}} = \mu_{\{i,j\}} \circ \mu_{\{j,k\}}$, and
cancellation follows from the $\Etwo$-algebra axiom (associativity
up to coherent homotopy, encoded by the FM operad structure).

For the algebraic contribution: iterated residues at the same
stratum $S$ produce the square $\mu_S \circ \mu_S$, which vanishes
by the $A_\infty$ relations encoded in the $\Etwo$ structure.

The argument generalizes Idrissi's result~\cite{Idrissi22} that
Poincar\'e duality on configuration spaces ensures $d^2 = 0$ for
the propagator-based differential, specialized to $n = 2$ with
explicit Totaro generators.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Comparison with the chiral bar complex]
\label{rem:e2-vs-chiral}
\index{bar complex!$\Etwo$ vs chiral}
The $\Etwo$ bar complex (Definition~\ref{def:e2-bar-complex})
and the chiral bar complex (Chapter~\ref{chap:bar-cobar}) differ in propagator and dependence:
the chiral complex uses the holomorphic $\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$ and depends on the complex structure,
while the $\Etwo$ complex uses the topological $G = (1/2\pi)\, d\arg(z_1 - z_2)$ and depends only on oriented topology.
For a chiral algebra $\cA$ on~$X$,
$\barB_{\Etwo}(\cA^{\mathrm{top}}) \simeq \barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)^{\mathrm{top}}$.
The chiral bar complex carries strictly more information: genus-$g$ quantum corrections
$\kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality})
live in the chiral bar complex but not in the $\Etwo$ bar complex.
\end{remark}

\section{\texorpdfstring{$\En$ Koszul duality}{En Koszul duality}}
\label{sec:en-koszul-duality}
\index{Koszul duality!$\En$|textbf}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} bar-cobar adjunction]
\label{def:en-bar-cobar}
For each $n \geq 1$, the \emph{$\En$ bar functor} and \emph{$\En$
cobar functor} are defined by:
\begin{align}
\barB_{\En} &\colon
  \En\text{-}\mathrm{Alg} \;\longrightarrow\;
  \En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg},
  \label{eq:en-bar}\\
\Omega_{\En} &\colon
  \En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg} \;\longrightarrow\;
  \En\text{-}\mathrm{Alg},
  \label{eq:en-cobar}
\end{align}
where $\En\text{-}\mathrm{Alg}$ denotes the $\infty$-category of
$\En$-algebras in chain complexes and $\En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg}$
denotes the $\infty$-category of $\En$-coalgebras.  The bar
construction uses the chain complex
$C_*(\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n))$ with the differential of
\eqref{eq:e2-bar-diff} generalized to dimension~$n$, and the cobar
construction is its linear dual.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} Koszul duality;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:en-koszul-duality}
\index{Koszul duality!$\En$}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Lurie~\cite{HA}, \S5.2)}\quad
  The functors $\barB_{\En}$ and $\Omega_{\En}$ form an adjoint pair
  \[
  \barB_{\En}
  \;\dashv\;
  \Omega_{\En}
  \colon
  \En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg}
  \;\rightleftarrows\;
  \En\text{-}\mathrm{Alg}.
  \]
\item \textup{(Francis~\cite{Francis2013}, Theorem~4.20)}\quad
  For an $\En$-algebra $A$ that is \emph{$\En$-Koszul}
  \textup{(}the $\En$ bar spectral sequence collapses\textup{)},
  the counit
  $\Omega_{\En}(\barB_{\En}(A)) \xrightarrow{\sim} A$
  is a quasi-isomorphism.
\item \textup{(Ayala--Francis~\cite{AF15}, Theorem~7.8)}\quad
  For a closed oriented $n$-manifold~$M$ and an $\En$-algebra~$A$,
  Poincar\'e--Koszul duality gives a natural equivalence:
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:poincare-koszul}
  \int_M A
  \;\simeq\;
  \int_M A^\vee[-n],
  \end{equation}
  where $\int_M$ denotes $\En$-factorization homology and $A^\vee$
  is the $\En$-Koszul dual coalgebra.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Chain-level content of this monograph]
\label{rem:chain-level-en}
Ayala--Francis prove part~(iii) at the $\infty$-categorical level.
Main Theorems~A, B, C are the chain-level refinement at $n = 2$:
explicit propagators, residue formulas, and spectral sequences.
The genus-$g$ quantum corrections (Theorem~B) and complementarity
(Theorem~C) are specific to curves with complex structure, invisible
to the topological $\Etwo$ theory.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Recovery of chiral bar-cobar at \texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2};
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:n2-recovery}
For a chiral algebra $\cA$ on a complex curve~$X$, the chiral
bar-cobar adjunction of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}} is recovered
from the $\Etwo$ bar-cobar adjunction
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:en-koszul-duality}(i))} by
equipping the underlying $\Etwo$-algebra $\cA^{\mathrm{top}}$ with
the holomorphic propagator $\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$ in
place of the topological propagator $G$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The holomorphic propagator $\eta_{ij}$ on a curve~$X$ is a specific
representative of the $\Etwo$ propagator class
$[\alpha_{ij}] \in H^1(\Conf_2(X))$; different representatives give
quasi-isomorphic bar complexes (since they differ by exact forms,
and the bar differential is insensitive to exact corrections in
cohomology).  The Arnold relations on~$X$ are the Totaro relations at
$n = 2$, so $d^2 = 0$ follows from
Theorem~\ref{thm:e2-d-squared}.  The residue operation
$\Res_{z_i = z_j}$ is the $n = 2$ linking-sphere residue
(Proposition~\ref{prop:linking-sphere-residue}): the linking sphere
$S^1$ around the diagonal in a surface is the contour of integration
in the OPE residue formula.
\end{proof}

\section{Connection to Ayala--Francis}
\label{sec:ayala-francis-connection}
\index{Ayala--Francis!chain-level refinement}
\index{Poincar\'e--Koszul duality!chain-level}

Ayala--Francis~\cite{AF15} establish Poincar\'e--Koszul duality as
an equivalence of $\infty$-categories:

\begin{theorem}[Poincar\'e--Koszul duality, AF {\cite{AF15}};
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:af-pkd}
\index{Poincar\'e--Koszul duality}
Let $M$ be a closed oriented $n$-manifold and $A$ an $\En$-algebra.
There is a natural equivalence
\[
\int_M A
\;\simeq\;
\mathrm{Map}_{\En\text{-}\mathrm{CoAlg}}
\bigl(\barB_{\En}(\mathbf{1}),\, \barB_{\En}(A)\bigr),
\]
where $\mathbf{1}$ is the unit $\En$-algebra.
For $A$ augmented, this simplifies to the Poincar\'e--Koszul duality
\eqref{eq:poincare-koszul}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proposition}[Our construction refines AF at \texorpdfstring{$n = 2$}{n = 2};
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:refines-af}
At $n = 2$, the chain-level bar complex
$\barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$ of
Chapter~\textup{\ref{chap:bar-cobar}} is a
strict model for the $\Etwo$ bar construction of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:af-pkd}}, in the following sense:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item The FM propagator integrals
  $\int_{\overline{C}_k(X)} \prod_{(i,j)} \eta_{ij}
  \otimes \prod_v a_v$
  compute the factorization homology $\int_X \cA$ as a chain
  complex.
\item The Verdier duality pairing on $\overline{C}_k(X)$
  \textup{(}\S\textup{\ref{sec:verdier-ayala-francis})} realizes the
  Poincar\'e duality of Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:af-pkd}} at the
  cochain level.
\item The spectral sequence from bar filtration to bar cohomology
  is the descent spectral sequence of factorization homology with
  respect to the FM cover.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Part (a): by the fundamental theorem of factorization
homology~\cite{AF15}, $\int_X \cA$ is computed by a \v{C}ech complex
with respect to any Weiss cover of~$X$ by disks.  The FM
compactification $\overline{C}_k(X)$ provides a canonical
resolution: configuration space integrals against the propagator
$\eta_{ij}$ compute the \v{C}ech differential, and the bar
elements $\bigotimes_v a_v$ provide the coefficients.  This is the
content of the Prism Principle
(Theorem~\ref{thm:prism-higher-genus}).

Part (b): Verdier duality $\mathbb{D}_{\overline{C}_k(X)}$ exchanges the
FM compactification with the open configuration space.  This is the
geometric incarnation of the Poincar\'e duality in
Theorem~\ref{thm:af-pkd}: the duality pairing between
$\int_X A$ and $\int_X A^\vee$ is realized by the intersection
pairing on $\overline{C}_k(X)$.  See
Theorem~\ref{thm:verdier-bar-cobar} for the precise statement
at $n = 2$.

Part (c): the FM cover filtration
$F_p = \bigoplus_{k \leq p} C_*(\overline{C}_k(X))
\otimes (s^{-1} \bar{A})^{\otimes k}$
gives a spectral sequence with
$E_1^{p,q} = H^q(\overline{C}_p(X)) \otimes (s^{-1}\bar{A})^{\otimes p}$
converging to $H^*(\int_X A)$.  This is the bar spectral sequence
of Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-spectral-sequence}, reinterpreted as
the descent spectral sequence for the factorization homology
presheaf.
\end{proof}

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{The $\En$ bar complex for arbitrary $n$}{The En bar complex for arbitrary n}}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} bar complex, general \texorpdfstring{$n$}{n}]
\label{def:en-bar-general}
\index{bar complex!general $n$}
For $n \geq 1$ and an $\En$-algebra $A$, the \emph{$\En$ bar complex}
is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:en-bar-general}
\barB_{\En}(A)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{k \geq 1}
\Omega^*\bigl(\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)\bigr)
\otimes_{\Sigma_k} (s^{-1}\bar{A})^{\otimes k},
\quad
d \;=\; d_{\mathrm{dR}} + d_{\mathrm{res}},
\end{equation}
where $d_{\mathrm{res}} = \sum_S \Res_{\partial_S}(G_S \wedge \mu_S)$
uses the $\En$ propagator of Definition~\textup{\ref{def:en-propagator}}
and linking-sphere residues
\textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:linking-sphere-residue})}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[\texorpdfstring{$d^2 = 0$}{d squared = 0} for the \texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} bar complex;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:en-d-squared}
\index{bar complex!$d^2 = 0$}
For any $n \geq 1$ and any $\En$-algebra $A$, the $\En$ bar
differential satisfies $d^2 = 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The argument is identical to the $n = 2$ case
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e2-d-squared}), with the Arnold/Totaro
relations in $H^*(\Conf_3(\bR^n))$ providing the cancellation of
$d_{\mathrm{res}}^2$.  For general~$n$, the Totaro relation holds
in degree $2(n-1)$, and the cancellation works in any degree.

The result also follows from the general formality framework of
Idrissi~\cite{Idrissi22}: Poincar\'e duality on the FM
compactification $\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ provides an explicit
quasi-isomorphism between the de~Rham complex of
$\overline{\Conf}_k(\bR^n)$ and its cohomology algebra (the Totaro
algebra), and the propagator differential is compatible with this
quasi-isomorphism.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The dimensional ladder]
\label{rem:dimensional-ladder}
\index{dimensional ladder}
The transition from $n$ to $n+1$ trades algebraic structure for
topological generality:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{clll}
$n$ & Propagator & Relations & Bar complex computes \\
\hline
$1$ & $d\log|t_1 - t_2|$ & Stasheff polytopes & Hochschild cohomology \\
$2$ & $d\log(z_1 - z_2)$ & Arnold (OS) & Chiral Koszul dual \\
$3$ & linking $2$-form & graph cohomology & Vassiliev invariants \\
$n$ & $(n{-}1)$-form & Totaro & $\En$-factorization homology
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
At each level, the propagator is a closed $(n-1)$-form, the
relations in $H^*(\Conf_3(\bR^n))$ ensure $d^2 = 0$, and the bar
cohomology computes the Koszul dual in the appropriate operadic
sense.
\end{remark}


\subsection{Knudsen's higher enveloping algebras}

\begin{theorem}[Higher enveloping algebras; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:knudsen-higher-enveloping}
\index{enveloping algebra!higher}
\textup{(}Knudsen~\cite{Knudsen18}\textup{)}\quad
For a Lie algebra $\fg$ and $n \geq 1$, the \emph{$n$-th enveloping
algebra} $U_n(\fg)$ is an $\En$-algebra satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $U_1(\fg) = U(\fg)$ is the classical universal enveloping
  algebra.
\item \textup{(PBW)}\quad There is an equivalence of graded objects
  $\mathrm{gr}\, U_n(\fg) \simeq \mathrm{Sym}^{\En}(\fg[n-1])$,
  where $\mathrm{Sym}^{\En}$ is the free commutative $\En$-algebra.
\item \textup{(Koszul duality)}\quad
  $\barB_{\En}(U_n(\fg))$ is quasi-isomorphic to
  $\mathrm{CE}^{\En}_*(\fg)$, the $\En$-Chevalley--Eilenberg
  complex of~$\fg$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

This result is the higher-dimensional analogue of the PBW theorem
and the classical bar-CE comparison.  At $n = 2$, the $\Etwo$
enveloping algebra $U_2(\fg)$ is the topological shadow of the
chiral universal enveloping algebra
$\widehat{U}(\fg_k) = V_k(\fg)$ (the vacuum module; see Theorem~\ref{thm:e2-d-squared} for the precise $\Etwo$ statement).  Knudsen's
Koszul duality at $n = 2$ is the topological shadow of our chiral
Koszul duality.


\subsection{Kontsevich formality and deformation quantization}

\begin{theorem}[Formality of \texorpdfstring{$\Etwo$}{E2}; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:e2-formality}
\index{formality!$\Etwo$ operad}
\textup{(}Kontsevich~\cite{Kon03},
Tamarkin~\cite{Tamarkin00}\textup{)}\quad
The $\Etwo$ operad is formal over $\bQ$: there is a
quasi-isomorphism of operads
\[
C_*(\Etwo;\, \bQ)
\;\simeq\;
H_*(\Etwo;\, \bQ).
\]
Consequently, every $\Etwo$-algebra structure on a
cochain complex $A$ is determined up to quasi-isomorphism by the
induced $H_*(\Etwo)$-algebra structure on cohomology.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Formality and deformation quantization]
\label{rem:formality-dq}
\index{deformation quantization!from $\Etwo$ formality}
Kontsevich formality~\cite{Kon03} implies star products on
arbitrary Poisson manifolds.  In our framework
(Chapter~\ref{chap:chiral-deformation}), $\Etwo$ Koszul duality
exchanges the bar complex of polyvector fields with that of
polydifferential operators---Poisson brackets with star products.
The explicit Kontsevich star product arises from propagator integrals
on $\overline{\Conf}_k(\mathcal{H})$, a specific $\Etwo$ bar complex
model with boundary conditions.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[\texorpdfstring{$\En$}{En} formality for \texorpdfstring{$n \geq 2$}{n >= 2};
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]

The $\En$ operad is formal over $\bR$ for all $n \geq 2$.

\section{The Swiss-cheese case: holomorphic--topological
factorization}
\label{sec:swiss-cheese}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad|textbf}
\index{bar complex!Swiss-cheese structure|textbf}
\index{holomorphic--topological!factorization|textbf}

The dimensional ladder has so far been one-dimensional: $n = 1$
(associative), $n = 2$ (chiral), $n = 3$ (Chern--Simons).  But the
bar complex of this monograph already carries a \emph{mixed}
structure that does not fit on this line.  The differential
$d_{\barB}$ lives on $\FM_k(\bC)$ (the $n = 2$ slot); the
coassociative coproduct~$\Delta$ lives on $\Conf_k(\bR)$ (the
$n = 1$ slot).  Together, a bar element of degree~$k$ is
parametrized by
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bar-product-space}
\FM_k(\bC) \times \Conf_k(\bR),
\end{equation}
the product of holomorphic and topological configuration spaces.
This product structure is the operadic fingerprint of a
\emph{two-colored} operad: the Swiss-cheese operad
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$.

\begin{definition}[Holomorphic--topological Swiss-cheese operad]
\label{def:SC}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad!definition}
Define the two-colored topological operad $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$
with colors $\{\mathsf{ch}, \mathsf{top}\}$ (closed/holomorphic
and open/topological) by:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Closed output, closed inputs only:}
  $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}
  ((\mathsf{ch},\ldots,\mathsf{ch});\, \mathsf{ch})
  := \FM_k(\bC)$.
\item \emph{Open output, mixed inputs:}
  $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}
  ((\underbrace{\mathsf{ch},\ldots}_{k},
    \underbrace{\mathsf{top},\ldots}_{m});\, \mathsf{top})
  := \FM_k(\bC) \times E_1(m)$.
\item \emph{No open-to-closed:}
  $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}
  (\ldots,\mathsf{top},\ldots;\, \mathsf{ch})
  = \varnothing$.
\end{enumerate}
Composition is componentwise: FM insertion in~$\bC$, interval
insertion in~$E_1$.  The no-open-to-closed rule ensures that
the closed color is an operadic retract: it receives no information
from the open color.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bar complex as Swiss-cheese coalgebra;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:bar-swiss-cheese}
\index{bar complex!Swiss-cheese coalgebra}
Let $\cA$ be an augmented chiral algebra on a curve~$X$.  The bar
complex $\barB^{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$, equipped with its bar
differential~$d_{\barB}$ and deconcatenation coproduct~$\Delta$,
is a dg coalgebra over
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$:
\begin{center}
\small
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\textbf{Bar structure} & \textbf{SC color} &
  \textbf{Configuration space} \\
\hline
Differential $d_{\barB}$
  & closed (holomorphic)
  & $\FM_k(\bC)$: collision residues \\[2pt]
Coproduct $\Delta$
  & open (topological)
  & $\Conf_k(\bR)$: interval splitting \\[2pt]
Coderivation: $d_{\barB} \circ \Delta
  = \Delta \circ d_{\barB}$
  & compatibility
  & product structure~\eqref{eq:bar-product-space}
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The coproduct is the deconcatenation
$\Delta[a_1|\cdots|a_n] =
\sum_{i=0}^{n} [a_1|\cdots|a_i] \otimes [a_{i+1}|\cdots|a_n]$,
which is coassociative by construction (tensor coalgebra).  The
ordering of tensor factors corresponds to the ordering of points
$t_1 < \cdots < t_n$ on~$\bR$; the splitting at~$i$ corresponds to
cutting the interval at $t_i$.  This is the $E_1$ coalgebra
structure.

The differential $d_{\barB}$ extracts residues along collision
divisors $D_S \subset \partial\FM_k(\bC)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-nilpotency-complete}).  It is a coderivation
of~$\Delta$: the Leibniz rule
$d_{\barB} \circ \Delta = (\id \otimes d_{\barB}
  + d_{\barB} \otimes \id) \circ \Delta$
holds because collision residues in~$\bC$ commute with interval
splitting in~$\bR$.  This is the content of the product structure:
$\FM_k(\bC)$ and $\Conf_k(\bR)$ contribute independently.

The no-open-to-closed constraint is automatic: the coproduct
(open-color structure) does not appear in the differential
(closed-color structure).  The bar differential $d_{\barB}$ is
built purely from the chiral product~$\mu$ and the FM boundary
strata, with no reference to the tensor ordering.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Physical interpretation]
\label{rem:sc-physical}
A three-dimensional holomorphic--topological (HT) quantum field
theory on $\bC_z \times \bR_t$ produces local observables that
factorize holomorphically in~$z$ and associatively in~$t$.  The BV
quantization of such a theory produces an algebra over
$W(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}})$ (the Boardman--Vogt resolution),
and the resulting higher operations $m_k$ are realized by
configuration-space integrals on
$\FM_k(\bC) \times \Conf_k(\bR)$ --- the same spaces that
parametrize bar elements of degree~$k$.  Volume~II develops this
identification in full.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Curved Swiss-cheese at higher genus]
\label{rem:sc-higher-genus}
\index{Swiss-cheese operad!curved|textbf}
At genus~$g \geq 1$, the bar complex $\barB^{(g)}(\cA)$ is a
\emph{curved} Swiss-cheese coalgebra: the coproduct~$\Delta$
remains coassociative (it is the tensor coalgebra structure,
independent of genus), but the differential is curved:
$\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$.  The curvature is
\emph{scalar} (Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}), hence
central in the coalgebra, hence a scalar coderivation: the product
structure~\eqref{eq:bar-product-space} persists.  The corrected
differential $\Dg{g}$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-diff-squares-zero})
restores flatness over $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ by incorporating
period integrals that couple the $\bR$-ordering to the Hodge
bundle~$\mathbb{E}$.  The genus tower of curved Swiss-cheese
algebras is controlled by the modular characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$
and the $\hat{A}$-genus generating function
(Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Position in the dimensional ladder]
\label{rem:sc-dimensional-ladder}
The Swiss-cheese operad $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ is the
$(n_{\mathrm{hol}}, n_{\mathrm{top}}) = (2, 1)$ entry in a
two-parameter family of mixed operads.  The one-parameter diagonal
$n_{\mathrm{hol}} = n$, $n_{\mathrm{top}} = 0$ recovers the $\En$
ladder of this chapter.  The off-diagonal entries mix holomorphic
and topological factorization in different dimensions.  The
classical Swiss-cheese of Voronov~\cite{Voronov99} is the
$(n, n-1)$ case; ours is the holomorphic refinement at $(2, 1)$.
\end{remark}

\section{Summary and the dimensional ladder}
\label{sec:en-summary}

\noindent
\textbf{What is proved.}
\begin{enumerate}
\item The Totaro presentation of $H^*(\Conf_k(\bR^n))$ generalizes
  Arnold's presentation from $n = 2$ to all~$n$
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:totaro-presentation}).
\item The $\Etwo$ bar complex satisfies $d^2 = 0$ via the Totaro
  relations (Theorem~\ref{thm:e2-d-squared}).
\item The chiral bar-cobar duality of Main Theorem~A is recovered
  as the $n = 2$ case of $\En$ Koszul duality
  (Corollary~\ref{cor:n2-recovery}).
\item Our construction is the chain-level refinement of
  Ayala--Francis Poincar\'e--Koszul duality at $n = 2$
  (Proposition~\ref{prop:refines-af}).
\item $\En$ Koszul duality, $\En$ formality, and higher enveloping
  algebras are established in the literature for all~$n$
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:en-koszul-duality},
  Theorem~\ref{thm:knudsen-higher-enveloping},
  Proposition~\ref{prop:en-formality}).
\item The bar complex is a Swiss-cheese coalgebra over
  $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$: the differential is the
  closed $(n=2)$ color, the coproduct is the open $(n=1)$ color
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-swiss-cheese}).  At higher genus the
  structure is curved, with curvature controlled by $\kappa(\cA)$
  (Remark~\ref{rem:sc-higher-genus}).
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
\textbf{What remains as computational refinement.}
The abstract $\mathsf{E}_n$ Koszul duality is fully established
for all~$n$ (formality, Totaro presentation, Knudsen's higher
enveloping algebras; see
Theorem~\ref{thm:en-koszul-duality} and
Remark~\ref{rem:en-scope}).
The following are concrete calculations beyond the proved theorems:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Explicit closed-form formulas for the $\En$ bar differential
  in terms of Totaro generators at each $n \geq 3$
  (the propagator and FM compactification exist and $d^2 = 0$ is
  proved, but the individual formulas have not been tabulated).
\item The $n = 3$ Chern--Simons identification
  (Conjecture~\ref{conj:e3-chern-simons}): connecting the
  $\mathsf{E}_3$ bar complex to perturbative CS invariants at the
  chain level.
\item Higher-genus $\En$ theory: for $n \geq 3$, the analogue of
  genus-$g$ quantum corrections would require a notion of
  ``higher-dimensional moduli'' replacing $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$.
  The correct framework may be cobordism categories or extended
  TQFTs, but this is far from the current state of the art.
\end{enumerate}

The dimensional ladder makes precise the sense in which this
monograph's results---chiral bar-cobar duality, quantum
complementarity, genus expansions---are the $n = 2$ chapter
of a general story that extends through all dimensions.

\begin{remark}[The \texorpdfstring{$\Eone$}{E1}-\texorpdfstring{$\Etwo$}{E2} obstruction]
\label{rem:e1-e2-obstruction}
\index{E1-E2 obstruction@$\Eone$-$\Etwo$ obstruction|textbf}
\index{Drinfeld associator!and E2 coherence@and $\Etwo$ coherence}
The Yangian is $\Eone$-chiral (Theorem~\ref{thm:yangian-e1}):
binary fusion with braiding ($R$-matrix level).  Upgrading to
$\Etwo$ requires coherent many-point transport: an associator
$\Phi \in \exp(\widehat{\mathrm{Lie}}(t_{12}, t_{23}))$
satisfying pentagon and hexagon.  The Drinfeld--Kohno theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:derived-dk-affine}) is the $\Eone$-level
comparison (KZ monodromy $=$ $R$-matrix); full $\Etwo$ coherence
requires associator input, governed by the
Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group]
\label{rem:grothendieck-teichmuller}
\index{Grothendieck--Teichmuller group@Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group|textbf}
\index{E2 operad@$\Etwo$ operad!formality}
$\Etwo$ formality (Tamarkin~\cite{Tamarkin00}) is not canonical:
it depends on a Drinfeld associator~$\Phi$, and the non-uniqueness
is governed by $\operatorname{GRT}$.  For the Yangian, the
$R$-matrix is $\Eone$-level data (braid); the associator~$\Phi$
provides $\Etwo$-level coherence.  DK is naturally an
$\Eone$-factorization theorem; full $\Etwo$ coherence is a
separate, GT-dependent upgrade.
\end{remark}
