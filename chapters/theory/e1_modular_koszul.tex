\chapter{\texorpdfstring{$E_1$}{E1} Modular Koszul Duality}
\label{chap:e1-modular-koszul}

The scalar curvature $\kappa(\cA)$ is a single number: the
$\Sigma_2$-coinvariant of a meromorphic function $r(z)$ that records
the full spectral scattering of the algebra. The cubic shadow
$\mathfrak{C}(\cA)$ is the $\Sigma_3$-coinvariant of the Drinfeld
associator $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$. At every degree the $E_\infty$
invariant is a scalar projection of an ordered object that carries
strictly more information, and the data killed by the coinvariant
projection is the associative factorization pattern governed by
Stasheff associahedra rather than symmetric groups.

The primitive object that carries this ordered data is the ordered bar
complex ${\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ with its
deconcatenation coproduct: the tensor coalgebra on the desuspended
augmentation ideal, before any symmetric-group averaging has been
performed. Its $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant shadow
$\Barch(\cA) = {\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)_{\Sigma_n}$
is what the commutative modular theory sees, and the projection is
irreversible. The averaging map
$\mathrm{av}\colon \fg^{E_1} \to \fg^{\mathrm{mod}}$ provably does
not split at genus~$0$ (the obstruction is controlled by the
Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group $\mathrm{GRT}_1$) or at genus~$1$
(the obstruction is the quasi-modular Eisenstein series $E_2^*$).
What the symmetric theory discards, the ordered theory retains:
the classical $r$-matrix $r(z)$, the Knizhnik--Zamolodchikov
associator, and the full genus tower of ribbon-graph amplitudes.
In Drinfeld's terms, the $E_1$ structure hiding inside a chiral
algebra is the quantum group that governs its representation
theory; the passage from ${\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}$ to $\Barch$ is
the passage from the quantum group to its classical shadow.

Replacing the commutative modular operad $\cM_{\Com}$ by the ribbon
modular operad $\cM_{\Ass}$ and its Feynman transform $F\!\Ass$
recovers this ordered structure. The $E_1$ modular Koszul theory is
not a variant of the commutative theory but its refinement: every
commutative invariant sits at the bottom of an $E_1$ invariant, and
the five main theorems A--H admit ordered counterparts
$\mathrm{A}^{E_1}$--$\mathrm{H}^{E_1}$ whose native objects are the
spectral $r$-matrix, the KZ associator, and the ordered genus tower.
The $E_\infty$ theory is their shadow.

\section{The associative modular operad and \texorpdfstring{$E_1$}{E1}
convolution}
\label{sec:e1-associative-modular-operad}
\index{associative modular operad|textbf}
\index{FAss@$F\Ass$|textbf}
\index{ribbon modular operad|textbf}
\index{E1 algebra@$E_1$-algebra!modular convolution}

The combinatorial unit of $\cM_{\Com}$ is a stable graph with
symmetric vertex labels: ordering data is gone before composition
starts. One modular operad retains the ordering without breaking
the genus decomposition: the ribbon modular operad $\cM_{\Ass}$,
whose vertices carry cyclic orderings of half-edges and whose
composition maps are the ribbon-graph analogues of edge contraction
and self-sewing.

\begin{definition}[Ribbon modular operad {\cite{GeK98,CG17}}]
\label{def:ribbon-modular-operad}
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere
\index{ribbon modular operad!definition}
The \emph{ribbon modular operad}
$\cM_{\Ass}$ has components
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ribbon-modular-operad}
\cM_{\Ass}(g,n)
\;:=\;
C_*\!\bigl(\overline{\cM}_{g,n}^{\,\mathrm{rib}}\bigr),
\qquad 2g - 2 + n > 0,
\end{equation}
where $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}^{\,\mathrm{rib}}$ is the moduli space
of stable genus-$g$ Riemann surfaces with~$n$ parametrised boundary
circles. A ribbon structure on a stable graph $\Gamma$ is a cyclic
ordering of the half-edges at each vertex; equivalently,
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}^{\,\mathrm{rib}}$ classifies surfaces-with-%
pants-decomposition in which each pair-of-pants carries a boundary
parametrisation. The composition maps are:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Self-sewing} $\xi_{ij}\colon
 \cM_\Ass(g,n) \to \cM_\Ass(g+1,n-2)$: identifies the
 $i$-th and $j$-th boundary circles with reversed orientation.
\item \emph{Grafting} $\circ_i\colon
 \cM_\Ass(g_1,n_1) \otimes \cM_\Ass(g_2,n_2)
 \to \cM_\Ass(g_1+g_2, n_1+n_2-2)$: sews the $i$-th
 boundary of the first surface to a boundary of the second.
\end{enumerate}
In genus~$0$: $\cM_{\Ass}(0,n) = C_*(K_n)$, where $K_n$ is the
Stasheff associahedron (dimension~$n-2$), and grafting is face
inclusion. The forgetful map that remembers the surface but forgets the
ordering of the $n$ external boundary components gives a
$\Sigma_n$-quotient
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}^{\,\mathrm{rib}} \to
\overline{\cM}_{g,n}^{\,\mathrm{rib}}/\Sigma_n$;
forgetting the ribbon structure at vertices (cyclic orderings of
half-edges) is a separate operation.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Ordered configurations on curves vs.\ over a point]
\label{rem:e1-ordered-configs-curve-vs-point}
\index{ordered configurations!on curves vs.\ over a point}
The ordered configuration space $C_n^{\mathrm{ord}}(X)$ of~$n$
labelled points on a smooth curve $X$ has nontrivial topology
beyond the permutation group. The Fulton--MacPherson
compactification $\overline{C}_n^{\mathrm{ord}}(X)$ has
exceptional divisors whose intersection pattern encodes the
Arnold relations, and the fundamental groups of
$C_n^{\mathrm{ord}}(X)$ are braid groups on~$X$
\textup{(}pure braid groups of the surface\textup{)}, not
merely the symmetric group $\Sigma_n$. Over a point, the
``ordered configurations'' are simply the set
$\{1, \ldots, n\}$ with its $\Sigma_n$-action: there is
no topology. The $E_1$ bar complex
$\Barord(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ with its
deconcatenation coproduct is built on the ordered
configuration spaces of the curve, and the KZ associator
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ at degree~$3$ is the monodromy of a
flat connection on $C_3^{\mathrm{ord}}(\mathbb{P}^1
\setminus \{0,1,\infty\})$. The passage from the ordered
bar complex to the symmetric bar complex
$\BarSig(\cA) = \Barord(\cA)_{\Sigma_n}$ via
the averaging map $\mathrm{av}$ is a $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant
projection that discards the braid-group data and retains
only the scalar shadows. This projection is genuinely lossy
\textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:e1-nonsplitting-obstruction})}:
$\ker(\mathrm{av})$ is controlled by $\mathrm{GRT}_1$ at
genus~$0$ and by the quasi-modular anomaly $E_2^*$ at
genus~$1$.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Feynman transform of the associative modular operad]
\label{def:feynman-transform-ass}
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere
\index{FAss@$F\Ass$!definition}
The \emph{associative Feynman transform} $F\!\Ass$ is the modular
cooperad with $(g,n)$-component
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:fass-definition}
(F\!\Ass)(g,n)
\;:=\;
\bigoplus_{\Gamma \in \mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{rib}}_{g,n}}
\frac{1}{|\!\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}\,
\det\bigl(E(\Gamma)\bigr)
\;\otimes\;
\bigotimes_{v \in V(\Gamma)}
\cM_{\Ass}\bigl(g(v), n(v)\bigr)^{\vee},
\end{equation}
summed over connected ribbon graphs $\Gamma$ of genus~$g$ with~$n$
legs. The differential
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:fass-differential}
D_{F\!\Ass}
\;=\;
\sum_{e \in E(\Gamma)}
\pm\; \xi_e^*
\end{equation}
is dual to edge contraction in $\cM_\Ass$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:fass-d-squared-zero}
\index{FAss@$F\Ass$!$D^2=0$}
$D_{F\!\Ass}^2 = 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
$D^2$ pairs with codimension-$2$ boundary strata of
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}^{\,\mathrm{rib}}$. Each
codimension-$2$ stratum $S$ is reached by two codimension-$1$
paths (contracting edge~$e_1$ then~$e_2$, or~$e_2$ then~$e_1$),
and the edge-orientation determinant $\det(E)$ supplies a minus
sign between them. The cancellation uses only the associativity
of face composition in the face poset
(Getzler--Kapranov \cite{GK98}), which holds for ribbon graphs
by the same argument as for ordinary stable graphs: ribbon
structure does not alter the codimension-$2$ face pairing.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:fcom-coinvariant-fass}
\index{FAss@$F\Ass$!abelianization}
The composite of the two forgetful maps
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:fass-to-fcom-composite}
(F\!\Ass)(g,n)
\;\xrightarrow{\;\pi_{\mathrm{rib}}\;}
(F\!\Ass)(g,n) / {\sim_{\mathrm{rib}}}
\;\xrightarrow{\;\pi_{\Sigma_n}\;}
(F\!\Com)(g,n)
\end{equation}
is a quasi-isomorphism of modular cooperads, where
$\sim_{\mathrm{rib}}$ identifies ribbon graphs that share an
underlying stable graph and $\pi_{\Sigma_n}$ is the coinvariant
projection on the external labels.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Two distinct operations are composed. First, $\pi_{\mathrm{rib}}$
forgets the cyclic ordering of half-edges at each vertex; the fibre
over a stable graph $\Gamma$ of genus~$g$ with~$n$ legs is the finite
set of cyclic orderings at its vertices, and the edge-orientation
determinant supplies signs so that $\pi_{\mathrm{rib}}$ intertwines
edge contraction in $F\!\Ass$ with edge contraction in the
intermediate ``symmetric $F\!\Ass$''
(Getzler--Kapranov \cite{GK98}, \S3).
Second, $\pi_{\Sigma_n}$ takes external $\Sigma_n$-coinvariants on
the labels of the~$n$ legs; this is well-defined because
$\Sigma_n$ acts on the set of stable graphs by permuting leg labels
and the differential commutes with relabelling. The composite is a
quasi-isomorphism by the standard comparison between ribbon and
commutative modular operads (Getzler--Kapranov \cite{GK98}, Thm.~4.9).
Only the external-label step is used by the averaging map
$\operatorname{av}$ of~\eqref{eq:e1-to-einfty-projection}; the
ribbon-forgetting step is absorbed into the identification of the
target at the Hom-level, where $\operatorname{End}_\cA(n)$ sees only
external legs.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[$E_1$ modular convolution dg~Lie algebra]
\label{def:e1-modular-convolution}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
\index{E1 algebra@$E_1$-algebra!modular convolution|textbf}
\index{modular convolution dg Lie algebra!E1@$E_1$ (ordered)}
Let $\cA$ be a cyclic $E_1$-chiral algebra
(Definition~\ref{def:e1-chiral}).
The degree-$n$ component of the ordered bar complex is the tensor
coalgebra $T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ built on the augmentation ideal
$\bar\cA = \ker(\varepsilon)$, with $n$ linearly ordered external
legs and deconcatenation coproduct (distinct from the coshuffle
coproduct of $\mathrm{Sym}^c$). The modular grading is supplied
separately by $\cM_{\Ass}(g,n)$ above.
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:e1-modular-convolution}
{\gAmod}^{E_1}
\;:=\;
\prod_{\substack{g,n \\ 2g-2+n > 0}}
\operatorname{Hom}\!\bigl(
\cM_{\Ass}(g,n),\,
\operatorname{End}_{\cA}(n)
\bigr).
\end{equation}
The Hom carries no $\Sigma_n$-equivariance; this is the structural
distinction from $\gAmod =
\prod \operatorname{Hom}_{\Sigma_n}(\cM_\Com(g,n),
\operatorname{End}_\cA(n))$
(Definition~\ref{def:modular-convolution-dg-lie}), whose Hom-source
uses the symmetric cooperad $\mathrm{Sym}^c$ obtained from $T^c$ by external
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariants. The dg~Lie structure is inherited from
$F\!\Ass$: $D$ from $D_{F\!\Ass}$, $[-,-]$ from ribbon-graph
composition.
The \emph{averaging map}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:e1-to-einfty-projection}
\operatorname{av}\colon
{\gAmod}^{E_1}
\;\twoheadrightarrow\;
\gAmod,
\qquad
\operatorname{av}(\phi)(g,n)
\;:=\;
\frac{1}{n!}\sum_{\sigma \in \Sigma_n}
\sigma \cdot
\bigl(\phi(g,n) \circ \iota^{\mathrm{rib}}_{g,n}\bigr)
\end{equation}
takes external $\Sigma_n$-coinvariants after pullback along a choice
of section $\iota^{\mathrm{rib}}_{g,n}\colon \cM_\Com(g,n) \to
\cM_\Ass(g,n)$ of the ribbon-forgetting quotient
(Theorem~\ref{thm:fcom-coinvariant-fass}). The result is
$\Sigma_n$-invariant by construction and independent of the section
up to boundary. The map $\operatorname{av}$ is a surjective dg~Lie
morphism: the symmetrization converts $T^c$-convolution to
$\mathrm{Sym}^c$-convolution, which is exactly the passage from ordered
ribbon combinatorics to commutative modular combinatorics.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[$E_1$ Maurer--Cartan element; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:e1-mc-element}
\index{E1 algebra@$E_1$-algebra!Maurer--Cartan element}
The element
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:theta-e1}
\Theta_\cA^{E_1}
\;:=\;
D_\cA^{E_1} - \dzero
\;\in\;
{\gAmod}^{E_1}
\end{equation}
satisfies the Maurer--Cartan equation in the ordered dg~Lie algebra
${\gAmod}^{E_1}$:
$D\Theta_\cA^{E_1}
+ \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta_\cA^{E_1}, \Theta_\cA^{E_1}] = 0$.
Under averaging\textup{:}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:e1-to-einfty-mc}
\operatorname{av}\bigl(\Theta_\cA^{E_1}\bigr)
\;=\;
\Theta_\cA.
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Definition~\ref{def:e1-modular-convolution}, the differential and
bracket on ${\gAmod}^{E_1}$ are the ones induced from $F\!\Ass$.
The ordered genus-completed bar differential is
$D_\cA^{E_1} = \dzero + \Theta_\cA^{E_1}$, so the identity
$(D_\cA^{E_1})^2 = 0$ is equivalent to
$D\Theta_\cA^{E_1}
+ \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta_\cA^{E_1}, \Theta_\cA^{E_1}] = 0$
in ${\gAmod}^{E_1}$. This square-zero identity is the ordered
bar-intrinsic construction: $D_\cA^{E_1}$ is built from the
associative Feynman transform, and $D_{F\!\Ass}^2 = 0$
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:fass-d-squared-zero}}\textup{)}
forces its induced coderivation to square to zero.

For the projection statement,
$\operatorname{av}(D_\cA^{E_1}) = D_\cA$ because the ordered
genus-completed bar differential on
${\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ descends to $\Barch(\cA)$ under
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariants, and $\operatorname{av}(\dzero) = \dzero$.
Therefore
\[
\operatorname{av}\bigl(\Theta_\cA^{E_1}\bigr)
=
\operatorname{av}(D_\cA^{E_1} - \dzero)
=
D_\cA - \dzero
=
\Theta_\cA,
\]
where the final equality is the definition of the symmetric MC
element in Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[$E_1$ non-splitting obstruction; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:e1-nonsplitting-obstruction}
\index{E1 algebra@$E_1$-algebra!non-splitting obstruction|textbf}
\index{Grothendieck--Teichmuller group@Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group!and non-splitting}
\index{Drinfeld associator!non-splitting obstruction}
The short exact sequence of dg~Lie algebras
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:e1-nonsplitting-ses}
0
\;\longrightarrow\;
\ker(\operatorname{av})
\;\longrightarrow\;
{\gAmod}^{E_1}
\;\xrightarrow{\;\operatorname{av}\;}
\gAmod
\;\longrightarrow\;
0
\end{equation}
does \emph{not} split as dg~Lie algebras.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Fixed-degree triviality.}
 At each fixed degree~$n$, the inclusion section
 $s\colon \operatorname{End}^{\Sigma_n}(V^{\otimes n})
 \hookrightarrow \operatorname{End}(V^{\otimes n})$
 is a Lie subalgebra embedding, so the extension $2$-cocycle
 $c(A,B) = [s(A), s(B)] - s([A,B]) = 0$
 vanishes identically. The extension is Lie-trivial at
 each degree separately.
\item \emph{Cross-degree obstruction.}
 The bar differential $D\colon {\gAmod}^{E_1}(n) \to
 {\gAmod}^{E_1}(n{-}1)$ contracts an internal edge, reducing
 degree by~$1$. This contraction is $\Sigma_{n-2}$-equivariant
 but \emph{not} $\Sigma_n$-equivariant: it breaks the symmetry
 from $\Sigma_n$ to $\Sigma_{n-2}$, leaking from
 $\ker(\operatorname{av}_n)$ into
 $\operatorname{im}(\operatorname{av}_{n-1})$.
 No Lie-algebra section can simultaneously respect
 the fixed-degree splitting and intertwine with~$D$.
\item \emph{Associator representative.}
 At degree~$3$, the linearised Drinfeld associator
 $[\Omega_{12}, \Omega_{23}] \in \operatorname{End}(V^{\otimes 3})$
 lies \emph{entirely} in $\ker(\operatorname{av})$: it is
 antisymmetric under $(1 \leftrightarrow 3)$, so its
 $\Sigma_3$-average vanishes. The cubic shadow
 $\mathfrak{C}(\cA) = \operatorname{av}(\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}})$
 receives contributions only from the symmetric products
 $\Omega_{12}\Omega_{23} + \Omega_{23}\Omega_{12}$
 in the full KZ associator.
\item \emph{$\mathrm{GRT}_1$~torsor.}
 The space of dg~Lie sections of~$\operatorname{av}$
 satisfying the MC equation up to gauge is a torsor for
 $\mathrm{GRT}_1$, the pro-unipotent Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller
 group of Drinfeld. This is the algebraic shadow of
 Etingof--Kazhdan non-uniqueness of quantisation: a splitting
 would canonically reconstruct the quantum group from the
 modular shadow~$\kappa$ alone.
\end{enumerate}
For Heisenberg \textup{(}$\dim V = 1$\textup{)}:
$\ker(\operatorname{av}) = 0$ at every degree, the extension
splits trivially, and no $\mathrm{GRT}_1$ ambiguity arises.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Part~(i): if $A, B \in \operatorname{End}^{\Sigma_n}$, then
$[A,B] \in \operatorname{End}^{\Sigma_n}$
(the commutant of $\Sigma_n$ is a Lie subalgebra), so
$c(A,B) = (1 - \operatorname{av})([A,B]) = 0$.

Part~(ii): the partial-trace map
$D_{ij}\colon \operatorname{End}(V^{\otimes n}) \to
\operatorname{End}(V^{\otimes (n-1)})$
satisfies
$D_{ij} \circ \sigma = \sigma|_{\{1,\ldots,n\}\setminus\{j\}}
\circ D_{ij}$
only for permutations $\sigma$ fixing both~$i$ and~$j$,
i.e.\ $\Sigma_{n-2} \subset \Sigma_n$. A
$\Sigma_n$-invariant endomorphism in $\ker(\operatorname{av}_n)$
can map under~$D$ to an endomorphism with nonzero
$\operatorname{av}_{n-1}$ component. The engine
\texttt{e1\_non\-split\-ting\_ob\-struc\-tion\_engine.py}
verifies the cross-degree leakage
$D(\ker(\operatorname{av}_3)) \not\subset
\ker(\operatorname{av}_2)$ for $V = \C^2$ (degree~$3$, 60 tests).

Part~(iii): the $\Sigma_3$-isotypic decomposition of
$[\Omega_{12}, \Omega_{23}]$ consists of sign and standard
components only, with zero trivial component (verified
in $\operatorname{End}((\C^2)^{\otimes 3})$). The
infinitesimal braid relation
$[\Omega_{12}, \Omega_{13} + \Omega_{23}] = 0$ ensures that the
commutator is not an artifact of the representation.

Part~(iv) follows from Drinfeld's theorem on the
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor structure of associators:
the space of Drinfeld associators is a $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor
(Drinfeld~\cite{Drinfeld90}), and each associator determines a
splitting of the MC equation at degree~$3$ up to gauge equivalence.
The Etingof--Kazhdan quantisation theorem~\cite{EK96} guarantees
that the space of quantisations of any Lie bialgebra is a
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor, which through the $E_1$ primacy lens
is exactly the fibre of liftings of $\kappa$ to a full $E_1$
MC element.
\end{proof}


\begin{proposition}[$E_1$ non-splitting at genus~$1$: quasi-modular obstruction; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:e1-nonsplitting-genus1}
\index{E1 algebra@$E_1$-algebra!non-splitting obstruction!genus 1}
\index{quasi-modular forms!non-splitting obstruction}
\index{Eisenstein series!$E_2$ and non-splitting}
The short exact sequence~\eqref{eq:e1-nonsplitting-ses} restricted to
genus~$1$ does not split as dg~Lie algebras. The three genus-$0$
obstructions of
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:e1-nonsplitting-obstruction}}
persist, and a fourth obstruction arises from the quasi-modular
anomaly of the Eisenstein series~$E_2$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Persistence of genus-$0$ obstructions.}
 The cross-degree obstruction (part~\textup{(ii)} of
 Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:e1-nonsplitting-obstruction}}),
 the associator representative (part~\textup{(iii)}), and the
 $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor structure (part~\textup{(iv)}) remain
 active at genus~$1$: the degree-$3$ component of the genus-$1$ bar
 differential inherits the cross-degree leakage from genus~$0$
 via the inclusion $\overline{\cM}_{0,n} \hookrightarrow
 \partial\overline{\cM}_{1,n}$ of boundary strata.
\item \emph{Quasi-modular obstruction.}
 At genus~$1$ and degree~$2$, the $E_1$ propagator on the elliptic
 curve $E_\tau$ is
 $\eta_{12}^{(1)} = (\vartheta_1'/\vartheta_1)(z_1 - z_2 \mid
 \tau)\,(dz_1 - dz_2)$
 (Definition~\textup{\ref{def:elliptic-log-form}}).
 The self-energy regularisation produces the Eisenstein
 series~$E_2(\tau)$
 (Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:eta-properties-genus1}}).
 The averaging map
 $\operatorname{av}\colon {\gAmod}^{E_1}(1,1) \to \gAmod(1,1)$
 performs the non-holomorphic completion
 \[
 E_2(\tau)
 \;\longmapsto\;
 \widehat{E}_2(\tau)
 \;:=\;
 E_2(\tau) - \frac{3}{\pi\,\operatorname{Im}(\tau)},
 \]
 the unique almost-holomorphic modular form of weight~$2$ and
 depth~$1$ on $\operatorname{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$. A section
 $s\colon \gAmod(1,1) \to {\gAmod}^{E_1}(1,1)$ of
 $\operatorname{av}$ would lift $\widehat{E}_2$ back to a
 holomorphic object on the $E_1$ side; the non-holomorphic
 correction $-3/(\pi\,\operatorname{Im}(\tau))$ has no preimage
 in the holomorphic $E_1$ convolution Lie algebra, so no such
 section exists.
\item \emph{Independence from the genus-$0$ obstructions.}
 The quasi-modular obstruction is visible already at degree~$1$
 (genus~$1$, one marked point), where the genus-$0$ obstructions
 are trivial: at degree~$1$ the $\Sigma_1$-averaging is the
 identity, $\ker(\operatorname{av}_1) = 0$, and the cross-degree
 and associator obstructions vanish. The non-holomorphic
 correction $-3/(\pi\,\operatorname{Im}(\tau))$ lives in the
 genus-$1$ fibre direction and is independent of the purely
 combinatorial genus-$0$ obstructions.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Part~(i): the boundary stratum $\overline{\cM}_{0,n+2} \subset
\partial\overline{\cM}_{1,n}$ (obtained by pinching a non-separating
cycle on a genus-$1$ surface to produce a rational curve with two
identified points) embeds the genus-$0$ bar complex into the
genus-$1$ bar complex. The cross-degree leakage
$D(\ker(\operatorname{av}_3)) \not\subset \ker(\operatorname{av}_2)$
of Proposition~\ref{prop:e1-nonsplitting-obstruction}(ii) propagates
through this embedding: a genus-$1$ section would restrict to a
genus-$0$ section on the boundary, contradicting
Proposition~\ref{prop:e1-nonsplitting-obstruction}.

Part~(ii): the genus-$1$ propagator
$K^{(1)}_{\mathrm{reg}}(\tau) = -(\pi^2/3)\,E_2(\tau)$ is
quasi-modular of weight~$2$ and depth~$1$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:eta-properties-genus1}). On the $E_1$ side,
${\gAmod}^{E_1}(1,n)$ is built from holomorphic data on
$E_\tau$: the ordered bar complex uses the meromorphic propagator
$\vartheta_1'/\vartheta_1$ and holomorphic modular forms $E_4, E_6,
\ldots$. The non-holomorphic correction $-3/(\pi\,
\operatorname{Im}(\tau))$ that converts $E_2$ to $\widehat{E}_2$
arises from the $\tau$-periodicity defect of
$\vartheta_1'/\vartheta_1$ (the shift
$(\vartheta_1'/\vartheta_1)(z + \tau) =
(\vartheta_1'/\vartheta_1)(z) - 2\pi i$, cf.\
Theorem~\ref{thm:eta-properties-genus1}): averaging over the
fundamental domain of $\operatorname{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ absorbs this
defect into the $\operatorname{Im}(\tau)$-dependent correction.
Since $1/\operatorname{Im}(\tau)$ is not holomorphic in~$\tau$, it
cannot lie in the image of any holomorphic section.

Part~(iii): at degree~$1$, the averaging map
$\operatorname{av}\colon {\gAmod}^{E_1}(1,1) \to \gAmod(1,1)$
is the identity on symmetric invariants (since $\Sigma_1$ is
trivial), and $\ker(\operatorname{av}_1) = 0$. The three genus-$0$
obstructions (cross-degree, associator, $\mathrm{GRT}_1$) vanish at
degree~$1$. The quasi-modular obstruction is nonetheless present:
it concerns the genus-$1$ fibre (dependence on~$\tau$), not the
degree (dependence on~$n$). The two obstructing mechanisms are
therefore linearly independent in the relevant $\operatorname{Ext}$
group.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Genus-$1$ non-splitting, the holomorphic anomaly,
and the information lost by the modular shadow]
\label{rem:genus1-nonsplitting-anomaly}
\index{holomorphic anomaly!and non-splitting}
\index{BCOV holomorphic anomaly equation!genus-1 non-splitting}
\index{Eisenstein series!non-holomorphic completion}
\index{modular shadow!information loss}
The quasi-modular obstruction of
Proposition~\ref{prop:e1-nonsplitting-genus1} merits a closer
examination from three angles: the physical origin of the
obstruction, the mechanism that produces it, and the information
it destroys.

\emph{(a) Physical origin: the BCOV holomorphic anomaly.}
The genus-$1$ free energy of any chiral algebra~$\cA$ with
$\kappa = \kappa(\cA) \neq 0$ is
$F_1 = -\log\,\eta(\tau)^{2\kappa}$
\textup{(}Theorem~D at genus~$1$\textup{)}.
The logarithmic derivative
$\eta'(\tau)/\eta(\tau) = (\pi i/12)\,E_2(\tau)$
\textup{(}standard $q$-expansion identity\textup{)} gives
\[
 \partial_\tau F_1
 \;=\;
 -2\kappa\,\frac{\eta'(\tau)}{\eta(\tau)}
 \;=\;
 -\frac{\pi i\,\kappa}{6}\,E_2(\tau).
\]
The genus-$1$ amplitude is therefore controlled by the
quasi-modular Eisenstein series~$E_2$, the unique holomorphic
modular form of weight~$2$ on
$\operatorname{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$; except that it is not
modular. This failure of modularity is the genus-$1$ instance
of the Bershadsky--Cecotti--Ooguri--Vafa holomorphic anomaly
equation~\cite{BCOV93}: the genus-$1$ HAE reads
$\partial_{\bar{\tau}} F_1 \neq 0$, with the anomaly
proportional to $\kappa/(\operatorname{Im}\tau)^2$.
The BCOV equation at higher genus
\textup{(}Proposition~\ref{prop:bcov-mc-projection}\textup{)}
is the MC projection of $D\Theta + \frac{1}{2}[\Theta,\Theta] = 0$;
at genus~$1$ the bracket term vanishes
\textup{(}there is no partition $1 = h + (1-h)$ with
$h \geq 1$\textup{)}, and the sewing term reduces to the
single statement that $E_2$ acquires a non-holomorphic
correction under $\operatorname{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$.

\emph{(b) Mechanism: $E_2 \longmapsto \widehat{E}_2$.}
The ordered $E_1$ bar complex on the elliptic curve~$E_\tau$
is built from the meromorphic propagator
$\vartheta_1'/\vartheta_1$ and the holomorphic modular forms
$E_4, E_6, \ldots$; the series $E_2(\tau)$ enters through the
self-energy regularisation of the genus-$1$ propagator.
The averaging map
$\operatorname{av}\colon {\gAmod}^{E_1}(1,n) \to \gAmod(1,n)$
replaces $E_2$ by
\[
 \widehat{E}_2(\tau)
 \;=\;
 E_2(\tau) - \frac{3}{\pi\,\operatorname{Im}(\tau)},
\]
the unique almost-holomorphic modular form of weight~$2$ and
depth~$1$. The correction $-3/(\pi\operatorname{Im}\tau)$
arises from the quasi-periodicity defect
$(\vartheta_1'/\vartheta_1)(z + \tau)
= (\vartheta_1'/\vartheta_1)(z) - 2\pi i$: integrating over
the fundamental domain converts this shift into the
$\operatorname{Im}(\tau)$-dependent term. Since
$1/\operatorname{Im}(\tau)$ is not holomorphic in~$\tau$,
$\widehat{E}_2$ has no preimage in the holomorphic $E_1$
convolution algebra, and no section of $\operatorname{av}$
exists.

\emph{(c) Information loss and degree-$1$ independence.}
The passage $E_2 \mapsto \widehat{E}_2$ destroys information.
The holomorphic $E_2$ and the almost-holomorphic
$\widehat{E}_2$ differ by $3/(\pi\operatorname{Im}\tau)$,
a function that depends on~$\bar{\tau}$ and therefore lies
outside the holomorphic category in which the $E_1$ bar complex
is defined. In physical terms, the modular shadow retains the
almost-holomorphic completion $\widehat{E}_2$ but loses the
holomorphic anomaly that distinguishes $E_2$ from a true
modular form. The lost datum is precisely the obstruction to
splitting: the non-holomorphic correction generated by
$\operatorname{av}$ has no preimage on the $E_1$ side, so
the modular shadow cannot detect the mechanism that prevents
the section from existing.

This information loss is visible already at degree~$1$.
At $(g,n) = (1,1)$, the symmetric group $\Sigma_1$ is trivial:
$\operatorname{av}_1$ is the identity,
$\ker(\operatorname{av}_1) = 0$, and the three genus-$0$
obstructions \textup{(}cross-degree leakage, associator,
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor\textup{)} vanish identically.
The quasi-modular obstruction persists, because it lives in
the genus-$1$ fibre direction \textup{(}dependence on~$\tau$\textup{)},
not in the degree direction
\textup{(}dependence on~$n$\textup{)}.
Degree~$1$ is therefore the cleanest possible witness to the
independence of the genus-$1$ obstruction from the genus-$0$
obstructions: every genus-$0$ mechanism is invisible, yet
the non-splitting survives. The physical content is that the
conformal anomaly \textup{(}controlled by~$\kappa$\textup{)} is
a genuinely genus-$1$ phenomenon, not a shadow of any
combinatorial structure at genus~$0$.

The strength of the obstruction is proportional to~$\kappa$:
at $\kappa = 0$ \textup{(}the uncurved case\textup{)}, $F_1 = 0$
and both the genus-$0$ and genus-$1$ obstructions survive through
the $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor, but the quasi-modular channel closes.
For Heisenberg $\cH_\kappa$ at $\kappa \neq 0$
\textup{(}$\kappa(\cH_\kappa) = \kappa$, landscape
census~\S\textup{\ref{sec:landscape-census})}, all four
obstructions are simultaneously active.
\end{remark}

\section{Ordered chiral homology}
\label{sec:ordered-chiral-homology}
\index{chiral homology!ordered|textbf}
\index{ordered Ran space|textbf}
\index{D-module@$\cD$-module!ordered factorisation}

The ordered bar complex $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$
is an algebraic object: a graded coalgebra with deconcatenation
coproduct. The geometric object it represents is a
$\cD$-module on the \emph{ordered Ran space}.

\begin{definition}[Ordered Ran space]
\label{def:ordered-ran-space}
\index{Ran space!ordered}
Let $X$ be a smooth algebraic curve over~$\bC$. The
\emph{ordered Ran space} is the disjoint union
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ordered-ran}
\Ran^{\mathrm{ord}}(X)
\;:=\;
\coprod_{n \ge 0} U_n(X),
\qquad
U_n(X) = \{(z_1, \ldots, z_n) \in X^n : z_i \neq z_j
\text{ for } i \neq j\},
\end{equation}
where $U_n(X) = X^n \setminus \bigcup_{i < j} \Delta_{ij}$
is the ordered configuration space of $n$ distinct points on~$X$.
The Beilinson--Drinfeld Ran space is the
$\Sigma_\bullet$-coinvariant quotient
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ran-as-quotient}
\Ran(X) \;=\; \Ran^{\mathrm{ord}}(X) \,/\, \Sigma_\bullet,
\end{equation}
where $\Sigma_n$ acts on $U_n(X)$ by permuting coordinates.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Ordered factorisation $\cD$-module]
\label{def:ordered-fact-dmod}
\index{D-module@$\cD$-module!ordered factorisation!definition}
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on~$X$ and let
$j \colon U_n \hookrightarrow X^n$ denote the inclusion of the
ordered configuration space. The \emph{ordered factorisation
$\cD$-module} of~$\cA$ on $\Ran^{\mathrm{ord}}(X)$ is the
collection
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ordered-fact-dmod}
\cF^{\mathrm{ord}}_n(\cA)
\;:=\;
j_* j^* (\cA^{\boxtimes n})
\;\in\;
\cD\text{-}\mathrm{mod}(X^n),
\qquad n \ge 0,
\end{equation}
where the OPE of~$\cA$, meromorphically extended across the
diagonals, provides the $\cD$-module structure. No
$\Sigma_n$-equivariance is imposed: the factorisation product,
the KZ connection, and the chiral differential are encoded in
the single object
$\cF^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ on $\Ran^{\mathrm{ord}}(X)$.
The Beilinson--Drinfeld factorisation $\cD$-module on $\Ran(X)$
is the $\Sigma_n$-equivariant descent:
$\cF^\Sigma(\cA) = (\cF^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA))^{\Sigma_\bullet}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Ordered chiral homology]
\label{def:ordered-chiral-homology}
\index{chiral homology!ordered!definition}
The \emph{ordered chiral homology} of $\cA$ over $X$ is the
derived pushforward
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ordered-chiral-homology}
\int_X^{\mathrm{ord}} \cA
\;:=\;
R\Gamma_{\mathrm{dR}}\bigl(
\Ran^{\mathrm{ord}}(X),\;
\cF^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)\bigr)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{n \ge 0}
R\Gamma_{\mathrm{dR}}\bigl(X^n,\,
\cF^{\mathrm{ord}}_n(\cA)\bigr).
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

The coinvariant quotient~\eqref{eq:ran-as-quotient}
induces the \emph{averaging map}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ordered-descent}
\av \colon
\int_X^{\mathrm{ord}} \cA
\;\longrightarrow\;
\int_X \cA,
\end{equation}
from ordered chiral homology to the Beilinson--Drinfeld chiral
homology. This is the $\cD$-module incarnation of the algebraic
averaging map $\av \colon \barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)
\to \barB^\Sigma(\cA)$ that sends the tensor coalgebra to its
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariant quotient.

\begin{proposition}[Symmetric descent]
\label{prop:symmetric-descent}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
\index{averaging map!symmetric descent}
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on a smooth proper curve $X$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item If $\cA$ is $\Einf$-chiral \textup{(}vertex algebra with
  symmetric OPE\textup{)}, the averaging
  map~\eqref{eq:ordered-descent} is a quasi-isomorphism:
  ordered and Beilinson--Drinfeld chiral homologies agree.
\item If $\cA$ is genuinely $\Eone$-chiral \textup{(}the vertex
  $R$-matrix $S(z) \neq \tau$\textup{)}, the descent is lossy:
  $\ker(\av)$ carries $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-dependent data from the Drinfeld
  associator that the $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant quotient discards.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
(i) When $\cA$ is $\Einf$-chiral, $\cF^{\mathrm{ord}}_n(\cA)$
is $\Sigma_n$-equivariant and the descent spectral sequence
$E_2^{p,q} = H^p(\Sigma_n, H^q_{\mathrm{dR}}(X^n,
\cF^{\mathrm{ord}}_n))$ collapses at $E_2$ in characteristic~$0$:
group cohomology of a symmetric group over~$\bQ$ is concentrated
in degree zero.

(ii) When $S(z) \neq \tau$, the monodromy of the KZ connection
on $U_n(X)$ is governed by the Drinfeld associator
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}} \in \exp(\hat{\mathfrak{t}}_3)$,
and the $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant projection annihilates the
$\Sigma_n$-non-invariant Eulerian components. The resulting
kernel is a $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor at each degree $n \ge 3$
(at degree $2$ the symmetric group is $\Sigma_2$ and the kernel
consists of the $R$-matrix data $r(z)$ modulo its scalar
$\Sigma_2$-coinvariant $\av(r(z))$).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Ordered chiral homology and the bar complex]
\label{rem:ordered-ch-hom-bar}
\index{ordered chiral homology!bar complex relationship}
The algebraic bar complex $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ computes the
ordered chiral homology on the formal disk~$D$: the degree-$n$
component $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}_n(\cA)$ is the de~Rham cohomology
of $\cF^{\mathrm{ord}}_n(\cA)$ restricted to the formal
neighbourhood of the $n$-fold diagonal in $D^n$. On a global
curve $X$ of genus $g \ge 1$, the ordered chiral homology
acquires additional structure from the Hodge bundle: the
fiberwise differential satisfies
$d^2_{\mathrm{fib}} = r(z) \cdot \omega_g$
(matrix-valued in $\operatorname{End}(V^{\otimes 2})
\otimes H^{2}(\overline{\cM}_g)$ at degree~$2$), which upon
$\Sigma_2$-averaging reduces to the scalar curvature
$d^2_{\mathrm{fib}} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ of
the symmetric theory.
\end{remark}

\begin{example}[Ordered chiral Hochschild of the Heisenberg]
\label{ex:ord-ch-hoch-heisenberg}
\index{Heisenberg algebra!ordered chiral Hochschild}
The Heisenberg $\cH_k$ ($\Einf$-chiral, class $\mathbf{G}$,
$r(z) = k/z$, $\kappa = k$) has ordered chiral Hochschild
cohomology on the formal disk with Poincar\'e polynomial
\begin{equation}\label{eq:heis-poincare-poly}
P_{\cH_k}(t)
\;=\; 1 + t + t^2.
\end{equation}
Degree by degree: $\mathrm{HH}^0 = \bC$ (the centre consists
of scalars, since $J_{(1)}J = k \neq 0$ kills all
non-scalar central elements);
$\mathrm{HH}^1 = \bC$ (one outer derivation, the
translation $D(J) = \mathbf{1}$; inner derivations vanish
because $J_{(0)}J = 0$);
$\mathrm{HH}^2 = \bC$ (the level deformation cocycle
$\phi_k(J \otimes J) = k \cdot \mathbf{1}$, the unique
deformation $J(z)J(w) \sim (k + \epsilon)/(z-w)^2$).
The KZ connection at degree~$2$ is
$\nabla = d + k\,d\log w$ with monodromy $e^{2\pi i k}$;
for generic $k \notin \bZ$, the twisted de~Rham cohomology
of~$\bC^\times$ vanishes and $\mathrm{HH}^2$ is
one-dimensional. Total dimension: $1 + 1 + 1 = 3$.
Since $\cH_k$ is $\Einf$-chiral, the averaging map is a
quasi-isomorphism and the symmetric chiral Hochschild gives
the same answer
(Theorem~H: $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^*(\cH_k)$ concentrated in
degrees $\{0,1,2\}$ with total dimension~$3$).
\end{example}

\begin{example}[Ordered chiral homology of $Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$
on an elliptic curve]
\label{ex:ord-ch-ell-yangian}
\index{Yangian!ordered chiral homology on $E_\tau$}
\index{elliptic curve!ordered chiral homology}

% AP27: bar propagator weight 1 (d log E is weight 1).
% AP118: period matrix is 1x1 (tau) at g=1; scalar.
The passage from genus~$0$ to genus~$1$ replaces $\bC^\times$

introduces two decisive changes: the bar propagator
acquires the elliptic function
$\wp_1(z,\tau) = \theta_1'(z|\tau)/\theta_1(z|\tau)$ in

KZB connection carrying a $d\tau$ term from the Hodge class
$\omega_1 = c_1(\lambda)$ on $\overline{\cM}_{1,n}$.

\noindent
\textbf{Bar propagator.} On $E_\tau$:

\end{equation}

\noindent

\end{equation}
At genus~$0$, $H^*(D^\times)$ contributes only in
degree~$0$, giving dimension~$3$; the passage to

\smallskip
\noindent
\textbf{Degree $2$: KZB flat sections and the Belavin
$r$-matrix.}
% PE-1: r-matrix. Family: affine KM sl_2 (elliptic).
%   r(z,tau) = k * r^{Belavin}(z,tau). Level: k. Trace-form.
%   k=0 check: r|_{k=0} = 0. Match: Y.
%   verdict: ACCEPT
The degree-$2$ collision residue is the Belavin elliptic

\begin{equation}\label{eq:v1-belavin-r}
  r^{\mathrm{ell}}(z, \tau)
  = k \Bigl(
  \frac{\wp_1(z, \tau)}{2}\,H \otimes H
  + \phi_+(z, \tau)\,E \otimes F
  + \phi_-(z, \tau)\,F \otimes E
  \Bigr),
\end{equation}
where $\phi_\pm$ are Belavin's $\phi$-functions.
At $k = 0$, $r^{\mathrm{ell}} = 0$ (AP126).
The KZB monodromy at degree~$2$ has three generators:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Puncture}: $M_\gamma
  = \exp(2\pi i\,\hbar\,\Omega)$, the $R$-matrix.
\item \emph{$A$-cycle}: $\Phi(w+1) = R\cdot\Phi(w)$.
\item \emph{$B$-cycle}: $\Phi(w+\tau)
  = q^{\Omega}\cdot\Phi(w)$ where
  $q = e^{2\pi i \hbar}$.
\end{enumerate}
For generic $\hbar$, the monodromy has no invariant
vectors ($H^0 = 0$), and the Euler characteristic of the
rank-$4$ KZB local system on $E_\tau \setminus \{0\}$ gives
$\dim H^1 = 4$. The shadow surviving $\mathrm{av}_2$ is
% AP1: kappa(V_k(sl_2)) = 3(k+2)/4. From C3.
$\kappa = 3(k+2)/4$.

\smallskip
\noindent
\textbf{Genus-$1$ enhancement of the averaging kernel.}
The kernel $\ker(\mathrm{av}_2)$ at genus~$1$ is strictly
larger than at genus~$0$: the $B$-cycle monodromy
$q^{\Omega}$ introduces new antisymmetric classes in the
$\bigwedge^2 V$ channel (eigenvalue
$e^{3\pi i/(k+2)}$ of $M_B$) that have no genus-$0$

$\Eone$ enhancement: the ordered theory at genus~$1$ sees
the quantum group parameter $q = e^{2\pi i \hbar}$ through
the $B$-cycle, while the symmetric shadow retains only
the scalar traces (WZW conformal blocks).

\textbf{Degeneration.} In the limit $\tau \to i\infty$,

\begin{equation}\label{eq:v1-ell-degen}

  \int_{E_\tau}^{\mathrm{ord}}
  Y_\hbar(\mathfrak{sl}_2)

\end{equation}

\end{example}

\subsection{Formality: when ordered equals symmetric}
\label{subsec:formality-bridge}
\index{formality!ordered vs symmetric|textbf}
\index{Kontsevich--Tamarkin!operad formality}

Four formality conditions control the relationship between
ordered chiral homology and its symmetric shadow.
Condition (a) is unconditional; conditions (b)--(d) are
logically ordered implications that, on the standard
landscape, are all equivalent to class~$\mathbf{G}$.

\begin{remark}[Topological Hochschild homology and the stratified comparison]
\label{rem:thh-vs-ord-ch}
\index{topological Hochschild homology!definition}
\index{factorisation homology!over $S^1$}
The disk $D$ is a stratified space with closed stratum
$\partial D = S^1$ and open stratum $D^\times$. Two chain
complexes compute invariants of a chiral algebra~$\cA$
from these strata.

The \emph{topological Hochschild homology} is the
factorisation homology of $\cA$, viewed as an
$\Eone$-algebra, over $S^1 = \partial D$:
\[
  \HH^{\mathrm{top}}_*(\cA)
  := \int_{S^1} \cA.
\]
This is computed by the cyclic bar complex
$B^{\mathrm{cyc}}_n(\cA) = \cA^{\otimes n}/\text{cyclic}$
with the Hochschild differential; it uses $m_2$ (and higher
$\Ainf$-operations if present) but no meromorphic data,
no Arnold relations, and no spectral parameter.

The \emph{ordered chiral homology of $D^\times$} is the
factorisation homology of the ordered factorisation
$\cD$-module $\cF^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ over
$\operatorname{Ran}^{\mathrm{ord}}(D^\times)$
(Definition~\ref{def:ordered-chiral-homology}); it depends
on the full OPE, the Arnold forms, and the FM
compactification.

The comparison map
$\int_{D^\times}^{\mathrm{ord}} \cA \to
\HH^{\mathrm{top}}_*(\cA)$ is induced by the boundary
retraction $D^\times \to S^1$, which forgets the complex
structure. Operad formality
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-formality-bridge} below) makes this a
quasi-isomorphism for $\Einf$-chiral algebras;
Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-formality-failure} shows it fails for
genuinely $\Eone$-chiral algebras, where the ordered complex
carries holomorphic data (the $r$-matrix, the spectral
parameter, the Arnold relations) invisible to the topological
theory on~$S^1$.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Formality hierarchy]
\label{def:e1-formality-hierarchy}
\index{formality!hierarchy}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item \emph{Operad formality.} The chain operad
  $C_*(\overline{\operatorname{FM}}_n(\bC))$ is formal:
  quasi-isomorphic to its cohomology (the Arnold algebra)
  via a quasi-isomorphism depending on a Drinfeld associator
  $\Phi \in \exp(\hat{\mathfrak{t}}_3)$
  (Kontsevich, Tamarkin). Unconditional: depends only on the
  operad, not on any algebra.
\item \emph{Algebra formality.} The transferred
  $A_\infty$-structure on $H^*(\cA)$ has
  $m_k^{\mathrm{tr}} = 0$ for $k \ge 3$.
  Holds for class $\mathbf{G}$ (Heisenberg); fails for
  classes $\mathbf{L}$, $\mathbf{C}$, $\mathbf{M}$.
\item \emph{Convolution algebra formality.}
  The $L_\infty$ brackets $\ell_k = 0$ for
  $k \ge 3$ in~$\gAmod$. Equivalent to class~$\mathbf{G}$
  (Proposition~\ref{prop:sc-formal-iff-class-g}).
\item \emph{SC-formality.} The Swiss-cheese type
  operations $m_k^{\mathrm{SC}} = 0$ for $k \ge 3$.
  Equivalent to class~$\mathbf{G}$.
\end{enumerate}
Level (a) guarantees that ordered and symmetric give the
same answer \emph{as chain complexes} for $\Einf$-chiral
algebras; levels (b)--(d) control whether the answer is
\emph{simple} (no higher operations).
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Formality bridge]
\label{thm:e1-formality-bridge}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
\index{formality!bridge theorem}
Let $\cA$ be an $\Einf$-chiral algebra. The
Kontsevich--Tamarkin operad formality quasi-isomorphism
induces a quasi-isomorphism of ordered chiral chains on the
punctured disk to topological Hochschild chains:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:e1-formality-bridge}
\cC_n^{\mathrm{ord}}(D^\times, \cA)
\;\xrightarrow[\Phi]{\;\sim\;}\;
B^{\mathrm{cyc}}_n(\cA).
\end{equation}
The transferred $A_\infty$-structure on~$H^*(\cA)$ may
carry non-trivial higher operations $m_k^{\mathrm{tr}}$
for $k \ge 3$: what operad formality guarantees is that
the ordered and symmetric complexes compute the same
cohomology, not that the cohomology is formal.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The Kontsevich--Tamarkin quasi-isomorphism
$\varphi_\Phi \colon
C_*(\overline{\operatorname{FM}}_n(\bC))
\xrightarrow{\sim}
H^*(\overline{\operatorname{FM}}_n(\bC))$
is $\Sigma_n$-equivariant and respects the operad
composition. For $\Einf$-chiral $\cA$, the ordered
factorisation $\cD$-module
$\cF^{\mathrm{ord}}_n(\cA)$ is $\Sigma_n$-equivariant
(the OPE is symmetric by hypothesis), so the
tensor product $\varphi_\Phi \otimes \id$ transfers the
ordered chiral complex (de~Rham model on FM
compactifications) to the cyclic bar complex (Arnold model).
The $\Sigma_n$-equivariance of both factors ensures the
quasi-isomorphism descends to the coinvariant quotient.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Formality failure for genuinely
$\Eone$-chiral algebras]
\label{thm:e1-formality-failure}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
\index{formality!failure for $\Eone$-chiral}
Let $\cA$ be genuinely $\Eone$-chiral
\textup{(}tier~(c): $R$-matrix not derived from any
$\Einf$-chiral OPE\textup{)}. The chain-level
formality bridge~\eqref{eq:e1-formality-bridge} does not
exist: the $\cD$-module $\cF_n^{\mathrm{ord}}$ is not
$\Sigma_n$-equivariant \textup{(}$S(z) \neq \tau$ breaks
the symmetry\textup{)}, and the ordered chiral complex
is strictly richer than the cyclic bar complex. By
Arnold's theorem $\dim H^*(\Conf_n(\bC)) = n!$, the
ordered complex at degree~$n$ has
\begin{equation}\label{eq:e1-dim-ratio}
\dim \cC_n^{\mathrm{ord}} \;=\;
(\dim \cA)^n \cdot n!,
\qquad
\dim B_n^{\mathrm{cyc}} \;=\; (\dim \cA)^n.
\end{equation}
The ratio $n!$ measures the holomorphic data in the
ordered theory that the symmetric quotient discards.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Arnold's Poincar\'e polynomial for
$H^*(\Conf_n(\bC))$ is
$P_n(t) = \prod_{j=0}^{n-1}(1 + jt)$; setting $t=1$
gives $P_n(1) = n!$. The ordered chiral complex is
$\Omega^*_{\log}(\overline{\operatorname{FM}}_n^{\mathrm{ord}})
\otimes (s^{-1}\bar\cA)^{\otimes n}$; at the
cohomological level the $\Omega^*_{\log}$ factor
contributes the Arnold algebra of total dimension~$n!$.
The $\Sigma_n$-non-equivariance of the bar differential
(from $S(z) \neq \tau$) obstructs the descent to the
symmetric quotient that the formality bridge requires.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Comparison with Ben-Zvi--Brochier--Jordan and Latyntsev]
\label{rem:bbj-latyntsev-comparison}
\index{Ben-Zvi--Brochier--Jordan!comparison with ordered chiral homology}
\index{Latyntsev!factorisation coalgebra chiral homology}
Two existing programmes integrate braided structures over
surfaces. Ben-Zvi, Brochier, and Jordan integrate braided
monoidal \emph{categories} over surfaces: for a ribbon
category~$\cC$ and a surface~$\Sigma$,
$\int_\Sigma \cC$ is a categorical invariant. Ordered chiral
homology integrates chiral \emph{algebras} over curves at the
chain level. For affine Kac--Moody at level~$k$, the expected
comparison is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bbj-comparison}
\mathrm{HH}_*\!\Bigl(\int_\Sigma \operatorname{Rep}_q(\fg)\Bigr)
\;\simeq\;
\int_X^{\mathrm{ord}} V_k(\fg),
\end{equation}
where $q = e^{2\pi i/(k+h^\vee)}$: the categorical Hochschild homology
of the categorical invariant recovers the ordered chiral
homology of the algebraic one. For class~$\mathbf{M}$
(Virasoro at generic~$c$), the BBJ framework requires a
finite ribbon category, which is unavailable; ordered chiral
homology applies without restriction.
Latyntsev constructs chiral homology for factorisation
\emph{coalgebras} on curves, providing an algebraic
counterpart to the analytic construction here; the
chain-level comparison for irregular singularities
(class~$\mathbf{M}$) remains open.
\end{remark}

\section{The \texorpdfstring{$E_1$}{E1} shadow obstruction tower}
\label{sec:e1-shadow-tower}
\index{shadow obstruction tower!E1@$E_1$ (ordered)|textbf}
\index{R-matrix!shadow tower}
\index{Drinfeld associator!as degree-3 E1 shadow@as degree-$3$ $E_1$ shadow}

The $E_\infty$ shadow obstruction tower extracts scalar invariants
$\kappa, \mathfrak{C}, \mathfrak{Q}, \ldots$ from the MC element
$\Theta_\cA$. Before taking $\Sigma_r$-coinvariants, the genus-$0$
degree-$r$ component of $\Theta_\cA^{E_1}$ is a meromorphic function
of $r-1$ collision coordinates valued in
$\operatorname{End}(V^{\otimes r})$: the $E_1$ shadow at degree~$r$.

\begin{definition}[$E_1$ shadow at degree~$r$]
\label{def:e1-shadow-degree-r}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
\index{E1 shadow@$E_1$ shadow!degree $r$|textbf}
The \emph{$E_1$ shadow at degree~$r$} of a cyclic $E_1$-chiral
algebra $\cA$ is the component
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:e1-shadow-degree-r}
r_r(z_1, \ldots, z_{r-1})
\;:=\;
(\Theta_\cA^{E_1})_{0,r}
\;\in\;
\operatorname{End}(V^{\otimes r})
\otimes
\Omega^{r-2}\bigl(
\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_r^{\mathrm{ord}}(\mathbb{C})
\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $(\Theta_\cA^{E_1})_{0,r}$ is the genus-$0$, degree-$r$
component of the $E_1$ MC element
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-mc-element}).
The variables $z_1, \ldots, z_{r-1}$ are the consecutive
difference coordinates $z_i = w_{i+1} - w_i$ on the ordered
configuration space; the form degree $r-2 = \dim K_r$ reflects
the quotient by translation and dilation.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[$E_1$ MC equation at finite degree; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:e1-mc-finite-degree}
\index{E1 shadow@$E_1$ shadow!MC equation}
The Maurer--Cartan equation for $\Theta_\cA^{E_1}$, projected to
genus~$0$ and degree~$r$, gives the following identities:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Degree~$2$ \textup{(}$r = 2$\textup{)}:} The classical
 Yang--Baxter equation\textup{:}
 \begin{equation}
 \label{eq:e1-mc-degree2}
 [r_{12}(z_1), r_{13}(z_1 + z_2)]
 + [r_{12}(z_1), r_{23}(z_2)]
 + [r_{13}(z_1 + z_2), r_{23}(z_2)]
 \;=\; 0.
 \end{equation}
 This is the content of $d^2 = 0$ on
 $\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_3^{\mathrm{ord}}(\mathbb{C})$
 \textup{(}the Yang--Baxter equation from $d^2 = 0$).
\item \emph{Degree~$3$ \textup{(}$r = 3$\textup{)}:} The
 pentagon\slash quasi-associativity relation for the
 \emph{Drinfeld associator shadow}
 $r_3(z_1, z_2) \in \operatorname{End}(V^{\otimes 3})$\textup{:}
 \begin{equation}
 \label{eq:e1-mc-degree3}
 d_{\mathrm{bar}} r_3
 + [r_{12}, r_3]_{1,23}
 + [r_{23}, r_3]_{12,3}
 \;=\; 0,
 \end{equation}
 where the notation $[-, -]_{I,J}$ denotes the Lie bracket in
 which tensor factors in~$I$ are on the left and those in~$J$
 on the right. The boundary faces of the associahedron $K_4$
 give the five pentagon terms.
\item \emph{Degree~$4$ \textup{(}$r = 4$\textup{)}:} The quartic
 $R$-matrix identity\textup{:} the obstruction to extending the
 triple $(r, r_3)$ to degree~$4$ is
 $o_5 \in H^2(F^5 {\gAmod}^{E_1} / F^6)$, and
 \begin{equation}
 \label{eq:e1-mc-degree4}
 d_{\mathrm{bar}} r_4
 + \bigl\{r_3 \circ_2 r_3 \text{ boundary terms}\bigr\}
 + [r, r_4]
 \;=\; 0.
 \end{equation}
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The MC equation
$D\Theta^{E_1} + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta^{E_1}, \Theta^{E_1}] = 0$
on ${\gAmod}^{E_1}$
(Definition~\ref{def:e1-modular-convolution})
projected to genus~$0$ and degree~$r$ uses the face structure of the
Stasheff associahedra $K_{r+1}$:
$\partial K_3$ has $2$ faces (CYBE);
$\partial K_4$ has $5$ faces (pentagon);
$\partial K_5$ has $9$ faces (the quartic identity).

Part~(i) is
the Yang--Baxter equation from $d^2 = 0$:
$K_3$ is the interval $[0,1]$, whose two endpoints give
the two compositions $r \circ_1 r$ and $r \circ_2 r$; their
expansion on three tensor factors yields the three-term CYBE.
Part~(ii): the genus-$0$, degree-$3$ MC component on
$\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_4^{\mathrm{ord}}(\mathbb{C})$ identifies
with the boundary analysis of $K_4$ (the pentagon), whose five
edges give the five terms of the quasi-associativity relation.
Part~(iii) follows by the same mechanism at degree~$4$,
using the nine codimension-$1$ faces of $K_5$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Coinvariant projection: $E_1$ shadows to $E_\infty$ shadows; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:e1-coinvariant-shadow}
\index{E1 shadow@$E_1$ shadow!coinvariant projection}
The $\Sigma_r$-coinvariant of the $E_1$ shadow at degree~$r$ is the
$E_\infty$ shadow at degree~$r$\textup{:}
\begin{align}
\label{eq:e1-coinvariant-degree2}
\operatorname{av}_{r=2}\bigl(r(z)\bigr)
&\;=\; \kappa(\cA), \\
\label{eq:e1-coinvariant-degree3}
\operatorname{av}_{r=3}\bigl(r_3(z_1, z_2)\bigr)
&\;=\; \mathfrak{C}(\cA), \\
\label{eq:e1-coinvariant-degree4}
\operatorname{av}_{r=4}\bigl(r_4(z_1, z_2, z_3)\bigr)
&\;=\; \mathfrak{Q}(\cA).
\end{align}
More precisely: the averaging map
$\operatorname{av}\colon {\gAmod}^{E_1} \twoheadrightarrow \gAmod$
of Definition~\textup{\ref{def:e1-modular-convolution}} sends each
$E_1$ shadow to the corresponding $E_\infty$ shadow at the same

\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The averaging map
$\operatorname{av}(\phi)(g,n) =
(1/n!)\sum_{\sigma \in \Sigma_n} \sigma \cdot \phi(g,n)$
sends $\operatorname{Hom}(\cM_\Ass(g,n), \operatorname{End}_\cA(n))$
to $\operatorname{Hom}_{\Sigma_n}(\cM_\Com(g,n),

correspond: the degree-$r$ component of
$\operatorname{av}(\Theta^{E_1})$ is
$\operatorname{av}(r_r)$, and this equals $\Theta^{\leq r}_{0,r}$
by construction. The identification of $\Theta^{\leq 2}_{0,2}$

$\mathfrak{C}(\cA)$, and $\Theta^{\leq 4}_{0,4}$ with

Definition~\ref{def:shadow-postnikov-tower}

\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[$F\!\Com = F\!\Ass$ at the scalar level]

\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Eulerian weight of $\kappa$: parity-dependent]
\label{rem:kappa-eulerian-weight}
\index{Eulerian idempotent!kappa weight}

At degree~$2$, the first Eulerian idempotent $e_1 = \tfrac{1}{2}(\mathrm{id} - \sigma_{12})$

generator $s^{-1}\phi$ has degree $h - 1$ where $h$ is the conformal weight.
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Even desuspended degree} ($h$ odd: Heisenberg $h{=}1$, $\beta\gamma$ $h{=}1$):
 $\Sigma_2$ acts trivially on $[s^{-1}\phi | s^{-1}\phi]$.

 \emph{entirely in Eulerian weight~$2$} (symmetric), invisible to Harrison.
\item \emph{Odd desuspended degree} ($h$ even: Virasoro $h{=}2$, $W_3$ generator $T$ at $h{=}2$):
 $\Sigma_2$ acts by the sign; $e_1$ preserves it.
 Here $\kappa$ lives \emph{entirely in Eulerian weight~$1$} (Harrison).
\end{itemize}
For multi-generator algebras the decomposition is mixed:
$\kappa(\cW_3) = \tfrac{5c}{6} = c/2 + c/3$ splits as

$\kappa_{WW} = c/3$ at weight~$2$ (even desuspended $W$),

\begin{remark}[The MC equation is not Eulerian-weight-graded]
\label{rem:mc-not-eulerian-graded}
\index{Eulerian idempotent!MC equation non-grading}
\index{Maurer--Cartan equation!Eulerian weight}
Neither the Lie bracket on $\gAmod$ nor the differential $D$
preserves Eulerian weight. The Lie bracket is determined by
the weight-$1$ (Harrison/Lie$^c$) restriction via the PBW

for even-degree generators) live in higher weights.

$(\kappa, \mathfrak{C}, \mathfrak{Q}, \ldots)$ cannot be recovered
from the Harrison part alone. Instead, the \emph{derivative tower}
$\{\partial^k\phi\}_{k \geq 0}$, with desuspended degree $h{+}k{-}1$
alternating in parity, supplies all Eulerian weight classes at
every degree, explaining how the Virasoro algebra (single generator,
odd desuspended degree) achieves infinite shadow depth.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Three $r$-matrices: Laplace kernel, collision residue,
 pre-dualisation form]
\label{rem:three-r-matrices}
\index{r-matrix@$r$-matrix!disambiguation}
\index{r-matrix@$r$-matrix!Laplace kernel}
\index{r-matrix@$r$-matrix!collision residue}
\index{r-matrix@$r$-matrix!pre-dualisation form}
Three distinct objects share the notation~$r(z)$ in this text.
Each is a meromorphic function of the separation~$z = z_1 - z_2$;
they differ in pole orders, target spaces, and normalisation.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]

\item \emph{Laplace kernel}
 $r^{\mathrm{L}}_\cA(z)
 = \int_0^\infty e^{-\lambda z}\,
 \{{\cdot}\,_\lambda\,{\cdot}\}\,d\lambda
 \in \cA\,\widehat\otimes\,\cA[\![z^{-1}]\!]$:
 the full OPE generating function, encoding every singular term of
 the operator product expansion as a Laurent series in~$z^{-1}$.
 Pole orders match the OPE directly.
 \begin{center}
 \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.1}
 \begin{tabular}{ll}
 Heisenberg $\cH_k$\textup: &
 $r^{\mathrm{L}}(z) = k/z^2$ \\
 Affine $\hat\fg_k$\textup: &
 $r^{\mathrm{L}}(z)
 = k\,\kappa^{ab}/z^2
 + {f^{ab}}_c\,J^c/z$ \\
 Virasoro\textup: &
 $r^{\mathrm{L}}(z)
 = (c/2)/z^4 + 2T/z^2 + \partial T/z$
 \end{tabular}
 \end{center}

\item \emph{Collision residue}
 $r^{\mathrm{coll}}_\cA(z)
 = \operatorname{Res}^{\mathrm{coll}}_{0,2}(\Theta_\cA)
 \in \cA^!\,\widehat\otimes\,\cA^!$:
 the bar-theoretic $r$-matrix, extracted by residue along the
 collision divisor using the $d\log(z_1-z_2)$ measure.
 Because $d\log$ absorbs one power of $(z_1-z_2)$, every pole order
 drops by one relative to the Laplace kernel.
 For bosonic algebras, only odd-order poles survive
 (even-order OPE poles descend to odd-order residues).
 The target space is $\cA^!\otimes\cA^!$: passage to
 the Koszul dual is part of the construction.
 \begin{center}
 \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.1}
 \begin{tabular}{ll}
 Heisenberg $\cH_k$\textup: &
 $r^{\mathrm{coll}}(z) = k/z$
 \quad\textup{(simple pole; OPE had double)} \\
 Affine $\hat\fg_k$\textup: &
 $r^{\mathrm{coll}}(z)
 = k\,\Omega_\fg/z$
 \quad\textup{(level-prefixed; vanishes at $k=0$)} \\
 Virasoro\textup: &
 $r^{\mathrm{coll}}(z) = (c/2)/z^3 + 2T/z$
 \quad\textup{(cubic + simple; no even poles)}
 \end{tabular}
 \end{center}
 This is the master object from which the others descend:
 the genus-$0$, degree-$2$ shadow
 $r^{\mathrm{coll}}(z) = \mathrm{Sh}_{0,2}(\Theta_\cA)(z)$.

\item \emph{Pre-dualisation form}
 $r^{\mathrm{pre}}_\cA(z) \in \cA\otimes\cA$:
 the collision residue computed in $\cA\otimes\cA$, before
 applying the Koszul pairing to pass one or both factors to~$\cA^!$.
 Pole orders are identical to~\textup{(b)};
 the target space and normalisation differ
 \textup{(}the Koszul pairing absorbs a scalar factor\textup{)}.
 \begin{center}
 \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.1}
 \begin{tabular}{ll}
 Heisenberg $\cH_k$\textup: &
 $r^{\mathrm{pre}}(z) = k/z$ \\
 Affine $\hat\fg_k$\textup: &
 $r^{\mathrm{pre}}(z) = k\,\Omega_\fg/z$ \\
 Virasoro\textup: &
 $r^{\mathrm{pre}}(z) = (c/2)/z^3 + 2T/z$
 \end{tabular}
 \end{center}
\end{enumerate}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Relations.}
The three are connected by two operations.
\begin{itemize}
\item $d\log$ extraction:
 $r^{\mathrm{L}}(z) \;\xmapsto{\;\operatorname{Res}_{d\log}\;}
 r^{\mathrm{pre}}(z)$.
 Every pole order drops by one.
 Mnemonic: $z^{-n}\,dz = z^{-(n-1)}\,d\log z$,
 so the residue along $d\log(z_1-z_2)$ of a pole of order~$n$
 in the OPE is a pole of order~$n-1$ in the collision residue.
\item Koszul dualisation:
 $r^{\mathrm{pre}}(z) \;\xmapsto{\;\mathrm{id}\otimes\kappa\;}
 r^{\mathrm{coll}}(z)$.
 Same pole structure, different target space
 ($\cA\otimes\cA \to \cA^!\otimes\cA^!$).
\end{itemize}
The archetype table
\textup{(}Example~\textup{\ref{ex:e1-shadow-archetypes})} lists the
collision residue $r^{\mathrm{coll}}(z)$. The \emph{$E_1$ scalar shadow}
$r^{\mathrm{sc}}(z) \in \C(z)$ is its vacuum projection; the
$\Sigma_2$-coinvariant of $r^{\mathrm{sc}}(z)$ is the scalar curvature
$\kappa(\cA)$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Normalisation note for affine Kac--Moody.}
The archetype table
\textup{(}Example~\textup{\ref{ex:e1-shadow-archetypes})}
lists $r(z) = k\Omega/z$: the canonical affine collision residue,
with target fixed by context. Some formal constructions
\textup{(}e.g.\ the KZ connection,
eq.~\textup{\eqref{eq:kz-connection-bar})} use the
Sugawara-normalised coefficient $(k{+}h^\vee)^{-1}\Omega\, z^{-1}$ in the
connection kernel. That coefficient is not the canonical affine
$r$-matrix: the latter carries the explicit level prefix $k$ and
vanishes at $k=0$. For $k \neq 0$, the two expressions are related
by the separate Koszul-pairing and Sugawara normalizations, which
contribute factors of $k$ and $(k{+}h^\vee)$ respectively.
(The Koszul-dual level shift $k \mapsto -k - 2h^\vee$ is a
\emph{separate} operation on the underlying family of chiral
algebras; it is not accomplished by this rescaling alone.
See in the concordance.)

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Convention.}
When the text writes $r(z)$ unqualified, the context determines
which variant is meant. In the archetype table
\textup{(}Example~\textup{\ref{ex:e1-shadow-archetypes})} and
the holographic datum
\textup{(}\S\textup{\ref{sec:thqg-gravitational-yangian})},
$r(z) = r^{\mathrm{coll}}(z)$ is the collision residue.
In the $\Sigma_2$-coinvariant projection to $\kappa(\cA)$,
$r(z) = r^{\mathrm{sc}}(z)$ is the scalar shadow.
In explicit OPE computations,
$r(z) = r^{\mathrm{L}}(z)$ is the Laplace kernel.
\end{remark}

\section{The \texorpdfstring{$E_1$}{E1} five main theorems at
all genera}
\label{sec:e1-five-theorems}
\index{five main theorems!E1@$E_1$ (ordered)!all genera|textbf}

The genus-$0$ theory promoted each $E_\infty$ shadow invariant to
an $E_1$ shadow at the same degree: the scalar $\kappa$ to the
spectral $r$-matrix, the cubic shadow $\mathfrak{C}$ to the
Drinfeld associator. The genus tower so far still uses the
commutative modular operad $\cM_\Com$; the promotion is incomplete
until the same replacement happens at every genus. The genus-$g$
ordered bar complex on $\cM_\Ass$ supplies the missing structure:
the $r$-matrix acts on internal edges, the associator controls
vertex reordering, and the genus-$g$ partition function acquires a
ribbon surface decomposition invisible to $\Sigma_n$-coinvariants.
The five theorems $\mathrm{A}^{E_1}$--$\mathrm{H}^{E_1}$ below
extend the bar--cobar package at all cyclic genera $(g,n)$ with
$2g-2+n>0$.

\begin{theorem}[Theorem~$\mathrm{A}^{E_1}$ at all genera: ordered bar--cobar adjunction; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:e1-theorem-A-modular}
\label{thm:e1-theorem-A}
\index{five main theorems!E1@$E_1$ (ordered)!Theorem A (modular)}
Let $\cA$ be a cyclic $E_1$-chiral algebra. The genus-$g$ ordered
bar complex ${\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)(g,n)$ is an
$F\!\Ass$-coalgebra
\textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:feynman-transform-ass}})
with differential satisfying $D^2 = 0$.
The bar and cobar functors at genus~$g$ form an adjunction
\[
\Cobar^{(g)}
\;\dashv\;
{\Barch}^{(g)}
\;\colon\;
\mathsf{Alg}_{F\!\Ass}
\;\rightleftarrows\;
\mathsf{Coalg}_{F\!\Ass}.
\]
Koszul exchange carries the formal ordered degree-$2$ shadow series
$R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}(z;\hbar)$
\textup{(}Construction~\textup{\ref{constr:modular-r-matrix-genus1}}):
its genus-$g$ coefficient records the ordered degree-$2$ correction on
this bar-level surface. Interpreting this series as a genuine
higher-genus line-side modular $R$-matrix requires extra Yangian input
not proved here.
The coinvariant projection
$F\!\Ass/\Sigma_n \xrightarrow{\sim} F\!\Com$
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:fcom-coinvariant-fass}}\textup{)}
recovers Theorem~A from Theorem~$\mathrm{A}^{E_1}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The $F\!\Ass$-coalgebra structure on the ordered bar complex
follows from the universal property of the Feynman transform:
$D_{F\!\Ass}^2 = 0$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:fass-d-squared-zero}) gives the differential
on the graph complex, and the bar differential of $\cA$ at each
vertex is compatible with the operad composition maps of
$\cM_\Ass$. The adjunction follows by the same argument as in
the $E_\infty$ case
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-adjunction}),
replacing the commutative modular operad by $\cM_\Ass$:
the cobar construction $\Cobar^{(g)}$ is defined as the tensor
algebra over $F\!\Ass$ with differential dual to the coalgebra
structure.

The formal ordered degree-$2$ shadow package: at each genus~$g$, the
opposite-duality identification
${\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA^{\op}) \cong
{\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)^{\cop}$
is encoded at the degree-$2$ shadow level by
$R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}(z;\hbar)$ restricted to genus
$\leq g$. This is the $E_1$ analogue of Verdier intertwining on the
ordered bar surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Theorem~$\mathrm{B}^{E_1}$ at all genera: ordered bar--cobar inversion; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:e1-theorem-B-modular}
\label{thm:e1-theorem-B}
\index{five main theorems!E1@$E_1$ (ordered)!Theorem B (modular)}
On the $E_1$ Koszul locus, the counit
\[
\Cobar^{(g)}\bigl({\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)(g,\bullet)\bigr)
\;\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}\;
\cA
\]
is a quasi-isomorphism at every genus~$g$, and the
$E_1$ Koszul dual $\cA^{!,E_1}$ is an $E_1$-chiral algebra
at all genera.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
PBW-completeness transfers from genus~$0$ to genus~$g$ via the
MC perturbation lemma: the genus-$g$ differential is a perturbation
of the genus-$0$ differential by curvature $\kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$,
and this perturbation preserves the PBW filtration (the curvature is
in the center of the algebra). The inversion theorem for perturbed
complexes (the filtered quasi-isomorphism criterion of
Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-inversion-qi}) then gives the
result at each genus.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Theorem~$\mathrm{C}^{E_1}$ at all genera: ordered complementarity; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:e1-theorem-C-modular}
\label{thm:e1-theorem-C}
\index{five main theorems!E1@$E_1$ (ordered)!Theorem C (modular)}
At genus~$g$ and each degree~$n$ with $2g - 2 + n > 0$, the ordered
complementarity takes the form
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:e1-complementarity-modular}
Q_{g,n}^{E_1}(\cA) + Q_{g,n}^{E_1}(\cA^{!,E_1})
\;=\;
H^*\!\bigl(\overline{\cM}_{g,n}^{\,\mathrm{rib}},\,
Z^{E_1}(\cA)\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $Q_{g,n}^{E_1}(\cA) :=
C_\Delta^{(g,n)} \square_{C^{e,(g,n)}}^{\mathbf{R}} C_\Delta^{(g,n)}$
is the genus-$g$, degree-$n$ ordered partition function and
$Z^{E_1}(\cA)$ is the $E_1$ center \textup{(}the space of
$R$-matrix-equivariant elements\textup{)}.

The scalar complementarity identity
$\kappa(\cA) + \kappa(\cA^!) = 0$ for KM/free fields
\textup{(}$\kappa(\cA) + \kappa(\cA^!) = \varrho \cdot K$ for $W$-algebras,
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:modular-characteristic})} is
unchanged; the ordered refinement enriches it with
$R$-matrix-equivariance on the ambient cohomology.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The genus-$g$ ordered complementarity extends from genus~$0$
(the genus-$0$ ordered complementarity theorem) by the same modular extension
principle used for the $E_\infty$ complementarity
(Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}): the
$E_1$ MC element $\Theta^{E_1}_\cA$ gives a genus-$g$
deformation of the genus-$0$ diagonal bicomodule, and the
ordered Hochschild--coHochschild identification carries through
at each genus. The $E_1$ center $Z^{E_1}(\cA)$ is the subspace
of elements satisfying $R(z) \cdot a = a$ (braiding-invariant),
replacing the naive center of the $E_\infty$ theory.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Theorem~$\mathrm{D}^{E_1}$ at all genera: formal ordered degree-$2$ shadow series; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:e1-theorem-D-modular}
\label{thm:e1-theorem-D}
\index{five main theorems!E1@$E_1$ (ordered)!Theorem D (modular)}
\index{degree-2 shadow!as $E_1$ characteristic package}
The formal ordered degree-$2$ shadow series
$R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}(z;\hbar) = \sum_{g \geq 0}
\hbar^{2g}\,r_g(z)$
obtained from the genus-refined degree-$2$ projection of
$\Theta^{E_1}_\cA$ is the $E_1$ degree-$2$ characteristic package of
$\cA$. It satisfies the degree-$2$ projection of the ordered
Maurer--Cartan equation
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:modular-ybe}
D\,R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}} +
\tfrac{1}{2}[R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}, R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}]
\;=\; 0
\end{equation}
in ${\gAmod}^{E_1}$, and its properties mirror those of the scalar
$\kappa(\cA)$\textup{:}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Universality}\textup{:}
 $R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}$ depends only on the $E_1$-chiral algebra
 $\cA$, not on auxiliary choices.
\item \emph{Additivity}\textup{:} for $\cA = \cA_1 \otimes
 \cA_2$ with vanishing mixed OPE,
 $R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}_{\cA_1 \otimes \cA_2}
 = R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}_{\cA_1} \otimes 1 + 1 \otimes
 R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}_{\cA_2}$.
\item \emph{Anti-symmetry}\textup{:}
 $R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}_{\cA^{\op}}(z;\hbar)
 = R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}_{\cA}(z;\hbar)^{-1}$ on the ordered
 bar surface.
\item \emph{Coinvariant recovery}\textup{:}
 $\operatorname{av}\bigl(R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}(z;\hbar)\bigr)
 = \kappa(\cA) + O(\hbar^2)$.
\end{enumerate}
\noindent
This theorem packages the ordered degree-$2$ shadow data only. A
genuine higher-genus line-side modular $R$-matrix interpretation is
additional Yangian input and is not proved here.

\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The degree-$2$ ordered Maurer--Cartan relation~\eqref{eq:modular-ybe} is the
degree-$2$ MC equation in ${\gAmod}^{E_1}$: it follows from
$D_{F\!\Ass}^2 = 0$ by restriction to the two-point
component.

(i)~Universality: $R^{E_1,\mathrm{bin}}$ is determined by the
$F\!\Ass$-coalgebra structure on ${\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$,
which depends only on~$\cA$.

(ii)~Additivity: for $\cA = \cA_1 \otimes \cA_2$ with vanishing
mixed OPE, the ordered bar complex decomposes as a product
${\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA_1 \otimes \cA_2) \cong
{\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA_1) \otimes
{\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA_2)$
(ordered shuffle theorem), and the monodromy of a product
connection is additive.

(iii)~Anti-symmetry: opposite-duality gives
${\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA^{\op}) \cong
{\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)^{\cop}$,
and the monodromy of the co-opposite is the inverse.

(iv)~Coinvariant: by
Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-coinvariant-shadow} at degree~$2$.
\end{proof}

The $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant of the ordered Hochschild complex

\begin{proof}
The genus-$g$ extension of the Hochschild--coHochschild dictionary
(the genus-$0$ ordered Hochschild theorem) follows by the same modular
perturbation argument as
Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-theorem-B-modular}: the genus-$g$
perturbation preserves the bimodule-bicomodule equivalence.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[$E_1$ primitive kernel]

\index{primitive kernel!E1@$E_1$ (ordered)}
\index{E1 algebra@$E_1$-algebra!primitive kernel}
The cofree-coderivation principle for $F\!\Ass$ yields an
\emph{$E_1$ primitive kernel}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:e1-primitive-kernel}
K_\cA^{E_1}
\;:=\;
\log_\star\bigl(\Theta_\cA^{E_1}\bigr)
\;\in\;
\prod_{\substack{g,n \\ 2g-2+n > 0}}
\operatorname{End}(V^{\otimes n})
\otimes
\Omega^{n-2}\!\bigl(
\overline{\mathrm{FM}}_n^{\,\mathrm{ord}}(\mathbb{C})
\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $\log_\star$ is the operadic logarithm on the
$F\!\Ass$-coalgebra ${\Barch}^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$, decomposing the
full MC element into primitive (one-vertex) contributions.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Coinvariant recovery.}
The averaging map of
Definition~\textup{\ref{def:e1-modular-convolution}} sends the $E_1$
primitive kernel to the $E_\infty$ primitive kernel:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:e1-coinvariant-kernel}
\operatorname{av}\bigl(K_\cA^{E_1}\bigr)
\;=\;
K_\cA.
\end{equation}
This is the primitive-level refinement of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:e1-coinvariant-shadow}}: the coinvariant
projection is compatible with the operadic logarithm because
$\operatorname{av}$ is a dg~Lie morphism.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Low-degree identifications.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $(K_\cA^{E_1})_{0,2} = r(z)$, the classical $r$-matrix
 \textup{(}genus~$0$, degree~$2$\textup{)}. The coinvariant
 $\operatorname{av}(r(z)) = \kappa(\cA)$ recovers the scalar
 curvature.
\item $(K_\cA^{E_1})_{0,3} = \Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}(\cA)$, the
 KZ\slash Drinfeld associator \textup{(}genus~$0$, degree~$3$,
 for affine algebras;
 Construction~\textup{\ref{constr:kz-associator-e1-shadow}}).
 Coinvariant: $\operatorname{av}(\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}) =
 \mathfrak{C}(\cA)$, the cubic shadow.
\item $(K_\cA^{E_1})_{1,1}$ is the genus-$1$ primitive, whose
 coinvariant gives the genus-$1$ curvature $\kappa(\cA) \cdot
 \lambda_1$
 \textup{(all-weight, with cross-channel correction
 $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$; at $g=1$ this correction vanishes)}.
\end{enumerate}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{$E_1$ primitive master equation.}
The MC equation $D\Theta^{E_1} + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta^{E_1},
\Theta^{E_1}] = 0$ expressed in primitive generators takes the form
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:e1-primitive-master-equation}
dK_\cA^{E_1}
\;+\;
K_\cA^{E_1} \star_{E_1} K_\cA^{E_1}
\;=\; 0,
\end{equation}
where $\star_{E_1}$ is the convolution product on the $F\!\Ass$
modular cooperad, whose degree-$r$ component is governed by the face
structure of the Stasheff associahedron $K_{r+1}$. In particular,
the degree-$2$ component of~\eqref{eq:e1-primitive-master-equation} is
the classical Yang--Baxter equation, and the degree-$3$ component is
the pentagon equation. Averaging
recovers the $E_\infty$ primitive master equation
$dK_\cA + K_\cA \star K_\cA
+ \hbar\,\Delta_{\mathrm{ns}}(K_\cA) = 0$.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Lie/associative dichotomy in the averaging kernel]
\label{rem:lie-associative-dichotomy}
\index{averaging map!Lie/associative dichotomy}
\index{kernel of averaging map}
The averaging map
$\operatorname{av}\colon {\gAmod}^{E_1} \twoheadrightarrow \gAmod$
exhibits a Lie/associative dichotomy: at degree~$3$, the linearized
Drinfeld associator $[t_{12}, t_{23}]$ (a Lie commutator) lies
entirely in $\ker(\operatorname{av})$: its $\Sigma_3$-average
vanishes exactly because the commutator is antisymmetric under the
transposition $(1 \leftrightarrow 3)$. The modular cubic shadow
$\mathfrak{C}(\cA) = \operatorname{av}(\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}})$
receives contributions only from the symmetric/associative products
$t_{12}\, t_{23} + t_{23}\, t_{12}$ in the full KZ associator
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$. The modular shadow sees only the
associative content of quantization, not the Lie content.
Physically: the closed-sector (modular) invariants encode the
Hilbert-space tensor product structure (associative), while the
open-sector ($E_1$) invariants encode the braiding/$R$-matrix
structure (Lie bracket content).
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[$\Sigma_n$-irreducible decomposition of the ordered bar complex; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:sn-irrep-decomposition-bar}
\index{bar complex!Sn-decomposition@$\Sigma_n$-decomposition|textbf}
\index{Schur functor!bar complex decomposition}
\index{Frobenius characteristic!of ordered bar}
\index{averaging map!kernel structure}
Let $V = \bigoplus_{i} \C \cdot \phi_i$ be the generating space of a
chiral algebra~$\cA$, with $\dim V = d$ and desuspended degree
$|s^{-1}\phi_i| = h_i - 1$ where $h_i$ is the conformal weight of~$\phi_i$.
The $\Sigma_n$-action on the ordered bar component
$B^{\mathrm{ord},n}(\cA) \supset V^{\otimes n}$ has:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Schur--Weyl decomposition.}
 As a $\GL(V) \times \Sigma_n$-representation\textup{:}
 \begin{equation}
 \label{eq:schur-weyl-bar}
 V^{\otimes n}
 \;=\;
 \bigoplus_{\substack{\lambda \vdash n \\ \ell(\lambda) \leq d}}
 \mathbb{S}^\lambda(V)
 \;\otimes\;
 V_\lambda,
 \end{equation}
 where $\mathbb{S}^\lambda(V)$ is the Schur functor and
 $V_\lambda$ is the irreducible $\Sigma_n$-representation
 indexed by~$\lambda$. The multiplicity of~$V_\lambda$ equals
 $\dim \mathbb{S}^\lambda(V)$, computed by the Weyl dimension
 formula.
\item \emph{Frobenius characteristic.}
 The character of the $\Sigma_n$-representation on
 $V^{\otimes n}$ is
 \begin{equation}
 \label{eq:frobenius-char-bar}
 \operatorname{ch}(V^{\otimes n})
 \;=\;
 \sum_{\lambda \vdash n}
 \dim\bigl(\mathbb{S}^\lambda(V)\bigr)\,
 s_\lambda,
 \end{equation}
 where $s_\lambda$ is the Schur function. This encodes which
 $\Sigma_n$-irreps contribute at each bar degree.
\item \emph{Kernel of~$\operatorname{av}$.}
 The averaging map $\operatorname{av}$ projects onto a single
 isotypic component\textup{:} for generators of even desuspended
 degree~\textup{(}$h$ odd: Heisenberg, affine KM\textup{)},
 $\operatorname{av}$ retains the \emph{trivial}
 representation~$V_{(n)}$\textup{;} for generators of odd
 desuspended degree~\textup{(}$h$ even: Virasoro\textup{)},
 the Koszul sign makes $\operatorname{av}$ retain the
 \emph{sign} representation~$V_{(1^n)}$. All other
 isotypic components lie in $\ker(\operatorname{av})$
 and are annihilated.
\item \emph{Information ratio.}
 The fraction of ordered bar data surviving
 $\operatorname{av}$ is
 \begin{equation}
 \label{eq:information-ratio-bar}
 \frac{\dim \operatorname{im}(\operatorname{av}|_{V^{\otimes n}})}
 {\dim V^{\otimes n}}
 \;=\;
 \frac{\binom{d + n - 1}{n}}{d^n}
 \quad
 \textup{(even desuspended degree)},
 \qquad
 \frac{\binom{d}{n}}{d^n}
 \quad
 \textup{(odd desuspended degree)}.
 \end{equation}
 For $\mathrm{sl}_2$ \textup{(}$d=3$\textup{)} at
 $n=4$\textup{:} $\dim V^{\otimes 4} = 81$,
 $\dim \operatorname{Sym}^4(V) = 15$, ratio $\approx 18.5\%$.
 For Virasoro single generator \textup{(}$d=1$\textup{)}, the
 ratio is $1/1 = 100\%$ at every~$n$
 \textup{(}consistent with the trivial $\Sigma_n$-action
 on a one-dimensional space\textup{)}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Part~(i) is the Schur--Weyl theorem for $\GL_d \times \Sigma_n$
acting on $V^{\otimes n}$ (Fulton--Harris~\cite{FH91},
Appendix~A). Part~(ii) is its translation into the
symmetric-function basis. Part~(iii): the Reynolds operator
$(1/n!)\sum_{\sigma} \sigma$ projects onto the
$\Sigma_n$-invariant subspace, which for degree-$0$ elements is
$\mathrm{Sym}^n(V)$ (the trivial isotypic) and for degree-$1$
elements acquires Koszul signs that convert the trivial projection
into the sign projection.
Part~(iv): $\dim \mathrm{Sym}^n(V) = \binom{d+n-1}{n}$ and
$\dim \Lambda^n(V) = \binom{d}{n}$ are the standard
multiset and subset counts.
Computational verification:
\texttt{sn\_bar\_irrep\_decomp\_engine.py}
(Schur--Weyl decomposition, character tables for
$\Sigma_1$--$\Sigma_5$, hook-length formula, 120+ tests).
\end{proof}

\section{Cyclic trace, ribbon structure, and the
\texorpdfstring{$1/N$}{1/N} expansion}%
\label{sec:e1-ribbon-thooft}%
\index{ribbon structure!from cyclic trace|textbf}%
\index{t Hooft expansion@'t~Hooft expansion!from ribbon structure|textbf}

The $E_1$ modular theory organises amplitudes by genus through the
ribbon modular operad, but a bare stable graph carries no canonical
$N$-weight: the Euler characteristic $\chi(\Sigma_\Gamma)$ requires
a ribbon surface, hence a cyclic ordering at each vertex. The
missing datum is a cyclic trace on the open-color algebra.

\begin{lemma}[Bare graphs do not determine a 't~Hooft expansion; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]%
\label{lem:bare-graph-no-thooft}%
\index{t Hooft expansion@'t~Hooft expansion!bare graph obstruction}%
A bare modular stable-graph expansion does not
canonically define a 't~Hooft $N$-expansion.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The $N$-weight in a 't~Hooft expansion is
$N^{V - E + F} = N^{\chi(\Sigma_\Gamma)}$,
determined by the number of faces~$F$ of the
thickened surface~$\Sigma_\Gamma$.
A bare stable graph does not specify cyclic orderings
of half-edges at vertices; hence it does not determine
a ribbon surface, hence does not determine~$F$,
hence carries no canonical $N$-power.
The same underlying graph admits inequivalent ribbon
structures with different genera.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclicity is the ribbon-enabling datum;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]%
\label{thm:cyclicity-ribbon}%
\index{cyclic A-infinity@cyclic $A_\infty$!ribbon structure|textbf}%
\index{ribbon structure!from cyclicity|textbf}%
Let $(O, m_\bullet, \tau)$ be a graded vector space
with multilinear operations
$m_n\colon O^{\otimes n} \to O$ and a
degree-zero nondegenerate bilinear pairing
$\tau\colon O \otimes O \to k$.
Define the cyclic tensors
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cyclic-tensors}
\tilde{m}_n(x_0,\ldots,x_n)
\;:=\;
\tau\!\bigl(m_n(x_1,\ldots,x_n),\,x_0\bigr).
\end{equation}
If $\tilde{m}_n$ satisfies cyclic invariance
$\tilde{m}_n(x_0,\ldots,x_n) = (-1)^\epsilon\,
\tilde{m}_n(x_1,\ldots,x_n,x_0)$
\textup{(}with Koszul sign~$\epsilon$\textup{)}, then
each operation~$m_n$ determines a cyclic
$(n{+}1)$-tensor, and every Feynman graph built from
these tensors canonically thickens to a ribbon graph.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The pairing identifies
$\Hom(O^{\otimes n}, O) \cong
\Hom(O^{\otimes(n+1)}, k)$.
Under this identification, $m_n$ becomes the
$(n{+}1)$-linear form~$\tilde{m}_n$.
Cyclic invariance means $\tilde{m}_n$ descends to a
function on cyclic words of length $n{+}1$:
there is no distinguished output among the $n{+}1$ legs.
Each vertex therefore carries a cyclic order on its
incident half-edges, which is exactly the data needed
for canonical ribbon thickening: at each vertex, the
cyclic order determines the local surface, and gluing
via internal edges produces a global ribbon surface
$\Sigma_\Gamma$ with a well-defined face decomposition.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Operads are too small for traces;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]%
\label{cor:operads-too-small}%
\index{wheeled operad!necessity of}%
Any open/closed theory closed under Swiss-cheese
composition \emph{and} trace-contraction cannot be
encoded by a rooted operad. The minimal closure is
a wheeled two-colored operadic object\textup;
for multi-interface gluing, a properad.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Operadic composition is indexed by rooted trees
\textup{(}acyclic, one output per vertex\textup{)}.
Trace-contraction $\sum_i m_2(x, e_i) \otimes e^i$
creates a one-vertex graph with a loop, not an operadic
graph. The closure under both composition and
contraction generates graphs with arbitrary numbers of
loops: wheeled graphs. Properadic structure allows
multiple outputs per vertex, accommodating
multi-boundary and multi-interface gluing.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Exact $N^{\chi}$ weighting from traced open color; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]%
\label{thm:exact-n-chi-weighting}%
\index{t Hooft expansion@'t~Hooft expansion!exact weighting|textbf}%
Let $(O, m_\bullet, \tau)$ be a cyclic
$A_\infty$-algebra with matrix realization
$O_N := \operatorname{Mat}_N \otimes O_0$ and trace
$\tau_N := \operatorname{Tr}_N \otimes \tau_0$.
For a connected ribbon graph~$\Gamma$ with
$V$~vertices, $E$~edges, and $F$~faces, the bare
amplitude scales as~$N^F$ \textup(one factor per
closed index loop\textup).
Under 't~Hooft rescaling
$g^2 \mapsto \lambda/N$, the rescaled amplitude
factors as
\begin{equation}\label{eq:n-chi-amplitude}
\operatorname{Amp}_\Gamma^{\lambda}(O_N)
\;=\;
N^{V - E + F}\cdot
\operatorname{Amp}_\Gamma^{\mathrm{red}}(O_0,\lambda)
\;=\;
N^{\chi(\Sigma_\Gamma)}\cdot
\operatorname{Amp}_\Gamma^{\mathrm{red}}(O_0,\lambda).
\end{equation}
The total genus expansion is then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:thooft-literal}
\sum_{\Gamma\;\text{ribbon}}
\frac{N^{\chi(\Sigma_\Gamma)}}{|\operatorname{Aut}_{\mathrm{rib}}(\Gamma)|}
\,W_\Gamma\,\Phi_\Gamma^T
\;=\;
\sum_{g=0}^\infty N^{2-2g}\,F_g(\lambda),
\end{equation}
\textup{(all-weight, with cross-channel correction
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$).}
where $\lambda = g_{\mathrm{YM}}^2 N$. This is a
literal 't~Hooft expansion\textup: the $N$-power
is determined by the Euler characteristic of the
thickened surface.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Cyclic structure
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:cyclicity-ribbon}}\textup{)}
gives canonical ribbon thickening, so faces are
well-defined. We count $N$-powers in two steps.

\emph{Step~1: bare double-line counting.} With the
normalization $\tau_N = \operatorname{Tr}_N$ (not
$(1/N)\operatorname{Tr}_N$), the propagator
$\tau_N^{-1} = \sum_{i,j} E_{ij}\otimes E_{ji}$
carries no explicit $N$-factor: it merely routes
double-line indices through each internal edge.
Likewise, vertex traces route indices but produce no
bare $N$-powers. The only source of $N$ is the
summation over each closed index loop: every face of
the ribbon graph~$\Gamma$ corresponds to one such
loop, contributing a factor of~$N$. Hence the bare
amplitude scales as $N^F$.

\emph{Step~2: 't~Hooft rescaling.} Writing the action
with overall normalization $g^{-2}\tau_N(\cdots)$,
each propagator acquires $g^2$ and each vertex
$g^{-2}$, giving total coupling $g^{2(E-V)}$.
Setting $\lambda = g^2 N$ yields
$g^{2(E-V)} = (\lambda/N)^{E-V}$, so the rescaled
amplitude carries $N$-power $F - (E - V) = V - E + F
= \chi(\Sigma_\Gamma)$ by Euler's formula.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The logical chain]%
\label{rem:thooft-logical-chain}%

\;+\;
\text{cyclic trace}
\;+\;

\begin{definition}[Ribbonized modular Swiss-cheese theory]%

The \emph{ribbonized modular Swiss-cheese theory}
$\mathrm{RSC}_{\mathrm{tr}}^{\mathrm{mod}}$

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{closed color}: the modular/chiral sector
 \textup{(}Chapters~\textup{\ref{sec:koszul-across-genera}--\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul}}\textup{)}\textup;
\item \emph{open color}: the $E_1$/chiral or
 $A_\infty$-chiral line sector\textup;
\item \emph{mixed operations}: bulk-to-boundary and
 boundary-to-line interactions\textup;
\item \emph{trace data}: a nondegenerate invariant
 pairing~$\tau$ on the open color\textup;
\item \emph{wheel operations}: contractions of
 outputs with inputs via~$\tau$\textup;
\item \emph{ribbon data}: cyclic order at each open

 \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:cyclicity-ribbon}}\textup{)}.

The open input is a cyclic $A_\infty$-object
$(O, m_\bullet, \tau)$\textup; the closed input is
