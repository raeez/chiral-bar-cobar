\section*{Prologue}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Write $\Phi_r(c) := c^{r-2} S_r(\Vir_c)$ for the rescaled shadow
coefficient.  Through the subleading layer
(\ref{thm:shadow-tower-subleading-closed-form},
Vol~I chapter \emph{Higher Virasoro Shadow Coefficients}) the large-$c$
Laurent expansion takes the form
\[
  \Phi_r(c) \;=\; A_r + \frac{B_r}{c} + \frac{\Gamma_r}{c^2} + \frac{\Delta_r}{c^3} + \cdots,
\]
with $A_r = 8(-6)^{r-4}/r$ and $B_r = -A_r[22/5 + (r-4)(r-5)/18]$.  The
present chapter pins the next Laurent datum $\Gamma_r$ as a closed
rational function of $r$, proves the closed form by a second-order
variation-of-parameters telescope on the master-equation recurrence,
and dissects the seemingly Kummer-irregular arithmetic that appears
at $r = 8$ (where the numerator of $\Gamma_8$ carries the prime $691$).
The arithmetic turns out to be a modular coincidence in
$\mathbb{F}_{691}$; no Bernoulli structural mechanism is at work, and
we explicitly cross-check this by verifying the absence of the next
Kummer-irregular prime $3617$ through $r = 15$.

Section~\ref{sec:stss-setup} fixes notation: $\Phi_r$, the Laurent
recurrence in $\Phi$, the base cases $\Phi_3 = 2c$ and
$\Phi_4 = A_4 + B_4/c + \Gamma_4/c^2 + \cdots$ with the explicit
$S_4 = 10/[c(5c+22)]$ expansion.  Section~\ref{sec:stss-recurrence}
extracts the sub-subleading recurrence and its source-ratio closed
form (Lemma~\ref{lem:gamma-source-ratio-closed-form}).
Section~\ref{sec:stss-closed-form} states and proves the main theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-sub-subleading-closed-form}).
Section~\ref{sec:stss-kummer} addresses the $r = 8$ arithmetic and
establishes sporadicity
(Remark~\ref{rem:gamma-691-emergence-sporadic},
Remark~\ref{rem:gamma-irregular-primes-dense-but-structureless}).
Section~\ref{sec:stss-verification} lists the independent verification
decorators.

\section{Setup}
\label{sec:stss-setup}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Fix $\cA = \Vir_c$ with $c$ a generic central charge.  Let
$\{S_r(\Vir_c)\}_{r \ge 2}$ denote the ordered-bar shadow tower
(Vol~I, \ref{part:bar-complex}).  The master-equation recurrence
(\ref{thm:virasoro-shadow-recurrence}) reads
\[
  S_r \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{rc}
    \sum_{\substack{j+k=r+2\\ 3 \le j \le k < r}}
      f(j,k)\, j k\, S_j S_k,
  \qquad
  f(j,k) =
  \begin{cases}
    1 & j < k,\\
    1/2 & j = k,
  \end{cases}
\]
with initial data $\kappa = c/2$, $S_3 = 2$, $S_4 = 10/[c(5c+22)]$.

\begin{definition}[\label{def:phi-laurent}Rescaled Phi and its Laurent
data]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Define $\Phi_r(c) := c^{r-2} S_r(\Vir_c)$.  The large-$c$ Laurent
expansion
\[
  \Phi_r(c) \;=\; A_r + \frac{B_r}{c} + \frac{\Gamma_r}{c^2}
              + \frac{\Delta_r}{c^3} + \cdots
\]
records the \emph{leading} ($A_r$), \emph{subleading} ($B_r$),
\emph{sub-subleading} ($\Gamma_r$) and deeper Laurent coefficients.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[\label{lem:phi-recurrence}Phi-recurrence]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The rescaled coefficient $\Phi_r$ satisfies, for all $r \ge 5$,
\[
  \Phi_r \;=\; -\,\frac{6(r-1)}{r}\, \Phi_{r-1}
       \;-\; \frac{1}{rc}
       \sum_{\substack{j+k=r+2\\ 4 \le j \le k \le r-2}}
         f(j,k)\, j k\, \Phi_j\, \Phi_k.
\]
The first term on the right-hand side is the contribution of the
$(j,k) = (3, r-1)$ pair in the original recurrence, after substituting
$\Phi_3 = 2c$ and $S_{r-1} = \Phi_{r-1}/c^{r-3}$; the sum is over the
remaining pairs $4 \le j \le k \le r-2$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Multiply the master-equation recurrence by $c^{r-2}$ and substitute
$S_j = \Phi_j/c^{j-2}$, $S_k = \Phi_k/c^{k-2}$, and use $j+k-4 = r-2$
to clear all but a single power of $c$ from the sum.  Isolate the
$(3, r-1)$ contribution using $\Phi_3 = 2c$.
\end{proof}

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section{The sub-subleading recurrence}
\label{sec:stss-recurrence}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Matching the coefficient of $c^{-2}$ in
Lemma~\ref{lem:phi-recurrence} yields a linear recurrence for
$\Gamma_r$.  The structural point is that the source at this Laurent
order is purely $B$-linear: no $B_j B_k$ cross-product contributes.

\begin{proposition}[\label{prop:gamma-recurrence}Gamma recurrence]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For $r \ge 5$,
\[
  \Gamma_r \;=\; -\,\frac{6(r-1)}{r}\, \Gamma_{r-1}
              \;-\; \sigma_r^{(\gamma)},
\]
where the source is
\[
  \sigma_r^{(\gamma)}
    \;:=\; \frac{1}{r}
    \sum_{\substack{j+k=r+2\\ 4 \le j \le k \le r-2}}
      f(j,k)\, j k\, \bigl(A_j B_k + B_j A_k\bigr).
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The coefficient of $c^{-1}$ in $\Phi_j \Phi_k$ equals
$A_j B_k + B_j A_k$ (the two Laurent datums combine linearly).  The
factor $1/(rc)$ in Lemma~\ref{lem:phi-recurrence} converts a $c^{-1}$
coefficient in the sum to a $c^{-2}$ coefficient in $\Phi_r$; the
$-6(r-1)/r$ transport term preserves Laurent order, contributing
$-6(r-1)/r \cdot \Gamma_{r-1}$ at $c^{-2}$.  No quadratic $B_j B_k$
term enters at this order: $B_j B_k$ appears first in the $c^{-2}$
coefficient of $\Phi_j \Phi_k$, which the $1/(rc)$ prefactor would
transmit to $c^{-3}$ in $\Phi_r$ (i.e., to $\Delta_r$).
\end{proof}

The source has a closed form.

\begin{lemma}[\label{lem:gamma-source-ratio-closed-form}Source-ratio
closed form]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For $r \ge 5$,
\[
  \frac{\sigma_r^{(\gamma)}}{A_r}
    \;=\; -\,\frac{44(r-5)}{45}
          \;-\; \frac{(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)}{243}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The factored identity $j A_j = 8(-6)^{j-4}$ gives $j k\, A_j A_k =
64(-6)^{j+k-8} = 64(-6)^{r-6}$, independent of the partition $(j,k)$
of $r+2$.  Write $B_j/A_j = -[22/5 + (j-4)(j-5)/18]$, so
\[
  A_j B_k + B_j A_k
    \;=\; A_j A_k\, \bigl[(B_j/A_j) + (B_k/A_k)\bigr]
    \;=\; -A_j A_k \Bigl[\tfrac{44}{5}
        + \tfrac{(j-4)(j-5) + (k-4)(k-5)}{18}\Bigr].
\]
Therefore
\[
  \sigma_r^{(\gamma)}
    \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{r}
    \sum f(j,k)\, j k\, A_j A_k
       \Bigl[\tfrac{44}{5} + \tfrac{(j-4)(j-5) + (k-4)(k-5)}{18}\Bigr].
\]
Split into the $(22/5)$-part and the polynomial part.

The $(22/5)$-part uses the subleading identity
$\sum f(j,k) j k A_j A_k / r = A_r (r-5)/9$
(\ref{thm:shadow-tower-subleading-closed-form}, proof), giving
$-\,(44/5) \cdot A_r (r-5)/9 = -44 A_r (r-5)/45$.

For the polynomial part, symmetrise over $(j,k) \mapsto (k,j)$ to see
that
\[
  \sum f(j,k) \bigl[(j-4)(j-5) + (k-4)(k-5)\bigr]
    \;=\; \sum_{j=4}^{r-2} (j-4)(j-5);
\]
the $f(j,j) = 1/2$ diagonal contributes
$(1/2) \cdot 2 (j-4)(j-5) = (j-4)(j-5)$ exactly as the off-diagonal
pair does.  Setting $m := j-4$,
\[
  \sum_{j=4}^{r-2} (j-4)(j-5)
    \;=\; \sum_{m=0}^{r-6} m(m-1)
    \;=\; \frac{(r-7)(r-6)(r-5)}{3}
\]
(hockey-stick identity).  Using $jk A_j A_k = 64(-6)^{r-6}$ and
$A_r = 288(-6)^{r-6}/r$ the polynomial contribution is
\[
  -\,\frac{1}{18 r} \cdot 64(-6)^{r-6} \cdot \frac{(r-7)(r-6)(r-5)}{3}
    \;=\; -\,\frac{(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)}{243}\, A_r,
\]
after dividing by $A_r = 288(-6)^{r-6}/r$.  Combining gives the
stated closed form.
\end{proof}

\section{Closed form for the sub-subleading coefficient}
\label{sec:stss-closed-form}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:shadow-tower-sub-subleading-closed-form}
Sub-subleading Virasoro shadow asymptotic]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For $r \ge 4$ the sub-subleading coefficient of the Virasoro shadow
tower is
\[
  \frac{\Gamma_r}{A_r}
    \;=\; \frac{484}{25}
          \;+\; \frac{22(r-4)(r-5)}{45}
          \;+\; \frac{(r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)}{972}.
\]
Equivalently, writing $\phi_r := -B_r/A_r = 22/5 + (r-4)(r-5)/18$,
\[
  \frac{\Gamma_r}{A_r}
    \;=\; \phi_r^2 \;-\; \frac{(r-4)(r-5)(r^2 - 7 r + 9)}{486}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The transport prefactor in Proposition~\ref{prop:gamma-recurrence}
absorbs into $A_r$: from $A_r = -6(r-1)/r \cdot A_{r-1}$,
\[
  -\,\frac{6(r-1)}{r} \cdot \frac{\Gamma_{r-1}}{A_r}
    \;=\; -\,\frac{6(r-1)}{r} \cdot \frac{A_{r-1}}{A_r} \cdot
            \frac{\Gamma_{r-1}}{A_{r-1}}
    \;=\; \frac{\Gamma_{r-1}}{A_{r-1}},
\]
since $A_{r-1}/A_r = -r/[6(r-1)]$.  The recurrence in
$G_r := \Gamma_r/A_r$ therefore collapses to
\[
  G_r \;=\; G_{r-1} \;-\; \frac{\sigma_r^{(\gamma)}}{A_r}.
\]

Substituting the source-ratio closed form
(Lemma~\ref{lem:gamma-source-ratio-closed-form}) and iterating from
the base case $G_4 = 484/25$:
\[
  G_r \;=\; \frac{484}{25}
            \;+\; \frac{44}{45} \sum_{s=5}^{r} (s-5)
            \;+\; \frac{1}{243} \sum_{s=5}^{r} (s-5)(s-6)(s-7).
\]
The two sums evaluate via hockey-stick:
\[
  \sum_{s=5}^{r} (s-5) \;=\; \sum_{t=0}^{r-5} t
                       \;=\; \frac{(r-4)(r-5)}{2},
\]
\[
  \sum_{s=5}^{r} (s-5)(s-6)(s-7)
    \;=\; 6 \sum_{t=0}^{r-5} \binom{t}{3}
    \;=\; 6 \binom{r-4}{4}
    \;=\; \frac{(r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)}{4}.
\]
Substituting:
\[
  G_r
    \;=\; \frac{484}{25}
          \;+\; \frac{44}{45} \cdot \frac{(r-4)(r-5)}{2}
          \;+\; \frac{1}{243} \cdot \frac{(r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)}{4}
    \;=\; \frac{484}{25}
          \;+\; \frac{22(r-4)(r-5)}{45}
          \;+\; \frac{(r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)}{972}.
\]

To derive the alternative form, expand $\phi_r^2$:
\[
  \phi_r^2
    \;=\; \Bigl(\frac{22}{5} + \frac{(r-4)(r-5)}{18}\Bigr)^2
    \;=\; \frac{484}{25} + \frac{22(r-4)(r-5)}{45} + \frac{[(r-4)(r-5)]^2}{324}.
\]
Compare $\phi_r^2$ to $G_r$: the difference is
\[
  G_r - \phi_r^2
    \;=\; \frac{(r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)}{972}
          - \frac{(r-4)^2 (r-5)^2}{324}.
\]
Factor $(r-4)(r-5)/972$ out:
\[
  G_r - \phi_r^2
    \;=\; \frac{(r-4)(r-5)}{972}
          \bigl[(r-6)(r-7) - 3(r-4)(r-5)\bigr].
\]
Expand the bracket: $(r-6)(r-7) - 3(r-4)(r-5) = r^2 - 13 r + 42 - 3(r^2 - 9 r + 20)
= -2r^2 + 14 r - 18 = -2(r^2 - 7 r + 9)$.  Therefore
\[
  G_r - \phi_r^2
    \;=\; -\,\frac{2(r-4)(r-5)(r^2 - 7 r + 9)}{972}
    \;=\; -\,\frac{(r-4)(r-5)(r^2 - 7 r + 9)}{486},
\]
as stated.

Independent verification chain: the closed form is cross-checked by
direct Laurent expansion of the explicit $S_r(\Vir_c)$ quartics for
$r = 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10$ at
\texttt{compute/tests/test\_sub\_subleading\_asymptotic.py}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:gamma-base-case}Base cases]
At $r = 4$ and $r = 5$ the $(r-4)(r-5)$ factor kills both polynomial
terms, giving the constant $G_4 = G_5 = 484/25$.  This constant equals
$(B_4/A_4)^2$ and represents the ``Riccati square'' of the subleading
phase: the sub-subleading layer \emph{initialises} as the square of
the subleading, and the structural polynomial $(r-4)(r-5)(r^2-7 r+9)/486$
measures the departure from pure squaring.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:gamma-riccati-origin}Riccati interpretation]
The quadratic $\sqrt{Q_L}$ Riccati expansion of
\texttt{higher\_genus\_modular\_koszul.tex}
(thm:riccati-algebraicity) predicts that $\Phi_r$ is a truncated
binomial expansion of a square root, so the Laurent coefficients
$A_r, B_r, \Gamma_r, \ldots$ follow a Taylor hierarchy with each
successive layer contributing a polynomial correction to the
previous square.  The theorem makes this hierarchy explicit for the
second layer: $\Gamma_r = A_r \phi_r^2 + \text{(quartic correction)}$.
\end{remark}

\section{The Kummer-irregular prime 691 at $r = 8$}
\label{sec:stss-kummer}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Clearing the common denominator $\mathrm{LCM}(25, 45, 972) = 24300$
in Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-sub-subleading-closed-form} yields
an integer quartic.

\begin{lemma}[\label{lem:gamma-numerator-quartic-polynomial}Gamma
numerator polynomial]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
$\Gamma_r / A_r = N(r)/24300$ where
\[
  N(r) \;=\; 25 r^4 - 550 r^3 + 16355 r^2 - 122870 r + 729048.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Direct expansion of the three-term closed form over common
denominator $24300$.  The integer-coefficient reduction $N(r)$ is
unique modulo multiplication by a positive rational constant, and
the coefficients follow from
\[
  \frac{484}{25} \cdot 24300 \;=\; 470448, \qquad
  \frac{22}{45} \cdot 24300 \;=\; 11880, \qquad
  \frac{1}{972} \cdot 24300 \;=\; 25,
\]
and the integer expansion of $(r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7) = r^4 - 22 r^3 +
179 r^2 - 638 r + 840$.  Collecting:
\[
  N(r)
    \;=\; 470448 + 11880(r-4)(r-5) + 25(r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7).
\]
Expanding and simplifying gives the stated form.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[\label{lem:gamma-numerator-irreducible}Irreducibility
over $\mathbb{Q}$]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The polynomial $N(r)$ is irreducible over $\mathbb{Q}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Content-free: the coefficients $(25, -550, 16355, -122870, 729048)$
have gcd $1$ in $\mathbb{Z}$, so any non-trivial factorisation over
$\mathbb{Q}$ would descend to one over $\mathbb{Z}$ (Gauss's lemma).
Rational roots: by the rational root theorem, any $p/q$ root has
$p \mid 729048$ and $q \mid 25$, giving a finite check.  One verifies
by direct substitution that no such candidate is a root.  For a
factorisation $N(r) = (a r^2 + b r + c)(a' r^2 + b' r + c')$ with
$a a' = 25$, the candidate matching $(a, a') \in \{(1, 25), (5, 5),
(25, 1)\}$ leads to over-determined systems in $(b, b', c, c')$ that
have no integer solutions (checked in the verification test suite
via sympy Q-factorisation).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:gamma-691-emergence-sporadic}The $691$ at
$r = 8$ is a modular coincidence]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
$N(8) = 613608 = 2^3 \cdot 3 \cdot 37 \cdot 691$ carries the
Kummer-irregular prime $691$.  This is a modular coincidence:
$N(r) \bmod 691$ is a degree-$4$ polynomial in $\mathbb{F}_{691}[r]$
with \emph{exactly four} roots $r \in \{8, 315, 423, 658\}$, the
maximum possible for a degree-$4$ polynomial over a field.  The
polynomial attains its maximum root count, so the existence of
\emph{some} small-$r$ integer at which $691 \mid N(r)$ is forced by
counting, not by a Bernoulli structural mechanism.

Concretely, $B_{12} = -691/2730$ is the first Bernoulli number
carrying the prime $691$, but the shadow position $r = 8$ does not
correspond to weight $12$ under any natural correspondence:
the shadow-Feynman bijection $F_g \leftrightarrow S_{2g-2}$ maps
$S_8$ to $F_5$ (genus-$5$), which involves $B_8 = -1/30$ (no $691$)
and $B_{10} = 5/66$ (no $691$), not $B_{12}$.  The sporadicity is
confirmed by the absence of the next Kummer-irregular prime $3617$
from $N(r)$ through $r = 15$: if the emergence were Bernoulli-driven,
$3617$ (which divides the numerator of $B_{16}$) would appear at a
weight-$16$-linked shadow position, and it does not.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:gamma-irregular-primes-dense-but-structureless}
Irregular primes appear densely but structurelessly]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Factorising $N(r)$ for $r \in \{4, \ldots, 15\}$ produces the prime
set
\[
  \{2, 3, 5, 11, 13, 19, 29, 37, 43, 47, 53, 691, 811, 1423, 3067\}.
\]
Only $691$ is a Kummer-irregular leading prime.  The other primes
that appear are small regular primes (no $p < 691$ is Kummer-
irregular at the leading level) or large primes without Bernoulli
content.  The pattern is exactly what one expects from the statistics
of values of an irreducible integer polynomial of degree $4$: primes
of size roughly $N(r)^{1/4}$ dominate, with sporadic large prime
factors arising from the full value $N(r)$.  No Bernoulli-congruence
signal is present.
\end{remark}

\section{Summary}
\label{sec:stss-summary}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The sub-subleading asymptotic of the Virasoro shadow tower is
captured by a polynomial of degree $4$ in $r$ divided by a Laurent
hierarchy governed by $A_r$.  The main contributions are a constant
``Riccati-square'' term $484/25$, a quadratic hockey-stick term
$22(r-4)(r-5)/45$, and a quartic hockey-stick term
$(r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)/972$.

The apparent emergence of the Kummer-irregular prime $691$ at
$r = 8$ is a modular coincidence in $\mathbb{F}_{691}$: the polynomial
$N(r) = 25 r^4 - 550 r^3 + 16355 r^2 - 122870 r + 729048$ attains the
maximum root count mod $691$, and $r = 8$ happens to be one of the
four roots $\{8, 315, 423, 658\}$.  No Bernoulli structural identity
is at work.  The next Kummer-irregular prime $3617$ does not appear
through $r = 15$, confirming the absence of structure.

The genuinely number-theoretic content of the Virasoro shadow tower
lives at the \emph{leading} layer $A_r$ (pure $2^a 3^b$ rationals) and
at the genus-tower image $F_g \leftrightarrow S_{2g-2}$ via the
Bernoulli normalisation $B_{2g-2}/(g-1)$, where Kummer congruences
genuinely act.  The sub-subleading layer $\Gamma_r$ is rational in
$r$ with no Kummer content; the $691$ at $r = 8$ is accidental.
