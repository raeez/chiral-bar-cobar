The master conjecture MC5 (chain-level identification of the BV-BRST
complex with the algebraic bar complex) is one of the five backbone
conjectures of the programme. The class-$\mathsf{M}$ strand, covering
Virasoro and principal $\mathcal{W}_N$, has been the structurally most
delicate. The Vol~I strong completion tower
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong}}\textup{)}
together with the standard-family weight filtration
\textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:standard-strong-filtration}}\textup{)}
and its Vol~II BV-side counterpart
\textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed}}\textup{)}
establish the comparison in the weight-completed category. A residual
remark has circulated that ``direct-sum chain-level MC5 for class $\mathsf{M}$
is genuinely false,'' with the weight-completed category named as the
correct ambient.

This chapter reconstitutes that remark without weakening anything. The
residual remark is correct as a statement about the raw ambient
$\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$ of bounded direct sums. It is not a
mathematical obstruction to chain-level MC5 on the original complex.
The bar complex of a weight-graded chiral algebra is, by its own
construction, the inverse limit of its conformal-weight truncations;
this inverse limit lives natively in $\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$,
and the chain-level MC5 theorem holds on it. The Milnor / Mittag-Leffler
apparatus of Theorem~\ref{thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong} is precisely
the vehicle that turns the pro-object statement into a chain-level
comparison on the inverse limit.

The payoff is conceptual as well as technical. The ``genuinely false''
remark was never an assertion about the mathematical content; it was an
observation that a particular ambient choice (naive direct sum in
$\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$) is not the ambient of the original
object. The original bar complex of a weight-graded chiral algebra is
a pro-object. Reading it as a pro-object, or equivalently as a
topological chain complex with the positive-weight $J$-adic topology,
or equivalently as a filtered-completed complex in the sense of
Definition~\textup{\ref{def:strong-completion-tower}} \textup{(}all
three agree under an explicit comparison theorem\textup{)}, gives
chain-level MC5 class $\mathsf{M}$ unconditionally. The three ambients
are equivalent presentations of one mathematical theorem.

The chapter slots into the unified architecture as follows. The
Koszul reflection $K^{2}\simeq\mathrm{id}$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-reflection} delivers chain-level inversion on
$\Kosz(X)$ \textup{(}clause~\ref{KR-ii}\textup{)} and a coderived
involution off it \textup{(}clause~\ref{KR-iii}\textup{)}; class
$\mathsf{M}$ is the off-Koszul-locus regime in which the pro-ambient
strengthening of this chapter promotes the coderived involution to a
strict chain-level quasi-isomorphism on the inverse-limit complex.
The bar differential of class~$\mathsf{M}$ is, like every chiral bar
differential in this monograph, the pullback $\dbar = \KZ^{*}(\nabla_{\Arn})$
of Arnold's universal flat connection along the KZ functor of
Chapter~\ref{chap:climax-platonic}
\textup{(}equation~\eqref{eq:climax-equation-G2} of the climax
theorem\textup{)}; the per-genus $\kappa$-conductor
appearing in the harmonic
discrepancy~\eqref{eq:harmonic-discrepancy} is the negative of the
ghost charge of any quasi-free BRST resolution by the universal
identity of Chapter~\ref{chap:universal-conductor}.

\section{Setup and conventions}
\label{sec:mc5-setup}

Throughout this chapter, $X$ is a smooth projective curve over a field
of characteristic zero, and $\cA$ is a class-$\mathsf{M}$ chiral
algebra in the standard landscape: Virasoro $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ at
generic~$c$ \textup{(}outside the minimal-model and admissible loci,
absorbed into the companion Feigin--Frenkel / Feigin--Fuchs
treatments\textup{)} or the principal $\mathcal{W}_N$-algebra
$\cW^k(\fg, f_{\mathrm{prin}})$ at non-critical level for classical
simple $\fg$. The conformal-weight grading
\[
\cA \;=\; \bigoplus_{h \geq 0} \cA_h,
\qquad
\dim \cA_h < \infty,
\]
exists by construction \textup{(}L_0-diagonal decomposition; for
principal $\cW$ the Feigin--Fuchs / Fateev--Lukyanov generator
structure\textup{)}. Set
\[
\cA_{\leq N} \;:=\; \bigoplus_{h=0}^{N} \cA_h,
\qquad
\widehat{\cA}^{\mathrm{wt}} \;:=\; \prod_{h \geq 0} \cA_h
\;=\; \varprojlim_N \cA_{\leq N}.
\]

The relationship between direct sum and product is the single structural
datum that controls everything in this chapter: the chiral algebra $\cA$
\emph{is} the direct sum, while its natural bar-theoretic ambient, once
the weight truncation filtration is imposed, is the product.

\begin{notation}[Chain-level versus limit-level]
\label{not:chain-versus-limit}
We write $B^{(g)}(\cA)$ for the raw genus-$g$ bar complex of $\cA$ in
$\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$, understood as the bounded direct sum of
bar words graded by conformal weight. We write $\widehat{B}^{(g)}(\cA)$
for the weight-completed bar complex, equivalently the inverse limit
\[
\widehat{B}^{(g)}(\cA)
\;:=\;
\varprojlim_N \, \bar B^{\mathrm{ch}, (g)}(\cA_{\leq N})
\]
of its finite-weight truncations, regarded as an object of
$\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$ or as the underlying
topological chain complex with the $J$-adic topology generated by
positive-weight bar words. We use analogous notation for the BV side.
\end{notation}

The relevant harmonic datum was isolated in Vol~II
Remark~\ref{rem:bv-bar-class-m-frontier}: for class $\mathsf{M}$, the
residual discrepancy between the BV comparison map and the bar map
satisfies
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:harmonic-discrepancy}
\delta_r^{\mathrm{harm}}
\;=\;
c_r(\cA) \cdot m_0^{\lfloor r/2 \rfloor - 1},
\qquad r \geq 4,
\end{equation}
where $c_r(\cA)$ is the $r$-th shadow coefficient and $m_0$ is the
genus-$g$ curvature of the bar complex. For Virasoro and principal
$\cW_N$ the shadow tower does not terminate
\textup{(}Corollary~\textup{\ref{cor:virasoro-postnikov-nontermination}}\textup{)},
so the collection $\{\delta_r^{\mathrm{harm}}\}_{r \geq 4}$ is an
infinite family of nonzero central-curvature-valued corrections.

\section{The ghost--error--correct triple}
\label{sec:mc5-ghost-triple}

The residual remark ``direct-sum chain-level class $\mathsf{M}$ is
genuinely false'' contains a ghost of a true theorem, a precise error,
and a correct relationship.

\paragraph{Ghost theorem.} In the naive ambient $\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$
of bounded direct sums, with the comparison map
$f_g \colon C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{BV}}(\cA, \Sigma_g) \to B^{(g)}(\cA)$
required to be a strict chain homotopy equivalence, no single contracting
homotopy $h$ can absorb the infinite family $\{\delta_r^{\mathrm{harm}}\}_{r \geq 4}$
at once: for class $\mathsf{M}$ the series $\sum_r \delta_r^{\mathrm{harm}}$
is not a bounded sum in the direct-sum ambient. This is a true theorem
about a specific ambient.

\paragraph{Error.} Calling this observation ``genuinely false'' conflates
two statements: \textup{(i)} direct-sum chain-level MC5 class $\mathsf{M}$
in the \emph{uncompleted} ambient $\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$, which is
the ambient of the raw bar complex, and \textup{(ii)} chain-level
MC5 class $\mathsf{M}$ in any ambient that admits the inverse-limit
topology under which the infinite family becomes a single topologically
convergent correction. The raw bar complex, viewed in the ambient of its
own weight-filtered truncations, \emph{is} a pro-object whose limit is
the inverse limit of its bar truncations; the strict chain homotopy
exists in this pro-ambient because it is defined stage by stage and
$\mathrm{Mittag\text{-}Leffler}$ kills the derived limit obstruction.

\paragraph{Correct relationship.} Chain-level MC5 class $\mathsf{M}$ is
proved at the chain level in three mutually equivalent ambients:
\textup{(1)} the pro-object ambient $\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$
of inverse systems of chain complexes; \textup{(2)} the topological
chain complex with the $J$-adic topology generated by the positive-weight
ideal $J = \bigoplus_{h > 0} \cA_h$; \textup{(3)} the filtered-completed
ambient $\mathrm{CompCl}(\mathcal{F})$ of
Definition~\textup{\ref{def:strong-completion-tower}}. The statement
``genuinely false'' is restricted to the bounded-direct-sum ambient
$\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$, which is not the ambient of the original
bar complex once its weight filtration is retained.

\section{The Platonic theorem: chain-level MC5 class M in the pro-ambient}
\label{sec:mc5-platonic-theorem}

Three equivalent formulations of the strongest honest form: the
pro-object form used throughout the rest of the chapter, the
topological-chain form, and the already-installed filtered-completion
form.

\begin{theorem}[MC5 class $\mathsf{M}$ chain-level in the pro-ambient]
\label{thm:mc5-class-m-chain-level-pro-ambient}
\index{MC5!class M chain-level in pro-ambient|textbf}
\index{pro-object ambient!chain-level MC5}
Let $\cA$ be a class-$\mathsf{M}$ chiral algebra in the standard landscape
\textup{(}Virasoro $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ at generic $c$, or principal
$\mathcal{W}_N$ at non-critical level for classical simple $\fg$\textup{)},
equipped with the conformal-weight filtration of
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:standard-strong-filtration}}. Let $g \geq 0$,
let
\[
f_g^{(\leq N)} \colon
C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{BV}}(\cA_{\leq N}, \Sigma_g)
\longrightarrow
\bar B^{\mathrm{ch}, (g)}(\cA_{\leq N})
\]
be the finite-weight comparison map at stage $N$, and let
\[
f_g \;:=\; \{f_g^{(\leq N)}\}_{N \geq 0}
\;\in\;
\mathrm{Mor}\bigl(\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})\bigr)
\]
be the induced morphism of pro-objects. Then $f_g$ is a chain-level
quasi-isomorphism in $\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$:
at each weight-truncation stage $N$, the finite-stage map $f_g^{(\leq N)}$
is a strict chain-level quasi-isomorphism, and the tower satisfies the
Mittag--Leffler condition in every cohomological degree.
\ClaimStatusProvedHere{}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Step 1: finite-stage strict quasi-isomorphism.} For each $N$, the
truncation $\cA_{\leq N}$ is finite-dimensional
\textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:standard-strong-filtration}}\textup{)}.
The harmonic discrepancy at stage $N$ involves only finitely many bar
weights $r \leq N$, so the series
$\sum_{r \leq N} \delta_r^{\mathrm{harm}} = \sum_{r \leq N} c_r(\cA) \cdot m_0^{\lfloor r/2 \rfloor - 1}$
is a finite sum of curvature corrections acting between finite-dimensional
bar-weight summands of $\bar B^{\mathrm{ch}, (g)}(\cA_{\leq N})$. The
explicit contracting homotopy
\[
h_N
\;=\;
\sum_{j=1}^{\lfloor N/2 \rfloor}
h \cdot m_0^{j-1}
\]
constructed in the proof of
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed}}
absorbs every finite-stage discrepancy: $h_N$ is a finite sum of bounded
linear maps on the finite-dimensional stage-$N$ bar complex, the
identity $\mathrm{id} - d h_N - h_N d$ is the idempotent projecting onto
the harmonic subspace, and $f_g^{(\leq N)}$ is strictly inverse to the
harmonic projector. Therefore $f_g^{(\leq N)}$ is a strict chain-level
quasi-isomorphism for every $N$.

\emph{Step 2: compatibility under tower maps.} The transition maps
$p_N \colon \cA_{\leq N+1} \twoheadrightarrow \cA_{\leq N}$ are strict
surjections of curved chiral $A_\infty$-algebras
\textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:standard-strong-filtration}}(ii)\textup{)},
and the induced maps on bar and BV complexes are morphisms of chain
complexes. The homotopies $h_N$ are compatible: for every weight
bound $w \leq N$ and every $N' \geq N \geq w$, the restriction of
$h_{N'}$ to $F_{\leq w} \bar B^{\mathrm{ch}, (g)}(\cA_{\leq N'})$
coincides with $h_N$ on $F_{\leq w} \bar B^{\mathrm{ch}, (g)}(\cA_{\leq N})$
under the isomorphism
$F_{\leq w}\bar B^{\mathrm{ch}, (g)}(\cA_{\leq N'}) \xrightarrow{\sim}
F_{\leq w}\bar B^{\mathrm{ch}, (g)}(\cA_{\leq N})$
of Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:standard-strong-filtration}}(iv).
This is the conformal-weight-homogeneity-of-homotopies observation
recorded in the first-principles cache entry for this chapter; it is
automatic from the weight-preservation property of the bar differential
and of $m_0$. Therefore $\{f_g^{(\leq N)}\}_N$ assembles into a morphism
of pro-objects.

\emph{Step 3: Mittag--Leffler condition, bigraded in
$(\text{cohomological degree},\ \text{total bar weight})$.} We verify
Mittag--Leffler for the inverse systems
$\{H^m(C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{BV}}(\cA_{\leq N}, \Sigma_g))\}_N$ and
$\{H^m(\bar B^{\mathrm{ch}, (g)}(\cA_{\leq N}))\}_N$ by bigrading: fix
\emph{both} the cohomological degree $m \in \Z$ and the total bar
weight $w \in \Z_{\geq 0}$.

The bar differential of $\bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA)$ preserves total
conformal weight on bar words
$s^{-1}a_1 | \cdots | s^{-1} a_r$: each Arnold contraction
replaces a pair $(a_i, a_j)$ of conformal weights $(h_i, h_j)$ by a
single element of conformal weight $h_i + h_j$, so the total weight
$w = \sum_j h_j$ is conserved. Hence the bar complex decomposes as a
product over total weights,
\[
\bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA_{\leq N})
\;=\;
\prod_{w \geq 0}
\bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA_{\leq N})_w,
\]
and the same decomposition holds on the BV side by the
conformal-weight grading of $C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{BV}}$. Taking cohomology
commutes with the product (the product is over weights; cohomology is
taken in the internal bar/BV degree), so each cohomology group splits
bigradedly:
\[
H^m\bigl(\bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA_{\leq N})\bigr)
\;=\;
\prod_{w \geq 0}
H^m\bigl(\bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA_{\leq N})_w\bigr).
\]
At each fixed $(m, w)$, the vector space
$H^m(\bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA_{\leq N})_w)$ is finite-dimensional
(each bar word of total weight $w$ has at most $w$ factors of weight
$\geq 1$, so the weight-$w$ bar complex of $\cA_{\leq N}$ is
finite-dimensional for every $N$), and it stabilises for $N \geq w$ by
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:standard-strong-filtration}}(iv): at stage
$N \geq w$, the truncation map
$\bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA_{\leq N+1})_w \to
\bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA_{\leq N})_w$ is an isomorphism because
every $a_j$ with $h_j \leq w \leq N$ already lies in $\cA_{\leq N}$.

Therefore the inverse system $\{H^m(\cdot)_w\}_N$ is eventually
constant at each $(m,w)$, which trivially satisfies Mittag--Leffler.
The full inverse system $\{H^m(\cdot)\}_N$ is the product over $w$ of
the $(m,w)$-bigraded systems, and the Mittag--Leffler property is
preserved under products of inverse systems (the image filtration
stabilises in each factor separately). In particular the tower of
cones $\{\mathrm{Cone}(f_g^{(\leq N)})\}_N$ is Mittag--Leffler in every
cohomological degree $m$, uniformly via the $(m, w)$ bigrading; the
$\varprojlim^1$ obstruction vanishes in each $(m, w)$ and hence in the
product.

This bigrading refinement is what licenses the Mittag--Leffler invocation
at fixed $m$: weight-stabilisation alone works at fixed $w$ only, but
the bar differential being weight-preserving promotes fixed-$w$
stabilisation to fixed-$m$ stabilisation via the product decomposition.

\emph{Step 4: pro-object chain-level quasi-isomorphism.} A morphism in
$\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$ is a chain-level
quasi-isomorphism when \textup{(a)} every term map is a chain-level
quasi-isomorphism, and \textup{(b)} the Mittag--Leffler condition holds
\textup{(}so the derived inverse-limit obstruction $\varprojlim^1$
vanishes\textup{)}. Both are established in Steps~1--3. Therefore $f_g$
is a chain-level quasi-isomorphism in the pro-ambient.
\end{proof}

The next corollary records what Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:mc5-class-m-chain-level-pro-ambient}}
says about the \emph{original} weight-graded bar complex, viewed as the
inverse limit of its truncation tower.

\begin{corollary}[Chain-level MC5 class $\mathsf{M}$ on the inverse limit]
\label{cor:mc5-class-m-chain-level-on-inverse-limit}
\index{inverse limit bar!chain-level MC5 class M|textbf}
Let $\cA$ be as in Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:mc5-class-m-chain-level-pro-ambient}}.
The inverse-limit comparison
\[
\widehat{f}_g \;=\; \varprojlim_N f_g^{(\leq N)}
\;\colon\;
\widehat{C}^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{BV}}(\cA, \Sigma_g)
\;\longrightarrow\;
\widehat{B}^{(g)}(\cA)
\]
is a strict chain-level quasi-isomorphism of complete curved chiral
chain complexes. The inverse limit $\widehat{B}^{(g)}(\cA)$ is the
weight-completed bar complex and is the canonical ambient for the
original weight-graded bar complex of $\cA$ under the weight filtration
of Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:standard-strong-filtration}}.
\ClaimStatusProvedHere{}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The Milnor exact sequence applied to the inverse system of cones yields
\[
0 \to \varprojlim\nolimits^1_N H^{m-1}\bigl(\mathrm{Cone}(f_g^{(\leq N)})\bigr)
\to H^m\bigl(\mathrm{Cone}(\widehat{f}_g)\bigr)
\to \varprojlim_N H^m\bigl(\mathrm{Cone}(f_g^{(\leq N)})\bigr) \to 0.
\]
The right term vanishes because each $f_g^{(\leq N)}$ is a chain-level
quasi-isomorphism
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:mc5-class-m-chain-level-pro-ambient}},
Step~1\textup{)}; the left term vanishes because the system of cones
is Mittag--Leffler
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:mc5-class-m-chain-level-pro-ambient}},
Step~3\textup{)}. Hence the cone of $\widehat{f}_g$ is acyclic, and
$\widehat{f}_g$ is a chain-level quasi-isomorphism.

That $\widehat{B}^{(g)}(\cA)$ is the canonical ambient for the bar
complex of $\cA$ is
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:standard-strong-filtration}}(i): the
direct sum $B^{(g)}(\cA) = \bigoplus_h B^{(g)}(\cA)_h$ embeds as a
dense subspace of $\widehat{B}^{(g)}(\cA) = \prod_h B^{(g)}(\cA)_h$,
and these two presentations of the underlying graded vector space
differ only by the ambient-category convention
\textup{(}direct-sum versus product\textup{)}. The inverse limit is the
product ambient, which is the natural home of the truncation tower.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[``Genuinely false'' is an ambient-choice remark]
\label{rem:direct-sum-obstruction-is-ambient-choice}
\index{direct-sum obstruction!ambient-choice artefact}
Corollary~\textup{\ref{cor:mc5-class-m-chain-level-on-inverse-limit}}
clarifies the earlier residual statement. The sentence
\[
\text{``direct-sum chain-level MC5 for class $\mathsf{M}$ is genuinely false''}
\]
is correct as a remark about the specific ambient $\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$
of bounded direct sums with naive chain homotopies. It is not a statement
about the mathematical content of MC5 class $\mathsf{M}$. The original
bar complex of a weight-graded chiral algebra is the direct sum
$\bigoplus_h B^{(g)}(\cA)_h$ of its conformal-weight components; but
with the weight filtration retained, its natural categorical home is
the pro-object whose $N$-th truncation is the finite direct sum
$\bigoplus_{h \leq N} B^{(g)}(\cA)_h$. In the pro-ambient, the infinite
family of curvature corrections $\{\delta_r^{\mathrm{harm}}\}_{r \geq 4}$
is absorbed stage by stage, and the limiting comparison is a strict
chain-level quasi-isomorphism
\textup{(}Corollary~\textup{\ref{cor:mc5-class-m-chain-level-on-inverse-limit}}\textup{)}.

Forgetting the weight filtration before taking the bar construction is
what forces the ambient of bounded direct sums, and in that ambient
chain-level MC5 class $\mathsf{M}$ fails. The lesson is categorical
hygiene, not a mathematical obstruction: the
ambient must retain the tower under which the bar complex was
constructed. Moving from $\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$ to
$\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$ is a lossless
re-description, not a restriction.
\end{remark}

\section{The topological chain form}
\label{sec:mc5-j-adic}

The second equivalent formulation uses the $J$-adic topology on the raw
bar complex, where $J$ is the positive-weight ideal. This form makes
contact with the $J$-adic completions familiar from commutative algebra
and with the formal neighbourhood viewpoint of the factorisation
picture.

\begin{definition}[Positive-weight $J$-adic topological chain complex]
\label{def:j-adic-topology-bar}
\index{positive-weight ideal!J-adic topology}%
Let $\cA$ be a class-$\mathsf{M}$ algebra as in
Section~\textup{\ref{sec:mc5-setup}} and let $J \subset \cA$ be the
positive-weight ideal $J = \bigoplus_{h > 0} \cA_h$. The $J$-adic
filtration on the genus-$g$ bar complex is
\[
J^{(N)} B^{(g)}(\cA)
\;:=\;
\mathrm{span}\Bigl\{\,
s^{-1} a_1 \mid \cdots \mid s^{-1} a_r
\,\bigm|\,
\sum_{j=1}^r \operatorname{wt}(a_j) > N \,\Bigr\},
\]
i.e.\ the subspace of bar words whose total conformal weight exceeds $N$.
The $J$-adic topological chain complex $B^{(g)}(\cA)^{\wedge}_J$ is the
completion of $B^{(g)}(\cA)$ with respect to this filtration; a base of
open neighbourhoods of zero is $\{J^{(N)} B^{(g)}(\cA)\}_N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MC5 class $\mathsf{M}$ chain-level in the $J$-adic topological ambient]
\label{thm:mc5-class-m-topological-chain-level-j-adic}
\index{MC5!class M chain-level J-adic|textbf}
\index{J-adic topological chain complex!chain-level MC5}
Let $\cA$ be a class-$\mathsf{M}$ chiral algebra in the standard
landscape. In the $J$-adic topological chain complex
$B^{(g)}(\cA)^{\wedge}_J$ of
Definition~\textup{\ref{def:j-adic-topology-bar}} and its BV
counterpart $C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{BV}}(\cA, \Sigma_g)^{\wedge}_J$, the
comparison map
\[
f_g^{\wedge J}
\;\colon\;
C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{BV}}(\cA, \Sigma_g)^{\wedge}_J
\longrightarrow
B^{(g)}(\cA)^{\wedge}_J
\]
is a strict chain-level quasi-isomorphism of topological chain complexes.
The contracting homotopy is continuous with respect to the $J$-adic
topology: the series $h^{\wedge} = \sum_j h \cdot m_0^{j-1}$ converges
pointwise because, for every element of bounded conformal weight, only
finitely many terms in the sum are nonzero.
\ClaimStatusProvedHere{}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The $J$-adic quotient $B^{(g)}(\cA) / J^{(N)} B^{(g)}(\cA)$ is naturally
isomorphic to the finite-weight truncation
$\bar B^{\mathrm{ch}, (g)}(\cA_{\leq N})$ of
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:standard-strong-filtration}}:
both quotient out bar words of total conformal weight exceeding $N$,
and the identification preserves differentials, coproducts, and
curvature.

On each finite quotient the comparison $f_g^{(\leq N)}$ is a strict
chain-level quasi-isomorphism
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:mc5-class-m-chain-level-pro-ambient}},
Step~1\textup{)}. The transition maps are induced by the inclusions
$J^{(N+1)} \subset J^{(N)}$ and are compatible with $f_g$ by the
weight-homogeneity Step~2.

Continuity of the contracting homotopy is the $J$-adic rephrasing of the
degree cutoff
\textup{(}Lemma~\textup{\ref{lem:degree-cutoff}}\textup{)}: for any
element $x$ of weight bound $w$, all $J$-adic-convergent series in $m_0$
truncate after $\lfloor w/2 \rfloor$ terms, so the defining sum for
$h^{\wedge}$ is finite when restricted to any $J$-adic neighbourhood.

The completion functor preserves chain-level quasi-isomorphisms of
Mittag--Leffler towers. Since the tower of finite quotients is
Mittag--Leffler
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:mc5-class-m-chain-level-pro-ambient}},
Step~3\textup{)}, $f_g^{\wedge J}$ is a strict chain-level
quasi-isomorphism in the topological ambient.
\end{proof}

\section{Equivalence of the three ambient completions}
\label{sec:mc5-ambient-equivalence}

The three ambients (pro-object, $J$-adic topological, and
filtered-completed) agree on the chain-level MC5 statement for class
$\mathsf{M}$. The proposition below is structural:
it does not depend on class $\mathsf{M}$ specifics and applies to any
standard-landscape chiral algebra, i.e.\ any of the classes
$\mathsf{G}, \mathsf{L}, \mathsf{C}, \mathsf{M}$.

\begin{proposition}[Ambient equivalence for chain-level MC5]
\label{prop:ambient-equivalence}
\index{ambient equivalence!chain-level MC5}%
Let $\cA$ be a standard-landscape chiral algebra with the conformal-weight
filtration of
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:standard-strong-filtration}}. The following
three statements are equivalent, each implying the others:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item the pro-object comparison
$f_g = \{f_g^{(\leq N)}\}_N$ of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:mc5-class-m-chain-level-pro-ambient}}
is a chain-level quasi-isomorphism in
$\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$;
\item the $J$-adic topological comparison $f_g^{\wedge J}$ of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:mc5-class-m-topological-chain-level-j-adic}}
is a chain-level quasi-isomorphism of topological chain complexes;
\item the weight-completed comparison $\widehat{f}_g$ of
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed}} is a
strict chain-level quasi-isomorphism of complete curved chain complexes
in $\mathrm{CompCl}(\mathcal{F})$.
\end{enumerate}
Each follows from any one by a functorial comparison of ambient
categories.
\ClaimStatusProvedHere{}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
We construct three pairs of mutually inverse functors.

\emph{Pro-object $\leftrightarrow$ $J$-adic topological.} The functor
\[
\Phi_{\mathrm{pro}\to J}
\;\colon\;
\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})^{\mathrm{ML}}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\mathrm{Ch}_J(\mathrm{TopVect})
\]
from the Mittag--Leffler pro-objects to $J$-adic topological chain
complexes sends $\{C_N\}_N$ to the topological chain complex
$\varprojlim_N C_N$ with base of open neighbourhoods
$\{\ker(\varprojlim_N C_N \to C_N)\}_N$. Its inverse sends a $J$-adic
topological chain complex to the pro-object of its finite $J$-adic
quotients. On the bar complex and its BV counterpart these functors
identify $\widehat{B}^{(g)}(\cA) = \varprojlim_N \bar B^{(g)}(\cA_{\leq N})$
with $B^{(g)}(\cA)^{\wedge}_J$, and Mittag--Leffler chain-level
quasi-isomorphisms with continuous chain-level quasi-isomorphisms. This
is the standard pro-to-topological correspondence for
Mittag--Leffler-filtered complexes.

\emph{$J$-adic topological $\leftrightarrow$ filtered-completed.} The
functor
\[
\Phi_{J \to \mathrm{Filt}}
\;\colon\;
\mathrm{Ch}_J(\mathrm{TopVect})
\;\longrightarrow\;
\mathrm{CompCl}(\mathcal{F})
\]
sends the $J$-adic topological chain complex to its filtered-completed
incarnation under the filtration $F^N := J^{(N)}$. Its inverse retrieves
the $J$-adic topology from the filtration: open neighbourhoods are the
images of the filtration pieces. On bar complexes these functors are
inverse isomorphisms, because the conformal-weight filtration induces
exactly the $J$-adic filtration and vice versa.

\emph{Pro-object $\leftrightarrow$ filtered-completed.} Composition of
the above two equivalences gives the pro-to-filtered equivalence, and
the three ambient choices are therefore canonically identified. Under
these identifications, Theorem~\ref{thm:mc5-class-m-chain-level-pro-ambient},
Theorem~\ref{thm:mc5-class-m-topological-chain-level-j-adic}, and
Proposition~\ref{prop:bv-bar-class-m-weight-completed} assert the same
chain-level quasi-isomorphism.

Each implication in \textup{(i)}$\Leftrightarrow$\textup{(ii)}$\Leftrightarrow$\textup{(iii)}
follows from transport along the above functorial equivalences.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The choice of ambient is convention, not restriction]
\label{rem:ambient-convention}
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:ambient-equivalence}} removes any residual
unease about naming conventions. Selecting ``pro-object'',
``$J$-adic topological'', or ``weight-completed'' as the ambient
is a conventional choice among three equivalent presentations of
one underlying mathematical statement. Each is preferred in a different
context:
\begin{itemize}
\item the pro-object form is natural for categorical arguments that
use the model structure on $\mathrm{pro}\text{-}\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$;
\item the $J$-adic topological form is natural for formal-neighbourhood
arguments and for comparison with factorisation-algebra completions;
\item the filtered-completed form is natural for arguments that use
the strong completion-tower infrastructure of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong}}.
\end{itemize}
All three prove the same theorem; all three are equally ``chain-level'';
none is a downgrade.
\end{remark}

\section{Uniqueness of the central degree-2 curvature $m_0$: two
 independent inputs}
\label{sec:mc5-central-m0-uniqueness}

The harmonic discrepancy
$\delta_r^{\mathrm{harm}} = c_r(\cA) \cdot m_0^{\lfloor r/2 \rfloor - 1}$
depends on a choice of central degree-2 curvature element $m_0$. A
prior healing statement routed this uniqueness through a single phrase
\textup{(}``Hodge plus vacuum-proportionality''\textup{)}. This section
separates the two inputs explicitly: a trivial dimension count on the
curve fibre, and a substantive representation-theoretic argument
from translation invariance and vacuum action. The second input is the
load-bearing one; the first is merely a clean-up. Conflating the two
was the textual source of earlier confusion.

\begin{lemma}[Curve-fibre Hodge dimension]
\label{lem:curve-H20-vanishing}
\index{Hodge dimension!curve fibre}
Let $\Sigma$ be a smooth proper curve over a field of characteristic
zero. Then $H^{p,q}(\Sigma) = 0$ whenever $p + q > 1$; in particular
$H^{2,0}(\Sigma) = 0$ by dimension.
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere{}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
A smooth proper curve has complex dimension $1$, so its Hodge
decomposition has only $(0,0), (1,0), (0,1), (1,1)$ pieces. Every
$(p,q)$ with $p + q > 1$ reduces to $(1,1)$ or vanishes; $H^{2,0}$
has $p + q = 2$ with $p = 2 > \dim_\C \Sigma = 1$, hence vanishes.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Sub-argument (a): what the Hodge input kills]
\label{rem:central-m0-sub-a-hodge}
Lemma~\textup{\ref{lem:curve-H20-vanishing}} removes from consideration
any central degree-2 element obtained as the image of a
curve-fibre Hodge class of bidegree $(2,0)$: there are none. This
is a triviality; it excludes a nonexistent class of candidates.
It is \emph{not} the uniqueness mechanism.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Sub-argument (b): vacuum-proportionality uniqueness
 of the central degree-2 curvature]
\label{prop:central-m0-vacuum-proportionality}
\index{central degree-2 curvature!uniqueness|textbf}
\index{vacuum-proportionality!uniqueness of central m\_0}
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on $X$ equipped with a vacuum vector
$|0\rangle \in \cA_0$. Let $m_0^{(1)}, m_0^{(2)} \in
\bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA)$ be two central elements of total bar
length $2$ and cohomological degree $2$, each transforming as a
\emph{scalar} under the chiral translation $T_\cA$-action on
$\bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA)$ \textup{(}equivalently: each lies in
the kernel of the action of the Virasoro mode $L_{-1}$ on the relevant
bar-length-2 piece\textup{)}. Then $m_0^{(1)}$ and $m_0^{(2)}$ are
equal up to bar-coboundary.
\ClaimStatusProvedHere{}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Translation invariance forces every $L_{-1}$-invariant element of
$\bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA)$ of bar-length 2 and cohomological
degree 2 to factor through the vacuum module $\cA_{|0\rangle} \subset
\cA$ generated by $|0\rangle$ under the chiral operations: any
$L_{-1}$-invariant bar insertion of the form
$s^{-1} a \,|\, s^{-1} b$ satisfies
$L_{-1}(a) = L_{-1}(b) = 0$ because $L_{-1}$ is a derivation of the bar
product and acts as a derivation on each slot. The kernel of $L_{-1}$
on $\cA$ is spanned by the vacuum $|0\rangle$ up to
$L_{-1}$-coboundaries: this is the vacuum-axiom of a chiral algebra
\textup{(}Borcherds, Kac\textup{)}, namely $\ker(L_{-1}) = \C\cdot
|0\rangle \oplus \mathrm{im}(L_{-1})$. Thus any $L_{-1}$-invariant
degree-2 bar length-2 element is cohomologous to a scalar multiple of
$s^{-1}|0\rangle \,|\, s^{-1}|0\rangle$.

Writing $m_0^{(i)} = \lambda_i \cdot (s^{-1}|0\rangle | s^{-1}|0\rangle)
+ d_{\mathrm{bar}}(\eta_i)$ for scalars $\lambda_i$ and
$\eta_i \in \bar B^{\mathrm{ch},(g)}(\cA)$, we find
$m_0^{(1)} - m_0^{(2)}$ is $(\lambda_1 - \lambda_2)$ times the vacuum
bar-insertion modulo coboundaries. Centrality then forces
$\lambda_1 = \lambda_2$: pairing both sides against any bar-length-$2$
non-vacuum test element yields zero on $\lambda_i \cdot
(s^{-1}|0\rangle | s^{-1}|0\rangle)$ uniformly in $i$, but any
difference with nonzero $\lambda_1 - \lambda_2$ would pair nontrivially
against the dual vacuum insertion. Hence $m_0^{(1)} = m_0^{(2)}$ up to
bar-coboundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[How (a) and (b) combine in the harmonic discrepancy]
\label{rem:central-m0-sub-a-sub-b-combined}
The harmonic discrepancy
$\delta_r^{\mathrm{harm}} = c_r(\cA) \cdot m_0^{\lfloor r/2 \rfloor - 1}$
is unambiguous once $m_0$ is chosen. Lemma~\textup{\ref{lem:curve-H20-vanishing}}
ensures that there are no competing $H^{2,0}$-valued candidates for
$m_0$ coming from the curve fibre
\textup{(}trivial dimension count\textup{)};
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:central-m0-vacuum-proportionality}} is
the load-bearing step that pins $m_0$ up to bar-coboundary via
translation invariance and the vacuum axiom. Neither input suffices
alone; but the two together determine $m_0$ uniquely and so make the
harmonic discrepancy a well-defined invariant of~$\cA$. Separating
the two inputs resolves the earlier ambiguity in which ``Hodge plus
vacuum-proportionality'' read as a single argument.
\end{remark}

\section{First-principles cache entries}
\label{sec:mc5-cache-entries}

The Platonic reconstitution contributes four new confusion-pattern
entries to the programme's first-principles cache. Each follows the
ghost--error--correct triple template of 
\begin{description}
\item[PR-MC5-1 \textup{(}genuine-false as ambient-choice\textup{)}]
\emph{Ghost.} In the bounded-direct-sum ambient $\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$,
no single contracting homotopy absorbs
$\{\delta_r^{\mathrm{harm}}\}_{r \geq 4}$. \emph{Error.} Calling this
``genuinely false'' suggests a mathematical obstruction. \emph{Correct.}
It is an ambient-choice observation; chain-level MC5 holds in the
pro-ambient / $J$-adic topological / weight-completed ambient, all
equivalent.

\item[PR-MC5-2 \textup{(}ambient equivalence is structural\textup{)}]
\emph{Ghost.} Three different ambient completions (pro-object,
$J$-adic, filtered) seem to give three different chain-level
theorems. \emph{Error.} Treating them as genuinely distinct invites
artificial trade-offs \textup{(}``which ambient is right?''\textup{)}.
\emph{Correct.} They are canonically equivalent under the functorial
identifications of
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:ambient-equivalence}}; the three ambients
are three presentations of one theorem.

\item[PR-MC5-3 \textup{(}tower retention\textup{)}]
\emph{Ghost.} ``The bar complex of $\cA$ is the direct sum
$\bigoplus_h B^{(g)}(\cA)_h$, so its natural ambient is
$\mathrm{Ch}(\mathrm{Vect})$.'' \emph{Error.} This forgets the weight
filtration that was used to construct $\cA$ in the first place.
\emph{Correct.} The natural ambient retains the filtration and is a
pro-object whose limit is the product
$\prod_h B^{(g)}(\cA)_h$; the direct sum sits as a dense subspace.
Retaining the weight filtration is categorical hygiene, not additional
structure.

\item[PR-MC5-4 \textup{(}Milnor--Mittag-Leffler is the chain-level
vehicle\textup{)}]
\emph{Ghost.} Milnor / Mittag-Leffler is a ``technical device'' that
moves statements from stage-level to limit-level. \emph{Error.} Treating
it as technical obscures its structural role. \emph{Correct.} For a
Mittag--Leffler tower of chain-level quasi-isomorphisms, Milnor
upgrades stage-level strictness to strict chain-level quasi-isomorphism
on the inverse limit; this is the mechanism by which the pro-object
statement becomes a chain-level statement on the limit.
\end{description}

\emph{Let $\cA$ be a class-$\mathsf{M}$ chiral algebra in the standard
