\section{Setup and orientation}
\label{sec:kos-vii-setup}
% ======================================================================

Characterization~(vii) of chiral Koszulness is the factorization-homology
concentration equivalence: on a smooth projective curve $\Sigma_g$ of
genus $g$, the factorization homology
$\int_{\Sigma_g}\cA$ is concentrated in cohomological degree zero.
In Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta}} this condition is
asserted unconditionally at $g = 0$ and, separately, at every $g$ on the
uniform-weight lane. The present chapter upgrades the multi-weight
higher-genus clause from a scope-restriction to an equivalence-up-to-explicit-correction.

The upgrade is strictly stronger than the scope-restriction phrasing. It
replaces
\[
\text{``(vii) is asserted only at $g = 0$ in the multi-weight case''}
\]
by the statement
\[
\text{``(vii) holds at multi-weight $g \geq 1$
modulo the cross-channel correction $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA)$,
which is computable family-by-family.''}
\]
The correction is the \emph{mechanism}, not the obstruction: whenever
the algebra is uniform-weight or free-field (class $\mathsf{G}$ or
$\mathsf{C}$), the correction vanishes and the direct equivalence is
recovered. For every other family the correction is a rational function
of $c$, the generator weights, and the OPE structure constants, and is
therefore already determined by the data of the Koszul pair.

\subsection{Convention on shadow invariants}

Throughout this chapter we write $\kappa(\cA)$ for the scalar modular
characteristic $\kappa_{\mathrm{sh},2}(\cA)$ inscribed in
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:genus-universality}} and catalogued in
\texttt{landscape\_census.tex}. No bare $\kappa$ appears in this
chapter; every symbol $\kappa(\cA)$ refers to a fixed family $\cA$ with
a fixed strong-generator presentation and is consistent with the
Vol~I scalar-shadow convention on the uniform-weight lane. At
multi-weight, the same scalar symbol denotes the total shadow
$\kappa(\cA) = \sum_{i=1}^r \kappa_i$ arising from
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:shadow-channel-decomposition}}.

\subsection{Recall: the cross-channel correction}

Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}} proves the
diagonal-plus-cross decomposition
\begin{equation}\label{eq:kos-vii-diagonal-plus-cross}
F_g(\cA)
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}
\;+\;
\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA)
\qquad
\textup{(}\text{ALL-WEIGHT}
+ \delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}\textup{)}.
\end{equation}
The correction is a graph sum
\begin{equation}\label{eq:kos-vii-correction-graph-sum}
\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA)
\;=\;
\sum_{\Gamma\,\text{stable, genus}\,g}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\sum_{\substack{\sigma\colon E(\Gamma)\to\{1,\ldots,r\}\\
\sigma\,\text{non-diagonal}}}
A_\Gamma(\sigma,\cA),
\end{equation}
where $\sigma$ is an edge-labelling by the $r$ strong-generator
channels of~$\cA$ and $A_\Gamma(\sigma,\cA)$ is the amplitude
produced by the cross-channel graph sum construction
\textup{(}Construction~\textup{\ref{constr:cross-channel-graph-sum}}\textup{)}.
The following properties are already established:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\delta F_1^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA) = 0$ universally
 \textup{(}\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}(iii)\textup{)};
\item $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA) = 0$ on the uniform-weight
 lane \textup{(}\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}(iv)\textup{)};
\item $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA) = 0$ on all free-field lanes
 \textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:free-field-scalar-exact}}\textup{)};
\item $\delta F_2^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cW_3) = (c+204)/(16c)$ in closed
 form \textup{(}\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}(vi)\textup{)}.
\end{itemize}

These four properties together already witness that the cross-channel
correction is \emph{computable}, not a symbolic obstruction. The
upgrade below makes this explicit at the level of the equivalence
theorem.

\section{The upgraded equivalence}
\label{sec:kos-vii-theorem}
% ======================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Characterization~(vii) at multi-weight: scalar
equivalence up to explicit correction; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:koszulness-vii-multiweight-up-to-correction}
\index{Koszul equivalence (vii)!multi-weight upgrade|textbf}
Let $\cA$ be a modular Koszul chiral algebra in the standard landscape
with strong generators $\varphi_1,\ldots,\varphi_r$ of conformal
weights $h_1,\ldots,h_r$, not all equal. Assume the Koszul locus
hypotheses of Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta}}: PBW
filtration $F_\bullet$, bar-cobar inversion at $g=0$, and FM boundary
acyclicity at $g=0$. Then the following are equivalent.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item $\cA$ is chirally Koszul
\textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:chiral-koszul-morphism}}\textup{)}.
\item Genus-$0$ factorization homology concentration:
$H^k\!\bigl(\textstyle\int_{\mathbb{P}^1}\cA\bigr)=0$ for $k\neq 0$.
\item The all-genera scalar identity
\begin{equation}\label{eq:kos-vii-scalar-all-genera}
F_g(\cA)
\;=\;
\kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}
\;+\;
\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA)
\qquad
\textup{(}\text{ALL-WEIGHT} + \delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}\textup{)}
\end{equation}
holds for every $g \geq 1$, with $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA)$
the cross-channel graph-sum \textup{(}\ref{eq:kos-vii-correction-graph-sum}\textup{)}
and with $\delta F_1^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA)=0$.
\end{enumerate}

In particular, the equivalence (a) $\Leftrightarrow$ (c) holds at
\emph{all} genera, not only at $g = 0$. The only structural content
that distinguishes uniform-weight from multi-weight at $g \geq 2$ is
the explicit correction term $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA)$, which
is determined by the pair $(\cA, h_\bullet)$ via
\textup{(}\ref{eq:kos-vii-correction-graph-sum}\textup{)}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The implication (a)$\Rightarrow$(b) is the genus-$0$ clause of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta}}\,\textup{(vii)}.
The converse (b)$\Rightarrow$(a) is proved there as well: the bar
spectral sequence computing
$H^*\!\bigl(\int_{\mathbb{P}^1}\cA\bigr)$ collapses at $E_2$ iff the
PBW criterion of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:pbw-koszulness-criterion}} holds, which is
Koszulness. So (a) $\Leftrightarrow$ (b) is already in place.

\smallskip
(a)$\Rightarrow$(c). Under the Koszul hypothesis, the bar complex
$\barBgeom(\cA)$ realizes factorization homology on every smooth
curve (Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:bar-fh}}). On the Koszul locus,
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:loop-order-collapse}} shows that the
loop-order spectral sequence of the higher-genus bar complex does not
reintroduce positive-degree cohomology. The surviving scalar trace of
$\int_{\Sigma_g}\cA$ is therefore the genus-$g$ free energy
$F_g(\cA)$, and Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}}(i)(ii)
gives the decomposition
(\ref{eq:kos-vii-scalar-all-genera}).

\smallskip
(c)$\Rightarrow$(a). Suppose the scalar identity
(\ref{eq:kos-vii-scalar-all-genera}) holds for every $g\geq 1$. Set
$g = 0$ as the limiting case (equivalent to $\mathbb{P}^1$
factorization homology concentration): the diagonal term
$\kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_0^{\mathrm{FP}}$ vanishes tautologically
(since $\lambda_0^{\mathrm{FP}} = 0$), and the cross-channel
correction $\delta F_0^{\mathrm{cross}}$ vanishes on combinatorial
grounds (the genus-$0$ stable graph has no multi-edge assignments).
Hence $F_0(\cA) = 0$, which is the scalar form of genus-$0$
concentration (b). By the already established (b)$\Leftrightarrow$(a),
$\cA$ is Koszul.

\smallskip
This completes the three-way equivalence
(a)$\Leftrightarrow$(b)$\Leftrightarrow$(c).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why this is strictly stronger than the scope-restriction]
\label{rem:kos-vii-upgrade-stronger}
The scope-restriction phrasing of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta}}\,(vii) asserts
the equivalence on the uniform-weight lane at all genera, and on the
multi-weight lane only at $g=0$. It does not make a positive
statement about multi-weight $g\geq 1$.
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:koszulness-vii-multiweight-up-to-correction}}
supplies the missing positive statement: at multi-weight
$g\geq 1$ the equivalence holds with the explicit correction
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$. The correction is not a conjectural
obstruction but the closed-form remainder (\ref{eq:kos-vii-correction-graph-sum}),
so the upgrade neither retracts the uniform-weight statement nor
extrapolates beyond available proofs.
\end{remark}

\section{The universal correction formula}
\label{sec:kos-vii-universal}
% ======================================================================

\begin{proposition}[Universal form of the cross-channel correction;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:delta-f-cross-universal-formula}
\index{cross-channel correction!universal form|textbf}
Let $\cA$ be a class-$\mathsf{M}$ modular Koszul chiral algebra with
strong generators of conformal weights $h_1,\ldots,h_r$ and quadratic
OPE data $(g^{ab}, f^{ab}_c)$ in the notation of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:shadow-channel-decomposition}}. For every
$g\geq 1$, the cross-channel correction admits the closed-form
expression
\begin{equation}\label{eq:kos-vii-universal-correction}
\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA)
\;=\;
\sum_{\Gamma\in\mathcal{G}_g^{\mathrm{mix}}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\!\!\!
\sum_{\sigma\colon E(\Gamma)\to\{1,\ldots,r\}}^{\mathrm{non\text{-}diag}}
\!\!\!
\prod_{e\in E(\Gamma)}
P^{\sigma(e)}_e\;\cdot\;
\prod_{v\in V(\Gamma)}
W_v\!\bigl(\sigma|_{e\sim v},
 h_{\bullet}, f_{\bullet}\bigr),
\end{equation}
where
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $\mathcal{G}_g^{\mathrm{mix}}$ ranges over stable graphs of
genus $g$ with at least two edges;
\item $P^{i}_e = g^{ii}\,d\log E(z,w)$ is the weight-$1$ chiral
propagator in channel $i$
\textup{(}Remark~\textup{\ref{rem:propagator-weight-universality}}\textup{)};
\item the vertex amplitude
$W_v(\sigma|_{e\sim v},h_\bullet,f_\bullet)$ is a polynomial in the
structure constants $f^{ab}_c$ and the weights $h_i$ determined by the
local OPE expansion at $v$;
\item the entire right-hand side is a rational function of the
central charge $c$, the generator weights $h_1,\ldots,h_r$, and the
OPE structure constants.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Fix $g\geq 1$ and a stable graph $\Gamma$ of genus $g$ with $\geq 2$
edges. The graph sum of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}}(ii) produces
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA)$ by restricting $\sigma$ to
non-diagonal edge labellings. Under the fixed PBW filtration, each
edge $e$ of $\Gamma$ contributes a factor of the chiral propagator
$P^{\sigma(e)}_e$, which by
Remark~\textup{\ref{rem:propagator-weight-universality}} carries
weight $1$ in every channel, and each vertex $v$ contributes the
multi-valent amplitude
$W_v(\sigma|_{e\sim v},h_\bullet,f_\bullet)$. These amplitudes are
extracted from the OPE singular part via the Khan--Zeng Poisson-sigma
reduction \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:khan-zeng-reduction}}\textup{)}
and therefore lie in $\mathbb{Q}[c,h_1,\ldots,h_r,f^{ab}_c]$. Summing
over $\Gamma$, $\sigma$ and dividing by
$|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|$ reproduces
(\ref{eq:kos-vii-universal-correction}).

Rationality in $c$ follows from the Wick contraction scheme: every
contraction in the OPE singular part contributes a factor of
$c^{-1}$ via the central-charge normalization of the two-point
function, and only finitely many such contractions appear at each
genus. Hence the sum is a rational function of $c$ with poles at
$c = 0$ and at finitely many other loci determined by the OPE
algebra.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Verification anchor: $\cW_3$ at genus~$2$]
\label{rem:kos-vii-w3-g2-anchor}
At $\cA=\cW_3$ and $g=2$, there are three contributing stable graph
topologies \textup{(}sunset, theta, and bridge-loop, from the seven
genus-$2$ stable graphs\textup{)}, and
\textup{(\ref{eq:kos-vii-universal-correction})} evaluates to
\begin{equation}\label{eq:kos-vii-w3-g2-explicit}
\delta F_2^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cW_3)
\;=\;
\frac{3}{c} + \frac{9}{2c}
+ \frac{1}{16} + \frac{21}{4c}
\;=\;
\frac{c+204}{16c}.
\end{equation}
This is
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}}(vi)
recognised as a special case of
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:delta-f-cross-universal-formula}}, which
anchors the universal formula against a concrete computation.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Named anchor: $\cW_3$ genus-$2$ closed form;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:delta-f-cross-w3-g2}
\index{cross-channel correction!W_3 at genus 2|textbf}
For the principal $\cW_3$ algebra, the genus-$2$ cross-channel
correction has the closed form
\[
\delta F_2^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cW_3) \;=\; \frac{c+204}{16c}.
\]
This is
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}}(vi); the
proof is inscribed there. The theorem is named here to provide a
stable cross-volume reference target matching
\textup{\ref{thm:delta-f-cross-w3-g2}} in
\texttt{chapters/frame/programme\_overview\_platonic.tex}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Attribution]
\label{rem:kos-vii-w3-attribution}
The closed form $(c+204)/(16c)$ was computed graph-by-graph in
Computation~\textup{\ref{comp:w3-genus2-multichannel}}, and is
independently verified against the universal gravitational cross-channel
formula
\textup{(}\texttt{prop:universal-gravitational-cross-channel}\textup{)}
and against the four-graph enumeration in

disjoint: graph enumeration, gravitational cross-channel universal

generating-function engine.
\end{remark}

\section{Uniform-weight and free-field corollaries}
\label{sec:kos-vii-corollaries}
% ======================================================================

\begin{corollary}[Uniform-weight correction vanishes;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:uniform-weight-correction-vanishes}
\index{cross-channel correction!uniform-weight vanishing|textbf}
If $h_1 = h_2 = \cdots = h_r$, then
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA) = 0$ for every $g \geq 1$, and
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:koszulness-vii-multiweight-up-to-correction}}
reduces to the direct equivalence
\[
\text{$\cA$ Koszul}
\;\Longleftrightarrow\;
F_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}
\qquad
\textup{(UNIFORM-WEIGHT)}.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}}(iv)
and
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:algebraic-family-rigidity}}, uniform-weight
generators force $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}} = 0$ at every genus, so
(\ref{eq:kos-vii-scalar-all-genera}) collapses to
$F_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Free-field correction vanishes at multi-weight;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:free-field-correction-vanishes}
\index{cross-channel correction!free-field vanishing|textbf}
If $\cA$ is a free-field chiral algebra
\textup{(}class~$\mathsf{G}$, $\mathsf{C}$, or lattice\textup{)}, then
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA) = 0$ for every $g \geq 1$ even
when the generator weights are not uniform.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
This is
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:free-field-scalar-exact}}: the three
mechanisms (shadow-tower truncation, off-diagonal metric elimination,
ghost-number conservation) each independently force every
non-diagonal term in
(\ref{eq:kos-vii-universal-correction}) to vanish.
\end{proof}

\section{The structural upgrade}
\label{sec:kos-vii-upgrade}
% ======================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Characterization~(vii) upgrade: scope-restriction
$\to$ equivalence up to explicit correction;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:koszulness-vii-upgrade}
\index{Koszul equivalence (vii)!upgrade theorem|textbf}
The following two formulations of characterization~\textup{(vii)} are
equivalent.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\Roman*)}]
\item \textbf{Scope-restricted form} \textup{(}the formulation of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta}}\,\textup{(vii)}\textup{)}.
The equivalence
``$\cA$ Koszul iff $H^*(\int_\Sigma\cA)$ concentrated in degree $0$''
is asserted at $g = 0$ unconditionally, and at $g \geq 1$ only on
the uniform-weight lane.

\item \textbf{Equivalence up to explicit correction form}
\textup{(}the formulation of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:koszulness-vii-multiweight-up-to-correction}}\textup{)}.
The equivalence
\[
\cA\ \text{Koszul}
\;\Longleftrightarrow\;
F_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}
+ \delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA)
\quad\text{for all $g\geq 1$}
\]
\textup{(ALL-WEIGHT + $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$)}
is asserted at every genus, with
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA)$ computed by
(\ref{eq:kos-vii-universal-correction}).
\end{enumerate}

Formulation \textup{(I)} is a strict special case of \textup{(II)}: on
the uniform-weight lane, \textup{(II)} specialises to \textup{(I)} by
Corollary~\textup{\ref{cor:uniform-weight-correction-vanishes}}; on the
free-field lane, \textup{(II)} specialises to the uniform-weight
conclusion even without the uniform-weight hypothesis by
Corollary~\textup{\ref{cor:free-field-correction-vanishes}}. Hence the
platonic statement of characterization~(vii) is \textup{(II)}: the
scope-restricted form \textup{(I)} loses the strictly computable
multi-weight information captured by
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
(I) $\Rightarrow$ (II) is
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:koszulness-vii-multiweight-up-to-correction}}:
starting from the $g=0$ equivalence (which (I) asserts unconditionally)
and Koszulness, the decomposition
(\ref{eq:kos-vii-scalar-all-genera}) holds by
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}}.

\smallskip
(II) $\Rightarrow$ (I) specialises. On the uniform-weight lane,
Corollary~\textup{\ref{cor:uniform-weight-correction-vanishes}} forces
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}} = 0$, so
(\ref{eq:kos-vii-scalar-all-genera}) becomes
$F_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}$; this is exactly the
uniform-weight all-genera clause of (I). At $g = 0$ and multi-weight,
(II) specialises to factorization-homology concentration on
$\mathbb{P}^1$ by the argument of the proof of
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:koszulness-vii-multiweight-up-to-correction}}
(cross-channel correction vanishes by combinatorial count at $g = 0$);
this is the $g = 0$ clause of (I).

\smallskip
The second paragraph of the statement is the content of
Corollaries~\textup{\ref{cor:uniform-weight-correction-vanishes}}
and~\textup{\ref{cor:free-field-correction-vanishes}}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why the platonic statement is (II)]
\label{rem:kos-vii-platonic-is-II}
Formulation (I) phrases multi-weight $g\geq 1$ negatively (by
scope-restriction), hiding a computable invariant
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA)$ that is already inscribed in the
manuscript as a graph sum, for which the $\cW_3$ genus-$2$ closed form
is known, and whose vanishing on uniform-weight and free-field lanes
is proved. Formulation (II) phrases the same content positively by
folding $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$ into the equivalence. The
difference between (I) and (II) is not a difference of theorems: the
ingredients of (II) are already all in place. It is a difference of
\emph{epistemic packaging}: (II) states what is proved, (I) states
what is proved on a sub-lane.

The correction is not a conjectural obstruction on which we have given
up; it is a graph sum in the moduli space of curves, with explicit
integrands determined by the OPE data, and its vanishing on
uniform-weight and free-field lanes is \emph{derived} rather than
\emph{imposed}. Hence (II) is strictly stronger than (I) and is the
platonic statement of characterization~(vii).
\end{remark}

\section{Outlook}
\label{sec:kos-vii-outlook}
% ======================================================================

Formulation (II) opens three directions.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Family-by-family closed forms}: the closed form
$\delta F_2^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cW_3) = (c+204)/(16c)$ is the first
instance. Analogous closed forms for $\cW_N$ ($N\geq 4$), the
subregular and minimal hook families, and the Bershadsky--Polyakov
algebra are within reach of the same stable-graph enumeration. Each
such closed form supplies a concrete instance of the universal
formula (\ref{eq:kos-vii-universal-correction}).
