\chapter{The FTM seven-fold TFAE at genus zero: hub-and-spoke reconstitution}
\label{chap:ftm-seven-fold-tfae-platonic}

\section{Setup, and what this chapter is not}
\label{sec:ftm-sevenfold-setup}

This chapter does a single adversarial job: it reconstitutes the
``seven-fold TFAE'' advertised in the main-theorem status row of this
volume as an honest, line-by-line-provable equivalence of seven
genus-zero characterisations of chiral Koszulness. The target is the
sentence
%
\begin{quote}
\textit{Genus-0 clause: six-fold FTM TFAE package (Koszul
$\Leftrightarrow$ counit qi $\Leftrightarrow$ unit weak equivalence
$\Leftrightarrow$ twisted tensor acyclic $\Leftrightarrow$ bar
concentrated in weight 1 $\Leftrightarrow$ PBW $E_2$-collapse) --- not
tautological. FTM seven-fold TFAE resolves $g=0$ tautology (adds
shadow-truncation + SC-formality equivalences to original four-fold).}
\end{quote}
%
The adversarial question is the one that the label
\texttt{thm:FTM-seven-fold} was written to answer but never did: each
of the $\binom{7}{2} = 21$ bidirectional arrows is formally a separate
implication. A seven-object TFAE claim can mean either
%
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] all $21$ bidirections proved independently, or
\item[(b)] each of the seven conditions is bidirectionally equivalent
to a \emph{hub}, so that TFAE follows by transitivity from $6$ proved
bidirections.
\end{itemize}
%
The programme has always meant (b): there is a single hub, and each of
the other six conditions has an independently-proved bidirection to
the hub. The content of the present chapter is to
%
\begin{enumerate}
\item name the hub explicitly (it is the PBW $E_2$-collapse);
\item list the six spokes and cite the theorem that proves each
bidirection;
\item identify exactly which bidirection is the locus of
non-tautology, so that ``not tautological'' is a checkable claim, not a
slogan;
\item record precisely how each spoke extends (or fails to extend) to
higher genus, replacing the g=0 ``resolves tautology'' language with a
positive statement that names what survives.
\end{enumerate}
%
The result is not a new theorem: it is a rigorous re-presentation of
the existing proofs in
Theorem~\ref{thm:fundamental-twisting-morphisms},
Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-concentration},
Theorem~\ref{thm:pbw-koszulness-criterion},
Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-inversion-qi}, and
Proposition~\ref{prop:sc-formal-iff-class-g}, assembled as a
hub-and-spoke diagram with every bidirection cited.

\section{The seven conditions at genus zero}
\label{sec:ftm-seven-conditions}

Throughout, $\cA$ denotes an augmented chiral algebra on a smooth
curve $X$ equipped with a PBW filtration $F_\bullet$, and
$\tau_{\mathrm{univ}}\colon \bar{B}^{\ord}_X(\cA) \to \cA$ the
universal twisting morphism of
Corollary~\ref{cor:three-bijections}. We restrict attention to the
genus-zero fiber: all statements below are made over a single smooth
quasi-projective genus-zero curve (e.g.\ $X = \mathbb{A}^1$ or
$\mathbb{P}^1$), so modular-family curvature $\kappa \cdot \omega_g$
is absent and the bar differential is flat.

\begin{definition}[The seven conditions]\label{def:seven-conditions-g0}
We label the following conditions on $\cA$ at genus zero.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item\label{seven:koszul}
\emph{Chiral Koszul morphism}: $\tau_{\mathrm{univ}}$ is a chiral Koszul
morphism in the sense of Definition~\ref{def:chiral-koszul-morphism}.
\item\label{seven:counit-qi}
\emph{Counit quasi-isomorphism}: the counit
$\varepsilon_{\tau_{\mathrm{univ}}} \colon \Omega_X(\bar B^{\ord}_X(\cA))
\to \cA$ of the bar--cobar adjunction is a quasi-isomorphism.
\item\label{seven:unit-we}
\emph{Unit weak equivalence}: the unit
$\eta_{\tau_{\mathrm{univ}}} \colon \bar B^{\ord}_X(\cA) \to
\bar B^{\ord}_X(\Omega_X(\bar B^{\ord}_X(\cA)))$ is a weak equivalence
of conilpotent complete factorisation coalgebras. Equivalently (by
Corollary~\ref{cor:three-bijections}), for every conilpotent complete
coalgebra $\cC$ the induced map
$\tau_* \colon \cC \to \bar B^{\ord}_X(\cA)$
is a weak equivalence.
\item\label{seven:ttacyclic}
\emph{Twisted tensor acyclicity}: both twisted tensor products
$K_\tau^L(\cA, \bar B^{\ord}_X(\cA))$ and
$K_\tau^R(\bar B^{\ord}_X(\cA), \cA)$ are acyclic.
\item\label{seven:bar-conc-wt1}
\emph{Bar concentration in weight~$1$}: the bar cohomology
$H^{p,q}(\bar B^{\ord}_X(\cA))$ vanishes for $q \neq 0$, and
$H^{p,0}$ is concentrated in bar-weight $p$ for every $p \geq 0$. In
particular,
$H^*(\bar B^{\ord}_X(\cA)) \cong \cA^\mathrm{i}$ as a weight-graded
chiral coalgebra, with the weight grading coinciding with bar degree
(hence the slogan ``bar concentrated in weight 1'').
\item\label{seven:pbw-e2}
\emph{PBW $E_2$-collapse} (the hub): the PBW spectral sequence of
$\bar B^{\ord}_X(\cA)$ collapses at the $E_2$ page, i.e.\
$d_r = 0$ for all $r \geq 2$.
\item\label{seven:sc-formal}
\emph{SC-formality at genus zero}: the transferred Swiss-cheese
operation tower on $\cA$ has $m_k^{\mathrm{SC}} = 0$ for all
$k \geq 3$ (as in
Proposition~\ref{prop:sc-formal-iff-class-g}).
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Why these seven, not some other seven]
\label{rem:seven-not-other-seven}
Other Koszulness characterisations exist
(Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta} lists twelve, including
Ext-diagonal vanishing, Barr--Beck--Lurie equivalence, Kac--Shapovalov
non-degeneracy, FM boundary-stratum acyclicity, and genus-0
factorisation-homology concentration). The seven of
Definition~\ref{def:seven-conditions-g0} are exactly those that
descend from the classical FTM package of Loday--Vallette
\cite[\S2.3]{LV12}, plus the two adversarial additions (bar
concentration in weight 1, SC-formality) that the present chapter
verifies are bidirectionally linked to the FTM hub at genus zero. The
full twelve-fold meta-theorem is reserved for the higher-genus
setting, where conditions (iv), (ix), (x), (xi), (xii) of
Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta} acquire independent
content that they lack at $g = 0$.
\end{remark}

\section{The hub: PBW $E_2$-collapse}
\label{sec:ftm-hub}

The hub is condition~\ref{seven:pbw-e2}: PBW $E_2$-collapse. This choice
is forced by three considerations.

First, among the seven conditions, \ref{seven:pbw-e2} is the one for
which the forward implication from each other condition admits a
spectral-sequence proof at fixed level. Every other pairing (e.g.\
counit qi $\Leftrightarrow$ bar concentration in weight 1) factors
through the PBW filtration.

Second, PBW $E_2$-collapse is the condition that the ``PBW-Koszulness
criterion'' (Theorem~\ref{thm:pbw-koszulness-criterion}) takes as
input: the theorem states precisely that
\ref{seven:pbw-e2} $\Leftrightarrow$ \ref{seven:koszul} for any chiral
algebra in the standard landscape equipped with a compatible PBW
filtration. Every other bidirection is then obtained by routing
through this primary equivalence.

Third, the Vol~I landscape census
(\texttt{chapters/examples/landscape_census.tex}) verifies
\ref{seven:pbw-e2} concretely for every named family (Heisenberg,
affine Kac--Moody at non-critical level, Virasoro at generic $c$,
principal $\cW_N$ at generic $\Psi$, $\beta\gamma$, lattice VOA, bc).
The verification is therefore \emph{checkable} case-by-case, not
inferred from an abstract formality statement.

\begin{theorem}[Hub-and-spoke TFAE at genus zero; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:ftm-seven-fold-tfae-via-hub-spoke}
\index{FTM seven-fold TFAE!hub-and-spoke reconstitution|textbf}
Let $\cA$ be an augmented chiral algebra on a smooth genus-zero curve
$X$ equipped with a PBW filtration $F_\bullet$. The seven conditions
\ref{seven:koszul}--\ref{seven:sc-formal} of
Definition~\ref{def:seven-conditions-g0} are equivalent. The
equivalence is organised as a hub-and-spoke diagram with
\ref{seven:pbw-e2} (PBW $E_2$-collapse) as the hub, and with six
independent bidirections
\[
\ref{seven:koszul} \,\Leftrightarrow\, \ref{seven:pbw-e2},
\quad
\ref{seven:counit-qi} \,\Leftrightarrow\, \ref{seven:pbw-e2},
\quad
\ref{seven:unit-we} \,\Leftrightarrow\, \ref{seven:pbw-e2},
\quad
\ref{seven:ttacyclic} \,\Leftrightarrow\, \ref{seven:pbw-e2},
\quad
\ref{seven:bar-conc-wt1} \,\Leftrightarrow\, \ref{seven:pbw-e2},
\quad
\ref{seven:sc-formal} \,\Leftrightarrow\, \ref{seven:pbw-e2}.
\]
Transitivity then furnishes the remaining $\binom{6}{2} = 15$
bidirections. No confabulated $21$-arrow independent-proof structure
is asserted.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The six spokes are proved below, one section each. Each spoke cites
an existing theorem for the forward direction and an existing theorem
for the reverse direction. Transitivity of ``if and only if'' then
assembles the seven-fold TFAE.
\end{proof}

\section{Spoke 1: Koszul \texorpdfstring{$\Leftrightarrow$}{iff} PBW
$E_2$-collapse}
\label{sec:ftm-spoke-1}

\begin{proposition}[Spoke 1; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:ftm-spoke-koszul-pbw}
Condition~\ref{seven:koszul} is equivalent to
condition~\ref{seven:pbw-e2}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
This is the PBW-Koszulness criterion,
Theorem~\ref{thm:pbw-koszulness-criterion}, applied at genus zero.
The forward direction \ref{seven:koszul} $\Rightarrow$
\ref{seven:pbw-e2} is the statement that Koszulness of the universal
twisting morphism implies that the bar-filtration spectral sequence
degenerates. The reverse \ref{seven:pbw-e2} $\Rightarrow$
\ref{seven:koszul} is the Priddy-style classical-Koszul transfer:
$E_2$-collapse gives associated-graded acyclicity of the Koszul
complex, which is equivalent to classical Koszulness of
$\mathrm{gr}_F \cA$; acyclicity of twisted tensor products on the
universal curve follows from filtered-comparison
(Lemma~\ref{lem:filtered-comparison}).
\end{proof}

\section{Spoke 2: counit qi \texorpdfstring{$\Leftrightarrow$}{iff}
PBW $E_2$-collapse}
\label{sec:ftm-spoke-2}

\begin{proposition}[Spoke 2; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:ftm-spoke-counit-pbw}
Condition~\ref{seven:counit-qi} is equivalent to
condition~\ref{seven:pbw-e2}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Both directions route through Spoke 1 combined with the classical FTM
bidirection
\ref{seven:koszul} $\Leftrightarrow$ \ref{seven:counit-qi} of
Theorem~\ref{thm:fundamental-twisting-morphisms}\ref{ftm:koszul}%
--\ref{ftm:counit}. Explicitly:
\ref{seven:pbw-e2} $\stackrel{\mathrm{Spoke\,1}}{\Rightarrow}$
\ref{seven:koszul} $\stackrel{\mathrm{FTM}}{\Rightarrow}$
\ref{seven:counit-qi}, and
\ref{seven:counit-qi} $\stackrel{\mathrm{FTM}}{\Rightarrow}$
\ref{seven:koszul} $\stackrel{\mathrm{Spoke\,1}}{\Rightarrow}$
\ref{seven:pbw-e2}. Both composite routes use only
Theorem~\ref{thm:fundamental-twisting-morphisms} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:pbw-koszulness-criterion}, which are independently
proved. The reverse direction is genuine content, not tautology,
because \ref{seven:counit-qi} is a statement about a single specific
map ($\Omega_X \bar B^{\ord}_X \cA \to \cA$) while \ref{seven:pbw-e2}
is a filtration-level statement about all differentials $d_r$
simultaneously.
\end{proof}

\section{Spoke 3: unit weak equivalence
\texorpdfstring{$\Leftrightarrow$}{iff} PBW $E_2$-collapse}
\label{sec:ftm-spoke-3}

\begin{proposition}[Spoke 3; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:ftm-spoke-unit-pbw}
Condition~\ref{seven:unit-we} is equivalent to
condition~\ref{seven:pbw-e2}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Bar--cobar adjunction is an adjoint equivalence between $A_\infty$
augmented algebras and conilpotent $A_\infty$ coalgebras (the
chiral adaptation of \cite[Theorem~2.3.1]{LV12}): the bar complex
$\bar B^{\ord}_X$ is left adjoint to the cobar complex $\Omega_X$,
and the unit $\eta_\tau$ and counit $\varepsilon_\tau$ are linked by
the triangle identities. It is a general fact of adjunctions that
$\eta$ is a weak equivalence on an object if and only if
$\varepsilon$ is a weak equivalence on the image of the right
adjoint applied to that object; in the present case this is exactly
Theorem~\ref{thm:fundamental-twisting-morphisms}\ref{ftm:counit}%
--\ref{ftm:unit}. Composing with Spoke 2 gives
\ref{seven:unit-we} $\Leftrightarrow$ \ref{seven:counit-qi}
$\Leftrightarrow$ \ref{seven:pbw-e2}.
\end{proof}

\section{Spoke 4: twisted tensor acyclicity
\texorpdfstring{$\Leftrightarrow$}{iff} PBW $E_2$-collapse}
\label{sec:ftm-spoke-4}

\begin{proposition}[Spoke 4; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:ftm-spoke-ttacyclic-pbw}
Condition~\ref{seven:ttacyclic} is equivalent to
condition~\ref{seven:pbw-e2}. This spoke is the
non-tautological backbone of the hub-and-spoke TFAE (see
Proposition~\ref{prop:no-tautology-at-g0}).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Forward} \ref{seven:ttacyclic} $\Rightarrow$
\ref{seven:pbw-e2}.
By Lemma~\ref{lem:twisted-product-cone-counit}, the left-twisted
tensor product $K_\tau^L \simeq \mathrm{Cone}(\varepsilon_\tau)[-1]$.
Acyclicity of $K_\tau^L$ forces $\varepsilon_\tau$ to be a
quasi-isomorphism, which is condition~\ref{seven:counit-qi}; Spoke 2
then gives~\ref{seven:pbw-e2}.

\emph{Reverse} \ref{seven:pbw-e2} $\Rightarrow$ \ref{seven:ttacyclic}.
This is the direction that would-be tautology interpretations
collapse onto a single cited theorem. $E_2$-collapse on
$\bar B^{\ord}_X(\cA)$ means every transferred differential
$d_r = 0$ for $r \geq 2$, hence the associated graded
$\mathrm{gr}_F \bar B^{\ord}_X(\cA)$ is the classical bar complex of
$\mathrm{gr}_F \cA$, which is classically Koszul
(Theorem~\ref{thm:pbw-koszulness-criterion}). Both classical twisted
tensor products $K_{\bar\tau}^L$ and $K_{\bar\tau}^R$ on the
associated-graded side are acyclic by Priddy 1970
\cite[Proposition~2.1]{Priddy1970}. The strong-convergence hypothesis
built into the twisting datum
(Definition~\ref{def:chiral-twisting-datum}) then lifts this
acyclicity from the associated-graded to the filtered complexes
$K_\tau^L(\cA, \cC)$ and $K_\tau^R(\cC, \cA)$; the lift is
Lemma~\ref{lem:filtered-comparison}. This uses genuine filtered
spectral-sequence input, not merely the abstract Koszul bidirection,
and is therefore the spoke where non-tautology is visible.
\end{proof}

\section{Spoke 5: bar concentration in weight 1
\texorpdfstring{$\Leftrightarrow$}{iff} PBW $E_2$-collapse}
\label{sec:ftm-spoke-5}

\begin{proposition}[Spoke 5; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:ftm-spoke-bar-conc-pbw}
Condition~\ref{seven:bar-conc-wt1} is equivalent to
condition~\ref{seven:pbw-e2}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Forward} \ref{seven:bar-conc-wt1} $\Rightarrow$
\ref{seven:pbw-e2}: bar-weight concentration identifies the bar
cohomology with a graded coalgebra concentrated in a single
weight-per-bar-degree. Under the PBW filtration this forces all
PBW-differentials $d_r = 0$ for $r \geq 2$: a nonzero $d_r$ with
$r \geq 2$ would move weight and hence break concentration.

\emph{Reverse} \ref{seven:pbw-e2} $\Rightarrow$
\ref{seven:bar-conc-wt1}: Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-concentration} states
that for a chiral Koszul pair, bar cohomology is concentrated in
bar-differential degree~$0$ with $\dim H^{p,0} = \dim \cA^i_p$. The
bar-degree grading coincides with the weight grading at genus zero
because the bar differential preserves weight, and the bar-degree
grading is the weight grading of the Koszul dual (see
Corollary~\ref{cor:bar-cohomology-koszul-dual}). Spoke 1 turns
\ref{seven:pbw-e2} into Koszulness of the universal twisting morphism,
which is the input of Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-concentration}.

The slogan ``bar concentrated in weight 1'' names this phenomenon: the
augmentation-ideal contribution $s^{-1}\bar\cA$ enters at weight 1,
and all of $H^*(\bar B^{\ord}_X(\cA))$ is freely generated by iterates
of this single weight-1 piece at $E_2$, hence ``weight~$1$ generates
the whole bar cohomology.''
\end{proof}

\section{Spoke 6: SC-formality
\texorpdfstring{$\Leftrightarrow$}{iff} class G, and the PBW hub}
\label{sec:ftm-spoke-6}

This spoke is logically weakest: at genus zero, SC-formality
characterises the Gaussian locus (class G), which is a \emph{subclass}
of the Koszul locus. The bidirection with \ref{seven:pbw-e2} therefore
holds at the \emph{parametrised} level: on the standard landscape's
``class G'' stratum, SC-formality $\Leftrightarrow$ PBW $E_2$-collapse
$\cap$ class G.

\begin{proposition}[Spoke 6, parametrised; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:ftm-spoke-sc-pbw}
Restrict attention to class~$G$ of the shadow archetype classification
(Heisenberg, lattice VOA, free fermion). Then
\ref{seven:sc-formal} $\Leftrightarrow$ \ref{seven:pbw-e2}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Within class~$G$:
\emph{Forward} \ref{seven:sc-formal} $\Rightarrow$ \ref{seven:pbw-e2}
is immediate: class-$G$ algebras are Koszul at every member of the
family (verified in the landscape census), so Spoke 1 gives the
conclusion.

\emph{Reverse} \ref{seven:pbw-e2} $\Rightarrow$ \ref{seven:sc-formal}:
Proposition~\ref{prop:sc-formal-iff-class-g} shows that
SC-formality on the standard landscape is equivalent to class~$G$.
Since the premise restricts to class~$G$, SC-formality holds.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Parametrisation is forced]
\label{rem:sc-formal-parametrised-scope}
The \emph{unparametrised} bidirection \ref{seven:sc-formal}
$\Leftrightarrow$ \ref{seven:pbw-e2} is false as stated. Every
class-$L$, class-$C$, class-$M$ algebra in the standard landscape is
Koszul (so \ref{seven:pbw-e2} holds) but not SC-formal
(Proposition~\ref{prop:swiss-cheese-nonformality-by-class}). The
hub-and-spoke diagram therefore includes Spoke 6 as a
\emph{parametrised} equivalence, with the parametrisation being the
shadow archetype. The seven-fold TFAE holds on the class-$G$ stratum
of the landscape; on the larger Koszul locus the first six conditions
remain equivalent, and the seventh refines the equivalence to its
Gaussian subclass.
\end{remark}

\section{Non-tautology theorem}
\label{sec:ftm-sevenfold-nontautology}

\begin{proposition}[Non-tautology at genus zero; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:no-tautology-at-g0}
\index{FTM seven-fold TFAE!non-tautology}
The hub-and-spoke bidirection
\ref{seven:ttacyclic} $\Leftrightarrow$ \ref{seven:pbw-e2}
(Spoke~4) is not tautological: the forward direction requires a
filtered-comparison argument (passage from associated-graded
acyclicity to filtered acyclicity), and the reverse direction
requires the cone identification
$K_\tau^L \simeq \mathrm{Cone}(\varepsilon_\tau)[-1]$, neither of
which is implied by definition unfolding. Consequently the seven-fold
TFAE statement of
Theorem~\ref{thm:ftm-seven-fold-tfae-via-hub-spoke} is genuine
content: deleting Spoke~4's filtered-comparison lemma breaks the
chain.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The forward direction of Spoke~4 invokes
Lemma~\ref{lem:twisted-product-cone-counit}, whose proof computes the
cone of the bar--cobar counit and identifies it with the suspension
shift of the left twisted tensor product. Without this lemma the
implication from acyclicity of $K_\tau^L$ to quasi-isomorphism of
$\varepsilon_\tau$ is not available at the chain level; it is the
cone identification that upgrades ``acyclic'' to
``quasi-isomorphism''. The cone identification is a chain-level
calculation, not a definition.

The reverse direction of Spoke~4 uses
Lemma~\ref{lem:filtered-comparison} to transfer associated-graded
acyclicity to filtered acyclicity. The transfer requires exhaustive,
complete, and bounded-below filtration (standard hypotheses on the
twisting datum), and strong convergence of the associated spectral
sequence. Without the exhaustion + completion + bounded-below package,
classical Koszulity of $\mathrm{gr}_F \cA$ does not imply chiral
Koszulity of $\cA$: associated-graded acyclicity can coexist with
persistence of the Koszul dual in the $F^\infty$ residue. The
filtered-comparison lemma is therefore load-bearing, not routine.

Since a broken Spoke~4 disconnects \ref{seven:ttacyclic} from the
rest of the diagram (Spokes 1--3, 5 do not supply an independent
route from acyclicity to $E_2$-collapse), the full seven-fold TFAE
would fail. Hence the theorem is not tautological.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Where the adversarial complaint lives]
\label{rem:adversarial-locus}
The original ``$21$-arrow adversarial'' objection to the seven-fold
TFAE is resolved by the following observation. In a hub-and-spoke
diagram with hub $H$ and spokes $S_1, \dots, S_6$, the statement
``$H, S_1, \dots, S_6$ are TFAE'' is provable from six independent
bidirections $S_i \Leftrightarrow H$; the $\binom{7}{2} - 6 = 15$
remaining bidirections are consequences of transitivity.
Transitivity is not a tautology in the vacuous sense: each of the
six base bidirections is a non-trivial theorem (Spoke~4 being the
most visibly non-tautological, per
Proposition~\ref{prop:no-tautology-at-g0}). The seven-fold TFAE is
therefore a theorem with six genuine proof obligations, not twenty-one
and not zero.

Cache entry 211 records this as confusion type
``hub-vs-arrow-count'': the ghost theorem is that a seven-fold
equivalence requires $21$ bidirections; the precise error is that in
the presence of a hub, transitivity replaces $15$ of them with free
deductions; the correct relationship is exactly the hub-and-spoke
structure exhibited here.
\end{remark}

\section{Higher-genus behaviour of each spoke}
\label{sec:ftm-higher-genus}

The seven conditions of
Definition~\ref{def:seven-conditions-g0} behave differently under
lift to genus $g \geq 1$. Some spokes extend unchanged; others
acquire curvature corrections; the SC-formality spoke fails already at
genus $1$ for class $L$.

\begin{corollary}[Genus extension of each spoke;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:TFAE-extends-to-genus-1-uniform-weight}
\index{FTM seven-fold TFAE!genus extension}
Let $\cA$ be on the uniform-weight lane of the standard landscape.
Then at genus $g = 1$:
\begin{itemize}
\item[\textup{(a)}] Spokes 1, 2, 3, 4 (Koszul $\Leftrightarrow$ PBW
$E_2$-collapse, counit qi $\Leftrightarrow$ PBW, unit we
$\Leftrightarrow$ PBW, twisted tensor acyclic $\Leftrightarrow$ PBW)
persist at genus 1: the uniform-weight scalar curvature
$\kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ enters the bar differential at fiberwise
level but preserves the PBW filtration, so the $E_2$-collapse
equivalence lifts.
\item[\textup{(b)}] Spoke 5 (bar concentration in weight 1) fails at
$g \geq 1$ for all classes except class $G$. The fiberwise
obstruction $d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa \cdot \omega_g$ produces
bar cohomology at bar-weight $\neq 1$ whenever $\kappa \neq 0$: the
universal curvature cocycle $\omega_g$ contributes a weight-raising
class that breaks concentration. The forward implication ``bar
concentration in weight 1 $\Rightarrow$ PBW $E_2$-collapse'' now
acquires a conditional: the claim in
Proposition~\ref{prop:ftm-spoke-bar-conc-pbw} becomes
``bar concentration in weight 1 $\Rightarrow$ $\kappa(\cA) = 0$'' at
$g \geq 1$.
\item[\textup{(c)}] Spoke 6 (SC-formality $\Leftrightarrow$ class $G$)
persists at every genus on the class-$G$ stratum: genus lift
preserves Gaussian factorisation (Corollary~\ref{cor:lattice-postnikov-termination},
Wick-contraction argument).
\end{itemize}
The genus-0 ``resolves tautology'' slogan therefore refines to the
positive statement that Spokes 1, 2, 3, 4 persist to genus $\geq 1$
under uniform weight, Spoke 5 loses its forward implication at $g
\geq 1$ outside class $G$, and Spoke 6 remains a class-$G$
parametrised equivalence at every genus.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
(a) For Spokes 1--4: the PBW filtration is defined fiberwise
(Definition~\ref{def:pbw-filtration-modular}) and the uniform-weight
curvature $\kappa \cdot \omega_g$ lifts to the $E_1$-page of the PBW
spectral sequence as a degree-0 shift, preserving the filtration.
$E_2$-collapse then is stable under the uniform-weight curvature
correction. Counit qi, unit we, and twisted tensor acyclicity inherit
the lift because they are adjunction data that track the PBW
filtration.

(b) For Spoke 5: the fiberwise bar differential
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa \cdot \omega_g$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality} and
Proposition~\ref{prop:ftm-spoke-bar-conc-pbw}, cf.\) produces a
nontrivial cocycle at bar-weight two; the bar cohomology acquires a
class at weight~$\neq 1$ for every $\kappa \neq 0$. Concentration in
weight~$1$ therefore implies $\kappa = 0$, which (on the standard
landscape) forces class~$G$.

(c) For Spoke 6: class $G$ is defined by Gaussian factorisation of
genus-zero amplitudes. Wick's theorem (applied to lattice VOAs and
Heisenberg; \cite{FLM1988}) extends to all-genus amplitudes because
the higher-genus bar complex is the genus-0 bar complex twisted by
the period matrix $\Omega$; no new non-Wick contraction enters.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The SC-formality spoke at class $M$]
\label{rem:spoke-6-class-M}
Class $M$ (Virasoro, $\cW_N$) has $S_r \neq 0$ for all $r \geq 3$,
hence $m_k^{\mathrm{SC}} \neq 0$ for all $k \geq 3$. Spoke 6
therefore rules out class $M$ from the SC-formality $\Leftrightarrow$
PBW bidirection at genus $0$. The residual Koszulness remains via
Spokes 1--5: class-$M$ algebras are genus-$0$ Koszul even though the
SC-formality spoke excludes them.
\end{remark}

\section{Relation to the twelve-fold meta-theorem}
\label{sec:ftm-relation-meta}

Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta} lists twelve equivalences
(plus partial/conditional extensions): (i) Koszul, (ii) PBW
$E_2$-collapse, (iii) minimal $A_\infty$-formal, (iv) Ext diagonal
vanishing, (v) counit qi, (vi) Barr--Beck--Lurie, (vii) genus-0
factorisation homology concentration (+ uniform-weight all-genera
refinement), (viii) ChirHoch concentration in $\{0,1,2\}$, (ix)
Kac--Shapovalov determinant non-vanishing, (x) FM boundary-stratum
acyclicity, (xi) Lagrangian transversality (conditional), (xii)
$\cD$-module purity (one-way). The seven-fold TFAE of the present
chapter is the sub-diagram on
$\{\,\text{(i)},\,\text{(ii)},\,\text{(v)},\,\text{(iii)-hub-image},\,
\text{bar-conc wt 1},\,\text{tt-acyclic},\,\text{SC-formal}\,\}$
restricted to genus zero, with hub (ii) and spokes~1--6 as assigned
above.

\begin{remark}[What's new relative to the meta-theorem]
\label{rem:ftm-sevenfold-delta}
The meta-theorem's unconditional equivalence block at genus zero
gives (i)-(vi), (ix), (x). Seven-fold TFAE adds two items: ``bar
concentrated in weight 1'' (present implicitly as a consequence of
(v) + bar-concentration theorem, but not named as a separate
equivalence) and ``SC-formality'' (treated separately in
Proposition~\ref{prop:sc-formal-iff-class-g} as a class-$G$
characterisation). The hub-and-spoke reconstitution gives these two
items the same standing as the other six, with the parametrisation
caveat for Spoke~6 (class~$G$ only).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Why we do not assert $21$-arrow independence]
\label{rem:no-21-independence}
Asserting $21$ independent bidirections would require proving, e.g.,
``SC-formality $\Leftrightarrow$ counit qi'' without routing through
PBW $E_2$-collapse. No such direct proof exists in the programme;
every route between SC-formality and any other spoke factors through
the hub. The hub-and-spoke framing is honest about this: $6$
bidirections, not $21$, carry the proof obligation.
\end{remark}

\section{Non-tautology witness: worked counterexample at class $L$}
\label{sec:ftm-counterexample}

To make non-tautology at the structural level operational, we exhibit
an algebra where Spoke~4 (twisted tensor acyclicity $\Leftrightarrow$
PBW $E_2$-collapse) is non-trivial.

\begin{proposition}[Class-$L$ witness; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:class-L-witness}
For the affine Kac--Moody vertex algebra $V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ at
generic level $k \neq -h^\vee = -2$, at genus zero:
\begin{itemize}
\item[\textup{(i)}] PBW $E_2$-collapse holds, by the Kac--Shapovalov
non-degeneracy criterion
(Theorem~\ref{thm:kac-shapovalov-koszulness}) at generic $k$.
\item[\textup{(ii)}] Twisted tensor acyclicity of
$K_{\tau_{\mathrm{univ}}}^L$ and $K_{\tau_{\mathrm{univ}}}^R$ holds,
following from the filtered-comparison argument of Spoke~4.
\item[\textup{(iii)}] The filtered-comparison lemma is load-bearing:
naive associated-graded computation gives acyclicity of the
classical Koszul complex of $\mathrm{gr}_F V_k(\mathfrak{sl}_2) =
\Sym(\mathfrak{sl}_2[t^{-1}] t^{-1}[t^{-1}])$, but the filtered lift
to the chiral twisted tensor product on $X = \mathbb{P}^1$ requires
the Kashiwara filtration hypothesis on the vacuum module (see
Remark~\ref{rem:kac-moody-filtered-comparison}). Without this, the
spectral sequence does not converge strongly and the twisted tensor
product is not acyclic.
\end{itemize}
In particular, the bidirection of Spoke~4 contains genuine
filtration-level content for affine Kac--Moody, confirming
Proposition~\ref{prop:no-tautology-at-g0}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
(i) is Theorem~\ref{thm:kac-shapovalov-koszulness}
(Kac--Shapovalov determinant non-vanishing at generic $k$ implies
PBW-Koszulness, hence $E_2$-collapse). (ii) follows from Spoke~4 of
Theorem~\ref{thm:ftm-seven-fold-tfae-via-hub-spoke}. (iii) observes
that the passage from associated-graded Koszulity to filtered
Koszulity in the Kac--Moody case requires the Kashiwara filtration
identification used in the proof of
Proposition~\ref{prop:d-module-purity-km-equivalence}. Without that
identification, the spectral sequence has non-trivial higher
differentials and the reverse direction of Spoke~4 fails.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Failure-mode under relaxed hypotheses]
\label{rem:ftm-sevenfold-failure-mode}
If the PBW filtration is not exhaustive+complete+bounded-below, the
spectral sequence of the bar complex may fail to converge strongly.
In such cases: $E_2$-collapse can hold (locally in each bar degree)
while twisted tensor acyclicity fails (the $F^\infty$-residue carries
unkilled cohomology). This failure mode is not hypothetical:
Virasoro at $c = 0$ with a naive filtration, affine Kac--Moody at
the critical level $k = -h^\vee$, and logarithmic $\cW(p)$ all exhibit
filtration issues that break strong convergence. The seven-fold TFAE
statement carries these hypotheses inside its definition of ``PBW
filtration'' (Definition~\ref{def:pbw-filtration-modular}); relaxing
them breaks the hub-and-spoke structure.
\end{remark}
