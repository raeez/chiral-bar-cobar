\subsection{When does filtering degenerate to curved?}

The homotopy upgrade sharpens a question that arises as soon as one
leaves the quadratic regime: given a chiral algebra with an exhaustive
filtration, when is the filtered presentation merely a bookkeeping
device for a curved model, and when does the filtration carry
genuinely new algebraic information?  The answer sorts the standard
landscape into three regimes (quadratic, curved, genuinely
filtered), and each regime determines which completions, convergence
theorems, and shadow-tower truncations are needed.

\emph{Strictness as shadow}: a strict filtration that collapses to a
curved model (Virasoro) is a shadow of a single Maurer--Cartan element
$m_0$ in the completed convolution algebra; a genuinely filtered
algebra ($\mathcal{W}_3$, $\mathcal{W}_N$) cannot be captured by a
finite curvature datum but requires the full completed filtered bar
complex.
\emph{Examples as proving grounds}: the Heisenberg, Virasoro, and
$\mathcal{W}_3$ algebras are the three calibration points.  The first
is quadratic and requires no completion; the second is curved (one
generator, one curvature scalar $m_0 = c \cdot \mathbf{1}$); the
third is genuinely filtered (new composites such as ${:}LL{:}$ appear
at arbitrarily high weight).
\emph{Close the loop}: the regime classification feeds directly into
the convergence criterion
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-convergence}) and determines which
shadow-archetype (Gaussian, contact, mixed) applies to each family
in Part~II.

This subsection does not repeat the full filtered/cooperadic analysis of
Section~\ref{sec:filtered-vs-curved-comprehensive}.  Its role inside the
quantum-correction chapter is narrower: extract the algebra-side input used by
Theorem~\ref{thm:filtered-to-curved} and fix the three calibration regimes
that reappear throughout Part~II.

\begin{proposition}[Filtered \texorpdfstring{$\Rightarrow$}{=>} curved; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]\label{prop:filtered-to-curved-fc}
\index{filtered!vs curved}
\index{curved Ainfinity@curved $A_\infty$!filtered comparison}
Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a filtered chiral algebra with an
\emph{exhaustive, separated, complete} filtration
$\{F_k\mathcal{A}\}_{k \geq 0}$.  Here:
\emph{exhaustive} means
$\mathcal{A} = \bigcup_k F_k\mathcal{A}$;
\emph{separated} means
$\bigcap_k F_k\mathcal{A} = 0$;
\emph{complete} means the natural map to the inverse
limit
$\mathcal{A} \xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}
  \varprojlim_k \mathcal{A}/F_k\mathcal{A}$
is an isomorphism (equivalently, every Cauchy sequence in
the filtration topology converges).  Assume:
\begin{enumerate}
\item each filtration quotient is finite-dimensional:
\[
\dim(F_k\mathcal{A}/F_{k-1}\mathcal{A}) < \infty \qquad \text{for all } k;
\]
\item the associated graded algebra $\gr(\mathcal{A})$ is generated by
  $\gr^1(\mathcal{A})$ with quadratic defining ideal;
\item every higher relation in $\mathcal{A}$ differs from a quadratic
  consequence by a central filtration-$\ge 2$ correction.
\end{enumerate}
Then there exists a curved presentation of $\mathcal{A}$ with curvature element
\[
\mu_0 \in F_2\mathcal{A}
\]
such that $\mathcal{A}$ and this curved model are filtered quasi-isomorphic.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Write $V := \gr^1(\mathcal{A})$ and choose splittings
$s_k \colon \gr^k(\mathcal{A}) \to F_k\mathcal{A}$.
The \emph{completed free chiral algebra}
$\widehat{T}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)
:= \varprojlim_N T_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)/F_N$
is the inverse limit of the free chiral algebra
$T_{\mathrm{ch}}(V) = \bigoplus_{n \geq 0} V^{\otimes n}$
along its natural filtration by tensor degree; it exists
as a complete filtered algebra and satisfies the universal
property that any filtered algebra map $V \to \cA$ with
$\cA$ complete extends uniquely.  Completeness and
separatedness of~$\cA$ then produce a filtered surjection
\[
\pi \colon \widehat{T}_{\mathrm{ch}}(V)\twoheadrightarrow \mathcal{A}.
\]
Let $I = \ker(\pi)$.  Its quadratic part is determined by the defining
ideal of $\gr(\mathcal{A})$; denote it by $I_2$.

Assumption~(3) says that every higher relation class in $I$ is a quadratic
consequence modulo a central correction of filtration at least~$2$.  Collect
these correction terms by filtration degree:
\[
\mu_0 = \sum_{k \ge 2}\mu_0^{(k)}, \qquad
\mu_0^{(k)} \in F_k\mathcal{A}/F_{k-1}\mathcal{A}.
\]
The sum converges in~$\cA$ because $\cA$ is complete:
each partial sum $\sum_{k=2}^{N} \mu_0^{(k)}$ lies
in~$F_2\cA$, and the sequence is Cauchy in the filtration
topology (the $k$-th term has filtration $\geq k$), so
the limit exists in $F_2\cA$ by completeness.  Thus
$\mu_0 \in F_2\cA$ is well-defined.

Twist the differential of the quadratic model by~$\mu_0$:
define $d_{\mu_0}(a) := d(a) + [\mu_0, a]$.  The curved
Maurer--Cartan identity
\[
d_{\mu_0}^2(a) = [\mu_0, a]_\mu
\]
holds because Assumption~(3) ensures $\mu_0$ is
\emph{central} in the associated graded: $[\mu_0, a]
\in F_{|a|+2}\cA$ for all~$a$, so the commutator
$[\mu_0, -]$ acts by a filtration-$2$ shift.  Then
\begin{align*}
d_{\mu_0}^2(a) &= d(d(a) + [\mu_0, a]) + [\mu_0, d(a) + [\mu_0, a]] \\
  &= \underbrace{d^2(a)}_{=0} + d[\mu_0, a]
  + [\mu_0, d(a)] + [\mu_0, [\mu_0, a]] \\
  &= [d(\mu_0), a] + [\mu_0, [\mu_0, a]]
  = [\mu_0, a]_\mu
\end{align*}
where the last step uses $d(\mu_0) = 0$ (the correction
terms are cocycles by construction) and the graded Jacobi
identity $[\mu_0,[\mu_0,a]] = \tfrac{1}{2}[[\mu_0,\mu_0],a]$
which equals $[\mu_0, a]_\mu$ by the MC equation
$d(\mu_0) + \tfrac{1}{2}[\mu_0,\mu_0] = 0$.
Quotienting by the closed ideal generated by $I_2$ yields a curved model
$\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{curv}}$ together with a filtered morphism
\[
\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{curv}} \to \mathcal{A}
\]
that is an isomorphism on associated graded pieces.  Hence it is a filtered
quasi-isomorphism.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Dependencies]
This proposition is the algebra-side input for
Theorem~\ref{thm:filtered-to-curved}.  The required convergence control comes
from Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-convergence} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:conilpotency-convergence}; Appendix~\ref{app:nilpotent-completion}
supplies the completion technology in the genuinely filtered regime.
\end{remark}

\begin{example}[Virasoro: filtering is auxiliary]\label{ex:vir-filtered-to-curved-fc}
For the Virasoro algebra, the conformal-weight filtration records descendants
of a single field $T$.  The weight-$4$ quasi-primary
\[
\Lambda = {:}TT{:} - \frac{3}{10}\partial^2 T
\]
is not a new generator on the same footing as $T$; it is the first place where
the central term forces a curved correction.  Proposition~\ref{prop:filtered-to-curved-fc}
therefore identifies the filtered presentation with a curved model whose
essential datum is the curvature scalar $m_0 = c \cdot \mathbf{1}$.

In particular, Virasoro belongs to the curved regime rather than the genuinely
filtered one: completion is needed because one has infinitely many descendants,
not because new independent generators appear at each weight.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[\texorpdfstring{$W_3$}{W3}: filtering is intrinsic]\label{ex:w3-not-curved-fc}
The $W_3$ algebra sits on the other side of the boundary.  Besides the fields
$L$ and $W$, the OPE of $W$ with itself produces composite operators such as
${:}LL{:}$ that are not descendants of a single generator.  The filtration by
conformal weight therefore detects genuinely new pieces rather than bookkeeping
for one curved field.

Thus $W_3$ does not collapse to a finite-generator curved model.  It requires
the completed filtered framework of Section~\ref{sec:filtered-vs-curved-comprehensive},
and later higher-genus calculations must remember this distinction.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[Calibration by the Heisenberg frame]
The Heisenberg algebra from Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame} supplies the
quadratic endpoint of the hierarchy: genus-$0$ bar terms are already finite in
each weight, no completion is needed, and higher-genus corrections enter only
through the modular curvature package.  Together with
Examples~\ref{ex:vir-filtered-to-curved-fc} and
\ref{ex:w3-not-curved-fc}, this gives the three regimes used repeatedly later:
quadratic, curved, and genuinely filtered.
\end{example}

\begin{theorem}[Convergence criterion in the filtered/curved regimes; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]\label{thm:bar-convergence-fc}
Within the hierarchy
\[
\text{quadratic} \subset \text{curved} \subset \text{filtered},
\]
no new convergence theorem is needed beyond
Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-convergence}.  More precisely:
\begin{enumerate}
\item quadratic finite-type examples such as Heisenberg converge without
  completion;
\item filtered presentations that collapse to a curved model, such as Virasoro,
  are governed by the completed curved bar construction;
\item genuinely filtered examples such as $W_3$ require completion because
  infinitely many filtration pieces contribute non-descendant composites.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Part~(1) is the quadratic finite-type clause of
Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-convergence}.  Part~(2) combines
Proposition~\ref{prop:filtered-to-curved-fc} with the same theorem: once the
filtered presentation has collapsed to a curved model, convergence is governed
by the completed curved bar complex.  Part~(3) is Example~\ref{ex:w3-not-curved-fc}:
the appearance of non-descendant composites in arbitrarily high weights forces
passage to the completed filtered bar construction.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Operational takeaway]\label{rem:practitioner-takeaway-fc}
When a later chapter invokes higher-genus corrections for a non-quadratic
algebra, one should first answer four questions.
\begin{enumerate}
\item Are all higher-weight terms descendants of finitely many generators?
  If yes, the filtration is auxiliary.
\item Does the correction lie in the center?  If yes, the curved model carries
  the relevant modular data.
\item Do genuinely new composites appear in arbitrarily high weights?
  If yes, completion is intrinsic.
\item Does the generating space itself become infinite?  If yes, one has moved
  beyond the finite-type theory proved in this volume and into the completed
  infinite-generator package.
\end{enumerate}
This checklist is the local decision rule behind the passage from finite-type
$W_N$ algebras to the $W_\infty$/Yangian extension problem recorded in
Chapter~\ref{chap:concordance}.
\end{remark}
