\chapter{The Shadow L-function: Dirichlet encoding and the genus-slot bridge}
\label{ch:shadow-L-function-platonic}

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section*{Prologue}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The shadow tower of a chirally Koszul algebra $\cA$ is the sequence of
coefficients $\{S_r(\cA)\}_{r\geq 2}$ extracted from the ordered
Maurer--Cartan element. Its Dirichlet package is the formal series
\[
 \Lsh(\cA;s) \;=\; \sum_{r\geq 2} \frac{S_r(\cA)}{r^s}.
\]
The notation resembles an $L$-function, but the object is not an
automorphic $L$-function: no Euler product, Galois representation, or
completed functional equation is part of the data. The safe theorem is
therefore not a Riemann-style identity
\[
 F_g(\cA) \stackrel{?}{=} \Lsh(\cA;1-2g).
\]
The safe theorem is the Dirichlet-slot statement: at genus $g\geq 2$ the
shadow-Feynman dictionary reads the coefficient in the weight
$r=2g-2$ slot. Evaluation of the analytically continued function at a
negative integer is a different operation; it mixes Dirichlet
coefficients by Bernoulli--Hurwitz regularisation and cannot recover one
slot without extra structure.

This chapter records the corrected surface. Section~\ref{sec:shL-setup}
defines the formal Dirichlet series and the admissibility condition under
which it may be treated analytically. Section~\ref{sec:shL-continuation}
states the conditional analytic-continuation theorem: poles and residues
come from an explicit Hurwitz--Lerch or Mellin expansion, not from the
first few coefficients alone. Section~\ref{sec:shL-trivial-zeros}
explains why negative-integer values are Bernoulli--Hurwitz evaluations,
not automatic trivial zeros. Section~\ref{sec:shL-correct-bridge}
proves the coefficient-extraction bridge with Theorem~D and with the
multi-weight correction. Section~\ref{sec:shL-kummer} imports the
Bernoulli--Kummer witnesses from the $Z_g$ chapter. Section~\ref{sec:shL-motivic-upgrade}
states the motivic refinement only under weight-compatible
admissibility.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section{Setup: formal Dirichlet series and admissibility}
\label{sec:shL-setup}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

We work over the coefficient ring generated by the shadow coefficients
of the chosen family. For $\Vir_c$ this ring is the localisation of
$\mathbb Q(c)$ obtained by inverting $c$ and $5c+22$; the explicit
Virasoro data used below are the closed forms for
$S_2,S_3,\ldots,S_8$ in
Chapter~\ref{ch:shadow-tower-higher-coefficients}.

\begin{definition}[\label{def:shadow-L-series}Shadow Dirichlet series]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For a chirally Koszul algebra $\cA$ with shadow tower
$\{S_r(\cA)\}_{r\geq 2}$, its \emph{shadow Dirichlet series} is the
formal Dirichlet series
\[
 \Lsh(\cA;s) \;=\; \sum_{r\geq 2} S_r(\cA)\,r^{-s}.
\]
As a formal object this means the sequence
$(S_2(\cA),S_3(\cA),\ldots)$ together with the basis
$\{r^{-s}\}_{r\geq 2}$; analytic convergence is extra data, not part of
the definition.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:shL-two-reindexings}Two re-indexings]
The shadow tower is indexed by weight $r\geq 2$. The Dirichlet variable
$s$ is conjugate to $\log r$. The shadow-Feynman genus index is
different: for $g\geq 2$ it uses the slot
\[
 r = 2g-2.
\]
The genus bridge is therefore a coefficient-extraction statement at
$r=2g-2$, not an evaluation statement at $s=1-2g$.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[\label{prop:shL-convergence-half-plane}Formal
uniqueness and analytic growth datum]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $a_r\in\mathbb C$ be a sequence indexed by $r\geq 2$.
\begin{enumerate}[(i)]
\item The formal Dirichlet series $\sum_{r\geq 2} a_r r^{-s}$
determines and is determined by its coefficients $a_r$.
\item If there exist constants $C,N$ with $|a_r|\leq C r^N$, then the
series converges absolutely for $\operatorname{Re}(s)>N+1$ and defines a
holomorphic function on that half-plane.
\item No such polynomial-growth bound for the full Virasoro shadow tower
follows from the closed forms through finite weight. It must be proved
from the full recurrence or imposed as an analytic hypothesis.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Part (i) is the linear independence of the formal symbols $r^{-s}$ in
the completed product $\prod_{r\geq 2} R\cdot r^{-s}$. For (ii),
$\sum |a_r|r^{-\sigma}\leq C\sum r^{N-\sigma}$ converges when
$\sigma>N+1$. Part (iii) is a scope statement: finitely many closed
forms determine finitely many coefficients and do not control the
large-$r$ growth of the infinite tower.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[\label{def:hurwitz-lerch-shadow}Hurwitz--Lerch
admissibility]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
A shadow tower is \emph{Hurwitz--Lerch admissible} on a parameter domain
$U$ if, locally uniformly on $U$, its coefficients admit an expansion
\[
 S_r(\cA) \;=\;
 \sum_{(\alpha,m)\in I} A_{\alpha,m}(\cA)\,(r-\alpha)^{-m}
 \qquad (r\geq 2)
\]
with $I$ finite, $\alpha\notin\{2,3,4,\ldots\}$ for singular kernels, and
with the resulting component series
\[
 \Phi_{\alpha,m}(s)
 \;=\;
 \sum_{r\geq 2} r^{-s}(r-\alpha)^{-m}
\]
normally convergent in some right half-plane. The analogous definition
with countable $I$ is allowed only when the component sum is normally
convergent after meromorphic continuation on compact subsets away from
its polar set.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:hurwitz-lerch-analytic-continuation}Component
continuation]
Each component $\Phi_{\alpha,m}$ is a shifted Hurwitz--Lerch zeta
function. Classical Hurwitz--Lerch continuation gives a meromorphic
continuation of each component to the $s$-plane. Hence an admissible
shadow tower has a meromorphic continuation obtained by summing the
continued components. Without admissibility, the notation
$\Lsh(\cA;s)$ remains formal.
\end{remark}

\section{Analytic continuation: what is conditional}
\label{sec:shL-continuation}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:shadow-L-analytic-continuation}Conditional
meromorphic continuation of $\Lsh(\Vir_c;s)$]
\ClaimStatusConditional
Fix generic $c\in\mathbb C\setminus\{0,-22/5\}$. Suppose the full
Virasoro shadow tower is Hurwitz--Lerch admissible on a neighbourhood of
$c$, or equivalently that the Mellin transform of its exponential
generating kernel
\[
 G_c(t) := \sum_{r\geq 2} S_r(\Vir_c)e^{-rt}
\]
admits, as $t\to 0^+$, an asymptotic expansion by powers and logarithms
whose Mellin transform is meromorphic. Then $\Lsh(\Vir_c;s)$ admits a
meromorphic continuation to the $s$-plane. Its polar set and residues are
exactly the polar set and residues obtained from that admissible
Hurwitz--Lerch decomposition, equivalently from the singular terms in the
small-$t$ expansion of $G_c(t)$.

In particular, the finite data $S_2,\ldots,S_8$ do not determine a pole
at $s=1$ or $s=2$, and no residue formula can be proved from the finite
closed-form window alone.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Under Hurwitz--Lerch admissibility,
\[
 \Lsh(\Vir_c;s)
 =
 \sum_{(\alpha,m)\in I}
 A_{\alpha,m}(c)\Phi_{\alpha,m}(s)
\]
in a right half-plane, with normal convergence. Meromorphic continuation
of the components continues the sum, and normal convergence away from the
component polar set permits termwise residue extraction. Under the
Mellin hypothesis,
\[
 \Gamma(s)\Lsh(\Vir_c;s)
 =
 \int_0^\infty G_c(t)t^{s-1}\,dt
\]
in the initial convergence half-plane. The standard Mellin-continuation
theorem identifies poles with the powers and logarithms in the
$t\to0^+$ expansion of $G_c(t)$. A finite list of coefficients affects
$G_c(t)$ by a finite linear combination of $e^{-rt}$, whose Mellin
transform is entire after division by $\Gamma(s)$; such a finite window
cannot by itself force any pole.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:shL-pole-at-s-1-motivic}Motivic weight-one
obstruction]
In a weight-compatible motivic lift, a putative residue whose class lies
in $\MZV^{\mot}_1$ vanishes because
$\MZV^{\mot}_1=0$ (Remark~\ref{rem:wt-1-vanishes}). Thus any numerical
$s=1$ singularity, if present under an admissible continuation, must come
from the chosen numerical regularisation or from weight-zero boundary
data; it is not the period of a non-zero motivic weight-one class.
\end{remark}

\section{Negative integers: Bernoulli--Hurwitz values, not automatic zeros}
\label{sec:shL-trivial-zeros}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:trivial-zeros-structure}Negative-integer
values under admissible continuation]
\ClaimStatusConditional
Assume the shadow tower of $\cA$ is Hurwitz--Lerch admissible. For every
integer $n\geq 1$, the analytically continued value
$\Lsh(\cA;-n)$ is a finite or normally convergent sum of
Hurwitz--Lerch special values
\[
 \Lsh(\cA;-n)
 =
 \sum_{(\alpha,m)\in I}
 A_{\alpha,m}(\cA)\Phi_{\alpha,m}(-n).
\]
For the unshifted component one has
\[
 \Phi_{0,0}(-n)
 =
 \sum_{r\geq2}^{\mathrm{reg}} r^n
 =
 \zet(-n)-1
 =
 -\,\frac{B_{n+1}}{n+1}-1.
\]
Consequently the vanishing of a leading Bernoulli factor is not a
trivial-zero theorem for $\Lsh$: shifted components and boundary
subtractions may contribute non-zero rational terms.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
This is the special-value form of the admissible decomposition in
Definition~\ref{def:hurwitz-lerch-shadow}. The formula for
$\Phi_{0,0}(-n)$ is the classical value
$\zet(-n)=-B_{n+1}/(n+1)$ with the $r=1$ term removed because the shadow
series begins at $r=2$. The general shifted components are evaluated by
the Hurwitz--Lerch continuation. Since the component expansion may
contain shifted kernels and finite boundary subtractions, cancellation of
the unshifted Bernoulli factor does not force cancellation of the whole
sum.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:contrast-with-riemann}Contrast with Riemann
zeta]
The trivial zeros of $\zet(s)$ are consequences of the completed
functional equation. The shadow Dirichlet series has no Euler product and
no completed functional equation in the data above. Its negative-integer
values are regularised coefficient sums. They may vanish in special
families, but the vanishing must be proved from the full admissible
decomposition, not inferred from the word ``$L$-function''.
\end{remark}

\section{The correct bridge: the genus slot}
\label{sec:shL-correct-bridge}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:Fg-from-L-sh-correctly}Genus-slot bridge:
$F_g$ from the $r=2g-2$ coefficient]
\ClaimStatusConditional
Let $\cA$ be chirally Koszul. For $g\geq 2$ define the shadow-slot
projector
\[
 \Pi_g^{\mathrm{sh}}\Lsh(\cA)
 :=
 \Coeff_{r=2g-2}\left(\sum_{r\geq2}S_r(\cA)r^{-s}\right)
 =
 S_{2g-2}(\cA).
\]
On the uniform-weight lane of Theorem~D,
\[
 F_g(\cA)=F_g^{\mathrm{sc}}(\cA)
 =\kappa(\cA)\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}.
\]
On the all-weight lane the scalar coefficient is still
\[
 F_g^{\mathrm{sc}}(\cA)=\kappa(\cA)\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}},
\]
but the full coefficient is supplied by
Theorem~\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}:
\[
 F_g(\cA)
 =
 F_g^{\mathrm{sc}}(\cA)
 +
 \delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA),
\]
with $\delta F_1^{\mathrm{cross}}=0$ and with
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$ the mixed-channel graph sum for
$g\geq2$.

Thus the shadow Dirichlet series encodes the genus data by coefficient
projection at $r=2g-2$. Evaluation at $s=1-2g$ is a Bernoulli--Hurwitz
regularised sum over all $r$ and is not equal to the genus-$g$ free
energy without additional, separately proved identities.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The first displayed identity is the definition of the formal coefficient
projector. The uniform-weight formula is exactly the scalar lane of
Theorem~D. The all-weight formula is
Theorem~\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}; it records the
cross-channel correction that appears when more than one strong-generator
weight contributes. Directly from the definition
$\Lsh(\cA;s)=\sum_{r\ge2}S_r(\cA)r^{-s}$, an analytic value at
$s=1-2g$ is a regularised sum of all Dirichlet coefficients, whereas
$\Pi_g^{\mathrm{sh}}$ selects one coefficient. These operations agree
only under an extra identity identifying that regularised sum with the
single slot, and no such identity is part of the shadow-tower formalism.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:shL-why-naive-seemed-plausible}Why the
negative-integer guess was tempting]
The Bernoulli number $B_{2g-2}$ enters the leading coefficient of the
$\mathrm{SL}_2$ Verlinde polynomial $Z_g$ through the
Hurwitz--cosecant calculation
(Theorem~\ref{thm:z-g-leading-coefficient-bernoulli}). Negative zeta
values are also Bernoulli numbers. The shared Bernoulli vocabulary is not
a functional equation: the Verlinde leading term is a moduli-of-bundles
asymptotic, while $\Lsh(\cA;1-2g)$ is a regularised weighted sum of shadow
coefficients.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:shL-positive-content-of-correction}Positive
content of the correction]
The shadow tower supplies a Dirichlet coordinate system for the ordered
bar obstruction tower. Genus $g$ reads the coordinate $r=2g-2$, while
analytic continuation, when available, supplies additional arithmetic
constraints on the whole sequence.
\end{remark}

\section{Kummer witnesses and the Verlinde polynomial}
\label{sec:shL-kummer}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:kummer-congruence-prediction}Bernoulli--Kummer
witnesses for the genus slots]
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere
For the $\mathrm{SL}_2$ Verlinde polynomial $Z_g(k)$, the leading
coefficient is governed by
Theorem~\ref{thm:z-g-leading-coefficient-bernoulli}:
\[
 [n^{2(g-2)}]\,R_{g-2}(n^2)
 =
 (-1)^g\frac{2^{g-1}B_{2g-2}}{(2g-2)!}
\]
in the normalisation of Chapter~\ref{ch:z-g-kummer-bernoulli}.
Hence
\[
 691\mid \operatorname{num}([n^{18}]Z_7(k)),
 \qquad
 3617\mid \operatorname{num}([n^{24}]Z_9(k)),
\]
because $B_{12}=-691/2730$ and $B_{16}=-3617/510$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
This is Theorem~\ref{thm:z-g-kummer-congruence}, restated in the
shadow-slot indexing $r=2g-2$. The prefactor relating
$R_{g-2}$ to the leading term of $Z_g(k)$ is coprime to $691$ for
$g=7$ and to $3617$ for $g=9$, as checked in that theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:shL-falsification-test}Falsification test]
Direct computation of $Z_7(k)$ and $Z_9(k)$ from the Verlinde formula
must reproduce the divisibilities above. A contrary factorisation would
falsify the closed-form Verlinde polynomial or its Bernoulli leading
coefficient, not the formal definition of $\Lsh$.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:shL-classical-route}Classical and motivic
routes]
The classical route is Hurwitz--cosecant plus Kummer arithmetic on
Bernoulli numerators. The motivic route is
Theorem~\ref{thm:kummer-from-motivic}. The two routes address the same
indices $2g-2$, but neither turns $\Lsh$ into an automorphic
$L$-function.
\end{remark}

\section{Motivic refinement under weight-compatible continuation}
\label{sec:shL-motivic-upgrade}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{corollary}[\label{cor:shadow-L-motivic-upgrade}Motivic
shadow Dirichlet series under admissibility]
\ClaimStatusConditional
Assume the motivic shadow tower
$S_r^{\mot}(\cA)$ of Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-motivic-lift} is
Hurwitz--Lerch admissible and that the resulting meromorphic
continuation respects motivic weight. Then:
\begin{enumerate}[(i)]
\item any residue class lying in $\MZV^{\mot}_1$ vanishes;
\item a non-zero weight-$2$ residue, when present, lies in
 $\MZV^{\mot}_2\otimes\mathbb Q(\mathrm{family})$;
\item negative-integer numerical values of $\Lsh$ are not forced to
 vanish by motivic weight alone; rational weight-zero boundary terms
 must be analysed separately.
\end{enumerate}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The first assertion is $\MZV^{\mot}_1=0$
(Remark~\ref{rem:wt-1-vanishes}). The second is the definition of a
weight-compatible continuation: a pole created by a weight-$2$ singular
term has residue in the weight-$2$ motivic piece. For (iii), the
period map sends motivic classes to numbers, but a numerical
regularised value may also contain rational boundary terms. Such
weight-zero terms are invisible to a statement about positive motivic
weights and must be computed in the admissible decomposition.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:shL-motivic-period-map-collapse}Period map
discipline]
The period map cannot turn a zero motivic class into a non-zero number.
Whenever a numerical residue or negative-integer value survives while
the corresponding positive-weight motivic class vanishes, the surviving
term is a separate weight-zero or regularisation contribution. This is a
type distinction, not a paradox.
\end{remark}

\section*{Epilogue}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The shadow Dirichlet series has a formal theorem and a conditional
analytic theorem. The formal theorem is coefficient uniqueness:
$\Lsh$ stores the shadow tower, and genus $g\geq2$ reads the slot
$r=2g-2$. The conditional analytic theorem begins only after
Hurwitz--Lerch or Mellin admissibility is proved for the full tower; its
poles, residues, and negative-integer values are then consequences of
that admissible continuation.

The Bernoulli--Kummer primes $691$ and $3617$ belong to the
$Z_g$ leading-coefficient calculation and enter the shadow discussion
through the common index $2g-2$. They do not supply a functional equation
for $\Lsh$. The corrected bridge is therefore:
\[
 \text{genus }g
 \quad\longleftrightarrow\quad
 \Coeff_{r=2g-2}\Lsh,
\]
with the multi-weight correction
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$ added exactly where
Theorem~\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion} requires it.
