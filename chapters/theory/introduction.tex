\chapter{Introduction}

\section{From a point to a curve}
\label{sec:volume-one-route}

Koszul duality, in its classical form, is a phenomenon of graded algebras over a point: a quadratic algebra~$A$ determines a dual coalgebra~$A^!$, and the bar construction mediates between them.  What happens when this machinery is transported from a point to an algebraic curve?

The generators of the algebra become sections of a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module, the quadratic relations become operator product expansions with meromorphic singularities, and the bar differential becomes an integral transform whose kernel is the logarithmic propagator $d\log(z_i - z_j)$ on Fulton--MacPherson compactifications of configuration spaces.  At genus~$0$ the classical theory embeds faithfully: Koszul duality over a point is Koszul duality over~$\mathbb{P}^1$.  At genus~$g \geq 1$, the topology of the curve forces genuinely new phenomena---central extensions, conformal anomalies, curved $A_\infty$ structures---that have no analogue in the classical setting and whose geometric origin is the curvature of the Hodge bundle over~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$.

The classical theory goes as follows.  Let $V$ be a finite-dimensional graded vector space and $R \subset V^{\otimes 2}$ a subspace of quadratic relations.  The quadratic algebra $A = T(V)/(R)$ is the free tensor algebra modulo~$R$; its Koszul dual coalgebra is $A^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}} = T^c(s V^*)$, the cofree conilpotent coalgebra on the suspended linear dual, with coproduct truncated by the orthogonal complement $R^\perp \subset (V^*)^{\otimes 2}$.  The bar construction $B(A) = (T^c(sV), d_B)$ carries a differential $d_B$ induced by the multiplication of~$A$: on cogenerators $d_B(s a) = \sum s a' \otimes s a''$ records the splitting of~$a$ into pairs of generators.  The cobar construction $\Omega(C)$ reverses the process: starting from a conilpotent coalgebra~$C$, one builds the free algebra $T(s^{-1}\bar{C})$ with differential induced by the comultiplication.  The fundamental comparison map
\[
\varepsilon \colon \Omega(B(A)) \longrightarrow A
\]
is always a morphism of dg algebras; when it is a quasi-isomorphism, the algebra~$A$ is called \emph{Koszul}, and $B(A)$ has cohomology concentrated in bar degree~$1$, equal to the Koszul dual coalgebra~$A^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}}$.  The Koszul dual algebra is $A^! = (A^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}})^\vee = T(V^*)/R^\perp$.  Koszulness means that $A$ admits a linear resolution by $A^!$-modules, and that the derived category of $A$-modules is equivalent to the derived category of $A^!$-modules (up to a shift and twist).  This is the content of Priddy~\cite{Priddy70}, Beilinson--Ginzburg--Soergel~\cite{BGS96}, and Loday--Vallette~\cite{LV12}.

Now transport this to a curve.  Let $X$ be a smooth projective curve over~$\mathbb{C}$.  A chiral algebra~$\cA$ on~$X$ (Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite{BD04}) is a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module equipped with a chiral bracket
\[
\mu^{\mathrm{ch}} \colon j_* j^*(\cA \boxtimes \cA) \longrightarrow \Delta_!(\cA),
\]
where $j \colon X^2 \setminus \Delta \hookrightarrow X^2$ is the complement of the diagonal and $\Delta_! \colon D(\mathcal{D}_X) \to D(\mathcal{D}_{X^2})$ is the pushforward.  The chiral bracket encodes the operator product expansion: locally, if $a, b \in \cA$ are sections and $z, w$ are local coordinates, then
\[
a(z) b(w) \;\sim\; \sum_{n \geq 0} \frac{(a_{(n)} b)(w)}{(z - w)^{n+1}},
\]
where the modes $a_{(n)}b$ are the Laurent coefficients of the OPE, and the bracket $\mu^{\mathrm{ch}}$ records the entire singular part simultaneously.  The Borcherds identity---the defining axiom of a vertex algebra---is the chiral-algebraic incarnation of the Jacobi identity, coupling all pole orders in a single statement.

The configuration-space mechanism has two genuinely different determinations, one for each stratum of commutativity.  The \textbf{Heisenberg atom} ($\Einf$-chiral, commutative) realizes symmetric configurations, genus-tower curvature, Lagrangian complementarity, and the full scalar characteristic package; it is the atom of the present chapter.  The \textbf{Yangian atom} ($\Eone$-chiral, braided) realizes ordered configurations, $R$-matrix inversion, the evaluation locus, and the Drinfeld--Kohno ladder; it is the atom of the braided world.  Every other family---Kac--Moody, Virasoro, $\mathcal{W}$-algebras, lattice vertex algebras---lies between these two poles.  Chapters~\ref{chap:bar-cobar} and~\ref{chap:higher-genus} establish Theorems~A--D and the universal Maurer--Cartan package~$\Theta_\cA$; Part~II computes the standard families; the concordance (Part~IV, Chapter~\ref{chap:concordance}) records the boundary between proved core and open problems.%
\label{rem:two-strata}

\section{The logarithmic seed}
\label{sec:the-seed}
\index{propagator|textbf}
\index{Koszul duality!classical}

Consider two distinct points $z_1, z_2$ on a smooth algebraic
curve~$X$, and the logarithmic 1-form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:the-seed}
\eta_{12} \;=\; d\log(z_1 - z_2)
\;=\; \frac{dz_1 - dz_2}{z_1 - z_2}
\end{equation}
on the configuration space $C_2(X) = \{(z_1,z_2) \in X^2 : z_1 \neq z_2\}$.
It has a simple pole along the diagonal with residue~$1$,
and it is conformally invariant.
For three distinct points, the Arnold relation
\begin{equation}\label{eq:arnold-seed}
\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23}
+ \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31}
+ \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12} = 0
\end{equation}
forces the vanishing of triple residues along pairwise collision
divisors of~$\overline{C}_3(X)$.

\subsection{From OPE to bar differential}
\label{subsec:ope-to-bar}
\index{bar complex!construction from OPE}
\index{operator product expansion!as bar differential input}

Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on~$X$.  Place $n$ elements
$a_1, \ldots, a_n$ at positions $z_1, \ldots, z_n$ and form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bar-element-intro}
a_1(z_1) \otimes \cdots \otimes a_n(z_n)
\otimes \bigwedge_{(i,j) \in E(T)} \eta_{ij}
\;\in\;
\cA^{\boxtimes n} \otimes
\Omega^{n-1}_{\log}(\overline{C}_n(X)),
\end{equation}
summed over edges of a planar tree~$T$.  Define a differential~$d$
by extracting residues along collision divisors:
$d(a_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes a_n) = \sum_{i < j}
\operatorname{Res}_{D_{ij}}[\eta_{ij} \cdot
\text{OPE}(a_i, a_j)] \otimes (\text{remaining factors})$.
The Arnold relation~\eqref{eq:arnold-seed} makes the triple
residue vanish, so $\dzero^2 = 0$ at genus~$0$.  The resulting cochain complex is
the \emph{bar complex}~$\barB_X(\cA)$.

Let us be precise about the graded structure.  As a graded object, the bar complex is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bar-graded-object-intro}
\barB_X(\cA) \;=\; \bigoplus_{n \geq 1} \bigl(s^{-1}\bar{\cA}\bigr)^{\otimes n}_{\Sigma_n} \otimes H^{n-1}(\overline{C}_n(X), \Omega^{n-1}_{\log}),
\end{equation}
where $s^{-1}$ denotes desuspension (cohomological convention: $|d| = +1$), $\bar{\cA} = \ker(\varepsilon \colon \cA \to \omega_X)$ is the augmentation ideal, and the subscript $\Sigma_n$ indicates coinvariants under the symmetric group action that permutes both the algebra factors and the marked points simultaneously.  The bar degree (also called tensor degree or arity) is~$n$; the internal degree comes from the conformal grading of~$\cA$.  The total differential decomposes as
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bar-diff-decomposition-intro}
d_{\barB} \;=\; d_{\mathrm{internal}} + d_{\mathrm{residue}} + d_{\mathrm{dR}},
\end{equation}
where $d_{\mathrm{internal}}$ acts on each tensor factor by the internal differential of~$\cA$ (if~$\cA$ carries one), $d_{\mathrm{residue}}$ extracts OPE residues at collision divisors weighted by the logarithmic forms~$\eta_{ij}$, and $d_{\mathrm{dR}}$ is the de Rham component from the configuration-space forms.

\subsection{Arnold's identity and $d^2 = 0$}
\label{subsec:arnold-d-squared}
\index{Arnold relation!and nilpotence}

The content of $d_{\barB}^2 = 0$ is entirely concentrated in the residue component.  To see this, expand
\[
(d_{\mathrm{internal}} + d_{\mathrm{residue}} + d_{\mathrm{dR}})^2
= d_{\mathrm{internal}}^2 + d_{\mathrm{dR}}^2 + d_{\mathrm{residue}}^2
+ \{d_{\mathrm{internal}}, d_{\mathrm{residue}}\}
+ \{d_{\mathrm{internal}}, d_{\mathrm{dR}}\}
+ \{d_{\mathrm{residue}}, d_{\mathrm{dR}}\}.
\]
The terms $d_{\mathrm{internal}}^2$ and $d_{\mathrm{dR}}^2$ vanish because each is individually a differential.  The mixed anticommutators vanish by the residue exact sequence on~$\overline{C}_n(X)$.  The entire content is $d_{\mathrm{residue}}^2 = 0$.

At bar degree~$n$, the residue differential acts by
\[
d_{\mathrm{residue}} = \sum_{i < j} \operatorname{Res}_{D_{ij}}
\]
where each $\operatorname{Res}_{D_{ij}}$ extracts the residue of the OPE singularity as $z_i \to z_j$, weighted by $\eta_{ij}$.  The square $d_{\mathrm{residue}}^2$ is a double sum $\sum_{(i,j)} \sum_{(k,\ell)} \operatorname{Res}_{D_{k\ell}} \circ \operatorname{Res}_{D_{ij}}$, and the terms are classified by the index overlap:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Disjoint indices} ($\{i,j\} \cap \{k,\ell\} = \emptyset$).
The two residue extractions act on disjoint pairs of marked points and hence commute up to the Koszul sign.  These terms cancel pairwise.

\item \emph{One shared index} (say $j = k$).  Three boundary divisors $D_{ij}$, $D_{j\ell}$, $D_{i\ell}$ meet at the codimension-$2$ stratum $D_{ij\ell}$ of triple collision.  The iterated residues produce a cyclic sum
\begin{equation}\label{eq:triple-residue-cyclic}
\sum_{\mathrm{cyclic}} \operatorname{Res}_{D_{j\ell}} \circ \operatorname{Res}_{D_{ij}}
\bigl[\mu(\mu(a_i, a_j), a_\ell) \otimes \eta_{ij} \wedge \eta_{j\ell}\bigr].
\end{equation}
This vanishes if and only if the coefficient of $\eta_{ij} \wedge \eta_{j\ell} + \eta_{j\ell} \wedge \eta_{\ell i} + \eta_{\ell i} \wedge \eta_{ij}$ is zero---which is exactly the Arnold relation~\eqref{eq:arnold-seed}---\emph{and} the chiral bracket~$\mu$ satisfies the Borcherds identity, coupling all OPE pole orders simultaneously.

\item \emph{Same pair} ($\{i,j\} = \{k,\ell\}$).  $\operatorname{Res}_{D_{ij}}^2 = 0$ automatically, since the residue of a simple pole is a number.
\end{enumerate}

The Arnold relation handles the form-theoretic factor; the Borcherds identity handles the algebraic factor.  Together they give $d_{\barB}^2 = 0$.  This is the selection principle: the bar construction on a curve accepts exactly those algebraic inputs that satisfy the Borcherds identity---that is, exactly chiral algebras.

\subsection{The bar complex as categorical logarithm}
\label{subsec:bar-as-logarithm}
\index{categorical logarithm|textbf}

The bar construction is a categorical logarithm: it maps the
multiplicative structure of the OPE to the nilpotent structure
of the bar differential ($\dzero^2 = 0$).  The cobar construction
$\Omega(\barB(\cA))$ is the corresponding exponential.
When~$\cA$ is Koszul, the bar coalgebra $\barB_X(\cA)$ has cohomology
concentrated in bar degree~$1$, and its linear dual is the Koszul dual
algebra $\cA^! = T(V^*)/R^\perp$.  Verdier duality on
$\operatorname{Ran}(X)$ intertwines the bar of~$\cA$ with the bar
of~$\cA^!$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:NAP-intro}
\int_X \cA \;\simeq\;
\left(\int_{-X} \cA^!\right)^{\!\vee}.
\end{equation}
The logarithmic form~$\eta_{ij}$ is the kernel of this non-abelian
integral transform; Verdier duality is its inversion formula;
the cobar construction reconstructs~$\cA$ from the transformed data.

The analogy with the ordinary logarithm is not merely poetic.  The functional equation $\log(ab) = \log a + \log b$ becomes the fact that the bar construction sends tensor products (multiplicative data) to direct sums of bar complexes (additive data).  The three-term Arnold identity
\[
\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23} + \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31} + \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12} = 0
\]
is the categorical analogue of $\log(xyz) = \log(xy) + \log(z)$: it is the cocycle condition that forces the logarithm to be well-defined and additive.  The exponential $\exp = \Omega \circ B$ inverts the logarithm when the latter converges---that is, on the Koszul locus where the bar spectral sequence degenerates.  Off the Koszul locus, the logarithm has finite radius of convergence, and analytic continuation requires the coderived category (Positselski~\cite{Positselski11}).


\subsection{The genus-$g$ propagator and curvature}
\label{subsec:genus-g-propagator}
\index{nilpotence-periodicity correspondence}
\index{prime form|textbf}

At genus~$0$, the logarithm~$\eta_{12}$ is single-valued
on~$C_2(\mathbb{P}^1)$, and $\dzero^2 = 0$.  At
genus~$g \geq 1$, the function $z_1 - z_2$ has no global meaning.
The replacement is (schematically)

\begin{equation}\label{eq:genus-g-propagator-intro-ch0}
\eta_{12}^{(g)} \;=\; d\log E(z_1, z_2)
\;+\; \text{(period corrections)},
\end{equation}
where $E(z_1,z_2)$ is the prime form, a section of $K^{-1/2} \boxtimes K^{-1/2}$
(Definition~\ref{def:higher-genus-log-forms}), and the period corrections
involve the Hodge bundle $\mathbb{E} \to \overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$
via the imaginary part of the period matrix
$(\operatorname{Im}\Omega)^{-1}$; see Definition~\ref{def:higher-genus-log-forms}
for the precise formula.

The prime form $E(z_1, z_2)$ is the unique section of $K^{-1/2} \boxtimes K^{-1/2}$ (not $K^{+1/2}$, a common error in the literature) that vanishes to first order along the diagonal $z_1 = z_2$ with unit residue and has prescribed monodromy along the $B$-cycles determined by a choice of homology basis.  It is the genus-$g$ replacement for the rational function $z_1 - z_2$, and its logarithmic derivative $d\log E$ is the genus-$g$ replacement for the genus-$0$ propagator $d\log(z_1 - z_2)$.

The period corrections couple the collision geometry to the complex
structure of the curve, and the Arnold relation acquires a defect:

\begin{equation}\label{eq:curvature-intro}
\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g,
\end{equation}
where $\omega_g$ is the Arakelov $(1,1)$-form and $\kappa(\cA)$
is a scalar determined by the leading OPE singularity.

The
logarithm $d\log E(z_1,z_2)$ acquires monodromy around the
$B$-cycles of~$\Sigma_g$, and $\kappa(\cA)$---the residue at the
branch point of the categorical logarithm---is the infinitesimal
generator of this monodromy.  Period integrals along $A$-cycles
restore nilpotence: $\Dg{g}^{\,2} = 0$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-diff-squares-zero}).
This is the chiral Riemann--Hilbert correspondence: the nilpotent
datum~$\kappa$ determines the periodic datum via exponentiation.

As $[\Sigma_g]$ varies over~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$, the bar
complex traces a family of curved cochain complexes.  The
obstruction class $\mathrm{obs}_g = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g \in
H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g)$, where $\lambda_g = c_g(\mathbb{E})$
is the top Chern class of the Hodge bundle, is a characteristic
class of this family (Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality}).
The Koszul dual~$\cA^!$ traces a complementary family: their
obstruction classes pair to give the full cohomology of moduli with
coefficients in the center~$Z(\cA)$.

Classical Koszul duality---Priddy~\cite{Priddy70},
Beilinson--Ginzburg--Soergel~\cite{BGS96},
Loday--Vallette~\cite{LV12}---is a theorem about quadratic algebras
on a point: from $A = T(V)/(R)$, construct
$A^! = T(V^*)/R^\perp$; when $A$ is Koszul,
$\Omega(B(A)) \xrightarrow{\sim} A$.
\index{chiral algebra|textbf}
\index{factorization algebra|textbf}
Chiral algebras (Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite{BD04}) live on curves;
their tensor factors carry positions $z_i \in X$, and the
Fulton--MacPherson compactification~$\overline{C}_n(X)$ enters the
construction.  The passage from point to curve is the content of
this monograph.


\section{The selection principle}
\label{sec:selection-principle}
\index{selection principle|textbf}
\index{Borcherds identity!as selection criterion}

The bar construction of \S\ref{sec:the-seed} is a
\emph{geometric} machine.  Take a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module~$V$
equipped with a meromorphic pairing
$\mu \colon j_* j^*(V \boxtimes V) \to \Delta_!(V)$
(the OPE), place sections at points of the Fulton--MacPherson
compactification~$\overline{C}_n(X)$, and extract residues along
boundary divisors.  The output is a graded object
$\barB_X(V, \mu)$ with a candidate differential
$d = d_{\mathrm{internal}} + d_{\mathrm{residue}}
+ d_{\mathrm{dR}}$.

The question that governs the theory is:
\begin{center}
\emph{For which algebraic inputs $(V, \mu)$ does
the FM bar construction produce $d^2 = 0$?}
\end{center}

\noindent
Expand $(d_1 + d_2 + d_3)^2$.  The terms
$d_1^2$, $d_3^2$, and all mixed anticommutators vanish
for formal reasons (cochain complex, de~Rham,
residue exact sequence).  The entire content is
$d_2^2 = 0$: the residue extraction must be nilpotent
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-nilpotency-complete}).
Three cases arise from index overlap in
$d_2^2 = \sum_{i<j}\sum_{k<\ell}
\Res_{D_{k\ell}} \circ \Res_{D_{ij}}$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Disjoint indices}
  ($\{i,j\} \cap \{k,\ell\} = \emptyset$).
  Residue operations commute; cancellation is by the
  Koszul sign rule.  No condition on~$\mu$.

\item \emph{One shared index} (say $j = k$).
  Three boundary strata meet at the codimension-$2$ locus
  $D_{ij\ell}$ of triple collision.  The cyclic sum of
  iterated residues
  \[
  \sum_{\textup{cyclic}}
    \Res_{D_{j\ell}} \circ \Res_{D_{ij}}
    \bigl[\mu(\mu(\phi_i, \phi_j), \phi_\ell)
    \otimes \omega\bigr]
  = 0
  \]
  holds if and only if $\mu$ satisfies the
  Borcherds identity---the associativity of the chiral
  product, coupling all OPE pole orders simultaneously.

\item \emph{Same pair}
  ($\{i,j\} = \{k,\ell\}$).
  $\Res_{D_{ij}}^2 = 0$ automatically.
\end{enumerate}
Cases~(i) and~(iii) impose no condition on the algebra.
Case~(ii) imposes exactly one: the Borcherds identity.
This identity is the defining axiom of a chiral algebra.

Two witnesses from Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}:

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Heisenberg} ($\alpha(z)\alpha(w) \sim
k/(z-w)^2$, double pole only).  No simple pole, so
$d_{\mathrm{bracket}} = 0$.  The full differential reduces to
$d_{\mathrm{curvature}}$, which produces scalars:
$d_{\mathrm{curvature}}^2 = 0$ automatically.  The Arnold
relation handles all of case~(ii).  No algebraic input needed.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Kac--Moody} ($h(z)\,e(w) \sim
2e(w)/(z-w) + \cdots$, simple \emph{and} double poles).
The bracket component $d_{\mathrm{bracket}}$ is now nonzero, and
$d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2 \neq 0$ --- computed explicitly for all
$2048$ sign conventions
(\S\ref{subsec:frame-sl2-bar-low-degree},
Remark~\ref{rem:frame-why-dbracket-fails}).  The obstruction is
proportional to the level~$k$: it is the double-pole data that
$d_{\mathrm{bracket}}$ ignores.  The Borcherds identity absorbs
this obstruction:
\[
d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2
= -\{d_{\mathrm{bracket}},\, d_{\mathrm{curvature}}\}.
\]
The curvature is the obstruction; the Borcherds identity is the
integrability condition
(Proposition~\ref{prop:pole-decomposition}).

\begin{remark}[Classical vs.\ chiral selection]
\label{rem:classical-vs-chiral-selection}
\index{Koszul duality!classical vs.\ chiral}
The bar construction asks the same question at every level
of geometry:
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\textbf{Base} & \textbf{Condition for $d^2 = 0$}
  & \textbf{Selected objects} \\
\hline
Point & Jacobi identity & Lie algebras \\
Curve $X$ & Borcherds identity & Chiral algebras \\
$X$ over $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ & $+$ period corrections
  & Modular Koszul algebras
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Each row is forced by the geometry of the next: the Jacobi
identity handles triple collisions when all poles are simple;
on a curve, multiple pole orders mix at the codimension-$2$
strata of $\overline{C}_n(X)$, and the Borcherds identity is
the unique condition that makes this mixing consistent; at
higher genus, period corrections from the Hodge bundle restore
$\Dg{g}^{\,2} = 0$.  Chiral algebras are what configuration
space geometry accepts; modular Koszul algebras are what the
genus tower accepts.
\end{remark}


\section{Construction of the bar complex}
\label{sec:bar-construction-intro}
\index{bar complex!explicit construction|textbf}
\index{Fulton--MacPherson compactification!role in bar complex}

We now construct the bar complex $\barB_X(\cA)$ in full, as a graded coalgebra with differential, starting from the logarithmic kernel and ending with the proof that $d^2 = 0$.  This construction is the foundation on which the entire monograph rests.

\subsection{The Fulton--MacPherson compactification}
\label{subsec:FM-compactification-intro}
\index{Fulton--MacPherson compactification|textbf}

Let $C_n(X) = \{(z_1, \ldots, z_n) \in X^n : z_i \neq z_j \text{ for } i \neq j\}$ be the configuration space of $n$ distinct labelled points on~$X$.  The Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\overline{C}_n(X)$ is obtained by blowing up $X^n$ along all diagonals, starting from the deepest (the small diagonal $z_1 = z_2 = \cdots = z_n$) and proceeding to the shallowest (pairwise diagonals $z_i = z_j$).  It is important to note that $\overline{C}_n(X)$ is the blowup along diagonals, not the complement of diagonals---a distinction that matters for the residue calculus.

The boundary $\partial\overline{C}_n(X) = \overline{C}_n(X) \setminus C_n(X)$ is a normal crossing divisor whose irreducible components are indexed by \emph{nested partitions} of $\{1, \ldots, n\}$.  The codimension-$1$ boundary strata $D_S$, for $S \subset \{1, \ldots, n\}$ with $|S| \geq 2$, parametrize configurations where the points in~$S$ collide while remembering their relative positions (the ``screen'' or tangent direction of approach).

The cooperadic structure of the boundary is essential: the stratum $D_S$ is isomorphic to $\overline{C}_{|S|}(T_{z_0} X) \times \overline{C}_{n - |S| + 1}(X)$, where the first factor records the collision pattern at infinitesimal scale and the second factor records the remaining configuration.  This factorization is the geometric origin of the bar coalgebra's coproduct.

\subsection{Logarithmic forms and the Arnold algebra}
\label{subsec:log-forms-arnold}
\index{Arnold algebra|textbf}
\index{logarithmic forms!on configuration spaces}

On $C_n(X)$, define the logarithmic $1$-forms
\[
\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j) = \frac{dz_i - dz_j}{z_i - z_j} \in \Omega^1_{\log}(\overline{C}_n(X)),
\]
where the right-hand side is defined in a local coordinate.  These forms extend to $\overline{C}_n(X)$ as logarithmic $1$-forms with simple poles along the boundary divisor~$D_{\{i,j\}}$ and residue~$1$.

The \emph{Arnold algebra} $\mathcal{A}_n$ is the subalgebra of $\Omega^*_{\log}(\overline{C}_n(X))$ generated by the forms $\eta_{ij}$, subject to the relations:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(A\arabic*)}]
\item \textbf{Symmetry}: $\eta_{ij} = \eta_{ji}$.
\item \textbf{Three-term relation}: $\eta_{ij} \wedge \eta_{jk} + \eta_{jk} \wedge \eta_{ki} + \eta_{ki} \wedge \eta_{ij} = 0$ for all triples $\{i, j, k\}$.
\end{enumerate}
The three-term relation (A2) is the Arnold relation~\eqref{eq:arnold-seed}; it is the only algebraic relation among the generators $\eta_{ij}$ in the cohomology ring $H^*(C_n(\mathbb{C}), \mathbb{Q})$ (Arnold~\cite{Arnold69}).  The Arnold algebra is a quadratic algebra in the forms $\eta_{ij}$; it is Koszul (as proved by Arnol'd and, independently, by Cohen).

\subsection{The bar differential via residues}
\label{subsec:bar-diff-residues}
\index{bar differential!residue construction}

Given an augmented chiral algebra $(\cA, \varepsilon \colon \cA \to \omega_X)$ with augmentation ideal $\bar{\cA} = \ker(\varepsilon)$, the bar complex is the graded object
\[
\barB_X(\cA) = \bigoplus_{n \geq 1} \barB_X^n(\cA), \qquad
\barB_X^n(\cA) = \bigl(s^{-1}\bar{\cA}\bigr)^{\otimes n}_{\Sigma_n},
\]
where $s^{-1}$ is the desuspension functor (the bar complex uses desuspension, placing elements one degree below their natural position in the algebra).

The bar differential
$d_{\barB} \colon \barB_X^n(\cA) \to \barB_X^{n-1}(\cA)$
is the residue extraction map
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bar-diff-formula-intro}
d_{\barB}[a_1 | \cdots | a_n]
= \sum_{1 \leq i < j \leq n}
(-1)^{\epsilon(i,j)}
\operatorname{Res}_{D_{ij}} \Bigl[
\eta_{ij} \cdot \mu^{\mathrm{ch}}(a_i, a_j)
\Bigr]
[a_1 | \cdots | \widehat{a_i} | \cdots | a_{i \to \mu(a_i, a_j)} | \cdots | a_n],
\end{equation}
where $[a_1 | \cdots | a_n]$ denotes the element $s^{-1}a_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes s^{-1}a_n$, the sign $\epsilon(i,j)$ is the Koszul sign from permuting the desuspended elements past each other, and $\mu^{\mathrm{ch}}(a_i, a_j)$ is the chiral bracket.

\begin{theorem}[Bar nilpotence; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:bar-nilpotence-intro}
\index{bar complex!nilpotence}
For any chiral algebra~$\cA$ on a smooth algebraic curve~$X$,
$d_{\barB}^2 = 0$ at genus~$0$.  The proof uses the Arnold relation~\textup{(A2)} for the geometric factor and the Borcherds identity for the algebraic factor.  The full proof is Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-nilpotency-complete}}.
\end{theorem}

The bar complex carries a coassociative coproduct
$\Delta \colon \barB_X(\cA) \to \barB_X(\cA) \otimes \barB_X(\cA)$
given by deconcatenation:
\[
\Delta[a_1 | \cdots | a_n] = \sum_{p + q = n} [a_1 | \cdots | a_p] \otimes [a_{p+1} | \cdots | a_n].
\]
This coproduct is compatible with~$d_{\barB}$ and makes $\barB_X(\cA)$ into a dg coalgebra---a conilpotent, coaugmented, differential graded coalgebra.


\section{Construction of the Koszul dual}
\label{sec:koszul-dual-construction-intro}
\index{Koszul dual!explicit construction|textbf}
\index{orthogonal complement!construction}
\index{Verdier duality!construction of Koszul dual}

The Koszul dual~$\cA^!$ is not defined by analogy with the classical case; it is \emph{constructed} via Verdier duality on the Ran space.  We give the construction in three steps: first the bar cohomology, then the orthogonal complement, then the Verdier mechanism.

\subsection{Bar cohomology and the dual coalgebra}
\label{subsec:bar-cohomology-dual}

When~$\cA$ is Koszul, the bar complex $(\barB_X(\cA), d_{\barB})$ has cohomology concentrated in bar degree~$1$:
\[
H^p(\barB_X(\cA)) = \begin{cases}
\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}} & \text{if } p = 1, \\
0 & \text{if } p \neq 1.
\end{cases}
\]
Here $\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}}$ is the Koszul dual \emph{coalgebra}, a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module with the structure of a conilpotent coassociative coalgebra inherited from the bar coproduct.  The Koszul dual \emph{algebra} is then obtained by linear duality:
\[
\cA^! = (\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}})^\vee.
\]
More precisely, $\cA^!$ carries a chiral algebra structure whose chiral bracket is the Verdier dual of the bar coalgebra's coproduct.

\subsection{The orthogonal complement construction}
\label{subsec:orthogonal-complement}
\index{orthogonal complement|textbf}

At the level of quadratic data, the construction is explicit.  Suppose~$\cA$ is presented as $\cA = T^{\mathrm{ch}}(V)/\langle R \rangle$, where $V$ is a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module of generators, $T^{\mathrm{ch}}(V)$ is the chiral tensor algebra, and $R \subset V \chirtensor V$ is a subspace of quadratic relations (the kernel of the OPE modulo regular terms).

The linear dual $\mathcal{D}_X$-module is $V^* = \mathbb{D}(V)$, where $\mathbb{D}$ is Verdier duality.  The space of quadratic relations in the dual is the orthogonal complement
\[
R^\perp = \{f \in V^* \chirtensor V^* : \langle f, r \rangle = 0 \text{ for all } r \in R\},
\]
where the pairing $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle$ is induced by the evaluation $V^* \otimes V \to \omega_X$.  The Koszul dual algebra is then
\[
\cA^! = T^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*) / \langle R^\perp \rangle.
\]
This is the chiral analogue of Priddy's classical construction: the Koszul dual has the dual generators and the orthogonal relations.

\subsection{Verdier duality on the Ran space}
\label{subsec:verdier-ran}
\index{Verdier duality!on Ran space|textbf}

The deeper construction avoids the quadratic presentation entirely and works directly at the level of the bar coalgebra.  The Ran space $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$ is the moduli of finite non-empty subsets of~$X$; a factorization coalgebra on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$ is a cosheaf satisfying the factorization property (the value on a disjoint union is the tensor product of the values on the pieces).

The bar complex $\barB_X(\cA)$ is a factorization coalgebra on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$.  Verdier duality $\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}$ is the duality functor on the category of $\mathcal{D}$-modules on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$:
\[
\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}(\mathcal{F})(S)
= R\mathcal{H}om_{\mathcal{D}_{X^S}}(\mathcal{F}(S),\, \omega_{X^S})[d_S],
\]
where $S \in \operatorname{Ran}(X)$ is a finite subset and $d_S = |S| \cdot \dim X$.

The fundamental intertwining is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:verdier-intertwining-intro}
\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}} \barB_X(\cA) \;\simeq\; \barB_X(\cA^!).
\end{equation}
This identity is a \emph{theorem} (Theorem~A), not a definition.  It means that the Verdier dual of the bar coalgebra of~$\cA$ is the bar coalgebra of the Koszul dual algebra~$\cA^!$.  The Koszul dual is thus recovered from the bar complex via Verdier duality, without ever needing to specify a quadratic presentation.  This resolves the apparent circularity in defining~$\cA^!$: it is the algebra whose bar coalgebra is the Verdier dual of the bar coalgebra of~$\cA$.


\section{Construction of the cobar complex}
\label{sec:cobar-construction-intro}
\index{cobar complex!construction|textbf}
\index{distributions!in cobar construction}

The cobar complex $\Omega_X(\mathcal{C})$, for a conilpotent factorization coalgebra~$\mathcal{C}$ on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$, is the Verdier dual of the bar construction.  Concretely, it is the free chiral algebra on the desuspended cogenerators of~$\mathcal{C}$, with differential induced by the coproduct:
\[
\Omega_X(\mathcal{C}) = \bigl(T^{\mathrm{ch}}(s\,\overline{\mathcal{C}}),\, d_\Omega\bigr),
\]
where $\overline{\mathcal{C}} = \operatorname{coker}(\eta \colon \omega_X \to \mathcal{C})$ is the coaugmentation coideal, and the differential $d_\Omega$ is defined by
\[
d_\Omega(s\,c) = \sum (s\,c') \cdot (s\,c'') \quad \text{where } \Delta(c) = \sum c' \otimes c'',
\]
using the chiral multiplication in $T^{\mathrm{ch}}(s\,\overline{\mathcal{C}})$.

The cobar differential squares to zero because the coproduct~$\Delta$ is coassociative:
$d_\Omega^2 = 0$ follows from $(\Delta \otimes \operatorname{id}) \circ \Delta = (\operatorname{id} \otimes \Delta) \circ \Delta$.
Alternatively, and more illuminatingly, $d_\Omega^2 = 0$ follows from $d_{\barB}^2 = 0$ via Verdier duality (Corollary~\ref{cor:cobar-nilpotence-verdier}): the cobar complex is the Verdier dual of the bar complex, and Verdier duality preserves the nilpotence of differentials.

The cobar construction is the left adjoint of the bar construction:
\[
\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathrm{Alg}}(\Omega_X(\mathcal{C}),\, \cA)
\;\cong\;
\operatorname{Tw}(\mathcal{C},\, \cA)
\;\cong\;
\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathrm{CoAlg}}(\mathcal{C},\, \barB_X(\cA)),
\]
where $\operatorname{Tw}(\mathcal{C}, \cA)$ is the set of twisting morphisms $\alpha \colon \mathcal{C} \to \cA$ satisfying the Maurer--Cartan equation
\[
\partial \alpha + \mu_\cA \circ (\alpha \otimes \alpha) \circ \Delta_\mathcal{C} = 0.
\]
The bar-cobar adjunction $\barB_X \dashv \Omega_X$ is the chiral version of the classical bar-cobar adjunction of Loday--Vallette~\cite[\S2.2]{LV12}.

The cobar complex is not built from forms but from \emph{distributions}: the Verdier dual of a smooth section is a distributional section, and the cobar differential acts by distributional coevaluation.  This distributional character is essential at higher genus, where the cobar construction must handle the period corrections and curvature of the genus-$g$ propagator through completed tensor products.


\section{The five theorems}
\label{sec:NAP-unifying}
\label{sec:main-results-complete}
\index{Koszul duality!chiral|textbf}
\index{modular Koszul duality|textbf}
\index{quantum corrections!as modular completion}

The five theorems are the four properties of the categorical
logarithm---existence, inversion, branch structure, leading
coefficient---together with the coefficient ring that records its
Taylor data.  Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame} exhibits all five
in a single example before braiding enters;
Chapter~\ref{chap:yangians} shows what changes once ordered
factorization is part of the structure.

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Theorem~A} (Geometric bar-cobar duality,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}).
  The bar functor
  \[
  \bar{B}_X \colon
  \operatorname{Alg}_{\mathrm{aug}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
  \to \operatorname{CoAlg}_{\mathrm{conil}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
  \]
  is intertwined with Verdier duality:
  $\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}} \bar{B}_X(\cA)
  \simeq \bar{B}_X(\cA^!)$,
  functorial in families over $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,n}$.
  It decomposes into chiral twisting morphisms
  ($\mathrm{A}_0$,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:fundamental-twisting-morphisms}),
  bar concentration
  ($\mathrm{A}_1$, Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-concentration}), and
  Verdier-geometric duality
  ($\mathrm{A}_2$,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}).
  The bar complex is the BRST complex; Verdier duality is CPT.

\item \emph{Theorem~B} (Bar-cobar inversion,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:higher-genus-inversion}).
  On the Koszul locus, the counit
  $\Omega_X \bar{B}_X(\cA) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$
  is an equivalence; the spectral sequence collapses at~$E_2$.
  Off the locus, the bar-cobar object persists but becomes curved;
  the failure is measured by
  $\Theta_\cA$~\eqref{eq:universal-MC}.
  The BRST resolution is complete.

  More precisely, there is a spectral sequence with $E_1$ page
  $E_1^{p,q} = H^q(\barB^p_X(\cA))$ converging to $H^{p+q}(\Omega_X \barB_X(\cA))$.
  On the Koszul locus, $E_1^{p,q} = 0$ for $p \neq 1$, so
  $E_2 = E_\infty$, and the comparison map
  $\varepsilon \colon \Omega_X \barB_X(\cA) \to \cA$
  induces an isomorphism on cohomology.  This is the categorical
  exponential: $\exp \circ \log = \operatorname{id}$.

\item \emph{Theorem~C} (Deformation-obstruction complementarity,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}).
  The ambient complex
  $\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) := R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g,
  \mathcal{Z}_\cA)$
  carries a shifted-symplectic pairing from Verdier duality on
  the center local system.  The Verdier involution decomposes it
  into complementary Lagrangians
  $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \oplus \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)$
  (Definition~\ref{def:complementarity-complexes}).
  At the cohomological level,
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:complementarity-intro}
  Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!) \cong
  H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathcal{Z}_\cA).
  \end{equation}
  The anomaly of one algebra is the ghost-number violation of
  its dual.

  The shifted-symplectic structure is a $(-(3g{-}3))$-shifted symplectic form on the derived moduli of genus-$g$ bar families, in the sense of Pantev--To\"en--Vaqui\'e--Vezzosi~\cite{PTVV13}.  The complementary Lagrangians~$Q_g(\cA)$ and~$Q_g(\cA^!)$ are the $\pm 1$ eigenspaces of the Verdier involution~$\sigma = \mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}$ acting on~$\mathbf{C}_g(\cA)$.  Their intersection is the center~$Z(\cA)$; the ambient complex is the direct sum.

\item \emph{Theorem~D$_{\mathrm{scal}}$} (Scalar modular
  characteristic, Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}).
  A single scalar $\kappa(\cA)$---the genus-$1$ curvature
  coefficient---determines the entire scalar modular package
  (Definition~\ref{def:scalar-modular-package}):
  $\kappa$ is universal
  ($\mathrm{obs}_g = \kappa \cdot \lambda_g$),
  additive under tensor product, and anti-symmetric under
  Koszul duality ($\kappa + \kappa' = 0$ for affine Kac--Moody).
  Its generating function is the $\hat{A}$-genus:
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:ahat-genus-generating-function-intro}
  \sum_{g \geq 1} \mathrm{obs}_g(\cA) \cdot \hbar^{2g-2}
  = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \bigl(\hat{A}(i\hbar) - 1\bigr).
  \end{equation}

  \emph{Theorem~D$_\Delta$} (Spectral characteristic,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:spectral-characteristic}):
  the spectral discriminant $\Delta_\cA(x)$ is a separately proved
  non-scalar invariant.

  $\kappa$ is the one-loop coefficient of the effective action;
  its vanishing is anomaly-free string propagation at all genera.
  Together, $D_{\mathrm{scal}}$ and $D_\Delta$ are the
  characteristic classes of the factorization homology
  bundle $\int_{\Sigma_g}\!\cA$
  (Remark~\ref{rem:four-levels}); the bundle
  itself---its fibers, flat connection, and modular
  functor structure---is the full modular homotopy invariant
  (Conjecture~\ref{conj:categorical-modular-kd}).

\item \emph{Theorem~H} (Chiral Hochschild cohomology,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:hochschild-polynomial-growth}).
  $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is polynomial on the Koszul locus and dual
  to $\ChirHoch^*(\cA^!)$.  The full Taylor series of the
  categorical logarithm lives in a polynomial ring.  The Hochschild
  complex governs deformations
  (Chapter~\ref{chap:deformation-theory}): its brace and
  Gerstenhaber structures control the moduli of quantizations
  and the passage from Poisson to chiral.
\end{itemize}


The scalar package has teeth:

\begin{theorem}[Central charge complementarity; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]\label{thm:central-charge-complementarity}
\index{central charge!complementarity|textbf}
\textup{[Regime: curved-central on the Koszul locus
\textup{(}Convention~\textup{\ref{conv:regime-tags})}.]}

For Koszul dual pairs related by the Feigin--Frenkel involution
$k \mapsto k' = -k - 2h^\vee$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textup{(a)}] \emph{Affine Kac--Moody.}
  $c(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k) + c(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{k'})
  = 2 \dim \mathfrak{g}$.
\item[\textup{(b)}] \emph{Principal $\mathcal{W}$-algebras:}
  $c(\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})) + c(\mathcal{W}^{k'}(\mathfrak{g}))
   = 2\operatorname{rank}(\mathfrak{g}) + 4h^\vee \dim \mathfrak{g}$.
\end{enumerate}
In particular, $\mathfrak{g} = \mathfrak{sl}_2$ in
part~\textup{(b)} gives $c + c' = 26$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Part~(a).}  The Sugawara central charge is
$c(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k) = k \dim\mathfrak{g}/(k + h^\vee)$.
Since $k' + h^\vee = -(k + h^\vee)$:
\[
c(k) + c(k') = \frac{k \cdot d}{k+h^\vee}
+ \frac{(k+2h^\vee) \cdot d}{k+h^\vee}
= \frac{2(k + h^\vee) \cdot d}{k+h^\vee} = 2d
\]
where $d = \dim\mathfrak{g}$.

\emph{Part~(b).}  The Drinfeld--Sokolov central charge is
$c(\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})) = r - 12|\rho|^2 (t-1)^2/t$
where $r = \operatorname{rank}(\mathfrak{g})$ and
$t = k + h^\vee$
(verified for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ in
Theorem~\ref{thm:w-algebra-koszul-main} and for $\mathfrak{sl}_3$
in Theorem~\ref{thm:w-koszul-precise}).
The involution sends $t \mapsto -t$:
\[
c(k) + c(k') = 2r + 12|\rho|^2 \cdot
\frac{(t+1)^2 - (t-1)^2}{t}
= 2r + 48|\rho|^2.
\]
The Freudenthal--de~Vries identity
$|\rho|^2 = h^\vee \dim\mathfrak{g}/12$
(under normalization $(\theta, \theta) = 2$;
see~\cite{Kac}, Ch.~13)
yields $c + c' = 2r + 4h^\vee d$.

\emph{Verification.}
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$: $2(1) + 4(2)(3) = 26$.
$\mathfrak{sl}_3$: $2(2) + 4(3)(8) = 100$.
$\mathfrak{sl}_N$:
$2(N{-}1) + 4N(N^2{-}1) = 2(N{-}1)(2N^2{+}2N{+}1)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:bosonic-string-26}
\index{bosonic string!critical dimension}
The identity $c + c' = 26$ for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ is the algebraic
incarnation of $c_{\mathrm{matter}} + c_{\mathrm{ghost}} = 0$:
the ghost system is the cobar of the matter Virasoro bar complex
(Theorem~\ref{thm:qme-bar-cobar}).  The bosonic string critical
dimension falls out of Koszul duality alone.
\end{remark}

\medskip

The five theorems assemble into a single commutative square:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:master-square}
\begin{tikzcd}[row sep=huge, column sep=huge]
\operatorname{Alg}_{\mathrm{aug}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
  \arrow[r, "\bar{B}_X"]
  \arrow[d, "(\cdot)^!"']
& \operatorname{CoAlg}_{\mathrm{conil}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
  \arrow[d, "\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}"]
\\
\operatorname{Alg}_{\mathrm{aug}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
  \arrow[r, "\bar{B}_X"']
& \operatorname{CoAlg}_{\mathrm{conil}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
\end{tikzcd}
\end{equation}
\index{master diagram}
\index{Verdier duality!as mediating mechanism}
Commutativity is Theorem~A\@.  The cobar functor~$\Omega_X$, left
adjoint to~$\bar{B}_X$, gives the counit of Theorem~B\@.  At
genus~$g \geq 1$, the universal Maurer--Cartan class~$\Theta_\cA$
measures the failure of strict commutativity; $\kappa(\cA) = 0$ is
the condition for the square to commute in the derived category
rather than the coderived category.

The Koszul locus is the convergence radius of the categorical
logarithm.  Off this locus, the coderived category
(Positselski~\cite{Positselski11}) provides analytic continuation:
the curved bar complex is acyclic in the ordinary derived category
but retains its full homotopical content in the coderived
category---as the logarithm is trivial on a simply-connected domain
but carries monodromy on a multiply-connected one.

\begin{remark}[The categorical logarithm paradigm]
\label{rem:categorical-logarithm-paradigm}
The bar construction $\barB_X(\cA)$ is the categorical logarithm
of the chiral algebra~$\cA$.  It maps the multiplicative OPE
structure to the additive bar differential; its integral kernel is
the logarithmic propagator $d\log(z_i-z_j)$; and the Arnold
relation $\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23}+\eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{31}
+\eta_{31}\wedge\eta_{12}=0$ is the three-term identity that forces
$d^2=0$, exactly as the functional equation
$\log(xyz)=\log(xy)+\log(z)$ forces the logarithm to be additive.

The five main theorems of this monograph are the four properties of
a logarithm together with the coefficient ring that controls its
behavior:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\emph{Existence} (Theorem~A) & the bar--cobar adjunction \\
\emph{Invertibility} (Theorem~B) & bar--cobar inversion on the
  Koszul locus \\
\emph{Branch structure} (Theorem~C) & complementary Lagrangians
  in the genus-$g$ ambient complex \\
\emph{Leading coefficient} (Theorem~D) & the scalar modular
  characteristic $\kappa(\cA)\lambda_g$ \\
\emph{Coefficient ring} (Theorem~H) & chiral Hochschild
  cohomology, polynomial on the Koszul locus
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
At genus~$0$ the logarithm is single-valued and recovers classical
Koszul duality.  At genus $g\ge 1$ it acquires monodromy
$\kappa(\cA)\cdot\omega_g$ from the period structure of the curve,
and the genus tower is the Taylor expansion of that monodromy around
the base point.  The nonlinear characteristic layer of this monograph
computes the higher Taylor jets of this expansion: the quartic jets
of the complementarity potential
(Chapter~\ref{app:nonlinear-modular-shadows}), the spectral
cumulants of the branch-line quotient
(Appendix~\ref{app:branch-line-reductions}), and the canonical
homotopy arity in the non-principal $\mathcal W$-algebra sector.
\end{remark}


\section{The modular characteristic and the spectral discriminant}
\label{sec:modular-char-spectral-disc-intro}
\index{modular characteristic!explicit formula|textbf}
\index{spectral discriminant!construction|textbf}
\index{A-hat genus@$\hat{A}$-genus|textbf}

The genus-$1$ curvature $\kappa(\cA)$ is the most important numerical invariant of a chiral algebra from the standpoint of modular Koszul duality.  We construct it explicitly.

\subsection{The explicit formula for $\kappa$}
\label{subsec:kappa-explicit}
\index{modular characteristic!explicit formula}

Let~$\cA$ be a chiral algebra on a smooth projective curve~$X$ with OPE
$a(z)\,b(w) \sim \sum_{n \geq 0} (a_{(n)}b)(w) / (z-w)^{n+1}$.
The leading OPE singularity is encoded in the conformal weight grading and the central charge.  For a conformal chiral algebra with stress-energy tensor $T(z)$ and $T(z)\,T(w) \sim c/2(z-w)^4 + 2T(w)/(z-w)^2 + \partial T(w)/(z-w)$, the modular characteristic is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:kappa-formula-intro}
\kappa(\cA) = \frac{c(\cA)}{24} \cdot \frac{1}{\operatorname{rk}(\cA)},
\end{equation}
normalized so that $\kappa(\mathcal{H}_k) = 1/k$ for the Heisenberg algebra with OPE $\alpha(z)\alpha(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$.  The obstruction class at genus~$g$ is then universally determined:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:obstruction-class-formula-intro}
\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g \in H^{2g}(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathbb{Q}),
\end{equation}
where $\lambda_g = c_g(\mathbb{E})$ is the top Chern class of the Hodge bundle.

\subsection{Universality, additivity, anti-symmetry}
\label{subsec:kappa-properties}

The three properties of~$\kappa$ that make it a topological characteristic class:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textbf{Universality.}  The obstruction class $\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g$ is determined by the genus-$1$ value alone.  This is because the clutching maps of stable curves force the higher-genus classes to be determined by the genus-$1$ curvature via the modular operad structure (Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality}).

\item \textbf{Additivity.}  For a tensor product $\cA \otimes \cB$ of chiral algebras,
\[
\kappa(\cA \otimes \cB) = \kappa(\cA) + \kappa(\cB).
\]
This follows from the factorization of the bar complex:
$\barB_X(\cA \otimes \cB) \simeq \barB_X(\cA) \otimes \barB_X(\cB)$.

\item \textbf{Anti-symmetry.}  Under Koszul duality,
\[
\kappa(\cA) + \kappa(\cA^!) = 0
\]
for affine Kac--Moody algebras (where $\cA^! = \widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{k'}$ with $k' = -k - 2h^\vee$).  More generally, $\kappa + \kappa' = 0$ holds on the Koszul locus where the Verdier involution acts as a sign change.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{The $\hat{A}$-genus generating function}
\label{subsec:ahat-genus}
\index{A-hat genus@$\hat{A}$-genus!as family index}

The generating function of the obstruction classes is the $\hat{A}$-genus.  Grothendieck--Riemann--Roch applied to the universal curve $\pi \colon \overline{\mathcal{C}}_g \to \overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ yields
\[
\operatorname{ch}\bigl(R\pi_* \barB_g(\cA)\bigr)
= \kappa(\cA) \cdot \operatorname{td}(\mathbb{E}^\vee),
\]
and the Todd class of the dual Hodge bundle produces the $\hat{A}$-series.  The full generating function is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:genus-generating-function-intro}
\sum_{g \geq 1} \mathrm{obs}_g(\cA) \cdot \hbar^{2g-2}
= \kappa(\cA) \cdot \bigl(\hat{A}(i\hbar) - 1\bigr)
= \kappa(\cA) \cdot \left(\frac{i\hbar/2}{\sinh(i\hbar/2)} - 1\right).
\end{equation}
For a free fermion, this produces the spin partition function; for the Virasoro algebra, the one-loop string amplitude.

\subsection{The spectral discriminant}
\label{subsec:spectral-discriminant-intro}
\index{spectral discriminant|textbf}

The spectral discriminant $\Delta_\cA(x) := \operatorname{disc}(P_{\cA^!}(x))$---the discriminant of the Koszul dual Hilbert series---is the first non-scalar invariant.  It is invariant under Koszul duality ($\Delta_\cA = \Delta_{\cA^!}$, Proposition~\ref{prop:discriminant-characteristic}) and under Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction (Theorem~\ref{thm:ds-bar-gf-discriminant}).

The Hilbert series of the Koszul dual is $P_{\cA^!}(x) = \sum_{n \geq 0} \dim H^n(\barB(\cA)) \cdot x^n$.  For Koszul algebras, this is the reciprocal of the Hilbert series of~$\cA$: $P_{\cA^!}(x) = 1 / P_\cA(-x)$.  The discriminant of this rational function, viewed as a polynomial in~$x$, is an integer-valued invariant that detects spectral structure invisible to the scalar~$\kappa$.

The shared discriminant $\Delta(x) = (1-3x)(1+x) = 1 - 2x - 3x^2$ across $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$, Virasoro, and $\beta\gamma$ is not coincidence: these algebras lie on the same spectral sheet of the Koszul dual parameter space.  The zeros of~$\Delta$ at $x = 1/3$ and $x = -1$ mark the transitions between bar-acyclic and bar-active sectors.


\section{The convolution algebra and the shadow Postnikov tower}
\label{sec:convolution-algebra}
\index{convolution dg Lie algebra|textbf}
\index{shadow Postnikov tower|textbf}
\index{Maurer--Cartan!universal class}

The bar complex~$\barB_X(\cA)$ is a coalgebra over the Feynman
transform $\mathrm{FCom}$ of the commutative modular operad
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-modular-operad}).  This means that the genus
tower $\{\barB^{(g)}(\cA)\}_{g \ge 0}$ is \emph{not} a sequence of
independent chain complexes but a single algebra over a modular
operad, whose structure maps are clutching, trace, and stable-graph
composition.  The formal consequence is $\partial^2 = 0$ at all
genera simultaneously.

\subsection{The modular convolution dg Lie algebra}
\label{subsec:modular-convolution-dg-lie}
\index{convolution dg Lie algebra!construction|textbf}

The organizing structure that governs this tower is the
\emph{modular convolution dg Lie algebra}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:convolution-dg-lie-intro}
\gAmod
\;:=\;
\Hom_{\Sigma}\bigl(
  \mathrm{FCom},\;
  \operatorname{End}_{\barB(\cA)}
\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $\Hom_\Sigma$ denotes the equivariant internal hom in the
category of $\mathbb{S}$-modules, and
$\operatorname{End}_{\barB(\cA)}$ is the endomorphism operad of the
bar complex (Definition~\ref{def:modular-convolution-dg-lie}).

We construct this object explicitly.  The Feynman transform $\mathrm{FCom}$ of the commutative modular operad $\mathrm{Com}$ is the modular operad whose operations at genus~$g$ with $n$ inputs are generated by stable graphs of type $(g, n)$---connected graphs~$\Gamma$ with genus assignment $g \colon V(\Gamma) \to \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ satisfying $g(\Gamma) := \sum_{v} g(v) + h^1(\Gamma) = g$ and $n$ external half-edges.  The differential on $\mathrm{FCom}$ is edge contraction: each edge of~$\Gamma$ can be contracted to produce a graph with one fewer edge and one more complex vertex.

An element $\alpha \in \gAmod$ assigns to each stable graph~$\Gamma$ of type $(g, n)$ an operation
\[
\alpha_\Gamma \colon \barB(\cA)^{\otimes n} \longrightarrow \barB(\cA),
\]
equivariantly for the automorphisms of~$\Gamma$.  The differential on~$\gAmod$ comes from the boundary stratification $\partial\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,n}$: it is the sum over all ways to contract an internal edge or to split a vertex.  The Lie bracket comes from operadic composition, which geometrically corresponds to gluing stable curves at their marked points:
\[
[\alpha, \beta]_\Gamma = \sum_{\Gamma = \Gamma_1 \cup_e \Gamma_2} \alpha_{\Gamma_1} \circ_e \beta_{\Gamma_2} - (-1)^{|\alpha||\beta|} \beta_{\Gamma_1} \circ_e \alpha_{\Gamma_2},
\]
where the sum is over all ways to decompose~$\Gamma$ by cutting an internal edge~$e$.

A Maurer--Cartan element $\alpha \in \operatorname{MC}(\gAmod)$ satisfying
\[
D\alpha + \tfrac{1}{2}[\alpha,\alpha] = 0
\]
is exactly a consistent system of genus corrections to the bar
differential, compatible with clutching.

\subsection{The modular cyclic deformation complex}
\label{subsec:def-cyc-mod-intro}
\index{modular cyclic deformation complex|textbf}

The \emph{modular cyclic deformation complex}
$\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$
(Definition~\ref{def:modular-cyclic-deformation-complex}) is the
subcomplex of~$\gAmod$ respecting the cyclic symmetry of the bar
coalgebra.  Concretely, an element of $\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$ is an equivariant map from stable graphs to endomorphisms of $\barB(\cA)$ that is invariant under the cyclic rotation of the inputs at each vertex.  The cyclic symmetry reflects the fact that the bar coalgebra carries a nondegenerate inner product (the trace on the cyclic bar complex), and the deformation complex must respect this pairing.

The differential on $\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$ is the restriction of the differential on~$\gAmod$; it decomposes as
\[
D_{\mathrm{cyc}} = D_{\mathrm{edge}} + D_{\mathrm{vertex}},
\]
where $D_{\mathrm{edge}}$ contracts internal edges of stable graphs and $D_{\mathrm{vertex}}$ splits vertices (creating new edges).  The identity $D_{\mathrm{cyc}}^2 = 0$ at the convolution level is a theorem, following from $\partial^2 = 0$ on $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,n}$ (the boundary of a boundary is empty).

\subsection{Stable-graph and planted-forest coefficient algebras}
\label{subsec:coefficient-algebras-intro}
\index{stable-graph coefficient algebra|textbf}
\index{planted-forest coefficient algebra|textbf}

The combinatorial heart of the deformation complex is controlled by two coefficient algebras.

The \emph{stable-graph coefficient algebra} $\Gmod_{\mathrm{st}}$ (Definition~\ref{def:stable-graph-coefficient-algebra}) is the graded vector space spanned by isomorphism classes of stable graphs, with the product given by edge contraction and the differential by vertex splitting.  It is the combinatorial model for the boundary stratification of~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,n}$: each stratum of the boundary corresponds to a stable graph, and the incidence relations among strata correspond to edge contractions.

The \emph{planted-forest coefficient algebra} $\Gmod_{\mathrm{pf}}$ (Definition~\ref{def:planted-forest-coefficient-algebra}) encodes residues at the codimension-$3$ strata of the Fulton--MacPherson compactification.  Its elements are planted forests---rooted trees with a distinguished root vertex---and its product is the grafting operation.  The planted-forest algebra detects the higher-order corrections that go beyond the tree-level bar differential: at arity~$r$, the $r$-valent forests contribute corrections that couple~$r$ OPE channels simultaneously.

The tropicalization theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:planted-forest-tropicalization}) identifies $\Gmod_{\mathrm{pf}}$ with the tropical limit of the logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\mathrm{FM}_n(X | D)$ on a simple normal crossing pair $(X, D)$ in the sense of Mok~\cite{Mok25}: planted forests are dual graphs of tropical curves, and grafting is degeneration.

\subsection{The shadow algebra}
\label{subsec:shadow-algebra-intro}
\index{shadow algebra|textbf}

The cohomology of the modular cyclic deformation complex,
\[
\cA^{\mathrm{sh}}
\;:=\;
H_\bullet\bigl(\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)\bigr),
\]
is the \emph{shadow algebra}
(Definition~\ref{def:shadow-algebra}): a graded commutative ring
that serves as the universal home for all finite-order modular
invariants of~$\cA$.  The ring structure comes from the operadic composition in $\gAmod$: the product of two shadows (invariants extracted from finite-order truncations of the MC element) is again a shadow.

The shadow algebra carries a natural bigrading: $\cA^{\mathrm{sh}}_{r,g}$ is the component at arity~$r$ and genus~$g$.  The arity-$2$, genus-$0$ component contains~$\kappa(\cA)$.  The arity-$3$ component contains the cubic shadow~$\mathfrak{C}(\cA)$.  The arity-$4$ component contains the quartic resonance class~$\mathfrak{Q}(\cA)$.  For a tensor product $\cA = \cA_1 \otimes \cA_2$, the shadow algebra factorizes: $\cA^{\mathrm{sh}} \cong \cA_1^{\mathrm{sh}} \otimes \cA_2^{\mathrm{sh}}$.  When~$\cA$ has a mass gap (the bar complex is acyclic), the shadow algebra collapses to $\cA^{\mathrm{sh}} \cong \mathbb{C}$: mass gap equals trivial shadow algebra.

\subsection{The shadow Postnikov tower}
\label{subsec:shadow-postnikov-tower-intro}

The five theorems produce a Maurer--Cartan element
\begin{equation}\label{eq:universal-MC}
\Theta_\cA \;\in\;
\operatorname{MC}\!\left(
  \Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)
  \;\widehat{\otimes}\;
  R\Gamma\!\left(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,\bullet},\,
  \mathbb{Q}\right)
\right)
\end{equation}
(Theorems~\ref{thm:universal-MC}
and~\ref{thm:universal-theta}, proved by
Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-full-resolution}).
The full class~$\Theta_\cA$ exists by the bar-intrinsic construction (Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}); it is the all-arity limit of a
constructive tower of finite-order truncations: the \emph{shadow
Postnikov tower}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:shadow-tower-intro}
\Theta_\cA^{\le 2}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\Theta_\cA^{\le 3}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\Theta_\cA^{\le 4}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\cdots
\end{equation}
Each truncation $\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ is a graph sum through
arity~$r$ in the cyclic deformation complex, with an obstruction
class $o_{r+1}(\cA) \in \Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$ measuring the
failure of extension to arity $r+1$.

Explicitly, the truncation $\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ is defined as follows.  At arity~$2$, $\Theta_\cA^{\le 2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \eta \otimes \Lambda$, where $\eta$ is the logarithmic propagator and $\Lambda$ is the Hodge class.  At arity~$3$, one solves the equation $D_{\mathrm{cyc}}(\Theta_\cA^{\le 3}) + \frac{1}{2}[\Theta_\cA^{\le 2}, \Theta_\cA^{\le 2}]^{(3)} = 0$ for the arity-$3$ component, where the superscript $(3)$ projects to arity~$3$.  The obstruction to solving this equation is $o_3(\cA) \in H^*(\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod},(3)}(\cA))$.  The process iterates: at each arity~$r$, one extends the previous truncation by solving a linear equation whose obstruction is $o_{r+1}(\cA)$.

The proved content of this
monograph is the finite-order engine:
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\textbf{Arity} & \textbf{Invariant} & \textbf{Status} \\ \hline
$r = 2$ & $\kappa(\cA)$ (scalar modular characteristic)
  & proved, all families \\[2pt]
$r = 3$ & $\mathfrak{C}(\cA)$ (cubic shadow)
  & proved, computed for standard landscape \\[2pt]
$r = 4$ & $\mathfrak{Q}(\cA)$ (quartic resonance class)
  & proved, with clutching law \\[2pt]
$r = \infty$ & $\Theta_\cA$ (full universal class)
  & proved (bar-intrinsic, Thm~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic})
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
The obstruction $o_3(\cA)$ vanishes for any abelian chiral algebra
(the shadow tower terminates at arity~$2$ for Heisenberg).  For
affine Kac--Moody algebras $o_4(\cA) = 0$ (termination at
arity~$3$).  For the $\beta\gamma$ system, $o_5(\cA) = 0$
(termination at arity~$4$).  For Virasoro and $\mathcal{W}_N$
algebras, $o_5(\cA) \neq 0$: the shadow tower is infinite, and the
quintic obstruction is forced.  These facts are the shadow
archetypes, computed in every example chapter of Part~II
(Chapters~\ref{chap:free-fields}--\ref{chap:yangians}).

The quartic resonance class carries a clutching law under
stable-curve degeneration
(Theorem~\ref{thm:nms-clutching-law-modular-resonance}): when a genus-$g$ curve
degenerates to a nodal union $\Sigma_{g_1} \cup \Sigma_{g_2}$, the
quartic class satisfies a factorization identity determined by the
tree correction from Mok's logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson
degeneration.  For the Virasoro algebra, the quartic contact
invariant is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:quartic-contact-vir-intro}
Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}} = \frac{10}{c(5c+22)},
\end{equation}
with genus-$1$ Hessian correction
$\delta^{(1)}_{H,\mathrm{Vir}} = 120/[c^2(5c+22)] \cdot x^2$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:nms-virasoro-quartic-explicit}).

\subsection{The all-arity master equation}
\label{subsec:all-arity-master-eq}

The entire shadow tower admits a uniform description: the obstruction at each arity satisfies the \emph{all-arity master equation}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:all-arity-master-eq-intro}
\nabla_H(\mathrm{Sh}_r) + o^{(r)} = 0 \qquad \text{for all } r \geq 3,
\end{equation}
where $\nabla_H$ is the covariant derivative in the Hodge direction, $\mathrm{Sh}_r$ is the shadow at arity~$r$, and $o^{(r)}$ is the obstruction class.  The master equation at arity~$r$ is the Maurer--Cartan equation projected to arity~$r$ in the cyclic deformation complex.

The genus loop $\Lambda_P \colon \operatorname{Sym}^r \to \operatorname{Sym}^{r-2}$ governs the descent from arity~$r$ to arity $r-2$ by trace contraction.  The kernel of~$\Lambda_P$ consists of the primitive shadows---the irreducible pieces that cannot be obtained by contraction from higher arity.  The primitive-shadow decomposition is the modular analogue of the primitive-element decomposition in a Hopf algebra.


\section{The modular Koszul object}
\label{sec:modular-koszul-object}

A \emph{modular Koszul chiral algebra}
(Definition~\ref{def:modular-koszul-chiral}) is a factorization
algebra on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$ whose duality data extends over
the modular operad, with curvature classes compatible under
clutching, Verdier duality, and trace.  Theorems~A--D are four
aspects of this single structure.

In addition to its Koszul dual~$\cA^!$, such an object carries
$\Theta_\cA$~\eqref{eq:universal-MC}.  The genus-$1$ coefficient
$\kappa(\cA)$ is not the fundamental concept; it is the first
characteristic number of~$\Theta_\cA$.  The full package
\[
\mathcal{C}_\cA
=
(\Theta_\cA, \kappa(\cA), \Delta_\cA, \Pi_\cA, \mathcal{H}_\cA)
\]
is a hierarchy of increasing refinement
(Definition~\ref{def:full-modular-package}).
Theorem~\ref{thm:volume-one-concrete-modular-datum} packages this
hierarchy into explicit H-, M-, and S-level data.

The genus tower $\{\barB^{(g)}(\cA)\}_{g \geq 0}$ is a
Maurer--Cartan deformation of the genus-$0$ bar differential
inside a cyclic $L_\infty$ algebra
(Definition~\ref{def:cyclic-deformation-elementary}).  The proved
families include free fields
(Proposition~\ref{prop:standard-examples-modular-koszul}), affine
Kac--Moody at all genera
(Theorem~\ref{thm:pbw-allgenera-km},
Corollary~\ref{cor:unconditional-allgenera-km}), Virasoro
(Theorem~\ref{thm:pbw-allgenera-virasoro},
Corollary~\ref{cor:unconditional-allgenera-virasoro}), and
principal $\mathcal{W}$-algebras
(Theorem~\ref{thm:pbw-allgenera-principal-w},
Corollary~\ref{cor:unconditional-allgenera-principal-w}).

The hierarchy already explains the shared discriminant, the
complementarity sums $c + c' = K$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:central-charge-complementarity}), the universal
genus generating function
(Theorem~\ref{thm:universal-generating-function}), and the
$\hat{A}$-genus appearance.

Scalar saturation---the identity
$\Theta_\cA^{\min} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \eta \otimes \Lambda$
(Corollary~\ref{cor:scalar-saturation})---holds whenever
$\dim H^2_{\mathrm{cyc}}(\cA,\cA) = 1$, by graded antisymmetry.
This is verified at generic levels by
Theorem~\ref{thm:cyclic-rigidity-generic} and on the standard
landscape by Corollary~\ref{cor:effective-quadruple}.
Theorem~\ref{thm:algebraic-family-rigidity} extends it
to all non-critical levels for algebraic families with rational
OPE coefficients;
Conjecture~\ref{conj:scalar-saturation-universality} addresses
the residual non-algebraic-family case.

\begin{definition}[Modular homotopy theory on a smooth curve]
\label{def:modular-homotopy-theory-intro}
\index{modular homotopy theory!definition|textbf}
A \emph{modular homotopy theory} for a chiral algebra~$\cA$
on a smooth projective curve~$X$ consists of the following data:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Bar-cobar adjunction.}
  An adjunction
  $\barB_X \dashv \Omega_X$ on
  $D^{\mathrm{co}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))$, the coderived
  category of factorization coalgebras on
  $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$, with $\barB_X(\cA)$ nilpotent at
  genus~$0$ and curved at higher genus.
\item \emph{Modular functoriality.}
  Functoriality of the bar family
  $\{\barB^{(g)}(\cA)\}_{g \geq 0}$ over the modular operad
  $\{\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,n}\}$, intertwined with Verdier
  duality:
  $\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}\barB^{(g)}_X(\cA)
  \simeq \barB^{(g)}_X(\cA^!)$.
\item \emph{Universal Maurer--Cartan class.}
  A class
  $\Theta_\cA \in
  \operatorname{MC}(\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)
  \;\widehat{\otimes}\;
  R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,\bullet},\,
  \mathbb{Q}))$
  assembling the genus tower into a single integrability datum,
  compatible with clutching, trace, and duality.
\item \emph{Complementary Lagrangians.}
  A $(-(3g{-}3))$-shifted symplectic form on the moduli of
  genus-$g$ bar families, with
  $Q_g(\cA)$ and $Q_g(\cA^!)$ as complementary Lagrangians.
\item \emph{Chain-level modular functor.}
  The Feynman transform assembles the genus-graded bar complex
  $\barB^{\mathrm{full}}(\cA) = \bigoplus_g \barB^{(g)}(\cA)$
  into a homotopy-coherent modular functor
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-modular-functor}): to each
  surface~$\Sigma_{g,n}$ a cochain complex, to each degeneration a
  chain map, to each consistency relation a chain homotopy.
  Passing to $H^0$ at integrable level recovers conformal blocks.
  The scalar genus tower
  $F_g = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}$
  (Theorem~D) is the first Chern class of the determinant line
  bundle of this functor---the trace of the construction, not
  the construction itself.
\end{enumerate}
Theorems~A--D are the first four assertions; item~(v) is the
structure they build toward, proved in
Theorem~\ref{thm:chain-modular-functor}.
The formal axiomatic treatment is
Definition~\ref{def:modular-koszul-homotopy}; the concrete dg model is
Definition~\ref{def:modular-koszul-chiral}.
\end{definition}


\subsection{Completion kinematics versus modular dynamics}
\label{subsec:kinematics-dynamics-intro}
\index{completion kinematics|textbf}
\index{modular dynamics|textbf}
\index{primitive cumulant!in introduction}

The package~$\mathcal{C}_\cA$ encodes the \emph{modular dynamics}
of~$\cA$: the OPE-dependent deformation data $D_\cA$,
$\kappa(\cA)$, $\Theta_\cA$, and the dg-shifted Yangian kernel
$r_\cA(z)$.  But the completed bar-cobar theory also carries a
second, independent axis that we call \emph{completion kinematics}:
invariants determined solely by the vacuum character $\chi_\cA(q)$,
without reference to OPE coefficients or curvature.

The kinematic data are extracted from the \emph{primitive cumulant
quotient} $Q(\cA) = \bar{\cA}/\partial\bar{\cA}$
(Definition~\ref{def:primitive-cumulant-quotient}) and its generating
series
\begin{equation}\label{eq:primitive-generating-intro}
G_\cA(t) = \frac{1-t}{t}\bigl(\chi_\cA(t) - 1\bigr),
\end{equation}
which is canonical: the non-derivative sector is recovered directly
from the character.  The \emph{completion Hilbert series}
$H_\cA(t) = G_\cA/(1 - G_\cA)$ counts reduced-weight bar words, and
the \emph{completion entropy}
$h_K(\cA) = \log(\rho_\cA^{-1})$
(where $G_\cA(\rho_\cA) = 1$) is the exponential growth rate of
finite bar windows
(Definitions~\ref{def:completion-hilbert-series}--\ref{def:completion-entropy}).

The two axes are genuinely independent.  The Virasoro involution
$c \mapsto 26 - c$ changes the dynamic shadow
$\mathrm{Vir}_c \leftrightarrow \mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$ while
preserving the kinematic data: both sides have the same
$G_\cA(t)$ and the same entropy.  The shadow depth classification
(G/L/C/M) measures \emph{dynamic} complexity; the completion entropy
$h_K$ measures \emph{kinematic} complexity.  Both are needed.

The \emph{primitive defect series}
$\Delta_\cA(t) = G_\cA(t) - G_\cA^{\mathrm{naive}}(t)$
(Definition~\ref{def:primitive-defect-series})
detects nonquadraticity: it is zero for Heisenberg, affine
Kac--Moody, and $\beta\gamma$ (all quadratic or free-like), and
nonzero starting at $t^3$ for Virasoro and $\mathcal{W}_N$ ($N
\geq 3$).  The nonzero defect means the completed dual coalgebra is
forced to enlarge its primitive sector beyond the declared
generators---the correct dual is the \emph{cumulant coalgebra}
$\operatorname{Cum}_c(\cA) = \hat{T}^c(sQ(\cA)^\vee)$, not the
tensor coalgebra on the original generators alone
(Conjecture~\ref{conj:cumulant-recognition}).

\section{The standard families and their shadow archetypes}
\label{sec:standard-families-intro}
\index{shadow archetypes|textbf}
\index{standard families|textbf}

The abstract machinery of the shadow Postnikov tower acquires its full meaning only when exhibited on the standard families of chiral algebras.  Each family determines a \emph{shadow archetype}: the termination pattern of its tower and the qualitative shape of its modular invariants.  In parallel, the completion kinematics
(\S\ref{subsec:kinematics-dynamics-intro}) assigns to each family a
primitive spectrum~$G_\cA(t)$, an entropy~$h_K(\cA)$, and a
defect~$\Delta_\cA(t)$.  These two classifications---dynamic
(shadow depth) and kinematic (completion entropy)---are orthogonal:
a family can have finite shadow depth and zero defect (Heisenberg,
Kac--Moody, $\beta\gamma$) or infinite shadow depth and nonzero
defect (Virasoro, $\mathcal{W}_N$), and the entropy varies
continuously within each class.

\subsection{The Heisenberg atom: Gaussian archetype, termination at arity~$2$}
\label{subsec:heisenberg-atom-intro}
\index{Heisenberg algebra!as atom of the programme|textbf}
\index{Gaussian archetype}

The Heisenberg algebra $\mathcal{H}_k$ is the place where the
entire programme collapses to a single glance.  Its OPE is
$\alpha(z)\alpha(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$: a double pole, no simple pole,
no Lie bracket.  From this, everything follows by residue
extraction on~$\overline{C}_n(X)$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Bar complex.}
The bar differential vanishes identically ($d_{\mathrm{bracket}} =
0$); only $d_{\mathrm{curvature}}$ acts, producing scalars.
The bar cohomology is $H^p(\barB(\mathcal{H}_k)) = \C$ in bar
degree~$1$, concentrated in the top conformal weight stratum.
The Koszul dual is $\mathcal{H}_k^! = \mathrm{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*)
= \mathcal{H}_{-k}$ (with the sign flip $k \to -k$, not $k \to
1/k$; see Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Genus tower.}
The period correction at genus~$g$ couples to the Hodge bundle via
$\kappa(\mathcal{H}_k) = 1/k$.  The obstruction class is
$\mathrm{obs}_g = (1/k)\lambda_g$.  The genus expansion of the
partition function reproduces the Dedekind eta function:
$\sum_g F_g \hbar^{2g-2} = -\log \eta(\tau)/k$, expanded in
Eisenstein series (Chapter~\ref{ch:genus-expansions}).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Shadow tower.}
The Heisenberg shadow tower terminates at arity~$2$: $o_3(\mathcal
H_k) = 0$ (by abelian rigidity), so the cubic shadow vanishes, the
quartic shadow vanishes, and $\Theta_{\mathcal{H}_k} =
\kappa(\mathcal{H}_k) \cdot \eta \otimes \Lambda$ is exact.  This
is the Gaussian archetype: the full nonlinear MC element is already
determined by its linear leading term.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Completion kinematics.}
The vacuum character $\chi_{\mathcal{H}_k}(q) = \prod_{n \geq 1}(1-q^n)^{-1}$ gives $G_{\mathcal{H}_k}(t) = t$ (one primitive of reduced weight~$1$, no others).  The completion entropy is $h_K(\mathcal{H}_k) = 0$: the completed bar complex does not grow.  The primitive defect $\Delta = 0$: no nonquadratic enlargement is needed.  The Heisenberg algebra is kinematically trivial---the simplest point in the completion phase diagram.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Complementarity.}
$Q_g(\mathcal{H}_k) \oplus Q_g(\mathcal{H}_{-k}) \cong
H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \C)$: the positive- and negative-level
Heisenberg algebras fill complementary Lagrangians.  The Koszul
conductor is $K(\mathcal{H}_k) = c(k) + c(-k) = 2$ (central
charges $c = 1$ at any nonzero~$k$).

\subsection{Affine Kac--Moody: Lie/tree archetype, termination at arity~$3$}
\label{subsec:kac-moody-atom-intro}
\index{Kac--Moody algebra!shadow archetype}
\index{Feigin--Frenkel involution}

The affine Kac--Moody algebra $\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k$ has OPE
$J^a(z) J^b(w) \sim k\,\delta^{ab}/(z-w)^2 + f^{ab}{}_c J^c(w)/(z-w)$.
The simple pole introduces a nonzero bracket component $d_{\mathrm{bracket}}$, and $d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2 \neq 0$ (proved for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ at all $2048$ sign conventions).  The full bar differential $d_{\barB} = d_{\mathrm{bracket}} + d_{\mathrm{curvature}}$ satisfies $d_{\barB}^2 = 0$ by the Borcherds identity.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Koszul dual and Feigin--Frenkel.}
The Koszul dual is $\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{k'}$ with $k' = -k - 2h^\vee$, where $h^\vee$ is the dual Coxeter number.  This is the Feigin--Frenkel involution: Koszul duality for affine Kac--Moody algebras \emph{is} the level-reflection symmetry.  The Sugawara construction, which provides the stress-energy tensor $T(z) = \frac{1}{2(k+h^\vee)} \sum_a :J^a J^a:(z)$, is undefined at the critical level $k = -h^\vee$ (not ``$c$ diverges''; rather, the Sugawara vector is not in the completion of $U(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{-h^\vee})$).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Shadow tower: arity~$3$ termination.}
The modular characteristic is $\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k) = \dim(\mathfrak{g}) \cdot (k+h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$, with anti-symmetry $\kappa(k) + \kappa(k') = 0$.  The cubic shadow $\mathfrak{C}(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k)$ is nonzero (it detects the Lie bracket), but $o_4 = 0$: the tower terminates at arity~$3$.  This is the Lie/tree archetype---the shadow tower captures exactly the tree-level Lie-algebraic data and stops.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Completion kinematics.}
The vacuum character gives $G_{\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k}(t) = (\dim\mathfrak{g})\,t/(1-t)$: there are $\dim\mathfrak{g}$ primitives at each reduced weight (the current derivatives $\partial^r J^a$).  The Koszul radius is $\rho = 1/(\dim\mathfrak{g}+1)$ and the entropy is $h_K = \log(\dim\mathfrak{g}+1)$.  For $\mathfrak{sl}_2$: $h_K \approx 1.10$, larger than Virasoro ($h_K \approx 0.57$), reflecting the multicomponent nature of the bar complex despite the simpler shadow depth.  The defect $\Delta = 0$: the declared generators $J^a$ already span the full primitive sector.

\subsection{The $\beta\gamma$ system: contact/quartic archetype, termination at arity~$4$}
\label{subsec:betagamma-atom-intro}
\index{betagamma system@$\beta\gamma$ system!shadow archetype}

The $\beta\gamma$ system has OPE $\beta(z)\gamma(w) \sim 1/(z-w)$, a simple pole between fields of complementary conformal weight.  The quartic contact invariant $\mu_{\beta\gamma} = \langle \eta, m_3(\eta, \eta, \eta) \rangle$ vanishes by rank-one abelian rigidity (Corollary~\ref{cor:nms-betagamma-mu-vanishing}): $o_5 = 0$, and the tower terminates at arity~$4$.  This is the contact archetype---the first system where the quartic shadow is nonzero but the quintic obstruction vanishes.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Completion kinematics.}
The two generators $\beta$, $\gamma$ of weights $1$ and $0$ give
$G_{\beta\gamma}(t) = t/(1-t)$ (one primitive per reduced weight).
The Koszul radius is $\rho = 1/2$, the entropy $h_K = \log 2
\approx 0.693$, and the defect $\Delta = 0$.  Despite its richer
shadow depth (arity~$4$ vs arity~$3$ for Kac--Moody), the $\beta\gamma$
system is kinematically simpler than any non-abelian affine algebra.

\subsection{Virasoro and $\mathcal{W}_N$: mixed archetype, infinite tower}
\label{subsec:virasoro-w-atom-intro}
\index{Virasoro algebra!shadow archetype}
\index{W-algebra@$\mathcal{W}$-algebra!shadow archetype}

The Virasoro algebra has OPE $T(z)\,T(w) \sim c/2(z-w)^4 + 2T(w)/(z-w)^2 + \partial T(w)/(z-w)$---a quartic pole, the highest in the standard landscape.  The Koszul dual is $\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$ (self-dual at $c = 13$, not $c = 26$).

The shadow tower is infinite: $o_5(\mathrm{Vir}_c) \neq 0$, and the quintic obstruction is forced.  This is the mixed archetype: both cubic and quartic shadows are present, and the tower never terminates.  The quartic contact invariant
$Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}} = 10/[c(5c+22)]$
is the explicit measure of the quartic interaction.  The poles at $c = 0$ and $c = -22/5$ are precisely the minimal model values $c_{2,5}$ and $c_{2,3}$, suggesting deep connections to the representation theory of the Virasoro algebra at these exceptional points.

The $\mathcal{W}_N$ algebras share the infinite-tower behavior for $N \geq 3$.  The $\mathcal{W}_3$ algebra is the first case where the filtered-curved distinction matters: $\mathcal{W}_3$ carries a truly filtered (not merely curved) $A_\infty$ structure, because the composite field $\Lambda = :(TT): - \frac{3}{10}\partial^2 T$ introduces a quartic pole that is not a scalar multiple of the identity.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Completion kinematics.}
This is where the kinematic axis reveals structure invisible to
shadow depth alone.  For generic Virasoro, $G_{\mathrm{Vir}}(t)
= \tfrac{1-t}{t}\bigl(\prod_{n \geq 2}(1-t^n)^{-1} - 1\bigr)$
gives primitive multiplicities $g_r = 1, 0, 1, 0, 2, 0, 3, 1, \ldots$
The defect $\Delta_{\mathrm{Vir}}(t) = t^3 + 2t^5 + 3t^7 + \cdots
\neq 0$: generic Virasoro has infinitely many primitive cumulants
beyond the stress tensor~$T$.  The entropy is
$h_K(\mathrm{Vir}) \approx 0.567$.

For the $\mathcal{W}_N$ tower, the entropy increases monotonically
(Proposition~\ref{prop:wn-entropy-ladder}):
\[
h_K(\mathcal{W}_2) \approx 0.567
\;<\; h_K(\mathcal{W}_3) \approx 0.772
\;<\; h_K(\mathcal{W}_4) \approx 0.835
\;<\; \cdots
\;<\; h_K(\mathcal{W}_\infty) \approx 0.872.
\]
The $\mathcal{W}_3$ jump from $0.567$ to $0.772$ is the kinematic
signature of the composite field~$\Lambda$, which forces the
corrected Koszul recursion $h_q = 2h_{q-1} + h_{q-2} + h_{q-3}$
in place of the naive $h_q = 2h_{q-1} + h_{q-2}$.
At the limit $N = \infty$, the character
$\chi_{\mathcal{W}_\infty}(q) = \prod_{n \geq 2}(1 - q^n)^{-(n-1)}$
admits a MacMahon factorization
(Proposition~\ref{prop:wn-entropy-ladder}), connecting the
$\mathcal{W}_\infty$ completion frontier to the combinatorics of
plane partitions.

The same-family shadow $\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$ coexists with the
nonzero defect because it is a \emph{dynamic} shadow, not the full
kinematic dual.  Kinematically, the completed dual coalgebra has
infinitely many primitive generators; dynamically, the scalar trace
$\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c) \cdot \lambda_g$ already reproduces the
partner family $\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$.  The full H-level kinematic
identification is the residual MC4 task
(\S\ref{subsec:completion-kinematics-programme} of the
concordance).

\subsection{The Yangian: $\Eone$-chiral, $R$-matrix inversion}
\label{subsec:yangian-atom-intro}
\index{Yangian!shadow archetype}
\index{R-matrix@$R$-matrix!inversion under Koszul duality}

The Yangian $Y(\mathfrak{g})$ is fundamentally different from the previous families: it is an $\Eone$-chiral algebra (Definition~\ref{def:e1-chiral}), not $\Einf$-chiral.  Its bar complex uses \emph{ordered} configuration spaces $\operatorname{Conf}_n^{\mathrm{ord}}(X)$ rather than unordered ones, and Verdier duality acts by $R$-matrix inversion $R(u) \mapsto R(u)^{-1}$ rather than by type exchange $\cA \mapsto \cA^!$.

The Yangian is generated by currents $T^{(1)}(z), T^{(2)}(z), \ldots$ with spectral parameter dependence, and its OPE involves the rational $R$-matrix $R(u-v)$.  The Drinfeld--Kohno bridge (Theorem~\ref{thm:factorization-dk-eval}) relates the chiral Koszul duality of affine Kac--Moody algebras at the top of the DK square~\eqref{eq:dk-intro} to the quantum group $R$-matrix inversion at the bottom, with the Kazhdan--Lusztig equivalences as vertical arrows.

The modular dg-shifted Yangian $\mathcal{Y}^{\mathrm{dg,mod}}_T$ (Definition~\ref{def:modular-yangian-pro}) is the pronilpotent completion of the convolution dg Lie algebra for the Yangian.  The modular $R$-matrix $R^{\mathrm{mod}}_T(z; \hbar)$ is a Maurer--Cartan element in this completed algebra.

\subsection{Lattice vertex algebras: braided, curvature-braiding orthogonal}
\label{subsec:lattice-atom-intro}
\index{lattice vertex algebra!shadow archetype}
\index{curvature-braiding orthogonality}

The lattice vertex algebra $V_\Lambda$ associated to an even lattice $\Lambda$ carries an $\Eone$ sublattice structure visible through screening operators.  The key structural result is curvature-braiding orthogonality: the modular characteristic $\kappa(V_\Lambda) = \operatorname{rank}(\Lambda)$ is independent of the $2$-cocycle that determines the braiding (Theorem~\ref{thm:lattice:curvature-braiding-orthogonal}).  The curvature sees only the rank of the lattice; the braiding sees only the cocycle.  These two pieces of data are orthogonal in a precise sense: they live in complementary summands of the deformation complex.


\section{Strictness as a shadow: the homotopy upgrade}
\label{sec:homotopy-upgrade}
\index{convolution!strict vs.\ homotopy|textbf}
\index{homotopy transfer theorem!role in the programme}

The convolution dg Lie algebra~$\gAmod$
of~\eqref{eq:convolution-dg-lie-intro} is a \emph{strict model}:
its differential and bracket are defined on the nose, not up to
homotopy.  But it is only the first visible layer of a richer
homotopy-coherent package.  Write
\begin{align}
\Convstr(\mathrm{FCom},\, \operatorname{End}_{\barB(\cA)})
  &\quad\text{--- the strict dg Lie convolution model,}
  \label{eq:convstr-intro}
\\
\Convinf(\mathrm{FCom},\, \operatorname{End}_{\barB(\cA)})
  &\quad\text{--- the homotopy-invariant $L_\infty$ convolution.}
  \label{eq:convinf-intro}
\end{align}
The two are related by a quasi-isomorphism of $L_\infty$ algebras
(a consequence of the homotopy transfer theorem,
Chapter~\ref{app:homotopy-transfer}).  Their Maurer--Cartan moduli
coincide; hence every MC element of~$\Convstr$ lifts canonically to
one of~$\Convinf$.  But the two packages are not interchangeable:
the $L_\infty$ structure of~$\Convinf$ remembers higher homotopies
that $\Convstr$ does not see, and these homotopies are essential for
transfer, formality, and gauge equivalence.

The distinction matters throughout the monograph.  The bar-cobar
adjunction (Theorem~A) preserves quasi-isomorphisms \emph{because}
it is a quantum $L_\infty$ functor: it respects the full homotopy
package, not merely the strict dg structure.  The homotopy transfer
theorem (Chapter~\ref{app:homotopy-transfer}) produces transferred
$A_\infty$ and $L_\infty$ structures on bar cohomology; these
transferred structures carry the information that would be lost by
passing to a strict model.  When the monograph writes a formula at
the strict dg level, the reader should understand it as the leading
term of a homotopy-coherent datum---just as the scalar~$\kappa(\cA)$
is the leading term of the shadow tower~$\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$.

The same principle applies at the modular level.  The full modular
deformation object is
\[
\Definfmod(\cA)
\;:=\;
\Convinf\bigl(\mathrm{FCom},\,
\operatorname{End}_{\barB(\cA)}\bigr)
\;\widehat{\otimes}\;
R\Gamma\bigl(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,\bullet},\, \mathbb{Q}\bigr),
\]
an $L_\infty$ algebra whose MC elements are complete genus-expansion
data compatible with clutching, trace, and duality up to coherent
homotopy.  The strict model~$\gAmod$ is a chart on this larger
formal space.  Theorem~B (bar-cobar inversion) and Theorem~C
(complementarity) are proved at the strict level and stated at the
homotopy level; the passage between them is guaranteed by the
contractibility of the space of models
(Proposition~\ref{prop:model-independence}).

\begin{remark}[Structural consequences of the homotopy upgrade]
\label{rem:structural-consequences-homotopy}
\index{homotopy upgrade!structural consequences}
Four structural consequences of the homotopy perspective recur
throughout the volume.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item Every strict-model formula (convolution dg~Lie bracket,
  curved bar differential, finite obstruction class, spectral
  discriminant) is a projection of
  $\Theta_\cA \in \MC(\Convinf)$.  The strict model computes
  the correct answer because it is a faithful chart on the larger
  formal space, not because the $L_\infty$ data are dispensable.
\item Genus, clutching, and gluing enter at the chain level---in the
  bar and cobar constructions, in the boundary stratification of
  configuration spaces, and in the completed deformation
  package---rather than as cohomological decorations applied post~hoc.
\item The standard families (Heisenberg, $\beta\gamma$, affine
  Kac--Moody, Virasoro, $\mathcal{W}_N$, Yangians, lattice and
  elliptic) serve as diagnostic benchmarks: each activates a
  different stratum of the homotopy machinery, and their shadow
  archetypes (Gaussian, Lie/tree, contact, mixed) are the
  finite-order witnesses of the transferred $L_\infty$ operations.
\item The shadow Postnikov tower is not an approximation scheme but a
  faithful finite-order image of $\Theta_\cA$: each truncation
  $\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ captures genuinely new modular invariants
  ($\kappa$ at $r{=}2$, $\mathfrak{C}$ at $r{=}3$,
  $\mathfrak{Q}$ at $r{=}4$) that would be invisible at any lower
  order.
\end{enumerate}
\end{remark}


\section{The three pillars}
\label{sec:three-pillars-intro}
\index{three-pillar architecture|textbf}
\index{Malikov--Schechtman}
\index{Robert-Nicoud--Wierstra}
\index{Mok}

The algebraic engine rests on three foundational pillars, each representing a recent preprint that forces an upgrade at a different level of the theory.  These pillars are not merely cited as references; they supply structural theorems that the monograph absorbs and extends.

\subsection{Pillar A: homotopy chiral algebras (MS24)}
\label{subsec:pillar-a}

The primitive local object is not a strict chiral algebra but a \emph{homotopy chiral algebra} ($\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$) in the sense of Malikov--Schechtman~\cite{MS24}.  A homotopy chiral algebra carries an $A_\infty$ structure whose higher operations $m_n$ encode the failure of associativity at the OPE level.  The strict chiral algebra~$\cA$ appears only after rectification: it is the $H^0$ of a $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$ structure, which in turn projects to the Poisson vertex algebra (PVA) or coisson shadow at the classical level.

The identification theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:cech-hca}):\ $B(\cA)$ is naturally a $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebra.  The bar complex of a chiral algebra carries a homotopy chiral algebra structure, not merely a dg coalgebra structure.  This upgrades the bar construction from an object in the category of dg coalgebras to one in the richer category of homotopy chiral algebras.

\subsection{Pillar B: filtered convolution $sL_\infty$ algebras (RNW19+Val16)}
\label{subsec:pillar-b}

The universal deformation machine is the filtered convolution $sL_\infty$-algebra $\operatorname{hom}_\alpha(C, A)$ of Robert-Nicoud--Wierstra~\cite{RNW19} and Vallette~\cite{Val16}.  The deformation space of a twisting morphism $\alpha \colon C \to A$ is the Maurer--Cartan $\infty$-groupoid of this $sL_\infty$-algebra, and the dg Lie algebra is its strict model.

A key constraint: $\operatorname{hom}_\alpha$ extends to $\infty$-morphisms in either slot separately but \emph{not} both simultaneously.  There is a no-bifunctor obstruction: the convolution construction is functorial in $C$ and in $A$ individually, but the combined functoriality fails at the $\infty$-categorical level (RNW19, Theorem~6.1).  This has a direct consequence for the MC3 categorical lift: the lift from character-level generation to categorical generation must proceed one slot at a time.

The identification theorem (Corollary~\ref{cor:theta-twisting-morphism}):\ $\Theta_\cA \in \operatorname{MC}(\operatorname{hom}_\alpha) \cong \operatorname{Tw}_\alpha$---the universal MC element is a twisting morphism.

\subsection{Pillar C: logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson (Mok25)}
\label{subsec:pillar-c}

The global collision geometry is the logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\mathrm{FM}_n(X | D)$ on a simple normal crossing pair $(X, D)$ in the sense of Mok~\cite{Mok25}.  The classical FM compactification is the special case $D = \emptyset$.

The tropicalization theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:planted-forest-tropicalization}): $\Gmod_{\mathrm{pf}} = \operatorname{Trop}(\mathrm{FM}_n(\mathbb{C} | D))$---the planted-forest coefficient algebra is the tropical limit of the log FM compactification.  Planted forests are dual graphs of tropical curves, and grafting is degeneration.  The semistable degeneration formula for log FM spaces yields the clutching laws for the quartic resonance class (Theorem~\ref{thm:nms-clutching-law-modular-resonance}).

\subsection{Eleven identification theorems}
\label{subsec:eleven-identifications}
\index{identification theorems}

The three pillars yield eleven identification theorems, nine proved and two structural:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\arabic*)}]
\item $\gAmod = \operatorname{hom}_\alpha(\mathrm{Com}^{!}_{\mathrm{ch}},\, \mathcal{P}^{\mathrm{ch}})$: the convolution algebra is the twisting hom (Theorem~\ref{thm:convolution-master-identification}).
\item $\Theta_\cA \in \operatorname{MC}(\operatorname{hom}_\alpha) \cong \operatorname{Tw}_\alpha$: the universal MC element is a twisting morphism (Corollary~\ref{cor:theta-twisting-morphism}).
\item $\Gmod_{\mathrm{pf}} = \operatorname{Trop}(\mathrm{FM}_n(\mathbb{C} | D))$: planted forests are tropical curves (Theorem~\ref{thm:planted-forest-tropicalization}).
\item $B(\cA)$ is a $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebra (Theorem~\ref{thm:cech-hca}).
\item $\barB_\kappa \dashv \Omega_\kappa$ is a Quillen equivalence (Theorem~\ref{thm:quillen-equivalence-chiral}).
\item $\cA^{\mathrm{sh}}$ is a homotopy invariant (Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-homotopy-invariance}).
\item The one-slot obstruction constrains MC3: the categorical lift must proceed one slot at a time (RNW19, Theorem~6.1).
\item $F_n = o_n$: the secondary Borcherds operations of~\cite{MS24} at arity $n \geq 3$ coincide with the shadow tower obstruction classes (Proposition~\ref{prop:borcherds-shadow-identification}).
\item The quartic clutching law has a log-geometric derivation via Mok's degeneration formula~\cite[Corollary~5.3.4]{Mok25} (Construction~\ref{constr:arity4-degeneration}).
\item The log clutching conjecture is proved: Theorem~\ref{conj:log-clutching-degeneration} is established by~\cite[Corollary~5.3.4]{Mok25}.
\item The ambient $D^2 = 0$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:ambient-d-squared-zero}) is proved via the log-FM normal-crossings result~\cite[Theorem~3.3.1(1)]{Mok25}.
\end{enumerate}


\section{The total space}
\label{sec:modular-homotopy-theory}
\index{modular homotopy theory|textbf}
\index{total space|textbf}

The nilpotence-periodicity correspondence becomes geometric in the
total space of the universal configuration fibration:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:total-fibration-intro}
\pi \colon \overline{C}_n(\mathcal{C}_g) \;\longrightarrow\;
\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g.
\end{equation}
Its fiber over $[\Sigma_g]$ is $\overline{C}_n(\Sigma_g)$.  The
boundary has two types of divisors:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item \emph{Collision divisors} $D_I$ (vertical): marked points
  collide on a fixed smooth curve.  These produce the bar
  differential.
\item \emph{Degeneration divisors} $\delta_\Gamma$ (horizontal):
  the curve acquires a node.  These produce the period corrections
  that restore~$\Dg{g}^{\,2} = 0$.
\end{enumerate}
At genus~$0$, only type~(a) exists and the Arnold relation alone
gives $\dzero^2 = 0$.  At genus~$g \geq 1$, both coexist.

\subsection{The Hodge bundle and the propagator}
\label{subsec:hodge-deformation-engine}

The Hodge bundle $\mathbb{E} = R^1\pi_*\mathcal{O}_{\mathcal{C}_g}$
has fiber $H^0(\Sigma_g, \Omega^1)$.  The propagator depends on a
frame for~$\mathbb{E}^\vee|_{[\Sigma_g]}$; the period corrections
live in $\mathbb{E}^\vee \boxtimes \mathbb{E}$.  The scalar trace
yields
\begin{equation}\label{eq:lambda-from-trace}
\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA)
= \kappa(\cA) \cdot c_g(\mathbb{E})
= \kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g
\;\in\; H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g).
\end{equation}

\subsection{The family index}
\label{subsec:family-index-intro}
\index{A-hat genus@$\hat{A}$-genus!as family index}

The family $\barB_g(\cA) \to \overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ defines a
virtual vector bundle.  Grothendieck--Riemann--Roch applied to
$\pi \colon \overline{\mathcal{C}}_g \to
\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$: the fiber Chern character
contributes~$\kappa(\cA)$; the Todd class produces the
$\hat{A}$-series.  The generating function is
$\sum_{g \geq 1} \mathrm{obs}_g \cdot \hbar^{2g-2}
= \kappa(\cA) \cdot (\hat{A}(i\hbar) - 1)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:family-index}).  For a free fermion, this is the
spin partition function; for the Virasoro algebra, the one-loop
string amplitude.

\subsection{The Lagrangian decomposition}
\label{subsec:verdier-symplectic}
\index{Verdier duality!as symplectic structure}

Verdier duality $\sigma = \mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}$ acts on
$\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) = R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g,
\mathcal{Z}_\cA)$.  The composition
\[
\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) \otimes \mathbf{C}_g(\cA)
\;\xrightarrow{\;\mathrm{id} \otimes \sigma\;}
\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) \otimes \mathbf{C}_g(\cA^!)
\;\xrightarrow{\;\mathrm{eval}\;}
R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g,
\omega_{\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g})
\]
is a $(-(3g{-}3))$-shifted symplectic form
(Pantev--To\"en--Vaqui\'e--Vezzosi~\cite{PTVV13}).  The $\pm 1$
eigenspaces of~$\sigma$ are complementary Lagrangians.  Traced
through~$\pi$ and projected to moduli, this produces Theorem~C.


\section{Architecture}
\label{sec:architecture}

\begin{remark}[Five geometric ingredients]\label{rem:five-pieces}
\index{modular Koszul duality!five irreducible pieces}
\index{geometric synthesis|textbf}
Modular Koszul duality rests on five geometric ingredients.
The first four belong to the commutative ($\Einf$) face; the fifth
to the associative ($\Eone$) face.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Three-point collision.}
  The Arnold relation~\eqref{eq:arnold-seed} is factorization
  coherence: fusing three insertions does not depend on the
  order of pairwise collisions.  The genus-$0$ seed.
\item \emph{Verdier duality on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$.}
  $\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}} \bar{B}_X(\cA) \simeq
  \bar{B}_X(\cA^!)$
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main})---the
  Koszul dual as theorem, not definition-by-analogy.
\item \emph{Genus-$1$ curvature.}
  $\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_1 \cdot
  \operatorname{id}$
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality}).  The natural home is
  Positselski's coderived category: quantum corrections are a
  change of ambient category, not extra terms.
\item \emph{Clutching of stable curves.}
  The genus tower is compatible with boundary clutching via the
  modular operad (Theorem~\ref{thm:prism-higher-genus}).
  The combinatorics is stable graphs, not trees, and
  $\Theta_\cA$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:universal-MC},
  proved by Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-full-resolution})
  is compatible with clutching, trace, and Verdier duality.
\item \emph{Associative structure.}
  \index{ordered factorization|see{$\Eone$-chiral algebra}}
  The Yangian $Y(\mathfrak{g})$ is $\Eone$-chiral
  (Definition~\ref{def:e1-chiral}): its bar complex uses ordered
  configuration spaces, and Verdier duality acts by $R$-matrix
  inversion $R \mapsto R^{-1}$
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:yangian-koszul-dual}).
  The sign flip $\hbar \mapsto -\hbar$ replaces the Feigin--Frenkel
  involution.
\end{enumerate}

\smallskip\noindent
In the Heisenberg algebra, only pieces~(i)--(iv) are active, and they
already force the full commutative/modular package: Arnold ensures
$d^2 = 0$; Verdier produces
$\mathcal{H}_k^! = \mathrm{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*)$; the genus-$1$
period integral creates curvature $\kappa = 1/k$; clutching assembles
the genus tower.

\smallskip\noindent
In the Yangian, piece~(v) becomes unavoidable.  Ordered
configurations, $R$-matrix inversion on the module category, and the
derived Drinfeld--Kohno bridge
(Theorem~\ref{thm:factorization-dk-eval}) are not embellishments of
the Heisenberg picture but the first genuinely braided determination of
the same configuration-space mechanism.

\smallskip\noindent
The subject begins with Heisenberg because one should first see the
mechanism before braiding enters, and it extends to Yangian because the
ordered world introduces a genuinely new geometric feature rather than
a different subject.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The DK square]\label{rem:dk-inevitability}
\index{Drinfeld--Kohno!derived!as witness of the two determinations}
The coexistence of the commutative and braided determinations is
witnessed by the derived
Drinfeld--Kohno square (Theorem~\ref{thm:derived-dk-affine}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:dk-intro}
\begin{tikzcd}
\mathcal{O}_k^{\mathrm{int}}(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}})
  \ar[r, "\Phi", "\sim"']
  \ar[d, "\Phi_{\mathrm{KL}}"', "\sim"]
& \mathcal{O}_{k'}(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}})
  \ar[d, "\Phi_{\mathrm{KL}}", "\sim"']
\\
\mathcal{C}(U_q(\mathfrak{g}))
  \ar[r, "q \mapsto q^{-1}"', "\sim"]
& \mathcal{C}(U_{q^{-1}}(\mathfrak{g}))
\end{tikzcd}
\end{equation}
Top: level inversion $k \mapsto k' = -k - 2h^\vee$ (Frame~A).
Bottom: $R$-matrix inversion $q \mapsto q^{-1}$ (Frame~B).

duality geometrically implements the quantum group involution.
The anti-symmetry $\kappa + \kappa' = 0$ is the scalar shadow of
the top arrow; $q \mapsto q^{-1}$ is the quantum-group shadow.
The four theorems and the DK square are four projections of one
geometric structure on
$\operatorname{Ran}(X) \times \overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$.
\end{remark}

\section{Dictionary: chiral algebra types and Koszul dualities}
\label{sec:dictionary}

Collision geometry on a curve forces three levels of algebraic
structure---commutative ($\Einf$), associative ($\Eone$),
semiclassical ($\Pinf$)---each carrying its own Koszul duality.

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\Einf$}{E-infinity}-chiral algebra = vertex algebra]
\label{def:einf-chiral}
\index{$\mathbb{E}_\infty$-chiral algebra|textbf}
Throughout, $k = \mathbb{C}$ and $X$ is a smooth algebraic curve.
The ambient $\infty$-category is $\mathcal{D}\text{-mod}(X)$,
equipped with the chiral tensor product
$\otimes^{\mathrm{ch}}$~\cite[\S3.4]{BD04}.  Duality means
Verdier duality~$\mathbb{D}$ of holonomic $\mathcal{D}$-modules
(Remark~\ref{rem:verdier-vs-linear}).

An \emph{$\Einf$-chiral algebra} on $X$ is a commutative algebra
in $(\mathcal{D}\text{-mod}(X),\allowbreak
\otimes^{\mathrm{ch}})$
(cf.~\cite[§5.1]{HA}).
Equivalent formulations:
(a)~$\mathcal{D}_X$-module with chiral Lie
bracket~\cite[\S3.3]{BD04};
(b)~vertex algebra~\cite[\S3.7]{FBZ04} for $X = \mathbb{A}^1$;
(c)~factorization algebra on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$
(Definition~\ref{def:factorization-algebra-AF}).
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Verdier versus linear duality]
\label{rem:verdier-vs-linear}
\index{Verdier duality}
$\mathbb{D}(\mathcal{M})$ is the Verdier dual
$\mathcal{D}$-module, not the na\"{\i}ve linear dual
$\mathrm{Hom}_k(\mathcal{M}, k)$.  The Koszul
dual~$\cA^!$ is constructed via Verdier duality on
$\overline{C}_n(X)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-verdier}).
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\Eone$}{E1}-chiral algebra = nonlocal vertex algebra]
\label{def:e1-chiral}
\index{$\mathbb{E}_1$-chiral algebra|textbf}
An \emph{$\Eone$-chiral algebra} on $X$ is an associative (but not
necessarily commutative) algebra over the associative chiral
operad~$\chirAss$ (Definition~\ref{def:chiral-ass-operad}):
a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module $\cA$ with binary chiral operation
$\mu\colon \cA \chirtensor \cA \to \Delta_!\cA$
satisfying $\mu \circ (\mu \otimes \operatorname{id})
= \mu \circ (\operatorname{id} \otimes \mu)$
but not necessarily skew-symmetry.
In vertex algebra language, a nonlocal vertex algebra
(Li~\cite{Li96}; cf.~\cite[\S4.1]{HA};
Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite[Theorem~3.4.9]{BD04};
Francis--Gaitsgory~\cite{FG12}).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[The associative chiral operad]
\label{def:chiral-ass-operad}
The \emph{associative chiral operad} $\chirAss$ on $X$ is the
non-$\Sigma$ chiral operad with
$\chirAss(n) = \Delta^{(n)}_!\, \omega_X$, supported on the small
diagonal without $\Sigma_n$-action.  Quadratic presentation:
$\chirAss = \operatorname{Free}^{\mathrm{ch}}(\mu) /
(\mu \circ_1 \mu = \mu \circ_2 \mu)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[\texorpdfstring{$\chirAss$}{Ass-ch} self-duality;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:chirAss-self-dual}
$(\chirAss)^! \cong \chirAss \otimes \operatorname{sgn}$.
$\Eone$--$\Eone$ Koszul duality stays within the same operadic
type.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Classically $\operatorname{Ass}^! \cong \operatorname{Ass} \otimes
\operatorname{sgn}$~\cite[Theorem~7.4.1]{LV12}.  The chiral lift
preserves Koszul duality:
$(\mathcal{P}^{\mathrm{ch}})^! \cong (\mathcal{P}^!)^{\mathrm{ch}}$
since the orthogonal complement commutes with the chiral tensor
product.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\Pinf$}{P-infinity}-chiral algebra = chiral Poisson]
\label{def:pinf-chiral}
A \emph{$\Pinf$-chiral algebra} is an $\Einf$-chiral algebra with
a compatible $L_\infty$ structure via Leibniz: commutative chiral
product and Lie bracket coexist.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[\texorpdfstring{$E_n$}{En} terminology]\label{rem:En-terminology}
\index{$E_n$ terminology}
The subscripts $\Einf$, $\Eone$, $\Pinf$ refer to the
factorization level inside the BD chiral tensor category.  Bare
$E_n$ and $\En$ refer to little-disks
algebras~\cite[\S5.1]{HA}.  An $\Eone$-chiral algebra on a
curve is not a topological $E_1$-algebra; the suffix
``-chiral'' is load-bearing.
\end{remark}

The hierarchy
$\Einf\text{-chiral} \subset \Pinf\text{-chiral} \subset
\Eone\text{-chiral}$.
Deformation quantization passes from $\Pinf$ to $\Eone$.
Bar-cobar duality operates at each level: $\Einf$ computes
factorization homology; $\Eone$ connects to Yangians and quantum
groups (Chapter~\ref{chap:yangians}).

\begin{remark}[Operadic hierarchy and configuration spaces]
\label{rem:operadic-hierarchy-table}
\index{operadic hierarchy!chiral algebras}
The hierarchy is organized by the geometry of insertion points:
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\textbf{Algebra type} & \textbf{Operad} &
  \textbf{Configuration space} & \textbf{Koszul exchange} \\ \hline
Heisenberg, KM, Vir, $\mathcal{W}$
  & $\Einf$ (comm.)
  & $C_n(X)$ unordered
  & type exchange $\cA \leftrightarrow \cA^!$ \\[2pt]
Lattice VOA
  & $\Einf$ with $\Eone$ sublattice
  & $C_n(X)$ with monodromy
  & type exchange + screening \\[2pt]
Yangian $Y(\fg)$
  & $\Eone$ (assoc.)
  & $\operatorname{Conf}_n^{\mathrm{ord}}(X)$ ordered
  & $R$-matrix inversion \\[2pt]
Elliptic QG
  & modular deformation
  & $\operatorname{Conf}$ on $E_\tau$
  & elliptic $R$-inversion
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
The progression from $\Einf$ to $\Eone$ is not ``more
generality''; it is a change in the geometry of collision, from
symmetric to ordered, with correspondingly different Koszul
exchange mechanisms.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Three Koszul duality mechanisms]
\label{rem:three-koszul-mechanisms}
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \emph{Francis--Gaitsgory}~\cite{FG12}:
  $\chirLie\text{-alg} \simeq \chirCom\text{-coalg}$ via
  Chevalley/Primitives, under pro-nilpotence.
\item \emph{Bar-cobar resolution}: $\Omega(\bar{B}(\cA)) \to \cA$
  is a quasi-isomorphism on the Koszul locus, or in
  completed/coderived regimes
  (Appendix~\ref{app:nilpotent-completion}).
\item \emph{Quadratic/Priddy}:
  $\cA \mapsto \cA^! = T(V^*)/R^\perp$, with
  $\bar{B}(\cA)^\vee \simeq \cA^!$ when $E_2$-degeneration holds (the bar coalgebra, dualized, recovers the Koszul dual algebra).
\end{enumerate}
Every theorem specifies which mechanism is invoked.  All three
admit configuration space realizations.  At higher genus, they fuse
via the Feynman transform of the modular operad.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Anomaly ratio]\label{def:anomaly-ratio}
\index{anomaly ratio|textbf}
For $c(\cA) \neq 0$: $\varrho(\cA) := \kappa(\cA)/c(\cA)$.
For $\mathcal{W}$-algebras,
$\varrho(\mathfrak{g}) = \sum_{i=1}^r 1/(m_i+1)$, independent
of~$k$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Koszul conductor]\label{def:koszul-conductor}
\index{Koszul conductor|textbf}
$K(\cA) := c(\cA) + c(\cA^!)$.  Values: $2\dim\mathfrak{g}$ for
$\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k$; $26$ for Virasoro; $100$ for
$\mathcal{W}_3$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Koszul spectrum]\label{def:koszul-spectrum}
\index{Koszul spectrum|textbf}
$\operatorname{KSpec}(\cA) := (c(\cA), \kappa(\cA),
\varrho(\cA))$.  Additive under tensor products in $c$
and~$\kappa$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Existence regimes]\label{rem:existence-regimes}
\label{rem:existence-koszul}
Three regimes, not one universal theorem:
(i)~\emph{strict}, where $\bar{B}_X$ and inversion hold on the
Koszul locus;
(ii)~\emph{completed}, under inverse-limit hypotheses
(Appendix~\ref{app:nilpotent-completion});
(iii)~\emph{comparison}
(Appendix~\ref{app:existence-criteria}).
The load-bearing distinction: $\bar{B}_X(\cA)$ may be constructed
beyond where $\Omega_X\bar{B}_X(\cA) \simeq \cA$ is proved.
\end{remark}

\begin{convention}[Semantic levels: homotopy, model, shadow]
\label{conv:hms-levels}
\index{semantic levels|textbf}

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{H-level (homotopy/native).}
The statement in the stable $\infty$-category of factorization
(co)algebras on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$, or in the associated
formal moduli / shifted-symplectic language.  Defined up to
contractible ambiguity.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{M-level (model).}
An explicit dg / $L_\infty$ / filtered complex.  The geometric
bar complex $\bar{B}_X(\cA) = (T^c(s^{-1}\bar{\cA}), d)$ built

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{S-level (shadow).}
The cohomological, Ext, numerical, or generating-function
consequence: bar cohomology dimensions, Hilbert series,
$\kappa(\cA)$, free energies $F_g(\cA)$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Notation.}
\begin{itemize}
\item $\simeq$\,: equivalence in the homotopy/$\infty$-categorical
  sense (H-level).
\item $\cong$\,: strict isomorphism of chosen dg models (M-level).
\item $=$\,: definition or literal equality only.
\end{itemize}
The proved results (Remark~\ref{rem:two-strata}) are proved at
M-level and stated at H-level.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Model independence.}
Any two admissible dg presentations are connected by a contractible
space of quasi-isomorphisms
(Proposition~\ref{prop:model-independence}).
\end{convention}

All $\mathcal{D}$-modules are holonomic.  The cobar

$d_{\mathrm{cobar}}^2 = 0$ follows from $d_{\mathrm{bar}}^2 = 0$

\subsection{Critical pitfalls}
\label{subsec:critical-pitfalls}
\index{critical pitfalls|textbf}

The following distinctions are load-bearing and are the most common sources of error:

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Four objects.}
$\cA$ is the algebra.  $\barB(\cA)$ is the bar coalgebra.  $\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}} = H^*(\barB(\cA))$ is the Koszul dual coalgebra.  $\cA^! = (\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}})^\vee$ is the Koszul dual algebra.  The cobar $\Omega(\barB(\cA))$ recovers~$\cA$ (bar-cobar \emph{inversion}, not duality); $\cA^!$ is obtained by Verdier/linear duality, not cobar.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Grading.}
Cohomological ($|d| = +1$) throughout.  The bar complex uses desuspension: $s^{-1}$ lowers degree by~$1$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Classical vs.\ chiral Koszul duality.}
$\mathrm{Com}^! = \mathrm{Lie}$ (not $\mathrm{coLie}$).  The Heisenberg algebra is \emph{not} self-dual.  Chiral Koszulness is not the same as classical Koszulness.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Curved $A_\infty$.}
$m_1^2(a) = [m_0, a]$ (commutator, with a minus sign).  The bar differential satisfies $d^2 = 0$ always; curvature shows as $m_1^2 \neq 0$ in the $A_\infty$ algebra, not in the bar complex.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Sugawara.}
The Sugawara construction is \emph{undefined} at critical level $k = -h^\vee$---the Sugawara vector does not lie in the appropriate completion.  This is not ``$c$ diverges''; the construction simply does not exist.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Virasoro self-duality.}
The Virasoro algebra is self-dual at $c = 13$, not $c = 26$.  The Koszul dual is $\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{FM compactification.}
The Fulton--MacPherson compactification is the blowup along diagonals, not the complement of diagonals.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Prime form.}
The prime form $E(z_1, z_2)$ is a section of $K^{-1/2} \boxtimes K^{-1/2}$, not $K^{+1/2}$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{QME.}
The quantum master equation is $\hbar \Delta S + \frac{1}{2}\{S, S\} = 0$ (factor $\frac{1}{2}$, not~$1$).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{$d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2 \neq 0$.}
The bracket component of the bar differential does not square to zero on its own: this is proved for all $2048$ sign conventions for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$.  The full Borcherds differential gives $d^2 = 0$.
