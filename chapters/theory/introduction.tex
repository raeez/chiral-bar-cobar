\chapter{Introduction}

\section{From a point to a curve}
\label{sec:volume-one-route}

Koszul duality over a point is completely understood: a quadratic algebra~$A$ determines a dual coalgebra~$A^!$, the bar construction mediates between them, and the theory terminates.  Over a curve, every ingredient changes.  This monograph develops the resulting theory: Koszul duality for chiral algebras on algebraic curves, through all genera.

On an algebraic curve~$X$, the generators of the algebra become sections of a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module, the quadratic relations become operator product expansions with meromorphic singularities, and the bar differential becomes an integral transform whose kernel is the logarithmic propagator $d\log(z_i - z_j)$ on Fulton--MacPherson compactifications of configuration spaces.  At genus~$0$ the classical theory embeds faithfully: Koszul duality over a point is Koszul duality over~$\mathbb{P}^1$.  At genus~$g \geq 1$, the topology of the curve forces genuinely new phenomena (central extensions, conformal anomalies, curved $A_\infty$ structures) that have no analogue in the classical setting and whose geometric origin is the curvature of the Hodge bundle over~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$.

The classical theory goes as follows.  Let $V$ be a finite-dimensional graded vector space and $R \subset V^{\otimes 2}$ a subspace of quadratic relations.  The quadratic algebra $A = T(V)/(R)$ is the free tensor algebra modulo~$R$; its Koszul dual coalgebra is $A^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}} = T^c(s V^*)$, the cofree conilpotent coalgebra on the suspended linear dual, with coproduct truncated by the orthogonal complement $R^\perp \subset (V^*)^{\otimes 2}$.  The bar construction $B(A) = (T^c(s^{-1}\bar{A}), d_B)$ carries a differential $d_B$ induced by the multiplication of~$A$: on cogenerators $d_B(s^{-1}a) = \sum s^{-1}a' \otimes s^{-1}a''$ records the splitting of~$a$ into pairs of generators.  The cobar construction $\Omega(C)$ reverses the process: starting from a conilpotent coalgebra~$C$, one builds the free algebra $T(s^{-1}\bar{C})$ with differential induced by the comultiplication.  The fundamental comparison map
\[
\varepsilon \colon \Omega(B(A)) \longrightarrow A
\]
is always a morphism of dg algebras; when it is a quasi-isomorphism, the algebra~$A$ is called \emph{Koszul}, and $B(A)$ has cohomology concentrated in bar degree~$1$, equal to the Koszul dual coalgebra~$A^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}}$.  The Koszul dual algebra is $A^! = (A^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}})^\vee = T(V^*)/R^\perp$.  Koszulness means that $A$ admits a linear resolution by $A^!$-modules, and that the derived category of $A$-modules is equivalent to the derived category of $A^!$-modules (up to a shift and twist).  This is the content of Priddy~\cite{Priddy70}, Beilinson--Ginzburg--Soergel~\cite{BGS96}, and Loday--Vallette~\cite{LV12}.

Let $X$ be a smooth projective curve over~$\mathbb{C}$.  A \emph{chiral algebra}~$\cA$ on~$X$ (Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite{BD04}) is a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module equipped with a chiral bracket
\[
\mu^{\mathrm{ch}} \colon j_* j^*(\cA \boxtimes \cA) \longrightarrow \Delta_!(\cA),
\]
where $j \colon X^2 \setminus \Delta \hookrightarrow X^2$ is the complement of the diagonal and $\Delta_! \colon D(\mathcal{D}_X) \to D(\mathcal{D}_{X^2})$ is the pushforward.  The chiral bracket encodes the operator product expansion: locally, if $a, b \in \cA$ are sections and $z, w$ are local coordinates, then
\[
a(z) b(w) \;\sim\; \sum_{n \geq 0} \frac{(a_{(n)} b)(w)}{(z - w)^{n+1}},
\]
where the modes $a_{(n)}b$ are the Laurent coefficients of the OPE, and the bracket $\mu^{\mathrm{ch}}$ records the entire singular part simultaneously.  The Borcherds identity, the defining axiom of a vertex algebra, is the chiral-algebraic incarnation of the Jacobi identity, coupling all pole orders in a single statement.

\begin{remark}[Two extremes]
\label{rem:two-strata}
\index{two strata|textbf}
Two extremes organize the theory
(see Chapter~\ref{ch:algebraic-foundations}, \S\ref{sec:dictionary} for the formal definitions).
The \textbf{Heisenberg} is the commutative extreme: its OPE has only double poles, so collisions are symmetric and unordered ($\Einf$-chiral).
The \textbf{Yangian} is the ordered extreme: simple poles give collisions a canonical ordering ($\Eone$-chiral).
Every other family (Kac--Moody, Virasoro, $\mathcal{W}$-algebras, lattice vertex algebras) interpolates between these two extremes, and the bar complex on higher-genus curves classifies them.
\end{remark}
At genus~$g \geq 1$, the bar differential inherits a
curvature term $\kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ as the
coalgebraic shadow of the cyclic trace composed with
clutching: the Hodge class
$\omega_g = c_1(\mathbb{E})$ on
$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ enters through the annular
trace on the open sector, and the \emph{modular
characteristic}~$\kappa(\cA)$ is the universal scalar
measuring this obstruction
(Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}).
The bar construction on curves defines a
pronilpotent dg Lie algebra~$\gAmod$ (the modular
convolution algebra), whose Maurer--Cartan
element~$\Theta_\cA$ encodes the full genus expansion.
The finite-order projections $\Theta_\cA^{\leq r}$
(the shadow obstruction tower) extract successively
finer invariants: $\kappa$ at arity~$2$, a cubic
shadow~$C$ at arity~$3$, a quartic resonance class~$Q$
at arity~$4$.  The shadow depth $r_{\max}(\cA)$ is the
largest arity at which the shadow is nonzero; it equals
the $L_\infty$ formality level of the genus-$0$ part of $\gAmod$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-formality-identification}).
The Heisenberg has $r_{\max} = 2$ (formal,
$\Delta = 0$); affine Kac--Moody algebras have
$r_{\max} = 3$; the $\beta\gamma$ system has
$r_{\max} = 4$; Virasoro and $\mathcal{W}$-algebras
have $r_{\max} = \infty$.  These four cases exhaust the
possibilities: the \emph{critical discriminant}
$\Delta = 8\kappa S_4$, where $S_4$ is the quartic
shadow coefficient, forces $r_{\max}$ to lie in
$\{2, 3, \infty\}$ on any one-dimensional primary slice
(Theorem~\ref{thm:single-line-dichotomy}), with the
fourth class $r_{\max} = 4$ arising from a stratum
separation phenomenon.

Three structure theorems govern this tower.
All three are projections of the single Maurer--Cartan element
$\Theta_\cA \in \MC(\gAmod)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}): algebraicity describes its
restriction to a primary line, the formality identification describes
its genus-$0$ component, and complementarity describes its Verdier
dual.
\emph{Algebraicity}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:riccati-algebraicity}):
on each primary line~$L$, the shadow generating function satisfies
$H(t)^2 = t^4 Q_L(t)$
with $Q_L$ a computable quadratic polynomial in three invariants $(\kappa, \alpha, S_4)$ (the modular characteristic, cubic shadow coefficient, and quartic shadow coefficient, respectively), so the
tower is the Taylor expansion of~$\sqrt{Q_L}$, algebraic of degree~$2$.
The discriminant
$\Delta$ governs the fundamental dichotomy:
$\Delta = 0$ terminates the tower; $\Delta \neq 0$ makes it infinite.
\emph{Formality identification}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-formality-identification}):
the genus-$0$ shadow obstruction tower coincides with the $L_\infty$
formality obstruction tower of the convolution algebra
at all arities; genus $g \geq 1$ corrections form a separate layer.
The genus and arity directions are orthogonal
(Remark~\ref{rem:two-orthogonal-axes}).
The \emph{visibility formula}
(Corollary~\ref{cor:shadow-visibility-genus})
controls which shadow coefficients contribute at each genus:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-visibility}
g_{\min}(S_r) \;=\; \lfloor r/2 \rfloor + 1
\qquad (r \geq 3).
\end{equation}
Genus~$1$ sees only~$\kappa$ (arity~$2$).
Genus~$2$ first sees~$S_3$ (arity~$3$).
Genus~$3$ first sees~$S_4$ and~$S_5$ (arities~$4$,~$5$).
In general, two new shadow coefficients enter at each genus.
\emph{Complementarity} (Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}):
$Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!) \simeq H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, Z(\cA))$,
a Lagrangian decomposition that constrains the Koszul
dual pair.
Classical Koszul duality over a point is the genus~$0$,
arity~$2$, $\Delta = 0$ stratum of this picture: the
formal, Gaussian case where the tower terminates
immediately.  Everything beyond this stratum is new.

\section{The selection principle}
\label{sec:selection-principle}
\index{selection principle|textbf}
\index{Borcherds identity!as selection criterion}

The bar construction of \S\ref{sec:volume-one-route} is a
\emph{geometric} machine: take a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module~$V$
with a meromorphic pairing
$\mu \colon j_* j^*(V \boxtimes V) \to \Delta_!(V)$
(the OPE), place sections at points of
$\overline{C}_n(X)$, and extract residues along
boundary divisors.  The output is a graded object
$\barB_X(V, \mu)$ with a candidate differential
$d = d_{\mathrm{internal}} + d_{\mathrm{residue}}
+ d_{\mathrm{dR}}$.

The governing question:
\begin{center}
\emph{For which algebraic inputs $(V, \mu)$ does
the FM bar construction produce $d^2 = 0$?}
\end{center}

\noindent
Expanding $(d_1 + d_2 + d_3)^2$, the terms
$d_1^2$, $d_3^2$, and all mixed anticommutators vanish
for formal reasons (cochain complex, de~Rham,
residue exact sequence).  The content is
$d_2^2 = 0$: residue extraction must be nilpotent
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-nilpotency-complete}).
Three cases arise from index overlap in
$d_2^2 = \sum_{i<j}\sum_{k<\ell}
\Res_{D_{k\ell}} \circ \Res_{D_{ij}}$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Disjoint indices}
  ($\{i,j\} \cap \{k,\ell\} = \emptyset$).
  Residue operations commute; cancellation is by the
  Koszul sign rule.  No condition on~$\mu$.

\item \emph{One shared index} (say $j = k$).
  Three boundary strata meet at the codimension-$2$ locus
  $D_{ij\ell}$ of triple collision.  The cyclic sum of
  iterated residues
  \[
  \sum_{\textup{cyclic}}
    \Res_{D_{j\ell}} \circ \Res_{D_{ij}}
    \bigl[\mu(\mu(\phi_i, \phi_j), \phi_\ell)
    \otimes \omega\bigr]
  = 0
  \]
  holds if and only if $\mu$ satisfies the
  Borcherds identity, the associativity of the chiral
  product, coupling all OPE pole orders simultaneously.

\item \emph{Same pair}
  ($\{i,j\} = \{k,\ell\}$).
  $\Res_{D_{ij}}^2 = 0$ automatically.
\end{enumerate}
Cases~(i) and~(iii) impose no condition.
Case~(ii) imposes exactly the Borcherds identity
(equation~\eqref{eq:borcherds-axiom}),
axiom~(ii) of a chiral algebra
(Definition~\ref{def:chiral-algebra}).  The FM bar
construction is well-defined if and only if the OPE
satisfies it.  This is the \emph{selection principle}:
the geometry of configuration space compactifications
determines the algebraic axioms.

Two witnesses from Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}:

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Heisenberg} ($\alpha(z)\alpha(w) \sim
k/(z-w)^2$, double pole only).  No simple pole, so
$d_{\mathrm{bracket}} = 0$.  The full differential reduces to
$d_{\mathrm{curvature}}$, which produces scalars:
$d_{\mathrm{curvature}}^2 = 0$ automatically.  The Arnold
relation handles all of case~(ii).  No algebraic input needed.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Kac--Moody} ($h(z)\,e(w) \sim
2e(w)/(z-w) + \cdots$, simple \emph{and} double poles).
The bracket component $d_{\mathrm{bracket}}$ is now nonzero, and
$d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2 \neq 0$, computed explicitly for all
$2048$ sign conventions
(\S\ref{subsec:frame-sl2-bar-low-degree},
Remark~\ref{rem:frame-why-dbracket-fails}).  The obstruction is
proportional to the level~$k$: it is the double-pole data that
$d_{\mathrm{bracket}}$ ignores.  The Borcherds identity absorbs
this obstruction:
\[
d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2
= -\{d_{\mathrm{bracket}},\, d_{\mathrm{curvature}}\}.
\]
The curvature is the obstruction; the Borcherds identity is the
integrability condition
(Proposition~\ref{prop:pole-decomposition}).

\begin{remark}[Classical vs.\ chiral selection]
\label{rem:classical-vs-chiral-selection}
\index{Koszul duality!classical vs.\ chiral}
The bar construction asks the same question at every level
of geometry:
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\textbf{Base} & \textbf{Condition for $d^2 = 0$}
  & \textbf{Selected objects} \\
\hline
Point & Jacobi identity & Lie algebras \\
Curve $X$ & Borcherds identity & Chiral algebras \\
$X$ over $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ & $+$ period corrections
  & Modular Koszul algebras
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Each row is forced by the geometry of the next: the Jacobi
identity handles triple collisions when all poles are simple;
on a curve, multiple pole orders mix at the codimension-$2$
strata of $\overline{C}_n(X)$, and the Borcherds identity is
the unique condition that makes this mixing consistent; at
higher genus, period corrections from the Hodge bundle restore
$\Dg{g}^{\,2} = 0$.  Chiral algebras are what configuration
space geometry accepts; modular Koszul algebras are what the
genus tower accepts.
\end{remark}


\section{The five theorems}
\label{sec:NAP-unifying}
\label{sec:main-results-complete}
\index{Koszul duality!chiral|textbf}
\index{modular Koszul duality|textbf}
\index{quantum corrections!as modular completion}

The selection principle determines the admissible inputs; the five
theorems characterize the universal output.
The protagonist of this monograph is the universal Maurer--Cartan element
$\Theta_\cA \in \mathrm{MC}(\gAmod)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}),
defined by $\Theta_\cA := D_\cA - d_\cA^{(0)}$: the difference
between the full bar differential~$D_\cA$ and its genus-$0$
restriction.  Since $D_\cA^2 = 0$, the element $\Theta_\cA$ is automatically
Maurer--Cartan.  The five theorems characterize five structural properties
of~$\Theta_\cA$: \emph{existence} (Theorem~A constructs the arena
$\gAmod$), \emph{faithfulness} (Theorem~B
shows $\Theta_\cA$ determines~$\cA$), \emph{decomposition} (Theorem~C
splits the genus-$g$ projection into complementary Lagrangians),
\emph{leading coefficient} (Theorem~D extracts the universal scalar
$\kappa(\cA)$), and \emph{coefficient ring} (Theorem~H identifies
$\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ as the finite-dimensional coefficient space over which~$\Theta_\cA$ varies).
The shadow obstruction tower $\Theta_\cA^{\leq 2}$,
$\Theta_\cA^{\leq 3}$, $\Theta_\cA^{\leq 4}$, \ldots{}
consists of finite-order projections of this single element;
$\kappa$ is the arity-$2$ projection, the cubic shadow~$C$ is the
arity-$3$ projection, and the quartic resonance class~$Q$ is the
arity-$4$ projection.

The element~$\Theta_\cA$ is itself the closed-sector projection
of a larger structure.
The \emph{open/closed MC element}
$\Theta^{\mathrm{oc}}_\cA = \Theta_\cA + \sum \mu^{M_j}$
(Construction~\ref{constr:thqg-oc-mc-element},
Theorem~\ref{thm:thqg-oc-mc-equation})
packages both boundary data (the bar-intrinsic class~$\Theta_\cA$,
governing twisting morphisms between~$\cA$ and~$\cA^!$) and bulk data
(the chiral derived center
$\mathcal{Z}^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$, governing operators
in the interior).
The five theorems characterize the closed projection
$\Theta_\cA = \pi_{\mathrm{cl}}(\Theta^{\mathrm{oc}}_\cA)$; the bulk
projection is controlled by the Swiss-cheese structure of the bar complex
(Theorem~\ref{thm:thqg-swiss-cheese}).
Modularity (the genus expansion and its
tautological descent to~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$) belongs to the
trace and clutching operations on the open sector, not to the closed
algebra alone.

\subsection{The Heisenberg algebra}
\label{subsec:five-theorems-heisenberg}

Take $\cA = \cH_k$ with OPE
$J(z)\,J(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$.  One generator, one double pole, no
simple pole.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~A (bar-cobar adjunction).}\enspace
The bar complex is $\barB(\cH_k) = T^c(s^{-1}\C\cdot J)$, the
cofree coalgebra on a single cogenerator.  The bar differential on
a degree-$2$ element is
\[
d_{\barB}[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]
\;=\;
\Res_{z_1 = z_2}\!\left[
  \frac{k}{(z_1 - z_2)^2}\cdot d\log(z_1-z_2)
\right]
\;=\; k.
\]
At degree~$3$: the Arnold relation on $\FM_3(\C)$ gives
$d_{\barB}^2 = 0$.  The three collision divisors contribute
$k \cdot (\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23} + \eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{13}
+ \eta_{13}\wedge\eta_{12}) = 0$.

Verdier duality on $\Ran(X)$ produces the homotopy Koszul dual:
$\mathbb{D}_{\Ran}\,\barB(\cH_k) \simeq (\cH_k^!)_\infty$,
with $\kappa(\cH_k^!) = -k$.
The Heisenberg is not Koszul self-dual:
$\cH_k^! \neq \cH_{-k}$
(\S\ref{sec:frame-koszul-dual}).

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~B (bar-cobar inversion).}\enspace
The bar cohomology is $H^{-1}(\barB(\cH_k)) = \C\cdot s^{-1}J$
(concentrated in bar degree~$1$), so the Koszul dual coalgebra is
$\cH_k^{\mathrm{i}} = \C\cdot s^{-1}J$.  The Koszul dual algebra
is $\cH_k^! = (\cH_k^{\mathrm{i}})^\vee$: the symmetric chiral
algebra $\operatorname{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*)$ with curvature
$m_0 = -k\,\omega$ encoding the opposite level.
By the Verdier intertwining of Theorem~A,
$\kappa(\cH_k^!) = -k$, the opposite modular characteristic
(the Heisenberg is \emph{not} Koszul self-dual;
see~\S\ref{sec:frame-koszul-dual}).
The cobar of the bar is
$\Omega(\barB(\cH_k)) = T(s\,\C\cdot s^{-1}J) = \C[J]$,
which is $\cH_k$ as a graded algebra.  The counit
$\varepsilon\colon\Omega(\barB(\cH_k)) \to \cH_k$ is a
quasi-isomorphism: the Heisenberg is Koszul.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~C (complementarity).}\enspace
At genus~$g \ge 1$:
$Q_g(\cH_k) \oplus Q_g(\cH_k^!) \cong
H^*(\overline{\cM}_g, \cZ_{\cH_k})$.
The modular characteristic is $\kappa(\cH_k) = k$, and
$\kappa(\cH_k) + \kappa(\cH_k^!) = k + (-k) = 0$.  The
complementarity sum of central charges is
$c(\cH_k) + c(\cH_k^!) = 1 + 1 = 2$
(noting that the dual is curved, so $c(\cH_k^!)=1$ refers to
the underlying graded algebra).

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~D (modular characteristic).}\enspace
At genus~$1$: the self-loop graph $\Gamma_{\mathrm{irr}}$ on
$\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$ contributes
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = k \cdot \omega_1$.  The genus-$1$
obstruction number is $F_1 = k/24$.  The generating function:
\[
\sum_{g \ge 1} F_g(\cH_k)\,x^{2g}
\;=\;
k\!\left(\frac{x/2}{\sin(x/2)} - 1\right)
\;=\;
\frac{k}{24}\,x^2
+ \frac{7k}{5760}\,x^4
+ \frac{31k}{967680}\,x^6
+ \cdots
\]
The coefficients are
$F_g = k \cdot \frac{2^{2g-1}-1}{2^{2g-1}} \cdot
\frac{|B_{2g}|}{(2g)!}$,
where $B_{2g}$ is the $2g$-th Bernoulli number.
(The chiral partition function
$Z_{\mathrm{ch}} = 1/\eta(\tau)$ gives
$F_1 = \kappa(\cH_k)/24 = k/24$
as the genus-$1$ free energy on~$\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$.)

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~H (chiral Hochschild).}\enspace
$\ChirHoch^*(\cH_k) = (\C, \C, \C)$ concentrated in degrees
$\{0, 1, 2\}$: the class in degree~$0$ is the center (scalars),
the class in degree~$1$ is the outer chiral derivation
$D(\alpha) = \mathbf{1}$ (non-inner because $\alpha_{(0)} = 0$
when the OPE has no simple pole), and the class in degree~$2$ is
the level deformation (double-pole OPE datum).
The Koszul resolution has length~$2$, so
$\ChirHoch^n = 0$ for $n \ge 3$; Hilbert series $1 + t + t^2$,
the rank-$1$ case of the polynomial-growth theorem.

The full computation appears in Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}.

\subsection{The general theorems}
\label{subsec:five-theorems-general}

Here $\operatorname{Fact}(X)$ denotes the symmetric
monoidal category of factorization algebras on~$X$
(Chapter~\ref{ch:algebraic-foundations});
$\operatorname{Alg}_{\mathrm{aug}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))$
is the category of \emph{augmented} factorization algebras
(equipped with a map $\cA \to \omega_X$ to the vacuum);
$\operatorname{CoAlg}_{\mathrm{conil}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))$
is the category of \emph{conilpotent} factorization
coalgebras (the iterated reduced coproduct eventually
vanishes).

Each statement below is the av-image of a corresponding
$\Eone$ statement on the ordered convolution algebra
${\gAmod}^{\Eone}$
(see~\S\ref{subsec:e1-as-primitive} for the averaging map
$\operatorname{av}\colon{\gAmod}^{\Eone}\twoheadrightarrow\gAmod$
and Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul} for the ordered
counterparts $\mathrm{A}^{\Eone}$--$\mathrm{H}^{\Eone}$).
The five theorems are the invariants that survive averaging.

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Theorem~A} (Geometric bar-cobar duality,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}).
  The bar functor
  \[
  \bar{B}_X \colon
  \operatorname{Alg}_{\mathrm{aug}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
  \to
  \operatorname{CoAlg}_{\mathrm{conil}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
  \]
  is intertwined with Verdier duality
  (Chapter~\ref{chap:bar-cobar}):
  $\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}} \bar{B}_X(\cA)
  \simeq \cA^!_\infty$
  (factorization \emph{algebra}, not coalgebra),
  functorial in families
  over~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,n}$.
  It decomposes into chiral twisting morphisms
  ($\mathrm{A}_0$,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:fundamental-twisting-morphisms}),
  bar concentration
  ($\mathrm{A}_1$, Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-concentration}), and
  Verdier-geometric duality
  ($\mathrm{A}_2$,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}).
  On the proved genus-$0$ locus, the bar complex admits a BRST
  comparison; Verdier duality is the algebraic shadow of CPT.

\item \emph{Theorem~B} (Bar-cobar inversion,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:higher-genus-inversion}).
  On the Koszul locus, the counit
  $\Omega_X \bar{B}_X(\cA) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$
  is an equivalence; the spectral sequence collapses at~$E_2$.
  Off the locus, the bar-cobar object persists but becomes curved;
  the failure is measured by
  $\Theta_\cA := D_\cA - d_\cA^{(0)}$
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}).
  The BRST resolution is complete.

  More precisely, there is a spectral sequence with $E_1$ page
  $E_1^{p,q} = H^q(\barB^p_X(\cA))$ converging to $H^{p+q}(\Omega_X \barB_X(\cA))$.
  On the Koszul locus, $E_1^{p,q} = 0$ for $p \neq 1$, so
  $E_2 = E_\infty$, and the comparison map
  $\varepsilon \colon \Omega_X \barB_X(\cA) \to \cA$
  induces an isomorphism on cohomology.  This is the categorical
  exponential: $\exp \circ \log = \operatorname{id}$.

\item \emph{Theorem~C} (Deformation-obstruction complementarity,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}).
  The \emph{center local system} $\mathcal{Z}_\cA$ is the
  local system on $\overline{\cM}_g$ whose fibre over a
  smooth curve $[\Sigma_g]$ is the center
  $Z(\cA|_{\Sigma_g})$ (the commutant of~$\cA$ acting on
  itself via the chiral bracket).
  The \emph{ambient complex}
  $\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) := R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g,
  \mathcal{Z}_\cA)$
  (derived global sections of this local system)
  carries a shifted-symplectic pairing from Verdier duality.
  The Verdier involution decomposes it
  into \emph{complementary Lagrangians}
  $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \oplus \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)$:
  the $+1$ and $-1$ eigenspaces of the involution
  (Definition~\ref{def:complementarity-complexes}).
  At the cohomological level,
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:complementarity-intro}
  Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!) \cong
  H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathcal{Z}_\cA).
  \end{equation}
  The anomaly of one algebra is the ghost-number violation of
  its dual.

  The shifted-symplectic structure is a $(-(3g{-}3))$-shifted symplectic form on the derived moduli of genus-$g$ bar families, in the sense of Pantev--To\"en--Vaqui\'e--Vezzosi~\cite{PTVV13}.  The complementary Lagrangians~$Q_g(\cA)$ and~$Q_g(\cA^!)$ are the $\pm 1$ eigenspaces of the Verdier involution~$\sigma = \mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}$ acting on~$\mathbf{C}_g(\cA)$.  Their intersection is the center~$Z(\cA)$; the ambient complex is the direct sum.

\item \emph{Theorem~D$_{\mathrm{scal}}$} (Scalar modular
  characteristic, Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}).
  A single scalar $\kappa(\cA)$, the genus-$1$ curvature
  coefficient (Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality}), determines the
  entire \emph{scalar modular package}: the collection
  $(\kappa, \{\mathrm{obs}_g\}_{g \ge 1},
  \{F_g\}_{g \ge 1})$ consisting of the modular
  characteristic, its genus-$g$ obstruction classes, and the
  free energies.  For algebras whose strong generators have the
  same conformal weight (including all single-generator families),
  $\mathrm{obs}_g = \kappa \cdot \lambda_g
  \in H^*(\overline{\cM}_g)$ and
  $F_g = \kappa \cdot \hat{A}_g$ at all genera.
  For multi-weight algebras (e.g.\ $\mathcal{W}_N$, $N \geq 3$),
  the new rigidity theorem forces the minimal-model MC
  element into the one-dimensional cyclic direction
  $\Theta^{\min} = \eta \otimes \Gamma_\cA$; the stronger
  identification $\Gamma_\cA = \kappa(\cA)\Lambda$, and hence the
  all-genera identities $\mathrm{obs}_g = \kappa \cdot \lambda_g$
  and $F_g = \kappa \cdot \hat{A}_g$, hold on the uniform-weight
  lane but \emph{fail} for multi-weight algebras: the genus-$g$
  free energy receives a cross-channel correction
  $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$ from mixed-channel boundary graphs
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion},
  Open Problem~\ref{op:multi-generator-universality} resolved
  negatively).
  The scalar $\kappa$ is universal,
  additive under tensor product, and duality-constrained
  ($\kappa + \kappa' = 0$ for affine Kac--Moody and free
  fields, and a fixed nonzero constant for
  $\mathcal{W}$-algebras).
  On the uniform-weight lane, the generating function is the
  $\hat{A}$-genus
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:universal-generating-function}}\textup{)}:
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:ahat-genus-generating-function-intro}
  \sum_{g \geq 1} F_g(\cA) \cdot \hbar^{2g}
  = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \bigl(\hat{A}(i\hbar) - 1\bigr).
  \end{equation}
  At genus~$1$ this reduces to $F_1(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)/24$ for every
  family.

  \emph{Theorem~D$_\Delta$} (Spectral characteristic,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:spectral-characteristic}):
  the spectral discriminant $\Delta_\cA(x)$ is a separately proved
  non-scalar invariant.

  $\kappa$ is the one-loop coefficient of the effective action;
  its vanishing removes the scalar genus-$1$ anomaly, while
  higher-genus multi-weight corrections are a separate issue.
  Together, $D_{\mathrm{scal}}$ and $D_\Delta$ are the
  characteristic classes of the factorization homology
  bundle $\int_{\Sigma_g}\!\cA$
  (Remark~\ref{rem:four-levels}); the bundle
  itself (its fibers, flat connection, and modular
  functor structure) is the full modular homotopy invariant
  (Conjecture~\ref{conj:categorical-modular-kd}).

\item \emph{Theorem~H} (Chiral Hochschild cohomology,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:hochschild-polynomial-growth}).
  The \emph{chiral Hochschild cohomology}
  $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is the cohomology of the complex
  $\operatorname{CoDer}(\barB(\cA))$
  (coderivations of the bar coalgebra, with differential
  induced by the bar differential).
  On the Koszul locus,
  $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is polynomial and dual
  to $\ChirHoch^*(\cA^!)$.
  Its brace and Gerstenhaber structures control the moduli
  of quantizations and the passage from Poisson to chiral
  (Chapter~\ref{chap:deformation-theory}).
\end{itemize}

\begin{principle}[Five facets of $\Theta_\cA$]
\label{princ:five-facets}
\index{MC element!five facets|textbf}%
Let\/ $\cA$ be a modular Koszul chiral algebra on a smooth projective
curve~$X$, and let\/ $\Theta_\cA := D_\cA - d_\cA^{(0)} \in \gAmod$
be the bar-intrinsic Maurer--Cartan element
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}}\textup{)}.
The five main theorems characterize five
\emph{facets} of~$\Theta_\cA$: existence, faithfulness,
decomposition, leading coefficient, and coefficient ring.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\Alph*)}]
\item \emph{Arena}
  \textup{(}Theorem~A\textup{)}.
  $\Theta_\cA$ \textbf{exists}.
  The bar--cobar adjunction constructs the modular convolution
  algebra~$\gAmod$ in which~$\Theta_\cA$ lives; with it,
  $\Theta_\cA$ arises automatically as $D_\cA - d_\cA^{(0)}$.

\item \emph{Faithfulness}
  \textup{(}Theorem~B\textup{)}.
  $\Theta_\cA$ \textbf{determines}~$\cA$.
  On the Koszul locus, the counit
  $\Omega_X(\barB_X(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$ is a
  quasi-isomorphism.

\item \emph{Decomposition}
  \textup{(}Theorem~C\textup{)}.
  $\Theta_\cA$ \textbf{splits}.
  The genus-$g$ projection decomposes under the Verdier involution
  into complementary Lagrangians
  $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \oplus \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)
  = H^*(\overline{\cM}_g,\, Z(\cA))$.

\item \emph{Leading coefficient}
  \textup{(}Theorem~D\textup{)}.
  $\Theta_\cA$ has \textbf{universal scalar projection}.
  On the uniform-weight lane, the arity-$2$ genus-$g$ component is
  $\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)\,\lambda_g$ at all genera,
  with $\kappa(\cA)$ universal and governed by the
  $\hat{A}$-genus.  At genus~$1$ this holds unconditionally
  for all families.

\item \emph{Coefficient ring}
  \textup{(}Theorem~H\textup{)}.
  $\Theta_\cA$ has \textbf{polynomial coefficients}.
  $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is concentrated in degrees~$\{0,1,2\}$,
  controlling the deformation ring over which~$\Theta_\cA$ varies.
\end{enumerate}
\end{principle}

\begin{theorem}[Central charge complementarity; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]\label{thm:central-charge-complementarity}
\index{central charge!complementarity|textbf}
\textup{[Regime: curved-central on the Koszul locus
\textup{(}Convention~\textup{\ref{conv:regime-tags})}.]}

For Koszul dual pairs related by the Feigin--Frenkel involution
$k \mapsto k' = -k - 2h^\vee$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textup{(a)}] \emph{Affine Kac--Moody.}
  $c(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k) + c(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{k'})
  = 2 \dim \mathfrak{g}$.
\item[\textup{(b)}] \emph{Principal $\mathcal{W}$-algebras:}
  $c(\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})) + c(\mathcal{W}^{k'}(\mathfrak{g}))
   = 2\operatorname{rank}(\mathfrak{g}) + 4h^\vee \dim \mathfrak{g}$.
\end{enumerate}
In particular, $\mathfrak{g} = \mathfrak{sl}_2$ in
part~\textup{(b)} gives $c + c' = 26$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Part~(a).}  The Sugawara central charge is
$c(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k) = k \dim\mathfrak{g}/(k + h^\vee)$.
Since $k' + h^\vee = -(k + h^\vee)$:
\[
c(k) + c(k') = \frac{k \cdot d}{k+h^\vee}
+ \frac{(k+2h^\vee) \cdot d}{k+h^\vee}
= \frac{2(k + h^\vee) \cdot d}{k+h^\vee} = 2d
\]
where $d = \dim\mathfrak{g}$.

\emph{Part~(b).}  The Drinfeld--Sokolov central charge is
$c(\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})) = r - 12|\rho|^2 (t-1)^2/t$
where $r = \operatorname{rank}(\mathfrak{g})$ and
$t = k + h^\vee$
(verified for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ in
Theorem~\ref{thm:w-algebra-koszul-main} and for $\mathfrak{sl}_3$
in Theorem~\ref{thm:w-koszul-precise}).
The involution sends $t \mapsto -t$:
\[
c(k) + c(k') = 2r + 12|\rho|^2 \cdot
\frac{(t+1)^2 - (t-1)^2}{t}
= 2r + 48|\rho|^2.
\]
The Freudenthal--de~Vries identity
$|\rho|^2 = h^\vee \dim\mathfrak{g}/12$
(under normalization $(\theta, \theta) = 2$;
see~\cite{Kac}, Ch.~13)
yields $c + c' = 2r + 4h^\vee d$.

\emph{Verification.}
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$: $2(1) + 4(2)(3) = 26$.
$\mathfrak{sl}_3$: $2(2) + 4(3)(8) = 100$.
$\mathfrak{sl}_N$:
$2(N{-}1) + 4N(N^2{-}1) = 2(N{-}1)(2N^2{+}2N{+}1)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:bosonic-string-26}

\end{remark}

\section{The \texorpdfstring{$\Eone$}{E1} story as primitive: averaging and the modular shadow}
\label{sec:e1-as-primitive}
\label{subsec:e1-as-primitive}
\index{averaging map!av@$\operatorname{av}$|textbf}
\index{ordered bar complex!as primitive object|textbf}
\index{E1 algebra@$\Eone$-algebra!primacy|textbf}
\index{Yangian!as primitive datum}
\index{R-matrix!as primitive of $\kappa$}
\index{symmetric bar complex!as $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant shadow}

The bar complex $\barB(\cA)$ presented in \S\ref{sec:volume-one-route}
is the symmetric (commutative) bar: it lives on
$(s^{-1}\bar\cA)^{\otimes n}_{\Sigma_n}$ and forgets the order in
which collisions occur.  This presentation is convenient for
formulating the five main theorems and connecting to the modular
operad on~$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$, but it is not the natural
primitive of the theory.  The natural primitive is the
\emph{ordered} bar
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-bar-ord}
\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)
\;=\;
T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{n \ge 0}\,(s^{-1}\bar\cA)^{\otimes n}
\end{equation}
with deconcatenation coproduct, which by Stasheff's theorem
\textup{\cite{Sta63}} is the universal cofree conilpotent
coalgebra on~$s^{-1}\bar\cA$.  Every $A_\infty$-chiral algebra
admits a canonical ordered bar; the symmetric bar arises only
after applying $\Sigma_n$-coinvariants externally on each tensor
factor.  The unsymmetrized object retains strictly more
information (\S\ref{subsec:e1-as-primitive}).

\paragraph{The ordered convolution algebra and its MC element.}
The ordered convolution dg Lie algebra
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-gAmod-e1}
{\gAmod}^{\Eone}
\;:=\;
\prod_{g,n}
\Hom\!\bigl(F\!\Ass(g,n),\,\End_\cA(n)\bigr),
\end{equation}
built on the ribbon (associative) modular operad
$F\!\Ass$ rather than the commutative cooperad
$\mathrm{Sym}^c$, houses the bar-intrinsic Maurer--Cartan element
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-theta-e1}
\Theta_\cA^{\Eone}
\;:=\;
D_\cA^{\Eone} - d_\cA^{(0)}
\;\in\;
\MC\!\bigl({\gAmod}^{\Eone}\bigr).
\end{equation}
The arity-$2$ component of $\Theta_\cA^{\Eone}$ at
genus~$0$ is the \emph{classical $r$-matrix}
$r(z) \in \End(V^{\otimes 2}) \otimes \cO(*\Delta)$,
the meromorphic function whose residue at $z = 0$ controls the
spectral scattering of~$\cA$.  The arity-$3$ component is the
\emph{KZ associator} $r_3(z_1, z_2)$, and the arity-$\ge 4$
components are the higher Yangian/braided coherences that satisfy
the boundary equations of Stasheff associahedra:
classical Yang--Baxter for $r(z)$, the pentagon for $r_3$, the
quartic $R$-matrix identity for $r_4$, and so on
(Proposition~\ref{prop:e1-shadow-r-matrix}).
The components of $\Theta_\cA^{\Eone}$ are the full data of the
line-operator algebra: an $R$-matrix, a braided category, a
Drinfeld--Jimbo Yangian.

\paragraph{Averaging as the sole information loss.}
The modular convolution algebra $\gAmod$ is the
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariant of ${\gAmod}^{\Eone}$ via the
\emph{averaging map}
(Definition~\ref{def:e1-modular-convolution},
equation~\eqref{eq:e1-to-einfty-projection}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-averaging}
\operatorname{av}\colon
{\gAmod}^{\Eone}
\;\twoheadrightarrow\;
\gAmod,
\qquad
\operatorname{av}(\phi)(g, n)
\;=\;
\frac{1}{n!}\sum_{\sigma \in \Sigma_n} \sigma\cdot
\bigl(\phi(g, n) \circ \iota^{\mathrm{rib}}_{g,n}\bigr).
\end{equation}
The map $\operatorname{av}$ is a surjective dg Lie morphism
(Theorem~\ref{thm:fcom-coinvariant-fass}) sending the ordered MC
element to the symmetric one
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-mc-element}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-av-theta}
\operatorname{av}\bigl(\Theta_\cA^{\Eone}\bigr)
\;=\;
\Theta_\cA.
\end{equation}
At arity~$2$, this projection records only one number: the
$\Sigma_2$-coinvariant of the residue of $r(z)$ at $z = 0$ is the
modular characteristic
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-kappa-from-r}
\kappa(\cA)
\;=\;
\operatorname{av}\bigl(r(z)\bigr)
\;=\;
\tfrac{1}{2}\,\operatorname{tr}\!\bigl[\Res_{z = 0} r(z)\bigr],
\end{equation}
where the trace is the cyclic trace on $\End(V^{\otimes 2})$.  The
shadow obstruction tower
$(\kappa, \mathfrak{C}, \mathfrak{Q}, \ldots)$ is the
arity-by-arity image of
$(r(z), r_3, r_4, \ldots)$ under~$\operatorname{av}$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:e1-shadow-r-matrix}).  The $\Eone$ side
contains the matrix-valued meromorphic function; the $\Einf$ side
contains its $\Sigma_n$-symmetrized scalar.

\begin{theorem}[$\Eone$ primacy; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:e1-primacy}
\index{primacy thesis!E1@$\Eone$|textbf}
\index{averaging map!information loss}
\index{Reynolds operator!as averaging map}
Let\/ $\cA$ be a cyclic chiral algebra on\/~$X$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Surjectivity.)}
  The averaging map
  $\operatorname{av}\colon {\gAmod}^{\Eone}
  \twoheadrightarrow \gAmod$
  \textup{(}\eqref{eq:intro-averaging}\textup{)}
  is a surjective dg~Lie morphism.
\item \textup{(MC projection.)}
  $\operatorname{av}$ sends
  $\Theta_\cA^{\Eone} \mapsto \Theta_\cA$
  \textup{(}\eqref{eq:intro-av-theta}\textup{)}.
  In particular, the shadow obstruction tower
  $(\kappa, \mathfrak{C}, \mathfrak{Q}, \ldots)$
  is the arity-by-arity $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant image of the
  $R$-matrix tower $(r(z), r_3, r_4, \ldots)$.
\item \textup{(Bracket preservation.)}
  $\operatorname{av}$ preserves the dg~Lie bracket
  by $\Sigma_n$-equivariance of the operad composition maps.
\item \textup{(Non-splitting.)}
  The kernel\/ $\ker(\operatorname{av})$ is non-trivial:
  at arity~$3$ it contains the Drinfeld associator
  $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}(\cA)$, which is
  annihilated by\/ $\operatorname{av}$ but satisfies the
  pentagon equation in\/~${\gAmod}^{\Eone}$.
  In particular, the short exact sequence
  $0 \to \ker(\operatorname{av}) \to {\gAmod}^{\Eone}
  \xrightarrow{\operatorname{av}} \gAmod \to 0$
  does not split as dg~Lie algebras.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Parts (i)--(iii) are proved in
Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul}.
The map $\operatorname{av}$ is the Reynolds operator
$\phi \mapsto \frac{1}{n!}\sum_{\sigma} \sigma \cdot \phi$;
surjectivity follows because $\operatorname{av}$ restricts to
the identity on $\Sigma_n$-invariant elements, which span the
target~$\gAmod$.
The bracket is preserved because operad composition in
$F\!\Ass$ is $\Sigma_n$-equivariant: coinvariant projection
converts $T^c$-convolution to $\mathrm{Sym}^c$-convolution
(Theorem~\ref{thm:fcom-coinvariant-fass}).
The MC projection $\operatorname{av}(\Theta_\cA^{\Eone})
= \Theta_\cA$ follows from
$\operatorname{av}(D^{E_1}_\cA) = D_\cA$
(the genus-completed bar differential descends under
coinvariants) and $\operatorname{av}(\dzero) = \dzero$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-mc-element}).

For (iv), the Drinfeld associator
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}(\cA) \in \ker(\operatorname{av})_{0,3}$
satisfies the pentagon equation in ${\gAmod}^{\Eone}$
(the genus-$0$, arity-$3$ MC equation;
Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-mc-finite-arity},
equation~\eqref{eq:e1-mc-arity3}).
Its $\Sigma_3$-symmetrization vanishes because the
pentagon identity is antisymmetric under transposition
of the two internal edges.
Were $\operatorname{av}$ split, there would exist a
dg~Lie section $s\colon \gAmod \hookrightarrow
{\gAmod}^{\Eone}$ with $\operatorname{av} \circ s = \id$.
Such a section would lift the commutative MC element
$\Theta_\cA$ to an ordered element in the image of~$s$;
but the pentagon constraint on $\ker(\operatorname{av})$
forces the arity-$3$ component of any lift to involve
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$, which does not lie in the image
of any linear section.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Categorical $E_1$ primacy]

\begin{principle}[The $\Eone$ primacy thesis]
\label{princ:e1-primacy}
\index{averaging map!information loss}
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:e1-primacy}} admits a concise restatement.
The $\Eone$/ordered structure is the natural primitive of the
theory.  The $\Einf$/symmetric/modular structure is its
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariant shadow under~$\operatorname{av}$.
The five main theorems A--H are the invariants that survive
averaging.  Information flows in only one direction:
from the rich $\Eone$ side, which contains the $R$-matrix, the
KZ associator, the Drinfeld--Jimbo Yangian, the full braided category
of line operators, and the entire spectral scattering data,
to the coarser $\Einf$ side, which contains only the modular
characteristic~$\kappa$, the shadow obstruction tower, and the
genus expansion of the closed sector.
\end{principle}

\paragraph{Five reasons for the inversion.}
\textsc{Operadic primacy.}  In the hierarchy of $E_n$-operads,
$\Eone = \mathrm{Ass}$ is the primitive associative operad and
$\Einf = \Com$ arises from it as the colimit
$\Com = \colim_n E_n$ or, equivalently, by
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariants on each arity.  At the level of cofree
coalgebras, Stasheff's theorem identifies $T^c$ (deconcatenation)
as the universal cofree conilpotent coalgebra; the symmetric
cooperad $\mathrm{Sym}^c$ is recovered from $T^c$ by external
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariants.  The bar functor that respects this
universality is $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}} = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$, not the
symmetric bar.

\textsc{Information content.}  An equivariant
$\End(V^{\otimes n})$-valued meromorphic function strictly
contains its scalar trace.  The averaging map
$\operatorname{av}$ is surjective but not injective: the kernel
records the non-symmetric components of the line-operator data.
For Heisenberg, $r(z) = k\,\Omega/z$ with $\Omega$ the Casimir;
the symmetrization recovers the scalar
$\kappa(\cH_k) = k$, but the matrix structure of~$\Omega$ is
discarded.  For affine Kac--Moody, $r(z) = k\,\Omega/z$ contains the
full Yangian double; the symmetrization recovers
$\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k) = (k + h^\vee)\dim\mathfrak g/(2h^\vee)$
and discards the entire Drinfeld--Jimbo deformation.

\textsc{Physical primacy.}  The natural physical objects are
ordered.  Three-dimensional holomorphic-topological theories
have line operators along the topological direction $\R_t$
(Costello--Gaiotto~\cite{CostelloGaiotto2020}); these line operators
form the ordered convolution algebra ${\gAmod}^{\Eone}$ on
$\C_z \times \R_t$, and their fusion is the
$\Eone$-multiplication.  Open-string field theory and
factorization on bordered curves carry intrinsic boundary
orderings.  Integrable lattice models have $R$-matrices that
satisfy the (ordered) Yang--Baxter equation, not its symmetric
projection.  In every physical setting where the bar complex
appears, the line/boundary direction is ordered before any
$\Sigma_n$-symmetrization is taken.

\textsc{Categorical primacy.}  The categorical objects of
representation theory are ordered.  Quantum groups, Yangians, and
braided tensor categories are the natural framework for chiral
algebras coupled to a spectral parameter; their structure
constants are matrix-valued and obey ordered identities (CYBE,
pentagon, hexagon, mixed Yang--Baxter).  Symmetric monoidal
categories are obtained from braided ones by the
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariant procedure on the braid groupoid; this is
exactly the categorical analogue of the averaging
map~\eqref{eq:intro-averaging}.

\textsc{Operadic primacy of Swiss-cheese.}  The Swiss-cheese
operad $\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch},\mathrm{top}}$ that organizes the
open/closed bar complex
(Theorem~\ref{thm:thqg-swiss-cheese}) takes $\Eone$-data as its
input colour: the open colour is associative, the closed colour

The proved results are proved at

\subsection{Critical pitfalls}
\label{subsec:critical-pitfalls}
\index{critical pitfalls|textbf}

The following distinctions are the most common sources of error:

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Four objects.}
$\cA$ is the algebra.  $\barB(\cA)$ is the bar coalgebra.  $\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}} = H^*(\barB(\cA))$ is the Koszul dual coalgebra.  $\cA^! = (\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}})^\vee$ is the Koszul dual algebra.  The cobar $\Omega(\barB(\cA))$ recovers~$\cA$ (bar-cobar \emph{inversion}, not duality); $\cA^!$ is obtained by Verdier/linear duality, not cobar.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Grading.}
Cohomological ($|d| = +1$) throughout.  The bar complex uses desuspension: $s^{-1}$ lowers degree by~$1$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Classical vs.\ chiral Koszul duality.}
$\mathrm{Com}^! = \mathrm{Lie}$ (not $\mathrm{coLie}$):
the Koszul dual \emph{operad} of $\mathrm{Com}$ involves
an operadic suspension $\mathfrak{S}$ that shifts degrees
and twists by the sign representation
($\mathfrak{S}\mathcal{P}(n)
= \mathrm{sgn}_n \otimes \mathcal{P}(n)[n{-}1]$);
omitting the suspension yields $\mathrm{coLie}$ instead
of $\mathrm{Lie}$, which changes the sign of the
bracket in all convolution computations
(Appendix~\ref{app:signs}, Pitfall~9).
The Heisenberg algebra is \emph{not} self-dual.
Chiral Koszulness is not the same as classical
Koszulness.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Curved $A_\infty$.}
$m_1^2(a) = [m_0, a]$ (commutator, with a minus sign).  The bar differential satisfies $d^2 = 0$ always; curvature shows as $m_1^2 \neq 0$ in the $A_\infty$ algebra, not in the bar complex.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Sugawara.}
The Sugawara construction is \emph{undefined} at critical level $k = -h^\vee$; the Sugawara vector does not lie in the appropriate completion.  This is not ``$c$ diverges''; the construction does not exist.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Virasoro self-duality.}
The Virasoro algebra is chiral Koszul self-dual at $c = 13$ ($\mathrm{Vir}_c^! = \mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$), not $c = 26$.  Quadratic self-duality (uncurved bar, $\kappa = 0$) holds at $c = 0$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{FM compactification.}
The Fulton--MacPherson compactification is the blowup along diagonals, not the complement of diagonals.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Prime form.}
The prime form $E(z_1, z_2)$ is a section of $K^{-1/2} \boxtimes K^{-1/2}$, not $K^{+1/2}$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{QME.}
The quantum master equation is $\hbar \Delta S + \frac{1}{2}\{S, S\} = 0$ (factor $\frac{1}{2}$, not~$1$).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{$d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2 \neq 0$.}
The bracket component of the bar differential does not square to zero on its own: this is proved for all $2048$ sign conventions for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$.  The full Borcherds differential gives $d^2 = 0$.
