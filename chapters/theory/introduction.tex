\chapter{Introduction}

\section{From a point to a curve}
\label{sec:volume-one-route}

Classical Koszul duality terminates over a point: a quadratic algebra~$A$ has a Koszul dual coalgebra~$A^{!}$, the bar construction $B(A)$ mediates between them, and the inversion $\Omega(B(A))\xrightarrow{\sim}A$ closes the theory. Over a smooth algebraic curve~$X$ it does not terminate. Generators become sections of a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module; relations become operator product expansions; the bar differential becomes an integral transform with logarithmic kernel $\eta=d\log(z_1-z_2)$ on Fulton--MacPherson configuration spaces; the topology of higher-genus curves forces Hodge-curvature obstructions that have no classical counterpart.

This monograph studies one phenomenon: the chiral Koszul reflection $K\colon\mathrm{Kosz}(X)\to\mathrm{Kosz}(X)^{\mathrm{op}}$ on chirally Koszul algebras over~$X$. The atomic object is the ordered bar coalgebra
\[
\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}_X(\cA)\;=\;T^{c}\bigl(s^{-1}\bar\cA\bigr),
\]
a cofree $\Eone$-coalgebra with deconcatenation coproduct, R-matrix carrier, and Yangian source. Its degree-$2$ component is the classical $r$-matrix $r_\cA(z)$. Its symmetric coinvariant $\barB^{\Sigma}_X(\cA)=\Sym^{c}(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ is the modular shadow; the lossy projection that discards the $R$-matrix and retains only the scalar modular characteristic extracted from $\mathrm{av}(r_\cA(z))$. For non-abelian affine Kac--Moody the precise degree-$2$ formula is $\kappa(\cA)=\mathrm{av}(r_\cA(z))+\dim(\fg)/2$. The problem is to build and control the ordered bar on algebraic curves at all genera. This monograph solves it through two equations and seven theorems.

The two equations are the climax of Volume~I (Chapter~\ref{chap:climax-platonic}, \emph{Climax Theorem}). The first identifies the bar differential as the pullback of Arnold's flat connection on configuration spaces:
\[
\boxed{\;d_{\barB}\;=\;\mathrm{KZ}^{\ast}\!\bigl(\nabla_{\mathrm{Arnold}}\bigr).\;}
\tag{G2}
\]
The second identifies the universal Koszul conductor with the ghost charge of the BRST resolution:
\[
\boxed{\;K(\cA)\;=\;-c_{\mathrm{ghost}}\bigl(\mathrm{BRST}(\cA)\bigr).\;}
\tag{G1}
\]
From (G1) and (G2) the seven theorems descend as corollaries: the Koszul reflection $K^{2}\simeq\id$ (Theorem~A), bar--cobar inversion (Theorem~B), the Lagrangian decomposition into complementary halves $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA)\oplus\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^{!})$ with scalar shadow $\kappa+\kappa^{!}=\varrho_\cA K(\cA)$ (Theorem~C, $\varrho_\cA$ the family-specific anomaly ratio, $K(\cA)=c(\cA)+c(\cA^{!})$ the Koszul conductor of (G1)), the genus tensor curvature $\mathrm{obs}_g=\kappa\lambda_g$ (Theorem~D), the chiral Hochschild concentration in $\{0,1,2\}$ (Theorem~H), the universal holography functor $\Phi_{\mathrm{hol}}$ (Theorem~F), and the infinite-fingerprint completeness $\phi(\cA)$ (Theorem~G). The Trinity Theorem (Chapter~\ref{chap:universal-conductor}) records that $K$ is universal: $K_E=K_c=K_g$, that is, Euler characteristic of the bar tower equals sum of central charges equals ghost charge of BRST. The Quadrichotomy Theorem (Chapter~\ref{chap:shadow-quadrichotomy-platonic}) records that the Riccati identity $H(t)^{2}=t^{4}Q_c(t)$ on each primary line forces the depth $r_{\max}$ to lie in $\{2,3,4,\infty\}$, partitioning the standard landscape into classes $\mathbf{G}/\mathbf{L}/\mathbf{C}/\mathbf{M}$ with critical companion $\mathbf{FF}$.

The organising principle is as follows. The geometry of the
curve~$X$ determines an operad (the Fulton--MacPherson
compactifications $\FM_n(X)$ with their boundary strata); the
operad determines a bar complex (the cofree coalgebra
$T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ with its residue-extraction differential);
the bar complex computes the invariants (the modular
characteristic~$\kappa$, the shadow obstruction tower, the
chiral Hochschild cohomology $\ChirHoch^*$). At each step the
passage is forced: one form ($\eta = d\log(z_1 - z_2)$), one
relation (Arnold), one object ($\Theta_\cA$), one equation
($D\Theta + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta,\Theta] = 0$). The
two-equation climax \textup{(G1)+(G2)} is the deepest
inevitability: the bar differential is not primitive, but the
chiral instantiation of Arnold's universal connection through
the Knizhnik--Zamolodchikov functor; and the universal
conductor is not a per-family invariant, but the BRST ghost
charge functorially attached to every chirally Koszul algebra.

The obstruction is geometric. On an algebraic curve~$X$, the bar differential is an integral transform whose kernel is the logarithmic propagator $d\log(z_i - z_j)$ on Fulton--MacPherson compactifications of configuration spaces. Already at genus~$0$, chiral Koszul duality differs from its classical counterpart. Vertex algebras live on the formal disk~$D$, not on a bare point; the passage from~$D$ to a point discards the thickening data (completion, growth conditions) that the $\mathcal{D}$-module structure depends on. The affine line~$\mathbb{A}^1$ deformation-retracts onto a point, but this retraction is additional data: relating the bar construction on~$\mathbb{A}^1$ to the classical bar over a point requires specifying the retraction and its attendant homotopy transfer. Already on~$\mathbb{A}^1$, the configuration spaces $\mathrm{Conf}_n(\mathbb{A}^1)$ carry Fulton--MacPherson compactifications and the Arnol'd algebra $H^*(\mathrm{Conf}_n(\mathbb{A}^1))$, generated by the forms $\omega_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$ subject to the Arnold relation: these structures are entirely absent over a point. On~$\mathbb{P}^1 = \mathbb{A}^1 \cup \mathbb{A}^1$, compactness further changes the homotopy type of the configuration spaces. The classical theory embeds into this picture via the formal-disk restriction, but the embedding is not an equivalence: even at genus~$0$, the ordered-versus-unordered bar distinction and the configuration-space geometry are genuinely new. At genus~$g \geq 1$, the Hodge bundle $\mathbb{E}$ on~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ introduces curvature into the bar differential, producing central extensions, conformal anomalies, and curved $A_\infty$ structures that have no analogue over a point.

To state what changes, recall the classical theory. Let $V$ be a finite-dimensional graded vector space, $R \subset V^{\otimes 2}$ a subspace of quadratic relations, and $A = T(V)/(R)$ the quadratic algebra. The bar construction $B(A) = (T^c(s^{-1}\bar{A}), d_B)$ is the cofree conilpotent coalgebra on the desuspended augmentation ideal, with differential recording the splitting of elements into pairs of generators: $d_B(s^{-1}a) = \sum s^{-1}a' \otimes s^{-1}a''$. The cobar construction $\Omega(C)$ reverses the process. The comparison map
\[
\varepsilon \colon \Omega(B(A)) \longrightarrow A
\]
is always a dg algebra morphism. When it is a quasi-isomorphism, $A$ is \emph{Koszul}: the bar cohomology concentrates in degree~$1$, the Koszul dual coalgebra is $A^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !\textasciigrave}} = H^1(B(A))$, and the Koszul dual algebra is $A^! = (A^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !\textasciigrave}})^\vee = T(V^*)/R^\perp$. The derived categories of $A$-modules and $A^!$-modules become equivalent (Priddy~\cite{Priddy70}, Beilinson--Ginzburg--Soergel~\cite{BGS96}, Loday--Vallette~\cite{LV12}). The theory is complete: $\Omega(B(A)) \simeq A$ is bar-cobar \emph{inversion}, and nothing beyond this equivalence remains.

Over a curve, the theory does not terminate; the question is what replaces it. Let $X$ be a smooth projective curve over~$\mathbb{C}$. A \emph{chiral algebra}~$\cA$ on~$X$ (Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite{BD04}) is a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module equipped with a chiral bracket
\[
\mu^{\mathrm{ch}} \colon j_* j^*(\cA \boxtimes \cA) \longrightarrow \Delta_!(\cA),
\]
where $j \colon X^2 \setminus \Delta \hookrightarrow X^2$ is the complement of the diagonal and $\Delta_! \colon D(\mathcal{D}_X) \to D(\mathcal{D}_{X^2})$ is the pushforward. In local coordinates the chiral bracket encodes the operator product expansion:
\[
a(z) b(w) \;\sim\; \sum_{n \geq 0} \frac{(a_{(n)} b)(w)}{(z - w)^{n+1}}.
\]
The Borcherds identity, the defining axiom of a vertex algebra, is the chiral-algebraic Jacobi identity: it couples all pole orders in a single constraint.

\begin{remark}[CG opening and the $\Eone$ atom]
\label{rem:two-strata}
\index{two strata|textbf}
Two reference algebras organize the theory
(see Chapter~\ref{ch:algebraic-foundations}, \S\ref{sec:dictionary} for the formal definitions).
The \textbf{Heisenberg} algebra is the \emph{CG opening}: its OPE has only double poles, so collisions are symmetric and unordered ($\Einf$-chiral); it serves as the commutative base case in which every clause of the reconstruction thesis is computable, but it is \emph{not} an $\Eone$ atom.
The \textbf{Yangian} is the genuine $\Eone$ \emph{atom}: simple poles give collisions a canonical ordering ($\Eone$-chiral, genuinely nonlocal), and it is the unique nonlocal generator of the $\Eone$-chiral Koszul programme.
Every standard family (Kac--Moody, Virasoro, $\mathcal{W}$-algebras, lattice vertex algebras) sits between the CG opening and the $\Eone$ atom, and the bar complex on higher-genus curves classifies them.
\end{remark}
At genus~$g \geq 1$, the ordered bar differential acquires a
curvature: $(d_{\mathrm{fib}}^{\mathrm{ord}})^2 = r_\cA(z) \cdot
\omega_g$, matrix-valued in
$\operatorname{End}_\cA(2) \otimes H^{1,1}(\mathcal{C}_g)$.
Averaging over $\Sigma_2$ collapses this to the scalar equation
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$, where
$\omega_g = c_1(\mathbb{E})$ is the Hodge class on
$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ and
$\kappa(\cA)$ is the modular
characteristic
(Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}).

The curvature generates an infinite tower.
The ordered bar construction on curves defines an
$E_1$-modular convolution algebra~$\mathfrak{g}^{E_1}_\cA$,
whose Maurer--Cartan element~$\Theta_\cA$ encodes the full genus
expansion with its $R$-matrix data intact. Under
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariance, $\Theta_\cA$ projects to a pronilpotent
dg Lie algebra~$\gAmod$ (the modular convolution algebra). The
finite-order projections $\Theta_\cA^{\leq r}$ extract successively
finer invariants: the $R$-matrix $r_\cA(z)$ at degree~$2$ (averaging
to $\kappa(\cA)$), a cubic shadow~$C$ at degree~$3$, a quartic
resonance class~$Q$ at degree~$4$. The \emph{shadow depth} $r_{\max}(\cA)$, the
largest degree at which the shadow is nonzero, equals
the $L_\infty$ formality level of the genus-$0$ part of $\gAmod$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-formality-identification}).

The tower classifies chiral algebras into four shadow-depth classes
on the non-critical locus, with a fifth companion stratum at the
critical level completing the classification.
The Heisenberg has $r_{\max} = 2$ (formal,
$\Delta = 0$, class~$G$); affine Kac--Moody algebras at non-critical
level have $r_{\max} = 3$ (class~$L$); the $\beta\gamma$ system has
$r_{\max} = 4$ with nilpotent $R$-matrix (class~$C$); Virasoro and
$\mathcal{W}$-algebras have $r_{\max} = \infty$ (class~$M$); at the
critical level $k=-h^\vee$ the Sugawara construction is undefined,
Theorem~H fails, and the Feigin--Frenkel centre
$\operatorname{Fun}(\operatorname{Op}_{{}^L\fg})$ makes
$\ChirHoch^\bullet$ unbounded (Feigin--Frenkel stratum~$FF$). The
\emph{critical discriminant} $\Delta = 8\kappa S_4$ forces
$r_{\max}$ to lie in $\{2, 3, \infty\}$ on any one-dimensional
primary slice (Theorem~\ref{thm:single-line-dichotomy}); the
fourth class $r_{\max} = 4$ arises from stratum
separation.
The algebraic depth $d_{\mathrm{alg}} \in \{0, 1, 2, \infty\}$
admits no value~$3$ or any finite value $\geq 3$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:depth-gap-trichotomy}).
Swiss-cheese formality ($m_k^{\mathrm{SC}} = 0$ for
$k \geq 3$) holds if and only if the algebra belongs to
class~$\mathbf{G}$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:sc-formal-iff-class-g}).
For free-field algebras, the scalar formula
$F_g = \kappa \cdot \lambda_g$ is exact at all genera
\textup{(ALL-WEIGHT, with $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}} = 0$)}: the cross-channel correction
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$ vanishes identically
(Proposition~\ref{prop:free-field-scalar-exact}).


Three depths measure distinct aspects of the tower.
The \emph{shadow depth} $r_{\max}$ is the largest degree at which the shadow is nonzero.
The \emph{generating depth} $d_{\mathrm{gen}}$ is the smallest degree at which all higher operations are determined recursively.
The \emph{algebraic depth} $d_{\mathrm{alg}}$ records whether the tower terminates
(Definition~\ref{def:generating-depth}).
These are independent invariants: Virasoro has $d_{\mathrm{gen}} = 3$ but $d_{\mathrm{alg}} = \infty$; $m_3$ generates all higher operations, yet none vanish.

The tower is governed by three structure theorems, all projections of a single object: the Maurer--Cartan element
$\Theta_\cA \in \MC(\gAmod)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}).
\emph{Algebraicity}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:riccati-algebraicity}).
On each primary line~$L$, the shadow generating function satisfies
$H(t)^2 = t^4 Q_L(t)$,
where $Q_L$ is a quadratic polynomial in three invariants $(\kappa, \alpha, S_4)$. The
tower is the Taylor expansion of~$\sqrt{Q_L}$, algebraic of degree~$2$.
The discriminant
$\Delta$ governs a dichotomy:
$\Delta = 0$ terminates the tower; $\Delta \neq 0$ makes it infinite.

\emph{Formality identification}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-formality-identification}).
At genus~$0$, the shadow obstruction tower coincides with the $L_\infty$
formality obstruction tower of the convolution algebra
at all degrees. Genus~$g \geq 1$ corrections form a separate layer; the genus and degree directions are orthogonal
(Remark~\ref{rem:two-orthogonal-axes}).
The \emph{visibility formula}
(Corollary~\ref{cor:shadow-visibility-genus})
determines which shadow coefficients contribute at each genus:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-visibility}
g_{\min}(S_r) \;=\; \lfloor r/2 \rfloor + 1
\qquad (r \geq 3).
\end{equation}
Genus~$1$ sees only~$\kappa$ (degree~$2$).
Genus~$2$ first sees~$S_3$ (degree~$3$).
Genus~$3$ first sees~$S_4$ and~$S_5$ (degrees~$4$,~$5$).
Two new shadow coefficients enter at each genus.

\emph{Complementarity} (Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}).
$Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!) \simeq H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, Z(\cA))$:
the Verdier involution decomposes the ambient complex into complementary Lagrangians, constraining the Koszul
dual pair at every genus.

Classical Koszul duality over a point embeds into this picture via the formal-disk restriction of the genus~$0$, degree~$2$, $\Delta = 0$ stratum: the unique case where the tower terminates immediately. Already at genus~$0$ and degree~$\geq 3$, the Fulton--MacPherson compactifications of configuration spaces on the curve introduce Arnold relations and higher operations (the Jacobiator homotopy~$F_3$ on~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{0,4}$, for instance) that have no counterpart over a point. This monograph develops the theory at every stratum.

\section{The selection principle}
\label{sec:selection-principle}
\index{selection principle|textbf}
\index{Borcherds identity!as selection criterion}

The bar construction of \S\ref{sec:volume-one-route} accepts
any $\mathcal{D}_X$-module~$V$
with a meromorphic pairing
$\mu \colon j_* j^*(V \boxtimes V) \to \Delta_!(V)$.
Place sections at points of the Fulton--MacPherson compactification
$\overline{C}_n(X)$, extract residues along
boundary divisors, and assemble a graded object
$\barB_X(V, \mu)$ with candidate differential
$d = d_{\mathrm{internal}} + d_{\mathrm{residue}}
+ d_{\mathrm{dR}}$.
The construction is purely geometric; it imposes no algebraic axiom. The question is:
\begin{center}
\emph{For which inputs $(V, \mu)$ does
$d^2 = 0$?}
\end{center}

\noindent
Expanding $(d_1 + d_2 + d_3)^2$, the terms
$d_1^2$, $d_3^2$, and all mixed anticommutators vanish
for formal reasons (cochain complex, de~Rham,
residue exact sequence). The content is
$d_2^2 = 0$: residue extraction must be nilpotent
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-nilpotency-complete}).
Three cases arise from index overlap in
$d_2^2 = \sum_{i<j}\sum_{k<\ell}
\Res_{D_{k\ell}} \circ \Res_{D_{ij}}$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Disjoint indices}
 ($\{i,j\} \cap \{k,\ell\} = \emptyset$).
 Residue operations commute; cancellation is by the
 Koszul sign rule. No condition on~$\mu$.

\item \emph{One shared index} (say $j = k$).
 Three boundary strata meet at the codimension-$2$ locus
 $D_{ij\ell}$ of triple collision. The cyclic sum of
 iterated residues
 \[
 \sum_{\textup{cyclic}}
 \Res_{D_{j\ell}} \circ \Res_{D_{ij}}
 \bigl[\mu(\mu(\phi_i, \phi_j), \phi_\ell)
 \otimes \omega\bigr]
 = 0
 \]
 holds if and only if $\mu$ satisfies the
 Borcherds identity, the associativity of the chiral
 product, coupling all OPE pole orders simultaneously.

\item \emph{Same pair}
 ($\{i,j\} = \{k,\ell\}$).
 $\Res_{D_{ij}}^2 = 0$ automatically.
\end{enumerate}
Cases~(i) and~(iii) impose no condition.
Case~(ii) imposes exactly the Borcherds identity
(equation~\eqref{eq:borcherds-axiom},
Definition~\ref{def:chiral-algebra}).
The FM bar
construction produces $d^2 = 0$ if and only if the OPE
satisfies the Borcherds identity. This is the \emph{selection principle}:
configuration space geometry determines the algebraic axioms.

The two simplest families verify the principle by computation.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Heisenberg} ($\alpha(z)\alpha(w) \sim
k/(z-w)^2$, double pole only). No simple pole, so
$d_{\mathrm{bracket}} = 0$. The full differential reduces to
$d_{\mathrm{curvature}}$, which produces scalars:
$d_{\mathrm{curvature}}^2 = 0$ automatically. The Arnold
relation handles all of case~(ii). No algebraic input needed.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Kac--Moody} ($h(z)\,e(w) \sim
2e(w)/(z-w) + \cdots$, simple \emph{and} double poles).
The bracket component $d_{\mathrm{bracket}}$ is now nonzero, and
$d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2 \neq 0$, computed explicitly for all
$2048$ sign conventions
(\S\ref{subsec:frame-sl2-bar-low-degree},
Remark~\ref{rem:frame-why-dbracket-fails}). The obstruction is
proportional to the level~$k$: it is the double-pole data that
$d_{\mathrm{bracket}}$ ignores. The Borcherds identity absorbs
this obstruction:
\[
d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2
= -\{d_{\mathrm{bracket}},\, d_{\mathrm{curvature}}\}.
\]
The curvature is the obstruction; the Borcherds identity is the
integrability condition
(Proposition~\ref{prop:pole-decomposition}).

\begin{remark}[Classical vs.\ chiral selection]
\label{rem:classical-vs-chiral-selection}
\index{Koszul duality!classical vs.\ chiral}
The bar construction asks the same question at every level
of geometry:
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\textbf{Base} & \textbf{Condition for $d^2 = 0$}
 & \textbf{Selected objects} \\
\hline
Point & Jacobi identity & Lie algebras \\
Curve $X$ & Borcherds identity & Chiral algebras \\
$X$ over $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ & $+$ period corrections
 & Modular Koszul algebras
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Each row is forced by the geometry of the next. The Jacobi
identity handles triple collisions when all poles are simple.
On a curve, multiple pole orders mix at the codimension-$2$
strata of $\overline{C}_n(X)$; the Borcherds identity is
the unique condition that makes this mixing nilpotent. At
higher genus, period corrections from the Hodge bundle restore
$\Dg{g}^{\,2} = 0$. Chiral algebras are what configuration
space geometry accepts. Modular Koszul algebras are what the
genus tower accepts.
\end{remark}

Five existing programmes address aspects of the problem.
Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite{BD04} construct the factorisation
algebra on $\Ran(X)$ and prove chiral homology is well-defined,
but do not treat bar-cobar inversion, complementarity, or the
genus tower.
Francis--Gaitsgory~\cite{FG12} establish chiral Koszul duality
at genus~$0$ for Lie-type (coLie) bar complexes; their
framework does not reach higher genera, the full tensor bar,
or the shadow obstruction tower.
Costello--Gwilliam~\cite{CG17} develop factorisation algebras
as a framework for perturbative QFT and prove the
$\mathsf{E}_3$-algebra structure of perturbative
Chern--Simons theory; they do not treat Koszul duality,
complementarity, or the modular characteristic.
Gui--Li--Zeng~\cite{GLZ22} prove a quadratic duality for chiral
algebras at the cochain level; their result is a genus-$0$,
degree-$2$ theorem and does not address the shadow tower, higher
genera, or the $\Eone$ ordered bar.
Malikov--Schechtman~\cite{MS24} construct homotopy chiral
algebras ($\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebras) as the correct
homotopical input, but do not themselves prove the
five structural theorems.

\section{The two-equation climax and its seven corollaries}
\label{sec:NAP-unifying}
\label{sec:main-results-complete}
\label{sec:seven-theorems}
\label{sec:climax-and-corollaries}
\index{Koszul duality!chiral|textbf}
\index{modular Koszul duality|textbf}
\index{quantum corrections!as modular completion}
\index{climax theorem!introduction|textbf}
\index{universal conductor!introduction|textbf}

The selection principle determines which algebraic inputs are admissible: the bar construction accepts exactly the chiral algebras (those satisfying the Borcherds identity). The remaining question is what algebraic structure governs the resulting bar complex on a curve. The answer is the two-equation climax of Chapter~\ref{chap:climax-platonic}, from which seven theorems descend: five invariant-skeleton theorems A--D and~H extract the $\Sigma_n$-invariant content of the ordered bar, and two architectural theorems F and~G (universal holography and infinite-fingerprint completeness) complete the structural closure on the non-degenerate locus. The 2026-04-16 reconstitution closed four former frontiers (periodic CDG admissible-level, curved-Dunn $H^2=0$ at $g\ge 2$, Drinfeld--Sokolov--Hochschild in class~$\mathbf{M}$, chiral Deligne--Tamarkin), upgrading each theorem where the closure applies; on the non-degenerate locus $\{\kappa\ne 0,\ \text{non-critical level},\ \text{Koszul locus}\}$ every theorem admits at least two genuinely independent verification paths registered in the programme's independent-verification infrastructure (the \emph{bell theorem}); off the locus (critical stratum~$\mathbf{FF}$; Creutzig--Kanade--Linshaw logarithmic admissible lane; super-Yangian cluster; CY functor at $d\ge 3$) the bell rings and the residual frontier is explicit.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{The climax: bar differential = pulled-back Arnold connection.}
On configuration space $\mathrm{Conf}_n(\bC)$ Arnold's flat connection is
\[
\nabla_{\mathrm{Arnold}}\;=\;d\;-\;\sum_{i<j}\eta_{ij},
\qquad
\eta_{ij}\;=\;d\log(z_i-z_j),
\]
characterised by the three-term Arnold relation
$\eta_{ij}\wedge\eta_{jk}+\eta_{jk}\wedge\eta_{ki}+\eta_{ki}\wedge\eta_{ij}=0$.
The Knizhnik--Zamolodchikov functor
$\mathrm{KZ}\colon \mathrm{Conf}_n(\bC)\to (\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}_X(\cA))^{\otimes n}$
attaches to each configuration the corresponding tensor mode. The
\emph{Climax Theorem} (Theorem~\ref{thm:dbar-equals-kz-arnold} of
Chapter~\ref{chap:climax-platonic}) asserts the identification
\[
d_{\barB}\;=\;\mathrm{KZ}^{\ast}\!\bigl(\nabla_{\mathrm{Arnold}}\bigr).
\tag{G2}
\]
Drinfeld--Kohno (KZ holonomy at integer level), Verlinde (full curve at
integer level), Borcherds (BKM denominator), and the bar
differential itself are specialisations of (G2) at distinct geometric
fibrations of the universal pair (Arnold connection on
configuration space, Knizhnik--Zamolodchikov functor on chiral
algebras).

\medskip\noindent\textbf{The conductor: Koszul reflection = ghost charge of BRST.}
The universal conductor functor
\[
K\colon\mathrm{BRST\text{-}GaugedChirAlg}\;\longrightarrow\;\bZ
\]
of Chapter~\ref{chap:universal-conductor} sends a BRST-gauged chiral
algebra to its bar Euler characteristic. The \emph{Trinity Theorem}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:K-trinity}) records that
\[
K_{E}(\cA)\;=\;K_{c}(\cA)\;=\;K_{g}(\cA),
\]
where $K_{E}$ is the Euler characteristic of the bar tower, $K_{c}$ is
the sum of central charges $c+c^{!}$ of the Koszul pair, and $K_{g}$ is
the ghost charge of the BRST resolution. The \emph{Ghost Identity}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:ghost-identity-platonic}) is the closed form
\[
K(\cA)\;=\;-c_{\mathrm{ghost}}\bigl(\mathrm{BRST}(\cA)\bigr).
\tag{G1}
\]
Per-family kappa formulas
($\kappa(\cH_k)=k$, $\kappa(V_k(\fg))=\dim(\fg)(k+h^{\vee})/(2h^{\vee})$,

self-dual $c=13$, $250/3$ for $\cW_3$, $98/3$ for Bershadsky--Polyakov)
are evaluations of the same functor through the family-specific

where the Trinity $K(\cA)$ takes values $\{2\dim\fg,26,100,196\}$
on the same four families (principal $\cW_N$: $\varrho_N=H_N-1$;
KM/free: $\varrho_\cA=0$; BP: $\varrho_{\mathrm{BP}}=1/6$).

The shadow obstruction tower of $\Theta_\cA$ obeys a master Riccati

H(t)^{2}\;=\;t^{4}Q_{c}(t),
\qquad

\]

\medskip\noindent\textbf{The forced chain A $\Rightarrow$ B $\Rightarrow$ C $\Rightarrow$ D $\Rightarrow$ H.}

\textup{(unconditional at $g=1$)} for every family, while at genus $g \ge 2$ multi-weight

\subsection{The Heisenberg algebra}
\label{subsec:five-theorems-heisenberg}

Take $\cA = \cH_k$ with OPE
$J(z)\,J(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$. One generator, one double pole, no
simple pole.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~A (bar-cobar adjunction).}\enspace
The bar complex is
$\barB(\cH_k) = T^c(s^{-1}\overline{\cH_k}) = T^c(s^{-1}\C\cdot J)$.
The instant computation is the binary residue
\[
d_{\barB}[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]
\;=\;
\Res_{z_1 = z_2}\!\left[
 \frac{k}{(z_1 - z_2)^2}\cdot d\log(z_1-z_2)
\right]
\;=\; k.
\]
At degree~$3$: the Arnold relation on $\FM_3(\C)$ gives
$d_{\barB}^2 = 0$. The three collision divisors contribute
$k \cdot (\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23} + \eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{13}
+ \eta_{13}\wedge\eta_{12}) = 0$.

Theorem~A says that this bar coalgebra has a Verdier-dual partner:
$\mathbb{D}_{\Ran}\,\barB(\cH_k) \simeq (\cH_k^!)_\infty$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~B (bar-cobar inversion).}\enspace
The Heisenberg bar cohomology is concentrated in bar degree~$1$:
$H^{-1}(\barB(\cH_k)) = \C\cdot s^{-1}J$.
Hence the Koszul dual coalgebra is
$\cH_k^{\mathrm{i}} = \C\cdot s^{-1}J$ and the Koszul dual algebra
is the curved symmetric chiral algebra
$\cH_k^! = (\cH_k^{\mathrm{i}})^\vee$ with opposite modular
characteristic $\kappa(\cH_k^!) = -k$.
The cobar of the bar is
$\Omega(\barB(\cH_k)) = T(s\,\C\cdot s^{-1}J) = \C[J]$,
which is $\cH_k$ as a graded algebra. The counit
$\varepsilon\colon\Omega(\barB(\cH_k)) \to \cH_k$ is a
quasi-isomorphism: the Heisenberg is Koszul.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~C (complementarity).}\enspace
Here the center local system is constant, so the ambient complex
already splits in the form asserted by Theorem~C:
\[
Q_g(\cH_k) \oplus Q_g(\cH_k^!) \cong
H^*(\overline{\cM}_g, \cZ_{\cH_k}).
\]
For $g \ge 1$ the two summands are Verdier-Lagrangian partners.
On the scalar shadow one recovers the Heisenberg identity
$\kappa(\cH_k) + \kappa(\cH_k^!) = k + (-k) = 0$.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~D (modular characteristic).}\enspace
At genus~$1$: the self-loop graph $\Gamma_{\mathrm{irr}}$ on
$\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$ contributes
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = k \cdot \omega_1$. The genus-$1$
obstruction number is $F_1 = k/24$. The generating function:
\[
\sum_{g \ge 1} F_g(\cH_k)\,x^{2g}
\;=\;
k\!\left(\frac{x/2}{\sin(x/2)} - 1\right)
\qquad \textup{(UNIFORM-WEIGHT)}
\;=\;
\frac{k}{24}\,x^2
+ \frac{7k}{5760}\,x^4
+ \frac{31k}{967680}\,x^6
+ \cdots
\]
The coefficients are
$F_g = k \cdot \frac{2^{2g-1}-1}{2^{2g-1}} \cdot
\frac{|B_{2g}|}{(2g)!}$,
where $B_{2g}$ is the $2g$-th Bernoulli number.
(The chiral partition function
$Z_{\mathrm{ch}} = 1/\eta(\tau)$ gives
$F_1 = \kappa(\cH_k)/24 = k/24$
as the genus-$1$ free energy on~$\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$.)

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~H (chiral Hochschild).}\enspace
$\ChirHoch^*(\cH_k)$ is the three-term polynomial model
$(\C, \C, \C)$ concentrated in degrees
$\{0, 1, 2\}$: the class in degree~$0$ is the center (scalars),
the class in degree~$1$ is the outer chiral derivation
$D(\alpha) = \mathbf{1}$ (non-inner because $\alpha_{(0)} = 0$
when the OPE has no simple pole), and the class in degree~$2$ is
the level deformation (double-pole OPE datum).
The Koszul resolution has length~$2$, so
$\ChirHoch^n = 0$ for $n \ge 3$; Hilbert series $1 + t + t^2$,
the rank-$1$ case of the polynomial-growth theorem.

The full computation appears in Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}.

\subsection{The general theorems}
\label{subsec:five-theorems-general}

Here $\operatorname{Fact}(X)$ denotes the symmetric
monoidal category of factorization algebras on~$X$
(Chapter~\ref{ch:algebraic-foundations});
$\operatorname{Alg}_{\mathrm{aug}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))$
is the category of \emph{augmented} factorization algebras
(equipped with a map $\cA \to \omega_X$ to the vacuum);
$\operatorname{CoAlg}_{\mathrm{conil}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))$
is the category of \emph{conilpotent} factorization
coalgebras (the iterated reduced coproduct eventually
vanishes).

Each statement below is the av-image of a corresponding
$\Eone$ statement on the ordered convolution algebra
${\gAmod}^{\Eone}$
(see~\S\ref{subsec:e1-as-primitive} for the averaging map
$\operatorname{av}\colon{\gAmod}^{\Eone}\twoheadrightarrow\gAmod$
and Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul} for the ordered
counterparts $\mathrm{A}^{\Eone}$--$\mathrm{H}^{\Eone}$).
The five theorems are the invariants that survive averaging.

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Theorem~A} (Geometric bar-cobar duality,
 Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}).
 The deficiency is immediate: without Theorem~A the symbol
 $\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}\bar{B}_X(\cA)$ has no proved
 identification with a chiral algebra. The unique survivor is the
 chiral Koszul pair itself. For a chiral Koszul pair one has
 \[
 \cC_i \xrightarrow{\sim} \bar{B}_X(\cA_i),
 \qquad
 \Omega_X(\cC_i) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA_i,
 \qquad
 \mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}} \bar{B}_X(\cA_1) \simeq \cA_2,
 \]
 and similarly with the indices reversed. This is the quadratic
 proved lane. Instant check: for Heisenberg the binary residue
 $d_{\barB}[s^{-1}J|s^{-1}J]=k$ already produces the bar coalgebra whose
 Verdier dual recovers the opposite-level curved dual.

\item \emph{Theorem~B} (Bar-cobar inversion,
 Theorem~\ref{thm:higher-genus-inversion}).
 Without Theorem~B, the bar transform can forget the boundary algebra.
 Theorem~B removes that failure on the Koszul locus:
 \[
 \Omega_X \bar{B}_X(\cA) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA,
 \]
 genus by genus, with $E_2$-collapse of the bar-cobar spectral
 sequence. The genus-$0$ statement is unconditional. The genus-$g \geq 1$
 statement is proved on the modular pre-Koszul lane and is
 unconditional on the standard CFT-type landscape except
 integer-spin $\beta\gamma$. Off the locus, the curved bar-cobar object
 persists, but the coderived continuation is kept separate from
 Theorem~B itself. Instant check: for Heisenberg,
 $\Omega(\barB(\cH_k)) \simeq \cH_k$.

\item \emph{Theorem~C} (Deformation-obstruction complementarity,
 Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}).
 The deficiency here is higher-genus ambiguity: without a
 polarization, one cannot separate deformation from obstruction.
 Theorem~C says that the Verdier involution is the unique survivor.
 Writing
 $\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) := R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g,\mathcal{Z}_\cA)$,
 one has the homotopy decomposition
 \[
 \mathbf{C}_g(\cA)
 \simeq
 \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \oplus \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)
 \]
 for every genus, and for $g \geq 1$ the induced pairing is perfect
 with each summand Lagrangian. At the cohomological shadow level,
 \begin{equation}\label{eq:complementarity-intro}
 Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!) \cong
 H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathcal{Z}_\cA).
 \end{equation}
 The Verdier-side Lagrangian splitting is proved. The ambient
 shifted-symplectic upgrade is conditional on perfectness and
 nondegeneracy. Instant check: for Heisenberg the center local system
 is constant, so the decomposition is visible at once.

\item \emph{Theorem~D$_{\mathrm{scal}}$} (Genus universality,
 Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality}).
 The deficiency is scalar drift: genus by genus, the bar tower could
 produce unrelated numbers. Theorem~D forces every genus back to a
 single scalar~$\kappa(\cA)$, fixed at genus~$1$. For algebras whose
 strong generators have the same conformal weight,
 $\mathrm{obs}_g = \kappa \cdot \lambda_g
 \in H^*(\overline{\cM}_g)$ \textup{(UNIFORM-WEIGHT)} and
 $F_g = \kappa \cdot \hat{A}_g$ at all genera \textup{(UNIFORM-WEIGHT)},
 unconditionally. Three independent routes close the passage from
 genus~$1$ to all~$g \ge 2$: a $K$-theoretic push-forward on the Hodge
 bundle over~$\overline{\cM}_g$; clutching-uniqueness on the boundary
 divisor, which fixes the genus-$g$ class from its genus-$1$ and
 genus-$(g-1)$ restrictions; and an independent PTVV shifted-symplectic
 derivation on the universal curve, which bypasses the clutching step
 entirely. The three routes agree. For multi-weight algebras the
 diagonal term remains universal and the full free energy is
 \[
 F_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}
 + \delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}(\cA),
 \]
 with $\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$ the mixed-channel correction of
 Theorem~\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}. For
 $\mathcal{W}_3$ this correction is nonzero already at genus~$2$, so
 the all-weight scalar formula fails there; the diagonal kernel
 $\kappa(\cA)\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}$ itself remains unconditional.
 The scalar $\kappa$ is universal,
 additive under tensor product, and duality-constrained
 ($\kappa + \kappa' = 0$ for affine Kac--Moody and free
 fields, and a fixed nonzero constant for
 $\mathcal{W}$-algebras).
 On the uniform-weight lane, the generating function is the
 $\hat{A}$-genus
 \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:universal-generating-function}}\textup{)}:
 \begin{equation}\label{eq:ahat-genus-generating-function-intro}
 \sum_{g \geq 1} F_g(\cA) \cdot \hbar^{2g}
 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \bigl(\hat{A}(i\hbar) - 1\bigr)
 \qquad \textup{(UNIFORM-WEIGHT)}.
 \end{equation}
 At genus~$1$ this reduces to $F_1(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)/24$ for every
 family.

 \emph{Theorem~D$_\Delta$} (Spectral characteristic,
 Theorem~\ref{thm:spectral-characteristic}):
 the spectral discriminant $\Delta_\cA(x)$ is a separately proved
 non-scalar invariant.

 $\kappa$ is the one-loop coefficient of the effective action;
 its vanishing removes the scalar genus-$1$ anomaly, while
 higher-genus multi-weight corrections are a separate issue.
 Together, $D_{\mathrm{scal}}$ and $D_\Delta$ are the
 characteristic classes of the factorization homology
 bundle $\int_{\Sigma_g}\!\cA$
 (Remark~\ref{rem:four-levels}); the bundle
 itself (its fibers, flat connection, and modular
 functor structure) is the full modular homotopy invariant
 (Conjecture~\ref{conj:categorical-modular-kd}).

\item \emph{Theorem~H} (Chiral Hochschild cohomology,
 Theorem~\ref{thm:hochschild-polynomial-growth}).
 The deficiency is coefficient blow-up: the bulk derived center could
 a priori live in arbitrarily high degrees. Theorem~H says that on the
 Koszul locus it does not. One has
 $\ChirHoch^n(\cA) = 0$ for $n < 0$ and $n > 2$, and the Hilbert
 polynomial is
 \[
 P_{\cA}(t)
 =
 \dim Z(\cA)
 + \dim \ChirHoch^1(\cA)\, t
 + \dim Z(\cA^!)\, t^2.
 \]
 This is the sharp theorem. The specializations are family-sensitive:
 Heisenberg gives $1+t+t^2$; generic Virasoro and principal
 $\mathcal{W}$-algebras give $1+t^2$; generic affine Kac--Moody gives
 $\ChirHoch^1(V_k(\fg)) \cong \fg$, so no uniform four-dimensional
 bound is available. Critical level is outside the theorem's regime:
 there the Beilinson--Drinfeld comparison yields an unbounded oper-type
 center. The primitive step is the descent lemma
 (Lemma~\ref{lem:chirhoch-descent}), reducing Theorem~H to Theorem~A's
 Verdier intertwining.
\end{itemize}

\begin{warning}[Three Hochschild theories]
\label{warn:three-hochschild-intro}
\index{Hochschild cohomology!three variants}%
Three inequivalent notions of ``Hochschild cohomology'' coexist
in this programme; conflating them is a persistent source of
error.
\emph{Chiral Hochschild} $\ChirHoch^*(\cA) =
H^*(\operatorname{CoDer}(\barB(\cA)))$ is the cohomology of
coderivations of the bar coalgebra; it lives on the
curve~$X$ and has cohomological amplitude~$[0,2]$ on the
Koszul locus (Theorem~H).
\emph{Topological Hochschild} $\mathrm{HH}^*_{\mathrm{top}}$
is the factorization homology on the circle $S^1$; it lives
in dimension~$1$ higher and governs the $\Eone$-chiral bar of
Volume~II.
\emph{Categorical Hochschild} $\mathrm{HH}^*(\mathrm{Rep}(\cA))$
is the categorical Hochschild cohomology of the module category; it governs
the Drinfeld center and the $E_2$ structure.
See Principle~\ref{princ:geometric-origin-hochschild}
(Chapter~\ref{ch:algebraic-foundations}) for the geometric
origin that distinguishes all three.
\end{warning}

\begin{principle}[Five facets of $\Theta_\cA$]
\label{princ:five-facets}
\index{MC element!five facets|textbf}%
Let\/ $\cA$ be a modular Koszul chiral algebra on a smooth projective
curve~$X$, and let\/ $\Theta_\cA := D_\cA - d_\cA^{(0)} \in \gAmod$
be the bar-intrinsic Maurer--Cartan element
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}}\textup{)}.
The five main theorems characterize five
\emph{facets} of~$\Theta_\cA$: existence, faithfulness,
decomposition, leading coefficient, and coefficient ring.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\Alph*)}]
\item \emph{Arena}
 \textup{(}Theorem~A\textup{)}.
 $\Theta_\cA$ \textbf{exists}.
 The bar--cobar adjunction constructs the modular convolution
 algebra~$\gAmod$ in which~$\Theta_\cA$ lives; with it,
 $\Theta_\cA$ arises automatically as $D_\cA - d_\cA^{(0)}$.

\item \emph{Faithfulness}
 \textup{(}Theorem~B\textup{)}.
 $\Theta_\cA$ \textbf{determines}~$\cA$.
 On the Koszul locus, the counit
 $\Omega_X(\barB_X(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$ is a
 quasi-isomorphism.

\item \emph{Decomposition}
 \textup{(}Theorem~C\textup{)}.
 $\Theta_\cA$ \textbf{splits}.
 The genus-$g$ projection decomposes under the Verdier involution
 into complementary Lagrangians
 $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \oplus \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)
 = H^*(\overline{\cM}_g,\, Z(\cA))$.

\item \emph{Leading coefficient}
 \textup{(}Theorem~D\textup{)}.
 $\Theta_\cA$ has \textbf{universal scalar projection}.
 On the uniform-weight lane, the degree-$2$ genus-$g$ component is
 $\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)\,\lambda_g$ \textup{(UNIFORM-WEIGHT)} at all genera,
 with $\kappa(\cA)$ universal and governed by the
 $\hat{A}$-genus. At genus~$1$ this holds
 \textup{(unconditional at $g=1$)} for all families.

\item \emph{Coefficient ring}
 \textup{(}Theorem~H\textup{)}.
 $\Theta_\cA$ has \textbf{polynomial coefficients}.
 $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is concentrated in degrees~$\{0,1,2\}$,
 controlling the deformation ring over which~$\Theta_\cA$ varies.
\end{enumerate}
\end{principle}

\begin{conjecture}[The boundary-bulk reconstruction thesis;
 \ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:boundary-bulk-thesis}
\index{boundary-bulk reconstruction!thesis|textbf}%
\index{Drinfeld double!as universal boundary-bulk algebra|textbf}%
\index{holographic central charge|see{$\kappa$}}%
\index{$\kappa$!as holographic central charge}%
The five theorems A, B, C, D, H admit a single unifying
interpretation: the boundary chiral algebra~$\cA$ determines its
bulk via the chiral derived centre
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$, the modular
characteristic~$\kappa(\cA)$ is the holographic central charge, the
shadow obstruction tower $\{S_r(\cA)\}_{r \ge 2}$ is the
holographic correction hierarchy, and the Drinfeld double
$U_\cA = \cA \bowtie \cA^!$ is the universal boundary-bulk algebra
that reconstructs the gravitational theory from the VOA.
Each theorem corresponds to a single claim of the thesis.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\Alph*)}]
\item \emph{Existence of the dual}
 \textup{(}Theorem~A\textup{)}.
 The existence of the Verdier dual $\cA^!$ is implicit in
 Definition~\ref{def:chiral-koszul-pair} of a chiral Koszul
 pair, which equips the pair
 $(\cA_1, \cA_2)$ with Verdier-compatible identifications
 $\mathbb{D}_{\mathrm{Ran}}(\cC_1) \simeq \cC_2$ from the
 outset; Theorem~A part~(1), the bar-cobar adjunction, then
 supplies the unit and counit quasi-isomorphisms
 $\cC_i \simeq \barB_X(\cA_i)$ on the Koszul locus.
 Part~(2), the Verdier intertwining
 $\mathbb{D}_{\mathrm{Ran}}\barB_X(\cA_1) \simeq \cA_2$,
 then unpacks the Verdier clause of the definition by transport
 across part~(1). This supplies the algebraic pair
 $(\cA, \cA^!)$ on which the Drinfeld double $U_\cA$ is
 defined.

\item \emph{Non-degenerate reconstruction}
 \textup{(}Theorem~B\textup{)}.
 On the Koszul locus the counit
 $\Omega_X\barB_X(\cA) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$ is a
 quasi-isomorphism. The genus-$0$ clause is unconditional.
 The genus-$g \ge 1$ clause is proved on the modular pre-Koszul lane
 and is unconditional on the standard CFT-type landscape except
 integer-spin $\beta\gamma$.
 No boundary information is lost in the passage boundary
 $\mapsto$ bar coalgebra $\mapsto$ cobar $\mapsto$ boundary:
 the holographic transform is injective up to contractible
 ambiguity.

\item \emph{Lagrangian bulk splitting}
 \textup{(}Theorem~C\textup{)}.
 Theorem~C says first that the ambient genus-$g$ deformation complex

 \textup{(}Theorem~D\textup{)}.

\textup{(}UNIFORM-WEIGHT\textup{)}, with

\begin{theorem}[Central charge complementarity; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]\label{thm:central-charge-complementarity}
\index{central charge!complementarity|textbf}
\textup{[Regime: curved-central on the Koszul locus
\textup{(}Convention~\textup{\ref{conv:regime-tags})}.]}

For Koszul dual pairs related by the Feigin--Frenkel involution
$k \mapsto k' = -k - 2h^\vee$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textup{(a)}] \emph{Affine Kac--Moody.}
 $c(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k) + c(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{k'})
 = 2 \dim \mathfrak{g}$.
\item[\textup{(b)}] \emph{Principal $\mathcal{W}$-algebras:}
 $c(\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})) + c(\mathcal{W}^{k'}(\mathfrak{g}))
 = 2\operatorname{rank}(\mathfrak{g}) + 4h^\vee \dim \mathfrak{g}$.
\end{enumerate}
In particular, $\mathfrak{g} = \mathfrak{sl}_2$ in
part~\textup{(b)} gives $c + c' = 26$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Part~(a).} The Sugawara central charge is
$c(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k) = k \dim\mathfrak{g}/(k + h^\vee)$.
Since $k' + h^\vee = -(k + h^\vee)$:
\[
c(k) + c(k') = \frac{k \cdot d}{k+h^\vee}
+ \frac{(k+2h^\vee) \cdot d}{k+h^\vee}
= \frac{2(k + h^\vee) \cdot d}{k+h^\vee} = 2d
\]
where $d = \dim\mathfrak{g}$.

\emph{Part~(b).} The Drinfeld--Sokolov central charge is
$c(\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})) = r - 12|\rho|^2 (t-1)^2/t$
where $r = \operatorname{rank}(\mathfrak{g})$ and
$t = k + h^\vee$
(verified for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ in
Theorem~\ref{thm:w-algebra-koszul-main} and for $\mathfrak{sl}_3$
in Theorem~\ref{thm:w-koszul-precise}).
The involution sends $t \mapsto -t$:
\[
c(k) + c(k') = 2r + 12|\rho|^2 \cdot
\frac{(t+1)^2 - (t-1)^2}{t}
= 2r + 48|\rho|^2.
\]
The Freudenthal--de~Vries identity
$|\rho|^2 = h^\vee \dim\mathfrak{g}/12$
(under normalization $(\theta, \theta) = 2$;
see~\cite{Kac}, Ch.~13)
yields $c + c' = 2r + 4h^\vee d$.

\emph{Verification.}
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$: $2(1) + 4(2)(3) = 26$.
$\mathfrak{sl}_3$: $2(2) + 4(3)(8) = 100$.
$\mathfrak{sl}_N$:
$2(N{-}1) + 4N(N^2{-}1) = 2(N{-}1)(2N^2{+}2N{+}1)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Two critical points: $k = 0$ and $k = -h^\vee$]
\label{rem:two-critical-points}
\index{critical level!$k=0$ vs $k=-h^\vee$}%

theory), and $k = -h^\vee$ is the critical limit on the bulk (the
Sugawara stress tensor no longer exists and the Drinfeld double
presentation breaks).
The Feigin--Frenkel centre at $k = -h^\vee$ is the singular fibre
of the boundary-bulk correspondence, while the Drinfeld double
describes it on the regular locus $k \notin \{0, -h^\vee\}$.
\end{remark}

\section{The \texorpdfstring{$\Eone$}{E1} story as primitive: averaging and the modular shadow}
\label{sec:e1-as-primitive}
\label{subsec:e1-as-primitive}
\index{averaging map!av@$\operatorname{av}$|textbf}
\index{ordered bar complex!as primitive object|textbf}
\index{E1 algebra@$\Eone$-algebra!primacy|textbf}
\index{Yangian!as primitive datum}
\index{R-matrix!as primitive of $\kappa$}
\index{symmetric bar complex!as $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant shadow}

The bar complex $\barB(\cA)$ presented in \S\ref{sec:volume-one-route}
is the symmetric (commutative) bar: it lives on
$(s^{-1}\bar\cA)^{\otimes n}_{\Sigma_n}$ and forgets the order in
which collisions occur. This presentation is convenient for
formulating the five main theorems and connecting to the modular
operad on~$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$, but it is not the natural
primitive of the theory. The natural primitive is the
\emph{ordered} bar
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-bar-ord}
\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)
\;=\;
T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{n \ge 0}\,(s^{-1}\bar\cA)^{\otimes n}
\end{equation}
with deconcatenation coproduct, which by Stasheff's theorem
\textup{\cite{Sta63}} is the universal cofree conilpotent
coalgebra on~$s^{-1}\bar\cA$. Every $A_\infty$-chiral algebra
admits a canonical ordered bar; the symmetric bar arises only
after applying $\Sigma_n$-coinvariants externally on each tensor
factor. The unsymmetrized object retains strictly more
information (\S\ref{subsec:e1-as-primitive}).

\paragraph{The ordered convolution algebra and its MC element.}
The ordered convolution dg Lie algebra
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-gAmod-e1}
{\gAmod}^{\Eone}
\;:=\;
\prod_{g,n}
\Hom\!\bigl(F\!\Ass(g,n),\,\End_\cA(n)\bigr),
\end{equation}
built on the ribbon (associative) modular operad
$F\!\Ass$ rather than the commutative cooperad
$\mathrm{Sym}^c$, houses the bar-intrinsic Maurer--Cartan element
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-theta-e1}
\Theta_\cA^{\Eone}
\;:=\;
D_\cA^{\Eone} - d_\cA^{(0)}
\;\in\;
\MC\!\bigl({\gAmod}^{\Eone}\bigr).
\end{equation}
The degree-$2$ component of $\Theta_\cA^{\Eone}$ at
genus~$0$ is the \emph{classical $r$-matrix}
$r(z) \in \End(V^{\otimes 2}) \otimes \cO(*\Delta)$,
the meromorphic function whose residue at $z = 0$ controls the
spectral scattering of~$\cA$. The degree-$3$ component is the
\emph{KZ associator} $r_3(z_1, z_2)$, and the degree-$\ge 4$
components are the higher Yangian/braided coherences that satisfy
the boundary equations of Stasheff associahedra:
classical Yang--Baxter for $r(z)$, the pentagon for $r_3$, the
quartic $R$-matrix identity for $r_4$, and so on
(Proposition~\ref{prop:e1-shadow-r-matrix}).
The components of $\Theta_\cA^{\Eone}$ are the full data of the
line-operator algebra: an $R$-matrix, a braided category, a
Drinfeld--Jimbo Yangian.

\paragraph{Averaging as the sole information loss.}
The modular convolution algebra $\gAmod$ is the
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariant of ${\gAmod}^{\Eone}$ via the
\emph{averaging map}
(Definition~\ref{def:e1-modular-convolution},
equation~\eqref{eq:e1-to-einfty-projection}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-averaging}
\operatorname{av}\colon
{\gAmod}^{\Eone}
\;\twoheadrightarrow\;
\gAmod,
\qquad
\operatorname{av}(\phi)(g, n)
\;=\;
\frac{1}{n!}\sum_{\sigma \in \Sigma_n} \sigma\cdot
\bigl(\phi(g, n) \circ \iota^{\mathrm{rib}}_{g,n}\bigr).
\end{equation}
The map $\operatorname{av}$ is a surjective dg Lie morphism
(Theorem~\ref{thm:fcom-coinvariant-fass}) sending the ordered MC
element to the symmetric one
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-mc-element}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-av-theta}
\operatorname{av}\bigl(\Theta_\cA^{\Eone}\bigr)
\;=\;
\Theta_\cA.
\end{equation}
At degree~$2$, this projection records only one scalar: the
$\Sigma_2$-coinvariant of the residue of $r(z)$ at $z = 0$ is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-kappa-from-r}
\operatorname{av}\bigl(r(z)\bigr)
\;=\;
\tfrac{1}{2}\,\operatorname{tr}\!\bigl[\Res_{z = 0} r(z)\bigr],
\end{equation}
where the trace is the cyclic trace on $\End(V^{\otimes 2})$.
For abelian and scalar families,
\[
\kappa(\cA) = \operatorname{av}\bigl(r(z)\bigr).
\]
For the affine Kac--Moody vacuum algebra $V_k(\mathfrak{g})$ at
non-critical level, the averaging formula
\eqref{eq:intro-kappa-from-r} specializes to the Sugawara identity
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-sugawara-kappa}
\operatorname{av}\bigl(r_k(z)\bigr) + \tfrac{1}{2}\dim\mathfrak{g}
\;=\;
\kappa\bigl(V_k(\mathfrak{g})\bigr)
\;=\;
\frac{\dim\mathfrak{g}\,(k + h^\vee)}{2h^\vee},
\end{equation}
where $r_k(z) = k\,\Omega/z$ is the level-$k$ classical $r$-matrix
 and the shift
$\tfrac{1}{2}\dim\mathfrak{g}$ is the normal-ordering contribution
of the bulk Sugawara stress tensor.
Identity~\eqref{eq:intro-sugawara-kappa} is the boundary-level
realization of~$\kappa$ as the holographic central charge: the
$r$-matrix encodes the boundary coupling, the shift encodes the
bulk renormalization, and the sum is the one-loop coefficient that
enters Theorem~D. The
shadow obstruction tower
$(\kappa, \mathfrak{C}, \mathfrak{Q}, \ldots)$ is the
degree-by-degree image of
$(r(z), r_3, r_4, \ldots)$ under~$\operatorname{av}$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:e1-shadow-r-matrix}). The $\Eone$ side
contains the matrix-valued meromorphic function; the $\Einf$ side
contains its $\Sigma_n$-symmetrized scalar.

\begin{theorem}[$\Eone$ primacy; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:e1-primacy}
\index{primacy thesis!E1@$\Eone$|textbf}
\index{averaging map!information loss}
\index{Reynolds operator!as averaging map}
Let\/ $\cA$ be a cyclic chiral algebra on\/~$X$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(Surjectivity.)}
 The averaging map
 $\operatorname{av}\colon {\gAmod}^{\Eone}
 \twoheadrightarrow \gAmod$
 \textup{(}\eqref{eq:intro-averaging}\textup{)}
 is a surjective dg~Lie morphism.
\item \textup{(MC projection.)}
 $\operatorname{av}$ sends
 $\Theta_\cA^{\Eone} \mapsto \Theta_\cA$
 \textup{(}\eqref{eq:intro-av-theta}\textup{)}.
 In particular, the shadow obstruction tower
 $(\kappa, \mathfrak{C}, \mathfrak{Q}, \ldots)$
 is the degree-by-degree $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant image of the
 $R$-matrix tower $(r(z), r_3, r_4, \ldots)$.
\item \textup{(Bracket preservation.)}
 $\operatorname{av}$ preserves the dg~Lie bracket
 by $\Sigma_n$-equivariance of the operad composition maps.
\item \textup{(Non-splitting.)}
 The kernel\/ $\ker(\operatorname{av})$ is non-trivial:
 at degree~$3$ it contains the Drinfeld associator
 $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}(\cA)$, which is
 annihilated by\/ $\operatorname{av}$ but satisfies the
 pentagon equation in\/~${\gAmod}^{\Eone}$.
 In particular, the short exact sequence
 $0 \to \ker(\operatorname{av}) \to {\gAmod}^{\Eone}
 \xrightarrow{\operatorname{av}} \gAmod \to 0$
 does not split as dg~Lie algebras.
 At genus~$1$, a fourth obstruction to splitting arises from the
 quasi-modular anomaly of the Eisenstein series~$E_2$: the genus-$1$
 propagator transforms with a non-holomorphic correction that no
 $\Sigma_n$-equivariant section can absorb
 (Proposition~\ref{prop:e1-nonsplitting-genus1}).
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Parts (i)--(iii) are proved in
Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul}.
The map $\operatorname{av}$ is the Reynolds operator
$\phi \mapsto \frac{1}{n!}\sum_{\sigma} \sigma \cdot \phi$;
surjectivity follows because $\operatorname{av}$ restricts to
the identity on $\Sigma_n$-invariant elements, which span the
target~$\gAmod$.
The bracket is preserved because operad composition in
$F\!\Ass$ is $\Sigma_n$-equivariant: coinvariant projection
converts $T^c$-convolution to $\mathrm{Sym}^c$-convolution
(Theorem~\ref{thm:fcom-coinvariant-fass}).
The MC projection $\operatorname{av}(\Theta_\cA^{\Eone})
= \Theta_\cA$ follows from
$\operatorname{av}(D^{E_1}_\cA) = D_\cA$
(the genus-completed bar differential descends under
coinvariants) and $\operatorname{av}(\dzero) = \dzero$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-mc-element}).

For (iv), the Drinfeld associator
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}(\cA) \in \ker(\operatorname{av})_{0,3}$
satisfies the pentagon equation in ${\gAmod}^{\Eone}$
(the genus-$0$, degree-$3$ MC equation;
Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-mc-finite-degree},
equation~\eqref{eq:e1-mc-degree3}).
Its $\Sigma_3$-symmetrization vanishes because the
pentagon identity is antisymmetric under transposition
of the two internal edges.
Were $\operatorname{av}$ split, there would exist a
dg~Lie section $s\colon \gAmod \hookrightarrow
{\gAmod}^{\Eone}$ with $\operatorname{av} \circ s = \id$.
Such a section would lift the commutative MC element
$\Theta_\cA$ to an ordered element in the image of~$s$;
but the pentagon constraint on $\ker(\operatorname{av})$
forces the degree-$3$ component of any lift to involve
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$, which does not lie in the image
of any linear section.
\end{proof}

\begin{principle}[The $\Eone$ primacy thesis]
\label{princ:e1-primacy}
\index{averaging map!information loss}
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:e1-primacy}} admits a concise restatement.
The $\Eone$/ordered structure is the natural primitive of the
theory. The $\Einf$/symmetric/modular structure is its
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariant shadow under~$\operatorname{av}$.
The five main theorems A--H are the invariants that survive
averaging. Information flows in only one direction:
from the rich $\Eone$ side, which contains the $R$-matrix, the
KZ associator, the Drinfeld--Jimbo Yangian, the full braided category
of line operators, and the entire spectral scattering data,
to the coarser $\Einf$ side, which contains only the modular
characteristic~$\kappa$, the shadow obstruction tower, and the
genus expansion of the closed sector.
\end{principle}

\paragraph{Five reasons for the inversion.}
\textsc{Operadic primacy.} In the hierarchy of $E_n$-operads,
$\Eone = \mathrm{Ass}$ is the primitive associative operad and
$\Einf = \Com$ arises from it as the colimit
$\Com = \colim_n E_n$ or, equivalently, by
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariants on each degree. At the level of cofree
coalgebras, Stasheff's theorem identifies $T^c$ (deconcatenation)
as the universal cofree conilpotent coalgebra; the symmetric
cooperad $\mathrm{Sym}^c$ is recovered from $T^c$ by external
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariants. The bar functor that respects this
universality is $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}} = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$, not the
symmetric bar.

\textsc{Information content.} An equivariant
$\End(V^{\otimes n})$-valued meromorphic function strictly
contains its scalar trace. The averaging map
$\operatorname{av}$ is surjective but not injective: the kernel
records the non-symmetric components of the line-operator data.
For Heisenberg, $r(z) = k/z$ is already scalar, so the
symmetrization recovers $\kappa(\cH_k) = k$ without losing
matrix data. For affine Kac--Moody, $r(z) = k\,\Omega/z$ contains the
full Yangian double; the symmetrization recovers
$\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k) = (k + h^\vee)\dim\mathfrak g/(2h^\vee)$
and discards the entire Drinfeld--Jimbo deformation.

\textsc{Physical primacy.} The natural physical objects are
ordered. Three-dimensional holomorphic-topological theories
have line operators along the topological direction $\R_t$
(Costello--Gaiotto~\cite{CostelloGaiotto2020}); these line operators
form the ordered convolution algebra ${\gAmod}^{\Eone}$ on
$\C_z \times \R_t$, and their fusion is the
$\Eone$-multiplication. Open-string field theory and
factorization on bordered curves carry intrinsic boundary
orderings. Integrable lattice models have $R$-matrices that
satisfy the (ordered) Yang--Baxter equation, not its symmetric
projection. In every physical setting where the bar complex
appears, the line/boundary direction is ordered before any
$\Sigma_n$-symmetrization is taken.

\textsc{Categorical primacy.} The categorical objects of
representation theory are ordered. Quantum groups, Yangians, and
braided tensor categories are the natural framework for chiral
algebras coupled to a spectral parameter; their structure
constants are matrix-valued and obey ordered identities (CYBE,
pentagon, hexagon, mixed Yang--Baxter). Symmetric monoidal
categories are obtained from braided ones by the
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariant procedure on the braid groupoid; this is
exactly the categorical analogue of the averaging
map~\eqref{eq:intro-averaging}.

\textsc{Operadic primacy of Swiss-cheese.} The Swiss-cheese
operad $\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch},\mathrm{top}}$ governs the
open/closed derived center pair $(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA), \cA)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:thqg-swiss-cheese}); the bar complex itself
is a single-coloured $\Eone$-chiral coassociative coalgebra
over $(\mathrm{ChirAss})^!$. The SC structure emerges on the
derived center, not on the bar complex. The closed sector of any
Swiss-cheese algebra is recovered from the open sector by passing
to $\Sigma_n$-coinvariants along the open-to-closed boundary
operator. The closed sector is intrinsically a quotient of the
open sector, never an enrichment of it.

\paragraph{Historical context.}
The manuscript developed from the Beilinson--Drinfeld theory of
factorization algebras on the Ran space~\cite{BD04}, which is
unordered: a factorization algebra assigns sections to finite
subsets of points, with no ordering. That framing made the
symmetric bar complex the default starting point and treated the
ordered bar as a refinement. The reframing
recorded here reverses this: the ordered bar is the
universal object, and the symmetric bar is its
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariant. All five-theorem statements and proofs
are unchanged; only the reading
order of the dependency DAG shifts. Concretely:
Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul} presents the ordered theory
in full, and the present chapter and
Chapter~\ref{chap:bar-cobar} onward present the av-images of the
ordered constructions.

\begin{remark}[Scope of the $\Eone$ reframing]
\label{rem:e1-primacy-scope}
\index{primacy thesis!E1@$\Eone$!scope of reframing}
The proofs of Theorems~A--D and~H are unchanged.
The conceptual reordering is as follows: the modular convolution
algebra $\gAmod$ is now read as $\operatorname{av}({\gAmod}^{\Eone})$, the
universal MC element $\Theta_\cA$ as $\operatorname{av}(\Theta_\cA^{\Eone})$,
and the modular characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$ as
$\operatorname{av}(r(z))$ at degree~$2$.
The ordered theory of
Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul} is the upstream object;
the symmetric theory is its image.

The $\Eone$ side is strictly richer: at degree~$2$ alone it
distinguishes algebras that the symmetric side identifies.
The Heisenberg $\Eone$-shadow $r_{\cH_k}(z) = k/z$ and the
$\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$-shadow
$r_{\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2,k}(z) = k\,\Omega_{\mathfrak{sl}_2}/z$
already differ before averaging: the former is scalar, while the
latter is matrix-valued. The ordered bar separates what the symmetric
bar collapses.
\end{remark}

\begin{warning}[Five inequivalent notions of ``$\Eone$-chiral'']
\label{warn:five-e1-notions-intro}
\index{E1 chiral@$\Eone$-chiral!five inequivalent notions}%
The literature uses ``$\Eone$-chiral'' for at least five distinct
structures: (i) ordered chiral homology on $\Conf_n^<(X)$
(Definition~\ref{def:ordered-chiral-homology});
(ii) the $\Eone$-monoidal structure on the category of modules;
(iii) the braided-tensor structure from the $R$-matrix;
(iv) the factorization-algebra structure on ordered configuration
spaces; (v) the ribbon modular operad $F\!\Ass$ action on the bar
complex. These agree on the Koszul locus at generic level, but
differ at critical level, for non-Koszul algebras, and at the
chain level versus the derived level. Throughout this volume,
``$\Eone$-chiral'' refers to~(i) unless otherwise stated.
See Warning~\ref{warn:multiple-e1-chiral}
(Chapter~\ref{ch:algebraic-foundations}) for the full
disambiguation.
\end{warning}

\begin{remark}[The three-volume dimensional reduction]
\label{rem:three-volume-dimensional-reduction}
\index{dimensional reduction!three-volume hierarchy}
The three volumes of this work correspond to three levels of a
dimensional reduction:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Volume~I} (2d, the curve~$X$): the modular shadow.
 The five theorems A--D and~H are the invariants that survive the
 full reduction $4d \to 3d \to 2d$. The operadic structure is
 $E_\infty$ (symmetric, commutative factorisation on $\Ran(X)$).
\item \textbf{Volume~II} (3d, $\C_z \times \R_t$): the
 $\Eone$-chiral layer. The bar complex is a dg coalgebra over
 $(\mathrm{ChirAss})^!$; the SC structure emerges on the derived center.
 The operadic structure is $\Eone$ with spectral braiding from the
 $\C_z$-direction.
\item \textbf{Volume~III} (4d / CY): the geometric source.
 Calabi--Yau categories produce $\Eone$-chiral algebras for
 $d \geq 3$; the Drinfeld center
 $\cZ(\mathrm{Rep}^{\Eone}(\cA))$ recovers the $E_2$-braided
 structure.
\end{itemize}
At each level, information is lost: $4d \to 3d$ forgets one
topological direction ($E_2 \to \Eone$); $3d \to 2d$ forgets the
ordering ($\Sigma_n$-coinvariance, $r(z) \mapsto \kappa$). The
Drinfeld center reconstructs upward; the bar complex determines
$r(z)$ on the Koszul locus.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The $\En$ circle]
\label{rem:en-circle-intro}
\index{E-n circle@$\En$ circle}%
The dimensional reduction of
Remark~\ref{rem:three-volume-dimensional-reduction} is not a
one-way street; it closes into a circle. Five arrows connect
the $\En$-levels:
\[
\Ethree
 \xrightarrow[\text{topological reduction}]{\;\;(1)\;\;}
\Etwo
 \xrightarrow[\text{boundary restriction}]{\;\;(2)\;\;}
\Eone
 \xrightarrow[\text{Drinfeld center}]{\;\;(3)\;\;}
\Etwo
 \xrightarrow[\text{topologization}]{\;\;(4)\;\;}
\Ethree.
\]
Arrow~(1) forgets the third topological direction (Chern--Simons
$\to$ factorization on~$\bC$). Arrow~(2) restricts to the
boundary (holomorphic $\to$ chiral). Arrow~(3) takes the Drinfeld
center $\cZ(\mathrm{Rep}^{\Eone}(\cA))$, recovering the braided
tensor structure. Arrow~(4) is the topologization
(Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization} for affine Kac--Moody;
Conjecture~\ref{conj:topologization-general} in general):
the conformal vector promotes holomorphic $\Etwo$ to
topological $\Ethree$. The composite
$(4) \circ (3) \circ (2) \circ (1)$ returns to the $\Ethree$
level; the conjecture is that it is homotopic to the identity
on the Koszul locus at generic level
(Chapter~\ref{ch:en-koszul-duality},
\S\ref{sec:topologization}).
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Three-tier $R$-matrix classification]
\label{def:three-tier-r-matrix}
\index{R-matrix!three-tier classification|textbf}%
\index{R-matrix!pole-free tier (a)}%
\index{R-matrix!vertex-algebra tier (b)}%
\index{R-matrix!Eone@$\Eone$-primitive tier (c)}%
The collision residue $r(z)$ of a chiral algebra $\cA$ falls into
exactly one of three tiers, according to how its singular structure is
determined by the underlying data.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item \emph{Pole-free commutative tier.}
 $r(z) = \tau$, the Koszul-signed flip on $V \otimes V$, with no
 meromorphic dependence on~$z$. No collision forces scattering: the
 $R$-matrix is the identity up to sign, and the associated Yangian
 is the symmetric algebra on a finite-dimensional vector space.

\item \emph{Local OPE tier.}
 $r(z)$ has poles at $z = 0$, and its entire structure is determined
 by the local OPE of $\cA$ by the residue formula
 \eqref{eq:intro-kappa-from-r}: the pole orders are one less than
 the OPE pole orders, and the matrix
 coefficients are the OPE modes $a_{(n)}b$. The tier
 is closed under Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction and under free-field
 realizations.

\item \emph{Genuinely $\Eone$ tier.}
 $r(z)$ has poles at $z = 0$, but its structure is not determined by
 any local OPE of an $\Einf$-chiral algebra. The $R$-matrix is
 independent input data: there is no underlying vertex algebra whose
 OPE expands to $r(z)$ via tier (b). The natural algebraic
 framework is the quantum vertex algebra of
 Etingof--Kazhdan~\cite{EtingofKazhdan00} or the Yangian double,
 and the associated convolution algebra lives on ordered rather
 than unordered configurations.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Three-tier examples and the three-leaps hierarchy]
\label{rem:three-tier-examples}
\index{R-matrix!three-tier classification!examples}%
Tier (a) is the commutative extreme. Finite-dimensional Lie algebras
at level $0$, commutative vertex algebras, and the trivial chiral
algebra $\omega_X$ all sit here. $\kappa = 0$, the shadow
obstruction tower is empty, and the bar complex collapses to the
Koszul resolution of a polynomial ring. Pole-free commutative is the
boundary stratum; every richer tier specialises to it when all
singular modes vanish.

Tier (b) contains every standard-landscape vertex algebra:
the Heisenberg $\cH_k$ with $r(z) = k/z$, affine Kac--Moody
$\widehat{\mathfrak g}_k$ with $r(z) = k\,\Omega/z$ at level $k$,
Virasoro $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ with $r(z) = (c/2)/z^3 + 2T/z$,
the $\mathcal{W}_N$-algebras, the $\beta\gamma$ system, and the
lattice vertex algebras $V_\Lambda$. In every case the $R$-matrix is
\emph{derived} from the OPE by residue extraction; nothing new enters
at the $R$-matrix level beyond what the local OPE already carries.
Tier (b) is still $\Einf$-chiral in the three-leaps classification of
Volume~II: the algebra is commutative up to quasi-isomorphism, and
averaging recovers the scalar degree-$2$ shadow without
information loss beyond the $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant step: this is
$\kappa$ on abelian and scalar families, while for non-abelian
affine Kac--Moody it is $\kappa_{\mathrm{dp}}$ and the full
modular characteristic adds the Sugawara shift $\dim(\fg)/2$.

Tier (c) is the genuinely $\Eone$-primitive extreme. The Yangian
$Y(\mathfrak g)$ and the Etingof--Kazhdan quantum vertex algebras
sit here: their $R$-matrices are solutions to the classical and
quantum Yang--Baxter equations that do not arise as residues of any
vertex-algebra OPE. The associated convolution algebra
${\gAmod}^{\Eone}$ is strictly richer than its
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariant shadow, and the kernel of the averaging map
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-primacy}(iv)) is non-trivial at degree~$3$,
where the Drinfeld associator $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ records data that
no symmetric projection recovers. Tier (c) is where the $\Eone$
primacy thesis becomes nontrivial: the $R$-matrix is input, not
output.

The three tiers organise Part~\ref{part:seven-faces} (Seven Faces): tier (a) is the
trivial-scattering base case, tier (b) populates the seven-face
falsification checklist for every standard family, and tier (c)
activates the $\Eone$-refined checklist of
Chapter~\ref{chap:yangians}. The jump from (b) to (c) is the
single irreversible information step in the three-volume
dimensional reduction of
Remark~\ref{rem:three-volume-dimensional-reduction}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Three tiers as boundary-bulk coupling complexity]
\label{rem:three-tier-boundary-bulk}
\index{boundary-bulk reconstruction!three-tier coupling}%
\index{Drinfeld double!boundary-bulk realizations}%
Read through the boundary-bulk reconstruction thesis, the three
tiers of Definition~\ref{def:three-tier-r-matrix} correspond to
increasing complexity of the coupling between the boundary algebra
and the bulk it reconstructs.
\emph{Tier~(a)} is the free-field bulk: the boundary
is commutative, the bulk is a free chiral algebra with no
scattering, and the Drinfeld double degenerates to the tensor
product $\cA \otimes \cA^!$ with no cross-term. Bulk physics is
classical Chern--Simons on the commutative locus.
\emph{Tier~(b)} is the interacting but $\Einf$-chiral bulk: the
boundary has OPE poles, the bulk is interacting, but the time
ordering of bulk operators is trivial in the sense that all
scattering is recovered from the local OPE of the boundary. Every
standard vertex algebra (Heisenberg, affine Kac--Moody, Virasoro,
$\mathcal{W}_N$, lattice, $\beta\gamma$) sits here; the boundary
OPE determines the entire bulk.
\emph{Tier~(c)} is the genuinely gravitational bulk: the boundary
$R$-matrix is independent input data, and bulk operators have
non-trivial time ordering that no boundary OPE recovers. The
Yangian double and the Etingof--Kazhdan quantum vertex algebras
populate this tier. Here the boundary algebra alone does not
determine the bulk; the $\Eone$ structure of the ordered bar is
required, and the Drinfeld double $U_\cA$ must be constructed on
ordered configurations rather than unordered ones.
The three tiers are therefore the three levels of holographic
non-triviality: free bulk, $\Einf$-interacting bulk, and
time-ordered (gravitational) bulk.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Topologization: from $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ to $\Ethree$]
\label{rem:topologization-intro}
\index{topologization theorem!introduction}%
The $\Eone$-chiral bar construction, combined with
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$, produces an $\Etwo$-algebra
via the operadic center. When the chiral algebra admits a
conformal vector (equivalently, a Sugawara element at non-critical
level), the holomorphic $\Etwo$ structure is promoted to
topological $\Etwo$: holomorphic translations become $Q$-exact,
hence homotopically trivial. Dunn additivity then gives
$\Etwo^{\mathrm{top}} \otimes \Eone^{\mathrm{top}} =
\Ethree^{\mathrm{top}}$.
Theorem~\ref{thm:topologization}
(\S\ref{sec:topologization}) establishes this for affine
Kac--Moody $V_k(\fg)$ at non-critical level, where the
Sugawara element provides the inner conformal vector
explicitly. The conformal vector is the bridge from the
chiral ($n = 2$) row of the dimensional ladder to the
Chern--Simons ($n = 3$) row. For general chiral algebras
with conformal vector (Virasoro, $\cW$-algebras), the same
conclusion is expected but conditional on constructing the
bulk BRST complex
(Conjecture~\ref{conj:topologization-general}). At critical
level $k = -h^\vee$ the Sugawara element is undefined and
the promotion fails; the $\Etwo$ structure remains
holomorphic.
\end{remark}

At tier~(b), the passage from chiral algebra to quantum group
is an equivalence: for affine Kac--Moody $V_k(\fg)$ at generic
level, the ordered chiral homology $\int_X^{\Eone} V_k(\fg)$
recovers the quantum group $U_q(\fg)$ as a braided tensor
category (Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-qg-equiv};
Chapter~\ref{ch:ordered-associative-chiral-kd},
\S\ref{sec:ordered-chiral-homology}).
This is the content of the Kazhdan--Lusztig equivalence read
through the bar complex.

\section{Architecture}
\label{sec:architecture}

The five theorems and the $\Eone$ primacy thesis rest on five
geometric ingredients, which combine into a single functorial
correspondence between geometry, algebra, and physics.

\begin{remark}[Five geometric ingredients]\label{rem:five-pieces}
\index{modular Koszul duality!five irreducible pieces}
\index{geometric synthesis|textbf}
Modular Koszul duality rests on five geometric ingredients.
The first four belong to the commutative ($\Einf$) face; the fifth
to the associative ($\Eone$) face.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Three-point collision.}
 The Arnold relation
 ($\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23}+\eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{31}+\eta_{31}\wedge\eta_{12}=0$)
 is factorization
 coherence: fusing three insertions does not depend on the
 order of pairwise collisions. The genus-$0$ seed.
\item \emph{Verdier duality on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$.}
 $\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}} \bar{B}_X(\cA) \simeq
 \cA^!_\infty$
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}): the
 Koszul dual algebra as theorem, not definition-by-analogy.
\item \emph{Genus-$1$ curvature.}
 $\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_1 \cdot
 \operatorname{id}$
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality}). The natural home is
 Positselski's coderived category: quantum corrections are a
 change of ambient category, not extra terms.
\item \emph{Clutching of stable curves.}
 The genus tower is compatible with boundary clutching via the
 modular operad (Theorem~\ref{thm:prism-higher-genus}).
 The combinatorics is stable graphs, not trees, and
 $\Theta_\cA$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:universal-MC},
 proved intrinsically by Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic})
 is compatible with clutching, trace, and Verdier duality.
\item \emph{Associative structure.}
 \index{ordered factorization|see{$\Eone$-chiral algebra}}
 The Yangian $Y(\mathfrak{g})$ is $\Eone$-chiral
 (Definition~\ref{def:e1-chiral}): its bar complex uses ordered
 configuration spaces, and Verdier duality acts by $R$-matrix
 inversion $R \mapsto R^{-1}$
 (Theorem~\ref{thm:yangian-koszul-dual}).
 The sign flip $\hbar \mapsto -\hbar$ replaces the Feigin--Frenkel
 involution.
\end{enumerate}

\smallskip\noindent
In the Heisenberg algebra, only pieces~(i)--(iv) are active, and they
already force the full commutative/modular package: Arnold ensures
$d^2 = 0$; Verdier produces
$\mathcal{H}_k^! = \mathrm{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*)$; the genus-$1$
period integral creates curvature $\kappa = k$; clutching assembles
the genus tower.

\smallskip\noindent
In the Yangian, piece~(v) is required. Ordered
configurations, $R$-matrix inversion on the module category, and the
derived Drinfeld--Kohno bridge
(Theorem~\ref{thm:factorization-dk-eval}) are a genuinely braided determination of
the same configuration-space mechanism.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The DK square]\label{rem:dk-inevitability}
\index{Drinfeld--Kohno!derived!as witness of the two determinations}

witnessed by the derived

\mathcal{O}_k^{\mathrm{int}}(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}})
 \ar[r, "\Phi", "\sim"']
 \ar[d, "\Phi_{\mathrm{KL}}"', "\sim"]
& \mathcal{O}_{k'}(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}})
 \ar[d, "\Phi_{\mathrm{KL}}", "\sim"']
\\
\mathcal{C}(U_q(\mathfrak{g}))
 \ar[r, "q \mapsto q^{-1}"', "\sim"]
& \mathcal{C}(U_{q^{-1}}(\mathfrak{g}))
\end{tikzcd}
\end{equation}

duality geometrically implements the quantum group involution.

($\kappa + \kappa' = 0$ for KM/free fields;

\subsection{Koszulness and holographic reconstruction}

The bar-cobar adjunction $B \dashv \Omega$ encodes boundary data into a bar coalgebra and recovers it via the cobar construction. On the Koszul locus, Theorem~B guarantees exact recovery. Chapter~\ref{chap:holographic-codes-koszul} proves the equivalence: \emph{chiral Koszulness is equivalent to exact holographic reconstruction}.

The twelve characterisations K1--K12 of Koszulness (Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta}) comprise ten unconditional equivalences, one conditional (the Lagrangian criterion, conditional on perfectness; unconditional for the standard landscape), and one one-directional ($\mathcal{D}$-module purity, with only the forward direction proved). The bifunctor decomposition theorem is a further proved consequence outside the twelve numbered items.

Condition~(vii) of Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-equivalences-meta} (factorization homology concentrated in degree~$0$) is scoped to genus~$0$ unconditionally and to $g \geq 1$ on the uniform-weight lane; the class-M Virasoro counterexample is documented in Remark~\ref{rem:fh-vii-uniform-weight-scope}. Chiral Koszulness is closed under tensor products, dualization, and base change (Proposition~\ref{prop:koszul-closure-properties}); quotients such as Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction fall outside this closure (Remark~\ref{rem:koszul-closure-not-quotients}).

The Lagrangian isotropy of Theorem~C provides a symplectic code structure (Verdier-isotropic summands with non-degenerate cross-pairing), not an orthogonal one. Shadow depth classifies redundancy channels: class~G has no channels, class~L has one, class~M has infinitely many (convergent).

\subsection{Entanglement entropy from the shadow obstruction tower}

At the scalar level, the modular characteristic~$\kappa(\cA)$ (equal to $c(\cA)/2$ for the Virasoro algebra; the ratio $\kappa/c$ is family-dependent in general) determines the Calabrese--Cardy entropy of a single interval:
$S_{\mathrm{EE}} = \tfrac{c}{3}\log(L/\varepsilon)$, recovered as the genus-$1$ shadow of~$\Theta_\cA$ on the replica orbifold (Theorem~\ref{thm:ent-scalar-entropy}; Chapter~\ref{chap:entanglement-modular-koszul}).

Complementarity sharpens this for Virasoro: the sum $S_{\mathrm{EE}}(\mathrm{Vir}_c) + S_{\mathrm{EE}}(\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}) = \tfrac{26}{3}\log(L/\varepsilon)$ is a shadow of Theorem~C via the central-charge complementarity $c + c' = 26$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:central-charge-complementarity}), saturated at the self-dual point $c = 13$ where $\kappa + \kappa' = 13$.

Beyond the scalar level, the four shadow-depth classes G/L/C/M produce distinct entanglement complexity: class~G (Heisenberg) has Gaussian entanglement with no subleading corrections; class~L (Kac--Moody) acquires a single logarithmic correction from the cubic shadow; class~C ($\beta\gamma$) adds a quartic contact channel; class~M (Virasoro, $\mathcal{W}_N$) produces an infinite tower of R\'enyi corrections controlled by the shadow growth rate~$\rho(\cA)$. The full Knill--Laflamme condition for the holographic code requires the physical inner product and is conjectured at higher genus; at genus~$1$ it is automatic.

\textup{(}non-critical\textup{)}, $\mathrm{Vir}_c$ \textup{(}all~$c$\textup{)},

and the completed duality follows by conformal-weight induction.
$\mathcal{W}_N$ rigidity
\textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:winfty-all-stages-rigidity-closure}\textup{)}
resolves the finite-stage input.

\subsection{Critical pitfalls}
\label{subsec:critical-pitfalls}
\index{critical pitfalls|textbf}

The following distinctions are the most common sources of error:

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Five objects.}
$\cA$ is the algebra. $\barB(\cA)$ is the bar coalgebra. $\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !\textasciigrave}} = H^*(\barB(\cA))$ is the Koszul dual coalgebra. $\cA^! = (\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !\textasciigrave}})^\vee$ is the strict Koszul dual algebra. $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA) = C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$ is the derived center. The cobar $\Omega(\barB(\cA))$ recovers~$\cA$ (bar-cobar \emph{inversion}, not duality); $\cA^!$ is obtained by linear duality on bar cohomology, while $\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}\barB(\cA)$ gives the homotopy Koszul-dual factorization algebra.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Grading.}
Cohomological ($|d| = +1$) throughout. The bar complex uses desuspension: $s^{-1}$ lowers degree by~$1$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Classical vs.\ chiral Koszul duality.}
$\mathrm{Com}^! = \mathrm{Lie}$ (not $\mathrm{coLie}$):
the Koszul dual \emph{operad} of $\mathrm{Com}$ involves
an operadic suspension $\mathfrak{S}$ that shifts degrees
and twists by the sign representation
($\mathfrak{S}\mathcal{P}(n)
= \mathrm{sgn}_n \otimes \mathcal{P}(n)[n{-}1]$);
omitting the suspension yields $\mathrm{coLie}$ instead
of $\mathrm{Lie}$, which changes the sign of the
bracket in all convolution computations
(Appendix~\ref{app:signs}, Pitfall~9).
The Heisenberg algebra is \emph{not} self-dual.
Chiral Koszulness is not the same as classical
Koszulness.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Curved $A_\infty$.}
$m_1^2(a) = [m_0, a]$ (commutator, with a minus sign). The bar differential satisfies $d^2 = 0$ always; curvature shows as $m_1^2 \neq 0$ in the $A_\infty$ algebra, not in the bar complex.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Sugawara.}
The Sugawara construction is \emph{undefined} at critical level $k = -h^\vee$; the Sugawara vector does not lie in the appropriate completion. This is not ``$c$ diverges''; the construction does not exist.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Virasoro self-duality.}
The Virasoro algebra is chiral Koszul self-dual at $c = 13$ ($\mathrm{Vir}_c^! = \mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$), not $c = 26$. Quadratic self-duality (uncurved bar, $\kappa = 0$) holds at $c = 0$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{FM compactification.}
The Fulton--MacPherson compactification is the blowup along diagonals, not the complement of diagonals.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Prime form.}
The prime form $E(z_1, z_2)$ is a section of $K^{-1/2} \boxtimes K^{-1/2}$, not $K^{+1/2}$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{QME.}
The quantum master equation is $\hbar \Delta S + \frac{1}{2}\{S, S\} = 0$ (factor $\frac{1}{2}$, not~$1$).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{$d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2 \neq 0$.}
The bracket component of the bar differential does not square to zero on its own: this is proved for all $2048$ sign conventions for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$. The full Borcherds differential gives $d^2 = 0$.
