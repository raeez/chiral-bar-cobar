\chapter{Introduction}

\section{From a point to a curve}
\label{sec:volume-one-route}

Classical Koszul duality is a phenomenon of graded algebras over a point: a quadratic algebra~$A$ determines a dual coalgebra~$A^!$, and the bar construction mediates between them.  On an algebraic curve~$X$, the generators of the algebra become sections of a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module, the quadratic relations become operator product expansions with meromorphic singularities, and the bar differential becomes an integral transform whose kernel is the logarithmic propagator $d\log(z_i - z_j)$ on Fulton--MacPherson compactifications of configuration spaces.  At genus~$0$ the classical theory embeds faithfully: Koszul duality over a point is Koszul duality over~$\mathbb{P}^1$.  At genus~$g \geq 1$, the topology of the curve forces genuinely new phenomena (central extensions, conformal anomalies, curved $A_\infty$ structures) that have no analogue in the classical setting and whose geometric origin is the curvature of the Hodge bundle over~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$.

The classical theory goes as follows.  Let $V$ be a finite-dimensional graded vector space and $R \subset V^{\otimes 2}$ a subspace of quadratic relations.  The quadratic algebra $A = T(V)/(R)$ is the free tensor algebra modulo~$R$; its Koszul dual coalgebra is $A^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}} = T^c(s V^*)$, the cofree conilpotent coalgebra on the suspended linear dual, with coproduct truncated by the orthogonal complement $R^\perp \subset (V^*)^{\otimes 2}$.  The bar construction $B(A) = (T^c(sV), d_B)$ carries a differential $d_B$ induced by the multiplication of~$A$: on cogenerators $d_B(s a) = \sum s a' \otimes s a''$ records the splitting of~$a$ into pairs of generators.  The cobar construction $\Omega(C)$ reverses the process: starting from a conilpotent coalgebra~$C$, one builds the free algebra $T(s^{-1}\bar{C})$ with differential induced by the comultiplication.  The fundamental comparison map
\[
\varepsilon \colon \Omega(B(A)) \longrightarrow A
\]
is always a morphism of dg algebras; when it is a quasi-isomorphism, the algebra~$A$ is called \emph{Koszul}, and $B(A)$ has cohomology concentrated in bar degree~$1$, equal to the Koszul dual coalgebra~$A^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}}$.  The Koszul dual algebra is $A^! = (A^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}})^\vee = T(V^*)/R^\perp$.  Koszulness means that $A$ admits a linear resolution by $A^!$-modules, and that the derived category of $A$-modules is equivalent to the derived category of $A^!$-modules (up to a shift and twist).  This is the content of Priddy~\cite{Priddy70}, Beilinson--Ginzburg--Soergel~\cite{BGS96}, and Loday--Vallette~\cite{LV12}.

Let $X$ be a smooth projective curve over~$\mathbb{C}$.  A \emph{chiral algebra}~$\cA$ on~$X$ (Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite{BD04}) is a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module equipped with a chiral bracket
\[
\mu^{\mathrm{ch}} \colon j_* j^*(\cA \boxtimes \cA) \longrightarrow \Delta_!(\cA),
\]
where $j \colon X^2 \setminus \Delta \hookrightarrow X^2$ is the complement of the diagonal and $\Delta_! \colon D(\mathcal{D}_X) \to D(\mathcal{D}_{X^2})$ is the pushforward.  The chiral bracket encodes the operator product expansion: locally, if $a, b \in \cA$ are sections and $z, w$ are local coordinates, then
\[
a(z) b(w) \;\sim\; \sum_{n \geq 0} \frac{(a_{(n)} b)(w)}{(z - w)^{n+1}},
\]
where the modes $a_{(n)}b$ are the Laurent coefficients of the OPE, and the bracket $\mu^{\mathrm{ch}}$ records the entire singular part simultaneously.  The Borcherds identity, the defining axiom of a vertex algebra, is the chiral-algebraic incarnation of the Jacobi identity, coupling all pole orders in a single statement.

Two extremes organize the theory
(see \S\ref{sec:dictionary} for the formal definitions).%
\label{rem:two-strata}
The \textbf{Heisenberg atom} is $\Einf$-chiral: its OPE has only double poles, so collisions are commutative and unordered.
The \textbf{Yangian atom} is $\Eone$-chiral: simple poles give collisions a canonical ordering.
Every other family (Kac--Moody, Virasoro, $\mathcal{W}$-algebras, lattice vertex algebras) lies between these two poles, and the bar complex on higher-genus curves classifies them.
At genus~$g \geq 1$, the bar differential inherits a
curvature term $\kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ as the
coalgebraic shadow of the cyclic trace composed with
clutching: the Hodge class
$\omega_g = c_1(\mathbb{E})$ on
$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ enters through the annular
trace on the open sector, and the \emph{modular
characteristic}~$\kappa(\cA)$ is the universal scalar
measuring this obstruction
(Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}).
The bar construction on curves defines a
pronilpotent dg Lie algebra~$\gAmod$ (the modular
convolution algebra), whose Maurer--Cartan
element~$\Theta_\cA$ encodes the full genus expansion.
The finite-order projections $\Theta_\cA^{\leq r}$
(the shadow obstruction tower) extract successively
finer invariants: $\kappa$ at arity~$2$, a cubic
shadow~$C$ at arity~$3$, a quartic resonance class~$Q$
at arity~$4$.  The shadow depth $r_{\max}(\cA)$ is the
largest arity at which the shadow is nonzero; it equals
the $L_\infty$ formality level of the genus-$0$ part of $\gAmod$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-formality-identification}).
The Heisenberg atom has $r_{\max} = 2$ (formal,
$\Delta = 0$); affine Kac--Moody algebras have
$r_{\max} = 3$; the $\beta\gamma$ system has
$r_{\max} = 4$; Virasoro and $\mathcal{W}$-algebras
have $r_{\max} = \infty$.  These four cases exhaust the
possibilities: the \emph{critical discriminant}
$\Delta = 8\kappa S_4$, where $S_4$ is the quartic
shadow coefficient, forces $r_{\max}$ to lie in
$\{2, 3, \infty\}$ on any one-dimensional primary slice
(Theorem~\ref{thm:single-line-dichotomy}), with the
fourth class $r_{\max} = 4$ arising from a stratum
separation phenomenon.

Three structure theorems govern this tower.
\emph{Algebraicity}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:riccati-algebraicity}):
on each primary line~$L$, the shadow generating function satisfies
$H(t)^2 = t^4 Q_L(t)$
with $Q_L$ a computable quadratic polynomial in three invariants $(\kappa, \alpha, S_4)$, so the
tower is the Taylor expansion of~$\sqrt{Q_L}$, algebraic of degree~$2$.
The discriminant
$\Delta$ governs the fundamental dichotomy:
$\Delta = 0$ terminates the tower; $\Delta \neq 0$ makes it infinite.
\emph{Formality identification}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-formality-identification}):
the genus-$0$ shadow obstruction tower coincides with the $L_\infty$
formality obstruction tower of the convolution algebra
at all arities; genus $g \geq 1$ corrections form a separate layer.
The genus and arity directions are orthogonal
(Remark~\ref{rem:two-orthogonal-axes}).
The \emph{visibility formula}
(Corollary~\ref{cor:shadow-visibility-genus})
controls which shadow coefficients contribute at each genus:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-visibility}
g_{\min}(S_r) \;=\; \lfloor r/2 \rfloor + 1
\qquad (r \geq 3).
\end{equation}
Genus~$1$ sees only~$\kappa$ (arity~$2$).
Genus~$2$ first sees~$S_3$ (arity~$3$).
Genus~$3$ first sees~$S_4$ and~$S_5$ (arities~$4$,~$5$).
In general, two new shadow coefficients enter at each genus.
\emph{Complementarity} (Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}):
$Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!) \simeq H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, Z(\cA))$,
a Lagrangian decomposition that constrains the Koszul
dual pair.
Classical Koszul duality over a point is the genus~$0$,
arity~$2$, $\Delta = 0$ stratum of this picture: the
formal, Gaussian case where the tower terminates
immediately.  Everything beyond this stratum is new.

\section{The logarithmic seed}
\label{sec:the-seed}
\index{propagator|textbf}
\index{Koszul duality!classical}

The preface constructs the bar complex from the logarithmic $1$-form $\eta_{ij}$; this section gives the formal treatment.

Consider two distinct points $z_1, z_2$ on a smooth algebraic
curve~$X$, and the logarithmic 1-form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:the-seed}
\eta_{12} \;=\; d\log(z_1 - z_2)
\;=\; \frac{dz_1 - dz_2}{z_1 - z_2}
\end{equation}
on the configuration space $C_2(X) = \{(z_1,z_2) \in X^2 : z_1 \neq z_2\}$.
It has a simple pole along the diagonal with residue~$1$,
and it is conformally invariant.
For three distinct points, the Arnold relation
\begin{equation}\label{eq:arnold-seed}
\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23}
+ \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31}
+ \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12} = 0
\end{equation}
forces the vanishing of triple residues along pairwise collision
divisors of~$\overline{C}_3(X)$
(see \S\ref{subsec:intro-config-comb} for the precise definitions
of $\overline{C}_n(X)$ and the Arnold relation).

\subsection{From OPE to bar differential}
\label{subsec:ope-to-bar}
\index{bar complex!construction from OPE}
\index{operator product expansion!as bar differential input}

Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on~$X$.  Place $n$ elements
$a_1, \ldots, a_n$ at positions $z_1, \ldots, z_n$ and form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bar-element-intro}
a_1(z_1) \otimes \cdots \otimes a_n(z_n)
\otimes \bigwedge_{(i,j) \in E(T)} \eta_{ij}
\;\in\;
\cA^{\boxtimes n} \otimes
\Omega^{n-1}_{\log}(\overline{C}_n(X)),
\end{equation}
where $T$ is a spanning tree of the complete graph $K_n$ on $\{1,\ldots,n\}$
(selecting which pairs of log forms to wedge), and
$\Omega^{n-1}_{\log}(\overline{C}_n(X))$ denotes
$(n{-}1)$-forms on the FM compactification with at worst
simple poles along the boundary divisors
$D_{\{i,j\}}$.  A bar element of arity~$n$ is a sum of
such terms over all spanning trees, modulo the relations
from the Arnold identity.
Define a differential~$d$
by extracting residues along collision divisors:
$d(a_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes a_n) = \sum_{i < j}
\operatorname{Res}_{D_{ij}}[\eta_{ij} \cdot
\text{OPE}(a_i, a_j)] \otimes (\text{remaining factors})$.
The Arnold relation~\eqref{eq:arnold-seed} makes the triple
residue vanish, so $\dzero^2 = 0$ at genus~$0$, where $\dzero$ denotes the genus-$0$ bar differential.  The resulting cochain complex is
the \emph{bar complex}~$\barB_X(\cA)$.

As a graded object, the bar complex is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bar-graded-object-intro}
\barB_X(\cA) \;=\; \bigoplus_{n \geq 1} \bigl(s^{-1}\bar{\cA}\bigr)^{\otimes n}_{\Sigma_n} \otimes \Gamma(\overline{C}_n(X), \Omega^{n-1}_{\log}),
\end{equation}
where $s^{-1}$ denotes desuspension
($|s^{-1}a| = |a| - 1$; cohomological convention $|d| = +1$),
and $\bar{\cA} = \ker(\varepsilon \colon \cA \to \omega_X)$
is the \emph{augmentation ideal}, the kernel of the
vacuum map (\S\ref{subsec:intro-twisting}).
The subscript $\Sigma_n$ indicates coinvariants under the
symmetric group action
$\sigma \cdot (a_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes a_n
\otimes \omega)
= a_{\sigma^{-1}(1)} \otimes \cdots \otimes
a_{\sigma^{-1}(n)} \otimes \sigma^*\omega$
that permutes both the algebra factors and the marked
points simultaneously.
The bar degree (also called tensor degree or arity)
is~$n$; the internal degree comes from the conformal
grading of~$\cA$.  The total differential decomposes as
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bar-diff-decomposition-intro}
d_{\barB} \;=\; d_{\mathrm{internal}} + d_{\mathrm{residue}} + d_{\mathrm{dR}},
\end{equation}
where $d_{\mathrm{internal}}$ acts on each tensor factor
by the internal differential of~$\cA$;
$d_{\mathrm{residue}}$ extracts OPE residues at collision
divisors weighted by~$\eta_{ij}$
(the component encoding the bracket);
and $d_{\mathrm{dR}}$ is the de~Rham differential on
configuration-space forms.
Since the generators $\eta_{ij}$ are closed,
$d_{\mathrm{dR}} = 0$ on the logarithmic subcomplex.

\subsection{Arnold's identity and $d^2 = 0$}
\label{subsec:arnold-d-squared}
\index{Arnold relation!and nilpotence}

The content of $d_{\barB}^2 = 0$ is entirely concentrated in the residue component.  Expand $(d_{\mathrm{internal}} + d_{\mathrm{residue}} + d_{\mathrm{dR}})^2$: the squares $d_{\mathrm{internal}}^2$ and $d_{\mathrm{dR}}^2$ vanish because each is individually a differential, and the mixed anticommutators vanish by the residue exact sequence on~$\overline{C}_n(X)$.  The entire content is $d_{\mathrm{residue}}^2 = 0$.

The square $d_{\mathrm{residue}}^2 = \sum_{(i,j)} \sum_{(k,\ell)} \operatorname{Res}_{D_{k\ell}} \circ \operatorname{Res}_{D_{ij}}$ splits by index overlap into three cases:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Disjoint indices} ($\{i,j\} \cap \{k,\ell\} = \emptyset$): residue operations commute up to the Koszul sign and cancel pairwise.

\item \emph{One shared index} (say $j = k$): three boundary divisors meet at the codimension-$2$ stratum $D_{ij\ell}$ of triple collision, producing a cyclic sum
\begin{equation}\label{eq:triple-residue-cyclic}
\sum_{\mathrm{cyclic}} \operatorname{Res}_{D_{j\ell}} \circ \operatorname{Res}_{D_{ij}}
\bigl[\mu(\mu(a_i, a_j), a_\ell) \otimes \eta_{ij} \wedge \eta_{j\ell}\bigr]
\end{equation}
that vanishes if and only if the Arnold relation~\eqref{eq:arnold-seed} kills the form factor and the Borcherds identity kills the algebraic factor.

\item \emph{Same pair} ($\{i,j\} = \{k,\ell\}$): $\operatorname{Res}_{D_{ij}}^2 = 0$ automatically.
\end{enumerate}

Cases~(i) and~(iii) impose no condition.  Case~(ii) imposes exactly the Borcherds identity: $d_{\barB}^2 = 0$ if and only if the OPE defines a chiral algebra (\S\ref{sec:selection-principle}).

\subsection{The bar complex as categorical logarithm}
\label{subsec:bar-as-logarithm}
\index{categorical logarithm|textbf}

The adjunction $\barB_X \dashv \Omega_X$ sends the multiplicative
structure of the OPE to the conilpotent structure of the bar
differential.
The \emph{Koszul dual} of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ is constructed
in three steps (see \S\ref{subsec:intro-twisting} for the
underlying algebraic structures):
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \emph{Bar}: form the bar coalgebra
  $\barB(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$, a conilpotent
  dg coalgebra.
\item \emph{Cohomology}: take bar cohomology
  $\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}}
  := H^*(\barB(\cA))$,
  the \emph{Koszul dual coalgebra}.
\item \emph{Dualize}: take the graded linear dual
  $\cA^! := (\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}})^\vee$,
  the \emph{Koszul dual algebra}.
\end{enumerate}
When~$\cA$ is Koszul, $\barB(\cA)$ has cohomology concentrated
in bar degree~$1$, and the classical Koszul formula gives
$\cA^! = T(V^*)/R^\perp$.

\emph{Verdier duality} $\mathbb{D}_{\mathrm{Ran}}$
on the Ran space~\eqref{eq:intro-ran}
interchanges~$\cA$ and~$\cA^!$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-adjunction}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:NAP-intro}
\int_X \cA \;\simeq\;
\mathbb{D}\!\left(\int_{-X} \cA^!\right),
\end{equation}
where $\int_X \cA$ denotes factorization homology
(\S\ref{subsec:intro-homotopy-derived}) and
$\int_{-X}$ denotes factorization cohomology on the
orientation-reversed curve.

The counit $\varepsilon\colon \Omega_X(\barB_X(\cA)) \to \cA$
is a quasi-isomorphism on the Koszul locus, where the bar
spectral sequence collapses at~$E_2$.
Off the Koszul locus, ordinary cohomological visibility of the bar
object can collapse while its intrinsic coderived content survives
(\S\ref{subsec:intro-homotopy-derived};
Positselski~\cite{Positselski11}).


\subsection{The genus-$g$ propagator and curvature}
\label{subsec:genus-g-propagator}
\index{nilpotence-periodicity correspondence}
\index{prime form|textbf}

At genus~$0$, the logarithm~$\eta_{12}$ is single-valued
on~$C_2(\mathbb{P}^1)$, and $\dzero^2 = 0$.  At
genus~$g \geq 1$, the function $z_1 - z_2$ has no global meaning.
The replacement
(Definition~\ref{def:higher-genus-log-forms}) is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:genus-g-propagator-intro-ch0}
\eta_{12}^{(g)} \;=\; d\log E(z_1, z_2)
\;+\; \sum_{\alpha=1}^{g}
  (\operatorname{Im}\Omega)^{-1}_{\alpha\beta}\,
  \omega_\alpha(z_1)\,\overline{\omega_\beta(z_2)},
\end{equation}
where $E(z_1,z_2)$ is the prime form (a section of
$K^{-1/2} \boxtimes K^{-1/2}$),
$\{\omega_\alpha\}$ are the normalized abelian
differentials, and $\Omega$ is the period matrix.
The second term couples the collision geometry to the
complex structure of the curve via the Hodge
bundle~$\mathbb{E} \to \overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$.

The prime form $E(z_1, z_2)$ is the unique section of $K^{-1/2} \boxtimes K^{-1/2}$ (not $K^{+1/2}$, a common error in the literature) that vanishes to first order along the diagonal $z_1 = z_2$ with unit residue and has prescribed monodromy along the $B$-cycles determined by a choice of homology basis.  Its logarithmic derivative $d\log E$ is the genus-$g$ propagator, replacing $d\log(z_1 - z_2)$.

The Arnold relation acquires a defect:

\begin{equation}\label{eq:curvature-intro}
\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g,
\end{equation}
where $\mathbb{E} = \pi_*\omega_\pi$ is the Hodge bundle
on~$\overline{\cM}_g$,
$\omega_g = c_1(\det\mathbb{E})$,
and $\kappa(\cA)$ is a scalar determined by the leading
OPE singularity
(\S\ref{subsec:intro-config-comb} for $\overline{\cM}_g$).

The logarithm $d\log E(z_1,z_2)$ acquires monodromy around the
$B$-cycles of~$\Sigma_g$; the scalar $\kappa(\cA)$ is the infinitesimal
generator of this monodromy.  Period integrals along $A$-cycles
restore nilpotence: $\Dg{g}^{\,2} = 0$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-diff-squares-zero}).
This is the chiral Riemann--Hilbert correspondence: the nilpotent
datum~$\kappa$ determines the periodic datum via exponentiation.

As $[\Sigma_g]$ varies over~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$, the bar
complex traces a family of curved cochain complexes.
For uniform-weight algebras (single-generator, or
multi-generator with all generators of the same conformal
weight), the
obstruction class $\mathrm{obs}_g = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g \in
H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g)$, where $\lambda_g = c_g(\mathbb{E})$
is the top Chern class of the Hodge bundle, is a characteristic
class of this family at all genera
\textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality}\textup{)}.
For multi-weight families such as~$\mathcal{W}_N$,
the weight-$1$ propagator controls the edge contribution,
but the higher-genus factorization remains open beyond
genus~$1$
\textup{(}Remark~\textup{\ref{rem:propagator-weight-universality}}\textup{)}.
The Koszul dual~$\cA^!$ traces a complementary family: their
obstruction classes pair to give the full cohomology of moduli with
coefficients in the center~$Z(\cA)$.

\section{The selection principle}
\label{sec:selection-principle}
\index{selection principle|textbf}
\index{Borcherds identity!as selection criterion}

The bar construction of \S\ref{sec:the-seed} is a
\emph{geometric} machine.  Take a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module~$V$
equipped with a meromorphic pairing
$\mu \colon j_* j^*(V \boxtimes V) \to \Delta_!(V)$
(the OPE), place sections at points of the Fulton--MacPherson
compactification~$\overline{C}_n(X)$, and extract residues along
boundary divisors.  The output is a graded object
$\barB_X(V, \mu)$ with a candidate differential
$d = d_{\mathrm{internal}} + d_{\mathrm{residue}}
+ d_{\mathrm{dR}}$.

The question that governs the theory is:
\begin{center}
\emph{For which algebraic inputs $(V, \mu)$ does
the FM bar construction produce $d^2 = 0$?}
\end{center}

\noindent
Expand $(d_1 + d_2 + d_3)^2$.  The terms
$d_1^2$, $d_3^2$, and all mixed anticommutators vanish
for formal reasons (cochain complex, de~Rham,
residue exact sequence).  The entire content is
$d_2^2 = 0$: the residue extraction must be nilpotent
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-nilpotency-complete}).
Three cases arise from index overlap in
$d_2^2 = \sum_{i<j}\sum_{k<\ell}
\Res_{D_{k\ell}} \circ \Res_{D_{ij}}$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Disjoint indices}
  ($\{i,j\} \cap \{k,\ell\} = \emptyset$).
  Residue operations commute; cancellation is by the
  Koszul sign rule.  No condition on~$\mu$.

\item \emph{One shared index} (say $j = k$).
  Three boundary strata meet at the codimension-$2$ locus
  $D_{ij\ell}$ of triple collision.  The cyclic sum of
  iterated residues
  \[
  \sum_{\textup{cyclic}}
    \Res_{D_{j\ell}} \circ \Res_{D_{ij}}
    \bigl[\mu(\mu(\phi_i, \phi_j), \phi_\ell)
    \otimes \omega\bigr]
  = 0
  \]
  holds if and only if $\mu$ satisfies the
  Borcherds identity, the associativity of the chiral
  product, coupling all OPE pole orders simultaneously.

\item \emph{Same pair}
  ($\{i,j\} = \{k,\ell\}$).
  $\Res_{D_{ij}}^2 = 0$ automatically.
\end{enumerate}
Cases~(i) and~(iii) impose no condition on the algebra.
Case~(ii) imposes exactly one: the Borcherds identity
(equation~\eqref{eq:borcherds-axiom}).
This identity is axiom~(ii) of a chiral algebra
(Definition~\ref{def:chiral-algebra}): the FM bar
construction is well-defined if and only if the OPE
satisfies it.  This is the \emph{selection principle}:
the geometry of configuration space compactifications
determines the algebraic axioms.

Two witnesses from Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}:

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Heisenberg} ($\alpha(z)\alpha(w) \sim
k/(z-w)^2$, double pole only).  No simple pole, so
$d_{\mathrm{bracket}} = 0$.  The full differential reduces to
$d_{\mathrm{curvature}}$, which produces scalars:
$d_{\mathrm{curvature}}^2 = 0$ automatically.  The Arnold
relation handles all of case~(ii).  No algebraic input needed.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Kac--Moody} ($h(z)\,e(w) \sim
2e(w)/(z-w) + \cdots$, simple \emph{and} double poles).
The bracket component $d_{\mathrm{bracket}}$ is now nonzero, and
$d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2 \neq 0$, computed explicitly for all
$2048$ sign conventions
(\S\ref{subsec:frame-sl2-bar-low-degree},
Remark~\ref{rem:frame-why-dbracket-fails}).  The obstruction is
proportional to the level~$k$: it is the double-pole data that
$d_{\mathrm{bracket}}$ ignores.  The Borcherds identity absorbs
this obstruction:
\[
d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2
= -\{d_{\mathrm{bracket}},\, d_{\mathrm{curvature}}\}.
\]
The curvature is the obstruction; the Borcherds identity is the
integrability condition
(Proposition~\ref{prop:pole-decomposition}).

\begin{remark}[Classical vs.\ chiral selection]
\label{rem:classical-vs-chiral-selection}
\index{Koszul duality!classical vs.\ chiral}
The bar construction asks the same question at every level
of geometry:
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\textbf{Base} & \textbf{Condition for $d^2 = 0$}
  & \textbf{Selected objects} \\
\hline
Point & Jacobi identity & Lie algebras \\
Curve $X$ & Borcherds identity & Chiral algebras \\
$X$ over $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ & $+$ period corrections
  & Modular Koszul algebras
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Each row is forced by the geometry of the next: the Jacobi
identity handles triple collisions when all poles are simple;
on a curve, multiple pole orders mix at the codimension-$2$
strata of $\overline{C}_n(X)$, and the Borcherds identity is
the unique condition that makes this mixing consistent; at
higher genus, period corrections from the Hodge bundle restore
$\Dg{g}^{\,2} = 0$.  Chiral algebras are what configuration
space geometry accepts; modular Koszul algebras are what the
genus tower accepts.
\end{remark}


\section{The arithmetic face}
\label{sec:arithmetic-face-intro}
\index{arithmetic!of shadow tower|textbf}
\index{Dirichlet--sewing lift|textbf}

The genus-$1$ free energy
$F_1^{\mathrm{conn}}(\cA;q)=-\log\det(1-K_q)$ encodes
connected sewing amplitudes $a_\cA(N)$ as Fourier
coefficients, where $K_q$ is the
\emph{sewing kernel}: the operator on
$H^*(\barB^{(1,1)}(\cA))$ whose eigenvalues are $q^n$
weighted by the dimensions of the conformal-weight-$n$
subspace ($q = e^{2\pi i \tau}$ is the modular parameter
on~$\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$), and $\det(1-K_q)$ is its
Fredholm determinant.
The \emph{Dirichlet--sewing lift}
\[
S_\cA(u):=\sum_{N=1}^\infty a_\cA(N)\,N^{-u}
\]
is a Dirichlet series whose analytic structure is controlled by the shadow obstruction tower.  The sewing amplitudes define a reproducing kernel $K_\cA(s,t)=S_\cA(s{+}t)$, and the surface moment matrix $\mathscr M_{ij}=K_\cA(\alpha/2{+}i,\,\alpha/2{+}j)$ is positive definite for all $\alpha\geq 2$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:gram-positivity}).

Explicit values:
\[
S_{\cH}(u)=\zeta(u)\zeta(u{+}1),\quad
S_{\mathrm{Vir}}(u)=\zeta(u{+}1)(\zeta(u)-1),\quad
S_{\mathcal W_N}(u)=\zeta(u{+}1)\Bigl((N{-}1)\zeta(u)-\textstyle\sum_{j=1}^{N-1}H_j(u)\Bigr).
\]

\subsection{The three-tier Euler--Koszul classification}
\label{subsec:euler-koszul-intro}
\index{Euler--Koszul classification|textbf}

The Euler defect

\item $\mathfrak{K}_\cA \star \mathfrak{K}_\cA$:

\section{The three-dimensional reading}

The bar differential extracts residues in~$\C$; the bar coproduct

Kontsevich formality + classical Swiss-cheese Koszulity +

The product $\operatorname{FM}_k(\C) \times

(where $T$ denotes a holomorphic-topological theory,

category) carries a single MC element

\subsection{The Heisenberg algebra}
\label{subsec:five-theorems-heisenberg}

Take $\cA = \cH_k$ with OPE
$J(z)\,J(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$.  One generator, one double pole, no
simple pole.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~A (bar-cobar adjunction).}\enspace
The bar complex is $\barB(\cH_k) = T^c(s^{-1}\C\cdot J)$, the
cofree coalgebra on a single cogenerator.  The bar differential on
a degree-$2$ element is
\[
d_{\barB}[s^{-1}J \,|\, s^{-1}J]
\;=\;
\Res_{z_1 = z_2}\!\left[
  \frac{k}{(z_1 - z_2)^2}\cdot d\log(z_1-z_2)
\right]
\;=\; k.
\]
At degree~$3$: the Arnold relation on $\FM_3(\C)$ gives
$d_{\barB}^2 = 0$.  The three collision divisors contribute
$k \cdot (\eta_{12}\wedge\eta_{23} + \eta_{23}\wedge\eta_{13}
+ \eta_{13}\wedge\eta_{12}) = 0$.

Verdier duality on $\Ran(X)$ produces the homotopy Koszul dual:
$\mathbb{D}_{\Ran}\,\barB(\cH_k) \simeq (\cH_k^!)_\infty$,
with $\kappa(\cH_k^!) = -k$.
The Heisenberg is not Koszul self-dual:
$\cH_k^! \neq \cH_{-k}$
(\S\ref{sec:frame-koszul-dual}).

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~B (bar-cobar inversion).}\enspace
The bar cohomology is $H^{-1}(\barB(\cH_k)) = \C\cdot s^{-1}J$
(concentrated in bar degree~$1$), so the Koszul dual coalgebra is
$\cH_k^{\mathrm{i}} = \C\cdot s^{-1}J$.  The Koszul dual algebra
is $\cH_k^! = (\cH_k^{\mathrm{i}})^\vee$: the symmetric chiral
algebra $\operatorname{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*)$ with curvature
$m_0 = -k\,\omega$ encoding the opposite level.
By the Verdier intertwining of Theorem~A,
$\kappa(\cH_k^!) = -k$, the opposite modular characteristic
(the Heisenberg is \emph{not} Koszul self-dual;
see~\S\ref{sec:frame-koszul-dual}).
The cobar of the bar is
$\Omega(\barB(\cH_k)) = T(s\,\C\cdot s^{-1}J) = \C[J]$,
which is $\cH_k$ as a graded algebra.  The counit
$\varepsilon\colon\Omega(\barB(\cH_k)) \to \cH_k$ is a
quasi-isomorphism: the Heisenberg is Koszul.

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~C (complementarity).}\enspace
At genus~$g \ge 1$:
$Q_g(\cH_k) \oplus Q_g(\cH_k^!) \cong
H^*(\overline{\cM}_g, \cZ_{\cH_k})$.
The modular characteristic is $\kappa(\cH_k) = k$, and
$\kappa(\cH_k) + \kappa(\cH_k^!) = k + (-k) = 0$.  The
complementarity sum of central charges is
$c(\cH_k) + c(\cH_k^!) = 1 + 1 = 2$
(noting that the dual is curved, so $c(\cH_k^!)=1$ refers to
the underlying graded algebra).

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~D (modular characteristic).}\enspace
At genus~$1$: the self-loop graph $\Gamma_{\mathrm{irr}}$ on
$\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$ contributes
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^2 = k \cdot \omega_1$.  The genus-$1$
obstruction number is $F_1 = k/24$.  The generating function:
\[
\sum_{g \ge 1} F_g(\cH_k)\,x^{2g}
\;=\;
k\!\left(\frac{x/2}{\sin(x/2)} - 1\right)
\;=\;
\frac{k}{24}\,x^2
+ \frac{7k}{5760}\,x^4
+ \frac{31k}{967680}\,x^6
+ \cdots
\]
The coefficients are
$F_g = k \cdot \frac{2^{2g-1}-1}{2^{2g-1}} \cdot
\frac{|B_{2g}|}{(2g)!}$,
where $B_{2g}$ is the $2g$-th Bernoulli number.
(The physical partition function $\log Z_1 = -(k/24)\log\eta(\tau)$
recovers the same data on~$\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$.)

\smallskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~H (chiral Hochschild).}\enspace
$\ChirHoch^*(\cH_k) = (\C, \C, \C)$ concentrated in degrees
$\{0, 1, 2\}$: the class in degree~$0$ is the center (scalars),
the class in degree~$1$ is the outer chiral derivation
$D(\alpha) = \mathbf{1}$ (non-inner because $\alpha_{(0)} = 0$
when the OPE has no simple pole), and the class in degree~$2$ is
the level deformation (double-pole OPE datum).
The Koszul resolution has length~$2$, so
$\ChirHoch^n = 0$ for $n \ge 3$; Hilbert series $1 + t + t^2$,
the rank-$1$ case of the polynomial-growth theorem.

The full computation appears in Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}.

\subsection{The general theorems}
\label{subsec:five-theorems-general}

Here $\operatorname{Fact}(X)$ denotes the symmetric
monoidal category of factorization algebras on~$X$
(\S\ref{subsec:intro-homotopy-derived});
$\operatorname{Alg}_{\mathrm{aug}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))$
is the category of \emph{augmented} factorization algebras
(equipped with a map $\cA \to \omega_X$ to the vacuum);
$\operatorname{CoAlg}_{\mathrm{conil}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))$
is the category of \emph{conilpotent} factorization
coalgebras (the iterated reduced coproduct eventually
vanishes).

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Theorem~A} (Geometric bar-cobar duality,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}).
  The bar functor
  \[
  \bar{B}_X \colon
  \operatorname{Alg}_{\mathrm{aug}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
  \to
  \operatorname{CoAlg}_{\mathrm{conil}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
  \]
  is intertwined with Verdier duality
  (\S\ref{subsec:bar-as-logarithm}):
  $\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}} \bar{B}_X(\cA)
  \simeq \cA^!_\infty$
  (factorization \emph{algebra}, not coalgebra),
  functorial in families
  over~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,n}$.
  It decomposes into chiral twisting morphisms
  ($\mathrm{A}_0$,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:fundamental-twisting-morphisms}),
  bar concentration
  ($\mathrm{A}_1$, Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-concentration}), and
  Verdier-geometric duality
  ($\mathrm{A}_2$,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}).
  On the proved genus-$0$ locus, the bar complex admits a BRST
  comparison; Verdier duality is the algebraic shadow of CPT.

\item \emph{Theorem~B} (Bar-cobar inversion,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:higher-genus-inversion}).
  On the Koszul locus, the counit
  $\Omega_X \bar{B}_X(\cA) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$
  is an equivalence; the spectral sequence collapses at~$E_2$.
  Off the locus, the bar-cobar object persists but becomes curved;
  the failure is measured by
  $\Theta_\cA$~\eqref{eq:universal-MC}.
  The BRST resolution is complete.

  More precisely, there is a spectral sequence with $E_1$ page
  $E_1^{p,q} = H^q(\barB^p_X(\cA))$ converging to $H^{p+q}(\Omega_X \barB_X(\cA))$.
  On the Koszul locus, $E_1^{p,q} = 0$ for $p \neq 1$, so
  $E_2 = E_\infty$, and the comparison map
  $\varepsilon \colon \Omega_X \barB_X(\cA) \to \cA$
  induces an isomorphism on cohomology.  This is the categorical
  exponential: $\exp \circ \log = \operatorname{id}$.

\item \emph{Theorem~C} (Deformation-obstruction complementarity,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}).
  The \emph{center local system} $\mathcal{Z}_\cA$ is the
  local system on $\overline{\cM}_g$ whose fibre over a
  smooth curve $[\Sigma_g]$ is the center
  $Z(\cA|_{\Sigma_g})$ (the commutant of~$\cA$ acting on
  itself via the chiral bracket).
  The \emph{ambient complex}
  $\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) := R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g,
  \mathcal{Z}_\cA)$
  (derived global sections of this local system)
  carries a shifted-symplectic pairing from Verdier duality.
  The Verdier involution decomposes it
  into \emph{complementary Lagrangians}
  $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \oplus \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)$:
  the $+1$ and $-1$ eigenspaces of the involution
  (Definition~\ref{def:complementarity-complexes}).
  At the cohomological level,
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:complementarity-intro}
  Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!) \cong
  H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathcal{Z}_\cA).
  \end{equation}
  The anomaly of one algebra is the ghost-number violation of
  its dual.

  The shifted-symplectic structure is a $(-(3g{-}3))$-shifted symplectic form on the derived moduli of genus-$g$ bar families, in the sense of Pantev--To\"en--Vaqui\'e--Vezzosi~\cite{PTVV13}.  The complementary Lagrangians~$Q_g(\cA)$ and~$Q_g(\cA^!)$ are the $\pm 1$ eigenspaces of the Verdier involution~$\sigma = \mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}$ acting on~$\mathbf{C}_g(\cA)$.  Their intersection is the center~$Z(\cA)$; the ambient complex is the direct sum.

\item \emph{Theorem~D$_{\mathrm{scal}}$} (Scalar modular
  characteristic, Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}).
  A single scalar $\kappa(\cA)$, the genus-$1$ curvature
  coefficient~\eqref{eq:curvature-intro}, determines the
  entire \emph{scalar modular package}: the collection
  $(\kappa, \{\mathrm{obs}_g\}_{g \ge 1},
  \{F_g\}_{g \ge 1})$ consisting of the modular
  characteristic, its genus-$g$ obstruction classes, and the
  free energies.  For algebras whose strong generators have the
  same conformal weight (including all single-generator families),
  $\mathrm{obs}_g = \kappa \cdot \lambda_g
  \in H^*(\overline{\cM}_g)$ and
  $F_g = \kappa \cdot \hat{A}_g$ at all genera.
  For multi-weight algebras (e.g.\ $\mathcal{W}_N$, $N \geq 3$),
  the new rigidity theorem only forces the minimal-model MC
  element into the one-dimensional cyclic direction
  $\Theta^{\min} = \eta \otimes \Gamma_\cA$; the stronger
  identification $\Gamma_\cA = \kappa(\cA)\Lambda$, and hence the
  all-genera identities $\mathrm{obs}_g = \kappa \cdot \lambda_g$
  and $F_g = \kappa \cdot \hat{A}_g$, remain conditional on the
  strong scalar ansatz
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:multi-generator-universality},
  Open Problem~\ref{op:multi-generator-universality}).
  The scalar $\kappa$ is universal,
  additive under tensor product, and duality-constrained
  ($\kappa + \kappa' = 0$ for affine Kac--Moody and free
  fields, and a fixed nonzero constant for
  $\mathcal{W}$-algebras).
  On the uniform-weight lane, the generating function is the
  $\hat{A}$-genus
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:ahat-universality-intro}}\textup{)}:
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:ahat-genus-generating-function-intro}
  \sum_{g \geq 1} F_g(\cA) \cdot \hbar^{2g}
  = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \bigl(\hat{A}(i\hbar) - 1\bigr).
  \end{equation}
  At genus~$1$ this reduces to $F_1(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)/24$ for every
  family.

  \emph{Theorem~D$_\Delta$} (Spectral characteristic,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:spectral-characteristic}):
  the spectral discriminant $\Delta_\cA(x)$ is a separately proved
  non-scalar invariant.

  $\kappa$ is the one-loop coefficient of the effective action;
  its vanishing removes the scalar genus-$1$ anomaly, while
  higher-genus multi-weight corrections are a separate issue.
  Together, $D_{\mathrm{scal}}$ and $D_\Delta$ are the
  characteristic classes of the factorization homology
  bundle $\int_{\Sigma_g}\!\cA$
  (Remark~\ref{rem:four-levels}); the bundle
  itself (its fibers, flat connection, and modular
  functor structure) is the full modular homotopy invariant
  (Conjecture~\ref{conj:categorical-modular-kd}).

\item \emph{Theorem~H} (Chiral Hochschild cohomology,
  Theorem~\ref{thm:hochschild-polynomial-growth}).
  The \emph{chiral Hochschild cohomology}
  $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is the cohomology of the complex
  $\operatorname{CoDer}(\barB(\cA))$
  (coderivations of the bar coalgebra, with differential
  induced by the bar differential).
  On the Koszul locus,
  $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is polynomial and dual
  to $\ChirHoch^*(\cA^!)$.
  Its brace and Gerstenhaber structures control the moduli
  of quantizations and the passage from Poisson to chiral
  (Chapter~\ref{chap:deformation-theory}).
\end{itemize}

\begin{principle}[Five facets of $\Theta_\cA$]
\label{princ:five-facets}
\index{MC element!five facets|textbf}%
Let\/ $\cA$ be a modular Koszul chiral algebra on a smooth projective
curve~$X$, and let\/ $\Theta_\cA := D_\cA - d_\cA^{(0)} \in \gAmod$
be the bar-intrinsic Maurer--Cartan element
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}}\textup{)}.
The five main theorems characterize five
\emph{facets} of~$\Theta_\cA$: existence, faithfulness,
decomposition, leading coefficient, and coefficient ring.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\Alph*)}]
\item \emph{Arena}
  \textup{(}Theorem~A\textup{)}.
  $\Theta_\cA$ \textbf{exists}.
  The bar--cobar adjunction constructs the modular convolution
  algebra~$\gAmod$ in which~$\Theta_\cA$ lives; with it,
  $\Theta_\cA$ arises automatically as $D_\cA - d_\cA^{(0)}$.

\item \emph{Faithfulness}
  \textup{(}Theorem~B\textup{)}.
  $\Theta_\cA$ \textbf{determines}~$\cA$.
  On the Koszul locus, the counit
  $\Omega_X(\barB_X(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$ is a
  quasi-isomorphism.

\item \emph{Decomposition}
  \textup{(}Theorem~C\textup{)}.
  $\Theta_\cA$ \textbf{splits}.
  The genus-$g$ projection decomposes under the Verdier involution
  into complementary Lagrangians
  $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \oplus \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)
  = H^*(\overline{\cM}_g,\, Z(\cA))$.

\item \emph{Leading coefficient}
  \textup{(}Theorem~D\textup{)}.
  $\Theta_\cA$ has \textbf{universal scalar projection}.
  On the uniform-weight lane, the arity-$2$ genus-$g$ component is
  $\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)\,\lambda_g$ at all genera,
  with $\kappa(\cA)$ universal and governed by the
  $\hat{A}$-genus.  At genus~$1$ this holds unconditionally
  for all families.

\item \emph{Coefficient ring}
  \textup{(}Theorem~H\textup{)}.
  $\Theta_\cA$ has \textbf{polynomial coefficients}.
  $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$ is concentrated in degrees~$\{0,1,2\}$,
  controlling the deformation ring over which~$\Theta_\cA$ varies.
\end{enumerate}
\end{principle}

\begin{remark}[$\Eone$-chiral counterparts of the five theorems]
\label{rem:e1-five-theorems}
\index{$\mathbb{E}_1$-chiral algebra!five theorems}%
\index{ordered bar complex!five theorems}%
Each of Theorems~A--H has an $\Eone$ counterpart
(Theorems~$\mathrm{A}^{E_1}$--$\mathrm{H}^{E_1}$,
Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul}),
in which the symmetric bar $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ on unordered
configuration spaces is replaced by the
\emph{ordered bar} $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ on
$\operatorname{Conf}_n^{\mathrm{ord}}(X)$,
the chiral Koszul dual~$\cA^!$ is replaced by the
\emph{dg-shifted Yangian} $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$,
and the commutative modular operad $\mathrm{FCom}$ is replaced
by the ribbon modular operad $F\!\Ass$.
The five facets transfer:
$\Theta_\cA^{E_1}$ exists in the ordered convolution
algebra~$\mathfrak{g}_{\cA}^{\mathrm{mod},E_1}$ (Theorem~$\mathrm{A}^{E_1}$);
the ordered cobar inverts on the ordered Koszul locus
(Theorem~$\mathrm{B}^{E_1}$);
the genus-$g$ projection decomposes into complementary
Lagrangians via Verdier duality on ribbon graphs
(Theorem~$\mathrm{C}^{E_1}$);
the leading coefficient is the same scalar~$\kappa(\cA)$
(Theorem~$\mathrm{D}^{E_1}$);
and the coefficient ring is the ordered chiral Hochschild
cohomology (Theorem~$\mathrm{H}^{E_1}$).
The $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant map
$F\!\Ass \to \mathrm{FCom}$
sends $\Theta_\cA^{E_1} \mapsto \Theta_\cA$:
the symmetric theory is the unordered projection of the
ordered theory.

The two Koszul dualities are co-equal but geometrically
distinct.  The symmetric bar sees unordered collisions and
produces the chiral Koszul dual $\cA^!$---an
$\Einf$-chiral algebra on the curve.  The ordered bar
sees ordered collisions and produces the dg-shifted Yangian
$\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$---an $\Eone$-chiral algebra
whose $R$-matrix is the Koszul-dual spectral parameter.
The symmetric dual lives on~$X$; the ordered dual
lives on a line in the $3$d bulk $\C_z \times \R_t$.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Central charge complementarity; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]\label{thm:central-charge-complementarity}
\index{central charge!complementarity|textbf}
\textup{[Regime: curved-central on the Koszul locus
\textup{(}Convention~\textup{\ref{conv:regime-tags})}.]}

For Koszul dual pairs related by the Feigin--Frenkel involution
$k \mapsto k' = -k - 2h^\vee$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textup{(a)}] \emph{Affine Kac--Moody.}
  $c(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k) + c(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{k'})
  = 2 \dim \mathfrak{g}$.
\item[\textup{(b)}] \emph{Principal $\mathcal{W}$-algebras:}
  $c(\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})) + c(\mathcal{W}^{k'}(\mathfrak{g}))
   = 2\operatorname{rank}(\mathfrak{g}) + 4h^\vee \dim \mathfrak{g}$.
\end{enumerate}
In particular, $\mathfrak{g} = \mathfrak{sl}_2$ in
part~\textup{(b)} gives $c + c' = 26$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Part~(a).}  The Sugawara central charge is
$c(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k) = k \dim\mathfrak{g}/(k + h^\vee)$.
Since $k' + h^\vee = -(k + h^\vee)$:
\[
c(k) + c(k') = \frac{k \cdot d}{k+h^\vee}
+ \frac{(k+2h^\vee) \cdot d}{k+h^\vee}
= \frac{2(k + h^\vee) \cdot d}{k+h^\vee} = 2d
\]
where $d = \dim\mathfrak{g}$.

\emph{Part~(b).}  The Drinfeld--Sokolov central charge is
$c(\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})) = r - 12|\rho|^2 (t-1)^2/t$
where $r = \operatorname{rank}(\mathfrak{g})$ and
$t = k + h^\vee$
(verified for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ in
Theorem~\ref{thm:w-algebra-koszul-main} and for $\mathfrak{sl}_3$
in Theorem~\ref{thm:w-koszul-precise}).
The involution sends $t \mapsto -t$:
\[
c(k) + c(k') = 2r + 12|\rho|^2 \cdot
\frac{(t+1)^2 - (t-1)^2}{t}
= 2r + 48|\rho|^2.
\]
The Freudenthal--de~Vries identity
$|\rho|^2 = h^\vee \dim\mathfrak{g}/12$
(under normalization $(\theta, \theta) = 2$;
see~\cite{Kac}, Ch.~13)
yields $c + c' = 2r + 4h^\vee d$.

\emph{Verification.}
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$: $2(1) + 4(2)(3) = 26$.
$\mathfrak{sl}_3$: $2(2) + 4(3)(8) = 100$.
$\mathfrak{sl}_N$:
$2(N{-}1) + 4N(N^2{-}1) = 2(N{-}1)(2N^2{+}2N{+}1)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:bosonic-string-26}
\index{bosonic string!critical dimension}
The identity $c + c' = 26$ for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ is the algebraic
incarnation of $c_{\mathrm{matter}} + c_{\mathrm{ghost}} = 0$.
At the critical point, the relative BRST complex of the bosonic string
is quasi-isomorphic to the genus-$0$ bar complex
\textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:brst-bar-genus0}\textup{)}.  The identification
of the ghost system with the cobar of the matter bar complex
requires the heuristic BV/bar bridge of Chapter~\ref{ch:bv-brst};
the bosonic string critical dimension follows from Koszul duality at
the scalar level without this bridge.
\end{remark}

The five theorems assemble into a single commutative square:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:master-square}
\begin{tikzcd}[row sep=huge, column sep=huge]
\operatorname{Alg}_{\mathrm{aug}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
  \arrow[r, "\bar{B}_X"]
  \arrow[d, "(\cdot)^!"']
& \operatorname{CoAlg}_{\mathrm{conil}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
  \arrow[d, "\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}"]
\\
\operatorname{Alg}_{\mathrm{aug}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
  \arrow[r, "\bar{B}_X"']
& \operatorname{CoAlg}_{\mathrm{conil}}(\operatorname{Fact}(X))
\end{tikzcd}
\end{equation}
\index{master diagram}
\index{Verdier duality!as mediating mechanism}
Commutativity is Theorem~A\@.  The cobar functor~$\Omega_X$, left
adjoint to~$\bar{B}_X$, gives the counit of Theorem~B\@.  At
genus~$g \geq 1$, the universal Maurer--Cartan class~$\Theta_\cA$
measures the failure of strict commutativity.  Vanishing of the
scalar curvature term $\kappa(\cA)$ removes one obstruction, but the
manuscript's reduction from the intrinsic coderived/contraderived
comparison to ordinary derived categories is proved only on the
flat finite-type completed-dual loci singled out later in Part~I.

Off the Koszul locus, the coderived category
(Positselski~\cite{Positselski11}) is the correct ambient category:
the curved bar object can lose ordinary cohomological
visibility while remaining nontrivial in the coderived framework.
Ordinary derived categories are recovered only on the flat
finite-type completed-dual loci.

\subsection{The main theorem}
\label{subsec:main-theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Modular Koszul duality; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:modular-koszul-duality-main}
\index{modular Koszul duality!main theorem|textbf}

Let\/ $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on a smooth projective
curve~$X$ over\/ $\mathbb{C}$, equipped with a non-degenerate
invariant pairing.  The genus-graded bar complex
$\{\barB^{(g)}_X(\cA)\}_{g \geq 0}$ is an algebra over the
Feynman transform\/ $\mathrm{FCom}$ of the commutative modular operad
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-modular-operad}}\textup{)},
with genus-completed differential
\[
D_\cA = \sum_{g \geq 0} \hbar^g\, d_\cA^{(g)}
\quad\text{satisfying}\quad
D_\cA^2 = 0.
\]

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]

\item \emph{Bar--cobar duality.}
  The bar functor\/ $\barB_X$ and cobar functor\/~$\Omega_X$
  form an adjoint pair on augmented factorization algebras,
  with Verdier intertwining
  $\mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}\, \barB_X(\cA) \simeq
  \barB_X(\cA^!)$
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}}\textup{)}.
  On the Koszul locus, the counit
  $\Omega_X(\barB_X(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$ is a
  quasi-isomorphism at every genus
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-cobar-inversion-qi}}\textup{)}.

\item \emph{Universal Maurer--Cartan class.}
  The positive-genus correction
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:theta-main-theorem}
  \Theta_\cA
  \;:=\;
  D_\cA - d_\cA^{(0)}
  \;=\;
  \sum_{g \geq 1} \hbar^g\, d_\cA^{(g)}
  \end{equation}
  is a Maurer--Cartan element:
  $\Theta_\cA \in
  \MC\!\bigl(\Defcyc(\cA) \;\widehat{\otimes}\; \Gmod\bigr)$.
  The Maurer--Cartan equation holds because $D_\cA^2 = 0$;
  no construction is required beyond the bar differential itself.

\item \emph{Projections.}
  The scalar trace channel of\/ $\Theta_\cA$ is governed by the
  genus-$1$ coefficient $\kappa(\cA) \in \mathbb{C}$.
  For uniform-weight modular Koszul algebras,
  $\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)\,\lambda_g$ for all $g \geq 1$;
  for arbitrary families the unconditional scalar statement is
  $F_1 = \kappa/24$
  \textup{(}Remark~\textup{\ref{rem:propagator-weight-universality}}\textup{)}.
  The coefficient $\kappa(\cA)$ is universal, additive
  under tensor product, duality-constrained
  ($\kappa + \kappa' = 0$ for KM/free fields, a fixed constant for
  $\mathcal{W}$-algebras), and has $\hat{A}$-genus generating function
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}}\textup{)}.
  Evaluated against a conformal deformation $g_1 = e^{2\sigma}g_0$ of the worldsheet metric, $\kappa(\cA)$ yields the Polyakov formula with coefficient $\kappa/(6\pi)$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:polyakov-chern-weil}): the modular characteristic is the algebraic form of the conformal anomaly.
  The eigenspace decomposition under the Verdier involution gives
  the Lagrangian polarization
  $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \oplus \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)$
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}}\textup{)}.
  The coefficient ring is the polynomial chiral Hochschild
  cohomology $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:main-koszul-hoch}}\textup{)}.

\item \emph{Shadow Postnikov obstruction tower.}
  The finite-order projections
  $\Theta_\cA^{\leq 2} \to
  \Theta_\cA^{\leq 3} \to
  \Theta_\cA^{\leq 4} \to \cdots$
  give the shadow obstruction tower:
  $\kappa(\cA)$ at arity~$2$,
  the cubic shadow~$\mathfrak{C}(\cA)$ at arity~$3$,
  the quartic resonance class~$\mathfrak{Q}(\cA)$ at arity~$4$.
  The element $\Theta_\cA$ satisfies clutching factorization under
  stable-curve degeneration and Verdier duality:
  $\mathbb{D}(\Theta_\cA) = \Theta_{\cA^!}$.

\item \emph{The chiral center theorem.}
  The bar coproduct \textup{(}deconcatenation = factorization along
  an ordered line\textup{)} equips $\barB_X(\cA)$ with the
  structure of a coalgebra over the holomorphic--topological
  Swiss-cheese operad $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$.  The
  operadic center of this Swiss-cheese structure is the chiral
  Hochschild cochain complex:
  \[
  Z_{\mathsf{SC}}(\cA)
  \;\simeq\;
  C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA),
  \]
  and the pair
  $(C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA, \cA),\, \cA)$ is the
  terminal local chiral Swiss-cheese pair over~$\cA$
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:operadic-center-hochschild}},
  Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:chiral-deligne-tamarkin}}\textup{)}.
  This is the chiral analogue of the Deligne--Tamarkin theorem:
  the bulk of any holomorphic boundary theory is forced by
  locality to be the chiral Hochschild cochains.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}
\label{rem:categorical-logarithm-paradigm}
The genus tower is the Taylor expansion of the monodromy
$\kappa(\cA)\cdot\omega_g$.  The nonlinear characteristic layer
computes the higher Taylor jets of this expansion: the quartic jets
of the complementarity potential
(Chapter~\ref{app:nonlinear-modular-shadows}), the spectral
cumulants of the branch-line quotient
(Appendix~\ref{app:branch-line-reductions}), and the canonical
homotopy arity in the non-principal $\mathcal W$-algebra sector.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Two proofs of the center theorem]
\label{rem:center-theorem-intro}
Item~(v) is proved by two independent routes: an
\emph{operadic} proof via the recognition theorem and
homotopy-Koszulity of $\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$
(\S\ref{sec:operadic-center-theorem}), and a \emph{direct} proof
constructing the unique brace morphism
$\Phi \colon B \to C^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA,\cA)$
with explicit Koszul signs (\S\ref{sec:chiral-center-theorem}).
The key new difficulty versus the topological Deligne conjecture is
the passage from smooth to meromorphic propagators: Arnold
relations replace Stokes, and genus~$g \geq 1$ introduces curvature
$\kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g$ into the center (curved $E_2$-algebra).
\end{remark}

\subsection{Shadow separation, tautological classes, and analytic
realization}
\label{subsec:shadow-separation-intro}

\begin{corollary}[Shadow separation; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere;
  Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:shadow-separation}}]
\label{cor:shadow-separation-intro}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Completeness.}
  $[\Theta_\cA^{\leq r}] = [\Theta_\cB^{\leq r}]$ for
  all~$r \geq 2$ implies
  $[\Theta_\cA] = [\Theta_\cB]$ in
  $\operatorname{MC}(\widehat{\gAmod})/\!\!\sim$.
\item \emph{Strict refinement.}
  $\kappa(\cH_1) = \kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_2) = 1$ but
  $\mathfrak{C}_{\cH} = 0 \neq 2x^3 =
  \mathfrak{C}_{\mathrm{Vir}}$;
  on the weight-changing line,
  $\mathfrak{Q}_{\cH} = 0 \neq
  \operatorname{cyc}(m_3)
  = \mathfrak{Q}_{\beta\gamma}$.
\item \emph{Koszul invariance.}
  $r_{\max}(\cA^!) = r_{\max}(\cA)$
  \textup{(}Corollary~\textup{\ref{cor:shadow-depth-koszul-invariance}}\textup{)}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{corollary}

The cyclic trace of the graph-sum formula defines
shadow tautological classes
$\tau_{g,n}(\cA) \in
R^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,n+1})$
(Definition~\ref{def:shadow-tautological-classes},
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tautological-ring}):
$\tau_{g,2}(\cA) = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \pi^*\lambda_g$
at arity~$2$, with new classes at higher arities whose
vertex weights are the cyclic traces of the transferred
operations $\ell_n^{(g)}(\cA)$.
For every algebra in the standard landscape, the algebraic
shadow data extends to the \emph{sewing envelope}
$\cA^{\mathrm{sew}}$, the Hausdorff completion of~$\cA$,
with respect to the seminorms defined by sewing amplitudes
(Corollary~\ref{cor:analytic-shadow-realization}).


\section{The modular characteristic and the spectral discriminant}
\label{sec:modular-char-spectral-disc-intro}
\index{modular characteristic!explicit formula|textbf}
\index{spectral discriminant!construction|textbf}
\index{A-hat genus@$\hat{A}$-genus|textbf}

The genus-$1$ curvature $\kappa(\cA)$ is the scalar component
of the MC element at arity~$2$: it controls the genus-$1$
obstruction $d^2 = \kappa\,\omega_1$, the leading term of
the $\hat{A}$-genus generating function, and the Hodge-bundle
eigenvalue of the spectral measure.

\subsection{The explicit formula for $\kappa$}
\label{subsec:kappa-explicit}
\index{modular characteristic!explicit formula}

Let~$\cA$ be a chiral algebra on a smooth projective curve~$X$ with OPE
$a(z)\,b(w) \sim \sum_{n \geq 0} (a_{(n)}b)(w) / (z-w)^{n+1}$.
The modular characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$ is the genus-$1$ curvature coefficient:
$\kappa(\mathcal{H}_k) = k$ for the Heisenberg algebra,
$\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k) = (k+h^\vee)\dim\mathfrak{g}/(2h^\vee)$ for affine Kac--Moody, and
$\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c) = c/2$ for Virasoro
(Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality}).
For uniform-weight algebras
(Definition~\ref{def:uniform-weight-lane}),
the obstruction class at genus~$g$ is universally determined:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:obstruction-class-formula-intro}
\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g \in H^{2g}(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathbb{Q}),
\end{equation}
where $\lambda_g = c_g(\mathbb{E})$ is the top Chern class of the Hodge bundle.
This holds at all genera;
for multi-weight families such as~$\mathcal{W}_N$, the
unconditional statement is genus~$1$, while the higher-genus
factorization remains open
\textup{(}Remark~\ref{rem:propagator-weight-universality}\textup{)}.
The free-energy formula
$F_g = \kappa \cdot \frac{2^{2g-1}-1}{2^{2g-1}} \cdot
\frac{|B_{2g}|}{(2g)!}$
holds for uniform-weight algebras
\textup{(}Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}\textup{)}.
The first explicit multi-channel computation is the $\cW_3$
genus-$2$ cross-channel correction
\textup{(}Remark~\ref{rem:w3-genus2-cross-channel}\textup{)}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:w3-genus2-cross-intro}
F_2(\cW_3)
= \underbrace{\frac{7c}{6912}}_{\kappa \cdot \lambda_2^{\mathrm{FP}}}
+ \underbrace{\frac{c + 120}{16c}}_{\delta F_2},
\end{equation}
verified by graph-by-graph recomputation over all seven genus-$2$
stable graphs and $2^e$ channel assignments per graph.
The cross-channel correction $\delta F_2 = (c{+}120)/(16c)$ is
the multi-generator signal at genus~$2$; it tends to $1/16$ as
$c \to \infty$ and diverges as $c \to 0$.  The formula is
conditional on
the resolution of Open Problem~\ref{op:multi-generator-universality}.

\subsection{Universality, additivity, anti-symmetry}
\label{subsec:kappa-properties}

The three properties of~$\kappa$ that make it a topological characteristic class:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textbf{Universality.}  The cohomological identity $\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g$ and the Faber--Pandharipande free-energy formula hold at all genera for uniform-weight algebras (Theorem~\ref{thm:genus-universality}).  For arbitrary modular Koszul algebras, the unconditional statement is the genus-$1$ value $F_1=\kappa/24$; higher-genus multi-weight factorization remains open (Remark~\ref{rem:propagator-weight-universality}).  The scalar $\kappa$ is determined by the genus-$1$ value alone: the clutching maps of stable curves propagate the genus-$1$ curvature to all genera via the modular operad structure.

\item \textbf{Additivity.}  For a tensor product $\cA \otimes \cB$ of chiral algebras,
\[
\kappa(\cA \otimes \cB) = \kappa(\cA) + \kappa(\cB).
\]
This follows from the factorization of the bar complex:
$\barB_X(\cA \otimes \cB) \simeq \barB_X(\cA) \otimes \barB_X(\cB)$.

\item \textbf{Duality constraint.}  Under Koszul duality,
$\kappa(\cA) + \kappa(\cA^!) = 0$
for affine Kac--Moody algebras (where $\cA^! = \widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{k'}$ with $k' = -k - 2h^\vee$) and for free-field algebras.  For $\mathcal{W}$-algebras from Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction, $\kappa + \kappa'$ is a fixed nonzero constant depending on~$\mathfrak{g}$: $\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c) + \kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}) = 13$ for Virasoro (Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic}(iii)).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{The $\hat{A}$-genus generating function}
\label{subsec:ahat-genus}
\index{A-hat genus@$\hat{A}$-genus!as family index}
\index{A-hat genus@$\hat{A}$-genus!universality theorem|textbf}

The genus expansion of every uniform-weight modular Koszul algebra is
controlled by a single function from index theory.
Grothendieck--Riemann--Roch applied
to the universal curve $\pi \colon \overline{\mathcal{C}}_g \to
\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ yields
$\operatorname{ch}(R\pi_* \barB_g(\cA))
= \kappa(\cA) \cdot \operatorname{td}(\mathbb{E}^\vee)$,
and the Todd class of the dual Hodge bundle produces the
$\hat{A}$-series.

\begin{theorem}[$\hat{A}$-genus universality; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:ahat-universality-intro}
\index{genus expansion!universal generating function}
Let $\cA$ be a modular Koszul chiral algebra whose strong generators
have uniform conformal weight.  Then the free energies
$F_g(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)\cdot\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}$ at all
genera $g \geq 1$, where $\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}$ are the
Faber--Pandharipande evaluations of the Hodge class
\textup{(}$\lambda_1^{\mathrm{FP}} = 1/24$,
$\lambda_2^{\mathrm{FP}} = 7/5760$,
$\lambda_3^{\mathrm{FP}} = 31/967680, \ldots$\textup{)}.
The generating function is the $\hat{A}$-genus:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:genus-generating-function-intro}
\sum_{g \geq 1} F_g(\cA) \cdot \hbar^{2g}
= \kappa(\cA) \cdot \bigl(\hat{A}(i\hbar) - 1\bigr)
= \kappa(\cA) \cdot \left(\frac{\hbar/2}{\sin(\hbar/2)} - 1\right),
\end{equation}
with radius of convergence $|\hbar| = 2\pi$, independent of~$\cA$.
For arbitrary modular Koszul algebras \textup{(}including
multi-weight families\textup{)}, the genus-$1$ coefficient
$F_1(\cA) = \kappa(\cA)/24$ holds unconditionally.
\end{theorem}

\noindent
The function $(x/2)/\sin(x/2)$ is the Wick rotation of the
$\hat{A}$-genus $(x/2)/\sinh(x/2)$ that appears in the
Atiyah--Singer index theorem; the positivity of all coefficients
$\lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}} > 0$ reflects the Hodge index theorem
on~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$.
For a free fermion, the formula produces the spin partition function;
for the Virasoro algebra, the one-loop string amplitude.
See Theorem~\ref{thm:universal-generating-function} for the
full proof and
equation~\eqref{eq:ahat-universality-preface} for a summary.

\subsection{The spectral discriminant}
\label{subsec:spectral-discriminant-intro}
\index{spectral discriminant|textbf}

The spectral discriminant $\Delta_\cA(x) := \operatorname{disc}(P_{\cA^!}(x))$, the discriminant of the Koszul dual Hilbert series, is the first non-scalar invariant.  It is invariant under Koszul duality ($\Delta_\cA = \Delta_{\cA^!}$, Proposition~\ref{prop:discriminant-characteristic}) and under Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction (Theorem~\ref{thm:ds-bar-gf-discriminant}).

The Hilbert series of the Koszul dual is $P_{\cA^!}(x) = \sum_{n \geq 0} \dim H^n(\barB(\cA)) \cdot x^n$.  For Koszul algebras, this is the reciprocal of the Hilbert series of~$\cA$: $P_{\cA^!}(x) = 1 / P_\cA(-x)$.  The discriminant of this rational function, viewed as a polynomial in~$x$, is an integer-valued invariant that detects spectral structure invisible to the scalar~$\kappa$.

The shared discriminant $\Delta(x) = (1-3x)(1+x) = 1 - 2x - 3x^2$ across $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$, Virasoro, and $\beta\gamma$ is not coincidence: these algebras lie on the same spectral sheet of the Koszul dual parameter space.  The zeros of~$\Delta$ at $x = 1/3$ and $x = -1$ mark the transitions between bar-acyclic and bar-active sectors.

\section{Algebraic prerequisites}
\label{sec:algebraic-prerequisites}
% ================================================================

The definitions below are standard; full treatments appear in the chapters cited.

\subsection{Configuration spaces and their combinatorics}
\label{subsec:intro-config-comb}

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Fulton--MacPherson compactification.}
\index{Fulton--MacPherson compactification!definition}
For a smooth projective curve~$X$, the \emph{open configuration
space} $C_n(X) = \{(z_1,\ldots,z_n) \in X^n : z_i \neq z_j\}$
parametrises $n$~distinct labelled points.  The
\emph{Fulton--MacPherson compactification} $\overline{C}_n(X)$
is the iterated real-oriented blowup of~$X^n$ along all
diagonals, starting from the smallest (pairwise) and
proceeding to larger ones
(Definition~\ref{def:log-fm-compactification}).
The result is a smooth manifold with corners whose boundary strata
$D_S$, one for each subset $S \subseteq \{1,\ldots,n\}$ with
$|S| \ge 2$, parametrise configurations in which the
points labelled by~$S$ collide.  The
\emph{logarithmic one-forms}
$\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j) \in
\Omega^1_{\log}(\overline{C}_n(X))$ have
simple poles along~$D_{\{i,j\}}$ and no other poles.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Arnold relation.}
\index{Arnold relation}
On $\overline{C}_3(X)$, three logarithmic one-forms satisfy
the \emph{Arnold relation}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-arnold}
\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23}
\;+\; \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31}
\;+\; \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12}
\;=\; 0
\quad\text{as logarithmic differential forms on } \overline{C}_3(X).
\end{equation}
This is the geometric content behind
$d^2 = 0$ at genus~$0$: the bar differential, built from
residues along collision divisors, squares to zero
because any two codimension-one boundaries
meet transversally and the three-term
relation~\eqref{eq:intro-arnold} makes the
codimension-two contributions cancel in pairs.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Stable graphs.}
\index{stable graph!definition}
A \emph{stable graph}~$\Gamma$ of type $(g,n)$ consists of
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item a connected graph with vertex set $V(\Gamma)$
  and edge set $E(\Gamma)$;
\item a \emph{genus function} $g_\bullet \colon V(\Gamma) \to
  \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}$;
\item $n$~labelled \emph{tails} (half-edges) attached to vertices;
\item \emph{stability}: $2g(v) - 2 + \mathrm{val}(v) > 0$ for every
  vertex~$v$, where $\mathrm{val}(v)$ counts all
  half-edges (edges and tails) at~$v$.
\end{enumerate}
The total genus is
$g(\Gamma) = \sum_{v} g(v) + h^1(\Gamma)$, where
$h^1(\Gamma) = |E(\Gamma)| - |V(\Gamma)| + 1$ is the first
Betti number.  Every boundary stratum of the
Deligne--Mumford compactification $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$
is indexed by a stable graph:
$\overline{\cM}_\Gamma
= \prod_{v \in V(\Gamma)} \overline{\cM}_{g(v),\mathrm{val}(v)}$
(Definition~\ref{def:stable-graph}).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Ran space.}
\index{Ran space!definition}
The \emph{Ran space} of~$X$ is the colimit
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-ran}
\mathrm{Ran}(X) \;=\; \varinjlim_{I \in \mathrm{fSet}^{\mathrm{surj}}} X^I,
\end{equation}
taken over the category of non-empty finite sets with
surjections.  A surjection $I \twoheadrightarrow J$ induces a
diagonal embedding $X^J \hookrightarrow X^I$, so
$\mathrm{Ran}(X)$ is the space of finite non-empty subsets
of~$X$; points are allowed to collide
(Definition~\ref{def:ran-space}).

\subsection{Operadic algebra}
\label{subsec:intro-operadic}

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Symmetric sequences.}
\index{symmetric sequence}
A \emph{symmetric sequence} (or $\mathbb{S}$-module) is a
collection $V = \{V(n)\}_{n \ge 0}$ of chain complexes,
each equipped with a left $\Sigma_n$-action.  A morphism
$f \colon V \to W$ of symmetric sequences consists of
$\Sigma_n$-equivariant chain maps $f(n) \colon V(n) \to W(n)$
for each~$n$.  The \emph{equivariant hom}
$\Hom_{\Sigma_n}(V(n), W(n))$ denotes the chain complex
of $\Sigma_n$-equivariant maps.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Endomorphism operad.}
\index{endomorphism operad}
For a chain complex~$A$, the \emph{endomorphism operad}
$\operatorname{End}_A$ is the symmetric sequence with
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-end}
\operatorname{End}_A(n) \;=\; \Hom(A^{\otimes n},\, A),
\end{equation}
where $\Sigma_n$ acts by permuting the tensor factors
of~$A^{\otimes n}$.  Composition is given by substitution:
for $f \in \operatorname{End}_A(k)$ and
$g_1,\ldots,g_k \in \operatorname{End}_A(n_1),\ldots,
\operatorname{End}_A(n_k)$,
\[
(f \circ (g_1,\ldots,g_k))(a_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes a_N)
= f\bigl(g_1(a_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes a_{N_1})
  \otimes \cdots \otimes
  g_k(a_{N_{k-1}+1} \otimes \cdots \otimes a_{N_k})\bigr),
\]
where $N_j = n_1 + \cdots + n_j$, $N_0 = 0$,
and $N = N_k$.  An \emph{algebra over an
operad}~$\mathcal{P}$ is a morphism of operads
$\mathcal{P} \to \operatorname{End}_A$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Modular operad.}
\index{modular operad!definition}
A \emph{modular operad} (Getzler--Kapranov~\cite{GK98})
consists of a symmetric sequence
$\mathcal{O} = \{\mathcal{O}(g,n)\}$, indexed by pairs
$(g,n)$ satisfying the stability condition $2g - 2 + n > 0$,
equipped with two families of structure maps
(Definition~\ref{def:modular-operad}):
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \emph{Separating gluing}: for each decomposition
  $(g,n) = (g_1, n_1 + 1) \sqcup (g_2, n_2 + 1)$
  with $g = g_1 + g_2$ and $n = n_1 + n_2$,
  \[
  \xi_{\mathrm{sep}} \colon
  \mathcal{O}(g_1, n_1 + 1) \otimes \mathcal{O}(g_2, n_2 + 1)
  \;\longrightarrow\; \mathcal{O}(g, n),
  \]
  gluing two marked points (one from each factor) into a
  separating node.

\item \emph{Non-separating gluing}: for each
  $(g-1, n+2) \to (g, n)$,
  \[
  \xi_{\mathrm{ns}} \colon \mathcal{O}(g-1, n+2)
  \;\longrightarrow\; \mathcal{O}(g, n),
  \]
  gluing two marked points on the same component into a
  non-separating node, raising genus by~$1$.
\end{enumerate}
These satisfy equivariance under $\Sigma_n$, associativity
(iterated gluings are independent of order), and
commutativity (separating gluing is symmetric).

The canonical example is the \emph{modular operad of stable
curves}: $\overline{\cM}(g,n) = C_*(\overline{\cM}_{g,n})$,
with $\xi_{\mathrm{sep}}$ and $\xi_{\mathrm{ns}}$ induced by
the clutching maps of Knudsen and Mumford.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Feynman transform.}
\index{Feynman transform!definition}
Let $\mathcal{O}$ be a modular operad.  Its
\emph{Feynman transform} $F\mathcal{O}$ is the modular
cooperad whose underlying graded vector space is
(Definition~\ref{def:feynman-transform})
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-feynman}
F\mathcal{O}(g,n)
\;=\; \bigoplus_{\Gamma \in \mathcal{G}_{g,n}}
\biggl(\bigotimes_{v \in V(\Gamma)}
  \mathcal{O}\bigl(g(v),\mathrm{val}(v)\bigr)
\biggr)
\otimes \det\bigl(E(\Gamma)\bigr),
\end{equation}
summed over isomorphism classes of stable graphs~$\Gamma$ of
type~$(g,n)$, modulo $\mathrm{Aut}(\Gamma)$.  The
\emph{orientation line} $\det(E(\Gamma))
= \bigwedge^{\mathrm{top}} \Bbbk\langle E(\Gamma) \rangle$
is the one-dimensional vector space spanned by orderings of
the edge set; it controls signs.
The differential contracts edges:
\[
d_{F\mathcal{O}}(\gamma) = \sum_{e \in E(\Gamma)}
  \pm\, \xi_e(\gamma),
\]
where $\xi_e$ applies the appropriate gluing map of $\mathcal{O}$
at edge~$e$ and contracts $e$ from $\Gamma$, with sign
determined by the position of~$e$ in the orientation line.
The identity $d^2 = 0$ follows from the associativity axiom
of~$\mathcal{O}$.

The \emph{commutative modular operad} $\mathrm{Com}$ has
$\mathrm{Com}(g,n) = \Bbbk$ (with trivial $\Sigma_n$-action)
for every stable $(g,n)$, and all structure maps are the
identity $\Bbbk \otimes \Bbbk \xrightarrow{\sim} \Bbbk$.
Its Feynman transform $\mathrm{FCom} := F(\mathrm{Com})$ has components
$\mathrm{FCom}(g,n) = \bigoplus_\Gamma \det(E(\Gamma))$,
the direct sum of orientation lines over all stable graphs
of type $(g,n)$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Cofree conilpotent coalgebra.}
\index{cofree coalgebra}
For a chain complex~$V$, the \emph{cofree conilpotent
coalgebra} is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-cofree}
T^c(V) = \bigoplus_{n \ge 0} V^{\otimes n}
\end{equation}
with \emph{deconcatenation
coproduct}
$\Delta(v_1 \cdots v_n) = \sum_{i=0}^{n}
(v_1 \cdots v_i) \otimes (v_{i+1} \cdots v_n)$.
The coalgebra is \emph{conilpotent}: $\bar\Delta^{(k)}$ vanishes on $V^{\otimes n}$ for $k > n$.
The bar complex of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ has underlying
coalgebra $\barB(\cA) = \bar{T}^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$
(the reduced version, omitting the $n=0$ summand),
where $\bar\cA = \ker(\varepsilon)$ is the augmentation ideal
(the kernel of the vacuum map $\varepsilon \colon \cA \to \omega_X$)
and $s^{-1}$ is the desuspension shifting cohomological degree
by~$-1$.

\subsection{Twisting and convolution}
\label{subsec:intro-twisting}

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Convolution dg Lie algebra.}
\index{convolution dg Lie algebra!definition}
Let $(C, \Delta, d_C)$ be a conilpotent dg coalgebra and
$(A, \mu, d_A)$ a dg algebra.  The
\emph{convolution dg Lie algebra}
$\mathrm{Conv}(C, A) = \Hom(C, A)$ carries
(Definition~\ref{def:convolution-dg-lie}):
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \emph{Convolution product}:
  $f \star g := \mu \circ (f \otimes g) \circ \Delta$.
\item \emph{Differential}:
  $\partial(f) := d_A \circ f
  - (-1)^{|f|}\, f \circ d_C$.
\item \emph{Lie bracket}:
  $[f, g] := f \star g - (-1)^{|f||g|}\, g \star f$.
\end{enumerate}
The triple $(\Hom(C,A),\, [-,-],\, \partial)$ is a dg Lie
algebra.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Twisting morphism.}
\index{twisting morphism!definition}
A \emph{twisting morphism} $\tau \colon C \to A$
between a conilpotent dg coalgebra~$C$ and a dg algebra~$A$
is a degree~$+1$ linear map satisfying the Maurer--Cartan
equation in the convolution algebra
(Definition~\ref{def:chiral-twisting-datum}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-twisting-mc}
\partial(\tau) + \tau \star \tau = 0,
\qquad\text{i.e., }
d_A \circ \tau + \tau \circ d_C
+ \mu \circ (\tau \otimes \tau) \circ \Delta = 0.
\end{equation}
The set of twisting morphisms
$\mathrm{Tw}(C, A) = \mathrm{MC}(\mathrm{Conv}(C,A))$
is the Maurer--Cartan locus of the convolution algebra.
The set $\mathrm{Tw}(C, A)$ is in natural bijection with bar-coalgebra
maps $C \to \barB(A)$ and with cobar-algebra
maps $\Omega(C) \to A$; these three bijections constitute
bar-cobar adjunction
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-adjunction}).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Scalar saturation symbols $\eta$
and $\Lambda$.}
\index{scalar saturation!symbols}
In the simplest form of the universal MC element
$\Theta_\cA^{\le 2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \eta \otimes \Lambda$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}), two geometric
objects appear:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\eta = d\log(z_1 - z_2)
  \in \Omega^1_{\log}(\overline{C}_2(X))$:
  the universal logarithmic propagator, the same form
  that defines the bar differential at genus~$0$.
\item $\Lambda = \lambda_1
  \in H^2(\overline{\cM}_{1,1}, \C)
  \cong \C$:
  the first Chern class of the Hodge line bundle on
  $\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$, i.e.\ the generator of
  $H^*(\overline{\cM}_{1,1})$.
  At genus~$g$, $\Lambda$ extends to the Hodge class
  $\lambda_g = c_g(\mathbb{E})$ of the Hodge bundle
  $\mathbb{E} = \pi_*\omega_\pi$
  (pushforward of the relative dualizing sheaf along the universal
  curve $\pi \colon \mathcal{C}_g \to \overline{\cM}_g$).
\end{itemize}
The product $\eta \otimes \Lambda$ lives in
$\Omega^1_{\log}(\overline{C}_2(X)) \otimes
H^*(\overline{\cM}_{1,1})$, the arity-$2$, genus-$1$
component of the modular convolution algebra.

\subsection{Homotopy transfer and derived categories}
\label{subsec:intro-homotopy-derived}

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Strong deformation retract.}
\index{strong deformation retract}
A \emph{strong deformation retract} (SDR) of a chain
complex~$M$ onto its cohomology~$H = H^*(M)$ is a
quintuple $(M, H, \pi, \iota, h)$ satisfying
(Definition~\ref{def:sdr}):
\begin{alignat}{3}
&\pi \colon M \to H, \quad
&&\iota \colon H \to M, \quad
&&h \colon M \to M[-1], \notag\\
&\pi \circ \iota = \mathrm{id}_H, \quad
&&\iota \circ \pi - \mathrm{id}_M = dh + hd,
\label{eq:intro-sdr}
\end{alignat}
with side conditions
$h^2 = 0$, $h \circ \iota = 0$, $\pi \circ h = 0$.
The \emph{harmonic projection} $P := \iota \circ \pi$ projects
onto the image of cohomology; the
\emph{contracting homotopy}~$h$ (the propagator) inverts
$d$ up to chain homotopy.  Homological perturbation
theory transfers algebraic structures ($A_\infty$,
$L_\infty$, brackets, products) from~$M$ to~$H$ along the SDR:
transferred operations are graph sums whose vertices are
original operations and whose internal edges are~$h$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Factorization algebra.}
\index{factorization algebra!definition}
A \emph{factorization algebra}~$\mathcal{F}$ on a curve~$X$
assigns to each finite subset $S \subset X$ a chain
complex $\mathcal{F}_S$ with
\emph{factorization isomorphisms}
(Definition~\ref{def:fact-algebra-correct}):
\[
\mu_{S_1,S_2} \colon
\mathcal{F}_{S_1} \otimes \mathcal{F}_{S_2}
\;\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}\; \mathcal{F}_{S_1 \sqcup S_2}
\]
for disjoint finite subsets
$S_1, S_2 \subset X$, satisfying associativity,
commutativity, and unitality.
\emph{Factorization homology} $\int_X \cA$ is the
global sections of the associated constructible cosheaf on
$\mathrm{Ran}(X)$: the derived tensor product of
all fibres of~$\mathcal{F}$ as points collide.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Coderived category.}
\index{coderived category!definition}
For curved dg coalgebras ($d^2 = m_0 \neq 0$),
ordinary cohomology can vanish while nontrivial coderived invariants survive.  The
\emph{coderived category}
$D^{\mathrm{co}}(\mathcal{C})$
(Positselski~\cite{Positselski11})
replaces acyclic complexes by the smaller class of
\emph{coacyclic} complexes (totalizations of
short exact sequences of curved dg comodules),
preserving curved objects that ordinary cohomology destroys.
The bar-cobar adjunction
$\barB \dashv \Omega$ descends to
an equivalence on the coderived category
(Definition~\ref{def:provisional-coderived}).
At genus $g \ge 1$, curvature
$m_0 = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \omega_g \neq 0$ forces passage
to $D^{\mathrm{co}}$ rather than~$D^b$.

\section{The master object}
\label{sec:convolution-algebra}
\index{convolution dg Lie algebra|textbf}
\index{shadow obstruction tower|textbf}
\index{Maurer--Cartan!universal class}

\subsection{The modular convolution dg Lie algebra}
\label{subsec:modular-convolution-dg-lie}
\index{convolution dg Lie algebra!construction|textbf}

The genus-graded bar complex $\{\barB^{(g)}(\cA)\}_{g \ge 0}$ is an algebra over the Feynman transform $\mathrm{FCom}$ of the commutative modular operad (Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-modular-operad}).  The \emph{modular convolution dg Lie algebra}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:convolution-dg-lie-intro}
\gAmod
\;:=\;
\prod_{g,n}
\Hom_{\Sigma_n}\bigl(
  C_*(\overline{\cM}_{g,n}),\;
  \operatorname{End}_\cA(n)
\bigr)
\end{equation}
is the equivariant internal hom in $\mathbb{S}$-modules
(\S\ref{subsec:intro-operadic};
Definition~\ref{def:modular-convolution-dg-lie}),
where $C_*(\overline{\cM}_{g,n})$ denotes the singular
chain complex of the Deligne--Mumford moduli space
(rational coefficients) and
$\operatorname{End}_\cA(n) = \Hom(\cA^{\otimes n}, \cA)$
the endomorphism operad~\eqref{eq:intro-end}.
An element $\alpha \in \gAmod$ assigns to each stable
graph~$\Gamma$ of type $(g,n)$
(\S\ref{subsec:intro-config-comb}) an
$\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)$-equivariant operation
$\alpha_\Gamma \colon \barB(\cA)^{\otimes n}
\to \barB(\cA)$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Differential.}
The differential on $\gAmod$ has five components,
one per boundary stratum of
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:convolution-dg-lie-structure}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:five-component-diff}
D = d_{\mathrm{int}}
  + [\tau, -]
  + d_{\mathrm{sew}}
  + d_{\mathrm{pf}}
  + \hbar\Delta.
\end{equation}
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item $d_{\mathrm{int}}(\alpha)_\Gamma
  = d_\cA \circ \alpha_\Gamma
  - (-1)^{|\alpha|}\, \alpha_\Gamma \circ d_\cA$:
  the internal differential of~$\cA$, applied vertexwise.
\item $[\tau,-](\alpha)_\Gamma
  = \sum_{e \in E(\Gamma)} \pm\,
  (\tau \circ_e \alpha)_{\Gamma/e}$:
  twisting by the universal propagator
  $\tau = \eta_{12} = d\log(z_1-z_2) \in \Omega^1_{\log}(\overline{C}_2(X))$.
  Each edge~$e$ of $\Gamma$ inserts~$\tau$ and contracts.
\item $d_{\mathrm{sew}}(\alpha)_\Gamma
  = \sum \alpha_{\Gamma_1} \circ_i \alpha_{\Gamma_2}$,
  summed over separating-edge decompositions
  $\Gamma = \Gamma_1 \cup_e \Gamma_2$
  (clutching
  $\xi_{\mathrm{sep}} \colon
  \overline{\cM}_{g_1,n_1+1} \times
  \overline{\cM}_{g_2,n_2+1}
  \to \overline{\cM}_{g,n}$).
\item $d_{\mathrm{pf}}(\alpha)$:
  the planted-forest correction from Fulton--MacPherson
  codimension-$2$ boundary, encoding the failure of
  Arnold-type relations at higher genus.
\item $\hbar\Delta(\alpha)_\Gamma$:
  the non-separating (BV) operator, arising from the
  clutching map
  $\xi_{\mathrm{ns}} \colon
  \overline{\cM}_{g,n+2} \to
  \overline{\cM}_{g+1,n}$
  that glues two marked points on the same component
  and contracts with the invariant pairing
  $\langle -, - \rangle_\cA$.
\end{enumerate}

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Lie bracket.}
The Lie bracket is the convolution bracket of
\S\ref{subsec:intro-twisting}, lifted to the
modular operad:
\[
[\alpha, \beta]_\Gamma
= \sum_{\Gamma = \Gamma_1 \cup_e \Gamma_2}
  \alpha_{\Gamma_1} \circ_e \beta_{\Gamma_2}
  - (-1)^{|\alpha||\beta|}\,
  \beta_{\Gamma_1} \circ_e \alpha_{\Gamma_2},
\]
summed over all ways to decompose $\Gamma$ into
two subgraphs joined by a separating edge~$e$;
the operation $\circ_e$ composes the outputs and inputs
at the half-edges of~$e$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Why $D^2 = 0$.}
The identity $D^2 = 0$ is the
codimension-$2$ boundary cancellation $\partial^2 = 0$
on~$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$, transported through
the $\Hom$ functor
(Lemma~\ref{lem:stable-graph-d-squared};
at the ambient level,
Theorem~\ref{thm:ambient-d-squared-zero}
via~\cite[Theorem~3.3.1(1)]{Mok25}).

\subsection{The universal Maurer--Cartan element}
\label{subsec:feynman-transform-motivation}
\index{Feynman transform!conceptual motivation}
\index{Chriss--Ginzburg principle!Feynman transform}

The genus-completed bar differential decomposes as $D_\cA = d_\cA^{(0)} + \sum_{g \ge 1} \hbar^g\, d_\cA^{(g)}$.  Define
\begin{equation}\label{eq:universal-MC}
\Theta_\cA
\;:=\;
D_\cA - d_\cA^{(0)}
\;=\;
\sum_{g \geq 1} \hbar^g\, d_\cA^{(g)}
\;\in\;
\gAmod.
\end{equation}
Since $D_\cA^2 = 0$ and $(d_\cA^{(0)})^2 = 0$ (Arnold relation at genus~$0$), expanding $D_\cA^2 = (d_\cA^{(0)} + \Theta_\cA)^2 = 0$ gives
\[
d_\cA^{(0)}\,\Theta_\cA + \Theta_\cA\, d_\cA^{(0)} + \Theta_\cA^2 = 0,
\]
which is the Maurer--Cartan equation
\[
D\,\Theta_\cA + \tfrac{1}{2}[\Theta_\cA, \Theta_\cA] = 0
\]
in~$\gAmod$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}).  No construction is required: $\Theta_\cA$ is bar-intrinsic.  The positive-genus part of the bar differential \emph{is} a Maurer--Cartan element.

\subsection{The five theorems and the MC element}
\label{subsec:def-cyc-mod-intro}
\index{modular cyclic deformation complex|textbf}
\index{Chriss--Ginzburg principle!projection table}

with respect to the MC element
$\Theta_\cA \in \operatorname{MC}(\gAmod)$:

\begin{theorem}[Completeness of the MC element;
  \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:mc-completeness}
\index{MC element!completeness|textbf}
Let\/ $\cA$ be a modular Koszul chiral algebra on a smooth
projective curve~$X$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Existence.}
  The MC element\/ $\Theta_\cA := D_\cA - d_\cA^{(0)}$
  exists unconditionally: it is MC because $D_\cA^2 = 0$
  \textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic})}.
  No construction beyond the bar differential is required.

\item \emph{Completeness.}
  On the Koszul locus, $\Theta_\cA$ is a complete invariant
  of\/~$\cA$: the bar-cobar counit
  $\Omega_X(\barB_X(\cA)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \cA$ is a
  quasi-isomorphism
  \textup{(}Theorem~B,
  Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-cobar-inversion-qi})},
  so $\cA$ is recoverable from $\barB_X(\cA)$ and hence
  from~$\Theta_\cA$.

\item \emph{Structure.}
  The structural content of\/ $\Theta_\cA$ decomposes into
  three proved projections:
  the Verdier splitting into complementary Lagrangians
  $Q_g(\cA) \oplus Q_g(\cA^!)$
  \textup{(}Theorem~C\textup{)};
  the scalar modular characteristic $\kappa(\cA)$
  governing $\mathrm{obs}_g = \kappa\lambda_g$ on the
  uniform-weight lane and the
  $\hat{A}$-genus generating function
  \textup{(}Theorem~D\textup{)};
  and the polynomial Hochschild ring $\ChirHoch^*(\cA)$
  \textup{(}Theorem~H\textup{)}.
\end{enumerate}

Theorem~A provides the categorical framework:
the bar-cobar adjunction $\barB_X \dashv \Omega_X$ and

$\Theta_\cA$ and its projections live
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main})}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Item~(i) is Theorem~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic}: $D_\cA^2 = 0$
implies the MC equation for $\Theta_\cA = D_\cA - d_\cA^{(0)}$
by direct expansion.
Item~(ii) is Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-inversion-qi}: the
PBW spectral sequence collapse and Barr--Beck--Lurie comparison
prove the counit is a quasi-isomorphism on the Koszul locus, so
$\barB_X(\cA)$, and therefore the MC element
$\Theta_\cA$ extracted from its differential, faithfully
determines~$\cA$.
Item~(iii) is the conjunction of
Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main} (Theorem~C,
complementarity),
Theorem~\ref{thm:modular-characteristic} (Theorem~D, modular
characteristic), and
Theorem~\ref{thm:main-koszul-hoch} (Theorem~H, Hochschild
polynomial growth), each extracting a different component
of~$\Theta_\cA$.
\end{proof}

single element~$\Theta_\cA$.

\subsection{The modular cyclic deformation complex and coefficient algebras}
\label{subsec:coefficient-algebras-intro}
\index{stable-graph coefficient algebra|textbf}
\index{planted-forest coefficient algebra|textbf}

The Maurer--Cartan element $\Theta_\cA$ lives in the
\emph{modular cyclic deformation complex}
(Definition~\ref{def:modular-cyclic-deformation-complex}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:defcyc-intro}
\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)
\;:=\;
\prod_{\substack{g,n \\ 2g-2+n > 0}}
\operatorname{CoDer}^{\mathrm{cyc}}\!\bigl(
  \barB^{(g,n)}(\cA)
\bigr)[1],
\end{equation}
where $\operatorname{CoDer}^{\mathrm{cyc}}$ denotes
\emph{cyclic coderivations}: coderivations of
$\barB^{(g,n)}(\cA)$ that respect the pairing
$\langle -, -\rangle_\cA$ on~$\cA$ (i.e.\ the coderivation
$\xi$ satisfies $\langle \xi(a), b \rangle
= \pm\langle a, \xi(b) \rangle$).
The shift~$[1]$ is cohomological, making the Lie
bracket degree~$0$.  The genus-$0$ truncation recovers
the ordinary cyclic deformation complex:
$\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)|_{g=0}
= \Defcyc(\cA)$.

The MC element takes values in a completed tensor
product with a \emph{coefficient algebra}:
$\Theta_\cA \in
\MC\!\bigl(\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)
\;\widehat{\otimes}\; \Gmod\bigr)$,
where $\widehat{\otimes}$
is completion with respect to the genus filtration
$F^{\ge g}$.

Two coefficient algebras control the combinatorics, both completed commutative dg algebras.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Stable-graph coefficient algebra.}
\index{stable-graph coefficient algebra!generators}
The \emph{stable-graph coefficient algebra}
(Definition~\ref{def:stable-graph-coefficient-algebra})
is the completed dg algebra
\[
\Gmod_{\mathrm{st}}
\;:=\;
\prod_{\Gamma \in \mathsf{Gr}_{\mathrm{st}}}
\bQ \cdot e_\Gamma \Big/ \mathrm{Aut}(\Gamma),
\]
with \emph{generators}: basis vectors $e_\Gamma$,
one for each isomorphism class of connected stable
graph~$\Gamma$;
\emph{product}: disjoint union of graphs,
$e_{\Gamma_1} \cdot e_{\Gamma_2}
= e_{\Gamma_1 \sqcup \Gamma_2}$;
and \emph{differential}: edge contraction,
$d(e_\Gamma) = \sum_{e \in E(\Gamma)} e_{\Gamma/e}$,
where $\Gamma/e$ is the graph obtained by contracting
edge~$e$.  This models the boundary stratification of
$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$: boundary strata are
indexed by stable graphs, and the boundary operator
on $C_*(\overline{\cM}_{g,n})$ induces edge contraction.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Planted-forest coefficient algebra.}
\index{planted-forest coefficient algebra!generators}
The \emph{planted-forest coefficient algebra}
(Definition~\ref{def:planted-forest-coefficient-algebra})
is the completed dg algebra
\[
\Gmod_{\mathrm{pf}}
\;:=\;
\prod_{F \in \mathsf{For}_{\mathrm{plant}}}
\bQ \cdot e_F \Big/ \mathrm{Aut}(F),
\]
with \emph{generators}: planted forests
$F$ (forests of rooted trees where each vertex carries
a non-negative weight);
\emph{product}: disjoint union of forests;
and \emph{differential}: internal edge contraction
with Koszul sign,
$d_{\mathrm{pf}}(e_F) = \sum_{e \in E(F)} \pm\, e_{F/e}$.
Each planted forest indexes a boundary stratum of the
Fulton--MacPherson compactification
$\overline{C}_n(X)$: roots correspond to first-order
collision points, interior vertices to higher-order
nested collisions.  By the tropicalization theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:planted-forest-tropicalization}),
$\Gmod_{\mathrm{pf}}
= \operatorname{Trop}(\mathrm{FM}_n(\mathbb{C} | D))$:
planted forests are dual graphs of tropical curves under
Mok's logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson
compactification~\cite{Mok25}.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Differential decomposition.}
The differential on $\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$
decomposes as
$D_{\mathrm{cyc}} = D_{\mathrm{edge}} + D_{\mathrm{vertex}}$,
where $D_{\mathrm{edge}}$ contracts edges (applying
the gluing maps of $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$) and
$D_{\mathrm{vertex}}$ splits vertices (applying the
degenerations of the modular operad).
The identity $D_{\mathrm{cyc}}^2 = 0$ follows from
$\partial^2 = 0$ on~$\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.

\subsection{The shadow algebra and the shadow obstruction tower}
\label{subsec:shadow-algebra-intro}
\label{subsec:shadow-postnikov-tower-intro}
\index{shadow algebra|textbf}

The cohomology
\[
\cA^{\mathrm{sh}}
\;:=\;
H_\bullet\bigl(\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)\bigr)
\]
is the \emph{shadow algebra} (Definition~\ref{def:shadow-algebra}): the universal bigraded commutative ring ($\cA^{\mathrm{sh}}_{r,g}$ at arity~$r$ and genus~$g$) for finite-order modular invariants of~$\cA$.  It factorizes under tensor product ($\cA^{\mathrm{sh}} \cong \cA_1^{\mathrm{sh}} \otimes \cA_2^{\mathrm{sh}}$) and collapses to $\mathbb{C}$ when~$\cA$ has a mass gap.

The finite-order projections of $\Theta_\cA$ form the \emph{shadow obstruction tower}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:shadow-tower-intro}
\Theta_\cA^{\le 2}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\Theta_\cA^{\le 3}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\Theta_\cA^{\le 4}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\cdots
\;\longrightarrow\;
\Theta_\cA
\;=\;
\varprojlim\, \Theta_\cA^{\le r}
\end{equation}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:recursive-existence}).  Each truncation $\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ is a graph sum through arity~$r$, with obstruction class $o_{r+1}(\cA) \in H^*(\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod},(r+1)}(\cA))$ measuring failure of extension.  The base is $\Theta_\cA^{\le 2} = \kappa(\cA) \cdot \eta \otimes \Lambda$; at arity~$r \ge 3$, extension solves $D_{\mathrm{cyc}}(\Theta_\cA^{(r)}) + \frac{1}{2}[\Theta_\cA^{\le r-1}, \Theta_\cA^{\le r-1}]^{(r)} = 0$, and $o_{r+1}$ is the residual obstruction.

The arity-$r$ projections extract named invariants:
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\textbf{Arity} & \textbf{Invariant} & \textbf{Status} \\ \hline
$r = 2$ & $\kappa(\cA)$ (scalar modular characteristic)
  & proved, all families \\[2pt]
$r = 3$ & $\mathfrak{C}(\cA)$ (cubic shadow)
  & proved, computed for standard landscape \\[2pt]
$r = 4$ & $\mathfrak{Q}(\cA)$ (quartic resonance class)
  & proved, with clutching law \\[2pt]
$r = \infty$ & $\Theta_\cA$ (full universal class)
  & proved (bar-intrinsic, Thm~\ref{thm:mc2-bar-intrinsic})
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The shadow obstruction tower classifies chiral algebras into four \emph{shadow archetypes}, according to the termination arity $r_{\max}(\cA) := \min\{r : o_{r+1}(\cA) = 0\}$:
\begin{center}
\small
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
\begin{tabular}{@{}ccll@{}}
\textbf{Class} & $r_{\max}$ & \textbf{Archetype} & \textbf{Families} \\ \hline
G & $2$ & Gaussian & Heisenberg, free fermion, lattice (abelian) \\
L & $3$ & Lie/tree & Affine Kac--Moody $\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k$, $V_k(\mathfrak{g})$ \\
C & $4$ & Contact/quartic & $\beta\gamma$, $bc$, symplectic bosons \\
M & $\infty$ & Mixed & Virasoro, $\mathcal{W}_N$, non-principal $\mathcal{W}$-algebras
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Shadow depth classifies the complexity of Koszul algebras, not Koszulness itself: all four classes contain Koszul algebras.  The depth is a modular-homotopy-type invariant (Corollary~\ref{cor:shadow-depth-koszul-invariance}: $r_{\max}(\cA^!) = r_{\max}(\cA)$).
The class assignment depends on \emph{generator count}, not statistics: both $\beta\gamma$ (bosonic) and $bc$ (fermionic) are class~C because each has two generators; the single-generator free fermion is class~G because fermionic antisymmetry on one generator kills all higher bar cohomology.

\begin{remark}[Shadow depth as functional-integral complexity]
\label{rem:shadow-archetypes-polyakov}
\index{shadow depth!functional-integral interpretation}
The effective worldsheet action of a chirally Koszul algebra~$\cA$ decomposes by shadow depth:
\[
S_{\mathrm{eff}}(\cA)
\;=\;
\underbrace{\kappa \cdot S_{\mathrm{Polyakov}}}_{r=2}
\;+\;
\underbrace{[\mathfrak{C}] \cdot S_{\mathrm{cubic}}}_{r=3}
\;+\;
\underbrace{[\mathfrak{Q}^{\mathrm{contact}}] \cdot S_{\mathrm{quartic}}}_{r=4}
\;+\;\cdots
\]
Class~$\mathbf{G}$ truncates at $r = 2$: the Polyakov action $S_{\mathrm{Polyakov}} = (1/(4\pi\alpha'))\int \sqrt{g}\,g^{ab}\partial_a X \partial_b X$ captures the full content, and $F_g = \kappa \cdot \lambda_g^{\mathrm{FP}}$ determines the genus expansion completely.  Each subsequent class adds one order of nonlinear OPE interaction: tree-level Lie bracket for~$\mathbf{L}$, one-loop contact for~$\mathbf{C}$, all orders for~$\mathbf{M}$.
\end{remark}

The quartic resonance class satisfies a clutching law under stable-curve degeneration (Theorem~\ref{thm:nms-clutching-law-modular-resonance}).  For Virasoro:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:quartic-contact-vir-intro}
Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}} = \frac{10}{c(5c+22)},
\qquad
\delta^{(1)}_{H,\mathrm{Vir}} = \frac{120}{c^2(5c+22)} \cdot x^2
\end{equation}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:nms-virasoro-quartic-explicit}).

The shadow obstruction tower, a priori an infinite sequence
of obstruction classes, is algebraic of
degree~$2$ on each \emph{primary line} (a
one-dimensional subspace of
$H^2_{\mathrm{cyc}}(\cA, \cA)$
spanned by a single quasi-primary;
Theorem~\ref{thm:riccati-algebraicity}).
A single quadratic form~$Q_L$, the \emph{shadow metric}
(Definition~\ref{def:shadow-metric}), controls the entire tower; its Gaussian
decomposition
\begin{equation}\label{eq:shadow-metric-intro}
Q_L(t) = (2\kappa + 3\alpha\,t)^2 + 2\Delta\,t^2
\end{equation}
separates the free-field contribution from the
interaction correction, where
$\alpha := S_3$ is the \emph{cubic shadow coefficient}
(the arity-$3$ projection of $\Theta_\cA$ evaluated
on the primary line) and
$\Delta := 8\kappa S_4$ is the \emph{critical
discriminant} ($S_4$ being the arity-$4$ shadow
coefficient).
The signature of $Q_L$ classifies the shadow depth:
$\Delta = 0$ gives a perfect square and a terminating
tower (classes~$\mathbf{G}$ and~$\mathbf{L}$);
$\Delta \neq 0$ gives an irreducible quadratic and an
infinite but algebraic tower (class~$\mathbf{M}$).
The contact class~$\mathbf{C}$ ($r_{\max}=4$) escapes
the single-line dichotomy by stratum separation: its
quartic contact invariant lives on a charged stratum
whose self-bracket exits the complex
(Theorem~\ref{thm:single-line-dichotomy}).
The shadow metric defines a logarithmic
connection~$\nabla^{\mathrm{sh}}$ with
residue~$\frac{1}{2}$ at the branch points and
monodromy~$-1$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-connection}).
Under Koszul duality, the critical discriminants satisfy
a complementarity relation with constant numerator:
\[
\Delta(\cA) \;+\; \Delta(\cA^!) \;=\; \frac{6960}{(5c+22)(152-5c)}.
\]  For multi-channel algebras (algebras with more than one
primary generator), the connection acquires curvature
equal to the \emph{propagator variance}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:propagator-variance-intro}
\delta_{\mathrm{mix}}
= \sum_i \frac{f_i^2}{\kappa_i}
  - \frac{(\sum_i f_i)^2}{\sum_i \kappa_i},
\end{equation}
a Cauchy--Schwarz gap (Proposition~\ref{thm:propagator-variance})
that vanishes if and only if the quartic gradient
is proportional to the curvature vector (enhanced symmetry).

\subsection{The Hamilton--Jacobi equation and inter-channel coupling}
\label{subsec:hamilton-jacobi-intro}
\index{Hamilton--Jacobi equation!shadow tower|textbf}
\index{inter-channel coupling!introduction}

On a multi-generator deformation space $X = H^2_{\mathrm{cyc}}(\cA, \cA)$ of dimension~$r \geq 2$, the algebraicity theorem lifts from the Riccati ODE to a \emph{Hamilton--Jacobi PDE}.  Write $U = \sum_{s \geq 3} \mathrm{Sh}_s$ for the nonlinear shadow.  The MC equation on~$X$ is equivalent to
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:hamilton-jacobi-shadow-intro}
  2\,E(U) \;+\; \tfrac{1}{2}\lVert\nabla U\rVert^2_H
  \;=\;
  R,
\end{equation}
where $x_i$ are coordinates on the deformation space
$X = H^2_{\mathrm{cyc}}(\cA, \cA)$ (one coordinate per
primary generator of~$\cA$),
$E = \sum_i x_i \partial_i$ is the Euler operator
(counting arity),
$\lVert\nabla U\rVert^2_H = \sum_i P_i\,(\partial_i U)^2$
is the kinetic energy in the \emph{inverse-Hessian metric}
$H_{ij} = \operatorname{diag}(P_1, \ldots, P_r)$
(where $P_i = 1/\kappa_i$ is the reciprocal of the
channel curvature),
and $R$ is a polynomial of degree~$\leq 4$ encoding the
cubic and quartic OPE inputs
(Remark~\ref{rem:riccati-ode}).  On a $1$-D subline, $E(U) = rU$ reduces~\eqref{eq:hamilton-jacobi-shadow-intro} to the Riccati ODE $2tU' + (P/2)(U')^2 = R(t)$.  On the full multi-dimensional space,~\eqref{eq:hamilton-jacobi-shadow-intro} is a classical Hamilton--Jacobi equation with Hamiltonian
\[
  \mathcal{H}(x, p)
  \;=\;
  \tfrac{1}{2}\sum_i P_i\,p_i^2 \;+\; V(x),
\]
where $p_i = \partial U/\partial x_i$ are shadow momenta and $V$ is the OPE potential.  The shadow obstruction tower is the Hamilton principal function: its graph $\{(x, \nabla U(x))\}$ is a Lagrangian submanifold of~$T^*(X)$.  The genus expansion $\sum_{g \geq 1} F_g(\cA)\,\hbar^g$ formally resembles a WKB series with $U$ as classical action, but the quantised Hamiltonian needed to make this precise has not been constructed (Remark~\ref{rem:shadow-hamiltonian}).

The Hamilton--Jacobi structure explains the \emph{inter-channel coupling} on sublines (Proposition~\ref{prop:interchannel-coupling}).  On a subline $L \subset X$, the restriction $U|_L$ satisfies the $1$-D Riccati equation \emph{plus} a correction from the transverse kinetic energy $(P_\perp/2)\,(\partial_\perp U)^2|_L$: the ``transverse momentum'' $p_\perp = (\partial_\perp U)|_L$ need not vanish.  For $\mathcal{W}_3$ on the $T$-line ($x_W = 0$), conformal weight forces $p_W|_{x_W = 0} = 0$: autonomy.  On the $W$-line ($x_T = 0$), the cubic shadow $\mathrm{Sh}_3 = 2x_T^3 + 3x_T x_W^2$ has nonzero normal derivative $(\partial_{x_T}\mathrm{Sh}_3)|_{x_T=0} = 3x_W^2$: the transverse kinetic energy is nonzero, and the coupling correction first enters at arity~$6$:
\[
  \delta_6^{W\text{-line}}
  \;=\;
  \frac{576(5c+38)}{c^3(5c+22)^3}.
\]
The autonomous $W$-line recursion misses this.  The coupling correction vanishes on any direction where the propagator variance $\delta_{\mathrm{mix}} = 0$.

For the shadow generating function on each primary line, the pole-purity theorem (Proposition~\ref{prop:pole-purity}) gives
$S_r = \varepsilon_r P_r(c) / [c^{r-3}(5c{+}22)^{\lfloor(r-2)/2\rfloor}]$
for $r \geq 4$, where $\varepsilon_r \in \{+1, -1\}$ is a
sign and $P_r \in \mathbb{Z}[c]$ a polynomial of
degree $\lfloor(r-4)/2\rfloor$, with no factors beyond $c$
and $5c{+}22$ in the denominator at any arity, a direct
consequence of the Riccati algebraicity and the
Kac-determinant structure of the quasi-primary spectrum.

\subsection{The all-arity master equation}
\label{subsec:all-arity-master-eq}

The arity-$r$ projection of the MC equation is the \emph{all-arity master equation}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:all-arity-master-eq-intro}
\nabla_H(\mathrm{Sh}_r) + o^{(r)} = 0 \qquad \text{for all } r \geq 3,
\end{equation}
where $\nabla_H$ is the covariant derivative along the
Hodge class direction in
$\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(\cA)$ (it shifts genus by~$+1$
via the non-separating clutching map $\xi_{\mathrm{ns}}$),
$\mathrm{Sh}_r$ is the shadow at arity~$r$
(the degree-$r$ component of $\Theta_\cA$ in the
shadow algebra~$\cA^{\mathrm{sh}}$),
and $o^{(r)}$ is the obstruction class in
$H^*(\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod},(r+1)}(\cA))$.
The \emph{genus loop}
$\Lambda_P \colon \operatorname{Sym}^r \to
\operatorname{Sym}^{r-2}$ is the trace contraction
operator $\Lambda_P(f)(x_1, \ldots, x_{r-2})
= \sum_{i,j} P^{ij}\,
f(x_1, \ldots, x_{r-2}, e_i, e_j)$
(where $P^{ij}$ is the inverse Hessian and
$\{e_i\}$ a basis of generators):
it governs descent from arity~$r$ to arity~$r-2$,

By cofree-coderivation correspondence, $\Theta_\cA = \operatorname{FT}^{\log}_{\mathrm{mod}}(\mathfrak{K}_\cA)$, and the fundamental equivalence

\quad\iff\quad
d\,\mathfrak{K}_\cA + \mathfrak{K}_\cA \star \mathfrak{K}_\cA + \hbar\,\Delta_{\mathrm{ns}}(\mathfrak{K}_\cA) = 0
\]
(Theorem~\ref{thm:primitive-to-global-reconstruction}), where $\star$ is the primitive pre-Lie product from log-FM residues and $\Delta_{\mathrm{ns}}$ is the non-separating loop operator.  The three terms replace the genus-by-genus obstruction programme by a single equation on primitive data.

\section{The convolution architecture}
\label{sec:intro-convolution}

Every algebraic structure in this monograph is a Maurer--Cartan element in a convolution dg Lie algebra.  The bar complex is the analogue of the Steinberg variety; $\Theta_\cA$ is the analogue of the fundamental class; its projections recover all convolution products.

\subsection{The convolution ladder}

At three levels of generality, the bar-type construction is a coefficient of a convolution dg Lie algebra:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-convolution-ladder}
\begin{array}{r@{\;:\quad}c@{\qquad}l}
\text{algebra} &
  \Hom\bigl(B(\mathsf{Ass}),\,\End_A\bigr)
  & A_\infty\text{-structures on }A \\[4pt]
\text{operad} &
  \Hom_\Sigma\bigl(B(\cP),\,\End_V\bigr)
  & \cP_\infty\text{-structures on }V \\[4pt]
\text{modular} &
  \Hom_\Sigma\bigl(F\cO,\,\End_{\barB(\cA)}\bigr)
  & \text{consistent genus towers}
\end{array}
\end{equation}
The first line is the Hochschild cochain complex; the second is the deformation complex (Ginzburg--Kapranov~\cite{GK94}); the third, with $\cO = \mathsf{Com}$, governs this book.

\subsection{The modular operad and Feynman transform}

A \emph{modular operad}~$\cO$ (Getzler--Kapranov~\cite{GeK98}) has operations indexed by connected stable graphs~$\Gamma$ of arbitrary genus, with structure maps from separating and non-separating gluing of boundary divisors of $\overline{\cM}_{g,n}$.  Trees are the genus-$0$ case.

The \emph{Feynman transform} replaces trees by stable graphs:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-feynman-sum}
F\cO(g,n)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}}
\frac{\hbar^{g(\Gamma)}}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\;\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}\cO\bigl(g(v),\mathrm{In}(v)\bigr)
\;\otimes\;\det\bigl(E(\Gamma)\bigr).
\end{equation}
The differential contracts one edge; $d^2 = 0$ is the codimension-$2$ boundary cancellation on the moduli space.  At genus~$0$: the operadic bar construction.

When $\cO = \mathsf{Com}$ (the commutative modular operad), each vertex factor is one-dimensional, and the Feynman transform becomes the stable graph complex $F\!\mathsf{Com}$, a chain complex spanned by stable graphs with edge-contraction differential.  The bar complex $\barB_X(\cA)$ is an $F\!\mathsf{Com}$-algebra, and the modular convolution dg Lie algebra
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-gmod}
\mathfrak{g}^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA
\;:=\;
\Hom_\Sigma\bigl(F\!\mathsf{Com},\;\End_{\barB(\cA)}\bigr)
\end{equation}
is the ambient dg Lie algebra of $\Theta_\cA$.

The \emph{modular bar construction}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-modular-bar}
\barB^{(g)}_X(\cA)
\;=\;
\bigoplus_{\Gamma\in\mathsf{Gr}^{\mathrm{st}}_{g,n}}
\frac{1}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\;W_\Gamma\;\otimes\;
\bigotimes_{v\in V(\Gamma)}\Phi_v^\cA\,.
\end{equation}

Replacing $\mathsf{Com}$ by $\mathsf{Ass}$ equips each vertex with a cyclic ordering, producing the ribbon graph complex~$F\!\mathsf{Ass}$.  This is the algebraic form of the 't~Hooft $1/N$ expansion.

\subsection{Three formative preprints}

Three preprints require foundational upgrades:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*),nosep]
\item \emph{Malikov--Schechtman~\cite{MS24}}: the primitive local object is a \emph{homotopy chiral algebra} ($\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$), not strict.  The strict chiral algebra appears after rectification.
\item \emph{Robert-Nicoud--Wierstra~\cite{RNW19} $+$ Vallette~\cite{Val16}}: the universal deformation machine is the filtered convolution $s\mathsf{L}_\infty$-algebra $\hom_\alpha(\cC, \cA)$, functorial in either slot separately but \emph{not both simultaneously} (the no-bifunctor obstruction, \cite[Theorem~6.1]{RNW19}).
\item \emph{Mok~\cite{Mok25}}: the global collision geometry is the log FM compactification $\FM_n(X|D)$ on snc pairs, with tropicalisation $\mathrm{Trop}(\FM_n(C|D))$ recovering planted forests as combinatorial shadows.
\end{enumerate}
Nine of the eleven identification theorems connecting these pillars to the bar construction are proved; the remaining two are structural.

\subsection{Completion}

The strong completion-tower theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:completed-bar-cobar-strong}): the strong filtration axiom $\mu_r(F^{i_1}, \ldots, F^{i_r}) \subset F^{i_1 + \cdots + i_r}$ forces an arity cutoff (Lemma~\ref{lem:arity-cutoff}) making continuity and Mittag--Leffler automatic, so finite-stage bar-cobar passes to inverse limits.  The completion closure $\CompCl(\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{ft}})$ carries a quasi-inverse bar-cobar homotopy equivalence (Corollary~\ref{cor:completion-closure-equivalence}), stable under MC twisting (Theorem~\ref{thm:mc-twisting-closure}).

The MC4 splitting: MC4${}^+$ (positive towers, including $\cW_{1+\infty}$, affine Yangians, and RTT, solved by weight stabilisation) vs.\ MC4${}^0$ (resonant towers, including Virasoro and non-quadratic $\cW_N$, reduced to finite resonance by Theorem~\ref{thm:resonance-filtered-bar-cobar}).  The resonance rank~$\rho(\cA)$ classifies completion difficulty.  The resonance completion theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:platonic-completion}): the positive-energy axiom gives finite-dimensional weight spaces, so weight-compatible SDR and finiteness of the weight-zero resonance space force $\rho(\cA) < \infty$.

\begin{proposition}[Classification by modular homotopy type;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:modular-homotopy-classification}
\index{modular homotopy type!classification}
Two chiral algebras~$\cA$ and~$\cA'$ on~$X$ have equivalent
modular homotopy types, i.e., the formal moduli problems
$\cM^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA \simeq \cM^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\cA'}$, if
and only if their modular bar constructions are connected by a zigzag
of quasi-isomorphisms of modular-operad coalgebras.  The modular
homotopy type is the finest invariant visible to the bar
construction, coarser than isomorphism: it remembers
the deformation-obstruction pattern and forgets
multiplication tables.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By the Lurie--Pridham correspondence between $L_\infty$-algebras
and formal moduli problems
(cf.~\cite[Theorem~2.0.2]{Pridham17}), an equivalence
$\cM^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA \simeq \cM^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\cA'}$
is equivalent to a quasi-isomorphism
$\gAmod[\cA] \xrightarrow{\sim} \gAmod[\cA']$ of
$L_\infty$-algebras.  By the convolution identification
$\gAmod = \Convinf(\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}},\,
\operatorname{End}_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}(\cA))$, a
quasi-isomorphism of convolution algebras is induced by a
quasi-isomorphism of the modular bar constructions
$\barB^{\mathrm{mod}}_X(\cA) \xrightarrow{\sim}
\barB^{\mathrm{mod}}_X(\cA')$ as modular-operad coalgebras
(since the cooperad slot is common).  The converse holds by the
representability of twisting morphisms
(Theorem~\ref{thm:completed-twisting-representability} in the
completed regime).
\end{proof}

\begin{example}[Modular homotopy type distinguishes families]
\label{ex:modular-type-distinguishes}
The Heisenberg algebra $\cH_k$ and the affine algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2{}_k$ have the same underlying graded
vector space $\bigoplus_{n \ge 1} \Bbbk\cdot\partial^{n-1}\alpha$
(respectively $\bigoplus_{n \ge 1} \Bbbk^3\cdot\partial^{n-1}J^a$)
in the same conformal weight range.  At genus~$0$, the Heisenberg
bar complex has $d_B = 0$ (the bracket is abelian); the
$\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$ bar complex has
$d_B(sJ^a | sJ^b) = f^{ab}{}_c\,sJ^c$ (the Lie bracket).  The
$A_\infty$-minimal model of the Heisenberg has $m_n = 0$ for all
$n \ge 2$; the minimal model of $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2$ has
$m_2 = $ the Lie bracket and $m_3 = $ the Jacobiator homotopy $F_3$
on $\overline{\cM}_{0,4} \cong \mathbb{P}^1$.

The modular homotopy types are therefore inequivalent:
$\gAmod[\cH_k]$ has $\ell_n = 0$ for $n \ge 3$
(Gaussian archetype, shadow depth~$2$), while
$\gAmod[\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2{}_k]$ has
$\ell_3 \ne 0$ (Lie/tree archetype, shadow depth~$3$).  The cubic
shadow $\mathfrak{C}(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2{}_k)$, the
arity-$3$ component of $\Theta_\cA$, is the graph sum over
trivalent trees, encoding the Lie bracket's contribution to the
modular bar construction.  No quasi-isomorphism of modular bar
coalgebras can kill it, because it represents a nontrivial class in
$H^*(\Defcyc^{(3)}(\cA)|_{g=0})$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-massey-identification}).

Shadow depth is therefore a modular-homotopy-type invariant.  More
precisely: the $L_\infty$-minimal model of $\gAmod$ has
$\ell_r^{\mathrm{min}} = 0$ for $r < $ shadow depth and
$\ell_r^{\mathrm{min}} \ne 0$ at $r = $ shadow depth; this cannot
change under quasi-isomorphism.
\end{example}

\begin{proposition}[Genus-$0$ shadow obstructions $=$ $A_\infty$
Massey products; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:shadow-massey-identification}
\index{Sullivan dictionary|textbf}
\index{Massey product!shadow obstruction identification}
\index{shadow obstruction!as Massey product}
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on~$X$ with bar
complex~$\barB(\cA)$, and let $(H^*(\barB(\cA)), m_2, m_3,
m_4, \ldots)$ be the $A_\infty$-minimal model obtained by
homotopy transfer
\textup{(}Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:ainfty-formality-implies-koszul}}\textup{)}.
Then the genus-$0$ shadow obstructions and the $A_\infty$-Massey
products coincide:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:shadow-massey}
o_{r+1}(\cA)\big|_{g=0}
\;=\;
[m_{r+1}]
\;\in\;
H^*\bigl(\Defcyc^{(r+1)}(\cA)\big|_{g=0}\bigr)
\qquad \text{for all } r \ge 2.
\end{equation}
In particular:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item $m_n = 0$ for all $n \ge 3$ if and only if the genus-$0$
  shadow obstruction tower terminates at arity~$2$, the Gaussian archetype.
\item The shadow depth of~$\cA$ at genus~$0$ equals the
  $A_\infty$-depth of the bar cohomology: the smallest~$r$ such
  that $m_r \ne 0$ equals the smallest arity at which the shadow
  tower has a nontrivial obstruction.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The $A_\infty$-products $m_n$ on $H^*(\barB(\cA))$ are the
Taylor coefficients of the transferred twisting morphism
$\tau^{\mathrm{tr}}\colon H^*(\barB(\cA)) \to \cA$ through the
deformation retract $(\barB(\cA), H^*(\barB(\cA)), i, p, h)$.
The genus-$0$ shadow obstructions $o_{r+1}|_{g=0}$ are the
obstructions to extending the truncated MC element
$\Theta_\cA^{\le r}|_{g=0}$ by one arity in the genus-$0$
sector of $\Defcyc(\cA)$.  But the genus-$0$ sector of the
modular convolution algebra is the \emph{operadic} convolution
algebra $\mathrm{Hom}(\barB_{\mathrm{op}}(\mathrm{Com}),
\mathrm{End}_\cA)$, and the MC equation there is exactly the
$A_\infty$-structure equation.  The identification~\eqref{eq:shadow-massey}
follows from the standard comparison between the operadic
bar-cobar obstruction tower and the $A_\infty$-Massey product
tower (cf.~\cite[Theorem~3.4.1]{LPWZ09}).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The Sullivan dictionary]
\label{rem:sullivan-dictionary}
\index{rational homotopy type!modular analogue}
The identification of Proposition~\ref{prop:shadow-massey-identification}

Definition~\ref{def:modular-homotopy-theory-intro}:

moduli, obstruction tower, formality criterion) is unchanged.

The dictionary extends to arithmetic content.  For lattice vertex
algebras, the shadow obstruction tower detects the Hecke eigenform decomposition
of the theta function: each arity resolves one
Hecke eigenspace, and the depth counts them
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-spectral-correspondence}).  The depth
decomposition $d = 1 + d_{\mathrm{arith}} + d_{\mathrm{alg}}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:depth-decomposition}) separates the
\emph{arithmetic} invariant $d_{\mathrm{arith}}$ (Hecke eigenform
count, visible through $L$-functions and critical lines) from the
\emph{homotopy-theoretic} invariant $d_{\mathrm{alg}}$ (formal
non-formality depth, visible through Massey products and
$A_\infty$ structure).  For lattice algebras:
$d_{\mathrm{alg}} = 0$ (the shadow obstruction tower is purely arithmetic).
For Virasoro minimal models: $d_{\mathrm{arith}}$ is finite but
$d_{\mathrm{alg}} = \infty$ (the shadow obstruction tower detects the
\emph{algebra itself}, the self-referential OPE $T\in T_{(1)}T$, not
its representation theory).  The shadow obstruction tower is the
universal invariant that encodes both the Langlands programme
(arithmetic) and the Sullivan programme (homotopy theory) in a
single Maurer--Cartan element~$\Theta_\cA$.

The arithmetic content extends beyond the shadow obstruction tower.
The connected sewing amplitudes $a_\cA(N)$ define a
\emph{sewing inner product} on Dirichlet polynomials
(\S\ref{subsec:sewing-gram-matrix}), whose reproducing
kernel is the \emph{sewing kernel}
$K_\cA(s,t) = S_\cA(s{+}t) =
\zeta(s{+}t{+}1)\sum_i(\zeta(s{+}t) - H_{w_i-1}(s{+}t))$.
For Heisenberg, $K_\cH = \zeta\cdot\zeta$: a pure Euler
product.  For Virasoro,
$K_{\mathrm{Vir}} = \zeta(u{+}1)(\zeta(u){-}1)$: the
weight-$1$ mode is removed.
The surface moment matrix $\mathscr{M}_{ij} =
K_\cA(\alpha/2{+}i,\,\alpha/2{+}j)$ is a Gram matrix
$V^T\!DV$ with positive weights, hence
$\mathscr{M} \succ 0$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:gram-positivity}).
The shadow obstruction tower provides interacting corrections to this
kernel.  When all spectral atoms $\lambda_j$ of the shadow
spectral measure are negative (which holds for all unitary
theories with $c > 0$), the interacting Gram matrix is
unconditionally positive definite
(Theorem~\ref{thm:interacting-gram-positivity}(i));
the shadow resonance locus is confined to
non-unitary $c < 0$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-resonance-locus}).
The Euler--Koszul class
$\operatorname{ek}(\cA) = \max_i(w_i - 1)$
and shadow depth $\kappa_d(\cA)$ are independent invariants
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-euler-independence}):
the first reads the character, the second the OPE.

The \emph{Sewing--Hecke reciprocity} theorem
(Theorem~\ref{thm:sewing-hecke-reciprocity}) shows that the
two-variable $L$-object
$L_\cA(s,u) := \int_\cF^{\mathrm{reg}}
F_\cA^{\mathrm{conn}}\,E^*(\tau,s)\,y^{u-2}\,dx\,dy$
\emph{collapses}:
$L_\cA(s,u) = \Gamma(v)(2\pi)^{-v}S_\cA(v)$
with $v = s + u - 1$.
The zero-side Rankin--Selberg variable~$s$ and the
prime-side Dirichlet variable~$u$ are locked together by
the Mellin transform of the sewing free energy.
The Sewing--Selberg formula ($u = 0$) and the
Dirichlet--sewing lift ($s = 1$) are two faces of the same
Mellin transform.  The quartic
resonance class~$\mathfrak{Q}(\cA)$ is the Schur complement
of the $3 \times 3$ Hankel determinant of the mixed
$(s,u)$-transform, stripping quadratic and cubic contamination:
for Virasoro, $\Sigma_2 = 10/[c(5c{+}22)] = Q_{\mathrm{Vir}}^{\mathrm{ct}}$
exactly (Chapter~\ref{chap:arithmetic-shadows}).
\end{remark}

\begin{construction}[Arithmetic shadow chain]
\label{constr:arithmetic-shadow-chain}
\index{arithmetic shadow chain|textbf}
\index{Euler--Koszul class!in arithmetic chain}
The shadow obstruction tower connects modular Koszul duality to
arithmetic $L$-functions via a five-step chain:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Sewing $\to$ partition function}: the HS-sewing
  criterion (Theorem~\ref{thm:general-hs-sewing}) gives convergent
  genus-$g$ amplitudes $Z_g(\cA, q)$ for Koszul algebras.
\item \emph{Partition function $\to$ eta product}: for lattice VOAs,
  $Z_1(V_\Lambda, q) = 1/\eta(\tau)^{\operatorname{rank}(\Lambda)}$,
  encoding the modular characteristic
  $\kappa = \operatorname{rank}(\Lambda)$.
\item \emph{Eta product $\to$ zeta data}: the Euler product structure
  of~$\eta$ decomposes $Z_1$ into prime-indexed factors; the sewing
  Dirichlet series
  $S_\cA(u) = \sum_{n \geq 1} a_n n^{-u}$
  extracts the arithmetic content.
\item \emph{Zeta data $\to$ bar complex}: the Euler--Koszul class
  $\operatorname{ek}(\cA) \in K_0(\mathrm{EK})$ classifies the
  relationship between sewing data and bar cohomology generating
  functions.
\item \emph{Bar complex $\to$ $L$-function}: the shadow generating
  function $G_\cA(t) = \sum_{n} \dim H^n(B(\cA))\, t^n$ and the

\end{enumerate}
The Euler--Koszul three-tier classification
(Definition~\ref{def:euler-koszul-tier}) partitions chiral algebras
into: exact ($\operatorname{ek} = 0$, lattice VOAs), finitely

($\operatorname{ek}$ infinite, Virasoro).
For lattice VOAs, this chain is complete (Stages~1--3 proved).
For non-lattice algebras, Stage~3 (Hecke decomposition) encounters the
non-multiplicativity obstruction: the sewing Dirichlet series is not a
Hecke eigenform in general.  The shadow-$L$ correspondence
$d(\cA) = 1 + \#\text{$L$-function factors}$ holds for all tested

\end{construction}

\subsection{Completion kinematics versus modular dynamics}
\label{subsec:kinematics-dynamics-intro}
\index{completion kinematics|textbf}
\index{modular dynamics|textbf}
\index{primitive cumulant!in introduction}

The package~$\mathcal{C}_\cA$ encodes the \emph{modular dynamics}
of~$\cA$: the OPE-dependent deformation data $D_\cA$,
$\kappa(\cA)$, $\Theta_\cA$, and the dg-shifted Yangian kernel
$r_\cA(z)$.  But the completed bar-cobar theory also carries a
second, independent axis that we call \emph{completion kinematics}:
invariants determined solely by the vacuum character $\chi_\cA(q)$,
without reference to OPE coefficients or curvature.

The kinematic data are extracted from the \emph{primitive cumulant
quotient} $Q(\cA) = \bar{\cA}/\partial\bar{\cA}$
(Definition~\ref{def:primitive-cumulant-quotient}) and its generating
series
\begin{equation}\label{eq:primitive-generating-intro}
G_\cA(t) = \frac{1-t}{t}\bigl(\chi_\cA(t) - 1\bigr),
\end{equation}
which is canonical: the non-derivative sector is recovered directly
from the character.  The \emph{completion Hilbert series}
$H_\cA(t) = G_\cA/(1 - G_\cA)$ counts reduced-weight bar words, and
the \emph{completion entropy}
$h_K(\cA) = \log(\rho_\cA^{-1})$
(where $G_\cA(\rho_\cA) = 1$) is the exponential growth rate of
finite bar windows
(Definitions~\ref{def:completion-hilbert-series}--\ref{def:completion-entropy}).

The two axes are independent.  The Virasoro involution
$c \mapsto 26 - c$ changes the dynamic shadow
$\mathrm{Vir}_c \leftrightarrow \mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$ while
preserving the kinematic data: both sides have the same
$G_\cA(t)$ and the same entropy.  The shadow depth classification
(G/L/C/M) measures \emph{dynamic} complexity; the completion entropy
$h_K$ measures \emph{kinematic} complexity.  Both are needed.

The \emph{primitive defect series}
$\Delta_\cA(t) = G_\cA(t) - G_\cA^{\mathrm{naive}}(t)$
(Definition~\ref{def:primitive-defect-series})
detects nonquadraticity: it is zero for Heisenberg, affine
Kac--Moody, and $\beta\gamma$ (all quadratic or free-like), and
nonzero starting at $t^3$ for Virasoro and $\mathcal{W}_N$ ($N
\geq 3$).  The nonzero defect means the completed dual coalgebra is
forced to enlarge its primitive sector beyond the declared
generators; the correct dual is the \emph{cumulant coalgebra}
$\operatorname{Cum}_c(\cA) = \hat{T}^c(sQ(\cA)^\vee)$, not the
tensor coalgebra on the original generators alone
(Conjecture~\ref{conj:cumulant-recognition}).

\section{The standard families and their shadow archetypes}
\label{sec:standard-families-intro}
\index{shadow archetypes|textbf}
\index{standard families|textbf}

Each standard family determines a \emph{shadow archetype}: termination pattern and qualitative shape of modular invariants.  Completion kinematics
(\S\ref{subsec:kinematics-dynamics-intro}) assigns a
primitive spectrum~$G_\cA(t)$, entropy~$h_K(\cA)$, and
defect~$\Delta_\cA(t)$.  The dynamic
(shadow depth) and kinematic (completion entropy) classifications are orthogonal:
finite shadow depth with zero defect (Heisenberg,
Kac--Moody, $\beta\gamma$) coexists with infinite shadow depth and nonzero
defect (Virasoro, $\mathcal{W}_N$), and entropy varies
continuously within each class.

\subsection{The Heisenberg atom: Gaussian archetype, termination at arity~$2$}
\label{subsec:heisenberg-atom-intro}
\index{Heisenberg algebra!as atom of the programme|textbf}
\index{Gaussian archetype}

The Heisenberg algebra $\mathcal{H}_k$ has OPE
$\alpha(z)\alpha(w) \sim k/(z-w)^2$: a double pole, no simple pole,
no Lie bracket.  All five theorems are verified by residue
extraction on~$\overline{C}_n(X)$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Bar complex.}
The bar differential vanishes identically ($d_{\mathrm{bracket}} =
0$); only $d_{\mathrm{curvature}}$ acts, producing scalars.
The bar cohomology is $H^p(\barB(\mathcal{H}_k)) = \C$ in bar
degree~$1$, concentrated in the top conformal weight stratum.
The Koszul dual is $\mathcal{H}_k^! = \mathrm{Sym}^{\mathrm{ch}}(V^*)$,
a curved chiral coalgebra with curvature $-k\omega_g$
(Chapter~\ref{ch:heisenberg-frame}).
Note: $\mathcal{H}_k^! \neq \mathcal{H}_{-k}$ as chiral algebras; the
Heisenberg algebra is \emph{not} Koszul self-dual.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Genus tower.}
The period correction at genus~$g$ couples to the Hodge bundle via
$\kappa(\mathcal{H}_k) = k$.  The obstruction class is
$\mathrm{obs}_g = k\cdot\lambda_g$.  The genus expansion of the
free energy reproduces the Dedekind eta function:
$\sum_{g \geq 1} F_g \hbar^{2g} = k(\hat{A}(i\hbar) - 1)$, with the
physical partition function $\log Z_1 = -(k/24)\log\eta(\tau)$
at genus~$1$ (Chapter~\ref{ch:genus-expansions}).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Shadow obstruction tower.}
The Heisenberg shadow obstruction tower terminates at arity~$2$: $o_3(\mathcal
H_k) = 0$ (by abelian rigidity), so the cubic shadow vanishes, the
quartic shadow vanishes, and $\Theta_{\mathcal{H}_k} =
\kappa(\mathcal{H}_k) \cdot \eta \otimes \Lambda$ (the tower terminates at arity~$2$).
The shadow metric is $Q_L = (2k)^2$ (constant);
the critical discriminant $\Delta = 0$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:single-line-dichotomy}).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Completion kinematics.}
The vacuum character $\chi_{\mathcal{H}_k}(q) = \prod_{n \geq 1}(1-q^n)^{-1}$ gives $G_{\mathcal{H}_k}(t) = t$ (one primitive of reduced weight~$1$).  Entropy $h_K(\mathcal{H}_k) = 0$; defect $\Delta = 0$.  The Heisenberg algebra is kinematically trivial: the simplest point in the completion phase diagram.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Complementarity.}
$Q_g(\mathcal{H}_k) \oplus Q_g(\mathcal{H}_k^!) \cong
H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \C)$: the Koszul pair
$(\mathcal{H}_k, \mathcal{H}_k^!)$ fills complementary Lagrangians.
The Koszul conductor is
$K(\mathcal{H}_k) = c(\mathcal{H}_k) + c(\mathcal{H}_k^!) = 1 + 1 = 2$.

\subsection{Affine Kac--Moody: Lie/tree archetype, termination at arity~$3$}
\label{subsec:kac-moody-atom-intro}
\index{Kac--Moody algebra!shadow archetype}
\index{Feigin--Frenkel involution}

The affine Kac--Moody algebra $\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k$ has OPE
$J^a(z) J^b(w) \sim k\,\delta^{ab}/(z-w)^2 + f^{ab}{}_c J^c(w)/(z-w)$.
The simple pole introduces a nonzero bracket component $d_{\mathrm{bracket}}$, and $d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2 \neq 0$ (proved for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ at all $2048$ sign conventions).  The full bar differential $d_{\barB} = d_{\mathrm{bracket}} + d_{\mathrm{curvature}}$ satisfies $d_{\barB}^2 = 0$ by the Borcherds identity.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Koszul dual and Feigin--Frenkel.}
The Koszul dual $(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k)^! = (H^*(\barB(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k)))^\vee$ is the chiral Chevalley--Eilenberg algebra $\mathrm{CE}^{\mathrm{ch}}(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{k'})$ at level $k' = -k - 2h^\vee$, where $h^\vee$ is the dual Coxeter number.  This is \emph{not} the same object as $\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{k'}$ itself: the Koszul dual and the Feigin--Frenkel level-reflected algebra share the same modular characteristic $\kappa$, but they are categorically distinct.  The Sugawara construction, which provides the stress-energy tensor $T(z) = \frac{1}{2(k+h^\vee)} \sum_a :J^a J^a:(z)$, is undefined at the critical level $k = -h^\vee$ (not ``$c$ diverges''; rather, the Sugawara vector is not in the completion of $U(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_{-h^\vee})$).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Shadow obstruction tower: arity~$3$ termination.}
The modular characteristic is $\kappa(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k) = \dim(\mathfrak{g}) \cdot (k+h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$, with $\kappa(k) + \kappa(k') = 0$ (anti-symmetry for the affine family).  The cubic shadow $\mathfrak{C}(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k)$ is nonzero (it detects the Lie bracket), but $o_4 = 0$: the tower terminates at arity~$3$.
The shadow metric $Q_L = (2\kappa + 3\alpha t)^2$ is a
perfect square; the critical discriminant
$\Delta = 0$ (Jacobi identity forces the quartic to be
entirely derived from the cubic self-bracket).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Completion kinematics.}
The vacuum character gives $G_{\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k}(t) = (\dim\mathfrak{g})\,t/(1-t)$: $\dim\mathfrak{g}$ primitives at each reduced weight.  Koszul radius $\rho = 1/(\dim\mathfrak{g}+1)$, entropy $h_K = \log(\dim\mathfrak{g}+1)$.  For $\mathfrak{sl}_2$: $h_K \approx 1.10$, exceeding Virasoro ($h_K \approx 0.57$) despite simpler shadow depth.  Defect $\Delta = 0$: the generators $J^a$ span the full primitive sector.

\subsection{The $\beta\gamma$ system: contact/quartic archetype, termination at arity~$4$}
\label{subsec:betagamma-atom-intro}
\index{betagamma system@$\beta\gamma$ system!shadow archetype}

The $\beta\gamma$ system has OPE $\beta(z)\gamma(w) \sim 1/(z-w)$, a simple pole between fields of complementary conformal weight.  The quartic contact invariant $\mu_{\beta\gamma} = \langle \eta, m_3(\eta, \eta, \eta) \rangle$ vanishes by rank-one abelian rigidity (Corollary~\ref{cor:nms-betagamma-mu-vanishing}): $o_5 = 0$, and the tower terminates at arity~$4$.  This is the contact archetype, the first system where the quartic shadow is nonzero but the quintic obstruction vanishes.  The single-line dichotomy (Theorem~\ref{thm:single-line-dichotomy}) shows that this value is impossible on a single primary line, where $r_{\max} \in \{2, 3, \infty\}$; the contact class escapes via stratum separation (Remark~\ref{rem:contact-stratum-separation}), with the quartic invariant living on a charged stratum of $\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}$ whose self-bracket is annihilated by the rank-one charge constraint.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Completion kinematics.}
Two generators of weights $1$ and $0$ give
$G_{\beta\gamma}(t) = t/(1-t)$.
Koszul radius $\rho = 1/2$, entropy $h_K = \log 2
\approx 0.693$, defect $\Delta = 0$.  Despite greater
shadow depth (arity~$4$ vs~$3$ for Kac--Moody), the $\beta\gamma$
system is kinematically simpler than any non-abelian affine algebra.

\subsection{Virasoro and $\mathcal{W}_N$: mixed archetype, infinite tower}
\label{subsec:virasoro-w-atom-intro}
\index{Virasoro algebra!shadow archetype}
\index{W-algebra@$\mathcal{W}$-algebra!shadow archetype}

The Virasoro algebra has OPE $T(z)\,T(w) \sim c/2(z-w)^4 + 2T(w)/(z-w)^2 + \partial T(w)/(z-w)$, a quartic pole, the highest in the standard landscape.  The Koszul dual is $\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$ (chiral Koszul self-dual at $c = 13$, not $c = 26$).

The shadow obstruction tower is infinite: $o_5(\mathrm{Vir}_c) \neq 0$, and the quintic obstruction is forced.  The structural mechanism is \emph{direct self-referentiality} in the OPE: the generator~$T$ appears in its own self-OPE via $T_{(1)}T = 2T$.  This direct self-loop, absent for Heisenberg ($J \notin J_{(k)}J$), affine ($E^\alpha \notin E^\alpha_{(k)}E^\alpha$), and $\beta\gamma$ ($\beta \notin \beta_{(k)}\beta$), sustains an infinite $A_\infty$ cascade that prevents the shadow obstruction tower from terminating
(Proposition~\ref{prop:self-referentiality-criterion}).
This is the mixed archetype: both cubic and quartic shadows are present, and the tower never terminates.  The quartic contact invariant
$Q^{\mathrm{contact}}_{\mathrm{Vir}} = 10/[c(5c+22)]$
is the explicit measure of the quartic interaction.  The poles at $c = 0$ and $c = -22/5$ are the minimal model values $c_{2,5}$ and $c_{2,3}$.  The single-line dichotomy
(Theorem~\ref{thm:single-line-dichotomy}) identifies the
structural mechanism: the Virasoro critical discriminant
$\Delta_{\mathrm{Vir}} = 40/(5c + 22)$ is nonzero for all
non-resonant~$c$, and the universal factorization
$S_r = \Delta \cdot R_r$ with generically nonvanishing
quotients~$R_r$ forces all arities $r \geq 4$ to be populated.

The $\mathcal{W}_N$ algebras share the infinite-tower behavior for $N \geq 3$.  The $\mathcal{W}_3$ algebra is the first case where the filtered-curved distinction matters: $\mathcal{W}_3$ carries a truly filtered (not merely curved) $A_\infty$ structure, because the composite field $\Lambda = :(TT): - \frac{3}{10}\partial^2 T$ introduces a quartic pole that is not a scalar multiple of the identity.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Completion kinematics.}
Kinematics detects structure invisible to shadow depth.  For generic Virasoro, $G_{\mathrm{Vir}}(t)
= \tfrac{1-t}{t}\bigl(\prod_{n \geq 2}(1-t^n)^{-1} - 1\bigr)$
gives primitive multiplicities $g_r = 1, 0, 1, 0, 2, 0, 3, 1, \ldots$
Defect $\Delta_{\mathrm{Vir}}(t) = t^3 + 2t^5 + 3t^7 + \cdots
\neq 0$: infinitely many primitive cumulants
beyond~$T$.  Entropy
$h_K(\mathrm{Vir}) \approx 0.567$.

For the $\mathcal{W}_N$ tower, the entropy increases monotonically
(Proposition~\ref{prop:wn-entropy-ladder}):
\[
h_K(\mathcal{W}_2) \approx 0.567
\;<\; h_K(\mathcal{W}_3) \approx 0.772
\;<\; h_K(\mathcal{W}_4) \approx 0.835
\;<\; \cdots
\;<\; h_K(\mathcal{W}_\infty) \approx 0.872.
\]
The $\mathcal{W}_3$ jump from $0.567$ to $0.772$ is the kinematic
signature of the composite field~$\Lambda$, which forces the
corrected Koszul recursion $h_q = 2h_{q-1} + h_{q-2} + h_{q-3}$
in place of the naive $h_q = 2h_{q-1} + h_{q-2}$.
At the limit $N = \infty$, the character
$\chi_{\mathcal{W}_\infty}(q) = \prod_{n \geq 2}(1 - q^n)^{-(n-1)}$
admits a MacMahon factorization
(Proposition~\ref{prop:wn-entropy-ladder}), connecting the
$\mathcal{W}_\infty$ completion frontier to the combinatorics of
plane partitions.

The same-family shadow $\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$ coexists with the
nonzero defect because it is a \emph{dynamic} shadow, not the full
kinematic dual.  Kinematically, the completed dual coalgebra has
infinitely many primitive generators; dynamically, the scalar trace
$\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c) \cdot \lambda_g$ already reproduces the
partner family $\mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$.  The full H-level kinematic
identification is the residual MC4 task
(\S\ref{subsec:completion-kinematics-programme} of the
concordance).

\subsection{The Yangian: $\Eone$-chiral, $R$-matrix inversion}
\label{subsec:yangian-atom-intro}
\index{Yangian!shadow archetype}
\index{R-matrix@$R$-matrix!inversion under Koszul duality}

The Yangian $Y(\mathfrak{g})$ is an $\Eone$-chiral algebra (Definition~\ref{def:e1-chiral}), not $\Einf$-chiral.  Its bar complex uses \emph{ordered} configuration spaces $\operatorname{Conf}_n^{\mathrm{ord}}(X)$ rather than unordered ones.

The Yangian is generated by currents $T^{(1)}(z), T^{(2)}(z), \ldots$ with spectral parameter dependence.  Its OPE is governed by the rational $R$-matrix
\[
R(u) \;=\; 1 \;+\; \frac{\Omega}{u},
\qquad
\Omega \;=\; \sum_a I^a \otimes I_a \;\in\; \fg \otimes \fg,
\]
where $\Omega$ is the Casimir tensor and $u$ is the spectral parameter.  The RTT relation
\[
R_{12}(u-v)\, T_1(u)\, T_2(v)
\;=\;
T_2(v)\, T_1(u)\, R_{12}(u-v)
\]
encodes the exchange algebra.  Verdier duality acts by $R$-matrix inversion $R(u) \mapsto R(u)^{-1}$ rather than by type exchange $\cA \mapsto \cA^!$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:yangian-koszul-dual}).  The Drinfeld--Kohno bridge (Theorem~\ref{thm:factorization-dk-eval}) relates the chiral Koszul duality of affine Kac--Moody algebras at the top of the DK square~\eqref{eq:dk-intro} to the quantum group $R$-matrix inversion at the bottom, with the Kazhdan--Lusztig equivalences as vertical arrows.

The modular dg-shifted Yangian $\mathcal{Y}^{\mathrm{dg,mod}}_T$ (Definition~\ref{def:modular-yangian-pro}) is the ambient pronilpotent completion of the convolution dg Lie algebra for the Yangian.  A conjectural modular $R$-matrix $R^{\mathrm{mod}}_T(z; \hbar)$ would be a Maurer--Cartan element in this completed algebra.

Under Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction, the transferred coproduct is
strictly primitive at all arities
(Principle~\ref{princ:gravitational-primitivity}): a
ghost-number obstruction kills every HPL tree correction to the
coproduct, while the $\Ainf$ product tower and the $r$-matrix
survive.  This separates the dg-shifted Yangian into two regimes:
for affine Kac--Moody (class~$\mathbf{L}$), both the product and
coproduct channels are active; for $\mathcal{W}$-algebras
(class~$\mathbf{M}$), the coproduct channel is trivial and all
dynamics resides in the $r$-matrix twist.
The ghost-number obstruction extends to all principal DS
reductions: the graviton is composite (it arises from the
BRST reduction of a Lie-type current); composites do not
split.

\subsection{Lattice vertex algebras: braided, curvature-braiding orthogonal}
\label{subsec:lattice-atom-intro}
\index{lattice vertex algebra!shadow archetype}
\index{curvature-braiding orthogonality}

The lattice vertex algebra $V_\Lambda$ associated to an even lattice $\Lambda$ carries an $\Eone$ sublattice structure visible through screening operators.  Curvature-braiding orthogonality holds: the modular characteristic $\kappa(V_\Lambda) = \operatorname{rank}(\Lambda)$ is independent of the $2$-cocycle that determines the braiding (Theorem~\ref{thm:lattice:curvature-braiding-orthogonal}).  The curvature sees only the rank of the lattice; the braiding sees only the cocycle.  These two pieces of data are orthogonal in a precise sense: they live in complementary summands of the deformation complex.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Unbounded finite depth and arithmetic content.}
While generic lattice VOAs have shadow depth~$2$ (Gaussian), the
vertex-operator OPE
$e^{i\lambda\cdot X}_{(0)}e^{i\mu\cdot X} \sim
\varepsilon(\lambda,\mu)\,e^{i(\lambda+\mu)\cdot X}$
is \emph{nonlinear} (it creates new fields), though not
self-referential ($e^{i\lambda\cdot X} \notin
e^{i\lambda\cdot X}_{(k)}e^{i\lambda\cdot X}$ since $2\lambda$ is
never a root of an even lattice).  The shadow depth of~$V_\Lambda$ is
\emph{finite but unbounded}: it is determined by the modular-form
content of the theta function~$\Theta_\Lambda$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-spectral-correspondence}).  For $E_8$
($r{=}8$, $\Theta = E_4$): depth~$3$.  For the Leech lattice
($r{=}24$, $\Theta = E_4^3 - 720\Delta$): depth~$4$, the first
example involving the Ramanujan cusp form $\Delta_{12}$ and its
$L$-function.  Even unimodular lattices of ranks $48, 72, 96, \dotsc$
achieve depths $5, 6, 7, \dotsc$: each independent cusp form
in~$S_{r/2}(\Gamma)$ contributes one critical line of the constrained
Epstein zeta~$\varepsilon^r_s(V_\Lambda)$ and raises the depth
by~$1$.  The shadow depth is a
\emph{Galois-theoretic invariant}: it counts the number of
independent automorphic $L$-functions encoded in the lattice's
Hecke eigenform decomposition
(Chapter~\ref{chap:arithmetic-shadows}).
All lattice VOAs are exact Euler--Koszul
($\operatorname{ek} = 0$), since every generator has
weight~$1$.  Their Dirichlet--sewing lift is a pure
Euler product:
$S_{V_\Lambda} = \operatorname{rank}(\Lambda)\cdot
\zeta(u)\,\zeta(u{+}1)$.


\section{Strictness as a shadow: the homotopy upgrade}
\label{sec:homotopy-upgrade}
\index{convolution!strict vs.\ homotopy|textbf}
\index{homotopy transfer theorem!role in the programme}

The convolution dg Lie algebra~$\gAmod$
of~\eqref{eq:convolution-dg-lie-intro} is a \emph{strict model}:
differential and bracket defined on the nose, not up to
homotopy.  It is only the first visible layer of a richer
homotopy-coherent package.  Write
\begin{align}
\Convstr(\mathrm{FCom},\, \operatorname{End}_{\barB(\cA)})
  &\quad\text{(the strict dg Lie convolution model),}
  \label{eq:convstr-intro}
\\
\Convinf(\mathrm{FCom},\, \operatorname{End}_{\barB(\cA)})
  &\quad\text{(the homotopy-invariant $L_\infty$ convolution).}
  \label{eq:convinf-intro}
\end{align}
A quasi-isomorphism of $L_\infty$ algebras relates them
(Chapter~\ref{app:homotopy-transfer}), so their MC moduli
coincide and every MC element of~$\Convstr$ lifts canonically to
$\Convinf$.  The packages are not interchangeable:
$\Convinf$ remembers higher homotopies
invisible to $\Convstr$, essential for
transfer, formality, and gauge equivalence.

Bar-cobar
(Theorem~A) preserves quasi-isomorphisms because
it is a quantum $L_\infty$ functor, respecting the full homotopy
package.  Homotopy transfer
(Chapter~\ref{app:homotopy-transfer}) produces transferred
$A_\infty$ and $L_\infty$ structures on bar cohomology carrying
information lost by passage to a strict model.  Every strict-level formula is the leading
term of a homotopy-coherent datum, as $\kappa(\cA)$
is the leading term of~$\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$.

The same principle applies at the modular level.  The full modular
deformation object is
\[
\Definfmod(\cA)
\;:=\;
\Convinf\bigl(\mathrm{FCom},\,
\operatorname{End}_{\barB(\cA)}\bigr)
\;\widehat{\otimes}\;
R\Gamma\bigl(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,\bullet},\, \mathbb{Q}\bigr),
\]
an $L_\infty$ algebra whose MC elements are complete genus-expansion
data compatible with clutching, trace, and duality up to coherent
homotopy.  The strict model~$\gAmod$ is a chart on this larger
formal space.  Theorem~B (bar-cobar inversion) and Theorem~C
(complementarity) are proved at the strict level and stated at the
homotopy level; the passage between them is guaranteed by the
contractibility of the space of models
(Proposition~\ref{prop:model-independence}).

\begin{remark}[Structural consequences of the homotopy upgrade]
\label{rem:structural-consequences-homotopy}
\index{homotopy upgrade!structural consequences}
Four structural consequences:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item Every strict-model formula (convolution dg~Lie bracket,
  curved bar differential, finite obstruction class, spectral
  discriminant) is a projection of
  $\Theta_\cA \in \MC(\Convinf)$.  The strict model computes
  the correct answer because it is a faithful chart on the larger
  formal space, not because the $L_\infty$ data are dispensable.
\item Genus, clutching, and gluing enter at the chain level (in the
  bar and cobar constructions, in the boundary stratification of
  configuration spaces, and in the completed deformation
  package) rather than as cohomological decorations applied post~hoc.
\item The standard families (Heisenberg, $\beta\gamma$, affine
  Kac--Moody, Virasoro, $\mathcal{W}_N$, Yangians, lattice and
  elliptic) serve as diagnostic benchmarks: each activates a
  different stratum of the homotopy machinery, and their shadow
  archetypes (Gaussian, Lie/tree, contact, mixed) are the
  finite-order witnesses of the transferred $L_\infty$ operations.
\item The shadow obstruction tower is a faithful finite-order
  image of $\Theta_\cA$: each truncation
  $\Theta_\cA^{\le r}$ captures new modular invariants
  ($\kappa$ at $r{=}2$, $\mathfrak{C}$ at $r{=}3$,
  $\mathfrak{Q}$ at $r{=}4$) that would be invisible at any lower
  order.
\end{enumerate}
\end{remark}


\section{The three pillars}
\label{sec:three-pillars-intro}
\index{three-pillar architecture|textbf}
\index{Malikov--Schechtman}
\index{Robert-Nicoud--Wierstra}
\index{Mok}

Three recent preprints force foundational upgrades at three levels of the theory.

\subsection{Pillar A: homotopy chiral algebras (MS24)}
\label{subsec:pillar-a}

The primitive local object is not a strict chiral algebra
but a \emph{homotopy chiral algebra} ($\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$)
in the sense of Malikov--Schechtman~\cite{MS24}.
A $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebra is a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module
$A$ equipped with an $A_\infty$ structure: multilinear
operations $m_k \colon A^{\otimes k} \to
A((\lambda_1)) \cdots ((\lambda_{k-1}))$
(formal Laurent series in spectral parameters
$\lambda_j = z_j - z_k$) satisfying the Stasheff
relations in each spectral variable.
Here $m_1$ is the differential, $m_2$ the
OPE product, and $m_k$ ($k \ge 3$) the failure
of strict associativity.
The strict chiral algebra~$\cA$ appears after
\emph{rectification}: it is $H^0$ of a
$\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$ structure, projecting
to the Poisson vertex algebra (PVA) shadow at
the classical level.

The identification theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:cech-hca}): $B(\cA)$ carries a natural $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebra structure, upgrading the bar construction from a dg coalgebra to a homotopy chiral algebra.

\subsection{Pillar B: filtered convolution \texorpdfstring{$sL_\infty$}{sL-infinity} algebras (RNW19+Val16)}
\label{subsec:pillar-b}

The universal deformation machine is the \emph{filtered
convolution $sL_\infty$-algebra}
$\operatorname{hom}_\alpha(C, A)$
(Robert-Nicoud--Wierstra~\cite{RNW19},
Vallette~\cite{Val16}), with underlying space
$\Hom(C, A)$ (\S\ref{subsec:intro-twisting}),
$L_\infty$ brackets $\ell_k$ ($k \ge 1$) where
$\ell_2$ is the convolution Lie bracket and
$\ell_k$ ($k \ge 3$) arise from the cooperadic
decomposition $\Delta^{(k)}_C$.
The deformation space of a twisting morphism
$\alpha \colon C \to A$ is the MC
$\infty$-groupoid $\mathrm{MC}_\bullet(\operatorname{hom}_\alpha)$;
the dg Lie algebra is its strict model.

The functor $\operatorname{hom}_\alpha$ extends to $\infty$-morphisms in either slot separately but \emph{not} both simultaneously (RNW19, Theorem~6.1).  The MC3 categorical lift must therefore proceed one slot at a time.

The identification theorem (Corollary~\ref{cor:theta-twisting-morphism}):\ $\Theta_\cA \in \operatorname{MC}(\operatorname{hom}_\alpha) \cong \operatorname{Tw}_\alpha$; the universal MC element is a twisting morphism.

\subsection{Pillar C: logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson (Mok25)}
\label{subsec:pillar-c}

The global collision geometry is the logarithmic Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\mathrm{FM}_n(X | D)$ on a simple normal crossing pair $(X, D)$ in the sense of Mok~\cite{Mok25}.  The classical FM compactification is the special case $D = \emptyset$.

The tropicalization theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:planted-forest-tropicalization}): $\Gmod_{\mathrm{pf}} = \operatorname{Trop}(\mathrm{FM}_n(\mathbb{C} | D))$.  Planted forests are dual graphs of tropical curves; grafting is degeneration.  The semistable degeneration formula yields the clutching laws for the quartic resonance class (Theorem~\ref{thm:nms-clutching-law-modular-resonance}).

\subsection{Eleven identification theorems}
\label{subsec:eleven-identifications}
\index{identification theorems}

The three pillars yield eleven identification theorems, nine proved and two structural:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\arabic*)}]
\item $\gAmod = \operatorname{hom}_\alpha(\mathrm{Com}^{!}_{\mathrm{ch}},\, \mathcal{P}^{\mathrm{ch}})$: the convolution algebra is the twisting hom (Theorem~\ref{thm:convolution-master-identification}).
\item $\Theta_\cA \in \operatorname{MC}(\operatorname{hom}_\alpha) \cong \operatorname{Tw}_\alpha$: the universal MC element is a twisting morphism (Corollary~\ref{cor:theta-twisting-morphism}).
\item $\Gmod_{\mathrm{pf}} = \operatorname{Trop}(\mathrm{FM}_n(\mathbb{C} | D))$: planted forests are tropical curves (Theorem~\ref{thm:planted-forest-tropicalization}).
\item $B(\cA)$ is a $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-algebra (Theorem~\ref{thm:cech-hca}).
\item $\barB_\kappa \dashv \Omega_\kappa$ is a Quillen equivalence (Theorem~\ref{thm:quillen-equivalence-chiral}).
\item $\cA^{\mathrm{sh}}$ is a homotopy invariant (Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-homotopy-invariance}).
\item The one-slot obstruction constrains MC3: the categorical lift must proceed one slot at a time (RNW19, Theorem~6.1).
\item $F_n = o_n$: the secondary Borcherds operations of~\cite{MS24} at arity $n \geq 3$ coincide with the shadow obstruction tower obstruction classes (Proposition~\ref{prop:borcherds-shadow-identification}).
\item The quartic clutching law has a log-geometric derivation via Mok's degeneration formula~\cite[Corollary~5.3.4]{Mok25} (Construction~\ref{constr:arity4-degeneration}).
\item The log clutching conjecture is proved by Mok~\cite[Corollary~5.3.4]{Mok25}: Theorem~\ref{thm:log-clutching-degeneration}.
\item The ambient $D^2 = 0$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:ambient-d-squared-zero}) is proved via the log-FM normal-crossings result~\cite[Theorem~3.3.1(1)]{Mok25}.
\end{enumerate}


\subsection{Chain-level synthesis}
\label{subsec:chain-level-synthesis-intro}
\index{chain-level synthesis|textbf}

The three pillars produce a single chain-level object.
For a cover~$\mathfrak{U}$ of~$X$, set
$A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty := \check{C}^\bullet(\mathfrak{U};\cA)$
(the $\mathrm{Ch}_\infty$-input) and
$\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}}(n) :=
C_\bullet(\operatorname{FM}_n(X|D))$
(the cooperadic coefficient system).
The logarithmic FM convolution algebra
\begin{equation}\label{eq:logfm-convolution-intro}
\gAmod
\;:=\;
\Convinf\!\bigl(
\cC^{\log\mathrm{FM}}_{\mathrm{mod}},\;
\operatorname{End}_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}(A^{\mathrm{ch}}_\infty)
\bigr)
\end{equation}
has five-component differential
$D^{\log}_{\mathrm{mod}}
= d_{\check{C}} + d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}
+ d_{\mathrm{coll}} + d_{\mathrm{sew}}
+ d_{\mathrm{loop}}$,
where $d_{\check{C}}$ is the \v{C}ech differential on
the cover~$\mathfrak{U}$,
$d_{\mathrm{Ch}_\infty}$ is the $A_\infty$ differential
from the homotopy chiral structure,
$d_{\mathrm{coll}}$ is the collision differential
(residue extraction along the pairwise diagonal divisors
of the log-FM compactification),
$d_{\mathrm{sew}}$ is pushforward along
codimension-one separating divisors,
and $d_{\mathrm{loop}}$ is pushforward along
non-separating divisors
(Construction~\ref{const:vol1-boundary-operators-residue}).
The identity $D^2=0$ is the cancellation of codimension-two faces
in Mok's normal-crossings compactification.

The \emph{graphwise cocomposition}
(Construction~\ref{const:vol1-graphwise-log-fm-cocomposition})
\[
\Delta^{\log}_\Gamma
\;:=\;
(\nu_\Gamma)_* \circ
\operatorname{Res}_{D^{\log}_\Gamma}
\]
decomposes the cooperadic structure over rigid stable
graphs: here $D^{\log}_\Gamma$ is the log-FM boundary
divisor indexed by the graph~$\Gamma$ (the stratum
where points collide according to the combinatorial
type of~$\Gamma$), $\operatorname{Res}$ denotes
the residue along this divisor, and
$\nu_\Gamma \colon D^{\log}_\Gamma \to
\prod_{v \in V(\Gamma)}
\overline{C}_{\mathrm{val}(v)}(X)$ is the
normalisation map that separates the colliding
clusters.  This yields Taylor coefficients
$\ell_k^{(g)} = \sum_\Gamma
|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|^{-1}\,\ell_\Gamma$ of the
modular $L_\infty$-structure.

The universal MC element is the graph sum
\[
\Theta_\cA = \sum_\Gamma
\frac{W_\Gamma^{\log\mathrm{FM}}}{|\operatorname{Aut}(\Gamma)|}
\otimes \Phi_\Gamma^\cA,
\]
summed over connected stable graphs~$\Gamma$, where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $W_\Gamma^{\log\mathrm{FM}}
  \in C_*(\overline{\cM}_\Gamma)$ is the
  \emph{graph weight}, the fundamental chain of the
  boundary stratum
  $\overline{\cM}_\Gamma
  = \prod_v \overline{\cM}_{g(v),\mathrm{val}(v)}$
  indexed by~$\Gamma$;
\item $\Phi_\Gamma^\cA
  = \bigotimes_{v \in V(\Gamma)}
  \Phi_v^\cA \circ
  \bigotimes_{e \in E(\Gamma)} P_e$
  is the \emph{algebra amplitude}, the tensor product
  of transferred chiral operations $\Phi_v^\cA$ at each
  vertex, contracted along each internal edge~$e$ by the
  propagator $P_e$ (the homotopy retraction data
  of~\S\ref{subsec:intro-homotopy-derived}).
\end{itemize}
This is the universal modular twisting morphism:
$\operatorname{MC}_\bullet(\gAmod) \simeq
\operatorname{Tw}_{\alpha^{\mathrm{mod}}}$
(Corollary~\ref{cor:vol1-theta-log-fm-twisting-data}).
Each coefficient of~$\Theta_\cA$ acquires a
\emph{tridegree} $(g,n,d)$
(Definition~\ref{def:vol1-rigid-planted-forest-depth-filtration}):
$g$ is the loop genus (from vertex genera and graph
loops), $n$ is the arity (number of external legs), and
$d$ is the \emph{log boundary depth} (the number of
nested log-FM strata traversed by the graph, counting
how many times the configuration passes through
codimension-$2$ or higher boundary faces of Mok's
compactification).

The weight filtration stratifies the MC equation by
internal-edge count.  At weight~$1$, the MC element
restricts to two types:
$\Theta_\cA^{[1]}|_{(0,4)}
= [\delta_s]\otimes\Phi_s
+ [\delta_t]\otimes\Phi_t
+ [\delta_u]\otimes\Phi_u$
(the Malikov--Schechtman Jacobiator~$F_3$, where
$[\delta_s]$, $[\delta_t]$, $[\delta_u]$ are
fundamental classes of the three boundary divisors
of $\overline{\cM}_{0,4} \cong \mathbb{P}^1$
corresponding to the $s$-, $t$-, $u$-channel
degenerations); and
$\Theta_\cA^{[1]}|_{(1,1)}
= [\delta_{\mathrm{irr}}]\otimes\Delta$
(the BV operator, where $[\delta_{\mathrm{irr}}]$
is the fundamental class of the irreducible
boundary divisor of $\overline{\cM}_{1,1}$).
At weight~$2$, the rigid planted-forest
corrections enter, the first genuinely logarithmic
layer
(Proposition~\ref{prop:vol1-first-two-weights-log-fm}).

The modular tangent complex
$T^{\mathrm{mod}}_{\Theta_\cA}
:= (\gAmod,\, d_{\Theta_\cA})$
with $L_\infty$-twisted differential
$d_{\Theta_\cA}(x) = \sum_{n,g}
(\hbar^g/n!)\,\ell_{n+1}^{(g)}(\Theta_\cA^{\otimes n},x)$
recovers all invariants as characteristic projections:
$\kappa = \operatorname{tr}(\Theta_\cA)_{2,0}$,
$\Delta_\cA = \operatorname{sdet}(1 - t\,H_{d_\Theta})$,
$\mathfrak{R}^{\mathrm{mod}}_4 =
\operatorname{pr}_4(\Theta_\cA)$
(Construction~\ref{const:vol1-modular-tangent-complex}).
The genus filtration on $L_{\mathrm{mod}}$ induces a spectral
sequence whose differentials are the obstruction
maps~$\operatorname{Ob}_g$, distinct from the PBW spectral sequence
(Construction~\ref{const:vol1-genus-spectral-sequence}).
The full chain-level architecture (weight filtration, clutching
law, genus-two shell decomposition, curved extension for
non-quadratic algebras) is in
\S\ref{subsec:three-preprints-chain-level}.


\section{The total space}
\label{sec:modular-homotopy-theory}
\index{modular homotopy theory|textbf}
\index{total space|textbf}

The nilpotence-periodicity correspondence becomes geometric in the
total space of the universal configuration fibration:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:total-fibration-intro}
\pi \colon \overline{C}_n(\mathcal{C}_g) \;\longrightarrow\;
\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g.
\end{equation}
Its fiber over $[\Sigma_g]$ is $\overline{C}_n(\Sigma_g)$.  The
boundary has two types of divisors:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item \emph{Collision divisors} $D_I$ (vertical): marked points
  collide on a fixed smooth curve.  These produce the bar
  differential.
\item \emph{Degeneration divisors} $\delta_\Gamma$ (horizontal):
  the curve acquires a node.  These produce the period corrections
  that restore~$\Dg{g}^{\,2} = 0$.
\end{enumerate}
At genus~$0$, only type~(a) exists and the Arnold relation alone
gives $\dzero^2 = 0$.  At genus~$g \geq 1$, both coexist.

\subsection{The Hodge bundle and the propagator}
\label{subsec:hodge-deformation-engine}

The Hodge bundle $\mathbb{E} = \pi_*\omega_\pi$
has fiber $H^0(\Sigma_g, \Omega^1)$.  The propagator depends on a
choice of symplectic basis for~$H_1(\Sigma_g,\mathbb{Z})$;
the period corrections live in $\mathbb{E} \boxtimes \overline{\mathbb{E}}$.  For uniform-weight algebras, the scalar trace
yields
\begin{equation}\label{eq:lambda-from-trace}
\mathrm{obs}_g(\cA)
= \kappa(\cA) \cdot c_g(\mathbb{E})
= \kappa(\cA) \cdot \lambda_g
\;\in\; H^*(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g)
\qquad\text{(uniform-weight, all $g$; arbitrary families, $g=1$)}.
\end{equation}

\subsection{The family index}
\label{subsec:family-index-intro}
\index{A-hat genus@$\hat{A}$-genus!as family index}

The family $\barB_g(\cA) \to \overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ defines a
virtual vector bundle.  Grothendieck--Riemann--Roch applied to
$\pi \colon \overline{\mathcal{C}}_g \to
\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$: the fiber Chern character
contributes~$\kappa(\cA)$; the Todd class produces the
$\hat{A}$-series.  The generating function is
$\sum_{g \geq 1} F_g(\cA) \cdot \hbar^{2g}
= \kappa(\cA) \cdot (\hat{A}(i\hbar) - 1)$
(Theorems~\ref{thm:ahat-universality-intro}
and~\ref{thm:family-index}).  For a free fermion, this is the
spin partition function; for the Virasoro algebra, the one-loop
string amplitude.

\subsection{The Lagrangian decomposition}
\label{subsec:verdier-symplectic}
\index{Verdier duality!as symplectic structure}

Verdier duality $\sigma = \mathbb{D}_{\operatorname{Ran}}$ acts on
$\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) = R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g,
\mathcal{Z}_\cA)$.  The composition
\[
\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) \otimes \mathbf{C}_g(\cA)
\;\xrightarrow{\;\mathrm{id} \otimes \sigma\;}
\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) \otimes \mathbf{C}_g(\cA^!)
\;\xrightarrow{\;\mathrm{eval}\;}
R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g,
\omega_{\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g})
\]
is a $(-(3g{-}3))$-shifted symplectic form
(Pantev--To\"en--Vaqui\'e--Vezzosi~\cite{PTVV13}).  The $\pm 1$
eigenspaces of~$\sigma$ are complementary Lagrangians.  Traced
through~$\pi$ and projected to moduli, this produces Theorem~C.


\section{Architecture}

\begin{remark}[Five geometric ingredients]\label{rem:five-pieces}

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]

\item \emph{Clutching of stable curves.}

\end{enumerate}

\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\Einf$}{E-infinity}-chiral algebra = vertex algebra]
\label{def:einf-chiral}
\index{$\mathbb{E}_\infty$-chiral algebra|textbf}
Throughout, $k = \mathbb{C}$ and $X$ is a smooth algebraic curve.
The ambient $\infty$-category is $\mathcal{D}\text{-mod}(X)$,
equipped with the chiral tensor product
$\otimes^{\mathrm{ch}}$~\cite[\S3.4]{BD04}.  Duality means
Verdier duality~$\mathbb{D}$ of holonomic $\mathcal{D}$-modules
(Remark~\ref{rem:verdier-vs-linear}).

An \emph{$\Einf$-chiral algebra} on $X$ is a commutative algebra
in $(\mathcal{D}\text{-mod}(X),\allowbreak
\otimes^{\mathrm{ch}})$
(cf.~\cite[§5.1]{HA}).
Equivalent formulations:
(a)~$\mathcal{D}_X$-module with chiral Lie
bracket~\cite[\S3.3]{BD04};
(b)~vertex algebra~\cite[\S3.7]{FBZ04} for $X = \mathbb{A}^1$;
(c)~factorization algebra on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$
(Definition~\ref{def:factorization-algebra-AF}).
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Verdier versus linear duality]
\label{rem:verdier-vs-linear}
\index{Verdier duality}
$\mathbb{D}(\mathcal{M})$ is the Verdier dual
$\mathcal{D}$-module, not the na\"{\i}ve linear dual
$\mathrm{Hom}_k(\mathcal{M}, k)$.  The Koszul
dual~$\cA^!$ is constructed via Verdier duality on
$\overline{C}_n(X)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-verdier}).
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\Eone$}{E1}-chiral algebra = nonlocal vertex algebra]
\label{def:e1-chiral}
\index{$\mathbb{E}_1$-chiral algebra|textbf}
An \emph{$\Eone$-chiral algebra} on $X$ is an associative (but not
necessarily commutative) algebra over the associative chiral
operad~$\chirAss$ (Definition~\ref{def:chiral-ass-operad}):
a $\mathcal{D}_X$-module $\cA$ with binary chiral operation
$\mu\colon \cA \chirtensor \cA \to \Delta_!\cA$
satisfying $\mu \circ (\mu \otimes \operatorname{id})
= \mu \circ (\operatorname{id} \otimes \mu)$
but not necessarily skew-symmetry.
In vertex algebra language, a nonlocal vertex algebra
(Li~\cite{Li96}; cf.~\cite[\S4.1]{HA};
Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite[Theorem~3.4.9]{BD04};
Francis--Gaitsgory~\cite{FG12}).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[The associative chiral operad]
\label{def:chiral-ass-operad}
The \emph{associative chiral operad} $\chirAss$ on $X$ is the
non-$\Sigma$ chiral operad with
$\chirAss(n) = \Delta^{(n)}_!\, \omega_X$, supported on the small
diagonal without $\Sigma_n$-action.  Quadratic presentation:
$\chirAss = \operatorname{Free}^{\mathrm{ch}}(\mu) /
(\mu \circ_1 \mu = \mu \circ_2 \mu)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[\texorpdfstring{$\chirAss$}{Ass-ch} self-duality;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:chirAss-self-dual}
$(\chirAss)^! \cong \chirAss \otimes \operatorname{sgn}$.
$\Eone$--$\Eone$ Koszul duality stays within the same operadic
type.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Classically $\operatorname{Ass}^! \cong \operatorname{Ass} \otimes
\operatorname{sgn}$~\cite[Theorem~7.4.1]{LV12}.  The chiral lift
preserves Koszul duality:
$(\mathcal{P}^{\mathrm{ch}})^! \cong (\mathcal{P}^!)^{\mathrm{ch}}$
since the orthogonal complement commutes with the chiral tensor
product.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{$\Pinf$}{P-infinity}-chiral algebra = chiral Poisson]
\label{def:pinf-chiral}
A \emph{$\Pinf$-chiral algebra} is an $\Einf$-chiral algebra with
a compatible $L_\infty$ structure via Leibniz: commutative chiral
product and Lie bracket coexist.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[\texorpdfstring{$E_n$}{En} terminology]\label{rem:En-terminology}
\index{$E_n$ terminology}
The subscripts $\Einf$, $\Eone$, $\Pinf$ refer to the
factorization level inside the BD chiral tensor category.  Bare
$E_n$ and $\En$ refer to little-disks
algebras~\cite[\S5.1]{HA}.  An $\Eone$-chiral algebra on a
curve is not a topological $E_1$-algebra; the suffix
``-chiral'' is essential.
\end{remark}

The hierarchy
$\Einf\text{-chiral} \subset \Pinf\text{-chiral} \subset
\Eone\text{-chiral}$.
Deformation quantization passes from $\Pinf$ to $\Eone$.
Bar-cobar duality operates at each level: $\Einf$ computes
factorization homology; $\Eone$ connects to Yangians and quantum
groups (Chapter~\ref{chap:yangians}).

\begin{remark}[Operadic hierarchy and configuration spaces]
\label{rem:operadic-hierarchy-table}
\index{operadic hierarchy!chiral algebras}
The hierarchy is organized by the geometry of insertion points:
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\textbf{Algebra type} & \textbf{Operad} &
  \textbf{Configuration space} & \textbf{Koszul exchange} \\ \hline
Heisenberg, KM, Vir, $\mathcal{W}$
  & $\Einf$ (comm.)
  & $C_n(X)$ unordered
  & type exchange $\cA \leftrightarrow \cA^!$ \\[2pt]
Lattice VOA
  & $\Einf$ with $\Eone$ sublattice
  & $C_n(X)$ with monodromy
  & type exchange + screening \\[2pt]
Yangian $Y(\fg)$
  & $\Eone$ (assoc.)
  & $\operatorname{Conf}_n^{\mathrm{ord}}(X)$ ordered
  & $R$-matrix inversion \\[2pt]
Elliptic QG
  & modular deformation
  & $\operatorname{Conf}$ on $E_\tau$
  & elliptic $R$-inversion
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
The progression from $\Einf$ to $\Eone$ is not ``more
generality''; it is a change in the geometry of collision, from
symmetric to ordered, with correspondingly different Koszul
exchange mechanisms.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Four Koszul duality mechanisms and three bar complexes]
\label{rem:three-koszul-mechanisms}
\index{bar complex!three variants}%
\index{ordered bar complex!as fourth mechanism}%
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \emph{Francis--Gaitsgory}~\cite{FG12}:
  $\chirLie\text{-alg} \simeq \chirCom\text{-coalg}$ via
  Chevalley/Primitives, under pro-nilpotence.
  Uses the FG bar $\barB^{\mathrm{FG}}(\cA)$
  (only the zeroth product $a_{(0)}b$).
\item \emph{Symmetric bar-cobar resolution}: $\Omega(\bar{B}(\cA)) \to \cA$
  is a quasi-isomorphism on the Koszul locus, or in
  completed/coderived regimes
  (Appendix~\ref{app:nilpotent-completion}).
  Uses the symmetric bar
  $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA) = \barB(\cA)$ (all OPE products,
  $\Sigma_n$-coinvariants).
\item \emph{Quadratic/Priddy}:
  $\cA \mapsto \cA^! = T(V^*)/R^\perp$, with
  $\bar{B}(\cA)^\vee \simeq \cA^!$ when $E_2$-degeneration holds (the bar coalgebra, dualized, recovers the Koszul dual algebra).
  Uses $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)^\vee$.
\item \emph{Ordered bar-cobar ($\Eone$-chiral Koszul duality)}:
  the ordered bar $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ retains the linear
  ordering on configuration spaces; no $\Sigma_n$ quotient.
  Its Koszul dual is the dg-shifted Yangian
  $\cA^!_{\mathrm{line}}$.  There is a natural map
  $\barB^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA) \to \barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$
  (take coinvariants), twisted by the $R$-matrix whenever the
  OPE has poles
  (Chapter~\ref{chap:e1-modular-koszul}).
\end{enumerate}
Every theorem specifies which mechanism is invoked.
Mechanisms~(i)--(iii) are the three $\Einf$ variants;
mechanism~(iv) is the $\Eone$ counterpart.
A filtration on $\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ whose associated
graded recovers $\barB^{\mathrm{FG}}(\cA)$ (retain only zeroth
poles) relates~(i) to~(ii); the coinvariant descent
$\barB^{\mathrm{ord}} \to \barB^{\Sigma}$ relates~(iv) to~(ii)
(Remark~\ref{rem:three-bar-variants}).
At higher genus, all four fuse via the Feynman transform
of the modular operad ($\mathrm{FCom}$ for (i)--(iii),
$F\!\Ass$ for~(iv)).
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Anomaly ratio]\label{def:anomaly-ratio}
\index{anomaly ratio|textbf}
For $c(\cA) \neq 0$: $\varrho(\cA) := \kappa(\cA)/c(\cA)$.
For $\mathcal{W}$-algebras,
$\varrho(\mathfrak{g}) = \sum_{i=1}^r 1/(m_i+1)$, independent
of~$k$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Koszul conductor]\label{def:koszul-conductor}
\index{Koszul conductor|textbf}
$K(\cA) := c(\cA) + c(\cA^!)$.  Values: $2\dim\mathfrak{g}$ for
$\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k$; $26$ for Virasoro; $100$ for
$\mathcal{W}_3$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Koszul spectrum]\label{def:koszul-spectrum}
\index{Koszul spectrum|textbf}
$\operatorname{KSpec}(\cA) := (c(\cA), \kappa(\cA),
\varrho(\cA))$.  Additive under tensor products in $c$
and~$\kappa$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Existence regimes]\label{rem:existence-regimes}
\label{rem:existence-koszul}
Three regimes, not one universal theorem:
(i)~\emph{strict}, where $\bar{B}_X$ and inversion hold on the
Koszul locus;
(ii)~\emph{completed}, under inverse-limit hypotheses
(Appendix~\ref{app:nilpotent-completion});
(iii)~\emph{comparison}
(Appendix~\ref{app:existence-criteria}).
The essential distinction: $\bar{B}_X(\cA)$ may be constructed
beyond where $\Omega_X\bar{B}_X(\cA) \simeq \cA$ is proved.
\end{remark}

\begin{convention}[Semantic levels: homotopy, model, shadow]
\label{conv:hms-levels}
\index{semantic levels|textbf}
\index{H/M/S convention|see{semantic levels}}

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{H-level (homotopy/native).}
The statement in the stable $\infty$-category of factorization
(co)algebras on $\operatorname{Ran}(X)$, or in the associated
formal moduli / shifted-symplectic language.  Defined up to
contractible ambiguity.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{M-level (model).}
An explicit dg / $L_\infty$ / filtered complex.  The geometric
bar complex $\bar{B}_X(\cA) = (T^c(s^{-1}\bar{\cA}), d)$ built
from configuration space integrals on $\overline{C}_n(X)$ is the
canonical model.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{S-level (shadow).}
The cohomological, Ext, numerical, or generating-function
consequence: bar cohomology dimensions, Hilbert series,
$\kappa(\cA)$, free energies $F_g(\cA)$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Notation.}
\begin{itemize}
\item $\simeq$\,: equivalence in the homotopy/$\infty$-categorical
  sense (H-level).
\item $\cong$\,: strict isomorphism of chosen dg models (M-level).
\item $=$\,: definition or literal equality only.
\end{itemize}
The proved results are proved at
M-level and stated at H-level.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Model independence.}
Any two admissible dg presentations are connected by a contractible
space of quasi-isomorphisms
(Proposition~\ref{prop:model-independence}).
\end{convention}

\begin{convention}[Regime tags]\label{conv:regime-tags}\label{conv:proof-architecture}
\index{regime tag}
Four regimes, in order of increasing generality.
The three bar differentials are:
$\dzero$ (the genus-$0$ bar differential),
$\dfib$ (the fibered bar differential, genus-$g$ but
uncorrected for period monodromy), and
$\Dg{g}$ (the full genus-$g$ corrected bar differential,
incorporating period corrections via the Hodge bundle).
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Quadratic.}  $\dzero^2 = 0$;
  Theorems~A--D hold without qualification.
  Heisenberg, free fermions, lattice VOAs.
\item \textbf{Curved-central.}
  $\dfib^{\,2} = \kappa \cdot \omega_g$; $\Dg{g}^{\,2} = 0$.
  $\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}_k$ at non-critical level, Virasoro
  with $c \neq 0$.
\item \textbf{Filtered-complete.}  Complete filtration with
  $\operatorname{gr}^F \cA$ quadratic Koszul.
  $\mathcal{W}$-algebras via DS reduction, deformation
  quantizations.
\item \textbf{Programmatic.}  Stratum~II
  (Remark~\ref{rem:two-strata}); stated as conjectures.
  Derived DK, $\mathbb{E}_n$ for $n \geq 2$.
\end{enumerate}
The four regimes correspond to increasing monodromy of the
categorical logarithm: single-valued, scalar curvature, filtered
convergence, full structure.
\end{convention}

\subsection{Ambient category}
\label{subsec:ambient-category}

All $\mathcal{D}$-modules are holonomic.  The cobar
construction is the Verdier dual of the bar complex
(Definition~\ref{def:geom-cobar-intrinsic}), so
$d_{\mathrm{cobar}}^2 = 0$ follows from
$d_{\mathrm{bar}}^2 = 0$
(Corollary~\ref{cor:cobar-nilpotence-verdier}).
Grading is cohomological ($|d| = +1$); see
Appendix~\ref{app:signs}.
\index{grading convention!cohomological}
All chiral algebras are augmented
($\varepsilon\colon \cA \to \omega_X$,
$\bar{\cA} = \ker(\varepsilon)$);
the bar complex is always reduced.
Forms $\eta_{ij} \in \Omega^1(\log D)$ and residue maps
are intrinsic to the FM compactification.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Bar complex notation.}
Several variants of the bar complex appear; here
is the dictionary:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\barB(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$: the
  \emph{algebraic} bar complex (underlying coalgebra, no
  curve dependence);
\item $\barB_X(\cA)$: the \emph{geometric} bar complex
  on~$X$ (incorporating configuration-space forms on
  $\overline{C}_n(X)$);
\item $\barB^{(g)}_X(\cA)$: the \emph{genus-$g$ bar
  complex} (the component of $\barB_X(\cA)$ over
  $\overline{\cM}_g$);
\item $\Omega_X(\mathcal{C})$: the \emph{cobar} of a
  factorization coalgebra~$\mathcal{C}$ (the right
  adjoint of~$\barB_X$).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Critical pitfalls}
\label{subsec:critical-pitfalls}
\index{critical pitfalls|textbf}

The following distinctions are the most common sources of error:

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Four objects.}
$\cA$ is the algebra.  $\barB(\cA)$ is the bar coalgebra.  $\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}} = H^*(\barB(\cA))$ is the Koszul dual coalgebra.  $\cA^! = (\cA^{\scriptstyle \text{\normalfont !`}})^\vee$ is the Koszul dual algebra.  The cobar $\Omega(\barB(\cA))$ recovers~$\cA$ (bar-cobar \emph{inversion}, not duality); $\cA^!$ is obtained by Verdier/linear duality, not cobar.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Grading.}
Cohomological ($|d| = +1$) throughout.  The bar complex uses desuspension: $s^{-1}$ lowers degree by~$1$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Classical vs.\ chiral Koszul duality.}
$\mathrm{Com}^! = \mathrm{Lie}$ (not $\mathrm{coLie}$):
the Koszul dual \emph{operad} of $\mathrm{Com}$ involves
an operadic suspension $\mathfrak{S}$ that shifts degrees
and twists by the sign representation
($\mathfrak{S}\mathcal{P}(n)
= \mathrm{sgn}_n \otimes \mathcal{P}(n)[n{-}1]$);
omitting the suspension yields $\mathrm{coLie}$ instead
of $\mathrm{Lie}$, which changes the sign of the
bracket in all convolution computations
(Appendix~\ref{app:signs}, Pitfall~9).
The Heisenberg algebra is \emph{not} self-dual.
Chiral Koszulness is not the same as classical
Koszulness.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Curved $A_\infty$.}
$m_1^2(a) = [m_0, a]$ (commutator, with a minus sign).  The bar differential satisfies $d^2 = 0$ always; curvature shows as $m_1^2 \neq 0$ in the $A_\infty$ algebra, not in the bar complex.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Sugawara.}
The Sugawara construction is \emph{undefined} at critical level $k = -h^\vee$; the Sugawara vector does not lie in the appropriate completion.  This is not ``$c$ diverges''; the construction does not exist.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Virasoro self-duality.}
The Virasoro algebra is chiral Koszul self-dual at $c = 13$ ($\mathrm{Vir}_c^! = \mathrm{Vir}_{26-c}$), not $c = 26$.  Quadratic self-duality (uncurved bar, $\kappa = 0$) holds at $c = 0$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{FM compactification.}
The Fulton--MacPherson compactification is the blowup along diagonals, not the complement of diagonals.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Prime form.}
The prime form $E(z_1, z_2)$ is a section of $K^{-1/2} \boxtimes K^{-1/2}$, not $K^{+1/2}$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{QME.}
The quantum master equation is $\hbar \Delta S + \frac{1}{2}\{S, S\} = 0$ (factor $\frac{1}{2}$, not~$1$).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{$d_{\mathrm{bracket}}^2 \neq 0$.}
The bracket component of the bar differential does not square to zero on its own: this is proved for all $2048$ sign conventions for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$.  The full Borcherds differential gives $d^2 = 0$.
