\section*{Setup and orientation}

Theorem~A of the Modular Koszul Duality Programme is, in its strongest
form, a \emph{single} structural statement: the chiral bar and chiral
cobar functors assemble into one \emph{Koszul reflection}
\[
K\;:\;\Alg^{\fact,\aug,\comp}_X
\;\rightleftarrows\;\CoAlg^{\fact,\conil,\co}_X
\;:\;K^{-1},
\qquad K(\cA):=\Bbarch_X(\cA),
\quad K^{-1}(\cC):=\Omegach_X(\cC),
\]
which is a symmetric-monoidal adjoint equivalence of stable presentable
$(\infty,1)$-categories satisfying the involutive identity
\[
K^{2}\;\simeq\;\mathrm{id}
\]
on the Koszul locus $\Kosz(X)\subset\Alg^{\fact,\aug,\comp}_X$ and up
to the canonical coderived correction off $\Kosz(X)$. The slogan is
\emph{the chiral bar is its own Koszul dual}; the diagram is one
display; the equation is one line.

The eight parallel statements that previously dispatched the content of
Theorem~A across six files of Vol~I and three files of the standalone
suite split into two layers. Seven of them (twisting-morphism
adjunction, counit quasi-isomorphism, unit weak equivalence, twisted
tensor acyclicity, bar concentration in weight~$1$, SC-formality in
class~$\mathsf{G}$, and the proved part of $R$-twisted
$\Sigma_n$-descent) are recovered as consequences of the single Koszul
reflection form. The remaining Francis--Gaitsgory properad-level
$(\infty,2)$ refinement is recorded later as
Conjecture~\ref{conj:A-infinity-2}. The $14$ downstream items and the
formal-smoothness consequence are therefore presented as expected
consequences of that refinement, not as proved corollaries.

\paragraph{Three uniform hypotheses (H1--H3).}
Let $X$ be a smooth projective curve over a field $k$ of
characteristic~$0$. The Koszul reflection of Theorem~A holds for
factorization $\Eone$-algebras $\cA$ on $X$ satisfying:
\begin{itemize}
\item[\textup{(H1)}] \emph{Augmentation.}
$\cA\in\Alg^{\fact,\aug}_X$. Required to define the augmentation
ideal~$\bar\cA=\ker(\varepsilon)$.
\item[\textup{(H2)}] \emph{Augmentation-ideal completeness.}
$\cA\simeq\varprojlim_n \cA/\bar\cA^{\,n}$. Required for the bar--cobar
twisted tensor product to converge unconditionally; equivalently,
$\cA$ is \emph{weakly curved} in Positselski's sense
(\cite[Mem.\ AMS]{Positselski2018}).
\item[\textup{(H3)}] \emph{Finite-dimensional graded bar pieces.}
Each conformal-weight space of $\Bbarch_X(\cA)$ is finite-dimensional.
Required for ordinary acyclicity to follow from coacyclicity
(\cite[Appendix~A.5, covariantly weighted]{Positselski2011}).
\end{itemize}
Hypotheses (H1)--(H3) are the strongest published bar--cobar Quillen
equivalence hypotheses (Vallette~\cite{Val16},
Positselski~\cite{Positselski2018,Positselski2011}, Hinich~\cite{Hinich2003})
specialised to the chiral ambient. They subsume the manuscript's prior
per-clause hypotheses (conilpotency, completeness, Koszulness,
finite-dim graded pieces) into a single uniform set.

The only locus on which any hypothesis is restrictive is~(H3): the
non-finitely-generated MC4-completion regime fails (H3) and is
therefore the sole genuinely open frontier
(Conjecture~$\Pi 4$ of \S\ref{sec:ainf2-platonic-obstructions}). The
finitely-generated standard landscape, including Heisenberg, all
affine Kac--Moody, Virasoro, and principal $W_N$, satisfies all three
hypotheses uniformly.

\paragraph{Notion of $\Eone$-chiral algebra in use.}
Five distinct notions of $\Eone$-chiral algebra coexist
in the current literature: (A) strict $\chirAss$-algebra; (B) $A_\infty$-algebra in
the chiral endomorphism operad $\End^{\ch}_\cA$; (C) Etingof--Kazhdan
quantum vertex algebra; (D) $A_\infty$-algebra in $\Eone$-chiral; (E)
factorization on $\Ran^{\ord}(X)$. Theorem~A is stated in
\emph{Notion~B}: $A_\infty$-algebras in the chiral endomorphism
operad $\End^{\ch}_\cA$ on the curve~$X$, with spectral parameters
realised as formal algebraic variables on $\Ran^{\ord}(X)$. The pole-free restriction~\ref{A2-ii} recovers
\emph{Notion~A} (strict $\chirAss$-algebras) at a marked point. The
ordered-to-symmetric descent~\ref{A2-iii} relates Notion~B
(ordered) to Notion~D (double $A_\infty$-promoted) via $R$-matrix
twist. The chiral coassociative coalgebra structure on $\Bbarch_X(\cA)$
is over $(\chirAss)^!$, never over $\SCchtop$: the
Swiss--cheese structure $\SCchtop$ emerges on the derived centre pair
$(C^{\bullet}_{\ch}(\cA,\cA),\cA)$, not on $\Bbarch_X(\cA)$ itself.

\section{The Koszul reflection: unified Theorem~A}\label{sec:ainf2-koszul-reflection}

\subsection{Statement}

\begin{theorem}[Theorem~A: the Koszul Reflection ($K^{2}\simeq\mathrm{id}$);
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:koszul-reflection}
\index{Theorem A!Koszul reflection unified form}
\index{Koszul reflection!K-squared involutive}
\index{bar-cobar!unified Koszul reflection}
Let $X$ be a smooth projective curve over a field~$k$ of
characteristic~$0$. Under hypotheses \textup{(H1)}--\textup{(H3)} of
\S\textup{Setup}, the chiral bar and chiral cobar functors assemble
into a \emph{Koszul reflection}
\[
K\;:\;\Alg^{\fact,\aug,\comp}_X
\;\rightleftarrows\;\CoAlg^{\fact,\conil,\co}_X
\;:\;K^{-1},
\qquad K(\cA):=\Bbarch_X(\cA),
\quad K^{-1}(\cC):=\Omegach_X(\cC),
\]
which is a symmetric-monoidal adjoint equivalence of stable presentable
$(\infty,1)$-categories with the following involutive structure:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \label{KR-i} \emph{Adjoint equivalence.}
$K\dashv K^{-1}$ with unit
$\eta_\cA\colon\cA\xrightarrow{\sim}K^{-1}K(\cA)=\Omegach_X\Bbarch_X(\cA)$
the universal Maurer--Cartan element, and counit
$\varepsilon_\cC\colon KK^{-1}(\cC)=\Bbarch_X\Omegach_X(\cC)
\xrightarrow{\sim}\cC$ its dual.
\item \label{KR-ii} \emph{Involutivity on $\Kosz(X)$.}
On the Koszul locus
$\Kosz(X)\subset\Alg^{\fact,\aug,\comp}_X$, the equivalence restricts
to one valued in \emph{ordinary} (non-coderived) factorization
coalgebras, with chain-level quasi-isomorphisms for both unit and
counit:
\[
K^{2}\;\simeq\;\mathrm{id}\quad\text{on}\quad\Kosz(X).
\]
\item \label{KR-iii} \emph{Coderived involutivity off $\Kosz(X)$.}
For $\cA\in\Alg^{\fact,\aug,\comp}_X\setminus\Kosz(X)$, the identity
$K^{2}\simeq\mathrm{id}$ holds in the coderived
$(\infty,1)$-category $D^{\co}(\Bbarch\text{-}\CoFact)$, with the
canonical coderived correction supplied by the
covariantly-weighted Positselski equivalence
$D^{\co}\simeq D^{\ctr}$ \textup{(}\cite{Positselski2011}\textup{)}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The abstract bar--cobar adjunction is
$\prop{universal-twisting-adjunction}$ (LV12 Theorem~2.2.9): there is
a natural bijection
$\Hom_{\dgCoAlg}(\cC,B(A))\simeq\Tw(\cC,A)\simeq\Hom_{\dgAlg}(\Omega(\cC),A)$
mediated by the convolution algebra. The chiral specialisation uses
the chiral operad, which is Koszul by Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite[\S3.6]{BD04}.
Vallette's bar--cobar Quillen equivalence
(\cite[Theorem~2.1]{Val16}) applies to the Koszul-resolved chiral
operad in the symmetric-monoidal ambient $\Fact(X)$ under~$\star$
(\cite{FG12}; promoted to $(\infty,2)$ in \cite[Chapter~IV.5]{GR17}, see
Proposition~\ref{prop:fg-ambient-properties}); promotion to a
symmetric-monoidal Quillen equivalence uses
Hinich~\cite[\S6]{Hinich2003}.

Curvature (the nonzero $m_0$ at higher genus) is absorbed by
Positselski~\cite[Theorem~9.1]{Positselski2018}: weakly curved
$A_\infty$-algebras over a complete local ring fit into an explicit
Quillen equivalence with conilpotent CDG-coalgebras under (H1)+(H2).
The factorization-compatible refinement used here is the operadic
Francis--Gaitsgory ambient together with the stratified Positselski
comparison; no properad lift is needed for the present theorem.
Promotion from the coderived equivalence~\ref{KR-iii} to the
chain-level qi of~\ref{KR-ii} on $\Kosz(X)$ follows from the
$E_2$-collapse of the bar--cobar spectral sequence
(Theorem~\ref{thm:ftm-seven-fold-tfae-via-hub-spoke}) plus
\cite[Appendix~A.5, covariantly weighted]{Positselski2011} which under
hypothesis~(H3) identifies coacyclicity with chain-level acyclicity.

The involutivity $K^{2}\simeq\mathrm{id}$ of \ref{KR-ii} is the unit
plus counit equivalence: $K^{-1}K(\cA)=\Omegach_X\Bbarch_X(\cA)
\xrightarrow{\sim}\cA$ via the chain-level counit on $\Kosz(X)$, and
$KK^{-1}(\cC)=\Bbarch_X\Omegach_X(\cC)\xrightarrow{\sim}\cC$ via the
unit. Off~$\Kosz(X)$, the same chain of equivalences holds in the
coderived category, giving \ref{KR-iii}.

A conjectural properad-level $(\infty,2)$ refinement, together with
the full global $R$-twisted descent package, is isolated later as
Conjecture~\ref{conj:A-infinity-2} in \S\ref{sec:ainf2-main}. The
proof of the unified Koszul reflection does not use that refinement.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The single diagram and the single equation}

The Koszul reflection is captured by one diagram:
\[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=large,row sep=large]
\Alg^{\fact,\aug,\comp}_X
  \arrow[r,bend left=15,"K\,=\,\Bbarch_X"]
  \arrow[r,phantom,"\rotatebox{90}{$\dashv$}"description]
&
\CoAlg^{\fact,\conil,\co}_X
  \arrow[l,bend left=15,"K^{-1}\,=\,\Omegach_X"]
\end{tikzcd}
\qquad
K^{2}\;\simeq\;\mathrm{id}.
\]
And by one equation:
\[
\boxed{
\Bbarch_X\dashv\Omegach_X,\qquad
\Omegach_X\Bbarch_X\simeq\mathrm{id}\text{ on }\Kosz(X),\qquad
\Bbarch_X\Omegach_X\simeq\mathrm{id}\text{ on }\Conil(X).
}
\]

\begin{remark}[Inner poetry: $K$ is the invariant, $\Bbarch_X$ and
$\Omegach_X$ are coordinates]
\label{rem:K-squared-id-poetry}
\index{Koszul reflection!invariance vs coordinates}
The Koszul reflection $K$ is the invariant categorical observable; the
factorisation
$K=\Bbarch_X$ versus $K^{-1}=\Omegach_X$ is a coordinate choice. In
the same way that Pontryagin duality is captured neither by
$\Hom(-,S^1)$ nor by its inverse but by the involutivity
$\Hom(\Hom(-,S^1),S^1)\simeq\mathrm{id}$, chiral bar--cobar duality is
captured by $K^{2}\simeq\mathrm{id}$, with bar and cobar interchanged
by the same reflection that interchanges algebras and coalgebras. One
symmetry, one diagram, one theorem.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Inner motion: four named morphisms animate Theorem~A]
\label{rem:four-named-morphisms}
\index{Koszul reflection!four named morphisms}
Four canonical morphisms animate the Koszul reflection:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\eta\colon\mathrm{id}\to K^{-1}K$ -- the \emph{twisting unit},
universal Maurer--Cartan element;
\item $\varepsilon\colon KK^{-1}\to\mathrm{id}$ -- the
\emph{twisting counit}, dual to $\eta$ and chain-level qi on
$\Kosz(X)$;
\item $K$ itself -- the \emph{Koszul reflection}, involutive symmetry
$K^{2}\simeq\mathrm{id}$;
\item $\psi(\hbar)$ -- the \emph{genus-completed counit}, the
$\hbar$-adic series
$\psi(\hbar)=\sum_g\hbar^{2g-2}\psi_g$ over the genus filtration on
$\Bbarch_g(\cA)$, with quantum correction
$m_0^{(g)}=\kappa(\cA)\,\omega_g$ as the $\hbar$-flatness
obstruction.
\end{itemize}
A reader asked ``what does Theorem~A do?'' should be able to point to
one of these four.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Bar coalgebra structure is over $(\chirAss)^{!}$,
not over $\SCchtop$]
\label{rem:bar-over-chirAss-shriek}

\index{SC-chtop!emerges on derived centre, not on bar}
The output coalgebra $\Bbarch_X(\cA)\in\CoAlg^{\fact,\conil,\co}_X$ is
a single chiral coassociative coalgebra over the chiral cooperad
$(\chirAss)^{!}$, with deconcatenation coproduct and the bar
differential as a single degree-$1$ map. The Swiss--cheese
$\SCchtop$ structure does \emph{not} live on $\Bbarch_X(\cA)$. Per
the $\SCchtop$ structure (or $E_3$-topological with conformal
vector) emerges on the derived centre pair
$(C^{\bullet}_{\ch}(\cA,\cA),\cA)$, computed \emph{using}
$\Bbarch_X(\cA)$ as a resolution. The bar coalgebra is the
$\Eone$-input of the Koszul reflection; the derived centre is the
$\SCchtop$/$E_3$-topological output.
\end{remark}

\section{The eight statements as corollaries}\label{sec:ainf2-eight-corollaries}
%================================================================

The eight prior parallel statements that previously dispatched
Theorem~A across six chapters of Vol~I and the standalone suite are
here recovered as corollaries of the unified Koszul reflection
Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-reflection}.

\begin{corollary}[Twisting morphism $\tau\colon\Bbarch_X(\cA)\to\cA$
is a Maurer--Cartan element; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:eight-cor-twisting-morphism}
\index{twisting morphism!as Maurer-Cartan}
The universal twisting morphism
$\tau\colon\Bbarch_X(\cA)\to\cA$ associated to the unit
$\eta_\cA\colon\cA\to\Omegach_X\Bbarch_X(\cA)$ of the Koszul
reflection is a Maurer--Cartan element of the convolution
$L_\infty$-algebra
$\fg^{\Eone}_\cA=\Hom^{\fact}_X(\Bbarch_X(\cA),\cA)$:
\[
d\tau+\tfrac{1}{2}[\tau,\tau]=0.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-reflection}\ref{KR-i}, the unit~$\eta_\cA$
is a coalgebra morphism that under the bar--cobar adjunction
$\Bbarch_X\dashv\Omegach_X$ corresponds bijectively to a twisting
morphism $\tau\colon\Bbarch_X(\cA)\to\cA$ (LV12 Theorem~2.2.9
specialised to the chiral ambient). The Maurer--Cartan condition
$d\tau+\tfrac{1}{2}[\tau,\tau]=0$ is exactly the compatibility
between the bar differential on $\Bbarch_X(\cA)$ and the
multiplication on~$\cA$, which is the chain-level statement
that~$\eta_\cA$ is a chain map.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Counit quasi-isomorphism on $\Kosz(X)$;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:eight-cor-counit-qi}
\index{counit!chain-level quasi-isomorphism on Kosz(X)}
For $\cA\in\Kosz(X)$, the counit
$\varepsilon_\cA\colon\Omegach_X\Bbarch_X(\cA)\to\cA$ is a chain-level
quasi-isomorphism.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
This is exactly the involutive identity
$K^{-1}K(\cA)\xrightarrow{\sim}\cA$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-reflection}\ref{KR-ii} on the Koszul locus,
read as a statement about the counit~$\varepsilon_\cA$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Unit weak equivalence on $\Conil(X)$;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:eight-cor-unit-weq}
\index{unit!chain-level weak equivalence on Conil(X)}
For $\cC\in\CoAlg^{\fact,\conil,\co}_X$ on the conilpotent locus, the
unit $\eta_\cC\colon\cC\to\Bbarch_X\Omegach_X(\cC)$ is a chain-level
weak equivalence.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-reflection}\ref{KR-ii} to
$K^{-1}K(\cC)\xrightarrow{\sim}\cC$ on the conilpotent side; under
hypothesis~(H2), the conilpotent-completeness condition makes the
unit a chain-level qi (not merely a coderived equivalence).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Twisted tensor acyclicity;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:eight-cor-twisted-tensor}
\index{twisted tensor product!acyclicity}
For $\cA\in\Kosz(X)$, the twisted tensor complex
$\Bbarch_X(\cA)\otimes^{\tau}\cA$ associated to the canonical
twisting morphism $\tau$ of
Corollary~\ref{cor:eight-cor-twisting-morphism} is acyclic, with
augmentation supplying the chain-level quasi-isomorphism
\[
\Bbarch_X(\cA)\otimes^{\tau}\cA\;\xrightarrow{\sim}\;k.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The twisted tensor complex computes $\Tor^{\Omegach_X\Bbarch_X(\cA)}_{*}(k,k)$
via the bar resolution; under
Corollary~\ref{cor:eight-cor-counit-qi},
$\Omegach_X\Bbarch_X(\cA)\simeq\cA$, so the Tor reduces to
$\Tor^{\cA}_{*}(k,k)=H_{*}(\Bbarch_X(\cA))$, which by definition of
$\Kosz(X)$ is concentrated in bar degree~$0$ at the augmentation. The
augmentation map then realises the twisted tensor complex as the
augmentation chain complex, which is acyclic
(\cite[Theorem~2.4]{LV12} specialised to the chiral ambient).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Bar concentrated in weight~$1$ at $g=0$ on
class~$\mathsf{G}$; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:eight-cor-bar-weight-1}
\index{bar complex!concentration in weight 1}
\index{class G!bar concentration}
For $\cA$ in class~$\mathsf{G}$ (PBW $E_2$-collapse class) at
genus~$0$, $\Bbarch_X(\cA)$ has cohomology concentrated in bar
weight~$1$:
\[
H^{*}(\Bbarch_X(\cA))=H^{1}(\Bbarch_X(\cA))\;[\text{shifted}\,]\,\quad
H^{i}(\Bbarch_X(\cA))=0\;\text{for}\;i\neq 1.
\]
The extension to $g\geq 1$ holds only in class~$\mathsf{G}$; for
class~$\mathsf{L}$, $\mathsf{C}$, $\mathsf{M}$ at $g\geq 1$, the
fiberwise curvature $d^{2}_{\mathrm{fib}}=\kappa\cdot\omega_g$
obstructs concentration in weight~$1$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-reflection}\ref{KR-ii}, the involutivity
$K^{2}\simeq\mathrm{id}$ on $\Kosz(X)$ is equivalent (via the PBW
spectral sequence of the bar filtration) to the $E_2$-collapse of the
bar--cobar spectral sequence at $E_2$-page. The $E_2$-collapse is
exactly the seven-fold hub-and-spoke TFAE of
Theorem~\ref{thm:ftm-seven-fold-tfae-via-hub-spoke}, which gives bar
concentration in weight~$1$ at $g=0$ for any class
$\cA\in\Kosz(X)$. At $g\geq 1$, the fiberwise differential
$d_{\mathrm{fib}}^{2}=\kappa\cdot\omega_g$ obstructs weight-$1$
concentration unless $\kappa=0$, which (per AP-class) holds in
class~$\mathsf{G}$ only.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[SC-formality on class~$\mathsf{G}$ only;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:eight-cor-sc-formality}
\index{SC-formality!class G only}
\index{class G!SC-formal}
For $\cA$ in class~$\mathsf{G}$, the convolution
$L_\infty$-algebra
$\fg^{\Eone}_\cA=\Hom^{\fact}_X(\Bbarch_X(\cA),\cA)$ is SC-formal: all
higher Stasheff--Massey operations $m_k$ for $k\geq 3$ vanish on
cohomology, and the $A_\infty$-quasi-isomorphism inverting
$\varepsilon_\cA$ collapses to a strict isomorphism. For
classes~$\mathsf{L}$, $\mathsf{C}$, $\mathsf{M}$, SC-formality fails;
the higher operations are precisely the shadow tower invariants
$S_k$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
SC-formality is, by Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-reflection}\ref{KR-ii},
equivalent to the chain-level $\Omegach_X\Bbarch_X(\cA)\simeq\cA$
being realised by a strict (not merely $A_\infty$) isomorphism.
The shadow tower $S_k$ measures the $A_\infty$-corrections to
$\varepsilon_\cA$; vanishing of $S_k$ for $k\geq 3$ is the
PBW-collapse-to-strict criterion, which by the hub-and-spoke TFAE
(Theorem~\ref{thm:ftm-seven-fold-tfae-via-hub-spoke}, spoke~7) is the
class~$\mathsf{G}$ condition exactly.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[$R$-twisted $\Sigma_n$-descent
$\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)\simeq B^{\ord}(\cA)^{R\text{-}\Sigma_n}$;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:eight-cor-R-descent}
\index{R-twisted descent!corollary form}
For $\cA$ an $\Eone$-chiral algebra with classical $R$-matrix~$R(z)$,
the restriction of $\barB^{\Sigma}_n(\cA)$ to the open configuration
space $\Conf_n(X)$ is the $R$-twisted $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant descent
of the restriction of $B^{\ord}_n(\cA)$ along the torsor
$\Conf_n^{\ord}(X)\to\Conf_n(X)$. If $R(z)=\tau$ identically, the same
identification extends chain-level on all of $\Ran(X)$. For general
$R(z)$, the extension across the diagonal strata is the cohomological
nearby-cycle comparison of Lemma~\ref{lem:R-twisted-descent}.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
This is Lemma~\ref{lem:R-twisted-descent} below, recast at corollary
level. The ordered unit~$\eta_\cA$ intertwines with the
$\Sigma_n$-equivariant local system~$L_R$ on the open stratum, and the
pole-free case has trivial local monodromy. The general boundary
comparison is the nearby-cycle statement supplied by the lemma.
\end{proof}

\begin{conjecture}[Francis--Gaitsgory $(\infty,2)$ properad
refinement; \ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:eight-properad-equiv}
\index{Francis-Gaitsgory equivalence!properad level}
The Koszul reflection of Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-reflection} lifts
along the factorization-properad transfer of
Conjecture~\ref{conj:factorization-properad-transfer} to an
$(\infty,2)$-categorical adjoint equivalence
\[
\Bbarch_X\;:\;\FactProp^{\aug}(X)
\;\rightleftarrows\;\CoFactProp^{\conil,\comp}(X)\;:\;\Omegach_X
\]
on the conilpotent-complete locus at properad level. The pole-free
restriction at $\{\mathrm{pt}\}\hookrightarrow\Ran(X)$ recovers
\cite[Theorem~11.4.1]{LV12} for $\Ass$ verbatim under the
$(\Dmod(X),\otimes^{!})\hookrightarrow\Fact(X)$ symmetric-monoidal
sub-inclusion.
\end{conjecture}

The seven proved corollaries above, together with the conjectural
properad refinement just stated, account for the full content of the
eight previously parallel Theorem~A statements. The unified Koszul
reflection Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-reflection} is \emph{prior} to the
whole package; the properad refinement is an expected strengthening,
not an ingredient in its proof.

\section{Francis--Gaitsgory factorization ambient}\label{sec:ainf2-setup}
%================================================================

The conjectural properad refinement
Conjecture~\ref{conj:eight-properad-equiv} requires an ambient in
which the chiral bar and cobar functors are genuine bifunctors between
stable presentable $(\infty,2)$-categories. The Beilinson--Drinfeld
chiral pseudo-tensor $(\Dmod(X),\otimes^{\ch})$ fails both conditions.
The Francis--Gaitsgory factorization ambient $\Fact(X)$ under
$\star$-product remedies them at the operadic level; the present
section records that proved machinery.

The classical statement of Theorem~A as a chiral bar--cobar adjunction
for the chiral associative operad~$\chirAss$ and its Koszul dual
$\chirAss^{!}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}) was proven in
Chapter~\ref{ch:chiral-koszul-pairs} by transferring the
Loday--Vallette statement~\cite[Theorem~11.4.1]{LV12} into the chiral
ambient $(\Dmod(X),\otimes^{\ch})$. The adversarial audit of
2026-04-16 (\S\ref{app:first-principles-cache}, entries~166--239)
established that three structural features of that proof require
upgrade:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textup{(F1)}] The pair $(\Dmod(X), \otimes^{\ch})$ is not strictly
a symmetric monoidal $\infty$-category in the sense required by
\cite[Theorem~11.4.1]{LV12}: Beilinson--Drinfeld~\cite[\S3.4.10]{BD04}
defines a chiral pseudo-tensor structure, which is a multilinear
operation structure without a bifunctor and without a genuine unit for
$\otimes^{\ch}$. A direct transfer of LV12 is therefore invalid.

\item[\textup{(F2)}] The Vallette model structure on conilpotent
coalgebras \cite[Theorem~2.1]{Val16} was stated in \emph{complexes over
a field}. Its verbatim transfer to $\Dmod(X)$-enriched cooperads
requires a different model structure; the correct ambient is the
Francis--Gaitsgory--Rozenblyum model structure on factorization
coalgebras \cite[Chapter~IV.5]{GR17}.

\item[\textup{(F3)}] The `E$_1$ primacy' headline of the programme
(\S\ref{sec:e1-first-prose-architecture}) asserts the ordered bar
$\bar B^{\ord}(\cA)$ as the primitive object, with the symmetric bar
$\bar B^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ obtained by averaging. The Heisenberg example
$\ex:heisenberg-e1-duality$ has an ordered bar that is already
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariant because the $R$-matrix is trivial; this did not
contradict the headline, but it did \emph{expose the absence} of an
explicit $R$-twisted descent theorem comparing $\bar B^{\ord}(\cA)$ and
$\bar B^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ for nontrivial $R$.
\end{enumerate}

The unified Theorem~A of \S\ref{sec:ainf2-koszul-reflection} resolves
(F1) and the operadic part of~(F2) on the live proved surface. The
remaining properad lift and the global chain-level form of~(F3) are
isolated below as Conjectures~\ref{conj:factorization-properad-transfer}
and~\ref{conj:A-infinity-2}.

Let $X$ be a smooth curve over a field~$k$ of characteristic~$0$.
Write $\Ran(X)$ for the Ran space of~$X$ (the colimit of finite powers
$X^n$ along surjective finite-order maps), and $\Ran^{\ord}(X)$ for the
ordered Ran space (the same colimit taken over strictly increasing
index maps on labelled finite sets).

\begin{definition}[Francis--Gaitsgory factorization ambient;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{def:fg-ambient}
\index{Francis--Gaitsgory ambient}
\index{factorization algebra!infinity-category}
The \emph{Francis--Gaitsgory factorization ambient} is the
$(\infty,2)$-category
\[
\Fact(X)
\;=\;
\Alg^{\fact}(\Shv^{\DR}(\Ran(X)))
\]
of factorization algebras on $\Ran(X)$ valued in de~Rham sheaves, with
objects augmented factorization algebras, $1$-morphisms factorization
maps, $2$-morphisms factorization homotopies, and monoidal structure
given by the $\star$-tensor product of factorization algebras as in
Francis--Gaitsgory~\cite{FG12} and
Gaitsgory--Rozenblyum~\cite[Chapter~IV.5]{GR17}.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Why $\Fact(X)$ and not $(\Dmod(X), \otimes^{\ch})$]
\label{rem:why-fact}
\index{chiral pseudo-tensor!not symmetric monoidal}
The Beilinson--Drinfeld chiral pseudo-tensor structure on $\Dmod(X)$
defines a sequence of multilinear operations
\[
\otimes^{\ch}_I \colon \prod_{i\in I} \Dmod(X) \to \Dmod(X),\qquad
|I|<\infty,
\]
indexed by finite sets~$I$; it does not collapse to a bifunctor, and
there is no strict unit object for $\otimes^{\ch}_{\{0,1\}}$ (a chiral
unit is a factorization structure, not a tensor unit; see
\cite[\S3.4]{BD04}). The minimal ambient in which Loday--Vallette
transfer is valid is therefore $\Fact(X)$ under $\star$: the star
product is genuinely a bifunctor with a strict unit, and factorization
algebras are $\star$-algebras by construction.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[$\Fact(X)$ is stable, presentable, symmetric
monoidal at the $(\infty,2)$-level; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{prop:fg-ambient-properties}
The $(\infty,2)$-category $\Fact(X)$ is stable, presentable, and
carries a symmetric monoidal structure compatible with colimits in
each variable. Its lax units are the factorization unit
(the constant factorization algebra on the structure sheaf $\cO_X$)
and the chiral unit (the Verdier-dual of $\omega_X$); the two coincide
after sheafification.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[References]
Stability and presentability of $\Shv^{\DR}(\Ran(X))$ is
\cite[Chapter~I.3]{GR17}; passing to factorization algebras preserves
both properties (\cite[Chapter~IV.5, \S1.3]{GR17}). The
symmetric-monoidal $\star$-formalism and its units are part of the
factorization package in \cite{FG12} and
\cite[Chapter~IV.5, \S1--2]{GR17}. The $(\infty,2)$-enhancement is
\cite[Chapter~IV.5, Theorem~3.1.2]{GR17}.
\end{proof}

\section{Factorization properads}\label{sec:ainf2-properads}
%================================================================

Operads parametrise multi-input / single-output operations. Properads
(Vallette~\cite{Val07}) parametrise multi-input / multi-output
operations, with composition along connected graphs (not only trees).
The conjectural $(\infty,2)$ properad refinement requires that the
bar--cobar pair lift from operads to properads: the pairing
$\cA \otimes^{\ch} \cA^! \to k$ is a (1,1)-valent operation, and the
higher twisting structure of $\theta_{\cA}$ (Maurer--Cartan in
$\fg^{\Einf}_A$) produces $(n,m)$-valent operations at each
bar-length.

\begin{definition}[Factorization properad; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{def:v1-factorization-properad}
\index{properad!factorization}
A \emph{factorization properad} on $X$ is an object $\cP$ of the
$(\infty,2)$-category $\FactProp(X)$ defined as follows. Its underlying
data is a collection of factorization algebras $\cP(n,m)$ on
$\Ran(X)^n \times \Ran(X)^m$ equipped with:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item $\Sigma_n \times \Sigma_m$-equivariance on the indexing;
\item horizontal composition along connected graphs with $n_1+n_2$
inputs and $m_1+m_2$ outputs (Vallette);
\item factorization-structure compatibility: on the locus where input
and output points are disjoint, $\cP(n,m)$ factorises through $\otimes$
of its restriction to each connected component.
\end{enumerate}
Morphisms are chain-level morphisms of the composition system,
equivariant under $\Sigma_n \times \Sigma_m$ and compatible with
factorization. 2-morphisms are homotopies of the composition system.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Why properads and not just operads]
The Koszul-dual pairing $\cA \otimes^{\ch} \cA^! \to k$ has one input
from~$\cA$ and one from~$\cA^!$: a $(1,1)$-valent operation.
Higher chiral Deligne brackets $[b_1,\dots,b_k; c]$ acting on
Hochschild $k$-chains with $c$-output present $(k,1)$-operations, and
the dual cooperations on $\bar B(\cA)$ are $(1,k)$-valent. At the
Maurer--Cartan level, $\theta_{\cA}$ in $\fg^{\ord}_\cA$ uses
$(n,1)$-valent operations; its dual $\bar\theta_{\cA^!}$ in
$\fg^{\ord}_{\cA^!}$ uses $(1,n)$-valent operations. All of these sit
inside a properad, not an operad; the Hackney--Robertson framework is
the natural abstract setting. What is missing on the live source
surface is the transfer of that abstract properad ambient to
factorization objects on $\Ran(X)$.
\end{remark}

\begin{conjecture}[Factorization properad transfer;
\ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:factorization-properad-transfer}
The $(\infty,2)$-category $\FactProp(X)$ carries a combinatorial model
structure whose weak equivalences are chain-level quasi-isomorphisms
(evaluated on each $(n,m)$-valent piece), cofibrations are retracts of
standard cell attachments along connected-graph compositions, and
fibrations are maps with the right-lifting property against trivial
cofibrations. The model structure restricts on the sub-$\infty$-category
of factorization operads to the Francis--Gaitsgory model structure on
operads in $\Fact(X)$.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Status of the transfer]
\label{rem:factorization-properad-transfer-status}
The live bibliography in Vol~I contains no local citation anchor for a
factorization-properad transfer theorem. The abstract
Hackney--Robertson properad machinery and the factorization ambient of
\cite{FG12,GR17} live in different ambients, and the passage between
them is the missing input isolated by
Conjecture~\ref{conj:factorization-properad-transfer}.
\end{remark}

\section{Conjecture~$A^{\infty,2}$: the explicit properad-level
refinement}\label{sec:ainf2-main}
%================================================================

Conjecture~\ref{conj:eight-properad-equiv} can be written as one
explicit properad-level statement between augmented factorization
algebras $\Fact^{\aug}(X)$ and conilpotent-complete factorization
coalgebras $\CoFact^{\conil,\comp}(X)$. The latter is the full
sub-$(\infty,2)$-category of $\CoFact(X)$ cut out by objects whose
coradical filtration is exhaustive and whose completion with respect
to that filtration is an equivalence.

\begin{conjecture}[Conjecture~$A^{\infty,2}$: Francis--Gaitsgory
bar--cobar $(\infty,2)$-equivalence at properad level;
\ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:A-infinity-2}
\index{Conjecture A-infinity-2}
\index{bar-cobar!(infinity,2)-equivalence}
Let $X$ be a smooth curve over a field of characteristic~$0$. In the
Francis--Gaitsgory factorization ambient $\Fact(X)$
\textup{(}Definition~\ref{def:fg-ambient}\textup{)}, the bar and cobar functors
\[
\Bbarch_X \;\colon\; \Fact^{\aug}(X)
\;\rightleftarrows\;
\CoFact^{\conil,\comp}(X)
\;\colon\; \Omegach_X
\]
constitute an \textup{($\infty,2$)}-categorical adjoint equivalence on
the conilpotent-complete locus:
\[
\Bbarch_X \dashv \Omegach_X,
\qquad
\mathrm{unit}\colon \id \xrightarrow{\sim} \Omegach_X \circ \Bbarch_X,
\qquad
\mathrm{counit}\colon \Bbarch_X \circ \Omegach_X \xrightarrow{\sim} \id.
\]
The expected equivalence satisfies three additional properties:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \label{A2-i} \emph{Properad lift.}
The adjunction lifts along the factorization-properad transfer of
Conjecture~\ref{conj:factorization-properad-transfer}:
$\Bbarch_X$ and $\Omegach_X$ extend to functors between factorization
properads and conilpotent-complete factorization coproperads, inducing
an adjoint equivalence
\[
\Bbarch_X \colon \FactProp^{\aug}(X)
\rightleftarrows \CoFactProp^{\conil,\comp}(X) \colon \Omegach_X
\]
that restricts to the operadic statement above.

\item \label{A2-ii} \emph{Pole-free-point restriction.}
At the pole-free point of $X$ -- i.e., pulled back along the inclusion
$\{\mathrm{pt}\} \hookrightarrow \Ran(X)$ corresponding to a single
marked point -- the equivalence restricts to the classical
Loday--Vallette Koszul pair $(\Ass, \Ass^!)$ via the symmetric-monoidal
sub-inclusion
\[
(\Dmod(X), \otimes^!) \;\hookrightarrow\; \Fact(X)
\qquad (\text{using the $!$-tensor of } \Dmod(X)).
\]
Explicitly: the pullback along $\{\mathrm{pt}\}$ of $\Bbarch_X$ and
$\Omegach_X$ agrees with the $k$-linear bar--cobar adjunction of
\cite[Theorem~11.4.1]{LV12} for $\Ass$.

\item \label{A2-iii} \emph{Ordered-to-symmetric descent.}
The equivalence extends the proved open-stratum and pole-free
comparison of Lemma~\ref{lem:R-twisted-descent} to a global chain-level
$R$-matrix-twisted $\Sigma_n$-descent relating the ordered bar
$B^{\ord}(\cA)$ of an $\Eone$-chiral algebra and the symmetric bar
$\barB^{\Sigma}(\cA)$ along the Ran-torsor
$\Ran(X)^{\ord} \to \Ran(X)$:
\[
\barB^{\Sigma}_n(\cA) \;\simeq\;
\bigl(B^{\ord}_n(\cA)\bigr)^{\Sigma_n\text{-coinv},\; L_R\text{-twisted}},
\]
where $L_R$ is the $\Sigma_n$-equivariant local system on
$\Ran^{\ord}(X)$ determined by the classical $R$-matrix $R(z)$ of $\cA$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:R-twisted-descent}).
\end{enumerate}
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Evidence and scope]
\label{rem:ainf2-evidence}
The conjecture splits into four parts.

\emph{Step 1: Adjunction in $\Fact(X)$.}
The chiral tensor coalgebra
$\Tc_{\Fact(X)}(s^{-1} \bar \cA)
= \bigoplus_{n\geq 0} (s^{-1}\bar\cA)^{\otimes_\star n}$
and its cobar dual are defined in the honest $\star$-monoidal ambient
of Proposition~\ref{prop:fg-ambient-properties}. The free/cofree
adjunction in this ambient is part of the factorization formalism in
\cite[Chapter~IV.5, \S2.4]{GR17} and \cite{FG12}.

\emph{Step 2: Operadic unit and counit on the conilpotent-complete
locus.}
The bar-length and cobar-length filtrations reduce the unit and counit
to the classical LV12 Koszul complexes on the point fibre. This is the
proved content already absorbed into
Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-reflection}.

\emph{Step 3: Properad lift.}
This is the missing point. The chapter contains no proved transfer from
the factorization operadic ambient to a factorization properad model
structure. That gap is isolated as
Conjecture~\ref{conj:factorization-properad-transfer}.

\emph{Step 4: Ordered-to-symmetric descent.}
Lemma~\ref{lem:R-twisted-descent} proves the chain-level comparison on
the open configuration stratum and proves the global chain-level
statement in the pole-free case $R(z)=\tau$. For general $R(z)$, the
present source surface only supplies the boundary comparison after
nearby cycles and on cohomology of the boundary strata. The full
chain-level global descent asserted in \ref{A2-iii} remains open.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Consistency with classical Theorem~A]
\label{rem:classical-A-compatible-with-ainf2}
\index{Theorem A!consistency with conjectural A-infinity-2}
Clause~\ref{A2-ii} of Conjecture~\ref{conj:A-infinity-2} is designed to
recover the geometric bar--cobar adjunction
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-cobar-isomorphism-main}})
and its Verdier-duality intertwining
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:bar-cobar-verdier}}) by restriction
to the pole-free point and cohomological truncation. Those classical
theorems are already proved independently of the conjectural properad
refinement.
\end{remark}

\section{$R$-matrix-twisted $\Sigma_n$-descent}\label{sec:ainf2-R-descent}
%================================================================

The descent lemma relating ordered and symmetric bar for
$\Eone$-chiral algebras is the explicit content of
Corollary~\ref{cor:eight-cor-R-descent} of the unified Koszul
reflection (equivalently, of
Conjecture~\ref{conj:A-infinity-2}\ref{A2-iii}).

\begin{lemma}[$R$-matrix-twisted $\Sigma_n$-descent;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{lem:R-twisted-descent}
\index{R-matrix!Sigma-descent}
\index{descent!R-twisted}
Let $\cA$ be an $\Eone$-chiral algebra with classical $R$-matrix
$R(z) \in \End(\cA \otimes \cA)((z))$ satisfying the classical
Yang--Baxter equation. Let
$\pi\colon \Ran^{\ord}(X) \to \Ran(X)$ be the ordered-to-symmetric
Ran-torsor \textup{(}stratified principal $\Sigma$-bundle, stratum-wise
principal at each cardinality\textup{)}. Then there exists a
canonical $\Sigma_n$-equivariant local system $L_R$ on the top stratum
$\Conf_n^{\ord}(X)\subset\Ran^{\ord}(X)_{=n}$ whose monodromy along an
elementary transposition across the diagonal $z_i = z_{i+1}$ is
$R(z_i - z_{i+1})$. It satisfies the following three statements.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \label{RTD-i} On the open configuration stratum,
\[
\barB^{\Sigma}_n(\cA)\big|_{\Conf_n(X)}
\;\simeq\;
\bigl(B^{\ord}_n(\cA)\big|_{\Conf_n^{\ord}(X)}\bigr)^{\Sigma_n\text{-coinv},\;L_R\text{-twisted}}.
\]
Equivalently, $\barB^{\Sigma}_n(\cA)\big|_{\Conf_n(X)}$ is the
(homotopy) descent of
$B^{\ord}_n(\cA)\big|_{\Conf_n^{\ord}(X)}\otimes L_R$ along
$\Conf_n^{\ord}(X)\to\Conf_n(X)$.
\item \label{RTD-ii} If $R(z)=\tau$ identically, then $L_R$ is trivial
and the comparison in \ref{RTD-i} extends chain-level on all of
$\Ran(X)_{=n}$, reducing to ordinary $\Sigma_n$-coinvariants.
\item \label{RTD-iii} For general $R(z)$, the logarithmic
Fulton--MacPherson compactification of Mok~\cite{Mok25} gives a
nearby-cycle comparison across the diagonal strata. The present chapter
uses this to identify the boundary comparison on cohomology, but does
not prove a global chain-level descent theorem beyond the pole-free
case.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Step 1: Construction of $L_R$.}
The ordered Ran space $\Ran^{\ord}(X)_{=n}$ is stratified by coincidence
type; its top stratum is the open configuration space
$\Conf^{\ord}_n(X) = X^n \setminus \Delta$. Assign to each point
$(z_1,\dots,z_n) \in \Conf^{\ord}_n(X)$ the vector space
$\cA^{\otimes n}$ at that point (a factorization-trivial line-system),
and to each elementary transposition $s_i$ crossing the diagonal
$\Delta_{i,i+1}$ the monodromy $R(z_i - z_{i+1}) \cdot \tau_{i,i+1}$,
where $\tau$ is the tensor swap and $R$ acts on the $i,i+1$ factors.
The classical Yang--Baxter equation
$R_{12}R_{13}R_{23} = R_{23}R_{13}R_{12}$ on codimension-2 loci
$\{z_i = z_j = z_k\}$ ensures that the representation of the pure braid
group $PB_n(X)$ extends to a representation of the symmetric group
$\Sigma_n$ on the boundary of a fundamental domain: this produces
$L_R$ as a $\Sigma_n$-equivariant local system.

\emph{Step 2: Descent at top stratum.}
On the open stratum $\Conf^{\ord}_n(X)$, the map $\pi$ is a principal
$\Sigma_n$-torsor, and $R$-twisted descent is a classical
Grothendieck-fibre-type statement: sheaves on the base
$\Conf_n(X)$ are equivalent to $\Sigma_n$-equivariant sheaves on the
total space, twisted by the monodromy of a chosen local system on the
base. The ordered bar $B^{\ord}_n(\cA)$ restricted to
$\Conf^{\ord}_n(X)$ is
$\cA^{\boxtimes n} \otimes \Omega^{n-1}_{\Conf^{\ord}_n(X)}(\log D)$,
and its descent to $\Conf_n(X)$ with twist by $L_R$ is by definition
the symmetric bar $\barB^{\Sigma}_n(\cA)$ of
Definition~\ref{def:symmetric-bar} (the bar coefficient in the
symmetric setting is the Arnold 1-form $d\log(z_i - z_j)$, which
transforms under $\Sigma_n$ by the sign character twisted by
monodromy precisely encoded by $R(z_i - z_j)$ acting on the fibres).
This proves \ref{RTD-i}.

\emph{Step 3: Extension across the diagonal strata.}
The Mok25 log Fulton--MacPherson compactification $\overline{\Conf}_n(X)$
handles the diagonal strata at the level of nearby cycles: the local
system $L_R$ on the open stratum has nearby-cycle limits along the
boundary divisors, and the degeneration $R(z-w)\to\tau$ controls the
monodromy at the exceptional divisor. This yields the cohomological
boundary comparison claimed in \ref{RTD-iii}. What it does \emph{not}
yet supply on the live source surface is a full chain-level descent
identification for nontrivial $R(z)$ on all strata.

\emph{Step 4: Pole-free specialisation.}
For a chiral algebra with $R(z)=\tau$ identically (the pole-free or
locally-constant case, e.g., Heisenberg; Example~\ref{ex:heisenberg-e1-duality}),
$L_R$ is the trivial local system, and the twisted descent reduces to
ordinary $\Sigma_n$-coinvariants. This reconciles the ordered and
symmetric bar in the locally-constant case, proving \ref{RTD-ii}. For
nontrivial $R(z)$ (e.g., Yangian; Example~\ref{ex:yangian-bar-cobar}),
the two bars differ by the monodromy twist recorded in
\ref{RTD-i}--\ref{RTD-iii}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Comparison with $\fg^{\Eone}_\cA$ versus $\fg^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA$]
\label{rem:av-via-descent}
\index{averaging map!via R-twisted descent}
The averaging map
$\mathrm{av}\colon \fg^{\Eone}_\cA \to \fg^{\mathrm{mod}}_\cA$
(\S\ref{sec:e1-first-prose-architecture}) is the dual of the descent
map $\pi^!$: dualising $\pi\colon \Ran^{\ord}(X) \to \Ran(X)$ and
tensoring with the structure sheaf, one recovers the
$\Sigma_n$-coinvariant projection with the $R$-twist suppressed at the
shadow-level. On the $R$-matrix itself, the image
$\mathrm{av}(r(z))$ at level prefix $k$ for affine Kac--Moody
$V_k(\fg)$ in trace-form convention $r(z)=k\,\Omega/z$ is the
trace of $L_R$ against the regular representation, which is
$\kappa_\mathrm{dp}=k\,\dim(\fg)/(2h^{\vee})$ for non-abelian affine
KM (adding the Sugawara shift $\dim(\fg)/2$ to recover the full
$\kappa(V_k(\fg))=\dim(\fg)(k+h^{\vee})/(2h^{\vee})$).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Resolution: split the proved and conjectural forms]

\section{Platonic obstructions: four named conjectures}\label{sec:ainf2-platonic-obstructions}
%================================================================

If a fully universal Koszul reflection on all of
$\Alg^{\fact,\aug}_X$ (without~(H3)) cannot be reached in the present
inscription, the obstruction is framed as a \emph{named open
conjecture}. There are four.

\begin{conjecture}[$\Pi 1$: Francis--Gaitsgory transfer]
\label{conj:fg-transfer}
The $(\infty,1)$-categorical bar--cobar Quillen equivalence transfers
from $\IndCoh(\Ran(X))$ to $\Alg^{\fact,\aug,\comp}_X$ and its
coalgebra dual without modification.
\end{conjecture}
This is essentially proved (Francis~HA appendix; Lurie HA~\S5.5); the
manuscript needs a named transfer proposition (the
$\prop:chiral-quillen-transfer$ slot) to make the use explicit.

\begin{conjecture}[$\Pi 2$: $E_n$-bar--cobar at higher chiral
dimension]
\label{conj:en-bar-cobar-higher-d}
For $d\geq 2$, the $E_n$-bar functor on $n$-dimensional factorization
$E_n$-algebras admits a left adjoint (cobar) that is a Quillen
equivalence onto conilpotent CDG-coalgebras over the appropriate
$n$-dimensional Maurer--Cartan complex. The $n=1$ case is
Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-reflection}.
\end{conjecture}
This is the load-bearing direction connecting Vol~I to Vol~III's
CY-to-chiral functor. Currently open in the chiral setting (proved in
Lurie~HA~\S5.5.3 for the topological setting).

\begin{conjecture}[$\Pi 3$: Lagrangian--Koszul converse]
\label{conj:lagrangian-koszul-converse}
Lagrangian transversality of $M_{\mathrm{comp}}$ at $[\cA]$ implies
chiral Koszulness of $\cA$.
\end{conjecture}
Genuinely open; requires the chiral PTVV framework. Stated as a
conjecture in the manuscript already (per Wave~13 Strengthening~5).

\begin{conjecture}[$\Pi 4$: Unbounded-rank Koszul reflection]
\label{conj:unbounded-rank-koszul-reflection}
On the locus of complete augmented chiral algebras with
non-finite-dim graded bar pieces (failing hypothesis~(H3)), the
Koszul reflection of Theorem~\ref{thm:koszul-reflection} still
holds in the coderived category but not at the chain level. The
chain-level upgrade requires a yet-unidentified Mittag-Leffler-type
condition on the bar filtration.
\end{conjecture}
The hypothesis~(H3) is satisfied throughout the standard landscape
(Heisenberg, Kac--Moody, Virasoro, $W_N$), so this obstruction is
decorative for current applications. It marks the precise frontier
between the Koszul reflection on
$\Kosz^{+}(X)$ and the fully universal Koszul reflection on all of
$\Alg^{\fact,\aug}_X$.
