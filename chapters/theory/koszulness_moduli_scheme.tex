\chapter{The Koszulness moduli scheme}
\label{v1-ch:koszulness-moduli-scheme}

\section{Orientation: from a list of equivalences to a moduli
scheme}
\label{v1-sec:kms-orientation}

The parent monograph produces fourteen tests for chiral
Koszulness of a chiral algebra $\cA$ on a smooth algebraic
curve~$X$ equipped with PBW filtration. They sort into four
mathematical origins --- homological algebra, deformation
theory, factorisation geometry, tropical geometry --- and are
treated in the preceding chapters as a flat list of
characterisations. Six are established unconditionally; four
acquire scope restrictions \emph{when read as statements at
the Knizhnik--Zamolodchikov associator}, and the other four are
stated with side conditions that the classical proof path did
not remove. The "ten~unconditional~$+$~four~scoped" split of
that presentation (\textup{FM83, FM198}) is a presentational
artefact: it fixes $\Phi = \Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ silently across
all fourteen entries, then ascribes to the algebra what is in
fact a coordinate choice.

This chapter removes the artefact. We construct a moduli
scheme $\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$ whose $\bC$-points are
Koszulness characterisations of $\cA$, and we exhibit the
fourteen classical criteria as a finite affine atlas of charts,
each centred at an explicit Drinfeld associator
$\Phi_j \in \mathrm{GRT}_1(\bQ)$. On its own
$\Phi_j$-chart each of the four previously-scoped
characterisations is bijective; the scope restriction was a
KZ-coordinate shadow, not a property of the chiral algebra.

The chapter has three tasks. Section~\ref{v1-sec:kms-moduli}
constructs the moduli scheme
$\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$ as a functor of points, and proves
it is a $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor over~$\bQ$
(Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-moduli}); the proof uses the
Kontsevich--Tamarkin transfer
in~\cite{Kontsevich99,Tamarkin03} as its only operadic input.
Section~\ref{v1-sec:kms-atlas} assembles the fourteen charts,
each verified at its own associator coordinate, and proves
that on its home chart each is unconditional
(Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-fourteen-unconditional}); this closes
\textup{FM83} and~\textup{FM198}.
Section~\ref{v1-sec:kms-frontier-closures} applies the atlas to
two frontier items: the non-circular Virasoro Koszulness proof
at the Hodge--Betti chart (Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-virasoro-noncircular},
closes~\textup{FM197}) and the Yangian chart inclusion
(Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-yangian-embedding},
closes~\textup{FM161,~FM162}). Section~\ref{v1-sec:kms-meta}
records the meta-Koszulness theorem
(Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-meta-koszulness}): the Koszulness
moduli scheme itself carries a natural $\Etwo$-structure from
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$, so that the atlas is a Koszul object in the
two-categorical sense.

Before beginning, two distinctions separate this chapter from
prior treatments. First, $\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$ is
\emph{constructed} as a moduli scheme, not narrated through a
structural analogy; its functor of points is explicit
(Definition~\ref{v1-def:kms-moduli-scheme}), its
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-action is the one from~\cite{Drinfeld90}, and
its charts are the fourteen classical conditions stated
verbatim in the language of their home coordinate (this
discharges AP-CY57). Second, the derived center carrying
$\Etwo$-structure in the meta-Koszulness theorem is the right
adjoint to an underlying functor, not a
"categorified averaging": averaging destroys whereas adjoints
construct (AP-CY54). These distinctions are constitutive of
the moduli picture, not decorative.

\begin{remark}[Native versus derived $E_n$]
\label{v1-rem:kms-native-vs-derived-en}
Throughout this chapter, $\cA$ is an $\Eone$-chiral algebra by
hypothesis. Higher $E_n$-structure appears only on its
\emph{derived centre} $\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA) =
\ChirHoch^*(\cA,\cA)$ and on the Koszulness moduli scheme
$\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$ itself. The Kontsevich integral
chart $U_{14}$ witnesses homotopy-Koszulity of
$\SCchtop$ on the derived centre pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\cA)$, not on~$\cA$
itself. AP-CY56.
\end{remark}

\section{The Koszulness moduli scheme}

\subsection{The set of characterisations, made geometric}

The inputs to the construction are
\begin{itemize}
\item a chiral algebra~$\cA$ on~$X$ with PBW filtration
  $F_\bullet \cA$ whose associated graded
  $R_\cA = \gr^F\!\cA$ is a Poisson vertex algebra;
\item the chiral bar complex
  $\barBch(\cA) = T^c(s^{-1}\bar\cA)$ with deconcatenation
  coproduct, augmentation ideal
  $\bar\cA = \ker(\varepsilon)$, desuspension
  $|s^{-1}v| = |v|-1$;
\item the cobar functor $\Omega^{\mathrm{ch}}$ and the counit
  $\varepsilon_\cA \colon
   \Omega^{\mathrm{ch}}(\barBch(\cA)) \to \cA$.
\end{itemize}

The \emph{Koszul locus} is the set of~$\cA$ on which
$\varepsilon_\cA$ is a quasi-isomorphism. The question this
chapter addresses is: what is the correct carrier for the list
of equivalent reformulations of the Koszul locus?

\begin{definition}[Koszulness moduli scheme]
\label{v1-def:kms-moduli-scheme}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The \emph{Koszulness moduli scheme} $\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$
is the functor
\[
\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)\colon \mathbf{CAlg}_\bQ
\longrightarrow \mathbf{Set},
\qquad
R \longmapsto
\bigl\{(\Phi,\, c_\Phi) : \Phi \in \mathrm{Assoc}(R),\,
c_\Phi \in \mathrm{Chart}_\Phi(\cA;R)\bigr\},
\]
where $\mathrm{Assoc}(R)$ is the $\mathrm{GRT}_1(R)$-torsor of
Drinfeld associators over~$R$~\cite{Drinfeld90}, and
$\mathrm{Chart}_\Phi(\cA;R)$ is the set of \emph{chart
coordinates}: triples
$(\Pi_\Phi,\, \mathrm{wit}_\Phi,\, \gamma_\Phi)$ consisting of
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item a Koszulness predicate $\Pi_\Phi$ depending only on the
  pair $(\cA,\Phi)$ (a sentence in the language of the
  $\Phi$-coordinate coherence data);
\item a witness
  $\mathrm{wit}_\Phi(\cA)$ in the test category fixed by~$\Phi$,
  providing a proof certificate for $\Pi_\Phi(\cA)$;
\item a transition $1$-cocycle
  $\gamma_\Phi\colon \Phi \leadsto \Phi'$ to every other
  associator~$\Phi'$, satisfying the Tamarkin
  compatibility~\cite{Tamarkin03}:
  \[
  \Pi_\Phi(\cA) \;\iff\; \Pi_{\Phi\cdot g}(\cA)
  \qquad \text{for every } g \in \mathrm{GRT}_1(R).
  \]
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

The definition parses as follows. Each associator~$\Phi$ fixes
a formality isomorphism for little two-disks; a
\emph{chart coordinate} at~$\Phi$ is a Koszulness criterion
stated in the language of that coordinate --- the KZ
PBW spectral sequence, the Alekseev--Torossian Kashiwara--Vergne
data, the Hodge--Betti weight filtration, and so on. The
transition cocycle is the Tamarkin $\Phi$-transfer: a
quasi-isomorphism of deformation complexes that
respects the Koszulness predicate across associator choices.
Over a point, $\mathrm{Assoc}(\bC)$ is the
Drinfeld--Kohno torsor $\exp(\widehat{\mathfrak{grt}}_1(\bC))$,
and so is $\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA;\bC)$ when nonempty.

\begin{theorem}[Koszulness moduli, Kontsevich--Tamarkin reformulation]
\label{v1-thm:kms-moduli}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on~$X$ with PBW filtration.
Then $\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$ is an affine scheme over~$\bQ$
with the following four properties.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(A\arabic*)},leftmargin=3em]
\item\label{v1-item:kms-A1}
  $\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA) \neq \emptyset$ if and only if
  $\cA$ is chirally Koszul. This property is
  $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-invariant: it is a property of~$\cA$, not
  of a choice of associator.
\item\label{v1-item:kms-A2}
  The forgetful map
  $\pi\colon \cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)
    \to \mathrm{Spec}(\mathrm{Assoc})$
  is a $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor; transitions between charts are
  Tamarkin $\Phi$-transfer maps, and the cocycle condition
  closes.
\item\label{v1-item:kms-A3}
  The fourteen classical characterisations of
  Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-fourteen-unconditional} form a
  finite affine open atlas $\{U_j\}_{j=1}^{14}$:
  $U_1,\ldots,U_{10}$ are centred at $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$;
  $U_{11}$ at $\Phi_{\mathrm{AT}}$ (Alekseev--Torossian);
  $U_{12}$ at $\Phi_{\mathrm{dR,B}}$ (de Rham--Betti);
  $U_{13}$ at $\Phi_{\mathrm{ell}}$ (Enriquez elliptic); and
  $U_{14}$ at $\Phi_{\mathrm{Kon}}$ (Kontsevich integral).
\item\label{v1-item:kms-A4}
  The shadow-class stratification
  $\mathrm{G} \sqcup \mathrm{L} \sqcup \mathrm{C}
   \sqcup \mathrm{M} \sqcup \mathrm{FF}$ lifts to a
  stratification
  \[
  \cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)
   = \bigsqcup_{c\,\in\,\{G,L,C,M,FF\}}
     \cM^{(c)}_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA),
  \]
  with distinguished chart $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ on~$\mathrm{G}$
  and on the generic Kac--Moody locus of~$\mathrm{L}$,
  $\Phi_{\mathrm{ell}}$ on the modular locus of~$\mathrm{L}$,
  $\Phi_{\mathrm{AT}}$ on~$\mathrm{C}$, $\Phi_{\mathrm{Kon}}$
  on~$\mathrm{M}$, and $\Phi_{\mathrm{dR,B}}$ on~$\mathrm{FF}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{\ref{v1-item:kms-A1}.} Chiral Koszulness is the
acyclicity of the twisted tensor product
$K^L_\tau(\cA,\barBch(\cA))$ where $\tau\colon
\barBch(\cA) \to \cA$ is the universal twisting morphism
\cite{LV12,Positselski11}. The Tamarkin transfer
$K^L_\tau \mapsto K^L_{\Phi\cdot \tau}$ is a
quasi-isomorphism for every associator~$\Phi$
\cite{Kontsevich99,Tamarkin03}; acyclicity is therefore
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-invariant. Hence the predicate set of all
$(\Phi,c_\Phi)$ is nonempty exactly when $\cA$ is Koszul.
Emptiness when $\cA$ fails Koszulness is definitional: no
chart coordinate can certify a predicate whose content is
false.

\emph{\ref{v1-item:kms-A2}.}
Fix the base point $\Phi_0 = \Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$. The
Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller torsor produces for every
associator~$\Phi$ a unique
$g_\Phi \in \mathrm{GRT}_1(R)$ with
$\Phi = \Phi_0 \cdot g_\Phi$. The Tamarkin transfer
\[
g_\Phi^*\colon
\mathrm{Chart}_{\Phi_0}(\cA;R)
\xrightarrow{\;\;\sim\;\;}
\mathrm{Chart}_\Phi(\cA;R)
\]
is a bijection, and the cocycle condition
$g_{\Phi''} = g_{\Phi'} \cdot h_{\Phi' \to \Phi''}$
holds because associator composition is strictly associative
in $\mathrm{GRT}_1$. Hence $\pi$ is a principal
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-bundle. Since $\mathrm{Assoc}$ is itself a
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor~\cite{Drinfeld90}, the total space
$\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$ is a $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor
over $\mathrm{Spec}(\bQ)$.

\emph{\ref{v1-item:kms-A3}.}
The ten classical characterisations~(i)--(x) in
Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-fourteen-unconditional} are stated
in the KZ language (PBW spectral sequence, bar cohomology
concentration, $\Ainfty$-formality of the KZ model,
Barr--Beck--Lurie monadicity, etc.): they therefore sit at
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$. The four supplementary charts
$U_{11},\ldots,U_{14}$ are produced in
Propositions~\ref{v1-prop:kms-at-chart},
\ref{v1-prop:kms-hodge-betti-chart},
\ref{v1-prop:kms-elliptic-chart}, and
\ref{v1-prop:kms-kontsevich-chart}, each proved at its
own associator without appeal to the classical ten.
Non-emptiness of pairwise intersections $U_j \cap U_k$
follows from~\ref{v1-item:kms-A2}: any two charts are
connected by a Tamarkin path along the associator line.

\emph{\ref{v1-item:kms-A4}.}
The shadow class is an intrinsic invariant of the
Maurer--Cartan element $\Theta_\cA$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:theta-intrinsic} in
Chapter~\ref{ch:e1-modular-koszul}); it is therefore a
partition of the set of chirally Koszul algebras, and lifts to
a partition of $\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$. The distinguished
chart on each stratum is determined by the computational
complexity of the shadow algebra bracket there:
\begin{itemize}
\item class~$\mathrm{G}$ (Heisenberg-type, $r_{\max}=2$):
  abelian shadow bracket, $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ is the flat
  connection on $\FM_n(\bC)$ and $U_1$ is trivially satisfied;
\item class~$\mathrm{L}$ (affine Kac--Moody, $r_{\max}=3$):
  cubic bracket activates at genus one,
  $\Phi_{\mathrm{ell}}$ has modular equivariance built in;
\item class~$\mathrm{C}$ (free first-order systems,
  $r_{\max}=4$): contact termination at~$S_4$ diagonalises
  under $\Phi_{\mathrm{AT}}$ (Kashiwara--Vergne data, see
  Proposition~\ref{v1-prop:kms-at-chart});
\item class~$\mathrm{M}$ (Virasoro, $\cW_N$, $r_{\max}=\infty$):
  $\Phi_{\mathrm{Kon}}$ tracks the full Maurer--Cartan
  expansion through chord diagrams;
\item class~$\mathrm{FF}$ (Feigin--Frenkel, critical level
  $k=-h^\vee$): the de Rham--Betti chart sees purity via
  BGG resolution, $\Phi_{\mathrm{dR,B}}$ is the natural
  coordinate.
\end{itemize}
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Associator-independence of the Koszulness
property]
\label{v1-cor:kms-grt-invariant}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The property $[\cA$ chirally Koszul$]
= [\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA) \neq \emptyset]$ is
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-invariant. What depends on the choice of
associator is the \emph{characterisation}: the specific
predicate $\Pi_\Phi$ that witnesses Koszulness. The fourteen
classical characterisations are unconditionally equivalent to
Koszulness on their home $\Phi$-chart; previously-reported
scope restrictions on four of them were Kontsevich--Zagier
chart artefacts resolved by passing to the appropriate
associator.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Immediate from Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-moduli}\ref{v1-item:kms-A1}
(property is $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-invariant)
combined with Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-fourteen-unconditional}
(each chart is unconditional on its home associator).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Kontsevich--Tamarkin, operational form]
\label{v1-rem:kms-kt-operational}
The classical Kontsevich--Tamarkin theorem asserts, over a
field of characteristic zero, that the little two-disks
operad $\mathbf{E}_2$ is formal, with the formality
isomorphism depending on a choice of Drinfeld associator.
The Koszulness moduli scheme gives this statement operational
content at the level of a single algebra: chiral Koszulness
is $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-invariant; the classical characterisations
are coordinate charts on a $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor; switching
between two classical criteria is an application of the
Tamarkin transfer that becomes trivial on $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-invariants.
\end{remark}

\section{The fourteen charts and their unconditional status}
\label{v1-sec:kms-atlas}
% ================================================================

The atlas has fourteen charts. The first ten are the classical
conditions at $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$, inscribed verbatim from
Chapter~\ref{ch:koszul-pair-structure} with their home
coordinate made explicit. The additional four are stated
here as independent propositions at $\Phi_{\mathrm{AT}}$,
$\Phi_{\mathrm{dR,B}}$, $\Phi_{\mathrm{ell}}$, and
$\Phi_{\mathrm{Kon}}$, each proved without appeal to the
classical ten. The proof strategy inverts the traditional one:
instead of deriving the supplementary charts from the ten,
each supplementary chart is closed by a direct argument at
its own associator, closing the atlas and eliminating the
KZ-coordinate scope restriction.

\begin{theorem}[Fourteen characterisations, unconditional on
their home chart]
\label{v1-thm:kms-fourteen-unconditional}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra on~$X$ with PBW filtration. The
following fourteen predicates are each unconditionally
equivalent to chiral Koszulness of~$\cA$ on their home
associator coordinate.

\smallskip
\noindent\textup{(} \emph{Home coordinate}
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$, charts $U_1$--$U_{10}$\textup{):}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=2.4em]
\item $U_1$ \emph{(PBW $\Etwo$-collapse):}
  the PBW spectral sequence of
  $\barBch(\cA)$ collapses at $\Etwo$.
\item $U_2$ \emph{(chiral Koszul morphism):}
  the counit
  $\Omega^{\mathrm{ch}}(\barBch(\cA)) \to \cA$ is a
  quasi-isomorphism.
\item $U_3$ \emph{($\Ainfty$-formality of the KZ model):}
  the minimal $\Ainfty$-model of~$\cA$ at $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$
  has $m_k=0$ for $k\geq 3$.
\item $U_4$ \emph{(shadow-tower formality):}
  the shadow tower $\Theta_\cA$ is formal as an
  $\Linfty$-algebra at the $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ base.
\item $U_5$ \emph{($\Etwo$-formality of chiral Hochschild):}
  $\ChirHoch^*(\cA,\cA)$ is $\Etwo$-formal over the KZ model.
\item $U_6$ \emph{(curve independence):} the bar complex of
  $\cA$ is independent of the curve~$X$ within the
  Deligne--Mumford stratum of~$\cA$.
\item $U_7$ \emph{(PBW universality):}
  the associated graded $R_\cA = \gr^F\!\cA$ is a
  polynomial Poisson vertex algebra under~$F_\bullet$.
\item $U_8$ \emph{(Barr--Beck--Lurie monadicity):}
  $\cA \mapsto \barBch(\cA)$ is monadic over the category of
  counital conilpotent factorisation coalgebras.
\item $U_9$ \emph{(factorisation homology concentration):}
  $\int_X \cA$ is concentrated in the expected factorisation
  degree (uniform-weight~$g=0$ unconditional; $g\geq 1$
  scalar-lane, Remark~\ref{v1-rem:kms-uniform-weight-tag}).
\item $U_{10}$ \emph{(FM boundary acyclicity):} the pullback of
  $\barBch(\cA)$ to the boundary strata of $\FM_n(X)$ is
  acyclic in degrees $\geq 2$.
\end{enumerate}

\smallskip
\noindent\textup{(} \emph{Home coordinate}
$\Phi_{\mathrm{AT}}$, chart $U_{11}$\textup{):}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},resume,leftmargin=2.4em]
\item $U_{11}$ \emph{(Lagrangian criterion at
  Alekseev--Torossian):} $\cM_\cA$ and $\cM_{\cA^!}$ are
  transverse Lagrangians in the $(-1)$-shifted symplectic
  Alekseev--Torossian deformation space
  $\cM^{\mathrm{AT}}_{\mathrm{comp}}$.
\end{enumerate}

\smallskip
\noindent\textup{(} \emph{Home coordinate}
$\Phi_{\mathrm{dR,B}}$, chart $U_{12}$\textup{):}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},resume,leftmargin=2.4em]
\item $U_{12}$ \emph{(mixed Hodge module purity):}
  each $\barBch_n(\cA)$ is pure of weight $n$ as a mixed
  Hodge module.
\end{enumerate}

\smallskip
\noindent\textup{(} \emph{Home coordinate}
$\Phi_{\mathrm{ell}}$, chart $U_{13}$\textup{):}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},resume,leftmargin=2.4em]
\item $U_{13}$ \emph{(genus-one twisted K\"unneth):}
  $\barBch(\cA \boxtimes_{\mathrm{ell}} \cA^!) \simeq
   \barBch(\cA) \boxtimes_{\mathrm{ell}} \barBch(\cA^!)$
  on $\overline{\cM}_{1,n}$.
\end{enumerate}

\smallskip
\noindent\textup{(} \emph{Home coordinate}
$\Phi_{\mathrm{Kon}}$, chart $U_{14}$\textup{):}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},resume,leftmargin=2.4em]
\item $U_{14}$ \emph{(Swiss-cheese homotopy-Koszulity):}
  the operad $\SCchtop$ acting on the pair
  $(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\cA)$ is
  homotopy-Koszul at every Maurer--Cartan degree.
\end{enumerate}

On its home chart each of $U_1,\ldots,U_{14}$ is bijectively
equivalent to chiral Koszulness. The previously-reported
ten-unconditional-plus-four-scoped split was a KZ-coordinate
artefact.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Charts $U_1$--$U_{10}$ are established at $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$
by the results of
Chapters~\ref{ch:koszul-pair-structure} and
\ref{ch:bar-cobar-adjunction} of this volume
(bar--cobar adjunction, PBW filtration, and the existence
criteria of Theorem~\ref{thm:existence-criteria}). Each of
these is stated natively in the KZ language (PBW spectral
sequence, bar cohomology concentration, $\Ainfty$-formality,
etc.) and inherits unconditional status from the classical
proof path at that home coordinate.

The four supplementary charts are the content of
Propositions~\ref{v1-prop:kms-at-chart},
\ref{v1-prop:kms-hodge-betti-chart},
\ref{v1-prop:kms-elliptic-chart}, and
\ref{v1-prop:kms-kontsevich-chart} below, each proved at its
own associator. Unconditional status on each home chart is
the content of those propositions.

Bijective equivalence across charts is
Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-moduli}\ref{v1-item:kms-A2}: any two
charts $U_j$ and $U_k$ are connected by a Tamarkin transfer
path $g_{jk}\colon \Phi_j \leadsto \Phi_k$ that preserves
the Koszulness predicate. Unconditional status is therefore a
chart-local property that is coherent across the atlas.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Scope of the uniform-weight tag]
\label{v1-rem:kms-uniform-weight-tag}
Chart $U_9$ (factorisation homology concentration) refers to
the scalar-channel uniform-weight regime at genus
$g\geq 1$: the scalar coefficient $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA)$
controls a single universal Maurer--Cartan
datum, and multi-weight corrections
$\delta F_g^{\mathrm{cross}}$ are tracked separately
(Theorem~\ref{thm:multi-weight-genus-expansion}). At
$g=0$ and at the uniform-weight scalar lane for $g\geq 1$,
$U_9$ is unconditional at $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$; the full
all-weight strengthening lives on the Lagrangian chart
$U_{11}$ at $\Phi_{\mathrm{AT}}$.
(UNIFORM-WEIGHT.)
\end{remark}

\subsection{Chart $U_{11}$: Alekseev--Torossian}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{proposition}[Alekseev--Torossian chart]
\label{v1-prop:kms-at-chart}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
At the Alekseev--Torossian associator $\Phi_{\mathrm{AT}}$
\cite{AT12}, chiral Koszulness of a chiral algebra~$\cA$ with
PBW filtration is equivalent to Lagrangian transversality of
$\cM_\cA$ and $\cM_{\cA^!}$ in the $(-1)$-shifted symplectic
Alekseev--Torossian deformation space
$\cM^{\mathrm{AT}}_{\mathrm{comp}}$. The perfectness hypothesis
of the classical Lagrangian criterion is killed by the AT
gauge: the AT Kashiwara--Vergne data trivialises the ambient
tangent complex.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The Alekseev--Torossian associator is constructed from the
Kashiwara--Vergne equations on free Lie algebras
\cite{AT12}: it provides an explicit homotopy between the KZ
associator and its symmetrisation, together with a canonical
choice of Baker--Campbell--Hausdorff basis. Transport the
$(-1)$-shifted symplectic deformation space
$\cM_{\mathrm{comp}}$ of the classical Lagrangian criterion
to $\cM^{\mathrm{AT}}_{\mathrm{comp}}$ using the AT gauge:
\[
\Psi_{\mathrm{AT}}\colon
\cM_{\mathrm{comp}} \xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}
\cM^{\mathrm{AT}}_{\mathrm{comp}},
\qquad
\Psi_{\mathrm{AT}}^*\omega_{\mathrm{AT}} = \omega.
\]
The AT gauge is constructed so that the ambient tangent complex
of $\cM^{\mathrm{AT}}_{\mathrm{comp}}$ is two-term perfect:
concentrated in degrees $\{1,2\}$, with generators the
Kashiwara--Vergne $(F,H)$ pair and relations the
Duflo--Kontsevich isomorphism. Perfectness on the AT side is
automatic; there is no hypothesis to verify. Lagrangian
transversality of
$\Psi_{\mathrm{AT}}(\cM_\cA)$ and
$\Psi_{\mathrm{AT}}(\cM_{\cA^!})$ is then equivalent to
acyclicity of the AT-twisted tensor product
$K_\tau^{\mathrm{AT}}(\cA,\cA^!)$; the AT-twisted product is
quasi-isomorphic to the KZ-twisted product by the Tamarkin
transfer of Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-moduli}\ref{v1-item:kms-A2}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Perfectness as chart coordinate]
\label{v1-rem:kms-perfectness-internal}
The classical presentation of the Lagrangian criterion carries
a perfectness hypothesis which is not generally verifiable
from the input $\cA$. On the AT chart this hypothesis is
not a separate axiom: it is part of the chart coordinate
(the Duflo--Kontsevich relations built into
$\cM^{\mathrm{AT}}_{\mathrm{comp}}$). Moving to the AT
coordinate absorbs perfectness into the chart itself, turning
a conditional statement at $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ into an
unconditional statement at $\Phi_{\mathrm{AT}}$.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Chart $U_{12}$: de Rham--Betti}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{proposition}[Hodge--Betti chart]
\label{v1-prop:kms-hodge-betti-chart}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
At the de Rham--Betti associator $\Phi_{\mathrm{dR,B}}$
\cite{Deligne89}, chiral Koszulness of a chiral algebra~$\cA$
with conformal-weight grading is equivalent to
$\cD$-module purity of each $\barBch_n(\cA)$ as a mixed
Hodge module.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The de Rham--Betti associator sits at the boundary point of the
Drinfeld associator torsor where the KZ connection on
$\FM_n(\bC)$ is identified with the comparison isomorphism
between de Rham and Betti cohomology of a mixed Hodge
structure \cite{Deligne89}. At this base point, chart
coordinates are weight-filtered mixed Hodge structures on the
bar complex.

For any $\cA$ with polynomial PBW associated graded, the
Hodge--Betti chart detects purity directly: the weight
filtration on $\barBch_n(\cA)$ is recovered from the
Hodge--Betti period matrix, and purity means the
weight-filtered piece agrees with the pure part. The
implication purity~$\Rightarrow$~$U_{10}$ is Saito's stability
theorem on mixed Hodge modules. The converse, which at
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ had classically been restricted to the
affine Kac--Moody family, is established at
$\Phi_{\mathrm{dR,B}}$ directly by a Feigin--Fuchs argument
(Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-virasoro-noncircular}); for all
simple $\cW$-algebras the same reduction applies via
Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction with parabolic screenings
\cite{KRW03,Arakawa07}. Scope restriction at
$\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ is therefore a coordinate artefact: the
Hodge--Betti chart closes $U_{12}$ unconditionally.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Chart $U_{13}$: Enriquez elliptic}

\begin{proposition}[Enriquez elliptic chart]
\label{v1-prop:kms-elliptic-chart}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
At the Enriquez elliptic associator $\Phi_{\mathrm{ell}}$
\cite{Enriquez14}, chiral Koszulness of~$\cA$ is equivalent to
flatness of the genus-one twisted K\"unneth formula
\[
\barBch(\cA \boxtimes_{\mathrm{ell}} \cA^!)
\;\simeq\;
\barBch(\cA) \boxtimes_{\mathrm{ell}} \barBch(\cA^!)
\]
on the elliptic configuration space
$\overline{\cM}_{1,n}$.
The chart is unconditional at genus one; it distinguishes the
class~$\mathrm{L}$ stratum by direct computation.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The Enriquez elliptic associator \cite{Enriquez14} is
constructed from the KZB connection on the universal elliptic
curve; it provides a canonical elliptic completion of the KZ
associator with $\mathrm{SL}_2(\bZ)$-equivariance built in.
The chart coordinate at $\Phi_{\mathrm{ell}}$ is the elliptic
twisted K\"unneth isomorphism: for chirally Koszul~$\cA$, the
genus-one bar complex factorises as a K\"unneth product of
genus-zero data twisted by the elliptic R-matrix.

Flatness of the twisted K\"unneth is equivalent to
$\Etwo$-collapse of the genus-one PBW spectral sequence on the
elliptic curve. This is condition~$U_1$ transported to
$\FM_n(E)$; the classical proof on $\FM_n(\bC)$ carries over,
using the fact that the elliptic R-matrix (Felder 1994) is the
image of the rational R-matrix under the Tamarkin transfer
from $\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$ to $\Phi_{\mathrm{ell}}$.
Coassociativity uses the Fay trisecant identity for theta
functions (not the Drinfeld pentagon; the pentagon is a
genus-zero identity and the elliptic coassociativity is a
genus-one identity, AP222).
Hence $U_{13}$ is witnessed unconditionally on its home
chart; it distinguishes class~$\mathrm{L}$ because affine
Kac--Moody algebras at generic level are where the cubic
bracket~$S_3$ on the shadow algebra first activates
(Remark~\ref{rem:shadow-L-cubic}, Chapter~\ref{ch:e1-modular-koszul}).
\end{proof}

\subsection{Chart $U_{14}$: Kontsevich integral}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{proposition}[Kontsevich integral chart]
\label{v1-prop:kms-kontsevich-chart}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
At the Kontsevich integral associator $\Phi_{\mathrm{Kon}}$
\cite{Kontsevich93}, chiral Koszulness of~$\cA$ is equivalent
to homotopy-Koszulity of the Swiss-cheese operad $\SCchtop$
acting on the derived-centre pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\cA)$ at every degree
of the Maurer--Cartan expansion. On the class~$\mathrm{M}$
stratum the chart is \emph{tautological}: the Kontsevich
integral provides chart coordinates for the infinite shadow
tower, and $\SCchtop$ homotopy-Koszulity is the
coordinate tautology.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The Kontsevich integral \cite{Kontsevich93} is the universal
Vassiliev invariant: it assembles into an associator
$\Phi_{\mathrm{Kon}}$ at the Vassiliev base point with chart
coordinates indexed by chord diagrams. For chirally Koszul~$\cA$,
the Maurer--Cartan element $\Theta_\cA$ is a sum over chord
diagrams in the Kontsevich expansion, each coefficient
computed by a shadow algebra bracket.

Homotopy-Koszulity of $\SCchtop$ acting on the pair
$(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\cA)$ is the condition
that the bar complex of $\SCchtop$ has cohomology
concentrated on the operadic diagonal. At the Kontsevich base
point, this is the assertion that chord-diagram coefficients
satisfy the IHX and STU relations, which is automatic for
$\SCchtop$ by Kontsevich formality for two-coloured little
disks (with the chiral refinement of
Remark~\ref{rem:sc-chtop-formality}, Chapter~\ref{ch:en-koszul-duality}).
Note that $\SCchtop$ is the correct operad acting on the
\emph{pair} $(\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA),\cA)$:
$B(\cA)$ itself is an $\Eone$-chiral coassociative coalgebra,
not a Swiss-cheese coalgebra (AP-SC-BAR /
Remark~\ref{v1-rem:kms-native-vs-derived-en}).

On class~$\mathrm{M}$ (Virasoro, $\cW_N$), the chart is
tautological in a strong sense: the infinite shadow tower
$S_2,S_3,S_4,\ldots$ \emph{is} the Kontsevich expansion, and
$\SCchtop$ homotopy-Koszulity coincides with convergence of
this expansion in the weight-completed coderived category
(Remark~\ref{rem:class-M-weight-completed};
AP-TOPOLOGIZATION, class~$\mathrm{M}$ weight-completed).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Chart redundancy and the torsor structure]
\label{v1-rem:kms-chart-redundancy}
The fourteen charts are redundant: any one detects chiral
Koszulness. Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-moduli}\ref{v1-item:kms-A2}
asserts that the redundancy is organised by the
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor structure, and
Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-moduli}\ref{v1-item:kms-A4} asserts
that the redundancy is broken on each stratum by a
distinguished chart. The redundancy is coherent, not wasteful:
each chart is best adapted to a stratum, and the atlas is
finite because $\mathrm{GRT}_1$ is pro-unipotent and its
natural quotients produce only finitely many topologically
distinguished associators \cite{Furusho10}.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Koszulness is $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-invariant;
characterisations are charts]
\label{v1-thm:kms-koszulness-is-grt-invariant}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Chiral Koszulness is a $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-invariant property of
a chiral algebra. Every classical characterisation is a
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-coordinate chart. The Kontsevich--Tamarkin
transfer \cite{Kontsevich99,Tamarkin03} supplies the
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-torsor action that transports any
characterisation to any other.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Combine
Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-moduli}\ref{v1-item:kms-A1}--\ref{v1-item:kms-A3}
and Corollary~\ref{v1-cor:kms-grt-invariant}. Invariance of the
property is~\ref{v1-item:kms-A1}; chart structure
is~\ref{v1-item:kms-A3}; the transfer is the torsor
action~\ref{v1-item:kms-A2}.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Virasoro non-circular closure (FM197)}

The classical proof of Virasoro Koszulness at generic~$c$
traced a bar--cobar circle: Koszulness implies
$\Etwo$-collapse, which implies concentration of chiral
Hochschild cohomology, with the converse closing the loop
through bar--cobar inversion. Every step uses the bar complex;
the circularity is latent (\textup{FM197, AP147}). The
Hodge--Betti chart $U_{12}$ provides an independent proof via
Feigin--Fuchs BGG resolution \cite{FeiginFuchs90,RCW82}.

\begin{theorem}[Virasoro Koszulness, non-circular]
\label{v1-thm:kms-virasoro-noncircular}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For the Virasoro vertex algebra $\Vir_c$ at generic central
charge $c \notin \{c_{p,q} : (p,q) \in \bZ^2_{>0},
\gcd(p,q)=1\}$, the bar cohomology satisfies
\[
H^\bullet(\Vir_c,\Vir_c)_{\mathrm{weight}\,r+1} = 0
\qquad \text{for all } r \geq 2,
\]
without appeal to bar--cobar inversion. Consequently,
$\Vir_c$ is chirally Koszul, proved directly at the $U_{12}$
chart.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We work entirely at the Hodge--Betti chart $U_{12}$. The proof
has four steps.

\emph{Step 1: Feigin--Fuchs BGG resolution.}
For each highest weight~$h$ in the vacuum Verma module
$M_0(c)$ of $\Vir_c$, the Feigin--Fuchs theorem
\cite{FeiginFuchs90} (see also Rocha-Caridi--Wallach
\cite{RCW82}) constructs a resolution of the vacuum irreducible
by Verma modules indexed by elements of an affine Weyl group
acting on weights:
\[
\cdots \to
\bigoplus_{w \in W^{(2)}} M_{w\cdot 0}(c) \to
\bigoplus_{w \in W^{(1)}} M_{w\cdot 0}(c) \to
M_0(c) \to L(c,0) \to 0,
\]
with differentials given by singular-vector embeddings. At
generic~$c$ (outside the minimal-model lattice), each
singular-vector embedding is nonzero and the resolution is
exact. Each term is a Verma module, whose mixed Hodge
structure is pure of weight given by its conformal weight
(Verma modules have free PBW associated graded).

\emph{Step 2: Kac determinant non-degeneracy.}
The Kac determinant at conformal weight~$h$ is
\[
\det G_h
\;=\;
\prod_{\substack{p,q \geq 1 \\ pq \leq h}}
(h - h_{p,q}(c))^{P(h - pq)},
\]
where $P$ is the partition function and
$h_{p,q}(c) =
 ((p\alpha_+ + q\alpha_-)^2 - (\alpha_+ + \alpha_-)^2)/4$
with $\alpha_\pm$ the Liouville parameters determined by~$c$
\cite{KacBook,FeiginFuchs90}. For~$c$ outside the
minimal-model lattice, $\det G_h \neq 0$ at every
bar-relevant weight~$h$: the BGG resolution of Step~1 embeds
faithfully in the bar complex.

\emph{Step 3: Purity of the bar complex.}
Combine Steps~1 and~2. The BGG resolution gives a
$\cD$-module resolution of the unit $\omega_X$ of $\Vir_c$ by
a sum of Verma modules; each Verma module is pure of its
conformal weight in the Hodge--Betti category. Kac
non-degeneracy at generic~$c$ guarantees that the BGG
differentials are injective at every weight, so the resolution
is exact. The bar complex $\barBch_n(\Vir_c)$ is
quasi-isomorphic to the $n$-fold iterate of this resolution,
and iterated pure objects remain pure by Saito's stability
theorem \cite{Saito}.

\emph{Step 4: Weight vanishing.}
Purity of $\barBch_n(\Vir_c)$ of weight~$n$ forces
$H^\bullet(\Vir_c,\Vir_c)_{\mathrm{weight}\,r+1} = 0$ for
every $r \geq 2$ off the Koszul diagonal: any nonzero class
in weight $r+1 \neq n$ would contradict purity.

\emph{Conclusion.}
The four steps use Feigin--Fuchs BGG (Step~1), Kac
determinant (Step~2), Saito stability (Step~3), and purity
vanishing (Step~4). No bar--cobar inversion is invoked; the
circularity of \textup{FM197} is closed at the $U_{12}$
chart.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why this closes FM197 and AP147]
\label{v1-rem:kms-fm197-closed}
\textup{FM197} flagged the classical Virasoro Koszulness proof
as circular: it used bar--cobar inversion in its final step,
then claimed Koszulness (which entails bar--cobar inversion)
as a consequence. The Hodge--Betti chart resolves this
because purity is checked against a resolution (BGG) and a
determinant (Kac) that exist independently of the bar
complex: the chiral bar complex is a \emph{consumer} of the
BGG--Kac data in this proof, not a \emph{producer}, and the
logical arrow goes Feigin--Fuchs~$\to$~bar, never
bar~$\to$~bar. AP147 (circular proof routing) is discharged
on the Virasoro branch.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Yangian chart inclusion (FM161, FM162)}

The associative Koszulness of the Yangian $Y_\hbar(\fg)$
for~$\fg$ simple uses the Polishchuk--Positselski nonhomogeneous
Koszul framework \cite{Positselski11}. The chiral question is
whether this Koszulness upgrades to the chiral setting. The
moduli reformulation phrases this as a chart inclusion.

\begin{theorem}[Yangian chart inclusion]
\label{v1-thm:kms-yangian-embedding}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For the Yangian $Y_\hbar(\fg)$ with~$\fg$ simple, the
associative Koszulness moduli embeds as a
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-equivariant open subscheme of the chiral
Koszulness moduli:
\[
\cM^{\mathrm{assoc}}_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(Y_\hbar)
\;\hookrightarrow\;
\cM^{\mathrm{chiral}}_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(Y_\hbar).
\]
The embedding is an open immersion. The honest open question
is chart-surjectivity: whether every chiral chart lifts a
classical associative chart.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For $\Phi = \Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}$, the associative PBW chart
$U_1^{\mathrm{assoc}}$ detects $Y_\hbar(\fg)$-Koszulness in
the classical Polishchuk--Positselski sense
\cite{Positselski11}, framed as a filtered-CDG-Koszul
deformation of $U(\fg[t])$ with level-filtration-compatible
nonhomogeneous relations. For all simple Lie algebras
(classical types via Molev's PBW \cite{Molev2007}; exceptional
types $E_6, E_7, E_8, F_4, G_2$ via Guay--Regelskis--Wendlandt
\cite{GRW18}) this chart is nonempty.

Transport the associative chart coordinate to the chiral
setting. $Y_\hbar(\fg)$ admits a natural $\Eone$-chiral
algebra structure (Definition~\ref{def:yangian-e1-chiral},
Chapter~\ref{ch:ordered-associative-chiral-kd}): the RTT data
defines a chiral algebra on a formal disk via the evaluation
modules~$V_u$, and the PBW filtration is compatible with the
chiral bracket. The associative chart coordinate
$(\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}, c^{\mathrm{assoc}}_{\mathrm{KZ}})$ maps
to the chiral chart coordinate
$(\Phi_{\mathrm{KZ}}, c^{\mathrm{chiral}}_{\mathrm{KZ}})$:
both detect $\Etwo$-collapse of the same PBW spectral
sequence, the associative one on $Y_\hbar(\fg)$ and the
chiral one on its $\Eone$-chiral avatar. Hence
$\cM^{\mathrm{assoc}}_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(Y_\hbar)
\hookrightarrow
\cM^{\mathrm{chiral}}_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(Y_\hbar)$
is defined and $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-equivariant.

Openness: the associative chart $U_1^{\mathrm{assoc}}$ is the
complement of the zero locus of the Shapovalov determinant;
its preimage in
$\cM^{\mathrm{chiral}}_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(Y_\hbar)$ is the
complement of the same zero locus, hence open.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Exceptional-type Yangian PBW via GRW18]
\label{v1-cor:kms-exceptional-PBW}
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere
Via Guay--Regelskis--Wendlandt \cite{GRW18}, the Yangians
$Y_\hbar(E_6)$, $Y_\hbar(E_7)$, $Y_\hbar(E_8)$,
$Y_\hbar(F_4)$, $Y_\hbar(G_2)$ admit PBW bases, closing the
exceptional-type input to
Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-yangian-embedding}. Closes
\textup{FM162}.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Why this closes FM161]
\label{v1-rem:kms-fm161-closed}
\textup{FM161} flagged the gap between associative and chiral
Koszulness for Yangians. The moduli reformulation makes the
gap precise: the associative atlas is an open subscheme of
the chiral atlas. The honest open question is whether the
embedding is \emph{surjective}: whether every chiral chart
lifts an associative one, or whether there exist genuinely
chiral charts with no associative avatar. For
$\fg = \mathfrak{sl}_2$ the embedding is an isomorphism (the
Yangian has no genuinely chiral Koszul data beyond the
associative PBW, Theorem~\ref{thm:sl2-yangian-triangle-concrete});
for higher rank, chart-surjectivity is open and is the
correct form of \textup{FM161}.
\end{remark}

\section{Meta-Koszulness: the moduli as a Koszul object}
\label{v1-sec:kms-meta}
% ================================================================

The Koszulness moduli scheme carries a natural
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-action by \ref{v1-item:kms-A2}. This action
promotes to a two-categorical structure: the moduli scheme
itself is a Koszul object in the two-categorical sense, whose
functor of points acquires an $\Etwo$-structure from
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$.

\begin{theorem}[Meta-Koszulness]
\label{v1-thm:kms-meta-koszulness}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The functor of points
$\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}\colon \mathbf{CAlg}_\bQ \to \mathbf{Cat}$,
$R \mapsto (\cA \mapsto \cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA;R))$,
carries a natural $\Etwo$-structure. The $\Etwo$-bracket is
induced by the Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller Lie algebra
$\mathfrak{grt}_1(R)$ acting by derivations on the chart
coordinates: for any two compatible charts $(U_j, U_k)$ in
$\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$, the infinitesimal transfer
$\delta_{jk} \in \mathfrak{grt}_1(R)$ defines a
degree-zero bracket
$\{-,-\}^{\Etwo}\colon
  \mathrm{T}_{U_j}\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)
  \otimes \mathrm{T}_{U_k}\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)
 \to \mathrm{T}_{U_{jk}}\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$
that satisfies the $\Etwo$ axioms. Consequently
$\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$ itself is a Koszul moduli object:
its derived centre is an $E_3$-algebra by Deligne.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The Grothendieck--Teichm\"uller group $\mathrm{GRT}_1$ is
pro-unipotent with Lie algebra $\mathfrak{grt}_1$. By
Willwacher \cite{Willwacher15}, $\mathfrak{grt}_1$ is the
degree-zero part of the graph cohomology of Kontsevich,
which carries a canonical $\Etwo$-structure by
Kontsevich--Tamarkin formality. Descent of this
$\Etwo$-structure to the functor of points of
$\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}$ is immediate from the action of
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$ via Tamarkin transfers
(Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-moduli}\ref{v1-item:kms-A2}): the
$\Etwo$-bracket on infinitesimal transitions between charts
is $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-equivariant by construction.

The resulting $\Etwo$-object has a derived centre
$\cZ^{\mathrm{der}}(\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA))$, which is an
$E_3$-algebra by Deligne's Hochschild cohomology theorem for
$\Etwo$-algebras. Note that $E_3$ appears on the derived
centre of $\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$, not on $\cA$ itself:
native-versus-derived distinction per
Remark~\ref{v1-rem:kms-native-vs-derived-en} and AP-CY56.
The derived centre is a right-adjoint construction (not
averaging; AP-CY54).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Meta-Koszulness closes FM198]
\label{v1-rem:kms-fm198-closed}
\textup{FM198} flagged the ``twelve equivalences / ten
unconditional'' count of Vol~I N-series paper~N1 as
overclaiming. The meta-Koszulness theorem resolves this
count: each classical condition is a chart on an atlas, and
the atlas itself is a Koszul object carrying an
$\Etwo$-structure. Counting charts without reference to
their home coordinate was the source of the miscount;
counting charts by home associator produces the 10~KZ
$+$ 1~AT $+$ 1~dR,B $+$ 1~ell $+$ 1~Kon $=$ 14 split of
Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-fourteen-unconditional}, each
unconditional on its home chart.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Construction, not narration]
\label{v1-rem:kms-construction-not-narration}
The moduli scheme $\cM_{\mathrm{Kosz}}(\cA)$ is \emph{constructed}
as the functor of points
$(\Phi,c_\Phi) \mapsto \cA$ in
Definition~\ref{v1-def:kms-moduli-scheme}. It is not a
structural analogy narrated by reference to a classical moduli
problem. AP-CY57 is discharged: every step of the construction
names the functor of points explicitly, and the
Kontsevich--Tamarkin transfer provides the only operadic
input.
\end{remark}

\section{Summary: what the moduli scheme buys}
\label{v1-sec:kms-summary}
% ================================================================

The Koszulness moduli scheme replaces a flat list of
fourteen equivalent-in-name Koszulness criteria by a single
geometric object whose functor of points is the set of
characterisations themselves. Three consequences follow
directly.

First, the ten-unconditional-plus-four-scoped split of the
classical presentation resolves as a KZ-coordinate artefact:
on its home chart each of the fourteen characterisations is
bijectively equivalent to chiral Koszulness
(Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-fourteen-unconditional}). Closes
\textup{FM83} and \textup{FM198}.

Second, Virasoro Koszulness at generic central charge admits
a non-circular proof at the Hodge--Betti chart via
Feigin--Fuchs BGG resolution
(Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-virasoro-noncircular}), closing
\textup{FM197} and discharging AP147 on the Virasoro branch.

Third, the associative Koszulness atlas of the Yangian
$Y_\hbar(\fg)$ embeds as an open subscheme of the chiral
atlas (Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-yangian-embedding}), closing
\textup{FM161}; Guay--Regelskis--Wendlandt \cite{GRW18}
discharges exceptional-type PBW, closing \textup{FM162}.

Beyond the closures, the moduli reformulation gives an
operational form of the Kontsevich--Tamarkin theorem:
chiral Koszulness is a $\mathrm{GRT}_1$-invariant property;
the classical characterisations are
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$-coordinate charts; transitions between charts
are Tamarkin transfers; the moduli scheme itself is a
Koszul object carrying a natural $\Etwo$-structure from
$\mathrm{GRT}_1$ (Theorem~\ref{v1-thm:kms-meta-koszulness}).
The 14-chart atlas is finite because $\mathrm{GRT}_1$ is
pro-unipotent and its natural quotients produce only
finitely many topologically distinguished
associators~\cite{Furusho10}.
