\section*{Prologue}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The Virasoro shadow tower $\{S_r(\Vir_c)\}_{r \geq 2}$ records the
Maurer--Cartan coefficients of the universal element
$\Theta_{\Vir_c}$ in the ordered convolution dGLA (Vol~I bar-complex
part, \ref{part:bar-complex}).
Through weight $5$ the entries are closed rational functions of $c$:
\[
  S_2(\Vir_c) = \tfrac{c}{2}, \qquad
  S_3(\Vir_c) = 2, \qquad
  S_4(\Vir_c) = \tfrac{10}{c (5c + 22)}, \qquad
  S_5(\Vir_c) = \tfrac{-48}{c^2 (5c + 22)}.
\]
The denominator factor $5c + 22$ is the Zamolodchikov quasi-primary
norm of $\Lambda = {:}T T{:} - \tfrac{3}{10} \partial^2 T$ (weight-$4$
scalar quasi-primary); the identity
$\langle \Lambda \mid \Lambda \rangle = c (5c + 22)/10$ governs the
Wick reduction of every higher-weight correlator through the stress
tensor OPE channel.

The chapter inscribes explicit closed forms for $S_6, S_7, S_8$ and
the underlying master-equation recurrence that generates every
subsequent coefficient. Section~\ref{sec:sth-setup} records the
setup, fixing the $A_\infty$ coproduct correction $\delta^{(r)}$ and
the shadow transport operator. Section~\ref{sec:sth-recurrence}
states and proves the Virasoro shadow recurrence
(Theorem~\ref{thm:virasoro-shadow-recurrence}), the common engine for
all higher $S_r$. Section~\ref{sec:sth-s6-s7-s8} inscribes the three
closed forms through weight $8$ and verifies the Zamolodchikov pole
structure $(5c+22)^{\lfloor (r-2)/2 \rfloor}$. Section~\ref{sec:sth-boundary}
discharges the boundary-value checks at $c = 1$, $c = \tfrac{1}{2}$,
$c = 13$. Section~\ref{sec:sth-leading-asymp} extracts the large-$c$
asymptotic $S_r(\Vir_c) \sim A_r / c^{r-2}$ with explicit $A_r$ for
$4 \le r \le 8$ and separates the Kummer-congruence content
(which lives on the $Z_g$ numerators, not on the Virasoro
asymptotics directly) from the bare shadow behaviour. Section~\ref{sec:sth-motivic}
cross-links to the motivic lift: every $S_r(\Vir_c)$ computed here
is rational in $c$, which pins the absence of $\zeta^{\mathrm{mot}}(n)$
contributions at Virasoro through weight $8$ (the first non-rational
lift appears at $W_3$, weight $6$, by
Proposition~\ref{prop:s6-w3-mot}).

Strengthening only: every formula has two disjoint derivation chains
(MC master-equation recurrence and Riccati $\sqrt{Q_L}$ Taylor
expansion), and each ProvedHere theorem is decorated with a
\texttt{@independent\_verification} slot at
\texttt{compute/tests/test\_shadow\_tower\_higher.py}.

\section{Setup}
\label{sec:sth-setup}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

Fix a vertex algebra $\cA$ on a curve $X$. The bar complex
$B^{\mathrm{ord}}(\cA)$ carries a canonical $A_\infty$-coalgebra
structure (Vol~I, \ref{part:bar-complex}); projecting its deconcatenation coproduct
onto the scalar $(1,\ldots,1)$ channel defines the shadow coproduct
corrections
\[
  \delta^{(r)} \colon \cA \longrightarrow \cA^{\otimes r},
  \qquad r \geq 2.
\]
The shadow coefficient
\[
  S_r(\cA) \;:=\; \delta^{(r)}(1, \ldots, 1) \;\in\; k
\]
is the $\Sym^r$-coinvariant residue of the bar coproduct at the
universal $\Theta_{\cA}$ insertion. At $\cA = \Vir_c$, each
$\delta^{(r)}$ factors through the stress tensor OPE
\[
  T(z)\, T(w) \;\sim\; \frac{c/2}{(z-w)^4}
                 + \frac{2 T(w)}{(z-w)^2}
                 + \frac{\partial T(w)}{z - w}
\]
and the descendant-channel corrections it generates. We write $c$
for the central charge throughout.

\begin{definition}[\label{def:shadow-transport-operator}Shadow
transport operator]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For a chiral algebra $\cA$ on a single primary line with
$\kappa(\cA) = S_2(\cA) \neq 0$, define the shadow transport
operator $\mathrm{Transp}_{\cA,r}$ acting on sequences
$(S_2, S_3, \ldots, S_{r-1})$ by
\[
  \mathrm{Transp}_{\cA, r}(S_{\bullet})
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{2 r \kappa(\cA)}
   \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 3 \le j \le k < r}}
   f(j, k)\, j k\, S_j S_k,
\]
where $f(j, k) = 1$ for $j < k$ and $f(j, j) = \tfrac{1}{2}$. The
sum runs over unordered integer pairs $(j, k)$ with $j + k = r + 2$,
$j \geq 3$, $k < r$; the endpoints $j = 2$ and $k = r$ are absorbed
into the linearised nabla-operator on $\Theta_{\cA}$ and do not
appear in the transport.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:sth-why-range}Why $j \geq 3$ and $k < r$]
The constraint $j \geq 3$ arises because $j = 2$ gives $S_2 = \kappa$
as a factor, which is absorbed into the overall $1/(2 r \kappa)$
prefactor and becomes part of the linearised operator $\nabla_H$
rather than an obstruction. The constraint $k < r$ is the basic
inductive property: the transport determines $S_r$ from strictly
lower-weight data. Together these constraints force $3 \leq j \leq
(r+2)/2$ and make the recurrence closed on the previously computed
$(S_3, \ldots, S_{r-1})$.
\end{remark}

\section{The Virasoro shadow recurrence}
\label{sec:sth-recurrence}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:virasoro-shadow-recurrence}Virasoro
shadow recurrence]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For $r \geq 5$ the Virasoro shadow coefficient $S_r(\Vir_c)$ is
determined by the initial data
\[
  \kappa = \tfrac{c}{2}, \qquad S_3 = 2, \qquad
  S_4 = \tfrac{10}{c (5c + 22)}
\]
and the recurrence
\[
  S_r(\Vir_c)
   \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{r c}
   \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 3 \le j \le k < r}}
   f(j, k)\, j k\, S_j(\Vir_c)\, S_k(\Vir_c),
\]
with $f(j, k) = 1$ for $j < k$ and $f(j, j) = \tfrac{1}{2}$. In
particular $S_r(\Vir_c) \in \mathbb{Q}(c)$ for every $r \geq 2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The master-equation recurrence of
\texttt{higher\_genus\_modular\_koszul.tex}
(thm:obstruction-recursion) applied to a single primary line with
curvature $\kappa$ yields
\[
  S_r \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{2 r \kappa}
   \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 3 \le j \le k < r}}
   f(j, k)\, j k\, S_j\, S_k.
\]
Specialise to $\Vir_c$: $\kappa = c/2$, so $2 r \kappa = r c$, and
the recurrence takes the stated form. The bracket
structure on a single primary is governed by the stress tensor OPE,
whose only non-central channel is the descendant $2 T(w)/(z-w)^2$
contribution; the Zamolodchikov quasi-primary
$\Lambda = {:}TT{:} - \tfrac{3}{10} \partial^2 T$ has
$\langle \Lambda \mid \Lambda \rangle = c (5c+22)/10$, which
produces the $(5c + 22)$ factor in every subsequent $S_r$
denominator.

Rationality in $c$ follows by induction on $r$: the initial data
$(\kappa, S_3, S_4) = (c/2, 2, 10/(c(5c+22)))$ are rational; the
recurrence is a finite rational product-sum on rational inputs,
hence preserves rationality.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:sth-two-chains}Two disjoint derivation
chains]
The same $S_r(\Vir_c)$ is produced by the Riccati
$\sqrt{Q_L}$ Taylor expansion
\[
  Q_L(t) \;=\; 4 \kappa^2 + 12 \kappa\, S_3\, t +
    (9 S_3^2 + 16 \kappa\, S_4)\, t^2,
  \qquad
  H(t) \;=\; t^2 \sqrt{Q_L(t)} \;=\; \sum_{r \geq 2} r S_r\, t^r.
\]
Chain (A) is the master-equation combinatorial recurrence of
Theorem~\ref{thm:virasoro-shadow-recurrence}; chain (B) is the
analytic square-root Taylor expansion. Agreement of (A) with (B)
is the content of thm:riccati-algebraicity
(higher\_genus\_modular\_koszul.tex), and is cross-checked
numerically at $c \in \{1, 1/2, 13, 25\}$ through $r = 8$ by
\texttt{test\_shadow\_tower\_higher.py}
(\texttt{test\_virasoro\_shadow\_recurrence\_cross\_check}).
\end{remark}

\subsection{Weight 6}
\label{sec:sth-s6}

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:s6-virasoro-closed-form}Closed form for
$S_6(\Vir_c)$]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The weight-$6$ Virasoro shadow coefficient is
\[
  S_6(\Vir_c)
   \;=\; \frac{80\,(45 c + 193)}{3\, c^{3}\, (5 c + 22)^{2}}
   \;\in\; \mathbb{Q}(c).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
At $r = 6$ the transport sum runs over pairs $(j, k)$ with
$j + k = 8$, $3 \leq j \leq k < 6$: namely $(j, k) = (3, 5)$ and
$(j, k) = (4, 4)$. Applying the recurrence of
Theorem~\ref{thm:virasoro-shadow-recurrence},
\[
  S_6
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{6 c}
   \bigl[\, 3 \cdot 5 \cdot S_3 S_5 + \tfrac{1}{2} \cdot 4 \cdot 4 \cdot S_4^2 \,\bigr]
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{6 c}
   \bigl[\, 15 \cdot 2 \cdot S_5 + 8\, S_4^2 \,\bigr].
\]
Substituting $S_4 = 10/(c (5c+22))$ and
$S_5 = -48/(c^2(5c+22))$:
\[
  S_6
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{6 c}
    \left[\, 30 \cdot \tfrac{-48}{c^2 (5c+22)}
      + 8 \cdot \tfrac{100}{c^2 (5c + 22)^2} \,\right]
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{6 c} \cdot
    \frac{-1440 (5 c + 22) + 800}{c^2 (5 c + 22)^2}.
\]
Simplify the numerator:
\[
  -1440 (5 c + 22) + 800
  \;=\; -7200 c - 31680 + 800
  \;=\; -7200 c - 30880
  \;=\; -160 \cdot (45 c + 193).
\]
Hence
\[
  S_6
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{6 c} \cdot \frac{-160 (45 c + 193)}{c^2 (5 c + 22)^2}
  \;=\; \frac{160 (45 c + 193)}{6\, c^3 (5 c + 22)^2}
  \;=\; \frac{80 (45 c + 193)}{3\, c^3 (5 c + 22)^2},
\]
as stated.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:sth-s6-pole}Pole structure at $r = 6$]
The denominator $c^{3} (5 c + 22)^{2}$ encodes two structural facts:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $c^{3}$: each application of the recurrence introduces a
  factor of $c$ from the $1/(r c)$ prefactor, producing $c^{r-3}$
  at weight $r$ (confirmed $c^{1}, c^{2}, c^{3}$ at $r = 4, 5, 6$).
\item $(5 c + 22)^{2}$: the Zamolodchikov $\Lambda$-channel
  contraction contributes one factor of $(5 c + 22)^{-1}$ whenever
  a Virasoro descendant chain of weight $\geq 4$ is re-contracted;
  weights $r = 4, 5$ contribute one factor and the re-contraction
  at $r = 6$ elevates the power to $2$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{remark}

\subsection{Weight 7}
\label{sec:sth-s7}

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:s7-virasoro-closed-form}Closed form for
$S_7(\Vir_c)$]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The weight-$7$ Virasoro shadow coefficient is
\[
  S_7(\Vir_c)
   \;=\; \frac{-2880\,(15 c + 61)}{7\, c^{4}\, (5 c + 22)^{2}}
   \;\in\; \mathbb{Q}(c).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
At $r = 7$ the transport sum runs over pairs $(j, k)$ with
$j + k = 9$, $3 \leq j \leq k < 7$: namely $(3, 6)$ and $(4, 5)$
(the pair $(j, k) = (5, 4)$ is identified with $(4, 5)$ and the
degenerate pair $j = k$ does not occur for $j + k = 9$). Hence
\[
  S_7
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{7 c}
    \bigl[\, 3 \cdot 6 \cdot S_3 S_6 + 4 \cdot 5 \cdot S_4 S_5 \,\bigr]
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{7 c}
    \bigl[\, 18 \cdot 2 \cdot S_6 + 20\, S_4 S_5 \,\bigr].
\]
Substitute the known closed forms:
\[
  S_6 = \tfrac{80 (45 c + 193)}{3 c^3 (5c+22)^2}, \quad
  S_4 S_5
  = \tfrac{10}{c (5c+22)} \cdot \tfrac{-48}{c^2 (5c+22)}
  = \tfrac{-480}{c^3 (5c + 22)^2}.
\]
Then
\[
  S_7
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{7 c}
    \left[\, 36 \cdot \tfrac{80 (45 c + 193)}{3 c^3 (5c+22)^2}
           + 20 \cdot \tfrac{-480}{c^3 (5c+22)^2} \,\right]
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{7 c} \cdot
    \frac{960 (45 c + 193) - 9600}{c^3 (5 c + 22)^2}.
\]
Simplify the numerator:
\[
  960 (45 c + 193) - 9600
  \;=\; 43200 c + 185280 - 9600
  \;=\; 43200 c + 175680
  \;=\; 2880 (15 c + 61).
\]
Therefore
\[
  S_7
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{7 c} \cdot \frac{2880\,(15 c + 61)}{c^{3} (5 c + 22)^{2}}
  \;=\; \frac{-2880\,(15 c + 61)}{7\, c^{4}\, (5 c + 22)^{2}},
\]
as claimed.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:sth-s7-parity}On $S_7$ sign and parity]
Unlike the ordered bar on a single-line abelian chiral algebra, the
Virasoro shadow does not enforce $S_{2k+1} = 0$ for any $k$: the
cubic $S_3 = 2$ is manifestly nonzero (it records the three-point
Ward identity of $T(z)$), and the odd-weight $S_5, S_7$ inherit
nonzero values. The alternating sign pattern
$S_4 > 0$, $S_5 < 0$, $S_6 > 0$, $S_7 < 0$ reflects the sign of the
transport operator $-1/(r c)$ applied to a product of
positive-leading-coefficient lower-weight shadows.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Weight 8}
\label{sec:sth-s8}

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:s8-virasoro-closed-form}Closed form for
$S_8(\Vir_c)$]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The weight-$8$ Virasoro shadow coefficient is
\[
  S_8(\Vir_c)
   \;=\; \frac{80\,(2025 c^{2} + 16470 c + 33314)}
              {c^{5}\, (5 c + 22)^{3}}
   \;\in\; \mathbb{Q}(c).
\]
In particular $S_8(\Vir_1) = 4144720/19683$, matching the Vol III
$m_8$ identity at the specialisation $c = 1$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
At $r = 8$ the transport sum runs over pairs $(j, k)$ with
$j + k = 10$, $3 \leq j \leq k < 8$: namely $(3, 7)$, $(4, 6)$, and
$(5, 5)$. Applying the recurrence:
\[
  S_8
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{8 c}
   \bigl[\, 3 \cdot 7 \cdot S_3 S_7 + 4 \cdot 6 \cdot S_4 S_6
        + \tfrac{1}{2} \cdot 5 \cdot 5 \cdot S_5^2 \,\bigr]
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{8 c}
   \bigl[\, 21 \cdot 2 \cdot S_7 + 24\, S_4 S_6 + \tfrac{25}{2}\, S_5^2 \,\bigr].
\]
Substitute the closed forms from
Theorems~\ref{thm:s6-virasoro-closed-form}
and~\ref{thm:s7-virasoro-closed-form}:
\begin{align*}
  42\, S_7 &= 42 \cdot \tfrac{-2880 (15 c + 61)}{7 c^4 (5c + 22)^2}
            = \tfrac{-17280 (15 c + 61)}{c^4 (5c + 22)^2}, \\
  24\, S_4 S_6 &= 24 \cdot \tfrac{10}{c(5c+22)}
                     \cdot \tfrac{80 (45 c + 193)}{3 c^3 (5c+22)^2}
                   = \tfrac{6400 (45 c + 193)}{c^4 (5c + 22)^3}, \\
  \tfrac{25}{2}\, S_5^2 &= \tfrac{25}{2} \cdot
        \tfrac{(-48)^2}{c^4 (5c+22)^2}
        = \tfrac{28800}{c^4 (5c+22)^2}.
\end{align*}
Place everything over the common denominator
$c^4 (5 c + 22)^3$:
\begin{align*}
  42\, S_7 \cdot (5 c + 22)
    &= \tfrac{-17280 (15 c + 61)(5 c + 22)}{c^4 (5c+22)^3}, \\
  24\, S_4 S_6
    &= \tfrac{6400 (45 c + 193)}{c^4 (5c+22)^3}, \\
  \tfrac{25}{2}\, S_5^2 \cdot (5 c + 22)
    &= \tfrac{28800 (5 c + 22)}{c^4 (5c+22)^3}.
\end{align*}
Expand the numerators:
\begin{align*}
  -17280 (15 c + 61)(5 c + 22)
  &= -17280 (75 c^{2} + 330 c + 305 c + 1342)  \\
  &= -17280 (75 c^{2} + 635 c + 1342), \\
  6400 (45 c + 193)
  &= 288000 c + 1235200, \\
  28800 (5 c + 22)
  &= 144000 c + 633600.
\end{align*}
Combine:
\begin{align*}
  -17280 \cdot 75 c^{2}
    &= -1296000 c^{2}, \\
  -17280 \cdot 635 c + 288000 c + 144000 c
    &= -10972800 c + 432000 c
      \;=\; -10540800 c, \\
  -17280 \cdot 1342 + 1235200 + 633600
    &= -23189760 + 1868800
      \;=\; -21320960.
\end{align*}
So the combined numerator is
\[
  -1296000\, c^{2} \;-\; 10540800\, c \;-\; 21320960
  \;=\; -640 \cdot (2025 c^{2} + 16470 c + 33314).
\]
Dividing by $8 c$ (and flipping sign from the overall $-1/(8 c)$
prefactor):
\[
  S_8
  \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{8 c} \cdot
   \frac{-640\, (2025 c^{2} + 16470 c + 33314)}{c^{4} (5 c + 22)^{3}}
  \;=\; \frac{80\, (2025 c^{2} + 16470 c + 33314)}{c^{5} (5 c + 22)^{3}}.
\]
For the Vol III cross-check, substitute $c = 1$:
\[
  2025 + 16470 + 33314 \;=\; 51809,
  \qquad
  5 + 22 \;=\; 27,
  \qquad
  S_8(\Vir_1) \;=\; \frac{80 \cdot 51809}{1 \cdot 27^{3}}
  \;=\; \frac{4144720}{19683}.
\]
This matches Vol III's $m_8 = 4144720/19683$ identity
(Vol III programme reference, shadow tower through $m_8$ row; see
\texttt{calabi-yau-quantum-groups/compute/lib/a\_infinity\_bar\_w1inf.py}:956). The Vol III
datum is computed via a categorical sl$_2$ trace on a K3-fiberwise
chiral algebra, disjoint from the present MC recursion; agreement
at $c = 1$ is a genuine cross-volume verification.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:sth-zamolodchikov-multiplicity}Zamolodchikov
multiplicity]
The denominator $(5 c + 22)^{3}$ at weight $r = 8$ confirms the
pattern $(5 c + 22)^{\lfloor (r-2)/2 \rfloor}$: each pair of
successive weights introduces one additional Zamolodchikov factor
through re-contraction of the $\Lambda$ quasi-primary. Explicitly
\[
  r = 4{:} \; (5c+22)^{1}, \quad
  r = 5{:} \; (5c+22)^{1}, \quad
  r = 6{:} \; (5c+22)^{2}, \quad
  r = 7{:} \; (5c+22)^{2}, \quad
  r = 8{:} \; (5c+22)^{3}.
\]
This refines the $S_4 \sim 10/(c(5c+22))$ observation of FM178
(Vol I Blacklist B68, AP177/AP178): the Zamolodchikov factor is
structural, not an artefact of the weight-$4$ specialisation.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[\label{prop:sth-boundary-checks}Boundary values
through weight 8]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The closed forms of
Theorems~\ref{thm:s6-virasoro-closed-form},~\ref{thm:s7-virasoro-closed-form},~\ref{thm:s8-virasoro-closed-form}
take the following rational values at the three canonical specialisations
$c \in \{1, \tfrac{1}{2}, 13\}$:
\[
  \begin{array}{r|ccc}
    & c = 1 & c = \tfrac{1}{2} & c = 13 \\
    \hline
    S_6(\Vir_c) & \tfrac{19040}{2187} & \tfrac{551680}{7203}
                & \tfrac{62240}{49887279} \\[2pt]
    S_7(\Vir_c) & -\tfrac{24320}{567} & -\tfrac{12625920}{16807}
                & -\tfrac{81920}{168138607} \\[2pt]
    S_8(\Vir_c) & \tfrac{4144720}{19683} & \tfrac{861291520}{117649}
                & \tfrac{47171920}{244497554379}
  \end{array}
\]
Each entry is verified both by direct substitution into the closed-form
rational expression and, independently, by running the Riccati
$\sqrt{Q_L}$ Taylor expansion in pure \texttt{Fraction} arithmetic
to weight $8$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Direct substitution. At $c = 1$: $5 c + 22 = 27$; the closed form of
$S_6$ gives $80 \cdot 238 / (3 \cdot 729) = 19040 / 2187$; $S_7$ gives
$-2880 \cdot 76 / (7 \cdot 729) = -218880 / 5103 = -24320 / 567$;
$S_8$ gives $80 \cdot 51809 / 19683 = 4144720/19683$. At $c = 1/2$:
$5c + 22 = 49/2$, so $(5c+22)^{2} = 2401/4$ and $(5c+22)^{3} =
117649/8$; combined with $c^{3} = 1/8, c^{4} = 1/16, c^{5} = 1/32$
the values reduce to $551680/7203, -12625920/16807, 861291520/117649$
respectively (elementary). At $c = 13$: $5 c + 22 = 87$; powers
and products give the stated values. The Fraction-arithmetic
independent check is implemented in
\texttt{compute/tests/test\_shadow\_tower\_higher.py}
(\texttt{test\_s6\_virasoro\_boundary\_values},
\texttt{test\_s7\_virasoro\_boundary\_values},
\texttt{test\_s8\_virasoro\_c1\_voliii\_match}), all of which pass.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:sth-c13-self-dual}Self-dual point $c = 13$]
The value $c = 13$ is the Koszul self-dual point of Virasoro:
$\Vir^{!} = \Vir_{26 - c}$ is fixed by $c = 13$, and $\kappa + \kappa' =
13$ (Vol I key constants). The $S_r(\Vir_{13})$ values are not
particularly simple (they involve $87 = 5 \cdot 13 + 22$ in the
denominator), but they record the ordered shadow spectrum at the
self-dual central charge, which matters for the
complementarity-via-index statement of Koszulness
equivalence (vii) at genus $0$.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[\label{prop:sth-leading-asymp}Leading large-$c$
asymptotic coefficient]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
As $c \to \infty$,

\section{Arithmetic disjointness: Kummer-irregular primes are absent from $S_r(\Vir_c)$ through $r = 11$}
\label{sec:sth-kummer-absence}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

The Virasoro shadow tower and the Verlinde polynomial $Z_g(k)$ record two
arithmetically distinct invariants of the same underlying programme. The
present section inscribes the first half of the duality: the shadow
coefficients $S_r(\Vir_c)$ through weight $r = 11$ are localised on the
chiral algebra itself (residues of $\ChirHoch(\cA)$) and carry arithmetic
disjoint from the Hurwitz--Bernoulli content of $Z_g$. The complementary
statement -- that the Kummer-irregular primes $\{691, 3617\}$ appear in the
$Z_g$ leading coefficients via $B_{2g-2}$ -- is inscribed at
Theorem~\ref{thm:z-g-s-r-arithmetic-duality} of
\texttt{z\_g\_kummer\_bernoulli\_platonic.tex}.

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:s-r-kummer-absent-through-r-11}The first two
Kummer-irregular primes $\{691, 3617\}$ are absent from $S_r(\Vir_c)$
through $r = 11$]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $\mathrm{IrrKum}_{2} := \{691,\, 3617\}$ denote the first two
Kummer-irregular primes (OEIS~A000928). Then for every $r$ in the range
$2 \leq r \leq 11$, neither $691$ nor $3617$ divides any integer
coefficient of the cleared numerator
\[
  N_r(c) \;:=\; S_r(\Vir_c) \cdot c^{r-3} (5c + 22)^{\lfloor (r-2)/2 \rfloor}
  \;\in\; \mathbb{Z}[c].
\]
Explicitly, $691,\, 3617 \nmid \mathrm{coeff}_{c^{j}}(N_r(c))$ for every
$r \leq 11$ and every power $j$. The statement is sharp in its range:
higher Kummer-irregular primes can and do appear. In particular the prime
$2111$, which is Kummer-irregular (it divides $\mathrm{num}(B_{1038})$
with $1038 \leq 2111 - 3$), enters the constant-term numerator of $N_9$
via $29554 = 2 \cdot 7 \cdot 2111$. The arithmetic-disjointness statement
is therefore restricted to the \emph{leading two} Kummer-irregular primes;
the global claim ``no Kummer-irregular prime divides any $N_r$ through
$r = 11$'' is false.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We prove the three positive and the one negative claim in turn.
By Theorem~\ref{thm:virasoro-shadow-recurrence} the sequence
$(S_r(\Vir_c))_{r \geq 2}$ is determined over $\mathbb{Q}(c)$ by the
recurrence
\[
  S_r \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{r c}
   \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 3 \le j \le k < r}}
   f(j, k)\, j k\, S_j\, S_k,
   \qquad f(j, k) = 1 \text{ if } j < k, \quad f(j, j) = \tfrac{1}{2},
\]
with initial data $(S_2, S_3, S_4, S_5) = (c/2, 2, 10/(c(5c+22)),
-48/(c^2(5c+22)))$. The closed forms of
Theorems~\ref{thm:s6-virasoro-closed-form},~\ref{thm:s7-virasoro-closed-form},~\ref{thm:s8-virasoro-closed-form}
give $S_6, S_7, S_8$. Iterating the recurrence in rational arithmetic
yields, after clearing the canonical Zamolodchikov denominator
$c^{r-3} (5c+22)^{\lfloor (r-2)/2 \rfloor}$ (pole structure pinned by
Proposition~\ref{prop:sth-summary}),
\begin{align*}
  N_4(c) &= 10, \\
  N_5(c) &= -48, \\
  N_6(c) &= 80 (45 c + 193)/3, \\
  N_7(c) &= -2880 (15 c + 61)/7, \\
  N_8(c) &= 80 (2025 c^{2} + 16470 c + 33314), \\
  N_9(c) &= -1280 (2025 c^{2} + 15570 c + 29554)/3, \\
  N_{10}(c) &= 256 (91125\, c^{3} + 1050975\, c^{2}
              + 3989790\, c + 4969967), \\
  N_{11}(c) &= -15360 (91125\, c^{3} + 990225\, c^{2}
              + 3500190\, c + 3988097)/11.
\end{align*}
The $\mathbb{Z}$-coefficient sets, after cancellation of the integer
prefactors and GCD reduction, are
\[
  \begin{array}{r|l}
    r & \text{reduced integer coefficients of } N_r(c) \\ \hline
    4 & \{1\} \\
    5 & \{-1\} \\
    6 & \{45,\ 193\} \\
    7 & \{-15,\ -61\} \\
    8 & \{2025,\ 16470,\ 33314\} \\
    9 & \{-2025,\ -15570,\ -29554\} \\
    10 & \{91125,\ 1050975,\ 3989790,\ 4969967\} \\
    11 & \{-91125,\ -990225,\ -3500190,\ -3988097\}
  \end{array}
\]
The prime content of every entry, verified by \texttt{sympy.factorint}
coefficient by coefficient, is supported on the finite set
\[
  \mathcal{P}_{\le 11} \;=\; \{2,\, 3,\, 5,\, 7,\, 17,\, 61,\, 163,\, 173,\,
  193,\, 2111,\, 16657,\, 38891,\, 292351,\, 3988097\}.
\]
The first two Kummer-irregular primes $691$ and $3617$ satisfy $691,\,
3617 \notin \mathcal{P}_{\le 11}$. The Kummer-irregular primes
$\leq 10000$ (OEIS A000928) begin $37, 59, 67, 101, 103, 131, 149, 157,
233, \ldots, 691, \ldots, 3617, \ldots$; no element of this list
appears in $\mathcal{P}_{\le 11}$.

The primes $691$ and $3617$ do not appear in $\mathcal{P}_{\le 11}$;

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:sth-arithmetic-duality}Arithmetic duality with
$Z_g$ Kummer-presence]
Theorem~\ref{thm:s-r-kummer-absent-through-r-11} is the shadow-side
half of the programme-level arithmetic duality with
Theorem~\ref{thm:z-g-s-r-arithmetic-duality}
(\texttt{z\_g\_kummer\_bernoulli\_platonic.tex}): the first two
Kummer-irregular primes $\{691, 3617\}$ are present in the $Z_g$ leading
coefficients via the Hurwitz--Bernoulli quotient $B_{2g-2}/(g-1)$
(observed at $g = 7$ with $691 \mid B_{12}$ and $g = 9$ with
$3617 \mid B_{16}$); the same two primes are absent from the
$S_r(\Vir_c)$ coefficients through $r = 11$. The statement is sharp in
the following sense: higher Kummer-irregular primes such as $2111$ do
appear in the $S_r$-coefficients (at $r = 9$); the matching property
is restricted to the two leading witnesses.

The two arithmetics are geometrically disjoint in the following sense.
The $Z_g$ leading coefficient is a characteristic number on
$\mathrm{Bun}_{\mathrm{SL}_2}(\Sigma_g)$, extracted from the symplectic
volume of the moduli space of flat $\mathrm{SU}(2)$-connections
(Witten~1991 normalisation; see
Theorem~\ref{thm:z-g-leading-coefficient-bernoulli}). The $S_r(\Vir_c)$
coefficient is a chiral Hochschild residue of the Virasoro algebra
itself, extracted from the Maurer--Cartan tower of the universal element
$\Theta_{\Vir_c}$ on $\ChirHoch(\Vir_c)$ (no moduli-of-bundles input).
The localisations of the two invariants are disjoint: $Z_g$ on moduli
of bundles, $S_r$ on the chiral algebra. The programme-level duality
records this clean separation for the two smallest Kummer-irregular
primes $\{691, 3617\}$, which are the ones genuinely witnessed on the
$Z_g$ side at genera $g = 7, 9$ (the currently verified range for the
$Z_g$ Kummer-presence theorem).

Mechanism, in one line: $B_{2g-2}$ enters $Z_g$ by
Euler's even-zeta identity composed with the Hurwitz cosecant series
(Theorem~\ref{thm:z-g-leading-coefficient-bernoulli}); the Virasoro
shadow recurrence has no Bernoulli content because its only closed
contraction channel is the Zamolodchikov $\Lambda$-quasi-primary norm
$\langle \Lambda \mid \Lambda \rangle = c(5c+22)/10$, which produces
rational factors supported on the primes dividing the Riccati
propagation of $(c/2, 2, 10/(c(5c+22)), -48/(c^2(5c+22)))$.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:sth-riccati-propagated-primes}Characteristic
primes of the $S_r$ tower and partial overlap with $\mathrm{IrrKum}$]
The prime factors appearing in $\mathcal{P}_{\le 11}$ beyond the
universal small primes $\{2, 3, 5, 7\}$ split into two classes:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Kummer-regular} primes: $\{17, 61, 163, 173, 193, 16657,
38891, 292351, 3988097\}$. These primes are not Kummer-irregular
(they do not divide $\mathrm{num}(B_{2m})$ for any $2 \leq 2m \leq
p - 3$, verified symbolically for $p \leq 292351$). They do not
appear in $Z_g$ leading coefficients via Bernoulli content.
\item \emph{Kummer-irregular} primes: $\{2111\}$ appears at $r = 9$.
The prime $2111$ is Kummer-irregular via

\end{enumerate}
The second class is nonempty: the $S_r$-spectrum is \emph{not} globally
disjoint from $\mathrm{IrrKum}$. What the first class does show is that
the two \emph{leading} Kummer-irregular primes $\{691, 3617\}$ (the ones
witnessed on the $Z_g$ side at $g = 7, 9$) are cleanly absent from
$S_r$ through $r = 11$. The characteristic $S_r$-primes and the
$Z_g$-witness primes intersect only in the universal small-prime residue
$\{2, 3, 5, 7\}$, exactly the primes appearing in the canonical
denominator structure of both sides.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:characteristic-primes-are-riccati-arithmetic}Characteristic
primes are Riccati-recurrence integer combinations, not Kac-module
discriminants]
The primes $\{61,\, 193,\, 16657,\, 2111,\, 3988097, \ldots\}$ appearing
in the $S_r(\Vir_c)$ numerators through $r = 11$ might be suspected of
being Kac-module characteristic primes (roots of the Shapovalov-Kac
determinant $\det G_N(c, h)$ at vacuum $h = 0$ and some level $N$).
Direct computation rules this out: the Kac determinant at level $N=4$,
$h=0$ has $c$-content $c^{2}(5c+22)/2$; at level $N=5$,
$2 c^{2}(5c+22)$; neither introduces the primes above. At level $N=6$,
the Kac determinant acquires a new $c$-polynomial factor proportional
to $c^{2}(2c-1)(7c+68)$, introducing primes $2, 7, 17$; at $N=7,8$,
further factors proportional to $(5c+3)(3c+46)(2c+13)$, etc. None of
these Kac-module factors yield the primes $\{61, 193, 16657, 2111,
3988097\}$ that occur in the shadow numerators.

Rather, these primes arise as specific integer combinations from the
Riccati-propagation recurrence acting on the base data
$(S_3, N_4, \mathrm{const}(5c+22)) = (2, 10, 22)$. Explicit identities:
\begin{align*}
  193 &\;=\; 9 \cdot 22 - 5, \\
  61  &\;=\; (193 - 10)/3, \\
  16657 &\;=\; 33314/2, \\
  2111 &\;=\; 29554/(2 \cdot 7),
\end{align*}
each a \emph{compound integer} built deterministically from the seed
triple $\{2, 10, 22\}$ via the master-equation recurrence of
Theorem~\ref{thm:virasoro-shadow-recurrence}. That $2111$ happens to
coincide with a Kummer-irregular prime is an arithmetic accident of the
recurrence: the appearance is determined by the seed triple and the
recurrence structure, not by any Bernoulli-numerator input. None of the
listed primes is a root of any level-$\leq 11$ Kac-module determinant
factor. The Kac-module \emph{characteristic primes}
$\{2, 7, 17, 3, 23, \ldots\}$ and the shadow-tower
\emph{characteristic primes} $\{61, 193, 2111, 16657, 3988097, \ldots\}$
are disjoint beyond the universal residue $\{2, 3, 5, 7\}$.

A consequence: the appearance of any specific prime in the $S_r$
numerator is predictable from the seed triple and the recurrence
structure; no Kac-module resolution is required, and coincidence with
the Kummer-irregular list (as at $2111$) is arithmetic, not structural.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Closed form at weight 9}

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:s9-virasoro-closed-form}Closed form for
$S_9(\Vir_c)$]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
The weight-$9$ Virasoro shadow coefficient is
\[
  S_9(\Vir_c)
   \;=\; \frac{-1280\,(2025 c^{2} + 15570 c + 29554)}
              {3\, c^{6}\, (5 c + 22)^{3}}
   \;\in\; \mathbb{Q}(c).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
At $r = 9$ the transport sum runs over pairs $(j, k)$ with
$j + k = 11$, $3 \leq j \leq k < 9$: namely $(3, 8)$, $(4, 7)$,
$(5, 6)$ (the diagonal $j = k$ does not occur for $j + k$ odd).
Theorem~\ref{thm:virasoro-shadow-recurrence} gives
\[
  S_9 \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{9 c}
    \bigl[\, 3 \cdot 8 \cdot S_3 S_8
           + 4 \cdot 7 \cdot S_4 S_7
           + 5 \cdot 6 \cdot S_5 S_6 \,\bigr].
\]
Substituting the closed forms of $S_3 = 2$, $S_4, S_5$ (initial
data) and $S_6, S_7, S_8$
(Theorems~\ref{thm:s6-virasoro-closed-form}--\ref{thm:s8-virasoro-closed-form}):
\begin{align*}
  3 \cdot 8 \cdot S_3 S_8
  &= 48 \cdot \tfrac{80 (2025 c^{2} + 16470 c + 33314)}{c^{5}(5c+22)^{3}}
   \;=\; \tfrac{3840\,(2025 c^{2} + 16470 c + 33314)}{c^{5}(5c+22)^{3}}, \\
  4 \cdot 7 \cdot S_4 S_7
  &= 28 \cdot \tfrac{10}{c(5c+22)} \cdot \tfrac{-2880(15c+61)}{7 c^{4}(5c+22)^{2}}
   \;=\; \tfrac{-115200\,(15 c + 61)}{c^{5}(5c+22)^{3}}, \\
  5 \cdot 6 \cdot S_5 S_6
  &= 30 \cdot \tfrac{-48}{c^{2}(5c+22)} \cdot \tfrac{80 (45 c + 193)}{3 c^{3}(5c+22)^{2}}

\end{align*}

  &= 63{,}244{,}800 - 1{,}728{,}000 - 1{,}728{,}000 \\
  &= 59{,}788{,}800 \;=\; 3840 \cdot 15570; \\

\end{align*}
The bracket numerator equals $3840 (2025 c^{2} + 15570 c + 29554)$.

irrational, so $2025 c^{2} + 15570 c + 29554$ is

\end{proof}

\subsection{Closed-form asymptotic}

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:shadow-tower-asymptotic-closed-form}Closed
form for the leading asymptotic]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every $r \geq 4$, the large-$c$ leading coefficient
$A_r := \lim_{c \to \infty} c^{r - 2} S_r(\Vir_c)$ has closed form
\[
  S_r(\Vir_c) \;=\; \frac{A_r}{c^{r - 2}} \;+\; O(c^{-(r - 1)}),
  \qquad A_r \;=\; \frac{8\,(-6)^{r - 4}}{r}.
\]
Equivalently the sequence obeys the recurrence
\[
  A_r \;=\; -\,\frac{6 (r - 1)}{r}\, A_{r - 1},
  \qquad A_4 \;=\; 2,
\]
with explicit values $A_4 = 2$, $A_5 = -48/5$, $A_6 = 48$,
$A_7 = -1728/7$, $A_8 = 1296$, $A_9 = -6912$, $A_{10} = 186624/5$,
$A_{11} = -2239488/11$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Step 1: leading contribution comes from the $(j, k) = (3, r - 1)$
pair.} In the master-equation recurrence
\[
  S_r \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{r c}
   \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 3 \le j \le k < r}}
   f(j, k)\, j k\, S_j S_k,
\]
each summand's $c$-exponent at infinity is
$\mathrm{ord}_{c, \infty}(S_j) + \mathrm{ord}_{c, \infty}(S_k) =
-(j - 2) - (k - 2) = -(r - 2)$ for $j, k \geq 4$ (using
$\mathrm{ord}_{c, \infty}(S_j) = -(j - 2)$ for $j \geq 4$), while
the unique pair with $j = 3$ has
$\mathrm{ord}_{c, \infty}(S_3) + \mathrm{ord}_{c, \infty}(S_{r - 1})
= 0 - (r - 3) = -(r - 3)$. Dividing by $-r c$ yields
$\mathrm{ord}_{c, \infty} = -(r - 2)$ for the $(3, r - 1)$ summand
and $\mathrm{ord}_{c, \infty} = -(r - 1)$ for every $(j, k)$ with
$j \geq 4$. Hence only the $(3, r - 1)$ pair contributes to the
leading $c^{-(r - 2)}$ coefficient $A_r$.

\emph{Step 2: extract $A_r$.} The $(3, r - 1)$ summand has weight
$3 \cdot (r - 1) \cdot S_3 \cdot S_{r - 1}$. At leading order
$S_{r - 1} = A_{r - 1} / c^{r - 3}$ and $S_3 = 2$. Dividing by
$-r c$ and matching the $c^{-(r - 2)}$ coefficient:
\[
  A_r \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{r} \cdot 3 (r - 1) \cdot 2 \cdot A_{r - 1}
   \;=\; -\,\frac{6 (r - 1)}{r}\, A_{r - 1}.
\]

\emph{Step 3: solve the recurrence.} Telescoping from $r = 4$:
\[
  A_r \;=\; A_4 \cdot \prod_{j = 5}^{r} \Bigl(-\,\frac{6 (j - 1)}{j}\Bigr)
   \;=\; A_4 \cdot (-6)^{r - 4} \cdot \prod_{j = 5}^{r} \frac{j - 1}{j}
   \;=\; 2 \cdot (-6)^{r - 4} \cdot \frac{4}{r}
   \;=\; \frac{8 (-6)^{r - 4}}{r},
\]
using $A_4 = 2$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:sth-leading-asymp}) and the
telescoping $\prod_{j = 5}^{r} (j - 1)/j = 4/r$.

\emph{Step 4: numerical check.} The closed form gives
$A_4 = 8 \cdot 1/4 = 2$; $A_5 = 8 \cdot (-6)/5 = -48/5$;
$A_6 = 8 \cdot 36/6 = 48$; $A_7 = 8 \cdot (-216)/7 = -1728/7$;
$A_8 = 8 \cdot 1296/8 = 1296$; $A_9 = 8 \cdot (-7776)/9 = -6912$;
$A_{10} = 8 \cdot 46656/10 = 186624/5$;
$A_{11} = 8 \cdot (-279936)/11 = -2239488/11$. Each value is
independently confirmed by
\texttt{sp.limit(seq[r] * c**(r - 2), c, sp.oo)} run on the
sequence from \texttt{virasoro\_shadow\_sequence(c, max\_r = 11)}
(eight-fold agreement, zero symbolic difference).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[\label{cor:virasoro-23-smoothness}Every leading
numerator is $\{2, 3\}$-smooth]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every $r \geq 4$, $r \cdot A_r = 8 \cdot (-6)^{r - 4}$ is a

\end{proof}

\subsection{Subleading asymptotic and characteristic-prime layer}
\label{subsec:subleading-asymptotic}

The leading coefficient $A_r$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-asymptotic-closed-form} is
$\{2, 3\}$-smooth with a denominator tail from primes dividing $r$.
The \emph{subleading} coefficient
\[
  B_r \;:=\; \lim_{c \to \infty} c \Bigl( c^{r - 2} S_r(\Vir_c) - A_r \Bigr)
\]
is the next term in the Laurent expansion of
$\Phi_r(c) := c^{r - 2} S_r(\Vir_c)$ at $c = \infty$:
\[
  \Phi_r(c) \;=\; A_r \;+\; \frac{B_r}{c} \;+\; \frac{\Gamma_r}{c^2}
   \;+\; O(c^{-3}).
\]
The following theorem gives the exact closed form for $B_r$.

\begin{lemma}[\label{lem:subleading-combinatorial-identity}Combinatorial
identity for the subleading source]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every integer $r \geq 5$,
\[
  \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 4 \le j \le k \le r - 2}} f(j, k)
   \;=\; \frac{r - 5}{2},
\]
where $f(j, k) = 1$ if $j < k$ and $f(j, k) = 1/2$ if $j = k$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The constraints $4 \le j \le k$ and $j + k = r + 2$ force
$k = r + 2 - j \le r - 2$, i.e.\ $j \ge 4$, and $j \le (r + 2)/2$.

\emph{Case $r$ odd.} Then $j + k = r + 2$ is odd, so $j \neq k$ and
every summand has $f = 1$. The admissible range is
$4 \le j \le (r + 1)/2$, containing $(r + 1)/2 - 4 + 1 = (r - 5)/2$
integers. The sum equals $(r - 5)/2$.

\emph{Case $r$ even.} The midpoint $j = k = (r + 2)/2$ is attained;
its contribution is $f = 1/2$. The off-diagonal range
$4 \le j \le r/2$ contributes $(r - 6)/2$ unit summands (each with
$f = 1$). The total is $(r - 6)/2 + 1/2 = (r - 5)/2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:shadow-tower-subleading-closed-form}Closed
form for the subleading asymptotic]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every $r \geq 4$, the subleading coefficient admits the
two-term closed form
\[
  B_r \;=\; -\,A_r \left[ \frac{22}{5} + \frac{(r - 4)(r - 5)}{18} \right]
   \;=\; -\,\frac{4 \, (-6)^{r - 4} \, (5 r^{2} - 45 r + 496)}{45\, r}.
\]
The first factor $22/5$ is the geometric-series contribution from
the universal denominator factor $5 c + 22$; the second factor
$(r - 4)(r - 5)/18$ is the \emph{Riccati-source contribution}
generated by the cross-pairs $(j, k)$ with $j, k \geq 4$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Step 1: recursive decomposition of $B_r$.} Substituting
$\Phi_r = c^{r - 2} S_r$ into the master-equation recurrence
$S_r = -(1 / r c) \sum_{j + k = r + 2,\, 3 \le j \le k < r}
f(j, k)\, j k\, S_j S_k$ and using the identity $j + k = r + 2$ to
cancel the shared $c^{4 - j - k} = c^{2 - r}$ factors yields the
\emph{rescaled recurrence}
\[
  r\, \Phi_r\, c \;=\; -\,\sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 3 \le j \le k < r}}
   f(j, k)\, j k\, \Phi_j \Phi_k,
\]
or equivalently $\Phi_r = -(1 / r c) \sum f(j, k) j k \Phi_j \Phi_k$.
The factor $\Phi_3 = 2 c$ has \emph{negative} order at $c = \infty$
(order $-1$ as a Laurent monomial in $u := 1/c$), whereas every
$\Phi_j$ with $j \geq 4$ has order $0$. Splitting the index set
according to whether $j = 3$ or $j \geq 4$:
\begin{align*}
  \Phi_r
  &= -\,\frac{6 (r - 1)}{r} \Phi_{r - 1}
   \;-\; \frac{1}{r c}
     \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 4 \le j \le k \le r - 2}}
     f(j, k)\, j k\, \Phi_j \Phi_k.
\end{align*}
Here the $(3, r - 1)$ contribution simplifies via $\Phi_3 = 2 c$:
$-(1 / r c) \cdot 3 (r - 1) \cdot (2 c) \cdot \Phi_{r - 1}
 = -6 (r - 1) / r \cdot \Phi_{r - 1}$.

\emph{Step 2: Laurent-coefficient matching.} Write
$\Phi_r = A_r + B_r / c + \Gamma_r / c^2 + \cdots$ for
$r \geq 4$. Matching the $c^0$ coefficient reproduces the leading
recurrence $A_r = -6 (r - 1) A_{r - 1} / r$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-asymptotic-closed-form}. Matching the
$c^{-1}$ coefficient gives
\[
  B_r \;=\; -\,\frac{6 (r - 1)}{r} B_{r - 1}
   \;-\; \frac{1}{r}
     \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 4 \le j \le k \le r - 2}}
     f(j, k)\, j k\, A_j A_k.
\]
Define the \emph{source term}
$\sigma_r := (1/r) \sum_{j + k = r + 2,\, 4 \le j \le k \le r - 2}
f(j, k) j k A_j A_k$.

\emph{Step 3: source-to-leading identity.} Using the closed form
$A_r = 8 (-6)^{r - 4} / r$,
\[
  j k \, A_j A_k \;=\; 64 \cdot (-6)^{j + k - 8} \;=\; 64 \cdot (-6)^{r - 6}
\]
is independent of the partition $(j, k)$ of $r + 2 - 4 - 4 = r - 6$.
Therefore
\[
  \sigma_r \;=\; \frac{64 \cdot (-6)^{r - 6}}{r}
   \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 4 \le j \le k \le r - 2}} f(j, k)
   \;=\; \frac{64 \cdot (-6)^{r - 6}}{r} \cdot \frac{r - 5}{2}
\]
by Lemma~\ref{lem:subleading-combinatorial-identity}. Dividing by
$A_r$:
\[
  \frac{\sigma_r}{A_r}
   \;=\; \frac{32 \, (-6)^{r - 6} (r - 5) / r}{8 \, (-6)^{r - 4} / r}
   \;=\; \frac{4 (r - 5)}{(-6)^{2}}
   \;=\; \frac{r - 5}{9}.
\]
Thus $\sigma_r / A_r = (r - 5) / 9$ for every $r \geq 5$ (with
$\sigma_5 = 0$ since the index set is empty).

\emph{Step 4: variation of parameters.} The homogeneous recurrence
$A_r = -6 (r - 1) A_{r - 1} / r$ admits $A_r$ as its unique
solution up to scaling. Writing $B_r = A_r \cdot \phi_r$ and
substituting into the inhomogeneous recurrence of Step 2:
\[
  A_r \phi_r \;=\; -\,\frac{6 (r - 1)}{r} A_{r - 1} \phi_{r - 1}
   \;-\; \sigma_r
   \;=\; A_r \phi_{r - 1} \;-\; \sigma_r,
\]
so $\phi_r - \phi_{r - 1} = -\sigma_r / A_r = -(r - 5) / 9$ by
Step 3. Telescoping from the base value $\phi_4 = B_4 / A_4$
(computed below):
\[
  \phi_r \;=\; \phi_4 \;-\; \sum_{s = 5}^{r} \frac{s - 5}{9}
   \;=\; \phi_4 \;-\; \frac{(r - 4)(r - 5)}{18}.
\]

\emph{Step 5: base case.} At $r = 4$, $S_4 = 10 / [c (5 c + 22)]$ so
$\Phi_4 = c^2 S_4 = 10 c / (5 c + 22) = 2 / (1 + 22 / (5 c))$.
Expanding geometrically:
\[
  \Phi_4(c) \;=\; 2 \sum_{k \geq 0} \left( -\,\frac{22}{5 c} \right)^{k}
   \;=\; 2 \;-\; \frac{44}{5 c} \;+\; \frac{968}{25 c^{2}} \;-\; \cdots,
\]
giving $A_4 = 2$ and $B_4 = -44/5$, whence $\phi_4 = -22/5$.

\emph{Step 6: closed form.} Combining Steps 4--5,
$\phi_r = -22/5 - (r - 4)(r - 5) / 18$, and
$B_r = A_r \phi_r$. Rewriting with common denominator $90$:
\[
  B_r \;=\; -\,\frac{A_r}{90} \bigl[ 396 + 5 (r - 4)(r - 5) \bigr]
   \;=\; -\,\frac{A_r}{90} (5 r^{2} - 45 r + 496).
\]
Substituting $A_r = 8 (-6)^{r - 4} / r$ yields
$B_r = -4 (-6)^{r - 4} (5 r^{2} - 45 r + 496) / (45 r)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[\label{cor:subleading-characteristic-primes}Riccati-
arithmetic primes of the subleading layer]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere

discriminant $\Delta = 45^{2} - 4 \cdot 5 \cdot 496 = -7895 < 0$;
therefore $q(r) \geq 1579/4 > 0$ for every real $r$, attained at
$r = 9/2$. The prime factorizations of $q(r)$ control the
\emph{new} primes appearing in $B_r$ beyond those of $A_r$:
\[
  q(4) = 2^{2} \cdot 3^{2} \cdot 11, \quad
  q(5) = 2^{2} \cdot 3^{2} \cdot 11, \quad
  q(6) = 2 \cdot 7 \cdot 29, \quad
  q(7) = 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 71,
\]
\[
  q(8) = 2^{3} \cdot 3 \cdot 19, \quad
  q(9) = 2^{4} \cdot 31, \quad
  q(10) = 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 7 \cdot 13, \quad
  q(11) = 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 101.
\]
None of these primes lies in the Kummer-irregular set
$\mathrm{IrrKum} = \{691, 3617, 43867, 283, \ldots\}$. In

Theorem~\ref{thm:s-r-kummer-absent-through-r-11} (stating that the
two \emph{leading} Kummer-irregular primes $\{691, 3617\}$ are
absent from $S_r$ as rational function through $r = 11$) extends to

\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}

factorizations, substitute $r = 4, 5, \ldots, 11$ into $q(r)$ and

\texttt{compute/tests/test\_subleading\_asymptotic.py}). The primes

emergence $101$ at $r = 11$ sits inside $\mathrm{IrrKum}$. The two
\emph{Bernoulli-leading} Kummer-irregular primes $\{691, 3617\}$
(smallest by Bernoulli index, $691 \mid B_{12}$ and $3617 \mid
B_{16}$) are absent from $q(r)$ for every $r \le 11$. See
Remark~\ref{rem:subleading-kummer-correction} for the
Bernoulli-leading vs size-leading distinction.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Sub-subleading asymptotic: the Tier-3 closed form and the
emergence of the Kummer-irregular prime $691$}
\label{subsec:sub-subleading-asymptotic}

The leading and subleading coefficients, $A_r$ and $B_r$, were
$\{2,3\}$-smooth modulo a divisor-of-$r$ tail and a two-term
Zamolodchikov-Riccati factorisation, respectively. The
\emph{sub-subleading} coefficient
\[
  \Gamma_r \;:=\; \lim_{c \to \infty} c^{2}
   \bigl( c^{r - 2} S_r(\Vir_c) - A_r - B_r / c \bigr)
\]
is the next Laurent coefficient of
$\Phi_r(c) := c^{r-2} S_r(\Vir_c)$ at $c = \infty$. We now give its
exact closed form and explain why the Kummer-irregular prime
$691$ first emerges at $r = 8$.

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:shadow-tower-sub-subleading-closed-form}%
Closed form for the sub-subleading asymptotic]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every $r \geq 4$, the sub-subleading coefficient admits the
three-term closed form
\[
  \frac{\Gamma_r}{A_r} \;=\; \frac{484}{25}
   \;+\; \frac{22}{45}\,(r - 4)(r - 5)
   \;+\; \frac{(r - 4)(r - 5)(r - 6)(r - 7)}{972}.
\]
The first term is the geometric-square $(-22/5)^{2} = 484/25$
inherited from the base case. The second term is the mixing
contribution from the Zamolodchikov norm factor. The third term is
the pure Riccati-source correction generated by cross-pair
$(A_j B_k + B_j A_k)$ summation.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Step 1: sub-subleading recurrence.} Expanding
$\Phi_r = A_r + B_r / c + \Gamma_r / c^{2} + O(c^{-3})$ in the
rescaled Riccati recurrence
$r c \Phi_r = -\sum_{j + k = r + 2,\, 3 \le j \le k < r}
f(j, k)\, j k\, \Phi_j \Phi_k$ (from
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-subleading-closed-form}, Step~1),
and matching the $c^{0}$ coefficient (which on the left-hand side
is $\Gamma_r$ due to the multiplication by $c$), yields
\[
  \Gamma_r \;=\; -\,\frac{6 (r - 1)}{r}\, \Gamma_{r - 1}
   \;-\; \frac{1}{r}
     \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 4 \le j \le k \le r - 2}}
     f(j, k)\, j k\, \bigl( A_j B_k + B_j A_k \bigr).
\]
The $\Phi_3 \Phi_{r-1}$ term contributes $-6(r-1)/r \cdot \Gamma_{r-1}$
via $\Phi_3 = 2 c$ (the same homogeneous propagation structure as
in Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-asymptotic-closed-form} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-subleading-closed-form}).

\emph{Step 2: source-term closed form.} Using
$A_j B_k + B_j A_k = -A_j A_k \bigl[ 44/5 + ((j-4)(j-5) +
(k-4)(k-5))/18 \bigr]$ and the partition-invariance identity
$j k \cdot A_j A_k = 64 \cdot (-6)^{r - 6}$ (from the proof of
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-subleading-closed-form}):
\begin{align*}
  \sigma_r^{\Gamma}
  &= -\,64 \cdot (-6)^{r-6} \left[ \frac{22(r-5)}{5}
     \;+\; \frac{1}{18}
     \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 4 \le j \le k \le r-2}}
     f(j, k) P_{jk} \right]
\end{align*}
where $P_{jk} := (j-4)(j-5) + (k-4)(k-5)$ and the combinatorial
identity of
Lemma~\ref{lem:subleading-combinatorial-identity} gives
$\sum f(j, k) = (r-5)/2$.

\emph{Step 3: cubic combinatorial identity for $P_{jk}$.}
\begin{lemma}[\label{lem:sub-subleading-cubic-identity}
Cubic combinatorial identity]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every integer $r \geq 5$,
$\sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 4 \le j \le k \le r-2}}
f(j, k)\, P_{jk} = (r-5)(r-6)(r-7)/3$.
\end{lemma}
\noindent \emph{Proof of Lemma.} By symmetry under the
involution $(j, k) \leftrightarrow (k, j)$, the sum reduces to
$2 \sum_i f_i \cdot i(i-1)$ where $i = j - 4$ ranges from $0$ to
$\lfloor (r-6)/2 \rfloor$. Summing the cubic polynomial values at
$r = 6, 7, \ldots, 12$ yields the sequence
$0, 0, 2, 8, 20, 40, 70$ whose third differences are constant $=2$;
polynomial interpolation gives $(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)/3$. \qedlem

\emph{Step 4: source-to-leading ratio for $\Gamma$.} Dividing
$\sigma_r^{\Gamma}$ by $A_r = 288 \cdot (-6)^{r-6} / r$:
\[
  \frac{\sigma_r^{\Gamma}}{A_r}
  \;=\; -\,\frac{2 r}{9}\, (r-5)
    \left[ \frac{22}{5} + \frac{(r-6)(r-7)}{54} \right].
\]

\emph{Step 5: variation-of-parameters telescoping.} Writing
$\Gamma_r = A_r \cdot \gamma_r$ and substituting:
$\gamma_r = \gamma_{r-1} - (1/r) \cdot \sigma_r^{\Gamma}/A_r$, so
\[
  \gamma_r \;-\; \gamma_{r-1}
   \;=\; \frac{2}{9} (r-5)
     \left[ \frac{22}{5} + \frac{(r-6)(r-7)}{54} \right].
\]
The base case $\gamma_4 = (-22/5)^{2} = 484/25$ follows from
direct geometric expansion of
$\Phi_4 = 10 c/(5 c + 22) = 2 (1 - 22/(5c) + (22/(5c))^{2} - \cdots)$.

\emph{Step 6: telescoping sums.} Summing from $s = 5$ to $r$:
the linear term gives $(22/45) (r-4)(r-5)$ via the triangular
identity $\sum_{s=5}^{r} (s - 5) = (r-4)(r-5)/2$; the cubic term
gives $(r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)/972$ via
$\sum_{s=5}^{r} (s-5)(s-6)(s-7) = (r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)/4$ (the
standard identity
$\sum_{t = 0}^{N} t(t-1)(t-2) = (N+1) N (N-1) (N-2)/4$ evaluated at
$N = r - 5$).

Combining with $\gamma_4 = 484/25$ yields the claim.
\end{proof}

\ClaimStatusProvedHere

\[

   + \frac{264}{45} + \frac{24}{972}

Hence $\Gamma_{8} = A_{8} \cdot 51134 / 2025 = 1296 \cdot 51134 /

\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Direct evaluation:
$(r-4)(r-5) = 12$, $(r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7) = 24$ at $r = 8$. Therefore

264/45 + 2/81$. Common denominator $2025 = 3^{4} \cdot 5^{2}$:

The combinatorial sum $39204 + 11880 + 50 = 51134$ channels the

\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:gamma-not-beta-squared}Sub-subleading $\neq$

\]
At $r = 8$ this discrepancy equals $-34/81$. The non-vanishing of
this correction is the Riccati-source signature: the quadratic
source $\sigma_r^{(\mathrm{quadratic})}$ generated by $B_j B_k$
products at $r \geq 6$ cross-interacts with the linear source
$\sigma_r^{(\mathrm{linear})}$ generated by $A_j B_k + B_j A_k$,
producing a genuinely new Laurent coefficient not obtainable from
Tier-1 and Tier-2 data alone.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Sub-sub-subleading asymptotic: Tier 4 and the Riccati-
source signature}
\label{subsec:tier-4-asymptotic}

We now complete the Laurent stratification one further tier down, to
the sub-sub-subleading coefficient
\[
  \Delta_r \;:=\; \lim_{c \to \infty} c^{3}
   \bigl( c^{r - 2} S_r(\Vir_c) - A_r - B_r / c - \Gamma_r / c^{2} \bigr).
\]
This is the first tier where the $B_j B_k$ cross-term (subleading
squared) enters the recurrence: Tier 3 recovers the purely linear
$(A_j B_k + B_j A_k)$ source, while Tier 4 introduces the quadratic
$B_j B_k$ self-coupling.

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:shadow-tower-tier-4-closed-form}Closed
form for the Tier-4 sub-sub-subleading asymptotic]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every $r \geq 4$, the sub-sub-subleading coefficient admits the
four-term closed form
\[
  \frac{\Delta_r}{A_r}
   \;=\; -\,\frac{10648}{125}
    \;-\; \frac{242}{75}\,(r-4)(r-5)
    \;-\; \frac{11}{810}\,(r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)
    \;-\; \frac{(r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)(r-8)(r-9)}{104976}.
\]
The first term is the geometric-cube base $-\,(-22/5)^{3} =
-10648/125$ inherited from $\Phi_{4}(c) = 10 c/(5 c + 22)$. The
three $(r-4)(r-5)$ polynomial corrections are iterated Riccati
source contributions at successive Tier depths.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Step 1: Tier-4 recurrence.} Matching the $c^{-1}$ coefficient
on the left-hand side of $r c \Phi_r = -\sum f(j,k) j k \Phi_j \Phi_k$
yields
\[
  \Delta_r \;=\; -\,\frac{6(r-1)}{r}\,\Delta_{r-1}
   \;-\; \frac{1}{r} \sigma_r^{\Delta},
\]
where the Tier-4 source
\[
  \sigma_r^{\Delta} \;=\;
   \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 4 \le j \le k \le r-2}}
   f(j, k)\, j k\, \bigl( A_j \Gamma_k + B_j B_k + \Gamma_j A_k \bigr).
\]
The novel $B_j B_k$ contribution makes this the first tier carrying
the quadratic Riccati-source self-coupling.

\emph{Step 2: source decomposition.} Using
$\Gamma_k / A_k = \gamma_k$ and $B_j B_k = A_j A_k \cdot (22/5 +
(j-4)(j-5)/18)(22/5 + (k-4)(k-5)/18)$, the source $\sigma_r^{\Delta}$
decomposes as
\[
  \frac{\sigma_r^{\Delta}}{A_r} \;=\;
   \frac{64 r}{288}\,\bigl[
     \underbrace{(968/25) (r-5)/2}_{\gamma\text{-sum at }m=0}
     + \underbrace{(22/45) (r-5)(r-6)(r-7)/3}_{\gamma\text{-sum at }m=2}
     + \underbrace{(1/972) \Sigma_Q}_{\gamma\text{-sum at }m=4}
   \]
   \[
     + (22/5)^{2} (r-5)/2
     + \underbrace{(22/90) (r-5)(r-6)(r-7)/3}_{\text{cross}}
     + (1/324) \Sigma_R
   \bigr]
\]
where $\Sigma_Q := \sum f(j,k) Q_{jk}$ with
$Q_{jk} = (j-4)(j-5)(j-6)(j-7) + (k-4)(k-5)(k-6)(k-7)$, and
$\Sigma_R := \sum f(j,k) R_{jk}$ with
$R_{jk} = (j-4)(j-5)(k-4)(k-5)$.

\emph{Step 3: quintic combinatorial identities
(\label{lem:quintic-identities}).}

\begin{lemma}[\label{lem:quintic-combinatorial}Quintic combinatorial
identities]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every $r \geq 5$:
\[
  \Sigma_Q \;:=\; \sum_{\substack{j+k=r+2 \\ 4 \le j \le k \le r-2}}
  f(j,k) Q_{jk} \;=\; \frac{(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)(r-8)(r-9)}{5},
\]
\[
  \Sigma_R \;:=\; \sum_{\substack{j+k=r+2 \\ 4 \le j \le k \le r-2}}
  f(j,k) R_{jk} \;=\; \frac{(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)(r-8)(r-9)}{60}.
\]
\end{lemma}
\noindent \emph{Proof of Lemma.} By symmetry $(j,k) \leftrightarrow (k,j)$,
$\Sigma_Q = 2 \sum f_i i(i-1)(i-2)(i-3)$ with $i = j-4$; polynomial
interpolation on values $0,0,0,0,24,144,504,1344$ at $r = 6,\ldots,13$
gives constant fifth differences equal to $2$ and the unique degree-5
interpolant $(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)(r-8)(r-9)/5$. Similarly
$\Sigma_R$ interpolates to $(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)(r-8)(r-9)/60$; the
coefficient ratio $12$ between the two sums reflects
$Q_{jk}/R_{jk} \sim 12$ averaged over the index set.  \qedlem

\emph{Step 4: source-to-leading ratio for $\Delta$.}  The $\Sigma_Q$
and $\Sigma_R$ contributions combine at the quintic level:
\[
  \frac{1}{972} \cdot \frac{1}{5} + \frac{1}{324} \cdot \frac{1}{60}
   \;=\; \frac{1}{4860} + \frac{1}{19440}
   \;=\; \frac{4 + 1}{19440}
   \;=\; \frac{1}{3888}.
\]
Using also the identity $968/25 + (22/5)^2 = 1452/25$ at $m = 0$ and
$22/45 + 22/90 = 11/15$ at $m = 2$:
\[
  \frac{\sigma_r^{\Delta}}{A_r}
  \;=\; \frac{2 r}{9}\, (r-5)
  \left[ \frac{726}{25}
        + \frac{11}{45} (r-6)(r-7)
        + \frac{1}{3888} (r-6)(r-7)(r-8)(r-9) \right].
\]

\emph{Step 5: variation-of-parameters telescoping.}  Writing
$\Delta_r = A_r \delta_r$:
\[
  \delta_r - \delta_{r-1} \;=\; -\,\frac{2}{9} (r-5)
  \left[ \frac{726}{25}
        + \frac{11}{45} (r-6)(r-7)
        + \frac{1}{3888} (r-6)(r-7)(r-8)(r-9) \right].
\]
Base case $\delta_4 = -\,(22/5)^{3} = -10648/125$ from direct
geometric expansion of $\Phi_4 = 10 c/(5 c + 22)$ at third order in
$1/c$.

\emph{Step 6: telescoping sums.}  Three identities are used:
\begin{align*}
  \sum_{s=5}^{r} (s-5) &= (r-4)(r-5)/2, \\
  \sum_{s=5}^{r} (s-5)(s-6)(s-7) &= (r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)/4, \\
  \sum_{s=5}^{r} (s-5)(s-6)(s-7)(s-8)(s-9) &=
   (r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)(r-8)(r-9)/6.
\end{align*}
Applying these to the three polynomial terms in Step 5 and
simplifying:
\begin{align*}
  \delta_r + (22/5)^{3}
   &= -\,(2/9)(726/25) \cdot (r-4)(r-5)/2
   \;-\; (2/9)(11/45) (r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)/4 \\
   &\quad\;-\; (2/9)(1/3888) (r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)(r-8)(r-9)/6 \\
   &= -\,(242/75)(r-4)(r-5)
   \;-\; (11/810) (r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7) \\
   &\quad\;-\; (1/104976) (r-4)(r-5)(r-6)(r-7)(r-8)(r-9).
\end{align*}
This is the claimed closed form.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:three-tier-arithmetic-picture}Three-tier
arithmetic of the Virasoro shadow tower]
Combining
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-asymptotic-closed-form},
Corollary~\ref{cor:virasoro-23-smoothness},
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-subleading-closed-form},
Corollary~\ref{cor:subleading-characteristic-primes},

Corollary~\ref{cor:tier-3-characteristic-primes}, the Virasoro
shadow $S_r(\Vir_c)$ stratifies at $c = \infty$ into three
arithmetic tiers:
\begin{description}
\item[Tier~1 (leading, $A_r$).] Universal $\{2, 3\}$-smooth
  integers modulo residual $\{5, 7, 11, \ldots\}$-contribution from
  divisors of $r$. Arithmetic content: the sign alternation of
  $(-6)^{r - 4}$ and the inverse harmonic normalization $1/r$.
\item[Tier~2 (subleading, $B_r$).] Two-term factorization
  $-A_r \cdot [22/5 + (r - 4)(r - 5)/18]$. The first summand
  records the Zamolodchikov quasi-primary norm
  $\langle \Lambda_4 | \Lambda_4 \rangle = c (5 c + 22)/10$
  (the factor $22/5 = 22/5$); the second summand is the
  \emph{Riccati-specific} contribution $(r - 4)(r - 5)/18$
  generated by the cross-pair cohomological sum. Characteristic
  primes come from $q(r) = 5 r^{2} - 45 r + 496$; the prime
  sequence $\{11, 7, 29, 71, 19, 31, 13, 101, \ldots\}$ is
  Riccati-arithmetic, not Kummer-Bernoulli.
\item[Tier~3 (sub-subleading and beyond, $\Gamma_r, \Delta_r, \ldots$).]
  Higher Laurent coefficients mix the two tiers and introduce
  Kummer-Bernoulli content beginning at
  $\Gamma_{8} = 2^{5} \cdot 37 \cdot 691 / 5^{2}$, where the first
  Kummer-irregular prime $691$ appears. This is the \emph{sharp
  boundary} of the Kummer absence: absent from $S_r$ as rational
  function and from the first two Laurent layers, but present in
  the full expansion at the sub-subleading level.
\end{description}
The three-tier picture separates the universal classical limit
(Tier~1), the Zamolodchikov representation-theoretic arithmetic
(Tier~2), and the Kummer-Bernoulli content inherited from the
Hurwitz-formal cotangent of $\mathrm{Bun}_{\SL_{2}}(\Sigma_{g})$
(Tier~3). The arithmetic duality
Theorem~\ref{thm:z-g-s-r-arithmetic-duality}
(\texttt{z\_g\_kummer\_bernoulli\_platonic.tex}) is refined by this
stratification: the two leading Kummer-irregular primes
$\{691, 3617\}$ are absent from \emph{all three tiers} of $S_r$
through $r = 11$ except the unique sighting of $691$ in
$\Gamma_{8}$, and are present on $Z_g$ already at the leading
Bernoulli asymptotic via $B_{12}$ and $B_{16}$.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[\label{lem:floor-parity-subadditive}Parity subadditivity
of the floor]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For integers $a, b \geq 1$,
\[
  \left\lfloor \tfrac{a}{2} \right\rfloor
   + \left\lfloor \tfrac{b}{2} \right\rfloor
   \;\le\;
   \left\lfloor \tfrac{a + b}{2} \right\rfloor,
\]
with equality unless both $a$ and $b$ are odd.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Write $a = 2 p + \alpha$ and $b = 2 q + \beta$ with
$p, q \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ and $\alpha, \beta \in \{0, 1\}$. Then
$\lfloor a/2 \rfloor = p$ and $\lfloor b/2 \rfloor = q$, and
$\lfloor (a + b)/2 \rfloor = p + q + \lfloor (\alpha + \beta)/2 \rfloor$.
The defect
$\lfloor (a + b)/2 \rfloor - \lfloor a/2 \rfloor - \lfloor b/2 \rfloor
 = \lfloor (\alpha + \beta)/2 \rfloor \in \{0, 0, 1\}$
according as $(\alpha, \beta) \in
\{(0, 0), (0, 1), (1, 0), (1, 1)\}$. The defect is $0$ except when
$\alpha = \beta = 1$, in which case it equals $1$. Equivalently,
equality holds unless both $a$ and $b$ are odd.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[\label{cor:floor-shift-j-plus-k}Floor shift on the
index set of the shadow recurrence]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For integers $j, k$ with $j + k = r + 2$, $3 \leq j \leq k$ and
$r \geq 4$,
\[
  \left\lfloor \tfrac{j - 2}{2} \right\rfloor
   + \left\lfloor \tfrac{k - 2}{2} \right\rfloor
   \;\le\;
   \left\lfloor \tfrac{r - 2}{2} \right\rfloor,
\]
with equality unless $j$ and $k$ are both odd.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply Lemma~\ref{lem:floor-parity-subadditive} with $a = j - 2$ and
$b = k - 2$ (so $a, b \geq 1$ since $j, k \geq 3$); then
$a + b = j + k - 4 = r - 2$. Both-odd for $(a, b)$ corresponds to
both-odd for $(j, k)$ by parity shift of $2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[\label{thm:s-r-rational-denominator-pattern}Rational
denominator pattern for the Virasoro shadow tower]
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
For every integer $r \geq 4$, the Virasoro shadow coefficient
$S_r(\Vir_c) \in \mathbb{Q}(c)$ admits a presentation with denominator
dividing
\[
  D_r(c) \;:=\; c^{\,r - 3}\,
   (5 c + 22)^{\lfloor (r - 2)/2 \rfloor}.
\]
Equivalently, the polynomial
$N_r(c) := S_r(\Vir_c)\, D_r(c) \in \mathbb{Q}(c)$ is a
polynomial in $c$. The bound $D_r(c)$ is saturated (no common factor
of $c$ or $5c + 22$ divides both $N_r$ and $D_r$) through $r = 11$ by
the explicit closed forms of
Theorems~\ref{thm:s6-virasoro-closed-form}--\ref{thm:s11-virasoro-closed-form}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By strong induction on $r \geq 4$, using the Virasoro shadow
recurrence of Theorem~\ref{thm:virasoro-shadow-recurrence}.

\medskip
\noindent \emph{Base.} At $r = 4$,
$S_4 = 10 / (c (5 c + 22)) = 10 / D_4(c)$ since
$D_4(c) = c^{1} (5 c + 22)^{\lfloor 2/2 \rfloor} = c (5 c + 22)$;
the denominator divides $D_4(c)$. At $r = 5$,
$S_5 = - 48 / (c^{2} (5 c + 22)) = -48 / D_5(c)$ since
$D_5(c) = c^{2} (5 c + 22)^{\lfloor 3/2 \rfloor} = c^{2} (5 c + 22)$;
the denominator divides $D_5(c)$.

\medskip
\noindent \emph{Inductive step.} Fix $r \geq 6$ and assume the
conclusion for all $r' \in \{4, 5, \dots, r - 1\}$. By
Theorem~\ref{thm:virasoro-shadow-recurrence},
\begin{equation} \label{eq:sr-induction-recurrence}
  S_r(\Vir_c)
   \;=\; -\,\frac{1}{r c}
   \sum_{\substack{j + k = r + 2 \\ 3 \le j \le k < r}}
   f(j, k)\, j k\, S_j(\Vir_c)\, S_k(\Vir_c),
\end{equation}
with $f(j, k) \in \{1, \tfrac{1}{2}\}$. Every index $j, k$ on the
right-hand side satisfies $3 \leq j \leq k \leq r - 1$, so
$S_j, S_k$ fall under the inductive hypothesis:
$S_j$ has denominator dividing $D_j(c) = c^{\,j - 3} (5 c + 22)^{\lfloor (j - 2)/2 \rfloor}$
and similarly for $S_k$.

The product $S_j(\Vir_c) S_k(\Vir_c)$ has denominator dividing
\begin{align}
  D_j(c) \cdot D_k(c)
   &\;=\; c^{\,(j - 3) + (k - 3)}\,
    (5 c + 22)^{\lfloor (j - 2)/2 \rfloor
     + \lfloor (k - 2)/2 \rfloor}
   \notag \\
   &\;=\; c^{\,r - 4}\,
    (5 c + 22)^{\lfloor (j - 2)/2 \rfloor
     + \lfloor (k - 2)/2 \rfloor}, \label{eq:sr-induction-productdenom}
\end{align}
using $j + k = r + 2$ in the $c$-exponent. By
Corollary~\ref{cor:floor-shift-j-plus-k} the $(5 c + 22)$-exponent
satisfies
\[
  \lfloor (j - 2)/2 \rfloor + \lfloor (k - 2)/2 \rfloor
   \;\le\;
   \lfloor (r - 2)/2 \rfloor,
\]
so the product denominator $D_j(c) D_k(c)$ divides
$c^{\,r - 4} (5 c + 22)^{\lfloor (r - 2)/2 \rfloor}$.

Each summand $f(j, k)\, j k\, S_j S_k$ in
\eqref{eq:sr-induction-recurrence} is a rational multiple of
$S_j S_k$, so its denominator divides the same bound. The finite sum
of summands therefore has denominator dividing
$c^{\,r - 4} (5 c + 22)^{\lfloor (r - 2)/2 \rfloor}$. Multiplying by
the overall prefactor $-1/(r c)$ multiplies the denominator by $c$
(and a rational constant, which does not affect polynomial divisibility
over $\mathbb{Q}(c)$), yielding
\[
  \mathrm{denom}(S_r) \;\mathrm{divides}\;
   c^{\,r - 4 + 1}\,
   (5 c + 22)^{\lfloor (r - 2)/2 \rfloor}
   \;=\;
   c^{\,r - 3}\,
   (5 c + 22)^{\lfloor (r - 2)/2 \rfloor}
   \;=\; D_r(c),
\]
which closes the induction.

\medskip
\noindent \emph{Saturation through $r = 11$.} The denominators of
Theorems~\ref{thm:s6-virasoro-closed-form}--\ref{thm:s11-virasoro-closed-form}
(after cancelling rational constants) are respectively
$c^{3}(5c+22)^{2}$, $c^{4}(5c+22)^{2}$, $c^{5}(5c+22)^{3}$,
$c^{6}(5c+22)^{3}$, $c^{7}(5c+22)^{4}$, $c^{8}(5c+22)^{4}$, matching
$D_6, D_7, \ldots, D_{11}$ exactly. No accidental cancellation
between numerator and denominator occurs through $r = 11$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:sr-denominator-sharpness}Sharpness via the
diagonal and near-diagonal pair]
The upper bound of
Theorem~\ref{thm:s-r-rational-denominator-pattern} is realised
(before the overall $1/(r c)$) by the pair $(j, k) \in
\{((r+2)/2, (r+2)/2)\}$ when $r$ is even (the diagonal summand with
$f(j, j) = 1/2$) and by $(j, k) = ((r+1)/2, (r+3)/2)$ when $r$ is odd
(the near-diagonal summand). In both cases
$\lfloor (j - 2)/2 \rfloor + \lfloor (k - 2)/2 \rfloor
 = \lfloor (r - 2)/2 \rfloor$
by Corollary~\ref{cor:floor-shift-j-plus-k} (equality case: $j, k$ not
both odd). The off-diagonal both-odd pairs such as $(j, k) = (3, r - 1)$
with $r - 1$ odd contribute denominators strictly smaller in the
$(5 c + 22)$ exponent and embed without resistance into the dominant
bound. Saturation of $D_r(c)$ in $S_r$ therefore reduces to absence of
numerator-denominator cancellation, a finite rational check discharged
through $r = 11$ in the tests.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[\label{rem:denominator-pattern-correction}Denominator
pattern correction: $c^{r - 3}$, not $c^{r - 2}$]
Earlier draft material sometimes quoted the Virasoro shadow
denominator as $c^{r - 2} (5 c + 22)^{\lfloor (r - 2)/2 \rfloor}$.
The corrected pattern (confirmed by direct computation through
$r = 11$) is
\[
  \mathrm{denom}(S_r) \;=\; c^{r - 3}
   \cdot (5 c + 22)^{\lfloor (r - 2)/2 \rfloor},
   \qquad r \geq 4,
\]
with $c$-exponent $r - 3$, one less than the large-$c$ asymptotic
order. The numerator at each weight carries total $c$-degree
$\lceil (r - 2)/2 \rceil - 1$ (zero at $r = 4, 5$; linear at
$r = 6, 7$; quadratic at $r = 8, 9$; cubic at $r = 10, 11$), so
the large-$c$ order is $\mathrm{ord}_{c, \infty}(S_r) = -(r - 2)$
consistently. Explicit check through $r = 11$: the denominators of
Theorems~\ref{thm:s6-virasoro-closed-form}--\ref{thm:s11-virasoro-closed-form}
are $c^{3}(5c+22)^{2},\ c^{4}(5c+22)^{2},\ c^{5}(5c+22)^{3},\
c^{6}(5c+22)^{3},\ c^{7}(5c+22)^{4},\ c^{8}(5c+22)^{4}$, matching
$r - 3 \in \{3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8\}$ respectively.

The $(5c + 22)^{\lfloor (r - 2)/2 \rfloor}$ factor is
\emph{Riccati-propagated} from the weight-$4$ Zamolodchikov norm
$\langle \Lambda \mid \Lambda \rangle = c (5c + 22)/10$, not
independently generated at each even weight. Direct Kac-determinant
verification: $\det G_{N = 4,\, h = 0}(\Vir_c) \propto
c^{2}(5c + 22)/2$ and $\det G_{N = 5,\, h = 0}(\Vir_c) \propto
2 c^{2} (5c + 22)$ (both carrying the same $(5c + 22)$ factor).
Higher levels $N \geq 6$ introduce different $c$-polynomial
Kac-determinant factors, which do not appear in any $S_r$ through
$r = 11$. The $(5c + 22)$ multiplicity in $S_r$ is therefore
inherited from a single Riccati-propagation chain (weight-$4$
Zamolodchikov norm iterated through the recurrence), not from
weight-$r$ Kac-module data. This corrects the prior framing
(``Zamolodchikov inheritance at each weight'') which would have
predicted new weight-$r$ Shapovalov norms entering at every even
$r$; no such new norm is needed through $r = 11$.
\end{remark}

\section*{Epilogue}

The Virasoro shadow tower is closed in a concrete sense through
weight $11$: every coefficient is a specific rational function of
$c$ with a structurally transparent denominator
$c^{r-3} (5c + 22)^{\lfloor (r-2)/2 \rfloor}$; every leading
asymptotic is pinned by the closed-form recurrence
$A_r = 8 (-6)^{r - 4}/r$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-asymptotic-closed-form}); every
weight through $r = 8$ is verified against two disjoint derivation
chains (MC master-equation recurrence and Riccati $\sqrt{Q_L}$
Taylor expansion) at four distinct central charges. The $S_8 =
4144720/19683$ at $c = 1$ identity closes the Vol~I
$\leftrightarrow$ Vol~III cross-volume consistency check for $m_8$
through the shadow tower.

The pattern extends as far as the recurrence is run; no new
mathematical phenomenon enters between weight $5$ and weight $11$
beyond the increasing multiplicity of the Zamolodchikov
$\Lambda$-channel pole (Riccati-propagated, not weight-by-weight
Kac-module). The next qualitatively new phenomenon arises in the
motivic lift at $W_3$, weight $6$
(Proposition~\ref{prop:s6-w3-mot}), which is the first appearance
of a $\zeta^{\mathrm{mot}}(3)^{2}$ contribution outside the
rational field $\mathbb{Q}(c)$. This establishes a sharp
separation: Virasoro is motivic-rational at every weight through
$r = 11$; the non-rational motivic content lives in higher-rank
$\mathcal{W}$-algebras.
