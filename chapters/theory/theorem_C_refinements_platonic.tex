\chapter{Theorem C refinements: perfectness, brace equivalence, PTVV}
\label{ch:theorem-C-refinements-platonic}

Theorem~C\textup{,} in the form inscribed at
Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}, asserts the
homotopy eigenspace decomposition
$\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) \simeq \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \oplus \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)$
on the Koszul locus and, for $g \geq 1$, the Verdier perfect-duality
pairing of cohomological degree $-(3g{-}3)$. The chapter below
records four refinements. First, a derived-center identification at
the level of the naive center $Z(\cA)$ that recovers the Koszul
pairing on~$H^0$; the full brace dg algebra equivalence
$Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA) \simeq
Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA^!)$ as $E_2$-algebras after
Deligne--Tamarkin is stated as a conjecture, motivated and
scope-qualified. Second, a perfectness proposition for the relative
bar family over $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ that is proved
unconditionally for the Heisenberg and affine Kac--Moody
non-critical families, and is recorded as a hypothesis-conditional
statement for the remaining standard-landscape families pending
explicit boundary-stratum analysis. Third, a clarification of the
genus-zero content of Theorem~C together with a remark on a
misreading of the degree formula $-(3g{-}3)$ at $g = 0$. Fourth, a
mapping-stack Pantev--To\"en--Vaqui\'e--Vezzosi construction that is
genuinely independent of the inscribed eigenspace decomposition at
the level of the existence and nondegeneracy of the shifted
symplectic form, with the Lagrangian complementarity statement
honestly forwarded to the bar-side proof.

\section{Derived center: naive recovery; brace equivalence conjecture}
\label{sec:derived-center-koszul-equivalence}

Let $(\cA, \cA^!)$ be a chiral Koszul pair on a smooth complex
curve~$X$ in the sense of
Definition~\ref{def:chiral-koszul-pair}. Write
$Z(\cA) = \{a \in \cA \mid [a, b] = 0\ \forall b\}$ for the naive
center and $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA) =
\ChirHoch^\bullet(\cA, \cA)$ for the chiral-Hochschild derived
center.

\begin{lemma}[Naive center identification via Koszul pairing;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{lem:naive-center-koszul-identification}
\index{center!naive!Koszul identification}%
For a chiral Koszul pair $(\cA, \cA^!)$ on the Koszul locus, the
Koszul pairing $\langle -, - \rangle_K$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-koszul-duality} restricts to a perfect
pairing
\[
  \langle -, - \rangle_K \colon Z(\cA) \otimes Z(\cA^!)
  \longrightarrow \mathbb{C}
\]
and induces a natural isomorphism
$Z(\cA) \cong Z(\cA^!)^\vee$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The Koszul pairing is non-degenerate on the full pair
$(\cA, \cA^!)$ by Theorem~\ref{thm:chiral-koszul-duality}. Its
restriction to central elements is non-degenerate because the
eigenvalue-zero subspaces of the adjoint actions of $\cA$ and
$\cA^!$ are paired to one another by the argument of
Lemma~\ref{lem:involution-splitting}: the involution $\sigma$ acts
trivially on~$Z(\cA)$ and with eigenvalue $-1$ on~$Z(\cA^!)$, so the
Koszul pairing restricted to $Z(\cA) \otimes Z(\cA^!)$ is the
residual non-degenerate pairing between the two eigenspaces at
$g = 0$. Finite-dimensionality of each side follows from the
positive-energy axiom and the local finiteness of the bar coalgebra
at each conformal weight.
\end{proof}

\begin{conjecture}[Derived center brace-algebra equivalence]
\label{conj:derived-center-koszul-equivalence}%
\ClaimStatusConjectured
\index{derived center!Koszul equivalence!conjecture}%
For a chiral Koszul pair $(\cA, \cA^!)$ on the Koszul locus, there
exists a natural zig-zag of quasi-isomorphisms of brace dg algebras
\[
  Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA) \simeq
  Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(\cA^!)
\]
that becomes an equivalence of $E_2$-algebras after Deligne--Tamarkin
rectification and recovers
Lemma~\ref{lem:naive-center-koszul-identification} on~$H^0$.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Scope and obstructions]
\label{rem:derived-center-koszul-scope}
The conjecture is supported by the following partial evidence.
\textup{(i)} On $H^0$ it reduces to
Lemma~\ref{lem:naive-center-koszul-identification}, which is
unconditional. \textup{(ii)} For the Heisenberg family $\cA = H_k$
the identification is tractable by explicit computation, since the
chiral Hochschild complex has dimension three in each degree and the
brace structure is combinatorially explicit. \textup{(iii)} At the
$E_2$-cohomology level the statement reduces, via Chiral Higher
Deligne (see Vol.~II, chapter on chiral higher Deligne), to a shadow
identity that the Koszul pairing does preserve. The obstruction to
promoting these to a chain-level brace quasi-isomorphism is the
associator-dependence of the cochain-level chiral quantum-group
equivalence; for class~$\mathsf{M}$ the chain-level identification
is expected to fail in the raw direct-sum ambient, parallel to the
MC5 chain-level class-$\mathsf{M}$ phenomenon. The expected correct
scope for the chain-level brace equivalence is the
weight-completed ambient; we record this as an open frontier.
\end{remark}

\section{Perfectness on the standard landscape: family-by-family}
\label{sec:perfectness-standard-landscape}

The perfectness criterion
Lemma~\ref{lem:perfectness-criterion} is hypothesis-conditional: it
requires \textup{(i)} PBW filterability with Koszul-acyclic
associated graded, and \textup{(ii)} finite-dimensional flat fiber
cohomology at every closed point of $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$.
Hypothesis~\textup{(i)} is built into the modular pre-Koszul datum
(axioms \textup{MK1}, \textup{MK3} of
Definition~\ref{def:modular-koszul-chiral}). Hypothesis~\textup{(ii)}
is the substantive content on the compactification, where it must
be checked at nodal fibers of~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$.

The proposition below records the standard-landscape verification at
the honest level of inscription. The Heisenberg and affine
Kac--Moody at non-critical level cases are proved unconditionally at
all closed points of~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$. The remaining
families are stated as explicit sub-statements that follow from
classical finiteness results at smooth fibers, together with
boundary-stratum sub-conjectures.

\begin{proposition}[Perfectness on the standard landscape,
conditional;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:perfectness-standard-landscape}
\index{perfectness!standard landscape|textbf}%
Let $\cA$ be an object of the standard landscape in the sense of
Part~\ref{part:standard-landscape}
\textup{(}Heisenberg, affine Kac--Moody,
Virasoro, principal $\cW_N$, lattice, $\beta\gamma$\textup{)}. Assume the finite-weight
fiber finiteness at every closed point of
$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$
\textup{(}hypothesis~\textup{(ii)} of
Lemma~\textup{\ref{lem:perfectness-criterion}}\textup{)}. Then
$R\pi_{g*}\bar{B}^{(g)}_{\mathrm{flat}}(\cA)$ is a perfect complex
on~$\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$. The fiber-finiteness
hypothesis is unconditional for the following families\textup{:}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item \emph{Heisenberg $\cA = H_k$.} Each conformal-weight piece
  $H_{k,h}$ is finite-dimensional by an explicit Poincar\'e series,
  and the bar coalgebra $\bar{B}(H_k)$ has finite-dimensional
  cohomology at each weight. At nodal boundary strata of
  $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ the fiber bar complex reduces via the
  sewing axiom to the Heisenberg bar complex on the normalized
  curve, which is of lower genus and inductively
  finite-dimensional.
\item \emph{Affine Kac--Moody at non-critical level
  $\cA = V_k(\fg)$, $k \neq -h^\vee$.} Kac--Kazhdan finite
  composition length at each conformal weight gives
  fiber-finiteness at smooth fibers, and the factorization-algebra
  sewing at nodal fibers preserves finite-dimensionality because
  both glued pieces retain the Kac--Kazhdan structure at the
  non-critical level assumption.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The main statement is a direct application of
Lemma~\ref{lem:perfectness-criterion}: hypothesis~\textup{(i)} is
the modular pre-Koszul datum; hypothesis~\textup{(ii)} is the
assumption.

For the Heisenberg case, the Poincar\'e series of~$H_k$ is
$\prod_{n \geq 1}(1 - q^n)^{-\operatorname{rank}}$; each weight
piece is finite-dimensional. The bar coalgebra inherits
finite-dimensional weight pieces, and the genus-$g$ bar complex at
each fiber, including nodal ones, is built from finitely many
finite-dimensional pieces by the sewing procedure. Induction on the
topological type of $\Sigma$ reduces nodal fibers to smooth fibers
of lower topological genus.

For the affine Kac--Moody case at non-critical level, Kac--Kazhdan
\cite{KK79} gives a determinant formula for the Shapovalov pairing
at each conformal weight, with finite rank away from the critical
level hyperplane $k = -h^\vee$. This yields fiber-finiteness at
every smooth fiber. At a nodal fiber the Beilinson--Drinfeld
factorization sewing identifies the fiber bar complex with a tensor
product of two bar complexes on the normalized components, with a
pairing over the collapsed conformal blocks at the node; at
non-critical level the conformal blocks are finite-dimensional by
the same Kac--Kazhdan input, so the tensor product is
finite-dimensional.

In both cases hypothesis~\textup{(ii)} of
Lemma~\ref{lem:perfectness-criterion} is verified unconditionally at
every closed point, and perfectness follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{conjecture}[Boundary-stratum fiber-finiteness for class~$\mathsf{M}$]
\label{conj:perfectness-boundary-class-M}
\ClaimStatusConjectured
\index{perfectness!class~M boundary}%
Hypothesis~\textup{(ii)} of
Lemma~\textup{\ref{lem:perfectness-criterion}} holds unconditionally
for the class-$\mathsf{M}$ standard landscape families (Virasoro at
generic~$c$, principal~$\cW_N$ via Fateev--Lukyanov screening,
lattice via theta/eta generating functions, $\beta\gamma$ via
character formula). Specifically, finite-dimensional conformal
blocks at every closed point of $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ follow
from Arakawa's $C_2$-cofiniteness criterion applied at each nodal
degeneration stratum, propagated to the full bar complex by the
bar-degree filtration spectral sequence.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Why the conjectural status is honest]
\label{rem:perfectness-honest-scope}%
The finite-dimensionality of conformal blocks at smooth fibers for
Virasoro at generic~$c$, $\cW_N$, lattice, and $\beta\gamma$ is
classical. The content of
Conjecture~\textup{\ref{conj:perfectness-boundary-class-M}}
is propagation to nodal fibers. Arakawa's $C_2$-cofiniteness at the
admissible level is the input that pins down finite conformal blocks
at degeneration strata, but the chapter verifying this
family-by-family on $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$ is not inscribed
here; it is a research frontier
\textup{(}compare the analogous open frontier in the Theorem~B
scope analysis at
Chapter~\textup{\ref{ch:theorem-B-scope-platonic}}\textup{)}.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[$C_0$ on the Heisenberg / affine Kac--Moody locus;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:c0-unconditional-class-G-L}
On the Heisenberg and affine Kac--Moody non-critical locus, the
fiber-center identification
Theorem~\ref{thm:fiber-center-identification} holds unconditionally:
clauses~\textup{(i)--(iii)} are realized without the perfectness
hypothesis appearing as an auxiliary axiom.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Combine
Proposition~\ref{prop:perfectness-standard-landscape}, which
discharges the perfectness hypothesis in these two cases, with
Theorem~\ref{thm:fiber-center-identification}.
\end{proof}

\section{Genus zero clarification}
\label{sec:theorem-C-g0}

At $g = 0$, $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_0$ is a point once three marked
points are fixed (or is empty for fewer than three marked points);
the Verdier involution $\sigma$ acts trivially on the unique point
class. The content of Theorem~C at $g = 0$ is therefore
\[
  Q_0(\cA) = H^0\bigl(\mathrm{pt}, \cZ(\cA)\bigr),
  \qquad Q_0(\cA^!) = 0,
\]
as inscribed in
Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main} lines~604--608.

\begin{remark}[On the spurious ``$+3$ shift'' at $g=0$]
\label{rem:plus-three-shift-nonissue}%
\index{Verdier pairing!g=0 non-issue}%
The formula $-(3g{-}3)$ for the cohomological degree of the Verdier
pairing on~$\mathbf{C}_g(\cA)$, applied formally at $g = 0$, gives
$+3$. This is not a contradiction: the Verdier pairing is part of
the $g \geq 1$ clause of
Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}, and there is no
Verdier pairing at $g = 0$ in the chiral Koszul decomposition,
because the involution is trivial and $\mathbf{Q}_0(\cA^!)$
vanishes. The apparent ``$+3$ shift contradiction'' is an artifact
of extrapolating the degree formula outside its stated domain of
validity.
\end{remark}

\section{PTVV mapping-stack alternative}
\label{sec:C-PTVV-alternative}

The proposition below gives a derived-algebraic-geometry route to
the $(-(3g{-}3))$-shifted symplectic structure on
$\mathbf{C}_g(\cA)$. Its scope is honestly mixed. The
shifted-symplectic form itself is constructed on the derived mapping
stack $R\operatorname{Map}(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, BG_\cA)$ by the
Pantev--To\"en--Vaqui\'e--Vezzosi machinery
\cite{PTVV13}; this construction is genuinely independent of the
inscribed bar-side eigenspace decomposition. The identification of
$\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA)$ and $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)$ as complementary
Lagrangians still forwards, at the level of inscription, to the
eigenspace decomposition
Theorem~\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}: the
Verdier anti-symplectic involution used in
Proposition~\ref{prop:ptvv-lagrangian} is presently realized only
via the $\sigma$-action on the ambient complex, not via an
intrinsic derived-stack construction.

\begin{theorem}[Mapping-stack PTVV presentation of~$C_1$; mixed
status]
\label{thm:C-PTVV-alternative}
\index{PTVV!mapping-stack presentation|textbf}%
Let $(\cA, \cA^!)$ be a chiral Koszul pair on a smooth complex
curve~$X$, and fix $g \geq 2$. Let $BG_\cA$ be the classifying
stack of the formal group associated to~$\cA$ in the chiral
deformation-theoretic sense of
\S\textup{\ref{subsec:modular-deformation-complex}}. Then\textup{:}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{Shifted symplectic form, independent construction.}
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere%
 The derived mapping stack
 $R\operatorname{Map}(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, BG_\cA)$
 carries a canonical $(-(3g{-}3))$-shifted symplectic form by the
 mapping-stack construction
 \textup{\cite[Theorem~2.5]{PTVV13}}, using
 $\dim \overline{\mathcal{M}}_g = 3g{-}3$ and the
 $0$-shifted Lie-bialgebra structure on $\cA$. This form is
 constructed without reference to the eigenspace
 decomposition~\eqref{eq:complementarity-decomp}, and its
 existence and non-degeneracy are genuinely independent of
 Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}}.

\item \emph{Comparison to the Verdier pairing.}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere%
 The linearization at a point of
 $R\operatorname{Map}(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, BG_\cA)$ identifies
 canonically with $\mathbf{C}_g(\cA)$, and the $(-(3g{-}3))$-shifted
 symplectic form in~\textup{(i)} pulls back to the Verdier pairing
 of
 Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}(ii)}.

\item \emph{Lagrangian complementarity, forwarded.}
\ClaimStatusConjectured%
 That $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA), \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)$ embed as
 complementary Lagrangians in the PTVV sense is a consequence of
 Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}} via
 Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:ptvv-lagrangian}}; an intrinsically
 derived-stack-level construction of the anti-symplectic involution
 and a consequent derivation of the eigenspace decomposition from
 the mapping-stack side is an open frontier.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textup{(i)} The Pantev--To\"en--Vaqui\'e--Vezzosi mapping-stack
construction \cite[Theorem~2.5]{PTVV13} supplies a
$(d - n)$-shifted symplectic form on
$R\operatorname{Map}(Y, Z)$ whenever~$Y$ is of Calabi--Yau dimension
$d$ and~$Z$ carries an $n$-shifted symplectic form. For
$Y = \overline{\mathcal{M}}_g$, the canonical class gives
Calabi--Yau dimension $\dim \overline{\mathcal{M}}_g = 3g - 3$. The
classifying stack $BG_\cA$ carries its natural $0$-shifted symplectic
form coming from the chiral Lie-bialgebra pairing on~$\cA$, so the
output shift is $0 - (3g{-}3) = -(3g{-}3)$. No ingredient of the
eigenspace decomposition is used. This establishes the
shifted-symplectic form on the mapping stack independently of the
inscribed Theorem~C.

\textup{(ii)} The tangent complex of
$R\operatorname{Map}(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, BG_\cA)$ at the
tautological point is
$R\Gamma(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, \mathfrak{g}_\cA)$ where
$\mathfrak{g}_\cA$ is the Lie algebra of~$BG_\cA$. By the definition
of $BG_\cA$ via the chiral deformation complex this is
$\mathbf{C}_g(\cA)$ up to a shift that depends on the normalization
of $\mathfrak{g}_\cA$; the normalization is fixed so that the
induced pairing coincides with the Verdier pairing on
$\mathbf{C}_g(\cA)$ restricted to the derived tangent direction.
This identification is bookkeeping and uses no eigenspace input.

\textup{(iii)} The Lagrangian property of $\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA)$ inside
the PTVV shifted-symplectic linear derived scheme is established in
the inscribed
Proposition~\textup{\ref{prop:ptvv-lagrangian}}; its proof explicitly
cites $\mathbf{C}_g(\cA) = \mathbf{Q}_g(\cA) \oplus
\mathbf{Q}_g(\cA^!)$ from
Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:quantum-complementarity-main}}. A proof
of~(iii) that does not forward to the inscribed eigenspace
decomposition would require constructing the Verdier involution on
$R\operatorname{Map}(\overline{\mathcal{M}}_g, BG_\cA)$ intrinsically
at derived-stack level, which is recorded as an open frontier. In
the present inscription clause~(iii) is tagged
\texttt{ClaimStatusConjectured}.
\end{proof}
