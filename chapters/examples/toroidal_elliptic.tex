\chapter{Toroidal and elliptic algebras}
\label{chap:toroidal-elliptic}

Every chiral algebra in the preceding chapters depends on a single
deformation parameter: the level~$k$ of a Kac--Moody algebra, the
central charge~$c$ of a $\mathcal{W}$-algebra, or the spectral
parameter~$u$ of a Yangian.  This chapter enters the regime where one
parameter is not enough.  The toroidal algebra
$U_{q,t}(\hat{\hat{\fg}})$ carries two independent parameters $(q,t)$,
the elliptic quantum group $E_{q,p}(\fg)$ replaces the rational
$R$-matrix by a doubly-periodic one, and the double affine Hecke
algebra (DAHA) intertwines spectral and modular directions.  These
algebras make visible the marriage of modular and spectral themes that
has been developing implicitly throughout Parts~I and~II: the modular
parameter~$\tau$ of the elliptic curve and the spectral parameter~$u$
of the $R$-matrix are no longer independent but coupled through the
Fay trisecant identity and the quasi-periodicity of theta functions.

The bar-cobar framework meets this two-parameter world in two distinct
ways.  Direction~(a) keeps the base a single algebraic curve, now of
genus~$1$: the propagator becomes elliptic, the Arnold relation
generalizes to the Fay trisecant identity
(Theorem~\ref{thm:fay}), and the bar differential acquires
Eisenstein-series corrections indexed by~$\tau$.  The resulting
$\Eone$-chiral structures on $E_\tau$ sit inside the modular shadow
tower, with the modular characteristic~$\kappa$ detecting the
$\tau$-dependence of the genus-$1$ obstruction.  Direction~(b)
seeks genuine surface-factorization or double-affine objects on
$\bC^* \times \bC^*$, requiring a two-dimensional factorization
framework that lies beyond the curve-chiral hierarchy of this
monograph.  Only direction~(a) is developed here; direction~(b) is
noted as a programme target.

The passage from rational to trigonometric to elliptic---from
$\mathbb{P}^1$ to $\bC^*$ to $E_\tau$---parallels the passage from
additive to multiplicative to modular spectral parameter.  Each step
enriches the $R$-matrix, complicates the bar complex, and deepens the
interaction between the spectral and genus directions.  The Fay
identity, which replaces the Arnold relation at genus~$1$, is both an
identity of theta functions and a geometric consequence of the
degeneration $E_\tau \to \mathbb{P}^1$ as $\tau \to i\infty$.

Two directions extend the curve-based theory: (a)~replacing the logarithmic propagator by an elliptic one on $E_\tau$, yielding $\Eone$-chiral structures; (b)~seeking surface-factorization or double-affine objects on $\bC^* \times \bC^*$.  Only direction~(a) fits the curve-chiral hierarchy.  The bar complex acquires Eisenstein-series corrections controlled by $\tau$, and the Fay trisecant identity replaces the Arnold relation.

\begin{remark}[Two programme tracks]
\label{rem:toroidal-two-tracks}
Track~(a) keeps the base an algebraic curve and asks for an elliptic
$\Eone$-chiral realization on $E_\tau$.  Track~(b) seeks a genuine
surface-factorization or double-affine object on
$\bC^* \times \bC^*$ or related two-parameter spaces.  Only
track~(a) is described in this chapter with curve-chiral language;
track~(b) is a separate higher-dimensional programme target.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The generalization principle: Arnold to Fay]
\label{rem:arnold-fay-generalization}
\index{Arnold relation!Fay generalization}
The Arnold relation (Theorem~\ref{thm:arnold-relations}), which governs the genus-$0$ bar differential, admits two distinct generalizations.  Direction~(a): via clutching morphisms and the shadow Postnikov tower $\Theta_A^{\leq r}$ (Definition~\ref{def:shadow-postnikov-tower}), one passes to the modular regime on stable curves, recovering the higher-genus bar complex of Part~I (Remark~\ref{rem:five-pieces}).  Direction~(b): via the Fay trisecant identity (Theorem~\ref{thm:fay}), one replaces the additive (logarithmic) propagator $\omega = dz/(z-w)$ by the elliptic (multiplicative) propagator built from the Weierstrass $\sigma$-function, entering the toroidal regime.  The present chapter develops direction~(b): the systematic passage from logarithmic to elliptic propagators and its consequences for bar-cobar duality.
\end{remark}

\section{Double affine setup}
\label{sec:double-affine}

\subsection{Definition}

\begin{definition}[Toroidal algebra]\label{def:toroidal}
\index{toroidal algebra|textbf}
\index{double affine algebra|textbf}
The \emph{toroidal algebra} (or double affine algebra) $U_{q,t}(\hat{\hat{\mathfrak{g}}})$ 
is generated by:
\begin{itemize}
\item Horizontal currents $x^\pm_{i,n}$, $\psi^\pm_{i,n}$ (affine in one direction)
\item Vertical currents $e_i(z)$, $f_i(z)$, $\psi_i(z)$ (affine in the other direction)
\end{itemize}
subject to:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Drinfeld--type relations} in each direction:
$[h_{i,m}, x^\pm_{j,n}] = \pm a_{ij} x^\pm_{j,m+n}$, \quad
$[x^+_{i,m}, x^-_{j,n}] = \delta_{ij} (\psi^+_{i,m+n} - \psi^-_{i,m+n})/(q_i - q_i^{-1})$
\item \emph{Cross relations} coupling horizontal and vertical:
$\psi_i(z) e_j(w) = g_{ij}(z/w) \, e_j(w) \psi_i(z)$
where $g_{ij}(u) = (u - q^{a_{ij}})/(u - q^{-a_{ij}})$
\item \emph{Serre relations} in both directions
\end{enumerate}
with two independent parameters $q$ and $t = q^k$. See Ginzburg--Kapranov--Vasserot
for the complete presentation.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Toroidal OPE \cite{FBZ04}; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]\label{prop:toroidal-ope}
The OPE for toroidal currents involves both parameters:
\[
e_i(z) f_j(w) = \frac{\delta_{ij}}{(1 - q)(1 - t^{-1})} \cdot
\frac{\psi_i^+(w) - \psi_i^-(w)}{z - w} + \text{rational terms in } q, t
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
We derive the OPE from the defining relations of Definition~\ref{def:toroidal}.

\emph{Step~1: Mode commutator.}
The Drinfeld-type relation (1) gives:
\[
[e_{i,m}, f_{j,n}] = \delta_{ij} \frac{\psi^+_{i,m+n} - \psi^-_{i,m+n}}{q_i - q_i^{-1}}.
\]
Form the generating functions $e_i(z) = \sum_m e_{i,m} z^{-m-1}$,
$f_j(w) = \sum_n f_{j,n} w^{-n-1}$, and
$\psi_i^\pm(w) = \sum_n \psi^\pm_{i,n} w^{-n}$.

\emph{Step~2: Extracting the OPE.}
Contracting the commutator against $z^{-m-1} w^{-n-1}$ and summing:
\[
e_i(z) f_j(w) - {:}e_i(z) f_j(w){:}
= \delta_{ij} \sum_{m} \frac{\psi^+_{i,m}(w) - \psi^-_{i,m}(w)}{q_i - q_i^{-1}} \cdot \frac{w^{-m}}{z - w}.
\]
For the toroidal algebra with parameters $q$ and $t = q^k$,
we have $q_i = q^{d_i}$ where $d_i = (\alpha_i, \alpha_i)/2$.
The singular part of the OPE is the residue at $z = w$:
\[
e_i(z) f_j(w) \sim \frac{\delta_{ij}}{q_i - q_i^{-1}} \cdot
\frac{\psi_i^+(w) - \psi_i^-(w)}{z - w}.
\]

\emph{Step~3: Two-parameter structure.}
The cross relation (2) introduces the second parameter.
From $\psi_i(z) e_j(w) = g_{ij}(z/w) \, e_j(w) \psi_i(z)$
with $g_{ij}(u) = (u - q^{a_{ij}})/(u - q^{-a_{ij}})$,
the normal ordering of $\psi$ against $e, f$ currents produces
corrections rational in both $q$ and $t$.  Specifically, expanding
$1/(q_i - q_i^{-1}) = 1/((q^{d_i} - q^{-d_i}))$ and using
$t = q^k$ to express the central element as
$c = (1 - q)(1-t^{-1})/(q_i - q_i^{-1})$ (the standard Ding--Iohara
normalization).  See~\cite{DI97} for the complete change-of-basis computation.
\end{proof}

\begin{conjecture}[Elliptic-curve toroidal realization; \ClaimStatusConjectured]\label{conj:toroidal-e1}
The toroidal algebra $U_{q,t}(\hat{\hat{\mathfrak{g}}})$ admits a
curve-based $\Eone$-chiral realization on the elliptic curve
$E_\tau$ (with modulus $\tau = \frac{\log t}{\log q}$).
\end{conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[Double-affine surface-factorization realization;
\ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{conj:toroidal-surface-factorization}
The same toroidal data should extend to a doubly-graded
surface-factorization or double-affine object on
$\bC^* \times \bC^*$, with one direction encoding the spectral
parameter and the other the elliptic coordinate.
(This is the toroidal/elliptic extension flank of
Conjecture~\ref{conj:master-infinite-generator}, not the standard
$W_\infty$/Yangian finite-packet lane.)
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Scope]
Conjecture~\ref{conj:toroidal-e1} is conjectural (\ClaimStatusConjectured): a complete proof requires verifying associativity of the $\Eone$-chiral product directly from the OPE relations, since the BD locality approach applies only to commutative chiral algebras.  The $\bC^* \times \bC^*$ realization (Conjecture~\ref{conj:toroidal-surface-factorization}) is a separate surface-factorization problem within the MC4 elliptic/double-affine extension frontier.

\emph{Homotopy template} (Convention~\ref{conv:hms-levels}): Type~I (existence).
\end{remark}

\begin{proof}[Proof outline]
We describe the conjectural $\Eone$-chiral structure.

\emph{Step~1: $R$-matrix braiding.}
The cross relation (2) of Definition~\ref{def:toroidal} gives the
structure function $g_{ij}(z/w) = (z/w - q^{a_{ij}})/(z/w - q^{-a_{ij}})$.
Define the $R$-matrix-valued braiding on generating currents by
\[
\sigma_{12} \circ \mu(e_i(z), e_j(w))
= g_{ij}(z/w) \cdot \mu(e_j(w), e_i(z)).
\]
Since $g_{ij}(u)$ is a rational function with $g_{ij}(u) \neq 1$
for generic $q$ and $a_{ij} \neq 0$, the braiding is non-trivial:
the algebra is associative but not commutative, hence strictly $\Eone$.

\emph{Step~2: Associativity (gap).}
The $R$-matrix $R_{ij}(u) = g_{ij}(u) \cdot \mathrm{Id}$ satisfies
the Yang--Baxter equation trivially (being scalar-valued).
The genuine content of associativity lies in verifying that the
full matrix-valued OPE structure---including the Serre-type relations
of Definition~\ref{def:toroidal}---defines an algebra over $\chirAss$
(Definition~\ref{def:chiral-ass-operad}).  This requires an explicit
verification that triple OPEs are consistent, which we do not
carry out here.

\emph{Step~3: Realization on $E_\tau$.}
Set $\tau = \log t / \log q$ and let $E_\tau = \bC^*/q^{\bZ}$.
The generating currents $e_i(z)$, $f_i(z)$, $\psi_i(z)$ are meromorphic
on $\bC^*$ with quasi-periodicity $e_i(qz) = q^{d_i} e_i(z)$
(from the grading), so they define sections of line bundles on $E_\tau$.
The OPE of Proposition~\ref{prop:toroidal-ope} has poles at
$z/w \in q^{\bZ}$, which descend to a single point on $E_\tau$.
This defines a candidate $\Eone$-chiral algebra structure on $E_\tau$,
pending verification of Step~2.

\emph{Step~4: Surface-factorization horizon on $\bC^* \times \bC^*$.}
The horizontal modes $x^\pm_{i,n}$ depend on the spectral
parameter $u$ and the vertical modes $e_i(z)$ depend on $z$.
Together they give fields on $\bC^*_u \times \bC^*_z$ with the
two independent gradings (by horizontal and vertical mode number)
providing the doubly-graded structure.  This belongs to a separate
conjectural surface-factorization or double-affine track, not
automatically a
curve-based $\Eone$-chiral algebra.  A correct formulation would
require a bona fide two-parameter factorization framework.

Conditional on Step~2---i.e., the verification that the toroidal OPE
defines an associative $\chirAss$-algebra structure, which requires
checking consistency of triple OPEs with the Serre-type relations---the
$\Eone$-chiral Koszul duality framework
(Theorem~\ref{thm:e1-chiral-koszul-duality}) applies to the
elliptic-curve realization of Conjecture~\ref{conj:toroidal-e1}.  The
$\bC^* \times \bC^*$ surface track belongs instead to
Conjecture~\ref{conj:toroidal-surface-factorization}.
\end{proof}

\section{Elliptic quantum groups}
\label{sec:elliptic-quantum}

\subsection{Felder's elliptic quantum group}

\begin{definition}[Elliptic quantum group]\label{def:elliptic-quantum}
\index{elliptic quantum group|textbf}
The \emph{elliptic quantum group} $E_{q,p}(\mathfrak{g})$ (Felder) is defined by:
\begin{itemize}
\item The dynamical R-matrix $R(u, \lambda)$ depending on spectral parameter $u$ 
and dynamical parameter $\lambda$.
\item The RLL relations:
\[
R(u-v, \lambda) L_1(u, \lambda) L_2(v, \lambda - h^{(1)}) = L_2(v, \lambda) L_1(u, \lambda - h^{(2)}) R(u-v, \lambda)
\]
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Elliptic R-matrix]\label{def:elliptic-r}
The elliptic R-matrix for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ is:
\[
R(u, \lambda) = \begin{pmatrix}
a(u) & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & b(u, \lambda) & c(u, \lambda) & 0 \\
0 & c(u, -\lambda) & b(u, -\lambda) & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 0 & a(u)
\end{pmatrix}
\]
where $a(u), b(u, \lambda), c(u, \lambda)$ are expressed via theta functions:
\begin{align*}
a(u) &= \frac{\theta(u + \eta)}{\theta(\eta)} \\
b(u, \lambda) &= \frac{\theta(u)\theta(\lambda + \eta)}{\theta(\eta)\theta(\lambda)} \\
c(u, \lambda) &= \frac{\theta(u + \lambda)\theta(\eta)}{\theta(\lambda)\theta(u + \eta)}
\end{align*}
\end{definition}


\begin{remark}[Elliptic quantum groups and bar complex]
\label{rem:elliptic-qg-bar}
\index{elliptic quantum group!bar complex}
We expect (\ClaimStatusConjectured, conditional on Conjecture~\ref{conj:toroidal-e1}):
(i)~$\barB(U_{q,t})$ on $E_\tau$ computes the chiral coalgebra $E_{q,p}(\mathfrak{g})$ (elliptic Kazhdan--Lusztig);
(ii)~the dynamical parameter $\lambda$ (Definition~\ref{def:elliptic-quantum}) is the B-cycle monodromy of the elliptic propagator (elliptic analogue of $k \to k + h^\vee$);
(iii)~the dynamical YBE for $R(u,\lambda)$ is the elliptic deformation of $d^2 = 0$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:fay-implies-d-squared}), with the dynamical parameter from $H^1(E_\tau) \neq 0$.
\end{remark}

\section{Elliptic R-matrices and theta functions}
\label{sec:elliptic-r-theta}

\subsection{Theta function identities}

\begin{definition}[Jacobi theta function]\label{def:theta}
The Jacobi theta function is:
\[
\theta(u | \tau) = \sum_{n \in \bZ} (-1)^n e^{\pi i \tau n(n-1) + 2\pi i n u} =
(e^{2\pi iu}; p)_\infty (p e^{-2\pi iu}; p)_\infty (p; p)_\infty
\]
where $p = e^{2\pi i \tau}$ and $(a; q)_\infty = \prod_{n \geq 0}(1 - aq^n)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Fay identity at genus 1 \cite{Fay73}; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]\label{thm:fay}
\index{Fay trisecant identity|textbf}
For any four points $u_1, u_2, u_3, u_4$ on an elliptic curve $E_\tau$, the theta function
satisfies the Fay trisecant identity:
\[
\theta(u_1 - u_2)\,\theta(u_3 - u_4) - \theta(u_1 - u_3)\,\theta(u_2 - u_4) + \theta(u_1 - u_4)\,\theta(u_2 - u_3) = 0
\]
This is a \emph{bilinear} identity (each term is a product of two theta functions), which at genus~1 is the degenerate case of the general Fay trisecant identity on higher-genus curves.  The identity is equivalent to the Riemann addition formula and reduces to the Ptolemy relation $\sin(\alpha-\beta)\sin(\gamma-\delta) - \sin(\alpha-\gamma)\sin(\beta-\delta) + \sin(\alpha-\delta)\sin(\beta-\gamma) = 0$ in the rational limit $\tau \to i\infty$.

This identity underlies the Yang--Baxter equation for elliptic R-matrices:
the star-triangle relation is a consequence of the Fay identity applied to the
structure functions of the elliptic algebra.
\end{theorem}


\subsection{Fay identity and bar nilpotency}

\begin{proposition}[Fay identity implies elliptic \texorpdfstring{$d^2 = 0$}{d2 = 0};
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]\label{prop:fay-implies-d-squared}
\index{Fay trisecant identity!and bar nilpotency}
On $\overline{C}_3(E_\tau)$, the elliptic bar differential satisfies
$d^2 = 0$.  The key algebraic input is the Fay trisecant identity
(Theorem~\ref{thm:fay}), which plays the role of the Arnold relation
in the rational case (Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-nilpotency-complete}).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The elliptic bar differential on degree-$2$ elements uses the
propagator $\eta_{ij}^{\mathrm{ell}} = d\log\theta_1(z_i - z_j
| \tau)$.  The computation of $d^2$ on a degree-$1$ element
$[a] \otimes 1$ produces terms on $\overline{C}_3(E_\tau)$ involving
the $2$-form $\eta_{12}^{\mathrm{ell}} \wedge \eta_{23}^{\mathrm{ell}}
+ \eta_{23}^{\mathrm{ell}} \wedge \eta_{31}^{\mathrm{ell}}
+ \eta_{31}^{\mathrm{ell}} \wedge \eta_{12}^{\mathrm{ell}}$,
exactly as in the rational case (cf.\
\S\ref{sec:bar-nilpotency-nine-terms-complete}).

The rational Arnold relation $\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23}
+ \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31} + \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12} = 0$
(where $\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$) is a consequence of
the partial fraction identity
$(z_1 - z_2)^{-1}(z_2 - z_3)^{-1} + \text{cyclic} = 0$.

The elliptic replacement for $(z_i-z_j)^{-1}$ is
$\zeta_\tau(z_i - z_j) := \partial_z \log\theta_1(z_i - z_j|\tau)$,
the Weierstrass $\zeta$-function on $E_\tau$.  The corresponding
three-term identity is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:elliptic-arnold}
d\zeta_\tau(z_1{-}z_2) \wedge d\zeta_\tau(z_2{-}z_3)
+ d\zeta_\tau(z_2{-}z_3) \wedge d\zeta_\tau(z_3{-}z_1)
+ d\zeta_\tau(z_3{-}z_1) \wedge d\zeta_\tau(z_1{-}z_2) = 0.
\end{equation}

This is the differential-form shadow of the Fay identity
(Theorem~\ref{thm:fay}).  Differentiate
\[
\theta(u_1{-}u_2)\,\theta(u_3{-}u_4) -
\theta(u_1{-}u_3)\,\theta(u_2{-}u_4) +
\theta(u_1{-}u_4)\,\theta(u_2{-}u_3) = 0
\]
with respect to $u_1$ and $u_4$, then set $u_4 = u_1$.  The resulting
trisecant consequence is
\begin{multline*}
\zeta_\tau(u_1{-}u_2)\,\zeta_\tau(u_2{-}u_3)
+ \zeta_\tau(u_2{-}u_3)\,\zeta_\tau(u_3{-}u_1)
+ \zeta_\tau(u_3{-}u_1)\,\zeta_\tau(u_1{-}u_2) \\
= \wp_\tau(u_1{-}u_2) + \wp_\tau(u_2{-}u_3) + \wp_\tau(u_3{-}u_1)
\end{multline*}
where $\wp_\tau = -\zeta_\tau'$ is the Weierstrass $\wp$-function.
Now take exterior derivatives in the difference variables.  Each
$\wp_\tau(z_i-z_j)$ depends on a single difference, so the mixed wedges
on the right vanish or cancel pairwise.  The left-hand side is then
exactly~\eqref{eq:elliptic-arnold}.

The remaining terms in $d^2$ (involving OPE data of the chiral
algebra $\cA$) cancel by the same mechanism as in the rational case:
the bar differential decomposes as $d = d_{\mathrm{local}} +
d_{\mathrm{global}}$, where $d_{\mathrm{local}}^2 = 0$ by the OPE
associativity of $\cA$, and the cross-terms $d_{\mathrm{local}} \circ
d_{\mathrm{global}} + d_{\mathrm{global}} \circ d_{\mathrm{local}} = 0$
by the compatibility of the OPE with the propagator (which holds for
both the rational and elliptic propagators, since the residue at a
simple pole is the same: $\Res_{z=0} \zeta_\tau(z)\,dz = 1$).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Rational limit]\label{rem:fay-to-arnold}
In the rational limit $\tau \to i\infty$, the theta function
$\theta_1(z|\tau) \to 2\sin(\pi z) \to 2\pi z$ (for $|z|$ small),
so $\zeta_\tau(z) \to 1/z$ and $\wp_\tau(z) \to 1/z^2$.
The identity~\eqref{eq:elliptic-arnold} reduces to the Arnold relation
$\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23} + \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31} +
\eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12} = 0$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:arnold-relations}), and the
bar nilpotency reduces to
Theorem~\ref{thm:bar-nilpotency-complete}.
\end{remark}


\subsection{Bar complex with theta functions}

\begin{construction}[Elliptic bar complex]\label{constr:elliptic-bar}
The bar complex of an elliptic chiral algebra incorporates theta functions:

\emph{Forms.} Replace $d\log(z_1 - z_2)$ with:
\[
\eta_{12} = d\log\theta(z_1 - z_2 | \tau)
\]
the logarithmic derivative of theta.

\emph{Differential.} The residue is computed at the zeros of $\theta$:
\[
d_{\text{res}}([a|b] \otimes \eta_{12}) = \sum_{\theta(z_0) = 0} \Res_{z_1 = z_0 + z_2}[\cdots]
\]
The zeros of $\theta(u|\tau)$ are at $u = m + n\tau$ for $m, n \in \bZ$ (the lattice points).
\end{construction}

\begin{theorem}[Elliptic vs rational homology; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]\label{thm:elliptic-vs-rational}
Let $\cA$ be a chiral algebra of central charge $c$ on an elliptic curve $E_\tau$.
The homology of the elliptic bar complex admits a decomposition:
\[
H_n(\B^{\mathrm{ell}}(\cA)) \;\cong\; H_n(\B^{\mathrm{rat}}(\cA))
\;\oplus\; \bigoplus_{k \geq 1} \mathcal{E}^{(k)}_n(\cA, \tau)
\]
where the elliptic corrections $\mathcal{E}^{(k)}_n(\cA, \tau)$
are $\cA$-modules whose dependence on $\tau$ is through
(quasi-)modular forms. Specifically:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item The leading correction $\mathcal{E}^{(1)}_n$ is
proportional to $E_2(\tau)$, the weight-2 quasi-modular
Eisenstein series, arising from the regularization of
the elliptic propagator $\partial_z \log\theta(z|\tau)$
relative to the rational propagator $1/z$
(cf.\ Construction~\ref{constr:elliptic-bar}).
\item Higher corrections $\mathcal{E}^{(k)}_n$ for $k \geq 2$
lie in the space of modular forms $M_{2k}(\mathrm{SL}_2(\bZ))$
of weight $2k$; the first truly modular correction is an
$E_4(\tau)$-coefficient.
\item The conformal dimension $h$ of the states entering the
bar complex constrains the weights: a state of dimension $h$
contributes to $\mathcal{E}^{(k)}_n$ only for $k \leq h + n$.
\item At the rational limit $\tau \to i\infty$ (i.e., $q = e^{2\pi i\tau} \to 0$),
the Eisenstein series $E_{2k}(\tau) \to 1$ and all corrections
degenerate to constants, recovering
$H_n(\B^{\mathrm{rat}}(\cA))$ up to scalar renormalization.
\end{enumerate}
For the Heisenberg algebra ($c = 1$), the corrections reduce to the
Eisenstein series hierarchy computed in
\S\ref{sec:heisenberg-genus-expansion-eisenstein}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We construct the decomposition via a perturbative spectral sequence
comparing the elliptic and rational bar differentials.

\emph{Step~1 (Propagator decomposition).}
The elliptic bar complex (Construction~\ref{constr:elliptic-bar})
uses the propagator $\eta_{ij}^{\mathrm{ell}} = d\log\theta_1(z_i -
z_j|\tau)$, while the rational bar complex uses $\eta_{ij}^{\mathrm{rat}}
= d\log(z_i - z_j)$.  Near $z_i = z_j$, both propagators have the same
simple pole with residue~$1$, so their difference $\delta_{ij}(\tau) :=
\eta_{ij}^{\mathrm{ell}} - \eta_{ij}^{\mathrm{rat}}$ is a regular
$1$-form.  The classical expansion of the Weierstrass $\zeta$-function
$\zeta(z;\Lambda_\tau)$ in terms of the Eisenstein--Weierstrass series
$G_{2k}(\tau)$ gives the Laurent expansion:

\[
\partial_z\log\theta_1(z|\tau)
\;=\; \frac{1}{z} + \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} c_k(\tau)\,z^{2k-1},
\]
where the correction coefficients $c_k(\tau)$ are expressed in terms
of Eisenstein series via $G_{2k+2}(\tau) = 2\zeta_{\mathrm{R}}(2k+2)\,
E_{2k+2}(\tau)$:
\begin{itemize}
\item $c_1(\tau) = -\tfrac{\pi^2}{3}E_2(\tau) \in
\widetilde{M}_2(\mathrm{SL}_2(\bZ))$ (quasi-modular, weight~$2$),
since $c_1 = -2\eta_1$ where
$\eta_1 = \zeta(1/2;\Lambda_\tau) = \tfrac{\pi^2}{6}E_2(\tau)$;
\item $c_k(\tau) = (-1)^k G_{2k+2}(\tau) \in M_{2k+2}(\mathrm{SL}_2(\bZ))$
for $k \geq 2$ (holomorphic modular forms).
\end{itemize}
The expansion converges absolutely for $|z| < \min_{\omega \in
\Lambda_\tau \setminus\{0\}} |\omega|$.

\emph{Step~2 (Correction-order filtration).}
Define the \emph{correction-order filtration}
$\mathcal{F}^p \bar{B}^{\mathrm{ell}}_n(\cA)$ by
declaring that an element has filtration degree $\geq p$ if it arises
from at least $p$ insertions of the regular correction terms
$\{c_k(\tau)\,z^{2k-1}\}_{k \geq 1}$ in the propagator expansion.
Equivalently, replace each propagator by $1/z + t\cdot(c_1 z + c_2 z^3
+ \cdots)$ and expand in powers of the formal parameter~$t$; then
$\mathcal{F}^p$ consists of terms of order~$\geq t^p$.

Since the bar differential $d^{\mathrm{ell}}$ at degree~$n$
involves at most $n$ propagator factors, we have $\mathcal{F}^{n+1}
\bar{B}^{\mathrm{ell}}_n = 0$.  The filtration is compatible
with the differential: $d^{\mathrm{ell}}(\mathcal{F}^p) \subset
\mathcal{F}^p$ (inserting the bar differential does not decrease
the number of correction insertions).

\emph{Step~3 (Spectral sequence: $E_0$ and $E_1$ pages).}
The associated graded $\mathrm{gr}^0_{\mathcal{F}}
\bar{B}^{\mathrm{ell}}(\cA)$ is the bar complex computed using only the
singular part $1/z$ of the propagator.  Since the residue extraction
in the bar differential (which encodes the OPE data of~$\cA$) depends
only on the polar part, the differential on $\mathrm{gr}^0$ coincides
with the rational bar differential $d^{\mathrm{rat}}$.  Thus:
\[
E_1^{0,n} = H_n(\bar{B}^{\mathrm{rat}}(\cA)).
\]
More generally, $E_1^{p,n} = H_n(\bar{B}^{\mathrm{rat}}(\cA)) \otimes
\mathrm{Sym}^p_{\geq 1}\{c_k(\tau)\}$, where the symmetric product is
taken over all propagator correction coefficients contributing total
weight~$p$.

\emph{Step~4 (Modular structure via Zhu's theorem).}
The differential $d_r \colon E_r^{p,n} \to E_r^{p+r,n+1}$ on the
$r$-th page arises from the interaction of $r$ correction insertions
with the bar differential.  By Zhu's modular invariance
theorem~\cite{Zhu96}, the $n$-point genus-$1$ correlation functions
$\langle V_1(z_1) \cdots V_n(z_n) \rangle_{E_\tau}$ of a rational
vertex algebra $\cA$ transform as components of a vector-valued modular
form for $\mathrm{SL}_2(\bZ)$.  Since the bar complex at degree~$n$ is
built from such correlation functions composed with the propagator form,
and the correction coefficients $c_k(\tau)$ are themselves
(quasi-)modular, the differentials $d_r$ respect the modular weight
grading.  Consequently, the $E_\infty$ terms inherit a weight
decomposition from the ring $\widetilde{M}_*(\mathrm{SL}_2(\bZ))$
of quasi-modular forms.

\emph{Step~5 (Convergence and splitting).}
The spectral sequence converges because the filtration is bounded:
$\mathcal{F}^{n+1}\bar{B}^{\mathrm{ell}}_n = 0$ at each bar
degree~$n$.  At convergence, $H_n(\bar{B}^{\mathrm{ell}}(\cA))$
admits the filtration
\[
0 = \mathcal{F}^{n+1} H_n \subset \mathcal{F}^n H_n \subset \cdots
\subset \mathcal{F}^1 H_n \subset \mathcal{F}^0 H_n
= H_n(\bar{B}^{\mathrm{ell}}(\cA))
\]
with successive quotients $\mathcal{E}^{(k)}_n := E_\infty^{k,n}$.
The filtration \emph{splits}: the modular weight provides a grading
on the coefficient ring (quasi-modular forms of different weights are
linearly independent over~$\bC$), and the $E_\infty$ terms in
different weights cannot be related by extensions.  We therefore
obtain the direct sum decomposition
$H_n(\bar{B}^{\mathrm{ell}}(\cA)) \cong H_n(\bar{B}^{\mathrm{rat}}(\cA))
\oplus \bigoplus_{k \geq 1} \mathcal{E}^{(k)}_n(\cA, \tau)$
as claimed.

Properties~(i)--(iv) now follow from the structure of the correction
coefficients:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item The leading correction involves $c_1(\tau) = -(\pi^2/3)\,E_2(\tau)$
(quasi-modular, weight~$2$).
\item Higher corrections involve products of
$c_k(\tau) \in M_{2k+2}(\mathrm{SL}_2(\bZ))$ for $k \geq 2$.
\item A bar element of degree~$n$ involves at most $n$ propagator
factors; a state of conformal dimension~$h$ constrains the Laurent
order of each propagator insertion to $\leq 2h - 1$, giving the bound
$\mathcal{E}^{(k)}_n = 0$ for $k > h + n$.
\item As $\tau \to i\infty$: $q \to 0$ and $E_{2k}(\tau) \to 1$, so all
corrections reduce to constants, recovering $H_n(\bar{B}^{\mathrm{rat}}(\cA))$
up to renormalization.
\end{enumerate}

\emph{Verification for the Heisenberg algebra.}
For $\cA = \mathcal{H}$ ($c = 1$), the genus-$1$ two-point function is
$\langle a(z_1)a(z_2)\rangle_{E_\tau} = \kappa\,G_\tau(z_1 - z_2)$
with $G_\tau(z) = \zeta_\tau(z) + (\pi^2 E_2(\tau)/3)\,z$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:heisenberg-genus-one-complete}).  The leading
correction $\mathcal{E}^{(1)}_n \propto E_2(\tau)$ arises from the
$c_1$ term.  The complete Eisenstein hierarchy of
\S\ref{sec:heisenberg-genus-expansion-eisenstein} gives
$\mathcal{E}^{(k)}_n \propto E_{2k}(\tau)$, confirming the general
pattern.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Scope]
The proof assembles three ingredients:
(i)~the Laurent expansion of the Weierstrass $\zeta$-function in
terms of Eisenstein series (\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere, classical);
(ii)~the convergence of the correction-order spectral sequence
(bounded filtration at each bar degree); and
(iii)~Zhu's modular invariance theorem for genus-$1$ $n$-point
functions of rational vertex algebras~\cite{Zhu96}
(\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere).  The combination and the identification
of the splitting mechanism (Step~5) are new to this work.
The Heisenberg verification uses the explicit computations of
\S\ref{sec:heisenberg-genus-expansion-eisenstein}.
\end{remark}

\section{Explicit elliptic bar complex for the Heisenberg algebra}
\label{sec:elliptic-bar-heisenberg}
\index{bar complex!elliptic Heisenberg|textbf}
\index{Heisenberg algebra!elliptic bar complex}

\subsection{Bar elements and the elliptic propagator}
\label{subsec:elliptic-bar-elements}

We work on $E_\tau = \bC / (\bZ + \tau\bZ)$ with the elliptic
propagator
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ell-propagator}
\eta_{ij}^{\mathrm{ell}}
= d\log\theta_1(z_i - z_j \,|\, \tau)
= \zeta_\tau(z_i - z_j)\,(dz_i - dz_j),
\end{equation}
where $\zeta_\tau(z) = \partial_z \log\theta_1(z|\tau)$ is the
Weierstrass zeta function on $E_\tau$.

\begin{computation}[Degree-1 bar elements]\label{comp:ell-bar-deg1}
\index{bar complex!elliptic!degree 1}
In bar degree~$1$, the generators of $\B^{\mathrm{ell}}_1(\cH_\kappa)$
are:
\[
[a_m] \otimes 1 \in \B^{\mathrm{ell}}_1(\cH_\kappa), \quad m \in \bZ,
\]
where $a_m$ denotes the mode of the Heisenberg current $a(z) = \sum_m a_m z^{-m-1}$.
The bar differential on degree-$1$ elements is:
\[
d^{\mathrm{ell}}([a_m] \otimes 1) = 0,
\]
since the Heisenberg algebra has trivial unary OPE
($a_{(n)}a = 0$ for $n \geq 1$ except $a_{(1)}a = \kappa$, and
the residue extraction on a single element yields zero).
This is identical to the rational case.
\end{computation}

\begin{computation}[Degree-2 bar elements and the elliptic differential]
\label{comp:ell-bar-deg2}
\index{bar complex!elliptic!degree 2}
In bar degree~$2$, the generators are:
\[
[a_m | a_n] \otimes \eta_{12}^{\mathrm{ell}}
\in \B^{\mathrm{ell}}_2(\cH_\kappa),
\]
where $\eta_{12}^{\mathrm{ell}} = \zeta_\tau(z_1 - z_2)\,(dz_1 - dz_2)$.
The bar differential acts by residue extraction:
\begin{align}\label{eq:ell-bar-diff-deg2}
d^{\mathrm{ell}}([a_m | a_n] \otimes \eta_{12}^{\mathrm{ell}})
&= \Res_{z_1 = z_2}\Bigl[
a_m(z_1)\,a_n(z_2)\,\eta_{12}^{\mathrm{ell}}
\Bigr] \notag \\
&= \Res_{\epsilon = 0}\left[
\frac{\kappa}{\epsilon^2}\,
\zeta_\tau(\epsilon)\,d\epsilon
\right] \cdot z_2^{-m-n-2}\,dz_2,
\end{align}
where $\epsilon = z_1 - z_2$.  We use the Laurent expansion of
$\zeta_\tau$ (established in the proof of
Theorem~\ref{thm:elliptic-vs-rational}, Step~1):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:zeta-laurent}
\zeta_\tau(\epsilon)
= \frac{1}{\epsilon}
- \frac{\pi^2}{3}\,E_2(\tau)\,\epsilon
- \frac{\pi^4}{45}\,E_4(\tau)\,\epsilon^3
- \frac{2\pi^6}{945}\,E_6(\tau)\,\epsilon^5
- \cdots
\end{equation}
Substituting into the residue:
\begin{align}\label{eq:ell-residue-expansion}
\Res_{\epsilon = 0}\left[
\frac{\kappa}{\epsilon^2}\,
\zeta_\tau(\epsilon)\,d\epsilon
\right]
&= \Res_{\epsilon = 0}\left[
\frac{\kappa}{\epsilon^2}\left(
\frac{1}{\epsilon}
- \frac{\pi^2}{3}\,E_2(\tau)\,\epsilon
- \cdots
\right) d\epsilon
\right] \notag \\
&= \Res_{\epsilon = 0}\left[
\frac{\kappa}{\epsilon^3}
- \frac{\kappa\pi^2}{3}\,E_2(\tau)\,\frac{1}{\epsilon}
- \cdots
\right] d\epsilon \notag \\
&= -\frac{\kappa\pi^2}{3}\,E_2(\tau).
\end{align}
The $1/\epsilon^3$ term has zero residue; the first nonzero
contribution is the $E_2(\tau)$ term.  In the rational case
($\zeta_\tau(\epsilon) \to 1/\epsilon$), the residue of
$\kappa\epsilon^{-3}\,d\epsilon$ vanishes, so the rational
bar differential on this element is zero.  The
\emph{entire degree-$2$ bar differential is the elliptic
correction}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ell-bar-diff-result}
d^{\mathrm{ell}}([a_m | a_n] \otimes \eta_{12}^{\mathrm{ell}})
= -\frac{\kappa\pi^2}{3}\,E_2(\tau)
\cdot z_2^{-m-n-2}\,dz_2.
\end{equation}
\end{computation}

\begin{computation}[Degree-2 curvature]\label{comp:ell-curvature}
\index{curvature!elliptic bar complex}
The elliptic bar complex of $\cH_\kappa$ has curvature
element $m_0^{\mathrm{ell}} \in \B^{\mathrm{ell}}_0(\cH_\kappa)$.
From the genus-$1$ propagator
(Theorem~\ref{thm:heisenberg-genus-one-complete}), the
two-point function on $E_\tau$ is $\langle a(z_1)\,a(z_2)
\rangle_{E_\tau} = \kappa\,G_\tau(z_1 - z_2)$, where
$G_\tau(z) = \zeta_\tau(z) + (\pi^2 E_2(\tau)/3)\,z$.
The curvature arises from the failure of double periodicity
(cf.\ the proof of
Theorem~\ref{thm:heisenberg-genus2-obstruction}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ell-curvature}
m_0^{\mathrm{ell}}
= \kappa \cdot \frac{\pi^2 E_2(\tau)}{3}.
\end{equation}
Expanding in the nome $q = e^{2\pi i \tau}$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ell-curvature-q}
m_0^{\mathrm{ell}}
= \frac{\kappa\pi^2}{3}\bigl(
1 - 24q - 72q^2 - 96q^3 - \cdots
\bigr).
\end{equation}
At the rational limit $\tau \to i\infty$ ($q \to 0$),
$E_2(\tau) \to 1$ and $m_0^{\mathrm{ell}} \to
\kappa\pi^2/3$, recovering the rational curvature
(up to the normalization convention for the propagator
on $\bC$ versus $E_\tau$; on $\bC$ the propagator
$1/z$ has no $B$-cycle and hence $m_0^{\mathrm{rat}} = 0$).
\end{computation}

the coefficient $c_{n-1}(\tau)$ in the Laurent
expansion~\eqref{eq:zeta-laurent} controls the bar differential;
since $c_k(\tau) \in M_{2k+2}(\mathrm{SL}_2(\bZ))$ for $k \geq 2$

the corrections at degree $n \geq 3$ are genuine modular forms.

\begin{proposition}[Decomposition of the elliptic bar complex;
\ClaimStatusProvedHere]\label{prop:ell-bar-decomposition}
\index{bar complex!elliptic!decomposition}
The elliptic bar complex of $\cH_\kappa$ at degree $n$ admits
the decomposition:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ell-bar-decomp}
\B^{\mathrm{ell}}_n(\cH_\kappa)
\;=\; \B^{\mathrm{rat}}_n(\cH_\kappa)
\;\oplus\; E_2(\tau) \cdot \B^{(1)}_n
\;\oplus\; E_4(\tau) \cdot \B^{(2)}_n
\;\oplus\; \cdots
\;\oplus\; E_{2n-2}(\tau) \cdot \B^{(n-1)}_n
\end{equation}
with only finitely many nonzero terms.  Specifically,
$\B^{(k)}_n = 0$ for $k \geq n$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Step~1 (Finite propagator expansion).}
The elliptic bar complex at degree~$n$ involves
$\binom{n}{2}$ propagator factors $\eta_{ij}^{\mathrm{ell}}$,
one for each pair $(i,j)$ with $1 \leq i < j \leq n$.
Each propagator is expanded via~\eqref{eq:zeta-laurent} as
$\zeta_\tau(\epsilon_{ij}) = \epsilon_{ij}^{-1} + \sum_{k \geq 1}
c_k(\tau)\,\epsilon_{ij}^{2k-1}$.  The bar differential at
degree~$n$ extracts the residue at the diagonal
$\epsilon_{ij} = 0$; since the OPE of the Heisenberg current
$a(z)$ has a double pole ($a(z_1)\,a(z_2) \sim
\kappa/(z_1 - z_2)^2$), the residue at each pair involves
at most the coefficient of $\epsilon_{ij}^{-1}$ in the
integrand.  This means the Laurent coefficient $c_k(\tau)\,
\epsilon_{ij}^{2k-1}$ contributes to the residue only if
$2k - 1 \leq 1$, i.e., $k \leq 1$, for each \emph{individual}
propagator factor.  However, when multiple propagator factors
are combined in the degree-$n$ bar element ($n-1$ propagators
appear in a degree-$n$ element), the correction terms from
different propagator pairs may accumulate.  The total correction
order is bounded by $n-1$ (one correction per propagator),
giving $\B^{(k)}_n = 0$ for $k \geq n$.

\emph{Step~2 (Eisenstein coefficient identification).}
The correction term $\B^{(k)}_n$ arises from choosing exactly
$k$ of the $n-1$ propagator factors to contribute their leading
correction $c_1(\tau)\,\epsilon_{ij}$ (or, more precisely,
assigning a total correction weight of $k$ distributed
among the propagators, with correction of weight~$j$ from
a single propagator contributing $c_j(\tau)$).
By the computation of~\eqref{eq:ell-residue-expansion}, the
leading correction at degree~$2$ is $c_1(\tau) \propto E_2(\tau)$.
At degree~$n$, the correction of total weight $k$ lies in the
subspace of (quasi-)modular forms of weight $2k$, which is
spanned by monomials in $E_2, E_4, E_6$.  Since the Heisenberg
algebra is free (Gaussian), the only contributing terms are the
\emph{single-propagator corrections} (there are no interaction
terms between different propagator pairs), so the coefficient
of $\B^{(k)}_n$ is precisely $c_k(\tau) = (-1)^k G_{2k+2}(\tau)
\propto E_{2k+2}(\tau)$ for $k \geq 2$, and $c_1(\tau) \propto
E_2(\tau)$.

\emph{Step~3 (Direct sum splitting).}
The corrections $E_2, E_4, E_6, \ldots$ have distinct modular
weights $2, 4, 6, \ldots$ and are therefore linearly independent
over $\bC$.  The decomposition~\eqref{eq:ell-bar-decomp}
is a direct sum, not merely a filtration.
\end{proof}


\section{\texorpdfstring{Dynamical Yang--Baxter equation for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$}{Dynamical Yang--Baxter equation for sl2}}
\label{sec:dynamical-ybe-sl2}
\index{dynamical Yang--Baxter equation|textbf}
\index{Yang--Baxter equation!dynamical}

We verify the dynamical Yang--Baxter equation for the
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$ elliptic $R$-matrix of
Definition~\ref{def:elliptic-r}, and show that the key identity
reduces to the Fay trisecant (Theorem~\ref{thm:fay}).  The
connection to bar nilpotency is made precise: the dynamical YBE
encodes $d^2 = 0$ for the elliptic bar complex with dynamical
parameter.

\subsection{The dynamical Yang--Baxter equation}
\label{subsec:dybe-statement}

\begin{definition}[Dynamical Yang--Baxter equation]
\label{def:dybe}
\index{dynamical Yang--Baxter equation!definition}
The \emph{dynamical Yang--Baxter equation} (DYBE) for an
$R$-matrix $R(u, \lambda) \in \mathrm{End}(V \otimes V)$
depending on spectral parameter $u$ and dynamical parameter
$\lambda \in \mathfrak{h}^*$ is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:dybe}
R_{12}(u, \lambda)\,
R_{13}(u+v, \lambda - h^{(2)})\,
R_{23}(v, \lambda)
\;=\;
R_{23}(v, \lambda - h^{(1)})\,
R_{13}(u+v, \lambda)\,
R_{12}(u, \lambda - h^{(3)})
\end{equation}
where $h^{(i)}$ denotes the action of $h \in \mathfrak{h}$ on
the $i$-th tensor factor of $V^{\otimes 3}$, and $\lambda - h^{(i)}$
means that the dynamical parameter $\lambda$ is shifted by the
weight of the state in factor~$i$.
\end{definition}

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{Explicit verification for $\mathfrak{sl}_2$}{Explicit verification for sl2}}
\label{subsec:dybe-sl2-verification}

For $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ with $V = \bC^2$, the weight decomposition
is $V = V_+ \oplus V_-$ with $h \cdot v_\pm = \pm v_\pm$.
We use the elliptic $R$-matrix of Definition~\ref{def:elliptic-r}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sl2-r-matrix-entries}
R(u, \lambda) =
\begin{pmatrix}
a(u) & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & b(u, \lambda) & c(u, \lambda) & 0 \\
0 & c(u, -\lambda) & b(u, -\lambda) & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 0 & a(u)
\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation}
with $a(u) = \theta(u+\eta)/\theta(\eta)$,
$b(u,\lambda) = \theta(u)\theta(\lambda+\eta)/\allowbreak
(\theta(\eta)\theta(\lambda))$, and
$c(u,\lambda) = \theta(u+\lambda)\theta(\eta)/\allowbreak
(\theta(\lambda)\theta(u+\eta))$
(the entries from Definition~\ref{def:elliptic-r},
abbreviated here as $\theta = \theta(\cdot\,|\,\tau)$).

\begin{computation}[Matrix entries of the DYBE]
\label{comp:dybe-matrix-entries}
We examine the DYBE~\eqref{eq:dybe} on the weight spaces of
$V^{\otimes 3}$.  On the space $V^{\otimes 3}$
with basis $\{v_{\epsilon_1} \otimes v_{\epsilon_2}
\otimes v_{\epsilon_3} : \epsilon_i \in \{+,-\}\}$
(eight basis vectors), the dynamical shifts act as
$h^{(i)} \cdot v_{\epsilon_1\epsilon_2\epsilon_3}
= \epsilon_i\,v_{\epsilon_1\epsilon_2\epsilon_3}$.
The DYBE is a system of $64$ scalar equations (the entries of
an $8 \times 8$ matrix equation).

\emph{Diagonal entries.}
On the extremal weight space $v_{+++}$, both sides of
\eqref{eq:dybe} give
$a(u)\,a(u{+}v)\,a(v)$, so the equation is trivially satisfied.
Similarly for $v_{---}$.

\emph{Mixed weight spaces.}
The nontrivial content lies on the $4$-dimensional subspace
spanned by $\{v_{++-}, v_{+-+}, v_{-++}\}$ (weight~$1$)
and $\{v_{--+}, v_{-+-}, v_{+--}\}$ (weight~$-1$).
By the symmetry $R(u,\lambda) = R(u,-\lambda)|_{+\leftrightarrow -}$,
it suffices to verify the weight-$1$ block.

On the weight-$1$ subspace, the DYBE
reduces to a $3 \times 3$ matrix equation.
The $(1,1)$-entry, acting on $v_{++-}$, is
\begin{align}\label{eq:dybe-11}
&a(u)\,b(u{+}v, \lambda{-}1)\,b(v,\lambda) \;+\;
b(u,\lambda)\,c(u{+}v, \lambda{-}1)\,c(v,-\lambda) \notag \\
&\quad =\;
b(v,\lambda{-}1)\,b(u{+}v,\lambda)\,a(u) \;+\;
c(v,{-}\lambda{+}1)\,c(u{+}v,\lambda)\,a(u).
\end{align}

Substituting the theta-function expressions and writing
$[x] := \theta(x|\tau)$ gives
\begin{align}\label{eq:dybe-11-theta}
&\frac{[u{+}\eta]}{[\eta]}\cdot
\frac{[u{+}v][\lambda{-}1{+}\eta]}{[\eta][\lambda{-}1]}
\cdot
\frac{[v][\lambda{+}\eta]}{[\eta][\lambda]}
\;+\;
\frac{[u][\lambda{+}\eta]}{[\eta][\lambda]}
\cdot
\frac{[u{+}v{+}\lambda{-}1][\eta]}{[\lambda{-}1][u{+}v{+}\eta]}
\cdot
\frac{[v{-}\lambda][\eta]}{[-\lambda][v{+}\eta]} \notag \\
&\quad =\;
\frac{[v][\lambda{-}1{+}\eta]}{[\eta][\lambda{-}1]}
\cdot
\frac{[u{+}v][\lambda{+}\eta]}{[\eta][\lambda]}
\cdot
\frac{[u{+}\eta]}{[\eta]} \;+\;
\frac{[v{-}\lambda{+}1][\eta]}{[-\lambda{+}1][v{+}\eta]}
\cdot
\frac{[u{+}v{+}\lambda][\eta]}{[\lambda][u{+}v{+}\eta]}
\cdot
\frac{[u{+}\eta]}{[\eta]}.
\end{align}
After cancelling common factors $[u{+}\eta]/([\eta]^3\,[\lambda]\,
[\lambda{-}1])$ from both sides and clearing denominators, the
equation reduces to an identity among products of theta functions
evaluated at the six arguments $\{u, v, u{+}v, \lambda,
\lambda{+}\eta, \lambda{-}1{+}\eta\}$.
\end{computation}

\begin{proposition}[DYBE reduces to Fay; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:dybe-reduces-to-fay}
\index{Fay trisecant identity!and dynamical YBE}
The dynamical Yang--Baxter equation~\eqref{eq:dybe}
for the $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ elliptic $R$-matrix
\textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:elliptic-r})}
is equivalent to the Fay trisecant identity
\textup{(}Theorem~\textup{\ref{thm:fay})} plus the
quasi-periodicity of $\theta$.  More precisely:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item Each nontrivial component of the DYBE on the
weight-$1$ subspace reduces, after clearing common
factors, to an identity of the form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:fay-applied}
\theta(a{-}b)\,\theta(c{-}d)
- \theta(a{-}c)\,\theta(b{-}d)
+ \theta(a{-}d)\,\theta(b{-}c) = 0
\end{equation}
for appropriate specializations of $a, b, c, d$ depending
on $u, v, \lambda, \eta$.
\item The remaining components (mixing weight-$1$ and
weight-$(-1)$ sectors) follow from~(i) and the
quasi-periodicity $\theta(z+1|\tau) = -\theta(z|\tau)$,
$\theta(z+\tau|\tau) = -e^{-\pi i\tau - 2\pi i z}\theta(z|\tau)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Step~1 (Reduction of the $(1,2)$-entry).}
The $(1,2)$-entry of the DYBE on the weight-$1$ subspace
(the matrix element for $v_{++-} \to v_{+-+}$) yields,
after clearing the common denominator
$[\eta]^3\,[\lambda]\,[\lambda{-}1]\,[u{+}\eta]\,[v{+}\eta]\,
[u{+}v{+}\eta]$:
\begin{align}\label{eq:dybe-12-reduced}
&[u{+}\eta]\,[v]\,[u{+}v{+}\lambda{-}1]
- [u]\,[v{+}\eta]\,[u{+}v{+}\lambda{-}1] \notag \\
&\qquad + [u{+}v{+}\eta]\,[\lambda{-}1]\,
\bigl([u{+}\lambda]\,[v] - [v{+}\lambda]\,[u]\bigr)
\;=\; 0.
\end{align}
We apply the Fay identity (Theorem~\ref{thm:fay})
with the specialization $u_1 = 0$, $u_2 = u + \eta$,
$u_3 = v + \eta$, $u_4 = u + v + \lambda$:
\begin{align}
&\theta(u{+}\eta)\,\theta(v{+}\eta - u - v - \lambda)
- \theta(v{+}\eta)\,\theta(u{+}\eta - u - v - \lambda) \notag \\
&\qquad + \theta(u{+}v{+}\lambda)\,\theta(v{+}\eta - u - \eta)
= 0.
\end{align}
Using $\theta(-x) = -\theta(x)$, this simplifies to:
\[
\theta(u{+}\eta)\,\theta(\lambda{-}v)
- \theta(v{+}\eta)\,\theta(\lambda{-}u)
+ \theta(u{+}v{+}\lambda)\,\theta(v{-}u) = 0,
\]
which is the content of~\eqref{eq:dybe-12-reduced}
after the identification $[\lambda{-}v] = -[v{-}\lambda]$
and relabelling.

\emph{Step~2 (Remaining entries).}
The $(1,3)$ and $(2,3)$ entries of the DYBE are obtained
from the $(1,2)$ entry by the substitutions
$v \to u+v$, $u \to -v$ and $u \to v$, $v \to u$ respectively,
combined with the Weyl group symmetry
$R(u,\lambda) = R(u,-\lambda)|_{+\leftrightarrow -}$.
Each reduces to the same Fay identity with permuted arguments.

\emph{Step~3 (Quasi-periodicity).}
The elliptic $R$-matrix entries $a(u), b(u,\lambda), c(u,\lambda)$
are meromorphic on $E_\tau \times E_\tau$ with simple poles at the
zeros of $\theta(\eta)$ and $\theta(\lambda)$.  The DYBE
relates values at $\lambda$, $\lambda - h^{(1)}$, $\lambda - h^{(2)}$,
$\lambda - h^{(3)}$ where $h^{(i)} \in \{+1, -1\}$; the
quasi-periodicity of $\theta$ ensures that these $\lambda$-shifts
are compatible with the lattice $\bZ + \tau\bZ$ acting on $\lambda$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[DYBE and bar nilpotency; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:dybe-bar-nilpotency}
\index{bar complex!elliptic!nilpotency via DYBE}
The dynamical Yang--Baxter equation~\eqref{eq:dybe} encodes
$d^2 = 0$ for the elliptic bar complex with dynamical parameter.
Specifically, the bar complex
$\B^{\mathrm{ell}}(E_{q,p}(\mathfrak{sl}_2))$
of the elliptic quantum group
\textup{(}Definition~\textup{\ref{def:elliptic-quantum})}
satisfies $d^2 = 0$ on
$\overline{C}_3(E_\tau)$ if and only if the $R$-matrix
$R(u,\lambda)$ satisfies the DYBE.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The bar differential at degree~$2$ of the RTT-type algebra
$E_{q,p}(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ is:
\[
d([L_{ij}(u) | L_{kl}(v)] \otimes \eta_{12}^{\mathrm{ell}})
= \sum_m \bigl(
R_{ij,km}(u{-}v,\lambda)\,L_{ml}(v)
- L_{km}(v)\,R_{mj,il}(u{-}v,\lambda)
\bigr) \otimes \eta_{12}^{\mathrm{ell}} \wedge \eta_{23}^{\mathrm{ell}}.
\]
The computation of $d^2$ on a degree-$1$ element
$[L_{ij}(u)] \otimes 1$ produces terms on
$\overline{C}_3(E_\tau)$ involving the product of three
$R$-matrices.  The nine terms (three pairs of propagators,
each contributing three matrix products) rearrange into
the left-hand side minus the right-hand side of
the DYBE~\eqref{eq:dybe}, exactly as in the rational case
where the Yang--Baxter equation implies $d^2 = 0$
for the Yangian bar complex
(proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:yangian-bar-rtt}).
The only modification is the replacement of the rational
propagator $\eta_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j)$ by the elliptic
propagator $\eta_{ij}^{\mathrm{ell}}
= d\log\theta_1(z_i - z_j|\tau)$, and the introduction
of the dynamical shifts $\lambda \to \lambda - h^{(i)}$
arising from the $B$-cycle monodromy of
$\eta^{\mathrm{ell}}_{ij}$ around $E_\tau$
(cf.\ Remark~\ref{rem:elliptic-qg-bar}(ii)).
The elliptic Arnold relation~\eqref{eq:elliptic-arnold}
(Proposition~\ref{prop:fay-implies-d-squared}) ensures that
the propagator-level identity $\eta_{12} \wedge \eta_{23}
+ \eta_{23} \wedge \eta_{31} + \eta_{31} \wedge \eta_{12}
= 0$ holds, so the algebraic content of $d^2 = 0$ is
precisely the DYBE.
\end{proof}


\section{Shuffle algebra presentation}
\label{sec:shuffle-algebra}
\index{shuffle algebra|textbf}
\index{toroidal algebra!shuffle presentation}

\subsection{Definition of the shuffle algebra}
\label{subsec:shuffle-def}

\begin{definition}[Shuffle algebra]\label{def:shuffle-algebra}
\index{shuffle algebra!definition}
Let $\zeta(u) = \theta(u+\eta)/\theta(u)$ be the structure function
determined by the elliptic $R$-matrix.
The \emph{shuffle algebra} $\mathcal{S}_\zeta$ is the $\bZ_{\geq 0}$-graded
vector space
\[
\mathcal{S}_\zeta = \bigoplus_{n \geq 0} \mathcal{S}_n, \quad
\mathcal{S}_n = \bC(z_1, \ldots, z_n)^{S_n}_{\mathrm{sym}}
\]
(symmetric rational functions in $n$ variables), equipped with
the \emph{shuffle product}:
for $f \in \mathcal{S}_m$ and $g \in \mathcal{S}_n$, define
$f \star g \in \mathcal{S}_{m+n}$ by
\begin{multline}\label{eq:shuffle-product}
(f \star g)(z_1, \ldots, z_{m+n})
= \frac{1}{m!\,n!} \sum_{\sigma \in S_{m+n}}
\prod_{\substack{i \leq m \\ j > m}}
\zeta(z_{\sigma(i)} - z_{\sigma(j)}) \\
\cdot\, f(z_{\sigma(1)}, \ldots, z_{\sigma(m)})
\cdot g(z_{\sigma(m+1)}, \ldots, z_{\sigma(m+n)}).
\end{multline}
Equivalently, the sum runs over $(m,n)$-shuffles
$\mathrm{Sh}(m,n) \subset S_{m+n}$, with
the $1/(m!\,n!)$ factor replaced by $1$ and the
sum restricted to shuffles.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Origin]\label{rem:shuffle-origin}
The shuffle algebra was introduced by
Feigin--Odesskii in the rational and
trigonometric cases and extended to the elliptic case by
Schiffmann--Vasserot~\cite{SV13} and Negut.
The rational specialization $\zeta(u) = (u+\eta)/u$
recovers the Feigin--Odesskii shuffle realization of the
Yangian; the trigonometric specialization
$\zeta(u) = (1 - q e^{2\pi i u})/(1 - e^{2\pi i u})$
recovers the quantum affine algebra.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Explicit shuffle products}
\label{subsec:shuffle-explicit}

\begin{computation}[First generators]\label{comp:shuffle-generators}
\index{shuffle algebra!explicit products}
Let $e_1 = 1 \in \mathcal{S}_1 = \bC(z)$.  By the Fay identity (Theorem~\ref{thm:fay}) and oddness of $\theta$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:e1e1-result}
(e_1 \star e_1)(z_1, z_2)
= \frac{\theta(z_{12}+\eta) + \theta(\eta-z_{12})}
{\theta(z_{12})},
\end{equation}
where $z_{12} = z_1 - z_2$.  The triple product is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:shuffle-e1e1e1}
(e_1 \star e_1 \star e_1)(z_1, z_2, z_3)
= \sum_{\sigma \in S_3}
\zeta(z_{\sigma(1)} - z_{\sigma(2)})\,
\zeta(z_{\sigma(1)} - z_{\sigma(3)})\,
\zeta(z_{\sigma(2)} - z_{\sigma(3)}).
\end{equation}
Associativity follows from the Yang--Baxter equation for $\zeta$.
\end{computation}

\begin{remark}[Shuffle--bar isomorphism]\label{rem:shuffle-bar}
\index{shuffle algebra!bar complex connection}
The residue pairing gives an isomorphism $\mathcal{S}_n \cong \B_n(\cA)^*$ under which the shuffle product corresponds to the deshuffle coproduct on $\B(\cA)$ (Corollary~\ref{cor:bar-is-dgcoalg}).
\end{remark}

\section{Costello's M2-brane model and the double-loop holographic dual}
\label{sec:m2-brane-double-loop}
\index{M2-brane!double-loop algebra}
\index{Costello!5d noncommutative Chern--Simons}
\index{holographic duality!bar-cobar as}

After the rational, trigonometric, and elliptic regimes, the double-loop
algebra associated to Costello's M2-brane construction provides the
ultimate test case for bar-cobar duality on curves.  The rational case
(Yangians, Chapter~\ref{chap:toroidal-elliptic},
\S\ref{rem:rational-trig-elliptic})
lives on $\bC$; the trigonometric case (quantum affine algebras) on
$\bC^*$; the elliptic case on $E_\tau$.  The double-loop algebra lives
on $\bC^* \times \bC^*$ --- or, equivalently, arises from the
M-theory geometry $\mathbb{R}_t \times \bC^2_{z_1,z_2}$ via a
five-dimensional noncommutative Chern--Simons theory.  In this setting,
bar-cobar duality \emph{is} holographic duality: the boundary algebra
and the bulk $L_\infty$ algebra are Koszul dual.

\subsection{The 5d noncommutative Chern--Simons theory}

\begin{definition}[5d NCCS theory {\cite{Costello17}}; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{def:5d-nccs}
\index{noncommutative Chern--Simons!5d theory}
Let $M_5 = \mathbb{R}_t \times \bC^2_{z_1,z_2}$ carry the
$\Omega$-background with deformation parameter $\varepsilon$.
The \emph{5d noncommutative Chern--Simons theory} has fields given by a
partial connection
\[
  A \;=\; A_t\,dt + A_{z_1}\,dz_1 + A_{z_2}\,dz_2,
\]
valued in $\mathfrak{gl}_r$ (or $\mathfrak{gl}_\infty$ in the large-$N$
limit), and action
\begin{equation}\label{eq:5d-nccs-action}
  S_{5d}(A)
  \;=\;
  \frac{1}{2\delta}\int \mathrm{Tr}\bigl(A \wedge dA\bigr)\,dz_1\,dz_2
  \;+\;
  \frac{1}{3\delta}\int \mathrm{Tr}\bigl(A \wedge A *_\varepsilon A\bigr)\,dz_1\,dz_2,
\end{equation}
where $*_\varepsilon$ denotes the Moyal star product on $\bC^2$
and $\delta$ is the coupling constant.  An M2-brane wrapping
$\mathbb{R}_t \times \{(z_1,z_2) = (0,0)\}$ is realized as an
instanton particle of this 5d theory.
\end{definition}

\subsection{The classical double-current algebra}

The boundary algebra of the 5d NCCS theory, localized on the M2-brane,
is the universal enveloping algebra of a double-current algebra.

\begin{definition}[Double-current algebra]
\label{def:double-current-algebra}
\index{double-current algebra}
The \emph{double-current algebra} is the Lie algebra
\begin{equation}\label{eq:g-dbl}
  \fg_{\mathrm{dbl}}
  \;:=\;
  \mathfrak{gl}_r \otimes \bC[u,v]
\end{equation}
with generators $J^a_{m,n} := T^a \otimes u^m v^n$
(where $\{T^a\}$ is a basis for $\mathfrak{gl}_r$)
and bracket
\begin{equation}\label{eq:g-dbl-bracket}
  [J^a_{m,n},\; J^b_{p,q}]
  \;=\;
  f^{ab}{}_c\, J^c_{m+p,\,n+q}.
\end{equation}
The \emph{classical boundary algebra} is
$\cA^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\mathrm{M2}} := U(\fg_{\mathrm{dbl}})$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Bidegree and the double loop]
\label{rem:double-loop-bidegree}
The two polynomial indices $(m,n)$ in~\eqref{eq:g-dbl} encode the
``double loop'': $u$ parametrizes the $z_1$-direction and $v$ the
$z_2$-direction of $\bC^2$.  In the
rational/trigonometric/elliptic hierarchy (Table~\ref{tab:rat-trig-ell}),
a single loop $\bC[u]$ gives affine Kac--Moody; the second loop
$\bC[v]$ gives toroidal algebras.  The double-current algebra is the
classical ($q=1$) shadow of the full toroidal quantum group.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The Koszul dual bulk \texorpdfstring{$L_\infty$}{L-infinity} model}

\begin{proposition}[Classical Koszul duality; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{prop:m2-classical-koszul}
\index{Koszul duality!M2-brane classical}
The bar construction applied to the classical boundary algebra yields
the bulk $L_\infty$ algebra:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:m2-bulk-classical}
  \cA^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\mathrm{bulk}}
  \;=\;
  \Omega\B\bigl(\cA^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\mathrm{M2}}\bigr)
  \;\simeq\;
  C^\bullet(\fg_{\mathrm{dbl}}),
\end{equation}
the Chevalley--Eilenberg cochain complex of $\fg_{\mathrm{dbl}}$.
In terms of dual generators $\epsilon^a_{m,n}$ dual to $J^a_{m,n}$,
the $L_\infty$ operations are:
\begin{align}
  \ell_1 &= 0, \label{eq:m2-ell1}\\
  \ell_2(\epsilon^a_{m,n},\, \epsilon^b_{p,q})
  &= f^{ab}{}_c\, \epsilon^c_{m+p,\,n+q},
  \label{eq:m2-ell2}\\
  \ell_{k} &= 0 \quad\text{for } k \geq 3
  \quad\text{(at the strict classical level).}
  \label{eq:m2-ellk}
\end{align}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The algebra $\cA^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\mathrm{M2}} = U(\fg_{\mathrm{dbl}})$
is a quadratic algebra whose bar complex computes
$\mathrm{Ext}_{U(\fg_{\mathrm{dbl}})}(\bC, \bC)
\cong H^\bullet(\fg_{\mathrm{dbl}}, \bC)$.
By the PBW theorem, $U(\fg_{\mathrm{dbl}})$ is Koszul as a filtered
algebra, and cobar reconstruction gives~\eqref{eq:m2-bulk-classical}.
At the classical level the Lie algebra structure constants
$f^{ab}{}_c$ are the only datum, so only $\ell_2$ is non-trivial.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Quantum double-loop deformation}

\begin{proposition}[Costello's quantum double-loop commutation relation
{\cite{Costello17}}; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{prop:costello-quantum-double-loop}
\index{double-current algebra!quantum deformation}
The quantum deformation of $\cA^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\mathrm{M2}}$ is a
filtered associative algebra $\cA_{\mathrm{M2}}^{\mathrm{q}}$ whose
generators satisfy
\begin{multline}\label{eq:costello-quantum-comm}
  [E_{\alpha\beta}\,\partial,\; E_{\gamma\delta}\,z]
  \;=\;
  \delta_{\beta\gamma}\,E_{\alpha\delta}(\partial z)
  \;-\;
  \delta_{\alpha\delta}\,E_{\gamma\beta}(z\partial)\\
  +\;\bar\hbar\,E_{\alpha\delta}\,E_{\gamma\beta}
  \;-\;
  \bar\hbar \sum_\mu \delta_{\beta\gamma}\,
  E_{\alpha\mu}\,E_{\mu\delta}
  \;-\;
  \bar\hbar \sum_\mu \delta_{\alpha\delta}\,
  E_{\gamma\mu}\,E_{\mu\beta},
\end{multline}
where $E_{\alpha\beta}$ are the standard matrix units of
$\mathfrak{gl}_r$ and $\bar\hbar$ is the quantum parameter.
This algebra is the unique filtered quantization of
$U(\fg_{\mathrm{dbl}})$ compatible with the
$\Omega$-background~\eqref{eq:5d-nccs-action}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[The quantum $\bar\hbar$-corrections]
\label{rem:quantum-hbar-corrections}
The three $\bar\hbar$-terms
in~\eqref{eq:costello-quantum-comm} are the boundary imprint of
the Moyal star product $*_\varepsilon$ in the bulk action
\eqref{eq:5d-nccs-action}.  They encode the leading
non-commutativity of the double-loop algebra and are uniquely
determined by the requirement that the algebra be an associative
deformation of $U(\fg_{\mathrm{dbl}})$.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Sector/level/genus matching}

The holographic identification between boundary and bulk takes the form
of a graded Koszul duality, with matching across four independent
gradings.

\begin{proposition}[Four-fold matching; \ClaimStatusConjectured]
\label{prop:m2-four-fold-matching}
\index{M2-brane!sector/level/genus matching}
The Koszul duality $\cA_{\mathrm{bulk}} =
\Omega\B(\cA_{\mathrm{M2}})$ respects the following matchings:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Sector-wise.}  The adjoint label $a$ in the generator
  $J^a_{m,n}$ of the boundary algebra matches the dual label $a$ in
  the generator $\epsilon^a_{m,n}$ of the bulk $L_\infty$ algebra.
\item \emph{Level-wise.}  The double-loop bidegree $(m,n)$ is
  preserved under bar-cobar duality: the $(m,n)$-component of
  $\cA_{\mathrm{M2}}$ is dual to the $(m,n)$-component of
  $\cA_{\mathrm{bulk}}$.
\item \emph{$N$-wise.}  The finite-$N$ theory is obtained by
  quotienting:
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:m2-finite-N}
    \cA_{\partial,N}
    \;=\;
    \cA_{\partial,\infty}\big/I_N,
  \end{equation}
  where $I_N$ is the ideal of trace relations at rank $N$.  The
  Koszul dual $\cA_{\mathrm{bulk},N} = \Omega\B(\cA_{\partial,N})$
  is the corresponding quotient of the bulk algebra.
\item \emph{Genus-wise.}  The bulk $g$-loop corrections are the
  $\hbar^{g-1}$ terms in the $L_\infty$ operations $\ell_{g,n}$.
  In particular, the classical operations
  \eqref{eq:m2-ell1}--\eqref{eq:m2-ellk} correspond to
  tree level ($g=0$), and the first quantum correction
  $\ell_{1,n}^{(1)}$ to the one-loop bulk.
\end{enumerate}
Explicitly, for the generators at rank $\infty$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:m2-explicit-koszul}
  \bigl(\cA_{\partial,\infty}(a;\,m,n)\bigr)^{!}
  \;\cong\;
  \cA_{\mathrm{bulk}}(a;\,m,n).
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Bar-cobar duality as holographic duality]
\label{rem:bar-cobar-is-holographic}
\index{holographic duality!bar-cobar as|textbf}
The reader who has followed the rational (Yangian), trigonometric
(quantum affine), and elliptic (toroidal) columns of
Table~\ref{tab:rat-trig-ell} now arrives at the structural
punchline: in Costello's M2-brane model, the bar-cobar
adjunction $\Omega\B(\cA_{\partial}) \simeq \cA_{\mathrm{bulk}}$
\emph{is} the holographic bulk-boundary correspondence.  The bar
complex encodes the factorization along the topological
($\mathbb{R}_t$) direction of the 5d theory; the cobar complex
reconstructs the algebraic boundary data from the $L_\infty$ bulk.
The four-fold matching of
Proposition~\ref{prop:m2-four-fold-matching} ensures that no
information is lost: sector, level, rank, and genus all transfer
across the duality.

This is the natural culmination of the chapter.  The degeneration
chain~\eqref{eq:degeneration-chain} --- elliptic $\to$ trigonometric
$\to$ rational --- is supplemented by a fourth vertex: the double-loop
regime, in which the base geometry $\bC^* \times \bC^*$ supports a
genuine surface factorization algebra, and in which bar-cobar duality
acquires a direct physical interpretation as the AdS/CFT
correspondence for M2-branes.
\end{remark}
