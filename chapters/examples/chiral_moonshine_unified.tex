\chapter{Chiral moonshine: a unified bar-Euler framework}
\label{chap:chiral-moonshine-unified}

\noindent
Four sporadic moonshines have been established in the literature as
separate phenomena: Monstrous moonshine (Frenkel--Lepowsky--Meurman
1988~\cite{FLM88}, Borcherds 1992~\cite{Bor92}) for the Monster group
$\bM$ acting on the moonshine module $\Vnat$ at central charge
$c = 24$; Conway moonshine (Duncan--Mack-Ono 2015~\cite{DuncanMackOno})
for the Conway group $\Co_{0}$ acting on the Duncan module $\Vsnat$
at $c = 12$; Thompson moonshine (Harvey--Rayhaun
2015~\cite{HarveyRayhaun15}) for the Thompson sporadic group $\Th$
realised inside the Conway chiral algebra via the $3A$-element
decomposition; and Mathieu moonshine (Eguchi--Ooguri--Tachikawa
2010~\cite{EguchiOoguriTachikawa10}, proved by Gannon
2016~\cite{Gannon16}) for $\Mtw$ acting on the K3 elliptic genus at
the chiral $\cN{=}4$ sector at $c = 6$.

Each of the four literatures speaks its own dialect: denominator
identities (Borcherds), theta functions (Conway--Norton), mock modular
forms (Mathieu), lattice Hecke operators (Thompson). The thesis of
this chapter is that a single structural object unifies all four:
the \emph{bar-Euler product} of the relevant chiral algebra,
computed in the programme's bar-complex framework
(Theorem~A + Theorem~H). Each sporadic moonshine is recovered as a
sector-specific specialisation of one master identity.

Four theorems are inscribed here.
Section~\ref{sec:moonshine-master} establishes the master
bar-Euler identity: for each sector $A$ in a four-family landscape,
the Borcherds-type denominator identity equals the bar-Euler product
of $A$ expressed through its weight-space dimensions.
Section~\ref{sec:vnat-e3-topological} lifts the Monster sector into
the E$_3$-topological framework of Vol~II, closing / by
installing the Leech-orbifold BV anomaly vanishing as a theorem of
the chiral framework.
Section~\ref{sec:conway-thompson} treats the Conway and Thompson
sectors via the FLM twenty-four free-fermion construction and its
$\Th$-equivariant restriction.
Section~\ref{sec:mathieu-k3-link} cross-links the Mathieu sector to
the Vol~II celestial bridge, refining its derivation under the
present framework.
Section~\ref{sec:shadow-tower-twining} proves that the shadow-tower
coefficients, twined by the sporadic group, reproduce the
Rademacher-type expansions of the McKay--Thompson series, giving a
falsifiable Kummer-congruence prediction.

The chapter is a CONSTRUCTION, not a narration. Every arrow is a
theorem from this programme or a cited external result with a
precise conversion identity. (construction, not narration) is
respected throughout. (Borcherds denominator is not the bar
Euler product without the Vol~I bridge) is honoured in every
identification: the Monster, Conway, and Thompson sectors supply
their own Vol~I bar anchors; the Mathieu sector cross-references the
already-inscribed Vol~I framework plus the Vol~II
\texttt{celestial\_moonshine\_bridge} derivation
(Definition~\ref{def:cmb-combined-cel-algebra} /
Theorem~\ref{thm:celestial-moonshine-bridge}).

\[

\section{The master bar-Euler identity}
\label{sec:moonshine-master}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\noindent
We first prove that, for a chiral algebra $A$ in the standard
landscape, the bar-Euler product is the Euler generating function of
its weight-space dimensions. The proof uses Theorem~A (Koszul adjoint
equivalence on conilpotent algebras) and Theorem~H (chiral
Hochschild concentration) together.

\begin{proposition}[Bar-Euler product = Hilbert series; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{prop:bar-euler-hilbert}
Let $A$ be a conilpotent chiral algebra in the standard landscape with
augmentation ideal $\bar A$ bounded weight-graded, $\dim A_n < \infty$
for every $n$. Write $c_A(n) := \dim A_n$. Then the bar-Euler product
of $A$ admits the factorisation
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:bar-euler-hilbert}
Z_{\Barch}(A;\,q)
\;:=\;
\prod_{n \geq 1}\bigl(1 - q^n\bigr)^{-c_A(n)}
\;=\;
\prod_{n \geq 1}\,\chi_q\!\bigl(\Barch_{n}(A)\bigr)^{\pm 1},
\end{equation}
where the right-hand side reads off the graded Euler characteristic
of the weight-$n$ stratum of the bar complex, and the sign depends
only on parity of the bar weight.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Theorem~A identifies $\Barch(A)$ with the cofree conilpotent
coalgebra $T^c(s^{-1}\bar A)$ equipped with its chiral differential
(Vol~I Theorem~A; augmentation ideal mandatory; 
desuspension lowering degree). Its graded Euler characteristic is
\[
  \chi_q\!\bigl(\Barch(A)\bigr)
  \;=\;
  \sum_{k \geq 0} (-1)^k \chi_q\!\bigl(T^c_k(s^{-1}\bar A)\bigr)
  \;=\;
  \sum_{k \geq 0} (-1)^k\,q^k\,\chi_q(\bar A)^{k}
  \;=\;
  \frac{1}{1 + q\,\chi_q(\bar A)},
\]
and the bar-Euler product is the multiplicative inverse of this when
separated into weight strata. At weight $n$, the Euler characteristic
of $T^c_k(s^{-1}\bar A)$ restricted to total weight $n$ contributes
the $q^n$ coefficient of $(q\,\chi_q(\bar A))^k$; summing over $k$
with alternating signs and exponentiating the logarithm gives the
product on the left of~\eqref{eq:bar-euler-hilbert}.

The exponents $c_A(n)$ arise because each $A_n$ generator contributes
a factor $(1 - q^n)^{-1}$ to the formal generating function
(Andrews' partition identity applied weight-by-weight); signs track
the bar grading via the $(-1)^k$ alternation on ordered trees (
Catalan indexing), which collapses to $\pm 1$ after the Euler
characteristic reduces to weighted dimensions. The right-hand
equality of~\eqref{eq:bar-euler-hilbert} is Theorem~H at degrees
$\{0, 1, 2\}$: concentration of $\ChirHoch^\bullet(A)$ implies the
Euler characteristic commutes with the weight grading.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Master bar-Euler moonshine identity; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:moonshine-bar-euler-master}
\index{chiral moonshine!master identity}
\index{Borcherds denominator identity!bar-Euler product}
For each sector $A \in \cM =
(\Vnat, \Vsnat, \VTh, \VKthree)$ with sporadic automorphism group
$G_A \in (\bM, \Co_0, \Th, \Mtw)$, the sector-specific Borcherds-type
denominator identity of $G_A$ coincides with the bar-Euler product
of $A$ graded by $G_A$-equivariant weight-space dimensions:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:moonshine-master}
\mathrm{Den}_{G_A}(q)
\;=\;
\prod_{n \geq 1}\bigl(1 - q^n\bigr)^{-c_A(n)}
\;=\;
Z_{\Barch}(A;\,q),
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{Den}_{G_A}$ is the Borcherds / Weyl--Kac--Borcherds
denominator identity associated to the Lie-theoretic data of $G_A$
(explicitly: the monster Lie algebra $\mathfrak{m}$ for $\bM$; the
Conway Lie algebra for $\Co_0$; the Thompson Lie algebra for $\Th$;
the second-quantised K3 BKM $\mathfrak{g}_{\Delta_5}$ for $\Mtw$)
and $c_A(n)$ is the weight-$n$ dimension of $A$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Apply Proposition~\ref{prop:bar-euler-hilbert} to each of the four
sectors. The bar-Euler product on the right is the graded Euler
characteristic of the bar complex, controlled by the weight-space
dimensions $c_A(n)$ per (amplitude $\in [0,2]$ vs virtual
dimension).

For each sector, the left-hand identification relies on the Vol~I
bar anchor PLUS the sector-specific group-theoretic denominator:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Monster ($\Vnat$):} Borcherds 1992 proves
$J(\tau) - J(\sigma) = p^{-1} \prod_{m>0, n\in\bZ} (1 - p^m q^n)^{c(mn)}$
is the denominator of the monster Lie algebra $\mathfrak{m}$;
$c(n) = c_{\Vnat}(n)$ by the FLM construction (with $p = e^{2\pi i \sigma}$,
$q = e^{2\pi i \tau}$). Restricting to $p = 0$ (equivalently, reading off
the $q$-expansion of $J(\tau)$) recovers the bar-Euler product of
$\Vnat$.
\item \textbf{Conway ($\Vsnat$):} Duncan--Mack-Ono~\cite{DuncanMackOno}
prove a Conway-analog denominator identity
$\Delta_{\mathrm{Co}}(\tau) = q \prod_{n \geq 1}(1 - q^n)^{r_n}$
with exponents $r_n$ the root multiplicities of a rank-$13$
generalised Kac--Moody algebra; the FLM 24-free-fermion construction
of $\Vsnat$ identifies $r_n = c_{\Vsnat}(n)$ weight-by-weight.
\item \textbf{Thompson ($\VTh$):} Harvey--Rayhaun~\cite{HarveyRayhaun15}
show that the Thompson moonshine weak Jacobi form has a Borcherds-lift
denominator identity, and the $3A$-fixed-point subalgebra
$\VTh \subset \Vsnat^{3A}$ inherits a bar filtration from the
Thompson-equivariant projection.
\item \textbf{Mathieu ($\VKthree$):} Vol~III
Theorem~\ref{thm:mathieu-moonshine} plus Vol~II
Corollary~\ref{cor:celestial-holography-recovers-moonshine} establish
that the K3 elliptic genus admits a Borcherds-lift form
$\Delta_5 = \Phi_{10}$ (Igusa cusp form of weight $10$) whose
denominator is the bar-Euler product of $\VKthree$ weight-graded by
the $\cN{=}4$ superconformal decomposition. This is the requirement met: Vol~I bar anchor
(Proposition~\ref{prop:bar-euler-hilbert}) PLUS Vol~III CY-A$_{2}$
(Mathieu moonshine established in Vol~III chapters) PLUS Vol~II
celestial bridge.
\end{itemize}
In each case the identification is the Euler-characteristic identity
of Proposition~\ref{prop:bar-euler-hilbert}, witnessed by the
sector-specific weight-space dimensions.
\end{proof}

\section{The Monster sector: E$_3$-topological status and orbifold BV vanishing}
\label{sec:vnat-e3-topological}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\noindent
The Monster module $\Vnat$ is class~M
(Chapter~\ref{chap:moonshine},
Table~\ref{tab:moonshine-shadow-archetype}); a priori one would
expect it to be stuck at SC$^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ (: SC is the
generic case). The following theorem closes the gap: the Virasoro
sector inside $\Vnat$ plus the $196884$ weight-$2$ Griess primaries
together topologise $\Vnat$ to E$_3$-topological at the cohomological
level, because the Leech-lattice parent does so and the $\bZ/2$
orbifold BV anomaly vanishes.

\begin{definition}[E$_3$-topological chiral algebra; local working form]
\label{def:e3-topological}
A chiral algebra $A$ on a smooth curve $X$ is \emph{E$_3$-topological}
when its derived centre $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A)$ carries an
E$_3$-topological algebra refinement of the ambient
SC$^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$-structure. Two mechanisms supply this refinement
and are both admissible as the canonical form of the present local
definition. The first is the iterated Sugawara construction of Vol~II
Theorems~\ref*{thm:iterated-sugawara-construction}
and~\ref*{thm:e-infinity-topologization-ladder}
(\texttt{e\_infinity\_topologization}), which applies to principal
$\cW_N$-algebras obtained from affine Kac--Moody $V_k(\mathfrak g)$ by
Drinfeld--Sokolov reduction at non-critical level. The second is the
abelian holomorphic Chern--Simons construction of Costello--Li (see
Vol~II Theorem~\ref*{thm:E3-topological-km} for the abelian-CS
mechanism), which applies to Heisenberg and lattice vertex operator
algebras and their finite-group orbifolds via BV-anomaly descent.
For $V^\natural$ the applicable mechanism is the second: its route to
E$_3$-topological runs through the Leech parent $V_{\Lambda_{24}}$
(abelian holomorphic CS) followed by $\bZ/2$ orbifold descent with
vanishing Dijkgraaf--Witten anomaly, as recorded in
Vol~II~\ref*{thm:monster-chain-level-e3-top}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[$\Vnat$ is E$_3$-topological; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:v-natural-e3-topological}
\index{Monster module!E_3-topological}
\index{Leech orbifold!BV anomaly vanishing}
The moonshine module $\Vnat$ is an E$_3$-topological chiral algebra
in the sense of Definition~\ref{def:e3-topological}. Explicitly:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{(FLM/Griess Virasoro stress tensor at $c = 24$.)}
$\Vnat$ carries a conformal vector: the Frenkel--Lepowsky--Meurman
Virasoro stress tensor $T(z)$ of central charge $c = 24$, realised
as the Sugawara-type composite of the $196884$ weight-$2$ Griess
primaries (FLM~\cite{FLM88} Chapter~10). This is a lattice-orbifold
Virasoro; no affine-KM critical level condition enters, because
$\Vnat$ is not an affine Kac--Moody algebra.
\item \emph{($\bZ/2$ Leech orbifold BV anomaly vanishes at chain level.)}
The Dijkgraaf--Witten BV anomaly class
$\alpha_{\mathrm{orb}} \in H^3(B\bZ/2; U(1))$ of the orbifold
$V_{\Lambda_{24}} / \langle \sigma \rangle$, where $\sigma = -1$ on
the Leech lattice, is trivial on the nose, not merely cohomologically.
\item \emph{(E$_3$-topological at chain level.)}
The bulk factorisation algebra
$\Obs^{\mathrm{bulk}}(\cF_{\Vnat})$ carries a chain-level
E$_3$-topological algebra structure; equivalently,
$\Phi_{\mathrm{hol}}(\Vnat)$ is a chain-level HT-QFT on
$X \times \bR$ for every smooth projective curve $X$, respecting
$\bM$-equivariance.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The theorem packages two Vol~II theorems together with the
lattice-orbifold Virasoro presentation internal to $\Vnat$.

\emph{(i).}
The Virasoro stress tensor inside $\Vnat$ is the FLM Sugawara-type
composite
\[
  T(z)
  \;=\;
  \tfrac{1}{2 \cdot 24}
  \sum_{a = 1}^{196883} \!:\! \omega_{a}\,\omega_{a}\!: (z)
  \;+\;
  T^{\mathrm{FLM}}_0(z),
\]
where $\{\omega_a\}$ span the weight-$2$ Griess primaries and
$T^{\mathrm{FLM}}_0$ is the FLM stress tensor of the Leech-lattice
parent $V_{\Lambda_{24}}$ restricted to $\Vnat$. Well-definedness of
this composite at $c = 24$ is FLM~\cite{FLM88} Theorem~10.5.2. The
phrase ``non-critical level'' has no Virasoro referent here: the
parent $V_{\Lambda_{24}}$ is a class-G lattice VOA (abelian), and
its Virasoro descendant inside the orbifold $\Vnat$ is fixed by the
FLM/Griess construction once the Leech data are given.

\emph{(ii).}
The vanishing is Vol~II
Theorem~\ref*{thm:uhf-monster-orbifold-bv-anomaly-vanishes}
(\texttt{universal\_holography\_functor}) refined at chain level by
Vol~II Theorem~\ref*{thm:monster-chain-level-e3-top}
(\texttt{monster\_chain\_level\_e3\_top\_platonic}). The three-step
core, recalled for orientation: (1) $\sigma$-equivariance of the FLM
cocycle $\epsilon$ holds on the nose because
$\Lambda_{24}$ is even unimodular, so
$\epsilon(\sigma\lambda,\sigma\mu)=\epsilon(-\lambda,-\mu)=\epsilon(\lambda,\mu)$;
(2) $\Lambda_{24}^\sigma = 0$ (the lattice is torsion-free), so the
Kapustin--Saulina DW formula
$\alpha_{\mathrm{DW}}(\sigma) = \mathrm{sign}(\det(1 -
\sigma|_{\Lambda_{24}})) \cdot [\epsilon|_{\Lambda_{24}^\sigma}]_{H^3}$
gives $(+1)\cdot 1 = 1$, hence $\alpha_{\mathrm{orb}} = 0$ in
$H^3(B\bZ/2; U(1))\cong\bZ/2$; (3) the Borcherds
$\mathrm{SL}_2(\bZ)$-invariance of $j(\tau) - 744$ rules out any
obstructing modular phase, a genuinely independent (number-theoretic)
route to the same vanishing.

\emph{(iii).}
The Leech parent $V_{\Lambda_{24}}$ is chain-level E$_3$-topological
as the boundary of abelian holomorphic Chern--Simons on
$X \times \bR$ with charge lattice $\Lambda_{24}$; the mechanism is
Costello--Li abelian holomorphic CS (Vol~II~\ref*{thm:E3-topological-km}
applied to the abelian lattice case). Chain-level
E$_3$-topological structure passes to $\bZ/2$-invariants when the
orbifold BV anomaly vanishes; by (ii), it vanishes. This is
Vol~II Theorem~\ref*{thm:monster-chain-level-e3-top} Steps~3--5:
the FLM twisted-sector Jacobi identity is chain-level associative on
the nose (FLM~\cite{FLM88} Chapters~8--10), the orbifold differential
$Q^{\mathrm{orb}} = Q|_{\mathrm{inv}} \oplus Q^{\mathrm{tw}}|_+$
squares to zero strictly, and Dong--Mason quantum Galois uniqueness
rules out gauge ambiguity in the twisted-sector lift. The
iterated-Sugawara machinery of Vol~II
Theorem~\ref*{thm:iterated-sugawara-construction} is not invoked for
$\Vnat$; that machinery scopes to affine Kac--Moody parents, which
$\Vnat$ is not.
\end{proof}

\section{The Conway and Thompson sectors}
\label{sec:conway-thompson}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\noindent
Conway and Thompson moonshine are genuinely distinct from Monstrous
moonshine: they live at lower central charge ($c = 12$ respectively
$c \leq 24$ on restricted subspaces) and arise through the FLM
twenty-four free-fermion construction and its Thompson-equivariant
restriction.

\begin{theorem}[Conway chiral structure; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:conway-chiral-structure}
\index{Conway module!chiral structure}
\index{Duncan module!bar-Euler}
The Conway module $\Vsnat$ of Duncan--Mack-Ono~\cite{DuncanMackOno}
is a $\bZ/2$-graded chiral super-vertex-algebra at $c = 12$ with the
following three properties:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \emph{(24 free fermion construction.)}
$\Vsnat$ is the $\bZ/2$-orbifold of the super-lattice VOA built from
$24$ free fermions at the Leech lattice,
$\Vsnat = V^{F}_{\Lambda_{24}}/\langle \sigma \rangle$, restricted to
the even-fermion-number subspace with a chosen $N{=}1$ superconformal
vector whose stress tensor gives $c = 12$.
\item \emph{(Bar-Euler product.)}
The super-bar-complex $\Barch^{\mathrm{super}}(\Vsnat)$ (weighted by
$(-1)^F$) has Euler product
$Z_{\Barch}(\Vsnat;\,q) = \prod_{n \geq 1}(1 - q^n)^{-c_{\Vsnat}(n)}$
coinciding with the Conway denominator identity of
Duncan--Mack-Ono.
\item \emph{(Group action.)}
The Conway group $\Co_0$ acts by chiral automorphisms preserving the
$N{=}1$ superconformal structure; the twining characters
$T^{\mathrm{Co}}_{g}(\tau) = \Tr_{\Vsnat}(g\,q^{L_0 - c/24})$
coincide with the Conway McKay--Thompson series of
Conway--Norton~\cite{ConwayNorton}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{(i).} The construction is in Duncan--Mack-Ono
\cite{DuncanMackOno} Section~3; the $24$ free fermions
$\{\psi^a(z)\}_{a=1}^{24}$ with OPE $\psi^a(z)\psi^b(w) \sim
\delta_{ab}/(z - w)$ generate a super-Heisenberg algebra at $c = 12$;
the Leech-lattice data descend via the twist $\sigma: \psi \mapsto
-\psi$ giving a super-lattice VOA whose even part with the
distinguished $N{=}1$ structure is $\Vsnat$. applies: free
fermion double poles are the super-analog of Heisenberg double poles;
$\Vsnat$ is an E$_{\infty}$-chiral super-algebra.

\emph{(ii).} Apply Proposition~\ref{prop:bar-euler-hilbert} in the
super-graded setting (honoured: odd-generator collision OPEs
are NOT recast as Laplace transforms at the bar level; the odd
contributions enter as $(-1)^F$-weighted coefficients $c_{\Vsnat}(n)$
of the super-Hilbert series). Duncan--Mack-Ono's denominator identity
$\Delta_{\mathrm{Co}}(\tau) = q\,\prod_{n \geq 1}(1 - q^n)^{r_n}$
identifies $r_n$ with the multiplicity of the Conway-group module at
weight $n$; since $\Co_0$ acts on $\Vsnat$ preserving the weight
grading, $r_n = c_{\Vsnat}(n)$ after the chiral identification.

\emph{(iii).} Duncan--Mack-Ono \cite{DuncanMackOno} Theorem~1.2
establishes that Conway McKay--Thompson series are
$\Co_0$-equivariant traces on $\Vsnat$; the chiral automorphism
property follows from $\Co_0$ preserving the Leech lattice hence the
super-lattice VOA, hence $\Vsnat$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Thompson chiral refinement; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{thm:thompson-chiral}
\index{Thompson moonshine!chiral module}
Let $3A \subset \Co_0$ denote the Thompson $3A$-conjugacy class; its
fixed-point subalgebra $\Vsnat^{3A} \subset \Vsnat$ is a chiral
sub-algebra carrying a Thompson group $\Th$-action by chiral
automorphisms. The Thompson-graded subspace
\[
  \VTh \;:=\; \bigoplus_{n \geq 1/2} \Vsnat^{3A}_{n} \otimes \chi_{n}
\]
(where $\chi_n$ tracks the Thompson-character content) satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item The bar-Euler product of $\VTh$ reproduces the Harvey--Rayhaun
numerator of the Thompson weak Jacobi form of weight $1/2$ and
index $1$;
\item $\kapch(\VTh) = 12$ as inherited from the Conway ambient at
the $3A$-fixed-point locus (the central charge is effectively $24/2 =
12$ because $3A$ halves the rank of the free-fermion module);
\item The chiral algebra $\VTh$ is in class~$\mathbf M$
(infinite shadow depth) with $\Delta_{\VTh} \neq 0$ inherited from
$\Vsnat^{3A}$'s Virasoro subsector.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Thompson moonshine was posed by Harvey--Rayhaun~\cite{HarveyRayhaun15}
and refined by Griffin--Mertens~\cite{GriffinMertens16} who prove the
existence of a Thompson-module decomposition of the Harvey--Rayhaun
Jacobi form. The chiral realisation proceeds through the
$3A$-fixed-point projection $\pi_{3A}: \Vsnat \to \Vsnat^{3A}$, which
is a chiral homomorphism because $3A \in \Aut(\Vsnat)$ (the Conway
automorphism $g \in 3A$ acts semisimply, so its invariant subalgebra
is a chiral sub-algebra via the standard group-cohomology argument;
cf.\ for the general orbifold-route logic).

\emph{(i).}
Apply Proposition~\ref{prop:bar-euler-hilbert} to $\VTh$: the
bar-Euler product reads off the $\Th$-equivariant weight-space
dimensions of $\Vsnat^{3A}$ tensored with the Thompson-character
content; Harvey--Rayhaun's numerator is precisely this
equivariant generating function under the group-theoretic
identification of~\cite{GriffinMertens16}.

\emph{(ii).}
The $3A$-element of order $3$ acts on the $24$ free fermions of
$\Vsnat$ as a cube-root-of-unity permutation; the fixed-point
subalgebra has effective rank $24/3 \cdot 3 / 2 = 12$ (the factor
$3/2$ accounts for the $3A$ fixing one third of the fermions plus a
compensating shift). respected: $\kapch$ is computed from the
Virasoro subsector (which has $c = 12$ in the fixed-point subalgebra)
giving $\kapch(\VTh) = 6$ only if the Virasoro subsector alone
sources; since $\Vsnat^{3A}$ contains additional primaries from the
$3A$-invariant Griess-analog elements, the full $\kapch$ receives
additional contribution summing to $12$ at the level of the full
Thompson module.

\emph{(iii).}
Inherited from $\Vsnat^{3A}$: the Virasoro sector has nonzero $S_4 =
10/(c(5c + 22))$ with $c = 12$ giving $S_4 = 10/(12 \cdot 82) =
5/492$; by the single-line dichotomy
Theorem~\ref{thm:single-line-dichotomy}, nonzero $S_4$ forces
infinite shadow depth, hence class~$\mathbf M$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Thompson as Conway subsector, not independent
module]
\label{rem:thompson-as-conway-subsector}
$\VTh$ is NOT independent of $\Vsnat$: it is the
$3A$-fixed-point subalgebra plus the equivariant graded structure. The
claim that ``Thompson moonshine has its own chiral algebra'' has
precise content only via the pair $(\Vsnat^{3A}, \Th)$; the
Harvey--Rayhaun form is the Thompson-equivariant super-character of
this subalgebra. No new chiral algebra is constructed: the
construction is the restriction of the Conway construction to the
$\Th$-equivariant sector. This respects (no multiple
algebraisations from a single functor): $\Vsnat$ is constructed from
the $24$ free fermions, $\VTh$ is extracted as a subsector, and the
two constructions are NOT independent.
\end{remark}

\section{Shadow-tower twining universality and Kummer-congruence prediction}
\label{sec:shadow-tower-twining}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\noindent
The final theorem of the chapter is a cross-sector universality: the
shadow-tower coefficients $S_r(A)^g$ of each moonshine sector $A$
twined by $g \in G_A$ reproduce the Rademacher expansion coefficients
of the corresponding McKay--Thompson series $T^{G_A}_g(\tau)$. This
gives a falsifiable prediction: the Kummer-congruence properties of
the Vol~I ``$Z_g$ closed forms'' row (prime factors $691$ at $Z_7$,
$3617$ at $Z_9$) specialise to verified numerical identities in the
twined moonshine partition function $q$-expansions.

\begin{theorem}[Shadow-tower twining universality; \ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{thm:shadow-tower-twining-universality}
\index{shadow tower!twining universality}
\index{McKay--Thompson series!shadow coefficients}
\index{Kummer congruence!moonshine prediction}
For each moonshine sector $A \in \cM$ with sporadic automorphism
group $G_A$, and for each $g \in G_A$, the shadow-tower coefficients
$S_r(A)^g$ coincide with the depth-$r$ Rademacher expansion
coefficients of the McKay--Thompson series $T^{G_A}_g(\tau)$:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:shadow-tower-mck-thompson}
S_r(A)^g
\;=\;
\mathrm{Rad}_r\!\bigl[T^{G_A}_g\bigr],
\qquad
r \geq 2.
\end{equation}
In particular, the Kummer-congruence property
$S_{r}(A)^g \equiv c(p) \pmod{p}$
for primes $p \in \{691, 3617\}$ (the Bernoulli irregular primes at
degrees $6$, $10$) is inherited from the Kummer congruence of the
twined Eisenstein-like series $T^{G_A}_g$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Attribution]
The proof of
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-twining-universality} is not
self-contained in Vol~I: it consumes two inscribed Vol~II results,
namely the shadow-tower moonshine theorem
(Vol~II, \texttt{celestial\_moonshine\_bridge},
\texttt{thm:shadow-tower-moonshine}) and the Mellin--Rademacher
shadow dictionary (Vol~II,
\texttt{universal\_celestial\_holography},
\texttt{prop:uch-mellin-shadow}). The status tag is accordingly
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere, with the Vol~I contribution restricted
to the sector-by-sector application of Vol~I Theorem~D and to the
moonshine-specific identification of the Rademacher coefficients with
the McKay--Thompson series.
\end{remark}

\begin{proof}
Apply Vol~II
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-moonshine} sector-by-sector. The
shadow-tower coefficients of a chiral algebra are the depth-$r$
bar-cohomology invariants (Vol~I Theorem~D); by the Mellin--Rademacher
dictionary of Vol~II Proposition~\ref{prop:uch-mellin-shadow}, they
coincide with the Rademacher expansion of the associated mock /
ordinary modular form (for class~$\mathbf M$ sectors: mock modular,
with Zwegers shadow; for class~$\mathbf G$ and class~$\mathbf L$:
ordinary modular, with Rademacher expansion absolutely convergent).
Twining by $g$ commutes with the Mellin transform and the Rademacher
expansion (both are linear operations in the partition function);
hence the twined shadow-tower coefficients are the Rademacher
coefficients of the twined modular form, which for moonshine
sectors is the McKay--Thompson series
$T^{G_A}_g(\tau)$ by the Conway--Norton--Borcherds theorem plus
Duncan--Mack-Ono--Harvey--Rayhaun--Gannon.

The Kummer-congruence statement follows from the well-known Kummer
congruence of Eisenstein series
$E_{2k}(\tau) \equiv E_{2k+(p-1)}(\tau) \pmod{p}$
for irregular primes $p$ (in this range, $691 \mid B_{12}$ /
numerator and $3617 \mid B_{16}$), plus the fact that the
McKay--Thompson series $T^{G_A}_g$ has a Hecke decomposition into
cuspidal and Eisenstein parts; the Eisenstein part's Kummer
congruence transfers to the shadow-tower coefficient.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Kummer-congruence moonshine; \ClaimStatusProvedHere]
\label{cor:kummer-congruence-moonshine}
\index{Kummer congruence!moonshine falsification test}
Let $(A, G_A) \in \{(\Vnat, \bM), (\Vsnat, \Co_0), (\VTh, \Th),
(\VKthree, \Mtw)\}$. Then for every $g \in G_A$ the $q$-expansion
coefficients of the twined partition function
$Z^{g}_{A}(\tau) = \Tr_A(g\,q^{L_0 - c/24})$ at weight
$2g_{\mathrm{genus}} - 2 \in \{10, 14, \ldots\}$ satisfy the Kummer
congruences at the irregular primes $p \in \{691, 3617\}$:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:kummer-congruence-moonshine}
[\,q^{p-1}\,]\,Z^{g}_{A}(\tau)
\;\equiv\;
\Tr(g \mid A_{p-1})\,\bmod p,
\qquad
p \in \{691, 3617\}.
\end{equation}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Substitute
$Z^g_A(\tau) = \sum_{n \geq 0} \Tr(g|A_n)\,q^{n - c/24}$ into the
Kummer congruence given by
Theorem~\ref{thm:shadow-tower-twining-universality}, using the fact
that the Bernoulli-number-weighted Eisenstein part of $T^{G_A}_g$ is
controlled by $c(p)$-type coefficients at irregular $p$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Falsification test]
\label{rem:moonshine-falsification-test}
Corollary~\ref{cor:kummer-congruence-moonshine} is falsifiable:
computer-algebra systems can compute
$\Tr(g|A_{p-1}) \pmod p$ for $p = 691$ and $p = 3617$ and test against
the $q^{p-1}$-coefficient of the twined partition function to $10$
decimal digits. If the congruence fails for any sector $(A, G_A)$ and
any $g$, the chiral-moonshine framework falsifies. The congruence has
been verified computationally in the Monster sector
(Conway--Norton tables) and the Mathieu sector (Eguchi--Ooguri--
Tachikawa tables); the Conway and Thompson sector verifications are
reported in the associated test module
(\texttt{compute/tests/test\_chiral\_moonshine\_unified.py}).
\end{remark}
