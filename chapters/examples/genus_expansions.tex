\chapter{Explicit Genus Expansions}

\section{Free Boson at All Genera}

The free boson with $c=1$ has partition function:
$$Z_g^{\text{boson}} = \left[\det'(\Delta_g)\right]^{-1/2}$$
where $\det'$ denotes the zeta-regularized determinant with zero modes removed.

Explicit formulas by genus:
\begin{itemize}
\item $g=0$: $Z_0 = 1$ (trivial; the sphere admits no nontrivial conformal moduli)
\item $g=1$: $Z_1(\tau) = (\mathrm{Im}\,\tau)^{-1/2}|\eta(\tau)|^{-2}$ where $\eta(\tau) = q^{1/24}\prod_{n=1}^\infty(1-q^n)$ is the Dedekind eta function, $q = e^{2\pi i\tau}$.  Here $|\eta|^{-2}$ comes from $[\det'(\Delta_1)]^{-1/2}$ (oscillator modes) and $(\mathrm{Im}\,\tau)^{-1/2}$ from the zero-mode integral $\int d^2p \, e^{-\pi \mathrm{Im}\,\tau\,|p|^2}$ (for a non-compact boson; for a compact boson on a circle of radius $R$, the zero-mode sum replaces this by a lattice theta function)
\item $g=2$: $Z_2(\Omega) = [\det(\mathrm{Im}\,\Omega)]^{-1/2} \cdot |\mathcal{F}_2(\Omega)|^{-1}$ where $\mathcal{F}_2$ is expressible in terms of Riemann theta functions on the Siegel upper half-space $\mathcal{H}_2$. The Igusa cusp form $\Psi_{10}$ enters the \emph{bosonic string measure} (all 26 bosons) via the Belavin--Knizhnik theorem, not the single-boson partition function.
\item General $g$: the partition function depends on the full period matrix $\Omega \in \mathcal{H}_g$. The \emph{universal factor} from the conformal anomaly is $\exp(-\frac{1}{2}\zeta(-1) \chi(\Sigma_g))$ where $\chi(\Sigma_g) = 2-2g$ and $\zeta(-1) = -\frac{1}{12}$ (the Riemann zeta function at $s=-1$, not to be confused with $\zeta'(-1) = \frac{1}{12} - \ln A$ where $A$ is the Glaisher-Kinkelin constant), but this is only the moduli-independent prefactor.
\end{itemize}

For weight $\lambda$, the correlation functions are:
$$\beta(z)\gamma(w) \sim \frac{1}{z-w}$$

At genus $g$:
$$\langle \prod_i \beta(z_i) \prod_j \gamma(w_j) \rangle_g = \frac{\det G_{ij}^{(g)}}{\left[\det(\text{Im}\,\Omega)\right]^{(2\lambda-1)(g-1)/2}}$$
where $G_{ij}^{(g)}$ is the period-normalized Green's function and the exponent
matches the partition function normalization from \S\ref{subsec:heisenberg-dictionary-complete} at $g \geq 2$.

At genus 1, the partition function is (for the untwisted $\beta\gamma$ system on the torus, where the canonical bundle $\omega_{E_\tau}$ is trivial):
$$Z_1^{\beta\gamma} = |\eta(\tau)|^{-2}$$
independent of $\lambda$.  The conformal weight assignment $(\lambda, 1-\lambda)$ does not affect the genus-1 partition function because $\omega_{E_\tau} \cong \mathcal{O}_{E_\tau}$, so sections of $\omega^{\otimes \lambda}$ are ordinary functions regardless of $\lambda$.  The $\lambda$-dependence enters at genus $g \geq 2$, where $\omega_{\Sigma_g}$ is nontrivial and the index of $\bar{\partial}_\lambda$ depends on $\lambda$ via Riemann--Roch: $\chi(\omega^{\otimes \lambda}) = (2\lambda-1)(g-1)$.

At genus $g \geq 2$, the partition function depends on the full period matrix $\Omega \in \mathcal{H}_g$:
$$Z_g^{\beta\gamma}(\lambda) = \left[\det(\text{Im}\,\Omega)\right]^{(1-2\lambda)(1-g)/2} \cdot \left[\det'(\bar{\partial}_\lambda)\right]^{-1}$$
where $\bar{\partial}_\lambda$ is the $\bar{\partial}$-operator on sections of $\omega_X^{\otimes \lambda}$ and the determinant is zeta-regularized. The infinite product formula above is specific to genus~1, where $q = e^{2\pi i\tau}$ parametrizes the moduli.

\section{Lattice VOAs: From Torus to Higher Genus}

For a lattice $\Lambda$ of rank $d$:

\begin{itemize}
\item Genus 1: $Z_1 = \frac{\Theta_\Lambda(\tau)}{[\eta(\tau)]^d}$
\item Genus 2: Involves Riemann theta functions $\Theta[\delta](\Omega)$
\item General genus: Siegel theta series
\end{itemize}

For a unimodular even lattice $\Lambda$ (i.e., $\Lambda = \Lambda^*$ and $\langle v, v \rangle \in 2\mathbb{Z}$ for all $v \in \Lambda$), the Siegel theta series transforms as:
$$\Theta_\Lambda(\gamma \cdot \Omega) = \det(C\Omega + D)^{d/2} \, \Theta_\Lambda(\Omega)$$
for $\gamma = \begin{pmatrix} A & B \\ C & D \end{pmatrix} \in \mathrm{Sp}(2g, \mathbb{Z})$. For non-unimodular lattices, an additional phase factor (a root of unity depending on $\gamma$ and the discriminant form $\Lambda^*/\Lambda$) appears; the precise formula involves the Weil representation of $\mathrm{Sp}(2g, \mathbb{Z})$ on $\mathbb{C}[\Lambda^*/\Lambda]$.

\section{W-Algebras and Higgs Bundles}

The W-algebra $\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})$ at genus $g$ is related to the geometry of Higgs bundles through multiple distinct connections:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Classical limit}: The Poisson algebra underlying $\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})$ is the algebra of functions on the Hitchin base $\mathcal{B}_g(\mathfrak{g}) = \bigoplus_{i=1}^r H^0(\Sigma_g, \omega_{\Sigma_g}^{\otimes d_i})$, where $d_i$ are the degrees of the fundamental invariants of $\mathfrak{g}$ (i.e., the exponents plus one: for $\mathfrak{sl}_n$, the degrees are $2, 3, \ldots, n$).

\item \textbf{Symplectic volume}: The integral $\int_{\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{Higgs}}^g(\mathfrak{g})} \exp(k\,\omega_{\mathrm{Hitchin}})$ computes the symplectic volume of the Hitchin moduli space, which by the Verlinde formula equals the dimension of the space of conformal blocks at level $k$.

\item \textbf{AGT correspondence}: The genus-$g$ partition function of $\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})$ equals the Nekrasov partition function of the corresponding 4d $\mathcal{N}=2$ theory on $\Sigma_g$; this is \emph{not} the symplectic volume but rather involves the full quantum structure.
\end{enumerate}

The relationship to geometric Langlands:
\begin{itemize}
\item At critical level $k = -h^\vee$: $\mathcal{D}$-modules on $\mathrm{Bun}_G(\Sigma_g) \leftrightarrow$ quasi-coherent sheaves on $\mathrm{Loc}_{G^\vee}(\Sigma_g)$
\item At general level: quantum geometric Langlands (Gaitsgory--Lurie)
\end{itemize}

\chapter{Modular Forms and Higher Genus Phenomena}

\section{Eisenstein Series and Genus One}

At genus one, the universal chiral algebra contribution is:
$$\omega_{1,0}^{\text{univ}}(\tau) = -\frac{c}{24} \frac{E_2(\tau)}{2\pi i} dz$$
where $E_2(\tau) = 1 - 24\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \sigma_1(n)q^n$ is the weight-2 Eisenstein series.

This generates all genus-one corrections through:
$$\langle T \rangle_{\tau} = -\frac{c}{24} (2\pi i)^2 E_2(\tau)$$
where $E_2$ is the quasi-modular Eisenstein series. (The one-point function is $z$-independent by translation invariance of the torus; the Weierstrass $\wp$-function appears in the \emph{two}-point function $\langle T(z)T(0)\rangle_\tau$.)

\section{Siegel Modular Forms at Higher Genus}

At genus $g \geq 2$, correlation functions involve Siegel modular forms on $\mathcal{H}_g$:
$$F^{(g)}(\Omega) = \sum_{T \geq 0} a(T) \exp(2\pi i \operatorname{tr}(T\Omega))$$
where $T$ ranges over symmetric half-integral positive semi-definite $g \times g$ matrices.

Key examples:
\begin{itemize}
\item Genus 2: Igusa invariants $\psi_4, \psi_6, \chi_{10}, \chi_{12}$
\item Genus 3: Schottky form vanishes on Jacobian locus
\item General $g$: Theta constants $\theta[\delta](\Omega)$ for characteristics $\delta$
\end{itemize}

\section{Bosonization at All Genera}

The bosonization formula extends to:
$$Z_g^{\text{fermion}} = \sum_{\delta} (-1)^{\mathrm{Arf}(\delta)} \, Z_g^{\text{boson}}[\delta]$$
where the sum is over spin structures $\delta$ and $\mathrm{Arf}(\delta) \in \{0, 1\}$ is the Arf invariant of the quadratic form associated to $\delta$. Even spin structures have $\mathrm{Arf}(\delta) = 0$; odd spin structures have $\mathrm{Arf}(\delta) = 1$.

At genus $g$:
\begin{itemize}
\item Number of spin structures: $2^{2g}$
\item Even (periodic) spin structures: $2^{g-1}(2^g + 1)$
\item Odd (antiperiodic) spin structures: $2^{g-1}(2^g - 1)$
\end{itemize}

\section{Mumford Forms and Belavin-Knizhnik Measure}

For a CFT of central charge $c$, the Polyakov measure on $\mathcal{M}_g$ (for $g \geq 2$) takes the form:
$$d\mu_g^{\text{Pol}} = \frac{|\det(\text{CKV})|^2}{|\det'(\Delta_g)|^{c/2}} \prod_{k=1}^{3g-3} d^2\tau_k$$
where $\tau_k$ are Beltrami coordinates on $\mathcal{M}_g$. The Belavin--Knizhnik theorem expresses this in terms of the period matrix $\Omega$. For the \emph{bosonic string} ($c = 26$):
$$d\mu_g^{\text{bosonic}} = \prod_{k=1}^{3g-3} d^2\tau_k \left[\det(\mathrm{Im}\,\Omega)\right]^{-13}$$

The exponent $-13$ arises as follows:
\begin{itemize}
\item Each free boson contributes $[\det(\mathrm{Im}\,\Omega)]^{-1/2}$ to the partition function (from the zero-mode integration over the Jacobian torus).
\item For $c = 26$ free bosons: the matter contribution is $[\det(\mathrm{Im}\,\Omega)]^{-26/2} = [\det(\mathrm{Im}\,\Omega)]^{-13}$.
\item The $bc$ ghost system contributes a \emph{holomorphic} section of a line bundle on $\mathcal{M}_g$ (the Mumford form), which does not affect the $\det(\mathrm{Im}\,\Omega)$ exponent.
\item The Weyl anomaly cancels when $c + c_{\text{gh}} = 26 - 26 = 0$.
\end{itemize}

\section{The Genus-Graded Chiral Operad}

The chiral operad extends to:
$$\mathcal{P} = \bigoplus_{g \geq 0} \mathcal{P}^{(g)}$$
with composition:
$$\circ: \mathcal{P}^{(g_1)}(m) \otimes \mathcal{P}^{(g_2)}(n) \to \mathcal{P}^{(g_1+g_2)}(m+n-2)$$

This encodes:
\begin{itemize}
\item Gluing of surfaces along boundaries
\item Factorization at nodes
\item Modular operad structure
\end{itemize}

At higher genus, we get a Swiss-cheese operad:
\begin{itemize}

\item Open sector: Surfaces with boundaries

\end{itemize}

This relates to:

\section{String Amplitudes}

The genus-$g$ string amplitude:
$$A_g = \int_{\mathcal{M}_g} \langle \prod_i V_i \rangle_g \, d\mu_g^{\text{Pol}}$$

For critical strings ($c=26$ bosonic, $c=15$ superstring):
\begin{itemize}

\item One loop: Quantum corrections
\item Higher loops: Quantum gravity
\end{itemize}

\section{AGT Correspondence}

The Alday-Gaiotto-Tachikawa correspondence relates:
\begin{itemize}
\item 4d $\mathcal{N}=2$ gauge theory of class $\mathcal{S}$ on $\mathbb{R}^4$, obtained by compactifying the 6d $(2,0)$ theory on $\Sigma_g$
\item 2d Toda CFT (with $\mathcal{W}_N$-algebra symmetry) on $\Sigma_g$
\end{itemize}

More precisely, the Nekrasov partition function of the 4d theory in the $\Omega$-background $(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$ equals the conformal block of the W-algebra $\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})$ on $\Sigma_g$, with level $k$ determined by $\epsilon_1/\epsilon_2$.

The bar-cobar perspective: the genus-$g$ conformal block can be expressed as a correlation function in the chiral bar complex:
$$\mathcal{Z}_{\text{Nek}}^{(g)} = \int_{\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,n}} \langle V_1 \cdots V_n \rangle_{\mathcal{W}^k(\mathfrak{g})}$$
where $V_i$ are vertex operators for punctures on $\Sigma_g$.

$$E_1^{p,q} = H^q(\text{Bar}^{(p)}(\mathcal{A})) \Rightarrow H^{p+q}(\text{Bar}(\mathcal{A}))$$

The differentials encode:
\begin{itemize}

\item $d_2$: Bubble corrections (genus reduction)
\item $d_r$: Higher quantum corrections
\end{itemize}

\section{Feynman Rules for Higher Genus}

The contribution from a stable graph $\Gamma$:
$$A_{\Gamma} = \frac{1}{|\text{Aut}(\Gamma)|} \prod_{v \in V} C_v \prod_{e \in E} P_e$$
where:
\begin{itemize}
\item Vertices $v$: Correlation functions on $\Sigma_{g(v)}$
\item Edges $e$: Propagators (Green's functions)
\item Loops: Integration over moduli
\end{itemize}
