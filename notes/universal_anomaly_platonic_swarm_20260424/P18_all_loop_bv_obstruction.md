## Verdict

The all-loop statement is not currently theorem-grade.  The class
\[
  \alpha_X=[m_3,B^{(2)}]_X
\]
can be the base case of a higher obstruction tower only after three
distinct filtrations are separated:

1. \(A_\infty\)-arity: \(m_3,m_4,\ldots\).
2. bar/shadow length on the curve-side cyclic deformation complex.
3. BV loop order, the power of \(\hbar\) in the quantum master equation.

The current question at
`standalones/universal_anomaly_four_climax_simultaneously_2026_04_22.tex:1164`
identifies these three filtrations:
\[
  [m_n,B^{(n-1)}]_X=0
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  S_n=0
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  \text{all-loop BV closure}.
\]
That equivalence is not supplied by Costello--Gwilliam, Costello--Li,
Gilkey, or Adler--Bardeen.  It is exactly the new frontier theorem.

The repair is strong but conditional:

> \(\alpha_X\) is the arity-three / first cyclic-HKR obstruction.
> It controls all higher \([m_n,B^{(n-1)}]\) only under an all-higher
> closure theorem saying that every higher cyclic obstruction lies in
> the filtered \(L_\infty\)-ideal generated by \(\alpha_X\), after a
> BV/HKR diagonal comparison from loop order to arity.

Without that closure theorem, vanishing of \(\alpha_X\) kills at most
the first obstruction identified by P02:
\[
  \operatorname{sp}_{\Sigma,C}(\alpha_X)=o^{(0)}_3(A_C).
\]
It does not kill \(o^{(0)}_4,o^{(0)}_5,\ldots\), and it does not prove
strict Theorem B.

## What Costello--Gwilliam / BV formalism actually supports

Costello's renormalization framework and Costello--Gwilliam's
factorization-algebra formalism support the following structure.

Let
\[
  S=S_0+\sum_{\ell\ge1}\hbar^\ell S_\ell
\]
be a formal BV action and
\[
  d_{S_0}(-)=\{S_0,-\}.
\]
The quantum master equation
\[
  {1\over 2}\{S,S\}+\hbar\Delta S=0
\]
expands as:
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \hbar^0:\quad& {1\over2}\{S_0,S_0\}=0,\\
  \hbar^1:\quad& d_{S_0}S_1+\Delta S_0=0,\\
  \hbar^\ell:\quad&
    d_{S_0}S_\ell+
    \Delta S_{\ell-1}
    +{1\over2}\sum_{\substack{i+j=\ell\\ i,j\ge1}}
      \{S_i,S_j\}=0
    \qquad(\ell\ge2).
\end{aligned}
\]
At each order the obstruction is a cohomology class in the local
BRST/BV complex.  If the obstruction class vanishes, one may choose
a counterterm \(S_\ell\) and continue.  This is an order-by-order
formal construction.  It is not a theorem that the first obstruction
generates all later obstructions.

Costello--Gwilliam then packages a quantum BV theory satisfying the
renormalized QME as a factorization algebra of quantum observables.
This formalism gives the ambient and the obstruction groups.  It does
not identify those local BV obstruction classes with the cyclic-HKR
classes \([m_n,B^{(n-1)}]\) without a comparison map.

## Attacks

### CRITICAL 1: arity and loop order are being identified without a theorem

The symbol \([m_n,B^{(n-1)}]\) is an arity statement.  The symbol
\(S_n\) in the BV expansion is a loop-order or \(\hbar\)-order
statement.  There is no canonical equality
\[
  \text{arity }n
  =
  \text{loop order }n
\]
in BV formalism.  A loop-\(\ell\) Feynman graph may have several
external valences; an arity-\(n\) operation may receive tree and loop
contributions after transfer.  The paper needs a diagonal comparison
\[
  \chi_\ell:
  H^1_{\mathrm{loc},BRST,\ell}
  \longrightarrow
  H^2(\pi_{\ell+2,0}\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(A_C))
\]
with
\[
  \chi_1(\mathfrak a_1)=\operatorname{sp}_{\Sigma,C}(\alpha_X),
  \qquad
  \chi_\ell(\mathfrak a_\ell)=
  \operatorname{sp}_{\Sigma,C}([m_{\ell+2},B^{(\ell+1)}]_X)
  \quad(\ell\ge2).
\]
Without \(\chi_\ell\), all-loop BV vanishing and higher
\(A_\infty\)-arity vanishing are different claims.

### CRITICAL 2: Connes has one operator \(B\), not a proved hierarchy \(B^{(n-1)}\)

P01 already shows that \(B^{(2)}\) is not a theorem-grade operator
until the chain-to-cochain transport is fixed.  The all-loop section
uses a whole hierarchy \(B^{(n-1)}\) for \(n\ge4\).  That hierarchy is
not defined in the standalone.

Vol III uses "Connes hierarchy" language in a CY-A3 framing context,
but it also records the essential warning: individual commutators such
as \(\{b_3,B^{(2)}\}\) may be nonzero while the total differential
commutator \(\{b,B^{(2)}\}\) vanishes by TCFT coherence.  Therefore
the per-\(n\) assertion
\[
  [m_n,B^{(n-1)}]=0
\]
is too strict.  The honest object is a filtered total equation, not
termwise vanishing.

### CRITICAL 3: the BV recursion does not bootstrap vanishing from order \(\ell-1\)

At order \(\hbar^\ell\), the obstruction contains
\[
  \Delta S_{\ell-1}
  +{1\over2}\sum_{i+j=\ell}\{S_i,S_j\}.
\]
Even if the previous obstruction vanished and \(S_{\ell-1}\) was
chosen, this expression may define a new nonzero cohomology class.
Thus "order-\(\ell\) vanishing follows from order-\((\ell-1)\)
vanishing" is false without a generation or non-renormalisation
theorem.

The correct hypothesis is:
\[
  [\Delta S_{\ell-1}
  +{1\over2}\sum_{i+j=\ell}\{S_i,S_j\}]
  \in
  (\alpha_X)
\]
in the associated graded local BV/HKR obstruction algebra, for every
\(\ell\ge2\).

### CRITICAL 4: compactness is analytic input, not anomaly cancellation

Compact projective \(X\) supplies an elliptic analytic setting and heat
kernels.  It does not imply:

- the QME obstruction classes vanish;
- the hCS local BRST anomaly is one-loop exact;
- the BV anomaly is the Atiyah--Connes class;
- the \(\hbar\)-series converges beyond formal perturbation theory.

The sentence at line 846 should be read only as analytic background.
Anomaly cancellation and BV/HKR comparison remain separate hypotheses.

### CRITICAL 5: Gilkey heat coefficients do not compute the all-loop obstruction groups

The all-loop question says Gilkey 1995 identifies
\[
  H^{6-n}_{\mathrm{loc}}(X,\mathrm{Densities})
\]
as finite-dimensional at each order.  Heat-kernel asymptotics and
local invariant theory constrain counterterm densities.  They do not
compute the BRST cohomology of the hCS BV complex, do not prove
one-dimensionality at the relevant orders, and do not force vanishing.

Finite-dimensional obstruction spaces make an induction formulable.
They do not make it true.

### SERIOUS 1: one-loop exactness is a hypothesis, not imported from Adler--Bardeen

Adler--Bardeen supplies a physical template for one-loop exactness of
specified chiral anomalies.  It does not prove one-loop exactness for
the holomorphic CY3 hCS theory in the cyclic-HKR convention of this
paper.  P03's `(F5)` is the correct surface:

\[
  \text{higher-loop anomaly classes vanish or are functorially
  determined by the one-loop class.}
\]

### SERIOUS 2: the all-loop claim should be total-complex exactness, not termwise zero

Vol III's compatible message is:
\[
  \{b,B^{(2)}\}=0
\]
for the total \(A_\infty\)-Hochschild differential, while individual
pieces can be nonzero and cancel across weights.  The standalone should
therefore formulate closure as:
\[
  \Theta_{\mathrm{AC}}^{\mathrm{tot}}
  :=
  \sum_{n\ge3} [m_n,\Delta_{n-1}]
  \quad\text{is exact or lies in the ideal generated by }\alpha_X,
\]
not as \([m_n,\Delta_{n-1}]=0\) for every \(n\).

### SERIOUS 3: "CFG 2026" cannot be a proof input

A forthcoming construction can be named as expected future support, but
it cannot be a theorem hypothesis in the current paper unless it is
available with precise statements and hypotheses.  The paper should
state its own conditional all-loop closure axiom instead of leaning on
an unavailable citation.

## Repaired objects

### Arity tower

Fix the cyclic-HKR datum of P01.  Define, if the higher cochain
rotations \(\Delta_{n-1}\) have been constructed,
\[
  a_n(X):=[m_n,\Delta_{n-1}]_X
  \in
  \mathcal H_n^{\mathrm{cyc}}(X),
  \qquad n\ge3,
\]
where \(\mathcal H_3^{\mathrm{cyc}}(X)=H^2(X,\Omega_X^1)\) and
\[
  a_3(X)=\alpha_X.
\]
The spaces \(\mathcal H_n^{\mathrm{cyc}}(X)\) must be specified as
associated graded pieces of the negative-cyclic Hochschild deformation
complex.  They are not automatically coherent cohomology groups.

### Curve-side bar tower

For
\[
  A_C=\Sp^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\Sigma,C}(\Phi^{FA}_3(D^b_{\mathrm{dg}}(X))),
\]
P02/P13 give the correct target:
\[
  o^{(0)}_n(A_C)
  \in
  H^2(\pi_{n,0}\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(A_C)),
  \qquad n\ge3.
\]
The first comparison target is
\[
  \operatorname{sp}_{\Sigma,C}(\alpha_X)=o^{(0)}_3(A_C).
\]
The all-higher comparison would be
\[
  \operatorname{sp}^{(n)}_{\Sigma,C}(a_n(X))=o^{(0)}_n(A_C),
  \qquad n\ge4.
\]

### BV loop tower

Let
\[
  \mathfrak a_\ell(X,P)
  \in
  H^1_{\mathrm{loc},BRST,\ell}
  (\Obs_{\mathrm{hCS}}(X,\End T_X;P))
\]
be the obstruction at \(\hbar^\ell\) for a chosen hCS parametrix \(P\).
Then the bridge to the arity tower is the family of maps
\[
  \Theta^{(\ell)}_{\mathrm{BV/HKR}}:
  H^1_{\mathrm{loc},BRST,\ell}
  \to
  \mathcal H_{\ell+2}^{\mathrm{cyc}}(X).
\]
The first required equality is
\[
  \Theta^{(1)}_{\mathrm{BV/HKR}}(\mathfrak a_1)=\alpha_X.
\]
This is B2 at first order.  Higher equality is new:
\[
  \Theta^{(\ell)}_{\mathrm{BV/HKR}}(\mathfrak a_\ell)=a_{\ell+2}(X),
  \qquad \ell\ge2.
\]

## Theorem / conjecture split

### Proved or source-backed

1. Costello's perturbative BV formalism supplies the renormalized QME
   and an order-by-order obstruction problem in local BRST/BV
   cohomology.
2. Costello--Gwilliam's factorization-algebra formalism packages a
   quantum BV theory satisfying the QME as a factorization algebra of
   quantum observables.
3. Compactness / elliptic heat kernels are analytic prerequisites for
   the perturbative construction, not vanishing theorems.
4. Vol I gives strict bar-cobar inversion only on the Koszul lane and
   completed coderived inversion only under Positselski hypotheses.
5. Vol III supports total cyclic/TCFT coherence, with explicit warning
   that individual per-\(m_k\) commutators can be nonzero.

### Conditional theorem-grade surface

```tex
\begin{theorem}[Conditional all-loop Atiyah--Connes closure]
\label{thm:conditional-all-loop-ac-closure}
Let \(X\) be a smooth projective Calabi--Yau threefold with cyclic-HKR
datum \(\mathfrak c_X\), holomorphic BV hCS datum \(P\), and
stage-two specialisation datum \((\Sigma,C)\).  Let
\[
  a_3(X)=\alpha_X,\qquad
  a_n(X)=[m_n,\Delta_{n-1}]_X\quad(n\ge4)
\]
be the arity tower in the associated graded cyclic deformation
complex, and let
\[
  \mathfrak a_\ell(X,P)
  \in H^1_{\mathrm{loc},BRST,\ell}
  \bigl(\Obs_{\mathrm{hCS}}(X,\End T_X;P)\bigr)
\]
be the BV loop obstruction at order \(\hbar^\ell\).

Assume:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(L\arabic*)}]
\item \textup{(higher cyclic rotations)}
the operators \(\Delta_{n-1}\) are defined in the same cyclic-HKR
datum as \(B^{(2)}=\Delta_2\), with fixed degree and sign convention;
\item \textup{(BV/HKR diagonal)}
there are comparison maps
\[
  \Theta^{(\ell)}_{\mathrm{BV/HKR}}
  \colon H^1_{\mathrm{loc},BRST,\ell}\to
  \mathcal H_{\ell+2}^{\mathrm{cyc}}(X)
\]
satisfying
\[
  \Theta^{(1)}_{\mathrm{BV/HKR}}(\mathfrak a_1)=\alpha_X,
  \qquad
  \Theta^{(\ell)}_{\mathrm{BV/HKR}}(\mathfrak a_\ell)=a_{\ell+2}(X)
  \quad(\ell\ge2);
\]
\item \textup{(local BRST generation)}
every higher obstruction class \(\mathfrak a_\ell\) lies in the
filtered ideal generated by \(\mathfrak a_1\), up to BV-exact
counterterms;
\item \textup{(QME closure)}
the BV bracket and \(\Delta\)-operator preserve that ideal in the
renormalized QME recursion;
\item \textup{(stage-two filtration)}
stage-two specialisation sends \(a_n(X)\) to
\[
  o^{(0)}_n(A_C)
  \in H^2(\pi_{n,0}\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(A_C))
\]
for every \(n\ge3\).
\end{enumerate}
Then \(\alpha_X=0\) implies
\[
  a_n(X)=0,\qquad
  o^{(0)}_n(A_C)=0
  \quad(n\ge3),
\]
and hence full genus-zero bar-cobar Koszulness follows if the
remaining Vol I hypotheses on \(A_C\) are satisfied.
\end{theorem}
```

This theorem is conditional but mathematically clean.  It names exactly
what the all-loop slogan needs.

### Conjectural frontier

```tex
\begin{conjecture}[Atiyah--Connes all-loop generation]
\label{conj:atiyah-connes-all-loop-generation}
For the holomorphic CY3 BV theory attached to a smooth projective
Calabi--Yau threefold \(X\), after fixing the cyclic-HKR datum and
the Costello--Li parametrix, the associated graded local BRST anomaly
algebra is generated by its one-loop Atiyah--Connes class:
\[
  \operatorname{gr}\mathfrak a_{\ge1}
  =
  (\mathfrak a_1).
\]
Equivalently, the derived zero locus of \(\alpha_X\) equals the
derived zero locus of the full arity/BV obstruction tower:
\[
  \mathbf Z_{\alpha}
  =
  \bigcap_{n\ge3}\mathbf Z_{a_n}.
\]
\end{conjecture}
```

This is the paper's correct all-loop dream.  It is deep.  It is not
proved.

### Open / false as stated

The following statements must not appear as proved:

- \([m_3,B^{(2)}]=0\Rightarrow [m_n,B^{(n-1)}]=0\) for all \(n\).
- \(S_n=0\) in BV loop order is equivalent to arity-\(n\)
  \(A_\infty\) obstruction vanishing.
- Costello--Gwilliam Volume 2 supplies a bootstrap from order
  \(n-1\) to order \(n\).
- Gilkey heat-kernel asymptotics compute the local BRST cohomology
  groups needed for the induction.
- Compactness alone gives all-loop BV existence plus anomaly
  cancellation.
- Adler--Bardeen proves the hCS one-loop exactness needed here.
- A forthcoming CFG construction can serve as a current proof input.

## Recommended replacement for `q:all-loop`

The all-loop question should be rewritten as follows.

```tex
\subsection{All-higher Atiyah--Connes closure}

\begin{question}[All-higher cyclic obstruction; \ClaimStatusOpen]
\label{q:all-loop}
Fix the cyclic-HKR datum defining
\(\alpha_X=[m_3,B^{(2)}]_X\).  Do there exist higher cyclic rotations
\(\Delta_{n-1}\) and classes
\[
  a_n(X)=[m_n,\Delta_{n-1}]_X
  \in \mathcal H_n^{\mathrm{cyc}}(X),
  \qquad n\ge4,
\]
such that every \(a_n(X)\) lies in the filtered ideal generated by
\(\alpha_X\)?  Equivalently, after the BV/HKR diagonal comparison, is
the all-loop local BRST obstruction tower generated by its one-loop
Atiyah--Connes class?

The expected answer is yes on compact smooth projective CY3 targets
only after the following inputs are proved: the higher cyclic rotations,
the BV/HKR diagonal comparison, local BRST generation by the one-loop
class, and preservation of the bar-length filtration under
stage-two specialisation.  Without these inputs, \(\alpha_X=0\)
kills only the first obstruction \(o^{(0)}_3(A_C)\).
\end{question}
```

## Blocker list

1. **Cyclic-HKR base blocker.**  P01's cyclic-HKR datum must be
   inscribed.  Otherwise even \(\alpha_X\) is not a canonical class.
2. **Higher \(B\)-operator blocker.**  Define
   \(\Delta_{n-1}\) or stop writing \(B^{(n-1)}\).  Connes' \(B\)
   alone does not give the displayed hierarchy.
3. **Total-vs-termwise blocker.**  Decide whether the theorem asserts
   termwise vanishing or total-complex exactness.  The cross-volume
   evidence supports total coherence, not termwise zero.
4. **Arity-loop diagonal blocker.**  Construct
   \(\Theta^{(\ell)}_{\mathrm{BV/HKR}}\) identifying BV loop order
   \(\ell\) with arity \(\ell+2\) cyclic obstructions.
5. **Local BRST cohomology blocker.**  Compute the hCS local BRST
   cohomology in the relevant ghost/form degree.  This is the actual
   all-loop anomaly calculation.
6. **Non-renormalisation blocker.**  Prove one-loop generation or
   one-loop exactness in this holomorphic CY3 setting.  Adler--Bardeen
   is only a template.
7. **Counterterm-choice blocker.**  Show the classes \(a_n(X)\) and
   \(\mathfrak a_\ell(X,P)\) are independent of parametrix and
   counterterm choices up to the stated equivalence.
8. **Stage-two filtration blocker.**  Prove that
   \(\Sp^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\Sigma,C}\) preserves the arity/bar-length
   filtration and sends \(a_n(X)\) to \(o^{(0)}_n(A_C)\).
9. **Strict Theorem B blocker.**  Even after all-higher genus-zero
   vanishing, import Vol I with its exact hypotheses: strict Koszul
   lane, or completed Positselski lane, or a proved collapse theorem.
10. **Noncompact/Clemens blocker.**  Cylindrical ends, \(H\)-twists,
    and non-Kahler targets require separate BV and CDO constructions.
    They do not follow from compact CY3 hCS.

## Next falsifiers

1. **Finite cyclic \(A_\infty\) falsifier.**  Build a finite-dimensional
   cyclic \(A_\infty\)-algebra with \(m_3=0\), \(m_4\ne0\), and
   nondegenerate trace.  If the transported \([m_4,\Delta_3]\) is
   nonzero, the abstract implication
   \(\alpha=0\Rightarrow a_4=0\) is false.
2. **Local hCS falsifier.**  On a formal affine CY3 chart, compute the
   BRST anomaly at \(\hbar^2\).  If it is independent of the one-loop
   class modulo BV-exact terms, all-loop generation is false.
3. **Stage-two filtration falsifier.**  Compute
   \(d\Sp^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\Sigma,C}\) on an arity-four class in a toy
   cyclic chiral algebra.  If it mixes \(\pi_{4,0}\) with lower or
   higher bar lengths without a controlled homotopy, P02's all-higher
   extension fails.
4. **Flat calibration.**  On an abelian threefold with flat tangent
   connection, all Atiyah representatives vanish.  This should force
   every \(a_n\) to vanish.  Failure here means the cyclic-HKR
   convention is wrong.

## Integration recommendation

The paper should not promise "all-loop vanishing."  It should name:

- \(\alpha_X\): first cyclic-HKR obstruction;
- \(a_n(X)\): higher arity obstruction tower, conditional on higher
  cyclic rotations;
- \(\mathfrak a_\ell(X,P)\): BV loop obstruction tower;
- \(\Theta^{(\ell)}_{\mathrm{BV/HKR}}\): diagonal comparison;
- all-loop generation: conjecture.

That is the platonic form.  It turns a false all-loop slogan into a
precise programme: prove that the hCS local BRST anomaly algebra is
generated by the Atiyah--Connes class, then show stage-two
specialisation sends this generated tower to the Vol I bar-cobar
obstruction tower.
