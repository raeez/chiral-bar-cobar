## Verdict

The current rectified standalone has healed the worst categorical
falsehood: it no longer asserts that raw predicates, trace identities,
and homotopy witnesses are equal objects of `Pr^L_k`.  The remaining
gap is structural.  The paper says "derived loci" at the main theorem
surface, but it has not yet built the derived-loci machine.

The categorical native home is:

```tex
\[
  \mathcal B
  \quad\text{with}\quad
  \mathbb E := R\pi_*\Omega^1_{\mathcal X/\mathcal B}[2],
  \quad
  \alpha\in\Gamma(\mathcal B,\mathbb E),
  \quad
  \mathbf Z_\alpha:=\mathbf Z(\alpha).
\]
```

The four bridge vertices are then witness sheaves or derived zero
loci over the same base:

```tex
\[
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{BC}},\qquad
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{FA}},\qquad
  \mathbf Z_{\Delta_5},\qquad
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{tr}}.
\]
```

Only after equivalences of these derived loci have been proved may one
apply `QCoh` or `IndCoh` and obtain equivalent objects of
`\mathrm{Pr}^{\mathrm L}_k`.  The functor category is a shadow of the
geometric theorem, not the theorem's native ambient.

## Attack surface

### CRITICAL: `Pr^L_k` is still too close to the theorem surface

- `standalones/universal_anomaly_four_climax_simultaneously_2026_04_22.tex:857`

The paper correctly uses `Pr^L_{k,st}` to house dg enhancements and
colimit-preserving functors.  That use is legitimate.  It becomes
dangerous when it is asked to compare:

1. vanishing of a global obstruction class;
2. existence of a chain homotopy for a bar-cobar counit;
3. contractibility of a factorisation-algebra parametrix space;
4. membership in a Mukai/Igusa/K3 admissible locus;
5. vanishing of a trace anomaly functional.

These are not objects of one presentable category.  They are substacks,
formal moduli problems, or witness sheaves over a common base.

Heal: insert a derived-loci theorem before the bridge theorem.  Keep
`Pr^L_k` only in the corollary obtained by applying `QCoh` or `IndCoh`
to equivalent loci.

### SERIOUS: the base stack is not defined

The local-global supplement defines a Kuranishi moduli space
`\cM_{\mathrm{cx}}(X)` and a relative class in
`R^2(\pi_\cM)_*\Omega^1`, but the main categorical theorem needs a
larger base carrying all data used by the four vertices.

Heal: the base must parametrize:

- the smooth projective CY3 deformation type;
- a holomorphic volume-form trivialization or CY orientation;
- the cyclic dg enhancement used for negative cyclic/HKR comparison;
- the two-stage datum `(\Sigma_2,C)` used by `\Sp^{ch}_{\Sigma_2,C}`;
- the Mukai lattice/period datum on the K3-admissible branch;
- the trace normalization data used by the derived centre.

The real cycle `\Sigma_2` is not algebraic by itself.  Treat it as a
locally constant topological sheaf over the analytic/Kuranishi base, or
replace it by an algebraic curve/Hilbert datum when the intended
specialisation is algebraic.  Do not hide this point.

### SERIOUS: the zero locus is not an open complement

Local anchor:

- `standalones/universal_anomaly_local_global_arithmetic_supplement.tex:32`

Vanishing of a section of a coherent/perfect complex gives a derived
closed locus.  The underived smooth or admissible stratum may be open
inside that zero locus only after a constant-rank, smoothness, or
obstruction-theory splitting hypothesis.  The theorem must say
`\mathbf Z_\alpha`, not "open complement of the zero locus".

### SERIOUS: comparison loci cannot be equal without comparison maps

The bridge theorem uses `(B1)--(B4)` as hypotheses.  The derived-loci
framework should replace informal hypotheses by exact data:

```tex
\[
  c_v:\mathbb E|_{\mathcal B^\circ_v}\longrightarrow \mathbb O_v,
  \qquad
  \beta_v=c_v(\alpha),
  \qquad
  \mathbf Z_v=\mathbf Z(\beta_v)
\]
```

for each vertex `v`.  Equivalence
`\mathbf Z_\alpha\simeq\mathbf Z_v` requires `c_v` to be a
quasi-isomorphism on the relevant obstruction summand and requires
all higher independent obstructions to vanish or be functorially
generated by `\alpha`.

## Native derived stack

Use the following base in the manuscript.

```tex
\begin{definition}[Base of anomaly data]
\label{def:anomaly-base}
Fix a smooth oriented six-manifold \(M\) and a deformation type of
smooth projective Calabi--Yau threefold structures on \(M\).  Let
\[
  \mathcal B
  =
  \mathcal M^{\Omega,\mathrm{cyc},\Sigma,C,L,\mathrm{tr}}_{\mathrm{CY}_3}(M)
\]
be the derived analytic stack whose \(S\)-points are tuples
\[
  (X_S,\Omega_S,\mathcal A_S,\Sigma_{2,S},C_S,L_S,\tau_S).
\]
Here \(X_S\to S\) is a smooth projective Calabi--Yau threefold family
in the fixed deformation type, \(\Omega_S:\omega_{X_S/S}\simeq\cO_S\)
is a Calabi--Yau orientation, \(\mathcal A_S\) is a cyclic dg
enhancement of \(\Perf(X_S)\), \((\Sigma_{2,S},C_S)\) is an admissible
two-stage specialisation datum, \(L_S\) is a locally constant
Mukai/period lattice datum on the K3-admissible branch, and
\(\tau_S\) is the trace-normalisation datum for the derived centre.

The algebraic substack is obtained by replacing \(\Sigma_{2,S}\) by an
algebraic curve/Hilbert datum.  The topological two-cycle version is a
locally constant sheaf of spaces over the derived analytic CY moduli
stack.
\end{definition}
```

Use `\mathcal B^\circ` for the common domain where the four bridge
constructions are simultaneously defined:

```tex
\[
  \mathcal B^\circ
  =
  \mathcal B_{\mathrm{BC}}
  \cap
  \mathcal B_{\mathrm{FA}}
  \cap
  \mathcal B_{\Delta_5}
  \cap
  \mathcal B_{\mathrm{tr}} .
\]
```

This intersection is a derived fiber product over `\mathcal B`, not a
set-theoretic intersection.

## Obstruction complex and zero locus

The obstruction complex should be stated as a perfect complex over
`\mathcal B`.

```tex
\begin{definition}[Universal Atiyah--Connes obstruction complex]
\label{def:atiyah-connes-obstruction-complex}
Let
\[
  \pi:\mathcal X\longrightarrow\mathcal B
\]
be the universal Calabi--Yau threefold.  Define
\[
  \mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}
  :=
  R\pi_*\Omega^1_{\mathcal X/\mathcal B}[2].
\]
With the cohomological shift convention used here,
\[
  H^0\!\left(\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}|_b\right)
  =
  H^2(X_b,\Omega^1_{X_b})
  =
  H^{1,2}(X_b).
\]
The cyclic Hochschild construction gives a section
\[
  \alpha_{\mathrm{AC}}\in
  \Gamma(\mathcal B,\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}})
\]
whose value at \(b\) is
\[
  [m_3,B^{(2)}]_{X_b}\in H^2(X_b,\Omega^1_{X_b}).
\]
\end{definition}
```

The zero locus is the derived fiber product with the zero section.

```tex
\begin{definition}[Atiyah--Connes derived zero locus]
\label{def:atiyah-connes-zero-locus}
Let
\[
  \mathbb V(\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}})
  :=
  \underline{\mathrm{Spec}}_{\mathcal B}
  \operatorname{Sym}_{\cO_{\mathcal B}}
  (\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}^\vee)
\]
be the vector-stack associated to \(\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}\).  The
derived zero locus of the Atiyah--Connes section is
\[
  \mathbf Z_\alpha
  :=
  \mathcal B
  \times_{\mathbb V(\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}})}
  \mathcal B,
\]
where the two maps are the zero section and
\(\alpha_{\mathrm{AC}}\).  Its classical truncation is the ordinary
vanishing locus of the classes
\([m_3,B^{(2)}]_{X_b}\).
\end{definition}
```

Proof note: this construction is standard in derived algebraic
geometry for sections of perfect complexes.  Use Gaitsgory--Rozenblyum
2017 and Lurie `SAG` as the derived-stack anchors; use Porta--Yu or
Joyce only if the analytic/Kuranishi model is made explicit.

## Four comparison loci

### 1. Chiral bar-cobar/Positselski locus

For `b in \mathcal B^\circ`, put

```tex
\[
  A_b :=
  \Sp^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\Sigma_{2,b},C_b}
  \Phi^{\mathrm{FA}}_3(X_b).
\]
```

The witness is not the equivalence class alone; it is the space of
contracting homotopies for the counit.

```tex
\begin{definition}[Bar--cobar witness locus]
\label{def:bar-cobar-witness-locus}
Let \(\varepsilon_b:
\Omega_{C_b}^{\mathrm{ch}}B_{C_b}^{\mathrm{ch}}(A_b)\to A_b\)
be the chiral bar--cobar counit in the completed/coderived ambient.
Define \(\mathcal W_{\mathrm{BC}}\) over \(\mathcal B^\circ\) by
\[
  \mathcal W_{\mathrm{BC}}(b)
  =
  \left\{
    h\ \middle|\ [d,h]=\id-p,\ h^2=0,\ ph=hp=0
  \right\},
\]
the derived space of Positselski contracting homotopies for
\(\varepsilon_b\).  The bar--cobar locus is
\[
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{BC}}
  :=
  \operatorname{Supp}_{\mathcal B^\circ}(\mathcal W_{\mathrm{BC}}).
\]
\end{definition}
```

For a cleaner derived-obstruction formulation:

```tex
\[
  \mathbb O_{\mathrm{BC},b}
  :=
  R\!\operatorname{Hom}_{D^{co}_{ch}(C_b)}
  \left(\operatorname{cofib}(\varepsilon_b),
        \operatorname{cofib}(\varepsilon_b)\right)[1].
\]
\[
  \beta_{\mathrm{BC}}\in\Gamma(\mathcal B^\circ,\mathbb O_{\mathrm{BC}})
\]
```

is the obstruction to making the counit an equivalence.  The comparison
bridge is a morphism

```tex
\[
  c_{\mathrm{BC}}:
  \mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}|_{\mathcal B^\circ}
  \longrightarrow
  \mathbb O_{\mathrm{BC}}
  \quad\text{with}\quad
  \beta_{\mathrm{BC}}=c_{\mathrm{BC}}(\alpha_{\mathrm{AC}}).
\]
```

Equivalence
`\mathbf Z_\alpha\simeq\mathbf Z_{\mathrm{BC}}` is conditional on:

- `c_{\mathrm{BC}}` being a quasi-isomorphism on the primary
  obstruction summand;
- the higher Positselski/Malcev obstruction tower being generated by
  the primary class, or vanishing independently on the chosen completed
  Koszul lane;
- the witness space of homotopies being contractible over the zero
  locus.

### 2. Factorisation-algebra/parametrix locus

The factorisation vertex is a contractibility statement for local
parametrices satisfying holomorphic locality and Weiss descent.

```tex
\begin{definition}[Factorisation-parametrix locus]
\label{def:factorisation-parametrix-locus}
Let \(\mathcal P_{\mathrm{FA}}(b)\) be the derived space of
Costello--Li parametrices and holomorphic locality data making
\(\Phi^{\mathrm{FA}}_3(X_b)\) a factorisation algebra in the
holomorphic complex-three-dimensional ambient fixed in
\S\ref{sub:fa-precision}.  Let
\[
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{FA}}
  :=
  \{\,b\in\mathcal B^\circ\mid
      \mathcal P_{\mathrm{FA}}(b)\simeq *\,\}
  \subset \mathcal B^\circ
\]
be the derived locus where the parametrix choice space is contractible.
\end{definition}
```

The associated obstruction complex can be written abstractly as the
deformation complex of the prefactorisation-to-factorisation lift:

```tex
\[
  \mathbb O_{\mathrm{FA},b}
  :=
  R\!\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathrm{PFact}(X_b)}
  \left(
    \operatorname{cofib}
    (\Phi^{\mathrm{preFA}}_3(X_b)\to\Phi^{\mathrm{FA}}_3(X_b)),
    \Phi^{\mathrm{FA}}_3(X_b)
  \right)[1].
\]
```

The comparison bridge is:

```tex
\[
  c_{\mathrm{FA}}:
  \mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}|_{\mathcal B^\circ}
  \longrightarrow
  \mathbb O_{\mathrm{FA}},
  \qquad
  \beta_{\mathrm{FA}}=c_{\mathrm{FA}}(\alpha_{\mathrm{AC}}).
\]
```

Equivalence with `\mathbf Z_\alpha` is conditional on this comparison
identifying the Costello--Li local anomaly with the same cyclic HKR
class and on the absence of independent higher-loop/locality
obstructions.  The real locally constant object is `E_6`; the
holomorphic `E_3` statement is part of `c_{\mathrm{FA}}`, not a
formal identity.

### 3. K3/Mukai/Igusa locus

This vertex is not defined over all CY3 data.  It lives over the
Mukai-admissible branch.

```tex
\begin{definition}[Mukai--Igusa admissible base]
\label{def:mukai-igusa-base}
Let
\[
  \mathcal B_{\Delta_5}
  :=
  \mathcal B^\circ
  \times_{\mathcal M_{\mathrm{Hdg}}}
  \mathcal H_{\Delta_5}
\]
be the derived preimage of the Mukai/Kuga--Satake/Humbert period
condition defining the \(\Delta_5\)-admissible K3 branch.  Here
\(\mathcal H_{\Delta_5}\) denotes the period-theoretic Humbert/Heegner
locus with the Bruinier--Mukai trace normalisation fixed.
\end{definition}
```

The witness sheaf is the equivalence space in chiral algebras:

```tex
\begin{definition}[K3 bialgebra witness locus]
\label{def:k3-bialgebra-witness-locus}
Over \(\mathcal B_{\Delta_5}\), define
\[
  \mathcal W_{\Delta_5}(b)
  :=
  \operatorname{Eqv}_{\mathrm{ChirAlg}(C_b)}
  \left(
    \Sp^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\Sigma_{2,b},C_b}
    \Phi^{\mathrm{FA}}_3(X_b),
    \mathbf H_{\Delta_5,b}
  \right).
\]
The K3 vertex is
\[
  \mathbf Z_{\Delta_5}
  :=
  \operatorname{Supp}_{\mathcal B_{\Delta_5}}
  (\mathcal W_{\Delta_5}).
\]
\end{definition}
```

The comparison is a map into a deformation complex of the above
equivalence problem:

```tex
\[
  c_{\Delta_5}:
  \mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}|_{\mathcal B_{\Delta_5}}
  \longrightarrow
  \mathbb O_{\Delta_5}.
\]
```

Equivalence with `\mathbf Z_\alpha` is not global.  The strongest
honest statement is:

```tex
\[
  \mathbf Z_\alpha|_{\mathcal B_{\Delta_5}}
  \simeq
  \mathbf Z_{\Delta_5}
\]
```

conditional on stage-two Fubini, Mukai-period compatibility,
Bruinier normalisation, and the exact comparison between the
Atiyah--Connes class and the K3 bialgebra deformation complex.

The generic quintic is outside this branch.  It is a falsifier of a
global K3 conclusion, not evidence against this restricted branch.

### 4. Trace-anomaly locus

The trace vertex must be a family of functionals on
`\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}`, not a single scalar equation.

```tex
\begin{definition}[Trace anomaly locus]
\label{def:trace-anomaly-locus}
Let
\[
  T_{\mathrm{tr}}:
  \mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}|_{\mathcal B^\circ}
  \longrightarrow
  \mathbb L_{\mathrm{tr}}
\]
be the trace/normal-function period morphism attached to the derived
centre trace, with \(\mathbb L_{\mathrm{tr}}\) a perfect line or
finite-rank period complex over \(\mathcal B^\circ\).  The trace
anomaly section is
\[
  \beta_{\mathrm{tr}}
  :=
  T_{\mathrm{tr}}(\alpha_{\mathrm{AC}})
  \in
  \Gamma(\mathcal B^\circ,\mathbb L_{\mathrm{tr}}),
\]
and
\[
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{tr}}
  :=
  \mathbf Z(\beta_{\mathrm{tr}}).
\]
\end{definition}
```

There is always a map:

```tex
\[
  \mathbf Z_\alpha|_{\mathcal B^\circ}
  \longrightarrow
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{tr}}.
\]
```

It is an equivalence only if `T_{\mathrm{tr}}` is conservative on the
cyclic-skew obstruction summand:

```tex
\[
  \ker(T_{\mathrm{tr}})
  \cap
  \mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}^{\mathrm{cyc\text{-}skew}}
  =
  0.
\]
```

A single scalar trace cannot detect a high-dimensional
`H^2(X,\Omega^1_X)`.  The manuscript must use a separating period
family, a normal-function family, or a local-cohomology receptacle.
The curve group `H^2(C,\Omega^1_C)` cannot serve: it is zero.

## The theorem the paper should state

```tex
\begin{theorem}[Derived anomaly loci and the conditional bridge]
\label{thm:derived-anomaly-loci}
Let
\[
  \mathcal B
  =
  \mathcal M^{\Omega,\mathrm{cyc},\Sigma,C,L,\mathrm{tr}}_{\mathrm{CY}_3}(M)
\]
be the derived analytic base of anomaly data and let
\(\pi:\mathcal X\to\mathcal B\) be the universal family.  Put
\[
  \mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}
  =
  R\pi_*\Omega^1_{\mathcal X/\mathcal B}[2].
\]
The Atiyah--Connes construction defines a section
\[
  \alpha_{\mathrm{AC}}\in\Gamma(\mathcal B,\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}})
\]
whose fibre at \(b\) is
\[
  [m_3,B^{(2)}]_{X_b}\in H^2(X_b,\Omega^1_{X_b})=H^{1,2}(X_b).
\]
Let \(\mathbf Z_\alpha=\mathbf Z(\alpha_{\mathrm{AC}})\) be its
derived zero locus.

On the common domain \(\mathcal B^\circ\), the four bridge vertices
define derived loci or witness sheaves
\[
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{BC}},\qquad
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{FA}},\qquad
  \mathbf Z_{\Delta_5},\qquad
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{tr}}.
\]
Assume the following comparison data:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(D\arabic*)}]
\item a Positselski comparison morphism
\[
  c_{\mathrm{BC}}:\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}\to\mathbb O_{\mathrm{BC}}
\]
identifying \(c_{\mathrm{BC}}(\alpha_{\mathrm{AC}})\) with the primary
bar--cobar obstruction and reducing all higher completed
Positselski/Malcev obstructions to this class;
\item a holomorphic factorisation comparison morphism
\[
  c_{\mathrm{FA}}:\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}\to\mathbb O_{\mathrm{FA}}
\]
identifying \(c_{\mathrm{FA}}(\alpha_{\mathrm{AC}})\) with the
Costello--Li local anomaly/parametrix obstruction, with no independent
higher-loop locality obstruction;
\item on \(\mathcal B_{\Delta_5}\), a Mukai--Igusa comparison morphism
\[
  c_{\Delta_5}:\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}\to\mathbb O_{\Delta_5}
\]
identifying the stage-two chiral specialisation obstruction with the
deformation complex of the equivalence to \(\mathbf H_{\Delta_5}\);
\item a trace period morphism
\[
  T_{\mathrm{tr}}:\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}\to\mathbb L_{\mathrm{tr}}
\]
which is conservative on the cyclic-skew obstruction summand.
\end{enumerate}
If these comparison morphisms are equivalences on the relevant
obstruction theories and the corresponding witness sheaves have
contractible fibres over the zero locus, then there are equivalences of
derived loci over their common domain
\[
  \mathbf Z_\alpha
  \simeq
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{BC}}
  \simeq
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{FA}}
  \simeq
  \mathbf Z_{\Delta_5}
  \simeq
  \mathbf Z_{\mathrm{tr}}.
\]
Without \textup{(D1)--(D4)}, the arrows exist only as conditional
comparison maps or inclusions, and the pentagon is conjectural.
\end{theorem}
```

The proof is then short and honest:

```tex
\begin{proof}
The construction of \(\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}\) and
\(\mathbf Z_\alpha\) is the derived zero-locus construction for a
section of a perfect complex.  The four vertices are defined as
witness sheaves or zero loci of their obstruction sections.  Each
assumption \textup{(D1)--(D4)} identifies one vertex obstruction theory
with the Atiyah--Connes obstruction theory.  Equivalence of obstruction
theories and contractibility of the witness fibres identify the
corresponding derived zero loci.  The trace implication uses
conservativity of \(T_{\mathrm{tr}}\); absent that hypothesis one has
only \(\mathbf Z_\alpha\to\mathbf Z_{\mathrm{tr}}\).
\end{proof}
```

## `QCoh` and `IndCoh` corollary

After the derived-loci theorem, and only after it, the paper may state:

```tex
\begin{corollary}[Presentable-category shadow of the anomaly loci]
\label{cor:prl-shadow-anomaly-loci}
Assume the hypotheses of Theorem~\ref{thm:derived-anomaly-loci} and
assume the loci involved are perfect derived stacks.  Then pullback
along the equivalences of derived stacks gives equivalences
\[
  \QCoh(\mathbf Z_\alpha)
  \simeq
  \QCoh(\mathbf Z_{\mathrm{BC}})
  \simeq
  \QCoh(\mathbf Z_{\mathrm{FA}})
  \simeq
  \QCoh(\mathbf Z_{\Delta_5})
  \simeq
  \QCoh(\mathbf Z_{\mathrm{tr}})
\]
in \(\mathrm{Pr}^{\mathrm L}_{k,\mathrm{st}}\).  If the loci are
quasi-smooth and locally almost of finite type, the same statement
holds for \(\IndCoh\).
\end{corollary}
```

Do not use the converse unless a Tannakian reconstruction theorem is
stated with its hypotheses.  An equivalence of underlying presentable
categories is weaker than an equivalence of derived stacks; the
symmetric monoidal `QCoh` structure plus perfect-stack hypotheses are
the data that recover geometry.

## Exact equivalence conditions

The theorem must require all four of the following.

1. Common base: all loci are over the same derived base or over an
   explicitly named derived subbase such as `\mathcal B_{\Delta_5}`.

2. Same obstruction theory: for each vertex `v`, a perfect obstruction
   complex `\mathbb O_v` and comparison map
   `c_v:\mathbb E_{\mathrm{AC}}\to\mathbb O_v` are supplied.

3. Higher obstruction closure: the vertex has no independent higher
   obstructions after the primary class vanishes, or these higher
   obstructions are separately assumed zero.

4. Contractible witnesses: the space of choices over each point of the
   zero locus is contractible.  Otherwise the vertex is a torsor or
   gerbe over `\mathbf Z_\alpha`, not the same locus.

## Proved / conditional / conjectural split

Proved in the rectified paper:

- the corrected target of the obstruction class is
  `H^2(X,\Omega^1_X)=H^{1,2}(X)`;
- ordinary coherent restriction to a curve cannot be the return edge,
  because `H^2(C,\Omega^1_C)=0`;
- `Pr^L_k` is valid for dg enhancements and functorial shadows, not as
  the native home of the five raw statements.

Conditional but reachable:

- the derived-loci theorem above, once the base stack and witness
  sheaves are inserted;
- the `QCoh`/`IndCoh` corollary, once the derived loci are proved
  equivalent and perfect/quasi-smooth hypotheses are stated.

Conjectural:

- full equivalence of
  `\mathbf Z_\alpha`, `\mathbf Z_{\mathrm{BC}}`,
  `\mathbf Z_{\mathrm{FA}}`, `\mathbf Z_{\Delta_5}`, and
  `\mathbf Z_{\mathrm{tr}}` without the comparison hypotheses;
- any scalar trace return edge not upgraded to a separating period or
  normal-function family;
- any global K3/Igusa conclusion over the full CY3 base.
