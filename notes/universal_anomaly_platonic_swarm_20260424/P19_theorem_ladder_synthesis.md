## Verdict

The standalone should become:

```tex
\title[Atiyah--Connes obstruction]{The Atiyah--Connes obstruction to chiral bar--cobar specialisation}
```

The paper's spine is not the old four-climax diagram. The spine is the
Atiyah--Connes obstruction class and its first curve-side specialization:

```tex
\alpha_X \in H^2(X,\Omega_X^1)=H^{1,2}(X),
\qquad
\operatorname{sp}_{\Sigma,C}(\alpha_X)=o^{(0)}_3(A_C).
```

The first expression is the class once a cyclic-HKR datum is fixed. The
second expression is the first theorem that would make the standalone a
new result. Vol I does not prove it. Vol II and Vol III do not prove it.
It is the new bridge.

The paper should be reorganized into four epistemic layers:

1. **Core construction.** Define the cyclic-HKR datum, construct
   \(\alpha_X\), prove the Hodge target, prove the curve \(H^2\)
   falsifier, and build the derived zero locus.
2. **New theorem target.** Prove that stage-two specialization sends
   \(\alpha_X\) to the first genus-zero chiral bar-cobar obstruction.
3. **Conditional bridge.** Under higher-obstruction closure and the
   factorisation/K3/trace comparison hypotheses, identify the four
   comparison loci with the zero locus of \(\alpha_X\).
4. **Frontier realisations.** Motivic, graph-Duflo, Malcev, hCS,
   Clemens, arithmetic, and all-loop BV are real programmes, not support
   for the core theorem until their comparison maps are proved.

## The decisive first proof

The paper becomes a new result after the following proof package is
completed.

First, insert and prove the cyclic-HKR datum theorem from P01: Connes'
chain operator must be transported to the cochain side with a fixed
Calabi--Yau trace, total grading, sign, corrected HKR/Mukai
normalisation, and contraction
\[
  H^2(X,\wedge^2T_X)\xrightarrow{\iota_{(-)}\Omega_X}
  H^2(X,\Omega_X^1).
\]
This is the definitional gate. Without it, \([m_3,B^{(2)}]\) is still a
name, not a class.

Then prove the specialization theorem from P02:
\[
  \operatorname{sp}_{\Sigma,C}(\alpha_X)
  =
  o^{(0)}_3(A_C)
  \in H^2\!\left(\pi_{3,0}\Defcyc^{\mathrm{mod}}(A_C)\right).
\]
This is the first genuinely new theorem. It identifies a global CY3
cyclic-HKR obstruction with the first curve-side chiral bar-cobar
obstruction. Everything else in the paper should either prepare this
theorem, test it, or state the conditional consequences.

Full Theorem B is not the first result. It follows only after one of:

- \(A_C\in\operatorname{Kosz}(C)\);
- the completed pro-conilpotent Positselski hypotheses hold;
- an all-higher closure theorem proves that every
  \(o^{(0)}_{r+1}(A_C)\) is generated by \(o^{(0)}_3(A_C)\).

## Main theorem ladder

### 1. Definition: cyclic-HKR datum

This definition is not optional. It is the load-bearing gate found by
P01, P07, P08, P09, P10, and P12.

```tex
\begin{definition}[Cyclic-HKR datum]
\label{def:cyclic-hkr-datum}
Let \(X\) be a smooth projective Calabi--Yau threefold with holomorphic
volume form \(\Omega_X\), and let \(\Perf_{\mathrm{dg}}(X)\) be a
cyclic dg enhancement of \(D^b(\Coh X)\).  A cyclic-HKR datum
\(\mathfrak c_X\) consists of:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(AC\arabic*)}]
\item a Hochschild cochain model
\[
  C^\bullet_X=
  C^\bullet(\Perf_{\mathrm{dg}}(X),\Perf_{\mathrm{dg}}(X))
\]
with differential \(\delta_{\mathrm{Hoch}}\);
\item a cohomological \(S^1\)-operator
\[
  \Delta_2:C^\bullet_X\to C^{\bullet+\epsilon}_X
\]
obtained from Connes' chain operator \(B=(1-t)sN\) by the stated
Calabi--Yau trace convention, with sign and degree \(\epsilon\)
fixed;
\item the ternary Stasheff operation \(m_3\) acting on the same complex
with the stated suspension convention;
\item a corrected HKR/Mukai comparison and a projection
\[
  \pi^{\mathrm{HKR}}_{2,2}:\HH^4(X)\to H^2(X,\wedge^2T_X);
\]
\item the Calabi--Yau contraction
\[
  \iota_{(-)}\Omega_X:H^2(X,\wedge^2T_X)\to H^2(X,\Omega_X^1).
\]
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}
```

### 2. Definition: Atiyah--Connes class

```tex
\begin{definition}[Atiyah--Connes class]
\label{def:atiyah-connes-class}
Fix a cyclic-HKR datum \(\mathfrak c_X\).  Suppose the graded
commutator
\[
  \gamma_X=[m_3,\Delta_2]
\]
is \(\delta_{\mathrm{Hoch}}\)-closed of total Hochschild degree \(4\).
Put
\[
  \beta_X=\pi^{\mathrm{HKR}}_{2,2}([\gamma_X])
  \in H^2(X,\wedge^2T_X)
\]
and
\[
  \alpha_X=\iota_{\beta_X}\Omega_X\in H^2(X,\Omega_X^1).
\]
This is the Atiyah--Connes class attached to
\((X,\Omega_X,\mathfrak c_X)\).
\end{definition}
```

Interim status: definition conditional on the cyclic-HKR datum.
Publication status after proof: the theorem below may become
`\ClaimStatusProvedHere`.

### 3. Theorem: construction and invariance

```tex
\begin{theorem}[Atiyah--Connes HKR class; \ClaimStatusConditional]
\label{thm:atiyah-connes-hkr-class}
Let \(X\) be a smooth projective Calabi--Yau threefold equipped with a
cyclic-HKR datum \(\mathfrak c_X\).  Then the construction of
Definition~\ref{def:atiyah-connes-class} gives a class
\[
  \alpha_X\in H^2(X,\Omega_X^1).

It is independent of the Dolbeault representative of the Atiyah class
and invariant under cyclic \(A_\infty\)-quasi-equivalence preserving
\(\mathfrak c_X\).
\end{theorem}
```

Proof obligations:

### 4. Proposition: Hodge target

```tex

\[

the Calabi--Yau dual convention; it is not the sheaf-cohomology target.
\end{proposition}
```

\label{thm:sp-alpha-first-barcobar-obstruction}

## Examples and falsifiers

### Quintic detector

```tex
\begin{proposition}[Quintic Hodge target and Yukawa detector;
\ClaimStatusProvedElsewhere]
\label{prop:quintic-hodge-yukawa-detector}
Let \(Q_f\subset\mathbb P^4\) be a smooth quintic threefold and
\[
  R_f=\mathbb C[x_0,\ldots,x_4]/(\partial_0f,\ldots,\partial_4f).
\]
Griffiths' residue theorem gives
\[
  H^{2,1}(Q_f)\cong R_f^5,\qquad
  H^2(Q_f,\Omega^1_{Q_f})=H^{1,2}(Q_f)\cong R_f^{10}.
\]
For the Fermat quintic, and hence generically,
\[
  \dim R_f^5=\dim R_f^{10}=101,
\]
and the Yukawa coupling is the non-zero socle multiplication
\[
  R_f^5\otimes R_f^5\otimes R_f^5\to R_f^{15}\cong\mathbb C.
\]
\end{proposition}
```

Then the actual open question:

```tex
\begin{question}[Quintic Atiyah--Connes residue]
\label{q:quintic-atiyah-connes-residue}
Compute
\[
  a_f=\Psi_f(\alpha_{Q_f})\in R_f^{10}
\]
under the Griffiths residue identification.  Is there
\(\mu\in R_f^5\) such that
\[
  \operatorname{Soc}(a_f\mu)\ne0?
\]
\end{question}
```

Non-zero Yukawa is a detector, not a proof that \(\alpha_{Q_f}\ne0\).

### Product reduction for \(K3\times E\)

```tex
\begin{theorem}[Product reduction of the \(K3\times E\) obstruction]
\label{thm:k3e-product-reduction}
Let \(Y=S\times E\), with \(S\) a projective K3 surface and \(E\) an
elliptic curve.  Fix
\[
  \Omega_Y=\pi_S^*\sigma_S\wedge\pi_E^*\eta_E.
\]
Assume the cyclic-HKR datum is product-natural and sends the
Atiyah--Connes commutator to
\[
  \beta_Y\in H^2(Y,\wedge^2T_Y).
\]
Then the projection of
\(\alpha_Y=\iota_{\beta_Y}\Omega_Y\) to
\[
  H^1(S,\Omega_S^1)\otimes H^1(E,\mathcal O_E)
\]
is zero.  Hence
\[
  \alpha_Y=
  \lambda_{\mathrm{Cyc}}(S,E,\mathfrak c)\,
  [\bar\sigma_S]\otimes\eta_E
  \in
  H^2(S,\mathcal O_S)\otimes H^0(E,\Omega_E^1).
\]
Thus
\[
  \alpha_Y=0
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  \lambda_{\mathrm{Cyc}}(S,E,\mathfrak c)=0.
\]
Under the standard Atiyah--Chern/Rozansky--Witten normalisation, the
scalar is expected to be proportional to
\(\int_Sc_2(T_S)=24\).
\end{theorem}
```

The raw product is a scalar test, not a zero witness.

### Rigid and flat witnesses

Rigid CY3s with \(h^{1,2}=0\) are the cleanest strict CY3 zero
witnesses:
\[
  H^2(X,\Omega_X^1)=0.
\]
Abelian threefolds and complex tori have trivial tangent bundle and
\(\at=0\), but they violate the strict \(h^{1,0}=0\) hypothesis; they
are convention checks.

### Higher-order K3 automorphic quotients

For \(g_S^*\sigma_S=\zeta\sigma_S\) and
\(g_E^*\eta_E=\zeta^{-1}\eta_E\), the scalar line has character
