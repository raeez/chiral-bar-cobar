These items cannot be closed by any amount of internal-corpus audit. ## D. Where the programme's genuine mathematical frontier lies

Of the items enumerated above, **the programme's *genuine* external frontier consists of eight items (E1--E8)**, and of these the three truly structural are:

- **E1** (Brown motivic-GRT above weight 12): limits $\phi^{(n)}$ tower at $n \ge 13$ (doubly conditional) or $n \ge 25$ (blocked).
- **E2** (KRS 3d/4d for $\Phi_4$): limits the CY-$d$ tower at $d \ge 4$.
- **E3** (CLW BCOV non-perturbative on compact CY$_3$): limits CY-B at $d = 3$ compact case.

E4 (Kuznetsov Fano HPD) and E5 (HMS non-projective) are single-path blockers whose closures are not strictly required (alternative verification paths exist). E6 (Beauville--Voisin beyond $K3^{[5]}$) is a low-priority refinement. E7 (Andr\'e--Oort) is largely absorbed. E8 (Massey triple associator-free) is the sole internal-algebraic frontier that crosses into an external research programme (Brown motivic + Furusho associator-uniqueness).

**The programme is otherwise closed**: no other open item requires new external mathematics. All 19 internal-residue items (I1--I19) are within the programme's inscription reach; all 8 meta-decisions (M1--M8) are already effected.

**Beilinson's dictum holds.** The programme has been audited to the point where false ideas have been dismissed in proportion to the genuine structural work that remains. What is left is either (i) a small number of pointed external problems in adjacent fields, (ii) a bounded register of further in-programme inscriptions, or (iii) already-effected programme-level closures. The manuscript stands on its inscribed theorems; the frontier is sharply delimited.
