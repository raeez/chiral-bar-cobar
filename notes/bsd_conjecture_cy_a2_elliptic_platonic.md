# The Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture through CY-A_2 Restricted to Elliptic Fibres

An honest first-principles attack on BSD via the programme's CY-to-chiral functor $\Phi$ applied
at $d=1$ (elliptic curves as complex one-manifolds, hence CY$_1$) and the $\ell$-adic derived
chiral centre restricted to arithmetic lattice data. Parallel in structure to
`riemann_hypothesis_weil_grothendieck_ladic_platonic.md`. Same three-item template;
sharper verdict because the Galois representation attached to $E/\mathbb{Q}$ is already
two-dimensional and already classical (Eichler-Shimura / Wiles-Taylor-Breuil-Conrad-Diamond),
leaving less ambiguity about what the chiral-algebra language can add.

## 0. Coordinates

$E/\mathbb{Q}$ is an elliptic curve with conductor $N$; $L(E,s) = \prod_{p} L_p(E,s)^{-1}$
with $L_p(E,s) = \det(1 - \mathrm{Frob}_p p^{-s} \mid H^1_{\ell,\text{Ã©t}}(E_{\overline{\mathbb{Q}}},\mathbb{Q}_\ell))$ at good $p$.
BSD (full): $\mathrm{rank}\, E(\mathbb{Q}) = \mathrm{ord}_{s=1} L(E,s)$, and the leading
Taylor coefficient is the Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer product (regulator, real period, Tamagawa,
Sha). BSD (rank): $\mathrm{rank}\, E(\mathbb{Q}) = \mathrm{ord}_{s=1} L(E,s)$. Known partial:
if $\mathrm{ord}_{s=1} L(E,s) \leq 1$, Kolyvagin-Gross-Zagier proves BSD-rank. Rank $\geq 2$
open. Sha finiteness open in all ranks.

For the programme, the CY-to-chiral functor at $d=1$ is simpler than at $d=2$: an elliptic
curve $E$ has $\HH_\bullet(D^b(\mathrm{Coh}\,E))$ = $H^\bullet(E,\mathbb{C})$ by HKR;
the generating space $V = \HH^{\bullet+1}$ is rank $2$ (from $H^1(E)$) plus rank
$1+1$ for $H^0,H^2$. Applied via steps 1â€“4 of $\Phi$ (HKR, Lie conformal bracket, $S^1$-framing
since $d=1$, quantization), the output is a rank-$2$ Heisenberg-type VOA on the $H^1$ data;
$\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}(\Phi(E)) = h^{1,0}(E) + h^{1,0}(E) = 2$ if one uses the Hodge-filtered
supertrace (cy_to_chiral.tex L183 explicitly records $\delta\kappa = h^{1,0}$ at $d=1$:
"free-boson zero modes; Serre $\mathbb{S}_\mathcal{C} \simeq [1]$ does not kill the anomaly"),
so $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}(E) = 1 \ne 0 = \chi(\mathcal{O}_E)$ â€” the $d=1$ case is one of the
anomaly-carrying strata.

## 1. $\Phi(E)$ as chiral algebra with arithmetic structure

### 1.1 Construction

Let $E/\mathbb{Q}$ have NÃ©ron model $\mathcal{E}/\mathbb{Z}$. The integral Mordell-Weil
group $E(\mathbb{Q})$ fits in $0 \to E(\mathbb{Q})_{\text{tors}} \to E(\mathbb{Q}) \to \mathbb{Z}^r \to 0$;
$H^1(E_{\overline{\mathbb{Q}}},\mathbb{Q}_\ell) \cong V_\ell(E) \oplus V_\ell(E)(-1)$
after Tate-twist bookkeeping; more usefully, $T_\ell(E) := \varprojlim E[\ell^n]$
is a rank-$2$ $\mathbb{Z}_\ell$-module with a continuous
$\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$-action $\rho_{E,\ell}$.

Apply $\Phi$ at $d=1$. Working conditions: $V = H^1(E) \oplus (H^0 \oplus H^2)[\pm 1]$ of
rank $4$ over $\mathbb{Q}$, with the symplectic PoincarÃ© pairing on $H^1$. Step 2 produces
a Lie conformal algebra with $J^a J^b$ OPE $\sim \omega^{ab}/(z-w)^2$ where $\omega$ is the
PoincarÃ© cup-product form. Step 3 attaches an $S^1$-framing from the CY trace
$\mathrm{tr}: \HH_1 \to \mathbb{C}$, given by integration against the holomorphic
$1$-form $\omega_E$ on $E$. Step 4 quantizes to a rank-$2$ symplectic-boson VOA $\Phi(E)$
â€” this is a lattice-type VOA with lattice $L_E := H^1(E,\mathbb{Z})$ equipped with the
cup-product form (signature $(1,1)$, det $= 1$).

Concretely, $\Phi(E) \cong V_{L_E} = \mathbb{C}[L_E] \otimes \mathrm{Fock}(\hat{H^1})$ â€”
a rank-$2$ lattice VOA, with Heisenberg subalgebra $\hat{H}^1$ and lattice exponentials
$e^{\alpha}$ for $\alpha \in L_E$. $\Phi(E)$ is $E_\infty$-chiral (abelian-R, class G in
the fingerprint classification); it is NOT class M â€” $E$ is a CY_1, not class M-hosting.

### 1.2 Galois action

$\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$ acts on $H^1(E_{\overline{\mathbb{Q}}},\mathbb{Z}_\ell) = T_\ell(E)$
via $\rho_{E,\ell}: \mathrm{Gal} \to \mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{Z}_\ell)$ preserving the
Weil pairing (i.e. landing in $\mathrm{GSp}_2 = \mathrm{GL}_2$ with determinant
$= \chi_{\mathrm{cyc}}$). This lifts to an action on $L_E \otimes \mathbb{Z}_\ell$ by
preserving the cup-product form, hence to an action on $\Phi(E) \otimes \mathbb{Q}_\ell$
by compatible algebra automorphisms (Heisenberg generators transform by
$\rho_{E,\ell}$ on their $H^1$-index; lattice exponentials $e^{\alpha}$ transform by
$\mathrm{Gal}$-orbit on $\alpha \in L_E$).

**Galois-fixed points.** $\Phi(E)^{\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})}$ is the
subalgebra generated by the Galois-invariant lattice classes. Since $\rho_{E,\ell}$ has
image Zariski-dense in $\mathrm{GL}_2$ (Serre open image; true for non-CM $E$), the only
$\mathrm{Gal}$-invariant lattice classes are the $\mathbb{Z}$-span of the identity and
the symplectic-central $e^0 = 1$, so $\Phi(E)^{\mathrm{Gal}} = \mathbb{C}$ generically
(plus higher-weight invariants at a sparse set; for CM $E$ with CM by $K$, additional
$K$-invariants appear).

**Hecke eigenform identification.** The Mellin transform of $\chi(\Phi(E); q)$
= $\prod_{n \geq 1} (1-q^n)^{-2} \cdot \theta_{L_E}(q)$ where $\theta_{L_E}$ is the lattice
theta. For $L_E$ of signature $(1,1)$ and indefinite, $\theta_{L_E}$ is a non-holomorphic
Maass-Eisenstein-type object â€” NOT a cusp form, hence not a Hecke eigenform in the
Shimura-Taniyama sense. The cusp form associated to $E$ is $f_E$, with $L(f_E,s) = L(E,s)$
(modularity theorem); $f_E \ne \theta_{L_E}$ in general. So:

**Honest claim (proved).** $\Phi(E)$ carries a $\mathrm{Gal}$-equivariant structure inherited
from $T_\ell(E)$. Its lattice theta is NOT the modular form $f_E$; instead, $f_E$ enters via
the bar-spectral level of $\Phi(E)$, not at the VOA character level.

**Honest claim (conjectural).** The bar complex $B(\Phi(E))$ at weight-$1$ decomposes as
$T_\ell(E) \oplus T_\ell(E)(-1)$ as $\mathrm{Gal}$-module, and the Deligne representation
of the Hecke eigenform $f_E$ is recovered as $T_\ell(E)^\vee(1) \cong T_\ell(E)$
(Weil pairing + modularity). This bar-level identification with the modular-form Galois rep
is the $d=1$ restriction of the Vol III Deligne-pullback construction. Work through cases:
non-CM $E$ gives the generic case (Deligne attaches irreducible 2d rep to $f_E$); CM $E$
factors as Hecke characters of the CM field. This is straightforward once $f_E \leftrightarrow \rho_{E,\ell}$
is taken as classical modularity. Programme contribution: it organises this under
$\Phi$, no new content.

## 2. $L(E,s)$ via the $\ell$-adic bar complex

### 2.1 Construction

For good primes $p \nmid N\ell$, $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ acts on $V_\ell(E) = T_\ell(E) \otimes \mathbb{Q}_\ell$
with characteristic polynomial $1 - a_p X + p X^2$, $a_p = p + 1 - \#E(\mathbb{F}_p)$,
$|a_p| \leq 2\sqrt{p}$ (Hasse-Weil). Apply $\Phi$ at mod-$p$ reduction: $E_p := E \times_{\mathbb{Z}} \mathbb{F}_p$
is an elliptic curve over $\mathbb{F}_p$ (smooth at good $p$); $\Phi(E_p)$ is the
rank-$2$ lattice VOA over $\mathbb{F}_p$ with its own Galois action, and Frobenius acts on
the weight-$1$ piece of $B(\Phi(E_p)) \otimes \overline{\mathbb{F}_p}$ with eigenvalues
$\alpha_p, \beta_p$ where $\alpha_p \beta_p = p$, $\alpha_p + \beta_p = a_p$.

**$\ell$-adic bar local L-factor.**
$L_p^{\mathrm{bar}}(\Phi(E), s) := \det(1 - \mathrm{Frob}_p p^{-s} \mid B_1(\Phi(E))^{\mathrm{wt-1 rep}}_\ell)$.
Since the weight-$1$ bar restricts to $T_\ell(E)$ (step 1.2 construction at the bar-spectral
level), this is $(1 - a_p p^{-s} + p^{1-2s})^{-1}$ â€” identical to the classical $L_p(E,s)$.

**Global L-function.** $L^{\mathrm{bar}}(\Phi(E), s) := \prod_p L_p^{\mathrm{bar}}(\Phi(E), s)$.
At good $p$: $L_p^{\mathrm{bar}} = L_p(E,s)$. At bad $p \mid N$: $\Phi(E_p)$ degenerates
(multiplicative or additive reduction); the local factor depends on the NÃ©ron component
group. For multiplicative: $L_p^{\mathrm{bar}}(\Phi(E),s) = (1 - a_p p^{-s})^{-1}$
with $a_p \in \{\pm 1\}$ (split vs nonsplit), matching $L_p(E,s)$. For additive:
$L_p^{\mathrm{bar}} = 1$, matching.

**Therefore $L^{\mathrm{bar}}(\Phi(E), s) = L(E,s)$** as formal Dirichlet series (and hence
as analytic function by modularity). This is proved, not conjectural: every local factor
matches at every prime.

### 2.2 What this contributes

The identification $L^{\mathrm{bar}}(\Phi(E), s) = L(E, s)$ is a theorem but NOT a new one.
It repackages the classical definition in the programme's language. No new property
of $L(E,s)$ is derived; in particular:

- **Functional equation $s \leftrightarrow 2-s$.** $L(E,s)$ has a functional equation
  $\Lambda(E,s) = w_E \Lambda(E, 2-s)$, $w_E = \pm 1$. The bar framework does not derive
  this independently; modularity (Wiles et al.) supplies it via the cusp form $f_E$.
  The shadow L-function $L^{\mathrm{sh}}$ of Vol III lacks functional equation
  (cf. RH motivic note `rem:contrast-with-riemann`); $L^{\mathrm{bar}}(\Phi(E),s)$ coincides
  with $L(E,s)$ as a Dirichlet series, so it inherits the functional equation from
  modularity, but the bar construction itself does not produce the functional equation
  as output.

- **Analytic behavior at $s=1$.** $\mathrm{ord}_{s=1} L(E,s)$ is the analytic rank
  $r_{\text{an}}(E)$. BSD says $r_{\text{an}}(E) = \mathrm{rank}\, E(\mathbb{Q})$.
  The bar framework does not access $r_{\text{an}}(E)$ independently of
  $L(E,s)$'s analytic properties. Shadow L-function obstruction `rem:structural-obstruction`
  transports: algebraic constraints on real-line spectral coefficients cannot reach
  zero-structure at $s=1$ without analytic continuation, and the bar construction
  is a formal Dirichlet series that inherits analytic structure only through modularity.

**Verdict on item (2).** The programme's $L^{\mathrm{bar}}(\Phi(E),s) = L(E,s)$ is a
reformulation, not a derivation. This is the same pattern as RH Weil-Grothendieck lift
Route A/C: the chiral-algebra framework gives a clean packaging of an already-classical
object, with the analytic content supplied externally.

## 3. Kolyvagin-Heegner via the shadow tower

### 3.1 The Heegner point construction

For $E/\mathbb{Q}$ with conductor $N$ and $K$ an imaginary quadratic field with
Heegner hypothesis (every prime $p \mid N$ splits in $K$), Heegner points are CM-points
on the modular curve $X_0(N)$ mapped to $E$ via $X_0(N) \to E_f$ (modular parametrisation).
Gross-Zagier: $L'(E/K,1) = \langle y_K, y_K \rangle \cdot (\text{period})$, where $y_K \in E(K)$
is the Heegner point's image and $\langle,\rangle$ is the NÃ©ron-Tate height.
Kolyvagin: if $y_K \ne 0$ in $E(K) \otimes \mathbb{Q}$, then $\mathrm{rank}\, E(K) = 1$
and Sha$(E/K)$ is finite. Combined: BSD-rank for $E/\mathbb{Q}$ when $r_{\text{an}} \leq 1$.

The key mechanisms:
(a) Heegner points $y_K \in E(K)$ from CM-points on $X_0(N)$.
(b) Euler system of Kolyvagin classes $c_n \in H^1(K, E[\ell^k])$ annihilated by Galois
    derivatives, giving Selmer group control.
(c) Gross-Zagier formula matching $L'(E/K,1)$ to Heegner height.

### 3.2 Does the shadow tower give an internal derivation?

**Shadow tower candidate input.** The shadow coefficients of $\Phi(E)$ are
$S_r(\Phi(E))$. Since $\Phi(E)$ is a rank-$2$ lattice VOA (class G), its shadow tower
is $S_2 = \kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}(\Phi(E)) = 1$ (from the anomaly $\delta\kappa = h^{1,0} = 1$
at $d=1$), $S_3 = 0$ (no triple OPE for an abelian lattice VOA; shadow class G
has $S_r = 0$ for $r \geq 3$ on formal characters â€” this is the class-G-ness criterion,
from `landscape_census.tex` and AP-CY12). So the shadow tower of $\Phi(E)$ is
$(S_2, 0, 0, \ldots) = (1, 0, 0, \ldots)$: trivial beyond $S_2$. No depth. No class-M-ness.

**Separating-degeneration at K3 Ã— E.** AP157 (Vol II CLAUDE.md): separating degeneration
of genus-$2$ contains ZERO genuinely genus-$2$ information (everything determined by
genus-$0$ S-matrix + genus-$1$ R-matrix eigenvalues); non-separating carries new data.
For $X = K3 \times E$ the CY3 with $\Phi_3(X) = A_{K3 \times E}$ (class M via BKM
construction), the separating degenerations correspond to boundary strata where
$X$ limits to $K3 \sqcup E$ at a node; the separated $E$-factor contributes the
rank-$2$ lattice VOA $\Phi(E)$, trivial in the shadow sense. A "Heegner-style
divisor" would be the CM-locus in the moduli space $M_{K3 \times E}$ where $E$ has CM by
some $K$; on such loci, $\Phi(E)_{\text{CM}}$ has extra Hecke structure.

**Where Heegner-shadow connection fails.** Kolyvagin's Euler system lives in Galois
cohomology $H^1(K, E[\ell^k])$, not in the VOA or its bar complex. The Galois action on
$\Phi(E)$ restricts to the action on $T_\ell(E)$, which is what Kolyvagin uses â€” but the
shadow tower is defined at the VOA/OPE level, which is $\mathrm{Gal}$-invariant (built
from the quadratic form on $L_E$, which is $\mathrm{Gal}$-fixed by construction). Kolyvagin
classes are $\mathrm{Gal}$-twisted, so they live in a different place:
$H^1(\mathrm{Gal}, T_\ell(E))$ is cohomology of the Galois group; the shadow tower is
a Galois-equivariant Taylor expansion of the OPE, and its coefficients are absolute
(Galois-invariant rationals). The two operations don't commute in a way that would let
shadow-tower data detect Kolyvagin classes.

**Conceptual analogy (not derivation).** The shadow tower at $\Phi(K3 \times E)_{\text{class M}}$
encodes A_âˆž operations $m_k$ for $k \geq 3$; the BKM denominator formula $\Delta_5 \to \Phi_{10}$
has Fourier-Jacobi decomposition where the leading coefficient $\phi_1 = \phi_{10,1} = \eta^{18}\vartheta_1^2$
fixes everything via Hecke/Maass relations (from `k3e_bkm_chapter.tex` L654). This is
structurally parallel to how Heegner points generate Euler systems: one
leading datum propagates via a Hecke-type action. But the Hecke action on $\phi_{10,1}$
acts on Fourier-Jacobi coefficients of Siegel modular forms, not on the $L(E,s)$ side.
No non-tautological bridge to Kolyvagin.

**Verdict on item (3).** Negative. The shadow tower of $\Phi(E)$ is trivial (class G,
trivial beyond $S_2$); CM-loci of $K3 \times E$ give Hecke-enhanced versions of $\Phi(E)$
that mirror the structure of Heegner points, but Kolyvagin's Euler system lives in Galois
cohomology and the shadow tower is Galois-invariant. There is no mechanism in the bar
construction to produce Euler classes in $H^1(K, E[\ell^k])$ from shadow data. The
programme re-packages the algebraic side of modularity but does not generate the
$\mathrm{Gal}$-cohomology machine that powers Kolyvagin-Gross-Zagier.

## 4. What the programme genuinely contributes, does not

**Contributes:**
- Explicit $\Phi(E) = V_{L_E}$ rank-$2$ lattice VOA with $\mathrm{Gal}$-equivariant
  structure inherited from $T_\ell(E)$.
- Identification $L^{\mathrm{bar}}(\Phi(E), s) = L(E, s)$ as Dirichlet series with
  matching local factors at every prime â€” a clean restatement of modularity in
  bar-cobar language.
- Conceptual parallel between BKM Fourier-Jacobi propagation and Heegner Euler-system
  propagation, both of which propagate from a leading datum by a Hecke-type action
  (different Heckes â€” Siegel/Jacobi vs. GL_2).

**Does not contribute:**
- No derivation of the $L(E,s)$ functional equation independent of modularity. Shadow
  tower lacks functional equation; $L^{\mathrm{bar}}(\Phi(E),s)$ inherits it via
  modularity.
- No internal derivation of analytic rank $r_{\text{an}}(E) = \mathrm{ord}_{s=1} L(E,s)$.
  Bar framework does not access $L$-behavior at $s=1$.
- No mechanism to derive Kolyvagin's Euler system. Shadow tower is $\mathrm{Gal}$-invariant;
  Kolyvagin classes are $\mathrm{Gal}$-twisted. Category mismatch.
- No derivation of Mordell-Weil rank from VOA data. $\mathrm{rank}\, E(\mathbb{Q})$ is
  an arithmetic invariant of the NÃ©ron-Severi-lift; $\Phi(E)$ sees only
  $H^1(E)$ with its cup product, which is topologically rigid and tells us
  nothing about $E(\mathbb{Q})$.

## 5. Verdict

BSD is reformulation-not-solution as with Hodge. The programme's CY-A_2-restricted-to-$d=1$
construction produces a rank-$2$ lattice VOA $\Phi(E) = V_{L_E}$, $L^{\mathrm{bar}}(\Phi(E),s)
= L(E,s)$ matches the classical $L$-function exactly, and the $\ell$-adic derived chiral
centre inherits the Galois representation $\rho_{E,\ell}$. But:

1. The L-function identification is a restatement; modularity supplies the analytic
   content externally.
2. The Kolyvagin Euler system lives in Galois cohomology, which is orthogonal to
   the shadow tower's algebraic domain. No mechanism connects.
3. Mordell-Weil rank is an arithmetic invariant absent from the VOA data. $\Phi(E)$
   sees $H^1(E)$ with cup product â€” a topological invariant â€” not $E(\mathbb{Q})$.

The three irreducible obstructions here match exactly those in the RH Weil-Grothendieck
note: (a) inherited-not-derived analytic content; (b) $\mathrm{Gal}$-invariant vs
$\mathrm{Gal}$-twisted mismatch between shadow tower and Euler systems; (c) topological
vs arithmetic data gap. The programme's elegant packaging does not circumvent them.

**Bottom line.** No new progress on BSD. The chiral-algebra framework restates
$L(E,s)$ via the bar complex, organises modularity under $\Phi$, and offers
a structural parallel (BKM Fourier-Jacobi â†” Heegner Euler system) without a functional
bridge. Rank $\geq 2$ BSD remains open by the same obstructions that stop motivic
and adelic approaches; the programme articulates these obstructions in its native
language but does not resolve them. Analogy with RH attacks from this programme:
the chiral framework reveals the obstruction's shape without dissolving it.

The honest positive statement: the programme gives a "CY-A_1 enhancement" of
modularity â€” a natural $\mathrm{Gal}$-equivariant lattice VOA sitting above every elliptic
curve, whose bar-level $L$-function equals $L(E,s)$. This is a conceptual cleanup, worthwhile
in its own right, but it is a restatement not a proof.
