# The Programme: A Platonic Synthesis

## §1. The Programme at a Glance

Three volumes, one object. Let $X$ be a smooth curve over a field of characteristic zero. The programme studies chiral algebras on $X$ through the prism of Koszul duality, and finds that every such object admits three incarnations: an algebraic face (the bar–cobar primitive of Volume I), a physical face (the three-dimensional holomorphic–topological gauge theory of Volume II), and an arithmetic–geometric face (the Calabi–Yau shadow of Volume III). These are not analogies; they are three specializations of a single construction, related by functors whose domains and codomains one can write down precisely.

The backbone is the Platonic ladder $E_1 \to E_2 \to E_3$, built rung-by-rung via Dunn additivity. The first rung lives on a chiral algebra $A$ (Volume I); the second on its derived chiral centre $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A) = C^\bullet_{\mathrm{ch}}(A,A)$ (Volume II); the third emerges whenever $A$ carries a conformal vector at non-critical level, via the Sugawara identity $T(z) = [Q_{\mathrm{tot}}, G(z)]$, which renders the transverse real direction $Q$-exact and collapses the two colours of the swiss-cheese operad $\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$. Higher-spin stress towers extend the ladder to $E_\infty$. The Calabi–Yau functor of Volume III supplies an independent construction of the same ladder, indexed by the Calabi–Yau dimension.

## §2. Seven Master Theorems

**Theorem A$^{\infty,2}$ (bar–cobar properad equivalence).** In the Francis–Gaitsgory factorization ambient — the $(\infty,2)$-category of factorization algebras on $\mathrm{Ran}(X)$ — the bar and cobar functors constitute an adjoint equivalence on the conilpotent–complete locus, which lifts to factorization *properads* (multi-input/multi-output operations) at every genus. The descent from ordered to $\Sigma_n$-coinvariant bars is governed by the $R$-matrix twist $B^\Sigma_n(A) \simeq (B^{\mathrm{ord}}_n(A))^{\Sigma_n\text{-coinv},\,L_R}$, with Yang–Baxter supplying coherence on codimension-two strata.

**Modular Climax Pentagon.** The swiss-cheese operad $\mathrm{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ admits five equivalent presentations, each proved independently: operadic (generators and relations from the Fulton–MacPherson stratification), Koszul-dual (quadratic–linear duality with closed sector $E_2^{!} \simeq E_2^{\mathrm{shifted}}$), factorization (derived chiral centre of the boundary algebra), BV/BRST (quantum master equation as open–closed Maurer–Cartan), and convolution ($L_\infty$ presentation via the bar cooperad). The pentagon rigidifies the two-colour structure at every corner.

**Universal $\kappa$ (Arakelov anomaly class).** There exists a universal Arakelov anomaly class $\kappa_{\mathrm{univ}} \in H^2(\overline{\mathcal{M}}^{\mathrm{fam}}_{g,n};\,\mathbf{Q})$ on a classifying stack parametrising chiral algebras, whose pullback along the map that selects a family recovers every per-family curvature: $\kappa(\mathrm{Vir}_c) = c/2$, $\kappa(\mathrm{Heis}_k) = k$, $\kappa(V_k(\mathfrak{g})) = \dim(\mathfrak{g})(k+h^\vee)/(2h^\vee)$, $\kappa(W_N) = c(H_N - 1)$. A single tensorial curvature form $\kappa_{\mathrm{univ}} \otimes \omega_g$ on $\overline{\mathcal{M}}_{g,n}$ controls the full genus tower.

**Infinite Fingerprint.** The invariant $\varphi(A) = (p_{\max}, r_{\max}, \chi_{\mathrm{VOA}}, n_{\mathrm{strong}}, \mathrm{coset})$ is a complete classifier of the Koszul bar-structure of $A$. The coarse projection onto $r_{\max}$ yields the four classes G (formal), L (linear), C (cubic), M ($\infty$-depth) on the locus $\kappa \neq 0$; the degenerate stratum $\kappa = 0$ contributes a fifth canonical class FF (Feigin–Frenkel), where $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet$ presents the centre of the critical enveloping algebra as the algebra of functions on opers.

**Universal Holography.** For every chiral algebra $A$ carrying a conformal vector at non-critical level, there exists a canonical three-dimensional holomorphic–topological gauge theory $T_A$ on $X \times \mathbf{R}$ whose boundary observables recover $A$ as $E_1$-chiral algebra and whose bulk observables realise $Z^{\mathrm{der}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(A)$ as $E_3$-topological factorization algebra. The Drinfeld–Sokolov–Hochschild bridge $\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet(W_k(\mathfrak{g})) \simeq H^\bullet_{\mathrm{DS}}(\mathrm{ChirHoch}^\bullet(V_k(\mathfrak{g})))$ extends the correspondence to class M.

**Seven Faces GRT Torsor.** The faces of $r(z)$ form a torsor over the Drinfeld–Grothendieck–Teichmüller group $\mathrm{GRT}_1(\mathbf{Q})$. The bar-intrinsic hub $F_1$ is the identity coset; the Drinfeld-associator-dependent faces $F_2$ through $F_7$ (DNP twist, Knizhnik–Zamolodchikov, Gaiotto–Zeng, Yangian, Gaudin, top $A_\infty$) are $\mathbf{Q}$-rational orbit representatives; $F_8$ (Brown motivic, with multi-zeta coefficients) and $F_9$ (Willwacher operadic, via $H^0(\mathrm{GC}_2) = \mathfrak{grt}_1$) complete the enumeration. The torsor action is Tamarkin $\Phi$-transport.

**Calabi–Yau functor $\Phi_d$.** There is a functor $\Phi_d : \mathcal{D}^b(\mathrm{Coh}(X_d)) \to E_{\max(1,\,3-d)}\text{-ChirAlg}$ from the bounded derived category of coherent sheaves on a Calabi–Yau $d$-fold to the appropriate level of the $E_n$-chiral hierarchy. At $d=1$ the output is $E_\infty$ (classical Koszul regime); at $d=2$ it is $E_2$ (K3 abelian Yangian, Mukai lattice); at $d \geq 3$ it is $E_1$ (native chiral quantum group), with $E_2$-braiding supplied by the Drinfeld centre $Z(\mathrm{Rep}^{E_1}(\Phi_d(X)))$ rather than by the algebra itself.

## §3. Cross-Volume Bridges

**Volume I $\leftrightarrow$ Volume II.** The $E_\infty$/$E_1$ dichotomy of the locality hierarchy specialises the universal bar–cobar primitive of Volume I to the physical settings of Volume II. Volume I's five theorems (A, B, C, D, H) are modular invariants surviving $\Sigma_n$-coinvariance; Volume II's five corresponding theorems restore the ordered structure and lift them to the swiss-cheese operad. On the affine lineage, the bar-intrinsic $r$-matrix of Volume I is the classical shadow of the spectral $R$-matrix of Volume II; Kac–Moody, Virasoro, W-algebras, Heisenberg, and lattice vertex algebras are all $E_\infty$-chiral with non-trivial OPE poles (the locality hierarchy is about provenance, not poles).

**Volume II $\leftrightarrow$ Volume III.** The functor $\Phi_3 : \mathcal{D}^b(\mathrm{Coh}(X_3)) \to E_1\text{-ChirAlg}$ produces native $E_1$-chiral algebras whose derived chiral centre, by Universal Holography, carries an $E_3$-topological structure realised by six-dimensional holomorphic Chern–Simons. The spectral coproduct $\Delta_z$ of Volume III's $E_1$-chiral bialgebra is the Drinfeld coproduct computed at the Volume II bar–cobar level; the half-braiding $\sigma_A(z)$ realises the $R$-matrix as a natural transformation in the Drinfeld centre, not on the algebra itself.

**Volume I $\leftrightarrow$ Volume III.** The universal Arakelov class $\kappa_{\mathrm{univ}}$ of Volume I pulls back under the Calabi–Yau functor to the Borcherds–Kac–Moody class $\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}} = c_N(0)/2$ of Volume III, governed by Borcherds' weight theorem. The coincidence $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}} = \chi(\mathcal{O}_X)$ at $d=2$ (K3 case) is a specialisation; at odd $d$, $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}$ and $\chi(\mathcal{O}_X)$ diverge, and the universal class resolves the dimension-stratified formula.

## §4. Inner Music

Three voices, one chord. Volume I sounds the tonic: the bar–cobar primitive, the modular invariants, the universal anomaly. Volume II sounds the dominant: the derived centre, the holographic ascent, the $E_3$-topological climax. Volume III sounds the subdominant: the Calabi–Yau descent, the arithmetic shadow, the Borcherds algebra. The tonic states the theme; the dominant raises tension through holography; the subdominant resolves it through the Calabi–Yau functor, whose output is indexed by $d$ and whose three values of $d$ close the progression back to the tonic. The operadic spiral $\cdots \to E_{n+1} \to E_n \to \cdots \to E_1 \to E_2 \to \cdots \to E_{n+1} \to \cdots$ is the modulation.

## §5. Residual Frontier

One irreducible open problem survives the synthesis: the periodic curved differential-graded Koszul duality at admissible level (conj:periodic-cdg). Its resolution would close the Kazhdan–Lusztig bar–cobar adjunction globally at admissible level, extend Theorem H to a uniform statement across all integer and admissible levels, and unify the Feigin–Frenkel stratum FF with the $\kappa \neq 0$ locus. Every other apparent gap in the three volumes reduces, under the seven master theorems, to technical work already specified.
