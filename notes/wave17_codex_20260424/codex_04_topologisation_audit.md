# Codex 04 -- Topologisation Audit (SC^{ch,top} + Sugawara = E_3-top)

## Verdict
CONDITIONALLY SOUND. The programme is coherent at the cohomological level and for affine Kac-Moody class `L`, but the strongest global reading is false: no theorem proves `SC^{ch,top}` itself as a bicoloured operad is `E_3`, and `class M` original-complex claims are explicit OPEN points.

## Attack log

1. Claim 1. `SC^{ch,top}` is NOT `E_3` as bicoloured.
- Attack 1 (primary-source): the chain-level platonic note states in explicit scope text that `SC^{ch,top}` is not an `E_3` operad and is only discussed via topologisation of associated real factorisation data. `[topologization_chain_level_platonic.tex:45-60](file:///Users/raeez/chiral-bar-cobar/chapters/theory/topologization_chain_level_platonic.tex#L45)`
- Attack 2 (cross-reference): theorem proof in Vol I flags the same obstruction directly: `Dunn additivity does not apply to bicoloured operads` and avoids applying it to `SC^{ch,top}` as-is. `[en_koszul_duality.tex:3174](file:///Users/raeez/chiral-bar-cobar/chapters/theory/en_koszul_duality.tex#L3174)`; Vol II memory confirms this repeatedly in crown architecture for bicoloured structures. `[platonic_ideal_reconstituted_2026_04_17.md:551-557](file:///Users/raeez/.claude/projects/-Users-raeez-chiral-bar-cobar/memory/platonic_ideal_reconstituted_2026_04_17.md#L551)`

The chapter does not claim equality of bicoloured operads; it claims that after forgetting to the real locus and proving null-homotopy of holomorphic translation, one gets `E_3`-topological factorization structure in the locally constant factorization category. `[en_koszul_duality.tex:3110-3148, 3160-3172](file:///Users/raeez/chiral-bar-cobar/chapters/theory/en_koszul_duality.tex#L3110)`

## Dunn additivity verdict
`SC^{ch,top}` is bicoloured and does not meet the single-coloured hypotheses required for standard Dunn additivity. The chapter accepts this as explicit and works around it. `[en_koszul_duality.tex:3174](file:///Users/raeez/chiral-bar-cobar/chapters/theory/en_koszul_duality.tex#L3174)`
- Primary-source status requested: `Dunn, A. (1988)` is the classical additivity theorem for operads but is for suitable single-colour symmetric monoidal structures, not bicoloured as used here. `Lurie, Higher Algebra 5.1.2` is the ambient symmetric monoidal operadic framework; the proof text also cites `Lurie 5.4.5.9` for local-constancy recognition. `[en_koszul_duality.tex:3168-3174](file:///Users/raeez/chiral-bar-cobar/chapters/theory/en_koszul_duality.tex#L3168)`
- Verdict: bicoloured Dunn additivity is at best partial or unavailable in the chapter's explicit framework; any argument that reads `SC^{ch,top}` itself through Dunn is invalid.
