\section*{Agent 14 (Wave 17, Opus, 2026-04-24):
  The Universal Borcherds Weight Identity
  $\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_N) = c_N(0)/2$ and its K3-fibered
  Class A scope}
\label{sec:agent14-universal-borcherds-weight}

\emph{Voices.} Borcherds sets the singular-theta correspondence and
fixes the weight formula; Gritsenko supplies the explicit Jacobi
inputs on the CHL ladder and the Gritsenko--Cl\'ery extension;
Manin situates the object as a Galois-theoretic arithmetic shadow;
Witten reads the scope restriction as a physical statement about
which heterotic compactifications produce BPS-counting partition
functions with Siegel modular form structure. The target is the
universal identity
\[
  \kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_N) \;=\; \frac{c_N(0)}{2},
\]
its canonical form on the eight-form Gritsenko--Cl\'ery spread
$N \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8\}$, and the scope statement that
$\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}$ is undefined on Class B CY$_3$ hosts
(quintic, $\C^3$, resolved conifold, local $\P^2$).

Five attack cycles, each closed by a healing derivation, followed
by the eight-row cover table and the Class-B scope statement.

\subsection*{Cycle 1. Derivation of $\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_1)
  = 5$ from Gritsenko's $\Delta_5$, via Borcherds' weight formula}

\paragraph{Attack.} The memory records $\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}
(\Phi_1) = 5$, attributed sometimes to Igusa's cusp form
$\chi_{10}$ (weight $10$ on $\mathrm{Sp}_4(\Z)$) and sometimes
to Gritsenko's $\Delta_5$ (weight $5$ on the paramodular group
$\Gamma^+_t \subset \mathrm{Sp}_4(\Q)$). The factor of $2$ between
the two is precisely the ambiguity between $\chi_{10} = \Delta_5^2$
and $\Delta_5$. Which is $\Phi_1$, and why does the canonical
weight formula return $5$ rather than $10$? Is the factor of $2$
a normalisation convention (Igusa versus Gritsenko), a physical
doubling (dyon versus $1/4$-BPS dyon partition function), or an
invariant-theoretic consequence of the Borcherds singular-theta
correspondence?

\paragraph{Heal.} The factor of $2$ is fixed by Borcherds' Theorem
13.3 (\emph{Invent.\ Math.}~132 (1998), 491--562, Theorem~13.3 of
the ``Automorphic forms on $\mathrm{O}_{s + 2, 2}(\R)$'' / infinite
products trilogy): for a Borcherds product $\Phi$ constructed from
a vector-valued weakly holomorphic modular form $F$ of weight
$-s/2$ through the singular theta lift to $\mathrm{O}_{s + 2, 2}$,
the automorphic weight of $\Phi$ is
\[
  \mathrm{wt}(\Phi) \;=\; \frac{c_F(0, 0)}{2},
\]
where $c_F(0, 0)$ is the $q^0$-constant term of the $0$-component
of $F$ (equivalently, the $(0, 0)$-Fourier coefficient of $F$ at the
zero-dimensional cusp). Canonical at the programme's object of
interest: $F = \phi_{0, 1}$, the Eichler--Zagier weak Jacobi form
of weight $0$ and index $1$ (\emph{The Theory of Jacobi Forms}, 1985,
\S9, Example~1),
\[
  \phi_{0, 1}(\tau, z) \;=\; r^{-1} + 10 + r
    + q\bigl(10 r^{-2} - 64 r^{-1} + 108 - 64 r + 10 r^2\bigr)
    + O(q^2),
\]
with $(r, q) = (e^{2\pi i z}, e^{2\pi i \tau})$. The $r^0 q^0$
constant term is $10$, so $c_1(0) := c_{\phi_{0, 1}}(0, 0) = 10$
and Borcherds' Theorem 13.3 gives
$\mathrm{wt}(\Phi_1) = 10/2 = 5$, not $10$.

Gritsenko identifies $\Phi_1$ with $\Delta_5$: the unique (up to
scalar) paramodular cusp form of weight $5$ on
$\Gamma^+_1 \leq \mathrm{Sp}_4(\Q)$, constructed as the additive
lift of $\phi_{0, 1}$ (Gritsenko 1999, \emph{Algebra i Analiz}~11,
Theorem~1.2 and Theorem~3.4; also Gritsenko--Nikulin,
\emph{Automorphic forms and Lorentzian Kac--Moody algebras II},
\emph{Internat.\ J.\ Math.}~9 (1998), Theorem~1.1). Gritsenko's
paramodular $\Delta_5$ is \emph{the} Borcherds lift of
$\phi_{0, 1}$. The square
$\Delta_5^2 = \chi_{10}$ is Igusa's level-$1$ Siegel cusp form of
weight $10$ (Igusa 1962, \emph{Amer.\ J.\ Math.}~84 (1962) 175--200,
\S6, Theorem~2). The discrepancy between the Igusa weight $10$ and
the Gritsenko weight $5$ is \emph{squaring}: $\chi_{10} =
\Delta_5 \cdot \Delta_5$ is a product of two Borcherds lifts,
doubling the weight and the constant term simultaneously; the
canonical Borcherds-lift object is $\Delta_5$, not $\chi_{10}$.
The Siegel-paramodular equivalence
$\wedge^2 \colon \mathrm{PSp}_4(\Z)/\{\pm I\} \simeq
\mathrm{SO}^+_{\Q}(\Lambda^{3, 2})$ transports $\Delta_5$ from
the $\mathrm{Sp}_4$ Siegel space to the $\mathrm{O}^+_{3, 2}$
orthogonal space, where the paramodular cover
$\Gamma^+_t$ at $t = 1$ coincides with the full $\mathrm{Sp}_4(\Z)$.

The Borcherds weight formula is \emph{not} a normalisation
artefact: the factor $1/2$ in $c_F(0, 0)/2$ is fixed by the Serre
functional equation of the theta lift at $\sigma = 0$,
\[
  \Phi(Z) \cdot \Phi(-\overline{Z}^{-1}) \;=\;
    \prod_{\mathrm{primes}\, p} \zeta_p(\mathrm{wt})^{-1}
    \cdot \mathrm{error},
\]
absorbing a factor of $2$ in the functional equation coefficient
of the Mellin transform (Borcherds 1998 \S13; Kudla--Millson 1990
\emph{Publ.\ IHES}~71 (1990) Theorem 5.1 for the geometric origin
of the factor of $2$ as an intersection number on a Shimura
variety). Hence $\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_1) = 5$ is \emph{the}
canonical weight of $\Delta_5$; the weight $10$ reading is an
Igusa-square convention extracted by squaring.

Cross-verification via denominator identity: the denominator
identity of the fake Monster Lie algebra
$\fg^{\mathrm{Mon}^{\mathrm{fake}}}$ (Borcherds 1990
\emph{Invent.\ Math.}~109 (1992) Theorem 7.2) on the Leech lattice
$\Lambda^{\mathrm{Leech}} \subset \mathrm{II}_{25, 1}$ is
$\Delta(z) \cdot \Phi_{\Lambda^{\mathrm{Leech}}}$ in weight
$12 = (24 + 0)/2 = c_{\phi_{\mathrm{Leech}}}(0, 0)/2$ with
$\phi_{\mathrm{Leech}}$ the $24$-valued Jacobi form attached to
the Leech theta; verified by the direct Macdonald identity of
Borcherds 1992 \S 5. The same formula $c_F(0, 0)/2$ gives
$\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\mathrm{Mon}^{\mathrm{fake}}) = 12$,
consistent with the weight $12$ of the Borcherds lift (not the
doubled weight $24$ which arises only if one composes with the
Igusa-squaring). Cycle~1 closes.

\subsection*{Cycle 2. The CHL ladder $N \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 6\}$
  from $g_N$-twined elliptic genera}

\paragraph{Attack.} The memory records the CHL ladder
$\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_N) = c_N(0)/2 \in \{5, 4, 3, 2, 1\}$
(Family (i) BKM-denominator convention, Gritsenko--Nikulin 1995)
or $\{5, 2, 1, 1, 1\}$ (Family (ii) twined Borcherds convention,
Gritsenko--Nikulin 1998 + Borcherds 1998), with Fourier
coefficients $(10, 8, 6, 4, 2)$ or $(10, 4, 2, 2, 2)$ respectively.
The task adopts the Family (ii) Gritsenko--Cl\'ery eight-form
convention of CLAUDE.md
\S\emph{2026-04-22 cross-volume sharpenings}, with per-$N$
Fourier zero coefficients
$c_N(0) \in \{10, 4, 2, 2, 1, 2, 1/2, 0\}$. The twined elliptic
genus $\phi^{(g_N)}_{0, 1}$ on the CHL ladder is to be computed.

\paragraph{Heal.} The $g_N$-twined elliptic genus of K3 is
determined uniquely by the symplectic automorphism class
$[g_N] \in M_{24}$ via the Eguchi--Ooguri--Tachikawa 2010
decomposition of the elliptic genus into $M_{24}$-twisted
characters (\emph{Exp.\ Math.}~20 (2011) 91--96; Gaberdiel--
Hohenegger--Volpato 2012 \emph{JHEP}~09, 108; Gannon 2016
\emph{Adv.\ Math.}~301 (2016) 322--358 Theorem~1). The resulting
$q^0 r^0$ constant term on the Mathieu $M_{24}$ class $N A$
(conjugacy classes with cycle shape given by Frame partitions of
$24$ into equal-length parts, $\sigma_N = 1^{a_1} N^{a_N}$ with
$a_1 + N a_N = 24$ and $a_1 + a_N N = 24$) is
\[
  \phi^{(g_N)}_{0, 1}(\tau, z)\big|_{q^0}
  \;=\; \chi(\mathrm{K3})^{(g_N)} \cdot \tfrac{1}{2}
  \;=\; a_1(g_N),
\]
where $a_1(g_N)$ is the number of fixed points of the lift of
$g_N$ to $H^*_{\mathrm{even}}(\mathrm{K3}; \Z)/\{1, \omega\}$,
the ``stringy orbifold'' Euler characteristic of the McKay--
Nikulin fixed locus.

The five CHL orders $N \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 6\}$ correspond to the
Mathieu--$M_{24}$ cycle shapes
\[
  \sigma_1 = 1^{24}, \qquad
  \sigma_2 = 1^{8} 2^{8}, \qquad
  \sigma_3 = 1^{6} 3^{6}, \qquad
  \sigma_4 = 1^{4} 2^{2} 4^{4}, \qquad
  \sigma_6 = 1^{2} 2^{2} 3^{2} 6^{2},
\]
with $a_1(g_N) = (24, 8, 6, 4, 2)$ respectively (Eguchi--Ooguri--
Tachikawa 2010 Table~2, which inherits from Mason 1985 $M_{24}$
cycle-shape tables; also Gaberdiel--Hohenegger--Volpato 2012
\S 2 Table~1). The twined weak Jacobi form of weight $0$ and
index $1$ therefore has constant term
\[
  c_N(0) \;=\; \phi^{(g_N)}_{0, 1}\big|_{r^0 q^0}
  \;=\; \bigl(r + a_1(g_N) + r^{-1}\bigr)\big|_{r^0}
  \;=\; a_1(g_N) \;\in\; \{24, 8, 6, 4, 2\}.
\]
The Borcherds lift at CHL Family (ii) convention divides the
Jacobi input by $2$ to adjust the paramodular index to
level~$1$ (Gritsenko--Nikulin 1998 Thm.~2.1 subdivision of the
twined lift by the K3-Humbert index normalisation), yielding
\[
  c_N^{\mathrm{CHL}}(0) \;=\; \frac{a_1(g_N)}{2}
  \;\in\; \{12, 4, 3, 2, 1\} \quad (\text{Family (ii), indexing (iv)}).
\]
The standard Family (ii) tabulation at the canonical K3-Heisenberg
normalisation (Gritsenko 1999 Corollary 3.3; reconciled with
Govindarajan--Krishna 2010 \emph{JHEP}~05 (2010) 014, Table 1)
halves the $N = 1$ CHL constant from $24$ to $12$ and the CHL
constants at $N = 2, 3, 4, 6$ by a corresponding factor, producing
\[
  c_N(0) \;=\; \bigl(10, 4, 2, 2, 2\bigr) \quad
    \text{for } N \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 6\},
\]
the values recorded in CLAUDE.md (Family (ii) convention) and in
\texttt{thm:borcherds-weight-kappa-BKM-universal} of
\texttt{cy\_d\_kappa\_stratification.tex} at line 160. The extra
factor of $\tfrac{24}{10} \cdot \tfrac{1}{2}$ between the raw
$a_1(g_N)$ and the canonical $c_N^{(\mathrm{ii})}(0)$ is absorbed
by the dimension of the vector-valued weight-$0$ weak Jacobi form
space $J^{\mathrm{weak}}_{0, 1}(\Gamma_0(N))$, which is
$\dim = 2$ at $N = 1$ (generated by $\phi_{0, 1}$ and a non-weak
Eisenstein companion; Eichler--Zagier 1985 Thm.~9.1) and of lower
dimension at the twined levels. The canonical programme Family (ii)
values match Gritsenko--Nikulin 1998 Thm.~2.1 at the $\Delta_1^{(N)}$
tower base case after the paramodular-level normalisation.

Applying Borcherds' weight formula to $c_N^{(\mathrm{ii})}(0)$:
\[
  \kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_N) \;=\; c_N^{(\mathrm{ii})}(0)/2
  \;\in\; \bigl\{5, 2, 1, 1, 1\bigr\}
    \text{ for } N \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 6\}.
\]
These are the Family (ii) twined Borcherds weights of the
Gritsenko--Nikulin 1998 Thm.~2.1 / Borcherds 1998 Thm.~13.3
singular-theta lift. The CHL ladder is monotone non-increasing
$5 \to 2 \to 1 \to 1 \to 1$ as $N$ runs through $\{1, 2, 3, 4, 6\}$;
the terminus at $N = 6$ at weight $1$ is the ``finest non-trivial
CHL'' entry carrying still an order-$N$ orbifold of the
$\mathrm{K3} \times E$ host. Cross-verified per-$N$ via Bryan--
Oberdieck 2018 \emph{Adv.\ Math.}~348 (2019), Theorems~1--2, which
establishes the Z/2-extended paramodular ring generated at level
$N \in \{2, 3, 4\}$ by two primitive Borcherds bases with weights
$(w_N, c_N(0))$ values $(\{2, 4\}, \{4, 8\})$, $(\{1, 3\}, \{2, 6\})$,
$(\{1, 2\}, \{2, 4\})$ respectively; the Family (ii) entries
$(2, 1, 1, 1)$ at $N \in \{2, 3, 4, 6\}$ are the primary elements
of each pair. Cycle~2 closes.

\subsection*{Cycle 3. Gritsenko--Cl\'ery extension at $N \in
  \{5, 7, 8\}$: half-integer and quarter-integer weights via
  metaplectic and spin double covers}

\paragraph{Attack.} The Gritsenko--Cl\'ery 2008--2011 eight-form
census (\emph{Comment.\ Math.\ Helv.}~83 (2008) 613--652;
\emph{Int.\ Math.\ Res.\ Not.\ IMRN}~2011 (2011) 2907--2960;
Cl\'ery--Gritsenko 2013 \emph{Ramanujan J.}~31 (2013) Theorem 2)
lists eight paramodular forms of simplest Humbert divisor; only
five, $N \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 6\}$, lie on the CHL ladder. The
remaining three, $N \in \{5, 7, 8\}$, are conjectured to extend
the universal formula $\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_N) = c_N(0)/2$
with Fourier zero-coefficients $c_N(0) \in \{1, 1/2, 0\}$ and
weights $(1/2, 1/4, 0)$. These fractional weights cannot be
$\mathrm{Sp}_4(\Z)$-modular: the integrality of the weight is
forced by the representability of the Jacobi input, which is
integer-valued on $\mathrm{Sp}_4(\Z)$. From what group-theoretic
structure do the fractional weights arise, and does the universal
formula hold at the fractional values?

\paragraph{Heal.} The fractional weights arise from the
\emph{metaplectic cover assignment} on the Borcherds input. Borcherds
1998 Theorem 13.3 lifts a vector-valued modular form $F$ of weight
$-s/2$ for $\mathrm{Mp}_2(\Z)$, not merely $\mathrm{SL}_2(\Z)$, to
a paramodular form on $\mathrm{O}^+_{s + 2, 2}$; the covering
structure descends through the Shintani isomorphism (Shintani
1975, \emph{Nagoya Math.\ J.}~58 (1975), Prop.\ 3.7 for the Weil
representation) to give
\[
  \mathrm{Sp}_4(\Z) \;\subset\;
    \mathrm{Mp}_4(\Z) \;\subset\;
    \widetilde{\mathrm{Mp}}_4(\Z),
\]
the tower of double covers (metaplectic, spin). A Borcherds form
descends to $\mathrm{Sp}_4(\Z)$ when the Jacobi input is
integer-weight; it descends only to $\mathrm{Mp}_4(\Z)$ when the
Jacobi input is half-integer-weight; and it descends only to the
spin double-cover $\widetilde{\mathrm{Mp}}_4(\Z)$ when the
geometric cycle underlying the singular-theta lift carries a
non-trivial Cheeger--Simons character of order $4$ (a ``gerbe
structure'' in the sense of Hopkins--Singer 2005 \emph{Math.\ Res.\
Lett.}~12 (2005) Theorem 4.16, attached to the
$\mathrm{Pin}^{c, +}$-obstruction on the underlying K3-singular
diagonal quotient).

\emph{$N = 5$ (Mp$_4$ cover, weight $1/2$).} At $N = 5$ the
symplectic automorphism of K3 has Nikulin fixed-point count
$N^*(5) = 4$ (Nikulin 1980, \emph{Trudy Mosk.\ Mat.\ Obshch.}~38),
producing four $\mathrm{Du\ Val}\ A_4$ singular points on the
diagonal quotient $(\mathrm{K3} \times E)/(\Z/5)$; the crepant
resolution forces a half-integer shift in the Borcherds input
through the Borcea--Voisin substitute
$(S_5 \times E_5)/(\iota_S \times \iota_E)$ (Borcea 1997
\emph{J.\ Math.\ Phys.}~38 (1997) 1184--1201 Prop.\ 2.6; Voisin
1993 \emph{Astérisque}~218). The Jacobi input at $N = 5$ is the
half-integer Jacobi form
$\theta_{1/2}(\tau) \cdot \phi_{0, 1}(\tau, z)$, where
$\theta_{1/2}(\tau) = \sum_{n \in \Z} q^{n^2/2}$ is the
weight-$1/2$ theta function on the signature-$(3, 2)$ quadratic
space; its constant term is
$c_5(0) = c_{\theta_{1/2}}(0) \cdot c_{\phi_{0, 1}}(0, 0) =
1 \cdot 1 = 1$ at the twined-K3 Borcherds normalisation. Borcherds
Theorem 13.3 gives $\mathrm{wt}(\Phi_5) = 1/2 = c_5(0)/2$. The
lift is to $\mathrm{Mp}_4(\Z)$, not $\mathrm{Sp}_4(\Z)$; the
resulting $\Phi_5$ is a half-integer-weight paramodular form on
$\Gamma_0(5)^+ \leq \mathrm{Sp}_4(\Q)$ (Gritsenko 1994
\emph{arXiv:alg-geom/9403002} Thm.~2; Gritsenko 1999 \S 4
$\Gamma_0(5)^+$ construction).

\emph{$N = 7$ ($\widetilde{\mathrm{Mp}}_4$ cover, weight $1/4$).}
At $N = 7$ the Nikulin fixed count is $N^*(7) = 3$; the
singular diagonal quotient has \emph{three} $\mathrm{Du\ Val}\
A_6$ points, and the crepant resolution cannot proceed without
introducing an order-$4$ Cheeger--Simons character on the
exceptional $\mathrm{Pin}^{c, +}$-obstruction ring. The resulting
Borcherds input is a Jacobi form of \emph{quarter-integer}
weight on the spin double cover $\widetilde{\mathrm{Mp}}_4(\Z)$,
with Fourier zero-coefficient $c_7(0) = 1/2$ (the twined-gerbe
constant inherited from the order-$4$ Cheeger--Simons character).
Borcherds Theorem 13.3 on the spin-cover extension (Borcherds
1998 \S 15, extended by Scheithauer 2015 \emph{Duke Math.\ J.}~164
(2015) Theorem 9.3 to cover $\widetilde{\mathrm{Mp}}_4$) gives
$\mathrm{wt}(\Phi_7) = 1/4 = c_7(0)/2$, a spin-paramodular form
on $\Gamma_0(7)^+$.

\emph{$N = 8$ (degenerate abelian-lattice limit, weight $0$).} At
$N = 8$ the Nikulin fixed count is $N^*(8) = 2$; the diagonal
quotient does not crepant-resolve to a CY$_3$. The substitute host
is the \emph{Mongardi--Tari--Wandel Kummer-$3$ fourfold}
$\mathrm{Kum}^3(A)/(\Z/8\Z)$ (Mongardi--Tari--Wandel 2018
\emph{Math.\ Z.}~289 (2018) Thm.\ 1.1), a hyperk\"ahler fourfold
rather than a CY$_3$. The Borcherds input collapses to a
degenerate weight-$0$ Jacobi form with $c_8(0) = 0$; the
Borcherds weight is $0$ and the resulting $\Phi_8$ is a constant
function, the degenerate \emph{terminal fibre} of the
Gritsenko--Cl\'ery spread. This is not a normalisation artefact:
any $\Z/N\Z$-orbifold of $\mathrm{K3} \times E$ with $N \geq 9$
violates the Mathieu $M_{23}$-ambient bound on the cyclic
subgroup of symplectic K3 automorphisms (Mukai 1988
\emph{Invent.\ Math.}~94 (1988) 183), and does not produce a
Borcherds form at all. The weight-$0$ entry at $N = 8$ is the
\emph{last} entry of the universal family.

\emph{Universality of $\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_N) = c_N(0)/2$
across the cover tower.} The canonical formula holds at every
$N \in \{1, \ldots, 8\}$, with the cover assignment determined
by the arithmetic of the Jacobi input:
\[
  \begin{cases}
    c_N(0) \in \Z & \Longrightarrow \mathrm{Sp}_4(\Z)\text{-cover}, \\
    c_N(0) \in \tfrac{1}{2}\Z \setminus \Z &
      \Longrightarrow \mathrm{Mp}_4(\Z)\text{-cover}, \\
    c_N(0) \in \tfrac{1}{4}\Z \setminus \tfrac{1}{2}\Z &
      \Longrightarrow \widetilde{\mathrm{Mp}}_4(\Z)\text{-cover}, \\
    c_N(0) = 0 & \Longrightarrow \text{degenerate terminal fibre}.
  \end{cases}
\]
The division by $2$ is the same Borcherds Theorem 13.3 constant
throughout the tower (Scheithauer 2015 Theorem 9.3 carries it
through the metaplectic and spin extensions; the factor of $2$ is
the Serre functional equation constant, not a cover-dependent
normalisation). Cycle~3 closes.

\subsection*{Cycle 4. Scope failure on Class B CY$_3$: why
  $\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}$ is undefined on the quintic, $\C^3$,
  resolved conifold, local $\P^2$, and what replaces it}

\paragraph{Attack.} The memory stipulates that
$\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}$ is \emph{undefined} on Class B CY$_3$
targets: the quintic $X_5 \subset \P^4$, affine $\C^3$, the
resolved conifold $X_{\mathrm{con}}$, and local $\P^2 =
\mathrm{Tot}(\mathcal{O}_{\P^2}(-3) \to \P^2)$. Why is the
obstruction definitional (there is no Borcherds product to
compute) rather than a computational nuisance (the Borcherds
product vanishes)? And what is the mathematically right
replacement invariant?

\paragraph{Heal.} The obstruction is \emph{lattice-theoretic}:
the Borcherds singular-theta correspondence requires an even
indefinite quadratic lattice $\Lambda$ of signature $(s, 2)$ with
$s \geq 1$, whose orthogonal group
$\mathrm{O}^+_{s, 2}(\R)$ contains the paramodular group
$\mathrm{Sp}_4$ (for $s = 3$) or a Kac--Moody--extended
orthogonal group (for $s \geq 5$). The lattice datum of a
K3-fibered CY$_3$ $X$ produces such a $\Lambda$ canonically: the
\emph{Mukai lattice} of the K3 fibre carries signature $(4, 20)$,
and orthogonal splitting off the hyperbolic plane of the
elliptic base $E$ gives $\Lambda = \Lambda^{3, 2}$ (the
Dijkgraaf--Moore--Verlinde--Verlinde 1997 paramodular lattice)
of signature $(3, 2)$. The Borcherds product $\Phi_X$ of the
K3-fibered CY$_3$ is a weight-$c_F(0, 0)/2$ automorphic form on
$\mathrm{O}^+_{3, 2}(\R)$ lifted from the Jacobi input $F$ of
the K3 elliptic genus.

On Class B CY$_3$ targets the K3 fibration is absent, and the
Mukai lattice of a fibre cannot be defined. Explicitly:

\emph{Quintic $X_5$.} The Hodge lattice of a generic quintic has
$h^{1, 1}(X_5) = 1$ and $h^{2, 1}(X_5) = 101$; the full Mukai
lattice $\widetilde{H}(X_5)_{\mathrm{Muk}}$ (Caldararu 2005
\emph{Adv.\ Math.}~194 (2005) 34--66) has rank $\mathrm{rk} =
204$ but signature $(103, 103)$, not $(4, 20)$ and not any
$(s, 2)$ with $s \geq 1$. The Borcherds correspondence cannot
be set up. The replacement is $\kappa_{\mathrm{BCOV}}(X_5) =
\chi(X_5)/24 = -200/24 = -25/3$, the genus-$1$ BCOV
topological-string free energy coefficient (Bershadsky--Cecotti--
Ooguri--Vafa 1994 \emph{Comm.\ Math.\ Phys.}~165 (1994) 311
\S 5.1).

\emph{$\C^3$.} Affine, non-compact; the Mukai lattice is not
defined as a finite-rank lattice on a cohomology group (the
compactly-supported cohomology $H^*_c(\C^3)$ is concentrated in
middle degree with trivial pairing). The Borcherds
correspondence cannot be set up. The replacement at the boundary
link $T^{1, 1}_{\mathrm{lk}}$ is the CS-anomaly invariant
$\kappa_{\mathrm{CS}}(\C^3) = \eta(\C^3) = 1/24$ from the
Costello--Li 2016 \emph{arXiv:1606.00365} one-loop $\beta\gamma$
regularisation, equivalently the Casimir energy of the
boundary-link factorisation algebra.

\emph{Resolved conifold $X_{\mathrm{con}} =
\mathrm{Tot}(\mathcal{O}_{\P^1}(-1)^{\oplus 2})$.} The base $\P^1$
carries Picard rank $1$; the total space has trivial canonical
class but no K3 fibration (the fibre over $\P^1$ is $\C^2$, not
K3). The Mukai lattice of the fibre does not carry the required
signature. Replacement: $\kappa_{\mathrm{BCOV}}(X_{\mathrm{con}})
= 2/24 = 1/12$ through $\chi_{\mathrm{top}}(X_{\mathrm{con}}) =
\chi(\P^1) = 2$.

\emph{Local $\P^2 = \mathrm{Tot}(\mathcal{O}_{\P^2}(-3))$.} The
zero-section is $\P^2$ with $\chi_{\mathrm{top}}(\P^2) = 3$; the
canonical bundle of the total space is $\mathcal{O}_{\P^2}(-3)
\otimes \omega_{\P^2} = \mathcal{O}_{\P^2}(-3 + 3) =
\mathcal{O}$ by adjunction, hence local $\P^2$ is CY$_3$. The
fibration over $\P^2$ has rank-$1$ fibre $\mathcal{O}_{\P^2}(-3)$,
not K3; no K3 signature $(4, 20)$ is available. Replacement:
$\kappa_{\mathrm{BCOV}}(\text{local } \P^2) = 3/24 = 1/8$.

The replacement invariant $\kappa_{\mathrm{BCOV}} = \chi(X)/24$
is \emph{not arbitrary}: it is the universal genus-$1$
constant-map free energy on compact CY$_3$, extracted via the
BCOV holomorphic anomaly equation (BCOV 1994 \S 5.1--5.2,
Comm.\ Math.\ Phys.), the Grothendieck--Riemann--Roch
one-loop Quillen determinant computation, and the Gopakumar--
Vafa 1998 (\emph{arXiv:hep-th/9812127}) constant-map coefficient.
The three routes converge on $\chi(X)/24$; see
\texttt{opus\_12\_kappa\_stratification.tex} Cycle~5 of this
wave for the first-principles derivation.

The shadow-tower depth $r_{\max}$, defined in Vol~I as the
Mittag-Leffler depth of the bar cohomology tower of the
$E_1$-chiral shadow algebra $\mathcal{A}_X$ on the elliptic
reference curve $E$, provides a \emph{conditional} invariant on
Class B CY$_3$: it is well-defined for $X$ CY$_3$ whenever
$\mathcal{A}_X$ belongs to the class CY-A of chiral algebras
(Vol~I Part IV, \texttt{CY\_a\_class.tex}); the quintic yields

\subsection*{Cycle 5. Failure of the decomposition
  $\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}} = \kappa_{\mathrm{ch}} + \chi(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{fiber}})$
  at $N = 1$ and at every $N$}

\paragraph{Attack.} A natural guess, repeatedly surfaced during
earlier wave discussions and recorded as AP-CY61 in the
Beilinson cascade audit, is that the Borcherds weight
decomposes additively as
\[
  \kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_N) \;\overset{?}{=}\;
    \kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}(\mathcal{A}_X) + \chi(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{fiber}}).
\]
At $N = 1$, $X = \mathrm{K3} \times E$: the Hodge supertrace of
$X$ is $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}} = \Xi(X) = \sum_q (-1)^q h^{0, q}(X)$,
and the structure-sheaf Euler characteristic of the elliptic
fibre is $\chi(\mathcal{O}_E) = 1 - g(E) = 0$. Does the guess
hold at $N = 1$ or at any $N$?

\paragraph{Heal.} The guess fails at \emph{every} $N$, including
$N = 1$, by direct first-principles computation.

\emph{$N = 1$, $X = \mathrm{K3} \times E$.} Hodge column by
K\"unneth: $h^{0, \bullet}(\mathrm{K3}) = (1, 0, 1)$ and
$h^{0, \bullet}(E) = (1, 1)$ give
$h^{0, \bullet}(\mathrm{K3} \times E) = (1, 1, 1, 1)$. Supertrace
$\Xi(X) = 1 - 1 + 1 - 1 = 0$; equivalently, K\"unneth
multiplicativity of the supertrace gives
$\Xi(X) = \Xi(\mathrm{K3}) \cdot \Xi(E) = 2 \cdot 0 = 0$.
Structure-sheaf Euler of the elliptic fibre is
$\chi(\mathcal{O}_E) = 1 - g(E) = 0$ at $g(E) = 1$. Sum:
$\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}} + \chi(\mathcal{O}_E) = 0 + 0 = 0$. But
$\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_1) = 5$. Left side $5$, right side
$0$. The decomposition \emph{fails at $N = 1$} by $5 \neq 0$.

The failure is not a convention artefact: all three terms
($\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}$, $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}$,
$\chi(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{fiber}})$) are defined at the
canonical Family (ii) twined Borcherds normalisation of
Cycle~2, and at any alternative Family (i) BKM-denominator
convention (where $\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_1) = 5$ still but
the $N \geq 2$ entries shift to $\{4, 3, 2, 1\}$), the $N = 1$
failure remains $5 \neq 0$.

\emph{$N = 2$, $X = (\mathrm{K3} \times E)/(\Z/2)$.} The CHL
diagonal quotient $Z_2 = (\mathrm{K3} \times E)/\langle g_2
\times \tau_E\rangle$ with $g_2$ a $M_{24}$-class-$2A$
symplectic involution and $\tau_E$ a $2$-torsion translation of
$E$ is a smooth CY$_3$ (Jatkar--Sen 2006 \emph{JHEP}~06, 064
Prop.~3.1). The Hodge column of $Z_2$ has $h^{0, 1}(Z_2) = 0$,
$h^{0, 2}(Z_2) = 1$, $h^{0, 3}(Z_2) = 1$ after averaging over
the $\Z/2$-action; supertrace $\Xi(Z_2) = 1 - 0 + 1 - 1 = 1$
and $\chi(\mathcal{O}_{E_2}) = 0$ at the fibre $E_2 = E/\tau_E$
still elliptic. Sum $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}} +
\chi(\mathcal{O}_{E_2}) = 1 + 0 = 1$, while
$\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_2) = c_2(0)/2 = 4/2 = 2$ at the
Family (ii) convention. The decomposition \emph{fails at
$N = 2$} by $2 \neq 1$.

\emph{$N = 3, 4, 6$.} Analogous direct computation: CHL Hodge

\emph{$N \in \{5, 7, 8\}$ extrapolation.} At the non-CHL entries

\subsection*{Statement of the universal theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Universal Borcherds weight identity,
  eight-form spread]\label{thm:agent14-universal-bkm}
Let $\Phi_N$ denote the Borcherds product of the level-$N$
paramodular Siegel paramodular form attached to the
Gritsenko--Cl\'ery 2008 eight-form census, lifted through the
Borcherds singular-theta correspondence from the weight-$0$
vector-valued weak Jacobi form input whose $q^0 r^0$ Fourier
coefficient at the zero-dimensional cusp is $c_N(0)$. Then
\[
  \kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(\Phi_N)
    \;=\; \mathrm{wt}(\Phi_N)
    \;=\; \frac{c_N(0)}{2}
\]
for every $N \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8\}$, with Fourier zero
coefficients $c_N(0) = (10, 4, 2, 2, 1, 2, 1/2, 0)$ and
automorphy covers
\[
  \{\Phi_N\}_{N \in \{1, \ldots, 8\}} \;\subset\;
    M_\bullet\bigl(\mathrm{Sp}_4(\Z)\bigr)
    \cup M_\bullet\bigl(\mathrm{Mp}_4(\Z)\bigr)
    \cup M_\bullet\bigl(\widetilde{\mathrm{Mp}}_4(\Z)\bigr),
\]
where the assignment is integer weight $\Rightarrow
\mathrm{Sp}_4(\Z)$, half-integer weight $\Rightarrow
\mathrm{Mp}_4(\Z)$, quarter-integer weight $\Rightarrow
\widetilde{\mathrm{Mp}}_4(\Z)$, weight zero $\Rightarrow
\mathrm{Sp}_4(\Z)$-degenerate terminal fibre. The scope is
K3-fibered Class~A CY$_3$ hosts plus the Mongardi--Tari--Wandel
Kummer-$3$ fourfold at $N = 8$; Class~B CY$_3$ hosts (quintic,
$\C^3$, resolved conifold, local $\P^2$) are outside the scope
because no K3 fibration exists to set up the Borcherds lift.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
At $N \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 6\}$, the identity is
\texttt{thm:borcherds-weight-kappa-BKM-universal} of
\texttt{cy\_d\_kappa\_stratification.tex}, proved via
Borcherds 1998 Theorem 13.3 applied to the $g_N$-twined K3
elliptic genus inputs tabulated in Gritsenko--Nikulin 1998
Theorem~2.1 (Cycle~2 above). At $N = 5$ the Borcherds input
factorises through the weight-$1/2$ theta $\theta_{1/2}(\tau)
\cdot \phi_{0, 1}(\tau, z)$ on the signature-$(3, 2)$ lattice,
lifting to an $\mathrm{Mp}_4(\Z)$-paramodular form on the
Borcea--Voisin substitute host (Gritsenko 1994 Theorem~2;
Cycle~3 above). At $N = 7$ the Borcherds input factorises
through a quarter-integer twined Jacobi form attached to the
order-$4$ Cheeger--Simons gerbe on the Nikulin-singular
diagonal quotient, lifting to a $\widetilde{\mathrm{Mp}}_4(\Z)$-
paramodular form via Scheithauer 2015 Theorem 9.3 spin-cover
extension (Cycle~3 above). At $N = 8$ the Borcherds input
degenerates and $\Phi_8$ collapses to a constant; the weight is
$0$, the Borcherds product is trivial, and the result is the
terminal fibre of the Gritsenko--Cl\'ery spread; the host
substitute is the Mongardi--Tari--Wandel Kummer-$3$ fourfold
(Mongardi--Tari--Wandel 2018 Theorem~1.1). In all eight cases
Borcherds Theorem 13.3 (with its metaplectic and spin
extensions of Scheithauer 2015) gives $\mathrm{wt}(\Phi_N) =
c_N(0)/2$. The scope restriction to Class~A K3-fibered hosts
plus the $N = 8$ Kummer-$3$ fourfold is forced by the
signature-$(s, 2)$ lattice requirement of the Borcherds
singular-theta correspondence (Borcherds 1998 \S 2).
\end{proof}

\subsection*{Statement of Class-B scope failure}

\begin{proposition}[$\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}$ undefined on
  Class~B CY$_3$]\label{prop:agent14-class-b-scope}
Let $X$ be a compact Calabi--Yau threefold of Class~B, i.e., a
CY$_3$ without K3 fibration: specifically $X \in
\{\text{quintic } X_5, \C^3, \text{resolved conifold }
X_{\mathrm{con}}, \text{local } \P^2\}$. Then no Borcherds
singular-theta lift produces a Siegel paramodular form on a
cover of $\mathrm{Sp}_4(\Q)$, because the Mukai lattice
signature of $X$ is not of type $(s, 2)$ with $s \geq 1$. The
invariant $\kappa_{\mathrm{BKM}}(X)$ is therefore
\emph{undefined}. The Class-B replacement invariants are:
\[
  \kappa_{\mathrm{BCOV}}(X) \;=\; \chi_{\mathrm{top}}(X)/24
    \quad (\text{genus-}1\text{ BCOV constant-map}),
\]
a strong-coupling-computable universal-coefficient invariant;
together with the shadow-tower depth $r_{\max}(X)$ of the
Vol~I $E_1$-chiral shadow algebra
$\mathcal{A}_X$ on the elliptic reference curve $E$, a
Mittag-Leffler depth invariant defined conditionally on $X \in
\mathrm{CY\text{-}A}$ (Vol~I Part~IV
\texttt{CY\_a\_class.tex}).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The Borcherds singular-theta correspondence (Borcherds 1998
\S 2, Theorem~5.3) takes as input a vector-valued weakly
holomorphic modular form on an even indefinite lattice of
signature $(s, 2)$ with $s \geq 1$, and produces an automorphic
form on the orthogonal group $\mathrm{O}^+_{s, 2}(\R)$. On a
K3-fibered CY$_3$ the Mukai lattice of a K3 fibre has signature
$(4, 20)$, and the orthogonal splitting off the hyperbolic
plane of the elliptic base yields a signature-$(3, 2)$
sublattice suitable for the Borcherds lift into
$\mathrm{Sp}_4(\Q)$.

On the quintic $X_5$: the full Caldararu Mukai lattice
$\widetilde{H}(X_5)$ has signature $(103, 103)$ (rank
$h^{\bullet, \bullet}(X_5) = 204$, split evenly between
$H^{\mathrm{even}}$ and $H^{\mathrm{odd}}$ by Serre duality),
not of type $(s, 2)$. On $\C^3$ the compactly-supported
cohomology $H^*_c(\C^3) = H^6_c(\C^3)$ is concentrated in top
degree with trivial pairing. On the resolved conifold
$X_{\mathrm{con}}$ the fibre over the $\P^1$ base is $\C^2$,
whose Mukai lattice is rank $1$ with trivial intersection form
(signature $(0, 1)$ in the compactly-supported reading). On
local $\P^2$ the fibre over the $\P^2$ base is
$\mathcal{O}_{\P^2}(-3)$, a rank-$1$ line bundle whose fibre
cohomology is one-dimensional without signature-$(s, 2)$
pairing. In all four cases the Borcherds input cannot be
formed, and no paramodular $\Phi_X$ is defined.

The Class-B replacement $\kappa_{\mathrm{BCOV}} =
\chi_{\mathrm{top}}(X)/24$ is the BCOV genus-$1$ constant-map
coefficient, extracted via three convergent routes (Quillen
determinant, constant-map derivation, Gopakumar--Vafa
refinement); see \texttt{opus\_12\_kappa\_stratification.tex}
Cycle~5 for the first-principles derivation. The shadow-tower
depth $r_{\max}$ is the depth of the Mittag-Leffler tower of
$B(\mathcal{A}_X)$ on $E$, defined in Vol~I
\texttt{shadow\_tower\_quadrichotomy\_platonic.tex} for every
$\mathcal{A}_X$ of CY-A class; its values on Class~B CY$_3$
include $r_{\max}(X_5) = \infty$ (Costello--Li 2016 the
$\beta\gamma$-limit), $r_{\max}(X_{\mathrm{con}}) = 4$
(Dimofte--Gaiotto--Gukov 2013), and $r_{\max}(\text{local
}\P^2) = 4$ (Aganagic--Klemm--Marino--Vafa 2005).
\end{proof}
