## Converged theorem surface

The repair is to distinguish the two invariants everywhere:

- `\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}^{\mathrm{Hodge}}(X)
   := \sum_{q=0}^{d} (-1)^q h^{0,q}(X) = \chi(\cO_X)`
- bare `\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}` in Vol III means the operational
  K\"unneth-additive chiral characteristic, i.e. the route-B / Heisenberg /
  Mukai-rank convention used in the product computations

The Beauville--Bogomolov stratification then becomes:

- odd `d`: `\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}^{\mathrm{Hodge}}=0` by Serre pairing, while
  bare `\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}` can remain nonzero; `K3 \times E` gives the
  witness `0` versus `3`
- even strict-CY: the Hodge comparator is the genuine Serre-duality endpoint
  contribution `1 + (-1)^d = 2`; this coincides with bare
  `\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}` only on the `d=2` K3 slice
- even holomorphic-symplectic: the Hodge comparator is generated by
  `1,\sigma,\dots,\sigma^n`, hence equals `n+1` in dimension `2n`; this is a
  genuinely different branch from the generic strict-CY branch

The naive identity `\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}=\chi(\cO_X)` must therefore be
removed as the global CY-D statement. It survives only on the proved
`d=2`, `\HH_{-1}=0` slice.

```tex
\begin{theorem}[CY-D tri-stratum theorem]
\label{thm:cy-d-tri-stratum}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere
Let $X$ be a compact CY$_d$ manifold. Write
\[
  \kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}^{\mathrm{Hodge}}(X)
  := \sum_{q=0}^{d} (-1)^q h^{0,q}(X)
\]
for the Hodge-supertrace scalar, and keep bare
$\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}$ for the operational K\"unneth-additive chiral
characteristic used in Vol~III. Then the Beauville--Bogomolov
classification yields three comparison strata.
\begin{enumerate}
 \item[\textup{(I)}] \emph{Odd-$d$ stratum.}
       If $d$ is odd, then
       $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}^{\mathrm{Hodge}}(X)=0$ by Serre pairing
       $h^{0,q}=h^{0,d-q}$. This does \emph{not} force bare
       $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}$ to vanish: the K\"unneth-additive branch
       survives. In particular,
       $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}^{\mathrm{Hodge}}(K3 \times E)=0$ while
       $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}(K3 \times E)=3$.
 \item[\textup{(II)}] \emph{Even-$d$ strict-CY stratum.}
       If $d$ is even and $X$ has holonomy exactly $\mathrm{SU}(d)$,
       then the Hodge comparator is the genuine Serre-duality endpoint
       contribution
       \[
         \kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}^{\mathrm{Hodge}}(X)
         = h^{0,0}(X)+(-1)^d h^{0,d}(X)=2.
       \]
       This is the branch on which CY-D$_2$ lives; the coincidence with
       bare $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}$ is proved at $d=2$ and should not be
       extrapolated automatically to higher even dimension.
 \item[\textup{(III)}] \emph{Even-$d$ holomorphic-symplectic stratum.}
       If $X$ is irreducible holomorphic-symplectic of dimension
       $d=2n$, then the Hodge comparator is controlled by the powers of
       the symplectic form:
       \[
         \kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}^{\mathrm{Hodge}}(X)=n+1.
       \]
       This branch is therefore distinct from the generic strict-CY
       branch and remembers the symplectic structure.
\end{enumerate}
In particular, the global identity
$\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}=\chi(\cO_X)$ is false outside the proved
CY$_2$ slice. The product obstruction is structural: bare
$\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}$ is K\"unneth-additive, whereas
$\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}^{\mathrm{Hodge}}=\chi(\cO_X)$ is
K\"unneth-multiplicative.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Stratum \textup{(I)} is Serre cancellation on the
$\cO_X$-column. Stratum \textup{(II)} follows because strict even CY
has only the endpoint holomorphic forms in degrees $0$ and $d$.
Stratum \textup{(III)} follows because the only holomorphic forms are
$1,\sigma,\dots,\sigma^n$. The obstruction statement is witnessed by
$K3 \times E$: K\"unneth gives
$h^{0,\bullet}(K3 \times E)=(1,1,1,1)$ and hence
$\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}^{\mathrm{Hodge}}=0$, while the Vol~III
K\"unneth-additive convention gives
$\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}(K3 \times E)
=\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}(K3)+\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}}(E)=2+1=3$.
\end{proof}
```
