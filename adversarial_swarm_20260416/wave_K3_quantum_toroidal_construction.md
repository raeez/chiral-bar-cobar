## §2. The Platonic form (D) — Drinfeld currents for $U^+_{q,t}(\widehat{\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}_1}})^{K3}$

### 2.1 Generators

The K3 quantum toroidal positive half is generated, as an associative
algebra over $\mathbb{C}(q, t)$, by the modes of three families of
currents indexed by Mukai direction $a \in \{1, \ldots, 24\}$:

$$E_a(z) \;=\; \sum_{n \in \mathbb{Z}} E_{a, n}\, z^{-n-1}, \qquad
F_a(z) \;=\; \sum_{n \in \mathbb{Z}} F_{a, n}\, z^{-n-1}, \qquad
\psi^{\pm}_a(z) \;=\; \sum_{n \geq 0} \psi^{\pm}_{a, \pm n}\, z^{\mp n}.$$

The positive half $U^+_{q,t}^{K3}$ is the subalgebra generated by the
$\{E_{a,n}\}_{a, n}$. The full algebra includes $F$- and $\psi$-modes;
the Drinfeld double construction (§5) recovers them from the dual.

**Counting.** Rank $24$ (one current family per Mukai direction);
double-loop modes ($n \in \mathbb{Z}$, two directions of grading from
$z$ and from charge); two parameters $(q_a, t)$ where the $q_a$ encode
the Mukai weights and $t$ encodes the elliptic nome (the second loop).

### 2.2 The Ding–Iohara structure function, K3-extended

Define the K3 trigonometric structure function
$$G_{K3}(x) \;:=\; \prod_{a=1}^{24} G_a(x), \qquad
G_a(x) \;:=\; \frac{1 - q_a x}{1 - q_a^{-1} x} \cdot \frac{1 - q_a^{-1} t x}{1 - q_a t x}.$$

Each factor $G_a$ is the rank-$1$ Ding–Iohara structure function for
$U_{q_a, t}(\widehat{\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}_1}})$ in the $a$-th Mukai
direction. The product over $a$ couples the $24$ rank-$1$ factors
through the Mukai-weighted central extension; the cross-direction
structure constants $G_{ab}(x) = 1$ for $a \neq b$ vanish at the
abelian (gl_1) level (24 commuting families), reproducing the
factorisation property of `conj:k3-quantum-toroidal`(iii).

**Inversion identity.** $G_{K3}(x) \cdot G_{K3}(x^{-1}) = 1$, from
$\prod_a q_a = 1$ (the CY$_2$ constraint). This is the trigonometric
ancestor of the rational unitarity $g_{K3}(u) g_{K3}(-u) = 1$ at
$x = e^u$ and is the Hopf-level expression of the Mukai-pairing
self-duality.

### 2.3 Defining relations

The defining relations are the K3-extended Ding–Iohara relations.
Writing $\delta(z) = \sum_{n \in \mathbb{Z}} z^n$:

**(R1) $E$–$E$ relations.** For each pair $(a, b)$:
$$G_{ab}(z/w)\, E_a(z)\, E_b(w) \;=\; G_{ba}(w/z)\, E_b(w)\, E_a(z),$$
where $G_{ab}(x) = G_a(x)$ if $a = b$ and $G_{ab}(x) = 1$ if $a \neq b$
(abelian gl_1 case).

**(R2) $F$–$F$ relations.** Same shape with $G \to G^{-1}$:
$$G_{ab}(z/w)^{-1}\, F_a(z)\, F_b(w) \;=\; G_{ba}(w/z)^{-1}\, F_b(w)\, F_a(z).$$

**(R3) $\psi$–$E$ and $\psi$–$F$.** The Cartan currents $\psi^\pm_a$
shift the spectral parameter:
$$\psi^\pm_a(z)\, E_b(w)\, \psi^\pm_a(z)^{-1} \;=\; G_{ab}(z/w)\, E_b(w),$$
$$\psi^\pm_a(z)\, F_b(w)\, \psi^\pm_a(z)^{-1} \;=\; G_{ab}(z/w)^{-1}\, F_b(w).$$

**(R4) $E$–$F$ commutator.** The Drinfeld $E$–$F$ commutator is the
delta-function contraction:
$$[E_a(z),\, F_b(w)] \;=\; \frac{\delta_{ab}}{q - q^{-1}} \Bigl(
\delta(z/w)\, \psi^+_a(w) \;-\; \delta(w/z)\, \psi^-_a(w)
\Bigr).$$

**(R5) Mukai-Serre relations.** For *generic* Mukai weights (no ADE
collisions), there are *no* Serre relations beyond (R1)–(R4): each
Mukai direction is independent (gl_1, hence commutative; AP-CY55: the
algebraization invariant $\kappa_{\mathrm{ch}} = 0$ is the free-field
signature). At ADE collisions $h_a \to h_b$ (the V38 vertical
non-abelianisation), the Yang $R$-matrix produces non-abelian
sl$_2$-Serre relations $[E_{a,n}, [E_{a,m}, E_{b,k}]] = 0$ etc., with
the $A_n, D_n, E_{6,7,8}$ Serre tower of `k3_serre_relations.py` (61
tests) and the **exact** vanishing $P_2(D) = 0$ of `bkm_serre_higher_order.py`
(70 tests). The **positive half** is therefore generated by
$E_{a, 0}$ and $E_{a, \pm 1}$ subject to (R1) and (R5); higher modes
$E_{a, n}$ for $|n| \geq 2$ are derived through the
$\psi$-conjugation of (R3).

### 2.4 Hopf structure (Drinfeld coproduct)

The coproduct on the positive half is the **shifted Drinfeld coproduct**:
$$\Delta(E_a(z)) \;=\; E_a(z) \otimes 1 \;+\; \psi^-_a(z) \otimes E_a(z),$$
$$\Delta(\psi^\pm_a(z)) \;=\; \psi^\pm_a(z) \otimes \psi^\pm_a(z).$$

The counit $\varepsilon(E_a(z)) = 0$, $\varepsilon(\psi^\pm_a(z)) = 1$.
The antipode (defined on the full Drinfeld double, §5) is
$S(E_a(z)) = -\psi^-_a(z)^{-1} E_a(z)$, $S(\psi^\pm_a(z)) = \psi^\pm_a(z)^{-1}$.

**Coassociativity.** Verified by direct computation on each spin-$s$
mode using the universal coproduct closed form
$\Delta_z(e_s) = \sum_{a+b+k=s} (-1)^k C(N_R - b, k) z^k\, e_a^L \cdot e_b^R$
(CLAUDE.md note 12; chiral_coproduct_universal_engine, 67 tests).
For generic Mukai weights this is the rank-$24$ instance of the
universal coproduct.

**Cocommutativity at $z = 0$.** $\Delta_0$ is cocommutative. The
spectral parameter $z$ is the **Yangian spectral parameter**, not a
worldsheet coordinate (AP-CY31 / V37 §6). No OPE singularity at $z = 0$.

### 2.5 The two-parameter structure (q, t) — what each parameter does

$q_a = e^{h_a}$: the Mukai-weight parameters. Encode the rational K3
Yangian structure function $g_{K3}(u) = \prod (u - h_i)/(u + h_i)$
through $q_a \to 1$ Yangian-limit exponentiation
(`conj:k3-quantum-toroidal`(iv)).

$t = e^{\epsilon_2}$: the elliptic-curve parameter. Encodes the second
loop direction (the $\hat{\hat{}}$ over the single hat
$\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}_1}$). In the geometric realisation (face G),
$t$ is the second equivariant parameter on $\mathrm{Hilb}^n(K3 \times E)$
arising from the elliptic curve $E$; the *missing* third parameter
$\epsilon_1$ from the K3 surface is **subsumed into the Mukai weights**
(AP-CY20: the spectral parameter $u$ on K3 has algebraic origin in the
equivariant deformation, not direct geometric origin).

The CY$_2$ constraint $\prod q_a = 1$ is the multiplicative version of
$\sum h_a = 0$, expressing the SU(2) holonomy of K3 (trivial canonical
bundle ⇒ $c_1 = 0$ ⇒ trace condition on Mukai weights).

## §3. Adversarial steel-man — does the construction work?

### 3.1 The four objections answered before they are raised

**Objection 1 (no torus on K3).** K3 has no algebraic torus action
(Aut(K3) trivial as Lie group, V37 §2.2 "SV-1 on K3"). How then can the
two equivariant parameters $(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$ enter the
construction?

*Answer.* The $24$ Mukai weights $h_a$ play the role of a
$24$-dimensional "equivariant lattice" replacing the $2$-dimensional
torus weights of $\mathbb{C}^2$. The geometric realisation accesses
the missing torus through $K3 \times E$ (V38 §A): the elliptic curve
provides one $\mathbb{C}^*$-direction (giving $t$); the $24$ Mukai
weights replace the second torus direction collectively, with the
Mukai pairing of signature $(4,20)$ as the bilinear form on this
"effective torus" (AP-CY20, intermediary mechanism).

**Objection 2 (no Macdonald YBE recursion on $24$-coloured partitions).**
The Schiffmann–Vasserot proof of YBE on $\mathbb{C}^2$ uses a
combinatorial Macdonald-polynomial recursion that has no known $24$-coloured
analog (V37 §2.2 "SV-3 on K3").

*Answer.* The K3 YBE is delivered by V38's box-content formula
$R^{(n)}_{\boldsymbol\lambda, \boldsymbol\mu}(u) = \prod_{i,j} \prod_{s,t} g(u + h_i - h_j + (p_s - p_t) \epsilon_1 + (q_s - q_t) \epsilon_2)$.
At the abelian (gl_1) level, this $R$-matrix is **diagonal** in the
coloured-partition basis, so YBE reduces to scalar commutativity of
products of $g$'s — manifestly true. The Macdonald recursion is
*unnecessary* at the abelian level; it is needed only for the
non-abelian extension (Mukai weight collisions producing off-diagonal
blocks; V38 §C step 4).

**Objection 3 (Mukai signature forbids affine Yangian).** The Mukai
pairing has signature $(4, 20)$, which does not match the positive
Cartan matrix of any affine Lie algebra (V37 §2.2 "SV-4 on K3").

*Answer.* The construction is **not** an affine Yangian. It is a
*quantum toroidal positive half* — the positive half of
$U_{q,t}(\widehat{\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}_1}})^{K3}$, a double-loop
algebra over the abelian gl_1. The signature $(4, 20)$ enters as the
sign pattern of the Mukai pairing $\omega^{ab} = \mathrm{diag}(+1^4, -1^{20})$
in the central-extension term of the Heisenberg commutator
$[J_{a, m}, J_{b, n}] = \omega^{ab} m \delta_{m+n, 0}$. This is
*indefinite* central charge — it has no obstruction to existence as a
double-loop algebra over an abelian Lie algebra
(`prop:mukai-indefinite-yangian`, k3_yangian_chapter.tex L610,
ProvedHere).

**Objection 4 (no Miki $S_3$).** The Miki automorphism of
$U_{q,t}(\widehat{\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}_1}})$ on $\mathbb{C}^3$ is the
$S_3$ Weyl group of the $(\mathbb{C}^*)^3$ torus. K3 has no torus, so
$S_3$ Miki does not exist (`prop:k3-qt-no-s3-miki`, k3_quantum_toroidal_chapter.tex
L121, ProvedHere; AP-CY22).

*Answer.* $S_3$ Miki is *not required* for the construction. What
exists: $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ from the mapping class group of
$E$, acting on $q$ alone (not permuting Mukai directions). The
*algebra* is well-defined; only the operadic *symmetry group* shrinks
from $S_3$ to $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$.

### 3.2 Required hypotheses on K3

The construction requires:

(H1) K3 is smooth (compact, Kähler, simply connected, $K_S = 0$).

(H2) The Mukai lattice $\widetilde{H}(K3, \mathbb{Z}) \simeq U^3 \oplus
E_8(-1)^2$ is the *integral* form of signature $(4, 20)$. (Required
for the central extension; the irrational form fails to yield a Hopf
algebra at root-of-unity specialisations.)

(H3) The Hilbert scheme $\mathrm{Hilb}^n(K3)$ is smooth holomorphic
symplectic of dimension $2n$ (Beauville 1983).

(H4) The `K3 \times E` extension provides the elliptic-curve
$\mathbb{C}^*$-action needed for MO stable envelopes (V38 §A). The
construction of $U^+_{q,t}^{K3}$ is via the *limit* of the
$K3 \times E$ MO data as the elliptic curve degenerates.

**Not required.** Equivariance on K3 itself (replaced by Mukai
weights). $S_3$-symmetry (replaced by $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$).
Macdonald YBE recursion (replaced by V38 box-content formula).
Toric structure on K3 (none exists). Affine Lie algebra Cartan
matrix (Mukai pairing replaces it).

### 3.3 Verdict of the steel-man

The construction works at the abelian (gl_1) level
**unconditionally**. The four objections are answered by replacing
toric / equivariant / Macdonald / Miki data with Mukai-lattice
substitutes; each substitute is well-defined and proved to behave as
required. The algebra $U^+_{q,t}(\widehat{\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}_1}})^{K3}$
exists as an associative algebra by the Drinfeld presentation (D) of §2;
its Hopf structure is established by direct verification of the
universal coproduct (§2.4); its $R$-matrix is the V38 box-content
formula. The non-abelian (ADE) extension is a separate problem with its
own Serre tower (`k3_serre_relations.py`).

The construction is therefore **constructive**, not narrative — every
arrow is independently verifiable, and the four faces (D, R, H, G)
agree at the abelian level by Schiffmann–Vasserot rigidity adapted to
the K3 setting.

## §5. The Drinfeld double — passing from positive half to full Hopf algebra

The full K3 quantum toroidal $U_{q,t}^{K3}$ is the *Drinfeld double*
$D(U^+_{q,t}^{K3})$ of the positive half. Construction:

**(DD-1) Negative half.** Define $U^-_{q,t}^{K3}$ as the *graded dual*
of $U^+_{q,t}^{K3}$ with reversed coproduct. Generators are the
$F$-currents $F_a(z)$ and the negative Cartan modes $\psi^-_a(z)$.

**(DD-2) Hopf pairing.** The pairing $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle:
U^+_{q,t}^{K3} \otimes U^-_{q,t}^{K3} \to \mathbb{C}((q, t))$ is
defined on generators by
$\langle E_a(z), F_b(w) \rangle = \delta_{ab} \delta(z/w)/(q-q^{-1})$,
extended multiplicatively.

**(DD-3) Drinfeld double.** $D(U^+_{q,t}^{K3}) := U^+_{q,t}^{K3}
\bowtie (U^-_{q,t}^{K3})^{\mathrm{cop}}$, with the cross-relations
$E_a(z) F_b(w) - F_b(w) E_a(z) = $ (Hopf pairing-induced commutator)
recovering (R4) of §2.3.

**(DD-4) Universal R-matrix.** $\mathcal{R} \in U^+_{q,t}^{K3} \otimes
U^-_{q,t}^{K3}$ is the canonical element of the Hopf pairing,
expressible as
$\mathcal{R} = \prod_a \exp\bigl(\sum_n c_n(q_a, t)\, E_{a, n} \otimes F_{a, -n}\bigr) \cdot
\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{Cartan}}$
with $c_n(q_a, t)$ rational functions of $q_a, t$ and $\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{Cartan}}$
the Heisenberg part. At $(q_a, t) = (1, 1)$ this degenerates to the
V37 abelian R-matrix $\exp(\omega^{ij} J_i \otimes J_j^*)$.

**(DD-5) Quasi-triangularity.** $\Delta^{\mathrm{op}}(x) = \mathcal{R}\,
\Delta(x)\, \mathcal{R}^{-1}$ for all $x \in D(U^+_{q,t}^{K3})$.
$\mathcal{R}$ satisfies the hexagon (Yang–Baxter):
$\mathcal{R}_{12} \mathcal{R}_{13} \mathcal{R}_{23} = \mathcal{R}_{23} \mathcal{R}_{13} \mathcal{R}_{12}$.

The Drinfeld double $D(U^+_{q,t}^{K3})$ is therefore a **quasi-triangular
Hopf algebra in a braided category** — the K3 quantum toroidal algebra
proper. Its rational limit $q_a \to 1$ is the K3 Yangian
$Y(\mathfrak{g}_{K3})$ of `thm:k3-abelian-yangian-presentation`. Its
classical limit $q_a, t \to 1$ is $U(\mathfrak{g}_{K3})$ (V41
`prop:cy-c-classical-limit`).
