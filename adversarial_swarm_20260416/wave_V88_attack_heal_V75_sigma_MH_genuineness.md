### A4. Chain-level vs $E_2$-page commutativity

**Attack.** V73 asserts that $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{wt}}$ and
$\varepsilon_{\mathrm{par}}$ commute "because they act on disjoint
structural data" (worldsheet vs target). This is a heuristic, not a
chain-level proof. Chain-level commutativity requires:

(C1) $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{wt}}$ is a chain map: $[b + B,
\varepsilon_{\mathrm{wt}}] = 0$.
(C2) $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{par}}$ is a chain map: $[b + B,
\varepsilon_{\mathrm{par}}] = 0$.
(C3) The two chain maps commute: $[\varepsilon_{\mathrm{wt}},
\varepsilon_{\mathrm{par}}] = 0$.

For (C1): $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{wt}}$ is grading-by-ghost-number-parity.
The Hochschild differential $b$ shifts degree by $-1$ on the bar
complex. Crucially, $b = \sum_k b_k$ involves $A_\infty$-operations
$m_k$ which preserve ghost number (the BRST charge is intrinsic to the
chiral algebra). So $b$ commutes with $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{wt}}$ iff
$b$ preserves ghost-number parity, which holds because $m_k$ is
ghost-number-zero. The Connes $B$ operator is a degree-$+1$ map that
also preserves ghost-number (it cyclically permutes tensor factors).
Hence (C1) holds.

For (C2): $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{par}}$ is the Mukai-norm-parity, which
is constant on each Hodge piece $H^{p,q}(K3)$. The differential $b + B$
on the chiral Hochschild complex of $\Phi(K3)$ preserves Hodge bidegree
*at the $E_2$ page* of the Hodge--de Rham spectral sequence (Deligne
1968 K3 Hodge--de Rham degeneration). At the chain level, $b + B$ may
shift Hodge bidegree, but the Hodge filtration is preserved. For
chain-level commutativity, we need $b + B$ to preserve the
Mukai-norm-parity, which requires the Mukai pairing to be invariant
under the chain-level action.

This is where the Deligne $E_2$-degeneration enters. The Hodge--de Rham
spectral sequence for K3 degenerates at $E_2$ (Deligne 1968). This
means the chain-level Hochschild differential $b + B$, which a priori
shifts the Hodge filtration, in fact preserves the Hodge bidegree
*after restricting to the $E_2$ page*. Since the K3 Hodge structure has
no extension classes (no obstructions to lifting from $E_2$ to
$E_\infty$), the chain-level model can be chosen to make $b + B$
preserve the Hodge bidegree exactly. Under such a choice, (C2) holds at
the chain level.

For (C3): both $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{wt}}$ and $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{par}}$
are diagonal endomorphisms in compatible bases. Diagonal endomorphisms
in the same basis commute. The bases are compatible because the
ghost-number grading and the Hodge-bidegree grading are *both* induced
from the same underlying K3 Hodge structure: ghost-number $= p - q$,
Hodge-bidegree $= (p, q)$. So the two gradings refine each other, and
the two involutions are diagonal in the joint Hodge-bidegree basis.

**Verdict.** Chain-level commutativity (C1)+(C2)+(C3) holds for K3
*conditional on the choice of chain-level model that respects the
$E_2$-degenerated Hodge--de Rham spectral sequence*. This is a
non-trivial choice but it exists by Deligne 1968. For non-K3 inputs
where Hodge--de Rham degeneration is conditional (e.g. quintic with
$h^{2,1} = 101$ extension classes), the chain-level commutativity does
*not* automatically hold; one obtains *cohomology-level* commutativity
instead, with the discrepancy controlled by $\xi(A)$.

**Ghost theorem (AP-CY61).** What is right: V73's heuristic
"worldsheet vs target disjointness" is the correct intuition, and
chain-level commutativity holds for K3. What is wrong: V73 does not
flag the dependence on $E_2$-degeneration; the assertion is not
automatic for non-K3 inputs. The correct statement: **chain-level
commutativity of $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{wt}}$ and $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{par}}$
holds for K3 by Deligne's $E_2$-degeneration of Hodge--de Rham, and
fails (degenerates to cohomology-level) for inputs where Hodge--de Rham
does not degenerate at $E_2$**. The K3 case is exceptional in admitting
a chain-level $V_4$-structure; the general CY case has $V_4$ only at
cohomology level.
